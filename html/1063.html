SUBJECT: LATE WEEKEND ZIMBABWE SITUATION

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR MCDONALD FOR REASONS 1.5 (B),

(D)

1. (U) AS OF THE MORNING OF APRIL 24 HARARE IS QUIET.

THE WEEKEND BOMBING IN THE CITY CENTER (REFTEL) RAISED

FEARS AMONG CITY DWELLERS, BUT THE GENERALLY LESSENING

TENSION ON THE FARMS APPEARS TO HAVE MORE THAN OFFSET

THIS AS ZIMBABWE PREPARES TO RETURN TO WORK TOMORROW

AFTER A FOUR-DAY EASTER HOLIDAY. THERE HAS BEEN NO MORE

VIOLENCE IN HARARE SINCE THE BOMBING.

2. (U) THE BOTTOM LINE FROM THE RURAL AREAS IS THAT

THE SITUATION IS GENERALLY CALMER THAN IT WAS LAST WEEK.

THERE ARE, HOWEVER, SIGNIFICANT CAVEATS: NEW FARM

OCCUPATIONS CONTINUE, AT LEAST ONE COMMERCIAL FARMER AT

THE TIME OF THIS WRITING IS BARRICADED IN HIS HOME, AND

BLACK FARM WORKERS ARE SUFFERING SIGNIFICANTLY FROM

BEATINGS AND INTIMIDATION.

3. (U) THE PICTURE WE GET FROM OUR PHONE CONVERSATIONS

WITH REPRESENTATIVES OF THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION IS

GOOD BUT MIXED. CLEARLY DIFFERENT AREAS OF THE COUNTRY

ARE REPORTING DIFFERENT LEVELS OF TENSION. THE BEST

NEWS IS THAT THE FARMERS EAST OF HARARE, WHO HAD

EVACUATED THE AREA AFTER THE KILLING OF THE FIRST

COMMERCIAL FARMER, WILL ALL HAVE RETURNED TO THEIR FARMS

CONFIDENTIAL

BY THE END OF TODAY. ALSO ON A POSITIVE NOTE, THE

POLICE ARE BEGINNING TO PLAY A HELPFUL ROLE, EVEN ON THE

FARMS WHERE THE OCCUPATIONS ARE CONTINUING.

4. (C) THE WORST NEWS IS THAT THE BEATINGS OF FARM

WORKERS CONTINUE. ACCORDING TO ONE OF OUR SOURCES, THE

BEATINGS TAKE PLACE AT NIGHT AND APPEAR POLITICALLY

MOTIVATED. THE VICTIMS ARE BEING TOLD TO TURN IN THEIR

MEMBERSHIP CARDS FOR THE MOVEMENT FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE,

THE MAIN POLITICAL OPPOSITION PARTY. THEY ALSO ARE

BEING TOLD THAT THE OWNER OF THE FARM ON WHICH THEY WORK

WILL REFUND TO THEM THEIR MDC MEMBERSHIP FEE. THESE

STORIES SOUND PLAUSIBLE TO US, AS THE ENTIRE FARM

OCCUPATION SCHEME APPEARS TO HAVE BEEN A MEANS OF

POLITICAL INTIMIDATION FROM THE BEGINNING.

5. (U) ALSO ON THE BAD NEWS FRONT, ONE OPERATOR OF A

LARGE COMMERCIAL FARM NORTH OF HARARE IS BARRICADED

INSIDE HIS HOME. SOURCES SAY A LARGE CROWD--ESTIMATES

RANGE FROM SEVERAL HUNDRED UPWARDS TO A THOUSAND--

CONVERGED ON HIS ESTATE YESTERDAY. NEGOTIATIONS HAVE

BEEN ONGOING, HOWEVER, AND SOURCES WITH WHOM WE SPOKE

SAY IT APPEARS THE SITUATION WILL BE RESOLVED

PEACEFULLY.

MCDONALD
