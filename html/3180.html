SUBJECT: WOMAN IN DEFENSE: ITALY\'S EXPERIENCE AFTER TWO

YEARS

1. SUMMARY. AS THE GOI ATTEMPTS TO RESTRUCTURE AND

"PROFESSIONALIZE" THE MILITARY SERVICE, BRINGING FEMALES IN

IS VIEWED AS ONE WAY TO IMPROVE THE QUALITY OF THE ARMED

FORCES. ITALIAN WOMEN, WHETHER FOR ECONOMIC OR SOCIAL

REASONS, ARE HIGHLY MOTIVATED AND HAVE BEEN INTEGRATED

QUICKLY INTO THE MILITARY RANKS. END SUMMARY.

2. THE RECENTLY FOUNDED CENTER FOR STUDY OF DEFENSE AND

SECURITY ("CENTRO STUDI DIFESA E SICUREZZA) DEDICATED ITS

FIRST CONFERENCE ON 19 JUNE TO THE TOPIC OF "WOMEN IN THE

ARMED FORCES AND POLICE." PRESIDED BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE

DEFENSE COMMITTEE IN THE LOWER HOUSE OF PARLIAMENT, LUIGI

RAMPONI, THE CONFERENCE DREW REPRESENTATIVES FROM THE

MILITARY (INCLUDING A LARGE CONTINGENT OF UNIFORMED FEMALE

OFFICERS), POLICE, GOVERNMENT AND PRESS.

3. ITALY IS ONE OF THE LAST NATO MEMBERS TO ACCEPT FEMALES

IN THE ARMED FORCES. VOLUNTARY FEMALE MILITARY SERVICE WAS

INTRODUCED IN AN OCTOBER 20, 1999 LAW. WITH THE OFFICIAL

UNEMPLOYMENT RATE AT 9.2 PERCENT AND ITALIAN WOMAN HAVING THE

HIGHEST NUMBER OF WORKING HOURS AT HOME IN EUROPE, MANY SEE

MILITARY SERVICE AS A WAY TO EARN A LIVING AND TO ESCAPE

TRADITIONAL FEMALE ROLES. WHILE MANY NEW ENTRANTS ARE STILL

IN MILITARY ACADEMIES (ONE WOMAN IN THE AUDIENCE WAS

APPLAUDED FOR BEING AT THE TOP OF HER CLASS), THE EXPERIENCE

SO FAR SEEMS TO HAVE BEEN POSITIVE, WITH FEW PROBLEMS.

4. ACCORDING TO MINISTER FOR EQUAL OPPORTUNITY STEFANIA

PRESTIGIACOMO, HOWEVER, THE 1999 LAW AND SUBSEQUENT

REGULATIONS DESIGNED TO AVOID DISCRIMINATION ARE NOT ENOUGH

-- ITALY\'S TRADITIONAL CULTURAL VIEW OF WOMEN AS HOMEMAKERS

ALSO NEEDS TO CHANGE. PRESTIGIACOMO SAID THAT ITALIAN

SOCIETY AND THE ARMED FORCES HAVE ACCEPTED WOMEN WELL, BUT

THAT THE INSTITUTIONAL FRAMEWORK IS LAGGING. SHE SUGGESTED

THAT, DRAWING ON THE EXPERIENCE OF INTERNATIONAL

ORGANIZATIONS, THE GOI CAN MAKE NUMEROUS SMALL CHANGES IN THE

AREAS OF INSURANCE, SCHOOLING, AND HOUSING THAT WILL LEVEL

THE PLAYING FIELD FOR WOMEN WHOSE MILITARY CAREERS WOULD

OTHERWISE ENTAIL GREAT SACRIFICES FOR THEMSELVES AND THEIR

FAMILIES. CITING THE EXAMPLE OF ONE WOMAN WHO LOST HER PLACE

ON THE ADMISSIONS LIST BECAUSE SHE BECAME PREGNANT,

PRESTIGIACOMO STRESSED THAT THE MILITARY MUST BECOME MORE

"HUMAN."

5. ALTHOUGH MOST OF THE CONFERENCE DEALT WITH THE ISSUES

AFFECTING WOMEN IN THE MILITARY, SEVERAL SPEAKERS ALSO

ADDRESSED THE PROBLEM OF REVERSE DISCRIMINATION. THERE IS

SOME CONCERN AMONG THE MILITARY RANKS THAT WOMEN ARE BEING

AFFORDED SPECIAL TREATMENT TO THE DETRIMENT OF THEIR MALE

COUNTERPARTS. OVERALL, HOWEVER, THE CONSENSUS WAS THAT, TWO

YEARS LATER, THE ITALIAN EXPERIENCE WITH WOMEN IN THE

MILITARY HAS BEEN POSITIVE.
