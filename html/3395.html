SUBJECT: PROTESTANT SEMINARY TO OPEN?

REF: HO CHI MINH CITY 630

1. (U) CLASSIFIED BY POL/C SCOTT BELLARD, REASON: 1.5

(D)

2. (C) DURING A LUNCHEON WITH POL/C AND POLOFF ON JULY

26, ACTING HEAD OF THE EVANGELICAL CHURCH OF VIETNAM-

NORTH (ECVN) REVEREND AU QUANG VINH (PLEASE PROTECT)

SAID THAT THE EVANGELICAL CHURCH OF VIETNAM-SOUTH (ECVS

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HANOI 01885 290930Z

-- RECOGNIZED BY THE GVN ONLY IN 2001) WAS PREPARING TO

OPEN A SEMINARY IN HO CHI MINH CITY IN SEPTEMBER 2002,

ALTHOUGH IT STILL HAD NOT LOCATED A SUITABLE SITE.

(ITS PRE-1975 CAMPUS IN NHA TRANG IS NO LONGER

AVAILABLE, HE NOTED.) REVEREND VINH PREDICTED THE

NUMBER OF STUDENTS WOULD BE ABOUT 100. HE EXPRESSED

CONCERN, HOWEVER, THAT THE GVN\'S PERMISSION APPEARS TO

BE ONLY APPLICABLE TO THIS ONE CLASS; IT IS FAR FROM

CLEAR WHETHER THE SEMINARY WILL BE ABLE TO CONTINUE

AFTER THE GRADUATION OF THAT CLASS. HE POINTED TO THE

EXPERIENCE OF THE ECVN\'S SEMINARY IN HANOI, WHICH BOTH

VINH AND HIS WIFE ATTENDED; THE HANOI SEMINARY CLOSED

IN 1993 AFTER PRODUCING ONLY ONE CLASS OF 12 GRADUATES.

HE NOTED THAT THE GVN CONTINUED TO BLOCK THE ECVN\'S

EFFORTS TO GET ITS SEMINARY GOING AGAIN (UNDER THE

PRETEXT THAT THERE WAS A LACK OF QUALIFIED INSTRUCTORS)

OR EVEN TO HOLD A GENERAL MEETING OF THE ECVN

LEADERSHIP (THE LAST MEETING WAS HELD IN 1988).

3. (C) REVEREND VINH CONFIRMED OTHER REPORTS (REFTEL)

THAT THE LATE HEAD OF THE ECVS, REVEREND PHAM XUAN

THIEU, HAD COME UNDER MUCH CRITICISM WITHIN THE ECVS

FOR HAVING PARTICIPATED IN A MAY DELEGATION OF GVN AND

RELIGIOUS OFFICIALS TO THE U.S. HE SAID THAT REVEREND

THIEU (WHOM HE DESCRIBED AS ONE OF HIS OWN FORMER

TEACHERS) HAD FELT THAT HE HAD NO CHOICE BUT TO

PARTICIPATE, BUT WAS CLEARLY UNCOMFORTABLE IN BEING

PART OF THIS DELEGATION.

4. (C) REVEREND VINH ALSO EXPRESSED CONTINUED CONCERN

OVER GVN SUPERVISION OF ECVN ACTIVITIES. HE NOTED THAT

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HANOI 01885 290930Z

HE HAD BEEN EFFECTIVELY BARRED FROM TRAVELING TO LAO

CAI PROVINCE, FOR EXAMPLE, DUE TO EARLIER "PROBLEMS"

(WHICH HE DID NOT WISH TO DESCRIBE IN DETAIL) RELATING

TO HMONG CHRISTIANS. HE REMAINS CAUTIOUS ABOUT MEETING

WITH FOREIGNERS; HE URGED POL/C NOT TO TRY TO CONTACT

ANY OF THE WORSHIPPERS ENCOUNTERED DURING THE SUNDAY

SERVICES. REVEREND VINH EXPRESSED CERTAINTY THAT THERE

WAS MPS SUPERVISION, IF PROBABLY INDIRECT, OF THESE

WORSHIP SERVICES AT THE SOLE VIETNAMESE PROTESTANT

CHURCH IN HANOI. (REVEREND VINH NOTED THAT THERE HAD

BEEN NINE PROTESTANT CHURCHES IN 1954.) HE ADMITTED

HIS OWN RELUCTANCE TO MEET WITH FOREIGN DELEGATIONS,

INCLUDING THE UPCOMING VISIT OF AMBASSADOR-AT-LARGE FOR

RELIGIOUS FREEDOM JOHN HANFORD, BECAUSE HE FEELS HE

CANNOT ANSWER THEIR QUESTIONS FREELY AND OPENLY, AND

YET DOES NOT WANT TO SPOUT THE OFFICIAL LINE.

5. (C) COMMENT: REVEREND VINH\'S COMMENTS REFLECT THE

DUALITY OF RELIGIOUS LIFE IN VIETNAM: WHILE THERE IS

PROGRESS ON SOME FRONTS, SUCH AS THE PROBABLE OPENING

OF THE ECVS SEMINAR, OTHER GVN RESTRICTIONS AND ONGOING

SUPERVISION MAKE AT LEAST MANY RELIGIOUS LEADERS FEEL

FRUSTRATED AND HAMPERED IN THEIR RELIGIOUS WORK.
