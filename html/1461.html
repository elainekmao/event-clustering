SUBJECT: SIZEABLE SHORTFALL IN MAIZE PRODUCTION IN 2001

LOOMS AS A REAL POSSIBILITY FOR ZIMBABWE

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\' AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS 1.5 (B/D).

REFS: A. HARARE 6190, B. HARARE 6662

1. (SBU) SUMMARY: THE LATEST MAIZE SEED SALES DATA

INDICATE THAT NATIONAL MAIZE PLANTING IS DOWN 25 PERCENT

FROM NORMAL LEVELS. THE ZIMBABWE GRAIN PRODUCERS

ASSOCIATION (ZPGA), REPRESENTING LARGE SCALE COMMERCIAL

MAIZE PRODUCERS, PUBLISHED A STATEMENT ON DECEMBER 20

APPEALING FOR ITS MEMBERS TO PLANT MORE MAIZE BEFORE THE

END OF THE MONTH. SMALL-SCALE MAIZE PRODUCERS AND

COMMUNAL FARMERS MAY PLANT MORE MAIZE BEFORE THE END OF

DECEMBER, BUT THEY ARE HAVING DIFFICULTY AFFORDING THE

INPUTS NEEDED BECAUSE OF THE SHARP RISE IN THE PRICES OF

SEED, FERTILIZER, OTHER CHEMICALS, AND DIESEL, COMBINED

WITH THE FAILURE OF THE PARASTATAL GRAIN MARKETING BOARD

(GMB) TO PAY THESE FARMERS FOR THE 2000 HARVEST.

FORECASTS FOR NORMAL TO ABOVE-NORMAL RAINFALL IN THE

PRINCIPAL MAIZE GROWING REGIONS IN ZIMBABWE MAY HELP TO

OFFSET THE PLANTING SHORTFALL BY INCREASING YIELDS. THE

GMB CLAIMS TO HAVE 600,000 METRIC TONS OF MAIZE IN ITS

STRATEGIC GRAIN RESERVE, WHICH, IF ACCURATE, SHOULD

COVER THE PREDICTED SHORTFALL OF MAIZE IN THE 2001

HARVEST. ZIMBABWE ALSO FACES A LARGER THAN NORMAL

SHORTFALL IN WHEAT, NECESSITATING INCREASED WHEAT

IMPORTS AT A TIME WHEN THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE (GOZ)

HAS NO FOREIGN CURRENCY TO PURCHASE SUCH IMPORTS. THE

REAL CRUNCH IN THE MAIZE SUPPLY MAY COME IN THE 2001/02

SEASON. PLANTING OF MAIZE FOR SEED IS ONLY 38 PERCENT

OF LAST YEAR\'S TOTAL. IF THE GOZ FOLLOWS THROUGH ON ITS

"FAST-TRACK" RESETTLEMENT PROGRAM, REPLACING THE 2673

COMMERCIAL FARMS THAT HAVE BEEN GAZETTED FOR COMPULSORY

ACQUISITION WITH THOUSANDS OF ILL-EQUIPPED SMALL-SCALE

FARMERS, ZIMBABWE\'S FOOD SECURITY IS LIKELY TO SUFFER.

END SUMMARY.

2. (SBU) ON DECEMBER 20 THE CHAIRMAN OF THE ZIMBABWE

GRAIN PRODUCERS ASSOCIATION (ZGPA), ANDREW MEIKLE,

RELEASED A STATEMENT TO THE PRESS URGING LARGE SCALE

MAIZE GROWERS TO MAXIMIZE THE HECTARAGE PLANTED WITH

MAIZE BEFORE THE SEASON ENDS ON DECEMBER 31. THE

CHAIRMAN STATED THAT HE WAS TAKING THE ACTION FOR TWO

REASONS: FIRST, BECAUSE THE CURRENT CROP WILL NOT MEET

ZIMBABWE\'S MAIZE NEEDS AND WILL LEAD TO A HIGHER PRICE

FOR MAIZE, AND SECOND, TO COUNTER A ZIMBABWE

CONFIDENTIAL

BROADCASTING CORPORATION (ZBC) TELEVISION STORY THAT

STATED THAT COMMERCIAL FARMERS WERE SWITCHING FROM MAIZE

TO OTHER CROPS AS A PLOT (NOT STATED, BUT IMPLIED, TO

CAUSE A SHORTFALL IN THE NEXT MAIZE HARVEST THAT WOULD

BE BLAMED ON THE GOZ\'S LAND REFORM PROGRAM.) THE

CHAIRMAN WENT ON TO STATE THAT THE LARGE SCALE

COMMERCIAL MAIZE PRODUCER HAS ALWAYS FELT A MORAL

RESPONSIBILITY FOR FOOD SECURITY IN ZIMBABWE, AND THE

REAL REASONS THAT MAIZE HECTARAGE WAS DOWN ON COMMERCIAL

FARMS WAS DUE TO THE ON-GOING LAND INVASIONS AND WORK

STOPPAGES, THE LACK OF PROFITABILITY OF MAIZE AS A CROP,

AND THE INABILITY OF MANY COMMERCIAL GROWERS TO OBTAIN

FINANCING FROM BANKS DUE TO THE GOZ "FAST-TRACK" LAND

REFORM PROGRAM AND COMPULSORY ACQUISITION.

4. (SBU) ALL THE CONTACTS AGREED THAT THE REAL SHORTFALL

IN MAIZE PRODUCTION, AND CONSEQUENT THREAT TO ZIMBABWE\'S

FOOD SECURITY, WILL OCCUR DURING THE 2001/2002 SEASON IF

THINGS STAY THE SAME IN ZIMBABWE. THIS IS FOR VARIOUS

REASONS. IF THE GOZ FOLLOWS THROUGH ON THE COMPULSORY

ACQUISITION OF THE COMMERCIAL FARMS THAT ARE LARGE-SCALE

MAIZE PRODUCERS, THERE IS NO QUESTION THAT THE COUNTRY\'S

MAIZE PRODUCTION WILL SEE A SHARP DECREASE. IN THE

JUST-ENDED HARVEST SEASON, LARGE-SCALE MAIZE PRODUCERS

PRODUCED 850,000 METRIC TONS OF MAIZE ON JUST 162,000

HECTARES, WHILE THE SMALL-SCALE AND COMMUNAL FARMERS

PRODUCED 1,100,000 METRIC TONS, BUT ON 1,100,000

HECTARES. EVEN IF THE GOZ INSTRUCTED ALL RESETTLED

PEOPLE TO GROW MAIZE AND PROVIDED THEM WITH NEEDED CROP

INPUTS, A VIRTUAL IMPOSSIBILITY GIVEN THE GOZ\'S

BUDGETARY CONSTRAINTS AND PAST TRACK RECORD, IT IS

EXTREMELY DOUBTFUL THAT THESE NEWLY RESETTLED FARMERS

WOULD BE ABLE TO REPLACE THE MAIZE PRODUCTION LOST FROM

THE LARGE-SCALE COMMERCIAL PRODUCERS.

5. (U) ANOTHER FACTOR IN THIS SCENARIO IS THE RISING

COST OF INPUTS NEEDED FOR MAIZE PRODUCTION. IN

PARTICULAR, FERTILIZER AND DIESEL FUEL COSTS HAVE

INCREASED DRAMATICALLY THIS YEAR, AND WILL LIKELY

CONTINUE TO INCREASE SINCE BOTH PRODUCTS ARE IMPORTED.

THE SMALL-SCALE AND COMMUNAL FARMERS HAVE BEEN

STRUGGLING TO GET THE MONEY TO PLANT FOR THIS MAIZE

SEASON, AND THE SITUATION WILL LIKELY BE WORSE NEXT

YEAR. THE GOZ HAS SET A PRELIMINARY MARKET PRICE OF

5,500 ZIMBABWE DOLLARS FOR A METRIC TON OF MAIZE, WHILE

THE ZPGA ESTIMATES THAT IT CURRENTLY COSTS ITS MEMBERS

SMALL-SCALE AND COMMUNAL FARMERS\' COSTS ARE LOWER THAN

THAT OF LARGE-SCALE PRODUCERS; THEIR FINANCIAL VIABILITY

REMAINS QUESTIONABLE AT CURRENT GOZ DETERMINED MAIZE

PRICES.

6. (U) LASTLY, SEED MAIZE PLANTINGS ARE REPORTED BY THE

ZPGA TO BE ONLY 38 PERCENT OF THE HECTARAGE PLANTED LAST

YEAR. THIS IS DIRECTLY RELATED TO BOTH THE FARM

INVASIONS, BECAUSE SEED MAIZE CROPS ARE HIGHLY

SUSCEPTIBLE TO DAMAGE DUE TO CROSS-FERTILIZATION WITH

REGULAR MAIZE PLANTED BY FARM OCCUPIERS, AS WELL AS THE

CURRENTLY POOR FINANCIAL VIABILITY IN ZIMBABWE OF MAIZE

AS A CROP. BECAUSE THIS SEED MAIZE HAS BEEN SPECIALLY

ADAPTED TO THE GROWING CONDITIONS IN ZIMBABWE, IT CANNOT

BE IMPORTED FROM MANY PLACES OTHER THAN SOUTH AFRICA AND

MALAWI, COUNTRIES TO WHICH ZIMBABWE HISTORICALLY HAS

EXPORTED SEED MAIZE.

UNDERMINE PRESIDENT MUGABE AND ZANU-PF; OR MOST
