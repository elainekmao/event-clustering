SUBJECT: FOURTH OF JULY CONGRATULATORY MESSAGE FROM

PRESIDENT MUGABE

ON JULY 3, EMBASSY RECEIVED UNDER COVER OF A DIPLOMATIC NOTE

THE FOLLOWING CONGRATULATORY MESSAGE FROM PRESIDENT MUGABE

TO PRESIDENT CLINTON. THE ORIGINAL WILL BE POUCHED TO AF/S.

BEGIN TEXT:

HIS EXCELLENCY MR. WILLIAM JEFFERSON CLINTON

PRESIDENT OF THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA

YOUR EXCELLENCY

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 HARARE 03615 031355Z

ON BEHALF OF THE GOVERNMENT AND PEOPLE OF THE REPUBLIC OF

ZIMBABWE AND ON MY OWN BEHALF, I EXTEND TO YOU, AND TO THE

GOVERNMENT AND PEOPLE OF THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA, OUR

WARM CONGRATULATIONS ON THE OCCASION OF THE TWO HUNDRED AND

TWENTY FOURTH ANNIVERSARY OF YOUR COUNTRY\'S INDEPENDENCE.

I TAKE THIS OPPORTUNITY TO RE-AFFIRM THE GOOD RELATIONS

ENJOYED BY OUR TWO COUNTRIES AND TO EXPRESS OUR HOPE THAT

THEY WILL CONTINUE TO BE EXPANDED AND STRENGTHENED IN THE

YEARS AHEAD.

I WISH YOU PERSONAL GOOD HEALTH AND PEACE, PROGRESS AND

PROSPERITY FOR THE PEOPLE OF UNITED STATES OF AMERICA.

PLEASE ACCEPT, YOUR EXCELLENCY, THE ASSURANCES OF MY HIGHEST

CONSIDERATION.

ROBERT GABRIEL MUGABE

PRESIDENT OF THE REPUBLIC OF ZIMBABWE

END TEXT.
