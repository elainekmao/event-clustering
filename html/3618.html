SUBJECT: VATICAN ON CLONING CONVENTION: DON\'T MAKE THE

IDEAL THE ENEMY OF THE GOOD

REF: STATE 160115

1. (SBU) SUMMARY: VATICAN DFM MIGLIORE TOLD DCM AUGUST 30

THAT THE HOLY SEE SHARED U.S. GOALS ON THE CLONING CONVENTION

AND SUPPORTED USG EFFORTS TO ACHIEVE A UN CONVENTION TO BAN

ALL FORMS OF HUMAN CLONING. HOWEVER, THE VATICAN FEARED THAT

PRESSING TOO HARD FOR A COMPREHENSIVE BAN NOW MIGHT LEAD TO A

MISSED OPPORTUNITY TO ACHIEVE A GLOBAL BAN ON HUMAN CLONING

THAT THE HOLY SEE REGARDS AS FUNDAMENTAL. THE HOLY SEE IS

PREPARED TO WORK WITH THE U.S. IN THE LEAD UP TO THE UNGA IN

PURSUIT OF A TOTAL BAN, AND MIGLIORE TOOK ON BOARD THE NAMES

OF THE COUNTRIES WHICH REFTEL RECOMMENDS THEY APPROACH.

NEVERTHELESS, THE HOLY SEE IS PREPARED TO PURSUE AN

ALTERNATIVE APPROACH IF IT APPEARS THAT A COMPREHENSIVE BAN

IS IMPOSSIBLE TO ACHIEVE. THE ALTERNATIVE WOULD DELETE THE

LANGUAGE IN THE CURRENT DRAFT UN RESOLUTION THAT LEAVES OPEN

OTHER HUMAN CLONING ACTIVITIES, WHILE RETAINING EXPLICIT

REFERENCES TO THE INTENT TO MOVE AFTER PHASE ONE

(REPRODUCTIVE CLONING) TO A PHASE TWO THAT WOULD BAN ALL

OTHER FORMS OF HUMAN CLONING. THE HOLY SEE BELIEVES THIS

WOULD ESTABLISH THE PRINCIPLE THAT ALL FORMS OF HUMAN CLONING

ARE CURRENTLY REGARDED AS "UNACCEPTABLE." END SUMMARY.

2. (SBU) DCM PRESENTED REFTEL REQUEST TO VATICAN DFM

CELESTINO MIGLIORE ON AUGUST 30. MIGLIORE INDICATED HE HAD

HEARD FROM THE HOLY SEE\'S PERMANENT OBSERVER MISSION IN NEW

YORK ABOUT THE U.S. APPROACH, AND CAUTIONED THAT THE U.S.

MIGHT BE TOO AMBITIOUS. HE SAID THAT THE HOLY SEE FULLY

SUPPORTED US EFFORTS TO ACHIEVE A COMPREHENSIVE BAN ON HUMAN

CLONING BECAUSE WE SHARE THE SAME STRONG MORAL CONCERNS. HE

FEARED, HOWEVER, THAT IT WOULD PROVE IMPOSSIBLE TO GENERATE

THE VOTES AT THIS TIME TO AUTHORIZE A CONVENTION ON A

COMPREHENSIVE BAN ON HUMAN CLONING. IN THIS CASE, THE HOLY

SEE BELIEVED IT COULD PROVE IMPOSSIBLE TO REGAIN THE MOMENTUM

FOR SUCH A BAN IN THE FUTURE, AND THE MOMENT TO ACHIEVE A BAN

ON HUMAN CLONING COULD PASS. MIGLIORE PREFERRED TO PRESS NOW

FOR THE PHASE ONE BAN AS PROPOSED BY THE FRENCH AND GERMANS

BUT WITH CHANGED LANGUAGE IN THE AUTHORIZING RESOLUTION.

3. (SBU) WHILE THE HOLY SEE AGREED THAT THE TWO PHASE

APPROACH CARRIED RISKS, THEY HAD DEVELOPED AN ALTERNATIVE TO

THE FRENCH AND GERMAN PROPOSAL THAT THEY BELIEVED COULD

ESTABLISH THE PRINCIPLE AGAINST ALL FORMS OF HUMAN CLONING,

WHILE LOCKING IN A COMMITMENT TO NEGOTIATIONS ON THE SECOND

STEP. SPECIFICALLY, THE HOLY SEE WOULD PROPOSE DELETING OP

3(B), WHICH HE PARAPHRASED AS READING: "THE SOCIAL COMMITTEE

CLARIFIES THAT A BAN ON HUMAN REPRODUCTIVE CLONING IS NOT TO

PREJUDICE THE LEGALITY OR ILLEGALITY OF OTHER HUMAN CLONING

ACTIVITIES." THIS DELETION, COMBINED WITH EXISTING LANGUAGE

FURTHER DOWN IN THE RESOLUTION EXPLICITLY CALLING FOR BOTH A

BAN ON REPRODUCTIVE CLONING AND A SUBSEQUENT CONVENTION

BANNING ALL OTHER FORMS OF HUMAN CLONING WOULD ESTABLISH THE

PRINCIPLE THAT ANY FORM OF HUMAN CLONING WAS "INADMISSIBLE"

WHILE OFFERING A WAY TO MOVE FORWARD NOW, MIGLIORE MAINTAINED.

4. (SBU) DCM ASSURED MIGLIORE THAT THE USG APPRECIATED THE

HOLY SEE\'S DESIRE TO LOCK IN THE PRINCIPLE BANNING HUMAN

REPRODUCTIVE CLONING IN THE EVENT A COMPREHENSIVE BAN

APPEARED UNATTAINABLE. HE REITERATED THAT AT THIS POINT,

HOWEVER, THE US BELIEVED IT WAS POSSIBLE TO BUILD CONSENSUS

FOR THE CONVENTION ON A COMPREHENSIVE BAN AND INTENDED TO

WORK HARD TO BUILD A COALITION TO THAT END. DCM PRESSED

MIGLIORE TO JOIN US NOW IN PURSUIT OF A COMPREHENSIVE BAN,

WHILE KEEPING THEIR ALTERNATIVE APPROACH IN RESERVE FOR THE

TIME BEING. MIGLIORE INDICATED THE HOLY SEE WOULD WORK WITH

GOES TO THE CORE OF ITS MISSION WAS SURPRISING, PARTICULARLY

WITH POWERFUL ALLIES IN THE USG AND OTHER COUNTRIES THAT

SUPPORT A CONVENTION ADDRESSING A COMPREHENSIVE BAN. IN THIS

CASE, THE CAUTION REFLECTS NOT ONLY THE HOLY SEE\'S

PRAGMATISM, BUT ALSO THE EXTREME IMPORTANCE THE HOLY SEE

ATTACHES TO A BAN ON HUMAN REPRODUCTIVE CLONING AND DESIRE TO

ACHIEVE AGREEMENT ON SUCH A BAN. WE ARE CONFIDENT THE HOLY

SEE WILL ENCOURAGE ITS UN OBSERVER IN NEW YORK AND NUNCIOS IN

POLAND, THE PHILIPPINES, HONDURAS, ZIMBABWE, AND IVORY COAST

TO APPROACH THE HOST GOVERNMENTS TO SEEK OUT SUPPORT FOR OUR

APPROACH TO THE ISSUE, AND MIGLIORE HAS AGREED TO UPDATE US

ON THEIR DISCUSSIONS. WITH THEIR FINGER TO THE WIND,

HOWEVER, THEY WILL TURN TO THEIR COMPROMISE IF THEY DEEM IT

NECESSARY PRESERVE THE CHANCE FOR A FUTURE GLOBAL BAN.
