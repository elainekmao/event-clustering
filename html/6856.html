CLASSIFIED BY: POLOFF MITCH BENEDICT FOR REASONS 1.5 B AND D

1. (C) ON RECEIPT OF REF A, AMBASSADOR SPOKE EARLY MORNING

MARCH 27 WITH MIROSLAV KOVACIC, MFA NORTH AMERICA DIRECTOR,

WHO WAS IN THE MEETING WITH DEPUTY FM SIMONOVIC THAT WE

REPORTED IN REF C. THE AMBASSADOR RECALLED THE (HELPFUL BUT

EU-CAVEATED) POSITION THE GOC HAD DECIDED UPON AND THAT HAD

BEEN CONVEYED TO THE AMBASSADOR BY SIMONOVIC, AND SAID OUR

REPORTS FROM GENEVA WERE THAT THEIR DELEGATION WAS STILL

INTENDING TO ABSTAIN. THE AMBASSADOR ALSO NOTED INDICATIONS

REPORTED BY GENEVA THAT THE EU WOULD OPPOSE. KOVACIC

RECONFIRMED THE GOC STANCE WAS "NO, BUT WE WILL FOLLOW THE EU

LINE IF THERE IS ONE," AS REPORTED IN REF C, AND HE SAID HE

WOULD FOLLOW UP WITH HIS DELEGATION IN GENEVA.

2. (C) KOVACIC CALLED THE CROATIAN DELEGATION IN GENEVA AND

THEN CALLED US BACK TO CONFIRM -- THEIR CURRENT INSTRUCTION

IS TO OPPOSE, TO VOTE "NO" -- NOT "ABSTAIN." WERE THE EU TO

TAKE A COMMON POSITION, CROATIA WOULD LINE UP WITH THAT

POSITION, WHATEVER IT WAS. THE DELEGATION CONFIRMED IT KNOWS

WHAT ITS INSTRUCTIONS ARE.

3. (C) IF, AS GENEVA REPORTS, THE EU IS GOING TO VOTE "NO,"

THEN IT WOULD APPEAR THE GOC IS A LOCK AGAINST THE SPECIAL

IRAQ SESSION.
