SUBJECT: POPE CONDEMNS DESECRATION OF JEWISH CEMETARY AND

ANTI-SEMITISM

1. (U) POPE JOHN PAUL II CONDEMNED GROWING ANTI-SEMITISM IN

EUROPE AFTER THIRTY-FIVE JEWISH GRAVES IN A ROME CEMETERY

WERE DESECRATED JULY 18. IN A TELEGRAM TO ROME\'S CHIEF

RABBI JULY 19 PUBLISHED ON THE VATICAN\'S WEBSITE, THE POPE

DESCRIBED THE DESECRATION AS A "DETESTABLE ACT THAT FOLLOWS

OTHER SIMILAR EVENTS OF A SERIOUS NATURE THAT HAVE TAKEN

PLACE WITH ALARMING FREQUENCY IN EUROPE AND ON OTHER

UNCLASSIFIED

CONTINENTS." THE POPE "STRONGLY CONDEMNED SIMILAR IGNOBLE

ACTS AND ANTI-SEMITIC SENTIMENTS THAT INSPIRE SUCH ACTS, AND

OFFERED HIS SYMPATHY TO THE FAMILIES AFFECTED BY THE ACTS.

2. (SBU) COMMENT: THE POPE REGULARLY SENDS MESSAGES TO

COMMUNITY AND NATIONAL LEADERS IN RESPONSE TO MAJOR

CALAMITIES. HIS TELEGRAM TO ROME\'S JEWISH COMMUNITY IS THE

ONLY ONE IN RECENT MEMORY TO CONDEMN ANTI-SEMITIC ATTACKS,

AND SUGGESTS A GROWING CONCERN ON THE PART OF THE VATICAN

FOR THE RISE OF ANTI-SEMITISM IN EUROPE. A JOURNALIST

CONTACT TOLD US THAT OVER DINNER THE PREVIOUS NIGHT, THE

ISRAELI DCM HAD MENTIONED HE WAS NOT EXPECTING VATICAN

COMMENTARY ON THE DESECRATION. EMBASSY HAS RECENTLY

ENCOURAGED CURIA OFFICIALS TO USE THE HOLY SEE\'S MORAL VOICE

TO COUNTER RESURGENT ANTI-SEMITISM AND OTHER FORMS OF RACISM

AND XENOPHOBIA IN EUROPE. END COMMENT.
