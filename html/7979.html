SUBJECT: DEPUTY SECRETARY\'S REMARKS TEMPER CRITICISM OF

US IN UAE PRESS

1. SUMMARY: ON APRIL 18, PAO ARRANGED A TELEPHONE

PRESS CONFERENCE (TPC) FOR THE DEPUTY SECRETARY WITH THE

UAE\'S MOST WIDELY READ PAN-ARAB ARABIC DAILY "AL-

KHALEEJ" AND SISTER ENGLISH-LANGUAGE DAILY "GULF TODAY."

BOTH CARRIED THE ENTIRE TEXT OF THE INTERVIEW ON SUNDAY,

APRIL 20, 2003. THE D/S\'S REMARKS ON SYRIA, THE

ROADMAP, AND U.S. PLANS FOR THE RECONSTRUCTION OF IRAQ

WERE USED IN SUBSEQUENT ARTICLES AND EDITORIALS AND

HIGHLIGHTED BY OTHER UAE DAILIES AND REGIONAL SATELLITE

TV CHANNELS. THIS EXCLUSIVE INTERVIEW INJECTED BALANCE

INTO AL KHALEEJ\'S HARSH CONDEMNATIONS OF US FOREIGN

POLICY AND INTENTIONS IN THE REGION. END SUMMARY.

2. ON APRIL 20, 2003, "AL-KHALEEJ" HEADLINED FRONT-PAGE

EXCERPTS OF THE INTERVIEW. THE MAIN HEADLINES READ:

--"CONFIRMING THAT AMERICA WILL BE IN CHARGE OF IRAQ FOR

A SHORT PERIOD OF TIME, ARMITAGE TELLS \'AL-KHALEEJ\':

SYRIA IS AT A CROSSROADS"

--"THE ROADMAP IS NOT SUBJECT TO NEGOTIATION OR

BARGAINING"

--"THE UN ROLE IS VITAL, AND WE ARE NOT IN AGREEMENT

WITH PERLE ON ELIMINATING ITS ROLE."

3. "AL-KHALEEJ" ALSO DEVOTED AN ENTIRE INSIDE PAGE TO

THE FULL TRANSCRIPT OF DEPUTY SECRETARY ARMITAGE\'S

INTERVIEW. THE HEADLINES ON PAGE 12 READ:

--"ASSERTING THAT SYRIA MUST BE AWARE THAT THE SITUATION

HAS CHANGED, POWELL WILL DISCUSS THE PROPOSAL TO

ELIMINATE WMD FROM THE REGION WITH ASSAD"

--"ARMITAGE TELLS \'AL-KHALEEJ\': WE WILL RULE IRAQ FOR A

SHORT PERIOD AND HAVE NO LIST OF OTHER TARGETS."

4. ON THE SAME DAY, SISTER ENGLISH-LANGUAGE DAILY "GULF

TODAY" POSTED AN ARTICLE ON THE INTERVIEW ON ITS FIRST

PAGE WITH THE FULL TRANSCRIPT ON AN INSIDE PAGE. THE

FRONT-PAGE HEADLINE READ: "NO TIMEFRAME FOR IRAQ: US

WILL NOT DEPART WITHOUT ENSURING SECURITY, SAYS

ARMITAGE," "PEACE \'ROAD MAP\' IS NOT NEGOTIABLE," AND "US

WILL NOT STAY IN IRAQ ONE DAY LONGER THAN NECESSARY."

5. THIS INTERVIEW HAD A BROAD IMPACT ON UAE AND

REGIONAL MEDIA. OTHER UAE DAILIES AND THE THREE LEADING

TELEVISION STATIONS MBC, ABU DHABI, AND AL-ARABIYYA

PUBLISHED OR AIRED EXCERPTS OF THE DEPUTY SECRETARY\'S

REMARKS. COVERAGE HIGHLIGHTED THE DEPUTY SECRETARY\'S

DENIAL THAT THE ROAD MAP WILL BE SUBJECT TO NEGOTIATION

AND BARGAINING AND GENERALLY DESCRIBED THESE REMARKS AS

A POSITIVE DEVELOPMENT FOR A BREAKTHROUGH IN THE PEACE

PROCESS. THE COVERAGE ALSO REPEATED THE DEPUTY

SECRETARY\'S COMMENT THAT "THE US PRESENCE IN IRAQ WILL

SIPDIS

NOT BE ONE DAY LONGER THAN NECESSARY". OVERALL, THE

EMINENTLY QUOTABLE DEPUTY SECRETARY BROUGHT BALANCE AND

OBJECTIVITY TO THE UAE MEDIA\'S DEBATE ON IRAQ, THE

ROADMAP, AND U.S. FOREIGN POLICY IN THE REGION.

6. COMMENT: SINCE THE CESSATION OF HOSTILITIES IN IRAQ,

POST HAS NOTED AN INCREASED WILLINGNESS ON THE PART OF

LOCAL MEDIA TO ENGAGE WITH US AND WITH USG OFFICIALS.

THE DEPUTY SECRETARY\'S INTERVIEW WAS THE FIRST SUCH

INTERVIEW WE HAVE DONE WITH THE USUALLY ANTI-AMERICAN

(BUT INFLUENTIAL) AL-KHALEEJ. IT PAVED THE WAY FOR AN

INTERVIEW BY AL-KHALEEJ WITH AMBASSADOR WAHBA TWO WEEKS

LATER, AGAIN, A FIRST OF ITS KIND. WE HAVE AN EXCELLENT

OPPORTUNITY AT THIS TIME TO TAKE ADVANTAGE OF THIS

GREATER OPENNESS. WE ENCOURAGE THE DEPARTMENT TO

MAINTAIN THE INCREASED LEVEL OF INTERVIEWS BY SENIOR USG

OFFICIALS WITH THE ARAB MEDIA. END COMMENT
