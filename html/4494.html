SUBJECT: SODANO HINTS AT SHIFT IN HOLY SEE UN STATUS; TAKES

AIM AT PREEMPTIVE STRIKE, EU CONSTITUTION

1. (U) THE HOLY SEE IS CONSIDERING UPGRADING ITS STATUS

AT THE UN FROM PERMANENT OBSERVER TO FULL MEMBER. IN AN

INTERVIEW WITH CENTRIST ITALIAN DAILY "CORRIERE DELLA SERA"

PUBLISHED NOVEMBER 24, VATICAN SECRETARY OF STATE (PM

EQUIVALENT) CARDINAL ANGELO SODONO SAID THE HOLY SEE WAS

STUDYING WAYS TO AUGMENT ITS PRESENCE AT THE UN. ASKED IF

THIS MEANT BECOMING A FULL MEMBER, SODANO RESPONDED, "IF IT

WOULD BE USEFUL, I\'M NOT EXCLUDING IT." SODANO NOTED THAT

SWITZERLAND HAD RECENTLY CHANGED ITS STATUS TO MEMBER AND

THAT, "THE FORM OF OUR PRESENCE IS AN OPEN QUESTION."

2. (U) SODANO ALSO TOUCHED ON A POSSIBLE PREEMPTIVE STRIKE

ON IRAQ SAYING THAT THE TERM DID NOT FEATURE IN THE UN\'S

VOCABULARY AND THAT DIALOGUE AND NEGOTIATED SOLUTIONS SHOULD

BE EXHAUSTED BEFORE ANY OTHER HYPOTHESIS IS PROPOSED.

3. (U) SODANO REAFFIRMED THE HOLY SEE\'S DESIRE TO SEE

CHRISTIANITY, AND RELIGION IN GENERAL, GIVEN A PLACE IN THE

PROPOSED EUROPEAN CONSTITUTION (SEPTEL). HE SAID THE HOLY

SEE EXPECTS EUROPE\'S CHRISTIAN PATRIMONY TO BE MENTIONED IN

ARTICLE 2 OF THE CONSTITUTION -- DEALING WITH EUROPEAN

VALUES -- AND THAT A COMPLETELY NEW ARTICLE WOULD ENSHRINE

RELIGIOUS FREEDOM AND THE RIGHT OF RELIGIOUS COMMUNITIES IN

EUROPE TO SELF-GOVERNANCE AND ORGANIZATION.

4. (SBU) COMMENT: SODANO\'S COMMENTS ON THE HOLY SEE\'S

STATUS AT THE UN REFLECT SOME INTERNAL FRUSTRATION ABOUT THE

LIMITATIONS ON THE HOLY SEE\'S VOICE IN INTERNATIONAL FORA

ARISING FROM THEIR STATUS AS A PERMANENT OBSERVER.

TRADITIONALLY, THE HOLY SEE HAS ENJOYED THE FREEDOM ITS

UNIQUE STATUS OFFERS IT AS A VOICE WITHIN, BUT NOT OF, THE

UN SYSTEM. WHILE A MORAL VOICE HAS SOME CLOUT, MANY VATICAN

DIPLOMATS HAVE CONCLUDED THAT A VOTE -- ESPECIALLY IN THE

SECURITY COUNCIL AND AFFILIATED UN AGENCIES -- MAY BE MORE

EFFECTIVE IN PROMOTING THE HOLY SEE\'S INTERNATIONAL AGENDA.

FOR EXAMPLE, THE HOLY SEE\'S OUTGOING FAO REPRESENTATIVE

RECENTLY SUGGESTED IN AN "30 DAYS" ARTICLEARTICLE IN

CATHOLIC MONTHLY "30 DAYS" THAT THE HOLY SEE COULD HAVE BEEN

MORE INFLUENTIAL IN COMBATING WORLD HUNGER IF IT WERE A

ONGOING STRUGGLE OVER A GLOBAL BAN ON HUMAN CLONING HAS

STRENGTHENED THE DEBATE ON THIS ISSUE WITHIN THE HOLY SEE.

SWITZERLAND\'S CHANGE OF STATUS HAS CLEARLY SPARKED SOME

RECONSIDERATION WITHIN THE HOLY SEE AS TO WHETHER THE TIME

IS RIGHT TO MAKE A SHIFT. WITH CURRENT UN PERMREP RENATO

MARTINO RETURNING TO ROME AFTER FIFTEEN YEARS IN NEW YORK,

THIS ISSUE IS LIKELY TO BE INTENSIVELY DEBATED IN THE MONTHS

AHEAD. A DECISION TO UPGRADE WOULD MARK A SIGNIFICANT

DEPARTURE IN THE HOLY SEE\'S APPROACH TO PURSUIT OF ITS

FOREIGN POLICY GOALS.
