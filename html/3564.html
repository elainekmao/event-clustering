SUBJECT: BITTER AFTERLIFE OF GENERAL TRAN DO\'S FUNERAL

REF: HANOI 1787

1. (U) CLASSIFIED BY POL/C SCOTT BELLARD; REASON 1.5(D)

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HANOI 02079 230926Z

2. (C) IN TWO SEPARATE CONVERSATIONS ON AUGUST 20 AND 21,

THREE OF HANOI\'S LEADING DEMOCRACY ACTIVISTS TOLD POLOFF

THAT THEY VIEW GENERAL TRAN DO\'S AUGUST 14 FUNERAL AS AN

"HISTORIC POLITICAL EVENT" HIGHLIGHTING THE NATIONAL

LEADERSHIP\'S DISTANCE FROM THE PEOPLE. DESPITE A NEAR-

TOTAL LACK OF ADVANCE PUBLICITY, BETWEEN ONE AND TWO

THOUSAND PEOPLE ATTENDED THE FUNERAL, ACCORDING TO DR.

NGUYEN THANH GIANG. THEY INCLUDED MANY "REGULAR PEOPLE AND

VETERANS" AND "OLD PARTY MEMBERS," SAID ACTIVIST AND AUTHOR

HOANG TIEN ON AUGUST 20.

3. (C) PHAM QUE DUONG, DR. GIANG, AND TIEN (PLEASE

PROTECT) WERE ALL DISMISSIVE OF THE HIGHEST-RANKING

OFFICIAL REPRESENTATIVE AT THE FUNERAL, NATIONAL ASSEMBLY

FOREIGN AFFAIRS COMMITTEE CHAIRMAN AND CPV CENTRAL

COMMITTEE MEMBER VU MAO. THEY COMPLAINED THAT IT WAS

"INSULTING" THAT A POLITBURO MEMBER HAD NOT BEEN PRESENT.

TIEN NOTED THAT SENTIMENT IN THE CROWD AGAINST THE GVN\'S

AND CPV\'S HANDLING OF THE FUNERAL WAS REMARKABLY

WIDESPREAD. DR. GIANG DESCRIBED HOW EVEN SOME OF "THE OLD

PARTY MEMBERS" AT THE FUNERAL HAD BEGUN BEHAVING AS IF THEY

WERE AT A DEMONSTRATION, PUMPING THEIR FISTS IN THE AIR IN

APPARENT ANGER AT THE CPV\'S AND GVN\'S "SHABBY TREATMENT" OF

GENERAL DO. IT SHOWED A LACK OF RESPECT TOWARD A MAN MANY

REGARDED AS A NATIONAL HERO, GOING AGAINST VIETNAMESE

CULTURE AND TRADITION, GIANG AND TIEN SEPARATELY NOTED.

4. (C) TIEN HAS WRITTEN A SHARPLY CRITICAL TRACT ABOUT

THE FUNERAL ENTITLED "THE SOUND OF CLAPPING AT A FUNERAL"

(SEPTEL WILL PROVIDE TRANSLATION). HE AND FELLOW ACTIVISTS

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HANOI 02079 230926Z

PHAM QUE DUONG AND VAN KHAC KHIN ARE IN THE PROCESS OF

DISTRIBUTING COPIES TO TOP GVN AND CPV OFFICIALS AS WELL AS

TO OTHER ACTIVISTS.

5. (C) ACCORDING TO DUONG, THERE WERE POLICE BARRICADES

AROUND THE FUNERAL HALL. HE EXPRESSED DOUBT THAT ANY

FOREIGNERS, MUCH LESS A US EMBASSY OFFICIAL, WOULD HAVE

BEEN ALLOWED TO ATTEND, ALTHOUGH HE SAID THAT HE SAW A FEW

FOREIGN JOURNALISTS OUTSIDE THE BARRICADES.

6. (C) DR. GIANG DISCUSSED THE FUNERAL WITH POLOFF IN

THE PRESENCE OF ANOTHER MAN WHOM HE SIMPLY CALLED "ONG" (A

PRONOUN OF RESPECT) KHANH. KHANH CLAIMED TO BE A

"REVOLUTIONARY" AND CPV MEMBER BEGINNING IN 1944 AS WELL AS

AN ASSISTANT TO THEN-POLITBURO MEMBER TRAN XUAN BACH.

(NOTE: BACH WAS ELECTED TO THE POLITBURO AT THE SIXTH

PARTY CONGRESS IN 1986. END NOTE.) DR. GIANG SAID THAT

THE POLICE "DID NOT DARE" TO DO ANYTHING TO ELDERLY CPV

MEMBERS SUCH AS KHANH BECAUSE OF THEIR STATURE, AND CLAIMED

THAT KHANH REPRESENTED AN OLDER GENERATION OF CPV MEMBERS

THAT QUIETLY AGREES WITH THE DEMOCRACY ACTIVISTS. DR.

GIANG REMARKED THAT VIETNAMESE YOUTH DO NOT PAY MUCH

ATTENTION EITHER TO THE CPV OR TO DEMOCRACY ACTIVISTS, BUT

OPINED THAT THEY "LIKED" THE DEMOCRACY ACTIVISTS MORE.

7. (U) THERE WAS LIMITED BUT RESPECTFUL COVERAGE IN MAJOR

VIETNAMESE NEWSPAPERS ABOUT TRAN DO\'S DEATH (BUT NOT ABOUT

HIS FUNERAL, AS FAR AS WE CAN ASCERTAIN), WHILE OMITTING

ANY DISCUSSION ABOUT HIS FALL-OUT WITH AND EVENTUAL

EXPULSION FROM THE PARTY IN HIS LATER YEARS.

8. (C) COMMENT: EMBASSY AGREES WITH THE ASSESSMENT OF

THESE ACTIVISTS THAT THEY ARE UNLIKELY TO BE ALONE WITHIN

ELITE CIRCLES IN RESENTING THE TREATMENT OF GENERAL TRAN DO

BOTH BEFORE AND AFTER HIS DEATH. WE NONETHELESS DO NOT

EXPECT ANY STRONG GVN RESPONSE -- APART FROM A POSSIBLE NEW

ROUND OF QUESTIONING AND/OR HEIGHTENED PETTY HARASSMENT --

AGAINST THEM EVEN AFTER THE DISTRIBUTION OF TIEN\'S TRACT.

CPV AND GVN SENSITIVITIES ABOUT TRAN DO\'S UNIQUE STATURE

AND HISTORY, AS WELL AS SOME POSSIBLE TACIT SYMPATHIES

WITHIN THE CPV TOWARD DO\'S CRITICISMS, LIKELY WILL MAKE IT

DIFFICULT TO ARRIVE AT A SENIOR-LEVEL CONSENSUS ON ANY

LARGER CAMPAIGN OF RETALIATION OR ANY NEW CRACKDOWN AGAINST

DEMOCRACY ACTIVISTS.
