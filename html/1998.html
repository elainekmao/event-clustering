SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE NIXES INITIAL VISIT BY EU ELECTION OBSERVERS

REFS: A) HARARE 2710 B) HARARE 2690

CLASSIFIED BY POLITICAL SECTION CHIEF MATT HARRINGTON. REASONS:

1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) EU ACTING CHIEF OF MISSION ALEX KREMER, ACCOMPANIED BY

BRUSSELS-BASED EU STAFFER TIM SHEEHY, DESCRIBED TO POLITICAL

SECTION CHIEF ON SEPTEMBER 17 THE HEADACHES EXPERIENCED BY THE EU

IN TRYING TO GET A NINE-PERSON TECHNICAL TEAM ON THE GROUND TO LAY

THE GROUNDWORK FOR A SUSTAINED ELECTION OBSERVATION EFFORT.

ACCORDING TO KREMER, THE EU MISSION IN HARARE INFORMED THE

GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE IN A NOTE VERBALE ON SEPTEMBER 14 THAT THE

TEAM WOULD BE ARRIVING ON SEPTEMBER 23, AND PROVIDED THE TEAM\'S

TERMS OF REFERENCE. KREMER RECEIVED A POLITE BUT FIRM LETTER IN

RESPONSE FROM DEPUTY PRESIDENTIAL SPOKESMAN GEORGE CHARAMBA

STATING THAT THE EU SHOULD WAIT TO SEND ELECTION OBSERVERS UNTIL

IT IS INVITED TO DO SO. CHARAMBA OFFERED NO DETAILS ON WHEN THE

EU MIGHT EXPECT SUCH AN INVITATION.

2. (C) THE FOREIGN MINISTRY\'S DIVISION DIRECTOR FOR EUROPE AND

THE AMERICAS JONATHAN WUTAWUNASHE ASKED TO SPEAK WITH KREMER AND

THE BELGIAN AMBASSADOR ON SEPTEMBER 17 TO DISCUSS THE MATTER

FURTHER. WUTAWUNASHE REPORTEDLY SAID THE GOZ DOES NOT OBJECT "IN

PRINCIPLE" TO EU ELECTION SUPPORT, BUT EXPRESSED CONCERN THAT THE

LANGUAGE IN THE EU\'S NOTE VERBALE AND TERMS OF REFERENCE RAISED

QUESTIONS ABOUT THE TEAM\'S REAL OBJECTIVES. THE MFA OFFICIAL

RECOMMENDED THAT THE EU SEND ANOTHER NOTE VERBALE PROPOSING THE

VISIT OF THE TEAM AND SUGGESTING A DISCUSSION OF ITS TERMS OF

REFERENCE. KREMER TOLD US THAT THE EU PLANS TO PROCEED ALONG THE

LINES SUGGESTED BY WUTAWUNASHE. ASKED WHETHER THE EU CONSIDERED

THIS THE END OF THE ROAD FOR ITS ELECTION OBSERVATION EFFORT,

SHEEHY SAID NO, BUT HE FULLY EXPECTED THE GOZ TO ERECT ALL SORTS

OF OBSTACLES, ENSURING THAT NO OBSERVERS ARE ADMITTED UNTIL MUCH

CLOSER TO THE DATES OF THE ELECTION.

COMMENT

-------

3. (C) WE AGREE WITH KREMER THAT THE GOZ WILL MAKE ALL PROPOSED

OBSERVER GROUPS -- PARTICULARLY THOSE FROM WESTERN GOVERNMENTS AND

ORGANIZATIONS -- JUMP THROUGH A NUMBER OF INCREASINGLY NARROW

HOOPS IN THE RUN-UP TO THE ELECTION. THE GOZ\'S LIKELY GOAL IS TO

PREVENT OUTSIDE GROUPS FROM WITNESSING THE PRE-ELECTION VIOLENCE

AND INTIMIDATION THAT IS ALREADY A FACT OF LIFE FOR MANY

ZIMBABWEANS, AND TO ALLOW THEM IN AT THE LAST MINUTE, JUST IN TIME

TO PUT THEIR STAMP OF APPROVAL ON THE VOTING DAYS THEMSELVES,

WHICH HISTORICALLY HAVE BEEN PEACEFUL. THUS IT IS IMPORTANT TO

KEEP THE PRESSURE ON, AND WE WILL PRESS THE GOZ FOR A RESPONSE TO

OUR SEPTEMBER 13 DIPLOMATIC NOTE REGARDING THE IFES TEAM\'S

EXPULSION (REFTELS) BY THE END OF THIS WEEK.
