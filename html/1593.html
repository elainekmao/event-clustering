CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) FOLLOWING IS AN ACCOUNT BY CANADIAN DEPUTY HIGH

COMMISSIONER IN HARARE RON WILLSON (STRICTLY PROTECT) OF

THE SAME CONVERSATION REPORTED IN REFTEL. WILLSON

SUPPLIES ADDITIONAL DETAIL, PARTICULARLY ON ZIMBABWEAN

PERCEPTIONS OF THE RWANDA-UGANDA RIFT, STRAINS IN HIS

GOVERNMENT\'S RELATIONSHIP WITH ANGOLA IN THE WAKE OF

LAURENT KABILA\'S ASSASSINATION, INCLUDING THE SUSPICION

THE LATTER MAY HAVE HAD A HAND IN IT, AND THE FRENCH

ROLE IN THE CONGO.

2. (C) [BEGIN TEXT OF REPORT] SUMMARY: ZIMBABWE EXPECTS

NEXT MONTH TO START REPATRIATING TROOPS IN SUBSTANTIAL

NUMBERS. IT CLAIMS THAT THERE HAS BEEN NO FALLING OUT

WITH ANGOLA. IT SEES WESTERN POWERS AS BEING MORE

SUPPORTIVE NOW OF THE PEACE PROCESS, BUT BELIEVES

RWANDAN SECURITY CONCERNS ARE STILL GIVEN TOO MUCH

CREDENCE. SUSPICIONS REMAIN HIGH THAT RWANDA AND UGANDA

WILL TRY TO SCUTTLE THE PEACE PROCESS. [END SUMMARY.]

3. (C) REPORT: TOGETHER WITH US CHARGE WE MET ON 16

MARCH WITH DAVID HAMADZIRIPI, FORMERLY MFA

UNDERSECRETARY FOR AFRICA AND NOW ASSISTANT TO

ZIMBABWE\'S SPECIAL ENVOY IN KINSHASA. HE HAS BEEN OUT

OF KINSHASA FOR PAST MONTH, ACCOMPANYINQ MINISTER

MUDENGE IN LUSAKA AND NEW YORK AND PRESIDENT MUQABE IN

BRUSSELS AND PARIS.

CONFIDENTIAL

4. (C) HAMADZIRIPI REPEATED MUCH OF WHAT MUDENQE SAID

IN BRIEFINQ TO DIPLOMATIC CORPS REPORTED IN OUR WUGR0042

OF 14 MARCH. HE WAS AWARE OF OUR 15 MARCH PRESS RELEASE

IN ZIMBABWE AND WAS NOT PLEASED WITH IT, BUT THIS DID

NOT DETRACT FROM OTHERWISE FRIENDLY EXCHANGE.

5. (C) WHILE ARGUING THAT KABILA WAS KEEPING MUGABE

INFORMED, THEIR LAST EXCHANGE HAVING APPARENTLY BEEN IN

SIRTE (BUT NOT IN WINDHOEK, WHERE SADC BUSINESS CROWDED

OUT EVERYTHING ELSE), HAMADZIRIPI DID ALLOW THAT IN SOME

QUARTERS IN HARARE THERE WAS SIMMERING RESENTMENT THAT

KABILA HAD NOT YET VISITED HERE BUT WAS ALREADY ON HIS

SECOND TRIP TO EUROPE. BY WAY OF JUSTIFICATION, THOUGH,

HAMADZIRIPI SAID THAT IT WAS IMPORTANT DOMESTICALLY FOR

KABILA TO BE PERCEIVED AS HIS OWN MAN.

6. (C) HAMADZIRIPI SAID THAT WE SHOULD NOT BE CONCERNED

THAT ZIMBABWEAN TROOPS HAD NOT YET STARTED TO WITHDRAW

UNDER PHASE TWO. ORDERS HAD BEEN SENT OUT FROM HARARE

TO THE FIELD AND THE WITHDRAWAL SHOULD BEGIN SOON. NEXT

MONTH TROOPS WOULD BEGIN TO BE REPATRIATED TO ZIMBABWE,

FROM BOTH THE EXTRA TROOPS DISPATCHED AFTER THE

ASSASSINATION OF LAURENT KABILA AND THOSE BEING PULLED

BACK UNDER PHASE TWO. HE SAID MONUC HAD ASKED ALLIED

FORCES NOT TO OCCUPY PWETO EVEN THOUGH THEY WERE

ENTITLED TO DO SO UNDER KAMPALA AND THE HARARE SUBPLANS.

HE GAVE THE IMPRESSION THAT ZIMBABWE WOULD COMPLY.

7. (C) HAMADZIRIPI AS USUAL REMAINED SUSPICIOUS OF BOTH

UGANDA AND RWANDA, THOUGH NOT DISAPPOINTED WITH THEIR

FALLING OUT WITH EACH OTHER. UGANDA HAD SO FAR

CONFIDENTIAL

WITHDRAWN 550 SOLDIERS, NOT THE TWO BRIGADES IT WAS

TOUTING. RWANDA HAD OFFERED TO WITHDRAW 200 KILOMETERS

ONLY BECAUSE THIS WOULD SHORTEN ITS OVERSTRETCHED LINES

OF RESUPPLY. HE SAID THAT ZIMBABWE TOOK RWANDA\'S

SECURITY CONCERNS SERIOUSLY, CITING SOME DIRECT

BILATERAL EXCHANGES ON THE MATTER, BUT AT THE SAME TIME

ESSENTIALLY DISMISSED THEM AS A PRETEXT. WE ARGUED THAT

THEY HAD TO BE TAKEN SERIOUSLY. RWANDA\'S FIT OF ANGER

WITH ZAMBIA FOR ALLOWING SUSPECTED GENOCIDAIRES TO

ESCAPE WAS A FURTHER SIGN OF THE SERIOUSNESS WITH WHICH

THAT COUNTRY REGARDED THE ISSUE.

8. (C) HAMADZIRIPI

DISMISSED REPORTS THAT SERIOUS

DIFFERENCES HAD EMERGED BETWEEN ZIMBABWE AND ANGOLA IN

THE AFTERMATH OF LAURENT KABILA\'S ASSASSINATION. HE

ALSO DENIED THAT ZIMBABWE WAS IN CHARGE OF THE

INVESTIGATION OF THE ASSASSINATION. ZIMBABWE WAS ONLY

THE CHAIR OF WHAT WAS A SADC COMMISSION WHICH INCLUDED

ALL THE ALLIES. ON THE OTHER HAND, HAMADZIRIPI AT ONE

POINT SEEMED TO HINT THAT THE ANGOLANS COULD HAVE BEEN

INVOLVED IN THE ASSASSINATION.

9. (C) HAMADZIRIPI SAID THAT THE REASON ANGOLAN ARMED

FORCES COMMANDER DE MATOS HAD BEEN SACKED WAS FOR HIS

FREE-LANCING WITH UGANDA. HE HAD GONE TOO FAR IN HIS

OVERTURES TO UGANDA WITHOUT DOS SANTOS\' APPROVAL AND THE

PRESIDENT ALSO SAW HIM AS A POTENTIAL RIVAL. ZIMBABWE

WAS NOW CONFIDENT THAT ANGOLA HAD "DRAWN A LINE IN THE

SAND" AT MBANDAKA AGAINST THE MLC AND UGANDA.

ZIMBABWEAN AND ANGOLAN TROOPS WERE WORKING HARMONIOUSLY

IN KINSHASA, LUBUMBASHI AND WHEREVER ELSE THEY WERE

STATIONED TOGETHER.

10. (C) AS FOR THE POSITION OF THE INTERNATIONAL

PLAYERS, HAMADZIRIPI EXPRESSED SATISFACTION WITH THE US

POSITION, WHICH HE PERCEIVES AS HAVING EVOLVED IN

FAVORABLE DIRECTION UNDER THE NEW ADMINISTRATION. THE

UK IS STILL PERCEIVED AS A SPOILER. BELGIUM WAS GOING

ALL-OUT TO PLAY A LARGE ROLE, IN CONTRAST TO THE

PREVIOUS GOVERNMENT THERE, WHICH HAD BACKED AWAY FROM

AFRICA. BELGIUM WAS OFFERING, FOR EXAMPLE, TO HOST AND

PAY FOR THE INTER-CONGOLESE DIALOGUE. HE SAW THE

BELGIANS AS BEING SYMPATHETIC TO THE UNARMED OPPOSITION.

HAMADZIRIPI WAS ODDLY RETICENT ABOUT FRENCH POLICY. HE

ALLOWED THAT THE FRENCH DID NOT LIKE KAGAME, BUT SEEMED

TO PERCEIVE THE FRENCH POSITION AS BEING NOT ENTIRELY

FAVORABLE TO THE ZIMBABWEAN POSITION. [END TEXT OF

REPORT.]
