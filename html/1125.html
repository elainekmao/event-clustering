SUBJECT: CUBANS STILL IN JAIL, INS OFFICER ATTEMPTING TO

GAIN ACCESS FOR FACE-TO-FACE INTERVIEW

REF: HARARE 3096

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD FOR REASONS 1.5

B/D.

1. (C) ON JUNE 8 AMBASSADOR MCDONALD SPOKE WITH MINISTRY

OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS PERMANENT SECRETARY MACHIVENYIKA

MAPURANGA TO REITERATE OUR DESIRE TO SOLVE QUICKLY THE

REFUGEE CASE OF CUBAN DOCTORS LEONEL CORDOVA RODRIGUEZ

AND NORIS PENA MARTINEZ. THE AMBASSADOR INFORMED THE

PERMANENT SECRETARY THAT WE HAD HEARD FROM THE LOCAL

UNHCR REPRESENTATIVE THAT CUBAN AMBASSADOR RODOLFO

SARRACINO WAS PRESSURING THE MFA TO HAND OVER THE

CUBANS, A REQUEST THE AMBASSADOR EXPECTED THE ZIMBABWEAN

GOVERNMENT WOULD EXERCISE ITS SOVEREIGNTY TO DENY. THE

AMBASSADOR ALSO ASKED WHAT WOULD BE THE OPINION OF THE

ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT IF THE CUBANS WERE REMOVED TO

ANOTHER COUNTRY PENDING COMPLETION OF IMMIGRATION

FORMALITIES FOR THE UNITED STATES.

2. (C) MAPURANGA REPLIED THAT AS FAR AS THE GOVERNMENT

PAGE 03 HARARE 03105 091105Z

OF ZIMBABWE WAS CONCERNED THE CUBANS COULD GO WHERE THEY

WISHED; THE MATTER WAS NOW THE COMPETENCE OF UNHCR AND

THE RECEIVING COUNTRY. RESPONDING TO THE AMBASSADOR\'S

REMARK ABOUT THE CUBAN AMBASSADOR\'S EFFORTS, THE

PERMANENT SECRETARY SAID THAT HIS MINISTRY DID NOT

CHANGE ITS POSITION WITH "EVERY GUST OF WIND."

3. (C) AMBASSADOR MCDONALD LATER SPOKE WITH ACTING

CHIEF IMMIGRATION OFFICER MR. KARAGA TO SECURE THE

LATTER\'S COOPERATION IN ARRANGING ACCESS TO THE CUBANS

FOR EMBASSY CONSULAR OFFICER HEBRON AND NAIROBI INS

OFFICER SANJEETA VARSANI. THE CUBANS OPTED TO REMAIN IN

THE GOROMONZI DETENTION FACILITY AS THEY WOULD CONTINUE

TO BE HELD TOGETHER AND, IN ANY EVENT, LIVING CONDITIONS

WERE OSTENSIBLY BETTER THAN IN THE IMMIGRATION SERVICE\'S

TRANSIT CAMP, ACCORDING TO LOCAL UNHCR REPRESENTATIVE

JOHN ADU. THE CHIEF IMMIGRATION OFFICER DISCLAIMED

KNOWLEDGE OF THE ISSUE AND TRANSFERRED THE CALL TO THE

ACTION IMMIGRATION OFFICER, WHO INSISTED THAT THE UNHCR

REPRESENTATIVE CONTACT HIM DIRECTLY WITH THE REQUEST.

THE AMBASSADOR THEN SPOKE WITH UNHCR REPRESENTATIVE JOHN

ADU, WHO REPORTED THAT HE ALREADY HAD BEEN IN CONTACT

WITH THE ACTION OFFICER, BUT WOULD SEEK A FACE-TO-FACE

MEETING. AS THIS CABLE IS BEING ISSUED, THE INS OFFICER

AND EMBASSY CONSULAR OFFICER ARE ON THEIR WAY TO

GOROMONZI DETENTION FACILITY.

4. (C) COMMENT: WE CONTINUE TO THINK THAT EVACUATING

THE CUBANS FROM ZIMBABWE IS THE BEST COURSE OF ACTION TO

ENSURE THEIR WELFARE. IN FACT, AMBASSADOR MCDONALD

STRONGLY RECOMMENDS THAT THEY BE IMMEDIATELY REMOVED

FROM ZIMBABWE TO THE U.S. OR A THIRD COUNTRY. NO MATTER

HOW MANY ASSURANCES WE RECEIVE FROM THE GOZ, IT IS STILL

QUITE POSSIBLE SOMETHING SINISTER IS AFOOT. OUR

PREFERENCE WOULD BE FOR THEM TO GO TO THE U.S. FOR

PROCESSING OR CANADA, IF THEY MUST GO TO A THIRD COUNTRY

PENDING COMPLETION OF THEIR MEDICAL EXAMINATIONS OR

OTHER INS FORMALITIES. A THIRD OPTION WOULD BE KENYA.

END COMMENT.
