SUMMARY

1. THE CROATIAN SUPREME STATE COUNCIL MET ON

MARCH 16 TO CONSIDER ISSUES RELATING TO LAW AND

ORDER, CRIME AND THE TREATMENT OF SERBS IN

UNCLASSIFIED

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ZAGREB 00409 01 OF 03 180711Z

CROATIA. IN A SERIES OF DECLARATIONS, THE COUNCIL

(UNDER THE DIRECTION OF PRESDIDENT FRANJO TUDJMAN)

CALLED FOR RESPECTING THE LAW ON NATIONALITIES,

GRANTING AMNESTY TO THOSE COERCED INTO FIGHTING

AGAINST CROATIA AND THE PROSECUTION OF THOSE

GUILTY OF CRIMINAL ACTS. THE CROATIAN ASSEMBLY

WILL MEET ON MARCH 26-28 TO DISCUSS MANY OF THESE

ISSUES AS WELL AS OTHER CRITICAL LAWS,

PARTICULARLY THE NEW ELECTION LAWS. THE EC AND

CROATIAN GOVERNMENT REPRESENTATIVES HAVE MET TO

DISCUSS NATIONALITY ISSUES, TOO UNDER THE BACKDROP

OF THE ARRIVAL OF THE FIST CONTINGENT OF UN

TYROOPS TO EASTERN SLAVONIA. THE CROATIAN PRESS

HAS REPORTED A RUMOR THAT VANCE AND CARRINGTON

WILL VISIT THE SLOVENE CAPITAL THIS WEEK.

CROATIAN SUPREME STATE COUNCIL MEETS

2. IN A SESSION GIVEN VERY PROMINENT MEDIA

UNCLASSIFIED

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 04 ZAGREB 00409 01 OF 03 180711Z

COVERAGE, THE CROATIAN SUPREME STATE COUNCIL

PRESIDED OVER BY PRESIDENT FRANJO TUDJMAN MET

MARCH 16 TO CONSIDER ISSUES RELATED TO CRIME, LAW

AND ORDER AND TREATMENT OF MINORITIES. ACCORDING

TO THE PRESS, THE COUNCIL EXAMINED THE EFFECT OF

THE LAW ON NATIONAL MINORITIES AND ISSUED THE

FOLLOWING STATEMENTS:

-- THE LAW IS NOT FULLY RESPECTED AND THIS

SERIOUSLY ENDANGERS THE STABILITY OF CROATIAN

DEMOCRACY AND LEGAL ORDER;

-- ESPECIALLY UNFAVORABLE REPERCUSSIONS AND

CONSEQUENCIES HAVE OCCURRED IN THE THE CASE WHEN

THE SECURITY OF SERBS HAS BEEN THREATENED;

-- THE ORGANS OF THE CROATIAN REPUBLIC HAVE

INSTITUTED CRIMINAL CHARGES AGAINST 4,576 CITIZENS

FROM THE OCCUPIED TERRITORY OF CROATIA WHO THEY

ALLEGE HAVE BEEN GUILTY OF VIOLATING THE HUMAN AND

NATIONAL RIGHTS OF CITIZENS;

-- IT IS NECESSARY TO GRANT AMNESTY TO

PARTICIPANTS OF AGGRESSION ON CROATIA WHO WERE

FORCEFULLY MOBILIZED;

CROATIAN ASSEMBLY TO MEET

3. IN CONNECTION WITH THE UPCOMING ELECTIONS, THE

PRESIDENT OF THE CROATIAN ASSEMBLY, ZARKO DOMLJAN,

ANNOUNCED MARCH 16 THAT THE ASSEMBLY WILL HOLD ITS

NEXT SESSION MARCH 26-28. HE INFORMED THE PUBLIC

THAT THE ASSEMBLY WOULD CONSIDER THE FOLLOWING

ISSUES: THE 1992 BUDGET, RETURN OF REFUGEES AND

DISPLACED PERSONS TO THEIR HOMES, ELECTION LAWS,

DISCUSSION OF THE DEPLOYMENT OF UN PEACEKEEPING

FORCES IN CROATIA, AND AMENDMENTS TO THE

PRIVATIZATION LAW.

RELATIONS

4. A DELEGATION OF CROATIAN OFFICIALS CONSISTING

OF VICE-PRESIDENT OF THE CROATIAN GOVERNMENT

ZDRAVKO TOMAC, PRESIDENT OF THE ASSEMBLY COMMITTEE

FOR FOREIGN POLICY, HRVOJE KACIC, MINISTER IN THE

CROATIAN GOVERNMENT, ZIVKO JUZBASIC, AND VLADIMIR

DEGAN, MET MARCH 16 WITH EC OFFICIALS TO DISCUSS

HOW TO IMPROVE RELATIONS BETWEEN NATIONALITIES IN

A POST-WAR CROATIA. AMONG OTHER ISSUES THE

PARTICIPANTS AGREED ON THE NEED TO REPEAL CERTAIN

REGULATIONS WHICH WERE ENACTED DURING THE WAR,

ALTHOUGH NOT SPECIFIED. THE REGULATIONS ARE

APPARENTLY RELATED TO THE RETURN OF SERBS WHO WERE

FORCEFULLY MOBILIZED BY THE JNA. SOME OF THESE

SERBS WERE INCLUDED IN THE PRISONER EXCHANGES THAT

HAVE BEEN TAKING PLACE AND THESE PERSONS INTEND TO

GO BACK TO THEIR HOMES IN CROATIA.

CARRINGTON AND VANCE TO SLOVENIA?

6. THE ZAGREB DAILY "VECERNJI LIST" OF MARCH 17

QUOTES THE SLOVENIAN PRESIDENT MILAN KUCAN AS

HAVING TOLD A SLOVENIAN ASSEMBLY COMMITTEE THAT

THE EC MEDIATOR, LORD CARRINGTON, AND THE UN ENVOY

CYRUS VANCE MAY VISIT SLOVENIA THIS WEEK.

ACCORDING TO THE PRESS REPORTS, KUCAN IMPLIED THAT

VANCE AND CARRINGTON WOULD HOLD TALKS WITH

SLOVENIAN OFFICIALS ON SUCCESSOR STATE ISSUES.

KUCAN HAS COME OUT AGAINST THE "RUSSIAN MODEL",

WHICH ALLOWED FOR THE RUSSIAN REPUBLIC TO BECOME

RECOGNITION UPDATE

ALREADY RECOGNIZED CROATIA, PERU (ON MARCH 15) AND
