SUBJECT: VATICAN CLUELESS ON MADAGASCAR

REF: MORIARTY/FROWICK TELCON 04/12

CLASSIFIED BY: AMBASSADOR JIM NICHOLSON, REASON E.O. 12958 1.5 (B,D).

1. (C) PER REFTELCON, POLOFF MET MORNING OF MONDAY, APRIL 15

WITH VATICAN DESK OFFICER FOR MADAGASCAR AFFAIRS MONS. LUIGI

CAVALIERE. (NOTE: CAVALIERE, AN "OLD SCHOOL" VATICAN

DIPLOMAT WHO ALSO COVERS AUSTRIA, FRANCE, NETHERLANDS,

SWITZERLAND AND SEVERAL OTHER AFRICAN COUNTRIES, HAD -- LIKE

POLOFF -- TO READ UP ON THE SITUATION IN MADAGASCAR BEFORE

THE MEETING. END NOTE.) CAVALIERE HAD NOTHING MUCH TO OFFER

CONFIDENTIAL

BY WAY OF INSIGHT ON A WAY OUT OF THE DETERIORATING SITUATION

IN MADAGASCAR. CAVALIERE SAID THE SITUATION WAS DELICATE,

BUT VAGUE AND FLUID, AND HADN\'T MUCH MORE TO ADD. IN FACT,

TO UNDERSCORE THE HOLY SEE\'S LACK OF FOCUS ON MADAGASCAR, HE

CHARACTERIZED IT AS A "CIOCCOLATINO" (CHOCOLATE TIDBIT)

COMPARED TO THE HOLY SEE\'S WORRIES IN THE MIDDLE EAST.

INDEED, HE LIMITED HIMSELF TO RESTATING THE POPE\'S LAST

PRONOUNCEMENT ON MADAGASCAR, READ AT THE MARCH 3 ANGELUS

PRAYER, IN WHICH THE PONTIFF CALLED FOR PEACE, JUSTICE AND

DIALOGUE.

2. (C) NOTING THAT THE HOLY SEE HAD IN OTHER AFRICAN

CONFLICT AREAS BEEN ABLE TO EMPLOY A SPECIAL DIALOGUE WITH

INVOLVED PARTIES (E.G. THE JESUIT CONTACTS WITH MUGABE),

POLOFF ASKED WHETHER THE CAVALIERE KNEW OF ANY PERSONAL

CONTACTS CHURCH OFFICIALS MIGHT HAVE WITH RATSIRANGA. HE

KNEW OF NONE, BUT AVERRED THAT THE NUNCIO (WHO HAD JUST

RETURNED TO ANTANANARIVO FROM A FAMILY VISIT IN ITALY) WOULD

BE IN THE BEST POSITION TO TALK TO BOTH PARTIES.

3. (C) COMMENT: OBVIOUSLY CAVALIERE WAS NOT SO CONCERNED AT

DEVELOPMENTS IN MADAGASCAR ACTUALLY TO BE FOLLOWING THEM. HE

TOLD US HE HAD RELIED MOSTLY ON FRENCH DAILY LE MONDE

REPORTS. INDEED, HE SEEMED TO BE LIVING UP TO HIS NAME --

FOR EXAMPLE HE POOH-POOHED THE USG DECISION TO MOVE TO

ORDERED DEPARTURE. WE DO NOT BELIEVE HIS CAVALIER ATTITUDE

WOULD REFLECT THE CONCERNS OF HIS SUPERIORS.

NICHOLSON
