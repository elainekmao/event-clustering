SUBJECT: ITALY SUPPORTS SOMALI PEACE, NOT PUNTLAND

MILITARY; THINKS ETHIOPIA\'S MELES MAY BE STALLING

DEMARCATION

REF: FBIS AFP20020521000147

CLASSIFIED BY: POLMINCOUNS TOM COUNTRYMAN, REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D)

1. (C) SUMMARY: THE MFA DENIES REF REPORT THAT ITALY HAS

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 ROME 02602 291334Z

PROMISED MILITARY AID TO PUNTLAND. THE GOI HAS NO PLANS TO

AID PUNTLAND BUT IS OFFERING AID FOR TRAVEL EXPENSES RELATED

TO THE SOMALI PEACE PROCESS AND COULD EXTEND FURTHER PEACE

PROCESS AID ON REQUEST. THE MFA\'S READOUT ON A RECENT GOI

TRIP TO ETHIOPIA INDICATES THAT MELES IS INTERESTED IN

DRAGGING OUT THE DEMARCATION PROCESS AS LONG AS POSSIBLE.

END SUMMARY.

2. (C) POLOFF CONTACTED PAOLA IMPERIALE, MFA OFFICE DIRECTOR

FOR EAST AFRICAN AFFAIRS, ON MAY 24 TO INQUIRE ABOUT REF

REPORT OF COL. ABDULLAHI YUSUF AHMAD SAYING THAT PUNTLAND HAD

BEEN PROMISED MILITARY ASSISTANCE BY UGANDA, ETHIOPIA, AND

ITALY. IMPERIALE STATED THAT THE GOI HAD NOT OFFERED ANY

ASSISTANCE TO PUNTLAND. AHMAD HAD WRITTEN A LETTER TO THE

GOI REQUESTING AID FOR VARIOUS PURPOSES AND HAD NOT YET

RECEIVED A RESPONSE. HOWEVER, THE GOI WOULD HAVE TO REPLY IN

THE NEGATIVE, SINCE PUNTLAND\'S PRIORITIES WERE NOT COVERED BY

THE GOI\'S FOREIGN AID LAW.

3. (C) ON THE OTHER HAND, THE GOI WAS PREPARED TO SUPPORT

THE PEACE PROCESS FINANCIALLY. IN RESPONSE TO A REQUEST BY

KENYA\'S SPECIAL ENVOY FOR SOMALIA, THE GOI HAD DECIDED TO

PROVIDE UP TO 100,000E FOR TRAVEL TO NAIROBI BY THE MEDIATION

COMMITTEE OF FRONT-LINE STATES. FURTHERMORE, THE GOI

EXPECTED KENYA TO ASK THE IGAD PARTNERS\' FORUM TO PAY FOR

ACCOMMODATIONS FOR DELEGATES TO THE PEACE CONFERENCE, PLANNED

FOR JULY 10 IN NAIROBI. THE GOI WOULD CONSIDER THIS REQUEST

FAVORABLY.

4. (C) IMPERIALE HAD JUST RETURNED FROM A TRIP TO ETHIOPIA

WITH SENATOR RINO SERRI, THE SPECIAL REPRESENTATIVE FOR THE

PRESIDENCY OF THE EUROPEAN UNION FOR THE ETHIOPIA-ERITREA

CONFLICT. THEY SPOKE WITH ETHIOPIAN PRESIDENT MELES ABOUT

HIS 23-PAGE LETTER TO THE PRESIDENT OF THE BOUNDARY

COMMISSION REQUESTING CLARIFICATIONS ON THE DECISION. MELES

DESCRIBED THE LETTER AS A LEGAL, NOT POLITICAL, MATTER.

POLITICALLY, THE VERDICT WAS FINAL AND BINDING, BUT UNMEE

SHOULD STAY UNTIL THE LAST DEMARCATION MARKER WAS PLACED.

IMPERIALE\'S VIEW WAS THAT MELES WAS TAKING THIS STANCE MOSTLY

IN ORDER TO SPITE ERITREA, WHICH SOUGHT A SPEEDY DEMARCATION

AND THE DEPARTURE OF UNMEE.
