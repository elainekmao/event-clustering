1. CLASSIFIED BY A/DCM SCOTT BELLARD, REASON 1.5 (B) AND

(D)

2. (C) VIETNAM SUPPORTS THE ASEAN "10 MINUS X" FORMULA, SO

LONG AS IT IS CONFINED TO THE ECONOMIC REALM AND DOES NOT

INTERFERE WITH POLICY OR POLITICAL AFFAIRS. ACCORDING TO

MFA ASEAN DEPARTMENT DEPUTY DIRECTOR GENERAL PHAM HUU CHI,

VIETNAM ACCEPTS THE WISH OF OTHER ASEAN MEMBERS TO MOVE

FORWARD WITH THE 10 MINUS X POLICY AS A MEANS OF ADVANCING

"SEPARATE BUT COMPATIBLE AGENDAS." HANOI\'S MAIN CONCERN IS

THAT THIS FORMULA NOT ENCROACH INTO AREAS DEALING WITH

POLITICAL FORMULATIONS, CHI NOTED.

3. (C) UNDER THE CURRENT DESIGN, CHI SAID THAT ALL MEMBER

STATES WOULD BE FREE TO JOIN OR AVOID SIGNING ANY ASEAN

INITIATIVE AT THEIR OWN DISCRETION. HANOI BELIEVES THIS

FORMULA COULD WORK WITH AN X VARIABLE OF 2 TO 3 COUNTRIES,

BUT "ANY MORE WOULD BE TOO MUCH, AND WOULD JUST NOT WORK."

IF 5 TO 6 MEMBERS DECIDED AGAINST AGREEING TO A PLAN, THEN

"THAT INITIATIVE WOULD SIMPLY BE UNTENABLE UNDER ASEAN

STANDARDS," HE ADDED.

4. (C) WHEN ASKED HOW THIS MIGHT AFFECT THE DEVELOPMENT

GAP, CHI STATED THAT HE DID NOT THINK IT WOULD HAVE MUCH OF

AN EFFECT AS LONG AS THE MORE AFFLUENT AND DEVELOPED

MEMBERS CONTINUED TO PROVIDE THE DEVELOPING STATES WITH

EDUCATIONAL, SCIENTIFIC, AND INFRASTRUCTUAL ASSISTANCE.

CHI ADDED THAT VIETNAM WOULD LIKE TO SEE THE MINUS X

FORMULA GROW TO INCLUDE AREAS OF SCIENTIFIC AND EDUCATIONAL

COOPERATION AS WELL AS ECONOMICS.

5. (C) CHI ADMITTED THAT THIS WAS A NEW PROPOSAL AND WOULD

TAKE TIME TO REFINE. HE SAID THAT "SOME MEMBERS WERE MORE

EAGER THAN OTHERS" IN MOVING THIS INITIATIVE FORWARD AND

THAT FURTHER DISCUSSION ON THIS TOPIC IS STILL NEEDED.
