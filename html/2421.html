SUBJECT: MEDIA REPORT PAPER ACCUSES U. S. OF FUNDING A

PIRATE RADIO STATION; HARARE

1. THE JANUARY 25 EDITION OF THE GOVERNMENT-CONTROLLED

DAILY "THE HERALD" CARRIED AN ARTICLE ON PAGE ONE,

PICKED UP FROM THE JANUARY 24 EDITION OF THE BRITISH

GUARDIAN NEWSPAPER, ACCUSING THE U. S. OF FUNDING A

"PIRATE RADIO STATION" BROADCASTING TO ZIMBABWE. THE

ARTICLE, UNDER HEADLINE "U. S. FUNDING PIRATE RADIO

STATION: PAPER," ACCUSES THE U. S. OF PAYING OUT

MILLIONS OF DOLLARS TO THE STATION THROUGH A DEPARTMENT

OF THE U. S. AGENCY FOR INTERNATIONAL DEVELOPMENT

(USAID) AND THE OFFICE OF TRANSITION INITIATIVES. THE

STATION, WHICH HAS BEEN ON AIR FOR A MONTH AND

BROADCASTS THREE HOURS A NIGHT ON SHORT-WAVE IS HEADED

BY GERRY JACKSON, A BRITISH NATIONAL WHO WAS SACKED BY

THE STATE-RUN ZIMBABWE BROADCASTING CORPORATION FIVE

YEARS AGO. ARTICLE EXCERPTS FOLLOW:

2. "THE U. S. IS SECRETLY FUNDING A PIRATE RADIO STATION

IN LONDON, SW RADIO AFRICA, WIDELY ACCUSED OF FUELLING

POLITICAL VIOLENCE AND TRIBAL HATRED WITH ITS NIGHTLY

BROADCASTS TO ZIMBABWE. . .MILLIONS OF DOLLARS ARE

BEING CHANNELED TO THE STATION FROM A DEPARTMENT OF THE

USAID AND THE OFFICE OF THE TRANSITION INITIATIVES. .

.THE STATION\'S SPOKESWOMAN AND FORMER ZBC PRESENTER,

GEORGINA GODWIN, SAID THAT FUNDING FOR THE STATION CAME

FROM \'HUMAN RIGHTS AND MEDIA FREEDOM GROUPS,\' BUT WOULD

ANSWER NO FURTHER QUESTIONS. THE GUARDIAN REPORT SAID

THAT SW RADIO AFRICA. . .WAS GIVING THE OPPOSITION A

PLATFORM, PROVIDING AN ENDLESS DIET OF PROPAGANDA AND

FALSEHOODS ON THE ECONOMIC AND POLITICAL SITUATION IN

THE COUNTRY. . . ."
