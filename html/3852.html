1. (C) FOLLOWING THE HOLY SEE\'S REBUKE OF RUSSIA BEFORE OSCE

CHIEFS OF MISSION TO THE HOLY SEE FOR ITS SERIES OF

EXPULSIONS AND VISA DENIALS TO CATHOLIC CLERGY (REF A),

VATICAN OSCE DESK OFFICER TOLD POLOFF SEPTEMBER 30 THAT THE

HOLY SEE PLANS TO SEEK REDRESS WITHIN THE OSCE FOR THE

CANCELLATION OF VISAS AND/OR EXPULSION OF FIVE CATHOLIC

CLERGY BY RUSSIAN AUTHORITIES. FM JEAN-LOUIS TAURAN HAS

DECIDED TO PURSUE THE ISSUE IN THE OSCE BY INVOKING ALREADY

AGREED OSCE AND UN LANGUAGE FROM DECLARATIONS ON HUMAN RIGHTS

AND RELIGIOUS FREEDOM. IN DOING SO, THE HOLY SEE HOPES TO

AVOID SINGLING OUT RUSSIA FOR FAILINGS SHARED BY A NUMBER OF

OTHER MOSTLY FORMER SOVIET OSCE MEMBER STATES. THE HOLY SEE

BELIEVES THE RUSSIAN VISA REFUSALS AND EXPULSIONS HAVE

CREATED A &REAL OBSTACLE FOR PEOPLE TO EXERCISE THEIR

RELIGIOUS FREEDOM IN RUSSIA TODAY.8 END SUMMARY.

2. (C) HOLY SEE OSCE DESK OFFICER MONS. ETTORE BALESTRERO

OUTLINED FOR POLOFF SEPTEMBER 30 THE HOLY SEE,S OSCE-BASED

STRATEGY TO PRESSURE RUSSIA TO REVERSE A SERIES OF RECENT

VISA DENIALS AND EXPULSIONS OF CATHOLIC CLERGY (REFS C AND

D). THEY ARE SEEKING RUSSIAN ACKNOWLEDGMENT OF ITS

COMMITMENTS ON RELIGIOUS FREEDOM AS LEVERAGE TO GET RUSSIAN

AUTHORITIES TO ISSUE THE CATHOLIC CLERGY ENTRY AND VISAS THEY

HAVE RECENTLY DENIED. BALESTRERO TOLD US THE HOLY SEE

INTENDS TO PURSUE THE CASE IN THE OSCE, INITIALLY AT THE

OCTOBER 4 PC IN VIENNA. THEY HOPE TO GAIN AGREEMENT THERE TO

PURSUE A DECLARATION REAFFIRMING FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLES OF

RELIGIOUS FREEDOM TO BE ISSUED AT THE PORTO MINISTERIAL IN

DECEMBER. THE HOLY SEE CONSIDERS THE RUSSIAN GOVERNMENT\'S

VISA REFUSALS AND EXPULSIONS AS SERIOUS RELIGIOUS FREEDOM

ISSUES THAT HAVE CREATED A &REAL OBSTACLE FOR PEOPLE TO

EXERCISE THEIR RELIGIOUS FREEDOM IN RUSSIA TODAY.8 AT THE

SAME TIME, BALESTRERO SAID HE DIDN\'T DETECT ANY ANTI-CATHOLIC

SENTIMENT IN HIS RUSSIAN INTERLOCUTORS AT THE WARSAW HUMAN

DIMENSION MEETING, BUT HE ENCOUNTERED THE SAME COMMENT

REPORTED REF A -) THAT THIS WAS A MATTER FOR THE HOLY SEE TO

WORK OUT DIRECTLY WITH THE ORTHODOX CHURCH.

3. (C) CONTRARY TO THE CLAIM REPORTED REF. B, THE HOLY SEE

DOES NOT BELIEVE IT MISSTEPPED IN CREATING THE FOUR DIOCESES

THAT THE GOR CLAIMS TRIGGERED THE RECENT RASH OF VISA

REFUSALS/EXPULSIONS OF CATHOLIC CLERGY. THEY MAINTAIN THAT

ESTABLISHING THE DIOCESES WAS A NORMAL AND LONG OVERDUE

RESPONSE TO AN ABNORMAL SITUATION FOR THE CATHOLIC FAITHFUL

IN RUSSIA. BALESTRERO DID CONCEDE THAT MANY IN RUSSIA

UNFORTUNATELY DID NOT UNDERSTAND JUST HOW ROUTINE AN ACT THE

UPGRADING WAS. HE SPECULATED THAT SOME LOCAL RUSSIAN

ORTHODOX CLERGY MAY HAVE EXPRESSED CONCERN TO LOCAL GOR

OFFICIALS BASED ON A MISUNDERSTANDING OF THE MOVE, AND THAT

THOSE CONCERNS COULD HAVE BEEN PASSED UP TO CENTRAL

AUTHORITIES. IN THE HOLY SEE\'S VIEW THE RUSSIAN ORTHODOX

CLERGY AND SOME IN THE RUSSIAN GOVERNMENT WERE LOOKING FOR AN

EXCUSE TO SUPPRESS THE CATHOLIC CLERGY, AND GOT IT WHEN THE

HOLY SEE UPGRADED THE APOSTOLIC ADMINISTRATIONS TO DIOCESES.

HE BELIEVED THAT IF THE HOLY SEE HADN\'T MADE THE DIOCESAN

DECISION, SOMETHING ELSE WOULD HAVE SERVED AS THE OCCASION

FOR THE ACTIONS. AT THE END OF THE DAY, HE STRESSED, THE

VISAS AND ENTRY PERMITS WERE REFUSED BY CIVIL, NOT CHURCH

AUTHORITIES, IN VIOLATION OF RUSSIA\'S SOLEMN INTERNATIONAL

COMMITMENTS.

4. (SBU) COMMENT: THERE ARE VERY FEW APOSTOLIC

ADMINISTRATIONS ANYWHERE IN THE WORLD. MOST ARE IN PLACES

LIKE PRIZREN IN KOSOVO, OR THE CORMOROS. ALL OF THEM ARE

SEEN TO BE TEMPORARY MEASURES UNTIL THE MORE NORMAL CONDITION

OF A DIOCESE IS ESTABLISHED. THE CREATION OF A DIOCESE

ESSENTIALLY GIVES ITS BISHOP MORE AUTONOMY FOR THE DAY-TO-DAY

RUNNING OF CHURCH AFFAIRS. THE HOLY SEE UNDERTOOK NUMEROUS

MEASURES TO ALLEVIATE ANY HEARTBURN BY THE RUSSIAN ORTHODOX

CHURCH WHEN CREATING THESE DIOCESES AFTER MORE THAN A DECADE

OF APOSTOLIC ADMINISTRATION. FOR EXAMPLE, THEY AVOIDED

NAMING THEM FOR THEIR SEES, AS IN &MOSCOW,8 SO THAT THERE

WOULD NOT BE A CATHOLIC BISHOP OF MOSCOW AT THE SAME TIME AS

THERE WAS AN ORTHODOX BISHOP OF MOSCOW. AND IN SAKHALIN,

THEY NAMED A POLE, BISHOP MAZUR, INSTEAD OF THE JAPANESE,

BISHOP PETER JINUSHI, PRECISELY TO AVOID ANY IMPLICATION THAT

THE HOLY SEE SUPPORTS THE RETURN OF SOUTHERN SAKHALIN TO

JAPAN. NEVERTHELESS, THE HOLY SEE FEELS STRONGLY THAT THE

CHURCH HAS TO TAKE CARE OF ITS OWN BUSINESS IN RUSSIA AS

ELSEWHERE, AND CANNOT SACRIFICE ITS PASTORAL REQUIREMENTS

INDEFINITELY TO PLACATE THE UNFOUNDED FEARS OF THE RUSSIAN

ORTHODOX CHURCH.
