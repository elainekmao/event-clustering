SUBJECT: KABILA\'S BRIEF VISIT TO ZIMBABWE FOCUSES ON DRC

COMMERCIAL TIES TO THE ZANU-PF GOVERNMENT

REF: HARARE 1131

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS: 1.5 (B) AND (D).

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 01168 291404Z

1. (C) ON MARCH 28, POLOFF OBTAINED A READOUT ON DRC

PRESIDENT JOSEPH KABILA\'S TWO-DAY VISIT TO HARARE FROM

THE MFA\'S PRINCIPAL ADMINISTRATIVE OFFICER (PAO) ON THE

DRC, MAXWELL CHIKOROWONDO. REGARDING KABILA\'S ADDRESS

TO PARLIAMENT (REFTEL), THE PAO NOTED THAT KABILA IS

"PREPARED TO GO THE FULL MILE FOR PEACE" IN THE DRC, BUT

CONTINUES TO INSIST THAT THE "AGGRESSOR STATES" WITHDRAW

FROM DRC TERRITORY BEFORE THE ALLIES DO. HE ALSO NOTED

KABILA\'S STATEMENT THAT SADC\'S DECISION TO SEND TROOPS

TO THE DRC WAS A UNANIMOUS ONE.

2. (C) CHIKOROWONDO REPORTED THAT KABILA HELD A "LONG"

ONE-ON-ONE MEETING WITH PRESIDENT MUGABE, BUT THAT HE

WAS NOT PRIVY TO WHAT WAS DISCUSSED. KABILA ALSO MET

WITH TRANSPORT MINISTER SWITHUN MOMBESHORA AND LANDS AND

AGRICULTURE MINISTER JOSEPH MADE (BUT NOT THE FULL

CABINET) TO DISCUSS "BILATERAL ISSUES IN THEIR

RESPECTIVE FIELDS." KABILA HELD A BREAKFAST MEETING

WITH "NEARLY EVERY PROMINENT ZIMBABWEAN BUSINESSMAN,"

ACCORDING TO THE MFA OFFICIAL. KABILA REPORTEDLY

ENCOURAGED ZIMBABWEAN INDUSTRY TO INCREASE ITS ALREADY

SIGNIFICANT INVESTMENT IN THE DRC AND DISCUSSED WAYS IN

WHICH IT COULD HAPPEN.

3. (C) POLOFF ASKED CHIKOROWONDO TO COMMENT ON THE

PRESS ITEM QUOTING A MONUC REPORT THAT STATED THE ALLIED

FORCES HAD NOT BEGUN TO WITHDRAW FROM THE DRC FRONTLINES

AS THEY WERE SUPPOSED TO. CHIKOROWONDO STATED THAT THE

MONUC REPORT IN QUESTION WAS FROM THE FIRST FEW DAYS OF

THE DISENGAGEMENT PROCESS THAT BEGAN IN MID-MARCH. THE

REPORT HAS SINCE BEEN OVERTAKEN BY EVENTS AS THE ALLIED

FORCES ARE WITHDRAWING ON SCHEDULE, AND WILL COMPLETE

THEIR 15 KM PULLBACK IN KATANGA BY THE MARCH 29

DEADLINE, THE PAO ASSERTED. HE ALSO REMARKED THAT

INITIALLY THERE HAD BEEN SOME CONFUSION OVER THE

WITHDRAWAL OF FORCES FROM PWETO, BUT THAT HAS SINCE BEEN

RESOLVED. MONUC HAS YET TO CONFIRM THE PULLBACK OF

BEMBA\'S FORCES IN EQUATEUR, PROBABLY BECAUSE BEMBA HAS

NOT YET GIVEN THE ORDER TO WITHDRAW, CHIKOROWONDO ADDED.

4. (C) COMMENT: KABILA\'S VISIT TO ZIMBABWE APPEARED TO

HAVE HAD THREE MAIN OBJECTIVES: 1) TO THANK ZIMBABWE FOR

ITS INTERVENTION IN THE DRC, 2) TO ASSUAGE ZIMBABWEAN

CONCERNS ABOUT ITS CONTINUED PARTICIPATION, AND MOST

IMPORTANT, 3) TO IMPROVE THE ECONOMIC LINKAGES BETWEEN

THE TWO COUNTRIES. IT IS NOTEWORTHY THAT KABILA WOULD

SPEND A LARGE BLOCK OF HIS LIMITED TIME HERE CONSULTING

WITH MINISTERS WITH EXTRACTIVE-SECTOR PORTFOLIOS AND THE

INDIGENOUS BUSINESS COMMUNITY. ACCORDING TO OUR

CONTACTS, THE CEO OF METROPOLITAN BANK, WHO IS ALSO A

MEMBER OF THE INDIGENOUS BUSINESS ASSOCIATION, HOSTED

THE BREAKFAST MEETING. IT INVOLVED ONLY MEMBERS WITH

STRONG TIES TO ZANU-PF, AND NO MEMBERS OF THE WHITE

BUSINESS COMMUNITY ATTENDED. THESE MEETINGS, COUPLED

WITH THE OVER-THE-TOP RECEPTION OF PRESIDENT KABILA BY

PUTTING HIM ON THE SAME PLANE AS NELSON MANDELA,

UNDERSCORE THE GOZ\'S MAIN PURPOSE FOR ITS COSTLY

INVOLVEMENT IN THE DRC. END COMMENT.

IRVING
