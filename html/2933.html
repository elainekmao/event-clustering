SUBJECT: ITALIAN VIEWS ON THE ADVOCATE GENERAL\'S OPINION ON

OPEN SKIES CASES

UNCLASSIFIED

REF: STATE 37639

1. (U) SUMMARY: SENIOR ITALIAN TRANSPORT MINISTRY AND CIVIL

AVIATION AUTHORITY (ENAC) OFFICIALS, AS WELL AS INFORMED

SECTOR EXPERTS, AGREE THAT THE BILATERAL U.S.-ITALY OPEN

SKIES AGREEMENT WILL CONTINUE IN EFFECT UNTIL A NEW U.S.-EU

AGREEMENT IS SIGNED. ITALIAN OFFICIALS EXPECT TO BE

INVOLVED IN THE NEGOTIATION OF THE NEW ACCORD, AND

ANTICIPATE THAT IT WILL TAKE YEARS TO NEGOTIATE. END

SUMMARY.

2. (U) ECONOFFS SPOKE WITH SENIOR GOI INTERNATIONAL

AVIATION OFFICIALS IN THE TRANSPORT MINISTRY AND NATIONAL

CIVIL AVIATION AUTHORITY (ENAC), AND ANOTHER INFORMED

AVIATION EXPERT, REGARDING THE OPINION RELEASED BY THE

EUROPEAN COURT OF JUSTICE (ECJ) JANUARY 31. THAT OPINION

RELATED TO THE PENDING COURT CASES FILED IN 1998 BY THE EU

COMMISSION AGAINST EU MEMBER STATES THAT HAVE CONCLUDED

OPEN SKIES AGREEMENTS WITH THE UNITED STATES.

3. (SBU) ALESSIO QUARANTA, CHIEF OF INTERNATIONAL RELATIONS

OF THE NATIONAL CIVIL AVIATION AUTHORITY (ENAC), TOLD US

MAY 8 THAT, WHILE THE EC WILL EVENTUALLY GAIN A MANDATE TO

NEGOTIATE SUCH AGREEMENTS FOR MEMBER STATES, HE DOES NOT

BELIEVE THAT THIS WILL RENDER INVALID PREVIOUS BILATERAL

AGREEMENTS. QUARANTA BELIEVES THAT ANY EC-NEGOTIATED

AGREEMENT WOULD SIMPLY "COMPLEMENT" PREVIOUS BILATERAL

AGREEMENTS, AND ALL FREEDOMS CONTAINED WITHIN THEM WOULD BE

HONORED, EVEN IF NOT INCLUDED IN A LATER EC AGREEMENT.

QUARANTA BELIEVES THAT MEMBER STATES (OR AT LEAST ITALY)

WOULD SIT AS OBSERVERS IN ANY EC-LED NEGOTIATIONS WITH THE

USG.

4. (SBU) ENAC\'S MARINO BARZAGHI, ADVISOR TO THE PRESIDENT,

AND LUISA TIMPONE, DIRECTOR OF INTERNATIONAL AGREEMENTS

TOLD US THAT THEY BELIEVE THE STATUS QUO WILL CONTINUE

UNTIL THE EU NEGOTIATES A NEW EU-USG AGREEMENT, WHICH IS

UNLIKELY TO BE SOON.

5. (SBU) TRANSPORT MINISTRY DEPUTY DG FOR AVIATION ALDO

SANSONE TOLD US THAT, AFTER THE EUROPEAN COURT OF JUSTICE

DECISION, EU MEMBER STATES WILL DECIDE WITH THE EUROPEAN

COMMISSION WHETHER EACH STATE SHOULD AGREE TO EXTEND ITS

BILATERAL OPEN SKIES AGREEMENT TO ALL EU CARRIERS AND

NATIONS OR WHETHER THE EU WILL BE GIVEN A MANDATE TO

NEGOTIATE A USG-EU AGREEMENT (WHICH HE THINKS IS FAR MORE

LIKELY).

6. (SBU) SANSONE STRESSED THAT THE BILATERAL U.S.-ITALY

OPEN SKIES AGREEMENT WILL CONTINUE IN FORCE UNTIL THIS

COMMON EU DECISION IS MADE. THE ITALIAN GOVERNMENT WILL

CERTAINLY ABIDE BY THIS DECISION, ALTHOUGH ITALY AND THE

NETHERLANDS ARE NOT INCLUDED IN THE 1998 COURT CASES FILED

BY THE EU COMMISSION. SANSONE BELIEVES THE OMISSIONS WERE

MERELY AN OVERSIGHT.

7. (SBU) SANSONE EXPECTS THAT EU MEMBER STATES WILL

CONTINUE TO CONCLUDE BILATERAL AGREEMENTS WITH OTHER NON-EU

MEMBER STATES (FOR EXAMPLE, BETWEEN ITALY AND TUNISIA) WHEN

THE NEGOTIATED VALUE OF THE AGREEMENT IS NOT HIGH AND

THEREFORE NOT IN CONFLICT WITH THE INTERESTS OF OTHER EU

MEMBER STATES.

8. (SBU) GUIDO VITALI, HEAD OF HUB DEVELOPMENT FOR

SOCIETA\' AEROPORTI DI ROMA, THE ROME AIRPORT HANDLING

COMPANY AUTHORITY IS CONVINCED THAT THE EU COURT WILL RULE

AGAINST BILATERAL OPEN SKIES AGREEMENTS AND THAT A MANDATE

WILL BE GIVEN TO THE EC TO NEGOTIATE FOR THE MEMBER STATES.

HE EXPECTS THAT, "ALTHOUGH THE U.S. GOVERNMENT ALREADY HAS

SIGNED AGREEMENTS WITH CARIBBEAN AND APEC COUNTRIES AND MAY

SIGN A BILATERAL, UNRESTRICTED AGREEMENT WITH CANADA, THE

U.S. WILL NOT EASILY AGREE TO A SINGLE OPEN SKIES AGREEMENT

WITH THE EU." IN THE MEANTIME, WHILE NO U.S.-EU AGREEMENT

IS IN PLACE, THE BILATERAL OPEN SKIES AGREEMENTS WILL

CONTINUE IN FORCE. THE PROCESS, HE EXPLAINS, OF

INVALIDATING THE EXISTING AGREEMENTS AND GIVING EXCLUSIVE

COMPETENCE TO THE EC TO NEGOTIATE A SINGLE U.S.-EU

AGREEMENT, WILL BE LONG. NOT ONLY IS THE VALUE OF

NEGOTIATED RIGHTS EXTREMELY HIGH, BUT THE AGREEMENT WILL

SET A PRECEDENT FOR ALL FUTURE EU AVIATION AGREEMENTS.
