SUBJECT: ITALY PRIVACY DRAFT EXECUTVE ORDER WOULD

REDUCE TIME REQUIRED TO MAINTAIN TELEPHONE DATABASES

THIS MESSAGE IS SENSITIVE BUT UNCLASSIFIED.

1. (U) THIS IS AN ACTION REQUEST, SEE PARAGRAPH FIVE.

2. (SBU) IN A WORRISOME AND UNEXPECTED DEVELOPMENT, THE

GOI HAS ISSUED A LEGISLATIVE DECREE WITH A PROVISION

THAT, IF ADOPTED IN ITS CURRENT FORM, WOULD REDUCE THE

REQUIREMENT FOR TELECOM COMPANIES TO MAINTAIN TELEPHONE

DATABASES FROM FIVE YEARS TO 18 MONTHS. THE OMNIBUS

DECREE IS BEFORE THE JUSTICE COMMITTEES OF THE CHAMBER

R

AND SENATE, WHICH MUST ISSUE OPINIONS, ALTHOUGH NON-

BINDING, ON THE DECREE BEFORE JUNE 30. PARLIAMENT, IN A

2001 LAW, MANDATED THE EXECUTIVE BRANCH TO ISSUE PRIVACY-

RELATED DECREES, OF WHICH THIS OMNIBUS DECREE IS ONE.

3. (SBU) ACCORDING TO OUR CONTACTS IN THE SENATE AND

PRESS STORIES, THE SPECIFIC 18 MONTHS MEASURE WAS

REPORTEDLY VIGOROUSLY SUPPORTED BY THE ITALIAN PRIVACY

AUTHORITY, WHICH HAS RAISED CONCERNS ABOUT THE BIG

BROTHER ASPECTS OF A FIVE-YEAR RETENTION RULE. THE

AUTHORITY ALSO PURPORTEDLY ARGUED THAT COSTS TO MAINTAIN

DATA FOR FIVE YEARS WOULD BE EXCESSIVE. ITALIAN LAW

ENFORCEMENT OFFICIALS STRONGLY OPPOSE THIS MEASURE AND IN

CONTRAST, HAVE INDICATED THAT THEY WOULD LIKE TO SEE DATA

HELD FOR EVEN LONGER PERIODS, POSSIBLY EVEN TEN YEARS.

4. (U) AS NOTED ABOVE, THE DECREE, WHICH HAS NOT BEEN

PUBLICLY RELEASED, HAS BEEN TRANSMITTED TO THE CHAMBER

AND SENATE, WHICH MUST RETURN OPINIONS NO LATER THAN JUNE

30. ALTHOUGH OPINIONS ARE NON-BINDING, PARLIAMENT, IF IT

IT

HAS STRONG RESERVATIONS, COULD REQUEST THE EXECUTIVE

BRANCH TO REVIEW THE DECREE.

5. (SBU) ACTION REQUESTED: EMBASSY BELIEVES THERES A

SMALL WINDOW OF OPPORTUNITY TO INFLUENCE THE DEBATE IN

PARLIAMENT AND REQUESTS WASHINGTON FOR ASSISTANCE IN

DEVELOPING APPROPRIATE TALKING POINTS. INPUT FROM USEU

ALSO WOULD BE WELCOME. WE SPECIFICALLY SEE THE VISIT OF

NUALA KELLY, CHIEF PRIVACY OFFICER, DEPARTMENT OF

HOMELAND SECURITY, ON JUNE 25, AS AN OPPORTUNITY FOR THE

USG TO HELP INFLUENCE ITALIAN DECISION-MAKERS, ESPECIALLY

IN THE CHAMBER, SENATE AND PRIVACY AUTHORITY, REGARDING

THIS DRAFT DECREE.

6. (SBU) COMMENT: WE ARE CONCERNED THAT THE PRIVACY

AUTHORITY IS LAYING DOWN A MARKER ON PRIVACY ISSUES IN

THE LEAD-UP TO ITALYS EU PRESIDENCY. IN A RECENT LETTER

TO EU COMMISSIONER PRODI, STEFANO RODOTA, PRESIDENT OF

THE ITALIAN DATA COMMISSION, SHARPLY CRITICIZED THE

INTERIM PNR AGREEMENT NEGOTIATED BETWEEN THE U.S. AND EU.

POST WILL CLOSELY WATCH THE AUTHORITY AND ALERT

T

WASHINGTON AND USEU SHOULD THE AUTHORITYS RUMBLINGS

ABOUT PRIVACY BECOME STRONGER. END COMMENT.
