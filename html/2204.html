SUBJECT: UNEASY CALM RETURNS TO BULAWAYO AS CRACKDOWN ON MDC

INTENSIFIES

REF: HARARE 3400

CLASSIFIED BY POLITICAL SECTION CHIEF MATT HARRINGTON. REASONS:

1.5 (B) AND (D).

SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) IN A SEVERAL-HOUR RAMPAGE THROUGH BULAWAYO ON NOVEMBER 16,

WAR VETERANS AND ZANU-PF SUPPORTERS BURNED TO THE GROUND THE

OPPOSITION MDC\'S OFFICE, RANSACKED THE OFFICE OF THE CITY\'S MAYOR,

BEAT THOSE SELLING AND READING INDEPENDENT NEWSPAPERS, AND SMASHED

CAR WINDSHIELDS. THEY WERE ESCORTED BY RIOT POLICE WHO DID NOT

INTERVENE. IN RETALIATION, MDC SUPPORTERS BURNED DOWN A PRIVATE

COLLEGE OWNED BY A SENIOR ZANU-PF OFFICIAL AND A TRUCK BELONGING

TO A GOVERNMENT NEWSPAPER, AND CLASHES BETWEEN THE TWO GROUPS

CONTINUED INTO THE NIGHT IN THE HIGH-DENSITY SUBURBS. AN UNEASY

CALM HAS RETURNED TO ZIMBABWE\'S SECOND-LARGEST CITY, BUT FEW

OBSERVERS BELIEVE THE VIOLENCE IS OVER. REMARKS BY PRESIDENT

MUGABE ON NOVEMBER 19 WILL NOT HELP MATTERS, AS THE PRESIDENT

BLAMED THE RECENT MURDER OF A PROMINENT WAR VETERAN ON THE MDC,

ACCUSED THE OPPOSITION PARTY OF OPERATING TERRORIST TRAINING CAMPS

AND TORTURING ZANU-PF SUPPORTERS, AND INSISTING THAT THE MDC\'S

"DAYS ARE NUMBERED." THE MDC\'S NATIONAL COORDINATOR BELIEVES THE

GOZ IS TRYING TO FRAME THE MDC AS A TERRORIST ORGANIZATION WHICH

RECEIVES FOREIGN FUNDING IN VIOLATION OF ZIMBABWEAN LAW, THUS

PROVIDING A LEGAL BASIS FOR OUTLAWING THE PARTY AND PROHIBITING IT

FROM CONTESTING THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION. FOR OUR PART, WE

SURMISE THAT INTIMIDATION GUARANTEEING A ZANU-PF "VICTORY" AT THE

POLLS MAY BE MUGABE\'S BOTTOM LINE. END SUMMARY.

WAR VETERANS RAMPAGE THROUGH BULAWAYO

--------------------------------------------- ----------

2. (C) SEVERAL HUNDRED WAR VETERANS AND ZANU-PF SUPPORTERS

RAMPAGED THROUGH THE CENTRAL BUSINESS DISTRICT OF BULAWAYO ON

NOVEMBER 16 AFTER MEETING WITH SENIOR RULING PARTY OFFICIALS ABOUT

THE MURDER OF WAR VETERAN LEADER CAIN NKALA (REFTEL). ESCORTED BY

RIOT POLICE WHO APPARENTLY DID NOTHING TO RESTRAIN THEM, THE MOB

RANDOMLY ATTACKED WITH STICKS VENDORS AND READERS OF INDEPENDENT

NEWSPAPERS AND BROKE CAR WINDSHIELDS, FORCING A NUMBER OF

FRIGHTENED BUSINESS OWNERS TO SHUT DOWN. ACCORDING TO PRESS

REPORTS, ONE WHITE ELDERLY MAN WAS BEATEN BY THE MOB, AND THERE

WERE REPORTS THAT A GERMAN AID WORKER WHO HAPPENED TO BE IN THE

VICINITY WAS ALSO ACCOSTED. THE CROWD PROCEEDED TO CITY HALL,

WHERE SEVERAL MARCHERS RANSACKED THE OFFICE OF MAYOR JAPHET

NDABENI-NCUBE, ELECTED IN SEPTEMBER ON THE TICKET OF THE

OPPOSITION MOVEMENT FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE (MDC). NDABENI-NCUBE

TOLD US THAT HE ESCAPED JUST BEFORE EIGHT MEN COMPLETELY RANSACKED

HIS OFFICE -- HE LATER FOUND IMPORTANT PAPERS OUTSIDE IN A FLOWER

GARDEN -- AND STOLE HIS BRIEFCASE. HE REPORTED THE INCIDENT TO

POLICE IMMEDIATELY AFTERWARD, BUT THEY HAD YET TO APPEAR BY

NOVEMBER 19, THREE DAYS LATER. THE MOB CAPPED OFF ITS DESTRUCTIVE

AFTERNOON BY THROWING PETROL BOMBS AT THE OFFICE BUILDING OF THE

OPPOSITION MOVEMENT FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE (MDC), REDUCING IT TO A

SMOLDERING SHELL, AS POLICE STOOD BY.

RETALIATION

-----------

3. (C) THE BURNING OF THE MDC OFFICES SPARKED RETALIATION FROM

ANGRY MDC YOUTHS, WHO BURNED DOWN A PRIVATE COLLEGE OWNED BY

SIKHANYISO NDLOVU, ZANU-PF\'S ACTING POLITICAL COMMISSAR AND SET

ALIGHT A TRUCK BELONGING TO THE GOVERNMENT-CONTROLLED NEWSPAPER

"THE CHRONICLE." A CROWD OF SEVERAL THOUSAND BULAWAYO RESIDENTS

THEN GATHERED IN FRONT OF ZANU-PF OFFICES, INTENT ON DOING DAMAGE,

BUT DISPERSED AFTER A TENSE STANDOFF WITH RIOT POLICE. SHARI

EPPEL, DIRECTOR OF THE BULAWAYO OFFICE OF THE RESPECTED HUMAN

RIGHTS ORGANIZATION AMANI TRUST, SAID SKIRMISHES BETWEEN ZANU-PF

AND MDC SUPPORTERS CONTINUED INTO THE NIGHT IN SOME OF THE CITY\'S

HIGH-DENSITY SUBURBS, BUT THAT THE SITUATION HAD CALMED DOWN BY

THE NEXT MORNING. SHE SAID MOST OF THE WAR VETERANS AND RULING

PARTY THUGS WHO HAD STARTED THE TROUBLE ARRIVED ON TRAINS THE

NIGHT BEFORE, WHILE A PROMINENT INDEPENDENT JOURNALIST TOLD US THE

TROUBLEMAKERS WERE "NOT FROM HERE."

UNEASY CALM IN BULAWAYO

-----------------------

5. (C) SHARI EPPEL SAID THAT AN UNEASY CALM RETURNED TO BULAWAYO

OVER THE WEEKEND. SHE WAS GLAD NKALA HAD BEEN DECLARED A NATIONAL

HERO BY THE RULING PARTY POLITBURO. THAT DEVELOPMENT ENSURED THAT

THE "CIRCUS" MOVED TO HARARE FOR THE NOVEMBER 18 BURIAL OF NKALA

AT HEROES\' ACRE AND ALLOWED TENSIONS IN ZIMBABWE\'S SECOND-LARGEST

CITY TO COOL. EPPEL DESCRIBED BULAWAYO RESIDENTS AS ANXIOUS AND

IN A "WAIT AND SEE" MODE.

6. (C) AT NKALA\'S BURIAL, PRESIDENT MUGABE WAS NOT IN A

CONCILIATORY MOOD. IN VENOMOUS REMARKS, HE BLAMED THE LATE WAR

VETERAN\'S MURDER ON THE MDC, WHICH HE SAID OPERATES TERRORIST

TRAINING CAMPS INSIDE AND OUTSIDE ZIMBABWE AND REGULARLY TORTURES

ZANU-PF SUPPORTERS. HE INSISTED THAT NKALA\'S DEATH WAS THE RESULT

OF AN "ORCHESTRATED, MUCH WIDER AND CAREFULLY PLANNED TERRORIST

PLOT BY INTERNAL AND EXTERNAL ENEMY FORCES WITH PLENTY OF FUNDING

FROM...COMMERCIAL FARMERS" AND INTERNATIONAL AND REGIONAL

ORGANIZATIONS. "THE MDC AND THEIR SUPPORTERS," HE VOWED, "SHOULD

KNOW THAT THEIR DAYS ARE NUMBERED." MDC MP DAVID COLTART

EXPRESSED CONCERN TO US THAT THE PRESIDENT\'S COMMENTS COULD SPARK

MORE VIOLENCE IN BULAWAYO. MEANWHILE, A REPORTER AND A

PHOTOGRAPHER FROM THE INDEPENDENT "DAILY NEWS" WERE ARRESTED ON

NOVEMBER 18 PRIOR TO THEIR PUBLISHING AN INTERVIEW WITH SOMEONE

WHO CLAIMED TO KNOW THE FULL DETAILS OF NKALA\'S ABDUCTION AND

MURDER. GOVERNMENT MEDIA ALLEGED THAT THE "DAILY NEWS"

JOURNALISTS HAD TORTURED AN INDIVIDUAL INTO CONFESSING THAT HE WAS

A CIO AGENT AND THAT THEY HAD MURDERED NKALA. POLICE SEIZED THE

REPORTER\'S NOTES OF THE INTERVIEW AND THE PHOTOGRAPHER\'S PICTURE

OF THE INTERVIEWEE, AND THE TWO JOURNALISTS WERE STILL IN

DETENTION ON NOVEMBER 19.

BAN THE MDC

-----------

7. (C) MDC NATIONAL COORDINATOR ISAAC MAPOSA TOLD POLITICAL

SECTION CHIEF ON NOVEMBER 19 THAT 30 MDC OFFICIALS AND EMPLOYEES

HAD BEEN DETAINED SO FAR IN CONNECTION WITH THE NKALA MURDER, THE

MOST SENIOR OF WHOM WAS FLETCHER DULINI-NCUBE, THE PARTY\'S

NATIONAL TREASURER. MAPOSA DISMISSED GOZ ACCUSATIONS OF MDC

INVOLVEMENT IN NKALA\'S MURDER, SAYING A RELIABLE WAR VETERAN BASED

IN BULAWAYO HAD TOLD THE OPPOSITION PARTY IN CONFIDENCE THAT HE

HAD BEEN KILLED BY OTHER WAR VETERANS. MAPOSA ACKNOWLEDGED

FEELING FRIGHTENED BY THE WEEK\'S TURN OF EVENTS, NOTING THAT ONE

IS NOT DEALING WITH A RATIONAL, REASONABLE GOVERNMENT BUT, RATHER,

ONE WILLING TO TORTURE AND KILL THOSE WHO DO NOT AGREE WITH IT.

MAPOSA BELIEVES THE GOVERNMENT\'S STRATEGY IS TO BUILD A CASE

AGAINST THE MDC AS A TERRORIST ORGANIZATION BY TORTURING MDC

SUPPORTERS INTO MAKING FALSE CONFESSIONS IN THE NKALA CASE AND BY

IDENTIFYING ANY FOREIGN SOURCES OF FUNDING FOR THE PARTY, WHICH

WOULD PROVIDE A LEGAL BASIS FOR OUTLAWING THE MDC AND PREVENTING

IT FROM CONTESTING THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION. ASKED WHETHER THE

MDC RECEIVES FOREIGN SOURCES OF FUNDING, MAPOSA SAID YES, BUT ONLY

THREE PEOPLE IN THE PARTY ARE AWARE OF THE DETAILS: HIMSELF,

PRESIDENT MORGAN TSVANGIRAI, AND SECRETARY-GENERAL WELSHMAN NCUBE.

MAPOSA WAS CLEARLY ANXIOUS THAT HE WOULD BE HAULED IN AND FORCED

TO REVEAL WHAT HE KNOWS ABOUT THIS TOPIC.
