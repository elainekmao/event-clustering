1. (C) SUMMARY: IN A THREE HOUR MEETING NEARLY

HALF OF WHICH WAS SPENT ON LANO, PRESIDENT MUGABE

TOLD US THAT LANO ACQUISITION WAS AN OVERDUE

MATTER OF ACHIEVING JUSTICE AND ESCAPING FROM

UNOER THE YOKE OF COLONIALISM THAT THE PEOPLE OF

ZIMBABWE NOW OEMANO. THE WAR VET\'S OCCUPATIONS,

WHICH HE SUPPORTS BUT OID NOT INITIATE, ARE A

DEMONSTRATION OF "PRACTICAL SELF-HELP." WE

BELIEVE THAT IN CONDONING THE VET\'S ACTIONS HE

HAS UNLEASHEO A FORCE THAT IS POWERFUL,

DANGEROUS, EXTREMELY DIFFICULT TO CONTROL AND A

THREAT TO THE PRESIDENT\'S IRON GRIP. ZIMBABWE\'S

GUERILLAS ARE OPERATING AGAIN IN ZIMBABWE.

HAVING UNCHAINED SUCH A FORCE, HE NOW NEEDS TO

BUY IT OFF, MOST LIKELY WITH LAND HAVING ALREADY

EXERCISED THE CASH OPTION, IF HE IS TO MAINTAIN

CONTROL OVER THE REINS OF POWER.

2. (C) THE PRESIDENT IS ADAMANT THAT BRITAIN

MUST PAY FOR LAND ACQUISITION AS, ACCORDING TO

HIM, THEY COMMITTED TO IT AT LANCASTER HOUSE IN

1980. IF THEY DO NOT PROVIDE FUNDS, COMPULSORY

ACQUISITION WITHOUT COMPENSATION WILL GO AHEAD.

THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS, WHO RETAIN THE MENTALITY

3. (C) DURING THE EVENING OF APRIL 22 PRESIDENT

ROBERT MUGABE, UNACCOMPANIED BUT FOR A NOTETAKER,

SPENT THREE HOURS PLUS DISCUSSING THE PRIOR DAYS

MINI-SUMMIT OF REGIONAL LEADERS AT VICTORIA

FALLS, THE CONGO PEACE PROCESS AND ZIMBABWE\'S

LAND ISSUE WITH U.S. OFFICIALS. THE U.S. GROUP

CONSISTED OF S/E HOWARD WOLPE, AMBASSADOR

MCDONALD, LT. COL. GADDIS, SPECIAL ADVISOR

INITIAL 20 MINUTES SPENT DESCRIBING THE VICTORIA

FALLS GATHERING AND PROGRESS MADE THERE ON THE

CONGO ISSUE MUGABE, WITHOUT A PROMPT FROM US,

LAUNCHED INTO A 45-MINUTE HISTORICAL TOUR OF

ZIMBABWE\'S LAND ISSUE COVERING THE PRE-RHODES\'

DAYS TO THE PRESENT. DETAILED AND FULL OF

MINUTIA (THE PRESIDENT WAS A SCHOOLTEACHER FOR

YEARS BEFORE AND DURING THE LIBERATION STRUGGLE),

THE MESSAGE WAS THAT HISTORY SAW THE LAND STOLEN

FROM THE BLACKS WITH CONTROL CONSOLIDATED BY THE

BRITISH WITH VICIOUS DISREGARD FOR THE RIGHTS OF

BLACKS, A STATE THAT CONTINUES TODAY WITH ACTIVE

AND COULD ONLY SPARE THE SENIOR AFRICAN STATESMAN

10-15 MINUTES ONCE ON A PRODUCTION LINE BASIS

DURING A EUROPE/AFRICA SUMMIT. "I HAVE SPENT

MORE TIME WITH CLINTON" HE SNIFFED, "AND HAVE

EVEN MET WITH CHIRAC FOUR TIMES -- THOUGH HIS

8. (C) MUGABE DISPLAYED LUCIDITY AND A

REMARKABLE MEMORY ANO RECALL THROUGHOUT THE

CONVERSATION. HIS MOOO ANO EMOTION SWINGS

MATCHEO THE RHETORIC ANO WHEN WE RETURNEO TO

CONGO MATTERS AFTER THE LAND ISSUE HE WAS A

CHANGEO MAN. AMBASSAOOR MCOONALO IN A TELEPHONE

CALL BEFORE THE MEETING WITH THE PRESIDENT AND

DURING THE LONG SESSION THOUGHT THE MOOD WAS

CORDIAL EVEN WHEN THE ISSUES GOT DIFFICULT. WE

ARE THE ONLY WESTERN COUNTRY THAT MUGABE HAS MET

WITH ON THE TOPIC OF LAND. WE BELIEVE WE WERE

HEARD, BUT MUGABE WAS NOT PERSUADED. THE

AMBASSADOR PLANS TO CONTINUE HIS CONVERSATION

WITH THE PRESIDENT ON THE ISSUE.

9. (C) IT IS CLEAR THAT MUGABE IS STILL FIGHTING

THE GUERILLA STRUGGLE HE WON 20 YEARS AGO. HE

HAS DRAWN A LINE IN THE SAND AND SAID, "LAND NOW

AT ALL COSTS." THE PRESIDENT REALIZES HE IS IN A

FIGHT FOR HIS POLITICAL LIFE, A BATTLE HE MUST

WIN. UNLEASHING THE WAR VETS IS A DANGEROUS

TACTIC, REEKING OF DESPERATION. THEY ARE A PIT

BULL LOOSE IN THE COUNTRYSIDE AND GETTING THEM

BACK IN THE CAGE OR ON A STOUT CHAIN WILL REQUIRE

OFFERINGS AND THE PROMISE OF REGULAR FEEDINGS.
