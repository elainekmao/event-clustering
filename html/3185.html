1. SUMMARY: ESTOFFS MET WITH THE PRESIDENT OF THE ITALIAN

SPACE AGENCY (ASI), PROFESSOR SERGIO VETRELLA, ON JUNE 6,

2002. VETRELLA PROVIDED UPDATES ON GALILEO, ITALIAN SPACE

PLAN, COSMO SKYMED, AND THE BIDDING PROCESS FOR THE AGILE

LAUNCHER. IN PARTICULAR, HE STRESSED THAT ALL EXISTING

COOPERATIVE ACTIVITIES WITH NASA WERE INCLUDED IN THE PLAN.

HE ALSO AGREED IN PRINCIPLE FOR ASI TO PARTICIPATE IN A

BILATERAL PUBLIC DIPLOMACY PROJECT TO COINCIDE WITH THE

CENTENARY OF THE FIRST FLIGHT OF THE WRIGHT BROTHERS. AS A

COURTESY, ESTOFFS ALSO BRIEFED VETRELLA ON REFTEL USG

RESPONSE TO THE EUROPEAN REQUEST FOR A GOVERNMENT-LEVEL

MEETING ON THE INTERNATIONAL SPACE STATION (ISS). END

SUMMARY.

-------

GALILEO

-------

2. ESTOFFS INQUIRED ABOUT THE STATUS OF THE GALILEO GLOBAL

NAVIGATIONAL SATELLITE SYSTEM PROGRAM. VETRELLA HAD RECENTLY

RETURNED FROM AN EUROPEAN SPACE AGENCY (ESA) MEETING IN PARIS

ON GALILEO. ACCORDING TO VETRELLA, THE EUROPEAN COMMISSION

(EC) AND ESA HAVE NOT YET REACHED AN AGREEMENT OF A DIVISION

OF LABOR AND RESPONSIBILITY OVER THE DESIGN PHASE OF GALILEO.

WITHOUT THIS AGREEMENT, THE EC AND ESA WILL NOT BE ABLE TO

BEGIN COOPERATION TO DEVELOP GALILEO AS A JOINT UNDERTAKING.

ONCE THIS AGREEMENT HAS BEEN REACHED, VETRELLA ESTIMATED THAT

IT WILL TAKE A YEAR AND A HALF TO ESTABLISH THE TECHNICAL

SPECIFICATIONS FOR GALILEO. DURING THIS PROCESS, VETRELLA

EXPECTS THAT IT WILL BE POSSIBLE TO DEFINE FROM A TECHNICAL

POINT OF VIEW HOW GALILEO COULD BE FULLY INTEGRATED WITH GPS

AND GLONASS. BUT THE LEVEL OF INTEGRATION BETWEEN THESE

SYSTEMS WILL BE ULTIMATELY SUBJECT TO A POLITICAL DECISION

WITHIN THE EUROPEAN UNION (EU). VETRELLA CLAIMED THAT THERE

IS NO SUPPORT IN ITALY FOR A "NON-OPTIMIZED" SOLUTION. HE

DEFINED AN "OPTIMAL" SOLUTION AS ONE THAT ACHIEVED A

HIGH-LEVEL OF INTEGRATION WITH GPS AND GLONASS.

------------------

ITALIAN SPACE PLAN

------------------

3. WHEN ASKED ABOUT THE STATUS OF THE ITALIAN SPACE PLAN,

VETRELLA STATED THAT IT HAD BEEN SUBMITTED TO THE MINISTRY OF

UNIVERSITIES AND RESEARCH INSTITUTIONS (MUIR) FOR APPROVAL.

AN INTERMINISTERIAL COUNCIL WILL EXAMINE THE PLAN AND WILL

GIVE THE MINISTER OF UNIVERSITIES AND RESEARCH INSTITUTIONS A

RECOMMENDATION. ONCE THIS REVIEW PROCESS HAS BEEN COMPLETED,

IT WILL BE SUBMITTED TO PARLIAMENT FOR INCLUSION IN THE

BUDGET. HE EXPECTED THAT IT WOULD BE PUBLISHED AS LAW BY

MID-JULY 2002. ONCE APPROVED THE SPACE PLAN WILL COVER ASI

ACTIVITIES FOR THE 2003-2005 PERIOD. HE STRESSED THAT ALL

COOPERATIVE ACTIVITIES WITH NASA ARE INCLUDED IN THE PLAN.

IN PARTICULAR, VETRELLA NOTED THAT THE ITALIAN COMMITMENTS TO

THE SWIFT PROGRAM AND ISS HAVE BEEN INCLUDED IN THE PLAN. IN

DESCRIBING THE CHANGES HE MADE TO THE DRAFT SPACE PLAN THAT

HAD BEEN COMPLETED AT THE TIME OF HIS ARRIVAL AT ASI,

VETRELLA STATED THAT HE HAD TO RE-PRIORITIZE ASI OBJECTIVES

AND LIMIT FUTURE PROJECTS IN ORDER TO STAY WITHIN BUDGETARY

CONSTRAINTS ESTABLISHED BY THE NEW CENTER-RIGHT GOVERNMENT.

COSMO SKYMED

FIRST FLIGHT CENTENARY

----------------------

6. SCICOUNS REQUESTED ASI\'S COOPERATION IN A POTENTIAL

BILATERAL PROGRAM TO CELEBRATE THE CENTENARY OF THE WRIGHT

BROTHERS FIRST FLIGHT IN NOVEMBER 2002. HE BRIEFED VETRELLA

ON ONGOING INFORMAL DISCUSSIONS BETWEEN THE MISSION AND THE

DIRECTOR OF THE ITALIAN AEROSPACE MUSEUM IN NAPLES. MISSION

BELIEVES THAT THIS EVENT WOULD PROVIDE AN EXCELLENT

OPPORTUNITY TO ENHANCE PUBLIC AWARENESS ON BOTH SIDES OF THE

ATLANTIC OF THE RICH HISTORY OF AEROSPACE COOPERATION BETWEEN

ITALY AND THE UNITED STATES. MISSION WILL EXPLORE THE

POSSIBILITY OF JOINT PROGRAM BETWEEN THE NAPLES CENTER AND

THE NATIONAL AIR AND SPACE MUSEUM AND THE US AIR FORCE

MUSEUM. MISSION INTENDS TO INVITE A SHUTTLE CREW TO

PARTICIPATE IN THIS EVENT. VETRELLA AGREED IN PRINCIPLE TO

ASI PARTICIPATION IN THE EVENT. EST SECTION WILL CONTINUE TO

DISCUSS THIS PROJECT WITH OUR ASI CONTACTS.

----------------------------

ISS GOVERNMENT-LEVEL MEETING

----------------------------

7. AS A COURTESY, ESTOFFS BRIEFED VETRELLA ON THE USG

RESPONSE TO THE EUROPEAN REQUEST FOR A GOVERNMENT-LEVEL

MEETING IN WASHINGTON ON THE FUTURE OF ISS. VETRELLA WAS NOT

AWARE THAT THE REQUEST HAD BEEN MADE AND WAS LARGELY

DISMISSIVE OF IT. HE HAS NO ILLUSIONS ABOUT THE POLITICAL

AND BUDGETARY FACTORS IMPINGING ON USG ISS POLICY. SINCE THE

MEETING CAN NOT BE HELD IN LATE JUNE 2002, VETRELLA BELIEVES

THAT A MEETING WILL PROBABLY NOT TAKE PLACE UNTIL AUTUMN 2002

DUE TO THE GERMAN ELECTIONS. ALSO, HE THINKS THAT IT IS

HIGHLY UNLIKELY THAT ANY SUBSTANTIVE MULTILATERAL DECISION ON

ISS WILL TAKE PLACE PRIOR TO THE END OF THE YEAR.
