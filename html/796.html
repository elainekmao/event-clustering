SUBJECT: THE ART OF EDITING: CROATIAN TELEVISION FAILS

TO MENTION AMBASSADOR\'S CRITICISM OF BLASKIC PROMOTION

SUMMARY

-------

1. ALTHOUGH THE WAR CRIMES TRIBUNAL\'S INDICTMENT OF SIX

BOSNIAN CROATS WAS NOT BIG NEWS IN THIS COUNTRY, THE

NAMING OF ONE OF THEM, TIHOMIR BLASKIC, THE NEXT DAY TO

A HIGH POSITION IN THE CROATIAN ARMY WAS. BY DAY THREE,

HOWEVER, THE CROATIAN MEDIA -- THANKS, NO DOUBT, TO THE

HIDDEN HAND OF POLITICAL CONTROL EXERCISED OVER IT --

LOST INTEREST IN THE STORY. IN FACT, CROATIAN

TELEVISION, TO WHOM PRESIDENT TUDJMAN FIRST FAXED WORD

OF BLASKIC\'S NOMINATION, FAILED EVEN TO MENTION THAT

AMBASSADOR GAILBRAITH HAD PUBLICLY CRITICIZED THIS MOVE,

ALTHOUGH THEIR CAMERAS WERE PRESENT AND THEY REPORTED

EXTENSIVELY ON OTHER REMARKS HE MADE AT THE SAME TIME.

DEMONSTRABLY OUTSPOKEN

----------------------

2. ON NOVEMBER 15, AS THEY DO MOST DAYS, THE "MOTHERS

OF VUKOVAR" GATHERED IN THE PARK ACROSS THE STREET FROM

THE EMBASSY BETWEEN NOON AND ONE O\'CLOCK. DRESSED IN

BLACK, THEY STAND BEHIND PICTURES OF THEIR SONS, WHO

HAVE BEEN BEEN MISSING SINCE 1991. SOME HOLD PLACARDS

ALOFT, REMINDING THOSE IN DAYTON NOT TO FORGET THE

MISSING IN VUKOVAR.

3. ON NOVEMBER 15, AS HE DOES MOST DAYS, AMBASSADOR

GALBRAITH STROLLED OVER TO TALK TO THE THEM. THIS TIME,

UNLIKE MOST DAYS, IN ADDITION TO THE DEMONSTRATORS AND

THE USUAL KNOT OF CURIOUS ONLOOKERS A LARGE GROUP OF

REPORTERS, FROM BOTH THE DOMESTIC AND FOREIGN MEDIA, HAD

GATHERED. THEY PEPPERED THE AMBASSADOR WITH QUESTIONS

ABOUT THE ISSUE OF THE DAY -- THE U.S. GOVERNMENT\'S

UNCLASSIFIED

UNCLASSIFIED

REACTION TO BLASKIC\'S NEW POSITION. AMBASSADOR

GALBRAITH REPLIED BY REITERATING THE LONG-STANDING

AMERICAN POSITION -- THAT (TO QUOTE THE CROATIAN NEWS

AGENCY ACCOUNT) THE "CROATIAN GOVERNMENT HAS STATED THAT

IT WILL COOPERATE WITH THE INTERNATIONAL TRIBUNAL FOR

WAR CRIMES COMMITTED IN FORMER YUGOSLAVIA. WE EXPECT

THAT IT WILL DO SO." THE CROATIAN NEWS AGENCY\'S SHORT

REPORT ALSO QUOTED THE AMBASSADOR AS SAYING: "BLASKIC

AND KORDIC WERE INDICTED BY THE INTERNATIONAL WAR CRIMES

TRIBUNAL, AND ALL COUNTRIES IN THE REGION, IN THIS CASE

CROATIA AND BOSNIA-HERZEGOVINA, HAVE AN OBLIGATION TO

ARREST AND EXTRADITE THEM. . . . COOPERATION WITH THE

WAR CRIMES TRIBUNAL WILL BE ONE OF THE ELEMENTS THAT

WILL DETERMINE OUR RELATIONS WITH THE STATES OF THIS

REGION."

NOT THE TOP OF THE NEWS

-----------------------

4. MOST NATIONAL DAILIES CARRIED THIS REPORT, THOUGH

THEY HARDLY PLAYED IT PROMINENTLY. IT TENDED TO BE A

VERY SHORT SIDE BAR TO A LARGER STORY ON THE

AMBASSADOR\'S REMARKS TO THE MOTHERS OF VUKOVAR.

CROATIAN TELEVISION, HOWEVER, TOOK THIS TO ITS LOGICAL

EXTREME. ALTHOUGH IT SHOWED A PICTURE OF THE AMBASSADOR

ACTUALLY MAKING HIS STATEMENTS ABOUT BLASKIC AND KORDIC

ON THE MAIN EVENING NEWS, THE REPORTER\'S VOICE-OVER

MENTIONED NOT ONE WORD ABOUT THIS PART OF HIS REMARKS,

BUT INSTEAD DEVOTED SEVERAL MINUTES TO AN ACCOUNT OF THE

STATEMENTS THE AMBASSADOR MADE TO THE MOTHERS ABOUT THE

NEED TO ACCOUNT FOR THE MISSING.

5. THIS INTERESTING EXERCISE IN NEWS JUDGMENT WAS

ESPECIALLY IRONIC BECAUSE CROATIAN TELEVISION WAS THE

FIRST IN THIS COUNTRY TO RECEIVE THE ANNOUNCEMENT OF

BLASKIC\'S NEW POSITION, BEFORE EVEN THE PRESIDENCY

ITSELF. THE ANNOUNCEMENT, WHICH WAS PICTURED ON THE

NEWS ON THE EVENING OF NOVEMBER 13, HAD BEEN FAXED

DIRECTLY FROM DAYTON; APPARENTLY DONE ON AN AMERICAN

WORD-PROCESSOR, THE NECESSARY CROATIAN DIACRITICAL

MARKINGS HAD BEEN PENCILLED IN BY HAND. SOMEONE HAD

OVIOUSLY CHOSEN TO SACRIFICE GOOD FORM FOR GRE

AT SPEED.

6. SARAJEVO MINIMIZE CONSIDERED.
