SUBJECT: FINANCE MINISTRY ON IDA REPLENISHMENT; GOI

ATTENDANCE AT DEVELOPMENT CONFERENCE

PAGE 02 ROME 00563 051714Z

REF: JANUARY 17 EMAIL FROM TFERET TO EU POSTS

1. ITALIAN OFFICIALS SUPPORT AN INCREASE IN IDA LENDING AS

GRANTS RATHER THAN LOANS, BUT STILL ONLY AS MINOR SHARE OF

OVERALL LENDING. IN A FEBRUARY 4 MEETING WITH FINMIN

OFFICIALS, ECOUN AND ECONOFF PRESENTED PRESIDENT BUSH\'S

PROPOSAL THAT THE WORLD BANK AND OTHER DEVELOPMENT BANKS

DRAMATICALLY INCREASE THE SHARE OF FUNDING PROVIDED TO THE

WORLD\'S POOREST COUNTRIES AS GRANTS RATHER THAN LOANS.

2. RAFFAELA DI MARO AND GIORGIO LECCESI, OFFICER DIRECTOR

FOR BILATERAL ASSISTANCE AND OFFICE DIRECTOR FOR MDBS,

RESPECTIVELY, AT THE FINANCE MINISTRY TOLD ECOUN AND ECONOFF

THAT ITALY CONCURS WITH THE US THAT GRANTS ARE USEFUL

MECHANISMS AND THAT THEIR SHARE OF OVERALL DEVELOPMENT

ASSISTANCE FUNDING SHOULD BE INCREASED. ITALY DOES NOT,

HOWEVER, SUPPORT A GRANT INCREASE GREATER THAN TEN PERCENT OF

OVERALL IDA FUNDING. FOR REPLENISHMENT OF THE AFRICAN

DEVELOPMENT BANK, ITALY SUPPORTS A GRANT CEILING OF 7.5

PERCENT. ITALY WOULD SUPPORT THE USE OF GRANTS FOR EDUCATION,

HEALTH AND NUTRITION PROGRAMS. DI MARO UNDERSCORED ITALIAN

CONCERN ABOUT THE COST TO DONORS, IN TERMS OF FOREGONE IDA

REPAYMENTS, IF THE GRANT PORTION WERE INCREASED DRAMATICALLY.

3. WE ALSO DISCUSSED THE UPCOMING MONTEREY CONFERENCE ON

INTERNATIONAL FINANCING OF DEVELOPMENT, PAKISTAN AND ZIMBABWE

(THE LATTER TWO TO BE REPORTED SEPTEL). DI MARO POSTULATED

THAT PM BERLUSCONI, IN HIS DUAL CAPACITY AS FOREIGN MINISTER,

WOULD GO TO THE MONTERREY CONFERENCE. WE REPLIED THAT NO

DECISION HAS YET BEEN MADE ABOUT US PARTICIPANTS.
