SUMMARY

-------

2. (SBU) ITALY\'S LABOR UNIONS ARE GETTING A LOT OF PRESS FOR

THEIR ROLE IN DISRUPTING TRANSHIPMENT OF U.S. MILITARY

EQUIPMENT AND CARGO, BUT THE REAL HEADLINE IS THAT THEY\'RE

NOT THE MOST OBSTRUCTIVE, OR DANGEROUS, OBSTACLE. ITALIAN

LABOR SHOULD EXPRESS ITS OPPOSITION TO U.S. MILITARY

PLANNING AND ACTION WITHIN THE ESTABLISHED SYSTEM OF RULES

AND BEHAVIOR GOVERNED BY LABOR LAW AND ACCEPTED CONVENTION.

UNIONS WILL STAGE SYMBOLIC STRIKES AND REFUSE TO HANDLE U.S.

MILITARY CARGO. THEY SHOULDN\'T ILLEGALLY BLOCK PORT

ENTRANCES OR TRY TO PREVENT CARGO FROM MOVING FROM CAMP

DARBY TO THE PORT OF LIVORNO (THOUGH THE LARGEST UNION IS

STRETCHING THE LIMITS BY COORDINATING WITH LESS SCRUPULOUS

PROTESTORS). LABOR HAS BEEN AVOIDING OUR CALLS, BUT WE WILL

CONTINUE TO UNDERSCORE OUR HOPE THAT THEY WILL AVOID THE

KIND OF CONFRONTATIONAL TACTICS THAT COULD LEAD TO VIOLENCE.

BY EXTENSION, ITALIAN LABOR SHOULD POSE ONLY SYMBOLIC,

MINIMAL DISRUPTIONS TO U.S. MILITARY OPERATIONS FROM BASES

IN ITALY IF WE DECIDE TO PURSUE MILITARY ACTION AGAINST

IRAQ. OTHER OPPONENTS, ESPECIALLY THOSE DRAWN FROM THE ANTI-

GLOBALIZATION MOVEMENT, MAY ATTEMPT MORE CONFRONTATIONAL,

DISRUPTIVE ACTIONS (SEPTEL). END SUMMARY

3. (SBU) ITALIAN UNIONS, NEVER SHRINKING VIOLETS, HAVE

FIGURED PROMINENTLY OVER THE LAST TWO WEEKS IN PROTESTS

AGAINST TRANSHIPMENT OF U.S. MATERIEL AND OTHER MILITARY

CARGO IN NORTHERN ITALY. UNIONIZED FREIGHT ENGINEERS

REPORTEDLY ALERTED ANTI-WAR PROTESTORS TO IMPENDING CARGO

CONVOYS FROM SETAF IN VINCENZA, ENABLING THEM TO DELAY

SHIPMENTS ON FEBRUARY 21 (REFTELS). UNION OFFICIALS ALSO

HAVE ANNOUNCED THEIR MEMBERS WILL NOT HANDLE U.S. MILITARY

CARGO IN ITALIAN PORTS, INCLUDING LIVORNO (WHERE VEHICLES,

EQUIPMENT, AMMUNITION AND OTHER CARGO CURRENTLY LOCATED AT

CAMP DARBY WILL BE LOADED FOR SHIPMENT TO SW ASIA). THE

TRADE UNIONISTS\' NOISY ACTIVISM COULD SUGGEST THAT ITALIAN

UNIONS WOULD BE AT THE CENTER OF ANY AND ALL EFFORTS TO

IMPEDE OR BLOCK EQUIPMENT SHIPMENTS OR DISRUPT OPERATIONS ON

BASES FROM WHICH U.S. FORCES OPERATE. WE BELIEVE THAT

OVERSTATES THE CASE. UNION ANTIWAR ACTIVITY SHOULD POSE

ONLY LIMITED PRACTICAL OBSTACLES FOR U.S. MILITARY CARGO

SHIPMENTS AND OTHER SUPPORT OPERATIONS.

4. (U) THE POTENTIAL WAR IN IRAQ IS ONLY THE LATEST ISSUE

DIVIDING ITALY\'S THREE MAJOR LABOR CONFEDERATIONS. THERE IS

SUBSTANTIAL OVERLAP BETWEEN THE CONFEDERATIONS\' POSITIONS.

THEIR DIFFERENCES ARE SUBSTANTIAL ENOUGH, HOWEVER, TO

PREVENT THEM FROM PURSUING THE TYPES OF COORDINATED PROTESTS

THAT MIGHT REALLY CAUSE PROBLEMS FOR US AND FOR THE

BERLUSCONI GOVERNMENT.

5. (SBU) THE TWO LARGEST TRADE UNION CONFEDERATIONS OPPOSE

ANY USE OF FORCE IN IRAQ, THOUGH FOR DIFFERENT REASONS.

ITALY\'S LARGEST (5.4 MILLION MEMBERS), MOST-LEFTIST

CONFEDERATION, THE GENERAL CONFEDERATION OF ITALIAN LABOR

(CGIL) HAS SHOWN THE MOST FERVOR IN OPPOSING U.S. POLICY AND

PREPARATIONS. WITH 75% OF DOCKWORKERS AMONG ITS MEMBERSHIP,

IT\'S THE MOST IMPORTANT UNION IN THIS CONTEXT. CGIL\'S

HISTORICALLY CLOSE ASSOCIATION WITH ITALY\'S COMMUNIST PARTY

MAKES MOST OF ITS MEMBERSHIP IDEOLOGICALLY PREDISPOSED TO

HAS THE MOST NAKEDLY POLITICAL OBJECTIVES OF THE THREE

IN FAVOR OF CONFRONTATION. IN THAT CONTEXT, ITS STRIDENT

POSSIBLE POLITICAL COSTS ON THE BERLUSCONI GOVERNMENT FOR

ITALIANS OPPOSING WAR, BUYING IT AT LEAST SOME SHORT-TERM

POLITICAL GAINS.)

7. (U) BY CONTRAST, THE SMALLEST OF THE THREE CONFEDERATIONS

(THOUGH STILL A POTENT SOCIAL FORCE WITH 1.8 MILLION

MEMBERS), THE ITALIAN UNION OF LABOR (UIL), REFUSED TO

PARTICIPATE IN THE ROME PEACE MARCH FEBRUARY 15 FOR FEAR OF

DELEGITIMIZING THE UN. THE UIL WAS UNWILLING TO ACCEPT THE

OTHER TWO CONFEDERATIONS\' ASSERTIONS THAT WAR IS NEVER AN

ACCEPTABLE ALTERNATIVE, ARGUING THAT UNSC-SANCTIONED

CONFLICT MIGHT BE UNDESIRABLE BUT WOULD BE LEGITIMATE.

8. (U) REGARDLESS OF THEIR PHILOSOPHICAL POSITIONS ON

MILITARY ACTION, ALL THREE CONFEDERATIONS STRESS THE

IMPORTANCE OF LEGAL ACTION. WHEN THE TRANSHIPMENT ISSUE

FIRST BROKE O/A FEBRUARY 21, CGIL AND CISL LEADERS EPIFANI

AND PEZZOTA ISSUED A JOINT STATEMENT SUPPORTING TRAIN

ENGINEERS WHO DID NOT WISH TO TRANSPORT THE CARGO, ARGUING

THE CARGO COULD BE DANGEROUS AND THEREFORE COULD CREATE

HAZARDOUS WORKING CONDITIONS FOR THEIR MEMBERS. THEY ASKED

THE GOI TO PROMPTLY INFORM WORKERS ABOUT MATERIALS

TRANSPORTED. BUT THEY ALSO CALLED ON ALL THEIR MEMBERS ONLY

TO PURSUE LEGAL ACTIONS. AT A FEBRUARY 27 RALLY IN LIVORNO,

EPIFANI STRESSED THAT THE UNION MEMBERS WOULD REFUSE TO

HANDLE U.S. MILITARY CARGO WITHIN THE PORT, AND WOULD STRIKE

TO AVOID DOING SO. BUT HE ALSO SAID THEY WOULD NOT ATTEMPT

TO BLOCK DELIVERY OF U.S. VEHICLES AND EQUIPMENT OR JOIN

`DISOBEDIENT\' PROTESTORS IN OTHER ACTIONS - PERHAPS WITH AN

EYE TO THE FINES AND OTHER PENALTIES THAT COULD BE IMPOSED

ON THE FEDERATION FOR VIOLATING ELEMENTS OF ITALIAN LABOR

LAW. PEZZOTA AS WELL REPEATEDLY HAS STATED THAT "BOYCOTTS

AND RAIL BLOCKADES DON\'T ADVANCE THE CAUSE OF PEACE."

9. (SBU) THERE IS EVIDENCE OF AT LEAST INFORMAL, LOCAL

COORDINATION BETWEEN CGIL MEMBERS AND THE `DISOBEDIENTS,\'

MORE MILITANT PROTESTORS, MANY DRAWN FROM THE ANTI-

GLOBALIZATION MOVEMENT, WITH FEWER QUALMS ABOUT EMPLOYING

CONFRONTATIONAL TACTICS (SEE REFS C AND D). CGIL APPEARS TO

HAVE DECIDED TO CONFINE ITS ACTIONS TO WITHIN THE PORT,

LEAVING PROTESTS AND OTHER POTENTIALLY DISRUPTIVE MEASURES

BETWEEN DARBY AND THE PORT TO THE `DISOBEDIENTS.\'

10. (SBU) CGIL\'S ACTIONS MAY COMPLICATE THE MILITARY CARGO\'S

FINAL MOVEMENT FROM SHORE TO SHIP, BUT WE SEE NOTHING TO

INDICATE IT (OR THE OTHER ITALIAN UNIONS) ARE CONTEMPLATING

MORE AGGRESSIVE, POTENTIALLY VIOLENT ACTION TO PREVENT THE

CARGO\'S ULTIMATE DEPARTURE. MOREOVER, THE CONFEDERATIONS\'

STRONG EMPHASIS ON LEGAL ACTION SUGGESTS THAT ANY LABOR

ACTIONS ON U.S. BASES (WHERE CGIL IS NOT REPRESENTED) DURING

A CONFLICT SHOULD BE LARGELY SYMBOLIC, ALTHOUGH STRIKES OR

REFUSAL TO PERFORM CERTAIN TASKS, WHICH COULD POTENTIALLY

DISRUPT OPERATIONS, CANNOT BE COMPLETELY RULED OUT. WE WILL

REPORT SEPARATELY ON THE GREATER POTENTIAL FOR "NO-GLOBAL"

ORGANIZATIONS TO ORGANIZE TO DISRUPT TRANSIT AND USE OF

ITALIAN BASES IN EVENT OF ACTUAL HOSTILITIES.
