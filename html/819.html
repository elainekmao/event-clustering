SUBJECT: WHAT HAMAS IS -- AND ISN\'T -- IN JORDAN

REF: A) AMMAN 1993; B) 95 AMMAN 4285 (NOTAL)

1. (U) CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE ROBERT BEECROFT; REASON 1.5 (D).

2. (C) THERE IS NO/NO EVIDENCE TO LINK EITHER HAMAS

SPOKESPERSONS IBRAHIM GHAWSHAH OR MUHAMMAD NAZZAL TO OPERATIONS

CONDUCTED IN ISRAEL AND THE OCCUPIED TERRITORIES BY HAMAS\'

MILITARY WING. EMBASSY LIKEWISE HAS NO EVIDENCE TO VALIDATE

CONTENTIONS THAT THERE IS A CLANDESTINE LEADERSHIP GROUP

OPERATING IN JORDAN LINKED TO TERRORIST ACTIVITIES IN JERUSALEM,

THE TERRITORIES OR ISRAEL. THERE REMAINS NO DECLARED HAMAS

PRESENCE IN JORDAN BEYOND THE INFORMATION OFFICE. WHILE HAMAS

POLITICAL FIGURES SUCH AS MUSA ABU MARZUQ VISITED JORDAN IN THE

PAST, USUALLY TO SEE FAMILY MEMBERS RESIDENT HERE, SUCH VISITS

WERE STRICTLY CONTROLLED BY JORDANIAN INTELLIGENCE. THERE IS

SOME ANECDOTAL INFORMATION THAT HAMAS COURIERS CARRY CASH BETWEEN

JORDAN AND THE WEST BANK AND GAZA. JORDANIAN AUTHORITIES HAVE

SEVERAL TIMES NOTED THE DIFFICULTY OF MONITORING THE PASSAGE OF

SUCH FUNDS AND/OR IDENTIFYING BANK ACCOUNTS USED BY HAMAS.

3. (U) JORDANIAN OFFICIALS ARE INCENSED OVER RECENT ALLEGATIONS

BY PA OFFICIAL NABIL SHA\'TH LINKING JORDAN TO THE RECENT SPATE OF

HAMAS BOMBINGS IN TEL AVIV AND JERUSALEM. KING HUSSEIN, CROWN

PRINCE HASSAN AND PRIME MINISTER ABD AL-KARIM AL-KABARITI HAVE

ALL ISSUED STRONG STATEMENTS CONDEMNING THE BOMBING. FURTHER, ON

MARCH 4 THE GOJ DISPATCHED MINISTER OF INTERIOR AWAD KHLAYFAT AND

MINISTER OF WATER SAMIR QA\'WAR TO GAZA WITH A MESSAGE FROM THE

KING TO PALESTINE AUTHORITY (PA) LEADER ARAFAT REPORTEDLY

UNDERSCORING JORDANIAN SUPPORT FOR THE PA AND THE NEED FOR

GREATER JORDANIAN-PALESTINIAN COORDINATION TO COUNTER THE "WAVE

OF MILITANT VIOLENCE" THREATENING THE PEACE PROCESS.

4. (C) THE GOJ IS CLEARLY ANXIOUS TO AVOID ANOTHER WAR OF WORDS

WITH THE PALESTINIANS OVER THIS SUBJECT. LAST YEAR, THEN-FOREIGN

MINISTER KABARITI REPEATEDLY DENIED ALLEGATIONS OF COLLUSION WITH

HAMAS OR TOLERATION OF ACTIVITIES BEYOND THOSE OF ITS INFORMATION

OFFICE (AND THEN ONLY WITHIN EXTREMELY STRICT LIMITS). AFTER A

DRAMATIC REMINDER TO SPOKESMAN IBRAHIM GHAWSHAH LAST APRIL OF

WHAT THE GOJ WOULD ALLOW, THE GOJ IN MAY DEPORTED HAMAS OFFICIAL

IMAD AL-ALAMI, AT THE TIME HEAD OF THE HAMAS OFFICE IN TEHRAN AND

CURRENTLY HEAD OF HAMAS\' OFFICE IN DAMASCUS, IN ADDITION TO

POLITICAL OFFICE DIRECTOR ABU MARZUQ (NOW IN THE U.S. FACING

EXTRADITION TO ISRAEL).

5. (C) AS EMBASSY EARLIER REPORTED (REF B) THE GOJ, WARY OF

CREATING A CAUSE CELEBRE FOR THE JORDANIAN OPPOSITION AND

CONCERNED WHETHER THE PROSECUTION OF JORDANIAN CITIZENS WOULD

STAND UP IN COURT, OPTED TO KEEP THE INFORMATION OFFICE AND ITS

PERSONNEL UNDER CLOSE SURVEILLANCE. APART FROM PROVOCATIVE

SOUND-BITES, WHICH ARE NOT NEARLY AS INFLAMMATORY AS THEY WERE

BEFORE APRIL 1995, BOTH GHAWSHAH AND NAZZAL HAVE MAINTAINED LOW

PROFILES. INDEED, MFA SECRETARY GENERAL IBRAHIM NAGHAWI

GENERALLY DOWNPLAYED THE IMPORTANCE OF THE HAMAS OFFICE IN JORDAN

IN A CONVERSATION WITH CHARGE ON MARCH 6, THOUGH HE INDICATED

THAT JORDANIAN AUTHORITIES CONTINUE TO WATCH BOTH MEN. IN A

MARCH 5 MEETING WITH THE CHARGE, ISRAELI DCM JACOB ROSEN AFFIRMED

THAT ISRAEL FEELS SECURE IN THE FACT THAT JORDAN HAS HAMAS "FULLY

COVERED."

6. (C) IN THE WAKE OF THE PA\'S MOST RECENT ALLEGATIONS OF

JORDANIAN COMPLICITY IN HAMAS MILITARY WING OPERATIONS, MANY

OBSERVERS ANTICIPATE A CRACKDOWN BY THE GOJ, POSSIBLY IN RESPONSE

TO U.S. OF ISRAELI PRESSURE. AN INFORMED EMBASSY SOURCE TOLD

EMBOFF THAT THE CRACKDOWN COULD INCLUDE THE POSSIBLE

"DEPORTATION" OF PERSONS SUSPECTED OF MAINTAINING LINKS WITH

HAMAS OPERATIVES IN THE TERRITORIES. GOJ OFFICIALS HAVE,

HOWEVER, PUBLICLY DISCLAIMED ANY "OPERATIONAL" CONNECTIONS

BETWEEN HAMAS SYMPATHIZERS IN JORDAN AND MILITANTS ON THE OTHER

SIDE OF THE JORDAN RIVER. INTERIOR MINISTER KHLAYFAT TOLD THE

ARABIC-LANGUAGE DAILY "AL\'RA\'I" THAT HAMAS MEMBERS IN JORDAN "ARE

JORDANIANS WHO HAVE NO RELATION WITH TERRORISM OR WHATEVER IS

HAPPENING IN ISRAEL OR THE PALESTINIAN SELF-RULE AREAS." HE

CHARACTERIZED "THE PEOPLE WHO MAN THE HAMAS OFFICE IN AMMAN" AS

"JORDANIAN CITIZENS WHO ACT IN A PEACEFUL MANNER SUBJECT TO

JORDANIAN LAWS." SIGNIFICANTLY, THE MINISTER ADDED THAT THE GOJ

COULD NOT "DEPORT JORDANIAN CITIZENS -- THAT\'S AGAINST THE LAW."

7. (C) JORDANIAN AUTHORITIES\' SENSITIVITY ABOUT BEING PORTRAYED

AS OTHER THAN COMMITTED TO THE FIGHT AGAINST TERRORISM MAY PROMPT

THE GOJ TO HEIGHTEN ITS SURVEILLANCE OF HAMAS\' OFFICE AND

PERSONNEL IN JORDAN AND TAKE WHATEVER ACTION IS DEEMED BOTH

APPROPRIATE AND PRUDENT. AS IT HAS SHOWN IN THE PAST, THE GOJ

WILL, IF NECESSARY, DISREGARD OBJECTIONS THAT MIGHT BE RAISED BY

THE OPPOSITION. CROWN PRINCE HASSAN, IN ANNOUNCING A HEIGHTENED

STATE OF ALERT IN JORDAN MARCH 5, INDICATED THAT JORDAN WOULD DO

WHATEVER IS NECESSARY TO PRESERVE PUBLIC SECURITY WITHIN ITS

BORDERS.

BEECROFT
