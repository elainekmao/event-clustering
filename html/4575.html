CLASSIFIED BY: AMBASSADOR JIM NICHOLSON, REASON EO 12958 (B, D)

1. (C) SUMMARY: ALTHOUGH THE COLD WAR IS OVER FOR MOST,

THE HOLY SEE CONTINUES TO STRUGGLE AGAINST COMMUNIST REGIMES

IN ASIA. THE CATHOLIC CHURCH\'S ABILITY TO MANAGE ITS AFFAIRS

WITHOUT INTERFERENCE IS CURRENTLY BETTER IN

VIETNAM THAN IN CHINA, BUT THE HOLY SEE IS WORKING IN VIETNAM

TO ADVANCE ITS FREEDOM TO OPERATE ONE STEP AT A TIME.

ACCORDING TO HOLY SEE EAST ASIA AFFAIRS DIRECTOR MONSIGNOR

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 VATICA 05803 01 OF 02 030646Z

LUIS MONTEMAYOR, THE HOLY SEE IS BATTING ABOUT .500 DURING

ITS ANNUAL MEETINGS WITH THE GOV ON CHURCH ISSUES IN VIETNAM.

HOLY SEE DFM MIGLIORE IN AN OCTOBER 10-16 VISIT TO VIETNAM

REACHED AGREEMENT ON APPOINTMENT OF SOME BISHOPS AND

APOSTOLIC ADMINISTRATORS, BUT FAILED IN OTHERS. THE KEY

ISSUE OF WHO WILL REPLACE CARDINAL PHAM DIHN TUNG REMAINS TO

BE RESOLVED. OTHER ISSUES CURRENTLY OF CONCERN TO THE HOLY

SEE ARE THE OPPRESSION OF THE MONTAGNARDS AND THE

IMPRISONMENT OF OUTSPOKEN POLITICAL ACTIVIST FATHER VAN LEE

-- WHOM THE HOLY SEE VIEWS AS A VICTIM OF POLITICAL, NOT

RELIGIOUS, OPPRESSION. END SUMMARY.

PLAYING FOR THE LONG-TERM

2. (C) AS BEFITS THE REPRESENTATIVE OF AN ENTITY WITH OVER

2000 YEARS OF HISTORY BEHIND IT, HOLY SEE S/E ASIA COUNTRY

DIRECTOR MSGR. LUIS MONTEMAYOR WAS CONFIDENT THAT EVENTUALLY

THE HOLY SEE WOULD SUCCEED IN PRYING OPEN THE DOORS TO FULL

RELIGIOUS FREEDOM IN VIETNAM. "WE HAVE PLENTY OF TIME", HE

NOTED. THE HOLY SEE UNDERSTANDS THAT PROGRESS COMES SLOWLY

WHEN DEALING WITH A COMMUNIST GOVERNMENT. CONSEQUENTLY, IT

ACCEPTS THAT, AS IT SEEKS TO SHUFFLE THE DECK ON

APPOINTMENTS, IT IS LIKELY TO RECEIVE APPROVAL FOR ONLY SOME

OF ITS REQUESTS OF THE GOV FOR FREEDOM TO ADMINISTER THE

CHURCH. ACCORDING TO MONTEMAYOR, THE HOLY SEE IS SEEKING TO

MOVE CLERGY FROM THE MORE "COMPLIANT" 15 SOUTHERN DIOCESES

CLOSELY LINKED TO ROME TO LEADERSHIP POSITIONS IN THE MORE

"ALIENATED"

10 DIOCESES IN THE NORTH. OVER THE LONG RUN THE HOLY SEE

HOPES TO ENSURE BROADER ACCEPTANCE OF ROME\'S CENTRAL ROLE IN

CHURCH AFFAIRS IN VIETNAM.

3. (C) A KEY BAROMETER OF GOV FLEXIBILITY IS ITS ACCEPTANCE

OF VATICAN APPOINTEES TO BISHOPS\' SEATS. DURING THE OCTOBER

VISIT TO VIETNAM BY VATICAN DFM CELESTINO MIGLIORE, THE

VATICAN OBTAINED GOV AGREEMENT TO APPOINTING REV. JOSEPH VU

VAN THIEM AS THE NEW BISHOP IN HAI PHONG, A COADJUTOR BISHOP

IN CAN THO AND A SUCCESSOR TO THE BISHOP OF KONTUM. THE GOV

REJECTED THE HOLY SEE\'S CANDIDATES FOR THE POST OF COADJUTOR

OF HANOI AND THE BISHOP TO HUNG HOA AS WELL AS THE REQUEST TO

DIVIDE THE PROVINCE OF XUAN LOC INTO TWO APOSTOLIC

TERRITORIES. ALTHOUGH THE HOLY SEE AND GOV HAVE AGREED ON AN

APOSTOLIC ADMINISTRATOR FOR THE DIOCESE OF HANOI, THE ISSUE

OF NAMING A REPLACEMENT FOR 83-YEAR-OLD CARDINAL TUNG REMAINS

UNSETTLED (NOTE: TUNG IS CONTINUING IN HIS SEE BECAUSE THE

HOLY SEE DID NOT HAVE GOV SUPPORT FOR THE REPLACEMENT THEY

DESIRED). ACCORDING TO MONTEMAYOR, CARDINAL TUNG AND THE

HOLY SEE REMAIN AT ODDS OVER HIS REPLACEMENT, AND THE HOLY

SEE BELIEVES THE GOV IS BLOCKING THEIR CANDIDATE PER TUNG,S

REQUEST. THE GOV HAS ASKED FOR A LIST OF THREE CANDIDATES

FROM WHICH TO CHOOSE, BUT THE HOLY SEE WILL ONLY SUBMIT ITS

ONE CHOICE. SHOULD CARDINAL TUNG DIE BEFORE AN AGREEMENT IS

REACHED, THE HOLY SEE WILL LEAVE THE SEAT VACANT AND LET THE

GOV EXPLAIN TO THE WORLD WHY IT IS BLOCKING THE HOLY SEE\'S

RIGHT TO CHOSE HIS SUCCESSOR.

4. (C) MSGR. NGUYEN VAN PHUONG, WHO ACCOMPANIED DFM

MIGLIORE, TOLD US THAT THE GOV ACKNOWLEDGED RECEIVING WRITTEN

ACCOUNTS OF PERSECUTION OF CENTRAL HIGHLAND CATHOLICS FROM

THE VIETNAMESE BISHOPS\' CONFERENCE. HE ALSO SAID THAT THE

GOV WAS AWARE THAT LOCAL AUTHORITIES WERE HARASSING

CHRISTIANS, "THE RELIGION OF FOREIGNERS," AND FORCING THEM TO

SIGN STATEMENTS REJECTING CHRISTIANITY. THE GOV HAD

APOLOGIZED FOR THIS BEHAVIOR AND PROMISED A RESPONSE. TO

DATE, SOME 300-PLUS FAMILIES HAVE SUFFERED AT THE HANDS OF

THE LOCAL AUTHORITIES.

5. (C) THE HOLY SEE IS A STAUNCH PROPONENT OF RELIGIOUS

FREEDOM, AND SEEKS TO MAINTAIN CONSISTENCY, SAID FATHER

NGUYEN. FOR EXAMPLE, THE HOLY SEE RECOGNIZED THAT THE RECENT

IMPRISONMENT OF A LOCAL CATHOLIC PRIEST IN VIETNAM WAS A

POLITICAL ACTION BASED ON HIS OUTSPOKEN POSITION ON GOV

POLICIES RATHER THAN A SUPPRESSION OF RELIGIOUS FREEDOM. THE

IMPRISONED FATHER VAN LEE HAD SPOKEN AGAINST THE GOV POSITION

DURING A TRADE TREATY DISPUTE WITH THE U.S. THE HOLY SEE,

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 VATICA 05803 02 OF 02 030646Z

WHILE HARDLY APPROVING OF THE PRIEST\'S IMPRISONMENT, WILL NOT

CONDEMN IT AS A SUPPRESSION OF RELIGIOUS FREEDOM. THEY ARE

CAREFUL NOT TO PLAY THE RELIGIOUS FREEDOM CARD TOO OFTEN, IN

ORDER TO PRESERVE THEIR EQUITIES FOR TRULY EGREGIOUS

VIOLATIONS OF RELIGIOUS FREEDOM.

COMMENT

-------

6. (U) THE HOLY SEE REMAINS INTERESTED IN ESTABLISHING

OFFICIAL RELATIONS WITH GOV. CURRENTLY, THE LINES OF

COMMUNICATION ARE LIMITED TO THE ANNUAL HOLY SEE DELEGATION

VISIT AND THROUGH THE GOV EMBASSY TO ITALY. WHILE THE HOLY

SEE GENERALLY PUT A POSITIVE SPIN ON THE PROGRESS WITH THE

GOV THIS YEAR, MIGLIORE HAD COMPLAINED BEFORE HIS TRIP THAT

THEY WERE "TIRED" OF THE 50 PERCENT APPROACH BY THE GOV. THE

HOLY SEE APPRECIATES THE DEPARTMENT\'S FREEDOM OF RELIGION

REPORT, WHICH THEY BELIEVE STRENGTHENS THEIR ABILITY TO

CONTINUE PRESSING THE GOV FOR GREATER RELIGIOUS FREEDOM.
