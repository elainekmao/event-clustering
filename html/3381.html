SUBJECT: FAO RESPONSE TO DEMARCHE ON GMO FOOD AID

REF: A) STATE 139519

B) MAPUTO 1408

CLASSIFIED BY: ALTERNATE PERM REP, LUCY TAMLYN FOR REASONS

1.5 B AND D

1. (C) SUMMARY. FAO DIRECTOR GENERAL DIOUF IS EXPECTED TO

SEND A LETTER TO UN UNDERSECRETARY OSHIMA CORRECTING HIS

PREVIOUS CORRESPONDENCE AND STATING THAT THE CARTAGENA

PROTOCOL DOES NOT STIPULATE MILLING OF GM COMMODITIES TO

PREVENT PLANTING. COPIES OF THE CORRESPONDENCE SHOULD BE

SENT TO SOUTHERN AFRICAN FAO REPS AS WELL. WHILE FAO

RECOGNIZES THAT IT MADE A FACTUAL ERROR IN THE ORIGINAL

CORRESPONDENCE, THE PRIMARY FAO CONCERN (AS EXPRESSED BY FAO

DEPUTY HARCHARIK) IS THAT IT IS BEING "UNFAIRLY SCAPEGOATED"

IN THIS MATTER. END SUMMARY.

2. (U) ALTERNATE PERM REP AND HUMANITARIAN ATTACHE

DISCUSSED REFTEL (A) DEMARCHE WITH THE FAO\'S DEPUTY DIRECTOR

GENERAL, DAVID HARCHARIK, ON MONDAY JULY 22. WE NOTED THAT

THE FAO WAS APPARENTLY PROVIDING INCORRECT RECOMMENDATIONS

BASED ON A MISUNDERSTANDING OF THE CARTAGENA PROTOCOL AND

THAT THIS COULD POTENTIALLY HAVE DISASTROUS IMPLICATIONS IN

SOUTHERN AFRICA. HARCHARIK WAS NOT FAMILIAR WITH THE MATTER

BUT PROMISED TO LOOK INTO IT IMMEDIATELY.

3. (C) ALTERNATE PERM REP FOLLOWED-UP WITH HARCHARIK BY

PHONE ON 7/23, 7/24 AND 7/25. AS OF THE LAST CONVERSATION,

THE SITUATION IS AS FOLLOWS:

-- THE FAO RECOGNIZES THAT THE LETTER TO UN UNDERSECRETARY

OSHIMA WAS INCORRECT.

-- A NEW LETTER WILL BE TRANSMITTED SHORTLY TO UNDERSECRETARY

OSHIMA. WE UNDERSTAND THE LETTER WILL SAY, IN REFERENCE TO

THE FAO\'S PREVIOUS LETTER, THAT THE CARTAGENA PROTOCOL DOES

NOT STIPULATE MILLING OR ADVANCE INFORMED AGREEMENT (AIA) FOR

FOOD OR FEED. HOWEVER, (PER HARCHARIK), THE LETTER WILL NOT

SAY THAT "GMO\'S ARE SAFE" OR "NOT SAFE" BUT WILL REFER TO THE

PREVIOUS LETTER\'S STATEMENT THAT THE "PREVAILING SCIENTIFIC

OPINION IS THAT THEY ARE AS SAFE AS THEIR CONVENTIONAL

COUNTERPARTS." THE LETTER MAY GO OUT LATE TODAY OR TOMORROW

(FRIDAY 7/26).

-- A COPY OF THE LETTER SHOULD BE SENT TO THE FAO COUNTRY

REPRESENTATIVES.

-- ALTHOUGH EXPLICITLY ASKED, HARCHARIK WOULD NOT AGREE TO

SHARE THE NEW LETTER WITH THE MISSION SO WE DO NOT KNOW WHAT

THE EXACT CONTENTS WILL BE. HARCHARIK STATED THAT THE LETTER

HAS BEEN DELAYED IN PART BECAUSE "DIRECTOR GENERAL (DG) DIOUF

WANTS TO ADD SOME OTHER THINGS." (THE DG IS CURRENTLY IN

SENEGAL.)

4. (C) HARCHARIK\'S BIGGEST CONCERN, HOWEVER, WAS THAT THE

FAO WAS BEING "SCAPEGOATED" IN THIS AFFAIR. HARCHARIK NOTED

THAT THE ORIGINAL REQUEST FROM OSHIMA WAS FOR UN AGENCY INPUT

IN ORDER TO DEVELOP A COMMON UN POLICY. IN FACT, NO UN POLICY

HAD EMERGED FROM OSHIMA, AND, ACCORDING TO HARCHARIK, "LEAKED

FAO LETTERS" WERE BEING USED TO "UNFAIRLY IDENTIFY FAO AS THE

CULPRIT."

FAO IN THE FIELD (MAPUTO)

-------------------------

5. (C) ALTERNATE PERM REP RESPONDED THAT THE REAL PROBLEM

WAS NOT WHAT HAD HAPPENED IN NEW YORK, BUT WHAT WAS HAPPENING

IN THE FIELD, AND PARTICULARLY THE ROLE OF THE FAO

REPRESENTATIVES AND THE ADVICE THAT THEY WERE PROVIDING TO

GOVERNMENTS. IN THIS CONNECTION HARCHARIK MENTIONED THAT HE

HAD BEEN IN CONTACT WITH THE FAO REP IN MAPUTO, MR. PETER

VANDOR, WHO APPARENTLY "WANTED TO KNOW WHAT HE WAS TO SAY."

HARCHARIK REPORTED THAT HE HAD HAD TOLD VANDOR TO "HOLD OFF,"

BUT ADDED THAT VANDOR HAD ASSURED HIM THAT:

1) THERE WAS SUFFICIENT MILLING CAPACITY IN MOZAMBIQUE AND

WFP ACTION

----------------

6. (C) HUMANITARIAN ATTACHE TIM LAVELLE SPOKE 7/25 WITH THE

WFP\'S REGIONAL OFFICER IN JOHANNESBURG (JUDITH LEWIS) WHO WAS

AWARE OF FAO MOZAMBIQUE COUNTRY DIRECTOR PETER VANDOR\'S HIGH

VISIBILITY ON THE GM MAIZE ISSUE. LAVELLE QUOTED TO LEWIS

THE WFP\'S JUNE 28 LETTER TO OSHIMA ON THIS MATTER WHICH

STATED "THE UN SHOULD NOT UNDER ANY CIRCUMSTANCES PROMOTE THE

IDEA THAT ALL GM COMMODITIES IMPORTED EITHER COMMERCIALLY OR

AS FOOD AID NEED TO BE MILLED. MILLING SHOULD BE TREATED

ONLY AS AN OPTION FOR GOVERNMENTS WHICH PLACE RESTRICTIONS ON

GM FOOD IMPORTS."

7. (C) HUMANITARIAN ATTACHE SUGGESTED THAT THE WFP EXECUTIVE

DIRECTOR, JAMES MORRIS CALL FAO DG DIOUF AND RECOMMEND THAT

FAO OFFICERS THROUGH SOUTHERN AFRICA ADOPT A "MUTED" STANCE

ON GM MATTERS.

COMMENT

--------

8. (C) FODAG BELIEVES THAT THE UN NEEDS TO BE SPEAKING WITH

ONE VOICE ON FOOD AID ISSUES IN THE REGION -- THROUGH THE

WORLD FOOD PROGRAM. IN THIS REGARD WE NOTE THAT THE UNSYG

ANNAN APPOINTED ON JULY 23 WFP EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR MORRIS AS

HIS SPECIAL ENVOY ON THE HUMANITARIAN CRISIS IN AFRICA.
