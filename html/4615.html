SUBJECT: UKRAINE - GOI TELLS KUCHMA TO SHAPE UP; WANTS TO

KEEP LINES OF COMMUNICATION OPEN

REF: ROME 4912

CLASSIFIED BY: POLITICAL MINISTER-COUNSELOR TOM COUNTRYMAN

1. (C) SUMMARY: ON DECEMBER 2, RUSSIA/FSU OFFICE DIRECTOR

CONFIDENTIAL

ROSA ANNA CONIGLIO TOLD POLOFF THAT THE NOVEMBER 26/27 VISIT

OF UKRAINIAN PRESIDENT KUCHMA TO ITALY WAS CONSIDERED BY THE

GOI TO HAVE BEEN A "GREAT SUCCESS." KUCHMA HEARD A STRONG

MESSAGE FROM ALL HIS ITALIAN INTERLOCUTORS ON THE KOLCHUGA

RADAR ISSUE AND THE GENERAL NEGATIVE UKRAINIAN PERFORMANCE ON

HUMAN RIGHTS. AT THE SAME TIME, LINES OF COMMUNICATION TO

THE GOU WERE KEPT OPEN, AND ITALY MADE PROGRESS WITH UKRAINE

ON A NUMBER OF IMPORTANT BILATERAL ISSUES, SUCH AS ADOPTIONS

AND BUSINESS TAXATION ISSUES. THE GOI HAS OFFERED ITS

SERVICES TO THE U.S. AS A MEDIATOR AND MESSAGE CARRIER TO THE

GOU SHOULD WE DEEM IT USEFUL. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) CONIGLIO STARTED OFF WITH AN ACKNOWLEDGMENT THAT THE

TIMING OF THE VISIT COULD NOT HAVE BEEN WORSE, GIVEN THE

KOLCHUGA RADAR ISSUE AND NATO/OSCE/EU ATTEMPTS TO ISOLATE

KUCHMA. HOWEVER, SHE CONTINUED, WHILE KUCHMA\'S TRIP WAS

DOWNGRADED TO AN "OFFICIAL VISIT," IT WAS ORIGINALLY PLANNED

AS A STATE VISIT IN THE BEGINNING OF 2001. PRESIDENT

CIAMPI\'S AGENDA IS GENERALLY PLANNED TWO YEARS IN ADVANCE,

PARTICULARLY FOR STATE VISITS. THE GOI DECIDED TO GO AHEAD

WITH THE VISIT BECAUSE IT HEARD NO OBJECTIONS FROM ALLIES AND

THOUGHT THAT SOME GOOD COULD COME FROM DIALOGUE WITH KUCHMA.

THERE HAD ALSO BEEN TWO STATE VISITS TO UKRAINE BY ITALIAN

PRESIDENTS SINCE UKRAINIAN INDEPENDENCE FROM THE SOVIET UNION

3. (C) POLOFF ASSURED CONIGLIO THAT THE PRESS REPORTS

(PARTICULARLY ITALIAN MEDIA) JUST PRIOR TO THE VISIT IMPLYING

THAT THE GOI WAS THUMBING ITS NOSE AT THE ALLIES, ESPECIALLY

THE U.S., BY HOSTING KUCHMA HAD NO USG SOURCE. CONIGLIO WAS

ALREADY AWARE THAT EMBASSY ROME HAD ASKED WASHINGTON SOME

WEEKS PRIOR TO THE TRIP (REFTEL) WHETHER THE USG WANTED THE

TRIP CANCELED. THE SPECIFIC POINTS WE HAD RECEIVED IN THE

RESPONSE TO THE REQUEST HAD BEEN PASSED TO THE PRESIDENT\'S

OFFICE, AND HAD BEEN MADE PART OF CIAMPI\'S AND PM

BERLUSCONI\'S TALKING POINTS. CONIGLIO WAS RELIEVED TO HEAR

THAT OUR OFFICIAL PUBLIC LINE, IF ASKED, WAS THAT THE USG

KNEW ABOUT THE VISIT AND RAISED NO OBJECTIONS.

4. (C) CONIGLIO SAID THAT IN ADDITION TO DOWNGRADING THE

VISIT STATUS, THE GOI MADE CLEAR TO KUCHMA ITS DISPLEASURE

WITH HIS REGIME\'S ACTIVITIES BY POSTPONING A SIGNING CEREMONY

FOR A NUMBER OF BILATERAL AGREEMENTS. THE GOVERNMENTS HAD

REACHED CLOSURE ON LANGUAGE FOR THE AGREEMENTS DURING THE

VISIT, AND ITALY WILL EVENTUALLY SIGN BECAUSE THEY ARE

IMPORTANT TO ITALIAN NATIONAL INTERESTS. HOWEVER, THERE WILL

BE NO CEREMONY - THE ITALIAN AMBASSADOR IN KIEV WILL SIGN THE

AGREEMENTS. THE AGREEMENTS INCLUDE REGULATIONS ON ADOPTIONS

OF UKRAINIAN CHILDREN BY ITALIAN PARENTS, AND REFORM OF

DOUBLE-TAXATION LAWS PENALIZING SMALL/MEDIUM ITALIAN

ENTERPRISES DOING BUSINESS IN UKRAINE.

5. (C) KUCHMA MET WITH PRESIDENT CIAMPI, PM BERLUSCONI,

SENATE PRESIDENT PERA AND CENTRAL BANK GOVERNOR FAZIO. FM

FRATTINI MET SEPARATELY WITH FM ZLENKO. ACCORDING TO

CONIGLIO, KUCHMA HEARD FROM ALL OF HIS INTERLOCUTORS THE

FOLLOWING: YOU MUST DISCLOSE, NOW, ALL OF THE FACTS

REGARDING SALES/SHIPMENTS OF KOLCHUGA RADAR SYSTEMS, AND; YOU

SAY YOU WANT CLOSER INTEGRATION OF UKRAINE WITH THE REST OF

EUROPE, SO BACK UP YOUR WORDS WITH DEEDS. PM BERLUSCONI

EMPHASIZED TO KUCHMA THAT WHILE THERE HAD BEEN SOME PROGRESS,

OVERALL KIEV\'S RECORD ON IMPLEMENTATION OF COMMITMENTS TO

INTERNATIONAL ORGANIZATIONS, MEDIA FREEDOM, AND TREATMENT OF

OPPOSITION POLITICAL PARTIES WAS ABYSMAL, AND NOT

CHARACTERISTIC OF A REPRESENTATIONAL DEMOCRACY.

6. (C) CONIGLIO SAID THAT THROUGHOUT HIS VISIT AND WITH ALL

HIS INTERLOCUTORS, KUCHMA DENIED KNOWING ANYTHING ABOUT

KOLCHUGA SALES TO IRAQ, AND SAID THAT THE RECORDINGS WERE A

FABRICATION COMPOSED OF OTHER SPLICED TAPES. FURTHER,

CONTINUED KUCHMA, THESE SUSPECT TAPES WERE THE ONLY EVIDENCE

AGAINST HIM. KUCHMA SAID THAT UKRAINE HAD SOLD SOME SYSTEMS

TO RUSSIA PRIOR TO 2000 AND TO CHINA IN 2002, BUT HE COULDN\'T

SAY WHERE THE SYSTEMS ARE NOW. "YOU\'LL HAVE TO ASK THE

BUYERS, WE CAN\'T TELL YOU" KUCHMA TOLD BERLUSCONI AND CIAMPI.

7. (C) KUCHMA TOLD BERLUSCONI THAT "RICH, PRIVATE

BUSINESSMEN OWNED 90 PERCENT OF THE MEDIA" AND THAT THEREFORE

THERE COULD BE NO GOVERNMENT INFLUENCE. HE ALSO COMPLAINED

THAT HE WAS NOT SATISFIED WITH UKRAINE\'S POLITICAL SYSTEM,

SINCE THE PARLIAMENT BLOCKED MANY OF HIS ADMINISTRATION\'S

INITIATIVES, AND THEREFORE LITTLE WAS ACCOMPLISHED. KUCHMA

TOLD PERA THAT HE WANTED TO RESTRUCTURE THE GOU INTO A

PARLIAMENTARY SYSTEM SIMILAR TO ITALY\'S. PERA AND KUCHMA

AGREED TO AN "EXCHANGE OF EXPERTS" ON PARLIAMENTARY SYSTEMS

TO STUDY POSSIBLE REFORMS IN KIEV.

KUCHMA COMPLAINED THAT THE UKRAINE CENTRAL BANK WAS CREATED

DURING THE SOVIET ERA AND STILL OPERATED ON THAT DESIGN.

KUCHMA ASKED FOR, AND FAZIO AGREED TO PROVIDE, ASSISTANCE TO

REFORM THE BANKING SYSTEM, TO INCLUDE FRAUD DETECTION AND

PROTECTIONS AGAINST MONEY LAUNDERING. CONIGLIO SPECULATED

THAT KUCHMA, WELL AWARE OF SIMILAR REFORMS UNDERWAY IN

RUSSIA, DID NOT WANT KIEV TO FALL TOO FAR BEHIND MOSCOW IN

THE EYES OF THE WTO, IMF, ETC.

9. (C) CONIGLIO SAID THAT THE GOI\'S VIEW WAS THAT THERE

WERE SUFFICIENT SIGNS OF REFORM IN UKRAINE TO WARRANT

CONTINUING COMMUNICATIONS BETWEEN ROME AND KIEV. IN ADDITION

TO THE INITIATIVES DESCRIBED ABOVE, CONIGLIO OFFERED THE

GOI\'S SERVICES AS MEDIATOR AND MESSAGE COURIER BETWEEN THE

U.S. AND UKRAINE SHOULD WE THINK IT APPROPRIATE.

10. (C) COMMENT: THE GOI HAS ASSUMED THE ROLE OF

INTERMEDIARY BEFORE, MOST RECENTLY CARRYING OUR MESSAGES TO

PUTIN THROUGH BERLUSCONI ON IRAN DUAL-USE TECHNOLOGY

TRANSFER. IT SEEMS TO BE A ROLE BERLUSCONI ENJOYS AND ONE

THAT POST RECOMMENDS WE HOLD IN RESERVE SHOULD WE WANT OUR

OWN MESSAGES REINFORCED WITH THE GOU, OR THE POLITICAL

SITUATION IN KIEV DETERIORATES TO THE POINT WHERE WE WOULD

FIND IT MORE PRODUCTIVE TO TAKE BERLUSCONI UP ON HIS OFFER.
