SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) SUMMARY: ON SEPTEMBER 21, DCM AND POLOFF CALLED

ON NEWLY APPOINTED DEPUTY MINISTER FOR EDUCATION,

SPORTS, AND CULTURE AENEAS CHIGWEDERE. THE DEPUTY

MINISTER, A RELIABLE EMBASSY INTERLOCUTOR IN HIS EARLIER

POSITIONS, WAS CHARACTERISTICALLY FRANK IN HIS

DISCUSSION OF ZANU-PF POLITICS AND HIS WARNING OF DIRE

CONSEQUENCES FOR ZIMBABWE IF THE U.S. CONGRESS PASSES

THE ZIMBABWE DEMOCRACY ACT (ZDA). WHILE RECOGNIZING THE

GOVERNMENT\'S FAILINGS SINCE INDEPENDENCE, AND EVEN

CALLING MUGABE A LIABILITY, CHIGWEDERE ARGUED THAT

PASSAGE OF THE ZDA WOULD ONLY ACCELERATE CHAOS IN

ZIMBABWE. ALTHOUGH WE SEE CHIGWEDERE AS A REALIST,

ZIMBABWE HAS MUCH FARTHER TO FALL BEFORE HIS WORST CASE

SCENARIO IS REALIZED. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) ON SEPTEMBER 21, DCM AND POLOFF CALLED ON NEWLY

APPOINTED DEPUTY MINISTER FOR EDUCATION, SPORTS, AND

CULTURE AENEAS CHIGWEDERE. CHIGWEDERE, A SECOND-TERM

MEMBER OF PARLIAMENT, HAS BEEN A RELIABLE AND UNUSUALLY

FRANK INTERLOCUTOR FOR THE EMBASSY. DCM CONGRATULATED

CHIGWEDERE ON HIS APPOINTMENT, AND THE DEPUTY MINISTER

POINTED OUT THAT THE MINISTRY HAS AN ALL NEW LEADERSHIP,

INCLUDING A NEW MINISTER AND PERMANENT SECRETARY.

CHIGWEDERE WENT ON TO SAY BLUNTLY THAT PRESIDENT MUGABE

HAD APPOINTED NEW BLOOD INTO THE MINISTRY TO IMPLEMENT

REFORM, SINCE THE OLD LEADERSHIP WOULD HAVE JUST SLOWED

THINGS DOWN.

ZDA TO UNLEASH MILITARY RESPONSE?

---------------------------------

3. (C) THE DEPUTY MINISTER QUICKLY TURNED TO THE

ZIMBABWE DEMOCRACY ACT (ZDA) CURRENTLY BEFORE THE U.S.

HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES. (NOTE: THE ZDA, AS WRITTEN,

WOULD END ALL BILATERAL ASSISTANCE TO THE GOZ. HOWEVER,

NEARLY ALL U.S. ASSISTANCE TO ZIMBABWE IS DISBURSED

THROUGH NGOS AND WOULD BE EXEMPT. END NOTE.) DCM

EXPLAINED THAT WE CANNOT PREDICT IF THE ZDA WILL PASS OR

NOT. THE EXECUTIVE DOES NOT HAVE THE POWER TO TURN OFF

THE BILL, BUT IF IT IS NOT ACTED UPON BEFORE CONGRESS IS

DUE TO ADJOURN ON OR ABOUT OCTOBER 6, THE ZDA WILL DIE

IN COMMITTEE.

4. (C) CHIGWEDERE PREDICTED THAT IF THE ZDA DOES PASS,

IT WILL LEAD TO AN ARMY TAKEOVER. WHEN ASKED TO EXPLAIN

HOW THIS MIGHT HAPPEN, CHIGWEDERE SAID THE ZDA WOULD

ACCELERATE ZIMBABWE\'S ECONOMIC DECLINE AND LEAD TO

UNREST THAT WOULD FORCE THE ARMY TO INTERVENE.

ZIMBABWEAN URBANITES WOULD NOT "LIFT A FINGER" TO OPPOSE

THE MILITARY BECAUSE OF THE MEMORY OF THE RECENT

ELECTIONS AND THE MATABELELAND MASSACRES OF THE 1980S,

AND ZIMBABWE WOULD BE STUCK WITH A MILITARY GOVERNMENT

FOR THREE TO FIVE YEARS. THERE IS NO DOUBT OF THE

GOVERNMENT\'S FAILURES SINCE INDEPENDENCE, CHIGWEDERE

OPINED, BUT THE MILITARY, POLICE, AND WAR VETERANS WOULD

NEVER PERMIT A GOVERNMENT THAT WAS NOT SYMPATHETIC TO

THEIR CAUSE. CHIGWEDERE ADDED THAT THE MILITARY WOULD

NEVER SUPPORT TSVANGIRAI AS PRESIDENT SHOULD HE COME TO

POWER IN SOME KIND OF POPULAR REVOLUTION, BUT WAS MORE

VAGUE WHEN ASKED IF THE MILITARY WOULD SUPPORT A

NEW DEAL ON LAND?

-----------------

5. (C) ON THE LAND ISSUE, CHIGWEDERE REPORTED THAT THE

GOZ IS NEGOTIATING WITH THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS\' UNION

(CFU) THROUGH BACK CHANNELS TO OBTAIN SEVERAL HUNDRED

FARMS AND TO PAY FOR THEM THROUGH A TRUST FUND. THE

DEPUTY MINISTER SAID THE GOVERNMENT IS ALSO IN TOUCH

WITH THE BRITISH ON THIS ISSUE, AND CLAIMED THEY SUPPORT

THE FACE-SAVING IDEA OF A DONOR-FUNDED TRUST FUND TO

BREAK THE LAND REFORM IMPASSE. ZIMBABWE AND THE U.K.

HAVE BEEN SO AT ODDS OVER LAND REFORM THAT NEITHER IS

PUBLICLY WILLING TO SWALLOW THEIR PRIDE. CHIGWEDERE

OFFERED THAT IT IS NOT GOVERNMENT POLICY TO EXPEL ALL

WHITE FARMERS FROM THEIR LAND--THE MOST PRODUCTIVE FARMS

IN MASHONALAND WOULD BE EXEMPTED. ON THE OTHER HAND, IT

IS GOVERNMENT POLICY, HE STATED BLUNTLY, TO HARASS

FARMERS INTO SURRENDERING LARGE TRACTS OF LAND BORDERING

COMMUNAL AREAS BY PERMITTING COMMUNAL FARMERS TO CUT

DOWN TREES, STEAL WIRE, OR OCCUPY THE LAND OUTRIGHT.

MUGABE SEEN AS LIABILITY IN ZANU-PF

-----------------------------------

6. (C) CHIGWEDERE PROVIDED AN UNUSUALLY FRANK AND, IN

OUR JUDGMENT, ACCURATE DESCRIPTION OF THE CURRENT STATE

OF ZANU-PF POLITICS. HE FLAT-OUT STATED THAT MUGABE HAS

BECOME A LIABILITY FOR THE PARTY, AND THE SOONER HE

RETIRES, THE BETTER. IT WOULD BE VERY DIFFICULT FOR HIM

TO DO SO, HOWEVER, BECAUSE IT IS NOT IN THE AFRICAN

TRADITION FOR CHIEFS TO SIMPLY STEP ASIDE AND BECOME

"ORDINARY MEN." ONLY GOD CAN RETIRE A CHIEF, CHIGWEDERE

ADDED. HOWEVER, MUGABE HAS BEEN MISLED BY HIS CIRCLE OF

SYCOPHANTS, WHO, AS IN ANOTHER AFRICAN TRADITION, ONLY

WISH TO PLEASE THEIR CHIEF AND ARE LOATH TO SAY OR DO

ANYTHING THAT WOULD UPSET HIM. THIS HAS LED TO AN IVORY

TOWER SYNDROME IN WHICH MUGABE DOES NOT FULLY UNDERSTAND

THE DEPTH OF DISCONTENT IN THE COUNTRY. WHEN ASKED IF

THERE IS ANYONE WHO COULD SUCCEED MUGABE, THE DEPUTY

MINISTER SAID ONLY FINANCE MINISTER MAKONI WOULD COME

CLOSE TO FILLING HIS SHOES, AS MUGABE\'S INNER CIRCLE HAD

BEEN REJECTED BY THE PEOPLE IN THE JUNE 24-25 ELECTION.

MAKONI, HOWEVER, DOES NOT HAVE A BROAD BASE OF SUPPORT

SINCE MOST OF THE YOUNGER GENERATIONS HAVE NOT BEEN

PERMITTED TO ADVANCE IN THE PARTY. HE DID NOT SEE ANY

GOOD PROSPECTS FOR YOUNG BLOOD TO FILTER UP THE PARTY

HIERARCHY SO LONG AS THE OLD GUARD REMAIN FIRMLY

ENTRENCHED.

7. (C) COMMENT: A COMMITTED AFRICANIST, CHIGWEDERE IS,

IN ZANU-PF TERMS, SOMEONE WE WOULD DESCRIBE AS A

MODERATE. UNLIKE MOST OF MUGABE\'S CABINET, HE IS KNOWN

FOR TELLING IT LIKE IT IS, BUT HIS PREDICTION THAT A

MILITARY TAKEOVER IS IN THE WINGS IF THE ZDA PASSES IS

FARFETCHED. HIS ARGUMENT CONTAINED FEW SPECIFICS, BUT

PROBABLY REFLECTS A GROWING REALIZATION IN ZANU-PF THAT

THE GOZ IS INCREASINGLY ISOLATED, BOTH AT HOME AND

ABROAD. WHAT LITTLE POPULAR SUPPORT ZANU-PF\'S

LEADERSHIP HAS LEFT IS STEADILY ERODING, INSIDE AND

OUTSIDE THE PARTY. CHIGWEDERE\'S APPEAL TO HALT PASSAGE

OF THE ZDA BASED ON AN IMMINENT MILITARY COUP TRUMPETS

ZANU-PF\'S GROWING DESPERATION. IT IS VERY UNLIKELY THAT

PASSAGE OF THE ZDA WOULD HAVE ANY PRACTICAL EFFECT ON

ZIMBABWE\'S ECONOMY, BUT IT COULD BECOME A CONVENIENT

SCAPEGOAT FOR THE GOZ\'S OWN GROSS MISMANAGEMENT OF THE

ECONOMY. IT IS NOT INCONCEIVABLE THAT MUGABE WOULD

PREFER TO HAVE THE ACT BECOME LAW SO HE COULD ADD THE

U.S. TO HIS LONG LIST OF EXCUSES FOR HIS OWN

GOVERNMENT\'S POOR PERFORMANCE. END COMMENT.
