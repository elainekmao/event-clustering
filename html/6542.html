SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) ALMOST ALL OF CROATIA\'S POLITICIANS HAVE PUBLICLY

BACKED THE GOC DECISION NOT TO SUPPORT MILITARY ACTION IN

IRAQ ABSENT A NEW UNSC RESOLUTION (REFTEL). POLITICAL UNITY

IN CROATIA IS A GENUINE RARITY. SOME LEADING GOVERNMENT

FIGURES WANT TO TRY TO CATCH WHAT THEY THINK IS A WAVE OF

LOCAL SUPPORT FOR THE GOC\'S DECISION TO SAY "NO" TO THE USG

AND ARE CROWING ABOUT THEIR "BRAVE" DECISION. OTHER

POLITICAL OBSERVERS ARE DUMBFOUNDED BY THE GOC DECISION AND

BY ITS MANY PUBLIC STATEMENTS. THEY ARE WONDERING WHY

CROATIA\'S POLITICIANS ARE MISSING ANOTHER EXCELLENT

OPPORTUNITY TO SHUT UP. END SUMMARY.

MESIC: US ACTION WOULD HAVE QUESTIONABLE LEGITIMACY

--------------------------------------------- ------

2. (U) PRESIDENT MESIC, IN AN INTERVIEW PUBLISHED IN

"SLOBODNA DALMACIJA" ON MARCH 19, TOOK A HARD

INTERNATIONALIST LINE. HE SAID THAT THE U.S. "DEPARTURE FROM

THE UN CHARTER FRAMEWORK CALLS THE LEGITIMACY OF THE ACTION

INTO QUESTION." HE SAID THAT U.S. ACTION WOULD CREATE A

PRECEDENT WHEREBY A COUNTRY THAT IS "NOT AMERICA, SOMEONE WHO

IS STRONGER THAN THEIR NEIGHBOR, CAN IMPOSE THEIR SOLUTIONS"

AND CITED CROATIA\'S EXPERIENCE WITH MILOSEVIC. MESIC

QUESTIONED WHETHER CROATIA NEEDS "THIS KIND OF NATO."

DPM GRANIC: "NO" TO FUTURE USG REQUESTS FOR SUPPORT

--------------------------------------------- ------

3. (U) DEPUTY PRIME MINISTER GORAN GRANIC SAID THAT THE GOC

IS "CONCERNED ABOUT THE LATEST DEVELOPMENTS AND, OF COURSE

DOES NOT SUPPORT ANY ACTION OUTSIDE OF THE UN." GRANIC SAID

THAT THE GOC WOULD REJECT FUTURE U.S. REQUESTS FOR SUPPORT OF

MILITARY ACTION "ACCORDING TO THE DECISION TAKEN BY THE

COUNTRY\'S LEADERS, WHICH IMPLICITLY STATES THAT IT MAY NOT BE

AMENDED. I REPEAT THAT CROATIA WILL OPPOSE ANY ACTION WHICH

IS TAKEN OUTSIDE THE UN."

SMALLER PARTIES GET ON THE BANDWAGON

------------------------------------

4. (U) LEADERS OF CROATIA\'S SMALLER POLITICAL PARTIES HAVE

JUMPED ON BOARD AS WELL; THEIR COMMENTS WERE PUBLISHED IN THE

MASS-MARKET DAILY "VJESNIK." FORMER FM MATE GRANIC

(PRESIDENT OF THE OPPOSITION DEMOCRATIC CENTER - DC) DECLARED

THAT HIS PARTY DOES NOT SUPPORT MILITARY INTERVENTION WITHOUT

A NEW UNSCR BECAUSE IT WOULD DIVIDE THE COUNCIL AND

"COMPLETELY DESTABILIZE INTERNATIONAL RELATIONS AND THE UN

WITH UNFORESEEABLE CONSEQUENCES." DAMIR KAJIN OF THE

REGIONAL PARTY ISTRIAN DEMOCRATIC UNION (IDS) SAID THAT THE

U.S. DECISION TO GO AHEAD WITHOUT A NEW UNSCR "DELEGITIMIZES

THE ACTION AGAINST IRAQ, WHICH, LET\'S BE TRUTHFUL, AT THIS

MOMENT IS NOT THREATENING ANYONE." HE DERIDED THE STRONG

PRO-U.S. POSITION OF THE OPPOSITION HDZ BY SAYING THAT HDZ

PRESIDENT SANADER "LOOKS LIKE HE IS RUNNING HIS ELECTION

CAMPAIGN IN MASSACHUSETTS, NOT IN CROATIA." HE SAID THAT

CROATIA\'S POLITICIANS ARE SHOWING AN "ENVIABLE LEVEL OF

UNITY."

5. (C) LUKA TRCONIC, VICE PRESIDENT OF THE HSS (THE

COALITION\'S SECOND-LARGEST PARTY) SAID "WE SUPPORT THE

GOVERNMENT POSITION" BUT EXPLAINED THAT HIS PARTY "HAS

UNDERSTANDING FOR THE FRUSTRATION OF THE AMERICANS AND THEIR

DESIRE TO PUNISH SADDAM HUSSEIN." EVEN THE FRINGE PARTIES

ARE ECHOING THE RULING COALITION. ANTO DJAPIC, PRESIDENT OF

THE CROATIAN PARTY OF RIGHT (HSP) SAID THAT "THE HSP DOES NOT

ENDORSE THE USA AND ALLIES\' ACTION IN IRAQ WHICH IS NOT

BACKED BY A RESOLUTION OF THE UNSC. THE MOTIVES FOR THE USA

AND ALLIES TO ATTACK IRAQ ARE NOT IDEALS, BUT RAW INTERESTS

OF A GREAT POWER. (COMMENT: PM RACAN SHOULD BE WORRIED, NOT

COMFORTED BY THE SUPPORT OF THIS PARTY OF RIGHT-WING

EXTREMISTS.)
