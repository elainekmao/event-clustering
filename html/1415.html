2. (C) SUMMARY: THE PRESIDENT\'S TRIP TO VIETNAM ELECTRIFIED

THE VIETNAMESE PUBLIC -- AND SURPRISED THE COMMUNIST PARTY

LEADERSHIP. ENTHUSIASTIC CROWDS OF SEVERAL HUNDRED

THOUSAND LINED THE STREETS TO GREET THE PRESIDENT AND FIRST

LADY ON THEIR HISTORIC VISIT TO HANOI AND HO CHI MINH CITY.

THREE WEEKS AFTER THE VISIT, THE MESSAGE THE PRESIDENT

DELIVERED IN HIS NATIONALLY TELEVISED ADDRESS AND IN BOTH

PUBLIC AND PRIVATE EVENTS CONTINUES TO REVERBERATE IN

VIETNAMESE COFFEE SHOPS, AS WELL AS IN THE BACKROOMS OF THE

PARTY LEADERSHIP. IN ONE DRAMATIC EXAMPLE IN A PROVINCIAL

CAPITAL, A PRIEST CONVENED GROUPS OF SEVERAL HUNDRED

PARISHIONERS TO DISCUSS THE IDEAS OF FREEDOM AND GLOBAL

ENGAGEMENT CONTAINED IN THE PRESIDENT\'S ADDRESS. IN HANOI,

THE TRIP.

3. (C) SUMMARY (CONT\'D): FOR THE COMMUNIST PARTY (CPV)

AUTHORITIES, THE EFFUSIVE POPULAR REACTION TO THE PRESIDENT

UNDOUBTEDLY GIVES THEM PAUSE. POST-WAR VIETNAM HAS SEEN

NOTHING LIKE THE RECEPTION THE PRESIDENT RECEIVED, AND THIS

IS A VIVID REMINDER THAT POPULAR FEELING FOR THE RULING

AUTHORITIES IS, BY COMPARISON, LUKEWARM AT BEST. WE

BELIEVE THE PRESIDENT\'S VISIT, HIS CALL FOR FREE TRADE AND

FREE EXCHANGE OF IDEAS, AND THE OUTPOURING OF POPULAR

SUPPORT FOR HIM WILL BE A KEY TOPIC OF DEBATE AS THE CPV

LEADERSHIP MOVES INTO ITS PARTY CONGRESS IN MARCH. THIS

CUTS TWO WAYS IN THE ONGOING CPV DISCUSSIONS ABOUT

GLOBALIZATION. CAUTIOUS IDEOLOGUES SUCH AS GENERAL

SECRETARY PHIEU EMPHASIZE THE NEED TO PROCEED CAREFULLY TO

WHILE MORE REFORM-MINDED LEADERS MAY FEEL STRENGTHENED BY

THE POPULAR SENTIMENT IN FAVOR OF GREATER ENGAGEMENT WITH

THE U.S. AND THE REST OF THE WORLD. END SUMMARY.
