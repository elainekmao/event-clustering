SUBJECT: NORTHERN IRAQ: TURKMEN FRONT TO BOYCOTT OPPOSITION

CONFERENCE UNLESS 4 KDP-FRIENDLY TURKMEN ARE BLACK-LISTED

REF: A. ANKARA 7775

B. ANKARA 7550

C. ANKARA 7509

D. ANKARA 7148

(U) Classified by Pol-Mil Counselor Stuart Brown. Reasons:

1.5 (B and D).

1. (C) Operation Northern Watch POLAD met with Iraqi Turkmen

Front (ITF) Ankara rep and Executive Committee member Dr.

Mustafa Ziya November 12. Ziya said that although the

opposition conference steering committee had increased the

number of Turkmen seats, the total number of seats had also

been increased. The Turkmen, Ziya said, still end up with

only 6 percent. He complained that at the 1999 New York

meeting the ITF got all of the Turkmen seats, but for the

Brussels conference 2 percent were being given to Turkmen

Islamists, 2 percent to KDP-friendly Turkmen and only 2

percent to the ITF. Ziya said that the ITF wanted to

participate, but would not do so unless the situation were

fixed. He said that the ITF could live with 6 percent of the

seats, and even with sharing those with the Turkmen

Islamists. However, the ITF would not participate if the

KDP-friendly Turkmen were attending.

--------------------

Dr. Ziya Names Names

--------------------

2. (C) Ziya said that the Turkmen the ITF objected to are:

Walid Sharika, Turkmen Brotherhood Party; Saifat din Damerji,

Turkmen Union Party; Jawdat Najar, Kurdistan Turkmen Cultural

Association and minister in the Kurdistan Regional

Government; and Shirzat Oza\'iri, Democratic Turkmen Party of

Kurdistan. Ziya alleged that Sharika and Oza\'iri were not

really Turkmen at all and worked for KDP intel services, and

that Oza\'iri and din Damerji participated in the GOI\'s Anfal

campaign against the Kurds. All these individuals and their

groups had, Ziya said, at most a few dozen supporters (the

ITF, he said was "a thousand times" bigger) and received

their salaries from the KDP. It was, he said, impossible for

the ITF to agree to sit with them as part of the Turkmen

delegation to the conference. Ziya said these four

individuals really represented the KDP, and that the KDP was

using its position on the steering committee to "stack the

deck" in its favor at the conference. He urged the US to

press the KDP to get these individuals removed from the

invitation list.

------------------------------------------

ITF Seeks USG Intervention to Reign-in KDP

------------------------------------------

3. (C) Ziya said the ITF\'s impression was that the Iraqi

Kurds and Shi\'a were controlling the steering committee. It

would, he said, be useful for the USG to discuss the

conference problems with the "group of nine" (the steering

committee plus the ITF and Assyrians - we are unsure who

number 9 is). Ziya said the ITF rep in London would be

meeting with Iraqi National Congress leader Ahmed Chalabi to

discuss the conference, but would be in a listening mode and

would not try to form an alliance.

4. (C) Ziya also told us that per Ref A, the ITF had decided

to appoint him ITF representative responsible for external

relations. An official announcement will be made November

16.
