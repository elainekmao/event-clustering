SUBJECT: ITALIAN DEFENSE MINISTER ON ISAF/OEF PARTICIPATION

CLASSIFIED BY: POL M/C THOMAS COUNTRYMAN FOR REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) SUMMARY. ITALY MAY EXTEND ITS PARTICIPATION IN ISAF

UNTIL LATE JUNE IF REQUESTED, ACCORDING TO FRANCESCO GRECO

(PROTECT), DIPLOMATIC ADVISOR TO DEFENSE MINISTER ANTONIO

MARTINO. GRECO CONFIRMED COMMENTS MARTINO MADE TO THE PRESS

DURING A RECENT TRIP TO VISIT ITALIAN TROOPS IN THE PERSIAN

GULF AND AFGHANISTAN THAT ITALY WOULD REMAIN "AT LEAST UNTIL

JUNE" TO ALLOW THE LOYA JIRGA TO TAKE PLACE IF OTHER EU ISAF

COUNTRIES AGREE TO EXTEND. GRECO TOLD POL-MIL COUNSELOR THAT

MARTINO HAS SENT PRIME MINISTER BERLUSCONI A MEMO

RECOMMENDING THAT ITALY "REMAIN OPEN" TO EXTENDING

PARTICIPATION FOR AN ADDITIONAL THREE MONTHS IN KABUL AND

BAGRAM ONLY. NO FORMAL REQUEST IS EXPECTED OR NEEDED, GRECO

SAID; ITALY WILL ACT IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE OUTCOME OF THE

ISAF MEETING SCHEDULED FOR MARCH 11 IN LONDON. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) DESPITE HIS STRONG RESERVATIONS ABOUT CONTINUED

ITALIAN PARTICIPATION IN THE ISAF MISSION, DEFENSE MINISTER

MARTINO HAS RECOMMENDED TO PM BERLUSCONI THAT ITALIAN TROOPS

REMAIN IN KABUL AND BAGRAM AN ADDITIONAL THREE MONTHS IN THE

EVENT OTHER EU PARTICIPATING COUNTRIES DECIDE AN EXTENSION IS

NECESSARY, DIP ADVISOR GRECO SAID. A TURKISH COMMAND,

SUPPORTED BY GERMAN AND BRITISH SUBCOMMANDS, IS ACCEPTABLE,

HE ADDED. THE EXTENSION WILL BE DISCUSSED AND, APPARENTLY,

DECIDED AT AN ISAF MEETING SCHEDULED FOR MARCH 11 IN LONDON.

MARTINO MET WITH ISAF COMMANDER JOHN MCCOLL AND AFGHAN DEPUTY

PM/MOD FAHIM KHAN DURING HIS STOP IN KABUL, HOWEVER HE TOLD

THE PRESS THAT HE HAD NOT DISCUSSED AN ISAF EXTENSION WITH

EITHER OFFICIAL.

3. (C) MARTINO\'S TRIP TO THE GULF AND KABUL WAS PRIMARILY A

TROOP VISIT AND COINCIDED WITH A ROTATION OF ITALIAN ASSETS.

THE AIRCRAFT CARRIER "GARIBALDI" AND THREE OTHER SHIPS ARE

DUE BACK IN ITALY BY MARCH 20, AS ORIGINALLY PLANNED. THEIR

REPLACEMENTS, THE DESTROYER "DURAND DE LA PENNE" AND THE

FRIGATE "MAESTRALE," ARRIVED IN THE GULF ON MARCH 4. MARTINO

EMPHASIZED TO THE PRESS THAT ITALY\'S COMMITMENT TO OEF

REMAINS STRONG BUT THE SITUATION HAS EVOLVED SO THAT IT MAY

SOON BE POSSIBLE TO END OEF ALTOGETHER. (NOTE: AS GRECO

NOTED, THESE AND OTHER COMMENTS HE MADE ON MARCH 4 LOOKED

PREMATURE AFTER THE FIERCE FIGHTING THAT RECURRED ON MARCH

5-6. END NOTE)

4. (U) MARTINO, ALWAYS SENSITIVE TO PUBLIC CONCERNS ABOUT

ITALIAN INVOLVEMENT IN MISSIONS FAR FROM HOME, TOLD THE PRESS

THAT THE THREAT FROM INDIVIDUAL ACTS OF TERROR IN AFGHANISTAN

STILL EXISTS EVEN IF AL-QAIDA IS DEFEATED. FOR THIS REASON,

ITALY MAY HAVE TO EXTEND ITS ISAF COMMITMENT BEYOND THE

INITIAL THREE MONTHS, HE SAID. IF ITALY IS ASKED TO STAY ON

AND IF ITALY\'S EU PARTNERS IN ISAF REMAIN UNTIL JUNE, THE GOI

WILL CONSIDER AN EXTENSION, HE SAID. THE GOI\'S HEAVY

COMMITMENT IN THE BALKANS -- A PRIORITY REGION FOR ITALY --

WOULD MAKE IT DIFFICULT TO CONTRIBUTE MORE TROOPS OR SUSTAIN

A LONGER PRESENCE IN AFGHANISTAN, MARTINO EXPLAINED. (ITALY

ASSUMES THE COMMAND OF KFOR7 THIS FALL.)

5. (C) COMMENT. ONCE AGAIN, MINISTER MARTINO MADE THE NEWS

-- AND SEEMINGLY NEW POLICY -- WITH HIS SPONTANEOUS PUBLIC

COMMENTS. OUR CONTACTS HAVE ASSURED US THAT MUCH OF THE

MESSAGE WAS TAILORED FOR THE TROOPS HE WAS ADDRESSING. THEY

ADMIT THAT THEY HAVE BEEN BUSY RECONCILING SOME OF HIS

REMARKS WITH THE SITUATION ON THE GROUND. DESPITE MARTINO\'S

TENDENCY TO VOICE HIS GENUINE RESERVATIONS IN PUBLIC, HE HAS

ONCE AGAIN UPHELD ITALY\'S COMMITMENT TO THE COALITION BY

RECOMMENDING AN EXTENSION. UNLESS HIS EU COUNTERPARTS COME

TO A DIFFERENT CONCLUSION AT THE MARCH 11 MEETING, ITALIAN

TROOPS ARE LIKELY TO REMAIN IN KABUL UNTIL LATE JUNE. END

COMMENT. MINIMIZE CONSIDERED.
