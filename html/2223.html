SUBJECT: HERALD WARNS OF U.S.-ZIMBABWE "DIPLOMATIC ROW"

1. THE GOVERNMENT-OWNED DAILY "THE HERALD" PUBLISHED A

PAGE-ONE STORY ON NOVEMBER 23 ASSERTING THAT A "DIPLOMATIC

ROW" WAS IMMINENT BETWEEN THE US AND ZIMBABWE. THE SUBJECT

OF THE "ROW" IS A "LETTER" THE US SENT TO THE GOVERNMENT OF

ZIMBABWE (GOZ) IN PROTEST OF "WIDESPREAD BEATING OF WHITES

AND OTHERS IN RETALIATION TO THE ABDUCTION, TORTURE AND

MURDER OF NATIONAL HERO CDE CAIN NKALA." THE ARTICLE ALSO

SPECULATED THAT THE GOZ WOULD SUMMON AMBASSADOR SULLIVAN TO

PROTEST THE LETTER "AS THERE WAS NO INDISCRIMINATE BEATING

OF PEOPLE IN THE COUNTRY."

2. THE ARTICLE QUOTES AN UNNAMED GOZ SPOKESMAN WHO BLAMES

HARARE-BASED FOREIGN CORRESPONDENTS FOR "FALSE AND ALARMIST

REPORTS" AND FOR "GROSS AND OBSCENE MISREPRESENTATION OF

FACTS" THAT LED THE US GOVERNMENT TO BELIEVE THERE WERE

PROBLEMS IN ZIMBABWE. THE ARTICLE NAMES 5 REPORTERS IN

ZIMBABWE WHO REPORT FOR INTERNATIONAL MEDIA ORGANIZATIONS.

3. EXCERPTS FOLLOW: "THE U.S. YESTERDAY SENT A LETTER OF

PROTEST TO THE GOVERNMENT ALLEGING THAT THERE WERE

WIDESPREAD BEATINGS OF WHITES AND OTHERS IN RETALIATION TO

THE ABDUCTION, TORTURE AND MURDER OF NATIONAL HERO CAIN

NKALA. THE LETTER IS, HOWEVER, LIKELY TO TRIGGER A

DIPLOMATIC FURORE BETWEEN HARARE AND WASHINGTON AS IT WAS

SUSPECTED THAT THE ALLEGATIONS WERE BASED ON WIDESPREAD

FALSE MEDIA REPORTS BY SOME HARARE-BASED OVERSEAS

CORRESPONDENTS. DIPLOMATIC SOURCES TOLD "THE HERALD" LAST

NIGHT THAT HARARE COULD REACT BY SUMMONING THE U.S.

AMBASSADOR TO ZIMBABWE JOSEPH SULLIVAN AS THERE WAS NO

INDISCRIMINATE BEATING OF PEOPLE IN THE COUNTRY. THE

DIPLOMATIC SOURCES SAID IT WAS UNFORTUNATE THAT THE

AMERICANS HAD TAKEN AS AUTHENTIC, FALSE AND ALARMIST

REPORTS BY FOREIGN MEDIA CORRESPONDENTS BASED IN HARARE. .A
