1. (C) SUMMARY: IN MAY 9 CONSULTATIONS IN ROME, NATIONAL

INTELLIGENCE OFFICER (NIO) FOR AFRICA AMB. ROBERT HOUDEK

FOUND A BROAD IDENTITY OF VIEWS WITH ITALIAN MFA DIRECTOR

GENERAL FOR SUB-SAHARAN AFRICA AMB. BRUNO CABRAS AND EAST

AFRICA OFFICE DIRECTOR PAOLA IMPERIALE. CABRAS AGREED WITH

HOUDEK THAT ETHIOPIA WOULD LIKELY ACCEPT THE BORDER

DEMARCATION IN THE END, BUT EMPHASIZED THE NEED FOR THE

GREATEST POSSIBLE COORDINATION BETWEEN THE US AND EU TO

ENSURE THIS HAPPENED. THEY AGREED IN THEIR ANALYSIS OF THE

ISSUES THAT PRES. ISAIAS WOULD FACE ONCE THE BORDER ISSUE WAS

FINALLY RESOLVED. AMB. HOUDEK SAID THAT THE USG PAYS CLOSE

ATTENTION TO SOMALIA AS A TRANSIT POINT FOR TERRORISTS, BUT

ARGUED THAT AL-QAEDA IS MORE INTERESTED IN KENYA, WHICH

PROVIDES MORE OF THE MODERN INFRASTRUCTURE USEFUL FOR ITS

OPERATIONS. CABRAS ARGUED FOR MORE FUNDING OF PUBLIC

SERVICES IN SOMALIA, AS A MEANS OF PREVENTING AN ENTIRE

GENERATION FROM TURNING TO ISLAMIC FUNDAMENTALISM. HOUDEK

COUNTERED THAT PROVIDING MORE RESOURCES MIGHT BE

COUNTER-PRODUCTIVE, GIVING THE COMPETING FACTIONS SOMETHING

TO FIGHT OVER RATHER THAN A REASON TO COMPROMISE WITH EACH

OTHER IN FORMING AN INDIGENOUS GOVERNMENT. END SUMMARY

2. (C) EMBASSY ROME DEEPLY APPRECIATES EFFORTS OF NATIONAL

INTELLIGENCE COUNCIL, STATE/AF AND OF COURSE AMB. ROBERT

HOUDEK TO ARRANGE HIS STOP IN ROME. HIS CONSULTATIONS WITH

THE MFA\'S EAST AFRICA EXPERTS (DESCRIBED IN THIS CABLE)

RESPONDED EFFECTIVELY TO A LONG-STANDING REQUEST OF THE MFA

FOR AN EXCHANGE OF VIEWS ON LONG-TERM ISSUES. HIS EXCHANGES

WITH OTHER MFA AFRICA EXPERTS, AND WITH OTHER GOI SENIOR

ANALYSTS, WERE NOT ONLY FASCINATING FOR US TO LISTEN TO, THEY

ALSO DEMONSTRATED OUR INTENT TO KEEP UP CLOSE CONSULTATIONS

ERITREA/ETHIOPIA BORDER DISPUTE

3. (C) CABRAS BEGAN BY ASKING AMBASSADOR HOUDEK WHETHER HE

THOUGHT THE THREAT TO ETHIOPIAN PM MELES\' LEADERSHIP FROM

ACCEPTING THE EEBC\'S VERDICT WAS REAL OR EXAGGERATED. HOUDEK

RESPONDED THAT MELES IS SMART AND EXAGGERATES THE THREAT TO

HIM IN ORDER TO FIND OUT IF THE US AND EUROPE WILL FIND A WAY

TO HELP HIM ON BADME RATHER THAN RISK "LOSING" HIM. HE HAS

THUS FAR MANIPULATED THE BORDER ISSUE TO WITHSTAND CHALLENGES

TO HIS LEADERSHIP FROM HIS TIGRAYAN BASE. HIS DELAYING

TACTICS HAVE DEMONSTRATED DOMESTICALLY THAT HE DID ALL HE

COULD TO REVERSE THE DECISION. IN HIS GUT, HE KNOWS HE WILL

ULTIMATELY HAVE TO RESPECT THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY\'S

INSISTENCE THAT ETHIOPIA ACCEPT THE VERDICT. HOUDEK ALSO

NOTED THAT MELES KNOWS THAT RENEWING THE WAR WOULD BE

SUICIDAL TO ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT. HOUDEK ASSESSED THAT THE

WEST CAN BE SYMPATHETIC TO MELES\' DIFFICULT SITUATION, BUT

MUST BE FIRM AND UNITED IN INSISTING ON IMPLEMENTATION OF THE

BORDER DEMARCATION.

4. (C) CABRAS AGREED WITH HOUDEK\'S ANALYSIS OF MELES\'

MOTIVATIONS AND CONSTRAINTS. ETHIOPIA IS PLAYING ON THE

TENDENCY OF WESTERN COUNTRIES TO LOOK FOR A MORE AGREEABLE

SOLUTION TO ACCOMMODATE BOTH PARTIES. HOWEVER, SUCH ACTION

WOULD NOT ONLY UNDERMINE INTERNATIONAL LAW, BUT ALSO SET A

NEGATIVE PRECEDENT FOR FUTURE BORDER DISPUTE NEGOTIATIONS.

THE EU HAD ALREADY REMINDED BOTH LEADERS OF THEIR AGREEMENT

TO BE BOUND BY THE ARBITRATION; IT WAS IMPORTANT THAT THE EU

ONCE MELES HAS ACCEPTED AND IMPLEMENTED THE VERDICT, THE U.S.

AND THE EU MIGHT EASE THE EFFECTS OF THE DECISION FOR

ETHIOPIA (E.G., WITH FUNDING OF RESETTLEMENT OF FAMILIES AND

5. (C) CABRAS ASKED HOW THE RESOLUTION OF THE BORDER DISPUTE

WOULD AFFECT ISAIAS\' DOMESTIC POSITION. HOUDEK RESPONDED

THAT IT WOULD THROW ISAIAS BACK INTO THE "BOILING POT OF

DOMESTIC POLITICS;" TAKING AWAY THE BORDER DISPUTE FORCES

ISAIAS TO ADDRESS DOMESTIC PROBLEMS SUCH AS POLITICAL AND

ECONOMIC REFORM. HOUDEK NOTED THAT ISAIAS HAS PROFOUND

PROBLEMS WITH THE DIASPORA, A REMARKABLY SUCCESSFUL FOREIGN

COMMUNITY IN THE U.S. ALTHOUGH THE DIASPORA IS RESTRAINED IN

SPEAKING OUT PUBLICLY AGAINST THE ERITREAN GOVERNMENT (DUE TO

THEIR FINANCIAL INTERESTS IN ASMARA), ISAIAS IS SLOWLY LOSING

SUPPORT AMONG HIS OVERSEAS BRETHREN. CABRAS NOTED THAT ISAIAS

IS AN EXTREMELY SOLITARY PERSON AND EVEN HIS FEW CLOSEST

ASSOCIATES FOSTER AN INCREASING PARANOIA ABOUT THE WESTERN

WORLD AND A CONSPIRACY AGAINST HIM. HOUDEK REMARKED THAT

SINCE ASSUMING POWER ISAIAS HAS WANTED A CLOSER SECURITY

RELATIONSHIP WITH THE US AS A MEANS OF DETERRENCE. ACCORDING

TO CABRAS, ISAIAS HAS BEEN UNABLE TO FOSTER SUCH A

RELATIONSHIP BECAUSE OF HIS REFUSAL TO TALK WITH WESTERN

OFFICIALS OVER A VARIETY OF INCIDENTS, SUCH AS HIS DECISION

SOMALIA

6. (C) ASKED FOR AN ASSESSMENT OF TERRORIST ACTIVITY

EMANATING FROM SOMALIA, HOUDEK NOTED THE U.S. VIEWS SOMALIA

AS AN IMPORTANT TRANSIT POINT AND HIDING PLACE FOR

TERRORISTS. HOWEVER, SOMALIA DOES NOT PROVIDE A HOSPITABLE

ENVIRONMENT FOR A SUBSTANTIAL TERRORIST INFRASTRUCTURE

BECAUSE THERE IS NO FRIENDLY GOVERNMENT TO PROTECT THE

TERRORISTS. KENYA, ON THE OTHER HAND, IS A SOPHISTICATED

COUNTRY OFFERING EFFECTIVE PUBLIC INFRASTRUCTURE (I.E.

BANKING, TELECOM, TRANSPORTATION) ENABLING TERRORISTS TO

FUNCTION MORE EFFECTIVELY. ITS SECURITY SERVICES, WHILE

WILLING, ARE NOT EFFECTIVE ENOUGH TO PREVENT TERRORISTS FROM

USING KENYA, MORE THAN SOMALIA, AS AN OPERATIONAL BASE.

7. (C) ON SOMALIA RECONCILIATION EFFORTS, CABRAS NOTED THAT

BOTH OUR CARROTS AND OUR STICKS ARE TOO SMALL TO MAKE THE

FACTIONS SEE THE VALUE OF AN AGREEMENT. MEANWHILE, AN ENTIRE

GENERATION IS GETTING ITS SOCIAL SERVICES, MOST IMPORTANTLY

EDUCATION, FROM FUNDAMENTALIST ISLAMIC NETWORKS. PROVISION

OF EDUCATIONAL AND OTHER PUBLIC SERVICES IS IN THE WESTERN

INTEREST, BUT ITALY AND THE EU ARE STRETCHED TOO THIN TO MAKE

A MAJOR NEW EFFORT. HOUDEK QUESTIONED CABRAS\' ASSERTION THAT

RESOURCES AND PUBLIC SERVICES WOULD TRANSFORM SOMALIA. THE

REAL PROBLEM IS THAT SOMALIS VIEW LIFE AS A ZERO SUM GAME

AND, THUS, RELISH THE OPPORTUNITY TO TAKE ADVANTAGE OF

FOREIGNERS FOR THEIR OWN GAIN. THE SOMALIS WOULD BE OVERJOYED

TO SEE COMPETITION BETWEEN THE WEST AND THE MUSLIM WORLD, A

LA THE COLD WAR WHERE THE USG AND USSR COURTED THEM WITH

RESOURCES. HOUDEK SUGGESTED THAT A POLICY OF BENIGN NEGLECT

MIGHT DO MORE TO FORCE THE SOMALIS TO TAKE THE LEAD IN
