SUBJECT: THE NEXT PRIME MINISTER WILL BE . . .

1. (U) CLASSIFIED BY SCOTT BELLARD, POL/C; REASON 1.5(D)

CONFIDENTIAL

2. (SBU) SUMMARY: DEPUTY PRIME MINISTER NGUYEN TAN DUNG

IS EXPECTED BY MANY OBSERVERS TO BECOME PRIME MINISTER

AFTER THE MAY NATIONAL ASSEMBLY ELECTIONS. TRAN DUOC LUONG

WILL LIKELY REMAIN PRESIDENT. TRADE MINISTER VU KHOAN IS

WIDELY PREDICTED TO BECOME A DEPUTY PRIME MINISTER.

NATIONAL ASSEMBLY CHAIRMAN NGUYEN VAN AN, WHO ALSO CHAIRS

THE COMMUNIST PARTY OF VIETNAM (CPV) ORGANIZATION

COMMISSION, IS SEEN AS THE MOST INFLUENTIAL LEADER ON MANY

ISSUES, EVEN ECLIPSING PARTY GENERAL SECRETARY NONG DUC

MANH. END SUMMARY.

3. (SBU) SENIOR COLONEL TRAN NHUNG, INTERNATIONAL EDITOR

OF PEOPLE\'S ARMY NEWSPAPER, OUTLINED WHAT HE DEPICTED AS

PERSONAL VIEWS ON UPCOMING CHANGES IN VIETNAM\'S LEADERSHIP

DURING AN APRIL 5 MEETING WITH POLOFF. COLONEL NHUNG

PREDICTED THAT INCUMBENT PM PHAN VAN KHAI WILL LEAVE OFFICE

"SOON," BOTH BECAUSE KHAI WANTS TO RETIRE, AND BECAUSE "THE

PEOPLE" WOULD OPPOSE AN EXTENSION OF HIS TERM. (NOTE:

THERE ARE NUMEROUS RUMORS ABOUT UNSAVORY BUSINESS DEALINGS

BY HIS FAMILY MEMBERS, ESPECIALLY HIS SON. END NOTE.)

COLONEL NHUNG EMPHASIZED THAT KHAI WAS SUPPOSED TO RETIRE A

YEAR AGO, AFTER THE NINTH PARTY CONGRESS.

PRIME MINISTER AND DEPUTY PRIME MINISTER

----------------------------------------

4. (C) COLONEL NHUNG REVIEWED THREE PROBABLE CANDIDATES

FOR PRIME MINISTER -- DEPUTY PRIME MINISTER NGUYEN TAN

DUNG, HO CHI MINH CITY PARTY CHIEF NGUYEN MINH TRIET, AND

TRADE MINISTER VU KHOAN. DUNG, A PROTEGE OF FORMER PM VO

VAN KIET, WILL LIKELY BE THE NEXT PM, COLONEL NHUNG

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HANOI 00899 01 OF 02 120749Z

PREDICTED. HE REMARKED THAT DUNG AND TRIET WERE ALMOST

IDENTICAL IN POLICY TERMS, BUT THAT TRIET WANTED TO STAY

WHERE HE IS, AT VIETNAM\'S ECONOMIC CENTER. YOUNGER, MORE

OUTWARD-ORIENTED PARTY MEMBERS SUPPORT KHOAN, BUT KHOAN

DOES NOT YET HAVE ENOUGH SUPPORT AT SENIOR LEVELS TO BECOME

PM. KHOAN IS ALSO THE ONLY ONE OF THE THREE NOT ON THE

POLITBURO. FURTHERMORE, AS A NORTHERNER, KHOAN WOULD UPSET

THE REGIONAL BALANCE AMONG THE SRV\'S TOP THREE OFFICES.

THE PM MUST BE A SOUTHERNER, COLONEL NHUNG CLAIMED, SINCE

NEITHER GENERAL SECRETARY NONG DUC MANH NOR PRESIDENT TRAN

DUC LUONG IS APT TO LEAVE OFFICE. INSTEAD, COLONEL NHUNG

EXPRESSED THE EXPECTATION THAT VU KHOAN WILL MOVE UP TO ONE

OF THE FOUR DEPUTY PRIME MINISTER SLOTS, AS HAS BEEN WIDELY

RUMORED. (NOTE: MOST LIKELY, HE WILL TAKE OVER THE

PORTFOLIO NOW HELD BY NGUYEN MANH CAM. END NOTE.) COLONEL

NHUNG REFUSED TO SPECULATE ABOUT THE OTHER THREE DPM

POSITIONS. HE NOTED THAT THE MILITARY AND SECURITY

APPARATUS IS IN A "WEAK POSITION," WITH ITS LEADERS FACING

REPLACEMENT AND EXTERNAL THREATS ON THE WANE. THEREFORE,

THEY HAVE "LITTLE TO SAY" OVER GOVERNMENT LEADERSHIP, HE

CLAIMED.

PRESIDENT

PRESIDENCY. TRAN DUC LUONG IS NOT HIMSELF INFLUENTIAL AND

"NO ONE WANTS HIS JOB." WITH ONLY THE JUDICIARY AND THE

PROCURACY UNDER HIS PURVIEW, LUONG IS NOT NEARLY AS

POWERFUL AS HIS PREDECESSOR LE DUC ANH, WHO HELD STRONG

PARTY POSITIONS AND HAD SWAY OVER THE MFA, THE SECURITY

8. (C) THE CPV CHOSE NONG DUC MANH AS A TRANSITIONAL

LEADER, COLONEL NHUNG ANALYZED, BECAUSE RIVAL FACTIONS

COULD NOT AGREE BETWEEN TRAN DUC LUONG AND NGUYEN VAN AN.

(NOTE: WE DO NOT BELIEVE LUONG WAS TRULY IN THE RUNNING.

THE REAL RACE WAS PROBABLY AMONG THEN-GENERAL SECRETARY LE

KHA PHIEU, MANH, AND AN. END NOTE.) COLONEL NHUNG

CONTRASTED MANH WITH AN, CLAIMING THAT MANH COULD NOT MAKE

STRATEGIC DECISIONS WITHOUT CONSULTING OTHERS. WHILE

MANH\'S CONSENSUS-BUILDING SKILLS WERE VALUABLE AS NA

CHAIRMAN, SUCH AN APPROACH IS "INEFFECTIVE" AS GENERAL

SECRETARY. COLONEL NHUNG CRITICIZED THAT MANH CANNOT TAKE

SIPDIS

THE LEAD, ENFORCE ORDER, OR IMPOSE HIS DECISIONS; HE HAS

NO SUPPORT TO DO SO. COLONEL NHUNG EXPRESSED DOUBT THAT

MANH WOULD DEVELOP MUCH OR BECOME SUBSTANTIALLY MORE

EFFECTIVE AS PARTY LEADER. HE ADDED THAT THE RECENT CPV

MOVEMENT TOWARDS ALLOWING MEMBERS TO CONTINUE PRIVATE

BUSINESSES WAS NOT MANH\'S INITIATIVE DESPITE MANH\'S PUBLIC

CALL THAT THE PARTY CONSIDER CHANGING THE POLICY. THE

PROPOSAL ORIGINATED SEVERAL YEARS AGO, BUT WAS OPPOSED,

COLONEL NHUNG SAID, BY THEN-ADVISORS LE DUC ANH AND DO

MUOI.

9. (C) COLONEL NHUNG CHARACTERIZED THE MOVE TO ALLOW

PARTY MEMBERS TO CONTINUE IN BUSINESS AS REPRESENTATIVE OF

THE GROWING INFLUENCE OF YOUNGER -- MORE PRAGMATIC AND

EDUCATED -- MEMBERS WHO ARE GRADUALLY WINNING OVER THEIR

ELDERS. HE SAID ANOTHER SIGN OF THIS GENERATIONAL CHANGE

IS THE TREND FOR THE CPV TO STEP BACK TO MORE GENERAL

POLICY GUIDANCE AND ALLOW LAW TO DICTATE SPECIFIC

DECISIONS. THE PUBLIC, ACTING THROUGH THE NA, EXPECTS LAWS

TO SUPPLANT DETAILED PARTY GUIDELINES AND INSTRUCTIONS,

COLONEL NHUNG CLAIMED.

10. (C) COMMENT: WE OFFER COLONEL NHUNG\'S INDIVIDUAL

VIEWS BECAUSE OF THEIR APPARENTLY FRANK NATURE AND HIS

ACCESS TO LEADERSHIP CIRCLES GIVEN HIS POSITION. MUCH CAN

CHANGE BETWEEN NOW AND THIS SUMMER, ESPECIALLY GIVEN THE

STAKES INVOLVED IN LEADERSHIP DYNAMICS. COLONEL NHUNG\'S

CANDOR WAS STRIKING, BUT NOT UNPRECEDENTED, PARTICULARLY IN

ONE-ON-ONE DISCUSSIONS. HE ADMITTED THAT HE WAS NOT

SUPPOSED TO MEET FOREIGNERS ALONE, BUT THAT HE WAS NOT

WORRIED ABOUT THE CONSEQUENCES BECAUSE HE IS NEARING

RETIREMENT.
