SUBJECT: TURKS EXPRESS APPRECIATION FOR COOPERATION

AFFORDED TO TU LIAISON TEAMS IN MOSUL, TEL AFAR; CONCERNS

ABOUT LNOS IN KIRKUK

(U) Baghdad minimize considered.

(U) Classified by Ambassador W. Robert Pearson. Reasons:

1.5 (B and D).

(C) MFA U/S Ziyal and Ambassador briefly discussed the

functioning of the Turkish liaison officers in Iraq over

lunch June 10. Ziyal opened by saying that Turkish LNOs were

a source of confidence for Turkey. They relay information,

keep GOT officials informed about the real situation on the

ground and help alleviate speculation and unnecessary

worries. Ziyal stressed the GOT was appreciative of the

cooperation extended to the TU LNOs by the US forces, in

particular those under the command of 101st Commanding

General MG Petreaus. Ziyal noted, however, that TGS had

reported that the TU LNO teams in Kirkuk were experiencing

some problems. Specifically, Ziyal said the TU LNOs

reportedly had been denied permission to leave their base,

asked to change offices without justification, denied an

internet connection and restricted from entering certain

areas of the base. Ziyal expressed hope the situation

involving the TU LNOs in Kirkuk would improve in the future.

PEARSON
