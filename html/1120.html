SUBJECT: AMBASSADOR DISCUSSES STATE-SPONSORED

VIOLENCE AND INTIMIDATION WITH A PROGRESSIVE MINISTER

CLASSIFIED BY ECON/COMM OFFICER MARK PROKOP, REASONS

1. (C) SUMMARY: IN A SERIES OF CALLS BEING MADE ON

SENIOR GOVERNMENT OFFICIALS, ON JUNE 8 AMBASSADOR AND

ECONOFF MET WITH SIMON K. MOYO, MINISTER OF MINES,

ENVIRONMENT AND TOURISM, TO DISCUSS CONDITIONS IN

ZIMBABWE RELATED TO THE ELECTIONS, AND OUR ABHORRENCE

OF THE STATE-SPONSORED VIOLENCE AND INTIMIDATION THAT

HAS OCCURRED TO DATE. MOYO, CONSIDERED BY MANY TO BE

A ZANU-PF PROGRESSIVE, WAS GLIB, DISPLAYED AN

ADMIRABLE BOB-AND-WEAVE TECHNIQUE, AND PARROTED THE

PARTY LINE. HIS EXPRESSED A PERSONAL DISDAIN OF

VIOLENCE, LABELING IT GOOD FOR NO ONE, BUT CLAIMED

THAT BOTH PARTIES WERE EQUALLY TO BLAME FOR ITS

OCCURRENCE. HE ALSO CRITICIZED THE NATIONAL

DEMOCRATIC INSTITUTE (NDI) REPORT ON UNSATISFACTORY

ELECTION CONDITIONS, POSTULATING THAT A NINE-DAY TRIP

WITH VISITS TO ONLY THREE LOCATIONS WAS INSUFFICIENT

TO ACCURATELY ASSESS TRUE CONDITIONS ON THE GROUND.

END SUMMARY.

2. (C) THE AMBASSADOR KICKED OFF THE MEETING BY

ASKING IF A FRONT-PAGE HEADLINE IN THE INDEPENDENT

NEWSPAPER "THE DAILY NEWS", PROCLAIMING THAT "TOP

ZANU-PF LEADERS CALL FOR END TO VIOLENCE" WAS

CORRECT. DODGING THE DIRECT QUESTION, MINISTER MOYO

LAMENTED THE EXISTENCE OF VIOLENCE AND ATTRIBUTED IT

TO THE EXISTENCE, FOR THE FIRST TIME SINCE

INDEPENDENCE, OF AN ACTUAL, VIABLE, AND ORGANIZED

OPPOSITION, FIELDING 120 CANDIDATES. "IT HAS RAISED

THE TEMPERATURE", HE STATED, "ESPECIALLY SINCE BOTH

CONFIDENTIAL

SIDES VIEW THEMSELVES AS THE WINNERS." BOTH PARTIES

HAVE CAUSED THE VIOLENCE HE CONTINUED, AND THE

LEADERS OF BOTH NEED TO CONTROL THEIR FOLLOWERS. WE

ARE DOING THAT NOW, HE ADMITTED, TELLING US THAT THE

JUNE 7 POLITBURO MEETING PARTICIPANTS DECIDED THAT

THE RULING PARTY WOULD PUBLICLY BEGIN CALLING FOR AN

END TO THE VIOLENCE. THE POLICE NEED TO DO THEIR

JOB, HE ADDED, AND THAT SHOULD START NOW HE TOLD US,

INFERRING THAT THEY HAD BEEN GIVEN THE GREEN LIGHT BY

THE OFFICIAL DECISION-MAKING ORGAN.

3. (C) THE AMBASSADOR ACKNOWLEDGED THE MINISTER\'S

HIGH-MINDED SENTIMENTS, BUT POINTED OUT THAT THE

RULING PARTY HAS BEEN THE CLEAR AND EXCESSIVE

WRONG DOER TO DATE. AFTER AGAIN SINGING A PSALM OF

THE MERITS OF PEACE, THE WAR VETERAN AND CABINET

MEMBER REITERATED THE RULING PARTY\'S WISE, NEW,

PEACE-FOCUSED PUBLIC RELATIONS CAMPAIGN, AND AGAIN

BLAMED THE OPPOSITION FOR CAUSING VIOLENCE. HE

PROUDLY CITED THE EXAMPLE OF THE AFTER-BUSINESS-HOURS

POSTERING OF ZANU-PF HEADQUARTERS IN CHINHOYI WITH

MDC (MOVEMENT FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE) POSTERS BY

UNKNOWN PERPETRATORS. "IMAGINE THE VIOLENCE THAT

SUCH AN ACT WOULD HAVE CAUSED", HE PLEADED, "IF

ANYONE HAD BEEN IN THE BUILDING." WHEN THE

AMBASSADOR RAISED THE ALLEGATIONS OF TORTURE METED

OUT WITHIN HITLER HUNZVI\'S MEDICAL SURGERY, THE

MINISTER COULD ONLY REPEAT A MANTRA OF "UNPALATABLE,

UNPALATABLE...."

4. (C) WHEN THE AMBASSADOR RAISED THE BEATINGS AND

INTIMIDATION OF EDUCATORS (CITING RAPES,

HOUSEBURNINGS AND NEAR-FATAL BEATINGS), THE MINISTER

INFORMED US THAT TEACHERS WHO DIGRESS FROM THE

CURRICULUM TO PREACH THE MERITS OF THE OPPOSITION

MUST BE DISCIPLINED; THOUGH HE SUBSEQUENTLY ADMITTED

THAT AN ADMINISTRATIVE MEANS EXISTED FOR THIS. WHEN

ECONOFF POINTED OUT THAT FOR YEARS THE RULING PARTY

HAS DIRECTED AND ENCOURAGED TEACHERS TO DIGRESS FROM

THE CURRICULUM AND PREACH THE MERITS OF ZANU-PF; MOYO

RESPONDED THAT THIS WAS ONLY THE INCULCATION OF

"HISTORY", WHICH NONE COULD FIND FAULT WITH. "THE

STORY OF THE LIBERATION STRUGGLE AND THE GLORIOUS

ROLE OF ZANU-PF IN ITS SUCCESS" HE CROONED, "IS OUR

NATIONAL HERITAGE AND CAN NEVER BE QUESTIONED OR

DENIED US." "I AM A VETERAN AND IT IS OUR RIGHT", HE

SUMMARILY DECLARED, AS HE HASTENED OFF TO A MEETING

WITH A DELEGATION OF SOUTH AFRICAN ELECTION

OBSERVERS.
