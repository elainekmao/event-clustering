SUBJECT: GOC OFFERS MORE DETAIL ON SECTOR WEST KIA,

GRAVES, DETAINEES

REF: ZAGREB 2007

1. AN OFFICIAL OF THE UN CENTRE FOR HUMAN RIGHTS IN

ZAGREB (PROTECT) SAYS THAT CENTER DIRECTOR MAZOWIECKI,

IN THE WAKE OF HIS CHARGES THAT THERE WERE "MASS

GRAVES" IN PARTS OF SECTOR WEST, HAD TALKS WITH DEPUTY

PRIME MINISTER KOSTOVIC TO TRY TO RESOLVE SOME OF THE

REMAINING DISCREPANCIES AND HOLES IN INFORMATION

PROVIDED BY THE CROATIAN GOVERNMENT (REFTEL).

2. KOSTOVIC REPORTEDLY GAVE THE UNCHR THE FOLLOWING

DATA ON DETAINEES: 205 PERSONS REMAIN IN DETENTION

FROM THE SECTOR WEST OPERATION; 186 OF THESE ARE

SUBJECT TO POSSIBLE WAR CRIMES CHARGES AND THEY ARE

DISTRIBUTED AMONG FOUR DETENTION CENTERS, IN BJELOVAR,

POZEGA, OSIJEK, AND SPLIT. AN ADDITIONAL 19 PERSONS

ARE DESCRIBED AS "FOREIGN NATIONALS" BEING CONSIDERED

AS POWS, AND THEY ARE DETAINED IN SPLIT.

3. KOSTOVIC SAID THE GOC HAD TURNED OVER A COMPLETE

LIST OF THESE INDIVIDUALS TO THE CROATIAN RED CROSS.

MAZOWIECKI OBSERVED THAT THE CROATIAN RED CROSS MIGHT

NOT BE VIEWED BY SERBS AS THE MOST NEUTRAL

ORGANIZATION. KOSTOVIC AGREED AND PROMISED TO PROVIDE

THE DATA ALSO TO ICRC. THE ICRC HAS CONFIRMED TO THE

UNCHR THAT IS HAS BOTH A LIST AND/AND ACCESS TO THE

DETAINED PERSONS.

4. REGARDING THE CHARGES OF MASS GRAVES, (FYI, WE NOW

UNDERSTAND MAZOWIECKI\'S PEOPLE OBSERVED, NOT "MASS

GRAVES" AS SUCH BUT ROWS OF NEW GRAVEMOUNDS), KOSTOVIC

PROVIDED THE UNCHR A LIST OF THE BURIAL SITES WHERE A

TOTAL OF 188 FATALITIES FROM THE SECTOR WEST OPERATION

HAD BEEN BURIED. 156 OF THESE HAD BEEN IDENTIFIED; 20

WERE DEFINITELY CIVILIANS, MOST EVIDENTLY KILLED IN THE

CROSSFIRE OF A SKIRMISH NEAR NOVI VAROS. AN ADDITIONAL

34 WERE "POSSIBLE" CIVILIANS -- THOUGHT TO BE CIVILIANS

ALTHOUGH MANY WERE DRESSED IN UNIFORMS OR PARTIAL

UNIFORMS. KOSTOVIC REPEATED THE GOC\'S OFFER TO MAKE

INFORMATION ON THE IDENTITIES OF THE CIVILIAN DEAD

AVAILABLE TO INDIVIDUAL RELATIVES WHO MIGHT INQUIRE.

HE SPECIFICALLY REJECTED THE IDEA OF PROVIDING THE

NAMES OF THE MILITARY DEAD ON THE GROUNDS THAT THE

"RSK" HAD NEVER PROVIDED SIMILAR INFORMATION AFTER THE

FIGHTING IN EASTERN SLAVONIA (VUKOVAR).

6. ASKED IF UN OR OTHER AUTHORITIES INTENDED TO PURSUE

THE ISSUE OF MASS GRAVES OR OTHER ALLEGED VIOLATIONS

FURTHER, OUR SOURCE THOUGHT THIS RELATIVELY UNLIKELY.

REGARDING THE GRAVES, FM GRANIC HAD PROMISED WAR CRIMES

TRIBUNAL CHIEF PROSECUTOR GOLDSTONE DURING THE LATTER\'S

VISIT HERE THAT CROATIA WOULD COOPERATE WITH ANY

INVESTIGATION. IT SEEMED POSSIBLE, HOWEVER, THAT THE

TRIBUNAL WOULD FIND INSUFFICIENT CAUSE TO PURSUE THE

MATTER FURTHER OR TO SEEK EXHUMATION. OUR CONTACT

BELIEVED MAZOWIECKI WAS RELATIVELY SATISFIED WITH THE

EXPLANATIONS GIVEN BY THE GOC. REGARDING THE OVERBLOWN

ESTIMATE OF KIAS GIVEN BY DEFMIN SUSAK, HE FELT THERE

WERE ENOUGH CREDIBLE SOURCES SUGGESTING THAT SUSAK HAD,

IN EFFECT, BEEN SPEAKING WITHOUT BASIS. THE UN IN THE

BANJA LUKA AREA HAD REPORTED "SEVERAL HUNDRED" WOUNDED

AMONG THOSE WHO FLED FROM CROATIAN TO BOSNIAN

7. COMMENT: ALTHOUGH THE GOC HAS NOT BEEN PROACTIVE
