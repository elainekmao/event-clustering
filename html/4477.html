SUBJECT: FOREIGN PRESS CENTER\'S BUSINESS AND ECONOMIC

REPORTING TOUR - PROGRAM EVALUATION

1. SUMMARY: MS. LUBNA ABDUL HAQ, AN ECONOMIC REPORTER AT

"AL-ARAB AL-YAWM" ARABIC DAILY NEWSPAPER, AND MS. HALA AL

HADIDI, AN ECONOMIC REPORTER AT JORDAN NEWS AGENCY (PETRA)

WERE PARTICIPANTS IN THE FOREIGN PRESS CENTER\'S BUSINESS AND

ECONOMIC REPORTING TOUR, SEPTEMBER 15-28, 2002 (NON-IV

ALLOCATION). PAS STAFF MET WITH BOTH MS. ABDUL HAQ AND MS.

AL HADIDI TO GET THEIR FEED BACK ON THE TOUR. THEY BOTH

COMMENTED THAT IT WAS AN EXCELLENT, IF INTENSE, PROGRAM,

WHICH HELPED THEM GET A GOOD INSIGHT ON ECONOMIC REPORTING

IN THE U.S. THEY WERE PLEASED THAT THE TOUR PROVIDED THEM

WITH A VALUABLE OPPORTUNITY TO MEET WITH U.S. GOVERNMENT AND

MEDIA OFFICIALS TO EXCHANGE VIEWS AND TO UNDERSTAND MORE

ABOUT U.S. ECONOMIC POLICY AND JOURNALISM IN THE U.S. THEY

VALUED, IN PARTICULAR, THEIR INTERACTION WITH THE ARAB AND

MUSLIM COMMUNITY IN CHICAGO AND NEW YORK CITY. BOTH MS.

ABDUL HAQ AND MS. AL HADIDI WISHED THAT THE TOUR WAS THREE

WEEKS INSTEAD OF TWO WEEKS LONG TO HAVE ENOUGH TIME TO GRASP

EVERYTHING THEY LEARNED AND TO PROVIDE THEIR OWN FEED BACK

ON EACH LEG OF THE TOUR. END SUMMARY.

2. DESCRIPTION OF ACTIVITIES: THE FOREIGN PRESS CENTER\'S

BUSINESS AND ECONOMIC REPORTING TOUR TOOK PLACE SEPTEMBER 15-

28, 2002. THE ITINERARY INCLUDED WASHINGTON D.C.; CHICAGO

AND NEW YORK.

3. DATES/FISCAL YEAR/QUARTER: SEPTEMBER 15-28, 2002; FY

02; FOURTH QUARTER.

4. JUSTIFICATION AND OBJECTIVES:

POST NOMINATED TWO YOUNG REPORTERS WHO ARE AVID REPORTERS ON

JORDANIAN-AMERICAN ECONOMIC AND TRADE RELATIONS, INCLUDING

BILATERAL AGREEMENT SIGNINGS OF U.S. ASSISTANCE TO JORDAN.

HAVING HAD NO PRIOR EXPOSURE TO THE U.S. SOCIETY AND

POLITICAL SYSTEM, THEIR PARTICIPATION IN THIS THEMATIC TOUR

WAS ESSENTIAL TO U.S. MISSION OBJECTIVES.

5. MPP UMBRELLA THEME; THE PROGRAM SUPPORTED MPP OBJECTIVE

ON "DEMOCRACY AND FREE FLOW OF INFORMATION."

6. RESULTS AND IMPACT: DESPITE BEING QUITE INTENSIVE, MS.

ABDUL HAQ AND MS. AL HADIDI FOUND THE TOUR EXCELLENT AND

VERY USEFUL AS IT PROVIDED THEM WITH WORTHWHILE INSIGHT ON

U.S. ECONOMIC POLICY AND ECONOMIC/BUSINESS REPORTING. BOTH

REPORTERS WERE VERY PLEASED WITH ALL THE MEETINGS SCHEDULED

IN THEIR TOUR. THEY APPRECIATED THE GOOD EFFORTS EXERTED IN

SETTING UP MEETINGS, HOPING THAT FUTURE PARTICIPANTS WILL

HAVE MORE TIME BETWEEN EACH MEETING OR EVENT TO ORGANIZE

THEIR THOUGHTS AND TO REFLECT ON THE OUTCOME. BOTH REPORTERS

HIGHLY VALUED THEIR VISITS TO VARIOUS NEWSPAPERS AND TV

STATIONS. SOMETIMES THEY FELT THAT CERTAIN U.S. OFFICIALS

AND NEWSPAPER EDITORS LACKED PREPAREDNESS IN RESPONDING TO

PARTICIPANTS\' QUESTIONS, ESPECIALLY WHEN THE ANSWER INVOLVED

NUMBERS AND FIGURES ON U.S. ECONOMIC ASSISTANCE TO THE

MIDDLE EAST.

THEY ALSO LEARNED A GREAT DEAL FROM INTERACTING WITH THE

ARAB AND MUSLIM COMMUNITY IN CHICAGO AND NEW YORK CITY, AND

WERE VERY IMPRESSED WITH THE SUCCESS OF SO MANY BUSINESSES

RUN BY ARAB AMERICANS IN THE UNITED STATES. HAVING HAD NO

PRIOR EXPOSURE TO U.S. SOCIETY, BOTH REPORTERS WERE ALSO

IMPRESSED WITH THE FREEDOM OF EXPRESSION THE AMERICAN PEOPLE

ENJOY AND THE SIGNS OF PATRIOTISM THEY DISPLAY EVERYWHERE IN

THE WAKE OF 9/11.

MS. ABDUL HAQ AND MS. AL HADIDI WOULD HAVE LIKED TO MEET

WITH U.S. GROUPS AND FIGURES THAT OPPOSE U.S. POLICY IN THE

MIDDLE EAST TO GET THE FULL PICTURE ON HOW PEOPLE VIEW THIS

MATTER. THEY ALSO QUESTIONED THE SELECTION OF SEVERAL OF THE

PARTICIPANTS, NOTING THAT TWO OF THEM WERE NOT ECONOMIC AND

BUSINESS REPORTERS, AND DID NOT APPEAR TO RELATE TO THE

CONTENTS OF THE TOUR.

BOTH REPORTERS WERE NOT TOO HAPPY BY WHAT THEY VIEWED AS

STRICT SECURITY MEASURES, PARTICULARLY THE PHYSICAL

INSPECTIONS THEY HAD TO GO THROUGH AT U.S. AIRPORTS.

HOWEVER, THEY WERE WELL AWARE THAT THOSE MEASURES ARE VERY

IMPORTANT TO ENSURE THE SAFETY AND SECURITY OF CITIZENS AND

VISITORS ALIKE.
