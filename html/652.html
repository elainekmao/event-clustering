GENERAL ASSESSMENT

------------------

2. CROATIA CONTINUES BY AND LARGE TO IMPLEMENT

ITS CSCE OBLIGATIONS, THOUGH THERE HAVE BEEN

SOME INCIDENTS IN VIOLATION OF CSCE NORMS. THE

CONTINUED PREVALENCE OF UNSETTLED CONDITIONS --

WAR, A HOSTILE OCCUPATION AND EFFECTIVE CIVIL

WAR, AND MAJOR REFUGEE FLOWS -- COMPLICATES THE

GOVERNMENT\'S TASK AND MAKES THE SECURITY BASKET

ONCE AGAIN THE MAIN FOCUS OF DEVELOPMENTS THIS

YEAR.

3. DEVELOPMENTS IN THE SECURITY AREA, INCLUDING

ESPECIALLY THE SIGNING OF A PEACE AGREEMENT WITH

NEIGHBORING BOSNIA-HERZEGOVINA IN THE FIRST

QUARTER OF 1994, REFLECTED THE PURSUIT OF

RESPONSIBLE SECURITY POLICIES. BEFORE THAT

THERE WERE, HOWEVER, SOME CASES OF CROATIAN

MILITARY OPERATIONS THAT VIOLATED CSCE NORMS.

4. ROUGHLY A QUARTER OF CROATIA\'S TERRITORY IS

STILL MILITARILY OCCUPIED BY REBEL ETHNIC SERB

FORCES THAT HAVE STRONG LINKS TO THE GOVERNMENT

OF THE "FRY." THE POLICING OF THE OCCUPIED

AREAS BY THE UN PROTECTION FORCE (UNPROFOR) HAS

NOT SUCCEEDED COMPLETELY IN PREVENTING MILITARY

ATTACKS ON CROATIA FROM WITHIN THOSE AREAS, NOR

THE ABUSE AND HARASSMENT OF ETHNIC MINORITIES.

THE NUMBER OF NEW REFUGEES FLOWING INTO CROATIA

DIMINISHED SOMEWHAT DURING THE YEAR, BUT THERE

HAS BEEN LITTLE CHANGE IN THE OVERALL NUMBERS OF

REFUGEES AND DISPLACED PERSONS IN THE COUNTRY

WHO MUST BE CARED FOR.

5. IN OTHER CSCE AREAS, THERE WERE NO MAJOR

DEVELOPMENTS THIS YEAR. KEY FEATURES OF THE

LANDSCAPE ARE CONTINUED BUT SLOW PROGRESS TOWARD

ECONOMIC PRIVATIZATION, MUCH SLOWED BY THE

UNCERTAIN INVESTMENT CONDITIONS; AND A GENERAL

RESPECT FOR THE PRINCIPLES EMBODIED IN THE HUMAN

DIMENSION, MARRED BY CONTINUING PROBLEMS

CONCERNING THE RIGHTS OF MINORITIES AND PRESS

FREEDOMS, IN PARTICULAR.

SECURITY

--------

6. SECURITY ISSUES DOMINATE ALL OTHER MATTERS IN

CROATIA, DUE TO THE CONTINUING MILITARY

OCCUPATION OF LARGE PARTS OF THE COUNTRY BY

BREAKAWAY ETHNIC SERBS. IN 1993, THE UNSC

RECOGNIZED THESE "UN-PROTECTED AREAS" (UNPAS) AS

COOPERATE IN INTERNATIONAL EFFORTS TO NORMALIZE

THE UNPAS AND RETURN THEM TO CROATIAN GOVERNMENT

CONTROL, BY PEACEFUL MEANS, IN PARTICULAR BY

PROLONGING ITS AGREEMENT TO THE PRESENCE OF UN

AND MURDER OF SERB CIVILIANS, THE CROATIAN

GOVERNMENT INVESTIGATION OF WHICH HAS NOT YET

BEEN COMPLETED. CROATIA HAS NOW ENTERED INTO

SERIOUS NEGOTIATIONS, WHICH WERE UNDER WAY AS

THE REPORTING PERIOD CLOSED, FOR A CEASEFIRE

WITH THE SERBS WHO CONTROL MOST OF THE UNPAS.

ECONOMIC

--------

9. CROATIA CONTINUES TO MOVE, ALBEIT SLOWLY,

AWAY FROM THE SYSTEM OF STATE OWNERSHIP THAT

PREVAILED UNTIL 1991 TOWARD A FREE-MARKET

SYSTEM. PRIVATIZATION CONTINUES, THOUGH MOST

EFFORTS HAVE BEEN ON A SMALL SCALE. PROGRESS

HAS BEEN UNEVEN AND SLOW BECAUSE THE GOVERNMENT

UNCLASSIFIED

HAS BEEN PREOCCUPIED WITH THE WAR AND ITS

EFFECTS, SO THAT MANY NEW RULES AN LAWS REMAIN

TO BE PASSED. FORMER EXPORT MARKETS REMAIN

LARGELY CLOSED OFF AND HAVE NOT BEEN REPLACED,

AND THERE HAS BEEN LITTLE INTERNATIONAL

FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE. AS A RESULT, ECONOMIC

RECOVERY HAS BEEN BLOCKED AND THE AVERAGE

STANDARD OF LIVING REMAINS AT LEAST 50 PERCENT

BELOW ITS PRE-1991 LEVEL. FURTHER, THE

UNCERTAIN SECURITY SITUATION HAS PROBABLY CAUSED

INVESTORS, BOTH DOMESTIC AND FOREIGN, TO

POSTPONE THEIR DECISIONS.

10. A NEW ECONOMIC TEAM INSTALLED BY PRESIDENT

TUDJMAN INTRODUCED IN OCTOBER 1993 MEASURES THAT

LARGELY HALTED THE RAMPANT INFLACTION PREVAILING

LAST YEAR, AND THIS HAS MADE A SIGNIFICANT

CONTRIBUTION TO STABILIZING THE ECONOMIC

SITUATION. CROATIA REMAINS A MEMBER OF THE

WORLD BANK, IMF, AND ETRD AND HAS RECEIVED

TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE FROM THESE INSTITUTIONS TO

HELP STRENGTHEN ITS FREE MARKET BASE. THE

GOVERNMENT HAS OBTAINED U.S. AND EUROPEAN UNION

SUPPORT FOR EVENTUAL CLOSER ASSOCIATION WITH

WESTERN ECONOMIC AND FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS.

11. BECAUSE OF UNSETTLED CONDITIONS AND HIGHER

PRIORITIES, CROATIAN ENVIRONMENTAL PROTECTION

POLICY REMAINS IN ITS INFANCY BUT FOLLOWS IN

PRINCIPLE THE LINES OF WESTERN PRACTICE.

HUMAN DIMENSION

12. CROATIA HAS ENACTED MOST OR ALL OF THE

LEGAL STRUCTURES OF A PLURALISTIC, DEMOCRATIC

SOCIETY. HOWEVER, THE GOVERNMENT APPEARS TO

HAVE LEFT THIS MACHINERY TO OPERATE ON ITS OWN,

AND HAS NOT BEEN ACTIVE IN EXTENDING ITS

PRACTICAL AND FAIR APPLICATION, NOR AGGRESSIVE

IN ENSURING THAT ABUSES ARE AVOIDED.

13. ELECTIONS IN EARLY 1993 WERE GENERALLY

CONSIDERED FAIR AND OPEN BY BOTH OUTSIDE AND

DOMESTIC OBSERVERS AS WELL AS THE PARTICIPANTS.

BUT DEMOCRATIC, PLURALISTIC INSTITUTIONS ARE NOT

YET AS ROBUST AS IN OLDER WESTERN SOCIETIES.

THE SABOR (PARLIAMENT), FOR EXAMPLE, HAS LITTLE

EFFECTIVE POWER TO CHALLENGE THE PRESIDENT OF

THE REPUBLIC. FEW CONCRETE STEPS HAVE BEEN

TAKEN TO ADVANCE THE AGENDA OF GREATER PLURALISM

AND OPENNESS. THE GOVERNMENT AGAIN ENGAGED IN

14. CROATIA SHOWS A STRONG RESPECT, IN LETTER
