1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (U) DCM, ACCOMPANIED BY POLITICAL SECTION CHIEF, DELIVERED --

AND PROVIDED A NON-PAPER CONTAINING -- REFTEL TALKING POINTS ON

AUGUST 23 TO CLEOPHAS TSOKODAYI, THE FOREIGN MINISTRY\'S UNDER

SECRETARY FOR INTERNATIONAL ORGANIZATIONS. TSOKODAYI WAS

SIPDIS

ACCOMPANIED BY DESK OFFICER MARSHALL MUTUTU.

2. (C) DRAWING FROM REF TALKING POINTS, DCM DISCUSSED IN

SIGNIFICANT DETAIL EACH OF THE FIVE CRITICAL USG OBJECTIVES -- AND

FIVE OTHER PRIORITY ISSUES -- FOR THE UPCOMING UNGA SESSION.

ASKED WHETHER ZIMBABWE HAD TAKEN A POSITION ON ANY OF THESE

IMPORTANT ISSUES, TSOKODAYI DEMURRED, SAYING HE PREFERRED FIRST TO

STUDY THE USG NON-PAPER AND WOULD BE PREPARED TO ENGAGE IN A

SUBSTANTIVE DISCUSSION ABOUT GOZ THINKING ONLY AT A LATER DATE.

TSOKODAYI INQUIRED ABOUT THE STATUS OF U.S. PAYMENT OF ITS UN

SIPDIS

ARREARS. THE DCM REPLIED THAT THIS WAS AN ISSUE ON WHICH THE U.S.

ADMINISTRATION PLACES A VERY HIGH PRIORITY. THE UNITED STATES, HE

SAID, HAD PAID THE FIRST TRANCHE AND WAS WORKING WITH CONGRESS ON

THE SECOND TRANCHE, PER POINTS IN REFTEL. (COMMENT: JUDGING FROM

TSOKODAYI\'S REACTION, THE GOZ CLEARLY HAS NOT DEVELOPED FINAL

SIPDIS

POSITIONS IN PREPARATION FOR UNGA. WE WILL STAY IN CLOSE TOUCH

WITH TSOKODAYI AND HIS STAFF, AND WILL REPORT GOZ THINKING ON UNGA

AS IT EVOLVES. END COMMENT.)

3. (C) TSOKODAYI REPORTED THAT, ALTHOUGH NO FINAL DECISIONS HAVE

BEEN MADE, FOREIGN MINISTER MUDENGE LIKELY WILL LEAD ZIMBABWE\'S

UNGA DELEGATION.
