SUMMARY

-------

1. CROATIAN HUMAN RIGHTS GROUP CALLS FOR MORE

ACTION. TAX EVADERS TO BE THE TARGET OF ZAGREB\'S

NEW FINANCIAL POLICE. SERBIAN LEADER IN CROATIA

CALLS FOR TOUGHER UN MANDATE. OSIJEK CITY COUNCIL

CHAIRMAN RETALIATES AGAINST CRITICAL NEWS STORY.

B-H PRESIDENT IZETBEGOVIC CONTINUES TALKS WITH

CROATIAN LEADERS IN MOSTAR. END SUMMARY.

2. THE CROATIAN COMMITTEE FOR THE PROMOTION OF

HUMAN RIGHTS ANNOUNCED ON DECEMBER 10 -- THE

INTERNATIONAL DAY OF HUMAN RIGHTS -- THAT IT WANTS

TO DEVELOP NON-POLITICAL CRITERIA FOR HUMAN RIGHTS

AND TO ORGANIZE AN ACTIVE LOCAL HUMAN RIGHTS

MOVEMENT. THE COMMITTEE PLANS TO MAINTAIN A

POLITICALLY INDEPENDENT STANCE AND TO DIRECT

INTERNATIONAL ATTENTION TO THE SITUATION OF CROATS

LIVING IN SERBIAN-OCCUPIED PARTS OF CROATIA.

----------------------------------------

ZAGREB PLANS "FINANCIAL POLICE" FOR 1993

----------------------------------------

3. THE MINISTRY OF FINANCE HAS ANNOUNCED THAT

CROATIA WILL ESTABLISH ITS OWN SYSTEM OF

"FINANCIAL POLICE" STARTING IN 1993. THESE

OFFICIALS WILL BE A PART OF THE FINANCE MINISTRY

AND WILL CONCENTRATE ON TAX EVASION PROBLEMS. THE

INSPECTORS WILL BE AUTHORIZED TO TAKE ACTION "ON

THE SPOT" AND CAN ASSESS PENALTIES OR CLOSE DOWN A

BUSINESS. SUCH PENALTIES OR CLOSURES CAN BE

APPEALED TO THE FINANCE MINISTRY OR TO THE

CONSTITUTIONAL COURT.

IZETBEGOVIC IN MOSTAR CONSULTS CROATIAN LEADERS

--------------------------------------------- --

6. PRESIDENT OF B-H ALIJA IZETBEGOVIC CONTINUED

HIS EFFORTS TO WOO CROATIAN LEADERS, MEETING WITH

OTHER OFFICIALS FROM THE HERCEG-BOSNA CROATIAN

UNION IN MOSTAR. TWO DAYS EARLIER, IZETBEGOVIC

HAD BEEN IN SPLIT WHERE HE MET WITH PRESIDENT OF

THE CROATIAN UNION HERCEG-BOSNA MATE BOBAN.

NEITHER PARTY WAS WILLING TO SAY MUCH, ALTHOUGH

BOBAN CHARACTERIZED THE TALKS AS THOSE "BETWEEN

TWO FRIENDS," HELD IN A BETTER ATMOSPHERE THAN

EVER BEFORE.
