SUBJECT: GREEK VIEWS ON MARGIN OF PREFERENCE

PROPOSAL

REF: (A)STATE 166836; (B)ATHENS 02267

UNCLASSIFIED

1. SUMMARY: ON OCTOBER 3 AGATTACHE AND

AGSPECIALIST MET WITH MINISTRY OF

AGRICULTURAL POLICY DIVISION SECRETARY

GENERAL KORAKAS DG SARROS AND TWO OTHER MINAG

EMPLOYEES RESPONSIBLE FOR THE DIVISION OF

ARABLE CROPS TO DISCUSS GREECE\'S POSITION ON

THE MARGIN OF PREFERENCE (MOP). GREECE WILL

LIKELY SUPPORT REPLACEMENT OF THE MOP WITH

TARIFF RATE QUOTAS (TRQS)TO BOOST SUPPORT TO

THEIR WHEAT GROWERS. END SUMMARY

2. THE PROSPECT OF CHEAP WHEAT IMPORTS FROM

NEARBY BLACK SEA PORTS IS PERCEIVED BY THE

GOG AS A THREAT TO GREECE\'S PRIMARY SECTOR.

GREEK IMPORTS OF WHEAT FROM RUSSIA AND

UKRAINE CAN ARRIVE VIA THE BLACK SEA IN 24

HOURS. THE GREEK MINAG PERCEIVES THAT PARTIES

BENEFITTING MOST FROM THESE SALES ARE

SHIPPERS AND TRADERS. THE GOG\'S OVERRIDING

CONCERN IS TO CONTINUE TO SUPPORT THEIR WHEAT

PRODUCERS, NOT MILLERS, TRADERS, OR SHIPPERS.

IN ADDITION, SECRETARY GENERAL KORAKAS

POINTED OUT THAT UNDER EU POLICY REFORM

PROPOSALS OF THE MID TERM REVIEW (MTR), DURUM

PRODUCERS WOULD FACE SEVERE CUTS. THE GOG

WISHES TO PURSUE POLICIES TO SUPPORT ITS

WHEAT PRODUCERS. ACCORDING TO OUR

INTERLOCUTORS, GREECE ALSO SEES A POTENTIAL

THAT BLACK SEA IMPORTS COULD OPEN THE DOOR

FOR FUTURE COMPETITION FROM DURUM PRODUCERS

OF SYRIA OR TURKEY.

3. MINAG GAVE NO INDICATION THAT IT HAD

CONSULTED WITH MILLERS OR TRADERS OF WHEAT TO

DETERMINE A POSITION, NOR DID MINAG REPS

CONSISTENTLY MAKE DISTINCTIONS BETWEEN MARKET

EFFECTS OF TRQS ON TRADE AND PRODUCTION OF

SOFT, HARD, OR DURUM WHEAT. GREECE PRODUCES

ABOUT 1.4 MILLION TONS OF DURUM WHEAT

ANNUALLY AND ABOUT 500,00 TONS OF SOFT WHEAT.

IN RECENT YEARS GREECE HAS IMPORTED NO US

WHEAT, BUT ANNUAL IMPORTS OF CANADIAN HARD

WHEAT USED FOR MIXING WITH DOMESTIC SOFT

WHEAT HAVE BEEN 60,000 TONS. THE MINAG IS

AWARE THAT IF THE MOP WERE REPLACED BY A TRQ,

AND THE PRICE OF DURUM WHEAT ON THE EU MARKET

WERE TO RISE, GREEK PRODUCERS WOULD LIKELY

RESPOND BY PLANTING MORE DURUM, SWITCHING

PRODUCTION FROM SOFT WHEAT AND OTHER LESS

PROFITABLE CROPS AS WELL.

4. MINAG DID NOT DIRECTLY RESPOND TO AGATT\'S

COMMENTS ON POSSIBLE UNINTENDED INFLATIONARY

ASPECTS OF TRQS, ALTHOUGH MINAG ACKNOWLEDGED

THAT INFLATION OF FOOD PRICES IS A CONCERN.

WHEN QUESTIONED ABOUT THE TIMING OF ACTION ON

THE MOP, SECRETARY KORAKAS REMARKED THAT "THE

SOONER WE ESTABLISH REFERENCE PERIODS, THE

BETTER." SEMBLER
