1. (U) SUMMARY: MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS OFFICIALS

CHARACTERIZED THE 35TH ASEAN MINISTERIAL MEETING IN BRUNEI

AS "VERY SUCCESSFUL." HIGHLIGHTS INCLUDED THE SIGNING OF A

JOINT DECLARATION ON COUNTER TERRORISM BETWEEN ASEAN AND THE

U.S., A POSITIVE MEETING BETWEEN FOREIGN MINISTER NGUYEN DY

NIEN AND SECRETARY POWELL, LIMITED PROGRESS TOWARDS ADOPTING

A DECLARATION ON THE CODE OF CONDUCT FOR THE SOUTH CHINA

SEA, AND CONSENSUS ON ECONOMIC/DEVELOPMENT ISSUES. END

SUMMARY

--------------------------------------------- ------------

COUNTERTERRORISM DECLARATION "MOST IMPORTANT ACHIEVEMENT"

--------------------------------------------- ------------

2. (U) POLOFFS OBTAINED READOUT OF THE 35TH ASEAN

MINISTERIAL MEETING FROM MFA ASEAN DEPARTMENT DEPUTY

DIRECTOR GENERAL PHAM HUU CHI AND SENIOR EXPERT LE CHI DUNG

ON AUGUST 8. OVERALL, CHI SAID THE GVN VIEWED THE MEETING

AS "VERY SUCCESSFUL." ASEAN MEMBERS "WERE PLEASED TO SEE

THAT SECRETARY POWELL CAME TO THE MEETING WITH A "THICK

BOOK" OUTLINING US ONGOING PROJECTS IN ASIA, WHICH

DEMONSTRATED USG COMMITMENT TO THE REGION.

3. (U) CHI SPECIFICALLY POINTED TO THE ASEAN-U.S. JOINT

DECLARATION ON COUNTER TERRORISM, SIGNED AUGUST 1, AS THE

MEETING\'S "MOST IMPORTANT ACHIEVEMENT." CHI NOTED THAT,

REGARDING COUNTER TERRORISM, "ASEAN AND THE U.S. UNDERSTAND

EACH OTHER WELL AND THAT ASEAN IS DETERMINED TO WORK WITH

THE USG\'S CONCERNS." CHI PREDICTED THAT NEXT MONTH\'S ASEM

SUMMIT WOULD INCLUDE A SIMILAR, THOUGH "SOMEWHAT WEAKER,"

DECLARATION. CHI SAID IT WAS SIGNIFICANT THAT THE PARTIES

REACHED A CONSENSUS THREE DAYS BEFORE THE END OF THE

MEETING. TO EXPEDITE REACHING CONSENSUS, CERTAIN SPECIFICS

------------------------------

"POSITIVE" NIEN-POWELL MEETING

------------------------------

4. (U) CHI SAID THE FOREIGN MINISTER NIEN AND SECRETARY

POWELL HELD A "VERY POSITIVE" 20-MINUTE MEETING ON JULY

31ST. CHI CHARACTERIZED THE MEETING AS "FORMAL, NOT PULL-

ASIDE." ACCORDING TO CHI, NIEN RAISED SEVERAL ISSUES,

INCLUDING BTA IMPLEMENTATION, PERMANENT REMOVAL OF THE

JACKSON-VANIK WAIVER, AND SETTING UP OF "A PERMANENT

MECHANISM" BETWEEN THE U.S. AND VIETNAM FOR SETTLING ISSUES

SUCH AS CATFISH. CHI SAID SECRETARY POWELL TOLD NIEN HE

VIEWED THE CATFISH ISSUE AS NOTHING OUT OF THE ORDINARY; THE

SECRETARY DID NOT COMMENT ON THE OTHER ISSUES RAISED. NIEN

SIPDIS

ALSO MENTIONED TO THE SECRETARY THAT IT WOULD BE A GOOD IDEA

FOR PRIME MINISTER KHAI TO MEET PRESIDENT BUSH AT THE APEC

MEETING SCHEDULED FOR THIS NOVEMBER IN MEXICO. CHI ALSO

REPORTED THAT SECRETARY POWELL TOLD NIEN THAT HE HOPED THEY

COULD MEET AGAIN IN NEW YORK AT THE UPCOMING 57TH UN GENERAL

ASSEMBLY.

CODE OF CONDUCT-PROGRESS BUT STILL A LONG ROAD AHEAD

--------------------------------------------- -------

5. (U) IN A MEETING WITH EMBOFFS LAST MAY (REFTEL B), CHI

EXPRESSED OPTIMISM THAT THE DECLARATION OF THE SOUTH CHINA

SEA CODE OF CONDUCT (COC) WOULD BE ADOPTED AT THE BRUNEI

MEETING. THIS DID NOT OCCUR. CHI SAID THERE IS STILL NOT A

CONSENSUS ON THE SCOPE. THE STICKING POINT IS THAT MALAYSIA

WANTS TO INCLUDE ONLY THE AREA AROUND THE SPRATLEY ISLANDS,

WHILE OTHER ASEAN MEMBERS (AND THE PRC) WANT TO INCLUDE ALL

THE SOUTH CHINA SEA AREA. THAT SAID, CHI OPINED THAT THE

PRC IN PARTICULAR "WAS ANXIOUS TO SIGN A COC." CHI SAID

THAT ASEAN IS "WORKING TOWARDS A CONSENSUS" TO BREAK THE

DEADLOCK BY LEAVING THE SCOPE ISSUE OUT OF THE TEXT BUT

INCLUDE IT IN THE DOCUMENT\'S TITLE. ACCORDING TO PRESS

REPORTS, MALAYSIA AND VIETNAM DISAGREED OVER THE TITLE, AS

WELL, THOUGH VIETNAM EVENTUALLY RELENTED. ACCORDING TO CHI

AND PRESS REPORTS, THE DECLARATION\'S WORDING (ORIGINALLY

DRAFTED BY THE PHILIPPINES) WILL BE "REFINED" OVER THE NEXT

FEW MONTHS AND THEN SIGNED AT THE ASEAN SUMMIT THAT WILL BE

HELD THIS NOVEMBER IN CAMBODIA. CHI POINTED OUT, HOWEVER,

PROGRESS ON THE ECONOMIC FRONT

------------------------------

6. (U) ACCORDING TO CHI, ASEAN MADE CONSIDERABLE PROGRESS

REGARDING MEASURES TO NARROW THE DEVELOPMENT GAP AMONG THE

ASEAN MEMBERS AND TOWARDS THE FORMATION OF NEW (AND

SEPARATE) FREE TRADE AREAS (FTA) WITH, CHINA, JAPAN AND

SOUTH KOREA, RESPECTIVELY. SINGAPORE IN PARTICULAR WANTS TO

SEE AN ACCELERATED EFFORT TO DIMINISH THE DEVELOPMENT GAP,

AND "IS GROWING INCREASINGLY FRUSTRATED BY THE SLOWER

DEVELOPMENT OF ASEAN\'S NEWEST MEMBERS," ACCORDING TO CHI.

CHI COMMENTED THAT ASEAN AND THE PRC WERE "SLOWLY MOVING

FORWARD" ON THIS ISSUE AFTER WITNESSING THE SUCCESS OF NAFTA

AND THE EU. CHI WAS LESS ENTHUSIASTIC ABOUT AN FTA WITH

JAPAN. JAPAN STILL FAVORS BILATERAL COMPREHENSIVE ECONOMIC

PARTNERSHIPS RATHER THAN A MULTILATERAL FTA WITH ASEAN AS A

WHOLE.

------------------------

PROLIFERATION OF PLUSES

------------------------

7. (U) IN THE COMING MONTH\'S ASEAN WILL LOOK TO EXPANDING

UPON THE SUCCESS OF ITS "+ 3" RELATIONSHIP WITH CHINA, JAPAN

AND SOUTH KOREA BY PUSHING FOR THE ENHANCEMENT AND CREATION

OF NEWER "+" RELATIONSHIPS WITH OTHER SIGNIFICANT PARTNERS.

CHI SAID HE "WAS PLEASED TO NOTE" THAT THERE WILL BE AN

ASEAN + 1 MEETING WITH INDIA ON THE MARGINS OF THE UPCOMING

MEETING IN CAMBODIA THIS NOVEMBER. A SEPARATE, FIRST-EVER

"+ 1" MEETING IS SCHEDULED TO OCCUR EARLY NEXT YEAR WITH

AUSTRALIA. IN ADDITION TO THESE MEETINGS, ASEAN ALSO WOULD

CHI SUGGESTED THAT SUCH A MEETING COULD BE ATTEMPTED AS

ON THE MARGINS OF THE MEXICO APEC SUMMIT WOULD OFFER A GOOD

TEST FOR THIS IDEA.

--------------------------

PAST AND FUTURE LEADERSHIP

--------------------------

8. (U) CHI SAID BRUNEI HAD DONE A "GOOD JOB" OF HOSTING THE

CONFERENCE. CHI OPINED THAT CAMBODIA "SHOULD BE READY" TO

HOST THE NOVEMBER MEETING. HE NOTED THAT THE CAMBODIAN

GOVERNMENT HAS SENT DELEGATES TO HANOI AND BANDAR SERI

BEGAWAN FOR THE LAST TWO YEARS IN AN EFFORT TO LEARN THEIR

RESPONSIBILITIES AS HOSTS. CHI SAID THAT THIS PREPARATION

SHOULD PROVIDE FOR A SMOOTH TRANSITION. PORTER
