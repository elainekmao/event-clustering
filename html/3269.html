SUBJECT: MEDIA REPORT ZIMPAPERS BACK ON THE BOURSE; HARARE

The state-controlled Zimbabwe Newspapers Group (Zimpapers)

is back on the Zimbabwe Stock Exchange (ZSE), three days

after being suspended for flouting the bourse\'s regulations

and Companies Act requirements. The former blue-chip

company was suspended on Monday, July 1 after it failed to

produce and circulate its audited results to shareholders

as stipulated by the stock exchange. However, the

suspension was lifted on July 4 after the group managed to

release and circulate its belated audited accounts, that

posted a staggering Z$106 million loss, to shareholders and

the announcement of its annual general meeting, now

scheduled for July 25. Zimpapers shares have been trading

below 100 cents and last year it was among the strong

contenders for the wooden-spoon award on the ZSE. Analysts

say "the poor results mirror the negative light" in which

its six titles are seen by the public.
