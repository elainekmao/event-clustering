SUBJECT: MUGABE PUTS ON THE DOG FOR MOGAE

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 01419 260833Z

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (SBU) BOTSWANA\'S PRESIDENT, FESTUS MOGAE, ARRIVED IN

HARARE ON APRIL 25 FOR A FOUR-DAY STATE VISIT, THE

STATED PURPOSE OF WHICH IS FOR THE BOTSWANA CHIEF

EXECUTIVE TO OPEN THE ZIMBABWE INTERNATIONAL TRADE FAIR

IN BULAWAYO. THIS IS MOGAE\'S SECOND WORKING VISIT IN 8

MONTHS; HE WAS LAST HERE IN AUGUST TO OPEN AN

AGRICULTURAL EXHIBIT IN HARARE. THE MINISTRY OF FOREIGN

AFFAIRS CONVOKED HEADS OF DIPLOMATIC MISSIONS TO MEET

THE PRESIDENT AT THE AIRPORT, A RARE HONOR GRANTED TO

VISITING HEADS OF STATE IN ZIMBABWE. THE AIRPORT

CEREMONY ENTAILED A TWENTY-ONE-GUN SALUTE AND AN HONOR

GUARD REVIEW. THE TWO PRESIDENTS MET PRIVATELY FOR

THREE HOURS IN THE AFTERNOON AND, INTER ALIA, SIGNED A

TRADE AGREEMENT DESIGNED TO EASE RESTRICTIONS ON CROSS-

BORDER MOVEMENT OF MANUFACTURING INPUTS. INDUSTRY AND

INTERNATIONAL TRADE MINISTER NKOSANA MOYO INFORMED US

THAT HE ORGANIZED THE VISIT AT THE LAST MOMENT AND WAS

SURPRISED THAT IT ACTUALLY HAD COME TOGETHER.

2. (SBU) IN THE EVENING, PRESIDENT MUGABE HOSTED A SET-

PIECE STATE DINNER FOR HIS BOTSWANA COUNTERPART. THE

GUEST LIST CONSISTED MOSTLY OF DIPLOMATIC HEADS OF

MISSION, SELECTED MINISTERS, AND ZANU-PF LUMINARIES,

HALF OF WHOM DID NOT SHOW JUDGING FROM THE MASS OF EMPTY

TABLES. THE ZIMBABWEAN PRESIDENT DELIVERED A TWENTY-

MINUTE WELCOME SPEECH IN WHICH HE ADDRESSED MOGAE IN

CLOYING TERMS -- "MY DEAR BROTHER" BEING ONE OF THE LESS

SACCHARINE -- AND PLEDGED ETERNAL FRIENDSHIP. THE

SPEECH CONTAINED A NUMBER OF BRICKBATS FOR THE WEST, AND

PARTICULARLY THE BRITISH, FOR DISTORTING THE NATURE OF

ZIMBABWE\'S FAST TRACK RESETTLEMENT PROGRAM. HE WAS

DISMISSIVE OF THOSE WHO CLAIMED THAT WHAT HAD HAPPENED

IN ZIMBABWE SINCE 1999 BOILED DOWN TO A PROBLEM OF BASIC

GOVERNANCE AND A THREAT TO THE INDEPENDENCE OF THE

JUDICIARY. HE CLAIMED ZIMBABWE WAS READY TO ENGAGE

BRITAIN AND THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY, AS DISPLAYED IN

ITS RESPONSE TO THE LETTER OF UNDP ADMINISTRATOR MARK

MALLOCH-BROWN. HE THANKED BOTSWANA FOR ITS HELP IN

GETTING VITAL FUEL AND OTHER SUPPLIES TO ZIMBABWE AND

CONDEMNED THE "VIRTUAL SANCTIONS IMPOSED FROM THE WEST,"

ON WHICH HE PARTIALLY BLAMED ZIMBABWE\'S CURRENT BAD

ECONOMIC PATCH. "WE ARE A DETERMINED NATION; AS GALLANT

FIGHTERS WE SHALL NEVER ALLOW OUR ECONOMY TO COLLAPSE."

MUGABE EXPRESSED CERTAINTY THAT WITH THE FORMATION OF

THE AFRICAN UNION IN JULY IN LUSAKA, THE IMF, WORLD BANK

AND WORLD TRADE ORGANIZATION WOULD BE OBLIGED TO TAKE

THE CONTINENT SERIOUSLY. MOGAE RESPONDED LARGELY WITH

PLEASANTRIES, CONCLUDING HIS REMARKS BY THANKING MUGABE

FOR THE "FULL BRIEFING" HE GAVE ON THE CHALLENGES

ZIMBABWE FACES AND THE MEASURES ITS GOVERNMENT IS TAKING

TO ADDRESS THEM.

3. (C) COMMENT: MOGAE IS THE SECOND AFRICAN HEAD OF

STATE TO VISIT ZIMBABWE IN THE LAST MONTH: THE

DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC OF THE CONGO\'S JOSEPH KABILA WAS

HERE MARCH 25-27. MUGABE REGALED BOTH VISITORS WITH

HOSPITALITY SO LAVISH IT BORDERED ON THE OBSCENE, GIVEN

THE COUNTRY\'S CURRENT CIRCUMSTANCES. IT HAS NOT GONE

UNNOTICED. MOST HEADS OF MISSION HERE ATTRIBUTE THE

LATEST STAGED HOOPLA TO MUGABE\'S WISH TO COURT THE

PEACEABLE MOGAE. BOTSWANA PROVIDES A LIFELINE FOR

ZIMBABWE, SUPPLYING INPUTS THAT ARE THE LIFEBLOOD OF

THIS COUNTRY\'S ECONOMY AND REMITTANCES FROM ZIMBABWEANS

LIVING AND WORKING IN THE NEIGHBORING COUNTRY. BOTSWANA

ALSO CHAIRS THE COMMONWEALTH MINISTERIAL ACTION GROUP

THAT CURRENTLY WISHES TO SEND A MISSION TO ZIMBABWE TO

INVESTIGATE THE REIGN OF TERROR BEING UNLEASHED AGAINST

SUSPECTED OPPOSITION SUPPORTERS, THE JUDICIARY, THE

INDEPENDENT PRESS AND MOST RECENTLY, COMPANY MANAGERS IN

URBAN AREAS. THE OPPOSITION MOVEMENT FOR DEMOCRATIC

CHANGE (MDC) HAS COURTED THE MOGAE GOVERNMENT

ASSIDUOUSLY -- A STRATEGY THAT HAS BEGUN TO PAY

DIVIDENDS -- AND MUGABE LIKELY HOPES TO PREVENT THAT

RELATIONSHIP FROM GETTING ANY WARMER. MUGABE SPENT

CONSIDERABLE TIME TELLING MOGAE THAT ALL WAS WELL IN

ZIMBABWE, BUT HEARD NO PUBLIC STATEMENT FROM HIS GUEST

ENDORSING THE FAST-TRACK RESETTLEMENT EXERCISE. END

COMMENT.
