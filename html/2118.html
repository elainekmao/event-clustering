SUBJECT: MEDIA REPORT ABUJA COMMONWEALTH FOLLOW-UP;

HARARE

1. FOLLOWING THE VISIT LAST WEEK BY A COMMONWEALTH

TEAM TO ZIMBABWE TO DETERMINE WHETHER THE COUNTRY IS

MEETING ITS COMMITMENTS UNDER A NIGERIAN-LED LAND

AGREEMENT SIGNED IN ABUJA ON SEPTEMBER 6, ALL THREE

SUNDAY NEWSPAPERS PUBLISHED ON OCTOBER 28 CARRIED

EDITORIALS CENTERING ON THE MEETING IN HARARE:

2. UNDER HEADLINE "ZIMBABWE IS A SOVEREIGN STATE," THE

GOVERNMENT CONTROLLED WEEKLY "THE SUNDAY MAIL" COMMENTS:

". . .WHY SHOULD FOREIGNERS COME HERE TO MONITOR AND

SUPERVISE OUR ELECTIONS WHEN WE NEVER MEDDLE IN THEIR

INTERNAL POLITICS? THE WHOLE WORLD WITNESSED HOW

GEORGE W. BUSH\'S REPUBLICAN PARTY RIGGED ITS WAY TO

VICTORY IN THE UNITED STATES\' LAST PRESIDENTIAL

ELECTIONS. DID TONY BLAIR AND HIS ILK COMPLAIN ABOUT

THIS? THE PROGRESSIVE FORCES OF AFRICA SHOULD BE

WORRIED TODAY THAT THE ACCORD. . .RUNS THE DANGER OF

BEING HIJACKED BY BRITAIN, CANADA AND AUSTRALIA. WE

HOPE REASON WILL PREVAIL OVER VANITY IN THESE TALKS."

3. UNDER HEADLINE "GAME UP FOR MUGABE," THE

INDEPENDENT WEEKLY "THE STANDARD" COMMENTS:

"IF THERE IS ONE ADMINISTRATION AROUND THE WORLD WHICH

HAS PERFECTED THE ART OF PLAYING TRUANT WHEN IT COMES TO

HONORING INTERNATIONAL AGREEMENTS, IT IS THAT OF MR.

ROBERT MUGABE. . . THE LATEST AGREEMENT. . .IS ABUJA. .

.WHICH THE STATE IS NOW TRYING TO WRITHE OUT AGAINST THE

BACKGROUND OF DEMANDS BY THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY FOR

POLITICAL CLEANLINESS. . . WITH THIS WEEK\'S SPITING OF

THE EU (EUROPEAN UNION) DELEGATION, ONE IS. . .FORCED TO

UNCLASSIFIED

ASK WHETHER MUGABE REALIZES - OR CARES - THAT HE HAS

ALSO TOLD HIS REGIONAL COLLEAGUES TO GO TO HELL. . . IT

IS TIME THAT MUGABE\'S TRICKS ARE BROUGHT TO FINAL CHECK.

WE HOPE THAT THE DECISIONS OF THE DELEGATION (WILL)

FINALLY BRING THE MESSAGE TO HIM THAT THE REST OF THE

WORLD, INCLUDING ZIMBABWE, EXPECT HIM TO BEHAVE IN A

CIVILIZED MANNER."

4. UNDER HEADLINE "NO AMOUNT OF LIES WILL STOP LAND

REFORM," THE OCTOBER 28 EDITION OF THE GOVERNMENT-

CONTROLLED WEEKLY "THE SUNDAY MAIL" COMMENTS:

". . .THE VISIT BY THE COMMONWEALTH MINISTERS. . .

REVEALED HOW TORTUOUS IT IS TO KEEP THE (ABUJA) ACCORD

GOING. IT ALSO SHOWS HOW MUCH THE ZIMBABWE GOVERNMENT

IS COMMITTED TO MAKING IT SUCCEED. THEY WERE CLEARLY

GOING OUT OF THEIR WAY TO RESTRAIN THEMSELVES IN THE

FACE OF RACIST INSULTS AND OPEN BIAS. . . THIS IS A

REVOLUTION THAT PEOPLE WITH A HISTORY OF DISPOSSESSION,

LIKE THE MINISTERS FROM THE AFRICAN COUNTRIES IN THE

COMMITTEE, CAN EASILY APPRECIATE. IT IS ALSO A

REVOLUTION THAT THE LIKES OF MR. (DAVID) KILGOUR

(CANADIAN SECRETARY OF STATE - AFRICA AND LATIN AMERICA)

AND THEIR VANQUISHED COLLEAGUES WILL FIND DIFFICULT TO

SWALLOW."
