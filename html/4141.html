3. (C) IN THREE SEPARATE MEETINGS FROM OCTOBER 12 TO

OCTOBER 18, PROTESTANT HOUSE CHURCH LEADER PASTOR

"STEPHEN" AND A THIRD COUNTRY NATIONAL (TCN) WHO HAS

REQUESTED THAT HIS NAME NOT BE USED INFORMED POLOFF OF

RECENT DEVELOPMENTS AFFECTING VIETNAM\'S CHRISTIANS,

ESPECIALLY PROTESTANTS IN THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS AND

NORTHWEST HIGHLANDS. BOTH SOURCES HAVE PROVIDED

RELIABLE INFORMATION TO THE MISSION IN THE PAST.

PASTOR STEPHEN NOTED THAT HIS INFORMATION WAS MOSTLY

SECOND-HAND, PASSED TO HIM FROM PROTESTANT PASTORS AND

LEADERS IN THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS AND NORTHWEST WHO

LOOK TO HIM FOR LEADERSHIP AND ASSISTANCE. THE TCN

IDENTIFIED HIS SOURCES AS AN EDE PASTOR, Y KEH

(PHONETIC), WHOM HE SAYS HE HAS KNOWN FOR OVER THIRTY

YEARS, AS WELL AS VARIOUS PROTESTANTS IN HANOI WHOM HE

CENTRAL HIGHLANDS

-----------------

4. (C) PASTOR STEPHEN DESCRIBED A THREE-DAY

CONFERENCE DURING JULY IN DAK LAK IN WHICH GVN

AUTHORITIES REACHED A DECISION TO "PURIFY

PROTESTANTISM IN VIETNAM." HE CLAIMED THAT THIS MEANT

"DESTROYING," OR AT LEAST "LIMITING" PROTESTANTISM.

HE REPORTED THAT PROVINCIAL AND DISTRICT-LEVEL

PEOPLE\'S COMMITTEE LEADERS FROM KON TUM, GIA LAI, LAM

DONG, AND DAK LAK PROVINCES ATTENDED THE MEETING, BUT

SAID THAT HE DID NOT KNOW IF NATIONAL-LEVEL OFFICIALS

PARTICIPATED. THE INFORMATION, HE INDICATED, CAME

FROM SECRETLY CHRISTIAN OFFICIALS WHO HAD ATTENDED THE

MEETINGS. SEPARATELY, THE TCN CLAIMED THAT LOCAL

SECURITY OFFICIALS WERE TELLING SOME PROTESTANT

BELIEVERS IN THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS THAT INSTRUCTIONS

TO ACT AGAINST THEM CAME FROM THE NATIONAL LEVEL.

DAK LAK

-------

SITUATION IN DAK LAK HAD BECOME "DRAMATICALLY WORSE"

SINCE AUGUST. THE TCN OPINED THAT THIS WAS SOMEWHAT

SURPRISING BECAUSE GIA LAI HAD PREVIOUSLY BEEN

GENERALLY TOUGHER AGAINST PROTESTANTS. ACCORDING TO

HIS SOURCE Y KEH, DAK LAK AUTHORITIES PROHIBIT ALL

MEETINGS, CLOSELY CONTROL MOVEMENT BY BOTH ETHNIC

8. (C) ON AUGUST 28, IN M\'DRAK DISTRICT IN

NORTHEASTERN DAK LAK, SECURITY FORCES REPORTEDLY

ATTEMPTED TO DETAIN A HOUSE CHURCH PASTOR AND HIS TWO

ADULT SONS. A GROUP OF LOCAL PEOPLE GATHERED AND

PREVENTED THE AUTHORITIES FROM CARRYING OUT THEIR

PLANS. THE AUTHORITIES LEFT, BUT LATER RETURNED WITH

REINFORCEMENTS. A TOTAL OF ABOUT 120 LOCAL RESIDENTS

GATHERED TO PROTECT THE PASTOR AND HIS SONS. IN THE

ENSUING "DONNYBROOK," AS THE TCN DESCRIBED IT, ONE

VILLAGER WAS SHOT IN THE FOOT OR THE LEG (APPARENTLY

THE WOUND WAS NOT SERIOUS) AND AUTHORITIES DETAINED

ALL 120 VILLAGERS. AGAIN, AUTHORITIES QUICKLY

RELEASED MOST, BUT AS OF MID-OCTOBER, BETWEEN 20 AND

30 HAD NOT RETURNED HOME. AUTHORITIES HAVE NOT

ACKNOWLEDGED DETAINING THEM. THE PASTOR AND HIS SONS

ALSO REPORTEDLY HAVE NOT BEEN SEEN SINCE AUGUST 28.

THE HMONG

---------

9. (C) ALSO IN AUGUST, ACCORDING TO THE TCN, HMONG

"SPONTANEOUS MIGRANTS" WHO HAD MOVED TO THE CENTRAL

HIGHLANDS FROM THEIR HOMES IN THE NORTHWEST HIGHLANDS

BEGINNING IN THE MID- TO LATE-1990\'S, RECEIVED VISITS

FROM OFFICIALS OF THEIR HOME PROVINCES. THESE

OFFICIALS, WITH COOPERATION FROM CENTRAL HIGHLANDS

AUTHORITIES, WERE REPORTEDLY TRYING TO INDUCE THE

HMONG TO MOVE BACK TO THE NORTHWEST. THE TCN SAID

THAT IT WAS NOT CLEAR WHAT MEANS THEY WERE USING TO

CONVINCE THE HMONG TO DO THIS. SO FAR, NONE OF THE

HMONG ARE THOUGHT TO HAVE ACCEPTED THE INVITATION TO

A BRIGHTER PICTURE IN LAU CAI

10. (C) ACCORDING TO PASTOR STEPHEN, DURING THE

SUMMER, POLICE IN BAO YEN DISTRICT OF LAU CAI INFORMED

GATHER TO WORSHIP, THEY COULD PRACTICE THEIR FAITH IN

FOUR FAMILIES FROM DIEN BIEN DONG

---------------------------------

11. (C) ACCORDING TO PASTOR STEPHEN, ON APRIL 12,

2001, OFFICIALS OF DIEN BIEN DONG DISTRICT OF LAI CHAU

CAME TO PHINH GIANG VILLAGE AND URGED THE FIVE

PROTESTANT FAMILIES AMONG THE 40 FAMILIES IN THE

VILLAGE TO RENOUNCE THEIR FAITH "BECAUSE THE VILLAGE

IS NOT PROTESTANT." ONE FAMILY COMPLIED, BUT THE

OTHER FOUR REFUSED. THE AUTHORITIES THEN TOLD THE

FOUR FAMILIES THAT THEY HAD TO LEAVE THE VILLAGE.

POLICE AND MILITIA REPORTEDLY THREW ALL OF THEIR

BELONGINGS OUT OF THEIR HOUSES AND BEAT ONE OF THE

FAMILY HEADS, MUA BUA SINH, UNTIL BLOOD CAME FROM HIS

MOUTH AND NOSE. SINH\'S WIFE REPORTEDLY FINALLY

13. (C) PASTOR STEPHEN REPORTED THAT IN EARLY

OCTOBER THE LEADERS OF FIVE PROTESTANT HOUSE CHURCHES

IN TUAN GIAO DISTRICT OF LAI CHAU HAD MET HIM IN HANOI

AND DESCRIBED EVENTS IN THEIR DISTRICT. THERE HAD

BEEN 21 PROTESTANT HOUSE CHURCHES IN THE DISTRICT, BUT

BEGINNING EARLY IN 2002, AUTHORITIES BEGAN PRESSURING

THEM TO ABANDON THEIR FAITH. SECURITY FORCES,

INCLUDING "REGULAR POLICE" AND "BORDER POLICE,"

ACCORDING TO PASTOR STEPHEN, HAVE BEEN APPEARING EVERY

WEEK AT HOUSE CHURCH SERVICES TO TELL WORSHIPPERS THAT

"THOSE WHO DO NOT DENY CHRIST ARE ANTI-COMMUNIST,"

THAT "PROTESTANTISM IS AN AMERICAN RELIGION," AND THAT

"IF YOU INTEND TO KEEP YOUR FAITH YOU SHOULD LEAVE AND

GO TO AMERICA." THE APPARENT LEADER OF THIS CAMPAIGN

WAS THE VICE-CHIEF OF THE PROVINCIAL POLICE. PASTOR

STEPHEN SAID THAT 16 OF THE HOUSE CHURCH CONGREGATIONS

ARE NOW DEFUNCT, BUT THAT THE OTHER FIVE ARE STILL

OPERATING.

THE ECVN REACHES OUT TO HMONG HOUSE CHURCHES

--------------------------------------------

14. (C) THE TCN TOLD POLOFF THAT THE OFFICIALLY

RECOGNIZED ECVN\'S GOVERNING BOARD HAD DECIDED IN MID-

SEPTEMBER TO TAKE MEMBERSHIP APPLICATIONS FROM HMONG

HOUSE CHURCHES. (NOTE: THE ECVN ELECTED THE CURRENT

BOARD IN 1988; ONLY THREE OF THE FIVE MEMBERS ELECTED

THEN ARE STILL ALIVE, BUT THE ECVN HAS NOT BEEN

PERMITTED TO HOLD NEW ELECTIONS OR HOLD A CONGRESS.

END NOTE.) THE ECVN RECEIVED OVER 300 APPLICATIONS

AND, BY OCTOBER 16, THE BOARD HAD APPROVED

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HANOI 02628 03 OF 03 310934Z

APPLICATIONS FROM 204 HMONG CONGREGATIONS IN LAI CHAU,

22 IN LAO CAI, AND ONE NON-HMONG CONGREGATION IN THAI

NGUYEN. THE BOARD STILL HAD ABOUT 100 APPLICATIONS TO

CONSIDER. THE TCN SAID THAT HE DID NOT KNOW IF ANY

WERE REFUSED. ON OCTOBER 20, THE TCN PROVIDED CONGEN

HCMC POLOFF A LIST OF THE 227 APPROVED CONGREGATIONS,

ALONG WITH A SAMPLE APPLICATION LETTER FROM ONE HMONG

CONGREGATION DATED JULY 17, 2002 AND AN ECVN APPROVAL

FORM FOR ANOTHER CONGREGATION DATED OCTOBER 9, 2002.

THE 227 CONGREGATIONS LISTED RANGED IN SIZE FROM 470

TO 25 MEMBERS. THE ECVN CURRENTLY CLAIMS ABOUT 8,000

MEMBERS IN ONLY 14 CONGREGATIONS. THE ECVN HAS NOT

INFORMED THE GVN ABOUT THIS ACTION YET, THE TCN

STATED. COMMENTING ON THIS UNUSUALLY BOLD MOVE, THE

TCN SAID THAT THE ECVN BOARD PROBABLY FELT THAT IT

"HAD NOTHING TO LOSE."

15. (C) COMMENT: THE BOTTOM LINE IS THAT THERE

REMAIN WIDE DISPARITIES BETWEEN HOW AUTHORITIES IN

DIFFERENT PROVINCES, EVEN IN DIFFERENT DISTRICTS,

TREAT PROTESTANTS IN THEIR JURISDICTIONS. THE ECVN\'S

MOVE TO INTEGRATE HMONG PROTESTANT HOUSE CHURCHES

REFLECTS THE INDEPENDENCE THE CHURCH HAS ASSERTED IN

REFUSING TO ACCEPT THE GVN\'S CONDITIONS FOR HOLDING AN

ANNUAL MEETING SINCE 1988. ECVN TIES TO THE HMONG

DATE BACK AT LEAST TO THE EARLY 1990\'S. THE ECVN\'S

INFORMAL LEADER, PASTOR AU QUANG VINH OF HANOI, HAS

EXPERIENCED DIFFICULTIES WITH THE AUTHORITIES SINCE

1994, WHEN HE HELPED PUBLISH AN UNAUTHORIZED HMONG

LANGUAGE BIBLE. THE GVN HAS CLAIMED THAT THE HMONG DO

NOT REALLY UNDERSTAND PROTESTANTISM AND ARE OFTEN
