SUBJECT: THE ECB, NATIONAL CENTRAL BANKS AND THE BANK

OF ITALY

SUMMARY

-------

1. (SBU) THE BANK OF ITALY (BOI), LONG A CORNERSTONE OF

THE ITALIAN ECONOMY, FACES CHANGED RESPONSIBILITIES AND

ROLES. WITH THE ADVENT OF THE EUROPEAN CENTRAL BANK,

THE BOI IS ONE OF THIRTEEN NATIONAL CENTRAL BANKS WITH

A VOICE IN THE SINGLE ECB MONETARY POLICY. THE ECB IS

ORGANIZED WITH "CENTRALIZED DECISION-MAKING,

DECENTRALIZED OPERATIONS." ACTING UNDER ECB DIRECTION,

THE BOI RETAINS AN IMPORTANT OPERATIONAL ROLE IN

PRINTING CURRENCY AND MINTING COINS, MANAGING FOREIGN

RESERVES, CONDUCTING PAYMENT OPERATIONS, MANAGING

REPURCHASE AGREEMENTS. THE BOI REMAINS INDEPENDENT IN

TWO KEY AREAS: BANK SUPERVISION AND ECONOMIC STUDIES.

THE BOI CONTINUES TO HAVE AN AUTHORITATIVE, INDEPENDENT

VOICE IN ITALIAN ECONOMIC POLICY. THIS CABLE EXAMINES

THE ROLE OF THE BANK OF ITALY, IN PARTICULAR, AND, MORE

GENERALLY, THE ROLE OF NATIONAL CENTRAL BANKS OF THE

ECB. WE CONCLUDE THAT THE ECB MAY BE ONLY AS STRONG AS

ITS KEY NATIONAL CENTRAL BANKS. END SUMMARY.

THE BANK OF ITALY

-----------------

2. (U) ITALY\'S CENTRAL BANK, THE BANK OF ITALY (BOI),

IS ONE OF THE COUNTRY\'S MOST HIGHLY RESPECTED ENTITIES.

FOUNDED IN 1893, THE BANK HAS ENJOYED A POWERFUL,

INFLUENTIAL POSITION IN ITALIAN ECONOMIC AND BUSINESS

LIFE. THE BOI IS LARGELY AUTONOMOUS. ITS GOVERNOR IS

TRADITIONALLY A TECHNOCRAT, RATHER THAN A POLITICIAN,

ALTHOUGH APPOINTED BY THE PRIME MINISTER AND APPROVED

BY THE PARLIAMENT. ITALIAN PRESIDENT CARLO CIAMPI WAS

THE PREVIOUS CENTRAL BANK GOVERNOR. THE BOI IS

EXPECTED TO, AND REGULARLY DOES, VOICE INDEPENDENT AND

OFTEN QUITE CRITICAL OPINIONS ON THE GOVERNMENT\'S

ECONOMIC POLICY.

3. (U). BEFORE THE ADVENT OF THE ECB, THE BOI HAD A

STAFF OF OVER 9,400 EMPLOYEES. GOVERNOR ANTONIO FAZIO,

A WELL-RESPECTED ECONOMIST, WAS APPOINTED IN 1993, AND

IS FORMER DEPUTY DIRECTOR GENERAL OF THE BANK. HIS

DEPUTY, VINCENZO DESARIO, ALSO STARTED HIS CAREER IN

THE BANK AND IS EXPERT OF BANKING SUPERVISION. THE

BOI HAD 11 DEPARTMENTS, INCLUDING BANK SUPERVISION,

ECONOMIC RESEARCH, INTERNATIONAL AFFAIRS, NOTE ISSUE,

CENTRAL BANKING AND MARKETS, PAYMENT SYSTEM AND

TREASURY OPERATIONS.

ROLE OF THE NATIONAL CENTRAL BANKS (NCBS)

-----------------------------------------

4. (U) WHEN THE EUROPEAN CENTRAL BANK (ECB) WAS

ESTABLISHED IN 1998, NATIONAL CENTRAL BANKS (NCBS)

FOUND THEMSELVES STRIPPED OF MANY OF THEIR POWERS. THE

UNCLASSIFIED

ECB WAS GIVEN AUTHORITY OVER MONETARY POLICY FOR MEMBER

STATES. AS SUCH, THE CREATION OF THE EUROPEAN CENTRAL

BANK HAS DRAMATICALLY ALTERED THE STRUCTURE AND

RESPONSIBILITIES OF THE BOI. WHILE THE BOI RETAINS

RESPONSIBILITY FOR BANK SUPERVISION AND FOR INDEPENDENT

ECONOMIC ANALYSIS, IT NO LONGER HAS AN INDEPENDENT

MONETARY POLICYMAKING ROLE. INSTEAD, THE BOI AND THE

OTHER MEMBER STATE NCBS TOGETHER CONTRIBUTE TO EURO-

ZONE MONETARY POLICY DECISIONS AND ECONOMIC STABILITY

AND GROWTH. WHILE THE GOVERNING COUNCIL OF THE

EUROPEAN CENTRAL BANK SETS MONETARY POLICY, NCBS

IMPLEMENT THIS POLICY. WHILE THE ECB\'S COUNCIL FIXES

CRITERIA FOR INVESTMENTS OF FOREIGN CURRENCY RESERVES

AND SETS FOREIGN EXCHANGE POLICY (ALONG GUIDELINES

APPROVED BY FINANCE MINISTERS) , THE NCBS HOLD AND

INVEST A SUBSTANTIAL PART OF THE RESERVES AND THE

LARGER NCB\'S CONDUCT FOREIGN EXCHANGE OPERATIONS.

5. (U) THE ECB HOLDS EURO 46.8 BILLION OF THE

EUROSYSTEM\'S EURO 386.7 BILLION IN OFFICIAL RESERVE

ASSETS. THE REST ARE HELD BY NCBS IN ACCORDANCE WITH

ECB GUIDELINES AND INSTRUCTIONS. THE DISTRIBUTION OF

RESERVES HAS BEEN SET BY THE GOVERNING COUNCIL AND

LEGAL STATUS OF THE BOI AND NATIONAL CENTRAL BANKS

--------------------------------------------- -----

15. (U) THE BANK OF ITALY, LIKE EACH OF THE NCBS, IS A

SEPARATE LEGAL ENTITY ACCORDING TO ITALIAN LAW. A

LEGISLATIVE DECREE OF THE ITALIAN PARLIAMENT STATES:

"THE BANK OF ITALY, THE CENTRAL BANK OF THE ITALIAN

REPUBLIC, IS AN INTEGRAL PART OF THE ESCB. IT SHALL

PERFORM THE TASKS AND FUNCTIONS ATTRIBUTED TO IT IN

THAT CAPACITY IN COMPLIANCE WITH THE STATUTE OF THE

ESCB. IT SHALL PURSUE THE OBJECTIVES ASSIGNED TO THE

ESCB IN CONFORMITY WITH ARTICLE 105 OF THE TREATY AND

ACT IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE GUIDELINES AND THE

INSTITUTIONS OF THE ECB".

16. (U) DESPITE BEING SEPARATE LEGAL ENTITIES, THE NCBS

FORM AN INTEGRAL PART OF THE EUROSYSTEM. AS SUCH, THE

BANK OF ITALY AND THE OTHER NCBS CARRY OUT THE TASKS

ASSIGNED TO THE EUROSYSTEM IN ACCORD WITH ECB RULES.

THROUGH THE PARTICIPATION OF THEIR RESPECTIVE

REPRESENTATIVES IN THE VARIOUS ESCB COMMITTEES, THE

BANK OF ITALY AND THE OTHER NCBS ALSO CONTRIBUTE TO THE

WORK OF THE ESCB. THE BANK OF ITALY MAY PERFORM

FUNCTIONS DEEMED APPROPRIATE BY THE ITALIAN GOVERNMENT,

AS LONG AS THE ECB GOVERNING COUNCIL FINDS THAT SUCH

FUNCTIONS DO NOT INTERFERE WITH THE OBJECTIVES AND

TASKS OF THE EUROSYSTEM.

ESCB COMMITTEES

---------------

17. (U) ESCB COMMITTEES PLAY AN IMPORTANT ROLE IN THE

EUROSYSTEM/ESCB. AT THE REQUEST OF BOTH THE GOVERNING

COUNCIL AND THE EXECUTIVE BOARD, THEY PROVIDE EXPERTISE

IN THEIR FIELDS OF COMPETENCE AND FACILITATE THE

DECISION MAKING PROCESS. ESCB COMMITTEES ARE COMPOSED

OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE NCBS, INCLUDING THE BANK OF

ITALY AND, WHERE APPROPRIATE, OTHER COMPETENT BODIES,

INCLUDING NATIONAL SUPERVISORY AUTHORITIES IN THE CASE

OF THE BANKING SUPERVISION COMMITTEE. THERE ARE

THIRTEEN ESCB COMMITTEES: ACCOUNTING AND MONETARY

INCOME (AMICO), BANKING SUPERVISION (SBC), BANKNOTE

COMMITTEE (BANCO), EXTERNAL COMMUNICATIONS (ECCO),

INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (ITC), INTERNAL AUDITORS (IAC),

INTERNATIONAL RELATIONS (IRC), LEGAL (LEGCO), MARKET

OPERATIONS (MOC), MONETARY POLICY (MPC), PAYMENT AND

SETTLEMENT SYSTEM (PSSC) AND STATISTICAL COMMITTEE

(STC). TWO REPRESENTATIVES OF THE BANK OF ITALY

PARTICIPATE IN EACH OF THESE COMMITTEES.

ECB EMPLOYMENT

--------------

18. (U) THE BANK OF ITALY AND THE OTHER ELEVEN MEMBER

COUNTRIES HAVE REPRESENTATIVES WITHIN THE ECB. THE

GOVERNOR OF THE BANK OF ITALY IS A MEMBER OF THE ECB

BOARD. OTHER BANK OF ITALY EMPLOYEES ARE TEMPORARILY

TRANSFERRED TO FRANKFURT FOR A THREE-YEAR TERM. THESE

PEOPLE REPRESENT THE ECB AS AN INSTITUTION AND NOT THE

INTEREST OF THEIR COUNTRY OR THEIR NATIONAL BANK.

CERTAIN BANK OF ITALY REPRESENTATIVES PARTICIPATE IN

MONTHLY ECB MEETINGS. OTHERS PARTICIPATE IN WORKING

GROUPS AS REPRESENTATIVES OF THE BANK OF ITALY.

19. (SBU) ACCORDING TO MANFRED KOLBER, ECB

COMMUNICATIONS DIRECTOR, THE ECB EMPLOYS ABOUT 1,100

PEOPLE. THE DISTRIBUTION OF ECB EMPLOYMENT BY

NATIONALITY IS NOT FULLY IN LINE WITH THE NCB\'S

CONTRIBUTION TO ECB CAPITAL. KOLBER SAID THAT IN HIS

DEPARTMENT, THE CRITERIA FOR RECRUITMENT ARE

CANDIDATE\'S PROFESSIONAL AND LANGUAGE SKILLS. ONLY IN

A CASE OF EQUALLY QUALIFIED CANDIDATES MIGHT

NATIONALITY BECOME A CRITERION, TO COMPENSATE UNDER-

EURO, WHICH IS EXPECTED TO INCREASE TO FIVE BILLION

EURO OVER THE NEXT FEW YEARS. THE PRINCIPAL PURPOSE OF

ECB CAPITAL IS TO ACT AS A RESERVE FUND, PROVIDING THE

BANK WITH SUFFICIENT INCOME WHILE MAINTAINING AN

EUROSYSTEM IS STILL BASED ON THE BASIC PRINCIPLE OF

"CENTRALIZED DECISION-MAKING, DECENTRALIZED

OPERATIONS". NATIONAL CENTRAL BANKS, INCLUDING THE

BANK OF ITALY, CONTINUE TO PLAY A KEY ROLE. AS THE

EUROSYSTEM EXPANDS THROUGH EU ENLARGEMENT, THE

INEFFICIENCIES AND COSTS OF DECENTRALIZED OPERATIONS

ARE LIKELY TO INCREASE. THIS WILL BE A CHALLENGE FOR

THE EUROSYSTEM. THE LARGEST AND MORE EFFICIENT -- SUCH

AS THE BANK OF ITALY -- ARE EXPECTED TO HOLD THE SYSTEM

TOGETHER. THE EUROSYSTEM MAY ONLY BE AS STRONG AS

THESE KEY NATIONAL CENTRAL BANKS.
