1. (C) SECRETARY TAURAN IS TRAVELLING TO BUCHAREST

SPECIFICALLY TO MEET WITH THE SECRETARY. HE TOLD

AMBASSADOR NICHOLSON ON NOVEMBER 20(REF A) THAT THE

POPE HAD INSTRUCTED HIM TO MEET THE SECRETARY AS

QUICKLY AS POSSIBLE. THE FOREIGN MINISTER SAID THAT

THE TWO TOPICS THAT THE POPE ASKED HIM TO RAISE WITH

THE SECRETARY ARE THE CURRENT SITUATION IN AFGHANISTAN

AND THE MIDDLE EAST.

2. (C) AFGHANISTAN: VATICAN CONCERNS AT THE U.S.-LED

AFGHANISTAN CAMPAIGN BOIL DOWN TO FEARS OF AN

UNCONTROLLED HUMANITARIAN CRISIS, DISTRUST OF THE

PAKISTAN GOVERNMENT, AND A JUDGMENT THAT IRAQ SHOULD

NOT BE THE TARGET OF ANY EXTENSION OF THE CONFLICT.

WORRIES OF A HUMANITARIAN MELTDOWN TRIGGERED A NUMBER

OF PUBLIC STATEMENTS FROM THE POPE, NONE OF WHICH

CRITICIZED OF THE WAR EFFORT. INDEED, THE POPE

RESERVED THE HARSHEST CRITICISM THAT WE\'VE EVER SEEN

FROM HIM FOR THE PERPETRATORS OF THIS "INHUMAN ATTACK

OF SEPTEMBER 11." FM TAURAN HIMSELF SUPPORTED THE

U.S.\' RIGHT TO SELF DEFENSE (A KEY COMPONENT, ALONG

WITH PROPORTIONALITY OF RESPONSE, OF THE VATICAN\'S

THEORY OF JUST WAR) IN AN INTERVIEW WITH THE FRENCH

MAGAZINE LA CROIX. VATICAN FM EQUIVALENT CARDINAL

SODANO EXPRESSED NOVEMBER 19 HIS HOPE THAT THE FALL OF

THE TALIBAN WOULD "LEAD TO THE FORMATION OF A

DEMOCRATIC STATE, RESPECT FOR HUMAN RIGHTS, FREEDOM

AND ESPECIALLY RELIGIOUS LIBERTY." HE ALSO DEBUNKED

THE NOTION THAT THE AFGHANISTAN CONFLICT IS

THREATENING INTER-RELIGIOUS DIALOGUE.

3. (C) CARITAS, A VATICAN AID ARM, HIGHLIGHTED THE

VATICAN\'S CONCERN BY LAUNCHING A WORLDWIDE APPEAL FOR

HUMANITARIAN RELIEF FOR AFGHANISTAN ON 10/25,

INITIALLY SEEKING USD 11 MILLION. ABOUT THE SAME

TIME, THE VATICAN DFM CONVEYED THAT SHOULD THE

CONFLICT DRAG ON AND THE HUMANITARIAN CRISIS THUS

WORSEN, THE VATICAN WOULD FIND IT HARD TO REFRAIN FROM

TAKING A MORE CRITICAL STANCE TOWARD THE CAMPAIGN.

VATICAN WORRIES HAVE LESSENED SOMEWHAT FOLLOWING ANTI-

TALIBAN TERRITORIAL GAINS, WHICH THE VATICAN HOPES

FAVOR THE RESUMPTION, INCREASE, AND MORE EFFICIENT

DISTRIBUTION OF HUMANITARIAN ASSISTANCE. AFGHANISTAN

AND THE VATICAN SHARE NO HISTORICAL RELATIONSHIP AND

CHRISTIANS IN AFGHANISTAN ARE FEW IN NUMBER, THESE

BEING THE FACTORS THAT COMMONLY DRIVE VATICAN

BILATERAL FOREIGN POLICY. STILL, THE VATICAN EXPRESSES

CONCERN THAT THE CONFLICT COULD UNDERMINE THE

STABILITY OF PAKISTAN, THUS WORSENING THE DIFFICULT

SITUATION OF THAT NATION\'S ONE MILLION CATHOLICS. THE

VATICAN MFA MOREOVER DOUBTS THE STEADINESS AND

TRUSTWORTHINESS OF GENERAL MUSHARRAF, REPROACHED FOR A

FAILURE TO PROTECT CHRISTIAN MINORITIES AND FOR

FOMENTING VIOLENCE IN KASHMIR. LASTLY, THE VATICAN

MFA HAS INDICATED -- QUIETLY THUS FAR -- THAT IT WOULD

VIEW POORLY AN EXTENSION OF THE WAR INTO IRAQ (WHERE

MANY MORE CHALCEDONIAN CHRISTIANS ENJOY RELATIVE --

FOR AN ISLAMIC COUNTRY -- FREEDOM UNDER THE BAATHIST

REGIME). INDEED, THE DIPLOMATIC COMMUNITY WILL BE

LISTENING ATTENTIVELY SHOULD THE POPE PUBLICLY DECLARE

THAT IRAQ MUST REMAIN OFF-LIMITS.

4. (C) THE MIDDLE EAST: THE VATICAN WISHES TO CLARIFY

ITS POSITION ON THE MIDDLE EAST. IT IS A POSITION

THAT HAS EVOLVED SUBSTANTIALLY OVER THE LAST THREE

YEARS. FOR MANY YEARS, THE VATICAN CALLED FOR THE

"INTERNATIONALIZATION" OF JERUSALEM. IN THE LATE 90\'S

THIS POSITION CHANGED TO THE "INTERNATIONALIZATION" OF

JERUSALEM "INTRAMURA" (WITHIN THE WALLS). FOR

APPROXIMATELY A YEAR NOW, THE VATICAN HAS REFORMULATED

ITS POSITION. IT HAS DROPPED THE CALL FOR

INTERNATIONALIZATION. IT IS NOW ASKING THAT THE

SHRINES OF THE THREE RELIGIONS PRESERVE THEIR UNIQUE

AND SACRED CHARACTER THROUGH "INTERNATIONAL

GUARANTEES", IN SUCH A WAY THAT IN THE FUTURE "NONE OF

THE PARTIES WILL BE ABLE TO CLAIM FOR ITSELF EXCLUSIVE

CONTROL OF THESE SACRED PARTS OF THE CITY". POLITICAL

SOVEREIGNTY OVER THE CITY WOULD BE A MATTER FOR THE

ISRAELIS AND THE PALESTINIANS TO NEGOTIATE. THE POPE

HAS PUBLICLY CALLED FOR BOTH/BOTH SIDES TO STOP THE

SPIRAL OF VIOLENCE. ANOTHER INDICATION THAT THE

VATICAN IS ATTEMPTING TO PLAY A CONSTRUCTIVE ROLE IN

THE PEACE PROCESS IS THE OCTOBER 30 MEETING BETWEEN

POPE JOHN PAUL AND CHAIRMAN ARAFAT. AT THAT MEETING
