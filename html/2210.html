1.(C) VATICAN FOREIGN MINISTER JEAN-LOUIS TAURAN CALLED

CONFIDENTIAL

AMBASSADOR NICHOLSON IN FOR A MEETING TUESDAY, NOVEMBER

20. THE FOREIGN MINISTER SAID HE HAD MET WITH POPE JOHN

PAUL II THE PREVIOUS EVENING, AND THE POPE INSTRUCTED HIM

TO TRAVEL TO WASHINGTON AS QUICKLY AS POSSIBLE. DESPITE

ITS OVERALL SUPPORT FOR THIS CONDUCT OF THE WAR, SO FAR,

THE VATICAN REMAINS CONCERNED WITH BOTH THE CURRENT

SITUATION IN AFGHANISTAN, AS WELL AS THE

ISRAELI/PALESTINIAN CONFLICT. THE FOREIGN MINISTER

SPECIFICALLY SAID HE WOULD LIKE TO CLARIFY TO THE

SECRETARY THE VATICAN\'S POSITION ON JERUSALEM.

SIPDIS

2.(C) TAURAN PROPOSES FLYING TO WASHINGTON ON SATURDAY,

DECEMBER 1. HE IS REQUESTING APPOINTMENTS WITH THE

SECRETARY AND DR. RICE ON MONDAY, DECEMBER 3 OR TUESDAY

SIPDIS

DECEMBER 4. HE WOULD ALSO BE AVAILABLE WEDNESDAY,

DECEMBER 5, BUT WOULD PREFER DECEMBER 3 OR 4 IF POSSIBLE.

IN ADDITION, HE WOULD WELCOME THE OPPORTUNITY TO MEET

WITH PRESIDENT BUSH IF THE PRESIDENT\'S SCHEDULE WOULD

PERMIT.

3.(C) COMMENT: THE FOREIGN MINISTER RAISED IN A POSITIVE

LIGHT HIS NOVEMBER 5 TELEPHONE CONVERSATION WITH

SECRETARY POWELL (REF), DURING WHICH THEY DISCUSSED A

SIPDIS

FACE TO FACE MEETING SOON. A FOLLOW-UP MEETING WOULD BE

EXTREMELY USEFUL IN SHORING UP VATICAN SUPPORT FOR THE

U.S. FIGHT AGAINST TERRORISM AND DEFLECTING ANY NEGATIVE

BLOWBACK AS WE GO FORWARD. IN ADDITION, EXPLAINING

CURRENT U.S. PROPOSALS FOR A COMPREHENSIVE PEACE IN THE

MIDDLE EAST WOULD BE USEFUL IN ELICITING VATICAN

COOPERATION. NICHOLSON
