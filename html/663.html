SUBJECT: TURKEY DESTROYS PKK\'S ZALEH CAMP -- AGAIN

1. (U) THE TURKISH PRESS REPORTED ON MAY 19TH THAT

TURKISH F-16S AND F-4S HAD AGAIN BOMBED THE PKK\'S ZALEH

CAMP IN NORTHERN IRAQ, AND BOASTED THAT THE CAMP WAS

"RAZED TO THE GROUND."

2. (C) THE TAF HAD PREVIOUSLY BOMBED ZALEH CAMP ON

JANUARY 29, AND AT THAT TIME CLAIMED THAT HUNDREDS OF PKK

PAGE 02 RUFTWPA1266 C O N F I D E N T I A L ADANA 0290

MILITANTS WERE KILLED. HOWEVER, THE PKK WAS WARNED OF THE

JANUARY RAID, ALLEGEDLY BECAUSE ADVANCE NOTIFICATION GIVEN

TO IRAQI KURDISH LEADER JALAL TALABANI WAS PASSED ON TO

THE PKK. AS A RESULT, DESPITE TURKISH CLAIMS OF HUNDREDS

OF CASUALTIES IN THE JANUARY RAID, CONSULATE RECEIVED

CREDIBLE REPORTS FROM THE AREA THAT PUT THE NUMBER OF

FATALITIES AT A FRACTION OF THAT, PERHAPS AS LOW AS 10.

AT ANY RATE, THE PKK QUICKLY RECLAIMED THE CAMP AND MOVED

BACK.

3. (C) THIS TIME, THERE WAS NO WARNING, AND THE TAF

CLAIMS OF HEAVY PKK CASUALTIES MAY BE MORE ACCURATE.

SECONDHAND REPORTS COMING OUT OF NORTHERN IRAQ INDICATE

THAT AS MANY AS 100-200 PKK MILITANTS MAY HAVE BEEN KILLED

IN THE RAID. INJURED MILITANTS WERE REPORTEDLY BROUGHT AS

FAR AS DAHUK HOSPITAL FOR TREATMENT, INDICATING THAT

FACILTIES CLOSER TO THE CAMP SITE MAY HAVE BEEN

OVERLOADED.

4. (C) COMMENT: CONSULATE HAS BECOME EXTREMELY CYNICAL

ABOUT OFFICIAL CASUALTY FIGURES RELEASED AFTER LARGE

OPERATIONS LIKE THE AIR STRIKE AGAINST ZALEH CAMP, OR THE

RECENT CROSS-BORDER OPERATION. IRRESPECTIVE OF THE TOTAL

CASUALTIES, HOWEVER, SUCH STRIKES MAY FORCE THE PKK TO

CHANGE ITS TACTICS, SCATTER ITS MILITANTS INTO SMALLER

OPERATING UNITS, AND STOP CONGREGATING IN LARGE CAMPS THAT

ARE EASILY RECOGNIZABLE AND HARD TO DEFEND FROM AIR

ATTACKS. END COMMENT.
