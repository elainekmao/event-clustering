1.(C) SUMMARY: VATICAN OFFICIALS AND OBSERVERS DESCRIBE

POPE JOHN PAUL II\'S SEPTEMBER 22-27 TRIP TO KAZAKHSTAN AND

ARMENIA AS SUCCESSFUL. THE VATICAN HAD THREE OBJECTIVES FOR

THIS TRIP: 1) CONTINUE THE PRESSURE ON THE RUSSIAN ORTHODOX

CHURCH TO ALLOW A VISIT TO MOSCOW; 2) BOLSTER THE "MODERATE

ISLAMIC" GOVERNMENT IN KAZAKHSTAN AND HOLD IT UP AS AN

EXAMPLE; 3) INCREMENTALLY MOVE FORWARD THE DIALOG BETWEEN

THE ROMAN CATHOLIC CHURCH AND THE ARMENIAN APOSTOLIC CHURCH

WHICH THE POPE HOPES WILL LEAD TO REUNIFICATION. END

SUMMARY

2.(C) EMBASSY OFFICERS MET WITH SEVERAL VATICAN OFFICIALS

AND OTHER DIPLOMATIC OBSERVERS IN WAKE OF POPE JOHN PAUL

II\'S VISIT TO KAZAKHSTAN AND ARMENIA SEPTEMBER 22-27.

VATICAN DIRECTOR FOR RUSSIA AND THE FORMER STATES OF THE

SOVIET UNION, ARCHBISHOP TRICARICO TOLD DCM THAT THE VISIT

WAS AN IMPORTANT STEP IN FURTHER ISOLATING RUSSIAN ORTHODOX

PATRIARCH ALEXI AND MAINTAINING PRESSURE TO PERMIT A VISIT

TO MOSCOW IN THE RELATIVELY NEAR FUTURE. TRICARICO SAID

THAT WITH THE VISIT TO KAZAKHSTAN, THE ANNOUNCED 2002 VISIT

TO BULGARIA, AND PROBABLE VISIT TO BELARUS, THE POPE WILL

HAVE "SURROUNDED" ALEXI. MORE IMPORTANTLY, THE POPE WILL

HAVE SHOWN ALEXI THAT RUSSIAN ORTHODOX CLAIMS OF

TERRITORIAL-CANONICAL PREROGATIVES ARE MEANINGLESS IN THE

MODERN WORLD. IRONICALLY, ON OCTOBER 2, RUSSIAN EMBASSY TO

THE VATICAN DCM, DMITRI SHTODIN SECONDED THIS VIEW TO DCM.

SHTODIN SAID THAT HIS GOVERNMENT WAS PUTTING "PRESSURE" ON

ALEXI TO INVITE THE POPE TO MOSCOW. HE NOTED THE RELATIVELY

MODERATE STATEMENTS OF THE RUSSIAN ORTHODOX\'S CHURCH

"MINISTER FOR FOREIGN RELATIONS" KYRIL CONCERNING THE POPE

VISIT TO KAZAKHSTAN AS AN INDICATION THAT THE GOVERNMENT IS

HAVING AN INFLUENCE. (NOTE: THE RUSSIAN ORTHODOX CHURCH DID

NOT COMPLAIN ABOUT THE POPE\'S VISIT TO ARMENIA AS THE

ARMENIAN APOSTOLIC CHURCH IS NOT IN COMMUNION WITH THE

THE ISSUE WAS DISCUSSED WITH THE ARMENIAN PRESIDENT.

3.(C) VATICAN DEPUTY FOREIGN MINISTER MIGLIORE (SEPTEL),

TOLD POLOFF AND DCM THAT THE VATICAN IS CONVINCED THAT THE

RUSSIAN ORTHODOX ARE FEELING THE PRESSURE, AND THAT A TRIP

TO MOSCOW IS NO LONGER AN UNREACHABLE GOAL. DCM ALSO MET

WITH FATHER GIOVANNI MATERA, THE RECTOR OF THE BASILICA OF

SAINT NICOLAS IN BARI, ON OCTOBER 8. ALTHOUGH A ROMAN

CATHOLIC CHURCH, BARI AND THE CHURCH ARE THE CENTER OF

RUSSIAN ORTHODOX CHURCH\'S OFFICIAL REPRESENTATION TO ITALY

(HAVING REJECTED ROME BECAUSE OF THE POPE\'S PRESENCE).

FATHER GIOVANNI SAID THAT THERE WILL BE A CATHOLIC/ORTHODOX

MEETING HOSTED BY THE BASILICA ON NOVEMBER 25 WHICH

METROPOLITAN KYRIL IS EXPECTED TO ATTEND; THE QUESTION OF

THE POPE\'S VISIT TO MOSCOW IS ON THE AGENDA.

4.(C) COMMENT: IN ADDITION TO FURTHER ISOLATING PATRIARCH

ALEXI, THE VISIT TO KAZAKHSTAN WAS DESIGNED TO EXTOL

RELIGIOUS TOLERANCE IN A PROMINENTLY MUSLIM COUNTRY.

KAZAKHSTAN IS THE FIRST MUSLIM COUNTRY WHICH HAS CONCLUDED A

CONCORDAT (EG. A TREATY REGULATING RIGHTS AND OBLIGATIONS OF

THE CATHOLIC CHURCH) WITH THE VATICAN. THE VISIT TO

KAZAKHSTAN IS PART OF THE VATICAN\'S AGENDA FOR THE POPE TO

5.(C) COMMENT CONT\'D: THE MAIN THRUST OF THE VISIT TO

ARMENIA WAS TO FURTHER THE PROCESS OF DIALOG BETWEEN THE

ROMAN CATHOLIC CHURCH AND THE APOSTOLIC CATHOLIC CHURCH. A

SIDE BENEFIT WAS THE FACT THAT ARMENIA, BEING ANOTHER FORMER

SOVIET REPUBLIC, WAS AN ADDITIONAL BLATANT REMINDER OF THE

MOSCOW PATRIARCH\'S OBSTINACY. THEOLOGICALLY, THE ROMAN

CHURCH AND THE ARMENIAN CHURCH HAVE MADE GREAT STRIDES IN

ELIMINATING THEIR DIFFERENCES. THE REMAINING PROBLEMS

INCLUDE THE ROLE OF THE POPE, AS WELL AS THE ARMENIAN

CHURCH\'S COMMUNION WITH THE COPTIC CHURCH WHICH WANTS TO GO

MUCH MORE SLOWLY ON ANY DISCUSSIONS ON REUNIFICATION WITH
