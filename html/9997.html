SUBJECT: STOPPING ITALIAN FAST BOAT TRANSFERS TO IRAN

REF: A. SECSTATE 230222

B. ROME 3386 AND PREVIOUS

CLASSIFIED BY: ACTING ECONOMIC MINISTER COUNSELOR DAVID MULENEX FOR REA

SONS 1.5 (B), (C) AND (D)

1. (S) ACTING ECMIN AND ECONOFF RAISED REFTEL POINTS AUGUST 8

WITH THE MFA\'S MASSIMO LEGGERI, DEPUTY DIRECTOR GENERAL FOR

ECONOMIC COOPERATION AND DEVELOPMENT (IN THE ABSENCE OF

DIRECTOR GENERAL GIANDOMENICO MAGLIANO, WITH WHOM PM DAS TURK

MAGGI DISCUSSED THIS ISSUE ON JULY 22). LEGGERI TOOK CAREFUL

NOTE OF THE NEW INFORMATION REGARDING FB DESIGN\'S RECENT

CONTACT WITH IRANIAN ENTITIES, AS WELL AS THE SHIPMENT OF TEN

DRIVES FROM THE FIRM ZF TRIMAX TO AN IRANIAN MILITARY

PROCUREMENT FIRM. HE PROMISED TO INQUIRE ABOUT THE ACTIVITIES

OF BOTH COMPANIES AND WOULD BE BACK IN TOUCH WITH US (HE DID

NOT SAY WHETHER MFA NONPROLIFERATION COORDINATOR UGO DE MOHR

HAD OBTAINED ANY FURTHER INFORMATION FROM FB DESIGN SINCE

JULY 22 -- DE MOHR IS NOW ON LEAVE).

2. (S) A/ECMIN ALSO RAISED OUR CONTINUED OPPOSITION TO

ITALY\'S PROPOSED SALE OF BOATS AND NIGHT VISION EQUIPMENT TO

LIBYA. LEGGERI RESPONDED THAT THE GOI UNDERSTANDS U.S.

OBJECTIONS, BUT VIEWS LIBYA MUCH DIFFERENTLY THAN IT DOES

IRAN.
