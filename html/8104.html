1. SUMMARY: A NEW SECTORAL LABOR CONTRACT FOR ITALY\'S

METALWORKERS HAMMERED OUT MAY 8 HAS REVERBERATED ACROSS THE

BUSINESS AND LABOR LANDSCAPE. BOTH ITS CONTENT AND

NEGOTIATION ARE IMPORTANT STEPS FORWARD IN MODERNIZING

TRADITIONAL PRACTICES AND POLICIES THAT HAVE LONG BEDEVILED

THE ITALIAN ECONOMY. LABOR\'S SPLIT OVER THE PACT HAS

RENEWED CALLS BY THE SECTOR\'S LARGEST, MOST MILITANT

FEDERATION FOR LEGISLATION REQUIRING VERIFICATION OF UNIONS\'

ACTUAL REPRESENTATION WITHIN A SECTOR. AT A MINIMUM, THE

PACT OFFERS AT LEAST ONE INDUSTRIAL SECTOR THE PROSPECT OF

RELAXING THE RIGID NATIONAL CONTRACTS THAT HAVE STIFLED WAGE

FLEXIBILITY AND LIMITED PRODUCTIVITY GAINS, TWO FREQUENT

DRAGS ON ITALIAN COMPETITIVENESS. MOREOVER, ITS INNOVATIVE

PROVISIONS MAY CARRY OVER AS PRECEDENTS INTO RENEGOTIATION

THIS SUMMER OF ITALY\'S GLOBAL PACT GOVERNING COLLECTIVE

BARGAINING AGREEMENTS, A PROSPECT THAT HAS THE THREE MAIN

LABOR CONFEDERATIONS WRESTLING OVER THE SHAPE OF THEIR

FUTURE RELATIONSHIP WITH MANAGEMENT.

2. FEDERMECCANICA, THE METAL MANUFACTURERS\' ASSOCIATION,

SIGNED A NEW SECTORAL CONTRACT WITH TWO OF ITALY\'S THREE

PRINCIPAL METALWORKER FEDERATIONS MAY 8. THE NEW CONTRACT

HAS BEEN HAILED AS AN INNOVATIVE DEPARTURE FROM PAST

SECTORAL AGREEMENTS. THE SPECIFICS SUPPORT THAT ASSESSMENT,

IN THAT THE CONTRACT:

- REPLACES RIGID WAGE INDEXATION TO GOVERNMENT INFLATION

PROJECTIONS WITH A SET OF PRE-NEGOTIATED ADJUSTMENTS DERIVED

FROM CURRENT INFLATION ESTIMATES;

- CREATES A NEW LABOR-MANAGEMENT COUNCIL TO MANAGE TRAINING

AND OTHER LABOR SERVICES WITHIN THE SECTOR;

- AUTHORIZES OVERHAUL OF A 30-YEAR POSITION CLASSIFICATION

SYSTEM, WITH THE IDEA OF ELIMINATING A RIGID 7-TIER

STRUCTURE IN FAVOR OF MORE FLEXIBLE SYSTEM OF PAY \'BANDS."

THE CONTRACT ALSO INCLUDES PROVISIONS THAT WILL PERMIT

LOCAL/REGIONAL BONUSES FOR PRODUCTIVITY INCREASES; PRIOR

CONTRACTS REQUIRED THE MAIN PORTION OF PRODUCTIVITY BONUSES

TO BE APPORTIONED NATIONALLY TO ALL SECTORAL WORKERS.

3. NOT SURPRISINGLY, THE PACT\'S MOST INNOVATIVE PROVISIONS

FUELED FURTHER DISCORD AMONG THE UNION CONFEDERATIONS. THE

DEAL PROVOKED AN ANGRY REBUTTAL FROM FIOM, THE LARGEST

METALWORKERS FEDERATION (WITH SOME 30% OF ALL EMPLOYEES IN

THE SECTOR, AND OVER HALF OF UNIONIZED WORKERS, ON ITS

ROLLS). FIOM\'S PARENT CONFEDERATION, CGIL, IS ITALY\'S

LARGEST AND MOST MILITANT TRADE UNION CONFEDERATION. IT HAS

LONG OPPOSED ANY CHANGES TO ITALY\'S TRADITIONAL PRACTICE OF

NATIONAL CONTRACTS THAT SET WAGES, BONUSES AND WORK

CONDITIONS FOR ALL WORKERS, FEARING THEY WOULD ERODE

WORKERS\' WAGES/LIVING STANDARDS AS EMPLOYERS SHOP FOR THE

LOWEST POSSIBLE LABOR COSTS IN THE COUNTRY. THE OTHER

CONFEDERATIONS\' AGREEMENT TO THE CONTRACT NOT ONLY LEAVES

FIOM/CGIL STANDING ALONE IN PRINCIPLE; IT ALSO LEAVES THE

FEDERATION CONFRONTING A LEGALLY-BINDING CONTRACT THAT

APPLIES TO ITS MEMBERS AS WELL.

4. ALTHOUGH ITALY\'S CONSTITUTION UPHOLDS THE CONCEPT OF

COLLECTIVE REPRESENTATIVE CONTRACTS THAT APPLY TO ALL

WORKERS IN A SECTOR, PARLIAMENT HAS NEVER ADOPTED

LEGISLATION TO IMPLEMENT THE PRINCIPLE IN PRACTICE. AS A

RESULT, THE JUDICIARY STANDS AS SOLE ARBITER OF WHETHER A

CONTRACT IS VALID AND "REPRESENTATIVE." PAST RULINGS

GENERALLY HAVE DEEMED LABOR AGREEMENTS NEGOTIATED AND SIGNED

BY UNIONS AFFILIATED WITH THE MAIN CONFEDERATIONS, EVEN IF

THEY REPRESENT A MINORITY OF WORKERS, AS REPRESENTATIVE AND

APPLICABLE TO ALL WORKERS IN THE SECTOR. FIOM/CGIL IS

EXPECTED TO EMPLOY DECIDEDLY ITALIAN TACTICS TO CONFRONT THE

"IMPOSED CONTRACT," NOISILY STRIKING TO PROTEST THE NEW

PACT\'S PROVISIONS AND THREATENING TO CHALLENGE LEGALLY ITS

APPLICABILITY WHILE ITS MEMBERS QUIETLY POCKET THE WAGE

INCREASES (WHICH IT CLAIMED WERE INADEQUATE) PROVIDED UNDER

THE AGREEMENT.

5. EMPLOYERS ARE PLEASED, VIEWING THE AGREEMENT AS THE FIRST

PRACTICAL MANIFESTATION OF THE OTHER TWO UNION

CONFEDERATIONS\' COMMITMENT TO A NEW TYPE OF LABOR-MANAGEMENT

COOPERATION. THAT COMMITMENT WAS FIRST AGREED BY THE

GOVERNMENT, THE TWO CONFEDERATIONS AND EMPLOYERS IN LAST

SUMMER\'S "PACT FOR ITALY," A BROAD STATEMENT OF PRINCIPLES

THAT FIRST SPLIT THE THREE LABOR CONFEDERATIONS (WITH CGIL

REFUSING TO SIGN AND STRIKING IN PROTEST). EMPLOYERS\'

ASSOCIATIONS BELIEVE THE METALWORKERS CONTRACT WILL

STIMULATE BUSINESS AND INVESTMENT AND, PERHAPS, STIMULATE

GROWTH IN SOUTHERN ITALY BY ALLOWING COMPANIES TO TAKE

ADVANTAGE OF REGIONAL INCOME/COST-OF-LIVING DISPARITIES.

6. EVEN AS IMMEDIATE ATTENTION SHIFTS TO NEGOTIATION OF

OTHER SECTORAL CONTRACTS, BOTH LABOR AND MANAGEMENT HAVE

THEIR GAZES FIXED FIRMLY ON RENEGOTIATION LATER THIS SUMMER

OF THE UMBRELLA ACCORD GOVERNING THE TERMS OF COLLECTIVE

BARGAINING AGREEMENTS. THE PARTIES TO THE METALWORKERS

CONTRACT WILL WORK TO EMBED ITS PROVISIONS IN THIS UPDATED

UMBRELLA AGREEMENT; CGIL WILL TRY TO ENSURE THEY ARE

CONFINED TO THIS ONE SECTOR.

7. THE AGREEMENT WILL PROVOKE DEBATE, IF NOT MUCH MOVEMENT,

ON LONG-MORIBUND LEGISLATION TO FLESH OUT THE CONSTITUTIONAL

PROVISION GUARANTEEING REPRESENTATIVE CONTRACTS. FIOM/CGIL

AND LIKE-MINDED LEGISLATORS FROM THE FAR LEFT HAVE VOWED TO

PRESS FOR THE LEGISLATION\'S PASSAGE. CISL, THE SECOND

LARGEST, "CATHOLIC" CONFEDERATION, HAS DENOUNCED THIS AS A

GAMBIT TO GIVE THE FEDERATION, ITALY\'S LARGEST, DE FACTO

VETO POWER OVER ANY FUTURE CONTRACT; IT ARGUES THAT LABOR

AND MANAGEMENT SHOULD WORK OUT A MUTUALLY-ACCEPTABLE

SOLUTION WITHOUT INVOLVING PARLIAMENT. THE SMALLEST OF THE

THREE CONFEDERATIONS, CENTRIST UIL PRIVATELY AGREES WITH

CGIL\'S POSITION BUT IS HOLDING ITS TONGUE FOR TACTICAL

REASONS.

8. COMMENT: THE METALWORKERS\' INNOVATIVE CONTRACT IS

ANOTHER SIGN THAT THE MOST SUCCESSFUL ASPECT OF THE

BERLUSCONI GOVERNMENT\'S COMPREHENSIVE REFORM PLAN WILL BE IN

THE AREA OF LABOR MARKETS (REFTEL). IN SOME RESPECTS,

THAT\'S NOT SURPRISING, SINCE THE REFORMS TO DATE COME

LARGELY WITH SUPPORT OF MUCH OF ORGANIZED LABOR; GIVEN

ITALY\'S TRADITION AND CULTURE OF CONSENSUS DECISION-MAKING,

THE IMPORTANCE OF THAT SUPPORT SHOULDN\'T BE UNDERESTIMATED.

TO TRY TO MAINTAIN THAT APPROACH, EMPLOYERS MAY WELL DECIDE

TO ACCEPT A MORE LIMITED SET OF THESE INNOVATIONS IN THE NEW

ACCORD GOVERNING COLLECTIVE BARGAINING AGREEMENTS IN ORDER

TO SECURE CGIL\'S APPROVAL. THE GOVERNMENT FACES A MUCH

TOUGHER TEST ON PENSION REFORM GIVEN THE CONFEDERATIONS\'

UNIFIED OPPOSITION TO MOST OF ITS PROPOSED LEGISLATION

(SEPTEL). THE OUTCOME OF THAT DEBATE WILL GO A LONG WAY TO

DETERMINING WHETHER THE BERLUSCONI REFORM SCORECARD WILL BE

MODEST OR MEANINGFUL. THERE\'S LITTLE LIKELIHOOD THAT CGIL\'S

PUSH FOR REPRESENTATIVE CONTACT LEGISLATION WILL AMOUNT TO

ANYTHING; SPLITS AMONG THE CONFEDERATIONS MIRROR THOSE

WITHIN THE CENTER-LEFT OPPOSITION, AND THE GOVERNMENT\'S

PARLIAMENTARY MAJORITY IS SUFFICIENT IN ANY CASE TO REJECT

AN UNWANTED BILL.
