SUBJECT: UN/UNDP MISSION MEMBERS SEEK EMBASSY VIEWS

ON RESUMING LAND REFORM DIALOGUE WITH THE GOZ

REF: (A) HARARE 5514, (B) STATE 186563

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD FOR REASONS 1.5

(B) AND (D).

1. (C) SUMMARY: DURING AN HOUR\'S MEETING OCTOBER 2

THREE MEMBERS OF THE UN/UNDP MISSION SEARCHED FOR AN

OPENING IN OUR POSITION ON THE CURRENT LAND REFORM

IMPASSE TO REKINDLE DONOR DIALOGUE WITH THE GOVERNMENT

OF ZIMBABWE. THROUGHOUT THE DISCUSSION THE AMBASSADOR

AND EMBASSY OFFICERS STRESSED THAT THE USG WANTS TO BE

HELPFUL BUT RULE OF LAW AND MACROECONOMIC STABILITY MUST

BE ESTABLISHED IN ZIMBABWE. THE UN/UNDP TEAM SKETCHED

OUT THE LINES OF A POSSIBLE PACKAGE THAT AT ONCE WOULD

BE FACE SAVING FOR MUGABE, RETAINING THE FAST TRACK

APPROACH TO LAND REFORM, AND SATISFY DONOR QUALMS ABOUT

INVESTING HERE. ESSENTIALLY THE DONORS WOULD OFFER TO

PAY FOR 211 UNCONTESTED FARMS CURRENTLY ON OFFER AND THE

GOVERNMENT WOULD END THE LAWLESSNESS ON THE FARMS AND

EMBARK ON A DIALOGUE ON LARGER POLITICAL ISSUES.

WITHOUT KNOWING ALL THE DETAILS, WE WERE POSITIVE BUT

REGISTERED OUR CONCERN THAT THE PLAN BE RATIONAL AND

ACHIEVABLE. THE TEAM APPEARS GENUINELY INTERESTED IN

REACHING A RESOLUTION TO THE CURRENT IMPASSE AND IT IS

FACILITATING THE SORT OF COMMUNICATION AND DIPLOMACY

THAT IS CURRENTLY IN SHORT SUPPLY HERE. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) ON OCTOBER 3 UN/UNDP MISSION MEMBERS CHONCHOL,

OKOTH AND BARUT MET WITH AMBASSADOR MCDONALD, THE DCM,

ACTING USAID DIRECTOR MARTIN AND USAID OFFICER LOKEN FOR

AN HOUR TO TAKE SOUNDINGS ON THIS EMBASSY\'S VIEW ON

RESUMING DIALOGUE WITH THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE ON

LAND REFORM. BY WAY OF PREFACE, AMBASSADOR MCDONALD

WELCOMED THE MISSION MEMBERS AND CONVEYED HIS

UNDERSTANDING OF THE COMPLEXITY OF THEIR TASK. HE

CONTINUED THAT THE USG VIEWED A TRANSPARENT AND ORDERLY

LAND REFORM PROGRAM AS NECESSARY FOR ZIMBABWE AND INDEED

A MATTER OF SOCIAL JUSTICE, HOWEVER, WHAT WAS OCCURRING

IN THE COUNTRY NOW DID NOT MEET THAT DEFINITION. HE

ARGUED THAT THE GOVERNMENT HAD TO RESTORE RULE OF LAW ON

THE FARMS AND THE USG REMAINED CONCERNED FOR THE FARM

WORKERS AND THEIR FAMILIES WHO WOULD BE DISPLACED IN THE

GOVERNMENT\'S FAST TRACK EXERCISE. IF PRESIDENT MUGABE

WERE TO MAKE A CLEAR STATEMENT THAT IT WAS TIME TO MOVE

OFF THE LAND, THE AMBASSADOR REASONED, MOST OF THE

OCCUPIERS WOULD LEAVE AND THE POLICE COULD MOP UP ANY

RECALCITRANT RINGLEADERS.

3. (C) AMBASSADOR MCDONALD CONTINUED THAT THE USG HAD

PLEDGED TO PROVIDE TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE TO ZIMBABWE\'S

LAND REFORM PROGRAM THROUGH THE UNIVERSITY OF WISCONSIN

LAND TENURE CENTER, BUT THAT RECENT EVENTS HAD SEVERELY

CURTAILED SUCH SUPPORTING ACTIVITIES. HE OBSERVED THAT

IMPLEMENTATION OF THE PROGRAM AND EVEN RESETTLEMENT, BUT

IT WOULD BE VERY DIFFICULT TO OBTAIN FUNDS TO BUY LAND.

HANDING OVER A NON-PAPER THAT REFLECTED DONOR

PERSPECTIVES ON WHAT WOULD BE NECESSARY TO RESUME

DIALOGUE ON LAND REFORM, THE AMBASSADOR ASKED THE

MISSION MEMBERS TO WORK CLOSELY WITH US, LEST THEY GET

AHEAD OF OUR POSITION ON THIS MATTER.

4. (C) CHONCHOL CONCEDED THAT THE MISSION HAD MET ONLY

WITH THE INDIGENOUS COMMERCIAL FARMER\'S UNION (ICFU) AND

THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS\' UNION (CFU) AND WOULD MEET WITH

THE MINISTRY OF AGRICULTURE ON OCTOBER 3. HE OBSERVED

THAT THE GOVERNMENT WANTED TO ACCELERATE ITS LAND REFORM

GOAL BUT IT HAD LITTLE MONEY, AND SO IT CHANGED THE

CONSTITUTION. "PEOPLE SAY," CHONCHOL CONTINUED, THAT

THERE\'S NEED FOR DIALOGUE AND THAT THE GOVERNMENT COULD

CONDUCT LAND REFORM IF IT HAD FUNDS. THE DONORS, ON THE

OTHER HAND, WILL NOT GIVE MONEY UNLESS THERE IS RESPECT

FOR THE LAW. "WHAT IS FOR YOU THE RULE OF LAW?"

AMBASSADOR MCDONALD REPLIED THAT THE U.S. WAS NOT

INTERESTED IN GETTING INTO A DEBATE ON SEMANTICS. THE

FARM OCCUPATIONS AND THE ATTENDANT VIOLENCE HAD BEEN A

ZANU-PF CAMPAIGN TACTIC, BUT NOW THE ELECTION WAS OVER.

5. (C) CHONCHOL THEN ASKED WHERE SHOULD THE PEOPLE

ALREADY ON THE FARMS BE PUT, SINCE TO REMOVE THEM

SUMMARILY WOULD PRESENT THE GOVERNMENT A POLITICAL

PROBLEM. THE AMBASSADOR RESPONDED THAT THE COMMERCIAL

FARMERS\' UNION HAD OFFERED FARMS VOLUNTARILY. CHONCHOL

ACKNOWLEDGED THAT 211 FARMS HAD BEEN OFFERED, BUT THAT

THE GOVERNMENT HAD NOT REACTED TO THE OFFER. THE

AMBASSADOR SPECULATED THAT THE GOVERNMENT WOULD ACCEPT

THE FARMS BUT WOULD WANT MORE. USAID OFFICER LOKEN

EXPLAINED THAT THE ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT HAD EXPENDED

ITS ENTIRE BUDGET AND NOW HAD NO MONEY TO PAY FOR FARMS.

6. (C) "COULD DONORS HELP WITH MONEY?" CHONCHOL

INQUIRED. THE AMBASSADOR RESPONDED THAT THE DONORS WERE

PREPARED TO EXPLORE SEVERAL OPTIONS, PROVIDED THE RULE

OF LAW WAS RESPECTED. OKOTH OBSERVED THAT THE RULE OF

LAW ARGUMENT APPEARED TO EMBRACE GOVERNANCE IN GENERAL

IN THE ZIMBABWEAN CONTEXT. "WOULD IT BE POSSIBLE TO

CORDON IT OFF ONLY TO APPLY TO LAND?" THE AMBASSADOR

ANSWERED THAT, "WE WANT TO FOCUS ON LAND, BUT LIFE

DOESN\'T HAPPEN IN A VACUUM. WE WANT TO SOLVE THE LAND

QUESTION, BUT THERE IS NO CLEAR LINE BETWEEN WHAT IS

HAPPENING ON THE FARMS AND IN THE REST OF THE COUNTRY\'S

POLITICAL LIFE." OKOTH REJOINED THAT IN 1998 THE DONORS

HAD COMMITTED TO PARTICIPATE IN THE LAND REFORM PROCESS,

WHEREAS NOW THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE COMPLAINED THAT

THE DONORS STARTED RAISING EXTRANEOUS ISSUES. USAID

OFFICER LOKEN REPLIED THAT THE RULE OF LAW QUESTION WAS

SIMILAR TO THE MACROECONOMIC SITUATION. UNTIL

MACROECONOMIC STABILITY WAS ACHIEVED, NO DONOR WOULD BE

PREPARED TO INVEST IN ZIMBABWE. OKOTH THEN SAID THAT

PERHAPS THE WAY TO PUT IT WAS THAT IF THE GOVERNMENT

MADE A COMMITMENT TO SOLVE THE LAWLESSNESS ISSUE THEN WE

CAN HAVE A DIALOGUE ON THE BIGGER POLITICAL ISSUE.

7. (C) BARUT RETURNED TO THE ISSUE OF WHETHER THERE WAS

ROOM FOR DIALOGUE ON THE GOVERNMENT\'S FAST TRACK

PROGRAM, AND WHETHER ANY TINKERING COULD BE DONE TO MAKE

IT ACCEPTABLE TO THE DONORS. THE AMBASSADOR RESPONDED

MISSION WAS ATTEMPTING TO FIND OUT AS WELL. HE THEN

ASKED WHETHER THE DONOR COMMUNITY DISPUTED THE 5 MILLION

HECTARE GOAL THAT THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE INTENDED TO

ACQUIRE. THE AMBASSADOR RESPONDED THAT MUCH DEPENDED ON

RATIONAL WAY. THE AMBASSADOR CONTINUED THAT THE LAND

ACQUISITION SHOULD PROCEED IN STAGES, IN ACCORDANCE WITH

CAPACITY AND RESOURCE CONSTRAINTS, YET WITHIN A LONGER-

TERM FRAMEWORK TO ACHIEVE THE GOVERNMENT\'S GREATER 5M HA

OBJECTIVE. OKOTH THEN ASKED WHETHER IT WOULD MAKE SENSE

8. (C) OKOTH AGREED THAT IT HAD TO BE A RATIONAL AND

ACHIEVABLE PROGRAM. BARUT SUGGESTED ONE APPROACH MIGHT

BE TO START WITH THE 211 FARMS, UNDERSTANDING THAT THE

GOVERNMENT NEEDED MONEY BOTH TO PURCHASE THESE FROM THE

METHODS. IF A TRANSPARENT TRUST FUND IS SET UP AND THE

PROGRAM WAS THE WAR VETERANS, MANY OF WHOM ARE THUGS

MCDONALD CONCURRED, NOTING THAT THE GOVERNMENT HAD

CREATED A PROBLEM FOR ITSELF AND THE DONORS COULD NOT BE

SEEN TO BE REWARDING LAWLESSNESS.

LEVEL MISSIONS.

10. (C) OKOTH OBSERVED THAT IN TALKING TO THE CFU HE

WAS STRUCK BY HOW CONFIDENT IT SEEMED ABOUT TAKING ON

THE GOVERNMENT. IT WAS ALMOST AS IF THE FARMERS HAD A

GODFATHER BEHIND THEM. AMBASSADOR MCDONALD REPLIED THAT

IF SO, IT WAS NOT THE UNITED STATES. THE BRITISH ARE

PREPARED TO PAY FOR LAND WHEN IT IS APPROPRIATE, BUT THE

USG WAS NOT PREPARED TO DO SO AT THIS TIME. CHONCHOL

OBSERVED THAT THE GOVERNMENT WOULD RESPOND TO CFU

INTRANSIGENCE IN KIND AND THAT PRESIDENT MUGABE WAS A
