PM MEETING IN NY

----------------

1. (SBU) Milinda Moragoda called Charge today to check

whether a U.S. principal could attend a GSL-proposed

meeting hosted by the GSL that would include PM

Wickremesinghe, the Norwegian FM, Kofi Annan, and a U.S.

rep such as D. (Note: Per our class e-mails to you,

Moragoda had mentioned the idea of such a meeting to us

late last week.) The precise timeframe of the proposed

meeting has not yet been set, but the GSL\'s plan is to

have it take place in the September 15-17 timeframe. We

are not sure how much the proposed meeting has been

worked with the UN yet. The purpose of the meeting would

be to underscore international support for the Sri Lankan

peace process. What advice should we give Moragoda on

which U.S. official might be able attend? (POL)

PRO-PEACE MARCH

---------------

2. (C) The UNP is sponsoring a pro-peace process march

in Colombo today. It\'s supposed to be quite large. The

GSL apparently wants to remain well ahead of the curve

vis-a-vis any possible protests against its lifting of

the ban on the LTTE. So far, the JVP and anti-LTTE

deproscription elements in the PA have remained

relatively muted in their criticisms. We expect the JVP

might try to mobilize some street actions (rallies,

demos) soon, however. (POL)

CONFUSION RE PROPOSED AMENDMENT

-------------------------------

3. (C) We have heard reports that the GSL plans to table

in Parliament soon its much discussed constitutional

amendment limiting presidential powers to call new

elections. There is some confusion as to whether or not

the draft amendment will also include language allowing

for crossover voting in Parliament. There is also

confusion as to whether President Kumaratunga will

concede and support the amendment in the end. We\'ll

report more as things get clearer, but wanted you to know

that it appears we\'re at make-or-break time re the

proposed amendment. (POL)

NORWEGIAN AMB RE TALKS

----------------------

4. (C) Please pass to EUR/NB and EAP/BCLTV: Norwegian

Ambassador Westborg told Charge today that he did not

expect the September 16-18 talks in Thailand to be too

substantive at this point. The focus of the two sides

would be establishing cordial atmospherics and agreement

on modalities, i.e., settling on the structure, agenda,

and date for future talks. There also would be some

discussion re development-related issues, such as

demining, etc. The sides seem set to issue a joint call

for international humanitarian and demining assistance at

the close of the meeting. Westborg reported that the GSL

for its part would not mind discussing substantive

issues, but the LTTE has indicated that it is not ready

yet. One reason it is apparently not ready is that

Balasingham has not yet received his detailed briefing

from Prabhakaran on the way forward. (POL)
