SUBJECT: HOLY SEE SIGNS ACCORD WITH CZECH REPUBLIC

1. (SBU) THE HOLY SEE SIGNED A JURIDICAL ACCORD WITH THE

CZECH REPUBLIC JULY 25 REGULATING THE STATUS OF THE

CATHOLIC CHURCH. THE AGREEMENT, REACHED AFTER THREE YEARS

OF OCCASIONALLY DIFFICULT NEGOTIATIONS, GUARANTEES THE

CATHOLIC CHURCH FREEDOM TO CONDUCT ITS ACTIVITIES, IN

PARTICULAR WORSHIP, GOVERNMENT OF ITS JURISDICTIONS, AND

EDUCATION. IT ALSO RECOGNIZES THE CIVIL EFFECTS OF A

CHURCH MARRIAGE. THE CZECH GOVERNMENT GUARANTEES THE

CHURCH THE RIGHT TO OFFER PASTORAL CARE IN HOSPITALS,

SOCIAL CARE CENTERS AND PRISONS. CATHOLIC SCHOOLS WILL

HAVE THE SAME STATUS AS STATE INSTITUTIONS WITH

COMMENSURATE RIGHTS AND DUTIES. THE AGREEMENT DID NOT

ADDRESS THE ISSUE OF THE RESTITUTION OF CHURCH PROPERTY

EXPROPRIATED BY THE FORMERLY COMMUNIST GOVERNMENT BEYOND

HORTATORY LANGUAGE COMMITTING TO A RESOLUTION AT THE

EARLIEST POSSIBLE DATE.

2. (SBU) THE CZECH REPUBLIC IS ONE OF THE LAST EUROPEAN

COUNTRIES TO ENTER INTO SUCH A JURIDICAL AGREEMENT WITH

THE HOLY SEE. ACCORDING TO MONSIGNOR MICHAEL BANACH,

DESK OFFICER RESPONSIBLE FOR THE CZECH REPUBLIC, THE

NEGOTIATIONS HAD BEEN DIFFICULT, AND THE HOLY SEE

ULTIMATELY ACCEPTED LESS BINDING AND MORE NEUTRAL

LANGUAGE THAN THEY HAD SOUGHT IN SOME AREAS IN ORDER TO

ACHIEVE A TEXT ACCEPTABLE TO THE CABINET THAT WOULD HAVE

A CHANCE OF SECURING PARLIAMENTARY APPROVAL. BECAUSE OF

ANTI-CLERICAL SENTIMENTS THAT STILL PREVAIL IN THE CZECH

REPUBLIC, THE HOLY SEE IS NOT CERTAIN HOW DIFFICULT

PARLIAMENTARY APPROVAL IS LIKELY TO BE.

3. (SBU) BANACH SAID THE HOLY SEE WAS PARTICULARLY

PLEASED WITH THE OFFICIAL RECOGNITION OF CATHOLIC

MARRIAGES AND THE RECOGNITION OF CATHOLIC EDUCATION ON A

PAR WITH THE STATE EDUCATION SYSTEM. HE NOTED THAT THE

CATHOLIC SCHOOL SYSTEM THE CZECH REPUBLIC HAD REMAINED

RELATIVELY STRONG, EVEN DURING THE COMMUNIST ERA.

4. (U) COMMENT: THE HOLY SEE HAS CONCLUDED SIMILAR

AGREEMENTS OR BROADER CONCORDATS WITH MOST COUNTRIES IN

CENTRAL AND EASTERN EUROPE AS PART OF ITS EFFORTS TO

STRENGTHEN THE JURIDICAL FOUNDATION OF ITS PRESENCE

THROUGHOUT THE REGION. POLAND, WITH A COMPLETE CONCORDAT

IS THE FURTHEST ALONG, AND HUNGARY AND THE SLOVAK

REPUBLIC WITH A SERIES OF AGREEMENTS ARE ALSO FAR

ADVANCED. THE CZECH REPUBLIC AGREEMENT HAD BEEN THE MOST

DIFFICULT TO CONCLUDE WITHIN THE REGION. THE HOLY SEE

REGARDS AGREEMENTS WITH PREDOMINANTLY ORTHODOX ROMANIA AS

UNLIKELY IN THE NEAR FUTURE, WHILE AGREEMENTS WITH

BULGARIA ARE SEEN AS POSSIBLE BUT NOT IMMINENT.
