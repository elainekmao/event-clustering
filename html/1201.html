SUBJECT: FOREIGN MINISTER PROTESTS ZIMBABWE DEMOCRACY

ACT

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD REASONS 1.5 B/D.

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 03616 031658Z

1. (C) ON JULY 3 FOREIGN MINISTER STANISLAUS MUDENGE

GRANTED AMBASSADOR MCDONALD AN AUDIENCE TO REGISTER HIS

CONCERN ABOUT PASSAGE OF THE ZIMBABWE DEMOCRACY ACT IN

THE SENATE. IN THIS CONNECTION, THE MINISTER SAID HE

WAS UNHAPPY ABOUT THE FLURRY OF LEGISLATIVE ACTIVITIES

IN THE UNITED STATES TARGETING ZIMBABWE FOR "SANCTIONS".

HE SAID THAT HE HAD INSTRUCTED HIS AMBASSADOR IN

WASHINGTON TO PROTEST THE HOUSE SUBCOMMITTEE ON AFRICA\'S

FAILURE TO INVITE ANYONE FROM THE ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT

TO TESTIFY AT ITS JUNE 13 HEARING.

2. (C) "WE ARE A DEVELOPING NATION AND IT IS UNFAIR TO

JUDGE US BY THE STANDARDS OF YOUR COUNTRY," MUDENGE

STATED, WAVING AWAY AMBASSADOR MCDONALD\'S POINTED

ASSERTIONS THAT THE SENATE\'S BILL WAS A BIPARTISAN

RESPONSE TO THE TRAGIC EVENTS THAT HAVE OCCURRED IN

ZIMBABWE SINCE EARLY THIS YEAR, PARTICULARLY THE

CAMPAIGN OF VIOLENCE AND INTIMIDATION PERPETRATED BY THE

RULING ZANU-PF PARTY IN THE RUN-UP TO THE ELECTIONS.

THE AMBASSADOR URGED THE MINISTER TO TREAT THE SENATE

BILL AND THE HOUSE HEARING AS A "WAKE-UP CALL" TO THE

ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT TO CHANGE ITS POLICIES. THE

MINISTER RESPONDED THAT "WE WILL HAVE TO STUDY THE

CONSEQUENCES" OF THE BILL IF IT BECOMES LAW. "THEN

ZIMBABWE WOULD HAVE TO CRAFT A PROGRAM TO SURVIVE," HE

ADDED. AMBASSADOR MCDONALD REJOINED THAT ZIMBABWE COULD

DO A NUMBER OF THINGS BETWEEN NOW AND THE POTENTIAL

ENACTMENT OF THE BILL IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES IN

ORDER TO BEGIN TO GARNER SOME GOODWILL IN WASHINGTON.

THE AMBASSADOR CONTINUED, "STOP TALKING ABOUT YOUR

NEGATIVE PUBLIC RELATIONS IMAGE AND TAKE CONSTRUCTIVE

ACTION TO DO SOMETHING ABOUT IT." PRESIDENT MUGABE\'S

STATEMENT TO CNN ON SATURDAY, JULY 1, ABOUT CONTINUING

THE FARM OCCUPATIONS AND NOT FORCING THE REMOVAL OF WAR

VETERANS, THE AMBASSADOR TOLD THE MINISTER, WERE VERY

UNHELPFUL IN TONE AND WILL NOT ASSIST IN SOLVING THE

LAND PROBLEM. "ZIMBABWEANS WILL BE ZIMBABWEANS," THE

MINISTER NOTED, AND "WE\'LL RESIST ANYONE WHO TRIES TO

DIRECT US." "WE\'LL FIGHT WITH OUR WORDS," HE CONCLUDED.

3. (C) COMMENT: THE SENATE LEGISLATION AND IMPLICATIONS

THAT IT HAS FOR ZIMBABWE IN THE WORLD HAS CAUGHT THE

ATTENTION OF THE HIGHEST LEVELS OF GOVERNMENT HERE.

MUDENGE DID NOT FOLLOW THROUGH ON WHAT COURSE OF ACTION

HIS GOVERNMENT MIGHT TAKE, SHOULD IT FIND ITSELF AN

ISOLATED STATE, ALTHOUGH WE SPECULATE THAT IT MIGHT

ACCELERATE THE ACQUISITION OF COMMERCIAL FARMS FOR RE-

SETTLEMENT. THE FOREIGN MINISTER THROUGH ALL HIS

BLUSTER HAS MADE A POINT: THE ZANU-PF GOVERNMENT WANTS

OUR MONEY BUT IS NOT WILLING TO CHANGE ITS WAYS. AS WE

DISCUSS POTENTIAL USG INVOLVEMENT IN ASSISTING LAND

REFORM IN THE FUTURE, WE MUST KEEP THIS POINT IN MIND.

END COMMENT.
