SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) SINCE FEBRUARY 1, THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE

(GOZ) HAS INTENSIFIED AND EXPANDED THE SCOPE OF ITS

INTIMIDATION CAMPAIGN IN URBAN AREAS. IT HAS SENT ARMY

AND POLICE UNITS INTO TOWNSHIPS NEAR HARARE TO

INDISCRIMINATELY BEAT PEOPLE IN THEIR HOMES AND PUBLIC

PLACES. ON FEBRUARY 3, POLICE SQUASHED A SMALL, BUT

PEACEFUL DEMONSTRATION AGAINST MEDIA ATTACKS WITH

OVERWHELMING POLICE FORCE. ON FEBRUARY 4 AND 5 IT BEGAN

TARGETING PROMINENT MEMBERS OF THE OPPOSITION MDC FOR

BEATINGS AND ARREST. THE GOZ\'S LEVEL OF INTIMIDATION IS

NOW HIGHER THAN AT ANY TIME SINCE LAST YEAR\'S PRE-

ELECTION PERIOD. IF THE GOZ SUCCESSFULLY CLAMPS DOWN ON

THE OPPOSITION AS A POLITICAL FORCE, OPPOSITION SOURCES

FEAR EXTREMIST MEMBERS OF THE URBAN POPULACE MAY RESORT

TO ISOLATED ACTS OF VIOLENCE TO VENT THEIR ANGER. END

SUMMARY.

RENEWAL OF INDISCRIMINATE BEATINGS

----------------------------------

2. (C) ON THE NIGHT OF FEBRUARY 1, ARMY AND POLICE

UNITS DESCENDED ON CHITUNGWIZA, THE SPRAWLING TOWNSHIP

30 KM SOUTH OF HARARE, TO BEAT RESIDENTS, APPARENTLY AT

RANDOM. "THE DAILY NEWS" REPORTED THAT SOLDIERS STORMED

SEVERAL NIGHTCLUBS AND CLUBBED PARTY-GOERS WITH BATONS,

ACCUSING THEM OF BURNING COPIES OF THE GOVERNMENT-

INFLUENCED "THE HERALD." IN ANOTHER INCIDENT, SOLDIERS

REPORTEDLY RAIDED A RESTAURANT, ORDERED ALL THE PATRONS

TO THE FLOOR, AND BEAT THEM WITH BATONS FOR 15 MINUTES.

AN EMBASSY SOURCE CONFIRMED THAT MILITARY, NOT POLICE,

VEHICLES WERE SEEN ALL OVER CHITUNGWIZA THE NIGHT OF

FEBRUARY 1. SOLDIERS RETURNED TO ONE NIGHTCLUB IN THE

HIGH-DENSITY AREA ON FEBRUARY 4 AND AGAIN BATTERED ITS

PATRONS, ACCORDING TO THE PRESS. THE ATTACKS FOLLOWED A

WEEK OF TENSION AND VIOLENCE INVOLVING VENDORS OF THE

AFOREMENTIONED RIVAL NEWSPAPERS, WHICH WAS PRECIPITATED

BY THE BOMBING OF "THE DAILY NEWS" PRINTING PLANT ON

JANUARY 28 (REFTEL).

PEACEFUL DEMONSTRATIONS BROKEN UP

---------------------------------

3. (U) ON FEBRUARY 2, POLICE USED BATONS AND TEAR GAS

TO BREAK UP A DOWNTOWN HARARE DEMONSTRATION BY ABOUT 100

MDC YOUTHS, WHO WERE PROTESTING THE BREAKDOWN OF LAW AND

ORDER IN THE COUNTRY. POLICE CHASED AND BEAT UP THE

DEMONSTRATORS UNTIL THEY DISPERSED, ACCORDING TO PRESS

REPORTS.

4. (SBU) THE ZIMBABWE UNION OF JOURNALISTS (ZUJ) MET ON

THE EVENING OF FEBRUARY 2 TO ORGANIZE A DEMONSTRATION IN

COMMENT

-------

7. (C) THE LEVEL OF GOZ-SPONSORED INTIMIDATION IS NOW

HIGHER THAN AT ANY TIME SINCE LAST YEAR\'S PRE-ELECTION

PERIOD. THE SCOPE AND INTENSITY OF THE INTIMIDATION

CAMPAIGN, AND THE INCREASING INVOLVEMENT OF MILITARY

UNITS, IS A FLASHING SIGN OF THE GOZ\'S GROWING

DESPERATION--AND DETERMINATION--TO DEFEAT THE MDC. WE

ARE HEARING RUMORS THAT MUGABE MAY RESIGN AS EARLY AS

APRIL THIS YEAR AND CALL A SNAP ELECTION THAT WOULD

CATCH THE MDC OFF-GUARD. SUCH A MOVE IS PLAUSIBLE,

CONSIDERING THE INTENSITY OF THE CAMPAIGN AGAINST THE

MDC\'S STRONGHOLDS AND THE MDC ITSELF. A CAMPAIGN OF

URBAN VIOLENCE WAS THE FINAL STAGE IN THE GOZ\'S WAVE OF

INTIMIDATION IN THE RUN-UP TO LAST YEAR\'S PARLIAMENTARY

ELECTIONS. IT IS DIFFICULT TO IMAGINE THE GOZ

MAINTAINING THIS LEVEL OF VIOLENCE IN THE URBAN AREAS

WITHOUT RISKING A GREATER BACKLASH FROM URBAN RESIDENTS,

WHO WILL BE KEY TO ANY PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION. HOWEVER,

IF THE GOZ SUCCESSFULLY CLAMPS DOWN ON THE OPPOSITION

AND DESTROYS IT AS A POLITICAL FORCE, WHICH APPEARS TO

BE ITS INTENTION, OUR CONTACTS IN THE OPPOSITION FEAR

THAT EXTREMIST MEMBERS OF THE OPPOSITION OR URBAN

POPULACE MAY RESORT TO ISOLATED, RANDOM ACTS OF VIOLENCE

TO VOICE THEIR ANGER. WE CANNOT RULE OUT THE KILLING OF

POLICE OR ARMY OFFICERS, OR EVEN ATTEMPTS ON THE LIVES

OF GOVERNMENT OFFICIALS, IN THE NEAR- TO MEDIUM-TERM.

WE DO NOT, HOWEVER, ANTICIPATE THAT FOREIGNERS WILL BE

THE TARGETS OF THESE ACTS. END COMMENT.
