SUBJECT: IMMINENT WFP FOOD PIPELINE BREAK IN THE CAUCASUS

-------

SUMMARY

-------

> > 1. US MISSION ROME PROGRAM

SPECIALIST TRAVELED TO ARMENIA, GEORGIA AND AZERBAIJAN FROM

FEBRUARY 23 THROUGH MARCH 8, 2003. WFP\'S PROTRACTED RELIEF

AND RECOVERY OPERATIONS (PRROS) IN EACH OF THOSE COUNTRIES

WERE ASSESSED AND DETERMINED TO BE IN NEED OF EXPEDITED

SUPPORT. END SUMMARY.

------------------

ARMENIA BACKGROUND

------------------

2. PRRO 10053.0 BEGAN IN JULY OF 2001 AND IS SCHEDULED TO

END IN JUNE OF 2004. APPROXIMATELY U.S. DOLLARS 32.8

MILLION FOR 60,794 METRIC TONS OF COMMODITIES IS REQUIRED TO

ASSIST 140,000 BENEFICIARIES A YEAR CONSISTING OF REFUGEES

OR INTERNALLY DISPLACED PERSONS, HOMELESS EARTHQUAKE

VICTIMS, DISABLED PERSONS, SINGLE ELDERLY PENSIONERS,

DESTITUTE WOMEN AND SCHOOLCHILDREN.

3. AS A SOVIET REPUBLIC, ARMENIA WAS A HEAVY INDUSTRIALIZED

COUNTRY. IN 1985, ARMENIA ATTRIBUTED ALMOST 75 PERCENT OF

ITS GDP TO INDUSTRY. THE BREAK-UP OF THE SOVIET UNION AND

THE CONFLICT WITH AZERBAIJAN OVER NAGORNO-KARABAGH RESULTED

IN AN ENERGY CRISIS AND ECONOMIC BLOCKADE. THESE FACTORS,

TOGETHER WITH THE 1998 RUSSIAN FINANCIAL COLLAPSE, CAUSED A

FURTHER DECLINE IN AND REARRANGEMENT OF THE ECONOMY.

4. TODAY, AGRICULTURE PRODUCES ABOUT 30 PERCENT COMPARED

WITH INDUSTRY\'S 20 PERCENT OF THE COUNTRY\'S GDP.

UNEMPLOYMENT, WHICH HAS FORCED A LARGE PERCENTAGE OF THE

POPULATION TO RELY ON SUBSISTENCE FARMING, HOVERS AT 25

PERCENT. THE GINI COEFFICIENT, A MEASURE OF INCOME INEQUITY,

WAS RATED AT 0.690 IN 1999 AND CONTINUES TO REMAIN

STUBBORNLY HIGH.

5. THE PRRO HAS RECEIVED ABOUT U.S. 10.6 MILLION SINCE ITS

INCEPTION, AND NOW STOCKS OF COMMODITIES ARE ALMOST

EXHAUSTED. THE APPROVED FOOD BASKET CONSISTS OF WHEAT

FLOUR, PULSES, VEGETABLE OIL, AND SUGAR, A REQUIREMENT OF

ABOUT 1,700 METRIC TONS PER MONTH. SHORTFALLS IN VEGETABLE

OIL BEGIN TO APPEAR THIS MONTH AND IN WHEAT FLOUR NEXT

MONTH.

---------------

ARMENIA COMMENT

---------------

6. IF CONTRIBUTIONS DO NOT MATERIALIZE IMMEDIATELY, WFP IN

ARMENIA WILL BE FACED WITH A HOBSON\'S CHOICE OF REDUCING THE

AMOUNT OF FOOD DISTRIBUTED OR BENEFICIARIES RECEIVING IT. A

SMALL INVESTMENT NOW WILL PROVIDE AN IMMENSE BENEFIT TO

GROUPS IN BOTH URBAN AND RURAL AREAS.

GEORGIA COMMENT

---------------

10. TO DATE, OF THE U.S. DOLLARS 23.4 MILLION REQUIRED, ONLY

2 PERCENT (U.S. 500,000 DOLLARS) HAS BEEN PLEDGED BY DONORS.

WITHOUT IMMEDIATE SUPPORT, GEORGIA WILL HAVE TO CUT BACK ON

DISTRIBUTIONS AS EARLY AS APRIL WHEN THEY WILL FACE A

SHORTFALL IN CEREALS. A COMPLETE PIPELINE BREAK IN ALL

COMMODITIES IS FORESEEN IN MAY UNLESS NEW CONTRIBUTIONS ARE

RECEIVED IN THE NEAR FUTURE. END COMMENT.

AZERBAIJAN BACKGROUND

---------------------

11. PRRO 10168.0 FOR AZERBAIJAN, ONLY RECENTLY APPROVED IN

MAY OF 2002 WITH EARLY AND STRONG USG ENDORSEMENT, BEGAN IN

JANUARY 2003 AND IS SCHEDULED TO END IN DECEMBER OF 2005.

12. WAR WITH ARMENIA IN 1992 AND 1993 RESULTED IN THE LOSS

OF 20 PERCENT OF AZERBAIJAN\'S TOTAL TERRITORY, 70 PERCENT OF

ITS FERTILE LAND, AND THE DISPLACEMENT OF 600,000 PEOPLE

PLUS ANOTHER 200,000 REFUGEES.

13. THESE 800,000 INDEPENDENT DISPLACED PEOPLE AND REFUGEES

CONSTITUTE 10 PERCENT OF THE TOTAL POPULATION, REPRESENTING

ONE OF THE HIGHEST PROPORTIONS OF DISPLACED POPULATIONS IN

THE WORLD. WITHOUT SIGNIFICANT RESETTLEMENT, LAND

ALLOCATION AND EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITIES, THE TARGETED

430,500 BENEFICIARIES, REQUIRING AN ESTIMATED U.S. DOLLARS

20 MILLION FOR 43,087 METRIC TONS OF COMMODITIES WILL

NECESSARILY RELY ON FOOD AID IN THE INTERMEDIATE TERM.

MEANWHILE, THE LONG ANTICIPATED ECONOMIC BOOM BORNE BY OIL

REVENUES HAS YET TO MATERIALIZE.

14. IN ADDITION, IN DESCRIBING CHRONIC MALNUTRITION, THE

UNITED NATIONS DEVELOPMENT PROGRAM\'S AZERBAIJAN HUMAN

DEVELOPMENT REPORT 2002 STATES THAT ABOUT 30.5 PERCENT OF

THE YOUNG CHILDREN OF REFUGEES AND INDEPENDENT DISPLACED

PEOPLE (IDPS) ARE STUNTED.

15. LESS THAN 5 PERCENT OF PLANNED CONTRIBUTIONS HAVE BEEN

PLEDGED. THESE FUNDS ARE DESPERATELY NEEDED TO MAINTAIN THE

VULNERABLE GROUPS THROUGH TARGETED INTERVENTIONS AND TAKE-

ALSO PROMOTING TARGETED INTERVENTIONS AND ACTIVITIES LEADING

AND INCOME GENERATION. SCHOOL FEEDING WILL PROVIDE

INCENTIVES FOR SCHOOL ATTENDANCE, ESPECIALLY AMONG GIRLS,

WHILE ADDRESSING IODINE AND OTHER MICRONUTRIENT DEFICIENCIES

OF ALL CHILDREN.

16. IN APRIL WFP WILL EXPERIENCE A SHORTFALL OF ABOUT 940

TONS. BY THE END OF JULY, THE ESTIMATED SHORTFALL WILL BE

ALMOST 3,900 METRIC TONS IN WHEAT FLOUR ALONE.

--------------------------------------------- -------

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS FOR THE WFP PRROS IN

ARMENIA, GEORGIA AND AZERBAIJAN

--------------------------------------------- --------

18. INTENDED BENEFICIARIES OF ALL THREE OF THE WFP PRROS IN

THE CAUCASUS INCLUDE A LARGE POPULATION OF REFUGEES AND IDPS

AS WELL AS OTHER FOOD INSECURE POPULATIONS INCLUDING THE

ELDERLY AND CHILDREN.

19. IN EACH COUNTRY THE US MISSION PROGRAM SPECIALIST

OBSERVED AND ASSESSED WFP\'S LOGISTICS CAPABILITY AND

CONTROLS, SELECTED FIELD PROJECTS, INTERNAL SYSTEMS AND

STAFF EFFECTIVENESS, AND BENEFICIARY SELECTION AND

MONITORING.

20. DESPITE THE OCCASIONAL TENDENCY FOR HUMANITARIAN

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ROME 01069 03 OF 03 141556Z

ASSISTANCE TO CREATE DEPENDENT POPULATIONS, THIS PHENOMENON

IS NOT SUPPORTED BY GROWTH IN THE RECIPIENT POPULATIONS. ON

THE CONTRARY, WFP ASSISTANCE, OCCASIONALLY IN PARTNERSHIP

WITH OTHER WELL-RUN ORGANIZATIONS SUCH AS WORLD VISION, IS

WELL TARGETED AND PROVIDED EFFECTIVELY AND EFFICIENTLY.

21. HUMANITARIAN ASSISTANCE IN THE CAUCASUS CONTINUES TO BE

REQUIRED, IF ONLY ON A SMALL SCALE IN COMPARISON TO MANY

OTHER AREAS OF THE WORLD. IRONICALLY, THE MODEST SCALE OF

ASSISTANCE MAY EXPLAIN WHY DONOR COUNTRIES HAVE NEGLECTED TO

CONTRIBUTE TO WFP\'S PRROS. CONTRIBUTIONS OF 154,000 METRIC

TONS, WHICH REPRESENT TOTAL REQUIREMENTS, WOULD ENABLE WFP

TO CARRY OUT ITS ASSISTANCE TO 881,000 INTENDED RECIPIENTS

IN ALL THREE COUNTRIES AS PLANNED. DELAYS WILL ENTAIL

REDUCTIONS IN PROGRAM SCOPE.

22. THE USAID MISSION DIRECTORS IN THE SOUTH CAUCASUS

SUPPORT THE WFP PROGRAMS IN THEIR COUNTRIES AND ENCOURAGE A

POSITIVE RESPONSE TO THEIR APPEALS ON THE PART OF USAID/FOOD

FOR PEACE.

23. IN VIEW OF THE FAVORABLE ASSESSMENT OF WFP ACTIVITIES IN

THE SOUTH CAUCASUS, THE US MISSION URGES USG FUNDING

ORGANIZATIONS, PRINCIPALLY THE FOOD FOR PEACE OFFICE WITHIN

USAID, TO TAKE EARLY ACTION TO SUPPORT ALL 3 PRROS.

23. MINIMIZE CONSIDERED. HALL
