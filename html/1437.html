SUBJECT: MORE ON ZIMBABWE\'S POSITION ON UN SCALES OF

ASSESSMENT

REFS: A) SECSTATE 239542, B) USUN NEW YORK 03004, C)

REASONS: 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) POST DELIVERED REF A NON-PAPER TO ZIMBABWE\'S

MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS ON DECEMBER 19. ON DECEMBER

20, POLOFF SPOKE TO ISIDORE FUNGAI, ACTING

UNDERSECRETARY FOR INTERNATIONAL ORGANIZATIONS IN THE

MFA TO OBTAIN ZIMBABWE\'S REACTION TO THE NON-PAPER.

FUNGAI STATED THAT THE AGREEMENT EMERGING IN THE

NEGOTIATIONS SOUNDS "MORE REASONABLE," AND THAT HE IS

GLAD TO SEE THAT INDIA AND OTHER G-77 COUNTRIES APPEAR

TO BE ON BOARD WITH THE AGREEMENT.

2. (C) COMMENT: THE MFA STILL HAS NOT MADE AVAILABLE TO

US FOREIGN MINISTER MUDENGE\'S LETTER TO SECRETARY

ALBRIGHT (REF C). GIVEN RECENT REMARKS FROM THE MFA,

HOWEVER, IT APPEARS THAT ZIMBABWE IS LEANING TOWARDS

SUPPORTING THE US POSITION ON UN SCALES OF ASSESSMENT

REFORM. END COMMENT.
