SUBJECT: BERLUSCONI,S FOREIGN POLICY ADDRESS: ITALY REMAINS

STRONGLY IN EUROPE, BUT THINKS OF NATIONAL INTERESTS

CLASSIFIED BY: CHARGE WILLIAM P. POPE, REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D).

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 ROME 00241 01 OF 02 151734Z

SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) IN HIS JANUARY 14 FOREIGN POLICY SPEECH BEFORE

PARLIAMENT, PRIME MINISTER BERLUSCONI AGAIN EMPHASIZED

ITALY\'S SOLID SUPPORT FOR AND COMMITMENT TO REMAIN WITHIN

EUROPE. HE COMMENDED THE EURO AND REAFFIRMED THAT ITALY\'S

FOREIGN POLICY WOULD NOT EXPERIENCE A SEA CHANGE DURING HIS

TIME AT THE HELM AT THE MFA. THE MFA ITSELF, HE SAID, WOULD

BE "REFORMED," INCLUDING TO BETTER REPRESENT ITALIAN ECONOMIC

AND COMMERCIAL INTERESTS. BERLUSCONI GAVE NO HINT OF WHO

MIGHT BE APPOINTED ITALY\'S NEXT FOREIGN MINISTER, BUT SAID HE

WOULD HOLD THE REINS HIMSELF AS LONG AS NECESSARY. THE

OPPOSITION, PREDICTABLY, FOUND HIS SPEECH INSUFFICIENT.

WHICH FOREIGN POLICY WILL ITALY FOLLOW, THE DS\' FASSINO ASKED

RHETORICALLY? WE CONTINUE TO SEE THIS AS AN INTERNAL DEBATE,

WITH THE OPPOSITION SEEKING TO MAKE POLITICAL HAY OVER AN

ACTION THAT RESULTED FROM A CLASH OF PERSONALITIES.

BERLUSCONI IS NOT/NOT SEEKING TO DIVORCE ITALY FROM EUROPE.

END SUMMARY.

"ITALY VS. EUROPE" -- WHERE\'S THE BEEF?

---------------------------------------

2. (U) BERLUSCONI CHARACTERIZED RECENT POLEMICS IN ITALY

AND ABROAD OVER THE GOI\'S DEGREE OF EUROPEANISM AS "MUCH ADO

ABOUT NOTHING." HE SAID THE GOI\'S COMMITMENT AND THAT OF ITS

GOVERNING MAJORITY IS TO "CHANGE ITALY, MODERNIZE THE STATE,

AND RE-LAUNCH THE ECONOMY," BUT ONLY BY "REMAINING FIRMLY

ANCHORED TO EUROPE." HE SAID THE "EURO WORKS; THIS IS A GOOD

AND INDISPUTABLE TRUTH."

CONFIDENTIAL

3. (U) WHAT REMAINS, THE PRIME MINISTER CONTINUED, IS FOR

EUROPE TO WORK TOWARD POLITICAL INTEGRATION. SUCH A PROCESS

IS LONG AND INVOLVED; IT IS NOT "A BOOK THAT IS ALREADY

WRITTEN BY AN UNKNOWN AUTHOR, NOR A MANUAL OF PROVISIONS AND

DOGMA THAT MUST BE IMPLEMENTED WITHOUT DISCUSSION."

POLITICAL INTEGRATION WILL RISE FROM THE COLLECTIVE WORK OF

THE EU\'S ELECTED INSTITUTIONS, NATIONAL PARLIAMENTS, AND

GOVERNMENTS, WITH THE CONTRIBUTION OF THE EU COMMISSION, THE

EU CONVENTION, AND ALL SECTORS OF SOCIETY, INCLUDING CULTURE,

LAW, BUSINESS, DIPLOMACY, CIVIL SOCIETY, AND RELIGIOUS

INSTITUTIONS.

4. (U) ON THE "PLAN OF ACTION" FOR A POLITICALLY INTEGRATED

EUROPE, BERLUSCONI SAID THE ITALIAN DEBATE IS OPEN AND

INVITED THE OPPOSITION TO JOIN IT, IN ORDER TO FACILITATE THE

CONSTRUCTION OF THE NEW EUROPE AND DETERMINE ITALY\'S ROLE

THEREIN. HE SAID THE GOI WOULD APPOINT ITS DELEGATES TO THE

EU CONVENTION BY THE END OF JANUARY. HE SAID HE COULD NOT

PREDICT THE OUTCOME OF THEIR WORK, BECAUSE SEVERAL DECISIVE

ISSUES REMAIN OPEN AND ARE STILL BEING DEBATED BY MANY

COUNTRIES AND GOVERNMENTS. LISTING SOME, HE MENTIONED FULL

LIBERALIZATION OF THE ENERGY SECTOR, THE REFORM OF THE LABOR

MARKET AND OF WELFARE, AGRICULTURE POLICIES, THE

HARMONIZATION OF TAX LAWS, SUPRA-NATIONAL RULES FOR CORPORATE

GOVERNANCE, A COMMON FOREIGN POLICY, AND A COMMON DEFENSE

POLICY WITHIN NATO. HE STRESSED THAT THE GOI "WILL WORK

TOWARD POLITICAL INTEGRATION BASED ON THE WIDEST POSSIBLE

BASIS OF CONSENT AND LEGITIMIZATION," BECAUSE "NO EUROPEAN

SOVEREIGNTY CAN BE ESTABLISHED WITHOUT FULL-FLEDGED EUROPEAN

CITIZENSHIP." WITH THIS IN MIND, HE NOTED THE NEED FOR THE

"ABSOLUTELY NECESSARY SYNERGY BETWEEN SUPRA-NATIONAL ISSUES

AND INTRA-GOVERNMENTAL ISSUES" IN THE CONSTRUCTION OF

EUROPEAN UNIFICATION. HE ALSO STATED THAT THE GOI WOULD

"CONTINUE TO WORK AGAINST ALL VISIONS OF CENTRALIZED

BUREAUCRACY."

5. (U) BERLUSCONI ALSO NOTED THAT THE SYSTEM OF "ENHANCED

COOPERATION" WITHIN EUROPE WORKS, BUT ARGUED THAT IT

ABSOLUTELY MUST TAKE INTO CONSIDERATION THE NEEDS OF NATIONS

THAT HAVE REMAINED OUTSIDE OF THE ORGANIZATION AND WILL JOIN

IN THE FUTURE. HE UNDERSCORED THAT "DIRECTORATES" ARE A

"CLAMOROUS STRATEGIC MISTAKE," AND REMARKED THAT "ITALY WILL

KNOW HOW TO MAKE ITSELF HEARD TO PROTECT ITS NATIONAL

INTERESTS ALONGSIDE THE COMMON INTERESTS OF A SPEEDY AND

EFFICIENT -- ALBEIT SOLIDLY LEGITIMIZED -) INTEGRATION." HE

ADVOCATED ITALY\'S HISTORIC AND SOLID EUROPEANIST TRADITION

AND ITS "CONVICTION" THAT EUROPE\'S FOUNDATIONS "WERE NOT BORN

OUT OF POLITICS, BUT OUT OF CHRISTIANITY THAT FUSED HELLENISM

AND ROMAN CIVILIZATION." RECALLING ITALY\'S FORMER PM AND FM

ALCIDE DE GASPERI (1881-1954), ONE OF THE EU\'S FOUNDING

FATHERS, BERLUSCONI ARGUED THAT EUROPE IS "PHENOMENON OF

CIVIL UNITY: A RELIGIOUS, CULTURAL, AND CIVIL UNITY THAT

UNITED POPULATIONS ON THIS SIDE AND THAT OF THE ATLANTIC

OCEAN UNDER THE NAME OF WEST."

6. (SBU) ON RUGGIERO\'S RECENT "RESIGNATION" (SIC) AS FM,

BERLUSCONI REMARKED THAT, IN ACCEPTING THE TENURE AT THE MFA,

RUGGIERO HAD STATED HIS INTENTION TO GIVE HIMSELF A DEADLINE,

FOR PERSONAL REASONS. (THE OPPOSITION BOOED BERLUSCONI\'S

REFERENCE TO RUGGIERO\'S DEPARTURE FOR "PERSONAL REASONS.")

BERLUSCONI THANKED RUGGIERO FOR HIS LOYALTY WHILE IN

GOVERNMENT. HE ADDED THAT HE WOULD FILL IN FOR RUGGIERO

"WITH DEDICATION AND ENTHUSIASM" FOR THE LENGTH OF TIME

NECESSARY TO "RE-ORIENT" THE MFA\'S WAY OF WORKING. "WE MUST

KNOW HOW TO SELL THE PRODUCT OF OUR TALENT WITHOUT ANY

COMPLEXES... WE MUST BE ON THE MARKET WITH ALL OF ITALY\'S

FORCE AND ITS POLITICAL, ECONOMIC, AND CULTURAL WEIGHT."

BERLUSCONI ADDED THAT HE DID NOT INTEND TO DECIMATE THE MFA

OR OVERHAUL IT COMPLETELY.

OPPOSITION: "WHICH EUROPEAN POLICY IS ITALY\'S?"

--------------------------------------------- ---

7. (SBU) THE CENTER-LEFT REACTED PREDICTABLY TO PM

BERLUSCONI\'S SPEECH: REGARDLESS OF HIS WORDS, THEY DECLARED

&EUROSCEPTICISM8 THE CAUSE OF RUGGIERO,S DISMISSAL AND

BERLUSCONI,S ASSUMPTION OF THE FOREIGN MINISTRY. DEMOCRATS

OF THE LEFT (DS) LEADER PIERO FASSINO CHARACTERIZED

BERLUSCONI,S DECLARATIONS AND COMMITMENTS TO EUROPE AS

VAGUE, FOLLOWED BY TOO MANY \'IFS\' AND \'BUTS\'. HE ADDED THAT

THERE ARE TWO DIFFERENT IDEAS IN THE GOVERNMENT: ONE THAT

SEES EUROPE AS A NECESSARY EVIL FROM WHICH TO DEFEND ONESELF

AND LIMIT DAMAGES, AND THE OTHER, RUGGIERO\'S LINE, DECIDEDLY

PRO-EUROPE. FASSINO SAID BERLUSCONI FAILED TO EXPLAIN WHICH

EUROPEAN POLICY THE GOVERNMENT INTENDS TO DEVELOP OVER THE

NEXT FEW MONTHS. DAISY PARTY LEADER FRANCESCO RUTELLI

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 ROME 00241 02 OF 02 151740Z

CHARGED THAT, WITH HIS SPEECH, BERLUSCONI STEPPED OUT OF THE

PRO-EUROPEAN FURROW PLOWED BY ITALY OVER HALF A CENTURY. HE

CONTINUED THAT THE GOVERNMENT CHOSE A BAD MOMENT TO LAUNCH A

EUROSCEPTICAL OFFENSIVE WHICH WOULD DAMAGE ITALY\'S NATIONAL

INTEREST COMING ON TOP OF A SERIES OF POLEMICS, FROM THE

EUROPEAN ARREST WARRANT, TO DECISIONS ON THE AIRBUS, TO

LOCATIONS FOR EU COMMITTEES. FINALLY, MASSIMO D\'ALEMA, DS

PRESIDENT AND FORMER PM, DISMISSED THE PRIME MINISTER\'S

SPEECH AS "POINTLESS PROVOCATION" WHICH OFFERED NO

EXPLANATION FOR RUGGIERO\'S RESIGNATION.

8. (SBU) IN HIS SPEECH, FASSINO MADE REFERENCE TO

STATEMENTS BY DEFENSE MINISTER ANTONIO MARTINO AND JUSTICE

MINISTER ROBERTO CASTELLI THAT SUGGEST ANY MAGISTRATE IN ANY

COUNTRY COULD WAKE UP ONE MORNING AND DECIDE TO ARREST ANYONE

HE PLEASES. FASSINO RECALLED THIS WAS SPARKED BY THE LAW ON

ROGATORIES. FASSINO ENDED BY SAYING THAT &THE OPPOSITION

WILL TAKE ACTION, IF THIS GOVERNMENT DOESN\'T, TO KEEP ITALY

IN EUROPE."

COMMENT

-------

9. (C) UPON READING A COMPLETE TEXT OF BERLUSCONI\'S SPEECH,

THERE IS LITTLE TO JUSTIFY THE ALLEGATIONS OF ITALY\'S

CENTER-LEFT OPPOSITION. THEY ARE, IN SHORT, DOING WHAT IS

NORMAL IN POLITICS -- SEEKING AN ELECTORAL AND POPULAR

ADVANTAGE. AS NOTED, INTER ALIA, IN THE JANUARY 15 NEW YORK

TIMES ARTICLE "BERLUSCONI SAYS ITALY WON\'T TAKE EUROPE\'S

ORDERS," ITALIANS ARE STRONGLY PRO-EUROPE. THEY ARE ALSO BY

AND LARGE PRO-BERLUSCONI, SOMETHING THE OPPOSITION CLEARLY

WANTS TO CHANGE. WHAT WE FIND MORE DIFFICULT TO EXPLAIN IS

THE OVERWHELMING TENOR OF THE MEDIA HEADLINES (NOT ONLY IN

ITALY) WHICH, REGARDLESS OF WHETHER THEY SIT ATOP LARGELY

ACCURATE ARTICLES, INVARIABLY REFLECT THE TONE AND TENOR OF

ITALY\'S OPPOSITION.

10. (C) BERLUSCONI IS FLAGGING ISSUES NOT UNFAMILIAR, FROM

OUR UNDERSTANDING HERE, TO BRITAIN\'S PRIME MINISTER TONY

BLAIR OR TO OUR OWN PRESIDENT. THE QUESTIONS HE RAISES ARE

ONES THAT THE EUROPEAN UNION WILL INDEED HAVE TO TACKLE AS IT

WRESTLES WITH ITS DEVELOPMENT AND THE SHAPE OF THINGS TO COME

AND SEEKS TO COME TO GRIPS WITH ITS "DEMOCRATIC DEFICIT."

PROMOTION OF NATIONAL INTERESTS IS NOT TANTAMOUNT TO

BETRAYING INSTITUTIONS OF WHICH A NATION IS A MEMBER, OR IN

THIS CASE, DOES NOT EQUATE TO BEING "ANTI-EUROPE." WE REMAIN

CONVINCED THAT THE "ITALY VS. EUROPE"DEBATE IS LITTLE BUT

SMOKE AND MIRRORS. ITALY\'SFOREIGN POLICY HAS NOT CHANGED

WITH THE DEPARTUR OF RUGGIERO AND BERLUSCONI\'S

AUTO-APPOINTMENT A FOREIGN MINISTER. WHAT WE ARE WATCHING

IS A DOMSTIC SPAT BETWEEN SITTING GOVERNMENT AND OPPOSITIO,

SPARKED BY THE OUTSPOKEN AND FRANKLY INSUBORDIATE BEHAVIOR

OF AN OUTSIDER CABINET MEMBER.
