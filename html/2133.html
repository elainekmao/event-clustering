SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE RULING PARTY DUE TO TABLE LEGISLATION

BANNING POLITICAL FUNDING OF NGOS

CLASSIFIED BY POLITICAL OFFICER TODD FAULK FOR REASONS:

1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) IN A LUNCH MEETING WITH POLOFF NOVEMBER 1, NEW

DEPUTY JUSTICE MINISTER PAUL MANGWANA -- AND ZANU-PF

MEMBER OF PARLIAMENT -- REVEALED THAT HIS MINISTRY IS

CURRENTLY DRAFTING LEGISLATION THAT WOULD BAN FOREIGN

FUNDING CHANNELED THROUGH NGOS FOR "POLITICAL TASKS,"

SUCH AS VOTER EDUCATION. HOWEVER, THE LEGISLATION WOULD

CLEARLY EXEMPT MONEY HEADED TO NGOS FOR USE IN

HUMANITARIAN EFFORTS, MANGWANA SAID. A PREVIOUS BILL

THAT WOULD BAN ALL FOREIGN FUNDING OF NGOS IS NO LONGER

UNDER CONSIDERATION. THE DEPUTY MINISTER INTIMATED THAT

THE BILL IS DESIGNED TO PROTECT ZIMBABWE\'S SOVEREIGNTY.

IT IS NOT FOR FOREIGN GOVERNMENTS TO INTERFERE IN

ZIMBABWE\'S ELECTORAL PROCESSES; IT IS UP TO THE

GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE ALONE TO "EDUCATE" VOTERS, HE

STATED. THE JUSTICE MINISTRY IS PLANNING ON SUBMITTING

THE BILL TO PARLIAMENT BY DECEMBER SO THAT IT CAN BE

PASSED BEFORE THE CHRISTMAS RECESS, MANGWANA CONCLUDED.

2. (C) COMMENT: BY DUMPING A BLANKET BAN ON FOREIGN

NGO FUNDING AND DIFFERENTIATING BETWEEN "POLITICAL" AND

"HUMANITARIAN" NGOS IN NEW LEGISLATION, THE GOZ APPEARS

TO BE BOWING TO THE REALITY OF LOOMING FOOD SHORTAGES IN

THE COUNTRY AND THE NEED FOR FOREIGN NGO HELP. IT IS

UNCLEAR WHETHER FUNDING FOR SPECIFIC NGOS WILL BE

BANNED, OR JUST CATEGORIES OF ORGANIZATIONS. IT IS ALSO

UNCLEAR HOW "POLITICAL TASKS" MAY BE DEFINED IN THE

FINAL LEGISLATION. IN ANY CASE, IT WILL PROBABLY PROVE

IMPOSSIBLE FOR THE GOZ TO TRACK HOW FOREIGN FUNDS GOING

TO ZIMBABWEAN NGOS ARE USED -- ALL IT WILL NEED IS A
