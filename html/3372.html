SUBJECT: ITALY PROVIDES SCHOLARSHIPS TO CUBANS SELECTED BY

GOC

CONFIDENTIAL

1. (C) IN RESPONSE TO REFTEL DEMARCHE, MFA OFFICE DIRECTOR

FOR CENTRAL AMERICA NICOLO\' GORETTI DE FLAMINI TOLD POLOFF

JULY 23 THAT THE GOI PROVIDES SCHOLARSHIPS TO CUBANS AS PART

OF A FIFTEEN-YEAR OLD CULTURAL-SCIENTIFIC ACCORD BETWEEN THE

GOI AND GOC. UNDER THE TERMS OF THE ACCORD, THE GOC CHOOSES

THE CANDIDATES ITSELF. ASKED ABOUT THE POSSIBILITY OF

OFFERING SCHOLARSHIPS TO NON-GOC CANDIDATES, GORETTI SAID

THAT THE EU WAS CONSIDERING ESTABLISHING A SCHOLARSHIP

PROGRAM CALLED ALIS FOR LATIN AMERICANS. HE AGREED TO ASK

THE MFA\'S EUROPEAN INTEGRATION BUREAU TO ADVOCATE THAT THE

AID BE PROVIDED THROUGH NON-OFFICIAL CHANNELS.

2. (C) GORETTI DESCRIBED AS DIFFICULT BOTH THE SITUATION

IN CUBA AND EUROPEAN-CUBAN RELATIONS. THREE FACTORS HAVE

CONTRIBUTED TO INCREASED REPRESSION AND CUBAN GOVERNMENT

POSTURING: (1) THE SPLIT BETWEEN "HAVES" -- CUBANS WHO WORK

WITH TOURISTS OR RECEIVE REMITTANCES FROM ABROAD -- AND

"HAVE-NOTS," LIVING ON AN AVERAGE OF $12 A MONTH, IS CREATING

A CRISIS IN THE SOCIALIST STRUCTURE. (2) THE CARTER AND BUSH

SPEECHES AND THE VARELA PROJECT HAVE EMBOLDENED DISSIDENTS.

(3) ELECTIONS HAVE BEEN CALLED FOR OCTOBER. "THE EU IS

FURIOUS WITH CUBA" OVER THE RESULTING CRACKDOWN, GORETTI

CONFIDED. FURTHERMORE, THE DANISH (EU) PRESIDENCY IS "QUITE

TOUGH" ON THE GOC.
