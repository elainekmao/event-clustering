SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE\'S COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION MAKES A

COUNTEROFFER TO THE GOVERNMENT\'S 3,000 FARMS PROGRAM

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR MCDONALD, REASON 1.5 (B),

(D)

REFS: A) HARARE 4362, B) HARARE 4297

1. (C) ON AUGUST 14 THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION

(CFU) DELIVERED A PROPOSAL TO VICE PRESIDENT MSIKA

(IN HIS CAPACITY AS CHAIRMAN OF THE ZIMBABWE NATIONAL

LAND ACQUISITION COMMITTEE), LAYING OUT A

COUNTEROFFER TO THE GOVERNMENT\'S ANNOUNCED PLANS TO

GAZETTE OVER 3000 FARMS FOR IMMEDIATE SEIZURE AND

RESETTLEMENT (REF A). THIS FOLLOWS THE ANNOUNCEMENT

LAST WEEK ON AUGUST 10 THAT IT WAS WITHDRAWING ITS

LEGAL ACTION AGAINST PRESIDENT MUGABE, POLICE

COMMISSIONER CHIHURI, AND WAR VETERANS LEADER HUNZVI

OVER THE FARM ACQUISITIONS AND OCCUPATIONS, AND THE

DISINTEGRATION OF THE RULE OF LAW. IN ADDITION,

ACCORDING TO WILLIAM HUGHES, VICE PRESIDENT FOR

REGIONS AT CFU, THE PETITION THE UNION HAD SENT TO

PRESIDENT MUGABE ASKING FOR HIS DIRECT INTERVENTION

TO STOP THE INVASIONS "HAS FALLEN AWAY." IN A PRESS

ANNOUNCEMENT, THE CFU\'S PRESIDENT TIM HENWOOD STATED

THAT THE MOVES WOULD ENABLE "MORE MEANINGFUL DIALOGUE

BETWEEN THE CFU AND THE GOVERNMENT TO RESOLVE THE

ONGOING FARM CRISIS." THE CFU\'S ACTIONS ON THE 10TH

WERE CLEARLY INTENDED TO SET THE STAGE FOR THE CFU\'S

LATEST REBUTTAL OFFER TO THE GOZ.

2. (C) IN THE PROPOSAL, THE CFU OFFERS THE GOZ 200

FARMS NOW (ON A WILLING SELLER, COMMERCIAL VALUE

BASIS), ANOTHER 400 BY APRIL 30, 2001, AND 400 MORE

ANNUALLY FOR THE NEXT 4 YEARS (A TOTAL OF 2,200

FARMS). THE CFU PROPOSAL IS CONTINGENT UPON A

COMPLETE AND IMMEDIATE STOPPAGE OF THE INVASIONS AND

LAWLESSNESS, AND THE REMOVAL OF THE WAR VETERANS FROM

THE FARMS THEY CURRENTLY OCCUPY, HUGHES TOLD US. IN

ADDITION HE FLATLY DENIED (CONTRARY TO RUMORS WE HAD

HEARD OVER THE WEEKEND) THAT A DEADLINE HAD BEEN SET

BY THE CFU FOR THE WAR VETS REMOVAL, OR THAT THE

UNION WOULD BACK A NATIONWIDE BOYCOTT OF TOBACCO

PLANTING IF THE LAWLESSNESS CONTINUES.

3. (C) COMMENT: BECAUSE THE CFU PROPOSAL CONTAINS NO

RADICAL NEW OFFERS, NOR ANY QUICK-TO-EXECUTE

SOLUTIONS THAT WOULD YIELD POLITICAL CAPITAL FOR THE

PRESIDENT, WE DOUBT THAT THIS LATEST PACKAGE WILL BE

EMBRACED BY EITHER MUGABE OR THE GOZ. IN ADDITION,

WE ARE NOT SURE THAT THE FARM UNION\'S MEMBERSHIP

WOULD ENDORSE THE OFFER IF A REFERNDUM OR VOTE WAS

HELD TODAY ON THE MATTER. THIS IS BECAUSE WE ARE

BEGINNING TO SEE AND SENSE THAT THE AVERAGE

COMMERCIAL FARMER IS BECOMING INCREASINGLY

DISILLUSIONED WITH NOT ONLY HIS OWN PLIGHT, BUT ALSO

THE LACK OF RESULTS COMING FROM CFU\'S NEGOTIATIONS

WITH THE GOVERNMENT SINCE THE INVASIONS BEGAN IN LATE

FEBRUARY. BEING TOLD TO "KEEP COOL" FOR A FEW MONTHS

IS ONE THING, BUT BEING TOLD THIS FOR GOING ON SIX

MONTHS IS ANOTHER. AT THE MOMENT WE PREDICT THAT THE
