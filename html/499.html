SUMMARY

---

1. THE CROATIAN PRESS CONTINUES TO FOCUS ON THE

PROPOSED LAW ON THE PRINT AND ELECTRONIC MEDIA,

NOTING THAT PRIVATE OWNERSHIP OF TV AND RADIO

STATIONS WOULD BE PERMITTED IF THE LAW AS DRAFTED

IS PASSED BY THE CROATIAN ASSEMBLY. SLOVENE

DEFENSE MINISTER JANEZ JANSA HAS PRAISED THE

DEPLOYMENT OF THE UN FORCES, BUT ALSO CAUTIONED

THAT JNA DEMOBILIZATION WOULD NOT END THE THREAT

OF FUTURE JNA AGGRESSION. LOCAL CROATIAN

OFFICIALS FROM AREAS AROUND KNIN HAVE DISCLOSED

DETAILS OF ALLEGED MISTREATMENT OF CROATS IN THAT

REGION. THEY CLAIM THAT THOUSANDS HAVE BEEN

FORCED FROM JOBS AND HOMES BY SERB CIVILIAN AND

MILITARY OFFICIALS. LOOKING TOWARD

RECONSTRUCTION, THE CROATIAN GOVERNMENT HAS

RECEIVED A DELEGATION FROM THE WORLD BANK TO

2. IN ADDITION TO WHAT WE REPORTED YESTERDAY, THE

NEW DRAFT LAW ON THE PRESS AND ELECTRONIC MEDIA

WHICH WILL BE SUBMITTED TO THE ASSEMBLY ALLOWS

PRIVATE OWNERSHIP OF RADIO AND TV STATIONS. THE

ASSISTANT TO THE MINISTER OF INFORMATION, IGOR

MRDOLJAS, EXPLAINED THAT THE PRIVATE BROADCAST

MEDIA "WILL NOT COMPETE WITH THE STATE OWNED RADIO

AND TV." ACCORDING.TO MRDOLJAS, IT WILL BE A

PARALLEL MODEL BASED ON THE SYSTEM USED IN ITALY.

3. THE PROPOSED NEW LAW ALSO CALLS FOR LARGE

FINANCIAL PENALTIES TO BE IMPOSED ON PUBLIC

OFFICIALS FOR NON-DISCLOSURE OF PUBLIC INFORMATION

TO JOURNALISTS.

5. JANSA EXPRESSED SKEPTICISM CONCERNING THE

DEMOBILIZATION OF JNA SOLDIERS AND OFFICERS IN THE

OCCUPIED TERRITORY OF CROATIA. HE TOLD

JOURNALISTS THAT THESE DEMOBILIZED UNITS COULD BE

EASILY REACTIVATED, ESPECIALLY AFTER THE UN FORCES

LEVE THE TERRITORY. ACCORDING TO THE PRESS

ACCOUNTS, JANSA STATED THAT THE ONLY SOLUTION FOR

GETTING THE JNA PERMANENTLY OUT OF B-H IS

RECOGNITION. JANSA EXPRESSED THE VIEW THAT A

COMPLETE JNA WITHDRAWAL FROM B-H WOULD HAVE A

SALUTARY EFFECT ON THE SITUATION IN THE KNIN

KRAJINA. JANSA CLAIMED THAT THE SLOVENIAN SIDE

HAD WARNED THE UN AND CARRINGTON THAT JNA

DEMOBILIZATION WILL NOT ALLEVIATE THE THREAT OF

FUTURE JNA AGGRESSION.

CROATS THREATENED IN CROATIA

-----

6. LOCAL CROATIAN OFFICIALS FROM OBROVAC AND

BENKOVAC, TWO PREDOMINANTLY SERBIAN TOWNS NEAR

KNIN, ADDRESSED A LETTER TO THE COMMITTEE FOR THE

PROTECTION OF HUMAN RIGHTS OF THE CROATIAN

ASSEMBLY. THEY DESCRIBED CRIMES WHICH HAD

OCCURRED FROM THE BEGINNING OF THE WAR TO CROATS

LIVING IN THE REGION. THEY ASKED THE COMMITTEE TO

FORWARD THE DETAILS OF THESE CSES TO THE

INTERNATIONAL HUMANITARIAN INSTITUTIONS AND

ORGANIZATIONS FOR HUMAN RIGHTS. THE LOCAL

OFFICIALS CRITICIZED INTERNATIONAL ORGANIZATIONS

FOR NOT ACCEPTING THEIR INVITATIONS TO VISIT TOWNS

AND VILLAGES UNDER JNA OCCUPATION WHERE CROATS ARE

IN A MINORITY.

7. ACCORDING TO THE OFFICIALS\' REPORT, IN THE

OBROVAC COMMUNITY, 11 PERSONS WERE KILLED, 10

CITIZENS WERE IMPRISONED AND 4 ARE STILL BEING

HELD IN CUSTODY IN KNIN. ALL CATHOLIC CHURCHES IN

THOSE AND SURROUNDING TOWNS HAVE BEEN DESTROYED AS

WELL AS THE HOMES OF 800 FAMILIES. FROM THE

OBROVAC COMMUNITY, OUT OF 3,640 CROATS, 3,430 HAVE

BEEN EXPELLED. ONLY ONE CROATIAN WORKER IS

EMPLOYED IN OBROVAC.

SERBS FROM KNIN, SIBENIK AND SERBIA HAVE MOVED

INTO MANY OF THE ABANDONED HOUSES. BEFORE THE

WAR, 4,436 WORKERS WERE EMPLOYED IN THE BENKOVAC

COMMUNITY, OF WHICH 435 WERE CROATS. TODAY ONLY 7

CLAIM THAT THE OTHERS WERE DISMISSED FROM THEIR

JOBS AND THUS COMPELLED TO ABANDON THEIR PLACE OF

WORLD BANK DELEGATION VISITS ZAGREB

UNCLASSIFIED

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ZAGREB 00395 03 OF 03 131457Z

9. THE CROATIAN DIRECTOR OF THE AGENCY FOR

RECONSTRUCTION AND DEVELOPMENT, ZLATKO MALESA,

RECEIVED ON MARCH 12 A DELEGATION FROM THE WORLD

BANK. THE CROATIAN PRESS OF MARCH 13 REPORTED

THAT THE DELEGATION MEMBERS EXPRESSED GREAT

INTEREST IN THE PRIVATIZATION PROCESS AND THE

POSSIBILITIES AND CONDITIONS OF FOREIGN CAPITAL

INVESTMENT. REPRESENTATIVES OF THE BANK OFFERED

TO SEND TWO EXPERTS TO CROATIA TO PARTICIPATE IN

THE PREPARATION OF THE RECONSTRUCTION PROGRAM.
