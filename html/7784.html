SUBJECT: ITALIAN POSITION ON ARTICLE 28

NEGOTIATIONS TO MODIFY RICE IMPORT REGIME

REF A: ROME O0839

REF B: STATE LO8223

1. CONSISTENT WITH OUR INITIAL REPORTING IN

REFTEL A, THE GOI HAS NOT CHANGED ITS

POSITION REGARDING ARTICLE XXVIII

NEGOTIATIONS. THEY WOULD LIKE TO OPEN THE

NEGOTIATIONS TO TAKE A NEW LOOK AT RICE

IMPORTS.

2. ACCORDING TO OUR CONTACT IN THE MINISTRY

OF AGRICULTURE, THE BLOCKING MINORITY HAS

NOT, TO HIS KNOWLEDGE, CHANGED ITS POSITION

AND THUS THE OPENING OF ARTICLE XXVIII IS NOT

LIKELY.

UNCLASSIFIED

UNCLASSIFIED

SIPDIS

3. THE COMMISSION PROPOSAL TO REVISE CAP

SUPPORT FOR THE RICE SECTOR IS LOWER THAN THE

CURRENT LEVEL OF SUPPORT TO PRODUCERS, AND

HENCE, NOT DESIRED BY ITALIAN AGRICULTURAL

INTERESTS. THE REDUCED INTERVENTION PRICE, IN

GOI TERMS, IS NOT ACCEPTABLE. WHILE THE GOI

AGREES, IN PRINCIPLE, TO REDUCE THE

INTERVENTION PRICE FOR RICE, THEY BELIEVE

THAT THE LEVEL SHOULD GO TO EUROS 150 PER TON

FOR PADDY RICE.

4. ITALY, THE LARGEST RICE PRODUCER IN THE

EU, HAS SLIGHTLY SHIFTED RICE PRODUCTION BY

ABOUT 5 PERCENT FROM INDICA VARIETIES TO MORE

TRADITIONAL JAPONICA VARIETIES DUE TO IMPORT

PRESSURE. FOR 2003 OVERALL RICE PRODUCTION

AREA IS UNCHANGED. ANNUAL AGGREGATE

PRODUCTION, MILLED BASIS, IN THE PAST THREE

YEARS HAS AVERAGED 850,000 TONS.
