SUBJECT: HA GIANG: SLEEPY BORDER PROVINCE TRYING TO

-- WAKE UP

REF: 01 HANOI 3341

1. (U) SUMMARY. HA GIANG PROVINCIAL OFFICIALS ARE HOPEFUL

THAT THE RECENTLY DECLARED "BORDER ECONOMIC ZONE" WILL

STIMULATE DEVELOPMENT IN ONE OF NORTHERN VIETNAM\'S POORER

MOUNTAIN PROVINCES. THE DISTANCE AND DIFFICULTY IN

TRAVELING TO HANOI AND OTHER LOWLAND PROVINCES REMAIN

HINDRANCES TO ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT. SEPTELS REPORT ON

TERRORISM AND NARCOTICS-RELATED DISCUSSIONS. END SUMMARY.

------------------------------------

A LONG ROAD TO THE NORTHERN FRONTIER

------------------------------------

2. (U) POLOFF AND POL FSN VISITED HA GIANG PROVINCE

JANUARY 14-16. HA GIANG IS ABOUT 300 KILOMETERS FROM HANOI

AND SHARES A NEARLY 400 KILOMETER BORDER WITH CHINA. THE

MAIN PROVINCIAL CROSSING POINT TO CHINA IS ABOUT 20

KILOMETERS FROM HA GIANG TOWN AT THANH THUY. CITIZENS FROM

THE DISTRICTS NEAR THE BORDER MAY CROSS OVER WITH A SPECIAL

PERMIT, BUT ONLY FOR DAY TRIPS (UNLIKE MORE LENIENT

PROVISIONS IN EFFECT IN QUANG NINH PROVINCE - REFTEL).

LONGER JOURNEYS REQUIRE PASSPORTS AND VISAS.

3. (U) ACCORDING TO VO DUC HONG, CHIEF OF THE BORDER

AFFAIRS DIVISION OF THE PEOPLE\'S COMMITTEE, ABOUT 80 PCT OF

HA GIANG\'S POPULATION OF 610,000 PEOPLE ARE ETHNIC

MINORITIES. THE LARGEST GROUPS ARE THE HMONG AND TAY NHUNG.

THE 9,000 SQUARE KILOMETER PROVINCE INCLUDES HIGH MOUNTAIN

AREAS (4,000 SQUARE KILOMETERS), HIGHLAND AREAS (1,000

SQUARE KILOMETERS), AND LOWLAND AREAS (4,000 SQUARE

KILOMETERS). ONLY ABOUT 13 PERCENT OF THE PROVINCE IS

CONSIDERED SUITABLE FOR FARMING. ITS MAIN AGRICULTURAL

EXPORT IS TEA GROWN AT HIGH ELEVATIONS, POPULAR IN TAIWAN,

HONG KONG, KOREA, AND RUSSIA, HONG CLAIMED. FRUIT, RICE,

AND CORN ARE OTHER MAIN CROPS.

-------------------------

STILL POOR, BUT IMPROVING

-------------------------

4. (U) LE VAN BANG, THE DIRECTOR OF HA GIANG\'S PLANNING

AND INVESTMENT DEPARTMENT, CLAIMED THAT WHILE THE PROVINCE

WAS STILL "QUITE POOR," THE ECONOMIC SITUATION IS IMPROVING.

BANG NOTED THAT THE POVERTY RATE FROM 1995 TO 2001 HAD

DECLINED FROM 46 PERCENT OF THE TOTAL POPULATION TO ABOUT 26

PERCENT, THOUGH PER CAPITA INCOME IS STILL ONLY ABOUT 30

PERCENT OF THE NATIONAL AVERAGE OF USD 380 PER YEAR, HONG

ADMITTED. THE DECLINE IN POVERTY CAN BE ATTRIBUTED MAINLY

TO DIVERSIFIED AGRICULTURE, SOME INDUSTRIAL DEVELOPMENT, AND

MINERAL EXPLOITATION, INCLUDING IRON ORE, LEAD, AND ZINC,

BANG EXPLAINED. IN THE PAST FEW YEARS, A CEMENT FACTORY,

THREE SMALL PAPER MILLS, AND TWO BRICK FACTORIES HAVE

OPENED, ALTHOUGH BANG DID NOT HAVE AVAILABLE STATISTICS ON

HOW MANY PEOPLE ARE EMPLOYED IN THOSE FACTORIES OR IN

MINERAL EXPLOITATION. BANG EXPRESSED INTEREST IN FINDING A

COMPANY TO INVEST IN A FRUIT PROCESSING PLANT, SINCE IN THE

PAST FEW YEARS FARMERS HAVE PLANTED MORE AND MORE FRUIT

TREES (ESPECIALLY ORANGES); TRANSPORTING THE FRESH FRUIT TO

MARKET IS PROBLEMATIC. POLOFF PROMISED TO SHARE THIS

OPPORTUNITY WITH THE USCS.

5. (U) WHILE THE ENTIRE PROVINCE IS CONNECTED TO THE

NATIONAL ELECTRICAL GRID, ONLY ABOUT 50 PERCENT OF THE

POPULATION HAS ACCESS TO ELECTRICITY IN THEIR HOMES, BANG

SAID. HE NOTED THAT THE DISTANCE FROM AND DIFFICULTY IN

TRAVELING TO HANOI AND OTHER LOWLAND PROVINCES REMAIN MAJOR

PROBLEMS. WHILE TRAVEL TIME TO HANOI HAS BEEN REDUCED TO

ABOUT 7 HOURS AND NATIONAL HIGHWAY 2 IS BEING UPGRADED,

TRANSPORTATION INFRASTRUCTURE STILL "MAY DISCOURAGE"

POTENTIAL INVESTORS FROM COMING TO HA GIANG, HE ADMITTED.

(NOTE: THERE ARE SEVERAL SHORT STRETCHES WHERE THE ROAD IS

TWO FULL LANES AND NEWLY PAVED, BUT THERE ARE LONG STRETCHES

WHERE THE ROAD IS ONLY ABOUT ONE AND A HALF LANES AND VERY

BUMPY. THERE ARE ALSO SEVERAL ONE LANE BRIDGES. THE GVN\'S

MASTER PLAN CALLS FOR COMPLETING THE UPGRADING OF HIGHWAY 2

BY THE END OF 2005. END NOTE.) SO FAR, THE PROVINCE HAS HAD

NO DIRECT FOREIGN INVESTMENT.

6. (U) OFFICIALS EXPRESSED OPTIMISM THAT IMPLEMENTING

PLANS FOR A NEW "BORDER ECONOMIC ZONE" WILL SPUR DEVELOPMENT

IN THE PROVINCE. IN DECEMBER 2001, THE GVN DECLARED THE

AREA AROUND THE THANH THUY BORDER CROSSING A BORDER ECONOMIC

ZONE. SPECIAL ADVANTAGES INCLUDE A NUMBER OF PROVISIONS TO

STIMULATE COMMERCIAL ACTIVITY, SUCH AS FAVORABLE CONDITIONS

ON TAXES, A DUTY FREE MARKET, AND CONCESSIONS ON LAND FOR

DEVELOPING COMMERCIAL ENTERPRISES. BANG ADMITTED THAT,

WHILE THE NATIONAL GOVERNMENT HAD DECLARED THE BORDER

ECONOMIC ZONE, "IT IS UP TO PROVINCIAL OFFICIALS TO WORK OUT

THE DETAILS." ACCORDING TO BANG, HIS OFFICE IS WORKING ON A

DEVELOPMENT PLAN THAT HE HOPES WILL BE READY "BY THE END OF

2002."

7. (U) DURING A MID-AFTERNOON VISIT TO THE BORDER AREA,

NOT MUCH ACTIVITY WAS EVIDENT. ABOUT 10 TRUCKS WERE WAITING

ON THE HA GIANG SIDE TO RECEIVE GOODS COMING OVER FROM

CHINA. ACCORDING TO THE ARMY OFFICER WHO ESCORTED EMBOFFS,

ONLY ABOUT "NINE OR TEN" TRUCKS A DAY RECEIVE LOADS IN

EITHER DIRECTION. A SLOW STREAM OF PEDESTRIANS WAS VISIBLE

CROSSING THE BORDER DURING EMBOFFS\' VISIT. THE BORDER AREA

ALSO INCLUDES A TAX OFFICE, CUSTOMS HOUSE, AND A MODEST IF

SOMEWHAT RUNDOWN SHOPPING CENTER. THE PEOPLE\'S COMMITTEE\'S

HONG POINTED OUT TO EMBOFFS AN AREA ABOUT 100 METERS SOUTH

OF THE BORDER WHERE LAND WAS BEING LEVELED IN PREPARATION

----------------------------

WE DON\'T BUY FROM THE BORDER

----------------------------

8. (U) CONVERSATIONS WITH SHOP OWNERS AROUND HA GIANG TOWN

PROVIDED SOME INSIGHT AS TO WHY THE BORDER CROSSING AT THANH

THUY IS RELATIVELY INACTIVE. IN ONE SHOP SELLING HOUSEHOLD

ITEMS, THE OWNER SAID THAT ALTHOUGH MOST OF HER INVENTORY

CAME FROM CHINA, SHE DID NOT BUY IT FROM THE BORDER AREA.

SHE INSTEAD USED A HANOI SUPPLIER WHO BOUGHT GOODS COMING

FROM CHINA VIA THE LANG SON BORDER CROSSING TWO PROVINCES TO

THE EAST. (NOTE: LANG SON IS CLOSER TO HANOI AND CONNECTED

BY A GOOD ROAD. TRAVEL TIME TO HANOI IS ABOUT 2.5 HOURS.

END NOTE.) THIS SUPPLIER TOOK THE GOODS TO HANOI, AND THEN

TRANSSHIPPED THEM TO HA GIANG. IN RESPONSE TO A QUESTION,

THE OWNER SAID THAT SHE DID NOT BUY FROM THE BORDER AREA

BECAUSE "THE CHINESE SELL US INFERIOR PRODUCTS AND WE CANNOT

RETURN THEM. IF I HAVE A PROBLEM, MY HANOI SUPPLIER WILL

EXCHANGE THE ITEMS THAT ARE DEFECTIVE."

9. (U) ANOTHER SHOP OWNER SELLING CHILDRENS\' CLOTHES STATED

THAT SHE BOUGHT HER INVENTORY IN A SIMILAR MANNER, NOTING

THAT HER SUPPLIER SHIPPED GOODS TO HER VIA A "RELIABLE" BUS

DRIVER WHO MAKES REGULAR TRIPS BETWEEN HANOI AND HA GIANG.

BOTH SHOP OWNERS SAID THEY MADE OCCASIONAL DAY TRIPS TO

CHINA FOR PLEASURE AND PERSONAL SHOPPING. THEY WERE AWARE

THAT THE GVN HAD ESTABLISHED THE BORDER ECONOMIC ZONE.

HOWEVER, THE CLOTHING SHOP OWNER SAID THAT SUCCESS WOULD

DEPEND "ON HOW THE GOVERNMENT IMPLEMENTS ITS PLANS." BOTH

-------

COMMENT

-------

10. (U) IN RECENT YEARS, THE GVN HAS CLEARLY TAKEN STEPS

TO ASSIST HA GIANG TO IMPROVE ITS ECONOMIC SITUATION. IN

THE ABSENCE OF RELIABLE INFRASTRUCTURE OR MORE AGGRESSIVE

SELF-PROMOTION BY PROVINCIAL OFFICIALS, HOWEVER, GROWTH IS

APT TO REMAIN SLOW AND POVERTY WILL REMAIN A DIFFICULT ISSUE

TO RESOLVE. THE UNUSUALLY HIGH PERCENTAGE OF MINORITY

POPULATION -- PROBABLY THE HIGHEST IN THE NATION -- ALSO

MEANS THAT COMPETENT LOCAL OFFICIALS FLUENT IN VIETNAMESE

MAY BE ANOTHER MAJOR LOCAL CONSTRAINT, HOWEVER MUCH IN GOOD

FAITH THE GVN TRIES TO ASSIST THEIR DEVELOPMENT AND

INTEGRATION.
