SUBJECT: POTUS VISIT AND BTA STIMULATED PROTESTANT

- REGISTRATION PROCESS

REF: HANOI 2591

1. (U) CLASSFIED BY POLITICAL COUNSELOR SCOTT D.

BELLARD, REASON 1.5 (D)

2. (C ) THEN-PRESIDENT CLINTON\'S VISIT TO VIETNAM IN

NOVEMBER 200 CAUSED THE SRV TO FACILITATE PROTESTANT

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HANOI 02740 190724Z

CHURCH EFFORTS TO REGISTER, ACCORDING TO PASTOR TINH,

WHO DESCRIBED HIMSELF AS THE CHAIRMAN OF VIET NAM GOOD

NEWS MISSIONS (STRICTLY PROTECT). TINH, WHO MET WITH

POLOFF ON OCTOBER 11 IN HANOI, SAID THAT GVN RELIGION

OFFICIALS LAST YEAR BEGAN TRAVELLING AROUND THE

COUNTRYSIDE ENCOURAGING AND ASSISTING PROTESTANT GROUPS

IN BEGIN LEGALIZING THEIR STATUS. HE CLAIMED THAT A

SYMPATHETIC OFFICIAL OF THE GOVERNMENT COMMITTEE ON

RELIGION TOLD HIM RECENTLY THAT THE SRV HAD LAST YEAR

DIRECTED THE COMMITTEE TO SEEK OUT PROTESTANT GROUPS

AND TRY TO REGISTER THEM IN ORDER TO "HAVE SOMETHING TO

SHOW THE AMERICAN PRESIDENT" AND TO INFLUENCE FAVORABLE

CONSIDERATION OF THE BTA. TINH ADDED THAT WHEN IT

BECAME CLEAR THAT PRESIDENT CLINTON WOULD NOT SIGN THE

BTA DURING HIS VISIT, GOVERNMENT INTEREST IN CHURCH

REGISTRATION TAPERED OFF.

3. (C ) AT LEAST TWO PROTESTANT CHURCHES ARE STILL IN

THE REGISTRATION PROCESS, INCLUDING HIS, TINH SAID.

HOWEVER, GVN OFFICIALS ARE NO LONGER GOING OUT OF THEIR

WAY TO FACILITATE THE PROCESS, HE ADDED. TINH

EXPRESSED A GENERAL BELIEF THAT A "HOLD" ON THE BTA

MIGHT HAVE HAD SOME FURTHER BENEFICIAL INFLUENCE ON

CHURCH REGISTRATION. PASTOR TINH SAID THAT HE BELIEVED

THAT CONGRESSIONAL CONSIDERATION OF THE VIETNAM HUMAN

RIGHTS ACT (VHRA) COULD SIMILARLY INDUCE PROGRESS ON

NEW CHURCH REGISTRATION, ADDING A HOPE THAT THE VHRA BE

APPROVED.

4. (C ) COMMENT: MUCH AS THE GVN DECRIES FOREIGN

INTERFERENCE IN ITS INTERNAL AFFAIRS, IT REMAINS

SENSITIVE TO OUTSIDE PRESSURE AND CONCERN. REFTEL

NOTED THE IMPACT OF THE VHRA VOTE ON THE BAPTIST

REGISTRATION PROCESS. WHILE IT IS NOT POSSIBLE TO LINK

DIRECTLY THE MARCH 2001 OFFICIAL REGISTRATION OF THE

SOUTHERN EVANGELICAL CHURCH TO THE POTUS VISIT OR BTA

CONSIDERATION, GVN POLICY TOWARD A MORE INCLUSIVE

APPROACH TO UNAUTHORIZED RELIGIOUS GROUPS IS CLEARLY

NOT IMMUNE TO THE EBBS AND FLOWS OF POLICY

DELIBERATIONS ABROAD, ESPECIALLY IN WASHINGTON.
