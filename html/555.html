--------------------------------------

TRIAL OF JNA GENERAL MLADINIC IN SPLIT

--------------------------------------

2. THE DISTRICT COURT IN SPLIT OPENED LEGAL

PROCEEDINGS AGAINST FORMER JNA GENERAL NIKOLA

MLADINIC (FROM RIJEKA) AND 17 JNA OFFICERS WHO ARE

ACCUSED OF WAR CRIMES. MLADINIC IS IN CUSTODY,

AND THE OTHER OFFICERS ARE TO BE TRIED IN

ABSENTIA. ALL THE OFFICERS ARE ACCUSED OF

BLOCKADING CROATIA\'S ADRIATIC PORTS AND SHELLING

SPLIT, SOLIN, KASTEL, TROGIR, PLOCE, AND THE

ADRIATIC ISLANDS. THE ATTACKS CAUSED CASUALTIES

AND HEAVY PROPERTY DAMAGE.

------------------------------------------

CROATIAN/RUSSIAN AGREEMENT FOR NATURAL GAS

------------------------------------------

3. CROATIA AND RUSSIA HAVE SIGNED A LONG-TERM

CONTRACT IN MOSCOW FOR DELIVERY OF RUSSIAN NATURAL

GAS TO CROATIA. IN 1993, RUSSIA WILL SEND 900

MILLION CUBIC METERS OF GAS WITH A POSSIBLE

INCREASE TO 1.2 BILLION CUBIC METERS BY THE YEAR

2010. CROATIAN MINISTER FOR INDUSTRY FRANJO

KAJFEZ TOLD THE PRESS THAT THIS CONTRACT SECURES

NEEDED SUPPLIES UNTIL NEW DEPOSITS OF NATURAL GAS

CAN BE FOUND IN CROATIA.

4. THE PRESIDENT\'S CABINET ANNOUNCED NOVEMBER 11

PRESIDENT TUDJMAN\'S DECISION TO PARDON 104 PERSONS

UNDER THE NEW AMNESTY LAW. THE BENEFICIARIES

INCLUDED CROATIAN SOLDIERS SENTENCED FOR MINOR

CRIMINAL ACTS, 38 SERBIAN PEASANTS FROM BUDIMCI

SENTENCED FOR WEAPONS VIOLATIONS, AND OTHER

CIVILIANS.

---------------------------------

MARTIC THREATENS UNPROFOR FORCES

UNCLASSIFIED

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ZAGREB 02362 02 OF 03 121730Z

---------------------------------

5. ON NOVEMBER 9, A SERBIAN "KRAJINA" DELEGATION

LED BY MILAN MARTIC, RETURNING FROM OKUCANI, HAD

TO WAIT ONE HOUR AT THE UNPROFOR CONTROL POINT ON

THE SAVA BRIDGE NEAR BOSANSKA GRADISKA. MARTIC

ANNOUNCED THAT IN THE FUTURE HE WILL NOT TOLERATE

SUCH UNPROFOR DELAYS AND, IF HE WERE DELAYED

AGAIN, UNPROFOR WOULD "SUFFER THE CONSEQUENCES."

MARTIC OPPOSES DISARMAMENT OF THE KRAJINA MILITIA

BY UNPROFOR AND ORDERED UN FORCES TO WITHDRAW FROM

THE CONTROL POINT ON THE BRIDGE NEAR BOSANSKA

GRADISKA BY NOVEMBER 13 SO THAT THE "KRAJINA

POLICE" CAN TAKE CHARGE.

KRSKO A-PLANT RESTARTED AFTER ONE DAY SHUTDOWN

--------------------------------------------- ---

6. THE NUCLEAR PLANT LOCATED IN KRSKO, SLOVENIA,

WAS RESTARTED NOVEMBER 11 AT 20 PERCENT CAPACITY

AFTER MINOR MECHANICAL TROUBLE HAD CAUSED A

SHUTDOWN NOVEMBER 10. THE FACILITY IS EXPECTED TO

REACH FULL GENERATING CAPACITY IN SEVEN TO TEN

DAYS.

SERBIAN ATTACKS IN SECTOR NORTH ON MOSCENICA

--------------------------------------------

8. AFTER A LULL OF MANY WEEKS IN UNPA SECTOR

NORTH, SERBIAN PARAMILITARY FORMATIONS NEAR

PETRINJA OPENED MACHINE-GUN FIRE ON THE VILLAGE OF

MOSCENICA NOVEMBER 10. APPROXIMATELY 3,000

DISPLACED PERSONS HAD RETURNED TO MOSCENICA AND

REGULAR BUS LINES FROM SISAK TO MOSCENICA HAVE

BEEN RE-ESTABLISHED. THE PRIMARY SCHOOL IN

OF THIS VILLAGE BUT ALSO FOR DISPLACED STUDENTS

FROM THE PETRINJA AREA.

GASOLINE COSTS 50 PERCENT MORE

------------------------------

9. ON NOVEMBER 12, THE GOVERNMENT RAISED THE

PRICE OF GASOLINE BY 50 PERCENT. HEATING-OIL

PRICES REMAIN UNCHANGED. PETROLEUM PRODUCTS HAVE

INCREASED 482.4 PERCENT SINCE DECEMBER 1991.
