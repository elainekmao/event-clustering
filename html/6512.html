SUBJECT: UAE: REQUEST FOR CASH ASSISTANCE FOR

WFP PROVISION OF IRAQI FOODSTUFFS

REF: STATE 70827

1. (U) Classified by Ambassador Marcelle M. Wahba

for reasons 1.5 (B) and (D).

2. (C) Polchief delivered reftel demarche on cash

assistance for WFP provision of Iraq foodstuff to

MFA Assistant U/S for Political Affairs Abdullah

Rashid Al-Nuaimi on March 19. Al-Nuaimi indicated

that visiting WFP officials had held a meeting

earlier in the day with the UAE Foreign Ministry to

discuss the expeditious establishment of a WFP

regional office in Dubai. The Emiratis have

authorized the establishment of the office and Al-

Nuaimi believes it will be opened almost

immediately in order to facilitate the movement of

foodstuffs to Iraq. He noted that the visiting WFP

officials had not made mention of the need for cash

assistance.

3. (C) Al-Nuaimi promised to brief MFA Minstate

Hamdan (who is also the Chairman of the UAE Red

Crescent Authority) immediately on our request, but

noted that the UAE would be channeling significant

amounts of humanitarian assistance, including

foodstuffs, via the UAE Red Crescent Authority,

which already has a presence in Iraq and a

relationship with the Iraqi Red Crescent Society.

The UAE RCA employees intend to remain in Iraq

during the conflict stage in order to ensure the

continuing flow of assistance to the Iraqi people.

Polchief suggested that it would be useful if the

UAEG regularly briefed USG officials on their

humanitarian assistance plans as that would help us

understand the types of aid going to Iraq.

WAHBA
