SUBJECT: AFGHAN TRANSIT TRADE AGREEMENT AND USE OF PORT BIN

QASIM, PAKISTAN

-------

SUMMARY

-------

1. WFP\'S INTERNATIONAL CARGO SURVEYOR IN KARACHI SUGGESTS

THAT THE 1965 AFGHAN TRANSIT TRADE AGREEMENT BE AMENDED TO

INCLUDE PORT BIN QASIM. THIS MODERN PAKISTANI PORT FACILITY,

BUILT IN THE MID-1970S, COULD HELP FACILITATE MOVEMENT OF

MATERIAL FOR THE AFGHAN RECONSTRUCTION EFFORT. END SUMMARY.

----------

BACKGROUND

----------

2. USMISSION HUMANITARIAN ATTACHE TIM LAVELLE SPOKE WITH JAY

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ROME 02030 01 OF 02 260820Z

KOTECHA (FINANCE DIRECTOR) OF THE INTERNATIONAL FORWARDING

AGENCY (PVT) LTD (IFA), BASED IN KARACHI, WHO WAS ATTENDING

THE WORLD FOOD PROGRAM\'S SECOND ANNUAL SHIPPING CONFERENCE

HELD IN ROME APRIL 18-19. IFA HAS BEEN WFP\'S GENERAL

SUPERINTENDENT (SURVEYOR) IN KARACHI FOR THE PAST 32 YEARS.

3. KOTECHA BRIEFED ON ASPECTS OF THE AFGHAN TRANSIT TRADE

AGREEMENT SIGNED ON MARCH 2, 1965 BY THE GOVERNMENTS OF

PAKISTAN AND THE KINGDOM OF AFGHANISTAN FOR

REGULATION OF TRAFFIC IN TRANSIT FROM PAKISTAN TO

AFGHANISTAN. IN THIS AGREEMENT, ONLY KARACHI PORT WAS

MENTIONED AS A PAKISTANI PORT POINT OF ENTRY, WITH NO

FLEXIBILITY FOR INCLUSION AT A FUTURE DATE OF ADDITIONAL

PORTS DEVELOPED ALONG THE PAKISTANI COAST LINE.

4. IN THE EXTANT AGREEMENT, THERE ARE TWO PREREQUISITES FOR

ALLOWING A PORT TO HANDLE AFGHAN TRANSIT TRADE CARGO, WHICH

ARE:

A) SEPARATE SHED(S) TO STORE THE CARGO, AND

B) A RAILWAY LINK FROM THE PORT TO THE DESIGNATED PAKISTANI

BORDER EXIT POINTS OF PESHAWAR AND CHAMAN.

--------------

PORT BIN QASIM

--------------

5. AS PER KOTECHA, PORT BIN QASIM CAME INTO OPERATION IN MID

1970\'S FOR BULK CARGO, AND NOW HAS A MODERN TERMINAL FOR

CONTAINERIZED VESSELS - CAPABLE OF HANDLING MOTHER VESSELS

INSTEAD OF FEEDER VESSELS. WITHIN THE CONFINES OF THE PORT,

THERE ARE AMPLE AREAS AVAILABLE, WHICH COULD BE ALLOCATED

FOR EXCLUSIVE HANDLING OF AFGHAN TRANSIT TRADE CARGO. IN

KOTECHA\'S VIEW, FOR THE REBUILDING OF AFGHANISTAN IT IS

ADVISABLE TO HAVE A SECOND PORT (SUCH AS BIN QASIM) TO

HANDLE AFGHAN TRANSIT TRADE. BIN QASIM MEETS THE TWO

PREREQUISITES MENTIONED ABOVE. NOTE: WFP HAS EXTENSIVELY

USED THIS PORT FOR BULK GRAIN SHIPMENTS FOR APPROXIMATELY 13

YEARS. END NOTE.

6. KOTECHA OPINED FURTHER THAT THE FACILITIES PRESENTLY

AVAILABLE AT KARACHI PORT WOULD BE HEAVILY STRETCHED TO

ALONE HANDLE THE ANTICIPATED INFLUX OF SHIP CARGO FOR

AFGHANISTAN.

-------

COMMENT

-------

USMISSION FEELS THAT THE PORT BIN QASIM OPTION MIGHT BE

PROFITABLY EXPLORED BY THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY. BOTH IFA

AND WFP HAVE OFFERED TO ACCOMPANY ANY ASSESSMENT TEAM
