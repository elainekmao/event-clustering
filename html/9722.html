SUBJECT: UAE OIL MINISTER SAYS OPEC QUOTAS WILL REMAIN

UNCHANGED

1. (U) UAE PETROLEUM MINISTER OBEID BIN SAIF AL-NASIRI TOLD

LOCAL REPORTERS ON JULY 27 THAT HE EXPECTS OPEC\'S PRODUCTION

QUOTAS TO REMAIN UNCHANGED AT THE CARTEL\'S JULY 31 MEETING

IN VIENNA. HE SAID THAT OPEC COULD NOT JUSTIFY A PRODUCTION

INCREASE AT THIS TIME BECAUSE OIL PRICES ARE WITHIN A RANGE

OF $22 TO $28 PER BARREL.

2. (U) AL-NASIRI CONFIRMED THAT THE UAE CONTINUES TO

PRODUCE AT ITS JUNE 1 QUOTA OF 2.2 MILLION BARRELS/DAY. HE

ALSO SAID THAT HE BELIEVES WORLD DEMAND FOR OIL WILL

INCREASE FROM ABOUT 1 MILLION TO 1.5 MILLION BARRELS PER DAY

DURING THE FOURTH QUARTER OF 2003. "OPEC WILL BE READY TO

MEET ANY INCREASE IN DEMAND," SAID AL-NASIRI, AND WILL

REVIEW PRODUCTION LEVELS AGAIN AT ITS REGULAR MEETING IN

SEPTEMBER.

3. (SBU) AL-NASIRI\'S TECHNICAL CONSULTANT DR. IBRAHIM

ISMAEL PRIVATELY TOLD ECONOFF ON JULY 28 HE DID NOT EXPECT

MUCH DEBATE AT THE JULY 31 MEETING. THE CARTEL WAS SEEKING

TO MAINTAIN THE STATUS QUO -- LOWER OVERALL OPEC PRODUCTION

IN JUNE AND CONTINUED DELAYS IN THE RESUMPTION OF IRAQI OIL

EXPORTS HAD PUSHED OIL PRICES TO THE UPPER END OF THE OPEC

PRICE RANGE.
