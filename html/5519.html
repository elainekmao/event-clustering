SUBJECT: UAE CENTRAL BANK ANNOUNCES SECOND INTERNATIONAL CONFERENCE ON

HAWALA, APRIL

2-3, 2003

REF: 02 ABU DHABI 2522

1. (U) Classified by Charge D\'Affaires Thomas Williams for reasons 1.5

(B) and (C).

2. (U) This is an action request; please see paragraph 5.

3. (C) On the heels of the very successful May 2002 Hawala Conference

in Abu Dhabi

(reftel), UAE Central Bank Governor Sultan Al-Suweidi officially

notified the

Ambassador on February 5 of his intention to host the Second

International Conference

on Hawala in Abu Dhabi, April 2-3, 2003. The preliminary agenda

includes panel

discussions by representatives from FATF, law enforcements agencies,

and academia,

and presentations from banking representatives from Central Asia, the

Middle East,

and Africa among others.

4. (C) Comment: Additional information regarding the conference

agenda and a

registration form will become available on the UAE Central Bank\'s

website

(WWW.CBUAE.GOV.AE) in the next few days. The draft agenda does not

mention charities

and charitable organizations, despite the Governor\'s earlier

indications that the

topic would be discussed during or on the margins of the Second Hawala

Conference.

We will raise this with the Governor in subsequent meetings and report

any response

we receive. End comment.

5. (C) Action request: Post requests that USG participants -- once an

official

delegation is constituted via the interagency process -- allow the

Embassy to

coordinate travel and accommodations and that they not/not register

directly for the

conference via the UAE Central Bank website. This will ensure that the

U.S.

delegation can be located together, simplifying considerably the

logistical

arrangements.

Williams
