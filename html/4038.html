SUBJECT: GENEVA GROUP DISCUSSES TERM LIMITS

REF: STATE 201776

1. SUMMARY: THE GENEVA GROUP DISCUSSED TERM LIMITS ON

OCTOBER 16. GERMANY HAS REQUESTED THAT THE ISSUE BE

INCLUDED IN THE AGENDA OF THE FAO COUNCIL WHICH MEETS

OCTOBER 28 TO NOVEMBER 2. ACCORDING TO THE GROUNDWORK

DONE BY DENMARK AS PRESIDENT OF THE EU, THERE IS

CONSIDERABLE OPPOSITION TO THIS INITIATIVE, ESPECIALLY

FROM AFRICANS WHO SEE IT AS AN EFFORT TO UNSEAT THE

CURRENT AFRICAN DG. MEMBERS APPLAUDED THE US DEMARCHE

AIMED AT SOFTENING RESISTANCE TO INCLUDING TERM LIMITS ON

THE AGENDA AND SOME OFFERED TO MAKE SIMILAR DEMARCHES TO

CAPITALS OF COUNCIL MEMBER COUNTRIES. THE MOST DESIRABLE

OUTCOME OF DISCUSSION IN THE FAO COUNCIL WOULD BE

FORMATION OF AN OPEN ENDED WORKING GROUP TO DISCUSS

ISSUES RELATED TO TERM LIMITS: HOW MANY TERMS, WHEN

LIMITS WOULD TAKE EFFECT, AND SHORTENING THE LENGTH OF

TERMS. END SUMMARY.

OF THE MATTER. (COMMENT: WE HAVE POINTED OUT TO THEM

3. THE AFRICANS ARE CONCERNED THAT THIS REFORM

INITIATIVE IS AIMED AT UNSEATING THE CURRENT DG, JACQUES

DIOUF OF SENEGAL. THEY MAY ALSO FEAR THAT A NEW DIRECTOR

GENERAL WOULD NOT CONTINUE TO SKEW THE PROGRAM EMPHASIS

MERIT SINCE AFRICA NEEDS THE MOST HELP, BUT THERE ARE

MANY POOR FARMERS IN RURAL AREAS ESPECIALLY OF OTHER

REGIONS WHO RECEIVE VERY LITTLE ASSISTANCE.) UGANDA,

SUPPORTED BY MAURITANIA, NIGERIA AND ERITREA THOUGHT THAT

TERM LIMITS WOULD BE POSITIVE FOR INSTITUTIONAL

DEVELOPMENT. MALI WAS THE MOST VOCAL OPPONENT. HE SAID

THE ISSUE WOULD TAKE THE COUNCIL\'S TIME AND ATTENTION

AWAY FROM MORE IMPORTANT ISSUES LIKE FOLLOW-UP TO THE

WORLD FOOD SUMMIT: FIVE YEARS LATER AND THE MEDIUM TERM

PLAN. ZIMBABWE SAID THE AFRICAN GROUP DID NOT WANT TO
