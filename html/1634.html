SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE\'S COMMERCIAL FARMERS MEET WITH GOZ

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 01293 111239Z

SECURITY SERVICES TO DISCUSS LAND REFORM

REFS: A. HARARE 1276, B. HARARE 1102

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\' AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS 1.5 (B/D).

1. (C) ON APRIL 10 EDGAR GUNDRY, CHAIRMAN FOR THE

COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION (CFU) REGION OF MASHONALAND

WEST-SOUTH, TOLD ECONOFF THAT A GROUP OF CFU OFFICIALS

MET ON APRIL 9 WITH ZIMBABWE\'S FIVE SECURITY SERVICES.

THESE SERVICES, ACCORDING TO GUNDRY, ARE THE PRESIDENT\'S

OFFICE, THE CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE ORGANIZATION, THE

ZIMBABWE REPUBLIC POLICE, THE ZIMBABWE NATIONAL ARMY,

AND THE ZIMBABWE AIR FORCE. THE PURPOSE OF THIS MEETING

WAS FOR THE CFU TEAM TO PRESENT THEIR "FARMING INTO THE

FUTURE" PLAN, A BLUEPRINT AGREED TO AT THE CFU SPECIAL

CONGRESS HELD IN MARCH (REF B). GUNDRY DID NOT KNOW THE

NAMES OF THE OFFICIALS WHO HAD REPRESENTED EACH OF THE

SECURITY SERVICES, BUT SAID THAT THE PRESENTATION WAS

VIDEOTAPED BY SECURITY OFFICIALS. HE BELIEVED THIS WAS

DONE SO THAT THE PRESENTATION COULD BE VIEWED BY

PRESIDENT MUGABE.

2. (C) GUNDRY WAS INFORMED BY THE CFU EXECUTIVE THAT THE

PRESENTATION WAS RECEIVED POSITIVELY BY THE FIVE

SECURITY SERVICE REPRESENTATIVES. HOWEVER, THE CFU TEAM

WAS ALSO TOLD BY ONE, APPARENTLY IN REFERENCE TO THE

OPPOSITION MOVEMENT FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE, THAT "YOU

CREATED A MONSTER, AND WE ARE NOT GOING TO FORGET THAT.

WE WILL FORGIVE YOU AND MOVE ON, BUT WE WILL NOT FORGET

THAT."

3. (C) GUNDRY SAID THAT THE MEETING WITH THE SECURITY

SERVICES WAS PART OF THE CFU\'S ONGOING EFFORT TO QUIETLY

RE-ENGAGE THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE (GOZ) ON THE LAND

ISSUE (REF A). AS PART OF THIS STRATEGY, GUNDRY

REVEALED THAT THE CFU TEAM WAS SCHEDULED TO MEET ON

APRIL 10 WITH THE PARLIAMENTARY COMMITTEE ON LAND TO

PRESENT THE CFU PLAN. GUNDRY HAS BEEN TOLD BY THE CFU

EXECUTIVE THAT THE GOZ OFFICIALS WHO HAVE BEEN PRESENTED

THE PLAN HAVE REACTED FAVORABLY TO IT, BUT THAT NONE OF

THESE OFFICIALS HAS THE AUTHORITY TO ACCEPT THE PLAN AS

A WAY FORWARD ON THE LAND ISSUE. IT IS CLEAR, GUNDRY

ADDED, THAT THE FINAL DECISION LIES WITH ONE PERSON --

PRESIDENT MUGABE.

4. (C) COMMENT: THE CONTINUING QUIET MEETINGS BETWEEN

THE CFU AND THE GOZ ARE POSITIVE INDICATORS. IT IS

CLEAR TO ALL THAT THE GOZ\'S "FAST TRACK" LAND

RESETTLEMENT PROGRAM IS GETTING NOWHERE FAST. OUR BEST

GUESS, THROUGH TALKING TO COMMERCIAL FARMERS AND

BUSINESSES, AND TRACKING PRESS REPORTS, IS THAT NO MORE

THAN 5,000 FAMILIES HAVE BEEN RESETTLED NATIONWIDE UNDER

"FAST TRACK". (NOTE: THE WORLD BANK ESTIMATES A TOTAL

OF 27,000 PEOPLE IN THESE 5,000 FAMILIES. THIS

CONTRASTS WITH THE LATEST GOZ CLAIM OF 80,000 FAMILIES

RESETTLED.) THE RESETTLEMENT THAT HAS OCCURRED HAS

ENTAILED GIVING PEOPLE A PIECE OF LAND ON AN OCCUPIED

FARM, AND PROVIDING NO OTHER INFRASTRUCTURE OR SUPPORT

SERVICES. GIVEN THAT THE GOZ HAS NO RESOURCES TO FUND A

REAL RESETTLEMENT PROGRAM, THE CURRENT FREEFALL IN THE

ZIMBABWEAN ECONOMY, AND THE CENTRAL ROLE THAT WHITE

COMMERCIAL FARMERS PLAY IN THE ECONOMY, THE PRESSURE IS

MOUNTING ON PRESIDENT MUGABE TO REACH A COMPROMISE ON

THE LAND ISSUE. HOWEVER, POLITICAL GAIN FROM THE LAND

ISSUE REMAINS THE OVERRIDING CONCERN FOR MUGABE. HE

WILL NOT AGREE TO A COMPROMISE WITH THE CFU AND WHITE

COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNLESS IT ALLOWS HIM A WAY TO

PROCLAIM VICTORY ON THE LAND ISSUE AND USE THIS TO

BOLSTER THE RULING PARTY IN THE PRESIDENTIAL RACE. OUR

VIEW IS THAT THERE IS ONLY A SLIM CHANCE MUGABE WILL

TAKE THIS OPTION. END COMMENT.
