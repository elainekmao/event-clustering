SUBJECT: ITALY ON THE SIERRA LEONE SPECIAL COURT: WE\'RE

BROKE

REF: STATE 94991

CLASSIFIED BY: A/POL MC GARY ROBBINS FOR REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D)

1. (C) POLOFF COORDINATED DELIVERY OF REFTEL DEMARCHE WITH

THE CANADIAN EMBASSY. MFA OFFICE DIRECTOR FOR WEST AFRICA,

SEBASTIANO FULCI, WAS FRANK IN HIS RESPONSE TO OUR (AND THE

UNSYG\'S) REQUEST FOR MORE SUPPORT: ITALY DOES NOT HAVE THE

MEANS. WHAT LIMITED GOI FUNDS ARE AVAILABLE FOR AFRICA AS A

WHOLE TEND TO GO TOWARDS THE HORN OF AFRICA. MR. FULCI, NEW

TO THE JOB IN SEPTEMBER 2002, NOTED THE GOI CHOSE TO FINANCE

ECOWAS IN THE IVORY COAST THIS YEAR FOR ABOUT 110,000 EURO,

SOAKING UP MONEY THAT HAD PREVIOUSLY BEEN EARMARKED FOR THE

SLSC DONATION.

2. (C) FULCI POINTED OUT THAT THE MAJORITY OF ITALIAN

FOREIGN-AID FUNDS ARE RESERVED BY LAW FOR DEVELOPMENT, AND

UNFORTUNATELY COULD NOT BE USED TO FINANCE THE COURT. WHEN

POLOFF POINTED OUT THE COURT SUPPORTS THE RULE OF LAW,

NECESSARY FOR ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT, FULCI SIMPLY SMILED AND

REPEATED THAT ITALIAN LAW PROHIBITED THE USE OF DEVELOPMENT

FUNDS FOR THE COURT. WHILE HE PERSONALLY DESIRED THAT THE

GOI SPEND MORE MONEY IN WEST AFRICA, IT SIMPLY WAS NOT NOW

AVAILABLE.

3. (U) MINIMIZE CONSIDERED.
