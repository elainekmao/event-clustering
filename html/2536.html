1. SUMMARY: EMBASSY ROME RECOMMENDS THAT ITALY REMAIN ON

THE 301 WATCH LIST. COPYRIGHT PIRACY LEVELS HAVE FALLEN

SHARPLY SINCE 2000, WHEN ITALY\'S NEW COPYRIGHT LAW WAS

ENACTED. LAW ENFORCEMENT IS FAR MORE AGGRESSIVE. HOWEVER,

PROBLEMS PERSIST, PARTICULARLY WITH THE JUDICIARY, CERTAIN

MUNICIPAL LEADERS, AND THE SIAE (ITALY\'S COPYRIGHT ROYALTY

COLLECTION AGENCY). END SUMMARY.

2. THE IPR SITUATION IN ITALY IMPROVED DURING 2001, WITH

PIRACY LEVELS DROPPING BY OVER 30 PERCENT FROM 1999,

ACCORDING TO THE IIPA. THE ENACTMENT OF THE 2000 COPYRIGHT

LAW IN SEPTEMBER 2001 RAISED CRIMINAL PENALTIES AND LED TO A

NEW AWARENESS AMONG LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICIALS AND THE PUBLIC

THAT PIRACY IS A CRIME. LAW ENFORCEMENT COOPERATION WITH THE

IPR INDUSTRY HAS BEEN VERY GOOD, PARTICULARLY AMONG THE

CARABINIERI (NATIONAL POLICE WITHIN THE DEFENSE MINISTRY) AND

THE GUARDIA DI FINANZA (FISCAL POLICE WITHIN THE FINANCE

MINISTRY). FAR MORE ARRESTS HAVE MEANT LESS PIRACY,

PARTICULARLY IN CITIES WHERE THE MAYORS AND PREFECTS SUPPORT

POLICE EFFORTS.

3. RESULTS HAVE VARIED FROM CITY TO CITY, DEPENDING UPON

MUNICIPAL OFFICIALS. FOR EXAMPLE, THE MAYOR AND PREFECT IN

ROME HAVE BEEN UNWILLING TO ALLOW POLICE TO ACT AGGRESSIVELY

AGAINST STREET VENDERS OF PIRATED MATERIAL. AS A RESULT,

SUCH STREET SALES HAVE CONTINUED UNIMPEDED. OFFICIALS IN

CERTAIN CITIES HAVE ATTEMPTED TO STOP LOCAL CARABINIERI FROM

ACTING. ON THE OTHER HAND, TRIESTE CITY OFFICIALS HAVE BEEN

VERY SUPPORTIVE. AS A RESULT, IN THAT CITY, STREET VENDORS

OF PIRATED MATERIAL HAVE ALMOST DISAPPEARED AND PIRACY RATES

ARE EXTREMELY LOW.

4. PROSECUTORS AND JUDGES VARY IN QUALITY AS WELL. MANY

PROSECUTORS ARE UNWILLING TO BRING CRIMINAL CHARGES AGAINST

STREET VENDORS, FACTORY WORKERS OR SHOP WORKERS. JUDGES ARE

OFTEN UNWILLING TO CONVICT, ARGUING THAT "MINOR" ECONOMIC

CRIMES DO NOT JUSTIFY CRIMINAL TREATMENT. ON THE OTHER HAND,

AS THE EMBASSY AND CGS HAVE HELD CONFERENCES AND SEMINARS

WITH THE JUDICIAL COMMUNITY, WE HAVE SEEN A GROWING NUMBER OF

MAGISTRATES TAKE THIS CRIME MORE SERIOUSLY. SOME JUDGES HAVE

BECOME IMPORTANT ALLIES IN OUR EFFORTS.

5. THE TYPE OF PIRACY HAS VARIED AS WELL. ACCORDING TO

FAPAV (THE AUDIO-VISUAL ANTI-PIRACY ASSOCIATION), CASSETTE

PIRACY HAS DROPPED DRAMATICALLY. HOWEVER, PIRATED DVDS HAVE

JUST BEGUN TO APPEAR, AND COULD BECOME A FAR MORE SERIOUS

PROBLEM. "SMART-CARD" PIRACY -- COUNTERFEIT PAY-TV CARDS --

IS EQUAL TO AT LEAST 50 PERCENT OF THE PAY-TV MARKET IN ITALY

(REF B). ON THE OTHER HAND, THE BUSINESS SOFTWARE ALLIANCE\'S

PRELIMINARY EVALUATION OF THE ITALIAN PIRACY RATES INDICATES

THAT THERE MAY BE A DECLINE IN WHITE COLLAR IN-FIRM ILLEGAL

DUPLICATION OF SOFTWARE. REGARDING THE MUSIC INDUSTRY, THE

FIMI REP INDICATED THAT IT IS DIFFICULT TO ESTABLISH HOW MUCH

OF THE DECLINE IN CD SALES IS ATTRIBUTABLE TO PIRACY AND HOW

MUCH TO OTHER FACTORS SUCH AS THE HIGH PRICE OF CDS (OR JUST

"NOT VERY GOOD MUSIC"). HOWEVER, FIMI CONFIRMED THAT PIRACY

SIAE: INSPECTING FOR STICKERS, NOT PIRACY

--------------------------------------------- ----------

6. WE REMAIN CONCERNED BY THE INEFFECTIVE ANTI-PIRACY

EFFORTS OF SIAE, THE COPYRIGHT COLLECTION ENTITY. IPR

INDUSTRIES ARE WORKING INTENSIVELY -- WITH ACTIVE SUPPORT

FROM EMBASSY ROME AND CONSULATES MILAN, NAPLES AND FLORENCE

-- TO IMPROVE ENFORCEMENT AND EDUCATION. HOWEVER, SIAE HAS

BEEN MORE AN OBSTACLE THAN AN ALLY.

7. IN ACCORD WITH ARTICLE 11 OF THE 2000 COPYRIGHT LAW, SIAE

IS RESPONSIBLE FOR ANTI-PIRACY EFFORTS IN ITALY. BECAUSE

SIAE RECEIVES ALL REVENUES FROM ITS STICKER, WHICH IS

REQUIRED FOR COPYRIGHTED MATERIALS UNDER THE LAW (WITH THE

EXCEPTION OF BUSINESS SOFTWARE), IT HAS ADEQUATE RESOURCES

FOR THESE EFFORTS. HOWEVER, WE UNDERSTAND THAT SIAE HAS TO

DATE DIRECTED ITS EFFORTS SOLELY AT INSPECTING LEGITIMATE

COPYRIGHTED GOODS TO ENSURE THAT THESE CARRY A SIAE STICKER.

SIAE HAS DONE NOTHING, TO OUR KNOWLEDGE, TO ACT AGAINST

ILLEGAL COPYRIGHTED GOODS.

8. WE HAVE REPORTED (REF C AND PREVIOUS) ON THE SIAE

STICKER ISSUE, AND OUR EFFORTS TO RESOLVE IT. IN LATE

JANUARY, BSA OFFICIALS MET WITH UNDER SECRETARY LETTA IN THE

WOULD BE REVISED TO PERMIT ROUTINE EXEMPTIONS FOR BUSINESS

9. INDUSTRY AND EMBASSY EFFORTS AT IPR EDUCATION AND

ENFORCEMENT, WHILE EXTENSIVE, ARE NOT ENOUGH. OUR COMBINED

EFFORTS HAVE IMPROVED THE SITUATION, PARTICULARLY WITH LAW

ENFORCEMENT AND, IN SOME CASES, WITH THE JUDICIARY. HOWEVER,

SIAE HAS THE NATIONAL RESPONSIBILITY AND THE RESOURCES. WE

HAVE PRESSED SIAE TO INVOLVE ITSELF IN EDUCATION AND

ENFORCEMENT EFFORTS, BUT HAVE SEEN NO ACTION YET.

AMENDMENTS TO EXISTING LEGISLATION

--------------------------------------------- ---

11. ITALY HAS NOT AMENDED ITS 2000 COPYRIGHT LAW AND THE

IMPLEMENTING REGULATIONS ENACTED IN 2001. AS STATED ABOVE,

WE ARE PRESSING FOR AN AMENDMENT TO CERTAIN REGULATIONS TO

PERMIT ROUTINE EXEMPTIONS FROM THE SIAE STICKER FOR BUSINESS

SOFTWARE.

GOVERNMENT SOFTWARE

------------------------------

12. WE HAVE RECEIVED NO COMPLAINTS/INFORMATION REGARDING

COMMENT

-------------

13. THE INTERNATIONAL INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ALLIANCE (IIPA)

RECOMMENDS THAT ITALY REMAIN ON THE WATCH LIST. IPR INDUSTRY

REPRESENTATIVES HERE SHARE THAT ASSESSMENT. WE AGREE.

PIRACY HAS FALLEN BY OVER 30 PERCENT IN THE LAST TWO YEARS --

SINCE ITALY PASSED ITS TOUGH COPYRIGHT LAW IN 2000 -- BUT

MUCH REMAINS TO BE DONE.
