2. THE 56TH SESSION OF THE UNIDROIT GENERAL ASSEMBLY MET

DECEMBER 6, 2002 IN ROME. AUSTRALIAN AMBASSADOR MURRAY

COBAIN WAS ELECTED CHAIRMAN OF THE ASSEMBLY. UNIDROIT

SECRETARY GENERAL HERBERT KRONKE PROVIDED UPDATES ON

SIPDIS

UNIDROIT PROJECTS IN THE AREAS OF PRINCIPLES OF

INTERNATIONAL COMMERCIAL CONTRACTS, TRANSNATIONAL CIVIL

PROCEDURES, MODEL LAW ON DISCLOSURE AND FRANCHISING AND

TAKING OF SECURITIES AND SECURITIZATION. HE WELCOMED THE

RECENT SECONDMENT OF A NEW STAFF MEMBER (FUNDED BY THE

GERMAN BANKING FEDERATION) TO WORK ON THE CAPITAL MARKETS

PROJECT. KRONKE NOTED THAT WORK ON THE SECOND AND THIRD

PROTOCOL TO THE RECENTLY AGREED CONVENTION ON MOBILE

ASSETS WAS ADVANCING. HE ALSO EXPLAINED THE (HERETO

UNMET) STAFFING AND RESOURCE IMPLICATIONS OF UNIDROIT\'S

NEW OBLIGATIONS AS THE DEPOSITARY TO THE RECENTLY

APPROVED CAPETOWN CONVENTION AND AIRCRAFT PROTOCOL.

3. AT THE REQUEST OF THE U.S. DELEGATE, KRONKE UPDATED

MEMBERS ON THE QUESTION OF EC MEMBERSHIP IN THE

COMMISSION. HE NOTED THAT SINCE EC ACCESSION TO THE

HAGUE CONFERENCES WAS "IN THE WORKS," HE EXPECTED EC

ACCESSION TO UNIDROIT TO BE "CONCLUDED UNDER THE ITALIAN

PRESIDENCY."

4. THE ASSEMBLY APPROVED THE FINAL ADJUSTMENTS TO THE

5. THE ITALIAN DELEGATE STATED THAT THE RECENT

ANNOUNCEMENT THAT THE GOVERNMENT OF ITALY WOULD LOWER ITS

PAYMENT TO UNIDROIT WAS A RESULT OF THE FACT THAT

UNIDROIT HAD HISTORICALLY BEEN "INCORRECTLY CLASSIFIED"

AS A NATIONAL INSTITUTE (RATHER THAN AN INTERNATIONAL

ORGANIZATION). IN THE PAST THIS HAD BEEN EFFECTIVE IN

FACILITATING PAYMENT; NOW IT WAS AN ALBATROSS, AS THE

GOVERNMENT\'S AUSTERITY PACKAGE COVERED ALL DOMESTIC

ORGANIZATIONS. THE DELEGATE SAID THAT EFFORTS WERE BEING

MADE TO CORRECT THE CLASSIFICATION. U.S.DEL EXPRESSED

GRATITUDE FOR THE SUPPORT OF THE ITALIAN GOVERNMENT AND

UNDERLINED THE IMPORTANCE OF ITALY MOVING TO A MORE

PREDICTABLE SYSTEM OF CONTRIBUTIONS FOR UNIDROIT.
