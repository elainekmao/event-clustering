1. (U) THIS IS AN ACTION REQUEST. SEE PARA 3.

2. (C) SUMMARY: SINCE THE START OF THE ZAMBIAN GMO FOOD AID

CRISIS, EMBASSY VATICAN HAS PRESSED HARD AT VARIOUS LEVELS OF

THE HOLY SEE FOR A STRONG STATEMENT IN SUPPORT OF U.S. FOOD

ASSISTANCE FOR SOUTHERN AFRICA AND BEYOND. BUILDING ON OVER

A YEAR OF DIALOGUE ON THE SAFETY OF GMO\'S, THE VATICAN IS

FINALLY MOVING TOWARD ACCEPTANCE OF THE SAFETY OF GMO\'S, BUT

HAS PREFERRED TO FOCUS NARROWLY ON THE SITUATION IN ZAMBIA.

OUR MUTUAL INTEREST IN FEEDING THE WORLD\'S HUNGRY OFFERS AN

OPPORTUNITY TO SECURE A MORE POSITIVE PUBLIC POSITION FROM

THE HOLY SEE ON THE SAFETY OF BIOTECH FOOD FOR HUMAN

CONSUMPTION. WHILE SUCH A STATEMENT WOULD DOUBTLESS BE

CAREFULLY WORDED AND NOT COMPLETELY ELIMINATE VATICAN

CONCERNS AT ENVIRONMENTAL DANGERS FROM BIOTECH CROPS, IT

COULD FACILITATE OUR BROADER EFFORT TO ERODE EUROPEAN AND

OTHER INTERNATIONAL NON-SCIENTIFIC OBJECTIONS TO BIOTECH

FOODS. END SUMMARY.

3. (C) ACTION REQUESTED: FOLLOWING UP ON THE SECRETARY\'S

STATEMENT ON GMO FOODS AT THE JOHANNESBURG SUMMIT, POST

REQUESTS DEPARTMENT CONSIDER A LETTER FROM SECRETARY POWELL

TO VATICAN FM TAURAN URGING THE HOLY SEE TO ISSUE A PUBLIC

4. (SBU) FOLLOWING REPORTS THAT CATHOLIC PRIESTS IN ZAMBIA

HAD SUPPORTED THE GOZ\'S DECISION TO WITHHOLD U.S. PROVIDED

FOOD AID (REF G), POLOFF MET AUGUST 22 WITH UNDERSECRETARY OF

THE PONTIFICAL COUNCIL FOR REFUGEES AND MIGRANT PEOPLES

FATHER MICHAEL BLUME, AND AUGUST 23 WITH JESUIT REFUGEE

SERVICE PROGRAM DIRECTOR FOR SOUTHERN AFRICA JENNY CAFISO TO

URGE THEM TO MAKE CLEAR THEIR SUPPORT FOR THE SAFETY OF U.S.

FOOD ASSISTANCE. BOTH TOOK ON BOARD OUR POINTS ON FOOD AID

FROM REF H, AND REFERRED US TO THE JESUITS AT THE LUSAKA

OFFICE OF THE JESUIT REFUGEE SERVICE. SUBSEQUENTLY, DCM AND

POLOFF MET AUGUST 29 WITH VATICAN DEPUTY FOREIGN MINISTER,

MONSIGNOR CELESTINO MIGLIORE. MIGLIORE HAD NOT HEARD OF THE

ZAMBIAN IMBROGLIO, BUT PROMISED TO LOOK INTO IT.

5. (C) AT A SEPTEMBER 5 FOLLOW-UP MEETING WITH VATICAN MFA

SOUTHERN AFRICA DIRECTOR MONS. FRANCESCO CAVINA, POLOFF

OFFERED COPIES OF THE EC AND UN STATEMENTS SUPPORTING US FOOD

ASSISTANCE. NOTING THAT THE USG AND HOLY SEE SHARE A STRONG

COMMON INTEREST IN HELPING TO FEED HUNGRY PEOPLE AROUND THE

WORLD, POLOFF URGED THE HOLY SEE TO ISSUE A STATEMENT SIMILAR

TO THOSE OF THE EC, UN, AND WHO. CAVINA, WHO HAD BEEN

BRIEFED BY MIGLIORE, SAID THE VATICAN HAD DECIDED TO TAKE

ACTION TO CLARIFY THE CHURCH\'S POSITION. THEIR PLAN OF

7. (C) FOR THE PAST EIGHTEEN MONTHS POST HAS WORKED TO GET TO

HOLY SEE TO BUILD ON ITS CAUTIOUSLY OPTIMISTIC PUBLIC STANCE

TOWARDS BIOTECHNOLOGY. IN OUR VIEW, A STRONG PUBLIC STANCE IN

SUPPORT OF AGRICULTURAL BIOTECHNOLOGY IS A NATURAL FOR ANYONE

INTERESTED IN PROMOTING FOOD SECURITY IN THE DEVELOPING

WORLD. THE ZAMBIAN FOOD CRISIS, AS TRAGIC AS IT IS, MAY

PRESENT A "DEFINING MOMENT" FOR OUR BIOTECH DIALOGUE WITH THE

HOLY SEE. NOBODY, AND ESPECIALLY NOT THE POPE, CAN ARGUE

THAT A PRECAUTIONARY PRINCIPLE SHOULD APPLY WHEN IT MEANS

REAL HUMAN BEINGS WILL GO HUNGRY OR EVEN STARVE. WITH U.S.

FOOD AID SO VITAL TO ADDRESSING THE SOUTHERN AFRICAN FOOD

CRISIS, THE HOLY SEE SHOULD BE SINGING FROM THE SAME SHEET

WITH US. WE ARE, IN FACT, COORDINATING POSITIONS AT THE UN ON

A CONVENTION TO BAN ALL FORMS OF HUMAN CLONING -- THE "FLIP
