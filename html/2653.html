SUBJECT: MEDIA REACTION CALL FOR POLITICAL PARTY

TOLERANCE IN ZIM; HARARE

1. UNDER HEADLINE "NEW POLITICAL CULTURE OVERDUE,"

THE

GOVERNMENT-CONTROLLED DAILY "THE HERALD" USED ITS

MARCH 20 EDITORIAL TO CALL ON THE TWO MAJOR

POLITICAL PARTIES - ZANU PF AND MDC - IN ZIMBABWE

TO CO-EXIST, SAYING THE PARTIES " HAVE TO LEARN TO

LIVE TOGETHER, ARGUE TOGETHER AND OPPOSE EACH OTHER

IN A WAY THAT BUILDS ZIMBABWE." EXCERPTS:

2. ". . .ZIMBABWE IS LONG OVERDUE FOR A CHANGE IN THE

WAY IT REGARDS POLITICS. OVER THE PAST TWO YEARS THE

COUNTRY HAS LIVED WITH A TWO-PARTY SYSTEM. YET THERE

ARE FAR TOO MANY REMNANTS OF A CULTURE WHERE THERE WAS

JUST ONE DOMINANT PARTY AND A BUNCH OF HAS-BEENS

PROVIDING A TRIVIAL OPPOSITION. REGRETTABLY, THE

INHERITED SYSTEM OF GOVERNMENT FROM THE COLONIAL DAYS

WAS ALSO BUILT AROUND THE IDEA OF A MONOLITHIC RULING

PARTY. . .

"THE FIRST CONCEPT WE NEED TO INTRODUCE INTO A NEW

POLITICAL CULTURE IS THAT THE OTHER PARTY IS NOT

THE ENEMY. THE OTHER PARTY MIGHT BE WRONG, IT

MIGHT EVEN BE PIGHEADED, BUT IT IS STILL PART OF

ZIMBABWE AND HAS TO BE REGARDED AS SUCH. PARTIES

CAN OPPOSE EACH OTHER, AND DO SO VIGOROUSLY, BUT

THEIR AIM SHOULD NOT BE TO DESTROY EACH OTHER. THE

SECOND THING WE HAVE TO ACCEPT IS THAT THE DAYS OF

A MONOLITHIC PARTY ARE GONE, FOREVER. . .

"IN SHORT, TALKS BETWEEN ZANU PF AND THE MDC SHOULD

NOT DWELL TOO MUCH ON TRYING TO FIND COMMON GROUND

IN POLICY. THEY DIFFER TOO MUCH. ONE OR THE OTHER

WOULD HAVE TO SELL OUT IN ITS CORE BELIEFS. BUT

PARTIES SHOULD HELP CREATE AND SUSTAIN A NEW

POLITICAL CULTURE THAT TAKES INTO ACCOUNT THE

REALITY OF OUR COUNTRY. WE HAVE TWO LARGE, WELL-

SUPPORTED PARTIES LED BY THE ONLY TWO MEN EVER TO

WIN MORE THAN 1 MILLION VOTES IN A ZIMBABWEAN

ELECTION. WE HAVE TO LEARN TO LIVE TOGETHER, ARGUE

TOGETHER AND OPPOSE EACH OTHER IN A WAY THAT BUILDS

ZIMBABWE. THAT IS THE CHALLENGE."

SULLIVAN
