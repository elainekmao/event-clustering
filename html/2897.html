-- REPORTING: MIDDLE EAST, VENEZUELA, UNGASS.

2. (SBU) AMBASSADOR HOSTED WHITE HOUSE PRE-ADVANCE FOR

DISCUSSION OF PRESIDENT\'S LATE MAY NATO SUMMIT VISIT TO A

BASE SEVERAL MILES OUTSIDE OF ROME. IT WAS NOTED THAT THERE

WAS NO PLAN FOR A BILAT IN VATICAN CITY WITH THE POPE.

(NOTE: IF THE PRESIDENT VISITS ROME -- I.E. THE CITY CENTER

-- AND DOES NOT SCHEDULE TIME WITH THE POPE, THIS WILL BE THE

FIRST TIME THAT HAS HAPPENED.)

3. (C) EVENING OF APRIL 19, AMBASSADOR AND POLOFF CALLED ON

FM TAURAN, ON INSTRUCTION, TO REQUEST APPROPRIATE VATICAN AND

CHURCH ENGAGEMENT IN VENEZUELAN "NATIONAL DIALOGUE" IN

SUPPORT FOR RECONCILIATION. (SEPTEL). AMBASSADOR AND TAURAN

ALSO DISCUSSED THE SITUATION IN BETHLEHEM AT THE CHURCH OF

THE NATIVITY AND VATICAN FEARS THAT IT WAS BECOMING UNSTABLE.

4. (U) AMBASSADOR HOSTED LUNCH APRIL 16 WITH MAIN VATICAN

INTERLOCUTORS TO DISCUSS PREPARATIONS FOR UPCOMING

ANTI-TRAFFICKING CONFERENCE. THE VATICAN OFFICIALS FROM THE

FOREIGN MINISTRY AND PONTIFICAL COUNCILS FOR JUSTICE AND

PEACE AND FOR MIGRANTS AND ITINERANTS WERE PLEASED AT THE

BROAD INTERNATIONAL INTEREST IN ATTENDING AND SPEAKING AT THE

CONFERENCE AND AGREED TO TAKE EMBASSY\'S SUGGESTIONS AS TO

VATICAN SPEAKING/MODERATING ROLES AT THE CONFERENCE.

5. (U) A/S BETH JONES PAID A COURTESY CALL ON AMBASSADOR

PAGE 03 VATICA 02238 01 OF 02 081435Z

APRIL 23. DISCUSSION FOCUSED ON MIDDLE EAST; GROWING

ANTI-SEMITISM IN EUROPE AND VATICAN REACTIONS; BENEFITS OF

HAVING THE POPE UP TO SPEED ON U.S. THINKING AND INTENTIONS.

AT HER TOWN HALL MEETING AT EMBASSY ROME, RESPONDING TO A

QUERY FROM POL/ADMINOFF, A/S JONES ENCOURAGED POST TO ELICIT

GREATER ENGAGEMENT FROM THE VATICAN ON ROMA/SINTI ISSUES IN

EUROPE.

6. (U) AMBASSADOR CALLED ON MILAN CARDINAL MARTINI APRIL 26.

MARTINI, UNTIL RECENTLY SEEN AS A STRONG CONTENDER IN THE

PAPAL SUCCESSION STAKES, IS RETIRING IN SEPTEMBER. HE WILL

RETURN TO THE MIDDLE EAST TO STUDY AND, "IF ASKED," TO DO

WHAT HE CAN TO PROMOTE RECONCILIATION BETWEEN THE DIFFERENT

RELIGIOUS COMMUNITIES THERE.

7. (U) AMBASSADOR\'S PUBLIC DIPLOMACY: DURING THE REPORTING

PERIOD AMBASSADOR CONDUCTED INTERVIEWS OR BACKGROUNDERS ON

U.S./VATICAN RELATIONS AND THE UPCOMING ANTI-TRAFFICKING

CONFERENCE WITH: PEGGY POLK OF THE RELIGIOUS NEWS SERVICE AND

SARAH DELANEY OF THE WASHINGTON POST APRIL 17; JEFF ISRAELI

OF TIME MAGAZINE APRIL 18; VATICAN RADIO AND ALESSIO VINCI OF

CNN APRIL 19; MELINDA HENEBERGER OF THE NEW YORK TIMES AND

JOHN HENDERSON OF THE DENVER POST APRIL 23.

8. (C) DCM ACTIVITIES:

-- DCM MET APRIL 16 WITH MICROSOFT GOVERNMENT RELATIONS

DIRECTOR FABIO FALEZA TO DISCUSS MICROSOFT\'S PROPOSAL TO

DIGITIZE VATICAN ARCHIVES. ESTIMATED COST IS $300 MILLION;

MICROSOFT SEES WORK UNDERTAKEN BY A CONSORTIUM OF COMPANIES.

DCM CHAIRED FOLLOW-UP MEETING APRIL 30, INCLUDING EMBASSY

9. (C) POLITICAL SECTION:

-- POLOFF TOOK ADVANTAGE OF 4/16 LUNCH (PARA 4 ABOVE) TO

PRESENT DEMARCHE POINTS TO VATICAN DFM AND JUSTICE AND PEACE

UNDERSECRETARY ON UN SPECIAL SESSION ON CHILDREN. POINTS

REQUESTED VATICAN HELP IN ENGAGING LATIN AMERICAN CAPITALS TO

SUPPORT APPROPRIATE LANGUAGE ON CHILDREN\'S HEALTHCARE RIGHTS

IN THE UNGASS. (SEPTEL).

-- IN ADDITION TO SPENDING AN AVERAGE OF 2-3 HOURS PER DAY

ORGANIZING SPEAKERS AND ATTENDEES FOR THE ANTI-TRAFFICKING

CONFERENCE, POLOFF MET WITH U/S MICHAEL BLUME, APRIL 17, TO

COORDINATE HOLY SEE SPEAKERS AND THEIR TEXTS. BLUME WAS

HAPPY TO TAKE A SPEAKING ROLE OUTLINING THE BROAD HUMAN

RIGHTS DIMENSION OF THE PROBLEM.
