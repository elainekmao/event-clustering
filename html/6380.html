SUBJECT: UNBLOCKING SFRY ASSETS: PICULA SAYS GOC STILL

LOOKING AT PROPOSAL

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 ZAGREB 00578 141741Z

REF: A. (A) STATE 50887

B. (B) ZAGREB 450

CLASSIFIED BY: NICHOLAS HILL, POLITICAL ECONOMIC

COUNSELOR, FOR REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D)

1. (C) THE AMBASSADOR DELIVERED DEMARCHE (REF A) CONCERNING

USG PLANS TO UNBLOCK SFRY ASSETS TO FOREIGN MINISTER PICULA

MARCH 14. PICULA WAS NON-COMMITTAL, BUT AGAIN POINTED TO THE

DIFFICULTY OF PROVIDING A HOLD HARMLESS IN THE ABSENCE OF A

RATIFIED SUCCESSION AGREEMENT. AS HE HAS IN PREVIOUS

MEETINGS WITH THE PRIME MINISTER AND FOREIGN MINISTER, THE

AMBASSADOR STRESSED SEVERAL TIMES THAT PROVIDING A HOLD

HARMLESS AGREEMENT WOULD NOT PREJUDICE CROATIA\'S CASE WITH

RESPECT TO THE ASI. IN THE ABSENCE OF A HOLD HARMLESS,

HOWEVER, CROATIA STOOD TO LOSE ALMOST $52 MILLION.

2. (C) THE AMBASSADOR UNDERSCORED THE APRIL 15 DEADLINE IN

THE DEMARCHE, BUT NOTED THAT ANY DECISION SHOULD COME

SUFFICIENTLY IN ADVANCE TO GIVE TIME TO REVIEW THEIR

POSITIONS TO OTHER SUCCESSOR STATES WHO HAVE BEEN TAKING

THEIR LEAD FROM THE GOC.
