SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) THE HOLY SEE DELEGATION TO THE 59TH UN HUMAN RIGHTS

COMMISSION CONVEYED TO EMBASSY ITS WILLINGNESS TO WORK WITH

US TO ADDRESS ISSUES OF MUTUAL INTEREST, PARTICULARLY

ECONOMIC AND DEVELOPMENT RIGHTS, RELIGIOUS FREEDOM, AND

MIGRATION. HOLY SEE DELEGATE TO THE UNHRC, BISHOP DIARMUID

MARTIN, SITS ON THE WORKING GROUP ON TRIPS AND PUBLIC HEALTH,

AND CAN BE EXPECTED TO PRESS THE HOLY SEE\'S POSITION THAT

DEVELOPED COUNTRIES MUST SHOW GREATER SOLIDARITY WITH THE

POOR. THE HOLY SEE DELEGATION, THOUGH NON-VOTING, IS WILLING

TO WORK BEHIND THE SCENES ON COUNTRY RESOLUTIONS, WITH THE

PROVISO THAT SUCH RESOLUTIONS ARE "CONSTRUCTIVE." THEY

SPECIFICALLY OFFERED TO WORK WITH US ON THE BELARUS

RESOLUTION. END SUMMARY.

---------------------

AVOIDING POLARIZATION

---------------------

2. (C) POLOFF AND POLINTERN MET MARCH 17 WITH THE HOLY

SEE\'S DIRECTOR FOR UN ISSUES, MSGR. ETTORE BALESTRERO TO

REVIEW THE U.S. POSITIONS ON KEY UNHRC ISSUES AND SOLICIT

VATICAN SUPPORT FOR OUR APPROACH. WHILE NOTING THAT THE HOLY

SEE DOES NOT VOTE ON THE COMMISSION, BALESTRERO SAID IT TAKES

THE COMMISSION\'S WORK SERIOUSLY AND WILL ENGAGE ACTIVELY AT

THE HRC, BOTH BEHIND THE SCENES AND OCCASIONALLY OPENLY WITH

PARTNERS, TO ADVANCE ITS HUMAN RIGHTS AGENDA. THE HOLY SEE\'S

GENERAL APPROACH AT THE 59TH UNHRC WOULD BE TO SEEK WHAT THEY

REGARD AS "CONSTRUCTIVE" HRC RESOLUTIONS THAT OFFER COUNTRIES

THE OPPORTUNITY TO "GO STRAIGHT" RATHER THAN SIMPLY

CONDEMNING AND PUNISHING THEM. BALESTRERO SAID THEY WOULD

URGE THE HRC TO BE SENSITIVE TO SIGNALS FROM COUNTRIES TRYING

TO DEVELOP, YET NOT FULLY IN LINE WITH HUMAN RIGHTS

STANDARDS. BALESTRERO STRESSED SEVERAL TIMES THE HOLY SEE\'S

WISH THAT THE HRC NOT BECOME POLITICIZED -- A DISTINCT

POSSIBILITY GIVEN THE COUNTRIES NOW SITTING ON THE

COMMISSION. HE SPECIFICALLY NOTED THE LIBYAN CHAIRMANSHIP AS

POTENTIALLY POLARIZING, AND NEEDING CAREFUL TREATMENT TO

ENSURE CONSTRUCTIVE OUTCOMES.

--------------------

AREAS OF COOPERATION

--------------------

3. (C) TURNING TO AREAS FOR POTENTIAL U.S.-HOLY SEE

COOPERATION AT THE HRC, MSGR. BALESTRERO FIRST NOTED THAT THE

HOLY SEE HAD PROBLEMS WITH THE LANGUAGE OF BEIJING 5, AND

THAT WE SHOULD WORK TOGETHER TO INSERT MUTUALLY BENEFICIAL

LANGUAGE ON REPRODUCTIVE HEALTH TO THE DRAFT TEXT. SECONDLY,

HE EXPLAINED THE HOLY SEE\'S DESIRE TO MONITOR CLOSELY THE

RESOLUTION ON AIDS FOR LANGUAGE WHICH MIGHT CREATE PROBLEMS

FOR BOTH OF US. READING FROM THE DRAFT AGENDA, BALESTRERO

LISTED FOUR ADDITIONAL AREAS IN WHICH THE HOLY SEE AND USG

COULD COOPERATE: RACISM; GLOBALIZATION AND HUMAN RIGHTS;

RELIGIOUS INTOLERANCE AND MIGRATION. AS FOR COUNTRY

RESOLUTIONS, HE OFFERED BELARUS AS ONE COUNTRY WHERE THE HOLY

SEE DELEGATION WILL TAKE AN ACTIVE ROLE IN NEGOTIATING A

RESOLUTION. THOUGH HE ADMITTED THE 2002 BELARUS LAW ON

RELIGIONS IS NOT SPECIFICALLY A ROMAN CATHOLIC ISSUE, IT DOES

HAVE IMPLICATIONS FOR THE CHURCH IN BELARUS. MOREOVER, THE

HOLY SEE IS COMMITTED TO PROMOTING RELIGIOUS FREEDOM

5. (C) THE HOLY SEE CONTINUES TO REGARD THE RIGHT TO

DEVELOPMENT AS A FUNDAMENTAL RIGHT, AND CAN THEREFORE BE

EXPECTED TO SEEK TO PUSH THE ENVELOP ON THESE ISSUES FURTHER

THAN WE WOULD LIKE TO SEE. BALESTRERO OBSERVED THAT THE U.S.

APPEARS TO THE HOLY SEE AT TIMES AS FOCUSING TOO MUCH ON

POLITICAL AND CIVIC RIGHTS AT THE EXPENSE OF SOCIAL AND

ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT RIGHTS. POLOFF REITERATED THE U.S. VIEW

THAT ECONOMIC AND SOCIAL DEVELOPMENT COULD ONLY TAKE PLACE IN

AN ENVIRONMENT WHERE POLITICAL RIGHTS WERE GUARANTEED AND

THERE WAS RESPECT FOR THE RULE OF LAW. FURTHERMORE, POLOFF

NOTED THAT DEVELOPMENT ISSUES WERE BEST ADDRESSED IN OTHER UN

FORA.

--------------------

HUMAN RIGHTS IN IRAQ

--------------------

6. (C) IN A BRIEF REVIEW OF THE HUMAN RIGHTS SITUATION IN

IRAQ, BALESTRERO CALLED THE HUMAN RIGHTS VIOLATIONS IN THAT

COUNTRY "CATASTROPHIC," AND EXPRESSED HIS HOPE THAT THE

SITUATION WILL BE SOLVED WITHOUT RESORTING TO VIOLENCE. HE

WAS OPEN TO WORKING ON AN EVENTUAL RESOLUTION, WHILE NOTING

THAT THE SITUATION IS NOW SO FLUID THAT ALL COUNTRIES WILL

HAVE TO BE FLEXIBLE.

COMMENT

-------

7. (C) THE HOLY SEE IS CLEARLY A WILLING PARTNER ON MOST

ISSUES FACING THE UNHRC. THOUGH WE DISAGREE WITH THEIR

DESIRE TO ADDRESS ECONOMIC AND DEVELOPMENT RIGHTS IN THIS

FORUM, ON OTHER ISSUES BEFORE THE COMMISSION, OUR VIEWS ARE

CLOSE AND COOPERATION IS POSSIBLE. BISHOP DIARMUID MARTIN,

FORMER SECRETARY OF THE PONTIFICAL COUNCIL FOR JUSTICE AND

PEACE (AND 1980\'S INTERNATIONAL VISITOR TO THE U.S.), WILL BE

A STRONG ADVOCATE FOR CORE HOLY SEE VIEWS IN THE

SOCIAL/ECONOMIC REALM AND WILL UNDOUBTEDLY PUSH FOR THOSE

ISSUES TO BE ADDRESSED AND \'CONSTRUCTIVELY\' RESOLVED AT THIS

YEAR\'S COMMISSION. (THIS CABLE WAS DRAFTED BY POLITICAL

SECTION INTERN MATT TAYLOR).
