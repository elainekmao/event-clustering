SUBJECT: UNGASS - FOLLOWUP TO DEMARCHE

REF: A) STATE 71201 B) VATICAN 1920

CLASSIFIED BY: AMBASSADOR JIM NICHOLSON, REASON E.O. 12958 1.5 (B,D).

1. (C) AS PROMISED REF B, POLOFF FOLLOWED UP WITH VATICAN

MFA UN DESK OFFICER MONS. DOMINIQUE MAMBERTI MAY 2, TO FIND

OUT WHETHER THE VATICAN HAD CONTACTED LATIN AMERICAN

COUNTRIES MENTIONED IN REF A INSTRUCTION. MAMBERTI HAD MET

WITH AND PASSED A PRO MEMORIA WITH THE VATICAN\'S POSITION ON

UNGASS LANGUAGE, WHICH HE SAID IS LARGELY PARALLEL TO USG

POSITION, TO CERTAIN LATIN AMERICAN EMBASSIES AT THE HOLY

SEE. IN ADDITION, PAPAL NUNCIOS HAD TRACKED THE DEMARCHE

WITH CERTAIN OTHERS.

2. (C) MAMBERTI SAID THAT THE VATICAN HAD NOT APPROACHED

ALL OF THE COUNTRIES ON THE LIST, BUT RATHER HAD CONCENTRATED

ON THOSE THAT ARCHBISHOP MARTINO, VATICAN PERMREP AT THE UN,

BELIEVED WOULD BE MOST AMENABLE TO PRESSURE. THOSE COUNTRIES

INCLUDED NICARAGUA, HONDURAS, GUATEMALA, COSTA RICA AND

URUGUAY. HE WAS NOT ABLE TO SAY IN WHICH CAPITALS NUNCIOS

HAD TRACKED THE MESSAGE. MAMBERTI SAID THE VATICAN

UNDERSTOOD THAT ALMOST ALL LATIN AMERICAN COUNTRIES HAD VERY

STRONG ANTI-ABORTION LEGISLATION ON THE BOOKS, BUT THAT

PUTTING IT INTO PRACTICE WAS ANOTHER MATTER. HE HINTED THAT

THE VATICAN\'S DEMARCHES FOCUSED ON THIS STRONG LEGISLATION

AND THE NEED TO AVOID COMMITTING STATES TO UN LANGUAGE THAT

HAD THE FUTURE POTENTIAL TO UNDERMINE NATIONAL LEGISLATION.

POST WILL CONTINUE TO FOLLOW THIS ISSUE WITH OUR

INTERLOCUTORS AS WE GET CLOSER TO THE UNGASS.

NICHOLSON
