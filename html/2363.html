SUBJECT: RUGGIERO\'S DEPARTURE DOES NOT EQUATE TO EUROPE

WITHOUT ITALY

1. (C) SUMMARY AND COMMENT: MEDIA ANALYSIS TO THE

CONTRARY, THE WEEKEND "RESIGNATION" (UNDER DURESS) OF FM

RENATO RUGGIERO DOES NOT EQUATE TO AN ABOUT-FACE IN ITALY\'S

PRO-EUROPEAN STANCE. ITALY WILL REMAIN SOLIDLY IN EUROPE --

AND IN NATO, AND A SOLID PARTNER OF THE U.S. RUGGIERO\'S

DEPARTURE IS THE END OF A FORCED MARRIAGE BETWEEN TWO

STRONG-WILLED INDIVIDUALS, ONE OF WHOM (RUGGIERO) HAD NO

ORGANIZED POLITICAL SUPPORT. BERLUSCONI IS LIKELY TO SELECT

(BUT NOT NECESSARILY SOON) A POLITICIAN TO REPLACE RUGGIERO,

SOMEONE WHO WILL ENSURE THAT IN FOREIGN POLICY, AS IN

DOMESTIC, THE GOI SPEAKS WITH ONE VOICE. AT THE SAME TIME,

THE BERLUSCONI GOVERNMENT WILL CONTINUE TO ASSERT ITALY\'S

RIGHT TO PROTECT ITS INTERESTS WITHIN THE INSTITUTIONS TO

WHICH IT BELONGS, SOMETHING THAT IS ANATHEMA TO ITALY\'S LEFT.

END SUMMARY AND COMMENT.

2. (C) DESPITE SOME MEDIA HEADLINES TRUMPETING THE TRIUMPH

OF THOSE IN THE BERLUSCONI GOVERNMENT WHO ARE "AGAINST

EUROPE," THE WEEKEND RESIGNATION OF ITALY\'S FOREIGN MINISTER

RENATO RUGGIERO DOES NOT SIGNIFY THAT THE GOI IS TURNING ITS

BACK ON EUROPE. THE PRIME MINISTER HIMSELF SAID ITALY\'S

FUTURE LIES "IN A STRONGER EUROPE" AND STRESSED "ITALY\'S

ABSOLUTE COMMITMENT TO THE EURO, TO EUROPE AND TO NATO." ALL

GOI CABINET-LEVEL OFFICIALS HAVE SAID MUCH THE SAME THING --

PERCEIVED CRITICISM OR LACK OF ENTHUSIASM FOR THE EURO

NOTWITHSTANDING. FINANCE MINISTER TREMONTI, A BLUNT,

LONG-TIME EURO-SKEPTIC, SAID THE GOI\'S "PRO-EUROPEAN ECONOMIC

POLICY...HAS BEEN PROVEN... WE BELIEVE IN THE EURO." AND

DEPUTY PRIME MINISTER FINI ADDED "WE REMAIN TOTALLY COMMITTED

TO EUROPE." WHILE BERLUSCONI AND HIS MINISTERS HAVE

OCCASIONALLY MADE CLEAR THEIR DESIRE TO ENSURE THAT ITALY\'S

INTERESTS ARE NOTED AND RESPECTED BY ITS EUROPEAN COLLEAGUES,

THEY HAVE NEVER THREATENED TO TAKE ITALY OUT OF EUROPE. ANY

MENTION OF "ITALIAN INTERESTS" AS SOMETHING DISTINCT FROM

"EUROPEAN INTERESTS," HOWEVER, IS ANATHEMA TO THE ITALIAN

LEFT. WHILE NOT WANTING TO GET ON THE BANDWAGON OF SEEING

PLOTS AND CONSPIRACIES, IT IS A FACT THAT MOST EUROPEAN

NATIONS ARE CURRENTLY HEADED BY CENTER-LEFT GOVERNMENTS, MANY

OF WHOM HAD, AND PROBABLY RETAIN, DEEP SUSPICIONS OF

BERLUSCONI HIMSELF AND HIS NORTHERN LEAGUE AND NATIONAL

ALLIANCE PARTNERS. THE EUROPEAN MEDIA ALSO TEND TO REFLECT

SUCH VIEWPOINTS.

3. (C) THE BERLUSCONI GOVERNMENT IS WARY THAT THE UNION IS

MOVING TOO FAR, TOO FAST ON MANY ISSUES, THAT ITALY\'S

INTERESTS ARE NOT BEING CONSIDERED (A REFLECTION PERHAPS OF

BEING SEEN AS A LESSER PLAYER IN THE EU), AND THAT THE

COMMISSION IS HAM-STRINGING ITALY IN DEMANDING THAT STABILITY

PACT TARGETS BE FULFILLED. FINI CONTINUED HIS QUOTE ABOVE

SAYING "WE ALSO RESERVE OUR RIGHT TO DEFEND OUR INTERESTS,

JUST AS THE FRENCH AND THE GERMANS AND THE BRITISH DO."

TREMONTI STRONGLY BELIEVES THAT THE SINGLE CURRENCY IS NOT

THE PANACEA TO EUROPE\'S ECONOMIC WOES, THOUGH IT WILL HELP TO

FACILITATE BUSINESS AND TRADE. HE WOULD PREFER THAT THE

COMMISSION STICK TO FUNCTIONAL AND ADMINISTRATIVE MATTERS.

RETICENCE TOWARD THE EURO AND THE COMMISSION ARE NOT,

HOWEVER, TANTAMOUNT TO OPPOSING EITHER THE UNION OR THE EURO.

AND THE EURO-SKEPTICS IN THE GOVERNMENT HAVE THEIR

COUNTERBALANCES. PRESIDENT OF THE REPUBLIC CIAMPI REMAINS ON

BOARD AND COULD BE EXPECTED TO PUT UP A STRONG FIGHT, AS

WOULD THE ITALIAN PUBLIC AND PARLIAMENT, IF SKEPTICISM LED TO

AN IDEA OF FORSAKING EUROPE -- WHICH THIS GOVERNMENT DOES NOT

INTEND TO DO.

4. (C) RUGGIERO\'S DEPARTURE IS THE RESULT OF A CLASH OF

PERSONALITIES AND STYLES, AS MUCH AS ANYTHING ELSE.

RUGGIERO, AS NOTED REF A AND C, WAS TO SOME DEGREE FOISTED ON

BERLUSCONI. HIS "MEDDLING" IN DEFENSE MATTERS (REF C), AND

MORE RECENTLY IN THE EURO (REF A) WAS GUARANTEED NOT TO

ENDEAR HIM TO HIS FELLOW MINISTERS. IN THE MORE RECENT

EXCHANGES, HIS TRUMPETING OF HIS OWN POPULARITY (WHICH

ACCORDING TO RECENT POLLS REMAINS HIGH -- HIGHER THAN

BERLUSCONI\'S), ESPECIALLY AMONG OPPOSITION POLITICIANS, WAS

EQUALLY CERTAIN TO RAISE THE HACKLES OF HIS BOSS, THE PM. AS

A "TECHNOCRATIC MINISTER" (WHETHER OR NOT HE LIKES THE TERM),

RUGGIERO LACKED A POLITICAL PARTY TO SUPPORT HIM, TO TELL THE

PM THAT "THIS IS OUR PERSON ON YOUR TEAM AND HE MUST STAY."

AS THE PROFESSIONAL DIPLOMAT, RUGGIERO MIGHT HAVE BEEN

EXPECTED TO SHOW LOYALTY, CALM MATTERS DOWN, OPERATE BEHIND

THE SCENES. BUT THIS WAS NOT THE CASE. WHILE BERLUSCONI MAY

HAVE PLAYED THE SAME GAME, RUGGIERO HAS AT TIMES ACTED IN A

PETULANT MANNER, COMING CLOSE TO CHORTLING OVER HIS VICTORIES

(AS IN THE ARREST WARRANT CASE) IN PUBLIC.

5. (C) RUGGIERO\'S DEPARTURE, AND MORE IMPORTANTLY,

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 ROME 00073 02 OF 02 071557Z

BERLUSCONI\'S PROMISE TO HOLD THE FOREIGN MINISTER\'S PORTFOLIO

FOR "AT LEAST SIX MONTHS," MAY SIGNAL CHANGES IN THE MFA, BUT

THESE ARE LIKELY TO BE MORE IN TERMS OF SOME PERSONNEL. AS

NOTED REF C, WE WONDER IF INDIVIDUALS WHO ARE PERCEIVED AS

BEING EITHER "RUGGIERO\'S" OR LESS THAN ENTHUSIASTIC IN

PROMOTING GOI POLICY MIGHT BE CHANGED. BERLUSCONI ALSO

INDICATED IN EARLY PRESS INTERVIEWS THAT HE WOULD LIKE TO SEE

INCREASED EMPHASIS BY ITALIAN AMBASSADORS ON SUPPORTING

ITALY\'S SMALL AND MEDIUM-SIZED BUSINESSES AND INCREASING

EXPORTS. HE SUGGESTED HE WOULD LIKE TO EVALUATE AMBASSADORS

ON THESE TERMS AND PERHAPS APPOINT MORE POLITICAL

AMBASSADORS. THESE KINDS OF CHANGES DO NOT PORTEND A DRASTIC

CHANGE IN FOCUS FOR ITALIAN FOREIGN POLICY, BUT RATHER A

CHANGE IN NUANCE REFLECTIVE OF BERLUSCONI\'S STYLE AND

PRIORITIES.

6. (C) SIMILARLY, RUGGIERO\'S DEPARTURE DOES NOT FORETELL A

WEAKENING OF ITALIAN SUPPORT FOR NATO OR A WEAKER PARTNERSHIP

WITH THE U.S. BERLUSCONI BELIEVES HIMSELF TO BE ON CLOSE

PERSONAL TERMS WITH PRESIDENT BUSH AND HAS EVERY INTENTION OF

FURTHER CEMENTING THAT RELATIONSHIP. BERLUSCONI APPEARS TO

HAVE SOLID PUBLIC SUPPORT FOR THIS POSITION, AS OPINION POLLS

CONTINUE TO REFLECT SOLID BACKING FOR STRONG ITALIAN TIES TO

BOTH EUROPE AND THE U.S.

7. (C) WE DO NOT DOUBT THAT THE OPPOSITION WILL TAKE

ADVANTAGE OF THE CHANGE TO WORK AGAINST THE GOVERNMENT. AS

ONE OBSERVER NOTED, "THAT\'S THE WAY OF ITALIAN POLITICS."

BUT THE GOVERNMENT RETAINS ITS STRONG MAJORITY IN PARLIAMENT;

PERHAPS THAT IS WHY BERLUSCONI WAS QUICK TO PRONOUNCE HIMSELF

READY FOR A PARLIAMENTARY DEBATE ON ITALIAN FOREIGN POLICY.

WE WOULD NOT EXPECT SUCH A DEBATE TO SHOW AN ITALY HOSTILE TO

EUROPE, TO NATO, OR TO THE U.S. RATHER, IT IS LIKELY THE

OPPOSITION WOULD LEVEL ANTI-EUROPE CHARGES AT THE GOVERNMENT,

WHICH WOULD RESPOND BY DECLARING ITS LOYALTY TO ALL THE ABOVE

INSTITUTIONS TO THE EXTENT THAT THEY PROTECT ITALY\'S

INTERESTS. WE ARE MORE LIKELY TO SEE AN ESCALATION OF CLAIMS

ABOUT WHO IS MORE LOYAL TO EUROPE THAN WHO IS MORE ANXIOUS TO

LEAVE IT.

8. (C) IN TERMS OF PREDICTING THE FUTURE, IT APPEARS

POSSIBLE THAT BERLUSCONI MAY HOLD THE MFA REINS FOR SEVERAL

MONTHS. FOLLOWING THAT, WE THINK IT MOST LIKELY HE WILL

APPOINT A TRUSTED POLITICAL ALLY TO HEAD THE MFA, SOMEONE HE

CAN COUNT ON TO PREVENT FUTURE PUBLIC EMBARRASSMENTS OF THE

KIND HE SUFFERED REPEATEDLY WITH RUGGIERO. IN THE MEANTIME,

WE DO NOT EXPECT A WHOLESALE WITCH-HUNT FOR THE "LEFTISTS"

PERCEIVED BY SOME ON THE RIGHT TO BE INFILTRATING THE

FARNESINA. BERLUSCONI MAY NEED SOME OF THE TOP LEVEL TO HELP

HIM RUN THE PLACE FOR A TIME. IN THE NEAR TERM, SECRETARY

GENERAL BALDOCCI WILL LIKELY BE A MORE IMPORTANT PLAYER. ON

THE OTHER HAND, POLITICAL DIRECTOR ARAGONA, COORDINATOR FOR

COUNTERTERRORISM ARMELLINI, AND OTHERS PERCEIVED AS DIRECT

"RUGGIERO MEN" MAY BE LOOKING FOR NEW POSITIONS OVER THE

MEDIUM TERM.
