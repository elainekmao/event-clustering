SUBJECT: DEMARCHE ON THE STATUS OF THREE INSTRUMENTS

RELATED TO THE PROTECTION OF CHILDREN

REF: A. REFTEL A: STATE 126276

B. REFTEL B: AMMAN 1357

Classified By: DCM GREGORY L. BERRY FOR REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D)

1. (C) Per reftel a, on April 19 A/PolCouns and PolOff

delivered message to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. MFA

Legal Adviser Samer Naber and Human Rights Officer Bisher

Khasawneh provided the following information:

a. The GOJ is a party to ILO Convention 182 (reftel B).

b. On the Protocol on the Sale of Children, Child

Prostitution and Child Pornography, Naber said that Jordan

has laws banning child slavery dating to 1927, and stiff

penalties for crimes involving child prostitution or

pornography (reftel b). Jordan is a signatory to the sexual

exploitation protocol, but has yet to join. He expressed

confidence that the GOJ is &moving toward8 joining the

protocol.

c. On the Protocol on Children in Armed Conflict, Naber said

the GOJ &in practice8 does not violate the terms of the

protocol, i.e. it is not currently engaging in forced

conscription of minors. However, the current law does allow

for the GOJ to draft males 16 and above, in extreme

circumstances. Jordan is not a party to the Protocol.

2. (SBU) We urged the GOJ to join the two Protocols. Naber

and Khasawneh took the point, and said they would pass the

message forward within the GOJ.
