SUBJECT: AMBASSADOR, ZANU-PF SECRETARY OF INFORMATION NATHAN

SHAMUYARIRA DISCUSS ZDERA, POLITICAL DEVELOPMENTS

REF: A) HARARE 3463 B) HARARE 3400

CLASSIFIED BY POLITICAL SECTION CHIEF MATT HARRINGTON. REASONS:

1.5 (B) AND (D).

SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) IN A NOVEMBER 20 MEETING WITH THE AMBASSADOR, SENIOR ZANU-

PF OFFICIAL NATHAN SHAMUYARIRA SAID THE GOZ WANTS TO MAINTAIN GOOD

RELATIONS WITH THE USG, AND HE URGED THAT THE ZIMBABWE DEMOCRACY

AND RECOVERY ACT (ZDERA), CURRENTLY BEFORE THE HOUSE OF

REPRESENTATIVES, BE TAKEN "OFF THE AGENDA." THE AMBASSADOR SAID

CONCRETE IMPROVEMENTS ON OUR ISSUES OF CONCERN -- RESTORATION OF

THE RULE OF LAW, A REDUCTION IN POLITICAL VIOLENCE, FREE AND FAIR

ELECTIONS -- ARE ESSENTIAL PRECURSORS TO AN IMPROVED RELATIONSHIP

WITH US. SHAMUYARIRA INSISTED THAT INTERNATIONAL ELECTION

OBSERVERS WILL BE INVITED AFTER THE ELECTION DATES ARE ANNOUNCED,

BUT HE STRESSED THAT "LONG-HAIRED SCANDINAVIANS" TRYING TO TEACH

US ABOUT DEMOCRACY WOULD NOT BE PERMITTED TO PARTICIPATE. HE

CLAIMED THE RULING PARTY IS URGING ITS SUPPORTERS TO CONDUCT A

PEACEFUL CAMPAIGN, BUT LAMENTED THAT THEY MIGHT BE DIFFICULT TO

CONTROL IN THE FACE OF PROVOCATIONS FROM THE OPPOSITION MDC. END

SUMMARY.

ZDERA

2. (C) IN A NOVEMBER 20 CONVERSATION WITH THE AMBASSADOR, ZANU-PF

ELDER STATESMAN -- AND THE RULING PARTY\'S SECRETARY FOR

INFORMATION -- NATHAN SHAMUYARIRA FIRST RAISED THE ZIMBABWE

DEMOCRACY AND ECONOMIC RECOVERY ACT (ZDERA), CURRENTLY BEFORE THE

U.S. HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES. SHAMUYARIRA SAID THE GOVERNMENT

WANTED TO "GET ZDERA OFF THE AGENDA" AND HE THOUGHT PRESIDENT

MUGABE MIGHT SOON WRITE TO PRESIDENT BUSH EXPRESSING HIS CONCERNS

ABOUT THIS PENDING LEGISLATION. THE AMBASSADOR NOTED THAT ZDERA

WAS AS MUCH AN INCENTIVES BILL AS A SANCTIONS BILL, AND THAT IT

RECOMMENDS THE IMPOSITION OF TARGETED PERSONAL SANCTIONS IN THE

ABSENCE OF PROGRESS ON RULE OF LAW AND HUMAN RIGHTS CONCERNS, AND

FREE AND FAIR ELECTIONS. THE AMBASSADOR SAID PRESIDENT MUGABE WAS

WELCOME TO SEND A LETTER BUT HE EMPHASIZED THAT CONCRETE ACTIONS

ADDRESSING THE AREAS OF CONCERN SPECIFIED IN THE BILL WOULD CARRY

MUCH GREATER WEIGHT WITH THE U.S. CONGRESS.

U.S.-ZIMBABWE RELATIONSHIP

--------------------------

3. (C) SHAMUYARIRA SAID THE GOZ WANTS TO KEEP RELATIONS WITH THE

USG "ON AN EVEN KEEL," AS THE UNITED STATES HAS ALWAYS USED ITS

INFLUENCE IN ZIMBABWE CONSTRUCTIVELY. HE SAID THE BRITISH ARE

"AGAINST US" AND HAVE CONVINCED THE EUROPEAN UNION TO TAKE THE

SAME TACK. THE GOZ HOPED THE USG WOULD DISTANCE ITSELF FROM THAT

APPROACH BY DROPPING ZDERA AND BY MAKING A GESTURE TOWARD MUGABE.

(NOTE: HE DID NOT ELABORATE ON WHAT KIND OF GESTURE MIGHT BE

HELPFUL. END NOTE.) HE URGED THAT PRESIDENT BUSH "PLEASE,

PLEASE" NOT PURSUE A CONFRONTATIONAL APPROACH WITH PRESIDENT

MUGABE, AS IT WOULD NOT BE PRODUCTIVE.

4. (C) THE AMBASSADOR SAID POLITICAL VIOLENCE AND RULE OF LAW ARE

MAJOR ISSUES FOR THE UNITED STATES, AND PROGRESS OR LACK OF

PROGRESS ON THOSE FRONTS WOULD BE KEY DETERMINANTS OF OUR

BILATERAL RELATIONSHIP. IT IS IMPORTANT THAT OUR TWO GOVERNMENTS

MAINTAIN OPEN CHANNELS OF COMMUNICATION, BUT HE SAID HE WANTED TO

MAKE IT VERY CLEAR THAT THE MOST IMPORTANT THING FOR THE UNITED

STATES IS TO SEE CONCRETE IMPROVEMENTS ON THE GROUND.

UNFORTUNATELY, THE POLITICAL ENVIRONMENT HAS DETERIORATED SINCE

THE MID-OCTOBER VISIT OF AF PDAS MARK BELLAMY. THE RECENT

POLITICAL VIOLENCE IN BULAWAYO (REFTELS), IN PARTICULAR, HAD

CAUSED US SERIOUS CONCERN. THE AMBASSADOR NOTED THAT HE HAD SEEN

MULTIPLE ACCOUNTS THAT WAR VETERANS BURNED DOWN THE MDC OFFICE

WHILE POLICE STOOD BY AND DID NOTHING. THAT IS THE SORT OF

ACTION, HE SAID, WHICH GARNERS INTERNATIONAL HEADLINES AND MAKES

IT MORE LIKELY THAT ZDERA WILL PASS. IF THAT REPORT IS TRUE AND

POLICE DID NOT TAKE ANY ACTION, SHAMUYARIRA REPLIED, THEY SHOULD

BE REPRIMANDED. POLICE, HE ASSERTED, SHOULD BE NEUTRAL AND DO

EVERYTHING IN THEIR POWER TO MAINTAIN LAW AND ORDER, REGARDLESS OF

WHO VIOLATES IT.

5. (C) ON LAND, THE AMBASSADOR CONTINUED, THE ABUJA AGREEMENT

APPEARS TO HAVE BEEN PREEMPTED BY EXECUTIVE ACTIONS INTENDED TO

PROHIBIT THE ABILITY OF COMMERCIAL FARMERS AFFECTED BY FAST TRACK

TO PURSUE LEGAL REMEDIES. REGARDING PREPARATIONS FOR THE

ELECTION, WE CONTINUE TO RECEIVE REPORTS OF POLITICAL VIOLENCE,

PARTICULARLY IN RURAL AREAS. IN ADDITION, THE PROPOSED AMENDMENTS

TO THE ELECTORAL ACT CAUSE US SERIOUS CONCERN, INCLUDING THE

APPARENT INTENTION TO PREVENT CIVIL SOCIETY FROM SERVING AS

DOMESTIC ELECTION MONITORS, A ROLE THEY HAVE TRADITIONALLY PLAYED

IN ZIMBABWE. THE AMBASSADOR REMINDED SHAMUYARIRA THAT WE ARE

STILL AWAITING WORD FROM THE GOZ ON WHETHER INTERNATIONAL

OBSERVERS WILL BE PERMITTED.

6. (C) SHAMUYARIRA REPLIED THAT HE WAS NOT FULLY BRIEFED ON THE

GOVERNMENT\'S RECENT MOVES ON LAND. HE WAS CONCERNED BY THE

ANNOUNCED INTENTION TO LIMIT THE SIZES OF FARMS TO AS LITTLE AS

250 HECTARES, WHICH WAS "TOO SMALL FOR VIABLE FARMING." REGARDING

THE ELECTION, THE ZANU-PF OFFICIAL SAID INTERNATIONAL OBSERVERS

WOULD BE PERMITTED AFTER THE ELECTION DATES ARE ANNOUNCED. HE

MADE IT CLEAR THAT INVITATIONS WOULD NOT BE ISSUED TO SCANDINAVIAN

COUNTRIES, WHICH HE CLAIMED STRONGLY SUPPORT THE OPPOSITION MDC

AND WOULD CAMPAIGN HEAVILY FOR THE OPPOSITION PARTY UNDER THE

GUISE OF ELECTION OBSERVATION. "WE DO NOT WANT LONG-HAIRED

SCANDINAVIANS TEACHING US ABOUT DEMOCRACY," HE SAID. HE ASSERTED

THAT THE RULING PARTY IS URGING ITS SUPPORTERS TO CONDUCT A

PEACEFUL CAMPAIGN, BUT HE WARNED THAT IT WOULD BE DIFFICULT TO

CONTROL THEM IN THE FACE OF CONTINUED PROVOCATIONS FROM THE MDC.

COULD "LIKELY KEEP OURS QUIET AS WELL."

OF COURSE OUR ELECTION WILL BE FREE AND FAIR

--------------------------------------------

7. (C) SHAMUYARIRA DECLARED THAT ZIMBABWE HAD HELD FREE AND FAIR

ELECTIONS SINCE INDEPENDENCE, AND HE INSISTED THAT THERE WOULD BE

NO RIGGING OR TAMPERING IN THE UPCOMING PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION. HE

DISMISSED CONCERNS THAT REGISTRAR-GENERAL TOBAIWA MUDEDE IS TOO

CLOSELY TIED TO THE RULING PARTY. MUDEDE, HE POINTED OUT, HAD

ORGANIZED THE FEBRUARY 2000 CONSTITUTIONAL REFERENDUM IN WHICH THE

GOVERNMENT HAD BEEN DEFEATED, SUPERVISED THE PARLIAMENTARY

ELECTIONS IN JUNE 2000 IN WHICH THE MDC WON A SIGNIFICANT NUMBER

OF SEATS, AND OVERSAW THE 2001 MAYORAL ELECTIONS IN MASVINGO AND

BULAWAYO, BOTH OF WHICH THE MDC WON. SINCE ANY SYSTEM CAN BE

MANIPULATED, SHAMUYARIRA SAID, THE STRENGTH OF ANY ELECTORAL

PROCESS DEPENDS ON THE INTEGRITY OF THE INDIVIDUALS WHO OVERSEE

IT, AND ZIMBABWE\'S ELECTORAL AUTHORITIES HAVE ALWAYS BEEN NON-

PARTISAN AND OF THE HIGHEST INTEGRITY. SHAMUYARIRA SAID HE DID

NOT THINK ANY ZIMBABWEAN BELIEVES PRESIDENT MUGABE OR ZANU-PF RIGS

ELECTIONS, ALTHOUGH THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY HAS HAMMERED THEM

FOR DOING SO. WHEN THE OPPOSITION PARTY CALLS FOR MUDEDE TO GO,

THEY ARE REALLY PUSHING FOR AN MDC SUPPORTER TO REPLACE HIM.

8. (C) THE AMBASSADOR ENCOURAGED THE GOZ TO ESTABLISH MECHANISMS

TO REASSURE POLITICAL PARTIES AND ZIMBABWEANS IN GENERAL OF THE

INTEGRITY AND FAIRNESS OF THE ELECTORAL PROCESS. ESTABLISHMENT OF

AN INDEPENDENT ELECTORAL COMMISSION, AS RECOMMENDED BY THE SADC

PARLIAMENTARY FORUM\'S ELECTORAL NORMS AND STANDARDS, IS ONE

IMPORTANT STEP. ANOTHER IS TO PERMIT INTERNATIONAL OBSERVATION OF

THE ELECTORAL PROCESS BY CREDIBLE, NON-PARTISAN ORGANIZATIONS. IN

ADDITION, VOTER EDUCATION CAN BE AN AREA FOR COOPERATION RATHER

THAN CONFRONTATION. TO THE EXTENT THAT ELECTORAL AUTHORITIES CAN

FIND WAYS TO MAXIMIZE THE INFORMATION GETTING TO ELIGIBLE VOTERS,

THAT HELPS BUTTRESS CONFIDENCE IN THE PROCESS. SHAMUYARIRA

RESPONDED THAT THEY WOULD ALLOW POLITICAL PARTIES TO CONDUCT VOTER

EDUCATION BUT WOULD HENCEFORTH PROHIBIT NGO\'S FROM DOING SO.

CIVIL SOCIETY IN ZIMBABWE IS NOT NEUTRAL, HE INSISTED BUT, RATHER,

IS CLOSELY ALIGNED WITH THE MDC. IF THE PLEA THE AMBASSADOR WAS

MAKING WAS TO ALLOW CIVIC BODIES TO CARRY OUT VOTER EDUCATION, HE

WOULD MAKE A COUNTER-DEMAND THAT THEY BE GENUINELY NEUTRAL. THAT

IS A FAIR POINT THAT SHOULD BE EXAMINED BY THE ELECTORAL

AUTHORITIES, THE AMBASSADOR REPLIED, BUT THE BEST PRINCIPLE, IN

GENERAL, IS THE MORE EDUCATION OF VOTERS, THE BETTER.

TWENTY YEARS, THEY KEPT TO THEMSELVES, "GOING FISHING ON LAKE

COMMENT

-------

10. (C) WE HAD BARELY SAT DOWN WHEN SHAMUYARIRA LAUNCHED INTO

ZDERA, AND THE IMPORTANCE TO THE GOZ OF BLOCKING CONGRESSIONAL

PASSAGE. ALTHOUGH ZDERA WOULD HAVE LITTLE EFFECT ON THE GROUND

HERE IN TERMS OF DECREASED ECONOMIC ASSISTANCE OR IMF/IBRD RE-

ENGAGEMENT, THE GOZ AND ZANU-PF REALIZE ITS PASSAGE WOULD BE A

SIGNIFICANT GESTURE OF U.S. DISAPPROVAL OF GOZ ACTIONS, AND

THEREFORE HOPES VERY MUCH THAT IT CAN BE TURNED OFF. THAT SAID,

ZDERA\'S APPROVAL WOULD CERTAINLY PROMPT THE GOZ TO ADD US TO THE

BURGEONING LIST OF FOREIGN ENEMIES WHICH ALREADY INCLUDES THE

UNITED KINGDOM, THE SCANDINAVIANS, AND THE EU (AT LEAST WE WOULD

BE IN GOOD COMPANY). WE WOULD EXPECT TO SEE AN INCREASE IN

OFFICIAL ANTI-USG RHETORIC AND REDUCED ACCESS TO GOZ OFFICIALS.

IN A WORST-CASE SCENARIO, THE GOZ WOULD USE ZDERA AS A PRETEXT TO

INVOKE SOVEREIGNTY CONCERNS AND LAUNCH A NO-HOLDS BARRED CRACKDOWN

ON THE MDC, THE INDEPENDENT PRESS, AND OTHER ELEMENTS OF CIVIL

SOCIETY SUPPORTED BY ITS NEW ENEMY. WE RECALL THAT FM MUDENGE

SAID IN PARLIAMENT SEVERAL MONTHS AGO THAT PASSAGE OF ZDERA COULD

PROMPT THE GOZ TO DECLARE A STATE OF EMERGENCY, A BLUFF THAT WE

ARE WILLING TO CALL.
