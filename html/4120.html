2. (C) THE NOVEMBER 8 HUMAN RIGHTS DIALOGUE WITH

VIETNAM WILL BE ANOTHER IMPORTANT STEP IN OUR EFFORTS

TO DEEPEN OUR BILATERAL RELATIONSHIP AND TO REITERATE

THE GENUINE CONCERN FELT IN THE U.S. -- WITHIN THE

ADMINISTRATION, CONGRESS, AND THE PUBLIC -- ABOUT HUMAN

RIGHTS ABUSES AND SYSTEMIC PROBLEMS IN VIETNAM.

EMBASSY APPRECIATES THE ONGOING PREPARATION BY DRL AND

EAP AND WELCOMES THE OPPORTUNITY TO SEND HUMAN RIGHTS

OFFICER TIM SWANSON TO PARTICIPATE IN THIS SESSION, AS

WELL AS THE SUBSEQUENT POLITICAL DIALOGUE ON NOVEMBER

15. THE US-VIETNAM HUMAN RIGHTS DIALOGUE OPERATES IN

PARALLEL WITH SIMILAR REGULAR OR SEMI-REGULAR EXCHANGES

(USUALLY NOT LABELED AS SPECIFIC "HUMAN RIGHTS"

DIALOGUES) WITH THE EU, AUSTRALIA, CANADA, SWEDEN,

NORWAY, AND SWITZERLAND.

3. (C) THERE SHOULD BE NO ILLUSIONS, HOWEVER, THAT WE

SHOULD EXPECT ANY MEETING OF THE MINDS BETWEEN THE TWO

DELEGATIONS ON THE NATURE OF HUMAN RIGHTS AND THE

EXTENT OF PROBLEMS IN VIETNAM. GVN OFFICIALS REMAIN

OFFICIALLY WEDDED TO THE PROPAGANDA THAT THE CPV HAS

BEEN THE SAVIOR OF THE VIETNAMESE PEOPLE FROM CENTURIES

OF WHAT THEY SEE AS TYRANNY BY OUTSIDE FORCES,

INCLUDING THE U.S. AS SUCH, THEY VIEW THE CPV (AND,

THROUGH IT, THE GVN ITSELF) AS THE CREATOR (NOT EVEN

RESTORER) OF THOSE RIGHTS NOW ENJOYED BY THE PEOPLE OF

VIETNAM, PRIMARILY THAT OF NATIONAL INDEPENDENCE BUT

ALSO OF THE LARGER SOCIETAL "RIGHTS" OF STABILITY,

ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT, AND EQUALITY. TO GVN OFFICIALS,

CONFIDENTIAL

THE BOTTOM LINE QUESTIONS WHEN TALKING ABOUT HUMAN

RIGHTS IN VIETNAM REMAIN "ARE THE LIVES OF THE

VIETNAMESE PEOPLE BETTER NOW THAN BEFORE 1986 (WHEN THE

POLICY OF DOI MOI BEGAN)? THAN BEFORE 1975? THAN

BEFORE 1954?" THE CPV CLAIM THAT THE ANSWER IN ALL

THREE CASES IS "YES" IS PROBABLY MOSTLY CORRECT, EXCEPT

FOR THE GREATER CIVIL LIBERTIES, E.G., PRESS AND

RELIGIOUS FREEDOM, ENJOYED IN THE SOUTH BEFORE 1975.

(REFTEL DESCRIBED SOME OF THE IMPORTANT EXPANSIONS IN

PERSONAL FREEDOMS AT LEAST SINCE 1986.)

4. (C) SUSPICIONS REMAIN DEEP AND REAL THAT THE U.S.

HAS SOME SINISTER AGENDA -- CALL IT PEACEFUL EVOLUTION,

OR GLOBALIZATION, OR HEGEMONY, OR WHATEVER -- IN

RAISING HUMAN RIGHTS WITH VIETNAM AND OTHER LIKE

COUNTRIES. GVN AND CPV OFFICIALS DO NOT BELIEVE THAT

OUR INTEREST IS BENIGN AND THAT WE GENUINELY CARE ABOUT

THE PEOPLE OF VIETNAM. MANY OF THEM STILL BELIEVE THAT

OUR REAL GOAL REMAINS TO DEFEAT GLOBAL COMMUNISM AND

ENSURE THAT THESE REGIMES TURN INTO AMERICAN-STYLE

DEMOCRACIES. THEY SEE THE U.S. AS A WORLD-CLASS BULLY

INTENT ON IMPOSING ITS OWN VALUES ON COUNTRIES --

INCLUDING VIETNAM -- THAT, BECAUSE THEY NEED ACCESS TO

OUR MARKETS, ARE OBLIGED TO LISTEN TO US COMPLAINTS AND

CRITICISMS, WHILE PUBLICLY REJECTING THE US\' RIGHT TO

"INTERFERE IN INTERNAL AFFAIRS." EVEN THE MEMBERS OF

THE DELEGATION WHO ARE MOST SAVVY ABOUT THE US SYSTEM

AND IDEALS LIKELY BELIEVE THAT AT THE VERY LEAST THESE

US CONCERNS ABOUT HUMAN RIGHTS IN VIETNAM ARE NO MORE

THAN NITPICKING ABOUT ISSUES AND SITUATIONS THAT WE DO

NOT TRULY UNDERSTAND.
