SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE FOREIGN MINISTER CONVENES DIPLOMATIC

CORPS TO URGE INTERNATIONAL COMMITMENT TO DRC PEACE

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 01019 141613Z

REASONS: 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) ON MARCH 14, ZIMBABWE FOREIGN MINISTER

STANISLAUS MUDENGE CONVENED ZIMBABWE\'S DIPLOMATIC CORPS

AND THE PRESS TO MAKE A STATEMENT TO URGE GREATER

INTERNATIONAL COMMITMENT TO THE DRC DISENGAGEMENT PLANS.

REPORTING ON THE ACCOMPLISHMENTS OF THE FEBRUARY 15

LUSAKA SUMMIT AND THE FEBRUARY 21 MINISTERIAL OF THE

BELLIGERENTS AT UN HEADQUARTERS IN NEW YORK, MUDENGE

URGED ALL GOVERNMENTS PRESENT TO USE THEIR INFLUENCE TO

ENSURE THAT THE UN SECURITY COUNCIL REMAINS COMMITTED TO

FULLY DEPLOYING MONUC. MUDENGE CALLED MARCH 15 "D-DAY"

FOR THE BEGINNING OF THE DISENGAGEMENT AND REDEPLOYMENT

OF BELLIGERENT FORCES IN THE DRC. THE FOREIGN MINISTER

OUTLINED THREE AREAS OF CONCERN THAT ZIMBABWE HAS WITH

MONUC DEPLOYMENT:

A) THE SIZE OF THE OBSERVER TEAM IS TOO SMALL. THE

UNSC ORGINALLY AGREED TO DEPLOY A FORCE OF 5,037,

THEN CUT IT BACK TO 3,000, THEN REAFFIRMED A

COMMITMENT TO 5,037. THIS SENDS AN "UNFORTUNATE"

MESSAGE OF A LACK OF COMMITMENT BY THE

INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY. THE DRC SHOULD HAVE A

FORCE OF 40,000 TO DO THE JOB PROPERLY, BUT CUTTING

BACK THE PALTRY 5,000 FIGURE WOULD JEOPARDIZE THE

ENTIRE MISSION, THE MINISTER CONTENDED.

B) THE PERIOD ALLOWED FOR DEPLOYMENT IS TOO SHORT.

BEGINNING MARCH 15, THE DRC WILL BE DIVIDED INTO

FOUR SECTORS, AND MONUC WILL HAVE ONLY 14 DAYS TO

DEPLOY TO EACH ONE, A TOTAL OF 56 DAYS IN ALL.

DEPLOYMENT TO KATANGA (SECTOR 4) WILL BEGIN MARCH

15, MUDENGE EXPLAINED. THE FOREIGN MINISTER URGED

THE UN TO ALLOW ENOUGH TIME TO COMPLETE THE

DEPLOYMENT OF ALL THE OBSERVERS.

C) RWANDA AND UGANDA ARE TRYING TO RENEGE ON

COMMITMENTS MADE UNDER THE KAMPALA AGREEMENT OF

APRIL 2000. SINCE THAT CONFERENCE, RWANDA AND

UGANDA HAVE ADVANCED THEIR FORCES UP TO 500 KM. IN

SOME AREAS. THEY WANT TO USE THEIR POSITIONS ON

THE GROUND NOW AS THE POINT OF WITHDRAWAL, WHEN

THEY SHOULD BE USING THE POINTS WHERE THEY WERE IN

APRIL 2000. KIGALI AND KAMPALA ARE TRYING TO

REINTERPRET THE LUSAKA AGREEMENT, AND THE

INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY SHOULD NOT ALLOW THIS,

MUDENGE REMARKED.

2. (C) THE FOREIGN MINISTER DESCRIBED THE MONUC

DEPLOYMENT AS "PHASE TWO" OF ENDING THE DRC WAR. "PHASE

THREE" WILL BE MUCH MORE DIFFICULT, HE OPINED, AND WILL

COMPRISE DISARMING NON-STATE BELLIGERENTS AND

WITHDRAWING ALL OF THE FOREIGN TROOPS IN THE DRC.

MUDENGE CONCLUDED BY SAYING THAT DRC BELLIGERENTS HAVE

AGREED TO GUIDING PRINCIPLES ON DISENGAGEMENT, BUT THE

INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY MUST DO THE SAME IF THE PEACE

PROCESS IS TO SUCCEED.

3. (C) MUDENGE\'S SUBSEQUENT STATEMENTS ON PLANS FOR AN

AFRICAN UNION, THE SADC SUMMIT ON MARCH 9, AND THE GOZ\'S

RESPONSE TO THE LETTER WRITTEN TO PRESIDENT MUGABE BY

UNDP ADMINISTRATOR MALLOCH-BROWN, WILL FOLLOW SEPTEL.

4. (C) COMMENT: MUDENGE DOES NOT OFTEN CONVENE THE

ENTIRE DIPLOMATIC CORPS TO MAKE STATEMENTS. THAT HE

CONVENED THE HEADS OF MISSION TO EXPRESS CONCERN OVER

MONUC DEPLOYMENT AND TO PLEA FOR INTERNATIONAL HELP

DEMONSTRATES THE GOZ\'S GROWING SENSE OF URGENCY TO BRING

PEACE TO THE DRC. IT IS EVER MORE EAGER TO BRING HOME

ITS TROOPS, WHICH COMPRISE HALF ITS MILITARY, TO STOP

THE RAPID DEPLETION OF ZIMBABWE\'S SCARCE FOREIGN

CURRENCY RESERVES, TO CONTEND WITH POSSIBLE UNREST

LEADING TO THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION SCHEDULED FOR EARLY

2002, AND TO REMOVE DRC INVOLVEMENT AS AN ISSUE IN THE

PRESIDENTIAL CAMPAIGN. END COMMENT.
