REASONS 1.5 (B) AND D).

1. SUMMARY: (C) VATICAN FOREIGN MINISTER ARCHBISHOP

JEAN-LOUIS TAURAN TOLD CHARGE JULY 25 THAT THE HOLY SEE

REMAINS SKEPTICAL OF PRIME MINISTER SHARON\'S WILLINGNESS TO

ACCEPT A POLITICAL SOLUTION, AND VIEWS THE JULY 22 ISRAELI

ACTION IN GAZA AS EVIDENCE OF SHARON\'S PREFERENCE FOR A

MILITARY SOLUTION. TAURAN WAS EVEN-HANDED, CONDEMNING

PALESTINIAN ACTIONS, AND EXPRESSING DOUBT THAT EITHER SHARON

OR ARAFAT WOULD BE ABLE TO SURMOUNT THEIR PREDILECTIONS AND

TAKE THE STEPS NEEDED TO MOVE TOWARD A PEACE PROCESS. FOR

THAT REASON, THE VATICAN CONTINUES TO FAVOR THE CONVENING OF

A PEACE CONFERENCE AT THE EARLIEST OPPORTUNITY. THE HOLY SEE

ALSO BELIEVES THAT SOME FORM OF INTERNATIONAL FORCE IS NEEDED

TO SEPARATE THE TWO SIDES AND PROVIDE A BREATHING SPACE TO

DAMPEN CURRENT HIGH EMOTIONS. TAURAN REITERATED THE HOLY

SEE\'S MOUNTING CONCERN FOR THE RUSSIAN GOVERNMENT\'S RECENT

ACTIONS TO DENY VISAS TO ARCHBISHOP MAZUR AND TO RESTRICT

VISAS FOR OTHER CATHOLIC PRIESTS. ARCHBISHOP TRICARICO, WHO

MOST CLOSELY TRACKS RUSSIAN ISSUES IN THE HOLY SEE, INDICATED

THAT NOW IS THE TIME FOR UNEQUIVOCAL INDICATIONS OF U.S.

SUPPORT FOR GREATER RELIGIOUS FREEDOM IN RUSSIA. END SUMMARY.

--------------------------------

SHARON SEEKING MILITARY SOLUTION?

--------------------------------

2. (C) ARCHBISHOP JEAN-LOUIS TAURAN PROVIDED CHARGE WITH THE

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 VATICA 03823 01 OF 02 021542Z

HOLY SEE\'S CURRENT ASSESSMENT OF THE MIDDLE EAST AND RUSSIA

DURING A JULY 25 COURTESY CALL. ON THE MIDDLE EAST, TAURAN

REGARDED ISRAEL\'S JULY 22 RAID ON GAZA AS FURTHER EVIDENCE

FOR HIS CONVICTION THAT SHARON WAS NOT REALLY INTERESTED IN A

POLITICAL SOLUTION. TAURAN, WHO SERVED AS PAPAL NUNCIO IN

LEBANON DURING ISRAEL\'S 1980\'S INCURSIONS, RECOUNTED A

CONVERSATION SHARON HAD WITH LEBANESE CHRISTIANS AT THAT

TIME. SHARON HAD OBSERVED THAT CHRISTIANS AND JEWS LIVING IN

A HOSTILE ARAB SEA COULD ONLY SURVIVE BY BEING IN A

CONTINUOUS STATE OF WAR. TAURAN WRYLY REMARKED THAT THIS HAD

BEEN TRIED CENTURIES BEFORE DURING THE CRUSADES, BUT THE

RESULTS DID NOT LEND MUCH SUPPORT SHARON\'S THESIS. THE HOLY

SEE SEES LITTLE EVIDENCE THAT SHARON HAS CHANGED HIS VIEWS,

AND REMAINS SKEPTICAL THAT SHARON IS COMMITTED TO A POLITICAL

SOLUTION. "SHARON PREFERS A MILITARY SOLUTION," TAURAN

CONCLUDED, "AND HAS YET TO REALIZE THAT THERE CAN BE NO

SATISFACTORY MILITARY SOLUTION."

3. (C) BECAUSE OF THE INTRANSIGENCE OF BOTH SHARON AND

ARAFAT, THE HOLY SEE BELIEVES THE PARTIES NEED THE IMPETUS OF

AN INTERNATIONAL CONFERENCE TO HELP THEM MOVE BEYOND THE

CURRENT STALEMATE. TAURAN ALSO BELIEVES THAT SOME FORM OF AN

INTERNATIONAL FORCE IS NECESSARY TO PREVENT THE TIT-FOR-TAT

CYCLE OF VIOLENCE AND GIVE BOTH SIDES THE BREATHING SPACE

THEY NEED TO DAMPEN EMOTIONS AND THINK SERIOUSLY ABOUT A

LASTING POLITICAL SOLUTION. CHARGE OUTLINED TO TAURAN THE

U.S. APPROACH, STRESSING THE IMPORTANCE OF IMPROVING THE

SECURITY SITUATION AND THEN BEGINNING PARALLEL WORK ON

PALESTINIAN REFORMS AND IMPROVEMENTS IN PALESTINIAN

CONDITIONS WHILE WORKING TOWARD THE PRESIDENT\'S VISION OF TWO

STATES WITHIN THREE YEARS. TAURAN, WHO HAD JUST RETURNED

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 04 VATICA 03823 01 OF 02 021542Z

FROM DISCUSSIONS WITH FOREIGN MINISTER DE VILLEPIN IN FRANCE,

WELCOMED THE CURRENT DIRECTION, BUT ALSO EMPHASIZED THE

IMPORTANCE OF TAKING URGENT STEPS TO IMPROVE CONDITIONS FOR

PALESTINIANS.

CLARIFICATION OF RUSSIA\'S DENIAL OF ENTRY TO POLISH

ARCHBISHOP MAZUR FOR THE IRKUTSK DIOCESE. TAURAN INDICATED

5. (C) ARCHBISHOP TRICARICO, THE VATICAN\'S TOP RUSSIA EXPERT

WAS MORE INSISTENT THAT OUTSIDE PRESSURE WOULD BE NECESSARY

TO FOCUS THE RUSSIAN GOVERNMENT ON THE IMPORTANCE OF

RELIGIOUS FREEDOM. TRICARICO LAMENTED THAT RUSSIA\'S RECENT

PRESSURE AGAINST THE CHURCH AND RELIGIOUS FREEDOM MORE

BROADLY APPEARED TO HAVE BEEN SUBMERGED BY BROADER WESTERN

PRIORITIES IN SEEKING RUSSIAN COOPERATION IN THE WAR ON

TERRORISM. HE BELIEVED, HOWEVER, THAT THE SITUATION WAS AT A

CRITICAL JUNCTURE, AND THAT RUSSIA NEEDED TO HEAR A LOUD AND

CONSISTENT MESSAGE FROM THE U.S. AND EUROPE THAT RUSSIA

NEEDED TO RESPECT RELIGIOUS FREEDOMS IF IT WANTED TO

INTEGRATE MORE CLOSELY WITH THE WEST.

6. (C) TRICARICO EXPRESSED PLEASURE WITH A RECENT PROVINCIAL

COURT DECISION IN THE SIBERIAN CITY OF MAGADAN ALLOWING AN

ALASKAN PRIEST TO REMAIN AS PASTOR OF A CATHOLIC PARISH IN

THAT REMOTE CITY. FATHER MICHAEL SHIELDS, PASTOR NATIVITY OF

JESUS PARISH SINCE 1994, HAD BEEN CHALLENGED BY THE

PROVINCIAL MINISTRY OF JUSTICE ON THE GROUNDS THAT FOREIGNERS

CANNOT BE PASTORS IN RUSSIA. THE COURT TOOK ONLY 10 MINUTES

TO DECIDE IN SHIELD\'S FAVOR. THE CASE COULD HAVE A POSITIVE

IMPACT BEYOND THE PROVINCE, TRICARICO SUGGESTED, BUT THAT IS

FAR FROM CERTAIN.

7. (C) TAURAN REITERATED HIS APPRECIATION FOR THE

OPPORTUNITY TO REVIEW MIDDLE EAST EVENTS WITH THE PRESIDENT

AND SECRETARY POWELL DURING THEIR MAY VISIT TO THE VATICAN,

AND REAFFIRMED HIS DESIRE TO STAY IN CLOSE TOUCH WITH THE

U.S. ON DEVELOPMENTS WITHIN THE QUARTET AIMED AT ADVANCING

THE PRESIDENT\'S VISION FOR PEACE.

8. (C) (COMMENT CONTINUED) ON THE CHURCH IN RUSSIA, THE

VATICAN\'S LEVEL OF CONCERN IS CLEARLY MOUNTING, AS THE ISSUE

HAS COME UP IN A RANGE OF DISCUSSIONS WITH SENIOR CURIAL

OFFICIALS. THE HOLY SEE SEEMS AT A LOSS FOR HOW TO PROCEED

-- GIVEN THAT PRESIDENT PUTIN APPEARS UNWILLING TO RESPOND TO

THE POPE -- AND IS CLEARLY SIGNALING ITS INTEREST IN

ADDITIONAL SIGNS OF U.S. SUPPORT FOR RELIGIOUS FREEDOM IN

RUSSIA AND SPECIFICALLY FOR CLARIFICATION OF THE VISA

SITUATION FOR PRIESTS. WE WOULD WELCOME EMBASSY MOSCOW\'S

ASSESSMENT OF WHAT IS BEHIND THE CURRENT STONEWALLING AND HOW

BEST TO ENGAGE THE RUSSIAN GOVERNMENT ON THIS ISSUE.
