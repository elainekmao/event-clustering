SUBJECT: SUPREME COURT OVERTURNS MUGABE ELECTORAL DECREE

...BUT EXPECTS THE UNEXPECTED FROM ZANU-PF

6. (C) MDC MP BITI TOLD POLOFF THAT HE EXPECTS THE

GOZ/ZANU-PF TO PULL OUT ALL THE STOPS IN DELAYING HIGH

COURT ACTION ON THE LEGAL CHALLENGES. HE WOULD NOT BE

SURPRISED, FOR INSTANCE, IF PRESIDENT MUGABE ENCOURAGED

PARLIAMENT TO PASS A LAW PROHIBITING ANY CHALLENGES TO LAST

JUNE\'S RESULTS. SUCH A LAW WOULD CLEARLY BE

UNCONSTITUTIONAL, BITI SAID, BUT IT WOULD ENSURE THAT THE

CASES IN THE HIGH COURT WOULD AGAIN COME TO A HALT UNTIL

THE SUPREME COURT STRUCK DOWN THE NEW LAW. IN A SEPARATE

CONVERSATION, MDC SENIOR ADVISOR GANDI MUDZINGWA TOLD US

THAT THE GOZ\'S GOAL IS TO FIND A WAY TO KEEP DELAYING HIGH

COURT ACTION UNTIL THE MDC GIVES UP THIS FIGHT IN ORDER TO

CONCENTRATE ITS SEVERELY LIMITED RESOURCES ON THE 2002

PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION.

COMMENT

-------

7. (C) WE WOULD HAVE BEEN SURPRISED HAD THE SUPREME COURT

RULED ANY OTHER WAY, AS MUGABE\'S DECREE WAS A CLEAR

VIOLATION OF THE CONSTITUTION. WE WERE TAKEN ABACK,

HOWEVER, BY THE SUBDUED REACTION FROM THE MDC

REPRESENTATIVES WITH WHOM WE SPOKE. THEIR EXPECTATION,

THOUGH, THAT THE GOVERNMENT WILL DO ALL IT CAN TO DELAY

ACTION IN THE HIGH COURT IS WELL-PLACED. THE MDC WOULD

ONLY HAVE TO WIN 20 BY-ELECTIONS IN ORDER TO ACQUIRE A

MAJORITY IN PARLIAMENT, AND THE GOZ WANTS TO AVOID THIS

POSSIBILITY AT ALL COSTS. END COMMENT.
