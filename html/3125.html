SUBJECT: GOI TO NAME SPECIAL COORDINATOR FOR CYPRUS; WILL

PUSH TIMETABLE FOR TURKEY\'S EU ENTRY

CLASSIFIED BY: POL MC TOM COUNTRYMAN. REASON: 1.5 (B)(D)

1. (C) SUMMARY: ON JUNE 10, THE ITALIAN MFA\'S DIRECTOR

GENERAL FOR EUROPEAN AFFAIRS, MAURIZIO MORENO, AND DIRECTOR

FOR SOUTHERN EUROPE, GIOVANNI RICCIULI, TOLD SPECIAL CYPRUS

COORDINATOR TOM WESTON THAT ITALY WOULD APPOINT ITS OWN

SPECIAL ENVOY FOR CYPRUS, SOMEONE OF APPROPRIATE RANK AND

EXPERIENCE WHO CAN FOCUS ON THE ISSUES, WITHIN THE NEXT FOUR

WEEKS. MORENO ALSO SAID THAT WHILE THE GOI WOULD CONTINUE TO

KEEP PRESSURE ON TURKEY REGARDING CYPRUS, IT WOULD ALSO LOBBY

THE EU TO ESTABLISH A TIMETABLE FOR TURKEY\'S ACCESSION. END

SUMMARY.

2. (C) AMB. WESTON OPENED THE MEETING BY SUMMARIZING FOR

MORENO THE STATUS OF CYPRUS NEGOTIATIONS TO DATE, AND NOTED

THAT THERE WAS NOW A "WINDOW OF OPPORTUNITY" FOR PROGRESS,

GIVEN THE DYNAMIC POLITICAL SITUATION IN ANKARA. WESTON

EXPLAINED THAT HIS VISIT WAS TIMED TO TAKE ADVANTAGE OF THIS

SITUATION, AND THAT HIS UPCOMING TALKS IN ANKARA, ATHENS, AND

NICOSIA WOULD BE DESIGNED TO PUSH HARD FOR PROGRESS ON ALL

SIDES, OR AT A MINIMUM, HALT ANY BACKSLIDING. IF THERE WERE

NO AGREEMENT BY JUNE, CONTINUED WESTON, THE TALKS WOULD

CONTINUE AFTER A SHORT BREAK.

3. (C) MORENO THANKED WESTON FOR THE SUMMARY, AND NOTED

THAT HIS VISIT TO THE REGION CAME AT A CRITICAL TIME. CYPRUS

IS AN ISSUE OF GREAT CONCERN TO THE GOI, AND ITALY WAS READY

TO DO WHAT IT COULD TO "SPEED UP" THE PROCESS IN CYPRUS.

PROGRESS WILL BE ESPECIALLY IMPORTANT CONSIDERING THAT IT IS

VERY LIKELY THAT CYPRUS WILL BE INCLUDED IN THE NEXT ROUND OF

EU ENLARGEMENT, CONTINUED MORENO, AND IT IS NOT AN ISSUE THAT

THE GOI WANTS ON THE AGENDA WHEN THE IT ASSUMES THE EU

PRESIDENCY IN THE LATTER HALF OF 2003. ACCORDINGLY, THE GOI

IS CONSIDERING TAKING SOME CONCRETE STEPS TO MOVE THE

PROCESS, INCLUDING NAMING A HIGH-LEVEL ENVOY TO FOCUS SOLELY

ON CYPRUS WITHIN THE NEXT FOUR WEEKS, A VISIT SOON TO THE

THREE CAPITALS BY UNDER SECRETARY OF STATE ROBERTO ANTONIONE,

AND A VISIT TO ANKARA BY PM BERLUSCONI IN JULY.

4. (C) MORENO SAID THAT IT WAS IMPORTANT FOR THE

INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY TO "KEEP THE PRESSURE ON" TURKEY; THE

GOI WOULD USE THE PROPOSED VISITS AND ENVO TO DO JUST THAT.

AT THE SAME TIME, IT WAS IMPORTANT TO ACKNOWLEDGE PROGRESS

THUS FAR BY TURKEY. INANCIAL AID MAY NOT BE ENOUGH, SAID

MORENO, ANDTHEREFORE THE GOI WOULD LIKE THE EU TO APPROVE

A TIMETABLE FOR

TURKISH ACCESSION. MORENO RECOGNIZE THAT THIS

WOULD NOT BE A POPULAR INITIATIVE AMON MANY EU MEMBERS.

NONETHELESS, HE SAID, ITALY BEIEVES THIS CONCESSION TO

TURKEY MAY BE NECESSARYTO KEEP THE CYPRUS TALKS ON TRACK.

5. (U) AMBSSADOR WESTON HAS CLEARED THIS CABLE.
