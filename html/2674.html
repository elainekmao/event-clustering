1. (C) SUMMARY. THE GOI BELIEVES THAT SOUTHEAST EUROPE

COOPERATIVE PROCESS STATES HAVE VERY RECENTLY BECOME MUCH

MORE REALISTIC IN EVALUATING THE ORGANIZATION\'S POTENTIAL AND

THE SEECP, AS A RESULT, IS POISED TO BECOME A MUCH MORE

EFFECTIVE ADVOCATE FOR REGIONALISM BOTH WITHIN THE STABILITY

PACT AND OTHERWISE. ITALY\'S ENCOURAGEMENT, HOWEVER, IS NOT

LIKELY TO INCLUDE MONEY. THE ITALIAN CHAIR OF WORKING TABLE

II IS SIMILARLY ENTHUSED ABOUT SEECP\'S CAPACITY TO TRANSCEND

THE SPLINTERED ADVOCACY FOR LOCAL PET PROJECTS HE FEELS HAS

HINDERED STABILITY PACT EFFORTS. THE GOI BELIEVES STABILITY

PACT COORDINATOR BUSEK WILL PROVE A GOOD CHOICE IF HE TRIAGES

SEVERAL OF HIS DISTRACTING OUTSIDE JOBS. END SUMMARY.

2. (SBU) THE SOUTHEAST EUROPE COOPERATIVE PROCESS (SEECP) HAS

VERY RECENTLY LOWERED ITS AMBITIONS IN LIGHT OF AVAILABLE

RESOURCES AND, AS A RESULT, COULD BECOME AN EFFECTIVE

FACILITATOR OF REGIONAL PROJECTS, MFA STABILITY PACT (SP)

COORDINATOR ALLESANDRO PIETROMARCHI TOLD POLOFF AND ECONOFF

ON MARCH 20. APPLAUDING WHAT HE SEES AS A NEW SEECP REALISM

AS ALBANIA PREPARES TO HAND THE PRESIDENCY TO YUGOSLAVIA,

PEITROMARCHI SAID, "IF YOU HAD ASKED ME 20 DAYS AGO, I WOULD

HAVE BEEN PESSIMISTIC; NOW I THINK THINGS ARE COMING ALONG

FINE." HE SAID SEECP HAS DROPPED PLANS FOR "GLORIOUS

STRUCTURES" SUCH AS A POLITICAL DIRECTORS COMMISSION IN FAVOR

OF MORE REALISTIC GOALS SUCH AS (FINALLY) NAMING A REGIONAL

REPRESENTATIVE TO SP\'S INFORMAL CONSULTATIVE COMMITTEE.

3. (SBU) PIETROMARCH SAID ITALY HAS NO "VEHICLE" FOR FUNDING

EXPENSES FOR A REGIONAL SECONDEE. REMINDED THAT MFA HAD

CREATIVELY FOUND SOME MONEY A COUPLE OF YEARS AGO TO PAY THE

EXPENSES OF TWO ITALIAN CUSTOMS AGENTS SECONDED TO THE

SOUTHEAST EUROPE COOPERATIVE INITIATIVE (SECI), HE SAID THAT

WAS EASIER BECAUSE THEY WERE ALREADY ON THE GOI PAYROLL AND,

BESIDES, "WE WANTED TO FIND THAT MONEY." IN THIS CASE, HE

SUGGESTED, THE SEECP STATES COULD, AS THEY EVIDENTLY HAVE IN

THE PAST, SECOND TO THE STABILITY PACT OFFICIALS ALREADY

POSTED TO BRUSSELS AND THEREBY AVOID ADDITIONAL EXPENSES.

4. (SBU) PIETROMARCHI SAID HE HAS NO STRONG FEELINGS EITHER

WAY ON THE MECHANICS OF SEECP PARTICIPATION IN SP. HE WOULD

NOT OPPOSE AN SEECP ROLE IN SELECTING A REPRESENTATIVE TO THE

INFRASTRUCTURE STEERING COMMITTEE OR THE INFORMAL

CONSULTATIVE COMMITTEE IF THE STATES OF THE REGION WANT IT.

HE DOES NOT THINK SUCH A ROLE WOULD UNDULY LIMIT THE POWERS

OF SP SPECIAL COORDINATOR ERHARD BUSEK.

5. (C) PIETROMARCHI OFFERED A QUALIFIED ENDORSEMENT OF

BUSEK\'S EARLY PERFORMANCE, SAYING HE TRUSTS BUSEK ("OUR MAN

IN THE REGION") AND BELIEVES HIM TO HAVE AN IDEAL MIX OF

COMPETENCE AND EXPERIENCE BUT JUDGES HIM VERY HARSHLY FOR

WHAT SEEMS TO BE A SCATTERED APPROACH TO SP WORK. ASKED

WHETHER HE THOUGHT BUSEK SHOULD CONTINUE TO SPLIT HIS TIME

BETWEEN SP AND SECI, PIETROMARCHI REPLIED THAT BUSEK ACTUALLY

HOLDS FIVE JOBS SIMULTANEOUSLY AND THAT THE SITUATION IS

COMPLETELY UNTENABLE. PIETROMARCHI CALLED THE BRUSSELS SP

OFFICE "AWFUL" FROM A BASIC BUSINESS PERSPECTIVE WITH THE

STAFF INCOMPETENT AND MANAGERIAL OVERSIGHT NONEXISTENT. HE

BELIEVES SECI, AT A MINIMUM, SHOULD BE FOLDED INTO SP.

6. (SBU) STABILITY PACT WORKING TABLE II CHAIR FABRIZZIO

SACCOMANNI WHOLEHEARTEDLY ENDORSED AN ENHANCED ROLE FOR SEECP

IN SP. IN A MARCH 15 MEETING IN HIS BANK OF ITALY OFFICE

WITH ECOUNS AND ECONOFF, SACCOMANNI ASSURED THAT THE STATES

OF THE REGION ENDORSE SUCH A ROLE FOR SEECP. HE ENVISIONED

IN PARTICULAR SEECP HELPING TO MOVE ITS MEMBER STATES TOWARD

A REGIONAL ORIENTATION THAT COULD MAKE THE WORKING TABLE

MEETINGS HE CHAIRS FAR MORE EFFICIENT BY OBVIATING LENGTHY

INTERVENTIONS BY INDIVIDUAL STATES IN FAVOR OF "COMPETING"

PET PROJECTS.

7. (C) LIKE PIETROMARCHI, SACCOMANNI IS GUARDED IN HIS

EXPECTATIONS OF THE CAPACITY OF SEECP TO TRANSCEND

LONG-ESTABLISHED BEHAVIOR PATTERN PERMUTATIONS AMONG ITS

MEMBER STATES. ALTHOUGH HE FAVORS SEECP\'S ASCENDANCY, HE

CAUTIONED THAT THE GROUP\'S PARTICIPATION IN SP\'S

INFRASTRUCTURE STEERING GROUP COULD CLOG UP DELIBERATIONS

THERE. HE SAID THE ISC MERITS PRESERVATION BECAUSE IT HAS

MADE UNWIELDY AND DECREASINGLY EFFECTIVE DONOR CONFERENCES

UNNECESSARY BY INSTITUTIONALIZING PROJECT REVIEWS BY THE SP

AND THE FIVE INVOLVED INTERNATIONAL FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS.

COMMENT. THE ISC IS SACCOMANNI\'S BABY, BORN AT A TIME (REF

B) SP WAS UNDER GREAT PRESSURE BY SOME OF ITS MEMBERS (MOSTLY

FRANCE) TO GET OUT OF THE INFRASTRUCTURE BUSINESS ALTOGETHER.
