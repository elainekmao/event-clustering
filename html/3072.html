SUBJECT: CENTRAL BANK GOVERNOR RAISES WARNING SIGNS

1. (U) SUMMARY: CENTRAL BANK GOVERNOR ANTONIO FAZIO

HAS HARSHLY CRITICIZED THE GOVERNMENT FOR FAILURE TO

REIN IN THE PUBLIC DEFICIT, WHICH HAS BALLOONED TO

NEAR E41 BILLION. THE CRITICISM, THE FIRST OPENLY SOUR

NOTE BETWEEN THE CENTRAL BANK AND THE GOVERNMENT, WAS

MADE AT THE BANK OF ITALYS ANNUAL MEETING ON MAY 31.

REACTING QUICKLY TO THE CRITICISM, FINANCE MINISTRY

TREMONTI DEFENDED THE GOVERNMENTS ECONOMIC PROGRAM.

DESPITE CONCEDING THAT THE DEFICIT WAS UP AND GROWTH

STAGNATING, TREMONTI SAID THERE WOULD BE NO MID-YEAR

CORRECTIVE BUDGET. END SUMMARY.

2. (U) ON MAY 31, THE BOI GOVERNOR ANTONIO FAZIO, AT

THE PRESENTATION OF THE BANKS ANNUAL REPORT, RAISED

CONCERNS ABOUT THE PUBLIC DEFICIT, WHICH HAS INCREASED

BY E4 BILLION OVER THE SAME TIME PERIOD OF 2001. HE

ARGUED FOR A SUPPLEMENTAL BUDGET PACKAGE TO BRING

PUBLIC FINANCES MORE IN LINE WITH THE STABILITY PACT

TARGETS. WARNING THE GOVERNMENT ABOUT THE FAILING

HEALTH OF THE PENSION SYSTEM, HE UNDERSCORED THE

URGENT NEED FOR REFORM. HE ALSO CALLED FOR

IMPLEMENTATION OF AN AMBITIOUS INFRASTRUCTURE PLAN, A

CORNERSTONE OF THEN-CANDIDATE SILVIO BERLUSCONIS

CAMPAIGN IDEAS TO JUMPSTART THE ECONOMY, AND THE

ACCELERATION OF PRIVATIZATION. FAZIO URGED THE

GOVERNMENT TO MOVE FORWARD ON TAX REFORM. THE BOI

GOVERNOR WAS NOT SANGUINE ABOUT GDP GROWTH, PREDICTING

A GROWTH RATE OF 1.5 PERCENT IN 2002. HE DID

ACKNOWLEDGE THAT GDP GROWTH COULD EXCEED 1.5 PERCENT,

IF INFRASTRUCTURE SPENDING WENT UP IN THE LATTER HALF

OF THE YEAR.

3. (U) ON JUNE 3, ECONOMY MINISTER TREMONTI REITERATED THAT

THERE WOULD BE NO MID-YEAR CORRECTIVE BUDGET, AND TERSELY

ADDED THAT THE GOVERNMENT HAS ALREADY PASSED NEEDED

STRUCTURAL REFORMS. TREMONTI DID ACKNOWLEDGE THAT GROWTH

PREDICTIONS OF 2.2 PERCENT ARE OPTIMISTIC. HE CONCEDED

THAT IN THE MOST PESSIMISTIC SCENARIO, GDP GROWTH IN 2002

WOULD BE JUST 1.3 PERCENT.

4. (SBU) COMMENT: THE BOI GOVERNORS WARNING FLAGS ABOUT

THE ITALIAN ECONOMY ARE ONES WE SHARE. THE GOVERNMENT HAS

FAILED TO ENACT FUNDAMENTAL ECONOMIC REFORMS AND ITS QUICK

POLICY FIXES HAVE FAILED TO PROVIDE A STRONG ECONOMIC

STIMULUS (TO BE REPORTED SEPTEL.) ALSO IMPORTANT TO NOTE IS

THE HARSHNESS OF THE GOVERNORS CRITICISM. A YEAR AGO,

WHEN THE BERLUSCONI GOVERNMENT TOOK OFFICE, THE GOVERNOR

WAS OPTIMISTIC ABOUT THE NEW GOVERNMENT AND ITS WILL TO

IMPLEMENT ITS AMBITIOUS ECONOMIC PROGRAM. FAZIOS CRITICISM

IS LEGITIMATE IN LIGHT OF ITALYS ECONOMIC PERFORMANCE. BUT

IT ALSO MAY BE A BIT OF POLITICAL MANEUVERING. EARLIER THIS

YEAR, MEMBERS OF THE MAJORITY COALITION PROPOSED TO

PARLIAMENT A BILL THAT WOULD TURN THE BANK OF ITALY INTO A

RESEARCH INSTITUTION AND TRANSFER ITS FUNCTIONS TO

INDEPENDENT AUTHORITIES. FAZIOS STRONG WORDS MAY BE HIS

RESPONSE.
