SUBJECT: VATICAN CONCERNED BY DRAFT RUSSIAN VISA RULES

REF: VATICAN 2239

CLASSIFIED BY: D. BRENT HARDT, CDA, REASON E.O. 12958 1.5 (B,D)

1. (C) SUMMARY: VATICAN DEPUTY FOREIGN MINISTER MIGLIORE

TOLD DCM JULY 12 THAT A NEW RUSSIAN DRAFT LAW ON RELIGIOUS

WORKER VISAS COULD HAVE A DEVASTATING IMPACT ON THE CHURCH\'S

ABILITY TO OPERATE IN RUSSIA. FOR EXAMPLE, MIGLIORE

INDICATED THAT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH\'S OPERATIONS IN IRKUTSK

WOULD HAVE TO CEASE IF THE LAWS WERE TO PASS IN ITS CURRENT

FORM, AND OTHER AREAS WOULD BE IMPEDED GIVEN THE SHORTAGE OF

RUSSIAN PRIESTS NATIONWIDE. THE VATICAN IS STILL SEEKING TO

DETERMINE WHETHER THIS DRAFT HAS HIGH-LEVEL POLITICAL SUPPORT

OR IS THE WORK OF THE BUREAUCRACY. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) DURING INCOMING DCM\'S JULY 12 COURTESY CALL ON

VATICAN DFM CELESTINO MIGLIORE, MIGLIORE RAISED HIS CONCERN

ABOUT THE IMPLICATIONS OF DRAFT RUSSIAN RELIGIOUS VISA LAWS

THAT WOULD REQUIRE RELIGIOUS WORKERS TO LEAVE RUSSIA EVERY

THREE MONTHS TO RENEW THEIR STATUS. HE SAID IT USUALLY TAKES

A MONTH FOR THOSE VISAS TO BE PROCESSED, AND WOULD REQUIRE

PRIESTS AND OTHER RELIGIOUS WORKERS FROM POLAND, THE

PHILIPPINES AND OTHER COUNTRIES OF ORIGIN TO LEAVE THEIR

PARISHES FOR ONE MONTH OUT OF EVERY FOUR AT GREAT EXPENSE AND

LOSS OF TIME. IN EFFECT, HE SAID, THE DECREE IS "JUST LIKE

SAYING CLOSE DOWN YOUR ACTIVITIES." MIGLIORE WAS ALSO

CONCERNED THAT THE GOR HAS LISTED A FURTHER 15 RELIGIOUS

LEADERS "TO BE EXPELLED."

3. (C) ASKED WHETHER BISHOP MAZUR (PREVIOUSLY DENIED A VISA

TO RETURN TO THE DIOCESE OF SAKHALIN -- SEE REFTEL) HAD HEARD

ANY NEWS ABOUT THE RENEWAL OF HIS VISA, MIGLIORE SAID THE

HOLY SEE HAD NOT EVEN HEARD FROM THE GOR THE LEGAL BASIS FOR

HIS VISA REFUSAL. MAZUR CONTINUES TO COOL HIS HEELS IN

POLAND. HE INDICATED THAT THERE IS A REAL RISK THAT THE

CATHOLIC CHURCH\'S ACTIVITIES IN IRKUTSK WOULD HAVE TO CEASE

IF MAZUR DOESN\'T GET HIS VISA AND IF THE NEW DECREE COMES

INTO FORCE. HE SAID THAT WHEN THE HOLY SEE ATTEMPTS TO

DISCUSS THESE ISSUES IN MOSCOW, THEY ARE TOLD THAT "LOWER

LEVEL OFFICIALS" ARE RESPONSIBLE, BUT NEVERTHELESS, THE

CHURCH\'S CLERGY ARE STILL DENIED THE CHANCE TO CONDUCT THEIR

PASTORAL WORK.

4. (C) COMMENT: MIGLIORE\'S COMMENTS ABOUT THIS NEW RUSSIAN

DECREE INDICATED A NEW LEVEL OF CONCERN WITHIN THE VATICAN

FOR THEIR ABILITY TO OPERATE IN RUSSIA. MIGLIORE RAISED THE

ISSUE IN RESPONSE TO A QUESTION ABOUT THE HOLY SEE\'S CURRENT

INTERNATIONAL PRIORITIES, GIVING IT TOP BILLING. THE NEW

DRAFT LAW COMES ON THE HEELS OF A VITUPERATIVE STATEMENT BY

THE RUSSIAN ORTHODOX CHURCH\'S SPOKESMAN FOR EXTERNAL

RELATIONS, ARCHPRIEST VSEVOLOD CHAPLIN JULY 5 IN WHICH HE

CONDEMNED THE CATHOLIC CHURCH\'S COMPETITIVE APPROACH AS

"EXPANSION AT ANY PRICE," AND ASSERTED THAT THE CATHOLIC

CHURCH HAS HAD "ALMOST ZERO SUCCESS." WE WOULD APPRECIATE

EMBASSY MOSCOW\'S TAKE ON WHETHER THERE IS A REAL THREAT THAT

THE CHURCH\'S OPERATIONS COULD BE "SHUT DOWN" OR OTHERWISE

SERIOUSLY IMPEDED BY THE NEW DRAFT LAW AND WHETHER THIS

REPRESENTS A NEW ESCALATION IN THE TENSE RELATIONS BETWEEN

THE TWO CHURCHES. WITH VATICAN MFA COUNTRY DIRECTOR FOR

RUSSIAN/NIS ISSUES ARCHBISHOP TRICARICO SOON TO RETIRE, THE

VATICAN IS THIN ON PERSONNEL AND NOT WELL POSITIONED TO PUSH

ITS INTEREST IN THIS AREA.
