Japan\'s Yosikawa Visit\'s Kilinochi

----------------------------------

1. (SBU) The Japanese Ambassador rang the Ambassador

last night to report on his and Mr. Yosikawa\'s attendance

at the first Subcommittee meeting on North-East

Reconstruction. The atmosphere was "positive and

friendly" but the details of how the Subcommittee and

related North-East Reconstruction Fund -- NERF -- will

work are still unclear. Ambassador Otsuka said he and

Yosikawa told the LTTE that donors needed maximal

flexibility to implement programs and donate funds but he

wasn\'t sure the LTTE got it. There apparently will be a

Secretariat for the Subcommittee -- bureaucracies do

SIPDIS

indeed proliferate -- staffed with an LTTE Director and

two Deputy Directors, one from the GSL and one

representing "minorities." The Japanese role in the

Subcommittee has been defined as "Principal Advisor", so

that will be Mr. Akashi\'s title. Akashi, by the way,

will be coming to the Oslo meeting. (FO)

Peiris on Balasingham\'s Wish to Visit North America

--------------------------------------------- ------

2. (C) The Ambassador lunched with GL at Parliament on

Saturday. Among many other topics discussed, GL raised a

"sensitive" matter relating to the LTTE\'s Anton

Balasingham. It seems Balasingham wants to visit Canada

and the US to "consult with the diaspora." The

Ambassador replied that Milinda had raised this with the

DepSec in September and had been told the USG would check

into it. EAW added that the checking was still going on

but was unlikely to lead to approval for a Balasingham

visit to the US until the Tigers are much closer to

coming off the FTO than they are now (the Ambassador

actually said - with typically mellifluous southern

elegance - "that dog don\'t hunt". Peiris related that he

had raised this possibility with the Canadian Foreign

Minister when he was in Ottawa last month. The Foreign

Minister replied that Canada would be willing to let in

Balasingham but was concerned lest its population think

the GOC and the USG have different approaches to FTO\'s.

According to Peiris, the Canadian asked the GSL to

intervene with the USG to give a signal to Ottawa that we

don\'t mind if the Canadians give Balasingham a visa. The

Ambassador said he would pass this along to Washington.

SA may want to alert the DepSec to the possibility the

GOC may be raising this with him directly. (FO)

Moragoda-Depsec TV Interview in Oslo?

-------------------------------------

3. (SBU) Milinda rang the Ambassador from Tokyo to find

out whether he can do his long-sought TV interview with

the Depsec while they are in Olso next week. Can SA

please check with Kara B to get an answer? (FO)
