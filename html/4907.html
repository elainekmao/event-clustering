SUBJECT: PROPOSED VISIT TO NORTH KOREA AND THE REGION

CLASSIFIED BY: CLASSIFIED BY: AMBASSADOR TONY P. HALL FOR REASONS 1.5 (

B) AND (D)

1. WFP EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR JIM MORRIS HAS INFORMED ME THAT UP

TO 4.5 MILLION HUNGRY WOMEN, CHILDREN AND ELDERLY PEOPLE ARE

BEING CUT OFF FROM WORLD FOOD PROGRAM ASSISTANCE BECAUSE OF A

LACK OF CONTRIBUTIONS. THESE ARE VULNERABLE PEOPLE THAT HAVE

BEEN RECEIVING FOOD AID ON A CONTINUAL BASIS AND WHO ARE NOW

BASICALLY HAVING TO FIND OTHER MEANS OF COPING IN THE MIDDLE

OF A HARSH KOREAN WINTER.

2. MOREOVER, MORRIS IS CLEARLY WORRIED THAT PLEDGING

SHORTFALLS COULD LEAD TO STEPS BACKWARD ON EVEN THE MODEST

GAINS THAT WFP HAS NEGOTIATED WITH THE NORTH KOREAN

GOVERNMENT SINCE 1995. IN THE WORST CASE, NORTH KOREA COULD

CLOSE THE WFP PROGRAM, RESULTING IN THE EXIT OF AROUND 50

INTERNATIONAL STAFF, PRESENTLY POSTED THROUGHOUT A LARGE PART

OF THE COUNTRY.

3. HAVING VISITED NORTH KOREA SIX TIMES DURING MY YEARS IN

CONGRESS, I AM KNOWN TO THE PYONGYANG LEADERSHIP AND HAVE

BUILT UP A DEGREE OF ACCESSIBILITY AND TRUST WITH THEM.

4. A HUMANITARIAN MISSION THERE IN THE IMMEDIATE FUTURE WOULD

PERMIT AN UPDATE ON RELIEF ASSISTANCE AND OTHER FRONTS AS

WELL. PRIOR TRIPS HAVE GENERATED A WELLSPRING OF WIDE RANGING

INFORMATION.

5. THIS REQUEST IS PROMPTED BY YOUR STATEMENT, MISTER

SECRETARY, ON 12/29 THAT: "WE ARE LOOKING FOR WAYS TO

SIPDIS

COMMUNICATE WITH THE NORTH KOREANS SO SOME SENSE CAN PREVAIL."

6. MY TRIP WOULD BE COMBINED WITH VISITS TO SOUTH KOREA AND

JAPAN IN ORDER TO STIMULATE ADDITIONAL DONOR SUPPORT FOR

WFP\'S 2003 APPEAL. HALL
