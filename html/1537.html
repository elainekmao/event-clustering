SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE NON-COMMITTAL ON U.S. CANDIDATE FOR

INTERNATIONAL CRIMINAL TRIBUNAL FOR YUGOSLAVIA

REF: STATE 28275

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS: 1.5 (B) AND (D).

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 00788 211237Z

1. (C) PER REF INSTRUCTIONS, POLOFF, ON FEBRUARY 21,

DELIVERED TALKING POINTS TO MUNYARADZI MOTSI, THE ACTING

UNDERSECRETARY FOR INTERNATIONAL ORGANIZATIONS IN

ZIMBABWE\'S MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS. MOTSI ASKED A

FEW GENERAL QUESTIONS ABOUT THE U.S. CANDIDATE FOR THE

FORMER YUGOSLAVIA TRIBUNAL, BUT WAS NON-COMMITTAL ABOUT

ZIMBABWE\'S SUPPORT. THE MFA OFFICIAL STATED THAT

ZIMBABWE DOES NOT WANT TO COMMIT TOO EARLY TO A

PARTICULAR NOMINEE AS IT LIKES TO LOOK AT ALL THE

CANDIDATES, INCLUDING THOSE THAT MAY BE LAST-MINUTE

NOMINATIONS.

2. (C) COMMENT: GIVEN THE MFA\'S RESPONSE AND THE GOZ\'S

CURRENT GENERAL ANTIPATHY TOWARD WESTERN GOVERNMENTS, WE

EXPECT ZIMBABWE TO FIRST FAVOR ANY NON-WESTERN

CANDIDATES OVER THE U.S. NOMINEE. END COMMENT.

IRVING
