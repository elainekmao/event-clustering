1. MISSION ITALY\'S BIOTECH WORKING GROUP

(BWG) HAS MAPPED OUT ITS ACTION PLAN FOR THE

NEXT SIX MONTHS.

UNCLASSIFIED

IT REFLECTS THE COLLECTIVE EFFORTS OF AGR,

ECON, SCI, PA, FCS, LABOR, CG-MILAN, CG-

NAPLES, AND CG-FLORENCE.

BECAUSE MISSION ITALY SHARES MANY COMMON

OBJECTIVES AND PRIORITIES REGARDING U.S.

BIOTECHNOLOGY POLICY WITH OUR COLLEAGUES FROM

FODAG AND EMBASSY VATICAN, THE PLAN ALSO

REFLECTS THEIR INPUT AND WORK PRIORITIES FOR

THE NEXT SEVERAL MONTHS.

2. THE ACTION PLAN IS DIVIDED INTO TWO

PARTS. PART I COVERS EMBASSY ITALY\'S

BILATERAL AGENDA; AND PART II COVERS THE

UNCLASSIFIED

COLLABORATIVE EFFORTS BETWEEN EMBASSY ROME,

EMBASSY VATICAN AND FODAG.

3. THE PRINCIPAL GOALS OF THE BIOTECHNOLOGY

WORKING GROUP ARE TO REMOVE BARRIERS TO

INTERNATIONAL TRADE IN U.S. APPROVED BIOTECH

PRODUCTS AND TO PROMOTE BIOTECHNOLOGY AS AN

IMPORTANT PART OF SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT IN

DEVELOPED AND DEVELOPING COUNTRIES. OUR

STRATEGY IN ITALY IS TO HELP THE GROWING

NETWORK OF GOVERNMENT, ACADEMIC, INDUSTRIA,

AND NGO OPINION LEADERS WHO SUPPORT

BIOTECHNOLOGY DEVELOP AN EFFECTIVE ACTION

PLAN TO COUNTER WELL-ORGANIZED BIOTECH

OPPONENTS.

BY SUPPORTING THIS POSITIVE NETWORK WITH U.S.

POLICY MESSAGES, SCIENTIFIC EXCHANGES, AND

GRASSROOTS ACTIVITIES SUCH AS FARMER-TO-

FARMER CONTACTS, AND DIALOGUE WITH OBJECTIVE

ENVIRONMENTAL AND CONSUMER GROUPS, WE WILL

STRESS THE BENEFITS OF BIOTECHNOLOGY TO

GROWERS AND CONSUMERS. WE WILL PROVIDE

ACCURATE AND UP-TO-DATE INFORMATION TO

OPINION LEADERS ABOUT ENVIRONMENTAL RISK, AND

ABOUT THE INCREASING SOPHISTICATION OF

BIOTECHNOLOGY.

UNCLASSIFIED

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 04 ROME 01942 01 OF 04 181506Z

WE WILL BREAK DOWN THE FALSE PERCEPTIONS THAT

HIGH-QUALITY AGRICULTURAL PRODUCTS AND

BIOTECHNOLOGY PRODUCTS ARE MUTUALLY EXCLUSIVE

BY DEMONSTRATING THAT BIOTECHNOLOGY CAN

REDUCE THE VULNERABILITY OF MANY QUALITY

PRODUCTS TO PESTS AND DISEASE WHILE

PRESERVING THEIR TRADITIONAL QUALITIES.

AT THE EU AND INTERNATIONAL LEVELS (E.G. WTO

AND UN AGENCIES), WE WILL SUPPORT EFFORTS TO

RESTART THE APPROVALS PROCESS AND REOPEN

TRADE, AND SUPPORT U.S. POLICY ON PRACTICAL

APPROACHES TO LABELING AND TRACEABILITY WHICH

ARE NON-DISCRIMINATORY. WE ALSO WILL SUPPORT

THE BIOSAFETY CLEARING HOUSE AND OTHER

EFFORTS TO INCREASE THE INFORMATION AND

DECISION MAKING CAPACITY OF DEVELOPING

COUNTRIES WITH REGARD TO BIOTECHNOLOGY.
