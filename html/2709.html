SUBJECT: (C) ITALY: LUKEWARM SUPPORT FOR PROJECT VARELA

REF: A. SECSTATE 55698

B. SECSTATE 49534

CLASSIFIED BY: POL MC TCOUNTRYMAN FOR REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D)

1. (U) POLOFF DELIVERED REF A DEMARCHE MARCH 26 TO

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 ROME 01649 031054Z

AMBASSADOR NICCOLO\' GORETTI DE FLAMINI, MFA OFFICE DIRECTOR

FOR CENTRAL AMERICA AND THE CARIBBEAN. GORETTI IS A FORMER

AMBASSADOR TO NICARAGUA.

2. (C) ACCORDING TO GORETTI, THE GOI WAS IN FAVOR OF THE

VARELA PROJECT AND WAS FOLLOWING IT CLOSELY. THE GOI HAD

INFORMATION THAT THE GOC WOULD HAMPER THE PROJECT BUT WOULD

WAIT TO SEE WHAT HAPPENED. HE WOULD FAX OUR DEMARCHE TO

THEIR AMBASSADOR TO CUBA. THEIR DCM IN HAVANA MET FREQUENTLY

WITH DISSIDENTS AND WOULD SUPPORT THIS PROJECT IN THOSE

MEETINGS.

3. (C) GORETTI NOTED THAT SOME CUBAN DISSIDENTS OPPOSED

PROJECT VARELA. THEY DID NOT BELIEVE THE GOC WOULD EVER

ALLOW A REFERENDUM.

4. (C) ASKED WHETHER THE GOI WOULD SUPPORT A RESOLUTION IN

THE UN COMMISSION ON HUMAN RIGHTS (UNCHR) ON HUMAN RIGHTS IN

CUBA (REF B), GORETTI SAID THE GOI WOULD HAVE TO SEE THE TEXT

AND COORDINATE WITHIN THE EU. PROBABLY THE GOI WOULD VOTE IN

FAVOR OF SUCH A RESOLUTION BUT WOULD NOT COSPONSOR IT. ITALY

WOULD BE LIKELY TO ABSTAIN ONLY IF THE TEXT MADE FAVORABLE

MENTION OF THE AMERICAN EMBARGO, WHICH THE GOI OPPOSES. A

BRITISH DEMARCHE ON THE SAME SUBJECT HAD SUGGESTED THAT THE

EU TAKE A LOW PROFILE BUT VOTE IN FAVOR OF A RESOLUTION. THE

GOI AND HMG PREFERRED THAT THE EMBARGO NOT BE MENTIONED IN A

RESOLUTION, BUT SOME "EU GOVERNMENTS ON THE LEFT" DISAGREED

AND, HE IMPLIED, WERE SEEKING TO INSERT A NEGATIVE REFERENCE

TO THE EMBARGO.

5. (C) COMMENT: GORETTI\'S PROFESSION OF SUPPORT FOR

PROJECT TO CONTINUE. HE SEEMED MORE EAGER TO TAKE POT SHOTS
