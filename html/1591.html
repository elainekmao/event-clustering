SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE HAPPY WITH US POSITION ON THE

CONGO, MYSTIFIED BY THE BELGIANS

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) DURING A ONE-AND-A-HALF-HOUR-MEETING MARCH 16 IN

HARARE, VISITING MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS CONGO

EXPERT DAVID HAMADZIRIPI SHARED HIS GOVERNMENT\'S VIEWS

ON CERTAIN RECENT DEVELOPMENTS IN THE CONGO. ACCORDING

TO HAMADZIRIPI:

-- THE ZIMBABWEANS ARE PARTICULARLY PLEASED WITH THE

U.S. ADMINISTRATION\'S CURRENT STANCE ON THE CONGO. HE

CITED AS THE REASON FOR HIS HAPPINESS A RECENT

CONVERSATION WITH AMBASSADOR CUNNINGHAM IN NEW YORK

DURING WHICH THE LATTER REPORTEDLY SAID THAT THE BUSH

ADMINISTRATION WAS PREPARED TO SUPPORT MONUC DEPLOYMENT;

-- THE FRENCH INFORMED PRESIDENT MUGABE DURING THE

LATTER\'S RECENT TRIP TO PARIS THAT AMERICAN POSITION ON

THE CONGO HAD "MOVED CLOSER" TO THEIR OWN (NFI);

-- ZIMBABWE IS MYSTIFIED BY BELGIUM\'S RECENT,

AGGRESSIVE INSERTION OF ITSELF INTO THE CONGO PEACE

PROCESS. HE SURMISED THAT BELGIUM FEELS A

RESPONSIBILITY AS FORMER COLONIAL METROPOLE FOR THE

CONGO\'S SECURITY, BUT UNDERSTOOD CLEARLY THAT BRUSSELS

HAS "OTHER INTERESTS" IN THE CONGO. THE ZIMBABWEANS SAW

THE BELGIANS AS BEING AMBIVALENT ABOUT JOSEPH KABILA,

CLOSE TO THE UNARMED OPPOSITION IN THE CONGO AND HAVING

LITTLE USE FOR ANY OF THE REBEL MOVEMENTS;

-- ZIMBABWE HAS NO QUARREL OR TENSIONS WITH THE

ANGOLANS OVER WHO HAS THE GREATEST INFLUENCE IN THE

CONGO, ALTHOUGH HE PROFESSED TO UNDERSTAND HOW SUCH AN

IMPRESSION MIGHT HAVE BEEN CREATED. HE CLAIMED THAT

PRESIDENT DOS SANTOS PERSONALLY SACKED FORMER ANGOLAN

ARMED FORCES CHIEF JOAO DE MATOS, WHOSE MAIN SIN WAS

ENTERING INTO AN EXCESSIVELY CLOSE EMBRACE WITH THE

UGANDANS. HAMADZIRIPI RECOUNTED HOW AT ONE SUMMIT IN

LUANDA THEN PRESIDENT LAURENT KABILA ASKED DOS SANTOS TO

PRACTICE TRANSPARENCY WITH RESPECT TO HIS DEALINGS WITH

OUTSIDERS AND EXPLAIN MATOS\' INVITATION TO HIS UGANDAN

COUNTERPART TO VISIT LUANDA. THE ANGOLAN PRESIDENT, WHO

HAD NOT BEEN BRIEFED ON THE INITIATIVE, REMAINED SILENT.

"JOAO DE MATOS BEHAVED AS IF HE WERE THE HEAD OF STATE

IN WAITING," THE ZIMBABWEAN DIPLOMAT STATED, AND WHEN

THE ANGOLAN PRESIDENT WENT ON A VISIT TO THE FAR EAST IN

JANUARY HE MADE CERTAIN TO INCLUDE THE ALREADY FIRED

FORMER GENERAL IN HIS TRAVELING PARTY;

-- RWANDA\'S CLAIM THAT IT IS IN THE CONGO BECAUSE IT

SLOWNESS IN DEPLOYING WAS ATTRIBUTABLE TO COMMUNICATIONS

PROBLEMS. ZIMBABWE AND THE CONGOLESE ARMY (FAC) ARE

PREPARED NOT TO REOCCUPY PWETO IF IT WOULD HELP MONUC.

ZIMBABWEAN ARMY COMMANDER CONSTANTINE CHIWENGA WAS

CURRENTLY IN KINSHASA NEGOTIATING THE WITHDRAWAL

MODALITIES WITH THE UN;

-- BEGINNING POSSIBLY IN APRIL, ZIMBABWE WILL START

BRINGING HOME THE TROOPS IT SENT TO THE CONGO IN THE

IMMEDIATE AFTERMATH OF LAURENT KABILA\'S ASSASSINATION

AND MORE. HE SAID THAT HIS COUNTRY\'S MILITARY ASSESSED

THAT IN SPITE OF CLAIMS OF MASSIVE TROOP WITHDRAWALS,

UGANDA HAD ONLY BROUGHT HOME 569 MEN. "THEIRS IS A

GAME, A DANGEROUS GAME," THE ZIMBABWEAN DIPLOMAT

AVERRED.
