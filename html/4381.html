1. SUMMARY: GREEK COTTON PRODUCTION IS

SUSTAINED BY EU PRICE SUPPORTS WHICH VARY

ACCORDING TO OVERALL PRODUCTION TARGETS.

BECAUSE OF OVERPRODUCTION IN THREE OF THE

LAST FIVE YEARS, THE AVERAGE PRICE PAID FOR

SEED COTTON HAS VARIED FROM 62.50 EUROS PER

100 KG TO 88.0 EUROS PER 100 KG.

2. KEY SUPPORT FOR GREECE\'S COTTON

PRODUCTION IS THE EU COTTON REGIME. EU

REGULATIONS 1051/2001 AND 1591/2001 SET NEW

POLICIES AND MEASURES FOR PREVENTING

OVERPRODUCTION OF EU COTTON AND NEW

CORESPONSIBILITY. THE LATTER IS THE

MECHANISM (LEVY) FOR PENALIZING COTTON

PRODUCERS FOR EXCEEDING A TARGET. THE

MAXIMUM PRODUCTION LEVEL WAS SET AT 1,031,000

MT OF RAW COTTON (SEED COTTON). WHENEVER

PRODUCTION EXCEEDS THIS LEVEL, THE TARGET

PRICE IS REDUCED FOR BOTH STATES, GREECE AND

SPAIN, AS DESCRIBED IN ARTICLE 3, PARAGRAPH 1

OF THE REGULATION NO.1051 IN CHAPTER II

(UNDER PRICE MECHANISMS).

3. IN 2001 GREEK COTTON PRODUCTION EXCEEDED

THE QUOTA BY ALMOST 200,000 MT (RAW COTTON

BASIS.) CONSEQUENTLY, HIGH PENALTIES WERE

IMPOSED BY THE EU ON FARMERS, REDUCING THEIR

TARGET PRICE BY OVER 20 PERCENT.

4. IN MAY 2001 THE GOG OFFERED SUGGESTIONS TO

COTTON FARMING GROUPS ON WAYS TO CONTINUE

FARMING BUT TO REDUCE COTTON ACREAGE. THIS

ADVICE WENT LARGELY UNHEEDED. NO INCENTIVE

OTHER THAN THE USUAL EU PAYMENT WAS OFFERED.

THIS ACTION PROVED TO BE UNSUCCESSFUL AND

BOTH THE GOG AND FARMERS WERE DISAPPOINTED TO

SAY THE LEAST. HOWEVER, IN 2002 A REDUCTION

OF COTTON ACREAGE BY 25,000 HA TOOK PLACE,

CAUSED BY THE \'DOCKING\' OF PRICES PAID OUT

FOR THE RECORD HIGH GREEK 2001 HARVEST. THIS

YEAR\'S (2003 HARVEST) OUTPUT IS EXPECTED TO

REACH APPROXIMATELY 1,000,000 MT (WITHIN THE

QUOTA), COMPARED TO A 2001 CROP OF 1,350,000

MT (RAW BASIS). ACREAGE FOREGONE FROM COTTON

WAS UTILIZED FOR DURUM WHEAT, CORN, ALFALFA

AND FORAGE PLANT CROPS.

5. ALL COTTON CROP ACREAGE IN GREECE IS

IRRIGATED. FARMERS PAY A FLAT FEE OF 120

EUROS PER HECTARE/YEAR REGARDLESS OF HOW MUCH

WATER THEY CONSUME. ON-FARM USE OF FUEL ALSO

IS SUBSIDIZED AT A DISCOUNT OF 10-15 PERCENT

UNDER MARKET PRICES.

7. FOR SEVERAL YEARS THE GOG HAS

UNSUCCESSFULLY PETITIONED BRUSSELS TO

INCREASE THE COTTON PRODUCTION QUOTA THE GOG

ARGUED THAT THE EU SHOULD ATTEMPT TO COVER

"PRAGMATIC" ACREAGE PLANTED TO COTTON TO

SUPPORT FARM INCOME. THE COMMISSION HAS

REJECTED ALL SUCH REQUESTS. TO THE CONTRARY,

UNDER THE COTTON REFORM OF MAY 2001 THE EU

HAS CHANGED BASIC REGULATIONS PERTAINING TO

COTTON CULTIVATION AND HAS IMPOSED STRICTER

PENALTIES FOR OVERPRODUCTION.
