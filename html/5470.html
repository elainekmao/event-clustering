SUBJECT: USAID/DCHA TEAM DISCUSSES POSSIBLE IRAQ

HUMANITARIAN EMERGENCY RESPONSE WITH FAO - JANUARY 24, 2003

MEETING IN ROME

REF: (A) ROME 00371 (B) 02 ROME 6133 (C) 02 ROME 6072

CLASSIFIED BY: CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TONY P. HALL FOR REASONS 1.5 (B

) AND (D).

-------

SUMMARY

-------

1. USAID/DAA/DCHA BILL GARVELINK AND D/FFP LAUREN LANDIS

VISITED FAO ROME HEADQUARTERS ON JANUARY 24 TO REVIEW FAO\'S

SIZABLE OIL-FOR-FOOD (OFF)-FINANCED OPERATIONS IN IRAQ - AND

ITS CAPABILITY TO DEVELOP AN AGRICULTURE-RELATED HUMANITARIAN

RESPONSE MECHANISM FOR IRAQ IN THE EVENT OF OUTBREAK OF

CONFLICT. FAO STRESSED THAT ANY SUCCESSFUL POST-CONFLICT

HUMANITARIAN INTERVENTION MUST BE FOCUSED ON URGENT REVIVAL

OF THE AGRICULTURAL SECTOR. FAO CURRENTLY HAS A TOTAL OF 72

INTERNATIONAL AND 600 NATIONAL STAFF WORKING WITHIN IRAQ.

PARTICULARLY IN THE THREE NORTHERN GOVERNORATES, WHERE FAO

SPENDS SOME U.S. DOLLARS 70 MILLION/ANNUM AND IS DIRECTLY

RESPONSIBLE FOR IMPLEMENTATION OF THE REGION\'S AGRICULTURE

PROGRAM, IT IS CLEAR THAT FAO WILL BE A KEY PLAYER IN

AGRICULTURAL REHABILITATION AND RECONSTRUCTION. IN US

MISSION\'S VIEW, IT IS URGENT THAT FAO CONTINUE TO BE STRONGLY

FACTORED INTO THE IRAQ HUMANITARIAN RESPONSE OPERATIONAL

DIALOGUE. END SUMMARY.

----------

BACKGROUND

----------

2. USAID/DAA/DCHA BILL GARVELINK AND D/FFP LAUREN LANDIS

VISITED FAO ROME ON JANUARY 24 TO REVIEW FAO\'S SIZABLE

OIL-FOR-FOOD (OFF)-FINANCED OPERATIONS IN IRAQ AND ITS

CAPABILITY TO DEVELOP AN AGRICULTURE-RELATED HUMANITARIAN

RESPONSE MECHANISM OR IRAQ IN THE EVENT OF OUTBREAK OF

CONFLICT. PRINCIPAL INTERLOCUTORS FROM FAO INCLUDED

ASSISTANT DIRECTOR GENERAL HENRI CARSALADE; ANNE BAUER,

DIRECTOR, SPECIAL RELIEF OPERATIONS SERVICE (TCOR); SENIOR

TCOR OPERATIONS OFFICER, LAURENT THOMAS; AND IRAQ TECHNICAL

SPECIALISTS RODRIQUE VINET AND FRANCESCO DEL RE.

3. SINCE 1997, APPROXIMATELY U.S. DOLLARS 800 MILLION WORTH

OF AGRICULTURE INPUTS AND EQUIPMENT HAVE BEEN IMPORTED INTO

IRAQ EVERY YEAR UNDER THE OFF PROGRAM TO SUPPORT LOCAL FOOD

PRODUCTION. OF THIS AMOUNT, APPROXIMATELY U.S. DOLLARS 730

MILLION/ANNUM ARE SPENT IN THE CENTER/SOUTH WHERE THE PROGRAM

IS IMPLEMENTED DIRECTLY BY THE GOVERNMENT OF IRAQ (GOI) AND

U.S. DOLLARS 70 MILLION/ANNUM IN THE NORTH WHERE FAO IS FULLY

RESPONSIBLE FOR PROVISION OF AGRICULTURAL INPUTS AND

REHABILITATION ACTIVITIES ON BEHALF OF THE GOI.

4. FAO CURRENTLY HAS A TOTAL OF 72 INTERNATIONAL AND 600

NATIONAL STAFF WORKING IN IRAQ AS FOLLOWS: 19 INTERNATIONAL

AND 116 NATIONAL STAFF IN THE CENTER/SOUTH REGION AND 53

INTERNATIONAL AND 484 NATIONAL STAFF IN THE THREE NORTHERN

GOVERNORATES.

5. IN THE NORTHERN GOVERNORATES OF DOHUK, IRBIL AND

SULEIMANIYAH, WHERE THE GOI WITHDREW ITS PUBLIC SERVICES IN

1991, FAO ACTIVITIES TARGET A WIDE RANGE OF AGRICULTURAL

SUB-SECTORS, INCLUDING WATER RESOURCES AND IRRIGATION, PLANT

PROTECTION AND PRODUCTION, FORESTRY AND EROSION CONTROL,

MECHANIZATION, AGRO-INDUSTRY, ANIMAL PRODUCTION AND HEALTH,

ETC. NOTE: CEREAL YIELDS IN NORTHERN IRAQ REACHED 600,000

MTS IN 2002, THE HIGHEST IN THE LAST FIVE YEARS. THE RATE OF

POULTRY PRODUCTION IN THE NORTH NOW STANDS AT 39,900 MTS OF

POULTRY MEAT/ANNUM AND 375 MILLION EGGS/ANNUM. END NOTE.
