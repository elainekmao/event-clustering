SUBJECT: WFP POST-DELIVERY LOSSES IN BANGLADESH

-------

SUMMARY

-------

1. IN BANGLADESHI PORTS DURING 2001, WFP SUSTAINED POST-

DELIVERY LOSSES OF 2,824 METRIC TONS (VALUED AT USD

343,388). WFP HAS INSTITUTED A POLICY (CONTAINED IN ITS

2001-2005 COUNTRY PROGRAM OPERATIONAL CONTRACT WITH THE

GOB) WHICH STIPULATES THAT FOR BULK WHEAT SHIPMENTS LANDED

QUANTITIES WILL EQUAL BILL OF LADING QUANTITIES AS

CONFIRMED BY DRAFT AND LIGHTER SURVEYS. ON ENDEMIC LOSSES,

A TASK FORCE COMPOSED OF THE GOB AND FOUR MAJOR DONORS IS

UNDERTAKING A FOUR-MONTH ANALYTICAL STUDY OF THE COMMODITY

LEAKAGE PROBLEM, WITH A FINAL REPORT WITH RECOMMENDATIONS

TO REDUCE LEAKAGES DUE IN EARLY 2003. END SUMMARY.

----------

BACKGROUND

----------

2. IN MAY 2001, THE WFP SECRETARIAT INFORMED OF A MAJOR

DIALOGUE WITH FOOD AID DONORS (U.S., CANADA, OTHERS) TO

ADDRESS THE PROBLEM OF SUSPECTED FOOD LEAKAGES TO NON-

TARGETED BENEFICIARIES THROUGH UNAUTHORIZED DISTRIBUTION

CHANNELS. WFP\'S INITIAL MISGIVINGS CAME FROM A 1997 REQUEST

IT MADE TO THE INTERNATIONAL FOOD POLICY RESEARCH INSTITUTE

(IFPRI) TO CARRY OUT A PRELIMINARY STUDY TO INVESTIGATE THE

MAGNITUDE OF THE PROBLEM OF FOOD AID LEAKAGES AND DEVELOP A

MONITORING METHODOLOGY. IN 1998 A COMPREHENSIVE FOOD

COMMODITY TRACKING SYSTEM APPRAISAL WAS CARRIED OUT. IN

1999, THE WFP COUNTRY OFFICE ESTABLISHED A FOOD TRANSPORT

AND STORAGE MONITORING UNIT. SINCE THEN, EFFORTS HAVE BEEN

MADE TO INFORM VULNERABLE GROUP AND EMERGENCY OPERATION

BENEFICIARIES, AS WELL AS THE PUBLIC AT LARGE, OF THEIR

RIGHTS AND ENTITLEMENTS RELATED TO RATIONS.

3. IN 2001, WITH THE ASSISTANCE OF THE GERMAN AGENCY FOR

TECHNICAL COOPERATION (GTZ), THE WFP COUNTRY OFFICE

DESIGNED A MONITORING AND EVALUATION (M&E) SYSTEM, WHICH

WFP HAS REPORTEDLY INTRODUCED ON A SYSTEMATIC BASIS IN ALL

ITS ACTIVITIES. SIX SUB-OFFICES HAVE BEEN ESTABLISHED USING

31 FIELD MONITORS. IN THE VULNERABLE GROUP DEVELOPMENT

ACTIVITY, THE FIRST THREE OF A PLANNED FORTY WHEAT FLOUR

MILLING AND FORTIFICATION PLANTS BEGAN OPERATING IN MARCH

2002, PROVIDING FORTIFIED WHEAT FLOUR TO BENEFICIARIES IN

SEALED BAGS, THUS REDUCING THE RISK OF LEAKAGE.

-----------

PORT LOSSES

-----------

4. WFP HAS ENGAGED ON A TEMPORARY BASIS A PORT CAPTAIN WHO

HAS RECOMMENDED THAT WFP ONLY USE ONE DISCHARGE PORT IN

FUTURE (CHITTAGONG), AND EMPLOYMENT OF COASTAL LIGHTERS TO

FORWARD FREIGHT NEEDED IN MONGLA PORT. LIMITING THE USE OF

MONGLA IS DUE TO PAST SIGNIFICANT LOSSES EXPERIENCED WITH

BULK WHEAT, OWING MAINLY TO WASTAGE DURING LIGHTERING OF

BULK COMMODITIES FROM LARGE VESSELS.

-------

COMMENT

-------

5. EVERY CASE OF COMMODITY HEMORRHAGING IN TODAY\'S

CONSTRAINED BUDGETARY ENVIRONMENT HAS TO BE SWIFTLY AND

VIGOROUSLY ADDRESSED. SUSPECTED FOOD LEAKAGES BOTH IN

BANGLADESHI PORTS AND TO NON-TARGETED BENEFICIARIES THROUGH

UNAUTHORIZED DISTRIBUTION CHANNELS HAVE CONSTITUTED A LONG-

TERM PROBLEM FOR THE WFP PROGRAM. IT IS EXPECTED THAT THE

WFP-DONOR STUDY WILL INDUCE THE GOVERNMENT OF BANGLADESH TO

TAKE THE REMEDIAL ACTIONS NECESSARY TO EFFECTIVELY END

LOSSES ASSOCIATED WITH WFP EFFORTS IN BANGLADESH. HEILEMAN
