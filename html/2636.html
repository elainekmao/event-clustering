SUBJECT: MUGABE INAUGURAL REMARKS PROMISE MORE OF THE SAME

CLASSIFIED BY POLITICAL SECTION CHIEF MATT HARRINGTON. REASONS:

1.5 (B) AND (D).

INAUGURATION CEREMONY

---------------------

1. (U) PRESIDENT MUGABE WAS SWORN IN FOR A FIFTH TERM ON MARCH 17,

ONLY TWO DAYS AFTER THE ANNOUNCEMENT OF THE APPARENTLY HASTILY-

ARRANGED INAUGURATION CEREMONY. FIVE AFRICAN HEADS OF STATE WERE

IN ATTENDANCE -- TANZANIA\'S BENJAMIN MKAPA; MOZAMBIQUE\'S JOAQUIM

CHISSANO; MALAWI\'S BAKILI MULUZI; NAMIBIA\'S SAM NUJOMA; AND DRC\'S

JOSEPH KABILA. SOUTH AFRICA WAS REPRESENTED BY VICE-PRESIDENT

JACOB ZUMA, BOTSWANA BY VICE-PRESIDENT IAN KHAMA, AND ZAMBIA BY

VICE-PRESIDENT ENOCK KAVINDILE. ANGOLA, LIBYA, AND RWANDA SENT

HIGH-LEVEL REPRESENTATIVES FROM CAPITALS. AFTER MUGABE WAS SWORN

IN, PRESIDENT MKAPA HUGGED THE ZIMBABWEAN PRESIDENT AND

ENTHUSIASTICALLY WAVED THE ZANU-PF SALUTE -- A CLOSED FIST -- IN

THE AIR. NEITHER AMBASSADOR SULLIVAN NOR HIS COUNTERPARTS FROM EU

MISSIONS, CANADA, OR AUSTRALIA ATTENDED THE CEREMONY.

PRESIDENT\'S REMARKS

-------------------

2. (U) PRESIDENT MUGABE\'S SPEECH CONTAINED THE FOLLOWING KEY

THEMES:

--ZIMBABWE\'S ELECTORAL PROCESS HAD BEEN QUINTESSENTIALLY DEMOCRATIC

("THE GREATEST WINNER, YOU WILL AGREE, WAS OUR DEMOCRATIC

PROCESS");

--A VAGUE OVERTURE TO THE OPPOSITION ("OUR ENERGIES AND

CAPABILITIES ARE SURELY BETTER SPENT ON CONSTRUCTIVE PEOPLE-
