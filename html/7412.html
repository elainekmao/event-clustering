SUBJECT: BLUE LANTERN LEVEL 3: PRE-LICENSE END USE CHECK

APPLICATION TA-1017-03

REF: STATE 062909

1. REFTEL REQUESTED POST CONDUCT A PRE-LICENSE CHECK ON

MIDDLE EAST OPTRONICS, LISTED AS THE FOREIGN CONSIGNEE ON

DTC CASE #TA-1017-03, WHICH PROPOSES THE TRANSFER BY FLIR

SYSTEMS OF TECHNICAL DATA FOR THE REPAIR AND MAINTENANCE OF

FLIR THERMAL IMAGING EQUIPMENT TO MIDDLE EAST OPTRONICS.

2. ON APRIL 5, 2003, AT THE REQUEST OF THE

POLITICAL/ECONOMIC OFFICER IN DUBAI, COMMERCIAL OFFICER MARY

O\'BRIEN MET WITH HADIF AL-MANSOURI OF EMIRATES AVIATION,

HAMDAN STREET, ABU DHABI. ON APRIL 6, 2003, COMMOFF O\'BRIEN

ALSO MET WITH MOUHIDDINE (MIKE) GHALAYINI, UAE

REPRESENTATIVE OF FLIR SYSTEMS AND MANAGING DIRECTOR OF

MIDDLE EAST OPTRONICS, DUBAI AIRPORT FREE ZONE, DUBAI.

3. IN CONVERSATIONS WITH THE TWO PRINCIPALS, THE FOLLOWING

INFORMATION WAS PROVIDED: CURRENTLY ALL FLIR THERMAL IMAGING

PRODUCTS IN THE GULF REGION, THOSE LOCALLY OWNED AND THOSE

OWNED BY US MILITARY STATIONED HERE, MUST BE SENT TO THE

U.S. FOR REPAIR. THIS PROCESS CAN TAKE UP TO 3 MONTHS DUE

TO TRANSPORTATION AND CUSTOMS PROCEDURES. IF FLIR HAD AN

APPROVED REPAIR STATION HERE IN THE UAE, THE TIME FOR REPAIR

COULD BE CUT TO 48 HOURS FOR UNITS IN THE UAE AND A WEEK FOR

UNITS IN APPROVED GULF COUNTRIES, AS WELL AS EGYPT, JORDAN

AND MOROCCO. FLIR APPROACHED EMIRATES AVIATION AS A PARTNER

DUE TO THEIR EXCELLENT REPUTATION WITH THE UAE MILITARY.

4. EMIRATES AVIATION HAS BEEN IN BUSINESS SINCE 1991 AS A

SUPPLIER OF SPARE PARTS AND OTHER SERVICES FOR THE UAE

MILITARY. THE COMPANY AND MR. AL MANSOORI ARE WELL KNOWN TO

THE COMMERCIAL OFFICE HERE IN ABU DHABI AND ENJOY AN

EXCELLENT REPUTATION. MR. AL-MANSOORI PROVIDED A COPY OF A

SIGNED CONTRACT #DP/A1-2-3-4/98/62/4/32 WITH THE UAE

MILITARY TO PROVIDE OVERHAUL, REPAIR AND MODIFICATION

SERVICES FOR HELICOPTERS, MISSILES, AMMUNITION, GROUND POWER

UNITS, GENERATORS, AND BATTLEFIELD-CONSUMABLE EQUIPMENT.

THEY ALSO ARE APPROVED BY THE UAE CIVIL AVIATION AUTHORITY

AS A STOCKIST AND SUPPLIER FOR AIRCRAFT PARTS AND EQUIPMENT,

AND ARE A MEMBER OF THE AIRLINE SUPPLIERS ASSOCIATION.

LASTLY THEY ARE REGISTERED WITH NATO AS A SUPPLIER OF

MILITARY SPARE PARTS AND EQUIPMENT. THE COMPANY HAS TEN

EMPLOYEES AND IN 2002 EMIRATES CLOSED 70 ORDERS WITH 23 U.S.

COMPANIES FOR A TOTAL OF $1,000,000.

5. FLIR SYSTEMS AND EMIRATES AVIATION ARE EACH 50%

SHAREHOLDERS IN MIDDLE EAST OPTRONICS (MEO), WHICH HAS BEEN

ESTABLISHED IN THE DUBAI AIRPORT FREE ZONE (DAFZA), LICENSE

#196 (DOCUMENTS BEYOND THIS INFORMATION ARE NOT PUBLIC

RECORD HERE AS THEY ARE IN THE US). THE OFFICES, MEASURING

OVER 300 SQUARE METERS, ARE LOCATED IN AN OFFICE/WAREHOUSE

FACILITY. ONLY THE OFFICES ARE IN CURRENT USE UNTIL THE

EXPORT LICENSE IS APPROVED TO ESTABLISH THE REPAIR FACILITY.

6. DAFZA IS IMMEDIATELY ADJACENT TO THE AIRPORT AND WILL

THEREFORE SHORTEN TRANSIT TIME FOR PRODUCTS TO BE REPAIRED.

IN ADDITION, MEO\'S LOCATION IN DAFZA ALLOWS IMPORTS AND

EXPORTS WITHOUT PAYMENT OF UAE TARIFFS. MEO WILL BEGIN AS A

REPAIR SHOP FOR FLIR BUT MEO IS ALSO LOOKING TO ESTABLISH

REPAIR AGREEMENTS WITH OTHER MANUFACTURERS AS WELL. MR.

GHALAYINI HAS BEEN SELLING FLIR MILITARY SYSTEMS, ALL OF

WHICH REQUIRE EXPORT LICENSES, IN THE GULF REGION FOR OVER

SIX YEARS AND HE IS WELL ACQUAINTED WITH THE EXPORT LICENSE

PROCESS AND COMPLIANCE WITH CONDITIONS ON SUCH LICENSES. HE

UNDERSTANDS THAT THERE MAY BE RESTRICTIONS REGARDING

TRANSFER OF THIS TECHNOLOGY, EVEN THOUGH IT IS MAINTENANCE

AND REPAIR TECHNOLOGY, NOT DESIGN OR PRODUCTION TECHNOLOGY.

COMMOFF O\'BRIEN CAN VERIFY MEO\'S INTENT TO ABIDE BY ANY

LICENSE CONDITIONS IMPOSED FOR THE REEXPORT OF SPARE PARTS.
