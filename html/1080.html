SUBJECT: ILO PRESENCE IN VIETNAM AND EMBASSY WORKING ON COOPERATION WITH AFL-CIO

REF: HANOI #0648

1. THE INTERNATIONAL LABOR ORGANIZATION (ILO) IS PRESENTLY

WORKING CLOSELY WITH THE MINISTRY OF LABOR (MOLISA) AND THE

VIETNAMESE GENERAL CONFEDERATION OF LABOR (VGCL) ON A

NUMBER OF PROJECTS WHICH INCLUDE ESTABLISHING A WORKERS

COMPENSATION AND SOCIAL SECURITY PROGRAM; A REVIEW OF

MONITORING OF WORKER STANDARDS IN THE WORKPLACE;

OCCUPATIONAL HEALTH AND SAFETY; PROVIDING TECHNICAL

ASSISTANCE THROUGH TRAINING OF INSPECTORS; PROVIDING

TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE RELATING TO PROVIDING THE CONFEDERATION

OF LABOR TRAINING IN NEGOTIATING COLLECTIVE BARGAINING

AGREEMENTS; REVIEW OF OVERTIME PROCEDURES; AND A NUMBER OF

OTHER ISSUES NOT THE LEAST OF WHICH IS THE ILO PROJECT

TOGETHER WITH UNDP TO IMPLEMENT VIETNAM\'S LABOR LAW. THE ILO

NOW MAINTAINS AN OFFICE HERE WITH THREE EXPERTS. THIS

SUMMER THE ILO PLANS TO UPGRADE ITS PRESENCE TO THAT OF A

\'RESIDENT OFFICE\'.\'

2. MADAME CU THI HAU, CHAIRWOMAN OF VGCL MADE A TOUR OF

THE UNITED STATES MARCH 12-18. WITH ROBERT SCHIFFER SENIOR

ADVISOR TO THE AMBASSADOR SHE MET WITH BARBARA SHAILOR,

DIRECTOR OF THE INTERNATIONAL RELATIONS DEPARTMENT AND PHIL

FISHMAN, DEPUTY DIRECTOR OF THE INTERNATIONAL RELATIONS

DEPARTMENT OF THE AFL-CIO. SHE ALSO MET WITH CONGRESSMAN

GEORGE MILLER AND CONGRESSMAN SANDER LEVIN ON LABOR MATTERS.

SHE THEN WENT TO NEW YORK TO MEET WITH THE EXECUTIVE

COMMITTEE OF THE HOSPITAL WORKERS UNION 1199-SEIU, AFL-CIO.

IN THOSE MEETINGS AFL-CIO REPRESENTATIVES DISCUSSED THE

PROVISION OF TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE TO VIETNAM THROUGH THE

ILO.

3. IN APRIL, VIETNAM\'S MOLISA ORGANIZED A TWO DAY SEMINAR

ON CHILD LABOR \'ENTITLED NATIONAL TRIPARTITE SEMINAR ON ILO

CONVENTIONS 138 AND 182 ON CHILD LABOR. THE SEMINAR WHICH

DREW THE PARTICIPATION OF 30 DELEGATES FROM VARIOUS

GOVERNMENT OFFICES, ILO. AND UNICEF FORMED PART OF A PROJECT

ON ADDRESSING LABOR MANAGEMENT ISSUES IN ORDER TO FACILITATE

EFFECTIVE MANAGEMENT OF THE LABOR LAW IN VIETNAM. MOLISA

VICE MINISTER DR. LE DUY DONG WAS QUOTED BY JOURNALIST, \'THE

SEMINAR IS "THE FIRST STEP TOWARDS THE POSSIBILITY OF THE

RATIFICATION\'S OF THE CONVENTIONS BY VIETNAM." EMBASSY

SUBSEQUENTLY CONTACTED MOLISA FOR FURTHER DETAIL ON WHETHER

AND WHEN VIETNAM MIGHT RATIFY THE TWO CONVENTIONS. MOLISA

MUST MAKE A PROPOSAL TO THE PRESIDENT OF VIETNAM REGARDING

RATIFICATION. MOLISA IS STUDYING THE MERITS OF DONG SO.

HOWEVER, THEY INDICATED THAT THE SEMINAR WAS AN INDICATION

OF THE SERIOUSNESS WITH WHICH THEY ARE STUDYING THE ISSUES.

4. IN A SEPARATE MEETING MR. SCHIFFER MET WITH OWEN

HERRNSTADT VICE PRESIDENT FOR INTERNATIONAL AFFAIRS OF THE

MACHINISTS UNION AFL-CIO TO DISCUSS A JOINT AFL-CIO AND ILO

PROGRAM. HERRNSTADT WHO HAS HAD AN IMPORTANT ROLE ON THE

OPIC MONITORING TRIPS TO VIETNAM AND WORKS CLOSELY WITH THE

INTERNATIONAL OFFICE OF THE AFL-CIO STATED BECAUSE OF ISSUES

RELATING TO FREEDOM OF ASSOCIATION THE AFL-CIO WOULD SUPPORT

SOME TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE FOR VIETNAM BUT WOULD NOT WANT

THIS ASSISTANCE TO GO DIRECTLY THROUGH VGCL SINCE IT IS THE

ONLY RECOGNIZED UNION IN VIETNAM . HE IS AWARE THERE HAVE

BEEN MANY INDEPENDENT INFORMAL ASSOCIATIONS FORMING IN

VIETNAM RELATED TO REPRESENTING WORKERS BUT THAT THE

GOVERNMENT HAS NOT FORMALLY RECOGNIZED THESE ASSOCIATIONS

AS OFFICIAL LABOR ORGANIZATIONS..

5. THE US EMBASSY IS PRESENTLY WORKING WITH THE ILO ON A

PROPOSAL TO SEND TO THE AFL-CIO . THE AFL-CIO HAS

PARTICIPATED IN TWO OPIC TRIPS RELATING TO WORKER RIGHTS

ISSUES AND BOTH MR. FISHMAN AND MR. HERRNSTADT HAVE

SEPARATELY VISITED VIETNAM SINCE THEN TO REVIEW WORKER
