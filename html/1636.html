SUBJECT: ZIMBABWEAN DEFENSE MINISTER DISCUSSES PROSPECTS FOR

IMPOSITION OF STATE OF EMERGENCY

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL IRVING. REASONS: 1.5

(B) AND (D).

1. (C) ON APRIL 11, A ZIMBABWEAN EMPLOYEE OF THE EMBASSY MET ALONE

WITH MINISTER OF DEFENSE MOVEN MAHACHI, A LONGTIME CLOSE FRIEND OF

THE FAMILY, AT MAHACHI\'S REQUEST. IN THE MEETING IN THE MINISTER\'S

OFFICE, MAHACHI SOLICITED THE INFORMAL LEGAL ADVICE OF OUR EMPLOYEE

--A LAWYER BY TRAINING -- REGARDING THE MDC\'S PENDING COURT

CHALLENGE OF HIS VICTORY IN LAST JUNE\'S PARLIAMENTARY ELECTION (ONE

OF THE 37 SEATS UNDER CHALLENGE). THE EMPLOYEE DEMURRED, STATING

THAT SHE HAD NOT FOLLOWED THE DETAILS OF HIS CASE CLOSELY.

2. (C) OUR EMPLOYEE TOOK THE OPPORTUNITY TO ASK MAHACHI ABOUT

FOREIGN MINISTER MUDENGE\'S COMMENTS IN PARLIAMENT ON APRIL 4

THREATENING IMPOSITION OF A STATE OF EMERGENCY (REF A). MAHACHI

APPEARED UNEASY AT THE QUESTION BUT CONFIRMED IT IS ZANU-PF\'S

INTENTION TO DECLARE A STATE OF EMERGENCY "IF SANCTIONS ARE IMPOSED

ON THE ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT." HE EMPHASIZED THAT THE LAW PERMITS

SUCH AN ACTION. MAHACHI MENTIONED SPECIFICALLY THE GOVERNMENT\'S

CONCERN THAT PASSAGE OF THE ZIMBABWE DEMOCRACY AND ECONOMIC

RECOVERY ACT WOULD PREVENT THE WORLD BANK AND IMF FROM PROVIDING

ANY ASSISTANCE TO ZIMBABWE. IN SUCH A CIRCUMSTANCE, THE GOVERNMENT

WOULD HAVE NO OTHER CHOICE BUT TO DECLARE A STATE OF EMERGENCY, AS

THE NATION COULD NOT SURVIVE WITHOUT AID FROM THE IFIS. THE

MINISTER CRITICIZED THE IMF AND WORLD BANK FOR PROVIDING ASSISTANCE

TO RWANDA AND UGANDA -- WHICH HAD INVADED THE DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC

OF CONGO -- WHILE CUTTING OFF ZIMBABWE, WHICH HAD GOTTEN INVOLVED

IN THE CONFLICT TO DEFEND A NATION\'S SOVEREIGNTY. (COMMENT: THE

MINISTER\'S HOARY AND ERRONEOUS CORRELATION OF ZIMBABWE\'S DRC

INVOLVEMENT WITH SUSPENSION OF FUND FLOWS FROM THE IFIS REFLECTS

THE US VS. THEM ATTITUDE PREVALENT IN THE POLITBURO. IT IGNORES

THE HOST OF OTHER FACTORS, INCLUDING ARREARS AND TECHNICAL DEFAULT,

WRONG POLICIES, BAD MANAGEMENT AND INACTION THAT THE GOZ ITSELF IS

DIRECTLY RESPONSIBLE FOR. END COMMENT.)

3. (C) UNPROMPTED, THE DEFENSE MINISTER WENT ON TO EXPLAIN IN SOME

DETAIL THAT A STATE OF EMERGENCY WOULD GIVE THE GOVERNMENT THE

POWERS IT NEEDS TO REASSERT CONTROL OVER THE ECONOMY.

SPECIFICALLY, THE GOZ WOULD DIRECT THAT ALL OF THE COUNTRY\'S SCARCE

FOREIGN EXCHANGE BE TRANSFERRED TO THE RESERVE BANK OF ZIMBABWE.

IN ADDITION, LIMITED FUEL SUPPLIES WOULD BE STRICTLY RATIONED AND

ALL POLITICAL ACTIVITY PROHIBITED. ASKED ABOUT THE IMPACT OF A

STATE OF EMERGENCY ON THE TIMING OF THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION

EXPECTED IN THE FIRST QUARTER OF 2002, MAHACHI REPLIED THAT IT

WOULD HAVE TO BE POSTPONED UNTIL AN ENVIRONMENT CONDUCIVE TO

ZIMBABWEANS FREELY EXERCISING THEIR RIGHT TO VOTE WAS POSSIBLE. HE

SAID UNEQUIVOCALLY THAT AN EARLY ELECTION WOULD NOT BE HELD.

COMMENT

-------

4. (C) MOVEN MAHACHI IS A CLOSE PERSONAL FRIEND OF PRESIDENT

MUGABE AND A MEMBER OF THE RULING PARTY POLITBURO. HE, THEREFORE,

CAN SPEAK AUTHORITATIVELY ABOUT THE PRESIDENT\'S THINKING AND THAT

OF OTHER SENIOR ZANU-PF INSIDERS. IT IS CLEAR FROM HIS COMMENTS --

AND THOSE OFFOREIGN MINISTER MUDENGE -- THAT HE AND OTHERS WIT

INFLUENCE HAVE DONE SOME THINKING ABOUT THE POSIBILITY OF A STATE

OF EMERGENCY, INCLUDING THE LEGAL BASIS FOR SUCH ACTION, WHAT MIGHT

PROMPT IT, ND WHAT STEPS THE GOVERNMENT WOULD TAKE IF ONE WER

IMPOSED. IT IS LESS CERTAIN, HOWEVER, WHETHER THE GOVERNMENT IS

LOOKING FOR AN EXCUSE TO IMPOSE A STATE OF EMERGENCY -- BECAUSE IT

REALIZES THAT IT CANNOT WIN A FREE AND FAIR PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION -

- OR WHETHER THE OMINOUS REMARKS ARE A BLUFF INTENDED TO PRESSURE

MEMBERS OF THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY NOT TO IMPOSE PUNITIVE

MEASURES. ALTHOUGH AT THIS TIME WE ARE INCLINED MORE TOWARD THE

SECOND INTERPRETATION, THE FACT THAT MAHACHI WAS ABLE TO PROVIDE A

FAIRLY DETAILED DESCRIPTION OF WHAT ZIMBABWE WOULD LOOK LIKE UNDER

A STATE OF EMERGENCY STRONGLY INDICATES THAT THE GOVERNMENT HAS

EXAMINED AND DISCUSSED SUCH AN EVENTUALITY, AND IS OUT OF THE GATE

IN PREPARING FOR IT.

5. (C) MAHACHI\'S REMARKS ON THE GOVERNMENT\'S WISH TO ASSERT

GREATER CONTROL OVER THE ECONOMY REFLECT THE THINKING OF A

PARTICULARLY HARDLINE TENDENCY WITHIN THE PARTY AND, TO OUR

KNOWLEDGE, DOES NOT CONSTITUTE OFFICIAL POLICY. ON THE CONTRARY,

FINANCE MINISTER MAKONI ASSURED US ON APRIL 11 THAT HE WOULD LIKE

TO FIND WAYS TO STIMULATE REAL GROWTH AND BROADER PARTICIPATION IN

THE ECONOMY AND LURE FOREIGN INVESTMENT BACK TO ZIMBABWE.
