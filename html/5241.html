1. THE FOLLOWING REPORT IS INTENDED AS AN OVERVIEW OF

RECENT DEVELOPMENTS IN ITALYS COMMUNICATIONS SECTOR TO

HELP WASHINGTON AGENCIES PREPARE FOR MARCH 6-7, 2003

BILATERAL MEETINGS IN ROME.

2. SUMMARY. ITALY HAS ONE OF THE LARGEST AND MOST

DEVELOPED COMMUNICATIONS MOBILE AND FIXED LINE MARKETS

IN THE WORLD. THE GOVERNMENT HAS LIBERALIZED THE

COMMUNICATIONS MARKET, HAS IMPLEMENTED EU-RELATED

DIRECTIVES AND IS GENERALLY CONSIDERED AMONG THE MORE

OPEN WITHIN THE EU IN COMMUNICATIONS POLICY. RECENTLY,

HOWEVER, GOVERNMENT INTERVENTION IN THE MARKET HAS BEEN

INCREASING. ON THE REGULATORY SIDE, THE COMPLEX LAYERS

OF LEGISLATION (EU, NATIONAL AND REGIONAL) ARE CAUSING

PROBLEMS FOR COMMUNICATIONS COMPANIES.

3. ITALY HAS AWARDED 3G (UMTS) LICENSES, BUT COMMERCIAL

APPLICATIONS OF 3G OPERATIONS ARE NOT EXPECTED SOON.

INTERNET USE IS GROWING, BUT COMMERCIAL USE, BOTH B2B

AND B2C, IS NOT AT THE LEVEL OF OTHER EUROPEAN

ECONOMIES. ITALY HAS AN AMBITIOUS STRATEGY TO BRING

BROADBAND TO ALL PARTS OF THE GOVERNMENT BY THE END OF

2005. END SUMMARY.

A LIBERALIZED MARKET, BUT STILL WITH PROBLEMS

---------------------------------------------

4. OVER THE LAST DECADE, ITALY HAS LIBERALIZED ITS

COMMUNICATIONS POLICIES AND IMPLEMENTED RELATED EU

DIRECTIVES IN A PRO-MARKET MANNER. OUR SECTOR CONTACTS

MAINTAIN THAT AFTER THE UK, ITALY HAS THE MOST OPEN

COMMUNICATIONS MARKET IN THE EU. SOME NOTABLE SIGNS OF

PROGRESS: TARIFFS ARE MORE ALIGNED WITH COSTS, IN

PARTICULAR REGARDING LEASED LINES; INTERCONNECTION

CHARGES REMAIN BELOW EU AVERAGE; THERE IS AN ESTABLISHED

REGULATORY REGIME REGARDING LOCAL LOOP UNBUNDLING;

CARRIER PRE-SELECTION IS POPULAR AND WIDESPREAD; AND

NUMBER PORTABILITY, ALTHOUGH DELAYED FROM THE MAY 2002

TARGET, IS SOON TO OCCUR.

5. DESPITE THESE POSITIVE DEVELOPMENTS, PROBLEMS

CLEARLY REMAIN. MOBILE TERMINATION RATES, FIXED TO

MOBILE IN PARTICULAR, REMAIN AMONG THE HIGHEST IN EUROPE

WITH LITTLE TRANSPARENCY REGARDING COST STRUCTURE FOR

CONSUMERS. IN ADDITION, SOME NEW MARKET ENTRANTS ARE

CONCERNED WITH TELECOM ITALIAS (THE FORMER INCUMBENT)

DELAYS AND LACK OF TRANSPARENCY WITH PROCEDURES IN

ALLOWING INTERCONNECTION AND LOCAL LOOP ACCESS. RIGHTS

OF WAY REMAIN A BOTTLENECK TO DEVELOPMENT OF ALTERNATIVE

INFRASTRUCTURE, ALTHOUGH PROBLEMS SEEM TO BE PRIMARILY

AT THE LOCAL LEVEL.

AND NEW COMPLEXITIES EMERGE

---------------------------

6. ONE EMERGING ISSUE CONCERNS THE GROWING LEGAL

COMPLEXITY OF THE ITALIAN MARKET. WITH A 2001 LAW, THE

CENTRAL GOVERNMENT HAS DEVOLVED SEVERAL POWERS TO

ITALYS 20 ADMINISTRATIVE REGIONS. COMPANIES NOW HAVE TO

TAKE INTO ACCOUNT REGIONAL LAWS IN ADDITION TO EU

DIRECTIVES AND NATIONAL LEGISLATION. WHILE REGIONS DO

NOT HAVE AUTHORITY IN THE FIELD OF COMMUNICATIONS, THEY

DO LEGISLATE ON ENVIRONMENTAL ISSUES, WHICH OFTEN HAVE

AN IMPACT ON COMMUNICATIONS AND INFRASTRUCTURE

DEVELOPMENT. ONE EXAMPLE PROVIDED BY A U.S. COMPANY IS

THAT OF THE NEWLY IMPOSED REGIONAL LIMITS ON ELECTRONIC

MAGNETIC TRANSMISSION. MOST REGIONS ARE ADOPTING

STANDARDS THAT ARE SIGNIFICANTLY LOWER THAN THE EU

CEILING. MOREOVER, EACH REGION IMPOSES DIFFERENT

LIMITATIONS, MAKING IT DIFFICULT FOR COMPANIES TO

DEVELOP COUNTRY-WIDE STRATEGIES.
