SUBJECT: NEW TRADE MINISTER MOYO IS A BREATH OF FRESH AIR

REF: HARARE 3910

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR MCDONALD, REASON 1.5 (B), (D)

SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) NEW INDUSTRY AND INTERNATIONAL TRADE MINISTER

NKOSANA MOYO TOLD THE AMBASSADOR AUGUST 15 THAT PRESIDENT

MUGABE WAS DETERMINED TO SEIZE 3,000 FARMS AND HAD NO

INTENTION OF PROVIDING COMPENSATION. MOYO CLAIMED THAT

MOST CABINET MEMBERS WERE IN FAVOR OF A FAIR,

TRANSPARENT LAND REFORM EFFORT, AND ADMITTED THAT THE

GOVERNMENT DOES NOT HAVE THE RESOURCES TO PROVIDE

SIGNIFICANT ASSISTANCE TO RESETTLED FAMILIES. MOYO

SHARED THE AMBASSADOR\'S CONCERN ABOUT LAWLESSNESS IN THE

RURAL AREAS. THE AMBASSADOR STRESSED THAT SEIZURE OF

FARMS WITHOUT COMPENSATION IS ILLEGAL AND IS NEARLY

ELIMINATING ANY NEW FOREIGN INVESTMENT, AND SAID THE

GOVERNMENT\'S ACTIONS WOULD SPEAK LOUDER THAN ITS WORDS.

MOYO IS AN ACCOMPLISHED BUSINESSPERSON AND A CAPABLE

POLITICAL INDEPENDENT WHO INTENDS TO WORK TO REVERSE SOME

OF THE GOVERNMENT\'S RUINOUS POLICIES. HE IS AN

INEXPERIENCED POLITICIAN, HOWEVER, AND WE ARE SKEPTICAL

THAT MUGABE WILL GIVE HIM SUFFICIENT ROOM TO MANEUVER,

-----------------

MOYO\'S OBJECTIVES

-----------------

2. (C) AMBASSADOR MCDONALD ASKED RECENTLY-APPOINTED

MINISTER OF INDUSTRY AND INTERNATIONAL TRADE NKOSANA MOYO

ON AUGUST 16 WHAT HE HOPED TO ACCOMPLISH IN HIS NEW

POSITION. MOYO SAID HIS PRIMARY FOCUS WOULD BE TO HELP

THE ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT FORGE A MORE COOPERATIVE

RELATIONSHIP WITH THE PRIVATE SECTOR. HE ALSO PLANNED TO

TRY TO REPAIR SOME OF ZIMBABWE\'S BILATERAL AND

MULTILATERAL RELATIONSHIPS.

-----------

LAND REFORM

-----------

3. (C) MOYO THEN TURNED TO THE LAND QUESTION,

ACKNOWLEDGING THE PROBLEM OF LAWLESSNESS ON THE FARMS.

HE TERMED THE GOVERNMENT\'S PUBLIC RELATIONS EFFORT

"APPALLING," AND SAID IT MUST DO A BETTER JOB OF

EXPLAINING THE RATIONALE FOR ITS ACTIONS. PRESIDENT

MUGABE IS SERIOUS ABOUT SEIZING 3,000 FARMS AND WANTS ALL

OF THEM IDENTIFIED NOW AND RESETTLED AS SOON AS POSSIBLE.

MOYO EXPRESSED HIS VIEW THAT MANY OF THESE FARMS HAD LONG

BEEN IDLE, SO THEIR RESETTLEMENT WOULD NOT HAVE A SERIOUS

IMPACT ON PRODUCTIVITY.

4. (C) THE AMBASSADOR NOTED THAT THE GOVERNMENT DOES NOT

HAVE THE NECESSARY RESOURCES FOR AN EFFECTIVE

RESETTLEMENT EFFORT AND THAT SUCH AN EFFORT WOULD

SUBSTANTIALLY HURT AGRICULTURAL PRODUCTION. THE FARM

OCCUPATIONS, HE STRESSED, ARE ILLEGAL, HAVE BEEN TERRIBLY

DISRUPTIVE, AND GIVE ZIMBABWE A BLACK EYE ABROAD. THE

UNITED STATES AGREES THAT LAND REFORM IS NECESSARY AND A

MATTER OF SOCIAL JUSTICE, BUT IT SHOULD BE DONE IN A

FAIR, TRANSPARENT PROCESS. IN ADDITION, SEIZURES OF

FARMS WITHOUT COMPENSATION WILL SOUR RENEWED DONOR

SUPPORT FOR LAND REFORM. IT IS CRITICAL THAT THE

GOVERNMENT ADDRESS THIS AND ITS OTHER ECONOMIC PROBLEMS

SERIOUSLY, AS MORE AND MORE INDUSTRIES, INCLUDING MINING,

ARE SUFFERING, AND THE PATIENCE OF THE INTERNATIONAL

COMMUNITY IS WEARING THIN.

5. (C) MOYO ACKNOWLEDGED THAT THE GOVERNMENT WOULD BE

UNABLE TO PROVIDE RESETTLED FAMILIES WITH SIGNIFICANT

RESOURCES, BUT THOUGHT MANY WERE PEASANT FARMERS WHO

WOULD BE ABLE TO LOOK AFTER THEMSELVES. HE AGREED THAT

REVERSE THE GOVERNMENT\'S DETERMINATION NOT TO PAY FOR THE

SEIZED FARMS. THE GOVERNMENT, MOYO ADMITTED, HAS DUG

NEW PARLIAMENT AND MOYO\'S ROLE IN CABINET

-----------------------------------------

7. (C) ASKED FOR HIS ASSESSMENT OF THE NEW PARLIAMENT,

MOYO SAID BOTH THE RULING ZANU-PF AND THE MDC ARE SHOWING

"INCREDIBLE IMMATURITY," WHICH WOULD PROBABLY PREVENT HIM

FROM JOINING A POLITICAL PARTY IN THE NEAR FUTURE. THE

AMBASSADOR ASKED WHETHER MOYO FELT UNCOMFORTABLE AS A

MEMBER OF THE CABINET, GIVEN HIS REPUTATION AS A

POLITICAL INDEPENDENT (REFTEL). ON THE CONTRARY, MOYO

REPLIED. HE FELT INCREDIBLE FREEDOM AND WAS NOW

CONVINCED THAT NOT JOINING A POLITICAL PARTY WAS THE BEST

THING HE HAD EVER DONE. HE HAD NOT BEEN REQUIRED TO JOIN

ZANU-PF AND, IN FACT, HAD GIVEN A SPEECH ON ZIMBABWEAN

TELEVISION AFTER HIS APPOINTMENT MAKING IT CLEAR THAT HE

WAS NOT A MEMBER OF THE RULING PARTY AND EXPRESSING HIS

DETERMINATION TO BE A MINISTER FOR THE COUNTRY AS A

WHOLE. MANY PEOPLE SUBSEQUENTLY HAD ASKED HIM WHY HE HAD

JOINED THE CABINET. HE TOLD THEM HE CONSIDERED IT HIS

OBLIGATION TO DO ALL HE COULD TO PREVENT THE ZIMBABWEAN

SHIP FROM SINKING.

----------------------

FINANCIAL DIFFICULTIES

----------------------

8. (C) ASKED HOW THE GOVERNMENT PLANNED TO ADDRESS ITS

SERIOUS ECONOMIC DIFFICULTIES, MOYO SAID HE IS WORKING

CLOSELY WITH FINANCE MINISTER SIMBA MAKONI ON NEXT STEPS.

IN THE NEAR TERM, THE GOVERNMENT HAD TALKED TO THE DUTCH

ABOUT ACQUIRING ASSISTANCE TARGETTED AT RESETTLEMENT OF A

LIMITED NUMBER OF FARMS, AS WELL AS THE FUEL AND

ELECTRICITY SECTORS. IN ADDITION, THE CABINET HAD

APPROVED THE MILLENIUM ECONOMIC RECOVERY PLAN THE

PREVIOUS WEEK. THE PLAN SEEKS, INTER ALIA, TO REDUCE THE

BUDGET DEFICIT FROM 14 TO 10 PERCENT, ESTABLISH A

REALISTIC EXCHANGE RATE, DOWNSIZE THE BLOATED CIVIL

SERVICE, AND RE-ESTABLISH RELATIONS WITH THE IFI\'S.

9. (C) THE ECONOMIC RECOVERY PLAN, MOYO CONTINUED, ALSO

STRESSED THE NEED TO BETTER "MANAGE" THE ISSUE OF TROOPS

IN THE DROC. HE ASKED WHY UN TROOPS HAD NOT YET BEEN

DEPLOYED THERE. MONUC DEPLOYMENT HAD NOT BEEN EXPANDED,

THE AMBASSADOR EXPLAINED, BECAUSE SUFFICIENT SAFETY

GUARANTEES WERE NOT IN PLACE. BEFORE SUCH A DEPLOYMENT

COULD OCCUR, KABILA HAD TO MEET HIS LUSAKA ACCORDS

COMMITMENTS, AND ZIMBABWE COULD PLAY A POSITIVE ROLE IN

PRESSING KABILA TO DO SO, THE AMBASSADOR POINTED OUT.

COMMENT

-------

11. (C) MOYO IS A WELL-REGARDED, CAPABLE FIGURE, WHOSE

APPOINTMENT TO THE CABINET WAS A SURPRISE, GIVEN HIS

REPUTATION AS A POLITICAL INDEPENDENT. A WELL-TO-DO

INVESTMENT BANKER AND CLOSE CONTACT OF THE EMBASSY, MOYO

APPEARS MOTIVATED BY A GENUINE DESIRE TO SERVE HIS

COUNTRY AND REVERSE THE GOVERNMENT\'S DISASTROUS POLICIES.

HE IS NOT AN EXPERIENCED POLITICIAN, HOWEVER, WHICH COULD

HINDER HIS EFFECTIVENESS, PARTICULARLY IN THE CURRENT

TOUGH POLITICAL ENVIRONMENT. IN THE ONE-ON-ONE AFTER THE

FORMAL MEETING, MOYO TOLD THE AMBASSADOR HE HAD NOT BEEN

CONSULTED ABOUT THE CABINET POSITION, BUT HAD LEARNED OF

HIS APPOINTMENT ON TELEVISION. ALTHOUGH MOYO BELIEVES HE

HAS THE PRESIDENT\'S CONFIDENCE, WE ARE SKEPTICAL THAT

MUGABE WILL GIVE HIM THE NECESSARY MANEUVERING ROOM TO

EFFECT A SIGNIFICANT CHANGE EITHER IN THE GOVERNMENT\'S

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 04611 03 OF 03 180009Z

HISTORICAL MISHANDLING OF THE ECONOMY OR ITS APPROACH ON

LAND. HE HAS BEEN OUTSPOKEN, HOWEVER. THE DAY AFTER HIS

MEETING WITH THE AMBASSADOR, MOYO REPORTEDLY TOLD THE

CONFEDERATION OF ZIMBABWE INDUSTRIES THAT THE LAWLESSNESS

WAS DAMAGING ZIMBABWE\'S INTERNATIONAL IMAGE AND THAT

FOREIGN INVESTMENT WAS UNLIKELY TO BE FORTHCOMING IN THE

CURRENT ENVIRONMENT. WE DO WANT TO HELP MOYO AND WILL

LOOK FOR WAYS TO DO SO.
