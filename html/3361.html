SUBJECT: NE ITALY\'S TRANSPORT INFRASTRUCTURE:

IMPRESSIVELY INTERMODAL

REFS: A) ROME 02912 B) 01 ROME 04158

1. SUMMARY: THE NORTHEASTERN ITALIAN TRANSPORTATION

CENTERS IN VENICE AND TRIESTE HAVE RELIED PRIMARILY ON

INCREASED INTERMODALITY AND INFRASTRUCTURE INVESTMENT

FOR ECONOMIC GROWTH. THE PORT OF TRIESTE, IN

PARTICULAR, HAS STRONG LINKS TO THE ITALIAN RAILWAYS AND

IS AGGRESSIVELY SEEKING INTERNATIONAL PARTNERSHIPS TO

BEAT ITS COMPETITION IN NORTHERN EUROPE. WHILE SECURITY

AT ALL SITES VISITED REMAINS A CONCERN, OFFICIALS

REPORTED THAT EVERY PRECAUTION IS BEING TAKEN TO PROTECT

TRANSPORTATION INFRASTRUCTURE. WE SEE GROWING MARKET

OPPORTUNITIES FOR U.S. FIRMS, INCLUDING THOSE RELATED TO

INCREASING THE EFFICIENCY OF INTERMODAL TRANSPORTATION

LINKS AND ENHANCING SECURITY THROUGH INFORMATION

TECHNOLOGY (IT). END SUMMARY.

----------

BACKGROUND

----------

2. AS PART OF AN ONGOING EFFORT TO GATHER INFORMATION ON

ITALY\'S TRANSPORTATION INFRASTRUCTURE, ECONOFF VISITED

MAJOR NORTHEAST ITALY TRANSPORTATION HUBS IN VENICE AND

TRIESTE. TO DETERMINE THE LEVEL OF INTERMODALITY IN THE

AREA, WE MET WITH OFFICIALS AT THE VENICE AIRPORT, THE

TRIESTE AIRPORT, THE TRIESTE RAILWAY STATION, AND THE

PORT OF TRIESTE.

3. WE FOUND THAT LEVELS OF INTERMODALITY VARIED GREATLY.

WHILE THE PORT OF TRIESTE (WITH EXCELLENT LINKS TO RAIL

AND ROAD LINES) IS A VIRTUAL CASE STUDY IN

INTERMODALITY, THE TRIESTE AIRPORT COULD NOT BE MORE

ISOLATED. THE VENICE AIRPORT (WITH A NEWLY INAUGURATED

AIR TERMINAL) IS WELL SUITED TO HANDLE THE MANY TOURISTS

IT RECEIVES AND AIRPORT AUTHORITIES ARE HOPING TO

ENHANCE ITS LEVEL OF SERVICE FOR THE BUSINESS COMMUNITY.

WHILE ALMOST NINE MONTHS HAD PAST BETWEEN THE SEPTEMBER

11 TERRORIST ATTACKS AND OUR TRIP, REASSURANCES OF

ENHANCED SECURITY (AND CONDOLENCES) WERE OFFERED IN MANY

OF OUR CONVERSATIONS.

------------

THE AIRPORTS

------------

THE VENICE AIRPORT

------------------

4. AIRPORT DIRECTOR VALERIO BONATO (RESPONSIBLE FOR BOTH

VENICE AND TRIESTE AIRPORTS) HOSTED A ROUND-TABLE

MEETING WITH ROBERTO COIN, DIRECTOR OF SAVE S.P.A. (THE

VENICE AIRPORT HANDLING COMPANY); SALVATORE FORTE,

AIRPORT SECURITY MANAGER; PAOLO FURLAN, POLICE CHIEF OF

THE AIRPORT AND PORT OF VENICE; AND DOMENICO MARIA

TEDESCHI OF THE CUSTOMS AGENCY. MUCH OF THE

CONVERSATION FOCUSED ON THE OVER 250 MILLION DOLLARS IN

INVESTMENTS SINCE 1996 TO IMPROVE THE AIRPORT\'S

INFRASTRUCTURE.

5. LOCATED ON A STRIP OF LAND BETWEEN THE PO RIVER AND

THE VENICE LAGOON, VENICE\'S MARCO POLO AIRPORT HANDLED

APPROXIMATELY 4.4 MILLION PASSENGERS IN 2001, UP FROM

THREE MILLION IN 1997. THE VAST MAJORITY OF ARRIVING

PASSENGERS VISIT VENICE FOR TOURISM, WITH ABOUT TWO

MILLION ALSO EMBARKING ON CRUISES ORIGINATING IN THE

CITY (SIXTY-FIVE PERCENT OF THE AIRPORTS SCHEDULED

TRAFFIC IS INTERNATIONAL). (NOTE: VENICE IS ALSO THE

FIRST ITALIAN REGIONAL AIRPORT WHERE A U.S. COMPANY

(DELTA) IS OPERATING, WITH DAILY FLIGHTS TO NEW YORK IN

THE SUMMER SEASON AND FIVE WEEKLY FLIGHTS IN THE WINTER

SEASON. THE COMMERCIAL MANAGER FOR DELTA TOLD US THAT,

IN CONTRAST TO MILAN\'S TROUBLED MALPENSA AIRPORT,

SERVICES PROVIDED BY MARCO POLO AIRPORT ARE EXCELLENT.

END NOTE.) BONATO POINTED OUT THAT THE AIRPORTS\' TWO

RUNWAYS SERVE THE OVER HALF MILLION COMPANIES IN THE

AREA. TO BETTER SERVE THE BUSINESS COMMUNITY,

AUTHORITIES INSTALLED A NEW CARGO AREA IN 2001, WHICH

INCLUDES A 6,000 SQUARE METER WAREHOUSE. OFFICIALS HOPE

THIS WILL ACCOMMODATE FUTURE CARGO, SIMILAR TO THE OVER

23,000 TONS OF CARGO TRANSITING THE AIRPORT IN 2001 (UP

FROM ONLY 13,000 IN 1996).

42 MILLION EURO CONSTRUCTION OF A NEW CONTROL TOWER (THE

OLD TERMINAL WILL STILL BE USED, BUT SOLELY FOR CHARTER

AIRPORT IS WELL CONNECTED VIA FERRIES AND BUSES TO THE

CITY, ITS TWO RAILWAY STATIONS (MESTRE AND THE MORE-

CENTRALIZED SANTA LUCIA STATION), AND OTHER AREA TOWNS.

THE NEW TERMINAL, ABOUT 300 YARDS AWAY FROM THE OLD ONE,

IS CONNECTED BY BUS TO THE OLD STATION AND SOON WILL

ALSO BE CONNECTED BY A MECHANIZED PEDESTRIAN WALKWAY.

8. POLICE CHIEF FURLAN EXPLAINED THAT ALL INVOLVED IN

THE AIRPORT\'S SECURITY MEET MONTHLY TO COORDINATE THEIR

EFFORTS AND TO DECIDE UPON NEW SECURITY PROCEDURES. ONE

IDEA HE IS HOPING TO IMPLEMENT IS THE PHOTOGRAPHING OF

PILOTS AND CREWS THE NIGHT BEFORE A FLIGHT. THE PHOTOS

WILL THEN BE MATCHED AGAINST CREWS BEFORE BOARDING AN

AIRCRAFT. FURLAN STATED THAT ALMOST ALL PILOTS WITH

WHOM HE HAS SPOKEN APPROVE OF THIS PROCEDURE.

THE TRIESTE AIRPORT

-------------------

9. IN CONTRAST TO VENICE\'S AIRPORT WHICH IS LARGE,

GROWING, AND WELL CONNECTED TO OTHER FORMS OF TRANSPORT,

TRIESTE\'S RONCHI DEI LEGIONARI AIRPORT (ABOUT 145

KILOMETERS AWAY) LIES ABOUT THIRTY-FIVE KILOMETERS FROM

THE MUCH SMALLER CITY\'S LIMITS AND THEREFORE IS LESS

PASSENGER-FRIENDLY (AND LESS UTILIZED). DEPUTY AIRPORT

DIRECTOR ENRICO MARTUCCI STATED THAT, WHILE THE AIRPORT

WAS BUILT TO HANDLE AN ANNUAL CAPACITY OF TWO MILLION

PASSENGERS, LESS THAN ONE MILLION ACTUALLY TRANSIT THE

AIRPORT. WHILE BUS LINES DO CONNECT THE AIRPORT TO THE

CITY, FREQUENT TRAFFIC PROBLEMS OFTEN MAKE THE TRIP AN

HOUR LONG. NO TRAINS RUN FROM TRIESTE RAIL STATION TO

THE AIRPORT. AIRPORT HANDLING COMPANY DIRECTOR PAOLO

STRADI ASSURED US THAT NEW SECURITY EQUIPMENT WOULD SOON

BE PURCHASED IN ORDER TO MEET THE EU-IMPOSED DEADLINE

FOR ONE HUNDRED PERCENT CHECKED BAGGAGE SCREENING.

THE PORTS

---------

THE PORT OF VENICE

------------------

10. VENICE AIRPORT POLICE CHIEF FURLAN IS ALSO IN CHARGE

OF SECURITY AT VENICE\'S HARBOR. HE STATED THAT ILLEGAL

STOWAWAYS ARE OFTEN FOUND HIDDEN IN CONTAINERS COMING

FROM ABROAD. HE ALLEGED THAT, IN ORDER TO BETTER DETECT

THESE INTENDING IMMIGRANTS, A NEW INSTRUMENT THAT PICKS-

UP A STOWAWAY\'S HEARTBEAT INSIDE A CONTAINER IS IN USE.

THE PORT OF TRIESTE -- GATEWAY TO CENTRAL AND EASTERN

EUROPE

--------------------------------------------- --------

11. MAURIZIO MARESCA, PRESIDENT OF THE TRIESTE PORT

AUTHORITY, AND HIS STAFF EXPLAINED HOW THE PORT OF

TRIESTE CONSIDERS ITS HOME NOT ONLY ITALY, BUT ALSO THE

WHOLE OF CENTRAL EUROPE. THE ORIGIN OR FINAL

DESTINATION OF MOST OF THE GOODS PASSING THROUGH THE

PORT LIE IN NORTHERN ITALY, SWITZERLAND, AUSTRIA,

GERMANY, THE CZECH REPUBLIC, SLOVAKIA, SLOVENIA,

HUNGARY, AND THE BALKANS. SHIPPING LINES SAILING

THROUGH THE PORT OF TRIESTE MAKE THEIR WAY TO PORTS ALL

OVER AFRICA, ASIA, AND THE AMERICAS (NOTE: THERE ARE NO

DIRECT ROUTES FROM TRIESTE TO THE U.S. AS ALL SHIPS

DESTINED TO THE US MAKE STOPS AT OTHER PORTS PRIOR TO

ARRIVAL. END NOTE).

12. THE PORT OF TRIESTE HAS OVER TWO MILLION SQUARE

METERS OF PORT AREA, 1.7 MILLION OF WHICH ARE FREE ZONES

(925,000 OF WHICH ARE WAREHOUSES AND STORAGE AREAS). IT

HAS FORTY-SEVEN OPERATIONAL BERTHS AND TWENTY-SIX

- GOODS ARE EXEMPT FROM DUTY AS LONG AS THEY REMAIN IN

- GOODS CAN BE STORED WITHIN FREE PORT ZONES WITHOUT

TIME LIMIT,

- ACTIVITIES SUCH AS PACKING AND LABELING CAN BE FREELY

CARRIED OUT ON FOREIGN GOODS,

- FOR GOODS IMPORTED INTO THE EU THROUGH THE FREE PORT,

PAYMENT OF RELEVANT CUSTOMS DUTIES AND FISCAL BORDER

DUTIES CAN BE DEFERRED BY UP TO SIX MONTHS AT A LOW

INTEREST RATE, AND

BENEFIT FROM FACILITATED TRANSIT ACROSS THE BORDERS WITH

AUSTRIA AND SLOVENIA.

13. MARESCA EXPLAINED THAT HIS BUSINESS VISION FOR THE

PORT INCLUDES GREAT ECONOMIC GROWTH THROUGH NEW AND

EXPANDED SEA-BORN TRAFFIC FLOWS, DUE TO ECONOMIC GROWTH

PAGE 03 ROME 03641 03 OF 04 270635Z

IN EASTERN EUROPE, AND AN ACCOMPANYING LACK OF DIRECT

ACCESS TO THE MEDITERRANEAN FOR COUNTRIES LIKE AUSTRIA,

SWITZERLAND, AND GERMANY. THE PORT\'S COMPETITION, HE

EXPLAINED, DOES NOT COME FROM OTHER PORTS IN ITALY, BUT

RATHER FROM NORTHERN EUROPEAN PORTS LIKE HAMBURG,

GERMANY. HE NOTED WITH PRIDE THAT TRIESTE IS 7,760

NAUTICAL MILES FROM HONG KONG WHILE THE PORT OF HAMBURG

IS ALMOST 10,000 NAUTICAL MILES DISTANT.

14. TO FULFILL THIS VISION, THE PORT AUTHORITY HAS

(APPARENTLY EFFECTIVELY) ESTABLISHED A FULLY INTERMODAL

STRATEGY TO COORDINATE ROAD, RAIL, AND MARITIME

TRANSPORT. LOCATED IN NORTHEAST ITALY, TRIESTE IS

WITHIN 450 KILOMETERS OF IMPORTANT URBAN CENTERS LIKE

VIENNA, BUDAPEST, PRAGUE, MUNICH, STUTTGART, ZURICH, AND

MILAN. INLAND CONNECTIONS TO ALL OF THESE LOCATIONS

INCLUDE NUMEROUS TUNNELS THROUGH THE ALPS (THE BRENNER,

TARVISIO, AND FERNETTI PASSES). TRIESTE\'S PORT IS THE

LARGEST RAIL-PORT IN SOUTHERN EUROPE, WITH SEVENTY-FIVE

KILOMETERS OF TRACK WITHIN THE PORT ITSELF (SERVING ALL

OF ITS DOCKS) AND LINKED TO THE EUROPEAN RAIL STATION

THROUGH THE AREAS RAIL STATIONS. THE CERVIGNANO

INTERMODAL CENTER (A FEW KILOMETERS OUTSIDE OF TRIESTE)

FACILITATES THE MOVEMENT OF GOODS BETWEEN RAILCARS AND

TRUCKS, AND IN TURN THE MOVEMENT OF THE PORT\'S CARGO.

IN ANOTHER STRATEGIC MOVE, PORT OFFICIALS HAVE ENTERED

INTO AN AGREEMENT WITH A RAILWAY TRANSPORT COMPANY IN

ORDER TO ASSURE THE TRANSPORT OF GOODS TO MUNICH.

15. PORT AUTHORITY OFFICIALS RECOGNIZE THAT THEIR

GREATEST CHALLENGES INCLUDE A LACK OF PHYSICAL

OVERCOME THESE SHORTCOMINGS, MARESCA IS FOCUSING ON THE

POSSIBILITY OF INTEGRATING WITH OTHER NORTHERN ADRIATIC

PORTS, IN PARTICULAR WITH THE PORT OF KOPER, SLOVENIA.

THIS INITIATIVE WOULD REQUIRE INCREASED TRANSPORTATION

INFRASTRUCTURE (TO INCLUDE ROAD AND RAIL) ORDER TO

FACILITATE TRAFFIC FLOWS BETWEEN THE TWO PORTS AND TO

ACTIONS FAVORABLE TO THE PORT LIKE THE CREATION OF

INTERMODAL LOGISTICS FACILITIES ALONG THE ROUTES TO

AUSTRIA AND GERMANY AND A HIGH-SPEED RAIL LINE TO

LJUBLJANA, BUDAPEST, AND KIEV. HE IS ALSO SEEKING A

REDUCTION IN RAIL TARIFFS FOR TRANSPORT ALONG ROUTES TO

MUNICH AND VIENNA TO LEVELS ON PAR WITH THOSE APPLIED BY

GERMAN RAILWAYS TO THE PORT OF HAMBURG. MARESCA HOPES

THIS WILL BE ACHIEVED BY SETTING UP A PRIVATE RAIL

COMPANY WHOSE SHAREHOLDERS WOULD INCLUDE AUSTRIAN,

SLOVENIAN, AND ITALIAN RAILWAYS AND LOCAL BUSINESSES.

COMMENT

-------

17. ENLIGHTENED MANAGEMENT AT THE PORT OF TRIESTE AND

THE VENICE AIRPORT, THROUGH GOOD STRATEGIC PLANNING AND

AMBITIOUS INVESTMENT, HAS WELL POSITIONED BOTH LOCATIONS

FOR ECONOMIC GROWTH. WE CAN EXPECT TO SEE THE PORT OF

TRIESTE (IN PARTICULAR) BECOMING EVEN MORE OF A PLAYER

ON THE EUROPEAN TRANSPORTATION SCENE, ESPECIALLY IF IT

IMPLEMENTS ITS STRATEGY OF FORMING EVER-INCREASING

INTERMODAL AND CROSS-BORDER NETWORKS. AS WE HAVE

REPORTED, OUR TRIESTE CONTACTS CONCUR THAT THERE ARE

MARKET OPPORTUNITIES FOR U.S. FIRMS IN THE CARGO

TRANSPORT INDUSTRY, ESPECIALLY THAT OF RAIL CARGO

(REFTELS). BOTH TRIESTE AND VENICE OFFICIALS ALSO CITE

A NEED FOR IMPROVED TRANSPORTATION-RELATED SECURITY AND

LOGISTICS CONTROLLING INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (IT)

SYSTEMS. THESE TOO ARE OPPORTUNITIES FOR U.S.

SUPPLIERS.

18. WHILE THE IMPACT OF SEPTEMBER 11 STILL WEIGHS HEAVY

ON MANY ITALIAN HEARTS, THE INITIAL SHOCK TO THE

TRANSPORTATION SECTOR SEEMS TO HAVE WORN OFF. ALL

SECURITY OFFICIALS WITH WHOM WE SPOKE HAVE TAKEN (OR ARE

TAKING) ALL MANDATED OR OTHERWISE NECESSARY SECURITY

MEASURES TO PROTECT THEIR INFRASTRUCTURE. THE SENSE OF

GREAT CONCERN THAT WE SAW THROUGH EARLY 2002 HAS GIVEN

WAY TO A COMMITMENT TO IMPROVE SECURITY.
