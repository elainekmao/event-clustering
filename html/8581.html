SUBJECT: EMBASSY DELIVERS DRAFT FINANCIAL AGREEMENT TO

TREASURY

REF: STATE 154013

1. (U) This is an action request -- see para 4.

2. (SBU) EconCouns delivered the draft financial agreement

and disbursement request document to Treasury Deputy U/S

Aydin Karaoz June 9, highlighting the agreement\'s economic

and political conditions. Karaoz said he or Deputy Director

General Melih Nemli would probably lead the Turkish team to

Washington for discussions, and promised to get back to us

shortly with proposed dates.

3. (SBU) Karaoz said Treasury had not planned to hire a U.S.

private counsel, but could do so if the U.S. considered it

essential. EconCouns responded that, per reftel, Turkey

would need to provide an opinion of counsel from a U.S.

private firm, as well as from government counsel. At Karaoz\'s

request, he offered to query Washington on whether (a) the

USG considered the presence of a U.S. private counsel in the

discussions essential (beyond providing the opinion noted

above), and (b) U.S. Treasury would also be represented by

private counsel.

4. (SBU) Action Request: Embassy requests clarification on

the role/necessity of private counsel in these discussions.
