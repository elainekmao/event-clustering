SUBJECT: IMPROVING AND OBSERVING THE ELECTION PROCESS

IN ZIMBABWE

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR JOSEPH G. SULLIVAN FOR REASONS

1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE AND THE RULING ZANU-PF

UTILIZED VIOLENCE, INTIMIDATION AND MANIPULATION IN LAST

YEAR\'S PARLIAMENTARY ELECTION, BUT STILL LOST ALMOST

HALF THE VOTE TO A STRONG MDC OPPOSITION CAMPAIGN. THAT

ELECTION WAS MONITORED BY A STRONG GROUP OF ZIMBABWEAN

MONITORS, LED BY THE ZIMBABWEAN ELECTORAL SUPPORT

NETWORK AND OBSERVED BY MANY INTERNATIONAL OBSERVERS,

INCLUDING THE EU, THE COMMONWEALTH AND THE SADC

PARLIAMENTARY FORUM AMONG OTHERS WHOSE SUBSTANTIAL

PRESENCE LIKELY HELPED REDUCE VIOLENCE AND INTIMIDATION

IMMEDIATELY PRIOR TO ELECTIONS.

2. (C) AS WE APPROACH PRESIDENTIAL ELECTIONS TO BE HELD

IN THE FIRST QUARTER OF 2002, THE COUNTRY\'S ECONOMIC

SITUATION HAS MARKEDLY DECLINED AND THE ZANU-PF

GOVERNMENT IS ALREADY UTILIZING VIOLENCE AND

INTIMIDATION AGAINST OPPOSITION SUPPORTERS, PARTICULARLY

IN RURAL AREAS, AND UTILIZING THE FAST TRACK LAND

DISTRIBUTION NOT ONLY AS A POLITICAL RALLYING CRY, BUT

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 03303 01 OF 04 051417Z

ALSO TO DISTRIBUTE ITS SUPPORTERS AND "TOUGHS"

THROUGHOUT THE RURAL AREAS AND TO DISLOCATE THE

AGRICULTURAL WORKERS WHO ARE ASSUMED TO BE OPPOSITION

SUPPORTERS. AT THE SAME TIME, THE GOVERNMENT OF

ZIMBABWE HAS REBUFFED EU AND US (INTERNATIONAL

FOUNDATION FOR ELECTION SYSTEMS - IFES) EFFORTS TO SEND

PRE-ELECTORAL ASSESSMENT TEAMS, CLAIMING THAT THE EU

REQUEST WAS AN UNACCEPTABLE ULTIMATUM AND INTERVENTION

IN SOVEREIGN AFFAIRS AND THAT THE IFES VISIT WAS NOT

PROPERLY REQUESTED. THE GOVERNMENT CLAIMS THAT IT HAS

ALWAYS WELCOMED OBSERVERS AND WILL AGAIN, BUT THAT IT IS

TOO EARLY AND THE GOVERNMENT WILL DECIDE WHOM TO INVITE.

PRESIDENT MUGABE RECENTLY TOLD ABUJA AGREEMENT VISITORS

THAT OBSERVERS FROM SADC, THE AFRICAN UNION AND COMESA

(WHICH DOES NOT EVEN DO ELECTION OBSERVATION) WOULD BE

WELCOMED, BUT REFUSED TO COMMIT TO INVITING COMMONWEALTH

OBSERVERS. IN SUM, THE GOVERNMENT HAS DONE EVERYTHING

POSSIBLE TO DELAY ELECTION OBSERVATION AS LONG AS

POSSIBLE AND TO MINIMIZE THE NUMBER OF INTERNATIONAL

OBSERVERS. THE GOVERNMENT SAYS IT WILL ALSO PERMIT

DOMESTIC ELECTION MONITORS, BUT IS SEEKING TO MAINTAIN

AS MUCH CONTROL AS POSSIBLE OVER THE ELECTORAL

MECHANISMS AND PROCEDURES.

3. (C) THE GOVERNMENT DOMINATES THE INSTRUMENTS OF

REPRESSION AND HAS BEEN UTILIZING SUCH OFFICIAL AND

UNOFFICIAL INSTRUMENTS TO INTIMIDATE OPPOSITION

SUPPORTERS. THE GOVERNMENT TRADITIONALLY HAS SPENT AND

WILL AGAIN, WITHIN THE LIMITS OF THE COUNTRY\'S RESOURCE

CONSTRAINTS BOTH STATE AND PARTY RESOURCES TO ADVANCE

ZANU-PF INTERESTS. THE GOVERNMENT ALSO CONTROLS BY

CONFIDENTIAL

CONSTITUTIONAL PROVISION THE REGISTRAR GENERAL, THE

ELECTIONS DIRECTORATE AND DELIMITATION COMMITTEES. THE

MEMBERS OF THE ELECTORAL SUPERVISORY COMMISSION, WHICH

OVERSEES THE REGISTRATION OF VOTERS AND THE CONDUCT OF

ELECTIONS ARE ALL APPOINTED BY THE PRESIDENT, ALTHOUGH

SOME ARE INDEPENDENT INDIVIDUALS. THESE BODIES HAVE IN

PAST ELECTIONS ADJUSTED RULES AND VOTING DISTRICTS AT

THE MARGINS AND TO ASSURE THAT MOST ELECTORAL DISPUTES

CAME OUT IN ZANU-PF\'S FAVOR. THE ABOVE FACTORS

NOTWITHSTANDING, ELECTION DAYS THEMSELVES HAVE GENERALLY

BEEN CALM AND THE VOTING PROCESS ITSELF HAS FUNCTIONED

RELATIVELY SMOOTHLY, ALBEIT WITH SOME CHEATING AT THE

EDGES.

4. (C) DESPITE ALL THE ABOVE ADVANTAGES, PRESIDENT

MUGABE AND ZANU-PF SEEK THE LEGITIMACY THAT WOULD BE

CONFERRED BY AN ELECTION THAT IS ACCEPTED

INTERNATIONALLY AND DOMESTICALLY AS REASONABLY FREE,

FAIR AND CREDIBLE AND THEY RECOGNIZE THAT AN ELECTION

WITHOUT A CREDIBLE OPPOSITION OR WITH MASSIVE FRAUD AND

VIOLENCE WILL NOT SERVE THEIR PURPOSE. AS A RESULT, THE

OPPOSITION, CIVIL SOCIETY AND THE INTERNATIONAL

COMMUNITY HAVE SOME LEVERAGE, IF ACTION IS TAKEN IN A

TIMELY AND CONCERTED FASHION. IT WOULD BE EASY FOR THE

US OR IFES TO DECLARE UNILATERALLY THAT THE DATE HAS

ALREADY PASSED OR WILL IMMINENTLY PASS WHEN EFFECTIVE

AND EARLY ELECTION OBSERVATION CAN BE ACCOMPLISHED AND

5. (C) ACCORDINGLY, WE PROPOSE THAT THE US CONSULT WITH

THE EU, KEY COMMONWEALTH AND ABUJA PARTNERS AND KEY SADC

COUNTRIES TO ADVANCE THE IDEA THAT IT IS IMPORTANT FOR

US TO LET THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE KNOW SOON,

POLITELY, BUT FIRMLY, WHAT WE NEED TO SEE IN ORDER TO

REGARD THE UPCOMING ELECTIONS AS FREE AND FAIR AND THAT

AN ELECTION WHICH FALLS SHORT OF SUCH STANDARDS WOULD

NOT BE REGARDED AS LEGITIMATE. WE PROPOSE USING THE

SADC PARLIAMENTARY FORUM NORMS AND STANDARDS FOR

ELECTIONS IN THE SADC REGION, WHICH WERE DEVELOPED AND

AGREED TO AT A FORUM IN MARCH 2001 IN WINDHOEK, NAMIBIA.

PARTICIPANTS IN THAT WINDHOEK MEETING INCLUDED SPEAKER

OF PARLIAMENT AND ZANU-PF SECRETARY GENERAL MNANGAGWA,

TWO OTHER ZANU MP\'S AND AN OPPOSITION MP. WE HAVE

EXCERPTED FROM THAT DOCUMENT IN THE LAST PARAGRAPH A SET

SUCCESSFUL, WE PROBABLY NEED TO HAVE SUFFICIENT

APPROACH TO SECURE BROAD INTERNATIONAL AGREEMENT,

INCLUDING OF KEY SADC COUNTRIES AND NIGERIA. WHILE

OPPOSITION AND CIVIL SOCIETY MIGHT HAVE DIFFERENCES AT

THE EDGES, WE KNOW THEY WOULD WELCOME THE BROAD THRUST

OF THIS APPROACH AS SUPPORTIVE OF THEIR OWN EFFORTS.
