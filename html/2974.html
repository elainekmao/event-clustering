3. (C) RETIRED SENIOR COLONEL TURNED ACTIVIST PHAM QUE

DUONG INVITED EMBOFFS TO HIS HANOI HOME ON MAY 3 TO DISCUSS

VIETNAMESE "CULTURE" AND, ON MAY 7, TOOK POLOFF TO HIS HOME

VILLAGE IN HA TAY PROVINCE TO VISIT TRADITIONAL TEMPLE

SITES AND PAGODAS IN THE AREA. HE DESCRIBED ONGOING

PROBLEMS RELATED TO GVN TREATMENT OF BUDDHIST PAGODAS AND

TRADITIONAL TEMPLES. DUONG (ONE OF THE SIGNATORIES OF THE

SEPTEMBER 2001 PROPOSAL TO SET UP A CITIZEN\'S GROUP "TO

HELP THE COMMUNIST PARTY OF VIETNAM (CPV) AND THE GVN FIGHT

CORRUPTION" -- REFTEL) STATED THAT HIS ACTIVISM DATES BACK

TO 1986-87, WHEN CONSTRUCTION OF THE HO CHI MINH MUSEUM IN

HANOI THREATENED THE FAMOUS ONE PILLAR PAGODA. ALARMED AT

THE GVN\'S INDIFFERENCE TO ONE OF VIETNAM\'S MOST HISTORIC

RELIGIOUS AND CULTURAL SITES, DUONG, AT THAT TIME STILL

EDITOR OF THE ARMY\'S HISTORICAL JOURNAL, HELPED LEAD A

SUCCESSFUL EFFORT TO CHANGE THE MUSEUM\'S DESIGN AND

PRESERVE THE PAGODA.

4. (C) DUONG SAID HE HAD DEVELOPED AN INTEREST IN

PROTECTING OTHER PAGODAS AND TRADITIONAL TEMPLES,

ESPECIALLY THOSE IN AND AROUND TU DUONG, HIS HOME VILLAGE.

ACCORDING TO DUONG AND LOCAL RESIDENTS, DISTRICT AND

PROVINCIAL OFFICIALS TORE DOWN THE VILLAGE PAGODA IN 1967-

68. THE SITE IS NOW A RICE FIELD, BUT THEY SAID THAT THE

GOVERNMENT SOLD THE LAND TO A HANOI COMPANY AS A "FACTORY

SITE" SEVERAL YEARS AGO. VILLAGE RESIDENTS RECOUNTED THAT

DISTRICT AND PROVINCIAL OFFICIALS HAD ALSO APPROVED THE

DESTRUCTION OF TWO OF TU DUONG\'S TEMPLES AND THE CONVERSION

OF A THIRD INTO A STORE DURING THE WAR. ONLY ONE TEMPLE,

INSIDE THE VILLAGE DINH (COMMUNITY HALL), REMAINS,

ALONGSIDE AN ALTAR DEDICATED TO HO CHI MINH. VILLAGE

RESIDENTS SAID WHEN THE TEMPLES WERE DESTROYED, THEY

BELIEVED THEY WOULD BE ALLOWED TO REBUILD THEM. DESPITE

ONGOING "COMPLAINTS" FROM VILLAGE RESIDENTS TO DISTRICT,

PROVINCIAL, AND NATIONAL ASSEMBLY OFFICIALS, TU DUONG

RESIDENTS SAID THERE HAD NEVER BEEN ANY SATISFACTORY ANSWER

TO THEIR COMPLAINTS.

5. (C) DUONG TOOK POLOFF TO TWO NEARBY BUDDHIST SITES,

HOI SA PAGODA AND CANH PHUC TU PAGODA, JUST OVER THE BORDER

IN HA NAM PROVINCE. MONKS AT THE 1000 YEAR-OLD HOI SA

PAGODA SAID THAT THE ARMY HAD APPROPRIATED LAND JUST NORTH

OF THE PAGODA\'S BUILDINGS FOR A WAREHOUSE DURING THE WAR.

THEY SAID THAT THE WAREHOUSE HAD NOT BEEN USED FOR MANY

YEARS, BUT THE ARMY WAS UNWILLING TO RETURN IT. ACCORDING

TO THE MONKS, THE WAREHOUSE SPOILS THE PAGODA\'S AESTHETICS.

RICE PADDIES BORDER THE PAGODA\'S OTHER THREE SIDES. CANH

PHUC TU PAGODA, ACCORDING TO DUONG, WAS ONE OF THE TWO MOST

BEAUTIFUL PAGODAS IN HA NAM, ENCOMPASSING 21 HECTARES WITH

A FAMOUS GARDEN. ALL BUT 1744 SQUARE METERS WERE LOST TO A

MILITARY WAREHOUSE DURING THE WAR, PAGODA ATTENDANTS NOTED,

AND IN THE LATE 1990\'S THE POLICE EXPROPRIATED MORE LAND

IMMEDIATELY SOUTH OF THE PAGODA\'S BUILDINGS, LEAVING ONLY

598 SQUARE METERS. ONE 300-400 YEAR-OLD TREE REMAINS OF

THE GARDEN. THEY SAID THAT THE POLICE HAD BUILT TWO

HOUSES, AMONG OTHER BUILDINGS, ON THE RECENTLY EXPROPRIATED

LAND. FOUR NUNS AT THE PAGODA ADDED THAT THEY CONTINUED TO

COMPLAIN ABOUT THE LOSS OF PAGODA LAND BUT HAD NEVER

RECEIVED AN ANSWER FROM DISTRICT AUTHORITIES AND THAT

PROVINCIAL AUTHORITIES HAD GIVEN AN ESSENTIALLY "UNETHICAL"

ANSWER. DUONG SAID THAT THE POLICE WERE PARTICULARLY ANGRY

WITH HIM AFTER HE BECAME INVOLVED WITH CANH PHUC TU PAGODA

IN 1999.

6. (C) DUONG POINTED OUT TWO MAIN REASONS HE SAID THE GVN

CONTINUES TO TREAT PAGODA AND TEMPLE LAND WITHOUT RESPECT.

ONE REASON IS IDEOLOGICAL: MARXISM-LENINISM IS

MATERIALISTIC AND UNCONCERNED WITH SPIRITUAL NEEDS. WHILE

HE CLAIMED REPEATEDLY TO BE A COMMUNIST, HE DISCUSSED AT

SOME LENGTH CONTRADICTIONS BETWEEN MARXISM-LENINISM AND

RELIGIOUS BELIEF, CONCLUDING THAT HE DID NOT KNOW HOW TO

RECONCILE THESE DIFFERENCES. AS A PATRIOT, HE SAID HE HAD

TO PROTECT VIETNAM\'S CULTURE AND TRADITIONS. THE SECOND

REASON, HE ALLEGED, IS THAT GVN OFFICIALS ARE SELLING OR

USING THE APPROPRIATED LAND FOR THEIR OWN BENEFIT.
