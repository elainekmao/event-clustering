1. (C) ON DECEMBER 12, POLOFF SPOKE TO CEPHAS

TSOKODAYI, UNDERSECRETARY FOR INTERNATIONAL

SIPDIS

ORGANIZATIONS IN ZIMBABWE\'S MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS

ABOUT ZIMBABWE\'S POSITION ON UN SCALES OF ASSESSMENT

REFORM. HE STATED THAT FOREIGN MINISTER STANISLAUS

MUDENGE IS SENDING A LETTER ADDRESSED TO SECRETARY

ALBRIGHT VIA EMBASSY HARARE ON ZIMBABWE\'S POSITION.

ACCORDING TO TSOKODAYI, THE MAIN MESSAGE OF THE LETTER

WILL BE THAT ZIMBABWE WILL SUPPORT THE US POSITION

"UNDER THE PARAMETERS THAT OTHER DEVELOPED NATIONS ARE

INDEED UNDERPAYING THEIR SHARE OF UN DUES," AND THAT

DEVELOPING NATIONS WILL NOT SEE AN INCREASE IN THEIR

REQUIRED CONTRIBUTIONS.

2. (C) AS OF DECEMBER 18, EMBASSY HARARE HAD NOT YET

RECEIVED THE FOREIGN MINISTER\'S LETTER. ACCORDING TO

THE MINISTER\'S OFFICE ON DECEMBER 18, THE INTERNATIONAL

ORGANIZATIONS OFFICE HAD NOT YET SENT THE LETTER TO THE

MINISTER FOR HIS SIGNATURE. WE WILL CONTINUE TO FOLLOW

UP WITH THE MINISTRY AND ADVISE IF THE CONTENT OF THE

LETTER VARIES SIGNIFICANTLY WITH TSOKODAYI\'S REMARKS.

IRVING
