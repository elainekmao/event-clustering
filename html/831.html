SUBJECT: THE RIFT BETWEEN THE CROWN PRINCE AND FOREIGN MINISTER

REF: KUWAIT 3753

1. (U) CLASSIFIED BY DCM GEORGIA DEBELL, REASON 1.5(B).

2. (C) KUWAITI FOREIGN MINISTER SHAYKH SABAH AL-AHMED AL-JABER

AL-SABAH REMAINS IN MOROCCO FOLLOWING HIS CONFRONTATION WITH THE

CROWN PRINCE ON THE EVE OF THE CAIRO SUMMIT (REFTEL). SHAYKH

SABAH HAS CONTINUED TO PERFORM THE RITUALS OF THE FOREIGN

MINISTER, AND HAS BEEN FEATURED IN THE LOCAL PRESS DISPATCHING

NATIONAL DAY GREETINGS AND CALLING ON MOROCCAN OFFICIALS

INCLUDING KING HASSAN. HIS MESSAGE OF CONDOLENCE TO SECRETARY

CHRISTOPHER AFTER THE DHAHRAN BOMBING RECEIVED SPECIAL ATTENTION,

AND WHILE THIS IS CERTAINLY NOT A ROUTINE VACATION, IT IS EQUALLY

CLEAR THAT SHAYKH SABAH WILL BE COMING BACK TO THE FOLD. THAT

MAY BE SOONER RATHER THAN LATER. THE FOREIGN MINISTER\'S CHIEF OF

PROTOCOL TOLD US JUNE 29 THAT THE AMIR HAD GIVEN HIM TWO WEEKS TO

COOL OFF, AND HE IS EXPECTED BACK IN KUWAIT THE END OF THIS WEEK

OR THE BEGINNING OF THE NEXT.

3. (C) WHILE SHAYKH SABAH MAY SOON BE BACK ON THE JOB, THE RIFT

BETWEEN KUWAIT\'S SECOND AND THIRD RANKING OFFICIALS WILL

CONTINUE, AND MAY BE MORE PRONOUNCED. INFORMATION MINISTER SAUD

NASIR, A BITTER CRITIC OF THE FOREIGN MINISTER (AND A

CORRESPONDINGLY CLOSE ALLY OF THE CROWN PRINCE), JOINED KUWAIT\'S

DELEGATION TO THE CAIRO SUMMIT AFTER SHAYKH SABAH DECAMPED, AND

WILL USE THE EPISODE TO MAKE THE POINT THAT THERE ARE OTHERS WHO

ARE WILLING AND ABLE TO TAKE ON THE FOREIGN AFFAIRS PORTFOLIO.

4. (C) AS NOTED REFTEL, WHILE THE IMMEDIATE CAUSE OF THIS

LATEST FLARE UP WAS THE ONGOING FEUD OVER RELATIONS WITH JORDAN,

THE TENSION BETWEEN THE FOREIGN MINISTER AND CROWN PRINCE IS

ROOTED IN FUNDAMENTAL DIFFERENCES IN PERSONALITY AND STYLE. THE

DEPUTY CHIEF OF STAFF OF KUWAIT\'S ARMED FORCES, FAHED AL-AMIR (A

SUPPORTER OF SHAYKH SABAH AND A RELATIVE BY MARRIAGE) TOLD THE

AMBASSADOR RECENTLY THAT THE FOREIGN MINISTER IS INCREASINGLY

FRUSTRATED WITH THE CROWN PRINCE\'S DECISION MAKING STYLE WHICH HE

DESCRIBED AS THE CAPACITY TO AVOID MAKING DECISIONS.

5. (C) WE SUSPECT THAT TIME AND AGE HAVE ALSO SHARPENED THE

RIVALRY FROM THE FOREIGN MINISTER\'S SIDE. AT 66, HE IS A YEAR

YOUNGER THAN THE AMIR AND A YEAR OLDER THAN THE CROWN PRINCE, AND

THE POSSIBILITY THAT HE WILL NEVER RUN THE GOVERNMENT (LET ALONE

THE STATE) MAY BE INCREASINGLY APPARENT TO HIM. WHATEVER THE

REASONS FOR THE DIVISION, THE FACT THAT IT IS A VERY PUBLIC ONE

MAKES KUWAITIS NERVOUS. FAHED AL-AMIR AND PARLIAMENTARY FOREIGN

RELATIONS CHAIRMAN JASSIM AL-SAGER COMMENTED SEPARATELY TO THE

AMBASSADOR THAT IT WOULD BE BETTER ALL THE WAY AROUND IF THE

SENIOR AL-SABAHS COULD KEEP THEIR DIFFERENCES TO THEMSELVES,

PARTICULARLY DURING SUCH CHALLENGING TIMES IN THE REGION.
