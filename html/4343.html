SUBJECT: ITALY MAINTAINS STATUS QUO AFTER ECJ OPEN SKIES

DECISION

REF: A. STATE 221334

B. ROME 4883

1. WE CONTACTED ALDO SANSONE, DIRECTOR OF FOREIGN RELATIONS

AT THE MINISTRY OF TRANSPORTATION AND MARIA LUISA TIMPONE,

INTERNATIONAL AGREEMENTS, ENAC (ITALY\'S CIVIL AVIATION

AUTHORITY) REGARDING THE EUROPEAN COURT OF JUSTICE (ECJ)

DECISION ON MEMBER STATE OPEN SKIES AGREEMENTS WITH THE U.S.

(REF A). BOTH STATED THAT AS ITALY WAS NOT A PARTY TO THE

SUIT, THEY DID NOT KNOW HOW ITALY WOULD BE AFFECTED, NOR DID

THEY KNOW WHEN OR HOW THE EU WOULD IMPLEMENT THE DECISION.

THEY FELT THAT THE DECISION BROUGHT THE EU COMMISSION ONE

STEP CLOSER TO GAINING A MANDATE TO NEGOTIATE AN EU/U.S. OPEN

SKIES AGREEMENT, BUT THEY DOUBTED THAT THIS WOULD HAPPEN

ANYTIME SOON. IN THE MEANTIME, ITALY\'S TREATIES IN FORCE

WILL REMAIN VALID AND THE SITUATION UNCHANGED.
