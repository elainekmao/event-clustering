SUBJECT: UNIDROIT REQUEST FOR VOLUNTARY CONTRIBUTIONS

REF: ROME 06012

1. SUMMARY AND ACTION REQUEST. THE ROME-BASED

INTERNATIONAL INSTITUTE FOR THE UNIFICATION OF

INTERNATIONAL PRIVATE LAW (UNIDROIT) HAS REQUESTED MEMBER

GOVERNMENTS TO CONSIDER MAKING A ONE-TIME VOLUNTARY

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 ROME 00261 221549Z

CONTRIBUTION TO ALLOW THE ORGANIZATION TO FULFILL ITS

DEPOSITARY FUNCTIONS FOR THE CAPETOWN CONVENTION IN 2003.

IN VIEW OF THE IMPORTANCE OF THE CONVENTION TO U.S.

AIRCRAFT EQUIPMENT INTERESTS, MISSION URGES FAVORABLE

CONSIDERATION OF THE REQUEST. END SUMMARY.

2. UNIDROIT\'S 56TH GENERAL ASSEMBLY (REFTEL) ADOPTED A

RESOLUTION INVITING GOVERNMENTS TO PROVIDE ONE-TIME

VOLUNTARY CONTRIBUTIONS TO ENABLE UNIDROIT TO CARRY OUT

THE DEPOSITARY FUNCTIONS CONFERRED UPON IT UNDER THE

CAPETOWN CONVENTION ON INTERNATIONAL INTERESTS IN MOBILE

EQUIPMENT AND THE PROTOCOL ON MATTERS SPECIFIC TO

AIRCRAFT EQUIPMENT (TEXT FAXED TO L/PIL HAROLD BURMAN)

UNTIL SUCH TIME AS THE REGULAR BUDGET PROVIDES SUFFICIENT

RESOURCES.

3. THE ADDITIONAL COSTS, AS ORIGINALLY PROPOSED FOR THE

2003 BUDGET, ARE ESTIMATED AT 73,000 EUROS FOR AN

ADDITIONAL STAFF MEMBER AND 25,000 EUROS FOR SOFTWARE.

(COMMENT: THE CAPETOWN CONVENTION HAS AN UNUSUALLY

COMPLEX SYSTEM OF DECLARATIONS (NO FEWER THAN 36 FROM

WHICH STATES MAY PICK AND CHOOSE), THUS COMPLICATING THE

TRADITIONAL DEPOSITARY FUNCTIONS OF NOTIFICATION AND

REPORTING. END COMMENT).

4. AS REPORTED IN REFTELS, UNIDROIT MEMBERS WERE NOT IN

A POSITION TO ADOPT A 2003 BUDGET WHICH INCLUDED THE

ADDITIONAL COSTS OF SERVING AS DEPOSITARY. MEMBERS DID,

HOWEVER, AGREE ON THE IMPORTANCE OF THE FUNCTION. A

NUMBER OF MEMBERS STATED THAT THEIR GOVERNMENTS WOULD BE

IN A POSITION TO SUPPORT THE REQUEST IN THE 2004 BUDGET.

5. IN VIEW OF THE IMPORTANCE OF THE CONVENTION TO U.S.
