SUBJECT: MINISTER OF STATE TELLS AMBASSADOR POLITICAL

VIOLENCE IS ZANU-PF ELECTION STRATEGY

CLASSIFIED BY POLITICAL OFFICER MAKILA JAMES, REASONS

1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) SUMMARY: MINISTER OF STATE IN THE OFFICE OF THE

PRESIDENT RESPONSIBLE FOR INDIGENIZATION (BLACK ECONOMIC

EMPOWERMENT), CEPHAS MSIPA, TOLD AMBASSADOR MCDONALD

JUNE 1 THAT ZANU-PF\'S PARLIAMENTARY ELECTION STRATEGY IS

TO USE THE "WAR VETERANS" TO INTIMIDATE THE PUBLIC INTO

SUPPORTING THE RULING PARTY CANDIDATES. A "SPECIAL

COMMITTEE" OF CABINET MINISTERS EXISTS TO ORCHESTRATE

THE WAR VETS COMMERCIAL FARM OCCUPATIONS, WHICH INCLUDES

THE MINISTERS OF JUSTICE AND STATE SECURITY. THE ZANU-

PF ORGANIZED POLITICAL VIOLENCE COULD RESULT IN A

PROTEST VOTE FOR THE MOVEMENT FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE

(MDC), ACCORDING TO MSIPA. AMBASSADOR MCDONALD STRONGLY

CONDEMNED THE HUMAN RIGHTS VIOLATIONS PRESENTLY

OCCURRING, EMPHASIZED THE DAMAGE THE ONGOING POLITICAL

VIOLENCE WAS DOING TO INVESTOR CONFIDENCE IN ZIMBABWE,

AND URGED THE MINISTER TO CONVEY THAT MESSAGE TO OTHER

SENIOR GOVERNMENT OFFICIALS. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) MINISTER OF STATE FOR INDIGENIZATION, CEPHAS

MSIPA, HAD A CANDID AND CORDIAL CONVERSATION WITH

AMBASSADOR MCDONALD, ACCOMPANIED BY POLOFF, ON JUNE 1 IN

WHICH HE EXPLAINED ZANU-PF\'S PARLIAMENTARY ELECTION

STRATEGY. MINISTER MSIPA BEGAN BY EXPLAINING HIS OWN

RECENT DEFEAT AS THE RULING PARTY\'S PARLIAMENTARY

CANDIDATE, AFTER SEVERAL PRIMARY RERUNS, WHICH HE

ATTRIBUTED TO THE WORK OF JUSTICE MINISTER, EMMERSON

MNANGAGWA. MNANGAGWA HAD ORGANIZED A CAMPAIGN AGAINST

THE INDIGENIZATION MINISTER IN THE MIDLANDS PROVINCE,

WHERE MSIPA IS FROM, IN RETALIATION FOR MSIPA HAVING

SUPPORTED THE CURRENT ZANU-PF CHAIRMAN, JOHN NKOMO,

DURING THE PARTY\'S DECEMBER CONVENTION, HE SAID.

(COMMENT: MSIPA IS REPORTEDLY CLOSE TO PRESIDENT MUGABE,

HOWEVER, MNANGAGWA ENLISTED THE SUPPORT OF SEVERAL KEY

POLITBURO MEMBERS TO DEFEAT MSIPA AND THE PRESIDENT

COULD NOT OVERRIDE THAT DECISION. END NOTE).

3. (C) IN RESPONSE AMBASSADOR MCDONALD\'S CONDEMNATION

OF THE ONGOING PRE-ELECTION VIOLENCE, THE MINISTER TOLD

US THAT ZANU-PF\'S PARLIAMENTARY ELECTION STRATEGY WAS TO

INTIMIDATE THE PUBLIC INTO BACKING THE RULING PARTY AND

TO CRUSH ANY OPPOSITION. SPECIFICALLY, MSIPA SAID EVERY

ZANU-PF CANDIDATE WAS ALLOCATED A NUMBER OF "WAR

VETERANS" IN THEIR AREA TO COERCE PEOPLE INTO SUPPORTING

THE RULING PARTY\'S CANDIDATE. MSIPA SAID HE TOO WAS

GIVEN A GROUP OF OSTENSIBLE WAR VETS (I.E. THUGS) TO

HELP HIM WIN HIS ELECTION. SOME SENIOR PARTY MEMBERS

SOUGHT TO WITHDRAW THEM, HOWEVER, AFTER MNANGAGWA BEGAN

ORGANIZING OPPOSITION TO MSIPA\'S CANDIDACY.

4. (C) MOREOVER, MSIPA SAID THERE WAS A "SPECIAL

COMMITTEE" OF SELECT CABINET MINISTERS WHO WERE TASKED

WITH ORCHESTRATING THE COMMERCIAL FARM OCCUPATIONS WHICH

HAD GIVEN RISE TO MUCH OF THE POLITICAL VIOLENCE. MSIPA

SAID THAT DURING THE REGULAR WEEKLY CABINET MEETINGS

MENTION WOULD BE MADE OF THIS "SPECIAL COMMITTEE" TAKING

SYDNEY SEKERAMAYI, WERE ON THIS COMMITTEE. THE MINISTER

ACQUIRED FOR RESETTLEMENT BECAUSE OF THE GOVERNMENT\'S

FAILURE TO PURSUE THAT OFFER. THIS VIOLENCE COULD,

HOWEVER, RESULT IN A PROTEST VOTE AGAINST ZANU-PF, THE

MINISTER SPECULATED. MSIPA NOTED THAT SOUTH AFRICA AND

SOME NORDIC COUNTRIES WERE REPORTEDLY TRYING TO IDENTIFY

FUNDS TO ASSIST ZIMBABWE IN ITS LAND REFORM PROGRAM.

AMBASSADOR, HOWEVER, INFORMED THAT HE UNDERSTOOD FROM

THE NORWEGIAN AMBASSADOR THAT HIS COUNTRY HAD NO SUCH

BAILOUT IN THE WORKS FOR ZIMBABWE AT THIS TIME.

6. (C) ADDITIONALLY, THE MINISTER SAID HE OBJECTED TO

THE OCCUPATION OF VACANT BUILDINGS IN URBAN AREAS BY A

BLACK ECONOMIC EMPOWERMENT ORGANIZATION, THE AFFIRMATIVE

ACTION GROUP (AAG), WHO WERE TAKING THEIR CUE FROM THE

WAR VETERANS. MSIPA SAID HE OECLINED TO GET INVOLVED,

HOWEVER, NOW THAT MINISTER DABENGWA WAS TRYING TO

RESOLVE THAT SITUATION AND THE AAG WAS NEGOTIATING WITH

THE LANDLORDS TO PAY A MOOEST RENTAL FEE FOR THOSE

PROPERTIES.

7. (C) AMBASSADOR MCDONALD STRONGLY CONDEMNED THE

POLITICAL VIOLENCE AND WIDESPREAD HUMAN RIGHTS

VIOLATIONS IN THE COUNTRYSIDE. HE ALSO URGED MSIPA TO

CONVEY TO HIS GOVERNMENT HOW DAMAGING THE POLITICAL

VIOLENCE, FARM OCCUPATIONS, AND URBAN SQUATTING WERE TO

ZIMBABWE\'S INVESTMENT CLIMATE, TOURISM, AND OVERALL

ECONOMY.

COMMENT

8. (C) MSIPA IS A REASONABLE, COMMITTED, AND HIGHLY

CREDIBLE GOVERNMENT OFFICIAL WHO HAS BEEN FRUSTRATED IN

DOING HIS JOB AND WHOSE ADVICE HAS APPARENTLY BEEN

SHUNNED BY THE MORE RAOICAL RULING PARTY MEMBERS. THUS,

HE IS POLITICALLY WEAK. NEVERTHELESS, MSIPA RETAINS

MUGABE\'S CONFIDENCE AND MAY RETAIN A POSITION IN THE

OFFICE OF THE PRESIDENT AFTER THE PARLIAMENTARY

ELECTION. MSIPA HAS CONFIRMED THE WIDESPREAD BELIEF

THAT PERPETUATING VIOLENCE IS ZANU-PF\'S ELECTION

STRATEGY AND THAT IT IS DIRECTED BY MUGABE. THE

MINISTER\'S COMMENTS ALSO TRACK THE EMBASSY\'S POSITION

THAT THE PRESENT FARM OCCUPATIONS ARE NOT ABOUT LAND

REFORM, BUT RATHER POLITICS. WE WILL CONTINUE TO

DELIVER A STRONG MESSAGE THAT THE ELECTION VIOLENCE AND

ECONOMIC SUICIDE THAT THE GOVERNMENT IS PURSUING WILL

MAKE IT DIFFICULT TO RETURN TO BUSINESS AS USUAL AFTER

THE ELECTION. END COMMENT.

MCDONALD
