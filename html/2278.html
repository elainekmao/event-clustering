SUBJECT: A/S KANSTEINER MEETS WITH CROSS SECTION OF

BULAWAYO SOCIETY

REF: HARARE 3643

CLASSIFIED BY POLITICAL OFFICER TODD FAULK FOR REASONS:

1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) SUMMARY: ON DECEMBER 9, A/S KANSTEINER MET WITH

A WIDE CROSS-SECTION OF BULAWAYO SOCIETY TO DISCUSS THE

RECENT POLITICAL VIOLENCE IN THAT CITY, ONGOING

GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE (GOZ) ATTEMPTS TO SUBVERT THE

PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION, THE DETERIORATING ECONOMY AND

LOOMING FOOD SHORTAGES, PASSAGE OF THE ZIMBABWE

DEMOCRACY AND ECONOMIC RECOVERY ACT (ZDERA), AND WHAT

ROLE THE U.S. MIGHT PLAY IN HELPING ZIMBABWE OUT OF ITS

CURRENT CRISIS. OVERALL, OUR INTERLOCUTORS PAINTED

BULAWAYO, AND THE ENTIRE MATABELELAND REGION, AS AN AREA

UNDER SIEGE BY THE GOZ, AND ONE LIVING IN FEAR OF MORE

MUGABE-LED VIOLENCE AND REPRESSION. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) ON DECEMBER 9, A/S KANSTEINER AND AMBASSADOR

SULLIVAN, ALONG WITH SPECIAL ASSISTANT THOMAS WOODS AND

POLOFF (NOTETAKER), MET WITH A WIDE CROSS-SECTION OF

CIVIL SOCIETY, OPPOSITION AND LOCAL OFFICIALS IN

BULAWAYO. IN SEPARATE MEETINGS, KANSTEINER MET: SHARI

EPPEL, DIRECTOR OF AMANI TRUST/BULAWAYO, A WELL-

RESPECTED HUMAN RIGHTS ORGANIZATION; ERIC BLOCH, A

PROMINENT ECONOMIST AND COLUMNIST; MOVEMENT FOR

DEMOCRATIC CHANGE VICE-PRESIDENT GIBSON SIBANDA, MDC

SECRETARY-GENERAL WELSHMAN NCUBE, AND MDC MP FOR

SIPDIS

BULAWAYO SOUTH DAVID COLTART; HUMAN RIGHTS ACTIVIST AND

"DAILY NEWS" PART-OWNER JUDITH TODD AND MICHAEL NDUBIWA,

THE CHAIRMAN OF THE NATIONAL AIDS COUNCIL; ARCHBISHOP

PIUS NCUBE; AND BULAWAYO MAYOR JAPHET NDABENI-NCUBE.

BULAWAYO UNDER SIEGE

3. (C) NEARLY ALL THOSE A/S KANSTEINER MET WITH HAD A

TALE TO TELL RELATED TO THE POLITICAL VIOLENCE THAT

WRACKED BULAWAYO IN MID-NOVEMBER. MAYOR NDEBENI-NCUBE,

ELECTED ON THE MDC TICKET IN SEPTEMBER, RECOUNTED HIS

OWN CLOSE CALL WITH ANGRY DEMONSTRATORS ON NOVEMBER 16.

SIKHANYISO NDLOVU AND DUMISO DABENGWA -- ZANU-PF

POLITBURO MEMBERS WHOSE POLITICAL STARS ARE WANING, THE

MAYOR STATED -- STARTED THE TROUBLE BY WHIPPING UP

EMOTIONS AT A ZANU-PF YOUTH RALLY; THE MAJORITY OF THE

YOUTH WERE SHIPPED IN BY TRAIN THE NIGHT BEFORE. THE

GROUP OF SEVERAL HUNDRED THEN PROCEEDED TO TEAR THROUGH

THE STREETS, ATTACKING BYSTANDERS AND LOOTING VENDORS OF

THEIR WARES. FIFTEEN MINUTES BEFORE THEY ARRIVED AT HIS

OFFICE AT CITY HALL, HE RECEIVED A PHONE CALL FROM AN

MDC OPERATIVE WARNING OF THE IMPENDING DANGER. TAKING

HIS REVOLVER WITH HIM, HE WAS WHISKED TO A DARK OFFICE

IN A DIFFERENT PART OF THE BUILDING AND LOCKED INSIDE,

THE MAYOR SAID. WHEN THE DEMONSTATORS ARRIVED, A GROUP

OF EIGHT WAR VETERANS LED BY THEIR NATIONAL SECRETARY

FOR PROJECTS ANDREW NDLOVU, SOMEHOW GOT INSIDE CITY HALL

AND FORCED A SECURITY GUARD TO TAKE THEM TO THE MAYOR\'S

OFFICE. THEY RANSACKED THE OFFICE, TORE UP PAPERS,

THREW FILES OUT THE WINDOW, AND STOLE HIS BRIEFCASE.

THEY LEFT TWO LOGS ON HIS DESK, AN APPARENT WARNING THAT

THEY WOULD BEAT HIM WITH THE CLUBS IF HE RETURNED,

NDABENI-NCUBE STATED. THE DEMONSTRATORS, ESCORTED BY

RIOT POLICE, THEN LEFT TO BURN DOWN THE MDC\'S BULAWAYO

THREATENING PHONE CALLS, AND STRANGE VEHICLES DRIVE

THE VIOLENCE AND INTIMIDATION OF THE LAST FEW WEEKS IN

6. (C) MDC VICE-PRESIDENT GIBSON SIBANDA SAID THE GOZ

ECONOMY SHRINKS AS FOOD SHORTAGES GROW

--------------------------------------

9. (C) ONE OF THE KEY FACTORS IN MUGABE\'S DECLINING

POPULARITY HAS BEEN THE RAPIDLY DETERIORATING ECONOMY

AND THE COUNTRY\'S FOOD SECURITY ALONG WITH IT, ACCORDING

TO ERIC BLOCH, A WIDELY RESPECTED ECONOMIST AND

COLUMNIST BASED IN BULAWAYO. A LOT OF FARMERS ARE

WAITING TO SEE IF THE GOZ CARRIES OUT ITS STATED

INTENTION TO TAKE OVER NEARLY ALL THE COUNTRY\'S

COMMERCIAL FARMS. THE REAPING OF TOBACCO IS DUE TO

BEGIN IN MID-DECEMBER, BUT FARMERS MAY DECIDE NOT TO IF

THERE IS NO EXPECTATION OF KEEPING THEIR FARMS, WHICH

WILL HAVE MAJOR IMPLICATIONS FOR THE COUNTRY\'S FOREX

FUTURE. THERE IS CURRENTLY ONLY US$30 MILLION PER MONTH

COMING INTO ZIMBABWE, AND NO MORE THAN US$50 TO 60

MILLION PER MONTH MAY BE EXPECTED IN APRIL WHEN THE

TOBACCO SALES BEGIN (US$100 MILLION/MONTH IS NORMAL).

10. (C) BLOCH SAID IN JUNE HE ACCOMPANIED FINANCE

MINISTER SIMBA MAKONI TO AN INTERNATIONAL CONFERENCE

WHERE MAKONI STATED THAT LITTLE CAN BE DONE TO SAVE THE

ZIMBABWEAN ECONOMY UNLESS THREE THINGS HAPPEN: 1) LAW

AND ORDER IS RESTORED, I.E. SQUATTERS ARE REMOVED FROM

FARMS, 2) THE GOZ "STOPS CHANGING ITS POLICIES AS OFTEN

AS WE CHANGE OUR UNDERWEAR," AND 3) EVERY FARMER IS ABLE

TO PLANT AND HARVEST HIS CROP IN SECURITY. ABSENT THAT,

MAKONI IS SPENDING MONEY HE DOES NOT HAVE TO KEEP THE

ECONOMY AFLOAT. GOZ DEBT HAS SOARED TO BETWEEN 15 AND

19 PERCENT OF GDP. TO RAISE MORE CAPITAL, HE WILL HAVE

ZIMBABWE DEMOCRACY ACT

----------------------

13. (C) MANY OF OUR INTERLOCUTORS EXPRESSED THANKS TO

A/S KANSTEINER AND THE USG FOR PASSING THE ZIMBABWE

DEMOCRACY AND ECONOMIC RECOVERY ACT (ZDERA). IN AN OPEN

DISCUSSION WITH HUMAN RIGHTS ACTIVIST JUDITH TODD AND

AIDS NATIONAL COUNCIL CHAIRMAN MIKE NDUBIWA (A LONG-TIME

ZANU-PF MEMBER WHO WAS AS ANTI-ZANU AS ANYONE WE MET),

KANSTEINER EXPLAINED THE PROVISIONS OF THE ZDERA AND

THAT IT IS UP TO THE ADMINISTRATION TO IMPLEMENT ANY

SANCTIONS. TODD ARGUED THAT IT WOULD BE IMPORTANT TO

THREATEN SANCTIONS JUST BEFORE THE ZANU-PF CONFERENCE

BEGINS DECEMBER 14 AND BEFORE THE AMENDMENTS TO THE

ELECTORAL ACT ARE PASSED IN PARLIAMENT (TODD REPORTED

THAT THE BILL IS DUE TO BE INTRODUCED DECEMBER 18).

AFTER THESE DATES, SHE SAID, IT WOULD BE TOO LATE TO

INFLUENCE ANY POSSIBLE CHANGES IN THE GOZ\'S COURSE OF

ACTION. AMBASSADOR SULLIVAN POINTED OUT THAT THERE IS

AN ARGUMENT AGAINST USING THE SANCTIONS "STICK" NOW --

WE WON\'T HAVE ANY OTHERS ONCE IT IS USED, AND IT COULD

FORCE MUGABE INTO A CORNER WITH NO ESCAPE ROUTES.

NDUBIWA MAINTAINED THAT "PULLING THE TRIGGER" OF

SANCTIONS SHOULD BE A PROCESS RATHER THAN AN EVENT.

CERTAINLY, THREATS OF USING SANCTIONS WOULD HAVE TO BE

MADE, BUT THEIR ULTIMATE USE SHOULD NOT BE LINKED TO ANY

ONE ACTION, SUCH AS PASSAGE OF THE ELECTORAL ACT

AMENDMENTS.

14. (C) WHEN ASKED HOW THE U.S. COULD HELP ZIMBABWE AT

THIS POINT, MDC VICE-PRESIDENT SIBANDA STATED THAT THE

MOST IMPORTANT THING WE COULD DO IS TELL PRESIDENT

MUGABE IN NO UNCERTAIN TERMS THAT WE WOULD NOT RECOGNIZE

THE ELECTION OUTCOME IF THE ELECTION WAS NOT FREE AND

FAIR. MUGABE WANTS THIS RECOGNITION MORE THAN ANYTHING,

SIBANDA SAID. USING OUR INFLUENCE WITH THE REGIONAL

STATES WOULD HELP, BUT AMONG THE SADC STATES, ONLY SOUTH

AFRICA CAN APPLY SUFFICIENT PRESSURE ON MUGABE TO CHANGE

HIS WAYS. SHARI EPPEL OF AMANI TRUST OPINED THAT

APPLYING PRESSURE TO THE SADC STATES TO TAKE A STAND

AGAINST MUGABE IS KEY TO CHANGING HIS BEHAVIOR. SIBANDA

APPEALED FOR HELP WITH MDC FINANCES, WHICH HE SAID WERE

BEING DRAINED BY GOZ ACTIONS, SUCH AS THE TORCHING OF

ITS BULAWAYO OFFICES AND LEGAL CASES. THE MDC IS

SPENDING BETWEEN ZIM$5 MILLION AND $7 MILLION (US$16,000

AND $23,000) ON LEGAL FEES; IT HAS SO FAR SPENT ZIM$1.3

MILLION (US$4,000) ON FLETCHER DULINI-NCUBE\'S CASE

ALONE. THEY SAID IT WOULD HELP IF THE U.S. COULD ASSIST

IN FINDING NGOS THAT COULD PROVIDE LEGAL ASSISTANCE OR

FUNDS FOR LEGAL EXPENSES.
