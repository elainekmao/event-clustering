1. SUMMARY: MISSION ITALY RECOMMENDS THAT ITALY REMAIN ON

THE 301 WATCH LIST. OVERALL FREQUENCY OF COPYRIGHT PIRACY HAS

LEVELED OFF OR FALLEN SLIGHTLY SINCE 2000, WHEN ITALY\'S NEW

COPYRIGHT LAW WAS ENACTED. NONETHELESS, TOTAL LOSSES DUE TO

PIRACY ARE MOUNTING AND ITALY\'S PIRACY AND COUNTERFEITING

RATES REMAIN AMONG THE HIGHEST IN WESTERN EUROPE. LAW

ENFORCEMENT IN SOME CITIES IS AGGRESSIVE. OFTEN, HOWEVER,

THEY ARE OUTPACED BY ORGANIZED CRIME NETWORKS THAT USE NEW

COPYING AND COUNTERFEITING TECHNOLOGIES AND DISTRIBUTION

MODALITIES TO EVADE THE LAW. TENACIOUS PROBLEMS REMAIN

REGARDING THE JUDICIARY, CERTAIN MUNICIPAL LEADERS, AND THE

SIAE (ITALY\'S COPYRIGHT ROYALTY COLLECTION AGENCY).

END SUMMARY.

2. THE IPR SITUATION IN ITALY IMPROVED ONLY SLIGHTLY IN

2002, WITH PIRACY LEVELS REMAINING THE SAME IN MOST SECTORS

ACCORDING TO THE INTERNATIONAL INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ALLIANCE

(IIPA). THE ENACTMENT OF THE 2000 COPYRIGHT LAW IN SEPTEMBER

2001 RAISED CRIMINAL PENALTIES AND LED TO INCREASED AWARENESS

AMONG LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICIALS AND THE PUBLIC THAT PIRACY IS

A CRIME. 2002 HAS BEEN AMONG THE BEST YEARS FOR PIRACY

ENFORCEMENT IN ITALY. FIFTEEN HUNDRED PEOPLE WERE ARRESTED

AND 2 MILLION CDS WERE SEIZED. HOWEVER, MOST OF THOSE

ARRESTED WERE IMMEDIATELY RELEASED. ALMOST ALL OF THOSE

SENTENCED RECEIVED NO MORE THAN SIX MONTHS AND SERVED NO TIME

IN JAIL. OF MANY RECENT RAIDS, ONE IN NOVEMBER 2002 SHUT

DOWN AN INTERNET PIRACY RING WITH REVENUES OF 60 MILLION USD

PER YEAR. THE RING WAS PIRATING A LARGE VARIETY OF ITEMS

SUCH AS CAD SOFTWARE WITH A RETAIL VALUE OF 20,000 USD PER

TITLE. MORE ARRESTS HAVE STEMMED CERTAIN FORMS OF PIRACY,

PARTICULARLY IN CITIES WHERE THE MAYORS AND PREFECTS SUPPORT

POLICE EFFORTS. BUT PIRATES ARE ALSO TAKING ADVANTAGE OF

NEW, MORE DISPERSED DUPLICATION TECHNOLOGIES AND DISTRIBUTION

NETWORKS WHICH ARE LESS VULNERABLE TO POLICE SEIZURES.

3. ENFORCEMENT VARIES FROM CITY TO CITY, DEPENDING UPON

MUNICIPAL OFFICIALS. FOR EXAMPLE, THE MAYOR AND PREFECT IN

ROME HAVE BEEN UNWILLING TO ALLOW POLICE TO ACT AGAINST

STREET VENDORS OF PIRATED MATERIAL. AS A RESULT, SUCH STREET

SALES HAVE CONTINUED UNIMPEDED. OFFICIALS IN CERTAIN CITIES

HAVE ATTEMPTED TO STOP LOCAL CARABINIERI FROM ACTING. ON THE

OTHER HAND, TRIESTE CITY OFFICIALS HAVE BEEN VERY SUPPORTIVE.

AS A RESULT, IN THAT CITY, STREET VENDORS OF PIRATED

MATERIAL HAVE ALMOST DISAPPEARED AND PIRACY RATES ARE

EXTREMELY LOW.

4. PROSECUTORS AND JUDGES VARY IN QUALITY AS WELL. MOST

PROSECUTORS ARE UNWILLING TO BRING CRIMINAL CHARGES AGAINST

STREET VENDORS, FACTORY WORKERS OR SHOP WORKERS. JUDGES ARE

USUALLY UNWILLING TO CONVICT, ARGUING THAT "MINOR" ECONOMIC

CRIMES DO NOT JUSTIFY CRIMINAL TREATMENT.

LEGISLATION

-----------

6. ITALY AMENDED THE IMPLEMENTING REGULATIONS TO THE 2000

COPYRIGHT LAW IN EARLY 2003 TO PERMIT ROUTINE EXEMPTIONS FROM

THE SIAE STICKER FOR BUSINESS SOFTWARE. THE ITALIAN

PARLIAMENT APPROVED THE 2001 EU DIRECTIVES OMNIBUS BILL,

WHICH DELEGATED AUTHORITY TO THE GOI TO INTEGRATE EU RULES

GOVERNING COPYRIGHT INTO THE ITALIAN LEGAL CODE. IN 2002 THE

ITALIAN GOVERNMENT PASSED LEGISLATION, INITIATED BY THE

MINISTRY OF INDUSTRY, WHICH CALLS FOR THE CREATION OF A DOZEN

NEW SPECIALIZED ADMINISTRATIVE AND CIVIL IPR COURT SECTIONS,

ALLOCATES FIVE MILLION EURO TO THE MODERNIZATION OF THE

ITALIAN PATENT AND TRADEMARK OFFICE, AND CLEARS THE WAY FOR

THE RATIONALIZATION OF ITALY\'S PATENT AND TRADEMARK LAW. THE

COMING YEAR WILL TELL WHETHER THESE INITIATIVES WILL BE

EFFECTIVELY IMPLEMENTED.

8. IN ACCORD WITH ARTICLE 11 OF THE 2000 COPYRIGHT LAW, SIAE

IS RESPONSIBLE FOR ANTI-PIRACY EFFORTS IN ITALY. BECAUSE

SIAE RECEIVES ALL REVENUES FROM ITS STICKER, WHICH IS

REQUIRED FOR COPYRIGHTED MATERIALS UNDER THE LAW (WITH THE

EXCEPTION OF BUSINESS SOFTWARE), IT HAS ADEQUATE RESOURCES

FOR THESE EFFORTS. HOWEVER, SIAE APPEARS TO DIRECT ITS

EFFORTS SOLELY AT INSPECTING LEGITIMATE COPYRIGHTED GOODS TO

ENSURE THAT THESE CARRY A SIAE STICKER. SIAE HAS DONE

NOTHING, TO OUR KNOWLEDGE, TO ACT AGAINST ILLEGALLY PRODUCED

COPYRIGHTED GOODS.

9. INDUSTRY AND EMBASSY EFFORTS AT IPR EDUCATION AND

ENFORCEMENT, WHILE EXTENSIVE, ARE NOT ENOUGH. OUR COMBINED

EFFORTS HAVE IMPROVED THE SITUATION, PARTICULARLY WITH LAW

ENFORCEMENT AND, IN SOME CASES, WITH THE JUDICIARY. HOWEVER,

SIAE HAS THE NATIONAL RESPONSIBILITY AND THE RESOURCES. WE

HAVE PRESSED SIAE TO INVOLVE ITSELF IN EDUCATION AND

ENFORCEMENT EFFORTS, BUT HAVE SEEN NO ACTION YET.

11. OVER THE PAST YEAR, THE LEVEL OF PIRACY HAS REMAINED

ALMOST UNCHANGED, BUT THE METHOD OF PIRACY HAS CHANGED FROM

LARGER VENUES TO SMALL-SCALE OPERATIONS USING COMMERCIAL CD-R

BURNERS. PIRACY ACCOUNTS FOR AN ESTIMATED 23 PERCENT OF THE

OVERALL MARKET IN ITALY, AND 50 PERCENT IN THE SOUTH OF

ITALY. BURNED DVDS ARE QUICKLY REPLACING PIRATED VIDEOS. THE

ESTIMATED LOSS TO THE U.S. MOTION PICTURE INDUSTRY DUE TO

AUDIOVISUAL PIRACY IN ITALY IS ESTIMATED TO BE 140 MILLION

USD. DVD PIRACY IS EXPECTED TO GROW IN 2003, REQUIRING

GREATER VIGILANCE BY THE POLICE AND DETERRENT SENTENCES BY

THE COURTS. ILLEGAL IMMIGRANTS ARE OFTEN EMPLOYED TO SELL

THE PIRATED GOODS ON STREET CORNERS THROUGHOUT ITALY. THERE

HAS BEEN INCREASED MEDIA COVERAGE OF THE ISSUE, AND JUDGES

ARE BEGINNING TO HAND DOWN SERIOUS SENTENCES FOR PIRACY.

COMMENT

-------

12. THE IIPA RECOMMENDS THAT ITALY REMAIN ON THE WATCH LIST.

IPR INDUSTRY REPRESENTATIVES HERE SHARE THAT ASSESSMENT. WE

AGREE. AS ITALY BEGINS TO IMPLEMENT ITS TOUGH COPYRIGHT LAW,

THE SITUATION IS IMPROVING, BUT MUCH REMAINS TO BE DONE. WE

NEED TO KEEP OUR MARKER DOWN AND VISIBLE. END COMMENT.
