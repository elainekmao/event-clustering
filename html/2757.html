SUBJECT: STEEL 201: ITALY NOT EXERCIZED

REF: STATE 65823

1. (SBU) SUMMARY. THE GOI CONTINUES TO SPEAK WITH RESTRAINT

ABOUT USG STEEL SAFEGUARDS -- DEFENDING LIMITED EU RESPONSIVE

SAFEGUARDS AND LOOKING TO BRUSSELS FOR A LEGAL OPINION ON THE

WTO-CONSISTENCY OF RETALIATION BUT AT THE SAME TIME CALLING

FOR AVOIDANCE OF A PROTECTIONIST SPIRAL AND FOREGOING

STRIDENT RHETORIC. ITALIAN INDUSTRY IS WATCHFUL BUT IS NOT

PUSHING THE GOI TO ACT. THE PRESS DOESN\'T TREAT STEEL AS A

MAJOR STORY. END SUMMARY.

2. (SBU) ITALIAN DEPUTY MINISTER FOR FOREIGN TRADE ADOLFO

URSO SET THE TONE OF GOI REACTION TO USG STEEL SAFEGUARDS

SHORTLY AFTER THEIR IMPLEMENTATION WHEN HE SAID THE EUROPEAN

STEEL GLUT HAD BEEN CAUSED IN GOOD MEASURE BY THE "INDULGENT"

EU MARKET ACCESS CONCESSIONS EXTENDED TO EU CANDIDATE STATES.

IN APRIL 9 PRESS COMMENTS, URSO DEFENDED EU STEEL SAFEGUARDS

BUT CONTINUED TO URGE RESTRAINT, SAYING, "THE MEASURES

ADOPTED BY THE EUROPEAN COMMISSION ARE NECESSARY, PRECISELY

TO FORCE THE UNITED STATES TO GO FOR THE POLITICAL SOLUTION

WE HAVE ALWAYS ADVOCATED AND PURSUED...BUT A PROTECTIONIST

SPIRAL MUST AT ALL COSTS BE PREVENTED (TO AVOID JEOPARDIZING)

THE NEGOTIATING ROUND SET IN MOTION IN DOHA AND THE MEASURES

TAKEN TO PROMOTE GROWTH IN THE DEVELOPING COUNTRIES."

3. (SBU) GIVING DEFINITION TO URSO\'S DEFENSE OF THE EU

SAFEGUARDS, SANDRO FANELLA, COMMON COMMERCIAL POLICY OFFICE

DIRECTOR AT THE DEPUTY MINISTRY FOR FOREIGN TRADE, TOLD

ECONOFF ON APRIL 11 THAT THE SAFEGUARDS WOULD BE ACTIVATED

ONLY FOR CATEGORIES OF STEEL FOR WHICH IMPORTS INCREASE IN

2002 TO LEVELS HIGHER THAN TEN PERCENT OF THE 1999-2001

YEARLY AVERAGE. DEFLECTING REFTEL\'S CRITICISM OF EU

SAFEGUARDS AS UNSUPPORTED BY AN INJURY DETERMINATION OR

IMPORT SURGE, FANELLA EXPLAINED THAT THE EU MEASURES HAD, IN

THIS MANNER, A BUILT-IN SURGE SENSOR. ECONOFF REPLIED THAT

THE SYSTEM AS DESCRIBED NEVERTHELESS APPEARS TO PRESUME

INJURY RATHER THAN ADJUDICATING THE QUESTION.

4. (SBU) FANELLA WAS LESS EXPANSIVE ON POSSIBLE EU

RETALIATION, SAYING HE IS AWAITING THE EU LEGAL OPINION OF

THE SAFEGUARDS AGREEMENT. HE SAID HIS MINISTRY IS NOT

SEPARATELY STUDYING THE AGREEMENT ON THE ARTICLES 8.2 AND 8.3

ISSUE. MORE GENERALLY, HE ALLEGED THAT THE USG "STARTED ALL

THIS" WITH UNILATERAL ACTION AND, IN HIS VIEW, IS NOT

WELL-POSITIONED TO BE COMPLAINING ABOUT COUNTERACTIONS. TO

ECONOFF\'S REFTEL DEFENSE OF USG SAFEGUARDS AS SUPPORTED BY

THE FINDINGS OF A NINE-MONTH ADJUDICATION, FANELLA SCOFFED

THAT THE U.S. FINDINGS ON INJURY WERE "IMAGINATIVE."

5. (SBU) FANELLA SAID HIS MINISTRY HAS NOT BEEN BESIEGED BY

ITALIAN INDUSTRY ABOUT THE U.S. SAFEGUARDS. INDUSTRY, HE

SAID, IS LOOKING CLOSELY FOR ANY SIGN OF A STEEL IMPORT SURGE

REFLECTING DIVERSION OF STEEL FROM THE U.S. MARKET BUT IS NOT

PUSHING THE GOI TO ADVOCATE RETALIATION WITHIN THE EU.

ECONOFF REPEATED REFTEL DEMARCHE AT THE MFA AND AT THE

MINISTRY OF PRODUCTIVE ACTIVITIES (NOW THE PARENT MINISTRY TO

THE DEPUTY MINISTRY FOR FOREIGN TRADE) WHERE GOI OFFICIALS

CONFIRMED THAT INDUSTRY IS WATCHING BUT NOT LOBBYING FOR A
