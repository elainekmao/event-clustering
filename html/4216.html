SUBJECT: VIETNAM - WHERE DISASTER PREPAREDNESS IS SERIOUS

BUSINESS

1. SUMMARY: USAID/DCHA/OFDA\'S REGIONAL COORDINATOR FOR

ASIA VISITED HANOI OCTOBER 27-30 TO REVIEW DISASTER

PREPAREDNESS AND RESPONSE ACTIVITIES INCLUDING THOSE FUNDED

BY OFDA. THE GVNGVN CONTINUES TO DEMONSTRATE AN EXCEPTIONAL

COMMITMENT TO DISASTER PREPAREDNESS THAT IS PERHAPS

UNMATCHED IN THE ASIA REGION. THIS COMMITMENT STEMS FROM A

DISASTER PREPAREDNESS CULTURE SHAPED BY VIETNAM\'S EXTREME

VULNERABILITY TO NATURAL DISASTERS SUCH AS FLOODS AND

TYPHOONS. FLOOD LEVELS IN THE MEKONG BASIN THIS YEAR WERE

COMPARABLE TO 2000 AND 2001, BUT EFFECTIVE GVN PREPAREDNESS

MEASURES GREATLY REDUCED LOSS OF LIFE AND PROPERTY.

USAID/OFDA IS SUPPORTING TWO ONGOING DISASTER PREPAREDNESS

PROJECTS IN VIETNAM, INCLUDING A FLOOD EARLY WARNING SYSTEM

IN CENTRAL VIETNAM AND A COASTAL STORM EARLY WARNING SYSTEM.

USAID/OFDA HAS ALSO PROVIDED EMERGENCY RELIEF ASSISTANCE TO

VIETNAM TOTALING ABOUT USD 1.3 MILLION SINCE 1999.

2. USAID/DCHA/OFDA\'S REGIONAL COORDINATOR FOR ASIA VISITED

3. A DISASTER PREPAREDNESS CULTURE: THE GVN CONTINUES TO

DEMONSTRATE AN EXCEPTIONAL COMMITMENT TO NATURAL DISASTER

PREPAREDNESS THAT IS PERHAPS UNMATCHED IN THE ASIA REGION.

GOVERNMENT INVESTMENTS IN DISASTER PREPAREDNESS DWARF

CONTRIBUTIONS FROM THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY, AND THE

DISASTER PREPAREDNESS AGENDA HERE IS CLEARLY DRIVEN BY

NATIONAL PRIORITIES AND NOT BY DONORS.

4. VIETNAM\'S COMMITMENT TO DISASTER PREPAREDNESS STEMS FROM

WHAT HAS BEEN CALLED A DISASTER PREPAREDNESS CULTURE, SHAPED

BY THE COUNTRY\'S HISTORIC VULNERABILITY TO FLOODS AND

TYPHOONS. RECENT REMINDERS OF THIS VULNERABILITY INCLUDE THE

DEVASTATING CENTRAL VIETNAM FLOODS OF 1999, AND THE MEKONG

RIVER FLOODS OF 2000, EACH RESULTING IN HUNDREDS OF DEATHS

AND PROPERTY LOSSES IN THE HUNDREDS OF MILLIONS OF DOLLARS.

EVEN HANOI ITSELF, PROTECTED FROM THE RED RIVER FOR

CENTURIES BY AN ELABORATE SYSTEM OF DIKES, IS FEARED TO BE

VULNERABLE NOW, AS NATURAL FLOOD PROTECTION SUCH AS UPSTREAM

FORESTATION IS DEPLETED.

5. THE GVN WAS REMARKABLY WELL-PREPARED FOR THIS YEAR\'S

MEKONG DELTA FLOODING WHICH PEAKED IN SEPTEMBER, ACCORDING

TO RELIEF AGENCIES WORKING IN THE AREA. WHILE FLOOD LEVELS

WERE COMPARABLE TO THOSE OF 2000 AND 2001, LOSS OF LIFE AND

PROPERTY WERE CONSIDERABLY REDUCED BY CREATION OF "ISLANDS"

AS SAFE HAVENS FOR EVACUEES AND THEIR BELONGINGS (IN MOST

CASES ELEVATED AND WIDENED ROADWAYS); EFFECTIVE PRE-

POSITIONING OF RELIEF SUPPLIES IN FLOOD-PRONE SUB-DISTRICTS;

AND STRENGTHENING OF SECONDARY DIKES WHICH PROTECTED MUCH OF

THE FOOD CROP LONG ENOUGH TO ENABLE HARVESTING.

6. THE COORDINATING BODY FOR NATURAL DISASTER RESPONSE AND

PREPAREDNESS IN VIETNAM IS THE CENTRAL COMMITTEE FOR FLOOD

AND STORM CONTROL, HEADED BY THE MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND

RURAL DEVELOPMENT, WHICH OVERSEES A NETWORK OF PROVINCIAL

AND DISTRICT-LEVEL COMMITTEES. THE PRIME MINISTER TAKES A

PERSONAL INTEREST IN THESE EFFORTS, AND LOCAL OFFICIALS IN

DISASTER-PRONE AREAS ARE SAID TO BE ACUTELY AWARE THAT JOB

SECURITY DEPENDS PARTLY ON THEIR PERFORMANCE IN PREPARING

FOR AND RESPONDING TO THESE DISASTERS.

PROJECTS IN VIETNAM: A FLOOD EARLY WARNING SYSTEM IN CENTRAL

8. THE USAID/OFDA-FUNDED CENTRAL VIETNAM PROJECT HAS

ALREADY RESULTED IN REGULAR FLOOD AND STORM WARNING

WILL EVENTUALLY RESULT IN STATE-OF-THE-ART FLOOD EARLY

TO THE VIETNAMESE LANGUAGE VERSION OF THE WEBSITE TO DATE.

9. THROUGH THE USAID/OFDA-FUNDED COASTAL STORM EARLY

WARNING PROJECT, TECHNOLOGY IS BEING DEVELOPED TO ENABLE

VIETNAM\'S SUBSISTENCE FISHING BOATS TO RECEIVE ADEQUATE

WARNING OF APPROACHING STORMS THROUGH LOW-COST, SIGNAL-

ACTIVATED RADIO RECEIVERS. VIETNAM HAS ABOUT 80,000

REGISTERED VESSELS ALONG ITS COASTLINE, MOST OF THEM SMALL

FISHING BOATS. THE VULNERABILITY OF THESE FISHING

COMMUNITIES TO COASTAL STORMS WAS DRAMATICALLY ILLUSTRATED

IN 1997 WHEN OVER 3,000 FISHERMEN AND WOMEN WERE LOST DURING

TYPHOON LINDA.

10. USAID/OFDA HAS ALSO PROVIDED EMERGENCY RELIEF THROUGH

EMBASSY HANOI IN RESPONSE TO FLOODS AND STORMS IN CENTRAL

AND SOUTHERN VIETNAM TOTALING ABOUT USD 1.3 MILLION SINCE

1999, MAINLY THROUGH THE RED CROSS AND NGOS. TWO TRAILER-

MOUNTED WATER PURIFICATION UNITS RECENTLY PROVIDED TO THE

GVN BY USAID/OFDA THROUGH CARE/VIETNAM, EACH CAPABLE OF

PRODUCING 200,000 LITERS OF DRINKING WATER DAILY, ARE NOW

BEING UTILIZED IN FLOOD-AFFECTED MEKONG COMMUNITIES.
