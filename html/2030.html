SUBJECT: MFA DECLINES TO SAY "YES" OR "NO" ON ELECTION OBSERVERS

BUT IMPLIES "NO"

CLASSIFIED BY POLITICAL SECTION CHIEF MATT HARRINGTON. REASONS:

1.5 (B) AND (D).

REF: A) STATE 169690 B) HARARE 2743 C) HARARE 2690

1. (C) IN AN OCTOBER 2 MEETING WITH MFA EUROPE AND AMERICAS

DIVISION DIRECTOR JONATHAN WUTAWUNASHE, CHARGE, ACCOMPANIED BY

POLITICAL SECTION CHIEF, REITERATED PREVIOUS EXPRESSIONS OF

CONCERN OVER THE GOZ\'S TREATMENT OF THE TEAM FROM THE

INTERNATIONAL FOUNDATION FOR ELECTION SYSTEMS (IFES). (N.B. THE

MEETING WAS A FOLLOW-UP TO A/S KANSTEINER\'S SEPTEMBER 27

DISCUSSION IN WASHINGTON WITH AMBASSADOR MUBAKO -- REF A). THE

CHARGE STRESSED THE USG\' S DISAPPOINTMENT THAT THE IFES TEAM HAD

BEEN ASKED TO LEAVE AFTER IT HAD REQUESTED APPOINTMENTS WITH

GOVERNMENT OFFICIALS RESPONSIBLE FOR OVERSEEING ELECTIONS. IFES

IS AN INTERNATIONALLY-RESPECTED ORGANIZATION THAT HAS OBSERVED

ELECTIONS WORLD-WIDE, AND THE USG STRONGLY ENDORSES ITS PROPOSED

ACTIVITIES IN ZIMBABWE. THE CHARGE POINTED OUT THAT ASSESSMENTS

OF THE ELECTORAL PROCESS FROM ORGANIZATIONS SUCH AS IFES WOULD BE

CRUCIAL TO INTERNATIONAL AND DOMESTIC PERCEPTIONS OF THE

LEGITIMACY OF THE 2002 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION. HE SAID HE

UNDERSTOOD THAT IFES INTENDS TO SEND ANOTHER TEAM TO ZIMBABWE IN

MID-OCTOBER, AND WE REQUESTED A SIMPLE "YES" OR "NO" AS TO WHETHER

THEY WOULD BE WELCOME. THE CHARGE STATED THAT WE WOULD INTERPRET

A LACK OF RESPONSE AS A "NO."

2. (C) WUTAWUNASHE REPLIED THAT THE GOZ WAS "AT A GREAT

DISADVANTAGE" LAST TIME BECAUSE IT DID NOT KNOW THE IFES TEAM WAS

COMING. HE SAID IT WOULD BE "PRUDENT" FOR ANYONE CONTEMPLATING A

VISIT TO A "SOVEREIGN COUNTRY" TO FAMILIARIZE HIMSELF WITH THE

NECESSARY PROCEDURES TO FOLLOW. THIS WAS NOT A VISIT THAT OUR TWO

GOVERNMENTS HAD DISCUSSED IN ADVANCE. THE CHARGE ACKNOWLEDGED THE

POINT BUT NOTED THAT IFES IS AN NGO, IS NOT AFFILIATED WITH THE

UNITED STATES GOVERNMENT, AND DID NOT REQUEST THE EMBASSY\'S

ASSISTANCE PRIOR TO ITS TEAM\'S VISIT IN EARLY SEPTEMBER.

WUTAWUNASHE SAID HE WAS NOT FAMILIAR WITH WHAT THE TEAM MEMBERS

HAD PUT ON THEIR VISA APPLICATIONS. PERHAPS THEY WERE NOT HONEST

ABOUT THEIR INTENDED WORK IN ZIMBABWE, AND IT MIGHT HAVE LOOKED TO

IMMIGRATION OFFICIALS LIKE A "SURREPTITIOUS ENTRY." IMMIGRATION

OFFICIALS ARE THE SAME THE WORLD OVER, WUTAWUNASHE ADDED.

3. (C) THE USG, THE CHARGE AFFIRMED, IS MORE INTERESTED IN

DETERMINING NEXT STEPS THAN IN REHASHING PAST DIFFICULTIES. HE

REMINDED WUTAWUNASHE THAT SPEAKER OF PARLIAMENT MNANGAGWA HAD

ASSURED US THAT INTERNATIONAL OBSERVERS WOULD BE WELCOMED BECAUSE

THAT WAS A TRADITION IN ZIMBABWE. THE USG IS NOW REQUESTING A

SIMPLE "YES" OR "NO" ON WHETHER THEY WOULD BE ALLOWED TO WORK

UNHINDERED. IN RESPONSE, WUTAWUNASHE STATED THAT HE WANTED THE

CHARGE TO UNDERSTAND THE PSYCHOLOGY PREVAILING IN OFFICIAL CIRCLES

IN HARARE. THERE IS A STRONG PERCEPTION AMONG MANY IN THE

ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT THAT THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY WANTS TO

REMOVE PRESIDENT MUGABE. THAT PERCEPTION, HE CONTINUED, HAD BEEN

STRENGTHENED BY RECENT CONFERENCES HELD IN WASHINGTON AND LONDON,

AT LEAST ONE ATTENDED BY FORMER A/S CHESTER CROCKER, WHERE

SCENARIOS WERE DISCUSSED ABOUT HOW MUGABE MIGHT BE CONVINCED OR

FORCED TO STEP DOWN. WHY ARE AFRICAN COUNTRIES NOT BEATING DOWN

THE DOOR TO OBSERVE THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION, HE ASKED, WHILE

OTHER NATIONS ARE ANXIOUS TO SEND PEOPLE AS SOON AS POSSIBLE? "WE

DON\'T EVEN HAVE A DATE SET FOR THE ELECTION YET," WUTAWUNASHE

SAID.

4. (C) THE EUROPE AND AMERICAS DIVISION DIRECTOR SAID HE HESITATED

TO PROVIDE A DEFINITIVE "YES" OR "NO" WITHOUT KNOWING MORE ABOUT

IFES. NGOS, HE DECLARED, ARE A DIME A DOZEN AND ARE NOT

CONSTRAINED BY DIPLOMATIC CONVENTIONS OR THE NEED TO MAINTAIN

BILATERAL RELATIONSHIPS. FOR THAT REASON, HE PERSONALLY PREFERRED

OFFICIAL OBSERVERS SENT BY GOVERNMENTS. THE CHARGE REITERATED

THAT IFES HAS AN OUTSTANDING INTERNATIONAL REPUTATION FOR

CREDIBILITY, NON-PARTISANSHIP, AND ELECTION-RELATED EXPERTISE. HE

CLARIFIED THAT IFES INITIALLY INTENDS TO CONDUCT AN ASSESSMENT OF

THE ELECTORAL ENVIRONMENT AND WILL THEN DETERMINE WHETHER IT WANTS

TO BECOME INVOLVED IN AN OBSERVATION EFFORT.

5. (C) WUTAWUNASHE AFFIRMED THAT THE GOZ WAS OPERATING "UNDER THE

SPIRIT OF THE ABUJA AGREEMENT," ACCORDING TO WHICH THE

INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY WOULD RESPOND TO GOZ REQUESTS FOR

ASSISTANCE WITH ITS ELECTORAL PROCESS. THE GOZ TOOK THE ABUJA

AGREEMENT SERIOUSLY, WUTAWUNASHE INSISTED, AND WOULD ISSUE

INVITATIONS "AT THE RIGHT TIME." IN THE MEANTIME, HE SUGGESTED

THAT GROUPS INTERESTED IN OBSERVING MAKE THE GOZ AWARE OF THAT

INTEREST. SHOULD IFES APPROACH THE ZIMBABWEAN EMBASSY IN

WASHINGTON TO VERIFY ITS BONA FIDES, THE CHARGE ASKED? YES,

WUTAWUNASHE RESPONDED, THEY SHOULD PROBABLY INTRODUCE THEMSELVES

TO THE EMBASSY AND EXPRESS THEIR INTEREST IN OBSERVING THE

ELECTION "WHEN THAT TIME COMES."

6. (C) WUTAWUNASHE\'S REMARKS SUGGEST THAT THE GOVERNMENT OF

ZIMBABWE IS VERY UNLIKELY TO PROVIDE, ANY TIME SOON, A CLEAR-CUT

"YES" OR "NO" ON THE ISSUE OF INTERNATIONAL ELECTION OBSERVERS.

HE IS RIGHT ABOUT THE PARANOIA REIGNING IN ZANU-PF\'S INNER CIRCLE,

WHERE MANY SENIOR OFFICIALS ARE GENUINELY CONVINCED THAT SOME

WESTERN GOVERNMENTS (INCLUDING THE U.S.) ARE USING NGOS AS A FRONT

TO WORK FULL-OUT FOR THE DEFEAT OF MUGABE AND THE RULING PARTY.

AS A RESULT, THE GOZ HAS CLEARLY GONE INTO STALL MODE, AND WILL

USE THE ABUJA AGREEMENT TO CLOAK ITS RELUCTANCE TO ADMIT

INTERNATIONAL OBSERVERS. WE SHOULD ASSUME THAT IF INVITATIONS ARE

ISSUED TO IFES AND SIMILAR GROUPS, IT WILL BE DONE ONLY AT THE

VERY LAST MINUTE, IN THE FIVE- TO EIGHT-WEEK PERIOD IN WHICH BY

REGULATION THE GOZ MUST ANNOUNCE ELECTIONS, AND TOO LATE FOR

OBSERVERS TO ASSESS FIRST-HAND THE DAMAGE DONE BY ZANU-PF\'S PRE-

ELECTION ANTICS. WUTAWUNASHE FAITHFULLY CONVEYED THE GOZ AND

RULING PARTY THINKING THAT THE WEST AND NGOS ARE NOT OBJECTIVE

OBSERVERS OF ZIMBABWE\'S POLITICAL SCENE, ALTHOUGH IT IS NOT CLEAR

THAT HE PERSONALLY SHARES THIS VIEW.
