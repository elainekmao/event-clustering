1. (C) SUMMARY: CARDINAL WALTER KASPER, THE VATICAN

OFFICIAL RESPONSIBLE FOR CHRISTIAN UNITY (INCLUDING THE

DIALOGUE WITH RUSSIAN ORTHODOX CHURCH) SAID THAT THE HOLY

CONFIDENTIAL

SEE\'S ATTEMPTS AT DIALOGUE WITH THE RUSSIAN ORTHODOX CHURCH

(ROC) ARE IN A DIFFICULT MOMENT AND NOT ADVANCING. KASPER

TOLD INCOMING DCM THAT THE ROC REMAINS TOO NATIONALISTIC AND

FALSELY PERCEIVES AS A THREAT THE TINY MINORITY OF CATHOLICS

IN RUSSIA -- JUST OVER 251,000 BAPTIZED CATHOLICS. ACCORDING

TO KASPER, THE POPE IS NOW RESIGNED TO THE LIKELIHOOD THAT HE

WILL NEVER VISIT RUSSIA. THE ROC NEVERTHELESS CONTINUES A

GOOD RELATIONSHIP WITH VARIOUS CATHOLIC INDIVIDUALS AND

AGENCIES - NOTABLY THOSE THAT FUND ROC ACTIVITIES. DESPITE

THE BECALMED HOLY SEE/ROC DIALOGUE, THE VATICAN WILL BE

PATIENT AND KEEP ITS DOORS OPEN TO COOPERATION WITH THE ROC

WHEREVER POSSIBLE. END SUMMARY.

DIALOGUE RUNS AGROUND

---------------------

2. (C) INCOMING DCM PAID A CALL JULY 16 ON CARDINAL WALTER

KASPER, PREFECT OF THE PONTIFICAL COUNCIL FOR PROMOTING

CHRISTIAN UNITY. QUERIED ON THE STATE OF CATHOLIC/RUSSIAN

ORTHODOX DIALOGUE, KASPER CONCEDED THAT IT WAS STALLED. HE

SHARED VATICAN DFM MIGLIORE\'S CONCERNS (REF B) AT THE GOR

REFUSAL OF VISAS TO CATHOLIC CLERGY -- ESPECIALLY BISHOP

MAZUR. HE SAID THE HOLY SEE HAD SENT A FORMAL, WRITTEN QUERY

FROM THE POPE TO PRESIDENT PUTIN SIX WEEKS AGO ASKING FOR

CLARIFICATION OF THE GROUNDS OF THAT VISA REFUSAL, BUT AS OF

YET HAD HAD NO RESPONSE (SOMETHING HE CHARACTERIZED AS

EXTRAORDINARY, UNDIPLOMATIC AND IMPOLITE). HE ALSO EXPRESSED

DISMAY AT THE RECENT HARSH RHETORIC COMING FROM THE RUSSIAN

PATRIARCH\'S SPOKESMAN ACCUSING THE CATHOLIC CHURCH OF

COMPETING WITH THE RUSSIAN ORTHODOX CHURCH.

RUSSIAN FEARS, VATICAN ASSURANCES

---------------------------------

3. (C) CARDINAL KASPER SAID THAT THERE WERE MANY REASONS

FOR THE LACK OF PROGRESS IN HOLY SEE/RUSSIAN ORTHODOX

DIALOGUE: THE ROC IS "TOO NATIONALISTIC," UNFOUNDED FEARS OF

A CATHOLIC THREAT TO ROC POWER AND INFLUENCE FROM THE 251,000

(BAPTIZED) CATHOLICS IN RUSSIA (VS. 105 MILLION RUSSIAN

CITIZENS), AND "HISTORICAL" CONCERNS RELATING TO

POLISH/RUSSIAN CONFLICTS IN CENTURIES PAST. (NOTE: IN REF A

WE REPORTED VATICAN NIS COUNTRY DIRECTOR TRICARICO\'S MORE

UNVARNISHED ASSESSMENT: "THEY ARE ALL FORMER COLLABORATORS

WHO HAVEN\'T BEEN ABLE TO SHAKE OLD HABITS." END NOTE.)

KASPER REPEATED THE VATICAN ASSERTION THAT THE CATHOLIC

CHURCH ABSOLUTELY DOES NOT "PROSELYTIZE" IN RUSSIA. HE SAID

THE CHURCH WOULD NOT TURN AWAY A RUSSIAN WHO IN GOOD

CONSCIENCE SOUGHT TO JOIN, AS THIS WAS A MATTER OF FREEDOM OF

RELIGION. BUT HE POINTED OUT THAT EVEN THE NUNS WHO CARE FOR

RUSSIAN ORPHANS ENSURE THAT THOSE CHILDREN GO TO ORTHODOX,

NOT CATHOLIC, CHURCHES EVERY SUNDAY. HE ACKNOWLEDGED THAT

SOME CATHOLICS MAY HAVE BEEN OVERZEALOUS, AS WERE SOME

ORTHODOX. BUT THAT WAS NOT VATICAN POLICY.

4. (C) KASPER EXPLAINED THAT THE RECENT CREATION OF FOUR

DIOCESES IN RUSSIA WAS NO MORE DECLARING RUSSIA TO BE

"VATICAN TERRITORY" THAN WAS DECLARING THE 300-PLUS DIOCESES

IN THE U.S.A. TO BE VATICAN TERRITORY. HE SAID THE CHURCH

HAD AN APOSTOLIC ADMINISTRATION IN RUSSIA IN CZARIST TIMES.

THE RENEWED APOSTOLIC ADMINISTRATIONS IN RUSSIA AND UKRAINE

KASPER REPLIED THAT IN HIS VIEW THE POPE HAD COME TO TERMS

WITH RUSSIAN PATRIARCH ALEXIY AND HAD GIVEN UP ON THAT

"DREAM." IT WAS PARTICULARLY TROUBLING TO THE PONTIFF

AND HAD BEGUN TO BUILD BRIDGES. KASPER THOUGHT THAT AS

FELLOW SLAVS, THE POPE AND ALEXIY WOULD HAVE A COMMON

6. (C) NOTWITHSTANDING CURRENT OBSTACLES, KASPER MADE CLEAR

THAT THE CHURCH WOULD BE PATIENT AND "KEEP ITS DOORS OPEN TO

THE ORTHODOX." KASPER NOTED (INNOCENTLY RATHER THAN

IRONICALLY) THAT ALTHOUGH THE RUSSIAN ORTHODOX CHURCH WON\'T

TALK TO THE HOLY SEE, IT DOES HAVE FRUITFUL CONTACTS WITH

CATHOLIC INDIVIDUALS AND AGENCIES -- ESPECIALLY THOSE, LIKE

THE SEVERAL GERMAN CHURCH FUNDING AGENCIES, WHICH PROVIDE

ALSO NOTED THE CATHOLIC/ORTHODOX DIALOGUE WORKS WELL IN

WESTERN COUNTRIES, WHICH KASPER ASCRIBED TO A MORE MODERN AND

OPEN WORLDVIEW OF MOST CITIZENS IN THE WEST.

COMMENT

-------

7. (C) KASPER\'S ASSERTION THAT THE POPE HAD GIVEN UP ON

VISITING MOSCOW WAS THE STRONGEST AFFIRMATION WE HAVE HAD

FROM A SENIOR CURIAL OFFICIAL THAT THE POPE HAS ACCEPTED THE

PROBABILITY THAT HE WILL NOT FULFILL HIS VISION OF OUTREACH

TO THE RUSSIAN ORTHODOX CHURCH. HE CONVEYED A STRONG SENSE

THAT HE AND THE POPE WERE TROUBLED BY THE HARDNESS OF RUSSIAN

ORTHODOX ATTITUDES, AND THAT HE WAS UNCERTAIN HOW TO OVERCOME

THEM. "IF PUTIN AND BUSH CAN COME TOGETHER," HE WONDERED,

"WHY CAN\'T THE PATRIARCH AND THE POPE?" KASPER AGREED THAT

CHANGES WOULD PROBABLY COME ONLY WHEN RUSSIA WAS BETTER

INTEGRATED WITH THE WEST AND HAD COME TO ACCEPT WESTERN NORMS

ON FREEDOM OF RELIGION.

8. (C) COMMENT CONTINUED: THE COUNCIL FOR PROMOTING

CHRISTIAN UNITY ORIGINATED IN THE VATICAN II REFORMS AND IS

RESPONSIBLE FOR PROMOTING DIALOGUE AND MUTUAL UNDERSTANDING

WITH OTHER CHRISTIAN DENOMINATIONS AS WELL AS WITH THE JEWISH

FAITH. CONSERVATIVES WITHIN THE ROMAN CURIA BELIEVE THE IDEA

OF DIALOGUE AS EQUALS WITH OTHER DENOMINATIONS VIOLATES CORE

PRECEPTS OF CATHOLICISM, AND THUS THEY ARE EQUIVOCAL ABOUT

THE COUNCIL\'S WORK. KASPER IS AN IMPORTANT THINKER ON THE

CHURCH\'S ROLE IN THE WORLD, AND FREQUENTLY ADDRESSES THE NEED
