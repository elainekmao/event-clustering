1. (C) SUMMARY: HOLY SEE CHINA DESK OFFICER, MONSIGNOR

GIANFRANCO ROTA-GRAZIOSI WELCOMED AMBASSADOR AT LARGE FOR

INTERNATIONAL RELIGIOUS FREEDOM HANFORD\'S EFFORTS TO

ENCOURAGE CHINA TO OPEN A DIALOGUE WITH THE HOLY SEE, BUT

EXPECTS LITTLE PROGRESS UNTIL AFTER THE LEADERSHIP PICTURE IS

CLEARER FOLLOWING THE CHINESE COMMUNIST PARTY\'S CONFERENCE

THIS FALL. HE DESCRIBED THE CHINESE SUGGESTION THAT THE

VATICAN\'S LACK OF CLARITY ON TAIWAN WAS THE BIGGEST OBSTACLE

TO PROGRESS AS A SMOKESCREEN FOR THE PRC\'S OWN INDECISION,

SINCE THE VATICAN HAD REPEATEDLY MADE CLEAR ITS WILLINGNESS

TO MOVE ITS EMBASSY TO BEIJING. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) DCM AND ACTING POLCHIEF MET AUGUST 12 WITH HOLY SEE

CHINA DESK OFFICER ROTA-GRAZIOSI TO REVIEW THE OUTCOME OF

AMBASSADOR HANFORD\'S AUGUST 5 VISIT TO CHINA (REFTELS) AND

DISCUSS POTENTIAL AREAS OF PROGRESS IN THE RUN-UP TO THE

PRESIDENT\'S OCTOBER MEETING WITH PRESIDENT JIANG. DCM

OBSERVED THAT AMBASSADOR HANFORD WAS SEEKING TO BUILD A

DIALOGUE WITH OFFICIALS RESPONSIBLE FOR RELIGIOUS FREEDOM

ISSUES AND TO IDENTIFY SPECIFIC AREAS WHERE PROGRESS MAY BE

POSSIBLE, INCLUDING RESUMPTION OF A DIALOGUE WITH THE

VATICAN, CHANGES IN RELIGIOUS REGISTRATION LAWS, AND THE

PROVISION OF RELIGIOUS EDUCATION TO CHILDREN.

3. (C) ON OPENING OF A DIALOGUE WITH THE VATICAN, DCM NOTED

THE PRC OFFICIALS\' ASSERTION THAT THEY WANTED TO NORMALIZE

RELATIONS WITH THE VATICAN, IF ONLY THE VATICAN COULD CLARIFY

ITS RELATIONS WITH TAIWAN. ROTA-GRAZIOSI SAW THIS AS A

DELAYING TACTIC TO MASK THE CHINESE OWN UNCERTAINTY OVER HOW

TO PROCEED ON RELIGIOUS ISSUES, AN UNCERTAINTY COMPOUNDED BY

THE UPCOMING COMMUNIST PARTY LEADERSHIP CONFERENCE. HE NOTED

THAT SECRETARY OF STATE SODANO HAS TWICE MADE EXPLICIT THE

HOLY SEE\'S WILLINGNESS TO MOVE ITS EMBASSY TO BEIJING

"TONIGHT NOT TOMORROW" IF THE PRC WAS PREPARED TO OPEN

RELATIONS, SO THERE IS LITTLE DOUBT WHERE THE HOLY SEE STANDS

ON TAIWAN. ROTA-GRAZIOSI SAID THE VATICAN WAS PREPARED TO

MAKE "THE LEAP OF FAITH" TO THE PRC\'S "ONE-CHINA" POLICY,

EVEN WHILE HE LAMENTED THAT THIS DID NOT REALLY REFLECT

TAIWAN\'S GLOBAL ROLE AND STATUS.

4. (C) REACTING TO THE CONCERN RAISED TO AMBASSADOR HANFORD

ABOUT THE NEED FOR THE POPE TO APOLOGIZE FOR THE OCTOBER 2000

CANONIZATION OF CHINESE MARTYRS, MONSIGNOR ROTA-GRAZIOSI

SAID THE POPE "WOULD NEVER APOLOGIZE FOR THE CANONIZATION."

ON THE OTHER HAND, HE NOTED THAT THE POPE HAD ALREADY

APOLOGIZED FOR THE CHURCH\'S "MISTAKES" IN THE PAST. HE

LAMENTED THAT THE HOLY SEE HAS YET TO HEAR ANY FORMAL

RECOGNITION OF OR RESPONSE TO THIS OCTOBER 2001 APOLOGY.

5. (C) ROTA-GRAZIOSI EXPRESSED APPRECIATION FOR USG EFFORTS

TO FOSTER A DIALOGUE BETWEEN THE HOLY SEE AND CHINA AND FOR

OUR EFFORT TO PRESS FOR SHORTER AND MEDIUM-TERM CHANGES IN

RESTRICTIVE CHINESE POLICIES. HE WAS SKEPTICAL, HOWEVER,

THAT CHINA WOULD BE ABLE TO MOVE FORWARD, ADDING THAT IT WILL

"TAKE YEARS, IF NOT DECADES" FOR THE CHINESE GOVERNMENT TO

TRULY BE PREPARED TO EMBRACE RELIGIOUS FREEDOM. HE EXPECTS

THE PRC WILL CONTINUE TO COMPLAIN ABOUT TAIWAN AND THE POPE\'S

CANONIZATION OF CHINESE MARTYRS TO AVOID AN OPEN DIALOGUE

WITH THE VATICAN. ROTA-GRAZIOSI SAID THE HOLY SEE BELIEVES

PROGRESS FOR THE CATHOLIC CHURCH IN CHINA CAN BEST BE MADE BY

PROMOTING RELIGIOUS FREEDOM IN THE BROADEST SENSE FOR ALL

BELIEFS. HE EXPRESSED HOPE THAT USG EFFORTS WILL PAY
