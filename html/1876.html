SUBJECT: SOUTH AFRICAN EMBASSY COMMENTS ON ZIMBABWE

CLASSIFIED BY DCM REWHITEHEAD DUE TO 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (U) SUMMARY. AS A CONTINUATION OF INITIAL COURTESY

CALLS, DCM MET WITH SOUTH AFRICAN COUNTERPART THOMAS RAMBAU

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 02398 01 OF 02 141109Z

ON AUGUST 8. RAMBAU ECHOED THE PESSIMISM THAT SEEMS TO BE

THE ORDER OF THE DAY IN HIS COMMENTS ON ELECTIONS, CONSULAR

ISSUES, WAR VETERAN VISITORS, PARLIAMENTARY DELEGATIONS,

AND DIPLOMATIC BANKING PROBLEMS. END SUMMARY.

------------------------

ELECTORAL ICEBERGS AHEAD

------------------------

2. (SBU) RAMBAU WAS NOT WELL INFORMED ON ANY POTENTIAL

SADC ELECTIONS MONITORING PROGRAM, BUT HE THOUGHT THAT IT

WAS POSSIBLE THAT SOUTH AFRICA MIGHT PROVIDE OBSERVERS

THROUGH SOME MECHANISM. HE AGREED THAT SOUTH AFRICA\'S

QUIET DIPLOMACY HAT YIELDED FEW RESULTS THUS FAR AND

EXPRESSED SURPRISE THAT MBEKI HAD SO FRANKLY ADMITTED THIS

DURING A BBC INTERVIEW EARLIER IN THE WEEK. HE SAID THAT

THE AIRING OF MBEKI\'S COMMENTS ON ZNBC WAS EVEN MORE

SURPRISING. HE SURMISED THAT THE LACK OF TACTICAL EDITING

HAD BEEN AN OVERSIGHT AND SAID THAT HE HAD HEARD THAT THE

PERSON RESPONSIBLE WAS IN THE CROSS-HAIRS OF INFORMATION

MINISTER MOYO.

------------------

CONSULAR HEADACHES

------------------

3. (C) RAMBAU RAISED THE ONGOING CONFLICT IN CHINHOYI,

OBSERVING THAT IT HAD LANDED PARTIALLY ON HIS DESK. FOUR

OF THE TWENTY-TWO COMMERCIAL FARMERS ARRESTED IN THE CASE

WERE BELIEVED TO HOLD SOUTH AFRICAN PASSPORTS. (SUBSEQUENT

REPORTS INDICATE THIS IS NOT CORRECT.) HE EXPLAINED THAT

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 02398 01 OF 02 141109Z

THIS WAS NOT AT ALL AN UNCOMMON OCCURRENCE. LARGE NUMBERS

OF WHITE ZIMBABWEANS HAD RESETTLED IN SOUTH AFRICA IN THE

EARLY 1980\'S AND TAKEN UP SOUTH AFRICAN CITIZENSHIP. MANY

OF THESE HAD CHOSEN TO RETURN TO ZIMBABWE LATER TO RECLAIM

FAMILY FARMS AND BUSINESSES, OFTEN IN PARTNERSHIP WITH

FAMILY MEMBERS WHO HAD RETAINED ZIMBABWEAN CITIZENSHIP. HE

DESCRIBED AN INCIDENT A WEEKEND EARLIER IN WHICH HE HAD

JUGGLED PHONE CALLS BETWEEN A SOUTH AFRICAN FARMER NEAR

MUTARE AND THE POLICE AS "WAR VETERANS" MOVED CLOSER TO THE

FARMER\'S HOUSE FROM SURROUNDING BARRICADES. THE FARMER,

WHO PLEADED FOR SOUTH AFRICAN INTERCESSION, NONETHELESS

REFUSED TO LEAVE THE HOUSE UNLESS HIS ZIMBABWEAN BROTHER

ACCOMPANIED HIM. RAMBAU SAID THAT EVENTUALLY HE CONVINCED

THE POLICE TO ESCORT BOTH OFF THE PROPERTY.

-----------------------

A RESPECTABLE WAR VET

-----------------------

4. (SBU) DCM QUERIED RAMBAU ON PRESS REPORTS OF A SOUTH

AFRICAN WAR VETERAN LEADER WHO HAD COME TO ZIMBABWE TO MEET

WITH HIS ZIMBABWEAN COUNTERPARTS. RAMBAU SAID THAT HE HAD

MET WITH THE MAN AND POINTED OUT THAT THE SOUTH AFRICAN WAR

VETERAN HAD PUBLICLY WAR VETERAN CONDEMNED VIOLENCE BECAUSE

IT SULLIED THE RESPECTABILITY OF THE ENTIRE MOVEMENT.

RAMBAU ADDED THAT THE SOUTH AFRICAN HAD CALLED FOR

COMPULSORY LAND ACQUISITION IN SOUTH AFRICA AS WELL AND

CALLED FOR A REGIONAL WAR VETERANS FRONT THAT INCLUDED

MOZAMBIQUE, MALAWI, AND ZAMBIA. DCM POINTED OUT THAT THIS

GOAL MIGHT PROVE ELUSIVE, GIVEN THAT TO THE BEST OF OUR

KNOWLEDGE THERE ARE NO WAR VETERANS IN TWO OF THESE THREE

PARLIAMENTARY DELEGATION OK, STATE VISIT NOT

--------------------------------------------

5. (C) RAMBAU SAID THAT PRESIDENT MBEKI, AT THE HIGH

COMMISSION\'S URGING, HAD NOT RESPONDED TO A RECENT

INVITATION FROM MUGAVE FOR A STATE VISIT. RAMBAU SAID THAT

THE SOUTH AFRICANS DID NOT WANT HIGH LEVEL CONTACTS TO BE

MISINTERPRETED AS CONDONING GOZ POLICY. A SOUTH AFRICAN

PARLIAMENTARY DELEGATION, HOWEVER, HAD JUST CONCLUDED A

VISIT TO ZIMBABWE. MOST OF THE MEETINGS TOOK PLACE BETWEEN

COMMITTEE COUNTERPARTS, BUT THERE HAD BEEN CONTACTS WITH

GOZ MINISTERS. RAMBAU DESCRIBED THE ENCOUNTER BETWEEN A

DR. MOHOWAN (SP), WHO CHAIRS THE SOUTH AFRICAN PARLIAMENT\'S

HOME AFFAIRS COMMITTEE, AND HOME AFFAIRS MINISTER NKOMO.

OF THE SOUTH AFRICAN PARLIAMENTARIAN, WHO TOLD NKOMO THAT

HE HAD READ WITH INTEREST THE GOZ FAST TRACK REGULATIONS,

MONEY TROUBLES FOR THE AFRICAN DIPLOMATIC CORPS

--------------------------------------------- --

7. (C) RAMBAU SAID THAT THE SOUTH AFRICAN MISSION HAS NOT

HAD SERIOUS PROBLEMS WITH THE GOZ, ALTHOUGH THIS IS NOT THE

CASE FOR A NUMBER OF AFRICAN EMBASSIES. THE FOREX SHORTAGE

HAD RESULTED IN SEVERAL BANKS INTERPRETING GOZ REGULATIONS

AS LIMITING THE FOREX THAT FOREIGN MISSIONS COULD ACCESS.

THIS HAD BEEN A REAL PROBLEM FOR EMBASSIES AND PERSONNEL

WHO SURVIVED ON DOLLAR TRANSFERS FROM HOME, AND WHO HAD NO

INTEREST IN BUYING ZIMBABWE DOLLARS AT THE OFFICIAL 55 RATE

OR WHO NEEDED U.S. CURRENCY FOR TRIPS HOME. A DELEGATION

FROM THE AFRICAN GROUP HAD MET THE MFA PERMANENT SECRETARY

TO COMPLAIN THAT THIS VIOLATING DIPLOMATIC NORMS. (THE

ZAMBIAN DCM OBLIQUELY REFERRED THIS MEETING IN A SEPARATE

MEETING.) RAMBAU ASKED IF NON-AFRICAN MISSIONS HAD

ENCOUNTERED SIMILAR PROBLEMS. DCM ADMITTED THAT AS A

NEWCOMER HE DID NOT KNOW.

8. (SBU) INITIAL MEETINGS WITH DIPLOMATS FROM ZIMBABWE\'S

AFRICAN NEIGHBORS HAVE THUS FAR BEEN CHARACTERIZED BY A

FRANKNESS THAT WE FIND UNUSUAL. THEIR COMMENTS ARE MORE OR

LESS CONGRUENT WITH WHAT WE ARE HEARING FROM EUROPEAN AND

CANADIAN COLLEAGUES. RAMBAU BROKE LITTLE NEW GROUND,

ALTHOUGH IT WAS AMUSING TO HEAR HIS COMMENTS ON THE

DIFFICULTY OF SCRIPTING VISITING PARLIAMENTARIANS, HARDLY A

NEW PHENOMENON ELSEWHERE.
