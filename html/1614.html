VATICAN SEEKS UN ROLE IN BIOTECH MONITORING

-------------------------------------------

2. THE VATICAN\'S PERMANENT OBSERVER TO UN FOOD AGENCIES,

ARCHBISHOP AGOSTINO MARCHETTO, OPINED THAT THE ENVIRONMENTAL

AND HUMAN HEALTH RISKS OF BIO-ENGINEERED FOOD DEMAND

STRONGER INTERNATIONAL NORMS AND CONTROLS. THE

REPRESENTATIVE SAID THE UN FOOD AND AGRICULTURAL

ORGANIZATION SHOULD CONSIDER TAKING A LEAD ROLE, ESPECIALLY

IN EDUCATION AND PREVENTATIVE MEASURES. ACCORDING TO

ARCHBISHOP MARCHETTO, HEALTH AND ENVIRONMENTAL ISSUES RAISED

BY GENETICALLY ALTERED FOODS HAVE A GLOBAL IMPACT AND THUS

REQUIRE INTERNATIONALLY GUARANTEED STANDARDS. HE SAID THE

FAO SHOULD ACTIVELY PURSUE "BIOSECURITY" QUESTIONS, DRAWING

ON ITS POSITION AS A CENTRALIZED AGENCY OF INFORMATION AND

RESEARCH ON AGRICULTURAL ISSUES. THE ARCHBISHOP SAID THIS

WOULD ALLOW THE ORGANIZATION TO CONTINUE TO HELP MEMBER

STATES ENACT CONTROL PROCEDURES FOR THE FOOD PRODUCTION

PHASE AND AS WELL AS WAYS TO COMBAT EMERGENCIES.

---------------------------------------------

VATICAN SAYS DRUG INJECTION ROOMS "UNETHICAL"

---------------------------------------------

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 VATICA 01744 01 OF 02 301041Z

3. THE VATICAN\'S PERMANENT OBSERVER THE UN AGENCIES IN

VIENNA, AUSTRIA, MONSIGNOR DOMINIQUE REZEAU, HAS DESCRIBED

DRUG INJECTION ROOMS WHERE ADDICTS CAN SHOOT UP UNDER

MEDICAL SUPERVISION AS "UNETHICAL AND MISGUIDED." REZEAU

SAID GOVERNMENT PROGRAMS SHOULD FOCUS INSTEAD ON ADDICTS\'

REHABILITATION AND REINTEGRATION INTO SOCIETY. HE SAID DRUG

DEPENDENCY WAS NOT A RIGHT OR A FREEDOM BECAUSE IT DAMAGED

THE ADDICT AND WAS A SELF-RENUNCIATION OF PERSONAL DIGNITY.

REZEAU SAID COUNTRIES IN FAVOR OF THE INJECTION ROOMS

POINTED TO REDUCTIONS IN OVERDOSE DEATHS, INFECTIONS AND

DISEASE TRANSMISSION AS BENEFITS OF THE INJECTING ROOM

PROGRAM. HE RETORTED THAT SUCH PROGRAMS HAD DISADVANTAGES

INCLUDING A NEGLIGIBLE DESIRE FOR SOCIAL REINTEGRATION.

MONSIGNOR REZEAU SAID DRUG REHABILITATION PROGRAMS EQUALLY

REDUCE INSTANCES OF OVERDOSE AND INFECTION, WHILE AT THE

SAME TIME HELPING ADDICTS TO RECOVER THEIR PLACE IN SOCIETY.

---------------------------------------

POPE ISSUES CHALLENGE TO SICILIAN MAFIA

---------------------------------------

4. IN A MESSAGE TO SICILY\'S BISHOPS, POPE JOHN PAUL II

STRESSED THE IMPORTANCE OF THE FIGHT AGAINST THE MAFIA. HE

SAID SICILY COULD ONLY DEVELOP ITS FULL POTENTIAL IF THE MOB

WERE DEFEATED. THE PONTIFF DESCRIBED THE MAFIA AS THE WORST

OF SICILY\'S EVILS.

VATICAN SPOKESMAN SAYS POPE IS "YOUNG FOR HIS AGE"

--------------------------------------------- ------

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 04 VATICA 01744 01 OF 02 301041Z

5. VATICAN SPOKESMAN, JOAQUIN NAVARRO-VALLS, CLAIMED MARCH

26 THAT POPE JOHN PAUL II REMAINS "YOUNG FOR HIS AGE" IN

SPIRIT AND ENTHUSIASM AND THAT IN THE 22 YEARS OF HIS PAPACY

HE HAS NEVER GIVEN UP ON A SINGLE THING HE SET OUT TO DO

BECAUSE OF HEALTH REASONS. (NOTE: NAVARRO-VALLS IS OVER-

ENTHUSIASTIC IN HIS APPRAISAL OF THE PAPAL CONSTITUTION. THE

POPE\'S SCHEDULE HAS IN FACT BEEN ALTERED ON MORE THAN ONE

OCCASION TO ACCOMMODATE HIS ILLNESS. HE ALMOST COLLAPSED

DURING ONE EVENT BECAUSE OF A STOMACH VIRUS AND HAD TO BE

HELPED BACK INTO HIS APARTMENT. END NOTE). NAVARRO-VALLS

CLAIMED THE POPE HAS THE PHYSICAL CAPACITY FOR THE WORK HE

IS DOING. THE COMMENTS CAME AHEAD OF THE PONTIFF\'S UP-COMING

PILGRIMAGE TO SYRIA, GREECE AND MALTA. RECENT VISITORS TO

VATICAN CITY, INCLUDING AN AMCIT KOREAN BISHOP, HAVE

DESCRIBED THE POPE AS HAVING GOOD AND BAD DAYS.
