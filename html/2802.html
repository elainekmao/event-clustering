SUBJECT: FAO LOCUST CONTROL EFFORTS IN AFGHANISTAN

-------

SUMMARY

-------

1. U.S. MISSION REPRESENTATIVES MET WITH FAO LOCUST EXPERTS

ON APRIL 17 TO DISCUSS FAO LOCUST CONTROL ACTIVITIES IN

AFGHANISTAN. FAO PROVIDED A STATUS REPORT ON LOCUST CONTROL

BY PROVINCE AND DISCUSSED CONCERNS OVER PRESSURES, IN CERTAIN

AREAS OF AFGHANISTAN, FOR FAO AND COOPERATING PARTNERS TO

PROVIDE FOOD TO FARMERS AS PAYMENT FOR PARTICIPATING IN

MECHANICAL LOCUST CONTROL. U.S. MISSION OPINED THAT UNDER

"NORMAL" CONDITIONS FOOD IN EXCHANGE FOR LOCUST CONTROL MAY

NOT BE A GOOD IDEA IN TERMS OF SUSTAINABILITY; HOWEVER ,

AFGHANISTAN MAY BE A SPECIAL CASE WHERE THIS OPTION SHOULD BE

CONSIDERED TO AVOID A SERIOUS OUTBREAK. FAO AGREED TO MONITOR

THE SITUATION CLOSELY AND TO PROVIDE U.S. MISSION/USAID WITH

FREQUENT UPDATES ON THE SITUATION. FAO ALSO EXPRESSED THE NEED

FOR DONOR SUPPORT OF A LONGER-TERM LOCUST CONTROL EFFORT IN

THE REGION. END SUMMARY.

----------

BACKGROUND

----------

2. ON APRIL 17 THE U.S. MISSION\'S HUMANITARIAN AFFAIRS

ATTACHE, TIM LAVELLE AND PROGRAM SPECIALIST JANE STANLEY MET

WITH THE FOLLOWING LOCUST EXPERTS IN FAO\'S PLANT PRODUCTION

AND PROTECTION DIVISION: DR. ABDERRAHMANE HAFRAOUI, HEAD OF

THE LOCUST, OTHER MIGRATORY PESTS AND EMERGENCY OPERATIONS

GROUP; DR CLIVE ELLIOTT, SENIOR OFFICER FOR MIGRATORY PESTS,

AND DR. ANNIE MONARD, LOCUST CONTROL OFFICER. DR. MONARD WAS

IN AFGHANISTAN IN FEBRUARY TO PARTICIPATE IN THE DESIGN OF

CURRENT LOCUST CONTROL ACTIVITIES.

3. AS BACKGROUND, FAO ADVISED THAT THEY SUCCESSFULLY

IMPLEMENTED MECHANICAL LOCUST CONTROL ACTIVITIES IN

AFGHANISTAN FROM 1990 TO 2000, AND PROVIDED A SIGNIFICANT

AMOUNT OF TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE AND TRAINING TO LOCAL FARMERS

AND AFGHAN AGRICULTURE OFFICIALS DURING THIS TIME PERIOD. FAO

DID NOT PROVIDE LOCUST CONTROL ASSISTANCE IN 2001 AND

THEREFORE THERE ARE NO RECENT SURVEYS OF THE EXTENT OF THE

PROBLEM. IN 2001, WFP DID SUPPORT SOME MECHANICAL LOCUST

CONTROL ACTIVITIES UNDER A FOOD FOR WORK ACTIVITY IN BAGHLAN

PROVINCE.

---------------------

THE CURRENT SITUATION

---------------------

4. FAO STATED THAT A TOTAL OF 90,000 COMMUNITY MAN-DAYS HAVE

BEEN MOBILIZED TO DATE FOR MECHANICAL LOCUST CONTROL EFFORTS.

FAO IS NOT CURRENTLY PROVIDING FOOD AS PAYMENT FOR LOCUST

CONTROL. THERE HAD BEEN SOME RESISTANCE TO THIS IN BAGHLAN

PROVINCE WHERE WFP SUPPORTED LOCUST CONTROL UNDER A FOOD FOR

WORK PROGRAM. FAO\'S EFFORTS CONSIST PRIMARILY OF MECHANICAL

CONTROL MEASURES BY LOCAL FARMERS AND AGRICULTURE OFFICIALS,

WITH SOME CHEMICAL CONTROL IN ROCKY AND INACCESSIBLE AREAS.

CHEMICAL CONTROL MAY ALSO BE USED IN AREAS LIKE BAGHLAN WHERE

THERE HAS BEEN RESISTANCE TO COMMUNITY MOBILIZATION. THE

CURRENT SITUATION IN THE NORTHERN PROVINCES, WHERE LOCUSTS ARE

MOST ABUNDANT, IS AS FOLLOWS:

SAMARGAN: THE LOCAL COMMUNITY IS MOBILIZED AND THE LOCAL

GOVERNMENT IS FULLY SUPPORTIVE WITHOUT FOOD PAYMENT.

BAGHLAN: THIS IS WHERE LOCAL FARMERS RECEIVED FOOD AS PAYMENT

FOR LOCUST CONTROL IN THE PAST AND THERE HAVE BEEN PROBLEMS

WITH COMMUNITY MOBILIZATION. FAO\'S REPRESENTATIVES IN THE

FIELD NOW REPORT THAT THIS PROBLEM HAS BEEN OVERCOME AND THAT

THE DEPUTY GOVERNOR HAS AGREED TO MOBILIZATION IN RETURN FOR

FUEL TO GET VEHICLES TO THE FIELD. FAO IS ALSO INITIATING

CHEMICAL CONTROL IN THIS PROVINCE.

5. FAO EXPERTS FURTHER NOTED THAT WHILE THEY HOPE EARLY

ACTION WITH MECHANICAL AND A LITTLE CHEMICAL CONTROL WILL BE

EFFECTIVE, THESE EFFORTS ARE MERELY PALLIATIVE. WHAT IS

REALLY NEEDED IS AN INITIATIVE DIRECTED AT LONG TERM

PREVENTION. IN PARTICULAR, SURVEYS ARE NEEDED IN SEMI ARID

AREAS TO LOCALIZE AND CONTAIN LOCUSTS BEFORE THEY BECOME A

PROBLEM.

6. US MISSION REP STATED THAT ACHIEVING RESULTS IS OF UTMOST

IMPORTANCE AT THIS STAGE IN THE REHABILITATION EFFORTS IN

AFGHANISTAN. HE OPINED THAT AFGHANISTAN IS NOT A "NORMAL"

SITUATION AND THAT FAO MAY HAVE TO CONSIDER OPTIONS (SUCH AS

FOOD AID PAYMENT FOR MECHANICAL LOCUST CONTROL) THAT THEY

WOULD NOT OTHERWISE USE IN DEVELOPMENT PROGRAMS. FAO WAS

FUNDING FOR LOCUST CONTROL

--------------------------

7. FAO ADVISED THAT CURRENT FUNDING IS ADEQUATE FOR ITS SHORT-

TERM LOCUST CONTROL EFFORTS. THE BREAKDOWN IS AS FOLLOWS:

USAID/OFDA USD. 260,000

FAO USD. 300,000

DFID/UK USD. 260,000

ECHO /EC USD. 300,000

TOTAL USD. 1,120,000

8. FAO REPS DID EXPRESS A NEED FOR FUNDING OF LONGER TERM

LOCUST CONTROL EFFORTS, INCLUDING THE REHABILITATION OF THE

AFGHAN PLANT PROTECTION DEPARTMENT AND THE DEVELOPMENT OF A

SOUTHWEST ASIA REGIONAL LOCUST CONTROL COMMISSION (WHICH WOULD

ALSO INCLUDE THE CENTRAL ASIAN REPUBLICS AND PAKISTAN),

SIMILAR TO THE REGIONAL LOCUST COMMISSIONS FAO IS SUPPORTING

IN AFRICA. U.S. MISSION REPS OFFERED TO PASS ALONG ANY FUNDING

REQUESTS TO APPROPRIATE USAID PERSONNEL IN WASHINGTON AND THE

FIELD.

COMMENT

-------

9. USMISSION RELAYED THE MESSAGE THAT: (1) FAO MANAGEMENT MUST

FOCUS ON "MOVING FROM PLAN TO ACTION;" AND (2) LET\'S NOT LET

THE "PERFECT BE THE ENEMY OF THE GOOD." FAO IS STRONGLY

ENCOURAGED TO RATCHET UP WHEREVER APPROPRIATE ITS EFFORTS WITH

LOCAL AIA AUTHORITIES, NGOS AND OTHERS IN THE UNITED NATIONS

FAMILY. WE DO BELIEVE THAT, WITH FAO\'S SPECIAL RELIEF

OPERATIONS (TCOR) SERVICE (DR. ANNE BAUER AND RICHARD CHINA)

PRESENTLY MANAGING THE OVERALL FAO RESPONSE IN AFGHANISTAN, A

RENEWED SENSE OF URGENCY IS BEING INSTILLED.

10. MINIMIZE CONSIDERED. HEILEMAN
