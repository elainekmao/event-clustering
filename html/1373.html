1. (C) SUMMARY: DURING AN HOUR AND A HALF MEETING

NOVEMBER 28 MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS PERMANENT

SECRETARY WILLARD CHIWEWE SOUGHT AMBASSADOR MCDONALD\'S

SIPDIS

PRACTICAL ADVICE ON HOW TO ENSURE THAT UNDP

ADMINISTRATOR MARK MALLOCH-BROWN\'S VISIT TO ZIMBABWE WAS

A SUCCESS. THE PERMANENT SECRETARY SAID HE WAS LOOKING

FOR WAYS TO BUILD PRESIDENT MUGABE\'S CONFIDENCE IN ORDER

TO ACCEPT CERTAIN REQUIREMENTS THAT INTERNATIONAL

COMMUNITY WILL HAVE WITH RESPECT ZIMBABWE\'S LAND REFORM

PROGRAM, AND SOUGHT A SIGNAL FROM THE USG TO BOLSTER THE

ADMINISTRATOR\'S MISSION. THE AMBASSADOR SAID HE WOULD

HAVE TO CONSULT WITH WASHINGTON REGARDING HIS REQUEST,

BUT ADVISED THE PRESIDENT NOT TO BE DEFENSIVE AND

READILY AGREE TO RESTORE THE RULE OF LAW WITH RESPECT TO

THE COMMERCIAL FARM OCCUPATIONS. CHIWEWE TOLD THE

AMBASSADOR THAT MUGABE WANTED TO SPEND SOME SUBSTANTIAL

TIME WITH MALLOCH-BROWN AND WOULD GIVE THOSE KINDS OF

ASSURANCES. WHEN THE AMBASSADOR SUGGESTED THAT THE

CLEAREST SIGNAL PRESIDENT MUGABE COULD SEND TO THE WEST

DEMONSTRATING HIS SERIOUSNESS WOULD BE TO CALL ON THE

WAR VETERANS TO LEAVE THE FARMS, THE PERMANENT SECRETARY

SAID THAT THAT WOULD NOT BE POSSIBLE UNDER THE

CIRCUMSTANCES. INSTEAD, HE SUGGESTED AN ACCELERATED

ACQUISITION AND RESETTLEMENT PROCESS. WE BELIEVE THAT

CHIWEWE HAS BEEN AUTHORIZED TO ENGAGE IN SERIOUS

NEGOTIATIONS WITH THE UNDP AND GENUINELY WANTS USG

INPUT. IF WE COULD REPACKAGE THE UNDERSTANDINGS REACHED

IN THE 1998 DONORS CONFERENCE AND SECURE ADDITIONAL

PLEDGES, WE MIGHT HAVE A WIN-WIN PROPOSITION ON OUR

HANDS. CHIWEWE SEEMED TO TAKE OUR POINTS ON BOARD AND

2. (C) DURING A ONE AND A HALF-HOUR MEETING NOVEMBER 28

MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS PERMANENT SECRETARY WILLARD

CHIWEWE INFORMED AMBASSADOR MCDONALD THAT HE HAD BEEN

BUSY MAKING PREPARATIONS FOR THE NOVEMBER 30 VISIT OF

UNDP ADMINISTRATOR MARK MALLOCH-BROWN. ON NOVEMBER 27

HE HAD CONSULTED UNDP RESIDENT REPRESENTATIVE VICTOR

ANGELO AND ZIMBABWEAN PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVE TO THE

UNITED NATIONS TICHAONA JOKONYA, AND THOUGHT HE SHOULD

TOUCH BASE WITH THE "CHIEF GLOBAL PLAYER," SO HE ASKED

THE AMBASSADOR TO COME OVER FOR A PRACTICAL DISCUSSION

ABOUT HOW TO GET SOMETHING DONE. CHIWEWE CAME STRAIGHT

TO HIS POINT: HE ASKED WHETHER THE USG COULD "GIVE A

SIGNAL" TO THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE THAT MALLOCH-BROWN

IS SERIOUS AND THAT THE DONOR COMMUNITY SUPPORTS HIM.

THE PERMANENT SECRETARY HAD ASKED ANGELO WHETHER THE

UNDP COULD CONTRIBUTE A SYMBOLIC AMOUNT TO THE FUND FOR

LAND REFORM, BUT THE LATTER UNDERSTANDABLY MADE NO

COMMITMENT.

3. (C) AMBASSADOR MCDONALD REPLIED THAT HE WOULD HAVE

TO CONSULT WITH WASHINGTON REGARDING HIS REQUEST. THAT

SAID, THE AMBASSADOR UNDERSCORED THE FACT THAT THIS WAS

A "CRITICAL, CRITICAL" MEETING, BECAUSE ZIMBABWE WAS AT

A FORK IN THE ROAD. HE TOLD CHIWEWE THAT PRESIDENT

MUGABE SHOULD EMBRACE THIS OPPORTUNITY TO MOVE FORWARD.

THE PERMANENT SECRETARY THEN ASKED THE AMBASSADOR, "WHAT

DO YOU WANT THE PRESIDENT TO DO?" CHIWEWE UNDERSCORED

THAT HE WAS LOOKING FOR CANDID ADVICE. THE AMBASSADOR

REPLIED THAT THE PRESIDENT SHOULD SAY TO MALLOCH-BROWN

FARMS AND MOVED PEOPLE ONTO THOSE FARMS. "IT HAS TO BE

ACQUISITION, REMOVAL, ACQUISITION, REMOVAL, IN ORDER TO

KEEP THE SITUATION CONTROLLED," HE ARGUED. IF THE

GOVERNMENT WERE TO PROVOKE POLITICAL DISCONTENT WE COULD

5. (C) AMBASSADOR MCDONALD THEN SUGGESTED THAT THE

PRESIDENT MIGHT WISH TO MEET MALLOCH-BROWN FOR AN

EXTENDED PERIOD ON THURSDAY AFTER HIS ARRIVAL AND THEN

BEGIN DRAFTING A CLEAR STATEMENT OF SUBSTANTIVE

PRINCIPLES, AFTER WHICH THE TWO WOULD MEET TO ANNOUNCE

IT. CHIWEWE AGREED WITH THE CONCEPT, BUT SUGGESTED THE

FOREIGN MINISTER AND MALLOCH-BROWN DO IT, AS THEY ARE ON

THE SAME LEVEL. THE AMBASSADOR SUGGESTED THAT THE UNDP

ADMINISTRATOR NEEDED TO LEAVE ZIMBABWE BEING ABLE TO SAY

THAT HE MET PRESIDENT MUGABE AND THAT THE PRESIDENT

WANTS THE RULE OF LAW TO PREVAIL ON THE COMMERCIAL

FARMS. HE WILL NEED THAT GUARANTEE FROM THE PRESIDENT,

THE AMBASSADOR STRESSED. "AND HE WILL GET IT," THE

PERMANENT SECRETARY CONCLUDED.

6. (C) COMMENT: IN HIS OWN BLUNT AND PECULIAR MANNER,

CHIWEWE IS ATTEMPTING TO BUILD BRIDGES FROM HIS

GOVERNMENT TO THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY. HE MUST HAVE

THE PRESIDENT\'S NOD TO ENGAGE IN THESE PRACTICAL

NEGOTIATIONS. THE WORK HE HAS PUT IN PREPARING FOR THE

MALLOCH-BROWN VISIT IS INDICATIVE OF THE IMPORTANCE HIS

GOVERNMENT ATTACHES TO IT. WE HAVE NOT SEEN THOROUGH

PREPARATION LIKE THIS BEFORE ANY OTHER SUCH GATHERING.

THE WAY WE SEE IT, THE GOVERNMENT EITHER WILL TAKE THE

OPPORTUNITY GIVEN BY THE VISIT TO DEFUSE TENSIONS AND

BEGIN A SANE LAND REFORM PROGRAM, OR STEP UP THE PACE

AND VIOLENCE OF THE CURRENT LAND GRAB. WE NEED TO THINK

CREATIVELY OF WAYS WE CAN ENCOURAGE THE GOZ IN THE RIGHT

DIRECTION.

7. (C) COMMENT CONTINUED: WE CAN SUGGEST ONE WAY: TAKE

THE LAND AND REFORM RESETTLEMENT PROGRAM THAT THE GOZ

AND THE DONORS HAD AGREED TO IN 1998; THROW IN SOME NEW

PLEDGES (FROM DONORS AND THE CFU) TO GET THE GOZ UP TO

THE 5 MILLION HECTARES IT IS LOOKING FOR; "REPACKAGE" IT

COMPLETE WITH A CLEVER NEW NAME THAT SINGS "LAND TO THE

PEOPLE" TO ZIMBABWEANS AND "RULE OF LAW" TO CIVIL

SOCIETY AND THE DONOR COMMUNITY; LET MUGABE DECLARE

VICTORY AND CALL THE WAR VETERANS OFF THE FARMS; AND GET

THE LAND PROGRAM BACK "ON-TRACK" AND THIS ECONOMIC AND

POLITICAL TRANSITION OFF THE DIME. WITH THE RIGHT

NEGOTIATING SKILLS, THIS COULD BE PULLED OFF IN AN

ENVIRONMENT WHERE EVERYONE NEEDS A WIN. END COMMENT.

MCDONALD
