PAPAL APPEAL FOR DEMOCRACY AND SOVEREIGNTY IN LEBANON

--------------------------------------------- --------

2. POPE JOHN PAUL II APPEALED AUGUST 19 TO LEBANESE

AUTHORITIES NOT TO SACRIFICE THE "VALUES OF DEMOCRACY AND

NATIONAL SOVEREIGNTY" BECAUSE OF TRANSITORY POLITICAL

INTERESTS. RESPONDING TO THE DETENTION OF CHRISTIAN ANTI-

SYRIAN ACTIVISTS IN LEBANON, THE PONTIFF SAID THAT A

"PLURALISTIC AND FREE LEBANON" IS OF BENEFIT TO THE ENTIRE

MIDDLE EAST REGION. IN A REGULAR SUNDAY ADDRESS, THE POPE

SAID LEBANON ALREADY SUFFERED FROM INTERNAL DIVISIONS. THE

PAPAL APPEAL FOR LEBANON WAS GIVEN FRONT-PAGE PROMINENCE IN

THE AUGUST 20 EDITION OF THE VATICAN\'S SEMI-OFFICIAL DAILY

"L\'OSSERVATORE ROMANO." THE TEXT OF THE POPE\'S WORDS WAS

FOLLOWED BY A DETAILED ASSESSMENT OF THE EVENTS SURROUNDING

THE DETENTION OF THE CHRISTIAN ACTIVISTS.

VATICAN EUROS TO HAVE PAPAL IMAGE

---------------------------------

3. EURO COINS BEARING THE IMAGE OF POPE JOHN PAUL II WILL

SOON BE USED TO PURCHASE EVERYTHING FROM A CUP OF COFFEE IN

ROME TO BAKLAVA IN ATHENS OR A PINT OF GUINNESS IN DUBLIN.

THE ITALIAN TREASURY ANNOUNCED AUGUST 17 THAT IT WILL MINT A

SMALL QUANTITY OF EURO COINS FOR THE VATICAN BEARING THE

IMAGE OF THE POPE. UNDER AN ACCORD WITH ITALY SIGNED EARLIER

OF EUROS 670,000 (US DOLLARS 615,000), WHICH IS ABOUT THE

SAME MINTED UNTIL NOW IN ITALIAN LIRA. LIKE OTHER

PARTICIPATING COUNTRIES, THE VATICAN WILL DESIGN ONE SIDE OF

ITS EURO COINS; THE OTHER SIDE IS COMMON TO THE WHOLE EURO

ZONE. IN THE PAST, EUROPEAN NEWSPAPERS HAVE REPORTED THAT

AUTHORITIES IN FRANCE RESISTED VATICAN EUROS BEARING AN

IMAGE OF THE POPE BECAUSE OF CHURCH-STATE CONCERNS.

-------------------------------

POSSIBLE PAPAL TRIP TO BULGARIA

-------------------------------

4. COMMENTS TO THE PRESS BY THE PAPAL REPRESENTATIVE IN

BULGARIA, ARCHBISHOP ANTONIO MENNINI, HAVE IGNITED

SPECULATION ABOUT A 2002 VISIT TO SOFIA BY POPE JOHN PAUL

II. SPEAKING TO JOURNALISTS AFTER A MEETING AT THE FOREIGN

MINISTRY, MENNINI SAID BULGARIAN AUTHORITIES "ARE LOOKING

FORWARD" TO A PAPAL VISIT. ALTHOUGH THE ORTHODOX CHURCH IN

BULGARIA HAS OBJECTED TO A PAPAL VISIT, ARCHBISHOP MENNINI

SAID A VISIT FROM THE POPE WOULD BE "A CLEAR SIGN OF THE

RESPECT WE HAVE FOR THE BULGARIAN ORTHODOX CHURCH."
