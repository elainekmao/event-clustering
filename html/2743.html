ECONOMIC AND SOCIAL COUNCIL SICHAN SIV AND L/HRR MICHAEL

DENNIS MET 4/10 WITH OFFICIALS FROM THE MINISTRIES OF

LABOR/WELFARE AND FOREIGN AFFAIRS TO DISCUSS ISSUES BEFORE

THE 5/8-10 UN SPECIAL SESSION ON CHILDREN (REF). BOTH OF

THEIR LABOR/WELFARE MINISTRY INTERLOCUTORS (UNDERSECRETARY

GRAZIA SESTINI AND DIPLOMATIC COUNSELOR ALBERTO COLELLA) WILL

BE MEMBERS OF THE ITALIAN DELEGATION TO THE SPECIAL SESSION.

OF THEIR MFA INTERLOCUTORS -- CLAUDIO BISOGNIERO, RAFAELE DI

LUTIO, EMANUELE PIGNATELLI -- THE LATTER WILL BE THE SENIOR

MFA OFFICIAL IN THE DELEGATION.

2. (C) AMBASSADOR SIV SAID THAT, WHILE THE U.S. CAN ACCEPT

THAT THE CONVENTION ON THE RIGHTS OF THE CHILD CONTAINS

STANDARDS OF IMPORTANCE, IT COULD NOT ACCEPT THAT THE

CONVENTION ESTABLISHED THE STANDARD FOR ACTIONS CONCERNING

THE PROTECTION OF CHILDREN. HE SAID THE U.S. SENATE IS

CONSIDERING RATIFICATION OF THE CONVENTION\'S TWO OPTIONAL

PROTOCOLS, AND THAT THE ADMINISTRATION HOPED TO SECURE ADVICE

AND CONSENT TO RATIFICATION BEFORE THE START OF THE SPECIAL

SESSION. HE NOTED, HOWEVER, THAT VARIOUS SENATORS HAD

EXPRESSED CONCERN ABOUT THE CONVENTION ITSELF. REFERRING TO

THE SESSION\'S OUTCOME DOCUMENT, AMBASSADOR SIV SAID THE USG

CANNOT ACCEPT THE PHRASE "REPRODUCTIVE HEALTH SERVICES" SINCE

OTHER DELEGATIONS HAVE ASSERTED THAT THIS REFERS TO ABORTION.

ON THE OTHER HAND, HE NOTED, "REPRODUCTIVE HEALTH CARE" IS

FINE, IF NOT LINKED TO THE PREVIOUS UN CONFERENCE OUTCOME

DOCUMENTS FROM CAIRO ON POPULATION AND DEVELOPMENT AND FROM

BEIJING ON WOMEN. AMBASSADOR SIV WELCOMED LANGUAGE IN THE

3/18 TEXT PROPOSED BY THE CHAIRMAN OF THE PREPARATORY

COMMITTEE AS A GOOD BASIS FOR NEGOTIATION. MR. DENNIS

FURTHER CLARIFIED SPECIFIC CHANGES THAT THE U.S. WOULD NEED

TO THE DRAFT TEXT CONCERNING REFERENCES TO THE CONVENTION ON

THE RIGHTS OF THE CHILD, REPRODUCTIVE HEALTH, CHILDREN IN

3. (C) WHILE OFFICIALS AT BOTH MINISTRIES WERE FORTHCOMING,

LABOR/WELFARE UNDERSECRETARY SESTINI SAID UNEQUIVOCALLY THAT

ITALY SHARES U.S. VIEWS ON ABORTION AND WOULD SUPPORT OUR

OPPOSITION TO "REPRODUCTIVE HEALTH SERVICES." UNDERSECRETARY

SESTINI NOTED A DIFFERENCE WITH U.S. VIEWS REGARDING HEALTH.

WHEREAS U.S. LAW GUARANTEES CITIZENS "ACCESS" TO REPRODUCTIVE

HEALTH CARE, THE ITALIAN CONSTITUTION GUARANTEES CITIZENS A

"RIGHT" TO HEALTH. THE GOI DELEGATION TO THE SPECIAL SESSION

ACCORDINGLY WILL SEEK LANGUAGE ON THIS POINT THAT IS

COMPATIBLE WITH NATIONAL LAW.

4. (C) COMMENT: AS AN ELECTED SENATOR (AND MEMBER OF THE

SAME FORZA ITALIA PARTY AS PRIME MINISTER BERLUSCONI),

UNDERSECRETARY SESTINI\'S SUPPORT FOR THE U.S. POSITION ON

ABORTION CAN BE CONSIDERED DEFINITIVE OF GOI VIEWS. THE

ITALIAN DELEGATION TO THE SPECIAL SESSION IS TO BE HEADED BY

LABOR/WELFARE MINISTER ROBERTO MARONI. SHOULD DOMESTIC

EVENTS PRECLUDE MARONI FROM ATTENDING, HOWEVER, SENATOR

SESTINI IS LIKELY TO HEAD THE DELEGATION IN HIS PLACE.

5. (C) OTHER ISSUES: AMB. SIV THANKED DEPUTY POLITICAL

DIRECTOR BISOGNIERO FOR ITALY\'S DECISION TO POSTPONE ITS

CANDIDACY FOR THE HUMAN RIGHTS COMMISSION, OPENING THE PATH

FOR THE U.S. TO RETURN. BISOGNIERO NOTED THAT IT WAS

IMPORTANT TO PRIME MINISTER (AND ACTING FM) BERLUSCONI THAT

THE U.S. RETURN. HE HOPED THE U.S. WOULD URGE THOSE
