SUBJECT: PROTESTANT LEADERS REPORT DESTRUCTION OF CHURCH IN CENTRAL HIGHLANDS, BUT CANNOT CONFIRM DEATHS

REF: A) HANOI 763 B) HANOI 673 C) HANOI 598

D) HANOI 247 E) PHNOM PENH 599 F) PHNOM PENH 571

1. (U) CLASSIFIED BY POLITICAL COUNSELOR JAMES BRUNO;

REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D).

2. (C) SUMMARY: VIETNAMESE PROTESTANT CHURCH SOURCES WITH

EXTENSIVE LINKS TO THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS INFORM US THAT

RESIDENTS OF GIA LAI PROVINCE, PARTICULARLY, FACE DIFFICULT

CONDITIONS, WITH HEAVY POLICE AND SECURITY PRESENCE

THROUGHOUT THE PROVINCE. THEY SAID THEIR CHURCH CONTACTS

REPORT FOUR CHURCH MEMBERS MAY HAVE BEEN KILLED, AND AT

LEAST 20 HAVE BEEN ARRESTED FOR PARTICIPATING IN THE EARLY

FEBRUARY DEMONSTRATIONS. THEY CONFIRM A CHURCH WAS

DESTROYED IN GIA LAI PROVINCE IN EARLY MARCH, BUT THEY ARE

NOT AWARE OF ALLEGATIONS THAT A DOZEN PEOPLE WERE KILLED IN

A RAID ON A CHURCH, AS ALLEGED BY REFUGEES IN CAMBODIA

(REF F). SINCE EMBASSY AND CONGEN OFFICERS CONTINUE TO BE

PROHIBITED FROM TRAVELING TO THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS,

PROTESTANT CHURCH LEADERS WITH EXTENSIVE LINKS TO FELLOW

CHRISTIANS IN THE HIGHLANDS PROVIDE ONE OF THE FEW MEANS WE

HAVE OF LEARNING OF EVENTS THERE. THOUGH BY NO MEANS

COMPREHENSIVE, THESE SOURCES HELP PROVIDE SOME PERSPECTIVE

ON THE WIDE-RANGING ALLEGATIONS CIRCULATING ABOUT EVENTS IN

THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS. END SUMMARY.

3. (C) IN ORDER TO TRY TO OBTAIN CLARIFICATION AND/OR

CONFIRMATION OF THE CHARGES MADE BY MONTAGNARD REFUGEES WHO

HAVE FLED TO CAMBODIA (REFS E AND F), EMBASSY AND CONSULATE

HO CHI MINH CITY POLOFFS HAVE CHECKED WITH LONG-STANDING

CONTACTS IN THE VIETNAMESE PROTESTANT RELIGIOUS COMMUNITIES

WHO HAVE BEEN AMONG OUR MOST RELIABLE CONTACTS ON THE

HIGHLANDS IN THE PAST. (THESE SOURCES INCLUDE LEADERS BOTH

OF THE "TIN LANH" EVANGELICAL CHURCH THAT WAS JUST

RECOGNIZED IN THE SOUTH AND THE UNDERGROUND HOUSE CHURCHES

THAT ARE UNRECOGNIZED. LARGE NUMBERS OF PROTESTANT

CHRISTIANS IN THE HIGHLANDS TEND TO BELONG TO HOUSE

CHURCHES THAT HAVE A HISTORIC AFFILIATION WITH THE SOUTHERN

"TIN LANH" MOVEMENT.) THE REPORTS WE HAVE RECEIVED FROM

THESE SOURCES ARE FRAGMENTARY BUT HELP PROVIDE SOME CONTEXT

TO REPORTS FROM OTHER SOURCES.

4. (C) THESE SOURCES CONFIRM, AS REPORTED REF E, THAT A

CHURCH IN GIA LAI PROVINCE WAS ATTACKED IN THE SECOND WEEK

OF MARCH, BUT THEIR ACCOUNTS DIFFER DRAMATICALLY FROM THOSE

REPEATED SECOND-HAND BY THE ETHNIC JARAI REFUGEES IN

CAMBODIA. (ACCORDING TO REF F "UNCONFIRMED REPORTS, POLICE

KILLED 12, WOUNDED 60 AND ARRESTED 74 JARAI DURING THE RAID

ON THE CHURCH.")

5. (C) ACCORDING TO A PROTESTANT CHURCH LEADER BASED IN HO

CHI MINH CITY WHO HAS EXTENSIVE LINKS AMONG HOUSE CHURCHES

IN THE HIGHLANDS THROUGH HIS "CHURCH-PLANTING MINISTRY"

NETWORK, SOLDIERS DEMOLISHED A CHURCH IN PHU NHON DISTRICT,

ABOUT 50 KILOMETERS SOUTH OF PLEIKU (CAPITAL OF GIA LAI

PROVINCE) MARCH 10. THE STORY TRACKS IN SOME WAYS WITH REF

F ACCOUNT: POLICE TRIED TO ARREST A PASTOR THERE, AND LARGE

NUMBERS OF VILLAGERS (PERHAPS MORE THAN 100) CLASHED WITH

POLICE AND SOLDIERS TO PREVENT THIS. TEAR GAS WAS USED TO

DISPERSE THE CROWD, AND THE POLICE USED ELECTRIC PRODS.

6. (C) OUR CHURCH CONTACTS ARE NOT AWARE OF AN ATTACK ON

CHURCHES IN PLEI LAO, XA NINH HOA, HUYEN CHU SE IN GIA LAI

PROVINCE, AS ALLEGED BY THE REFUGEES IN CAMBODIA TO UNHCR

(AS SUGGESTED BY THE REFUGEES IN CAMBODIA). IT IS POSSIBLE

THERE WAS ANOTHER ATTACK ON A CHURCH MARCH 10, THOUGH WE

CANNOT CONFIRM THIS FROM OUR CONTACTS HERE.

7. (S) COMMENT: ALTHOUGH OF COURSE OUR CONTACTS\' LACK OF

KNOWLEDGE OF ALLEGED DEATHS AT A CHURCH IN THE CENTRAL

HIGHLANDS DOES NOT PROVE OR DISPROVE THE CHARGES, THEIR

LACK OF KNOWLEDGE IS NOTABLE BECAUSE TWO OF THE CONTACTS WE

CHECKED WITH ARE OUR BEST SOURCES FOR DEVELOPMENTS IN

ETHNIC MINORITY REGIONS OF VIETNAM. THESE TWO VIETNAMESE

PASTORS BOTH TRAVEL WIDELY AROUND THE COUNTRY AND HAVE BEEN

ARRESTED AND DETAINED SEVERAL TIMES. ONE OF THEM HAS BEEN

INFORMED REPEATEDLY BY SECURITY OFFICIALS THAT HE IS

PROHIBITED FROM GETTING A PASSPORT AND THUS BANNED FROM

LEAVING THE COUNTRY. THESE ARE THE SOURCES WHO "BROKE THE

STORIES" ABOUT THE ONGOING ARRESTS AND DETENTIONS OF HMONG

CHRISTIANS IN THE NORTHWEST OVER THE PAST TWO YEARS, AS

WELL AS THE CHURCH DEMOLITIONS IN BINH PHUOC PROVINCE A

YEAR AGO. ONE OF THE TWO, AS WELL, WAS INFLUENTIAL IN

HELPING BRING TO LIGHT THE DOCUMENTS ON REPRESSION OF

PROTESTANTS THAT WERE PUBLISHED BY FREEDOM HOUSE LATE LAST

YEAR. THUS, WHILE IT IS CERTAINLY POSSIBLE THEY ARE "OUT

OF THE LOOP" ON THIS CHARGE, IT GIVES US PAUSE THAT THEY

HAVE NOT HEARD OF DEATHS IN THE ALLEGED CHURCH INCIDENT.

8. (S) COMMENT (CONT\'D): FURTHERMORE, AN ADDITIONAL FACTOR

THAT GIVES US PAUSE IS THAT MONTAGNARD SOURCES FROM

CAMBODIA AND SOUTH CAROLINA BOTH REPORT PRECISELY THE SAME

NUMBERS OF ETHNIC JARAI KILLED, WOUNDED AND ARRESTED, AS

CITED IN PARA 4 ABOVE. A POSSIBLE EXPLANATION, OF COURSE,

IS THAT A PRIMARY SOURCE RELAYED AN ACCOUNT DIRECTLY BOTH

TO HIS CAMBODIAN COUNTERPARTS, AS SUGGESTED IN REF F, AND

TO CONTACTS IN SOUTH CAROLINA. HOWEVER, NORMALLY IN AN

INCIDENT SUCH AS THE ONE DESCRIBED, WIDELY VARYING RUMORS

CIRCULATE ABOUT THE NUMBERS OF THOSE AFFECTED. IN THIS

CASE THERE ARE VIRTUALLY IDENTICAL REPORTS COMING FROM BOTH

9. (C) ONE PROMINENT PROTESTANT CHURCH LEADER IN HCMC WHO

HAS ACTIVE ONGOING LINKS TO HOUSE CHURCH LEADERS IN THE

CENTRAL HIGHLANDS TOLD POLOFF THAT PROTESTANT CHRISTIANS IN

THE HIGHLANDS PROVINCES OF GIA LAI AND DAKLAK HAVE LITTLE

ROOM TO OPERATE AT PRESENT. MOST CHURCHES ARE STILL ABLE

TO FUNCTION, BUT THEY ARE WATCHED CLOSELY, AND POLICE AND

SECURITY FORCES ARE OUT IN FORCE, PARTICULARLY IN GIA LAI.

HE SAID THAT WHILE A FORMAL CURFEW DOES NOT EXIST, POLICE

GUARD VILLAGES CLOSELY IN GIA LAI, AND PERSONS ARE NOT ABLE

TO MOVE AROUND FREELY; THEY ARE REQUIRED TO REMAIN IN THEIR

HOME VILLAGES. NORMALLY, HE SAYS, THERE IS LARGE-SCALE

MOVEMENT BACK AND FORTH ACROSS THE BORDER, SINCE THERE ARE

APPROXIMATELY 25,000 EDE AND JARAI MINORITY PERSONS WHO

LIVE IN CAMBODIA AND TRAVEL RELATIVELY FREELY OVER WHAT IS

NORMALLY A QUITE POROUS BORDER. (NOTE: ANOTHER INDEPENDENT

SOURCE ALSO NOTED THERE IS GENERALLY FREE MOVEMENT ACROSS

THE BORDER.) OUR SOURCE SAID HIS CHURCH CONTACTS IN GIA

LAI TELL HIM THAT AFTER THE FEBRUARY DEMONSTRATIONS,

11. (C) SIMILARLY, AN EXPATRIATE LEADER LINKED WITH THE

WORLD EVANGELICAL FELLOWSHIP WHO REGULARLY VISITS VIETNAM

AND MEETS WITH HOUSE CHURCH LEADERS, INCLUDING SOME FROM

THE HIGHLANDS, TOLD POLOFF THAT THE NETWORK OF PROTESTANT

CHURCHES IN THE HIGHLANDS IS ONE OF THE FEW ELEMENTS OF

"CIVIL SOCIETY" PRESENT IN THE AREA. THOUGH HIS CONTACTS

HAD TOLD HIM THE CHURCHES INSTITUTIONALLY WERE LARGELY

UNINVOLVED IN THE FEBRUARY DEMONSTRATIONS, SIGNIFICANT

NUMBERS OF INDIVIDUAL CHURCH MEMBERS AND SOME LEADERS

PARTICIPATED. HE SAID HIS CHURCH CONTACTS IN THE HIGHLANDS

WERE CONCERNED ABOUT THE POTENTIAL FOR "RADICALIZATION" OF

THE MONTAGNARD CHURCHES BECAUSE OF THE EXTREME POVERTY IN

THE REGION, THE LOSS OF TRADITIONAL LANDS TO ETHNIC

VIETNAMESE AND OTHER SETTLERS, WIDESPREAD SOCIETAL

DISCRIMINATION AGAINST MINORITY PEOPLES, AND LONG-STANDING

FRUSTRATION WITH TREATMENT BY CENTRAL AUTHORITIES,

INCLUDING HARASSMENT OF CHURCHES.

12. (C) THIS EXPATRIATE SOURCE SAID HIS CONTACTS IN GIA LAI

HAD INFORMED HIM THAT THE FEBRUARY 2 DEMONSTRATION IN

PLEIKU HAD BEEN A "PITCHED BATTLE." THE DEMONSTRATORS,

MANY OF THEM ARMED WITH KNIVES AND BAGS OF ROCKS, ATTEMPTED

TO DESTROY THE POST OFFICE, AND SOME DEMONSTRATORS AND

POLICE REPORTEDLY WERE SERIOUSLY INJURED. HIS CONTACTS HAD

HEARD REPORTS OF FOUR DEMONSTRATORS KILLED IN THESE

DEMONSTRATIONS IN PLEIKU, BUT THEY COULD NOT CONFIRM THIS.

BY CONTRAST, HIS CONTACTS SAID BUON ME THUOT IN DAKLAK

PROVINCE NEVER TURNED SO VIOLENT.

13. (C) THE EXPATRIATE SOURCE SAID HIS CONTACTS SAID AT

LEAST TWENTY MINORITY CHRISTIANS WHO HAD PARTICIPATED IN

THE DEMONSTRATIONS WERE PICKED UP SHORTLY AFTER. HE SAID

THE POLICE HAD TAKEN EXTENSIVE VIDEOTAPE OF THE

DEMONSTRATIONS AND USED THIS TO IDENTIFY THE PARTICIPANTS.

HE SURMISED THIS WAS ONE OF THE FACTORS THAT HAD DRIVEN

SOME OF THOSE TO FLEE, SINCE THOSE INVOLVED IN THE

DEMONSTRATIONS CLEARLY FEARED THEY WOULD BE PICKED UP.

14. (C) THIS EXPATRIATE SOURCE SAID ETHNIC MINORITY CHURCH

LEADERS WERE CAUGHT IN A DIFFICULT SPOT, SINCE THEY WANTED

TO BROADEN THEIR OWN SPACE TO OPERATE, BUT SOME FACED

PRESSURE TO EMBRACE A SEPARATIST AGENDA THAT WOULD CAUSE

THE AUTHORITIES TO CRACK DOWN. HE RELAYED AN ACCOUNT OF

FEBRUARY 2 EVENTS IN THE BUON ME THUOT AREA OF DAKLAK

PROVINCE THAT TYPIFIED THIS DILEMMA. ON THAT DATE, THREE

CHRISTIAN LEADERS WERE CALLED BY AUTHORITIES TO A MEETING

IN BUON ME THUOT AND THEN ASKED TO TRY TO SPEAK TO A CROWD

OF ABOUT 400 PROTESTERS. AFTER MEETING WITH AUTHORITIES,

15. (C) AN INTERESTING EXCEPTION TO THE PROHIBITION OF

WESTERN DIPLOMATIC ACCESS TO THE HIGHLANDS IS THAT AFFORDED

THE DANISH EMBASSY, WHICH HAS A HALF-DOZEN ADVISORS BASED

IN BUON ME THUOT WORKING ON AGRICULTURAL AND COMMUNITY

DEVELOPMENT PROJECTS. A DANISH EMBASSY POLOFF (WHO SPENDS

HALF HER TIME ON AID QUESTIONS) VISITED DAKLAK APRIL 1-3 AS

PART OF A PREVIOUSLY SCHEDULED EVALUATION TRIP TO SEE

PROJECTS SHE SUPERVISES. SHE MET WITH HER COUNTERPARTS

THERE, WHO PROVIDED READOUTS ON THE FEBRUARY 2

DEMONSTRATIONS IN BUON ME THUOT, SAYING THERE WERE ROUGHLY

500 LARGELY RURAL ETHNIC MINORITY PROTESTERS WHO CAME INTO

OFF THE STREETS ONCE THIS GROUP ASSEMBLED, PREVENTING

CURIOUS LOCAL RESIDENTS FROM BEING ABLE TO WITNESS THE

PROTESTS.

16. (C) THE DIPLOMAT TOLD EMBOFF THAT, DURING HER TRIP, SHE
