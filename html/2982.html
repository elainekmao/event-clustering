SUBJECT: JORDAN: MEDIA REACTION ON THE OPERATIONS IN

NETANYA AND GAZA, PM\'S VISIT TO PNA, AND VP CHENEY\'S

INTERVIEW, IRAQ

SUMMARY

NEWS REPORTS IN JORDAN\'S DAILIES ON MAY 20 DEALT WITH

THREE MAIN STORIES: THE BOMBINGS IN ISRAEL, WHICH WERE

DESCRIBED AS "COMMANDO OPERATIONS," PRIME MINISTER ABUL

RAGHEB\'S VISIT TO RAMALLAH IN WHICH HE HIGHLIGHTED

JORDAN\'S SUPPORT FOR PALESTINIAN REFORMS AND FOR THE

CREATION OF A PALESTINIAN STATE, AND VP CHENEY\'S

INTERVIEW WITH FOX NEWS IN WHICH HE WARNED OF "NEW

ATTACKS AGAINST THE U.S." AND ASSERTED THAT THE RETURN

OF THE INSPECTORS DOES NOT SOLVE THE PROBLEM WITH IRAQ.

EDITORIAL COMMENTARIES EXPRESSED DIVERSE OPINIONS ON

THE ISSUE OF PALESTINIAN REFORMS, BUT ALL AGREED THAT

THE AMERICAN ISRAELI CALLS FOR REFORMS ARE MADE IN BAD

FAITH, AND THAT THEY ARE AN ATTEMPT TO COMPLETE THE

ISRAELI MILITARY OPERATION\'S AIM TO ELIMINATE THE

PALESTINIAN AUTHORITY. OTHER COMMENTATORS WARNED THAT

DESPITE IRAQ\'S ACCEPTANCE OF UN DEMANDS, U.S.

PREPARATIONS FOR AN "AGGRESSION" AGAINST IT WILL

CONTINUE.

NEWS REPORTS

JORDANIAN DAILIES ON ALL LED WITH REPORTS ON THE

BOMBINGS IN NETANYA AND GAZA. THE HEADLINES DESCRIBED

THE BOMBINGS AS "OPERATIONS" OR "COMMANDO OPERATIONS,"

WHILE INDEPENDENT, MASS-APPEAL ARABIC DAILY AL-ARAB AL-

YAWM SAID: "HAMAS STRIKES AGAIN IN NETANYA."

DAILIES ALSO GAVE PROMINENT COVERAGE TO PRIME MINISTER

ALI ABUL RAGHEB\'S VISIT TO RAMALLAH, WITH HEADLINES

HIGHLIGHTING JORDAN\'S SUPPORT FOR THE PALESTINIAN

REFORMS AND THE QUEST TO ESTABLISH AN INDEPENDENT

STATE.

JORDANIAN DAILIES ALSO REPORTED ON THEIR FRONT PAGES VP

CHENEY\'S INTERVIEW WITH FOX NEWS, HIGHLIGHTING TWO

ELEMENTS: HIS ASSERTION THAT THE RETURN OF THE WEAPONS

INSPECTORS DOES NOT ELIMINATE THE PROBLEM WITH IRAQ,

AND HIS WARNING OF MORE "ATTACKS AGAINST THE U.S."

EDITORIAL COMMENTARIES

-- THE BATTLE OF PALESTINIAN REFORMS

IMPORTANT DAILY COLUMNIST BASSEM SAKEJHA WROTE IN

CENTER-LEFT, INFLUENTIAL ARABIC DAILY AL-DUSTOUR ON MAY

20: "CORRUPTION IN THE PNA IS NOT A SECRET, AND WHEN

THE PALESTINIANS ELECTED THE PALESTINIAN NATIONAL

ASSEMBLY, THEY DID NOT DO SO IN PERPETUITY. BUT

QUESTIONS OF REFORM ARE NOW RAISED NOT FROM WITHIN, BUT

BY THE ENEMY AND HIS ALLY, AS IF THE BATTLE WERE NOT

ABOUT THE CHURCH OF NATIVITY AND JENIN, BUT ABOUT THE

DATE OF ELECTIONS AND THE SIZE OF THE CABINET. THE

PROBLEM IS THAT THE PALESTINIAN PRESIDENCY CONSIDERS

THE TIMING OF THE ELECTIONS TO BE A CARD TO PRESSURE

THE ISRAELIS AND AMERICANS, NOT A PROCESS OF REAL

REFORM. WE ALL KNOW THAT THE AMERICAN TALK ABOUT

REFORM IS IN BAD FAITH, BUT THIS DOES NOT JUSTIFY

DELAYING THIS STEP, WHICH WOULD BE THE FIRST IN

BUILDING A DEMOCRATIC STATE. TEL AVIV AND WASHINGTON

WANT THEM AS A WAY OF BRINGING IN A PALESTINIAN

LEADERSHIP THAT WOULD BE LAX REGARDING PALESTINIAN

NATIONAL ISSUES, BUT EXPERIENCE HAS SHOWN THAT THEY

WILL NOT SUCCEED BECAUSE THE PALESTINIAN STREET ALWAYS

HAS THE MEANS TO CORRECT POLITICAL ORIENTATIONS."

-- THEY CALL FOR LIBERATING ANDALUSIA

TAHER AL-ADWAN, CHIEF EDITOR OF INDEPENDENT, MASS-

APPEAL ARABIC DAILY AL-ARAB AL-YAWM WROTE ON MAY 20:

"MANY THINGS HAVE CHANGED SINCE SHARON UNLEASHED HIS

TROOPS TO REOCCUPY THE LIBERATED PALESTINIAN

TERRITORIES AND REACHED THE DOORS OF THE PRESIDENT\'S

OFFICE, WITH UNPRECEDENTED AMERICAN SUPPORT. NOW THE

TALK IS ABOUT THE PREROGATIVES OF THE PALESTINIAN

PRESIDENT, AND HIS PALESTINIAN CRITICS JUSTIFY

THEMSELVES BY CLAIMING THAT THEY MAKE CALLS FOR REFORM.

BUT EVERYONE REMEMBERS THAT THE ORIGIN OF THESE CALLS

WERE PRESIDENT BUSH AND GENERAL SHARON, AND THEIR CALL

FOR `REFORM\' IS TO COMPLETE THE PRESSURE THAT THE

MILITARY OPERATION STARTED. ARAFAT NOW RESISTS

ATTEMPTS TO ELIMINATE HIM AND THE PALESTINIAN

AUTHORITY. HIS CRITICS, THEREFORE, ARE LIKE THOSE WHO

TOLL THE BELLS CALLING FOR PRAYER WHILE THE ENEMY

TROOPS SCALE THE WALLS OF THEIR CITY. WE FIND

OURSELVES LOOKING AT A CARICATURE BEST DESCRIBED BY

PALESTINIAN POET MAHMOUD DARWISH IN A FAMOUS POEM, IN

WHICH HE SAID: `THEY CALL FOR LIBERATING ANDALUSIA WHEN

ALEPPO IS UNDER SIEGE.\'"

-- LOOKING FOR PRETEXTS

"FORMER UNDER-SECRETARY OF CULTURE MOHAMMAD NAJI

AMAYREH WROTE IN SEMI-OFFICIAL INFLUENTIAL ARABIC

DAILY AL-RAI ON MAY 20: "AMERICAN THREATS OF A MILITARY

STRIKE AGAINST IRAQ HAVE NOT CEASED ALTHOUGH IRAQ HAS

ACCEPTED THE NEW SANCTIONS REGIME AND IT DID NOT OPPOSE

THE RETURN OF THE INSPECTORS, WHILE LOOKING FOR WAYS TO

PREVENT THEM FROM BECOMING SPIES FOR THE U.S. THE U.S.

AND ITS ALLY BRITAIN ARE NOW LOOKING FOR PRETEXTS FOR

THIS AGGRESSION, AND THEY WILL PROBABLY FIND SOME,

BECAUSE PREPARATIONS FOR THE AGGRESSION ONLY WAIT FOR A

MORE APPROPRIATE MOMENT TO LAUNCH IT. THIS HESITATION

DOES NOT MEAN THAT THEY HAVE RULED OUT MILITARY ACTION.

HOWEVER, AMERICA WILL FAIL TO ACHIEVE ITS AIMS FROM

THIS STRIKE, WHICH WILL ONLY WEAKEN U.S. SUPPORTERS IN

THE ARAB WORLD AND STRENGTHEN THOSE WHO CALL FOR

SEARCHING FOR ALTERNATIVE FRIENDS." GNEHM
