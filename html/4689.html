SUBJECT: BOMB THREAT CALLED IN TO AMEMBASSY COLOMBO

REF: COLOMBO 2231 (NOTAL)

SENSITIVE BUT UNCLASSIFIED - PROTECT ACCORDINGLY

1. (SBU) ON DECEMBER 12, 2002 AT APPROXIMATELY 1045 HOURS, AN

UNIDENTIFIED MALE CALLED THE EMBASSY AND SAID TO THE OPERATOR,

"THERE IS A BOMB IN THE EMBASSY." THE CALLER WAS DESCRIBED AS A

LOCAL MALE, WELL EDUCATED WITH CLEARLY SPOKEN ENGLISH. THE

CALLER IMMEDIATELY HUNG UP.

2. (SBU) GIVEN THE INFREQUENCY OF SUCH THREATS IN COLOMBO (THIS WAS

THE FIRST ONE IN AT LEAST FIVE YEARS, PERHAPS LONGER) ACTING RSO

REACTED THE MARINES AND CALLED THE SRI LANKA POLICE SPECIAL TASK

FORCE (STF).

3. (SBU) THE A/RSO CLOSED POST TWO AND THE PAC AND INSTRUCTED ALL

VISITORS IN THE CONSULAR SECTION TO LEAVE THE EMBASSY GROUNDS.

4. (SBU) POST ONE MADE A SELECTONE ANNOUNCEMENT CALLING ON ALL STAFF

TO SEARCH THEIR RESPECTIVE AREAS. THE LOCAL GUARDS SEARCHED THE

EMBASSY GROUNDS AND THE MARINES SEARCHED THE COMMON AREAS OF THE

CHANCERY. IN ADDITION, RSO STAFF CONDUCTED A SWEEP OF THE

CHANCERY USING ITEMISER TRAPS.

5. (SBU) NO UNUSUAL OBJECTS WERE DETECTED AND ALL ITEMISER RESULTS

WERE NEGATIVE. IN ONE HOUR, THE EMBASSY WAS REOPENED TO THE

PUBLIC. A FINAL SWEEP WITH AN STF BOMB DOG WAS ALSO NEGATIVE FOR

EXPLOSIVES.

6. (SBU) COMMENT: THE A/RSO IS CONCERNED THAT THIS CALL IS ANOTHER

REFLECTION OF AN INCREASE IN ANTI-AMERICAN SENTIMENT IN SRI

LANKA. IN LIGHT OF THE RECENT ROCK THROWING INCIDENT REPORTED IN

REFTEL, THE OVERALL INCREASE IN WORLD-WIDE ANTI-AMERICAN

SENTIMENT AND THE LACK OF A PRECEDENT OF BOMB THREATS AT

AMEMBASSY COLOMBO, THE A/RSO DECIDED TO RESPOND WITH ALL

AVAILABLE ASSETS. EMBASSY AND HOST NATION RESPONSE WAS EFFECTIVE

AND PRODUCED MINIMAL DISRUPTION TO EMBASSY OPERATIONS. A/RSO

HOPES THIS IS NOT A SIGN OF MORE TO COME. END COMMENT
