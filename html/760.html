INTRODUCTION AND SUMMARY

------------------------

1. DECONTROL ON RECEIPT. SENSITIVE BUT UNCLASSIFIED -

TREAT ACCORDINGLY.

2. (U) DRL A/S SHATTUCK TRAVELED TO TUZLA AND ZENICA JULY

31-AUGUST 1 TO ASCERTAIN THE DIMENSION OF THE HUMAN RIGHTS

CRIMES COMMITTED DURING THE BOSNIAN SERB ARMY\'S ATTACK AND

OCCUPATION OF TWO UN SAFE AREAS, SREBRENICA AND ZEPA. THE

FOLLOWING IS A FIRST-HAND ACCOUNT OF A DISPLACED TEENAGER

FROM THE SREBRENICA ENCLAVE WHO SURVIVED A MASS EXECUTION

OF MUSLIM MEN BY THE BSA. THE SUBJECT AGREED TO SPEAK

ONLY ON THE CONDITION THAT HIS NAME OR DESCRIPTION NOT BE

REVEALED PUBLICLY OR DISSEMINATED FOR FEAR THAT THAT WOULD

ENDANGER RELATIVES STILL UNDER SERBIAN DETENTION IN B-H.

END SUMMARY.

SEVENTEEN-YEAR-OLD BOY FROM THE VILLAGE OF SEBIOCINA, NEAR

VLASENICA IN SERB-OCCUPIED EASTERN BOSNIA. HE FLED THIS

VILLAGE IN MID-1992 WITH HIS PARENTS AND THREE YOUNGER

FLIGHT FROM SREBRENICA AND SURRENDER

------------------------------------

4. (U) ON JULY 6, N.A. HEARD THE FIRST DETONATIONS OF THE

BSA SHELLING OF THE SREBRENICA ENCLAVE. HE AND HIS FAMILY

FLED TO UNDERGROUND SHELTERS WHERE THEY HID FOR THREE

DAYS. WHEN DUTCH UNPROFOR TROOPS PULLED BACK INTO THE

TOWN OF SREBRENICA, MANY CIVILIANS FLED, HE SAID, SOME TO

THE WOODS, SOME TOWARDS THE TOWN. N.A. AND HIS FATHER

FLED INTO THE WOODS, AND HIS MOTHER AND SISTERS SOUGHT

SHELTER IN THE UNPROFOR CAMP IN POTOCARI. THE WOODS WERE

FILLED WITH MILITARY AND CIVILIAN MEN AND SOME WOMEN AND

CHILDREN, N.A. SAID. DURING THE COURSE OF BSA SHELLING,

HE AND HIS FATHER, A SOLDIER, WERE SEPARATED. AFTER ONE

DAY OF HIDING, THE SERB SOLDIERS SURROUNDED THE AREA AND

CALLED OUT OVER A MEGAPHONE FOR THE PEOPLE TO SURRENDER.

N.A. SAID THE CALL PROMISED THE MUSLIMS THEY WOULD BE

ACCORDED ALL THE RIGHTS OF THE GENEVA CONVENTION IF THEY

SURRENDERED.

AT THE SCHOOL

-------------

8. (U) AFTER A SHORT TIME, THE TRUCKS TURNED OFF AND

STOPPED IN FRONT OF A SCHOOL. THE BUSES BEHIND FILLED

WITH WOMEN LEFT THE CONVOY. THE MEN WERE FORCED TO FILE

INTO THE SCHOOL WHERE A SERB GUARD AT THE ENTRANCE HIT

EACH WITH THE BUTT OF HIS RIFLE. N.A. ENTERED THE LAST OF

4-5 ROOMS WITH MANY OTHERS. WHEN SOMEONE CALLED OUT FOR

WATER, A SHOT WAS FIRED THROUGH THE GLASS ABOVE THE DOOR,

RICOCHETED OFF THE CEILING AND WOUNDED FIVE MEN IN HIS

ROOM. THEY SPENT THE DAY WITHOUT ANY FOOD OR WATER. AT

DARK, GROUPS OF TWO-THREE MEN WERE CALLED OUT FOR

INTERROGATION. N.A. HEARD SHOTS FIRED IN THE CORRIDORS,

AND OUTSIDE THE BUILDING. HE ALSO HEARD LOTS OF MOANING

AND YELLING BUT HE DID NOT KNOW WHAT HAPPENED. N.A WAS

BROUGHT TO A SMALL ROOM WHERE HE WAS FORCED TO TAKE OFF

HIS SHIRT AND SHOES, AND A SOLDIER TIED HIS HANDS BEHIND

HIS BACK. HE THEN FILED OUT OF THE SCHOOL WHERE, IN THE

DIM MOONLIGHT, HE SAW MANY DEAD BODIES IN THE YARD. HE

COULD NOT SAY HOW THE DEAD HAD BEEN KILLED.

AT THE KILLING FIELD

9. (U) THE MEN WERE FORCED ONTO TRUCKS AGAIN AND ORDERED

TO SIT. THEY WERE PACKED TOO TIGHTLY AND COULD NOT MOVE.

A VOICE ORDERED THEM TO SIT AGAIN AND SOMEONE FIRED A

BURST OF MACHINE GUN FIRE INTO THE SIDE OF THE TRUCK,

WOUNDING SEVERAL PEOPLE. THE TRUCKS DROVE OFF AND AFTER

FIVE MINUTES TURNED ONTO A DIRT ROAD. AFTER ANOTHER FIVE

MINUTES, THEY STOPPED. N.A. SAID HE HEARD ROUNDS FIRED

INTO THE GROUND. ABOUT THREE MINUTES LATER, THE BACK OF

THE TRUCKS WERE OPENED AND MEN ORDERED OFF IN GROUPS OF

FIVE. N.A. SAID HE WAS IN THE MIDDLE OF THE LAST TRUCK.

AFTER EACH GROUP LEFT, THE MEN HEARD GUNFIRE. N.A. HEARD

ONE MUSLIM SAY TO A SERB SOLDIER, "WHY ARE YOU DOING THIS

TO INNOCENT PEOPLE? I SAVED YOUR WIFE AND CHILD IN OUR

10. (U) WHEN N.A. WAS FORCED TO GET OFF, STILL WITH HIS

HANDS TIED BEHIND HIM, HE SAW IN THE MOONLIGHT AN

OVERWHELMING NUMBER OF DEAD BODIES, COMPLETELY COVERING A

SMALL FIELD. HE WOULD NOT ESTIMATE HOW MANY BODIES HE

SAW. THERE WERE MANY MORE BODIES, HE SAID, THAN COULD

HAVE POSSIBLY FIT INTO THE TRUCKS THERE. A SERB VOICE

CALLED OUT AND TOLD THEM TO "CHOOSE A PLACE" TOGETHER IN A

LINE. HE STOOD WITH THE OTHER FOUR MEN IN A LINE. THEN A

VOICE ORDERED THEM TO FALL FORWARD. AT THE SAME TIME,

N.A. FELT A SHARP PAIN IN HIS RIGHT ELBOW AND RIGHT SIDE.

HE FELL FORWARD IN EXTREME PAIN, AS HE THOUGHT HE WAS

DEAD. A SERB CALLED OUT, "THIS IS ALLAH\'S STYLE OF WAR.

WE\'RE GOING TO EXCHANGE YOUR BODIES FOR THAT OF SILAJDIC."

HE LAY IN SEMI-CONSCIOUSNESS, HEARING PEOPLE SCREAMING

WITH PAIN. THE SERBS CONTINUED TO SHOOT PEOPLE. AS HE

LAY ALMOST DEAD, A STONE FROM THE GROUND RICOCHETED INTO

HIS LEFT FOOT. HE HEARD A SERB ORDER AN OLD MAN TO SAY

ALLAH AL AKBAR, THEN SHOOT HIM WITH SEVERAL ROUNDS AS THE

OLD MAN SPOKE. WHEN THE SHOOTING FINALLY STOPPED, A SERB

YELLED, "LOOK AT ALL THESE WELL-FATTED RABBITS. IT\'S BEEN

A GOOD HUNT." SOMEONE ORDERED THAT THE CORPSES BE CHECKED

TO SEE IF THEY WERE ALIVE. ALTHOUGH SOLDIERS WALKED AMONG

THE GROUP, NO ONE TOUCHED N.A. AFTER A WHILE, THE TRUCKS

STARTED UP AND LEFT.

ESCAPE

------

11. (U) N.A. LIFTED HIS HEAD AND SAW A BODY MOVE ABOUT

FIVE METERS AWAY. THE VOICE CALLED OUT TO UNTIE HIM.

N.A. ROLLED OVER SEVERAL DEAD BODIES TO REACH HIM. THE

MAN BIT THROUGH THE CORD TYING N.A.\'S HANDS, THEN N.A.

TRIED TO BITE THROUGH THE FOUR CORDS OF ROPE BINDING THE

OTHER. AFTER HE HAD BROKEN TWO, THEY HEARD VEHICLES

COMING BACK. THE OTHER MAN, O.H., ONLY SLIGHTLY GRAZED,

HELPED N.A., TO RUN TO THE WOOD. THEY HID IN A SMALL

WOODED CREEK DOWNHILL FROM THE FIELD. BOTH HEARD STRANGE,

THUD-LIKE NOISES AND SHOOTING, THOUGH MORE IRREGULAR THAN

PREVIOUSLY. N.A. BELIEVED THAT THE SERBS HAD RETURNED TO

CART OFF THE BODIES. O.H. WAS NOT ON THE SAME TRUCK AS

N.A. HE WAS WEARING A SHIRT, UNLIKE N.A., AND USED IT TO

BANDAGE N.A.\'S WOUNDS.

12. (U) AT DAWN, THE TWO TRIED TO WALK AWAY FROM THE

SITE. THEY ATE A FEW APPLES AND MUSHROOMS IN THE FOREST.

O.H. HAD TO CARRY N.A. MUCH OF THE WAY BECAUSE HE WAS SO

WEAK. AFTER FOUR DAYS, THEY CROSSED INTO MUSLIM-HELD

TERRITORY. MOST OF THE TIME THEY WERE LOST, AND WERE

GUIDED TO THE FRONT LINE BY GUNFIRE. N.A. HAD EATEN

ALMOST NOTHING FOR SIX DAYS AND DRUNK ONLY CREEK WATER.

N.A. SAID THE TWO CAME OUT NEAR THE VILLAGE OF SAPNA,

WHERE HE WAS HOSPITALIZED FOR A FEW DAYS. THEN HE WAS

TAKEN TO GRADINA HOSPITAL IN TUZLA, FROM WHERE HE WAS

RELEASED. HE FOUND HIS MOTHER AND SISTERS IN TUZLA, BUT

STILL FEARS FOR HIS FATHER.

13. (LOU) N.A. HAD BEEN DEBRIEFED BY THE GOBH, THOUGH HE
