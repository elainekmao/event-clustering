SUBJECT: PRESIDENTIAL TRAVELS SHORE UP RELATIONS WITHIN ASEAN

1. (U) SUMMARY: PRESIDENT LUONG\'S RECENT TRAVELS TO FOUR

FELLOW ASEAN MEMBERS PROMOTED GENERAL GOOD NEIGHBORLINESS

AND COMMON DESIRES FOR EXPANDED TRADE. WHILE "SUCCESSFUL,"

THESE VISITS SEEM MEMORABLE PRIMARILY AS PHOTO OPS. THE

PRESIDENT DID CALL FOR COOPERATION AGAINST INTERNATIONAL

TERRORISM ON AT LEAST ONE STOP. END SUMMARY.

2. (U) THE MONTH OF NOVEMBER HAS BEEN NOTEWORTHY FOR THE

NUMBER OF HIGH-LEVEL OFFICIAL VIETNAMESE VISITS ABROAD. IN

ADDITION TO THE CURRENT VISIT TO CHINA BY COMMUNIST PARTY

SECRETARY-GENERAL NONG DUC MANH, ONGOING VISITS TO SOUTH AND

SIPDIS

NORTH AMERICA BY DEPUTY PRIME MINISTER NGUYEN TAN DZUNG, AND

A EUROPEAN VOYAGE BY DEPUTY PRIME MINISTER NGUYEN MANH CAM,

VIETNAMESE PRESIDENT TRAN DUC LUONG VISITED FOUR ASEAN

NATIONS -- BRUNEI, CAMBODIA, INDONESIA, AND THE PHILIPPINES.

THIS FLURRY OF OFFICIAL VISITS COINCIDED WITH THE VIETNAMESE

NATIONAL ASSEMBLY\'S WINTER SESSION AND THE NA\'S NOVEMBER

28TH RATIFICATION OF THE US-VIETNAM BILATERAL TRADE

AGREEMENT.

2. (U) THE MFA BRIEFED US AND FRENCH DIPLOMATS ON PRESIDENT

LUONG\'S TRAVELS ON NOVEMBER 21. ACCORDING TO THE MFA, THE

COMMON THEME ON PRESIDENT LUONG\'S TRAVELS WAS PROMOTION OF

BILATERAL TIES RATHER THAN EXPANSION OF ASEAN RELATIONS,

ALONG WITH THE USUAL REFERENCES TO MUTUAL NON-INTERFERENCE

AND MUTUAL COOPERATION. LUONG\'S REMARKS ALSO EMPHASIZED

ENHANCING TRADE AND FOSTERING POLITICAL STABILITY IN THE

REGION. MOST STOPS INCLUDED SIGNING OF VARIOUS AGREEMENTS

AND PROMISES TO PROMOTE BILATERAL TRADE.

3. (SBU) BRUNEI: THE VISIT TO BRUNEI NOVEMBER 12-14 WAS

SIGNIFICANT FOR NEW AGREEMENTS ON TOURISM AND TRADE. BRUNEI

EMBASSY SECOND SECRETARY SITI ZULIA HAJI TENGAH TOLD POLOFF

THAT HER COUNTRY WAS ESPECIALLY PLEASED THAT THE VIETNAMESE

HEAD OF STATE HAD FINALLY BEEN ABLE TO ACCEPT THE SULTAN\'S

FOUR-YEAR-OLD INVITATION. DURING THE VISIT, THE TWO SIDES

SIGNED AN MOU ON BILATERAL COOPERATION DESIGNED TO ENCOURAGE

GREATER AGRICULTURAL TRADE AND TOURISM. SHE PREDICTED,

HOWEVER, THAT OIL AND NATURAL GAS DEVELOPMENT BETWEEN THE

TWO PETROLEUM-EXPORTING STATES WILL BE LIMITED, GIVEN THAT

MALAYSIA SEEMS TO HAVE BEATEN BRUNEI TO THE PUNCH IN

VIETNAM\'S OIL AND GAS SECTOR. SHE NOTED HER COUNTRY\'S HOPES

THAT ANTICIPATED DIRECT AIR-SERVICE LINKING BANDAR SERI

BEGAWAN AND HO CHI MINH CITY WILL SPARK MORE PRIVATE SECTOR

INTEREST BETWEEN THE TWO COUNTRIES. SHE ADDED THAT NO

GOVERNMENT-TO-GOVERNMENT DIRECT TRADE IS EXPECTED OR PLANNED

AT THIS TIME.

4. (SBU) CAMBODIA: LUONG\'S ARRIVAL IN PHNOM PENH ON

NOVEMBER 26TH MARKED THE FIRST VIETNAMESE PRESIDENTIAL VISIT

SINCE THEN-PRESIDENT LE DUCH ANH\'S VISIT IN 1995. CAMBODIAN

EMBASSY SECOND SECRETARY CHIM VICHANY RATED LUONG\'S MEETINGS

AS "VERY SUCCESSFUL." TRADE AND INVESTMENT, PARTICULARLY

ALONG THE BORDER, WERE THE PRIMARY TOPICS OF DISCUSSION.

BOTH SIDES AGREED THAT CITIZENS IN THE BORDER PROVINCES

WOULD NOT NEED TRAVEL DOCUMENTS TO CROSS, AND THAT EACH

INDIVIDUAL WOULD BE ALLOWED TO CARRY GOODS VALUED UP TO USD

35 DOLLAR DUTY FREE. VICHANY ALSO NOTED THAT PHNOM PENH WAS

LOOKING TO VIETNAM AND ITS RELATIVE ECONOMIC SUCCESS TO

ASSIST CAMBODIA IN DEVELOPING ITS OWN ECONOMY. CONTRARY TO

VIETNAMESE MEDIA REPORTS THAT DISCUSSIONS ON BORDER

DEMARCATION ARE CONTINUING IN AN ATMOSPHERE OF "MUTUAL

UNDERSTANDING AND GOOD NEIGHBORLINESS," VICHANY CLAIMED THAT

THIS ISSUE HAD ALREADY BEEN RESOLVED.

5. (U) INDONESIA: BOTH SIDES HAVE EXPRESSED SATISFACTION

THAT THE NOVEMBER 10-12 VISIT FURTHERED THE SPIRIT OF

COOPERATION BETWEEN THE TWO NATIONS, PARTICULARLY IN MAKING

PROGRESS ON ESTABLISHMENT OF DIRECT AIR SERVICE, INCREASING

TWO-WAY TRADE (IN PARTICULAR RICE EXPORTS FROM VIETNAM), AND

PROMOTION OF SCIENTIFIC AND TECHNOLOGICAL COOPERATION. THE

TWO PRESIDENTS ALSO REPORTEDLY ADDRESSED THE NEED TO

FINALIZE THE DELINEATION OF THE CONTINENTAL SHELF BETWEEN

THE TWO COUNTRIES, WITH LUONG REITERATING VIETNAM\'S DESIRE

TO KEEP THIS DISCUSSION IN A BILATERAL FORMAT. BOTH

PRESIDENTS ALSO VOICED THEIR SUPPORT FOR THE FIGHT AGAINST

TERRORISM. THE NOVEMBER 5-6 ASEAN MEETING AND THE OCTOBER

21-23 APEC SUMMIT\'S DISCUSSIONS ON TERRORISM WERE CITED AS

FRAMEWORKS TO FOLLOW IN FUTURE REGIONAL AND GLOBAL EFFORTS

AGAINST TRANSNATIONAL CRIMES.

6. (SBU) PHILIPINES: LUONG\'S NOVEMBER 14-16 VISIT WAS ONLY

THE SECOND VIETNAMESE PRESIDENTIAL VISIT TO THE PHILIPPINES

SINCE THE OPENING OF DIPLOMATIC RELATIONS IN 1976 (THEN-

PRESIDENT ANH VISITED IN 1995). BOTH COUNTRIES AGREED TO

INCREASE RICE EXPORTS AND THE TWO PRESIDENTS ALSO DISCUSSED

HOW TO DEAL WITH THE WORLDWIDE ECONOMIC SLUMP. IN ADDITION,

THE PHILIPPINES AGREED TO HELP SUPPORT VIETNAM\'S EVENTUAL

BID TO ENTER THE WTO. HOWEVER, MANILA WAS REPORTEDLY

DISAPPOINTED THAT THE TWO SIDES FAILED TO REACH AN AGREEMENT

ON DEFINITIVE NUMBERS ON RICE EXPORTS OR THE PROBLEMATIC

ISSUE OF VIETNAMESE VISA OVERSTAYERS.

7. (SBU) COMMENT: PREDICTABLY, PRESIDENT LUONG\'S TRAVELS

HAVE BEEN HAILED IN THE VIETNAMESE MEDIA AS GREAT SUCCESSES.
