SUBJECT: GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE INTENSIFIES SQUEEZE ON

THE INDEPENDENT MEDIA

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS 1.5 B/D.

1. (C) SUMMARY: THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE\'S ASSAULT

ON THE INDEPENDENT MEDIA HAS NOT ABATED IN THE WAKE OF

THE BOMBING OF THE "DAILY NEWS." THE CURRENT ATTACK

ON PRESS FREEDOM IS, HOWEVER, MORE SOPHISTICATED AND

MAY IN THE LONG RUN BE MORE EFFECTIVE THAN THE

DESTRUCTION OF THE "DAILY NEWS" PRINTING PRESSES. THE

METHODOLOGY INCLUDES SELECTIVE ACCREDITATION OF

JOURNALISTS, EXORBITANT FEES FOR CREDENTIALING FOREIGN

PRESS, DENYING ACCESS AND INFORMATION TO THE

INDEPENDENT MEDIA, AND LOOMING RESTRICTIONS ON FOREIGN

OWNERSHIP OR SUPPORT TO INDEPENDENT MEDIA. THE GOZ IS

PULLING OUT ALL THE STOPS TO MUZZLE THE INDEPENDENT

PRESS AND THE PUBLIC\'S ACCESS TO INFORMATION, A KEY

COMPONENT IN ITS STRATEGY FOR THE 2002 PRESIDENTIAL

ELECTION. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) IN SPITE OF INFORMATION AND PUBLICITY MINISTER

JONATHAN MOYO\'S (UNCONVINCING AND TEPID) EXPRESSION OF

CONCERN AFTER THE JANUARY 28 BOMBING OF THE "DAILY

NEWS" PRINTING PRESS, THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE\'S

(GOZ) CAMPAIGN TO RESTRICT FREEDOM OF THE PRESS AND

ACCESS TO INFORMATION HAS INTENSIFIED. A FEBRUARY 3

NOTICE IN GOVERNMENT-OWNED NEWSPAPERS INSTRUCTED ALL

JOURNALISTS TO APPLY FOR NEW CREDENTIALS TO COVER

PARLIAMENT. THE REGISTRATION PERIOD RUNS THROUGH

FEBRUARY 16, BUT THE APPARENT INTENT OF THIS MOVE IS

TO ALLOW THE GOVERNMENT TO RESTRICT PARLIAMENTARY

COVERAGE TO "FRIENDLY" JOURNALISTS.

3. (C) THE MINISTRY OF INFORMATION HAS ALSO SCRAPPED

CENTRALIZED ISSUANCE OF PRESS CREDENTIALS FOR COVERING

THE GOZ. JOURNALISTS MUST NOW APPLY TO EACH MINISTRY

OR BRANCH OF GOVERNMENT FOR THE CREDENTIAL REQUIRED

FOR ACCESS. INDEPENDENT JOURNALISTS ARE ALREADY

FEELING THE EFFECTS OF GOZ FIATS BANNING THEM FROM

SOME OFFICES AND EVENTS. ALL LEVELS OF THE ZIMBABWE

REPUBLIC POLICE, EVEN POLICE PUBLIC AFFAIRS OFFICIALS,

NOW FLATLY REFUSE TO SPEAK TO JOURNALISTS FROM THE

INDEPENDENT MEDIA. INDEPENDENT MEDIA HAVE ALSO

RECENTLY BEEN BARRED FROM ATTENDING GOZ PRESS

CONFERENCES AND WERE BANNED FROM THE PUBLIC VIEWING OF

LAURENT KABILA\'S BODY, AN EVENT THE GOVERNMENT-OWNED

MEDIA COVERED FULLY.

4. (U) IN JUNE 2000 THE GOZ INITIATED A US$100

ACCREDITATION FEE FOR FOREIGN JOURNALISTS WISHING TO

COVER ZIMBABWE\'S PARLIAMENTARY ELECTIONS. THAT FEE

REMAINS IN FORCE, SUPPLEMENTED BY A US$500 FEE FOR A

TEMPORARY IMPORT PERMIT REQUIRED TO BRING TELEVISION

EQUIPMENT INTO ZIMBABWE. THESE FEES ARE AFFORDABLE BY

7. (C) COMMENT: THIS SYSTEMATIC ATTACK ON THE

INDEPENDENT MEDIA FITS IN NEATLY WITH THE SIMULTANEOUS

GOZ EFFORT TO COW THE JUDICIARY AND THE OPPOSITION,

AND IS A KEY COMPONENT IN THE GOVERNMENT\'S STRATEGY TO

RESTRICT THE PUBLIC\'S ACCESS TO ALTERNATIVE VIEWS IN

THE RUN-UP TO THE 2002 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION. TAKEN

BY ITSELF, THE CAMPAIGN AGAINST THE INDEPENDENT MEDIA

IS AN INSIDIOUS THREAT TO FREEDOM OF THE PRESS. IN

COMBINATION WITH THE ATTACKS AGAINST THE JUDICIARY AND

THE OPPOSITION, IT IS A DANGEROUS ASSAULT ON

FUNDAMENTAL, CONSTITUTIONALLY-GUARANTEED HUMAN RIGHTS

AND WILL BOLSTER ZIMBABWE\'S INCREASINGLY AUTHORITARIAN

TREND. END COMMENT.
