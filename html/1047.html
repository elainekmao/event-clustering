SUBJECT: U.S. GOVERNMENT ELECTION SUPPORT ACTIVITIES

BELOW IS A COMPILATION OF USG ACTIVITIES IN SUPPORT OF

LEVELING THE PLAYING FIELD FOR ZIMBABWE\'S YEAR 2000

PARLIAMENTARY ELECTIONS PREPARED BY THE USAID MISSION IN

HARARE:

BACKGROUND: THE U.S. GOVERNMENT (USG) IS PROVIDING

SUBSTANTIAL ASSISTANCE TO SUPPORT ELECTION ACTIVITIES IN

ZIMBABWE. THIS ASSISTANCE RANGES FROM SUPPORTING DOMESTIC

MONITORING TO AUDITING THE VOTERS\' ROLL. MOST

SIGNIFICANTLY, USG ASSISTANCE IS PROVIDING SUPPORT FOR

10,200 DOMESTIC MONITORS, WHICH AT THE CURRENT NUMBER OF

POLLING STATIONS AMOUNTS TO THREE MONITORS PER STATION. WE

ARE ALSO PROVIDING TRAINING TO POLITICAL PARTIES FOR POLLING

AGENTS, AND IF PREDICTIONS STAND, THIS WILL RESULT IN SIX

POLLING AGENTS PER STATION. ALL TOLD, THE USG IS PROVIDING

TIMELY AND IMPORTANT ASSISTANCE THAT WILL HELP TO ENSURE

THAT ELECTORAL SYSTEMS WILL BE MONITORED, AUDITED, AND

STRENGTHENED.

DOMESTIC MONITORING

-------------------

-- SUPPORT TO THE ZIMBABWE COUNCIL OF CHURCHES FOR 3,000

DOMESTIC MONITORS AND ELECTRONIC MEDIA EVENTS. (IRI)

-- SUPPORT TO THE NATIONAL CONSTITUTIONAL ASSEMBLY FOR

7,200 DOMESTIC MONITORS. (OTI)

-- NOTE: THERE ARE EIGHT CSOS THAT ARE FIELDING A TOTAL OF

17,150 DOMESTIC MONITORS. THIS INCLUDES: CCJP (1,500), ZCC

(3,000), ZIMRIGHTS (1,400), FODEZI (1,000), NCA (7,200),

ZIMBABWE FELLOWSHIP (1,000), EFZ (2,000), MET FOUNDATION

(50). IF THERE ARE 3,600 POLLING STATIONS, THEN THIS

EQUATES TO APPROXIMATELY FIVE MONITORS PER STATION. WE CAN

CONFIRM THE ZCC AND NCA NUMBERS WHICH MEANS THAT THE USG IS

PROVIDING FUNDING FOR 10,200 DOMESTIC MONITORS WHICH AT THE

CURRENT NUMBER OF POLLING STATIONS AMOUNTS TO THREE MONITORS

PER STATION. FUNDING OF MONITORS FOR THESE OTHER

ORGANIZATIONS IS IN PROCESS. THE ELECTORAL SUPERVISORY

COMMISSION HAS CALLED FOR A MINIMUM OF FOUR MONITORS PER

STATION AND WITH THESE ESTIMATED NUMBERS, THAT GOAL WILL BE

ATTAINED.

-- TRAINING OF TRAINERS\' WORKSHOP FOR POLITICAL PARTY

POLLING AGENT TRAINERS: 720 TRAINERS REPRESENTING SIX

PARTIES WILL BE TRAINED. THESE TRAINERS WILL THEN TRAIN

THEIR RESPECTIVE POLLING AGENTS. THE NUMBER OF POLLING

AGENTS FOR EACH PARTY IS AS FOLLOWS: MDC (8,000 TO 10,000);

ZANU-PF (8,000 TO 10,000); AND ZUD, ZIP, UP, AND SAPU (TOTAL

FOR ALL FOUR PARTIES: 120). ALL TOLD, 20,120 POLLING AGENTS

WILL ALSO BE MONITORING THE ELECTION. IF THERE ARE 3,600

POLLING STATIONS, THEN THIS EQUATES TO APPROXIMATELY SIX

POLLING AGENTS PER STATION. (NDI)

REVIEW OF THE VOTERS ROLL

-------------------------

-- SUPPORT TO FODEZI TO REVIEW THE VOTERS ROLL AND CONDUCT

A VOTER\'S ROLL AUDIT. A REPRESENTATIVE SAMPLE OF HOUSEHOLDS

IN 30 CONSTITUENCIES WILL BE TESTED. IF RESEARCH SHOWS VAST

PROBLEMS, FUNDS MAY BE UTILIZED TO MOUNT A COURT CASE TO

STOP ELECTIONS. TRAINING OF 120 ENUMERATORS WILL BEGIN IN

THE SECOND WEEK OF APRIL FOR FIVE DAYS IN EACH CONSTITUENCY.

CODE OF CONDUCT

-----------------

-- A NUMBER OF WORKSHOPS HAVE BEEN ORGANIZED TO BRING

TOGETHER SIX POLITICAL PARTIES TO DEVELOP A CODE OF CONDUCT.

ALL THESE PARTIES, INCLUDING ZANU-PF, HAVE AGREED TO SIGN

IT. NEXT STEPS INCLUDE MORE WORK ON ADVANCING THE CODE WITH

ZANU-PF. (NDI)

ELECTORAL SUPERVISORY COMMISSION (ESC)

---------------------------------------

-- SUPPORT TO A LOCAL NGO (ZIMBABWE WOMEN\'S RESOURCE

CENTER AND NETWORK) WHICH WILL CHANNEL AND ACCOUNT FOR

RESOURCES TO THE ESC FOR ADMINISTRATIVE AND LOGISTIC ISSUES

RELATED TO THE ELECTION. (OTI)

STUDY TOURS

------------

-- STUDY TOUR FOR EIGHT REFORMERS TO WASHINGTON, DC TO

MEET A WIDE RANGE OF SENIOR U.S. PUBLIC OFFICIALS, POLITICAL

PROFESSIONALS, AND OTHER ORGANIZATIONS TO SHARE INSIGHTS ON

DEMOCRATIC REFORM IN ZIMBABWE AND LEARN FROM POLITICAL

ACTIVISTS IN THE U.S. (IRI)

-- STUDY TOURS FOR POLITICAL PARTIES AND GOVERNMENT

ELECTORAL OFFICIALS TO VISIT REGIONAL ELECTION COMMISSIONS

AND MONITOR REGIONAL ELECTIONS. ALL TOLD, FOUR TRIPS WERE

CONDUCTED AND ELECTION REFORM ISSUES WERE IDENTIFIED. BASED

ON THESE TRIPS, ROUNDTABLE DISCUSSIONS BEGAN WHICH FORMED

THE BASIS FOR THE WORK ON A CODE OF CONDUCT. (NDI)
