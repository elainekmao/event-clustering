SUBJECT: ITALY UNDERSTANDING OF U.S. VIEWS ON MERCURY, BUT

EU COUNCIL LIKELY TO ENDORSE BINDING INSTRUMENT PROPOSAL

REF: STATE 242216

1. EST COUNS MET DEC.4 WITH GIULIANA GASPARINNI, HEAD OF

MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT DIVISION RESPONSIBLE FOR CHEMICALS

POLICY AND EU AFFAIRS. SHE AGREED THAT THAT THE USG

SUGGESTION TO FORM A MERCURY UNIT WITHIN UNEP WOULD BE

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 ROME 05864 060637Z

USEFUL, AND ALSO AGREED THAT AN AMENDMENT TO THE STOCKHOLM

CONVENTION FOR MERCURY IN ADVANCE OF THE ENTRY INTO FORCE OF

THE CONVENTION WOULD BE AN INAPPROPRIATE MEASURE. SHE ALSO

SAID THAT THE PRIMARY JOB WITH GLOBAL CHEMICALS REGIMES WAS

TO IMPLEMENT THOSE ALREADY EXISTING, AND NOT TO NEGOTIATE NEW

ONES.

2. GASPARRINI THEN ADDED, WITH SPECIFIC REFERENCE TO THE

ALREADY DRAFTED CONCLUSIONS PREPARED FOR THE DEC. 9

ENVIRONMENT COUNCIL MEETING, THAT IT APPEARED LIKELY THAT THE

COUNCIL WOULD ENDORSE A BINDING INSTRUMENT FOR THE MANAGEMENT

OF MERCURY UNDER PRESSURE FROM THE NORDIC EU STATES. SHE

PROMISED TO DO WHAT SHE COULD TO MODERATE ANY LANGUAGE THAT

MIGHT EMERGE WITH REGARD TO SCOPE AND TIMING, AND TO ARGUE

THAT THE U.S.APPROACH PROVIDED THE OPPORTUNITY TO MOVE

FORWARD ON THE ISSUE WITHOUT FALSE STARTS.
