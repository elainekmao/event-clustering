2. (C) SUMMARY: SANT\'EGIDIO COMMUNITY HOPES TO HOST A

CONFIDENTIAL OFF-SITE DISCUSSION BETWEEN THE GOS AND SPLM

SIPDIS

TO UNBLOCK THE IGAD PROCESS. SANT\'EGIDIO SUDAN EXPERT

RICCARDI, JUST BACK FROM KHARTOUM, WOULD LIKE TO SPEAK WITH

S/E DANFORTH AND/OR AMB. OAKLEY IN ROME IN DECEMBER.

ACTION REQUESTED: POST WOULD APPRECIATE GUIDANCE ON

RESPONDING TO RICCARDI\'S OFFER. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) FRESH BACK FROM HIS TRIP TO SUDAN, SANT\'EGIDIO

SUDAN SPECIALIST FABIO RICCARDI (TOGETHER WITH MARIO GIRO,

SANT\'EGIDIO\'S WEST AFRICA SPECIALIST) WAS EAGER TO TALK

WITH EMBASSY VATICAN ABOUT HIS MEETINGS IN KHARTOUM. ON

11/09 RICCARDI TOLD DCM THAT SANT\'EGIDIO HAS GOOD CONTACTS

WITH BOTH SIDES ON THE CONFLICT, AND THAT IT IS NOT

INTERESTED IN THE BLAME-GAME PRACTICED BY SOME OF SUDAN\'S

CATHOLIC BISHOPS. HE SAID SANT\'EGIDIO WISHES TO ENCOURAGE

DIALOGUE BETWEEN THE PARTIES AND BELIEVES THE POST 9/11

ATMOSPHERE OFFERS OPPORTUNITIES TO DO SO. HE REPLAYED

SUBSTANTIALLY THE SAME POINTS IN REF A, FOCUSING ON THE

FACT THAT IGAD HAS STALLED, AND SOME CATALYST IS NEED TO

REINVIGORATE IT. HE SAID THAT NEITHER PARTY, BUT

ESPECIALLY THE GOS, TAKES IGAD SERIOUSLY -- MOSTLY THEY USE

IT TO SCORE PROPAGANDA POINTS ON EACH OTHER. RICCARDI

PROPOSES THAT SANT\'EGIDIO HOST AT ITS HEADQUARTERS IN ROME

A SERIES OF CONFIDENTIAL FACE-TO-FACE DISCUSSIONS BETWEEN

REPRESENTATIVES OF THE GOS AND SPLM, WHICH WOULD PERMIT

CONFIDENTIAL

THEM TO "AGREE TO DISAGREE AND MOVE ON" (REF B), THUS

UNBLOCKING THE EXISTING PROCESS. (NOTE: THIS IS A FORMULA

WHICH WORKED TEN YEARS AGO WHEN SANT\'EGIDIO MEDIATED THE

END TO THE MOZAMBIQUE CIVIL WAR).

2. (C) RICCARDI TOLD US THAT HE HAD RAISED HIS IDEA OF

PRIVATE OFF-SITE MEETINGS WITH SENIOR SUDANESE OFFICIALS,

INCLUDING GHAZZI SALAHADDIN AND THE FM (WHO HE SAID WAS

"STUPID"), NONE OF WHOM REJECTED IT. GARANG WOULD BE AN

OBSTACLE, HE SAID, INDICATING HE HAD SPOKEN WITH MUSEVENI

WHILE THE LATTER WAS IN LONDON, WHO GAVE HIM STRONG SIGNALS

THAT GARANG WOULD NEED PERSUADING. RICCARDI TOLD US THE

U.S. WOULD BE KEY, IF THIS PROPOSAL (WE HESITATE TO CALL IT

AN INITIATIVE) WERE TO GET OFF THE GROUND. IF GARANG COULD

BE PERSUADED, RICCARDI AND GIRO BOTH WERE CONFIDENT THEY

COULD GET MEANINGFUL GOS PARTICIPATION TOO.

3. (C) POST UNDERSTANDS THAT SPECIAL ENVOY DANFORTH AND

AF ARE CONTEMPLATING A VISIT TO EUROPEAN CAPITALS IN

DECEMBER. RICCARDI WOULD BE VERY INTERESTED IN MEETING THE

U.S. DELEGATION IF THAT WERE TO HAPPEN. WE SUPPORT SUCH A

MEETING AND REITERATE OUR OFFER (REF C) TO FACILITATE

MEETINGS BETWEEN THE S/E AND RICCARDI, AS WELL AS WITH ANY

OTHER INTERESTED VATICAN PARTIES.

NICHOLSON
