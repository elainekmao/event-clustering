SUBJECT: MUGABE TO DEPART FOR CONGO AFTERNOON OF JULY

12

REF: (A) HARARE 3465, (B) HARARE 3760

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD.

REASONS: 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) DEPUTY FOREIGN MINISTER NICHOLAS GOCHE TOLD

AMBASSADOR JULY 12 THAT PRESIDENT MUGABE WAS PLANNING TO

DEPART FOR LUBUMBASHI THAT AFTERNOON TO MEET WITH

PRESIDENT KABILA. HE IS LIKELY TO RETURN ON JULY 13.

ACCORDING TO GOCHE, MUGABE PROBABLY WILL BE ACCOMPANIED

BY DEFENSE MINISTER MOVEN MAHACHI AND MINISTER OF STATE

FOR NATIONAL SECURITY SYDNEY SEKERAMAYI. GOCHE SAID

THAT HE DID NOT BELIEVE REPRESENTATIVES OF ANY OTHER

GOVERNMENTS WOULD BE PRESENT AT THE MEETING.

2. (C) THE AMBASSADOR ASKED WHETHER MUGABE PLANNED TO

PRESS PRESIDENT KABILA TO ACCEPT SIR KETUMILE MASIRE AS

NATIONAL DIALOGUE FACILITATOR. HE NOTED THAT MUGABE AND

NAMIBIAN PRESIDENT SAM NUJOMA HAD APPARENTLY MADE A

STRONG PITCH TO KABILA IN SUPPORT OF MASIRE AT THE JUNE

25 MINI-SUMMIT IN HARARE (REF A). THE AMBASSADOR

POINTED OUT THAT REMOVING MASIRE AND APPOINTING ANOTHER

FACILITATOR WOULD SET THE PROCESS BACK BY MONTHS. GOCHE

REPLIED THAT HE HAD NO INFORMATION ON MUGABE\'S AGENDA

FOR THE MEETING, ALTHOUGH "OBVIOUSLY THE MASIRE ISSUE

WILL COME UP." HOWEVER, HE AGREED THAT IT WAS IMPORTANT
