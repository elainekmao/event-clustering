SUBJECT: MOVEMENT FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE LEADERSHIP TAKES

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR REASONS

SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) THE OPPOSITION MOVEMENT FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE (MDC)

HAS MOVED TO ADDRESS SERIOUS ORGANIZATIONAL PROBLEMS BY

HIRING A RESPECTED CIVIL SOCIETY ACTIVIST TO BE NATIONAL

COORDINATOR TO MANAGE THE PARTY\'S AFFAIRS AND A PUBLIC

RELATIONS PROFESSIONAL TO RUN ITS INFORMATION DEPARTMENT.

THE PARTY IS WORKING HARD TO DEVISE A RANGE OF THOROUGHLY

VETTED POLICIES, AND TO DISSEMINATE THEM WIDELY. HOWEVER,

LACK OF RESOURCES AND DISTRUST BETWEEN THE PARTY\'S YOUNG

AND OLD MEMBERS REMAIN SERIOUS PROBLEMS. DESPITE

INTENSIFYING INTIMIDATION AND HARASSMENT OF ITS SENIOR

LEADERS, THE MDC IS ACTIVELY PREPARING FOR THE UPCOMING

PRESIDENTIAL CAMPAIGN. THE RECENT ELECTION OF A PRO-MDC

SLATE TO THE LEADERSHIP OF THE ZIMBABWE CONGRESS OF TRADE

UNIONS (ZCTU) PROVIDES A SIGNIFICANT BOOST TO THE ELECTORAL

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 00930 01 OF 04 061550Z

STRATEGY OF THE OPPOSITION PARTY, WHICH ALSO IS MOVING

ENERGETICALLY TO COURT RURAL VOTERS, HISTORICALLY A ZANU-PF

STRONGHOLD. END SUMMARY.

2. (U) WE HAVE MET RECENTLY WITH A VARIETY OF SENIOR

OFFICIALS OF THE OPPOSITION MOVEMENT FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE

(MDC), INCLUDING DEPUTY SECRETARY-GENERAL GIFT CHIMANIKIRE,

MPS AND EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE MEMBERS TRUDY STEVENSON, DAVID

COLTART, PAUL NYATHI, AND TENDAI BITI, AND ASSISTANT TO THE

PRESIDENT GANDI MUDZINGWA. THEY PROVIDED KEEN INSIGHTS ON

THE MDC\'S INTERNAL DYNAMICS AND ITS PRIORITIES, INCLUDING

THE PARTY\'S STRATEGY FOR THE UPCOMING PRESIDENTIAL CAMPAIGN.

RESTRUCTURING

-------------

3. (C) THE LACK OF AN EFFECTIVE ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

HAS SERIOUSLY HAMPERED THE MDC\'S ABILITY TO MAINTAIN

INTERNAL COHESIVENESS, LET ALONE BUILD ON ITS ACHIEVEMENTS

IN LAST JUNE\'S PARLIAMENTARY ELECTIONS. IN A FEBRUARY 27

CONVERSATION WITH POLITICAL SECTION CHIEF, MP DAVID COLTART

DESCRIBED THE PARTY\'S INFORMATION DEPARTMENT AS A "DISASTER"

AND THE ACCOUNTING DEPARTMENT AS A "MESS." HIS COLLEAGUE

TRUDY STEVENSON AGREED, POINTING OUT TO US ON FEBRUARY 23

THAT EVEN SENIOR OFFICIALS IN THE PARTY ARE NOT KEPT IN THE

LOOP ON MAJOR PARTY EVENTS. FOR INSTANCE, SHE HAD HEARD

THAT MDC RALLIES WOULD BE HELD THE FOLLOWING DAY IN

HARARE, BUT SHE, ALTHOUGH AN EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE MEMBER,

HAD NOT BEEN INFORMED WHERE OR WHEN THEY WOULD BE HELD.

4. (C) THE MDC HAS RECENTLY MOVED TO ADDRESS THIS SERIOUS

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 04 HARARE 00930 01 OF 04 061550Z

SHORTCOMING, HOWEVER, BY HIRING A NATIONAL COORDINATOR WHO

WILL, QUITE LITERALLY, COORDINATE AND ALL OF THE PARTY\'S

ACTIVITIES. (COMMENT: THE PERSON SELECTED FOR THIS POSITION,

ISAAC MAPOSA, DEMONSTRATED AN IMPRESSIVE TALENT FOR

MARSHALING PEOPLE AND RESOURCES WHEN HE WAS EXECUTIVE

DIRECTOR OF THE NATIONAL CONSTITUTIONAL ASSEMBLY\'S EFFORT

IN 1999 TO DRAFT A NEW CONSTITUTION. HE IS WELL-LIKED AND

WELL-RESPECTED, AND ALL OF THE MDC MEMBERS WITH WHOM WE

SPOKE WERE CONFIDENT THAT HE WOULD BE ABLE TO IMPOSE SOME

DISCIPLINE ON A PARTY WHICH SORELY NEEDS IT. MAPOSA

REPORTEDLY TOOK UP HIS NEW DUTIES IN EARLY MARCH, IN SPITE

OF UNRESOLVED QUESTIONS ABOUT REMUNERATION. END COMMENT.)

5. (C) STEVENSON TOLD US THAT THE MDC PLANS TO HOLD

REGULAR POLICY RETREATS ON PARTICULAR ISSUES. THE FIRST

ONE -- HELD IN LATE JANUARY -- FOCUSED ON THE ECONOMY AND

LAND; THE PARTY\'S NATIONAL EXECUTIVE, MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT,

AND PROVINCIAL CHAIRPERSONS ALL PARTICIPATED. THE

OBJECTIVES OF THE RETREATS, STEVENSON STRESSED, ARE TO

THOROUGHLY VET POLICY POSITIONS BEFORE THEY ARE ANNOUNCED

PUBLICLY AND TO ENSURE THAT MPS AND OTHER SENIOR PARTY

OFFICIALS DELIVER A CONSISTENT MESSAGE. THE PARTY\'S

POSITIONS WILL SUBSEQUENTLY BE EXPLAINED IN REGULAR

MEETINGS TO CONSTITUENTS AROUND THE COUNTRY.

6. (C) STEVENSON LAMENTED THAT THE PARTY\'S INFORMATION

AND PUBLICITY DEPARTMENT HAS BEEN INEFFECTUAL, IN LARGE

PART BECAUSE IT WAS RUN BY A LAWYER -- MP LEARNMORE

JONGWE -- WHO HAS NO ACADEMIC PREPARATION OR PRACTICAL

EXPERIENCE IN PUBLIC RELATIONS. SHE EXPRESSED HOPE,

HOWEVER, THAT THE RECENT HIRING OF A PERSON WITH A

GRADUATE DEGREE IN COMMUNICATIONS WOULD HELP THE PARTY

SHAPE AND DISSEMINATE A CONSISTENT, EFFECTIVE MESSAGE

AND DEVELOP A DYNAMIC PUBLIC RELATIONS STRATEGY.

LACK OF RESOURCES

-----------------

7. (C) A LACK OF RESOURCES CONTINUES TO POSE A SERIOUS

IMPEDIMENT FOR THE MDC. MDC MP AND DIRECTOR OF ELECTIONS

PAUL NYATHI SAID THE PARTY LITERALLY HAS ALMOST NO MONEY

IN THE BANK. ONE REASON THE PARTY FINDS ITSELF IN SUCH

DIRE FINANCIAL STRAITS IS THE GOVERNMENT\'S STEADFAST

REFUSAL TO RELEASE FUNDS TO WHICH THE MDC IS ENTITLED

UNDER THE POLITICAL PARTIES ACT, BY VIRTUE OF ITS STRONG

SHOWING IN LAST JUNE\'S PARLIAMENTARY ELECTIONS. ON

FEBRUARY 22, THE GOVERNMENT FINALLY AGREED TO HONOR ITS

OBLIGATION UNDER THE LAW AND RELEASE WITHIN 30 DAYS 47

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 00930 02 OF 04 061551Z

MILLION ZIMBABWEAN DOLLARS (APPROXIMATELY U.S. $630,000)

TO THE MDC. MDC MPS DAVID COLTART AND PAUL NYATHI BELIEVE,

HOWEVER, THAT THE GOVERNMENT WILL FIND WAYS TO AVOID HONORING

THIS COMMITMENT. WHY, THEY BOTH ASKED, WOULD A GOVERNMENT

WHICH HAS DEMONSTRATED DISDAIN FOR THE RULE OF LAW PROVIDE

THE MDC WITH A FINANCIAL WINDFALL AS BOTH PARTIES GEAR UP

FOR A PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION? MANY OF THE MDC\'S DOMESTIC

SOURCES OF REVENUE HAVE DRIED UP, TESTAMENT TO THE

EFFECTIVENESS OF THE GOVERNMENT\'S CAMPAIGN OF INTIMIDATION

AGAINST THE PARTY\'S SUPPORTERS. COLTART AND NYATHI ARE

PLANNING A FUNDRAISING TRIP IN LATE MARCH TO LONDON,

NEW YORK, AND WASHINGTON, WHICH WILL TARGET PRIMARILY

ZIMBABWEAN EXPATRIATES.

INCREASING THREATS/HARASSMENT

-----------------------------

8. (C) THE MDC\'S EFFORT TO TRANSFORM ITSELF FROM A PROTEST

MOVEMENT INTO A GENUINE, EFFECTIVE POLITICAL PARTY HAS BEEN

HAMPERED BY INTENSIFYING GOVERNMENT-SANCTIONED HARASSMENT

AND INTIMIDATION. DURING THE LAST MONTH, SENIOR MDC

OFFICIALS -- INCLUDING PRESIDENT MORGAN TSVANGIRAI AND

VICE PRESIDENT GIBSON SIBANDA -- HAVE BEEN CHARGED WITH

INCITING VIOLENCE (REFS C AND D), WHILE SECURITY SECRETARY

JOB SIKHALA AND HIS PREGNANT WIFE WERE TWICE BEATEN IN THEIR

HOME BY MEN WEARING ARMY UNIFORMS. IN ADDITION, FOUR MEN IN

CIVILIAN CLOTHES ARRIVED AT SIKHALA\'S HOUSE IN A POLICE

VEHICLE AT MIDNIGHT ON FEBRUARY 25 THREATENING TO ARREST

SIKHALA, BUT HE AND HIS FAMILY HID IN A NEARBY FIELD AFTER

BEING WARNED BY THEIR GUARD. THE UNKNOWN MEN BEAT SIKHALA\'S

GUARD AND FIRED AT LEAST ONE SHOT AT HIM AFTER HE MANAGED TO

WERE ARRESTED AND CHARGED UNDER THE COLONIAL-ERA LAW AND ORDER

MAINTENANCE ACT (LOMA) WITH INCITING PUBLIC VIOLENCE AT A

GEARING UP FOR A PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION

--------------------------------------

10. (C) DESPITE THE CHALLENGES ELABORATED ABOVE, THE MDC

IS PREPARING ENERGETICALLY FOR THE UPCOMING PRESIDENTIAL

ELECTION. ACCORDING TO MDC DIRECTOR FOR ELECTIONS PAUL

NYATHI, THE PARTY HAS FORMED A "HUSH-HUSH" NATIONAL ELECTION

DIRECTORATE IN HARARE THAT IS PLOTTING STRATEGY. THE MDC

HAS BEEN HOLDING WELL-ATTENDED RALLIES NATIONWIDE, WHERE IT

IS ENCOURAGING PEOPLE TO HANG TOUGH IN THE FACE OF VIOLENCE

SECURITY FORCES HAVE BEEN BEATING SUSPECTED OPPOSITION

SUPPORTERS, "TO GIVE PEOPLE COURAGE."

11. (C) IN EARLY MARCH, MDC ACTIVISTS FROM RURAL AREAS WERE

PLANNING TO GATHER IN HARARE TO STRATEGIZE ON HOW TO BOLSTER

SUPPORT IN THE RURAL AREAS. IN THE MEANTIME, THE PARTY IS

DISSEMINATING ITS MESSAGE THROUGH RURAL CHURCHES AND

RURAL-BASED MEMBERS OF THE ZCTU. THE RECENT ELECTION OF A

PRO-MDC SLATE TO THE ZCTU LEADERSHIP, NYATHI POINTED OUT,

WAS CRUCIAL TO THE MDC\'S ABILITY TO PENETRATE THE RURAL AREAS.

IN LIGHT OF THE GOVERNMENT\'S ONGOING CAMPAIGN OF INTIMIDATION

AGAINST COMMERCIAL FARM OWNERS AND THEIR WORKERS, THE

120,000-STRONG FARM WORKERS UNION REPRESENTS ESPECIALLY

FERTILE GROUND FOR THE MDC. THE OPPOSITION PARTY IS ALSO

PAYING SUPPORTERS LIVING IN URBAN AREAS TO RETURN TO THEIR

RURAL VILLAGES TO CAMPAIGN QUIETLY. (COMMENT: WE DO NOT KNOW

THE SOURCE OF THE MONEY WITH WHICH THIS IS OSTENSIBLY BEING

DONE. END COMMENT). THIS IS POTENTIALLY AN EFFECTIVE APPROACH,

IS THE KEY BATTLEGROUND PROVINCE, CALLING IT "OUR FLORIDA."

THE SOUTHERN PROVINCE HAS THE LARGEST POPULATION AND IS

POLITICALLY ASTUTE. IN ADDITION, THE RECENT EXPULSION OF

LONG-TIME ZANU-PF STALWART AND MASVINGO "SUPREMO" EDDISON

ZVOBGO FROM THE POLITBURO HAS ANGERED MANY OF HIS SUPPORTERS

AND COULD LEAD THEM TO SHIFT THEIR ALLEGIANCE TO THE MDC.

PRIOR TO JANUARY 1, 2002. COLTART AND NYATHI WERE CONVINCED

THAT THE RECENT INTENSIFICATION OF VIOLENCE AND INTIMIDATION

-- AGAINST THE JUDICIARY, THE INDEPENDENT MEDIA, THE MDC, AND

ORDINARY CITIZENS IN HARARE\'S HIGH-DENSITY SUBURBS --

SUGGESTS AN EARLY ELECTION. ON THE OTHER HAND, TSVANGIRAI\'S

ASSISTANT GANDI MUDZINGWA BELIEVES MUGABE WILL NOT STAND AGAIN,

SPEAKER OF PARLIAMENT EMMERSON MNANGAGWA OR FINANCE MINISTER

SIMBA MAKONI AS HIS SUCCESSOR. TRUDY STEVENSON DOUBTED THE

POSSIBILITY OF AN EARLY ELECTION, AS ZANU-PF WAS NOT

WELL-ORGANIZED ENOUGH TO PULL OFF A VICTORY IN SO SHORT A TIME.

ELECTORAL CHALLENGES

--------------------

14. (C) THE MDC\'S CHALLENGES OF THE RESULTS IN 37 ELECTORAL

CONSTITUENCIES ARE PROCEEDING APACE IN THE HIGH COURT. FOUR

CASES HAVE NOW BEEN COMPLETED, BUT THE JUDGES HAVE NOT YET

RENDERED THEIR DECISIONS. IN FACT, MOST OBSERVERS EXPECT

THEM TO RESERVE JUDGMENT UNTIL ALL 37 CASES HAVE BEEN HEARD,

MEANING WE ARE UNLIKELY TO SEE A FINAL RESOLUTION FOR MANY

MORE MONTHS. COLTART SAID THE INITIAL CASES DID NOT GO

PARTICULARLY WELL -- SOME OF THE MDC\'S WITNESSES WERE TOO

AGGRESSIVE OR UNABLE TO EXPLAIN HOLES IN THEIR STORIES --

JUDGES DO NOT ULTIMATELY SET ASIDE THE RESULTS IN ANY OF

COMMENT

-------

15. (C) THE MDC APPEARS TO BE REGAINING SOME OF THE

POLITICAL MOMENTUM IT CEDED TO ZANU-PF IN THE MONTHS AFTER

LAST JUNE\'S PARLIAMENTARY ELECTION IN SPITE OF THE GOVERNMENT\'S INTIMID

ATION

CAMPAIGN -- OR PERHAPS BECAUSE OF IT. THERE IS

A SENSE OF EXCITEMENT AMONG ITS SENIOR OFFICIALS THAT WE HAVE

NOT SEEN SINCE THE EUPHORIA OF THEIR ELECTORAL SHOWING LAST

JUNE. THE HIRING OF A NATIONAL COORDINATOR AND A PUBLIC

RELATIONS PROFESSIONAL, ALONG WITH THE DEVELOPMENT OF A

RIGOROUS INTERNAL POLICY-VETTING SHOP, WILL HELP ADDRESS SOME

OF THE PARTY\'S STRUCTURAL WEAKNESSES, ESPECIALLY THE ABSENCE

OF CLEAR POLICY POSITIONS AND INEFFECTUAL INTERNAL AND

EXTERNAL COMMUNICATION. THE NATIONAL COORDINATOR LIKELY WILL

BE ABLE TO USE HIS IMPRESSIVE MANAGEMENT AND INTERPERSONAL

SKILLS TO ALLEVIATE SOME OF THE PARTY\'S FACTIONAL TENSIONS.

THE MDC IS BEGINNING TO SHOW SIGNS OF POLITICAL MATURITY AS

WELL, BY REACHING OUT TO THE DIPLOMATIC HEADS OF MISSION

(REF A) AND ADVOCATING THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY\'S

CONSTRUCTIVE ENGAGEMENT WITH THE MUGABE GOVERNMENT, AS OPPOSED

TO IMPOSITION OF SANCTIONS. THE LACK OF RESOURCES, HOWEVER,
