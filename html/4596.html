1. (C) SUMMARY. AFRICA DG CABRAS REQUESTED A MEETING WITH

POLMINCOUNS TO GET U.S. VIEWS ON THE CRISIS IN NIGERIA AND

REVIEW OTHER TIMELY ISSUES. SOME ITALIAN NGOS ARE CONCERNED

ABOUT THE APPLICATION OF THE DEATH PENALTY IN NIGERIA, BUT

CABRAS AGREED THAT THE GOI\'S FOREIGN POLICY PRIORITY MUST BE

FREE AND FAIR ELECTIONS. ON ZIMBABWE, CABRAS MENTIONED THAT

AN UPCOMING EU MEETING WOULD DISCUSS HOW TO HANDLE THE

QUESTION OF WHETHER TO INVITE MUGABE TO NEXT YEAR\'S EU-AU

MINISTERIAL. THE EU MEETING WOULD ALSO DISCUSS COTE

D\'IVOIRE, AND THE GOI WOULD PUSH FOR GREATER EU INVOLVEMENT

IN PEACE NEGOTIATIONS. POLMINCOUNS RAISED THE FOOD CRISIS IN

SOUTHERN AND EASTERN AFRICA, AND CABRAS SPOKE BRIEFLY ABOUT

TERRORISM IN KENYA. END SUMMARY.

----------------------------------

NIGERIA: DEATH PENALTY, ELECTIONS

----------------------------------

2. (C) ON DECEMBER 2, POLMINCOUNS MET WITH BRUNO CABRAS,

THE MFA DIRECTOR GENERAL FOR AFRICAN AFFAIRS. CABRAS

REQUESTED THE MEETING IN ORDER TO PREPARE THE FONMIN FOR AN

UPCOMING MEETING WITH NESSUNO TOCCHI CAINO (LET NOBODY TOUCH

CAIN), A LOCAL ANTI-DEATH PENALTY NGO. NOTE: THE DEATH

SENTENCES AGAINST TWO UNWED NIGERIAN MOTHERS ATTRACTED

WIDESPREAD CONCERN IN ITALY, PUTTING NIGERIA ON THE MFA\'S

RADAR SCREEN. END NOTE. POLMINCOUNS DREW FROM REF A POINTS

ON ELECTION ASSISTANCE, TO WHICH CABRAS REPLIED, "OUR

POSITIONS COINCIDE ALMOST PERFECTLY." THE GOI\'S FIRST

ETHNIC AND RELIGIOUS DIFFERENCES PER SE.

---------------------------------------

EU-AU: THE ZIMBABWE QUESTION, ROUND II

---------------------------------------

3. (C) IN A DECEMBER 4 MEETING IN BRUSSELS, EU AFRICA

HANDS WILL DISCUSS HOW TO HANDLE THE EU-AU MINISTERIAL

PLANNED FOR LISBON IN 2003, IN LIGHT OF THE FAILURE OF THE

EU-SADC MAPUTU "SOLUTION" TO THE DILEMMA OF ACCORDING MUGABE

THE HONOR OF AN INVITATION TO EUROPE. CABRAS ASSERTED THAT

SINCE THE PURPOSE OF THE MEETING WAS DIALOGUE, NO COUNTRY

COULD BE EXCLUDED. HE BELIEVED THAT POSTPONEMENT WOULD "SEND

A VERY BAD MESSAGE" AND THAT THE AU STATES WOULD NOT COME IF

MUGABE WERE NOT INVITED. POLMINCOUNS SAID THAT THE SUMMIT IS

MUCH MORE IMPORTANT TO THE AU THAN IT IS TO THE EUROPEAN

LEADERS. THE EU SHOULD NOT BE SO HESITANT TO USE THAT

LEVERAGE TO FIND A WAY TO EXCLUDE MUGABE.

4. (C) TURNING TO COTE D\'IVOIRE, CABRAS SAID THE GOI\'S

FIRST PRIORITY WAS THE SUCCESS OF THE ECOWAS NEGOTIATIONS,

WHICH HE NOTED, "ARE NOT GOING WELL." THE ROLE OF PRESIDENT

WADE OF SENEGAL, WHO "WANTED TO BE CENTER STAGE" IN SPITE OF

OTHER COUNTRIES\' DISSENSION, WAS PROBLEMATIC, AND THE REBELS

HAD CALLED INTO QUESTION THE ENTIRE CONSTITUTIONAL STRUCTURE

OF CDI. IF THE GCI COULD NOT RECAPTURE MAN, THERE WOULD BE

NO MILITARY OPTION FOR THE GCI UNLESS FRANCE BACKED IT.

THERE WAS ALSO THE POSSIBILITY THAT GCI FORCES COULD TURN

AGAINST PRESIDENT GBAGBO, LEADING TO CDI\'S PARTITION AND

"ECONOMIC DISASTER FOR THE COUNTRY AND THE REGION."

5. (C) AT THE BRUSSELS MEETING, WHICH THE GOI WOULD CHAIR,

A DISCUSSION OF WHETHER DIRECT ENGAGEMENT BY THE EU IN CDI

COULD HELP WOULD BE "VERY, VERY HIGH ON THE AGENDA." THE

GOI, WHICH FAVORS ENGAGEMENT BY THE EU, MIGHT PROPOSE SENDING

A SPECIAL ENVOY TO "ORIENT THE NEGOTIATION" BETWEEN THE

PARTIES. CABRAS WASN\'T SURE THAT FRANCE WOULD BE

ENTHUSIASTIC ABOUT AN EU ROLE BUT PREDICTED THAT IN A FEW

WEEKS OR MONTHS, THE GOF WOULD WELCOME THIS SHARING OF THE

BURDEN.

6. (C) FURTHER DISCUSSION FOCUSED ON FOOD SECURITY AND

TERRORISM IN KENYA. POLMINCOUNS RAISED THE ISSUE OF THE FOOD

CRISIS IN SOUTHERN AND EASTERN AFRICA. IN SPITE OF THE

CONTROVERSIAL NATURE OF THE GMO ISSUE IN ITALY, CABRAS SAID,

"WE ARE ON THE SAME LINE (WITH YOU)." ASKED ABOUT KENYA,

POLMINCOUNS MENTIONED THAT WE HAD RELEASED A TRAVEL ADVISORY

ALSO COVERING DJIBOUTI AND YEMEN. CABRAS AGREED THAT SUCH

ACTION WAS NECESSARY BUT REGRETTED THE IMPACT OF SUCH

WARNINGS ON THE ECONOMIES OF THE COUNTRIES NAMED.
