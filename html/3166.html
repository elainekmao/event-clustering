1. (C) SUMMARY: AMBASSADOR-AT-LARGE FOR WAR CRIMES ISSUES

PIERRE-RICHARD PROSPER AND NSC SR. DIRECTOR DR. JENDAYI

FRAZER DISCUSSED WITH RWANDAN PRESIDENT PAUL KAGAME ON JUNE

13 THE LAUNCHING OF AN AGGRESSIVE CAMPAIGN TO APPREHEND

PERSONS INDICTED FOR GENOCIDE BY THE INTERNATIONAL CRIMINAL

TRIBUNAL FOR RWANDA (ICTR). THESE FUGITIVES ARE ALSO FUELING

THE ONGOING CONFLICT IN THE CONGO. PRES. KAGAME EXPRESSED

GRATITUDE FOR THE INITIATIVE AND SAID HE WOULD PROVIDE ANY

INFORMATION THE GOVERNMENT OF RWANDA HAS THAT MAY CONTRIBUTE

TO THEIR APPREHENSION. DR. FRAZER CALLED FOR AN

INVESTIGATION OF THE SUPPRESSION OF THE MUTINY IN KISANGANI.

PRES. KAGAME DENIED ANY INVOLVEMENT AND COMPLAINED ABOUT

ASSUMPTIONS OF RWANDAN CONTROL OF THE RCD. END SUMMARY.

REWARDS FOR JUSTICE

-------------------

2. (C) AMBASSADOR-AT-LARGE FOR WAR CRIMES ISSUES

PIERRE-RICHARD PROSPER AND SPECIAL ASSISTANT TO THE PRESIDENT

AND NSC SR. DIRECTOR FOR AFRICAN AFFAIRS DR. JENDAYI FRAZER

MET ON JUNE 13 WITH RWANDAN PRESIDENT PAUL KAGAME, WHO WAS IN

ROME FOR A WORLD FOOD SUMMIT. AMB. PROSPER DESCRIBED THE

LAUNCHING OF THE REWARDS FOR JUSTICE CAMPAIGN (REFTEL A) THAT

IS TARGETING FELICIEN KABUGA IN NAIROBI, KENYA. AMB. PROSPER

STATED THAT HE PLANS TO EXPAND THIS CAMPAIGN TO THE CONGO

REGION TO APPREHEND PERSONS INDICTED FOR GENOCIDE WHO REMAIN

AT LARGE THERE. THESE FUGITIVES WHO HOLD POSITIONS OF

LEADERSHIP WITH ALIR (A TERRORIST GROUP RESPONSIBLE FOR THE

MURDER OF AMERICAN CITIZENS IN BWENDI, UGANDA IN 1999) ARE

FUELING THE WAR IN CONGO. DR. FRAZER VOICED WHITE HOUSE

SUPPORT TO APPREHEND THE INDICTED LEADERSHIP, SO THAT

LOWER-LEVEL FIGHTERS COULD BE DEMOBILIZED AND REPATRIATED.

AMB. PROSPER ASKED FOR ANY INFORMATION ON KABUGA OR OTHER

FUGITIVES THAT THE GOVERNMENT OF RWANDA MAY HAVE.

3. (C) PRES. KAGAME EXPRESSED HIS APPRECIATION FOR THIS

&CRUCIAL8 EFFORT. HE STATED THAT THESE FUGITIVES

CONTRIBUTE SUBSTANTIALLY TO THE SECURITY PROBLEM IN THE

REGION AND THEREFORE TO RWANDA,S PRESENCE IN THE CONGO. HE

COMMITTED TO SHARING ANY AVAILABLE INFORMATION THAT MAY

CONTRIBUTE TO THE APPREHENSION OF THESE FUGITIVES AND

WELCOMED THE REPATRIATION OF LOWER-LEVEL INDIVIDUALS FROM THE

CONGO.

4. (C) DR. FRAZER STRESSED THAT THE EFFORT TO APPREHEND

GENOCIDAIRES SOUGHT TO BUILD CONFIDENCE THAT RWANDA,S

SECURITY CONCERNS ARE BEING ADDRESSED. DR. FRAZER SAID THAT

THERE IS A NEED TO DEAL WITH BORDER SECURITY, SUGGESTING A

TECHNICAL TEAM LOOK INTO A POSSIBLE BUFFER ZONE.

5. (C) THE IDEA OF A BUFFER ZONE WAS A GOOD CONCEPT, KAGAME

STATED, ESPECIALLY WITHIN A LARGER CONTEXT OF DEALING WITH

RWANDA,S SECURITY CONCERNS. HE SAID IT IS NOT SUFFICIENT TO

SET A BUFFER ZONE IF THE OTHER SIDE CONTINUES TO TRAIN

COMBATANTS. PRES. KAGAME SAID RWANDA IS ANXIOUS TO LEAVE THE

CONGO, BUT THE QUESTION IS WHO WILL TAKE CARE OF THE FORCES

THAT WOULD COME IN TO THE VACUUM THERE AND THUS THREATEN

RWANDA.

6. (C) AMB. PROSPER RAISED WITH KAGAME HIS MEETING WITH

GENOCIDE SURVIVOR GROUPS ON JUNE 11 IN KIGALI DISCUSSING

THEIR COMPLAINTS ABOUT THE ICTR (REFTEL B) AND TOLD KAGAME

THAT HE SUBSEQUENTLY PRESSED THE ICTR REGISTRAR, ADAMA DIENG,

FOR FURTHER REFORMS AT THE ICTR TO ADDRESS THESE CONCERNS.

AMB. PROSPER STRESSED TO PRES. KAGAME THAT IF IT IS PERCEIVED

THAT RWANDA IS NOT COOPERATING WITH THE ICTR IT WILL CREATE

PROBLEMS WITH GETTING THE NECESSARY COOPERATION OF THE

DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC OF THE CONGO AND OTHER STATES TO

APPREHEND WANTED FUGITIVES. AMB. PROSPER TOLD PRES. KAGAME

THAT HE WOULD APPRECIATE HIS HELP IN GETTING THE SURVIVOR

GROUPS TO RESCIND THEIR SUSPENSION OF COOPERATION WITH THE

ICTR.

7. (C) PRES. KAGAME SAID HE HAD GIVEN FULL SUPPORT TO THE

ICTR DESPITE ITS SHORTCOMINGS. HINTING AT ICTR

INVESTIGATIONS OF THE RPA, PRES. KAGAME STATED THAT THE WORK

OF THE ICTR IS BECOMING POLITICIZED AND THAT THE FRENCH HAVE

A LOT OF INFLUENCE TO THE DETRIMENT OF THE WHOLE PROCESS.

AMB. PROSPER RESPONDED THAT HE WOULD LOOK INTO THIS, STATING

HE DID NOT WANT THE PROCESS TO BE POLITICIZED.

8. (C) AMB. PROSPER STATED THAT HE HOPED HIS NEXT MEETING

WITH PRES. KAGAME COULD EXPLORE IDEAS TO TRANSFER CASES FROM

THE ICTR TO RWANDA AS PART OF THE GOAL OF REACHING A

SUCCESSFUL CONCLUSION OF ICTR CASES BY 2008. PRES. KAGAME

STATED THAT HE WOULD BE MORE THAN WILLING TO HAVE SUCH

DISCUSSIONS. HE WOULD HAVE PREFERRED TO TRY GENOCIDAIRES IN

KISANGANI

---------

9. (C) DR. FRAZER STRESSED TO PRES. KAGAME THAT RWANDA NEEDS

TO INVESTIGATE THE SUPPRESSION OF THE MUTINY IN KISANGANI TO

HOLD THOSE RESPONSIBLE ACCOUNTABLE. PRES. KAGAME STATED THAT

HE WISHED A DISTINCTION COULD BE MADE BETWEEN RWANDA AND THE

RCD. HE ASKED WHY THE FAILURES OF THE MLC ARE NOT ATTRIBUTED

TO UGANDA, OR KABILA,S ACTIONS ATTRIBUTED TO ZIMBABWE OR

ANGOLA, TO WHICH DR. FRAZER COMMENTED THAT WE HAVE HAD A

SIMILAR MESSAGE TO UGANDA IN THE PAST. PRES. KAGAME STATED

THAT THE RWANDANS WERE THE FIRST TO LEAVE KISANGANI TWO YEARS

AGO, AND SINCE THEN THERE HAVE BEEN NO RWANDAN SOLDIERS

THERE. HE COMMENTED THAT THERE ARE CONGOLESE OF RWANDAN

ORIGIN IN KISANGANI WHO VISIT RWANDA, AND COMPLAINED THAT IF

THESE CONGOLESE SPEAK IN KENYARWANDA THEN PEOPLE CLAIM THEY

ARE RPA.
