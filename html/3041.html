SUBJECT: CODE OF CONDUCT FOR SOUTH CHINA SEA:

- ADOPTION BY JULY "50-50"

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 HANOI 01382 301028Z

1. (SBU) AT A WORKING LUNCH ON MAY 30, MFA ASEAN

DEPARTMENT DEPUTY DIRECTOR GENERAL PHAM HUU CHI TOLD POL/C

AND POLOFFS THAT HE RATED AGREEMENT AMONG ASEAN MEMBERS ON

THE FINAL LANGUAGE OF THE CODE OF CONDUCT IN TIME FOR THE

ASEAN MINISTERIAL MEETING IN JULY AS NO BETTER THAN "50-50."

(SEPTEL WILL REPORT ON MFA VIEWS OF THE RECENT SENIOR

OFFICIALS MEETING IN BRUNEI.)

2. (SBU) ACCORDING TO CHI, MALAYSIA CONTINUES TO INSIST

THAT THE CODE REFER ONLY TO THE SPRATLY ISLANDS -- NOT THE

SOUTH CHINA SEA OR "DISPUTED AREAS" -- BECAUSE IT DOES NOT

WANT THE CODE TO COVER THE SEVERAL ISLANDS MALAYSIA CLAIMS

IN THE SOUTHWEST PART OF THE SOUTH CHINA SEA. (THESE

ISLANDS ARE CONSIDERED OUTSIDE THE SPRATLY ARCHIPELAGO.)

DESPITE RECENT PHILIPPINE EFFORTS TO COME UP WITH A NEW

FORMULA, MALAYSIA IS STILL NOT SATISFIED, CHI NOTED. HE

NOTED THAT BEIJING HAD INDICATED THAT IT WOULD GO ALONG WITH

WHATEVER ASEAN CONSENSUS EMERGED ON THE EXACT WORDING.

3. (SBU) ASEAN WILL TRY TO RESOLVE THIS ISSUE AT A SENIOR

OFFICIALS MEETING IN JUNE IN VIENTIANE, WITH HOPES OF

AGREEMENT IN TIME FOR APPROVAL OF THE CODE AT THE AMM. CHI

SAID HE EXPECTS MFA VICE MINISTER NGUYEN DINH BIN WILL

REPRESENT THE GVN. INITIALLY, THE PLAN WAS FOR ONLY THE

FOUR ASEAN CLAIMANTS (BRUNEI, MALAYSIA, THE PHILIPPINES, AND

VIETNAM) TO ATTEND, BUT OTHER ASEAN MEMBERS ASKED TO

PARTICIPATE AS WELL.

4. (SBU) COMMENT: WHILE NO ONE HERE IS HOLDING HIS BREATH

ON THE IMMINENT RESOLUTION OF THIS LONG-STANDING ISSUE,

ASEAN APPEARS TO BE NUDGING CLOSER TO A WORKABLE CONSENSUS.

UNCLASSIFIED

WHILE THE CODE WOULD NOT BE LEGALLY BINDING, AS OFFICIALS

HERE STRESS, IT WOULD MARK AN IMPORTANT STEP FORWARD IN

REGIONAL SOLIDARITY AND FURTHER DIMINISHMENT OF TERRITORIAL

TENSIONS IN THE SOUTH CHINA SEAS.
