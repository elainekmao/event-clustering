SUBJECT: RESPONSE OF ZIMBABWEAN MUSLIM/ISLAMIC POLITICAL

LEADERS

REF: STATE 175484

1. ZIMBABWEAN MUSLIM/ISLAMIC LEADERS HAVE NOT OFFERED

PUBLIC COMMENTS ON EVENTS IN AFGHANISTAN.

2. MUSLIMS IN ZIMBABWE ARE PRIMARILY WELL EDUCATED AND

INFORMED MERCHANTS AND PROFESSIONALS, PEOPLE NOT EASILY

SWAYED BY EMOTIONAL RHETORIC. THE COMMUNITY\'S RELIGIOUS

LEADERS GENERALLY REFLECT THE SOPHISTICATION OF THEIR

CONSTITUENTS. ONE INFLUENTIAL MEMBER OF ZIMBABWE\'S SMALL

ISLAMIC COMMUNITY TOLD PAO THAT MUSLIMS HERE WERE

"HEARTBROKEN" ABOUT THE SEPTEMBER 11 ATTACKS AND DO NOT

IDENTIFY WITH BIN LADEN OR THE TALIBAN\'S RADICALLY

FUNDAMENTAL INTERPRETATION OF ISLAM. THE CONTACT SAID THAT

WHILE ZIMBABWEAN MUSLIMS MAY HAVE DOUBTS ABOUT THE U.S.

MILITARY ACTIONS AGAINST THE TALIBAN, THEY ARE MORE BROADLY

REFLECTIVE OF ZIMBABWEAN, RATHER THAN MUSLIM, MISGIVINGS

ABOUT THE WISDOM OF MILITARY ACTION AGAINST A TARGET AS

ELUSIVE AS BIN LADEN AND HIS TALIBAN SUPPORTERS.
