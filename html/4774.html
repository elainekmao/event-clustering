CLASSIFIED BY: POL MINISTER-COUNSELOR TOM COUNTRYMAN. REASON:1.5

(B)(D).

1. (C) SUMMARY: ON DECEMBER 17, MFA DIRECTOR GENERAL FOR

EUROPEAN INTEGRATION ROCCO CANGELOSI TOLD POLITICAL

MINISTER-COUNSELOR TOM COUNTRYMAN THAT THE GOI WAS, IN

GENERAL, PLEASED WITH THE RESULTS IN COPENHAGEN. WHILE

ACKNOWLEDGING THAT THE DECISION ON TURKEY COULD HAVE BEEN

MORE POSITIVE, HE FOCUSED ON THE NEED TO KEEP THE PRESSURE ON

TURKEY TO CONTINUE WITH REFORMS AND BE MORE COOPERATIVE ON

CYPRUS. CANGELOSI\'S RESPONSIBILITIES INCLUDE MFA

PREPARATIONS FOR THE ITALIAN EU PRESIDENCY BEGINNING JULY

2003, AND HE PROVIDED AN OUTLINE OF GOI PRIORITIES. FM

FRATTINI TOLD PARLIAMENT THAT THE LANGUAGE ON TURKEY WAS AN

"ACCEPTABLE COMPROMISE," PAVING THE WAY FOR TURKISH AGREEMENT

ON ESDP COOPERATION WITH NATO, AND CONFIRMED THE ITALIAN AND

GREEK PRESIDENCIES WOULD REFOCUS EU ATTENTION ON BALKAN AND

MED ISSUES. FRATTINI SAID THAT THE ECONOMIC BENEFITS OF

ENLARGEMENT WOULD OUTWEIGH THE COSTS. END SUMMARY

2. (C) CANGELOSI EMPHASIZED THE HISTORICAL IMPORT OF THE

DECISION TAKEN IN COPENHAGEN TO ENLARGE THE EU TO 25 MEMBERS

ON MAY 1, 2004. WHILE THE NEW MEMBERS WILL OF COURSE REQUIRE

AN "INTEGRATION PERIOD," THE PROCESS FOR THE 10 IS

IRREVERSIBLE AND HE PREDICTED THAT THERE WOULD BE "NO

SURPRISES" WITH ANY REQUIRED REFERENDA. ENLARGEMENT WILL

INCREASE STABILITY AND BOOST THE INTERNAL EUROPEAN MARKET; IT

ALSO MEANS THAT THE EU MUST REFORM ITS INSTITUTIONS. IT

CANNOT OPERATE AT 25 AS IT DID AT 15. REFORMS TO INCLUDE

QUALIFIED MAJORITY VOTING AND AMENDING THE SIX-MONTH ROTATING

PRESIDENCY WOULD BE NECESSARY, AND IT WILL BE UP TO THE GREEK

AND ITALIAN PRESIDENCIES TO DO THEIR PART TO PUSH THE REFORMS

THROUGH. CANGELOSI DESCRIBED THE REQUIRED REFORMS AS

EVOLUTION, NOT REVOLUTION; ADJUSTMENTS, NOT DEEP CHANGES.

3. (C) IN RESPONSE TO POLMIN\'S APPRECIATION FOR THE GOI\'S

STRONG SUPPORT FOR AN ACCESSION DATE FOR TURKEY, CANGELOSI

ASSERTED THAT IF NOT FOR PMS BERLUSCONI AND BLAIR\'S

INTERVENTION, THE SUMMIT STATEMENT WOULD HAVE BEEN EVEN LESS

FORWARD-LEANING ON TURKEY\'S EU ASPIRATIONS. TURKEY SHOULD

LOOK UPON COPENHAGEN AS A VICTORY. WE MUST NOW KEEP THE

PRESSURE ON TURKEY TO IMPLEMENT LEGISLATION AND KEEP ITS

PROMISES ON POLITICAL, ECONOMIC AND HUMAN RIGHTS REFORMS. HE

CONCEDED THAT PUBLIC AND PARLIAMENTARY OPINION IN EUROPE,

INCLUDING IN ITALY, WAS "SKEPTICAL" ON TURKEY, BUT ITS

INTEGRATION WITH EUROPE COULD DEMONSTRATE TO EUROPEAN AND

ISLAMIC COUNTRIES ALIKE THAT THERE IS A PLACE FOR A MUSLIM

NATION IN THE EU.

4. (C) MOVEMENT ON CYPRUS WAS LESS ENCOURAGING, BUT ACCORDING

TO CANGELOSI THE TURKISH CYPRIOTS WERE ANGRY AND FRUSTRATED

WITH DENKTASH FOR NOT MAKING PROGRESS IN COPENHAGEN. THEY

REALIZE THE IMPORTANCE OF THE EU TO THEIR OWN ECONOMIC

INTERESTS, AND WILL HOPEFULLY PRESSURE DENKTASH TO BE MORE

COOPERATIVE. FURTHER, HE CONTINUED, TURKEY WILL NOT ALLOW

THE PROCESS TO MOVE BACKWARD.

5. (C) THE GOI IS PLEASED WITH THE PROGRESS ON

ESDI/BERLIN-PLUS IN COPENHAGEN, SAID CANGELOSI, AND LOOKS

FORWARD TO THE FIRST TEST OF THE EUROPEAN DEFENSE CAPABILITY

IN MACEDONIA IN TASK FORCE FOX FOLLOW-ON AS EARLY AS SPRING

2003. THESE DEVELOPMENTS WOULD ALLOW EUROPE TO TAKE MORE

DIRECT RESPONSIBILITY FOR THE BALKANS. HE ACKNOWLEDGED THAT

MORE WORK WAS NEEDED ON THE DETAILS OF THE RELATIONSHIP

BETWEEN NATO AND ESDP TO AVOID DUPLICATION, AND EUROPE MUST

INCREASE DEFENSE SPENDING.

6. (C) CANGELOSI WAS PLEASED THAT THE COPENHAGEN

CONCLUSIONS HIGHLIGHTED THE IMPORTANCE OF CONTINUED PROGRESS

AND EU ENGAGEMENT IN THE BALKANS, AND NOTED THAT BOTH THE

GREEK AND ITALIAN PRESIDENCIES WOULD MAKE BALKAN (ZAGREB

PROCESS) AND MEDITERRANEAN (BARCELONA PROCESS) REGIONAL

STABILIZATION PRIORITIES IN THEIR RESPECTIVE PRESIDENCIES.

OTHER ITALIAN PRESIDENCY PRIORITIES WILL INCLUDE:

- THE INTER GOVERNMENTAL CONFERENCE AND SIGNING THE

"CONSTITUTION" DURING THE ITALIAN PRESIDENCY, WHICH WOULD

AVOID AN "INSTITUTIONAL BOTTLENECK" IN 2004, WHEN THE

EUROPEAN PARLIAMENTARY ELECTIONS, ENLARGEMENT, AND COMMISSION

SELECTION WOULD ALL OCCUR (SEPTEL REPORTS ON FRATTINI\'S AND

DEP PM FINI\'S REMARKS ON CONVENTION PROGRESS);

- FINAL ENLARGEMENT ARRANGEMENTS;

7. (U) LATER IN THE DAY, FM FRATTINI GAVE HIS VIEWS ON

COPENHAGEN RESULTS TO PARLIAMENT. HE CHARACTERIZED THE

LANGUAGE ON TURKEY AS AN "ACCEPTABLE COMPROMISE" AND SAID

THAT IF TURKEY CONTINUES TO PROGRESS ON REFORMS, IT SHOULD

BEGIN NEGOTIATIONS "WITHOUT DELAY" AFTER THE DECEMBER 2004

REVIEW. THE COMPROMISE ENGENDERED PROGRESS ON BERLIN-PLUS,

PAVING THE WAY FOR ESDP MILITARY DEPLOYMENT IN MACEDONIA POST

TASK FORCE FOX. HE CONFIRMED THAT THE GREEK AND ITALIAN

PRESIDENCIES WILL "REBALANCE" THE EU\'S ATTENTION TOWARDS THE

BALKANS AND MED.

8. (U) FRATTINI TOLD THE PARLIAMENT THAT THE BENEFITS FROM

ENLARGEMENT -- WIDER MARKETS, INCREASED INVESTMENTS -- WILL

BE GREATER THAN THE COSTS (APPROXIMATELY 40 BILLION EUROS).

HE ADDED THAT ITALY HAD OBTAINED A "GUARANTEE" THAT

STRUCTURAL FUNDS WOULD NOT BE REDUCED, AND WOULD BE INVOLVED

IN NEGOTIATIONS IN BRUSSELS TO PREVENT THEIR BEING CURTAILED.

FRATTINI MADE THESE REMARKS WITHOUT ELABORATION. POST WILL

FOLLOW UP WITH APPROPRIATE INTERLOCUTORS FOR FURTHER DETAILS.
