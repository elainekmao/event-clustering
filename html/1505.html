SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE LABOR FEDERATION OFFICIAL INSISTS THAT

LABOR STILL BACKS OPPOSITION MDC

REF: HARARE 573

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\' AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS 1.5 (B/D).

1. (C) SUMMARY: THE ASSISTANT SECRETARY-GENERAL OF THE

ZIMBABWE CONGRESS OF TRADE UNIONS (ZCTU) BELIEVES THAT

THE LABOR FEDERATION WILL MAINTAIN ITS CLOSE TIES TO THE

OPPOSITION MOVEMENT FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE (MDC) PARTY

AFTER ITS FEBRUARY CONGRESS. HE IS ALSO OF THE OPINION

THAT THE CONGRESS WILL PUBLICLY AFFIRM THAT THE

FEDERATION HAS NO FORMAL LINKAGE TO ANY POLITICAL PARTY,

THAT ZCTU EXECUTIVE COUNCIL MEMBERS SHOULD NOT BE SENIOR

OFFICIALS IN POLITICAL PARTIES, AND THAT INDIVIDUAL

MEMBERS ARE FREE TO SUPPORT THE POLITICAL PARTY OF THEIR

CHOICE. HOWEVER, HE PREDICTS THAT PRO-MDC PERSONNEL

WILL BE ELECTED TO FEDERATION LEADERSHIP, AND TRADE

UNIONISTS AND THE URBAN ELECTORATE WILL SUPPORT THE MDC

CANDIDATE IN THE 2002 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTIONS. END

SUMMARY.

2. (SBU) ON JANUARY 25, LABOFF MET WITH ONE OF TWO

ASSISTANT SECRETARIES-GENERAL OF THE ZCTU, NICHOLAS

MUDZENGERERE. MUDZENGERERE IS ALSO THE SECRETARY-

GENERAL OF THE ZIMBABWE CATERING AND HOTEL WORKERS

UNION, AND A MEMBER OF THE NATIONAL EXECUTIVE COUNCIL OF

THE MDC. MUDZENGERERE HAS BEEN ACTIVE IN UNION

ORGANIZATION HIS ENTIRE PROFESSIONAL LIFE. HE HAS BEEN

3. (C) MUDZENGERERE TOLD LABOFF THAT THE ZCTU CONGRESS

WOULD AFFIRM THE INDEPENDENCE OF THE LABOR FEDERATION

FROM ANY ONE POLITICAL PARTY, BUT ALSO INSISTED THAT THE

CONGRESS WOULD ELECT PRO-MDC UNION LEADERS TO THE

EXECUTIVE AND GENERAL COUNCIL. HE STATED THAT THE

CONGRESS WILL AFFIRM THAT MEMBERS OF THE EXECUTIVE

COUNCIL CANNOT ALSO BE SENIOR OFFICIALS OF POLITICAL

PARTIES. THIS IS BECAUSE THE ZCTU CONSTITUTION STATES

WHILE "EVERY MEMBER AND OFFICER OF A UNION, AND THE

ZCTU, IS FREE TO JOIN A POLITICAL PARTY OF THEIR CHOICE,

THAT RELATIONSHIP SHOULD NOT INTERFERE WITH HIS DUTIES

TO THE UNION AND CONGRESS." THIS CONSTITUTIONAL

PROVISION IS NOT NEW, AND COMES FROM THE COMMON SENSE

BELIEF THAT "ONE HEAD CANNOT WEAR TWO HATS," REMARKED

MUDZENGERERE.

4. (C) MUDZENGERERE CLARIFIED THAT THE ZCTU CONGRESS

WILL INTERPRET THE "NO INTERFERENCE WITH DUTIES" CLAUSE

AS ONLY EXCLUDING PARTY OFFICIALS AND LEADERS WHO HAVE

FULL-TIME AND NATIONAL PARTY RESPONSIBILITIES. LABOFF

ASKED IF THIS WOULD EXCLUDE HIM FROM REELECTION TO THE

ZCTU EXECUTIVE COUNCIL BECAUSE HE IS A MEMBER OF THE MDC

NATIONAL EXECUTIVE COUNCIL. MUDZENGERERE ANSWERED THAT

THIS WAS THE TYPE OF PARTY JOB THAT WAS NOT FULL TIME,

AND THEREFORE WOULD NOT PRESENT A CONFLICT. HE

INDICATED THAT MEMBERS OF BOTH HIS UNION AND OTHERS HAVE

ALREADY APPROACHED HIM PROPOSING THAT HE RUN FOR THE

7. (C) COMMENT: MUDZENGERERE MAY BE OVERLY OPTIMISTIC

REGARDING THE INABILITY OF MUGABE AND ZANU-PF TO GARNER

A PERCENTAGE OF UNION VOTES ONCE THEY PULL OUT ALL THEIR

DIRTY TRICKS AND IMPLEMENT AN "URBAN" STRATEGY. A

COMBINATION OF BRIBES, OFFERS OF PATRONAGE, AND COERCION

IS NOT OUT OF THE QUESTION IN THE RUN UP TO 2002.

HOWEVER, WE AGREE WITH HIS ASSESSMENT THAT ZANU-PF WILL

NOT WIN THE DAY AT THE UPCOMING FEBRUARY CONGRESS.

UNION WORKERS, INCLUDING THOSE IN MUDZENGERERE\'S

CATERING AND HOTEL WORKERS UNION, HAVE BEEN HAMMERED BY

THE ECONOMIC COLLAPSE BROUGHT ON BY MUGABE AND ZANU-PF.

THE WORKERS BELIEVE THEY HAVE BEEN BETRAYED, AND WILL

LIKELY ELECT A PRO-MDC EXECUTIVE AND GENERAL COUNCIL IN
