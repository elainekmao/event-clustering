1. (C) THE SWEDES, AS EU PRESIDENT, HAVE LED THE EU TROIKA\'S

DISCUSSIONS IN HARARE WITH THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE UNDER ARTICLE

8 OF THE COTONOU AGREEMENT, WHICH BEGAN EARLY THIS YEAR. THE

TROIKA\'S EFFORTS TO ENGAGE THE GOZ IN SUBSTANTIVE DISCUSSIONS ABOUT

ITS RANGE OF CONCERNS, HOWEVER, HAVE GOTTEN NOWHERE WITH MFA

PERMANENT SECRETARY WILLARD CHIWEWE, THE SENIOR GOZ INTERLOCUTOR IN

THIS EXERCISE.

2. (C) POLITICAL SECTION CHIEF WAS INFORMED BY HIS SWEDISH

COUNTERPART -- IRINA SCHOULGIN -- ON JUNE 26 THAT THE EU\'S GENERAL

AFFAIRS COUNCIL (COMPRISING FOREIGN MINISTERS) IS FED UP WITH THE

GOZ\'S REFUSAL TO ENGAGE SERIOUSLY WITH THE EU. THE COUNCIL DECIDED

IN BRUSSELS ON JUNE 25 TO DELIVER A TOUGH WARNING TO THE GOZ AND

INSTRUCTED THE SWEDISH AMBASSADOR IN HARARE TO DELIVER A LETTER TO

THE GOVERNMENT CONVEYING THE FOLLOWING POINTS:

--NOTING LACK OF SUBSTANTIAL PROGRESS IN ITS DIALOGUE WITH ZIMBABWE;

--EXPRESSING DEEP CONCERN OVER DEVELOPMENTS HERE, CALLING FOR:

A) AN END TO POLITICAL VIOLENCE;

B) AN INVITATION TO THE EU TO MONITOR THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION;

C) CONCRETE ACTIONS TO PROTECT FREEDOM OF THE MASS MEDIA;

D) INDEPENDENCE OF THE JUDICIARY AND RESPECT FOR ITS DECISIONS;

E) AN END TO ILLEGAL OCCUPATION OF PROPERTY;

--PROMISING TO FOLLOW CLOSELY DEVELOPMENTS IN THE ABOVE AREAS DURING

THE NEXT TWO MONTHS AND TO TAKE APPROPRIATE MEASURES IF SUBSTANTIAL

PROGRESS IS NOT ACHIEVED.

3. (C) THE LAST POINT, SCHOULGIN NOTED, IS A CLEAR THREAT TO MOVE

TO ARTICLE 96, WHICH COULD REQUIRE THE SUSPENSION OF ALL EU AID TO

COMMENT

-------

5. (C) ALTHOUGH WE HAVE NOT SEEN A COPY OF THE EU\'S LETTER, THE

DRAFT LANGUAGE DESCRIBED BY OUR SWEDISH COLLEAGUE IS VERY STRONG

INDEED. ACCORDING TO A NUMBER OF EU DIPLOMATS HERE, THE TWO-MONTH

DEADLINE IS FIRM; WITHOUT ACHIEVEMENT OF "SUBSTANTIAL PROGRESS"

DURING THAT PERIOD, THE EU APPEARS DETERMINED TO MOVE TO ARTICLE

96. IT IS REFRESHING TO SEE THE EU TAKING SUCH A HARD LINE.

ALTHOUGH WE ARE SKEPTICAL THAT THE LETTER WILL ELICIT SIGNIFICANT

PROGRESS, IT WILL PUT THE GOZ ON NOTICE THAT IT FACES SERIOUS

CONSEQUENCES FOR NOT TAKING CONCRETE STEPS TO ADDRESS EU CONCERNS.
