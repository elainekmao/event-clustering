(THE PARA WILL THEN READ): WHILE ROMAN CATHOLICISM IS NO

LONGER THE STATE RELIGION, ITS ROLE AS THE DOMINANT ONE

OCCASIONALLY GIVES RISE TO PROBLEMS - SOME OVERT, OTHERS

SUBTLY SOCIETAL. SUBSEQUENT TO A SERIES OF CHURCH

CONSULTATIONS WITH POLITICAL LEADERS PRIOR TO MAY 2001

ELECTIONS, PRESIDENT CARLO AZEGLIO CIAMPI UNDERLINED THE

SECULAR NATURE OF THE STATE AND THE CONSTITUTION\'S EXPLICIT

SEPARATION OF CHURCH AND STATE. RESPONDING TO THE POPE\'S

JANUARY 2002 CALL FOR CATHOLIC JURISTS TO BOYCOTT DIVORCE

CASES, JUSTICE MINISTER ROBERTO CASTELLI HIGHLIGHTED THAT

JUDGES CANNOT EXERCISE "CONSCIENTIOUS OBJECTION" IN

DISCHARGING THEIR DUTIES. IN JUNE 2002, PARLIAMENT PASSED A

VATICAN-INSPIRED, RESTRICTIVE ASSISTED FERTILITY BILL THAT

DREW SUPPORT FROM CATHOLIC LEGISLATORS ACROSS THE POLITICAL

SPECTRUM (AND WHICH SECULAR CONSERVATIVES AND COMMUNISTS

JOINED TO OPPOSE). THE CONTINUING PRESENCE OF CATHOLIC

SYMBOLS, SUCH AS CRUCIFIXES, WHICH MAY BE FOUND HANGING ON

COURTROOM OR GOVERNMENT OFFICE WALLS, HAS DRAWN CRITICISM AND

HAS BEEN THE OBJECT OF LAWSUITS. IN APRIL 2000, THE COURT OF

CASSATION RULED IN FAVOR OF A SCHOOLTEACHER WHO ASSERTED THAT

CRUCIFIXES SHOULD NOT BE PRESENT AT VOTING SITES MAINTAINED

BY A SECULAR STATE. ATTEMPTS BY INDIVIDUAL TEACHERS TO

REMOVE CRUCIFIXES FROM THE CLASSROOM, IN DEFERENCE TO MUSLIM

STUDENTS, NONETHELESS HAVE DRAWN NEWSPAPER EDITORIAL

CRITICISM FOR "EXCESSIVE ZEAL."
