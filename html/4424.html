SUBJECT: SOUTHEAST TURKEY PRESS SUMMARY

NOVEMBER 11-12, 2002

the reports summarized here.

POLITICS, SECURITY, HUMAN RIGHTS

--------------------------------

2. PRO-OCALAN PROTESTS

(Evrensel) A group of 50 youths gathered and lit

fires in Adana\'s Karasu quarter to protest a decision

barring PKK leader Abdullah Ocalan from meeting with

his attorneys. The group shouted pro-Ocalan slogans

and dispersed when police arrived. Denial of

permission for Ocalan to meet with his attorneys was

also protested in Mersin, Batman and Van. Protestors

claimed Ocalan was being isolated and demanded the

isolation be lifted.

ECONOMIC AND AGRICULTURAL DEVELOPMENTS

--------------------------------------

3. NEW FOUR-STAR HOTEL IN GAZIANTEP

(Dunya) Gaziantep\'s second four-star hotel, Ravanda,

has gone into service following an inauguration

ceremony attended by Tourism Minister Mustafa Tasar

and Gaziantep Mayor Celal Dogan. Ravanda has a total

of 86 rooms, including five suites, as well as a

sauna, swimming pool, Turkish bath, and a game center

for children and conference halls.
