SUBJECT: REINFORCED COCKPIT DOORS: JORDANIAN VIEWS

REF: STATE 135711

1. (U) Jordanian Civil Aviation Authority (CAA) Director

General Hanna Najjar informed us August 22 that he fully

expects state-owned carrier Royal Jordanian Airlines (RJ)

will be able to comply with the FAA regulation on reinforced

cockpit doors by April 9, 2003. RJ is currently in the

process of replacing its fleet of older Airbus aircraft which

do not have reinforced cockpit doors. Najjar said he had

informally discussed with TSA the possibility of a temporary

waiver or extension if one or two of the older planes have

not been replaced by the deadline. Najjar said he had

discussed with TSA the possibility of taking into

consideration for a temporary waiver the deployment of armed

sky marshals outside the cockpit on all RJ flights.
