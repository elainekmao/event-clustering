Baghdad, Basra and Points In Between

------------------------------------

1. (C) On October 2, a source in the Japanese Embassy\'s Iraq

Division provided A/Polcouns a readout of his recently

completed three week visit to Iraq. The source had been in

Iraq from September 7 to 27 and had traveled from Baghdad to

Basra and back by car September 14-16. Overall, he said that

the atmosphere throughout the country was "remarkably

normal." He didn\'t perceive any noticeable difference in

Baghdad from his last visit in June. The security presence

in the city was unchanged from earlier in the summer

(offering a different impression from that of USINT Chief

Bernacki (ref)) and Baghdad\'s hotels were full of foreign

visitors (including a Japanese musical troupe -- a first, he

noted), there for the Babylon festival.

2. (C) On his trip to the south, he said he was surprised by

the relatively good condition and bustle of Basra. He had

expected to see a "devastated city." Instead, he found a

city "full of street traffic and souks quite full of goods."

He saw no obvious military presence in the city during the

day he was there. These impressions were very different, he

noted, from those he had of a trip to Mosul earlier in the

year where he had found streetlife comparatively quiet and

the military/security presence much stronger.

3. (C) The 9/14-9/16 trip south took him through Karbala and

Najaf enroute to Basra and via Misan and Kut on the way back.

Karbala and Najaf, like Basra, were full of people -- both

Iraqis and Iranian pilgrims. All the shops were open even

though the day they passed through both cities was a Friday.

The one area where he said he encountered an overt military

presence was "in the middle of Misan province" where he saw

quite a number of soldiers.

--------------------------------------------- -------------

Upcoming Regional Visit of Japanese MFA Humanitarian Chief

--------------------------------------------- -------------

4. (C) On the issue of preparations for any possible U.S.

military action against Iraq, he told A/Polcouns that Tokyo

is dispatching a Mr. Kawakami, Director of the International

Humanitarian Support Division of the Japanese MFA, to the

region to assess potential needs and how Japan could help

address them. Kawakami will arrive in Jordan from Syria on

October 12 and then depart to Israel on October 14. While in

Amman he intends to meet with officials from the MFA, the

Ministry of Interior, and possibly the GID. Kawakami will be

in Geneva October 7-8 to discuss Tokyo\'s humanitarian

strategy in Africa, the Middle East and South Asia with

Japanese diplomats from posts in those regions, and with

Geneva-based humanitarian agencies.
