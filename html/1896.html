-------------------------

FRANCE: IT COULD BE WORSE

-------------------------

2. (C) COUNSELOR OF THE FRENCH EMBASSY JEROME SAUTIER

WAS BY FAR THE MOST RELAXED ABOUT THE STATE OF PLAY IN

ZIMBABWE. HE NOTED THAT THE POLITICAL SITUATION HAD

GONE DOWNHILL SINCE HIS ARRIVAL TWO YEARS EARLIER BUT

SPECULATED THAT ZIMBABWE COULD STILL MAKE IT THROUGH THE

2002 ELECTIONS WITHOUT MAJOR DAMAGE. HE OBSERVED THAT

ON THE AFRICAN CONTINUUM, ZIMBABWE RANKS SOMEWHERE IN

THE MIDDLE IN TERMS OF POLITICAL VIOLENCE. HE EXPRESSED

GREATER CONCERN FOR THE RAPID ECONOMIC DETERIORATION,

WHICH HE FEARED COULD BE IRREVERSIBLE.

--------------------------------

BELGIUM: ESCHEWING THE HARD LINE

--------------------------------

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 02499 01 OF 02 240558Z

3. (C) BELGIAN COUNSELOR ALEXIS DE CROMBRUGGHE SAID

THAT HE (AND HIS ABSENT AMBASSADOR) HAD TRIED TO TAKE A

PRAGMATIC VIEW IN DEALING WITH THE GOVERNMENT OF THIS

"VERY TROUBLED COUNTRY." HE SAID THAT HE WAS CONCERNED

THAT THE EU "ULTIMATUM" ON ARTICLE 8 CONSULTATIONS WITH

ZIMBABWE COULD BACKFIRE, WITH THE ZIMBABWEANS HARDENING

THEIR POSITION, AGGRAVATING AN ALREADY COMPLICATED

DYNAMIC. HE CITED SEVERAL TECHNICAL MISSTEPS IN

PREPARATION OF THE "ULTIMATUM" AND NOTED THAT THERE WERE

CLEAR DIFFERENCES AMONG THE EU DIPLOMATIC PRESENCE IN

HARARE ON HOW BEST TO PROCEED. DE CROMBRUGGHE

IDENTIFIED GOZ FLEXIBILITY ON EU ELECTION MONITORING AS

A KEY INDICATOR TO PROGRESS IN EU/ZIMBABWE

CONSULTATIONS.

4. (C) DE CROMBRUGGHE SAID THAT HE DOES NOT FULLY SHARE

THE VIEWS OF SEVERAL EU CHIEFS OF MISSION THAT THE MDC

WILL SWEEP TO VICTORY IN FREE AND FAIR ELECTIONS. THE

OUTCOME OF THE PARLIAMENTARY ELECTIONS, AND THE RECENT

ZANU-PF WINS IN BY-ELECTIONS, INDICATE THAT ZANU-PF

STILL ENJOYS SIGNIFICANT SUPPORT, ESPECIALLY IN ITS

TRADITIONAL STRONGHOLD OF MASHONALAND. HE PREDICTED

THAT THE PRESIDENTIAL VOTE COULD BE A CLOSE ONE, PERHAPS

THE LEAST IDEAL OUTCOME UNDER CURRENT CIRCUMSTANCES.

DCM NOTED THAT THE DECLINING ECONOMY WILL CERTAINLY

ERODE ZANU SUPPORT AND POINTED OUT "FREE AND FAIR" IS

THE OPERATIVE PHRASE. CONTINUED ZANU-PF INTIMIDATION

COUPLED WITH CONCERTED ATTACKS ON JUDICIAL AND ELECTORAL

INSTITUTIONS RISKS MUDDYING THE WATER TO THE DEGREE THAT

EVEN ELECTORAL VICTORIES IN LIKELY ZANU-PF STRONGHOLDS

COULD BE CALLED INTO QUESTION. DE CROMBRUGGHE FULLY

CONFIDENTIAL

AGREED THAT THE TIDE OF THE ECONOMY IS INCREASINGLY

RUNNING AGAINST THE MUGABE GOVERNMENT.

5. (C) IN A RELATED DEVELOPMENT, ON AUGUST 22 EU

DIPLOMAT ALEX KREMER TOLD POLITICAL SECTION CHIEF THAT

THERE HAS BEEN NO PROGRESS ON THE ARTICLE 8 DIALOGUE

WITH ZIMBABWE SINCE THE GENERAL AFFAIRS COUNCIL ISSUED

ITS ULTIMATUM IN LATE JUNE. ACCORDING TO KREMER, THE

GAC WOULD NOT DECIDE ON NEXT STEPS -- INCLUDING A

POSSIBLE MOVE TO ARTICLE 96 DISCUSSIONS -- UNTIL ITS

NEXT GATHERING IN EARLY OCTOBER. THAT SAID, THE EU

WORKING GROUP ON AFRICA PLANNED TO MEET IN EARLY

SEPTEMBER AND WOULD MAKE RECOMMENDATIONS THAT THE GAC

WOULD LIKELY ABIDE BY.

SEPARATE MEETING WITH POLCHIEF, DEE AND BRITISH

COMMENTED THE AFRICAN PARTICIPANTS IN ABUJA WOULD PLAY A

CRITICAL ROLE BUT EXPRESSED CONCERN THAT FM LAMIDO, WHO

HAD TAKEN A SOFTER VIEW OF MUGABE THAN HAS OBASANJO,

WOULD BE CHAIRING THE MEETING.) DEE SAID THAT

PROJECTING WHERE THE POLITICAL SITUATION WILL LEAD (A

STAPLE OF DIPLOMATIC EXCHANGES HERE) IS CRYSTAL BALL

GAZING, GIVEN THE VARIABLES. HOWEVER, SHE AND FOLEY

EXPRESSED DISMAY AT THE EFFECT OF THE ECONOMIC DOWNTURN

ON THE AVERAGE ZIMBABWEAN.
