SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE: REPRESSIVE MEDIA LEGISLATION GOES

INTO EFFECT

REF: HARARE 345

CLASSIFIED BY POLITICAL OFFICER TODD FAULK FOR REASONS:

1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (U) ON MARCH 15, PRESIDENT MUGABE SIGNED AND

GAZETTED INTO LAW THE ACCESS TO INFORMATION AND

PROTECTION OF PRIVACY ACT, WHICH THE PARLIAMENT OF

ZIMBABWE PASSED ON JANUARY 31 (REFTEL), AND WHICH PLACES

SEVERE RESTRICTIONS ON THE INDEPENDENT MEDIA. THE

GAZETTING OF THE ACT BRINGS IT INTO IMMEDIATE EFFECT.

2. (C) COMMENT: PRESIDENT MUGABE PROBABLY WAITED UNTIL

AFTER THE ELECTION TO SIGN THE ACT BECAUSE HE DID NOT

WANT TO GIVE HIS INTERNATIONAL DETRACTORS MORE

AMMUNITION FOR TOUGH ACTION DURING HIS CAMPAIGN. HE

ALSO KNEW THAT KEY PROVISIONS OF THE ACT WOULD NOT GO

INTO EFFECT UNTIL AFTER THE ACT WAS GAZETTED. ONE OF

THEM IS THE REQUIREMENT THAT MASS MEDIA OUTLETS REGISTER

WITH THE YET-TO-BE-ESTABLISHED MEDIA AND INFORMATION

COMMISSION WITHIN THREE MONTHS OF THE GAZETTING OF THE

ACT (WHICH NOW MAKES THE DEADLINE JUNE 15). MUGABE\'S

DECISION TO BRING THE ACT INTO EFFECT ONLY TWO DAYS

AFTER HE WAS DECLARED THE WINNER OF THE ELECTION

DEMONSTRATES HIS DETERMINATION TO CONTINUE SUPPRESSING

HIS CRITICS IN ZIMBABWE. END COMMENT.

SULLIVAN
