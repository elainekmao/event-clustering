1. (U) SUMMARY: IN HIS AUGUST 1 ANGELUS MESSAGE, POPE JOHN

PAUL II MADE A STRONG AND EMOTIONAL APPEAL FOR PEACE IN THE

MIDDLE EAST, CALLING ON ISRAELITES AND PALESTINIANS TO RETURN

TO THE PATH OF NEGOTIATIONS, AND URGING THE INTERNATIONAL

COMMUNITY TO COMMIT ITSELF TO A PRESENCE IN THE REGION "TO

CREATE THE CONDITIONS FOR A PRODUCTIVE DIALOGUE BETWEEN THE

PARTIES. THE POPE FORCEFULLY STATED THAT "THERE IS NO

JUSTIFICATION FOR THOSE WHO INDISCRIMINATELY KILL INNOCENT

DEFENSELESS CIVILIANS." HE EXPRESSED HIS CONCERN FOR THE

PLIGHT OF BOTH THE PALESTINIANS AND ISRAELIS, AND EMPHASIZED

THAT NEITHER ATTACKS, ARMS, NOR WALLS OF SEPARATION WOULD

LEAD TO A JUST SOLUTION. FINALLY, CALLING ATTENTION TO THE

INABILITY OF WORSHIPERS TO ATTEND SERVICES BECAUSE OF THE

CURFEWS, HE CONDEMNED WHAT HE TERMED A RESTRICTION ON FREEDOM

OF RELIGION AND APPEALED TO CHRISTIANS LIVING IN THE HOLY

LANDS NOT TO ABANDON THE REGION DESPITE THE CURRENT TROUBLES.

END SUMMARY.

-------------------------------------

POPE ANGUISHED BY CONTINUING VIOLENCE

-------------------------------------

2. (U) IN HIS FIRST SUNDAY ANGELUS MESSAGE SINCE HIS RETURN

FROM CANADA, GUATEMALA AND MEXICO, POPE JOHN PAUL II CHOSE TO

MAKE A POINTED APPEAL FOR AN END TO VIOLENCE IN THE MIDDLE

EAST. OBSERVING THAT HE "CAN\'T STOP THINKING" ABOUT THE HOLY

LANDS WHOSE PEOPLE HAVE NO RESPITE FROM THE "ALMOST DAILY

EPISODES OF ABOMINABLE VIOLENCE," THE POPE ASKED RHETORICALLY

WHEN THE ISRAELI AND PALESTINIAN PEOPLE WOULD COME TO

UNDERSTAND THAT THE ABILITY TO LIVE TOGETHER "DOES NOT SPRING

FROM ARMS." "NEITHER ATTACKS, NOR WALLS OF SEPARATION, NOR

RETALIATION WILL EVER LEAD TO AN EQUITABLE SOLUTION OF THE

CURRENT CONFLICT." HE SAID HE SUFFERS WITH THOSE WHO MOURN

THE DESTRUCTION, PARTICULARLY THE INNOCENTS WHO ARE PAYING

THE PRICE OF THE CURRENT VIOLENCE.

---------------------------

NO JUSTIFICATION FOR TERROR

---------------------------

3. (U) REINFORCING HIS REJECTION OF TERROR IN ALL FORMS, THE

POPE SAID HE WANTED TO REPEAT TO ALL FROM WHATEVER ETHNIC

GROUP THEY BELONG TO, THAT "THERE IS NO JUSTIFICATION FOR

THOSE WHO INDISCRIMINATELY KILL INNOCENT DEFENSELESS

CIVILIANS." CONVEYING EQUAL CONCERN FOR THE SUFFERING OF

BOTH SIDES, THE POPE OBSERVED THAT FROM 1967 TO THE PRESENT

THERE HAS BEEN "A TERRIBLE SUCCESSION OF UNSPEAKABLE

SUFFERING: SUFFERING OF THE PALESTINIANS, HUNTED FROM THEIR

OWN LANDS OR CONSTRAINED, IN RECENT TIMES, TO A STATE OF

PERMANENT SIEGE, OBJECTS OF A SORT OF COLLECTIVE PUNISHMENT;

SUFFERING OF THE ISRAELI POPULATION WHICH LIVES IN DAILY

TERROR OF BEING BOMBED BY ANONYMOUS ATTACKERS."

---------------------------------

APPEAL FOR INTERNATIONAL PRESENCE

---------------------------------

4. (C) IN RECENT DISCUSSIONS, THE HOLY SEE HAS INTENSIFIED

ITS PUSH FOR SOME FORM OF INTERNATIONAL INTERPOSITION FORCE

ON THE GROUND IN THE REGION (REFTELS). REINFORCING THIS

MESSAGE, THE POPE CALLED ON THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY "TO

COMMIT ITSELF WITH GREAT DETERMINATION TO BE PRESENT ON THE

GROUND, OFFERING THE NEEDED MEDIATION TO CREATE THE

AT THE SAME TIME, HE APPEALED TO THE ISRAELIS AND

PALESTINIANS TO RETURN TO HONEST NEGOTIATIONS."

POPE SEES FREEDOM OF RELIGION RESTRICTED IN HOLY LANDS

--------------------------------------------- ---------

5. (U) ADDING URGENCY TO HIS APPEAL TO PEACE, THE POPE

REITERATED HIS CONCERN FOR CHRISTIANS IN THE HOLY LANDS --

MANY OF WHOM HAVE BEEN EMIGRATING TO ESCAPE THE VIOLENCE AND

LACK OF OPPORTUNITY IN THE STRIFE-TORN REGION. IN THIS

CONNECTION, THE POPE ASSERTED THAT THE CURRENT RIGID CURFEW

IMPOSED BY THE ISRAELIS HAD RESULTED IN A FUNDAMENTAL

VIOLATION OF THE FREEDOM OF RELIGION, AS BELIEVERS OF ALL

FAITHS NO LONGER HAD ACCESS TO PLACES OF WORSHIP.

6. (C) RETURNING TO ROME AFTER HIS EXTENDED VISIT TO THE

AMERICAS, THE POPE WAS CLEARLY SEEKING TO USE HIS SUNDAY

ANGELUS TO REGISTER HIS DEEP PERSONAL CONCERN AT THE

CONTINUING SPIRAL OF VIOLENCE AND TO REINFORCE THE VATICAN\'S

CURRENT VIEW THAT SOME FORM OF AN INTERNATIONAL FORCE IS

NEEDED TO GIVE THE PARTIES A CHANCE TO COOL DOWN FROM CURRENT

OVERHEATED EMOTIONS ON BOTH SIDES. ITALIAN NEWSPAPER

ACCOUNTS OF THE POPE\'S STATEMENT SUGGESTED THAT PRESIDENT

BUSH HAD BEEN RECEPTIVE TO THE POPE\'S PROPOSAL FOR SUCH A

FORCE IN THEIR FIRST MEETING, BUT THAT THE U.S. HAD CHANGED

ITS MIND UNDER PRESSURE FROM ISRAEL. UNTIL GREATER PROGRESS

IS REGISTERED IN ENDING THE VIOLENCE AND REESTABLISHING

CONFIDENCE, THE VATICAN IS LIKELY TO CONTINUE TO INTENSIFY

ITS APPEAL FOR AN INTERNATIONAL PRESENCE IN THE MONTHS AHEAD.
