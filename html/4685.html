2. (C) AS FAR AS EMBASSY CAN ASCERTAIN FROM THIS

DISTANCE, VIETNAM IS AT BEST A MARGINAL PLAYER ON THE

UN HUMAN RIGHTS COMMISSION, AND AT TIMES IS ACTIVELY

OPPOSED TO US VALUES AND VIEWS. VIETNAM LIKELY SOUGHT

A SEAT ON THE COMMISSION PRIMARILY AS A MEANS OF

ENHANCING ITS INTERNATIONAL STATURE, A PROCESS TOWARD

WHICH IT HAS WORKED ASSIDUOUSLY AND SUCCESSFULLY OVER

THE PAST DECADE. THE OTHER PART OF THE GVN\'S CALCULUS

WAS PROBABLY TO BE WELL-PLACED TO PROTECT ITS OWN

INTERESTS -- AND THOSE OF "TRADITIONAL FRIENDS" LIKE

LIBYA, IRAN, AND IRAQ -- AGAINST WHAT IT CLAIMS IS THE

EROSION OF INTERNATIONAL PRINCIPLES RESPECTING NATIONAL

SOVEREIGNTY AND NON-INTERFERENCE IN INTERNAL AFFAIRS.

EMBASSY IS NOT OPTIMISTIC THAT THE USG CAN CHANGE

VIETNAM\'S VIEWS OR PARTICIPATION IN ANY MEANINGFUL

MANNER. (SEE REF B FOR A DISCUSSION ON VIETNAM\'S TAKE

ON HUMAN RIGHTS, AND REF C FOR ITS OVERALL WORLDVIEW.)

3. (C) COUNTRY PROFILE:

A. THE MFA DETERMINES VIETNAM\'S VOTES IN INTERNATIONAL

FORA, FOLLOWING OVERALL POLICY GUIDELINES SET DOWN BY

THE COMMUNIST PARTY OF VIETNAM (CPV). A CPV "CELL"

OVERSEEING BOTH THE MFA AND THE CPV\'S COMMISSION ON

EXTERNAL RELATIONS PROBABLY MAKES KEY DECISIONS, APART

FROM EXTREMELY SENSITIVE ONES THAT NEED TO BE PRESENTED

TO THE CPV POLITBURO. FEW, IF ANY, DECISIONS REGARDING

THE HUMAN RIGHTS COMMISSION ARE LIKELY VETTED AT THE

POLITBURO LEVEL. THE GVN REPRESENTATIVE IN GENEVA IS

PROBABLY ON A FAIRLY SHORT LEASE, WHILE THE AMBASSADOR

IN WASHINGTON ALMOST CERTAINLY PLAYS NO ROLE

WHATSOEVER. VIETNAM WILL NONETHELESS LISTEN

ATTENTIVELY TO THE VIEWS OF THE G-77 AND ASEAN ON MAJOR

ISSUES, AND LIKES WHENEVER POSSIBLE TO VOTE WITH THE

MAJORITY.

B. THE TOP MFA CONTACT IS FOREIGN MINISTER NGUYEN DY

NIEN, WHO SPEAKS GOOD ENGLISH (ALTHOUGH HE OFTEN

PREFERS TO USE AN INTERPRETER). HIS OFFICE TELEPHONE

IS 84-4-199-2218 OR 84-9-13238218 DURING WORKING HOURS

(12 HOURS DIFFERENCE FROM WASHINGTON), OR 84-4-845-2980

(NON-WORKING HOURS). THERE IS NO POINT IN TRYING TO

CONTACT CPV OFFICIALS DIRECTLY; THEY CLAIM THEIR ROLE

IS PARTY-TO-PARTY RELATIONS.

C. THE GVN IN GENERAL DOES NOT BELIEVE THAT

INTERNATIONAL INSTITUTIONS SHOULD INVOKE SANCTIONS OR

SINGLE OUT PARTICULAR COUNTRIES (APART FROM THE U.S. OR

ISRAEL) FOR CRITICISM. SENSITIVE TO INTERNATIONAL

CRITICISM ON ITS OWN HUMAN RIGHTS RECORD, VIETNAM WILL

SEEK TO ENSURE THAT THE COMMISSION SPENDS AS LITTLE

TIME AS POSSIBLE EXAMINING INDIVIDUAL COUNTRIES. ITS

REPRESENTATIVE IN GENEVA WILL GENERALLY DISCOURAGE

FORMAL CONDEMNATIONS OF SPECIFIC COUNTRIES -- UNLESS

THERE IS A CLEAR CONSENSUS AMONG THE G-77 OR OTHER

MAJOR GROUPING OF DEVELOPING COUNTRIES. EMBASSY DOES

NOT BELIEVE THAT THE GVN HAS A SPECIFIC AGENDA OF ITS

OWN TO ADVANCE WITHIN THE COMMISSION, AND EXPECTS IT

WILL BE A FOLLOWER, NOT A LEADER, IN ITS DELIBERATIONS.

D. A TOP GVN PRIORITY OVER THE COMING DECADE IN THE

INTERNATIONAL ARENA IS TO WIN A UN SECURITY COUNCIL
