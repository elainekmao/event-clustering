SUBJECT: NEPAL POLICE CLAIM VICTORY AGAINST MAOIST VALLEY

LEADERSHIP

1. (SBU) Speaking before a group of Nepal Police officers at

an Embassy-sponsored presentation, police chief Rana claimed

a victory over Maoist leadership in the Kathmandu Valley,

stating police efforts had netted the valley bureau leaders.

Afterwards, RSO queried Rana over the veracity of local press

reports that Krishna Sen, acting Maoist valley bureau chief,

had been arrested. (Note: his predecessor was arrested

approximately one month ago in a joint army and police

action). Rana confirmed the reports and added with pride

that it was his officers who had captured most of the valley

leadership over the last two months.

2. (SBU) COMMENT: If Rana\'s claim regarding the insurgent\'s

valley leadership is true, it is likely that lower level

cadre in Kathmandu will be ineffective in mounting any

coordinated campaign for the next several weeks (a period

that coincides with the anniversary of the palace killings

last year). While this does not rule out isolated incidents

in the city, it is very welcome news.
