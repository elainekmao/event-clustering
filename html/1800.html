G8: GENOA\'S CARDINAL ARCHBISHOP WANTS NON-VIOLENT PROTESTS

--------------------------------------------- -------------

2. (U) GENOA\'S ARCHBISHOP, CARDINAL DIONIGI TETTAMANZI,

HAS REJECTED VIOLENT PROTESTS AGAINST THE G8 SUMMIT LATER

THIS MONTH (REFTELS A AND B). IN AN INTERVIEW WITH CENTER-

LEFT ITALIAN DAILY "LA REPUBBLICA," THE CARDINAL DESCRIBED

THE "SEATTLE PEOPLE" AS A NEW CULTURAL PHENOMENON. INSISTING

THAT ORDINARY PEOPLE MUST HAVE A MEANS OF VOICING THEIR

OPINIONS AND INFLUENCING THE ECONOMIC PROCESS, HE ALSO SAID

THAT VIOLENCE IS NOT THE APPROPRIATE WAY TO ACHIEVE THAT

END. MEANWHILE, THOUSANDS OF YOUNG CATHOLICS ARE EXPECTED TO

DESCEND ON GENOA JULY 7-8 FOR PRE-G8 PROTESTS. ORGANIZERS

SAY THE YOUNG CATHOLICS ARE PROTESTING EARLY TO AVOID THE

EXPECTED "PANDEMONIUM" DURING THE SUMMIT. A SPOKESMAN FOR

THE CATHOLIC GROUPS SAID THEY HAD NOTHING AGAINST THE

"SEATTLE PEOPLE" BUT THAT THEY DID NOT WANT TO RUN THE RISK

OF BEING DRAGGED INTO "A DEGENERATION INTO VIOLENCE". THE

RELIGIOUS FREEDOM LACKING FOR MILLIONS OF CHRISTIANS

--------------------------------------------- -------

3. (U) THE VATICAN-SANCTIONED RELIGIOUS FREEDOM WATCHDOG

GROUP "AID TO THE CHURCH IN NEED" ALLEGES SOME 200 MILLION

CHRISTIANS EXPERIENCE RELIGIOUS PERSECUTION. A 400-PAGE

REPORT, THE THIRD OF ITS KIND, PROVIDED INFORMATION OF THE

STATUS OF RELIGIOUS FREEDOM IN EACH OF THE WORLD\'S NATIONS.

IT ALSO PROVIDED STATISTICAL INFORMATION ON NUMBERS OF

RELIGIOUS BELIEVERS AND REFUGEES. AID TO THE CHURCH IN NEED

ESTIMATES THAT 165,000 CHRISTIANS WERE KILLED LARGELY ON

ACCOUNT OF THEIR RELIGIOUS BELIEFS DURING 2000. THE VIOLENCE

WAS MOST PRONOUNCED IN INDONESIA, SUDAN, EAST TIMOR, INDIA

AND EGYPT. ACCORDING TO THE REPORT, DISCRIMINATION AND LEGAL

SANCTIONS AGAINST CHRISTIANS WERE MOST OBVIOUS IN CHINA,

SUDAN, INDONESIA, NIGERIA AND TURKMENISTAN. AID TO THE

CHURCH IN NEED ALSO POINTED OUT MORE SUBTLE FORMS OF

DISCRIMINATION IN EUROPEAN COUNTRIES. THE REPORT SAID A

RECENT FRENCH BILL THAT CRIMINALIZES "MENTAL MANIPULATION"

POTENTIALLY COULD BE TURNED AGAINST ANY PARENTS WHO RAISE

THEIR CHILDREN IN A FAITH OR MONKS AND NUNS WHO LIVE IN

COMMUNITIES THAT COULD BE VIEWED AS SECT-LIKE.

4. (U) POPE JOHN PAUL II HAS ENCOURAGED SCIENTISTS AND

ETHICISTS TO WORK TOGETHER TO RESOLVE THE MORAL ISSUES

SURROUNDING THE TRANSPLANT OF ANIMAL ORGANS INTO HUMANS. IN

A JULY 3 MESSAGE, THE PONTIFF SAID ANY MORAL RESPONSE MUST

TAKE INTO ACCOUNT RESPECT FOR HUMAN DIGNITY, POSSIBLE HEALTH

RISKS ASSOCIATED WITH TRANSPLANTING ORGANS FROM OTHER

SPECIES AND ADEQUATE RESPECT FOR THE DONOR ANIMALS. THE POPE

SAID XENOTRANSPLANT RESEARCH WAS IN THE INTEREST OF HUMANITY

BECAUSE OF THE SERIOUS LACK OF AVAILABLE HUMAN ORGANS, WHICH

HAS LED TO MANY TRANSPLANT PATIENTS DYING WHILE STILL ON

WAITING LISTS. THE PAPAL MESSAGE COINCIDED WITH THE THIRD

AND FINAL MEETING OF A WORKING GROUP OF THE PONTIFICAL

ACADEMY FOR LIFE DEVOTED TO ANIMAL ORGAN TRANSPLANTS.

JULY 4 EVENT: TRADE, CULTURE AND THE BOOGYING CARDINAL

--------------------------------------------- ---------

6. (U) POST\'S JULY 4 EVENT RECEIVED WIDESPREAD COVERAGE

FROM ROME-BASED MEDIA. WHILE SOME REPORTS DESCRIBED THE

EVENT AS "FLASHY," THE BUFFET DINNER AND CONCERT WAS JUDGED

BY MOST OF THE 500 GUESTS AS "THE BEST EVER." AN INCREASED

NUMBER OF GUESTS FROM BUSINESS AND INDUSTRY MEANT THE

EVENT\'S US CORPORATE SPONSORS WERE ABLE TO PROMOTE TRADE

OPPORTUNITIES. US CULTURE WAS HIGHLIGHTED BY THE PRESENCE OF

THREE MASTER CHEFS, FLOWN IN FROM CALIFORNIA AND WASHINGTON

DC, WHO PROVIDED GUESTS WITH A TASTE OF FINE AMERICAN

CUISINE. FORMER SUPREME MARY WILSON AND HER BAND GAVE A

ROUSING PERFORMANCE OF SOUL AND ROCK AND ROLL MUSIC. WHILE

THE MOST OF THE NORMALLY SOLEMN VATICAN GOVERNMENT GUESTS

LIMITED THEMSELVES TO TAPPING THEIR FEET, THE 74-YEAR OLD

GOVERNOR OF VATICAN CITY, US CARDINAL EDMUND SZOKA, BOOGIED
