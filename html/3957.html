1. (C) DRAWING ON POINTS REFTEL, POL MC ON OCTOBER 4

UNDERLINED TO DIRECTOR OF MFA BALKANS OFFICE MAURIZIO MASSARI

USG\'S VIEW THAT THE GOVERNMENT OF CROATIA SHOULD COMPLY

IMMEDIATELY AND IN FULL WITH THE INDICTMENT BY THE ICTY OF

GENERAL BOBETKO. HE UNDERSCORED THE SERIOUS RAMIFICATIONS OF

NON-COMPLIANCE, INCLUDING THE PRECEDENT THIS ACTION COULD SET

FOR RESPONSES TO FUTURE INDICTMENTS AND IC SUPPORT FOR

CROATIA\'S EU AND NATO ASPIRATIONS.

2. (C) MASSARI SAID THE GOI HAD THE SAME VIEW AS THE USG ON

THE IMPORTANCE OF THE GOC\'S COMPLETE COOPERATION WITH THE

ICTY ON THIS AND ALL INDICTMENTS. HE HAD INSTRUCTED THE

ITALIAN EMBASSY IN ZAGREB ON OCTOBER 3 TO DELIVER A STRONG

MESSAGE TO THE GOC, URGING THAT IT SURRENDER BOBETKO

IMMEDIATELY. ON SEPTEMBER 30, CROATIAN PARLIAMENT SPEAKER

ZLATKO TOMCIC HAD MET IN ROME WITH PRESIDENT OF THE CHAMBER

OF DEPUTIES PIER FERDINANDO CASINI. MASSARI SAID THAT WHEN

CASINI MADE SIMILAR TOUGH POINTS, TOMCIC RESPONDED THAT THE

GOC WOULD NEED "SEVERAL WEEKS" JUST TO EXAMINE THE ICTY

INDICTMENT, FOLLOWED BY AN INDETERMINATE PERIOD TO RESPOND.

3. (C) MASSARI SAID THAT WHILE THE GOI FAVORS A TOUGH LINE

WITH THE GOI, IT THOUGHT THAT A MORE "NUANCED" APPROACH WAS

NEEDED FOR PUBLIC DISCOURSE ON THE CASE. USE OF PHRASES SUCH

AS "UNCONDITIONAL SURRENDER" WERE COUNTERPRODUCTIVE, AND

COULD CAUSE A BACKLASH WITH THE GENERAL PUBLIC, POSSIBLY

RALLYING SUPPORT FOR HARDLINE HDZ VIEWS. THE PUBLIC LINE

SHOULD FOCUS ON THE POSITIVE ASPECTS OF COOPERATION, SUCH AS

THE BENEFITS TO THE GENERAL POPULATION OF CLOSER TIES TO THE
