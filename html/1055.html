1. (C) SUMMARY. PRESIDENT MUGABE\'S ADDRESS TO

THE NATION ON INDEPENDENCE DAY WAS

DISAPPOINTINGLY LACKING IN INDICATING ANY WAY OUT

OF THE CURRENT CYCLE OF FARM OCCUPATIONS AND

VIOLENCE. HE ALSO WAS SILENT ON THE TIMING OF

THE PARLIAMENTARY ELECTION. SUBSTITUTING BANAL

WORDS ABOUT PROGRESS SINCE INDEPENDENCE FOR THE

USUAL PUBLIC CELEBRATIONS, MUGABE MISSED AN

OPPORTUNITY TO PROVIDE LEADERSHIP TO A NATION

THAT NEEDS IT NOW MORE THAN EVER. HOWEVER, IN

UNSCRIPTED COMMENTS MOMENTS AFTER HIS PREPARED

SPEECH, MUGABE SAID HE IS FULL OF ANGER TOWARD

THE COUNTRY\'S FARMERS THAT ARE SEEING THEIR FARMS

OCCUPIED, AND BELIEVES THEY ARE ENEMIES OF

ZIMBABWE. THE EU DIPLOMATIC GROUP IN HARARE WILL

NOT UNDERTAKE A JOINT DEMARCHE WITH US TO PROTEST

THE CURRENT STATE OF AFFAIRS. THEY LOOK TO THEIR

MINISTERS IN EUROPE TO CARRY THE WATER.

AMBASSADOR MCDONALD BELIEVES IT IS TIME FOR THE

HIGHEST LEVELS OF THE USG TO ENGAGE MUGABE. END

SUMMARY.

2. (C) READING HIS PREPARED SPEECH TO THE

NATION ON APRIL 18, ZIMBABWE\'S INDEPENDENCE DAY,

PRESIDENT MUGABE MADE DISAPPOINTINGLY FEW

COMMENTS ON THE LAND OCCUPATION CRISIS OR THE

POLITICAL TURMOIL GRIPPING HIS COUNTRY. THE

OBSERVATIONS HE DID MAKE COULD MOST CHARITABLY BE

CALLED A MISSED OPPORTUNITY, SINCE THEY TENDED TO

LEGITIMIZE THE ACTIONS OF THE SO-CALLED "WAR

VETERANS" WHO HAVE OCCUPIED, AT VARIOUS TIMES,

OVER 1000 COMMERCIAL FARMS AND MURDERED TWO

FARMERS. MUGABE ALSO DISAPPOINTED THOSE WHO

THOUGHT HE WOULD USE THE OPPORTUNITY TO ANNOUNCE

AN ELECTION DATE.

3. (C) MUGABE OPENED HIS SPEECH WITH A

DISCUSSION OF THE EFFECTS ON ZIMBABWE OF THE

RECENT CYCLONE. HE REPEATED THE GOVERNMENT\'S

LINE THAT THE REASON THERE WERE NO PUBLIC

CEREMONIES TO MARK INDEPENDENCE DAY THIS YEAR IS

SO THE MONEY SAVED COULD INSTEAD GO TO THE

VICTIMS OF THE CYCLONE. IT IS SPECULATED THAT

PUBLIC EVENTS WERE NOT SCHEDULED BECAUSE THE

PRESIDENT FEARED NO ONE WOULD ATTEND OR HE WOULD

BE MET BY PROTESTERS. MUGABE DID NOT COME TO THE

LAND OCCUPATION ISSUE UNTIL ABOUT 20 MINUTES INTO

HIS SPEECH. MUCH OF HIS INITIAL REMARKS COVERED,

IN EXCRUCIATING DETAIL, STATISTICS SUCH AS THE

NUMBER OF BOREHOLES IN THE COUNTRYSIDE, INTENDED

TO PROVE THAT ZIMBABWE HAS IMPROVED SINCE

INDEPENDENCE.

6. (C) WE BELIEVE MUGABE\'S UNSCRIPTED REMARKS

ABOUT SEEING THE FARMERS AS ENEMIES OF ZIMBABWE

REVEAL HIS PRESENT STATE OF MIND. AFTER 20 YEARS

IN POWER HE APPEARS INCAPABLE OF DISTINGUISHING

HIS PERSONAL FEELINGS AND INTERESTS FROM THOSE OF

THE NATION AT LARGE AND DISINTERESTED IN TRYING

TO DO SO. WE ALSO THINK IT IS CLEAR THAT THE

TARGET OF HIS IRE IS NOT THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS,

WHITES, OR BRITAIN AS THE FORMER COLONIAL POWER

PER SE--ALTHOUGH ALL ARE HIGH ON HIS LIST OF

DISLIKES--BUT RATHER THAT HE IS TRYING TO GET AT

AND DISRUPT THE NEW AND RELATIVELY EFFECTIVE

OPPOSITION PARTY, THE MOVEMENT FOR DEMOCRATIC

CHANGE, HOWEVER HE CAN. BECAUSE THE FARMERS HAVE

EXERCISED THEIR DEMOCRATIC RIGHTS TO FREE SPEECH

AND POLITICAL ACTIVITY IN SUPPORT OF THE MDC,

THEY ARE TARGETS.

7. (C) I BELIEVE IT IS TIME FOR THE HIGHEST

LEVELS OF THE UNITED STATES GOVERNMENT TO MAKE

KNOWN TO MUGABE THAT WE WELL UNDERSTAND HIS

POSITION AND REJECT IT AS HE IS SENDING THE

COUNTRY IN A DISASTROUS DIRECTION. ZIMBABWE MUST

CONFIDENTIAL

BE A NATION BASED ON LAWS, NOT ON THE

CALCULATIONS OF POLITICAL EXPEDIENCY OF ONE MAN

OR ONE PARTY. THE INCREASING LAWLESSNESS HERE IS

ATTRIBUTABLE TO THE DELIBERATE ACTIONS AND

CALCULATED INACTION OF THE GOVERNMENT, STARTING

WITH PRESIDENT MUGABE. BY THE SAME TOKEN, THE

DOWNWARD TREND HERE IS REVERSABLE, SHOULD MUGABE

DECIDE TO FOLLOW THE PATH OF POLITICAL TOLERANCE,

WHICH IS, AFTER ALL IS SAID AND DONE, TRULY IN

HIS SELF INTEREST.

8. (C) ACCORDINGLY, I BELIEVE A TELEPHONE CALL

TO PRESIDENT MUGABE FROM PRESIDENT CLINTON, VICE

PRESIDENT GORE, OR SECRETARY ALBRIGHT SHOULD BE

PLACED AT THE EARLIEST POSSIBLE TIME. WE SHOULD

ALSO MOVE FORWARD TO CONFIRM THE PROPOSED VISIT

OF U/S PICKERING. BY URGING MUGABE TO STEP BACK

FROM THE BRINK WE WILL BE DOING WHAT WE CAN TO

SALVAGE DEMOCRACY AND RULE OF LAW IN ZIMBABWE,

NOT TO MENTION THE COUNTRY\'S ECONOMY AND THE

LIVES OF SOME OF ITS CITIZENS- END COMMENT.

MCDONALD
