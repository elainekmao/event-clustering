1. SUMMARY: THE INTERNATIONAL DEVELOPMENT LAW INSTITUTE

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 ROME 01544 01 OF 02 270916Z

(IDLI) ASSEMBLY OF MEMBER STATES MET IN ROME MARCH 20,

2002 TO APPROVE IDLI\'S WORK PLAN, ENDORSE NEW BOARD

MEMBERS, AND APPROVE CHANGES TO IDLI\'S BASIC TEXTS.

WHILE THE CHANGES WERE IN LINE WITH THE BOARD OF

DIRECTORS\' STRATEGIC VISION FOR IDLI, MEMBER STATES WERE

NOT READY TO APPROVE CHANGES TO IDLI\'S BASIC TEXTS, AND A

CORRESPONDENCE VOTE WILL BE CONDUCTED ON THE CHANGES. END

SUMMARY.

2. THE IDLI ASSEMBLY OF MEMBER STATES MET MARCH 20, 2002

IN ROME. IDLI MEMBERS ARE: U.S., TUNISIA, SENEGAL,

NORWAY, FRANCE, CHINA, BULGARIA, AUSTRALIA, COLOMBIA,

ECUADOR, EGYPT, THE NETHERLANDS, THE PHILIPPINES, ITALY

AUSTRIA AND SUDAN. USAID LEGAL COUNSEL JOHN GARDNER AND

MISOFF COMPRISED THE U.S. DELEGATION.

3. IDLI\'S ANNUAL REPORT, PREPARED BY DIRECTOR WILLIAM

LORIS (U.S.) NOTED THAT THE ORGANIZATION\'S STRATEGIC

PLAN, ENTITLED "VISION 2000," ENVISIONED IDLI GROWING IN

STATURE AS AN ORGANIZATION IN ORDER TO ACHIEVE

RECOGNITION INTERNATIONALLY AS A LEADER IN PROMOTING THE

RULE OF LAW AS A MEANS OF DEVELOPMENT (SEE DISCUSSION

REFTEL B). LORIS HIGHLIGHTED IN PARTICULAR THE WORK THAT

IDLI HAD DONE TRAINING JUDGES IN EAST TIMOR, ITS PROPOSED

PROJECT ON RESTORATION OF RULE OF LAW IN AFGHANISTAN, AND

THE IMPORTANCE OF ITS ALUMNI NETWORKS (SOME 29 LOCAL

ASSOCIATIONS OF IDLI-TRAINED LEGAL PROFESSIONALS FROM

DEVELOPING COUNTRIES AND COUNTRIES IN TRANSITION). THE

RECENT DECISION OF THE ITALIAN GOVERNMENT TO MAKE AN

ANNUAL CONTRIBUTION OF USD 200,000 TO IDLI WAS WELCOMED,

ALONG WITH HOPES THAT OTHER DONORS MIGHT CONSIDER MAKING

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ROME 01544 01 OF 02 270916Z

ANNUAL CONTRIBUTIONS AS WELL.

4. IN LINE WITH "VISION 2000," MEMBERS WERE ASKED TO

APPROVE TWO CHANGES TO IDLI\'S "ESTABLISHMENT AGREEMENT":

1) CHANGE THE NAME TO INTERNATIONAL DEVELOPMENT LAW

ORGANIZATION (VICE INSTITUTE) IN ORDER TO CORRECT THE

COMMON MISPERCEPTION THAT IDLI IS CONNECTED TO AN

ACADEMIC INSTITUTE AND 2) PROVIDE A SPECIFIC MANDATE FOR

RULE OF LAW AND GOOD GOVERNANCE (REFTEL A).

5. CHINA AND FRANCE ANNOUNCED THAT THEY WERE NOT IN A

POSITION TO SUPPORT THE CHANGES. CHINA STATED THAT HER

COUNTRY DID NOT WISH TO SEE IDLI CHANGE FROM A TRAINING

INSTITUTE TO A POLICY INSTITUTE AND THEREFORE WAS NOT IN

A POSITION TO ADOPT CHANGES TO THE BASIC TEXTS (COMMENT:

THE CHINESE POSITION MAY REFLECT THE FACT THAT

"GOVERNANCE" AND "RULE OF LAW" -- WHILE ALWAYS IMPLICIT

IN IDLI\'S MANDATE -- WERE HERETOFORE NOT SO EMPHASIZED.

END COMMENT). FRANCE DID NOT EXPLAIN HER POSITION TO THE

ASSEMBLY, BUT TOLD USDEL ON THE MARGINS THAT FRANCE WAS

OPPOSED TO CHANGING IDLI\'S NAME TO "ORGANIZATION"

BECAUSE, WITH ONLY 16 MEMBERS, IT WAS PREMATURE FOR THE

ORGANIZATION TO ASPIRE TO UN SPECIALIZED AGENCY STATUS

(REFERRING TO THE DIRECTOR\'S OPENING REMARKS WHERE HE

SAID THAT HE HOPED THAT ONE DAY IDLI WOULD BE TO "RULE OF

LAW" WHAT WHO WAS TO HEALTH). FURTHERMORE, FRANCE DID

NOT SUPPORT THE ORGANIZATION MOVING AWAY FROM A VOLUNTARY

TO ASSESSED BUDGET BASIS AND WAS CONCERNED THAT, AS AN

"ORGANIZATION," IDLI MIGHT NEXT PROPOSE A NEW FUNDING

BASIS.

6. U.S., NETHERLANDS, AUSTRALIA AND ITALY EXPRESSED

SUPPORT FOR THE PROPOSED CHANGES. OTHER DELEGATES STATED

THAT THEY WERE WITHOUT INSTRUCTIONS, OR NOT AUTHORIZED TO

VOTE. IT WAS AGREED THAT THE VOTE WOULD BE CONDUCTED BY

CORRESPONDENCE AND A RESOLUTION WAS ADOPTED TO THAT

EFFECT. (COMMENT: A THREE QUARTERS MAJORITY IS NEEDED TO

CHANGE THE BASIC TEXTS. END COMMENT).

7. THE MEMBERS APPROVED WITHOUT DEBATE THE DATE OF THE

NEXT SESSION (MARCH 2005), A RESOLUTION ENCOURAGING

DISCUSSIONS WITH THE EGYPTIAN GOVERNMENT ON THE

POSSIBILITY OF ESTABLISHING A REGIONAL OFFICE IN CAIRO,

APPROVAL OF A NEW STEERING COMMITTEE (AUSTRIA, CHINA AND

EGYPT) AND THE APPOINTMENT OF THREE NEW DIRECTORS:

JUSTICE CATHERINE BRANSON (AUSTRALIA), FEDERAL COURT OF

AUSTRALIA, SYDNEY

MRS. MAY.Y.OH (SINGAPORE), PARTNER, MAY OH AND WEE,

SINGAPORE

MR. KO-YUNG TUNG (U.S.), VICE PRESIDENT AND GENERAL

COUNSEL OF THE WORLD BANK, WASHINGTON D.C.
