SUBJECT: MDC LEADER CHARGED WITH POSSESSING UNLICENSED

RADIO

CLASSIFIED BY POLITICAL SECTION CHIEF MATT HARRINGTON.

REASONS: 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) ON DECEMBER 15, POLICE CHARGED OPPOSITION MOVEMENT

FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE (MDC) LEADER MORGAN TSVANGIRAI UNDER

THE POSTAL AND TELECOMMUNICATIONS ACT WITH POSSESSING AN

UNLICENSED RADIO. THE RADIO WAS A WALKIE-TALKIE USED BY

ONE OF HIS BODYGUARDS. AFTER INSTRUCTING TSVANGIRAI TO

WRITE A STATEMENT ACKNOWLEDGING THE PRESENCE OF THE RADIO

IN HIS HOME AND THAT NO LICENSE HAD BEEN OBTAINED FOR IT,

POLICE SAID HE COULD EXPECT A SUMMONS LATER IN THE WEEK TO

APPEAR IN COURT. TSVANGIRAI TOLD JOURNALISTS THAT THE

RADIO BELONGED TO THE MDC AS A PARTY, NOT TO HIM

PERSONALLY. INNOCENT CHAGONDA, TSVANGIRAI\'S ATTORNEY, TOLD

US THAT THE POSTAL AND TELECOMMUNICATIONS ACT PROVIDES FOR

A MAXIMUM PENALTY UPON CONVICTION ON THIS CHARGE OF A FINE

OF UP TO 200,000 ZIMBABWEAN DOLLARS (APPROXIMATELY U.S.

$600) AND/OR UP TO TWO YEARS IMPRISONMENT. HE TOLD US IT

IS NOT CLEAR THAT THE ACT APPLIES IN THIS CASE, AS THE

MDC HAD PURCHASED THE RADIO BEFORE THE LAW CAME INTO EFFECT

LAST YEAR. CHAGONDA DISMISSED THE CHARGE AS PURE

HARASSMENT, AND SAID HE HAD NO DOUBT THAT THE GOZ "WILL

COME UP WITH SOMETHING ELSE AGAINST TSVANGIRAI BY JANUARY."

2. (C) THE FILING OF CHARGES FOLLOWED SEVERAL DAYS OF

POLICE HARASSMENT OF TSVANGIRAI. AT 12:30 A.M. ON DECEMBER

3. (C) GANDI MUDZINGWA, TSVANGIRAI\'S SPECIAL ASSISTANT,

SURMISED THAT THE GOZ IS TRYING TO MAKE IT DIFFICULT FOR

TSVANGIRAI TO CAMPAIGN AROUND THE COUNTRY BY KEEPING HIM

TIED DOWN IN HARARE ON CONTRIVED LEGAL MATTERS. CLIVE

PUZEY, WHO SERVES AS TSVANGIRAI\'S INFORMAL INTELLIGENCE

CHIEF, EXPRESSED MORE SERIOUS CONCERN ABOUT THE LENGTHS TO

WHICH AN INCREASINGLY DESPERATE GOVERNMENT MIGHT GO. PUZEY

TOLD US HE HAS ARRANGED TO SPIRIT AWAY THE MDC LEADERSHIP

TO SAFEHOUSES AROUND THE COUNTRY SHOULD THE SECURITY

SITUATION DETERIORATE SIGNIFICANTLY. HE ALSO BELIEVES THE

PARTY OUGHT TO EXPLORE A LAST RESORT OPTION OF ESTABLISHING

A GOVERNMENT-IN-EXILE IN EITHER BOTSWANA OR SOUTH AFRICA

AND IS SEEKING TSVANGIRAI\'S GO-AHEAD TO FEEL OUT THOSE TWO

GOVERNMENTS ON THIS POSSIBILITY. PUZEY AND OTHERS AROUND

TSVANGIRAI ARE MORE AND MORE CONCERNED THAT AN INCREASINGLY

4. (C) IT IS LIKELY NO CONCIDENCE THAT THE DETENTION OF

TSVANGIRAI OCCURRED AT THE SAME TIME AS THE ZANU-PF

SIPDIS

CONGRESS IN VICTORIA FALLS, WHERE PRESIDENT MUGABE

OFFICIALLY LAUNCHED HIS PRESIDENTIAL CAMPAIGN BY DECLARING

WAR ON THE MDC (SEPTEL). THE TREATMENT OF TSVANGIRAI

CONSTITUTES SIMPLE HARASSMENT OF A LEGITIMATE POLITICAL

LEADER -- THE RADIO POSSESSION CHARGE PROVIDED MERELY A

HANDY PRETEXT -- AND WAS THE LATEST EPISODE IN THE GOZ\'S

ONGOING EFFORT TO KEEP THE MDC LEADERSHIP OFF-BALANCE AND

FORCE IT TO SPEND SCARCE RESOURCES ON LEGAL FEES.
