SUBJECT: USTICA CRASH BACK IN THE HEADLINES

CLASSIFIED BY: POLITICAL MINISTER COUNSELOR THOMAS

COUNTRYMAN FOR REASONS 1.5(B)(D)

1. (U) THIS IS AN ACTION REQUEST. EMBASSY REQUESTS THAT

DOCUMENTS DESCRIBED BELOW, WHICH WERE RELEASED PURSUANT TO

THE FREEDOM OF INFORMATION ACT, BE FORWARDED TO POST AS

RAPIDLY AS POSSIBLE. SEE PARA 4.

2. (U) MOST MAJOR ITALIAN SUNDAY NEWSPAPERS CARRIED A SPECIAL

REPORT AIRED ON STATE BROADCASTER RAI-TG3 ON JUNE 21 ALLEGING

"NEW REVELATIONS" ABOUT THE 1980 CRASH OF AN ITALIAN AIRLINER

OFF THE ISLAND OF USTICA IN WHICH 81 PEOPLE DIED. UNDER THE

HEADLINE, "USTICA: U.S. SPIED ON AMATO," LA REPUBBLICA\'S

FRONT PAGE ARTICLE CHARGED THE U.S. WITH INTERCEPTING A 1992

PHONE CALL BETWEEN THEN PRIME MINISTER GIULIANO AMATO AND

DEFENSE MINISTER SALVO ANDO\' (BOTH SOCIALIST PARTY MEMBERS)

IN CONNECTION WITH USG EFFORTS TO MONITOR COURT AND

PARLIAMENTARY PROBES INTO THE USTICA ACCIDENT. THE ARTICLES

INDICATE THAT THE U.S. AMBASSADOR USED THE INTERCEPTED

INFORMATION TO PRESSURE BETTINO CRAXI, LEADER OF THE

SOCIALIST PARTY, OVER THE GOI\'S DECISION TO BE A PLAINTIFF IN

A TRIAL RELATED TO THE CRASH.

3. (U) THE LA REPUBBLICA ARTICLE, WHICH WAS ONE OF THE MORE

EXTENSIVE PIECES, INCLUDES AN INTERVIEW WITH FORMER PM AMATO.

HE DESCRIBED THE 1992 PHONE CALL AS ONLY A DISCUSSION OF THE

FACTS, AND SAID THE ITALIAN GOVERNMENT HAD NOTHING TO HIDE.

OTHER MAJOR ITALIAN PAPERS ALSO CARRIED THE STORY PROMINENTLY

ON JUNE 22. THE REPORT OF THE ALLEGED 1992 WIRE-TAP WAS

BASED ON DOCUMENTS RELEASED UNDER THE FREEDOM OF INFORMATION

ACT AT THE REQUEST OF USTICA VICTIMS\' RELATIVES.

4. (C) COMMENT AND ACTION REQUEST. THE USTICA CRASH AND

CONSPIRACY THEORIES ABOUT A POSSIBLE USG ROLE ARE PERENNIAL

FAVORITES OF THE ITALIAN MEDIA. WHILE THE 1992 WIRE-TAP

STORY HAS ONLY LASTED THROUGH ONE NEWS CYCLE, WE CAN

CONFIDENTLY PREDICT USTICA WILL BE BACK IN THE NEWS IN SOME

FORM IN THE FUTURE. IN THE INTEREST OF BEING PREPARED TO

RESPOND TO AND LIMIT THE SPIN CONSPIRACY-THEORISTS ATTACH TO

NEW "REVELATIONS" ABOUT THE ACCIDENT, POST REQUESTS A COPY OF

THE DECLASSIFIED 1992 TELEPHONE INTERCEPT. GRAPHICS FOR THE

LA REPUBBLICA ARTICLE SHOWED A BLURRED AND INDECIPHERABLE

PHOTO OF WHAT LOOKS LIKE A STATE DEPARTMENT CABLE. ANOTHER

ARTICLE REFERS TO A RESTRICTED CABLE SENT IN 1992 FROM

AMBASSADOR SECCHIA TO THEN DEFENSE SECRETARY CHENEY. IT IS

NOT CLEAR WHERE THE MESSAGE ORIGINATED OR WHETHER IT WAS SENT

NODIS OR EXDIS. END COMMENT AND ACTION REQUEST.
