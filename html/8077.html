SUBJECT: GOI AGREES ON NEED TO COORDINATE "STEP BY

STEP"/BENCHMARKS PROCESS FOR BELARUS

REF: A) STATE 11642 B) MINSK 690

CLASSIFIED BY: POL MINISTER COUNSELOR TOM COUNTRYMAN. REASON: 1.5 (B)(

D)

1. (C) MFA RUSSIA OFFICE DIRECTOR ROSA ANNA CONIGLIO SAID

THAT POINTS REFTEL, WHICH POLOFF ORIGINALLY DELIVERED MAY 6,

WERE USED AS FOOD FOR THOUGHT AT THE EU COEST MEETING (AT 25)

IN BRUSSELS THE FOLLOWING DAY. SHE SAID THAT ALL EU MEMBERS

AND ASPIRANTS AGREED - THE US "STEP BY STEP" AND EU

BENCHMARKS PROCESSES WILL BE FAR MORE EFFECTIVE IF

COORDINATION CONTINUES. THE GOI AGREES THAT THE US AND EU

MUST CONTINUE TO "KEEP THE PRESSURE ON" THE LUKASHENKO REGIME

TO ENACT REAL, VERIFIABLE REFORMS. EXCEPT FOR SOME MOVEMENT

ON SECONDMENT OF THE HUMAN RIGHTS OFFICER AT THE OSCE

MISSION, THUS FAR THERE HAS BEEN NO PROGRESS, ONLY RHETORIC,

FROM THE GOB.

2. (C) CONIGLIO WONDERED IF NEWLY APPOINTED FM MARTYNOV,

WHO HAS WORKED ON NATO AND EU ISSUES AND "KNOWS THE SCORE,"

REPRESENTED AN OPPORTUNITY FOR PROGRESS; A SIGNAL THAT THIS

TIME MINSK WAS SINCERE IN ITS DESIRE TO REFORM. POLOFF NOTED

THAT MARTYNOV HAD AGREED IN PRINCIPLE, IN A LETTER TO THE

SECRETARY AND MEETING WITH EU PRESIDENCY REPRESENTATIVES, TO

SIPDIS

THE "STEP BY STEP" AND BENCHMARKS PROCESSES. HOWEVER,

RECENT, CONTINUED GOB EFFORTS TO CRUSH FREE EXPRESSION AND

THE WORK OF NGOS (REF B) IS A DISTURBING SIGN THAT THE

HARD-LINERS REMAIN THE DECISION MAKERS IN MINSK. CONIGLIO

AGREED. THERE COULD BE NO NEGOTIATION, EVEN WITH MARTYNOV,

ONLY DIALOGUE. ITALY WILL PUSH FOR LANGUAGE IN THE UPCOMING

EU TROIKA STATEMENT ON BELARUS TO REFLECT JUST SUCH AN

APPROACH.
