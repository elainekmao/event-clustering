SUBJECT: DELIVERY OF ATLANTIC BIRD DEMARCHE TO GOI AND

ALENIA

REF: SECSTATE 213498

CLASSIFIED BY: ESTCOUNS DAVID MULENEX FOR REASONS 1.5 B AND D.

1. (C) SUMMARY: EMBASSY DELIVERED REFTEL DEMARCHE TO

THE GOI AND ALENIA SPAZIO ON DECEMBER 18 AND 21,

RESPECTIVELY. BOTH CLEARLY UNDERSTOOD REFTEL MESSAGE THAT A

PRESIDENTIAL WAIVER OF THE EXPORT CONTROLS REGARDING ATLANTIC

BIRD WOULD BE HIGHLY UNLIKELY IN THE CONTEXT OF CURRENT

CHINESE UNRESPONSIVENESS TO U.S. NON-PROLIFERATION CONCERNS.

ALENIA REPRESENTATIVES EXPRESSED FRUSTRATION WITH THE

SITUATION, BUT THEY ALSO POSED A HYPOTHETICAL SOLUTION THE

ISSUE BY REMOVING THE U.S. ORIGIN COMPONENTS FROM ATLANTIC

BIRD. AN ALENIA OFFICIAL ADMITTED THAT HIS COMPANY WISHED TO

PURSUE ADDITIONAL CHINESE CONTRACTS BUT WAS CONCERNED ABOUT

POTENTIAL U.S. POLITICAL INTERFERENCE. ALENIA EXPRESSED THE

DESIRE FOR A BILATERAL ITALO-AMERICAN AGREEMENT LAYING OUT

CLEAR RULES ON COOPERATION WITH CHINA. ITALIAN AMBASSADOR TO

THE U.S. SALLEO WILL BE TAKING THIS ISSUE UP WITH THE

DEPARTMENT IN THE NEAR FUTURE. END SUMMARY.

------------------------------------

MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS REACTION

------------------------------------

2. (C) ON DECEMBER 18, 2001, ESTCOUNS DELIVERED REFTEL

DEMARCHE TO UGO DE MOHR, HEAD OF THE ITALIAN FOREIGN

MINISTRY\'S DUAL-USE EXPORT CONTROL OFFICE. DE MOHR INDICATED

THAT HE UNDERSTOOD THE ULTIMATE POINT OF THE DEMARCHE

REGARDING U.S. REASONS FOR NOT PURSUING A PRESIDENTIAL WAIVER

TO THE TIANANMEN SANCTIONS AND ACCEPTED THE DEMARCHE LARGELY

WITHOUT COMMENT. HIS ONLY QUESTION WAS WHETHER THE EVENTS OF

SEPTEMBER 11, 2001 HAD CHANGED U.S. ATTITUDES TOWARD CHINA.

ESTCOUNS POINTED OUT THAT THE DEMARCHE INDICATED THAT

SEPTEMBER 11 HAD NOT CHANGED U.S. CONCERNS REGARDING CHINA\'S

UNHELPFUL ATTITUDE TOWARD STEMMING THE PROLIFERATION OF

MISSILE RELATED TECHNOLOGY.

ALENIA SPAZIO

3. (C) DE MOHR ALSO ASKED WHETHER THE UPCOMING BILATERAL

"GLOBALIZATION" TALKS CONCERNING DEFENSE TRADE MIGHT INCLUDE

DISCUSSION OF DUAL-USE EXPORTS AND THIRD PARTY TRANSFERS. HE

REFERED TO EMBASSY DISCUSSIONS IN SUMMER 2001, IN WHICH HE

SUGGESTED THAT THE U.S. AND ITALY SHOULD SEEK SOME COMMON

GUIDELINES CONCERNING TRADE IN POTENTIALLY SENSITIVE ITEMS TO

CHINA. THE GOI GOAL IS TO STAY ABREAST WITH THE U.S. IN

EXPLORING TRADE OPPORTUNITIES WITH CHINA, WHILE REMAINING

SENSITIVE AND RESPONSIVE TO USG CONCERNS ABOUT TECHNOLOGY

TRANSFER. ESTCOUNS PROMISD TO SEEK CLARIFICATION FROM THE

DEPARTMENT AND DOD CONCERNING THE SCOPE OF THE TALKS.

4. (SBU) LENIA SPAZIO CEO GIUSEPPE VIRIGLIO AND ALENIA

MAKETING VP PIANTELLA, RECEIVED REFTEL DEMARCHE FROMEST

OFFICERS ON DECEMBER 21. ESTCOUNS STRESSED HE

CONFIDENTIALITY OF THE DEMARCHE, PARTICULARLY REGARDING OUR

SIPDIS

POLICY TOWARD CHINA AND NOTED THAT IT WAS HIGHLY UNUSUAL FOR

THE UNITED STATES TO MAKE SUCH A DOCUMENT RELEASABLE TO A

PRIVATE COMPANY. VIRIGLIO RESPONDED WITH AN EXPRESSION OF

APPRECIATION FOR THE DEMARCHE AND REITERATION OF ALENIA\'S

POSITION THAT THE LAUNCH INTO ORBIT OF ATLANTIC BIRD FROM

CHINESE SOILDOES NOT CONSTITUTE AN EXPORT TO CHINA.

5. (SBU) VIRIGLIO ASKED THAT IF ALENIA WERE PREPARED TO

REMOVE THE U.S. ORIGIN COMPONENTS FROM ATLANTIC BIRD AND

REPLACE THEM WITH EUROPEAN COMPONENTS, IF TIS WOULD OBVIATE

U.S. OBJECTIONS. HE ASKED WHETER THE U.S. GOVERNMENT WOULD

STILL ATTEMPT TO OBSTRUCT THE LAUNCH OF ATLANTIC BIRD ONBOARD

A CHINEE LONG MARCH SPACE LAUNCH VEHICLE (SLV), EVEN THOUH

THERE WOULD NOT BE ANY U.S. COMPONENTS ABOARD. VIRIGLIO

EXPANDED ON THIS QUESTION BY ASKING WHTHER THE UNITED STATES

WOULD BE OPPOSED TO ALENIA COMPETING FOR CHINESE AEROSPACE

CONTRACTS. HE EXPLAINED THAT IF ALENIA RENEGED ON THE USE OF

THE LONG MARCH SLV FOR ATLANTIC BIRD, ALENIA WOULD NOT BE

ABLE TO COMPETE FOR A NUMBER OF FUTURE CHINESE

TELECOMMUNICATIONS SATELLITE CONTRACTS. HE MENTIONED THE

DONGFENG IV AS AN EXAMPLE BUT DID NOT ELABORATE FURTHER. IN

EFFECT, VIRIGLIO INTIMATED THAT CHINA WOULD TREAT THE

ATLANTIC BIRD LAUNCH AS A LITMUS TEST IN DETERMINING ALENIA\'S

RELIABILITY AS A SUITABLE PARTNER FOR FUTURE CHINESE SPACE

PROJECTS.

6. (SBU) VIRIGLIO REITERATED ALENIA\'S INTENTION TO COMPLY

STRINGENTLY WITH RELEVANT U.S. LEGISLATION REGARDING EXPORT

CONTROLS. IN THE CASE OF CHINA, A POTENTIALLY LUCRATIVE

MARKET FOR ALENIA, VIRIGLIO INDICATED HIS DESIRE FOR A

BILATERAL GOVERNMENT TO GOVERNMENT AGREEMENT THAT WOULD SET

OUT CLEAR RULES FOR FUTURE COOPERATION WITH CHINA. HE

COMPLAINED THAT THE UNCERTAINTY OF A CASE-BY-CASE APPROACH

WAS MAKING IT EXTREMELY DIFFICULT FOR ALENIA TO ESTABLISH A

COORDINATED BUSINESS PLAN TOWARD THE CHINESE MARKET. HE

INDICATED THAT THE ITALIAN AMBASSADOR TO THE UNITED STATES,

7. (C) EMBASSY COMMENT. WE HEARD LOUD AND CLEAR FOR THE

FIRST TIME FROM ALENIA OFFICIALS THAT THE PRIMARY

SIGNIFICANCE OF ATLANTIC BIRD FOR THE COMPANY IS NOT THE SUNK

COSTS FOR THE CHINESE LONG MARCH SLV, BUT THE DOWNSTREAM

ORDER BOOK THAT MAY BE AT STAKE AND WORTH HUNDREDS OF

MILLIONS OF DOLLARS. WHILE WE HAVE NO REASON TO BELIEVE THAT

MFA WILL BACK OFF ITS REPORT FOR OUR DECISION, WE MAY HEAR

FROM AMB. SALLEO AND MFA SOME GROUSING AND PROPOSALS TO AVOID

INSTANCES SIMILAR TO ATLANTIC BIRD IN THE FUTURE. END
