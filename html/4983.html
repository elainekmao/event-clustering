SUBJECT: GOT PROTESTS PORT VISIT BY US NAVY TO RHODES

(U) Classified by Pol-Mil Counselor Stuart Brown. Reasons:

1.5 (B and D).

1. (U) The Embassy recently received a diplomatic note from

the Ministry of Foreign Affairs regarding visits by US Naval

vessels to the island of Rhodes.

2. (U) Begin text of MFA note No. 2002DHGY/485435:

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Turkey

presents its compliments to the Embassy of the United States

of America and has the honor to bring the following to the

kind attention of the Embassy:

The Ministry has been informed that two MK V type American

naval boats visited the island of Rhodes on 4 November, 2002.

The Ministry would like to emphasize that Rhodes and other

Dodecanese islands have been placed under a permanent

demilitarized status by the 1947 Paris Peace Treaty which

excludes all forms of military presence and activities on the

said islands, including port visits by naval ships.

The Ministry would therefore like to request the US

Administration to abide by the regime enshrined in the 1947

Paris Peace Treaty.

The Ministry of Foreign affairs of the Republic of Turkey

avails itself of this opportunity to renew to the Embassy of

the United States of America the assurance of its highest

consideration.

Ankara, 17 December 2002.

End text.

3. (C) Comment: The MFA\'s position as described in the

diplomatic note is consistent with the long-standing GOT

position on port calls by US ships to Rhodes and other

islands that the GOT considers de-militarized. MFA and TGS

officials most recently raised this issue during the Dec

16-18 High-Level Defense Group (HLDG) Political-Military

Working Group meeting in Garmisch, Germany, and we expect

them to raise it again during the HLDG meeting in the US in

May 2003.
