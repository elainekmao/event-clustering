SUBJECT: REENGAGING PM BERLUSCONI ON BIOTECH

CLASSIFIED BY: AMBASSADOR MEL SEMBLER, FOR REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D)

1. (C) JOINED BY AMBASSADORS HALL AND NICHOLSON I HAD DINNER

NOVEMBER 12 WITH PRIME MINISTER BERLUSCONI, HIS PRINCIPAL

ADVISOR GIANNI LETTA, AND SEVERAL OTHERS. WE USED THE

OPPORTUNITY TO IMPRESS UPON THE PM THE NEGATIVE EFFECT EU

POLICY WAS HAVING ON THE WILLINGNESS OF AFRICAN COUNTRIES TO

DISTRIBUTE BIOTECH FOOD TO THEIR STARVING POPULATIONS AND THE

MORAL IMPLICATIONS OF THESE ACTIONS. BERLUSCONI AGREED THAT

ITALY NEEDED TO HAVE A SCIENCE-BASED POLICY, AND SAID HE

WOULD HELP US WHERE HE COULD WITHIN EU COUNCILS. I TOLD

BERLUSCONI PRIVATELY ONCE AGAIN THAT HIS AG MINISTER

CONTINUED TO DRIVE A BIOTECH POLICY BASED MORE ON EMOTION

THAN REASON, AND THAT HE AS PM NEEDED TO GET MORE INVOLVED

PERSONALLY IF HE WANTED TO COUNTER THAT TREND. HE TOOK THE

POINT.

2. (C) BERLUSCONI APPEARED SURPRISED AND CONCERNED WHEN I

TOLD HIM THAT A WTO CASE WAS A DISTINCT POSSIBILITY IF NO

SOLUTION TO EUROPEAN INTRANSIGENCE WAS FOUND SOON. HE

CLEARLY UNDERSTOOD THE IMPLICATIONS FOR THE UPCOMING ITALIAN

EU PRESIDENCY, AND ASKED TO BE KEPT INFORMED.

3. (C) GETTING THE PM\'S ATTENTION ON BIOTECH AGAIN IS

IMPORTANT. WE NEED TO KEEP HIM ENGAGED, AND ONE WAY TO DO

THIS IS TO LET HIM KNOW DIRECTLY OUR CONCERNS AS WE MOVE

TOWARD DECISIONS ABOUT A WTO CASE AND OTHER ASPECTS OF OUR

BIOTECH POLICY. IN THE NEXT WEEK, THIS EMBASSY, EMBASSY
