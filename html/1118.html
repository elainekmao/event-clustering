SUBJECT: DETENTION OF CUBAN DEFECTORS BY ZIMBABWE

REF: STATE 108703 (NOTAL)

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD FOR REASONS 1.5

B/D.

1. (U) THIS IS AN ACTION REQUESTED MESSAGE. PLEASE SEE

PARAGRAPH 7.

2. (C) PER REFTEL INSTRUCTION, ON JUNE 8 AMBASSADOR

MCDONALD TELEPHONED MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS

PERMANENT SECRETARY MACHIVENYIKA MAPURANGA, THE HIGHEST

AVAILABLE OFFICIAL, TO REGISTER THE USG\'S SERIOUS

CONCERN WITH THE WELFARE OF CUBAN DOCTORS LEONEL CORDOVA

RODRIGUEZ AND NORIS PENA MARTINEZ. THE AMBASSADOR

EXPLAINED THAT THE USG HAD AN INTEREST IN THE CASE AS

THE CUBANS HAD EXPRESSED A DESIRE TO SETTLE IN THE

UNITED STATES. THE AMBASSADOR CONTINUED THAT HE HOPED

THE ZIMBABWEAN REFUGEE COMMITTEE WOULD GRANT THE CUBANS

REFUGEE STATUS SO AN AMERICAN IMMIGRATION OFFICIAL COULD

COME TO HARARE TO INTERVIEW THEM ON JUNE 9. THE

AMBASSADOR STRESSED THAT HE HOPED ZIMBABWE WOULD NOT

CONFIDENTIAL

EXPEL THEM BACK TO CUBA IN THE MEANTIME OR TO ANOTHER

COUNTRY FOR FORCIBLE REPATRIATION TO CUBA. IN RESPONSE,

MAPURANGA TOOK NOTE OF THE AMBASSADOR\'S DEMARCHE, BUT

STRESSED ZIMBABWE\'S SOVEREIGNTY TO DECIDE THE QUESTION.

3. (C) AFTER UPDATING CANADIAN HIGH COMMISSIONER JAMES

WALL ON JUNE 8 ON ACTIONS WE HAD TAKEN ON THE CASE, DCM

ASKED WHAT CANADA INTENDED TO DO WITH RESPECT TO THE

CUBANS. WALL SAID THAT HIS COUNTRY\'S INTEREST WAS

LIMITED, SINCE THE DOCTORS HAVE NEITHER FAMILY NOR ANY

OTHER TIES IN CANADA AND HAD MADE NO APPLICATION TO

ENTER CANADA. THE HIGH COMMISSIONER REPORTED THAT ON

JUNE 7 HE HAD MADE A REPRESENTATION TO MINISTRY OF

FOREIGN AFFAIRS DIRECTOR FOR EUROPE AND THE AMERICAS

NGONI CHIDEYA TO THE EFFECT THAT HIS GOVERNMENT VIEWED

ZIMBABWE AS BEING RESPONSIBLE FOR THE CUBANS\' WELL-

BEING. WALL NOTED THAT IF THE PRESS ACCOUNTS OF THEIR

FORCIBLE REPATRIATION WERE TRUE, THEN ZIMBABWE WAS IN

VIOLATION OF THE CONVENTION ON REFUGEES.

4. (C) UNITED NATIONS RESIDENT COORDINATOR CARLOS LOPES

EXPLAINED TO AMBASSADOR MCDONALD ON JUNE 8 THAT THE

LOCAL UN HIGH COMMISSIONER FOR REFUGEES (UNHCR)

REPRESENTATIVE, JOHN ADU, WAS TRYING HIS BEST TO RESOLVE

THE CASE IN SUCH A WAY THAT THE ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT

DID NOT SEE IT AS AN "AMERICAN" ONE. THE ZIMBABWEANS

WERE MOVING IN THE RIGHT DIRECTION, LOPES REPORTED. HE

SAID THAT BY ALLOWING THE UNHCR REPRESENTATIVE ACCESS TO

THE PAIR ON JUNE 7, THE ZIMBABWEANS IMPLICITLY

RECOGNIZED THAT FOLLOWING PROPER PROCEDURES WAS

IMPORTANT. LOPES CONFIDED THAT THE ZIMBABWEANS ALSO HAD

FROZEN OUT CUBAN AMBASSADOR, RODOLFO SARRACINO, BECAUSE

THE LATTER HAD COME TO HIM FOR INFORMATION ON

DEVELOPMENTS IN THE CASE AS RECENTLY AS JUNE 8. THE

REFUGEE COMMITTEE WOULD MEET ON JUNE 8 AND MAKE A

DECISION ON THE FATE OF THE CUBANS, BUT THAT DECISION

MIGHT TAKE A COUPLE OF DAYS TO BE COMMUNICATED, PROBABLY

THROUGH A POLITICAL FILTER. THE ZIMBABWEANS, THE UN

RESIDENT COORDINATOR, NOTED WOULD NOT ADMIT TO

WRONGDOING IN ATTEMPTING TO REPATRIATE THE CUBANS.

5. (C) WHATEVER DECISION THE ZIMBABWEANS MADE COULD

WORK OUT WELL FOR THE CUBANS, LOPES CONTINUED. IF THE

ZIMBABWEANS GRANTED THEM REFUGEE STATUS, THE CUBANS

COULD APPLY FOR RESETTLEMENT IN A THIRD COUNTRY,

SOMETHING THAT IS DOABLE IF TIME-CONSUMING. IF THE

ZIMBABWEANS DO NOT, THEN UNHCR GETS CUSTODY OF THEM AND

ATTEMPTS TO PLACE THEM IN A COUNTRY WILLING TO RECEIVE

THEM. IT\'S QUITE RARE TO HAVE A READY HOST, AS THE

UNITED STATES IS IN THE PRESENT CASE, LOPES AVERRED.

6. (C) AMBASSADOR MCDONALD ASKED THAT LOPES INSTRUCT

THE LOCAL UNCHR REPRESENTATIVE TO OBTAIN THE IMMEDIATE

RELEASE OF THE CUBANS FROM JAIL TO A TRANSIT CAMP OR

OTHER SAFE HOUSING, PENDING RESOLUTION OF THE CASE.
