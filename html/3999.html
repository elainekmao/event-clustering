SUMMARY

-------

1. A TEAM LED BY AMBASSADOR TONY HALL VISITED ZIMBABWE

OCTOBER 08-11 AND FOUND ZIMBABWE SLOUCHING TOWARD A MAJOR

HUMANITARIAN DISASTER. NEARLY HALF THE COUNTRY\'S POPULATION

IS NOW CLASSIFIED BY THE UN AS SERIOUSLY FOOD INSECURE (6.7

MILLION). WHILE EXACERBATED BY SUB-NORMAL RAINFALL THIS

YEAR, THE ALARMING FOOD SHORTFALL IS MAN-MADE - EMANATING

FROM THE ECONOMIC AND POLITICAL POLICIES OF THE GOVERNMENT

OF ZIMBABWE (GOZ). BIOTECH MAIZE, GOZ "IMPEDIMENTS" IMPOSED

ON FOOD IMPORTS AND NGO REGISTRATION - WERE HIGHLIGHTED.

AMBASSADOR HALL HIGHLIGHTED THE FACT THAT AMERICA IS A

GENEROUS AND COMPASSIONATE NATION THAT PROVIDED SIGNIFICANT

ASSISTANCE TO ZIMBABWE DURING THE 1991/1992 "WORST DROUGHT

OF THE CENTURY" - AND WOULD CONTINUE TO BE THE LEAD DONOR TO

THE PRESENT CRISIS. "WHILE WE DISAGREE WITH THE POLICIES OF

AND ARE GUIDED BY FORMER PRESIDENT REAGAN\'S PHILOSOPHY THAT

2. AMBASSADOR TONY HALL, US MISSION TO ROME; HUMANITARIAN

ATTACHE TIM LAVELLE; SPECIAL ASSISTANT TO THE AMBASSADOR MAX

FINBERG; AND USDA/FAS PRETORIA AGRICULTURAL COUNSELOR RICK

HELM, VISITED ZIMBABWE OCTOBER 08-11. IN ADDITION TO

MEETINGS IN HARARE WITH U.S. EMBASSY, UN AND GOZ

FUNCTIONARIES, THE TEAM SPENT TWO DAYS IN RURAL AND PERI-

URBAN AREAS REVIEWING UN AND NGO RELIEF OPERATIONS.

3. SINCE 1997, ZIMBABWE HAS BEEN EXPERIENCING AN ECONOMIC

AND SOCIAL CRISIS INDUCED BY DECLINING PRICES FOR ITS KEY

EXPORTS AND POOR ECONOMIC POLICIES, AND HEIGHTENED BY A

DECREASE IN TOBACCO EXPORTS FOLLOWING FARM INVASIONS,

INCREASED FISCAL DEFICIT, AND LOSS OF INVESTOR CONFIDENCE

ARISING FROM UNCERTAINTY ABOUT DOMESTIC POLICIES. FOREIGN

DIRECT INVESTMENT HAS DROPPED FROM USD $444 MILLION IN 1998

TO $3 MILLION (EST.) IN 2002. OVER THIS PERIOD, REAL GDP HAS

GONE DOWN BY 35 PERCENT. SOURCES ESTIMATE THE UNEMPLOYMENT

RATE AS HIGH AS 70 PERCENT. IN SHORT, THE ECONOMY IS

IMPLODING FROM SELF-INFLICTED WOUNDS.

4. THE FOOD SUPPLY SITUATION GIVES SERIOUS CAUSE FOR

CONCERN. THE 2002 MAIZE CROP (THE BASIC STAPLE) HARVEST IS

GOZ MAINTAINS A RIGID MONOPOLY ON GRAIN IMPORTS AND

EFFECTIVELY MARGINALIZE THE PRIVATE SECTOR). ACCORDING TO

7. IN JANUARY 2000, THE COMMERCIAL FARMING SECTOR EMPLOYED

MORE THAN 350,000 WORKERS, ROUGHLY ONE-THIRD OF ALL WAGE

EMPLOYMENT. BY MID-2002, UP TO 1.5 MILLION EX-COMMERCIAL

FARM WORKERS AND THEIR FAMILIES ARE OR SOON WILL BE IN LIFE

THREATENING CIRCUMSTANCES DUE TO FARM EXPROPRIATIONS AND

ENSUING INTIMIDATION. MANY HAVE STARTED TO MOVE SOMEWHERE,

ANYWHERE TO SURVIVE - LIVING ON LAND WITHOUT WATER OR

SANITATION, ALONG ROADS AND ON THE MARGINS OF CITIES. THE

FARM WORKERS ARE AT ODDS WITH THE GOVERNMENT FOR TWO

PRINCIPAL REASONS: (A) MOST VOTED FOR THE OPPOSITION; AND

(B) THEY WERE THE SKILLED WORK FORCE THAT KEPT THE WHITE

FARMERS IN BUSINESS. ADDITIONALLY, MANY ARE FROM NEIGHBORING

COUNTRIES. WHILE MANY HAVE RECEIVED "FORCED RETRENCHMENT"

SEVERANCE PACKAGES, THESE MONIES ARE NOT EXPECTED TO LAST

LONG, PARTICULARLY WITH RAMPANT INFLATION (CURRENTLY AT 135

PERCENT BY OFFICIAL ESTIMATES). AT PRESENT, VIRTUALLY NO

HUMANITARIAN ASSISTANCE IS REACHING "EXPELLED" FARM WORKERS.

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS

-------------------------------

10. THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY MUST KEEP UP THE "DRUM BEAT"

THAT ZIMBABWE\'S GRAIN MARKETING SYSTEM NEEDS DRASTIC

REVAMPING TO ALLOW MORE PRIVATE SECTOR PARTICIPATION IN THE

HANDLING AND DISTRIBUTION OF MAIZE AND OTHER STAPLE FOODS TO

INCREASE SUPPLIES, LOWER PRICES, AND ENSURE GREATER

AVAILABILITY TO THOSE WHO CAN AFFORD TO PURCHASE.

11. WFP NEEDS TO ACCELERATE ITS CEREALS FOOD AID IMPORTS TO

THE PROMISED 55,000 MTS/MONTH LEVEL. MONITORING IS PRESENTLY

INADEQUATE; WFP SHOULD LOOK AT THE POSSIBILITY OF DIRECT

IMPLEMENTATION OR SECONDING OF NGO STAFF TO WFP, IN ORDER TO

OVERCOME PROBLEMS OF NGO REGISTRATION AND LIMITED

IMPLEMENTING PARTNER CAPACITY. ALSO NEEDED IS A UN

CONTINGENCY PLAN TO ENSURE THAT FUEL IS AVAILABLE TO MOVE

HUMANITARIAN CARGOES (LACK OF FOREIGN EXCHANGE WILL RESULT

PAGE 04 ROME 05058 03 OF 04 171003Z

IN A FUEL CRISIS IF LIBYA, KUWAIT AND SOUTH AFRICA REFUSE TO

EXTEND MORE CREDIT TO GOZ).

12. A NUTRITION SURVEILLANCE SYSTEM NEEDS TO BE PUT IN PLACE

TO TRACK TRENDS IN NUTRITIONAL STATUS, FOOD SECURITY AND

RELATED ISSUES. SPECIFIC ATTENTION MAY BE PAID TO MONITORING

THE NUTRITIONAL STATUS OF CHILDREN UNDER FIVE AND CRUDE

MORTALITY RATE (CMR). IN GENERAL, THERE IS A NEED TO

STRENGTHEN UN INFORMATION AND MANAGEMENT SYSTEMS. UN IS

STRONGLY ENCOURAGED TO COMMENCE POST-DISTRIBUTION

MONITORING, RAPIDLY MAKING USE OF DONOR SUPPORT. SUCH

MONITORING SHOULD DRAW FROM ZIMBABWEAN AND OTHER SOURCES AND

ITS RESULTS SHOULD BE DISSEMINATED TO DONORS AND OTHER

STAKEHOLDERS AS WELL AS TO THE UN.

13. WFP AND ITS NGO PARTNERS ARE ADVISED TO USE GENERAL

RELIEF FOOD DISTRIBUTIONS TO IMPART KEY MESSAGES IN HIV/AIDS

AWARENESS, HEALTH AND NUTRITION. THE CAPTIVE AUDIENCES

PRESENT AN IDEAL OPPORTUNITY TO REITERATE IMPORTANT LESSONS.
