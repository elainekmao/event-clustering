SUBJECT: Ambassador urges GSL to expel post-Saddam

official Iraqis and protect diplomatic property

Refs: (A) State 93783

- (B) State 92281

- (C) Colombo 623 (All Notal)

(U) Classified by Ambassador E. Ashley Wills.

Reasons 1.5 (B,D).

1. (S/NF) The Ambassador spoke to Sri Lankan Foreign

Secretary Nihal Rodrigo on April 11, and reviewed Ref B

SIPDIS

points re expulsion of post-Saddam official Iraqis and

suspending the operations of its embassy. Rodrigo

replied that the situation was very "confusing" and that

the GSL was "polling" the views of other South Asian

countries. He said that as far as the government was

aware there were only two Iraqi officials left in the

country: Charge Wajdi Mardan Abbas and one other

person. (Note: See Ref C regarding a recent

communication by Abbas, a known Iraqi intelligence

operative, to the U.S. Embassy.) Two other officials

had recently left the country. As if he was feeling

sorry for the Iraqis, Rodrigo commented that Abbas was

here "with his wife and two children" and the other

Iraqi official seemed to be a courier.

2. (S/NF) Ambassador also reviewed Ref A points re

protecting Iraqi diplomatic property with Rodrigo.

3. (S/NF) COMMENT: Rodrigo said the GSL would review

the issues that the Ambassador raised and get back to us

shortly. The Ambassador was left with the strong

impression that the government did not plan to expel

Abbas, however. END COMMENT.

4. (U) Minimize considered.

WILLS
