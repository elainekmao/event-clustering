2. (C) CHARGE RAISED THE ISSUE OF LIBYA\'S CHAIRMANSHIP OF

THE UNHRC. BONIVER NOTED SHE WOULD ATTEND THE OPENING

SESSION AND THAT ITALY WOULD SEEK TO REGAIN ITS MEMBERSHIP ON

THE COMMISSION. THE UNHRC NEEDS "PROFOUND REFORM," SHE

ASSERTED. REVIEWING UPCOMING RESOLUTIONS, SHE FOUND SEVERAL

ON AFRICAN COUNTRIES, FIVE AGAINST ISRAEL/PRO-PALESTINIAN,

AND NOTHING ON CUBA. "THAT\'S GROTESQUE," SHE EXCLAIMED.

"WHAT IS THE POINT OF THIS KIND OF COMMISSION? WHERE IS THE

BALANCE?" CHARGE NOTED THAT REFORM WOULD BE IMPOSSIBLE UNDER

A LIBYAN CHAIRMANSHIP. IT WAS EQUALLY GROTESQUE, HE

REJOINED, TO HAVE A COUNTRY UNDER EVEN SUSPENDED UN SANCTIONS

CHAIRING THE COMMISSION. BONIVER\'S CHIEF OF STAFF,

ALESSANDOR DI FRANCO (ALSO IN THE MEETING), CONFIRMED WITH

CHARGE THAT THE U.S. WOULD ASK FOR A VOTE ON LIBYA AS CHAIR

AND PREDICTED A SPLIT EU VOTE. BOTH BONIVER AND CHARGE

AGREED ON THE DESIRABILITY OF REFORMING THE UNHRC, BUT THAT

THIS WAS UNLIKELY TO HAPPEN SOON.

3. (U) BONIVER SAID ITALY, AS EU PRESIDENT, WOULD HOST A

MAJOR HUMAN RIGHTS EVENT IN MILAN IN SEPTEMBER OR OCTOBER.

THE THEME WOULD BE SEEKING TO BRIDGE THE CULTURAL DIVIDE

BETWEEN ISLAM AND WESTERN DEMOCRACIES BY FOCUSING ON THE

UNIVERSAL VALUE OF HUMAN RIGHTS. PARTICIPANTS WOULD BE

MOSTLY NGOS, FORMER NOBEL PEACE PRIZE RECIPIENTS, "PEOPLE WHO

CAN TALK ACROSS THE CULTURAL DIVIDE." BONIVER ENCOURAGED

SUGGESTIONS FOR POSSIBLE U.S. INVITEES.

4. (U) BONIVER HOPES TO MAKE A TRIP TO WASHINGTON IN THE

NEAR FUTURE (NO DATES PLANNED AT THIS TIME). SHE WOULD BE

INTERESTED IN MEETING U/S DOBRIANSKY, U/S GROSSMAN, A/S

KELLY, AND A/S CRANER. WE WILL ADVISE WHEN BONIVER INDICATES

THE TRIP IS IN THE PLANNING STAGES.
