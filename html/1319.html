SUBJECT: TENSIONS ON COMMERCIAL FARMS CONTINUE

CLASSIFIED BY CDA EARL IRVING. REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) SUMMARY: THE SITUATION ON COMMERCIAL FARMS

REMAINS TENSE, WITH NEW OCCUPATIONS AND FEW EVICTIONS OF

LONG-STANDING SQUATTERS BEING CARRIED OUT. THE ACTIONS

OF THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE (GOZ) AND THE ZIMBABWE

REPUBLIC POLICE (ZRP) CONTINUE TO BE INCONSISTENT AND

INCOHERENT. COMMERCIAL FARMERS HAVE MANAGED TO PLANT

SOME CROPS, ALBEIT FEWER HECTARES. FARM WORKERS ARE

GETTING INCREASINGLY ANGRY AS THE CONTINUATION OF THE

STATUS QUO THREATENS THEIR LIVELIHOODS. HOME AFFAIRS

MINISTER NKOMO MET WITH THE CFU IN KAROI DISTRICT ON

OCTOBER 11 AND STATED THAT FARM INVADERS SHOULD NOT

INTERFERE WITH THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS\' PLANTING OF

CROPS. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) ECONOFF MET ON OCTOBER 12 WITH OFFICIALS OF THE

COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION (CFU) TO DISCUSS THE CURRENT

SITUATION ON COMMERCIAL FARMS.

---------------------------

ONGOING AND NEW OCCUPATIONS

---------------------------

3. (C) ACCORDING TO THE CFU THERE ARE CURRENTLY BETWEEN

900 AND 1000 OCCUPIED FARMS. THIS IS DOWN FROM A HIGH

OF AROUND 1700 FARMS INVADED DURING THE RUN-UP TO

PARLIAMENTARY ELECTIONS LAST JUNE. TWO TO THREE NEW

FARMS HAVE BEEN OCCUPIED EVERY WEEK, BUT THESE

OCCUPATIONS DO NOT ALL BECOME PERMANENT. SOMETIMES THE

OCCUPIERS LEAVE THE FARMS AFTER ONLY A FEW DAYS.

ACCORDING TO THE CFU, 10-15 PERCENT OF THE OCCUPIERS ARE

WAR VETERANS, AND THE REMAINDER ARE POOR, LANDLESS

ZIMBABWEANS FROM THE COMMUNAL AREAS THAT ARE CLOSE TO

THE OCCUPIED FARMS.

----------------------------------

MIXED SIGNALS FROM THE GOZ AND ZRP

----------------------------------

4. (C) GOZ OFFICIALS AND THE ZRP CONTINUE TO SEND MIXED

SIGNALS REGARDING THE FARM INVASIONS AND THE RULE OF

LAW. IN SOME PROVINCES, GOZ OFFICIALS, FROM GOVERNORS

ON DOWN, HAVE STATED THAT NO MORE FARM OCCUPATIONS WILL

BE TOLERATED, AND THE ZRP ARE ACTING TO PREVENT NEW

OCCUPATIONS, AS WELL AS ASSOCIATED CRIMINAL ACTS. IN

OTHER PROVINCES, THE OPPPOSITE IS OCCURRING. AN EXAMPLE

IS MASHONALAND CENTRAL, WHERE THE DISTRICT ADMINISTRATOR

HAS TOLD OWNERS OF FARMS LISTED FOR SEIZURE NOT TO

PLANT. THE CFU CONTINUES TO RECEIVE NUMEROUS REPORTS

FROM FARMERS THAT COMPLAINTS TO THE LOCAL POLICE

CONSTABLES ABOUT CRIMINALITY ASSOCIATED WITH THE FARM

OCCUPATIONS ARE OFTEN MET WITH ACKNOWLEDGEMENT THAT WHAT

IS OCCURRING IS ILLEGAL, BUT THAT IT IS A POLITICAL

ISSUE AND THEIR HANDS ARE TIED.

------------------------------------

MOST FARMERS HAVE PLANTED SOME CROPS

------------------------------------

5. (C) ACCORDING TO THE CFU, ALMOST ALL THEIR MEMBERS

HAVE MANAGED TO PLANT SOME CROPS. WHILE THE MAJORITY OF

FARMERS CONTINUE TO BE DENIED NEW FINANCING BY BANKS

BECAUSE OF THE UNSETTLED SITUATION, THEY HAVE MANAGED TO

SCRAPE TOGETHER THE RESOURCES TO PLANT SMALLER CROPS.

MANY TOBACCO FARMERS FOR INSTANCE, ARE PLANTING FEWER

HECTARES. BECAUSE THEY ARE ALSO PLANTING LATE, THEY

WILL BE PUTTING IN ONLY ONE TOBACCO CROP INSTEAD OF THE

NORMAL TWO. THE CFU ESTIMATES THAT 50 PERCENT FEWER

HECTARES OF MAIZE WILL BE PLANTED, PRINCIPALLY BECAUSE

THIS IS A CROP WITH A HIGH RATE OF THEFT, AND FARMERS

FEAR THAT THEIR CROPS WILL BE STOLEN AT HARVEST TIME.

WHILE MOST FARMERS HAVE MANAGED TO FIND THE MONEY TO

PLANT, THE CFU FEARS THAT BY JANUARY MANY WILL RUN OUT

OF MONEY FOR INPUTS NEEDED TO TEND AND HARVEST THEIR

CROPS, UNLESS THE BANKS ARE PERSUADED TO LOAN TO THEM.

SUBJECT: TENSIONS ON COMMERCIAL FARMS CONTINUE

ANGER AND FRUSTRATION AMONG FARM WORKERS, WHO ARE FED UP

WITH THE ABUSE BEING VISITED ON THEM BY WAR VETERANS.

SINCE THE GOZ IS LARGELY DOING NOTHING TO PROTECT THEM,

THEY ARE TAKING ACTION IN SELF-DEFENSE TO PROTECT THEIR

OWN LIVELIHOODS. THE CFU BELIEVES THAT MORE VIOLENCE IS

INEVITABLE AS THE CROP SEASON CONTINUES.

--------------------------------------------- --------

THE CFU AND HOME AFFAIRS MINISTER NKOMO MEET IN KAROI

--------------------------------------------- --------

7. (C) IN ONE PROMISING DEVELOPMENT HOME AFFAIRS

MINISTER NKOMO AND POLICE COMMISSIONER CHIHURI MET ON

OCTOBER 11 IN KAROI, ONE OF THE AREAS WORST-HIT BY LAND

RELATED VIOLENCE, WITH CFU EXECUTIVES, LOCAL FARMERS,

LOCAL BUSINESS PEOPLE, AND WAR VETERANS WHO HAVE BEEN

OCCUPYING NEARBY COMMERCIAL FARMS. NKOMO TOLD THE

GATHERING THAT THE FARMERS SHOULD GO AHEAD WITH THEIR

PLANTING AND THAT THE FARM OCCUPIERS SHOULD NOT

INTERFERE. HE ALSO SAID THAT THE OCCUPIERS SHOULD STAY

PUT WHERE THEY ARE, AND THAT THEY WILL BE RESETTLED ON

LAND AS IT BECOMES AVAILABLE. COMMISSIONER CHIHURI TOLD

THE ASSEMBLED GROUP THAT THE FARMERS, FARM WORKERS, AND

WAR VETERANS SHOULD AVOID CONFRONTATIONS, AND THAT THE

ZRP WOULD ARREST PEOPLE WHO INTERFERE WITH FARMING

ACTIVITIES OR COMMIT OTHER CRIMES ON THE LAND ON WHICH

THEY ARE SQUATTING. THE CFU BELIEVED THIS WAS A

PRODUCTIVE MEETING THAT MIGHT RESULT IN SOME REDUCTION

IN LAWLESSNESS IN THE AREA, WHICH HAS BEEN EXACERBATED

BY THE DISTRICT POLICE CHIEF, A FORMER WAR VETERAN WHO

OPENLY SYMPATHIZES WITH THE LAND OCCUPIERS.

8. COMMENT: IT IS CLEAR THAT THE SITUATION ON COMMERCIAL

FARMS HAS ONLY MARGINALLY IMPROVED. ALTHOUGH THE GOZ

HAS STATED THAT NO MORE FARM INVASIONS WILL BE ALLOWED,

NEW OCCUPATIONS CONTINUE TO OCCUR. THE RESPONSE BY

POLICE TO CRIMINAL ACTS OCCURRING ON OCCUPIED FARMS

VARIES WIDELY BY DISTRICT AND PROVINCE. MOST FARMERS

ARE PLANTING, BUT DOING SO UNDER THE MENACING EYES OF

WAR VETERANS ON THEIR LANDS, AND ARE CONCERNED THAT THEY

MAY NOT GET THE FINANCING TO HARVEST THE CROP THEY ARE

PUTTING IN THE GROUND. THE CFU MEETING WITH MINISTER

NKOMO IN KAROI WOULD APPEAR TO BE A PROMISING SIGN, BUT,

AS ALWAYS, WHAT ACTUALLY HAPPENS ON THE GROUND WILL

MATTER MUCH MORE THAN GOVERNMENT PROMISES. THERE ARE NO

INDICATIONS THAT THE GOZ IS LOOKING FOR A WAY TO COME TO

AN AGREEMENT WITH THE CFU ON A LAND REFORM PROGRAM THAT

BOTH PARTIES COULD ACCEPT AND WOULD RESTORE THE RULE OF

LAW. AGRICULTURE MINISTER MADE, FOR EXAMPLE, REFUSES TO

MEET WITH THE CFU. WE WILL CONTINUE TO SEE INCREASING

TENSIONS AND UNCERTAINTY SO LONG AS THE GOZ REFUSES TO

DEVELOP A FAIR, TRANSPARENT LAND REFORM PROGRAM AND

CONTINUES TO USE THE LAND ISSUE PRIMARILY AS A POLITICAL

TOOL. END COMMENT.
