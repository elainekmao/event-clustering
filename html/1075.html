1. SUMMARY: THIS CABLE PROVIDES A BRIEF HISTORY OF THE

BACKGROUND AND RATIONALE FOR U.S. INVOLVEMENT IN LAND REFORM IN

ZIMBABWE, THE STATUS OF ZIMBABWE\'S CURRENT SECOND PHASE OF ITS

LAND REFORM AND RESETTLEMENT PROGRAMME (LRRP II), USAID AND

OTHER DONOR ASSISTANCE FOR LRRP II, AND THE STATUS OF THAT

ASSISTANCE AS A RESULT OF THE PRESENT FARM OCCUPATION PROBLEM IN

ZIMBABWE. THIS IS AN UNCLASSIFIED DOCUMENT AND MAY BE SHARED

WITH THE U.S. CONGRESS. END SUMMARY.

ZIMBABWE\'S LAND REFORM AND RESETTLEMENT PROGRAM

2. IN JUNE 1998, THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE (GOZ) RELEASED A

DRAFT "POLICY FRAMEWORK AND PROJECT DOCUMENT" FOR PHASE II OF

THE LAND REFORM AND RESETTLEMENT PROGRAMME (LRRP II) SETTING

FORTH THE GOVERNMENT\'S PROPOSED POLICIES AND PLANS FOR PURSUING

A SECOND MAJOR PHASE OF LAND REFORM IN ZIMBABWE. (COMMENT:

WHILE DEFINITIONS MAY VARY, THE FIRST \'PHASE\' OF ZIMBABWE\'S LAND

REFORM PROGRAM PROCEEDED SPORADICALLY SINCE INDEPENDENCE, WITH

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 HARARE 02271 01 OF 07 271033Z

ONLY ABOUT 71,000 FAMILIES RESETTLED ON ABOUT 3.5 MILLION

HECTARES (HA) OF LAND. THIS CURRENT SECOND PHASE OF THE PROGRAM

WAS LAUNCHED AT LEAST PARTIALLY IN RESPONSE TO THE GOVERNMENT\'S

FAILED ATTEMPT TO COMPULSORILY ACQUIRE 1,471 COMMERCIAL FARMS IN

1997. AS A RESULT OF GOVERNMENT\'S INABILITY TO ADHERE TO THE

LEGAL REQUIREMENTS ASSOCIATED WITH THIS PROPOSED ACTION, THE NET

RESULT OF THIS EFFORT, ASIDE FROM THE CONSIDERABLE ECONOMIC

UNCERTAINTY THIS ACTION CAUSED, WAS THE IDENTIFICATION OF 118

FARMS WHICH WERE UNCONTESTED BY THE OWNERS FOR PROGRAM

ACQUISITION/USE (REF A). END COMMENT.) AS DESCRIBED IN THIS

PROGRAM DOCUMENT, LRRP II IS AN AMBITIOUS FIVE-YEAR PLAN FOR THE

RESETTLEMENT OF 91,000 FAMILIES (UP TO 550,000 PEOPLE, PLUS AN

UNSPECIFIED NUMBER OF SECONDARY \'SPIN OFF\' BENEFICIARIES) ON

FIVE MILLION HECTARES (HA) OF LAND IN THE COUNTRY\'S LARGE-SCALE

COMMERCIAL FARMING SECTOR, AT AN ESTIMATED COST OF US$ 1.9

BILLION.

INTERNATIONAL DONORS\' CONFERENCE ON LAND REFORM

3. THE GOZ CONVENED AN INTERNATIONAL DONORS CONFERENCE ON LAND

REFORM IN ZIMBABWE ON SEPTEMBER 9-11, 1998 TO ADDRESS DONORS\'

EXPRESSED INTEREST AND CONCERNS REGARDING LRRP II (REF B). THE

CONFERENCE WAS ATTENDED BY SENIOR REPRESENTATIVES OF ALL MAJOR

INTERNATIONAL, MULTILATERAL AND BILATERAL DONORS TO ZIMBABWE, AS

WELL AS KEY ZIMBABWEAN NON-GOVERNMENTAL, CIVIL SOCIETY, FARMERS\'

UNIONS AND PRIVATE SECTOR STAKEHOLDERS. AT THE CONFERENCE,

DONORS UNANIMOUSLY ENDORSED THE NEED FOR LAND REFORM IN ZIMBABWE

FOR PURPOSES OF POVERTY ALLEVIATION (FOR THE POOR INDIGENOUS

MAJORITY WITHIN THE COUNTRY\'S CONGESTED COMMUNAL AREAS), AND

ECONOMIC GROWTH AND STABILITY. THE DONORS\' MAJOR RESERVATIONS

REGARDING THE PROPOSED PROGRAM INCLUDED THE: (1) LACK OF

4. MARKET-BASED APPROACHES: AMONG THE MORE SIGNIFICANT

ACHIEVEMENTS OF THE DONORS\' LAND CONFERENCE WAS GOZ ACCEPTANCE

TO ALLOW ALTERNATIVE RESETTLEMENT MODELS AND TO PROCEED WITH

WORK ON LAND POLICY ISSUES THAT WOULD PAVE THE WAY FOR LAND

TENURE REFORM. ACCEPTANCE OF THE NEED TO WORK ON POLICY ISSUES,

SUCH AS LAND TAXATION, SUBDIVISION, FARM SIZE LIMITATIONS, LAND

TENURE, ETC., THAT WOULD STIMULATE A LAND MARKET AND CREATE

INCENTIVES FOR EFFICIENT LAND USE REPRESENTED A SIGNIFICANT

POLICY BREAKTHROUGH FROM PAST GOZ POSITIONS ON THIS SUBJECT.

DESPITE THE POLITICAL RHETORIC, GOZ TECHNICAL COUNTERPARTS

UNDERSTAND AND SUPPORT THESE CONCEPTS AND, IN FACT, PROGRESS IS

BEING MADE ON THESE ISSUES. IN THIS REGARD, UNDER THE NEW LAND

POLICY FRAMEWORK COMPLETED IN FALL 1999, A NEW PROGRESSIVE LAND

TAX HAS BEEN APPROVED BY CABINET FOR LEGISLATIVE ACTION, DRAFT

REGULATIONS ON FARM SIZE LIMITATIONS HAVE BEEN COMPLETED, NEW

REGULATIONS ON LAND SUBDIVISION ARE ACTIVELY BEING PURSUED AND

LONGER-TERM PLANS FOR ADDRESSING WOMENS\' AND MORE GENERAL LAND

TENURE RIGHTS ARE UNDER DEVELOPMENT. COMMENT: AS DESCRIBED

BELOW (SEE PARA 13.B.), USAID ASSISTANCE FOR THIS PROGRAM HAS

BEEN DESIGNED SPECIFICALLY TO PROVIDE SUPPORT ON THESE KEY

POLICY ISSUES. END COMMENT.

INCEPTION PHASE

6. ON NOVEMBER 1, 1999, THE GOZ ISSUED THE PILOT "INCEPTION

PHASE FRAMEWORK PLAN -1999-2000" DOCUMENT, CALLING FOR THE

RESETTLEMENT OF 33,800 FARM FAMILIES (PLUS SOME 85,000 NON-FARM

FAMILY SECONDARY \'SPIN OFF\' BENEFICIARIES) OVER AN INITIAL 24-

MONTH PERIOD ON ONE MILLION HA OF LAND. THIS INCEPTION PHASE

DOCUMENT DULY INCORPORATED ALL OF THE AGREEMENTS REACHED AT THE

DONORS\' LAND CONFERENCE, INCLUDING A FAIR, JUST, TRANSPARENT AND

HIGHLY CONSULTATIVE PROCESS; ALTERNATIVE AS WELL AS GOVERNMENT-

ADMINISTERED APPROACHES; AND WOMENS\' LAND RIGHTS AND

PARTICIPATION; ETC.). ACCORDINGLY, IT RECEIVED WIDESPREAD

SUPPORT FROM ALL KEY LRRP II STAKEHOLDERS (INCLUDING ALL THREE

OF THE FARMERS\' UNIONS - CFU, ICFU, ZFU).

INSTITUTIONAL FRAMEWORK

7. DUE TO ITS PARAMOUNT IMPORTANCE, LRRP II HAS A WIDE VARIETY

OF STAKEHOLDERS LOCATED IN SEVERAL DIFFERENT INSTITUTIONAL

STRUCTURES. ON THE GOVERNMENT SIDE, THE KEY INSTITUTIONAL

STRUCTURE IS THE CABINET COMMITTEE ON RURAL RESETTLEMENT AND

DEVELOPMENT (CRD), CHAIRED BY VICE-PRESIDENT JOSEPH MSIKA, AND

CONSISTING OF THE MINISTERS OF LANDS AND AGRICULTURE; LOCAL

GOVERNMENT AND NATIONAL HOUSING; RURAL RESOURCES AND WATER

COOPERATIVES; FINANCE; MINES, ENVIRONMENT AND TOURISM; TRANSPORT

CABINET-LEVEL, DECISION-MAKING GROUP IS SUPPORTED BY A \'WORKING

ABOVE MINISTRIES, WHICH IN TURN IS SUPPORTED BY THE INTER-

MINISTERIAL COMMITTEE ON RURAL RESETTLEMENT AND DEVELOPMENT

(IMCRD), COMPOSED OF HEADS OF PARTICIPATING GOVERNMENT

DEPARTMENTS.

8. THE KEY STRUCTURE FOR NON-GOVERNMENTAL INPUT INTO THE LAND

REFORM PROCESS IS THE LAND REFORM, RURAL RESETTLEMENT AND

DEVELOPMENT TASK FORCE (LAND TASK FORCE) OF THE NATIONAL

ECONOMIC CONSULTATIVE FORUM (NECF). THE NECF LAND TASK FORCE IS

COMPOSED OF SENIOR REPRESENTATIVES OF MOST KEY NON-

GOVERNMENTAL/CIVIL SOCIETY STAKEHOLDERS IN LAND REFORM IN

ZIMBABWE, INCLUDING ALL MAJOR FARMERS\' AND FARM WORKERS\' UNIONS,

WOMENS\' GROUPS, UNIVERSITY/ACEDEMIC INTERESTS, AND OTHER

CONCERNED PRIVATE/NON-GOVERNMENTAL INTERESTS. GOVERNMENT IS

ALSO REPRESENTED ON THE NECF LAND TASK FORCE AT THE WORKING

PARTY (PERMANENT SECRETARY) LEVEL OF THE CRD NOTED ABOVE.

9. DONOR AGENCIES "ACTIVELY ENGAGED IN SUPPORTING LRRP II" ARE

REPRESENTED THROUGH A JOINT CONSULTATIVE COMMITTEE OF GOVERNMENT

AND DONORS ON LAND REFORM ADMINISTERED THROUGH THE UNITED

THE KEY MANAGEMENT ENTITY FOR LRRP II IMPLEMENTATION. (NOTE: THE

SUBJECT: U.S. ASSISTANCE FOR LAND REFORM

OTHER DONOR ASSISTANCE

14. IN ADDITION TO THOSE COUNTRIES CONTRIBUTING TO THE ABOVE

UNDP/TSU PROJECT, THE WORLD BANK AND THE FRANCE ARE THE ONLY

OTHER COUNTRIES CURRENTLY PROVIDING SIGNIFICANT ASSISTANCE TO

LRRP II. ALTHOUGH NOT YET FULLY OPERATIONAL, THE WORLD BANK

(WB) HAS SIGNED A US$ 5 MILLION \'LEARNING AND INNOVATION\' LOAN

WITH THE GOVERNMENT TO SUPPORT INCEPTION PHASE IMPLEMENTATION,

WHICH INCLUDES ADDITIONAL STAFFING SUPPORT FOR THE TSU AS WELL

AS SIGNIFICANT FUNDS FOR ALTERNATIVE RESETTLEMENT ACTIVITIES

(BUT NOT FOR LAND ACQUISITION COSTS). THE BANK HAS ALSO

INDICATED ITS WILLINGNESS TO USE FUNDS FROM ITS EXISTING US$ 60

MILLION COMMUNITY ACTION PROJECT (CAP) FOR LRRP II RESETTLEMENT

PURPOSES. FRANCE IS ALSO PROVIDING ASSISTANCE TO THE TSU IN THE

FORM OF LONG-TERM TECHNICAL ADVISORY SERVICES FOR PROGRAM

PLANNING, AND MONITORING AND EVALUATION, AS WELL AS A SMALL

AMOUNT OF FUNDS FOR RESETTLEMENT ACTIVITIES IN MASHONALAND

PROVINCE.

15. BRITAIN AND THE EUROPEAN UNION (EU) HAVE STATED THEIR

INTENTION TO PROVIDE SUBSTANTIAL ASSISTANCE FOR LRRP II, AND

BOTH HAVE PREPARED PRELIMINARY PROJECT PROPOSALS FOR THIS

PURPOSE. SIGNIFICANTLY, BRITAIN WAS THE ONLY DONOR CONSIDERING

FUNDING LAND ACQUISITION COSTS. HOWEVER, WITH THE RECENT

TURBULENT DIPLOMATIC RELATIONS - EXACERBATED GREATLY BY THE NEW

CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENT OBLIGING BRITAIN, AS THE FORMER

COLONIAL RULER, TO PAY FOR LAND ACQUISITION COSTS AND THE RECENT

FARM INVASIONS - BOTH OF THESE DONORS\' PLANS FOR LRRP II SUPPORT

HAVE NOW BEEN PUT ON HOLD, PENDING THE OUTCOME OF IN-COUNTRY

DEVELOPMENTS AND DIALOGUE ON THIS SUBJECT.

PROGRESS TO DATE

16. AS A RESULT OF BOTH INTERNAL AND EXTERNAL FACTORS, PROGRESS

IN IMPLEMENTING BOTH OF THE USAID-ASSISTED ACTIVITIES, AS WELL

AS ALL OTHER DONOR SUPPORT FOR LRRP II, HAS BEEN SLOW TO DATE.

MAJOR INTERNAL FACTORS INCLUDE DELAYS IN RECRUITING KEY STAFF

(FOR THE UNDP/TSU ACTIVITY) AND MEETING PROGRAM CONDITIONALITY

REQUIREMENTS (FOR THE WORLD BANK LOAN). MAJOR EXTERNAL PROBLEMS

HAVE BEEN CAUSED BY PERIODIC GOZ RHETORIC AND/OR ACTIONS RELATED

TO THE LRRP II, SUCH AS SIGNIFICANT AND HIGHLY QUESTIONABLE LAND

ALLOCATIONS TO FAVORED BENEFICIARIES UNDER THE COMMERCIAL

RESETTLEMENT SCHEME, AND PROPOSED CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENTS

PROVIDING FOR LAND ACQUISITION WITHOUT DUE COMPENSATION. AS A

RESULT, FEW DONOR FUNDS HAVE BEEN DISBURSED TO DATE FOR LAND

ACQUISITION OR ACTUAL RESETTLEMENT PURPOSES, DESPITE SIGNIFICANT

PRIOR COMMITMENTS (NOTABLY AT THE DONOR CONFERENCE DISCUSSED

ABOVE) TO MAKE FUNDS AVAILABLE TO SUPPORT THESE MORE TANGIBLE

ASPECTS OF LRRP II.

17. AS A RESULT OF THESE DELAYS, PROGRESS HAS BEEN SLOW IN

90,000 HA) ACQUIRED WITH NOW-EXHAUSTED GOZ FUNDS AND 1,700

FAMILIES SETTLED (WITHOUT ADEQUATE INFRASTRUCTURE) TO DATE.

DATE HAS BEEN ACCORDING TO THE MORE REGRESSIVE AND TOP-DOWN

GOVERNMENT-ADMINISTERED APPROACHES TO PROGRAM IMPLEMENTATION

NOTED ABOVE. THEREFORE, LITTLE PROGRESS HAS BEEN ACHIEVED IN

TESTING AND PROMOTING THE MORE DESIRABLE ALTERNATIVE APPROACHES

FOR THIS CRITICAL AREA OF NATIONAL DEVELOPMENT.

18. AS MIGHT BE EXPECTED, THIS SITUATION HAS CAUSED INCREASING

FRUSTRATION AMONG KEY GOZ DECISION-MAKERS AND COUNTERPARTS,

THE RHETORIC ON THIS SUBJECT, HOWEVER, ALL FARMS ACQUIRED UNDER

THE PROGRAM TO DATE HAVE BEEN THROUGH CASH PAYMENT UP FRONT OF

FAIR MARKET VALUE FOR THE LAND AND IMPROVEMENTS. AS OF MARCH

CURRENT STATUS

19. AS REPORTED IN REF (C), UNDP RESIDENT REPRESENTATIVE

(RESREP) CARLOS LOPES CHAIRED A MEETING ON MARCH 14 OF ALL

DONORS CURRENTLY INVOLVED IN THE UNDP/TSU PROJECT TO DISCUSS AN

APPROPRIATE PROJECT AND DONOR RESPONSE TO THE CURRENT FARM

INVASIONS OCCURRING THROUGHOUT ZIMBABWE\'S LARGE-SCALE COMMERCIAL

FARMING AREAS (REFS D AND E). AS A RESULT OF GOVERNMENT\'S LACK

OF ANY EFFECTIVE RESPONSE TO THIS SITUATION, ALL DONORS INVOLVED

ARE INCREASINGLY CONCERNED ABOUT THE PROSPECTS FOR SUCCESSFUL

PROJECT (AND GREATER LRRP II) IMPLEMENTATION. AS A RESULT, ALL

PRESENT AT THE MEETING, INCLUDING AMBASSADOR MCDONALD,

UNANIMOUSLY RESOLVED THAT UNDP WOULD DRAFT A LETTER TO THE GOZ

INDICATING OUR JOINT CONCERN REGARDING THIS SITUATION, OUR

UNDERSTANDING THAT THESE ACTIONS (AND/OR LACK THEREOF)

CONSTITUTED A BREACH OF THE AGREED UPON COMMUNIQUE AGREEMENTS

(ESPECIALLY REGARDING THE \'RULE OF LAW\'), AND OUR JOINT DECISION

TO FREEZE PROJECT FUNDS, WITH NO NEW MAJOR PROJECT COMMITMENTS,

UNTIL THIS SERIOUS LAND INVASION SITUATION IS DISCUSSED AND

SUCCESSFULLY RESOLVED. THE LETTER ALSO REQUESTS A TRI-PARTITE

MEETING, AS CALLED FOR IN THE PROJECT AGREEMENT, TO DISCUSS THIS

DECISION AND SITUATION WITH THE GOVERNMENT AND IDENTIFY A WAY

FORWARD. THE TWO-WEEK DEADLINE INCLUDED IN THIS LETTER EXPIRED

ON APRIL 11, WITH NO RESPONSE RECEIVED TO DATE FROM THE GOZ.

20. IN ACCORDANCE WITH THIS DECISION, USAID SUBSEQUENTLY ALSO

NOTIFIED LTC, BOTH VERBALLY AND IN WRITING, OF OUR DECISION TO

EFFECT A SIMILAR FREEZE OR SUSPENSION OF ASSISTANCE UNDER THE

LTC COOPERATIVE AGREEMENT UNTIL THIS SITUATION IS PROPERLY

ADDRESSED. LTC HAS CONSENTED TO COMPLY WITH THIS REQUEST UNTIL

SUCH TIME AS THEY ARE NOTIFIED OTHERWISE. THE INTENT OF THESE

ACTIONS IS TO SEND A STRONG JOINT SIGNAL TO GOVERNMENT OF THE

DONORS\' SERIOUS CONCERN REGARDING THIS SITUATION, WITHOUT AT

THIS TIME EFFECTING ANY ACTIONS WHICH WOULD SERIOUSLY UNDERMINE

OUR ABILITIES TO CONTINUE OUR LONGER-TERM SUPPORT FOR LRRP II,

IF/WHEN THIS SITUATION IS PROPERLY RECTIFIED. THIS IS THE

PROGRAM\'S CURRENT STATUS. THE MISSION WILL INFORM OF ANY NEW

DEVELOPMENTS AS THEY OCCUR.

21. COMMENT: ALTHOUGH PROGRESS HAS BEEN SLOW TO DATE, WE BELIEVE

THE INCEPTION PHASE, AS AGREED TO IN THE JOINT DONORS\' LAND

CONFERENCE COMMUNIQUE (SEE PARA 3.), REMAINS NOT ONLY RELEVANT,

BUT ALSO CRITICAL FOR LONG-TERM LRRP II SUCCESS. BY TESTING

ALTERNATIVE POLICIES AND APPROACHES TO LAND REFORM AND

RESETTLEMENT IN ZIMBABWE, IT HAS THE POTENTIAL TO EXERT

SIGNIFICANT POSITIVE IMPACTS ON THE COURSE OF GREATER LRRP II

IMPLEMENTATION. GIVEN THE MASSIVE SCALE OF THIS PROGRAM AND ITS
