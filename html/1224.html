SUBJECT: WILL NEW FINANCE MINISTER BE ABLE TO STAND UP

TO MUGABE?

REF: HARARE 3967

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) SUMMARY: LESS THAN A WEEK AFTER THE APPOINTMENT

WAS ANNOUNCED, ALL EYES ARE ON FINANCE MINISTER SIMBA

MAKONI, A TALENTED TECHNOCRAT WHO MANY BELIEVE IS THE

CABINET\'S MOST IMPORTANT MINISTER, WHILE INDUSTRY AND

INTERNATIONAL TRADE MINISTER NKOSANA MOYO ARGUABLY HAS

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 04039 01 OF 03 210957Z

THE NUMBER TWO SPOT. THE CHOICE OF MAKONI SURPRISED

MANY, GIVEN THE ROCKY RELATIONSHIP HE HAS HAD WITH THE

PRESIDENT AND RECENT INDICATIONS THAT MACON MIGHT JOIN

THE OPPOSITION MOVEMENT FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE (MDC). WE

THINK THAT THE IDEOLOGICALLY UNRECONSTRUCTED MUGABE WILL

NOT YIELD ANY POWER AND WILL BE UNABLE TO RESIST

MEDDLING IN MAKONI\'S BRIEF. IF THAT TURNS OUT TO BE THE

CASE, MAKONI MAY NOT LAST LONG IN THE JOB AND HIS MOST

POWERFUL WEAPON WOULD BE A CRITICALLY WORDED RESIGNATION

LETTER. MUGABE MAY ATTEMPT TO CO-OPT HIS NEW FINANCE

MINISTER BY ELEVATING HIM TO ZANU-PF\'S POLITBURO, A MOVE

THAT SURELY WILL BE RESISTED BY INSIDERS LIKE EMMERSON

MNANGAGWA AND SYDNEY SEKERAMAYI. ZANU-PF OFFICIALS ARE

IN CAMPAIGN MODE FOR THE 2002 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION, AND

IT IS NOT REALISTIC TO EXPECT ENLIGHTENED BEHAVIOR FROM

THEM. THE BEST THAT CAN BE SAID IS THAT MAKONI IS

LIKELY TO FIGHT HARD TO PUT IN PLACE SOUND ECONOMIC

POLICIES, BUT WILL ONLY WIN SOME OF THEM. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) WHEN PRESIDENT MUGABE APPOINTED HIS NEW CABINET,

IT WAS WIDELY AGREED THAT THE BEST NEWS WAS THE

APPOINTMENT OF DR. SIMBARASHE "SIMBA" HERBERT STANLEY

MAKONI AS MINISTER OF FINANCE. THE CHOICE WAS SOMETHING

OF A SURPRISE, GIVEN PAST TENSIONS BETWEEN THE TWO MEN.

ONCE A RISING STAR IN CABINET AS ENERGY MINISTER,

MAKONI, LATER AS CHIEF EXECUTIVE OF THE STATE-OWNED

NEWSPAPER CORPORATION, ANGERED MUGABE AFTER TRYING TO

SUSPEND "SUNDAY MAIL" EDITOR CHARLES CHIKEREMA, A ZANU-

PF HEAVYWEIGHT. THE ZIMBABWEAN PRESIDENT ARRANGED FOR

POLITICALLY CENTRIST MAKONI TO BE PUSHED OUT OF HIS

POSITION AS EXECUTIVE SECRETARY OF THE SOUTHERN AFRICAN

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 04 HARARE 04039 01 OF 03 210957Z

DEVELOPMENT COMMUNITY, A POSITION HE HELD FROM 1984 TO

1993. MAKONI IS SEEN AS THE MOST IMPORTANT MINISTER IN

THE NEW CABINET, AND, LESS THAN A WEEK AFTER MAKONI\'S

APPOINTMENT, ALL EYES ARE ON HIM. MINISTER OF INDUSTRY

AND INTERNATIONAL TRADE NKOSANA MOYO, WHO UNTIL LAST

WEEK DID NOT HAVE A ZANU-PF PARTY CARD, IS IN THE SAME

LEAGUE AND HAS RECEIVED ALMOST AS MUCH ATTENTION.

3. (C) THERE ARE TWO STORIES CIRCULATING IN HARARE ON

THE APPOINTMENT OF THE CAPABLE SIMBA MAKONI AS MINISTER

OF FINANCE. THE FIRST IS THAT PRESIDENT MUGABE ASKED

VICE PRESIDENT JOSEPH MSIKA TO INFORM MAKONI OF HIS

DECISION. WHEN THE VICE PRESIDENT DID SO, THE MINISTER

DESIGNATE ASKED FOR AN AUDIENCE WITH MUGABE TO DISCUSS

HOW HE WOULD HANDLE THE PORTFOLIO. MSIKA TOOK THE

MESSAGE BACK, AND IN TYPICAL MUGABE FASHION NO AUDIENCE

TOOK PLACE AND THE PUBLIC ANNOUNCEMENT WAS MADE. IN THE

SECOND VERSION, MAKONI ACTUALLY MET WITH MUGABE FOR

THREE HOURS DURING WHICH TIME HE SPELLED OUT THE

CONDITIONS UNDER WHICH HE WOULD ACCEPT THE APPOINTMENT.

DEPENDING ON WHICH VERSION YOU BELIEVE, EITHER MUGABE OR

MAKONI HAS ALREADY WON THE FIRST OF WHAT ARE LIKELY TO

BE MANY BATTLES.

4. (C) SOME CRITICS, FOR EXAMPLE MDC PRESIDENT MORGAN

TSVANGIRAI, BELIEVE THAT MAKONI\'S ACCEPTANCE OF THE

SIPDIS

APPOINTMENT WAS IN ITSELF A CAPITULATION TO MUGABE. IT

CAUGHT THE OPPOSITION LEADER BY SURPRISE BECAUSE AT ONE

POINT IT APPEARED PROBABLE THAT MAKONI MIGHT JOIN THE

MDC, ADDING TO THE NEW PARTY\'S INTELLECTUAL HEFT AND

PRESTIGE. TSVANGIRAI TOLD AN OFFICER OF THE BRITISH

HIGH COMMISSION HERE THAT HE THOUGHT MAKONI WAS A

"COWARD," AND THAT IF HE THOUGHT THAT WAS THE WAY TO

ADVANCE HIS POLITICAL CAREER, HE WAS WRONG.

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 04039 02 OF 03 210957Z

NEVERTHELESS, MAKONI HAS A REPUTATION FOR INDEPENDENT

THINKING, AND IF MUGABE DOES WISH TO CO-OPT HIM,

ELEVATING HIM TO THE POLITBURO OF ZANU-PF IS PROBABLY

THE BEST WAY TO DO SO. THE BUSINESS COMMUNITY LIKELY

WILL APPLAUD THE MOVE, AS IT WILL GIVE MAKONI THE

POLITICAL CLOUT TO PURSUE HIS POLICIES. SIMILARLY, IT

MAY SHOW THE POPULATION THAT MUGABE IS FORGIVING, IN THE

TRADITION OF AFRICAN CHIEFS, OF A "LITTLE MAN" WHO ONCE

INSULTED HIM. FINALLY, MAKONI WOULD BE A "MADE MAN,"

DEPENDENT ON THE ONE WHO MADE HIM -- MUGABE.

5. (C) TSVANGIRAI\'S PERSONAL DISAPPOINTMENT

NOTWITHSTANDING, MANY OBSERVERS HERE SEE MAKONI AND MOYO

OPERATING FROM A POSITION OF STRENGTH. THEY ARGUE THAT

IT IS UNLIKELY THAT THE TWO MEN WOULD HAVE ACCEPTED

THEIR POSITIONS IF IT WAS CLEAR FROM THE OUTSET THAT

THEIR HANDS WOULD BE COMPLETELY TIED. FURTHERMORE, IF

THE PRESIDENT HAD SIMPLY WANTED A ZANU-PF PARTY HACK, HE

WOULD HAVE APPOINTED ONE. IN FACT, WE UNDERSTAND THE

PRESIDENT MADE THE APPOINTMENT AT THE PRODDING OF SOME

MEMBERS OF HIS ENTOURAGE, WHO SEE ADDRESSING THE

COUNTRY\'S ECONOMIC ILLS AS THE KEY TO ZANU-PF\'S (AS WELL

AS THEIR) OWN SURVIVAL. THE PRESIDENT HIMSELF IS

PROBABLY NOT CONVINCED THAT THE ECONOMY IS THE MOST

PRESSING ISSUE ON HIS AGENDA, BUT HE LIKES TO SURPRISE

AND CHALLENGE CONVENTIONAL WISDOM, AND THUS AGREED TO

MAKE THE APPOINTMENT. IF THE TWO MINISTERS DO FIND

THEMSELVES STYMIED BY MUGABE, THEIR MOST POWERFUL WEAPON

AT THE MOMENT WOULD BE A CRITICALLY WORDED LETTER OF

RESIGNATION, SHOULD PRESIDENTIAL INTERFERENCE IN THEIR

PORTFOLIO MAKE THEIR POSITION UNTENABLE.

6. (C) EVEN IF MUGABE DOES NOT ACT DIRECTLY TO THWART

MAKONI, THE NEW FINANCE MINISTER STILL FACES A DIFFICULT

TASK. WHILE THE OUTCOME OF THE JUNE 24-25 PARLIAMENTARY

ELECTION WAS THE BEST POSSIBLE FOR MINIMIZING THE

POTENTIAL FOR WIDESPREAD VIOLENCE, AND THE NATURE OF THE

DOMESTIC DEBATE IS SHIFTING TOWARD ECONOMICS, CURRENT

CONDITIONS MAKE IT DIFFICULT TO REACH DECISIONS ON THE

ECONOMY. WHILE MAKONI IS AN ABLE TECHNOCRAT, HE LACKS

THE POLITICAL BASE OR CLOUT TO ENFORCE THE TOUGH

ECONOMIC DECISIONS THAT HAVE TO BE MADE. HE ENJOYS THE

SUPPORT AND RESPECT OF THE BUSINESS COMMUNITY, BUT

GETTING TOO CLOSE TO IT WOULD RAISE THE PRESIDENT\'S

SUSPICIONS, AS MUGABE PERCEIVES IT AS THE ENEMY. IN

ADDITION, THE BOSSY AND IDEOLOGICALLY UNRECONSTRUCTED

MUGABE MAY FIND IT DIFFICULT TO RESIST MEDDLING WITH

MAKONI\'S PORTFOLIO. MEMBERS OF THE ZANU-PF INNER

CIRCLE, SUCH AS EMMERSON MNANGAGWA AND SYDNEY

SEKERAMAYI, WOULD SEE MAKONI\'S SUDDEN RISE INTO THE

POLITBURO AS A THREAT TO THEIR INTERESTS AND WOULD DO

EVERYTHING THEY COULD TO BLOCK IT.
