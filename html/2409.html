1. (C) AMBASSADOR TOOK ADVANTAGE OF A FRIDAY, 01/18 MEETING

WITH VATICAN FM EQUIVALENT JEAN-LOUIS TAURAN TO PRESS REFTEL

POINTS ON THE ZINNI MISSION AND TO ASK TAURAN TO REQUEST

PRESIDENT ARAFAT TO HALT THE VIOLENCE AND TAKE STEPS TO

PROSECUTE THOSE RESPONSIBLE FOR THE KARINE A WEAPONS

SHIPMENT. TAURAN, WHO IN THE PAST HAS BEEN AN ACTIVE

PROPONENT OF THE HOLY SEE\'S "TILT" TOWARD THE PALESTINIANS

AND OF VATICAN INTERESTS IN PROTECTING THE HOLY SITES IN

JERUSALEM, TOOK THE POINTS ON BOARD, SAYING ARAFAT "WILL HEAR

FROM US." TAURAN ASKED THE AMBASSADOR WHETHER HE PERSONALLY

KNEW GEN. ZINNI. (NOTE: WE TAKE ALL THIS TO MEAN A) THAT

SOMEONE IN THE VATICAN WILL SPEAK DIRECTLY EITHER TO ARAFAT

OR TO ONE OF HIS PEOPLE AND B) THE VATICAN WOULD BE

INTERESTED IN MEETING WITH GEN. ZINNI).

2. (U) IN THE MEANTIME, AT HIS WEEKLY ANGELUS MESSAGE THE

POPE REFERRED TO THE "DEAD END" INTO WHICH THE CONFLICTING

PARTIES HAVE FOUGHT THEMSELVES AND CALLED FOR THE

INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY NOT TO ABANDON THEM TO THEIR FATE.

THIS FALLS ON THE HEELS OF HIS ANNUAL ADDRESS TO THE

DIPLOMATIC CORPS IN WHICH HE DEFENDED ISRAEL\'S RIGHT TO

SECURITY WHILE DECRYING THE "FIFTY YEARS OF OPPRESSION" WHICH

THE PALESTINIANS HAVE SUFFERED.

3. (C) ON A SOMEWHAT RELATED ISSUE, THE EGYPTIAN AMBASSADOR

TO THE HOLY SEE, FAROUK RAAFAT, DROPPED IN TO SEE THE

AMBASSADOR FRIDAY. RAAFAT ASKED IF THE USG WAR ON TERRORISM

WOULD TURN NEXT TO IRAQ. HIS DCM MET SEPARATELY ON THE SAME

ISSUE WITH EMBASSY VATICAN DCM THE DAY BEFORE. BOTH PRESSED

FOR THE USG TO TAKE IT SLOW ON IRAQ -- AND SEE WHETHER WE

COULD "WAIT SADAAM OUT."

4. (C) COMMENT: CLEARLY THE VATICAN WILL SEEK TO DEFEND ITS

OWN INTERESTS IN THE ISRAEL/PALESTINIAN CONFLICT (THE RIGHTS

OF CHRISTIANS ABOVE ALL). BUT A FUNDAMENTAL VATICAN INTEREST

IS SEEING AN END TO THE VIOLENCE COMMITTED AGAINST BOTH

SIDES. WITH THAT IN MIND WE REQUEST DEPARTMENT CONSIDER A

ZINNI DROP BY DURING ONE OF HIS FUTURE VISITS TO THE REGION.

WE BELIEVE THE POPE WOULD WISH TO SPEAK TO HIM DIRECTLY ABOUT

POSSIBLE PATHS TO PEACE. ON IRAQ, WE NOTE THAT THE VATICAN

HAS NOT BEEN VOCAL ABOUT SANCTIONS IN RECENT WEEKS, INCLUDING

DURING THE POPE\'S ANNUAL ADDRESS. FROM THE VATICAN

PERSPECTIVE, SANCTIONS ARE ALWAYS BAD; FAR WORSE, HOWEVER,

WOULD BE A MILITARY STRIKE. IF THE USG WERE TO INITIATE A

BOMBING CAMPAIGN IN IRAQ, THE VATICAN WOULD MOVE FROM ITS

FIRM SUPPORT OF WHAT WE HAVE DONE SO FAR IN AFGHANISTAN TO

VIGOROUS AND PUBLIC OPPOSITION. END COMMENT.
