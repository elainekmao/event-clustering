SUBJECT: (SBU) ANOTHER SHOOTING NON-INCIDENT

Classified By: (U) AMBASSADOR RICHARD H. JONES; REASON 1.5 (a, d)

1. (C) On the morning of March 5, a USMC convoy from Camp

Commando had a 7-ton vehicle break down just past the on-ramp

onto Highway 80. Convoy personnel heard shots impacting

nearby. The convoy commander crossed the highway to find out

where the fire originated; he found some Kuwaiti officers who

told him that some Kuwaiti soldiers were firing on a rifle

range on a Kuwaiti military camp. The range was oriented

perpendicular to Hwy 80. Contrary to earlier reports, the

Marines did not return fire. No injuries were reported.

2. (C) The initial report was that Marines in a ccnvoy were

fired upon, that the Marines returned fire, that the

attackers fled, and that the Marines pursued them to a mosque

in which the attackers took refuge. The on-scene commander

assures that the Marines did not shoot and that there is no

truth whatsoever to the mosque story.

3. (U) Suggested press guidance follows, for use on an

if-asked basis; it has been coordinated with CFLCC PAO:

On March 5, US Marines who had stopped near a Kuwaiti rifle

range heard gunshots from the range. There was no exchange

of fire, and no injuries or damage.
