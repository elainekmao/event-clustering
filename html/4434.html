NOMINATING-OFFICER: Stephen Holgate

START MEMO NOMINATING-JUSTIFICATION:

Mr. Izzadeen at present is the Deputy Editor of "The

Sunday Times," a publication of the Wijaya

Newspapers Ltd. and works also for the Wijaya

Group\'s daily paper the "Daily Mirror." He is most

known for his Friday column on world affairs for the

"Daily Mirror," but writes also for "The Sunday

Times" on a variety of local and international

issues. He supervises the Subs Desk where more than

30 sub editors do do work for the two newspapers.

In his Friday column, he analyzes and comments on

world issues from a Third World perspective. His

interest in writing about international issues,

particularly as they touch on his deeply held

Islamic identity, distinguishes him from other

writers on this leading daily.

He is an ideal candidate for the SA/RP "Islam in

America." In participating in this project, Mr.

Izzadeen will get an opportunity of observing the

freedom with which Islam is practiced in America.

Also, he would benefit much from a visit at this

time to introduce him to U.S. foreign-policy making

and to the professionalism of American columnists

and their standards of accuracy and moderate tone.

END MEMO NOMINATING-JUSTIFICATION:

NOMINATING-OFFICER: Joe Novak

START MEMO NOMINATING-JUSTIFICATION:

Mr. Nihmath is a moderate Muslim from the Puttalam

region of Sri Lanka. Over the past year, Puttalam

has been the site of pro-Osama Bin Laden and anti-US

rallies. Mission\'s dealings with Mr. Nihmath have

focused primarily on the IDP issues he deals with as

Program Director of the Community Trust Fund. As a

Muslim with expertise in the human rights field in

Puttalam and as a person dealing with many Muslims

that have misperceptions of the United States and

its relationship to Islam, he can be a moderating

influence in a community that has been volatile in

the past. He is an ideal candidate for a program

involving Islam in America.

END MEMO NOMINATING-JUSTIFICATION:
