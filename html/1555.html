SUBJECT: DISSIDENT FATHER NGUYEN VAN LY AT HIS CHURCH UNDER VIRTUAL HOUSE ARREST

(U) CLASSIFIED BY POLCOUNS JAMES BRUNO,

REASONS: 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) SUMMARY: DISSIDENT CATHOLIC PRIEST NGUYEN VAN

LY IS UNABLE TO LEAVE HIS CHURCH COMPOUND IN HUE UNDER

CONDITIONS RESEMBLING HOUSE ARREST. A PARISH PRIEST

IN HO CHI MINH CITY TOLD VISITING REP. SENSENBRENNER

FEBRUARY 24 THAT FATHER LY HAS BEEN HELD UNDER "HOUSE

ARREST" SINCE HE SUBMITTED WRITTEN TESTIMONY HIGHLY

CRITICAL OF THE VIETNAMESE GOVERNMENT TO THE U.S.

COMMISSION ON INTERNATIONAL RELIGIOUS FREEDOM IN EARLY

FEBRUARY. SEPARATELY, VICE FOREIGN MINISTER BIN SAID

LOCAL OFFICIALS REPORT LY HAD "VIOLATED THE LAW" AND

SHOULD BE PROSECUTED ACCORDING TO VIETNAM\'S CRIMINAL

CODE. HOWEVER, BIN SAID OFFICIALS ARE INSTEAD

APPLYING "ADMINISTRATIVE MEASURES" TO RESTRICT HIS

MOVEMENTS, AND HE CLAIMED LY IS STILL ABLE TO FUNCTION

AS A PRIEST. EMBASSY AND CONSULATE CONTINUE TO URGE

THAT LY BE ALLOWED TO CARRY OUT HIS DUTIES PEACEFULLY

WITHOUT GOVERNMENT INTERFERENCE. END SUMMARY.

HOUSE ARREST IN HUE

-------------------

2. (C) REPRESENTATIVE F. JAMES SENSENBRENNER,

ACCOMPANIED BY HCMC CONGEN POLOFF AND CONGEN

INTERPRETER, MET FATHER FRANCIS XAVIER LE VAN NHAC AT

CHO QUAN CATHOLIC CHURCH ON SATURDAY, FEBRUARY 24, IN

HCMC\'S DISTRICT 5. FATHER NHAC IS THE SENIOR OF TWO

PRIESTS AT THE CHURCH, WHICH IS THE OLDEST CATHOLIC

CHURCH IN HCMC AND THE LARGEST PARISH IN CHOLON.

POLICE AND REPRESENTATIVES FROM MFA, MPS, AND THE

NATIONAL ASSEMBLY ESCORTED THE CONGRESSMAN INTO THE

CHURCH COMPOUND, BUT THE ESCORTS DID NOT ENTER THE

MEETING ROOM.

3. (C) REPRESENTATIVE SENSENBRENNER\'S FIRST QUESTION

WAS TO ASK WHETHER FATHER NHAC HAD ANY INFORMATION

PERTAINING TO THE SAFETY AND CONDITION OF FATHER

NGUYEN VAN LY IN HUE AFTER LY\'S WRITTEN TESTIMONY TO

THE COMMISSION ON FREEDOM OF RELIGION WAS MADE PUBLIC,

NOTING THAT THE U.S. MISSION HAD TRIED UNSUCCESSFULLY

TO REACH FATHER LY BY PHONE.

4. (C) IN A LOWERED VOICE, FATHER NHAC SAID THAT FOR

THE PAST TWO DAYS, THE HCMC POLICE HAD SURROUNDED THE

CHO QUAN CHURCH. TWICE THEY ENTERED THE COMPOUND TO

WARN HIM NOT TO TALK WITH THE CONGRESSMAN ABOUT FATHER

LY\'S PROBLEMS IN HUE. HE THEN PROCEEDED TO SAY THAT

FATHER LY IS UNDER HOUSE ARREST AT HIS HOME CHURCH IN

HUE. THE CHURCH IN HUE IS SURROUNDED BY POLICE, AND

FATHER LY IS NOT ALLOWED TO LEAVE THE COMPOUND.

POLICE ALSO CUT FATHER LY\'S PHONE COMMUNICATIONS.

FATHER NHAC SAID THAT AS FAR AS HE KNEW, FATHER LY

COULD PERFORM HIS PRIESTLY DUTIES, BUT HE DIDN\'T KNOW

HOW MANY, IF ANY, OF HIS PARISHIONERS ARE COMING TO

MASS.

ON TO DISCUSS RELIGIOUS FREEDOM AND CATHOLICISM IN

MORE GENERAL TERMS, INCLUDING PROBLEMS WITH

APPOINTMENT OF BISHOPS AND GOVERNMENT LIMITATIONS ON

NUMBERS OF INDIVIDUALS ALLOWED TO ENTER THE SEMINARY.

ON THE POSITIVE SIDE, FATHER NHAC SAID FOR THE PAST 5-

6 YEARS, HCMC GOVERNMENT HAS ALLOWED THE CHURCH TO

OPEN AND OPERATE KINDERGARTENS IN THE CITY. ALSO,

THERE ARE NO LONGER ANY RESTRICTIONS IN HCMC ON

SERVES AS CLASSROOMS TO ABOUT 500 SUNDAY SCHOOL

STUDENTS.

6. (C) FATHER NHAC SAID THAT AT CHO QUAN, PEOPLE WERE

STILL COMING TO CHURCH DESPITE THE HEAVY POLICE

PRESENCE OUTSIDE. (HE POINTED TO ABOUT 30 PERSONS

LINED UP AT THE BACK OF THE CHURCH TO GO TO

CONFESSION.) BUT HE WAS VISIBLY NERVOUS AND SAID HE

WAS CERTAIN TO BE CALLED IN FOR POLICE QUESTIONING

LATER THAT DAY OR THE NEXT.
