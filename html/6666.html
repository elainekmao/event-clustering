SUBJECT: KUWAIT OIL UPDATE -- MARCH 22, 2003

REF: KUWAIT 1020 AND PREVIOUS

Classified By: DCM Frank C. Urbancic for reasons 1.5 (B) and (D)

1. (C) A senior KPC official told EconChief that the Kuwait

Oil Company Firefighting team has been dispatched to Northern

Kuwait to assess the situation and prepare to assist in

fighting fires in Iraq\'s Rumaila field. Embassy understands

that KOC Chairman Ahmad Al-Arbeed acted on instructions from

Kuwait\'s acting Oil Minister Sheikh Ahmed Fahed Al-Ahmad

Al-Sabah in dispatching the team to the area at 0800 this

morning. Arbeed and the Oil Minister are also traveling to

the North today to assess the situation. The same KPC

official noted that crude from the leaking and burning wells

is encroaching on the Iraq-Kuwait border.

3.(C) Explanatory Comments:

Wafra Joint Operations refer to onshore concessions in

Saudi-Kuwait divided zone operated by Saudi Arabian Texaco

(SAT). R/B refers to the two producing reservoirs -- Ratawi

and Burgan.

KOC is Kuwait Oil Company

KNPC is Kuwait National Petroleum Corporation

KGOC is the Kuwaiti successor to Arabian Oil Company (AOC),

which previously operated a concession in the offshore

portions of the Saudi-Kuwait divided zone -- AOC still

operates the fields under a service agreement.

KEC refers to Kuwait Export Crude, the country\'s export

blend. Salt PTB refers to pounds of salt per thousand

barrels of crude oil.
