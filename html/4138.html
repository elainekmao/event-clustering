SUBJECT: ITALY-LIBYA: PM BERLUSCONI\'S OCT. 28 VISIT TO

TRIPOLI

REF: A. FBIS RESTON 282336ZOCT02

B. STATE 211463

C. ROME 5185

CLASSIFIED BY: POL MC THOMAS COUNTRYMAN, FOR REASONS 1/5 (B) AND (D)

1. (C) SUMMARY: ITALIAN MFA SAYS THAT PM BERLUSCONI CONVEYED

TO QADHAFI, AT OUR REQUEST, A STRONG MESSAGE ON THE NEED FOR

LIBYA TO COMPLY FULLY WITH THE SECURITY COUNCIL RESOLUTIONS

REGARDING LOCKERBIE. THE TWO SIDES FAILED TO REACH AGREEMENT

ON A "COMPENSATION" PACKAGE THAT WOULD MAKE LIBYA DROP ITS

COMPLAINTS ABOUT COLONIAL ITALIAN "CRIMES" AGAINST LIBYA.

THE ATMOSPHERE WAS VERY POSITIVE, THOUGH FEW SPECIFIC

AGREEMENTS WERE REACHED IN THE MANY AREAS OF COOPERATION

DISCUSSED. THE ITALIAN DELEGATION FOUND QADHAFI CALM AND

MODERATE IN HIS DISCUSSION OF IRAQ AND THE

ISRAELI-PALESTINIAN ISSUE. END SUMMARY

2. (U) PRIME MINISTER BERLUSCONI\'S OCTOBER 28 ONE-DAY VISIT

TO LIBYA WAS ONLY THE THIRD BY AN ITALIAN PRIME MINISTER

SINCE QADHAFI CAME TO POWER. UNLIKE PM D\'ALEMA IN 1999, WHO

WAS FORCED TO TOUR THE NATIONAL MUSEUM FOR THREE HOURS UNTIL

QADHAFI WAS READY TO RECEIVE HIM, BERLUSCONI HAD A SMOOTH

VISIT, INCLUDING TWO MEETINGS WITH QADHAFI AT BAB AL AZIZIA

BARRACKS, AND A WORKING LUNCH WITH PRIME MINISTER MUBARAK

SHAMAKH. REF A CONTAINS THE TEXT OF THE JOINT COMMUNIQUE

RELEASED BY THE TWO SIDES.

3. (C) THE MFA\'S NORTH AFRICA OFFICE DIRECTOR, ANTONIO

BELLAVIA (WHO WENT ON THE TRIP BUT WAS NOT IN THE TWO

MEETINGS WITH QADHAFI) BRIEFED POL MINCOUNS ON OCTOBER 30.

BELLAVIA SAID THAT BERLUSCONI HAD PRESSED THE LIBYAN LEADER

ON THE NEED TO COMPLY FULLY WITH THE UN RESOLUTIONS, AS

REQUESTED REF B. BERLUSCONI HAD TRIED TO SPIN THIS

POSITIVELY WITH QADHAFI, EMPHASIZING THE POSITIVE RETURN TO

TRIPOLI FROM BEING ABLE TO NORMALIZE ITS RELATIONS NOT ONLY

WITH EUROPE, BUT WITH THE US. ACCORDING TO BELLAVIA, THE

LIBYAN LEADER WAS RECEPTIVE TO THE MESSAGE AND EMPHASIZED HIS

READINESS TO MEET ALL OF LIBYA\'S OBLIGATIONS AND PUT

LOCKERBIE BEHIND THEM. THE ITALIAN DELEGATION GOT THE

IMPRESSION THAT THE ISSUE OF COMPENSATION WAS FAR LESS

DIFFICULT FOR THE GOL THAN THE ACKNOWLEDGMENT OF

RESPONSIBILITY. FINDING THE RIGHT FORMULATION, BELLAVIA

COMMENTED, WOULD REQUIRE REAL ART, BUT THIS WAS NOT DISCUSSED

WITH THE LIBYANS. POL MC ASKED ABOUT THE ITALIAN PRESS

REPORTS THAT BERLUSCONI HAD OFFERED HIS GOOD OFFICES WITH THE

USG. BELLAVIA SAID THIS WAS A PRESS INVENTION.

4. (C) AS NOTED REF C, THE GOI HAD HOPED THAT DURING THIS

VISIT IT COULD FINALLY CLOSE LIBYA\'S LONG-STANDING DEMAND FOR

"COMPENSATION" FOR COLONIAL-ERA WRONGS BY ANNOUNCING THE

FUNDING OF AN ORTHOPEDIC TREATMENT AND RESEARCH HOSPITAL IN

BENGHAZI. HOWEVER, THE LIBYAN SIDE RESURRECTED ITS

PREFERENCE FOR A MORE EXPENSIVE PROJECT, A 60-MILLION EURO

NORTH-SOUTH HIGHWAY. THE TWO SIDES AGREED TO CONTINUE TO

STUDY BOTH PROJECTS. BELLAVIA SAID THE ITALIANS WILL

PROBABLY KEEP THEIR PREFERENCE FOR THE HOSPITAL AS LESS

EXPENSIVE AND MORE USEFUL.

5. (C) BERLUSCONI ASKED QADHAFI ABOUT HIS INTENTION TO

WITHDRAW FROM THE ARAB LEAGUE. QADHAFI RESPONDED WITH A

LITANY OF THE LEAGUE\'S FAILURES TO ADDRESS THE REAL PROBLEMS

OF THE REGION, FROM PALESTINE TO IRAQ. THE ITALIAN

DELEGATION FOUND QADHAFI\'S RHETORIC TO BE CALMER THAN IN THE

PAST. FOR EXAMPLE, WHILE CRITICAL OF THE US INTENTION TO USE

FORCE AGAINST IRAQ, HE MADE NO APOLOGIES FOR SADDAM AND

INSISTED THAT BAGHDAD HAD NO CHOICE BUT TO COMPLY FULLY WITH

THE DISARMAMENT RESOLUTIONS. QADHAFI WAS ALSO CRITICAL OF

THE BARCELONA PROCESS, SAYING THAT THIS DIALOGUE BETWEEN THE

EU AND NORTH AFRICAN STATES DISTRACTED FROM WHAT HE

CONSIDERED A MORE IMPORTANT DIALOGUE, THAT BETWEEN THE EU AND

THE OAU. STILL, HE AGREED THAT THE "5 ON 5" DIALOGUE COULD

BE USEFUL FOR BUILDING COOPERATION AGAINST TERRORISM,

ORGANIZED CRIME, AND ILLEGAL IMMIGRATION. BELLAVIA SAID THAT

THESE LAST ISSUES WERE DISCUSSED IN GENERAL TERMS, BUT NO

SPECIFIC AGREEMENT WAS REACHED. (NOTE: SOME MEMBERS OF

PARLIAMENT HAD PUBLICLY URGED BERLUSCONI BEFORE HIS TRIP TO

PRESS LIBYA TO PROMISE TO ACT AGAINST ALIEN SMUGGLERS

OPERATING FROM LIBYAN PORTS, WHO HAVE BECOME A MUCH GREATER

PROBLEM FOR ITALY THAN THOSE OPERATING FROM THE ALBANIAN

SHORE.)

6. (C) BELLAVIA SAID THE OVERALL ATMOSPHERE WAS VERY WARM

BETWEEN THE TWO LEADERS. BERLUSCONI WENT OUT OF HIS WAY WITH

THE PRESS TO PRAISE QADHAFI\'S EXPERIENCE AND THE POTENTIAL

FOR IMPROVED ECONOMIC AND POLITICAL COOPERATION. THE TWO

7. (U) PRESS ACCOUNTS ADD A FEW FACTOIDS TO THOSE PROVIDED BY

THE MFA:

-- NICE PRESENTS: BERLUSCONI PROMISED TO RETURN TO LIBYA IN

THE IMMEDIATE FUTURE AN ANCIENT ROMAN STATUE, THE "VENUS OF

CYRENE" TAKEN BY ITALIAN FASCIST TROOPS. QADHAFI GAVE HIM AN

ITALIAN MUSKET CAPTURED BY THE LIBYANS IN 1924.

-- NO SPECIFIC AGREEMENTS, BUT MUCH DISCUSSION OF LIBYA

UNBLOCKING CREDITS WORTH APPROXIMATELY 877 MILLION EUROS FOR

ITALIAN COMPANIES DOING BUSINESS IN LIBYA, AND ADDITIONAL

ITALIAN TECHNICAL HELP IN DEMINING IN LIBYA.

-- BERLUSCONI REPORTEDLY STATED THAT ITALY DEPENDS ON LIBYA

FOR SOME 25 PERCENT OF ITS ENERGY NEEDS, AND ADDED THAT THIS

WOULD INCREASE TO AROUND 30 PERCENT ONCE A LIBYA-ITALY GAS

PIPELINE COMES INTO OPERATION IN 2004.

-- QADHAFI REPORTEDLY PROMISED TO GRANT VISAS TO THOSE

ITALIANS WHO HE HAD EXPELLED IN 1970. (COMMENT: THESE

PARTICULAR ITALIAN CITIZENS ARE FEELING THE IRONY OF THE

DIFFICULTY THEY NOW HAVE IN GETTING US VISAS BECAUSE OF THEIR

BIRTH IN LIBYA. WE RECENTLY MET WITH ONE JEWISH-ITALIAN BORN

IN LIBYA WHO WAS RECENTLY ALLOWED TO VISIT TRIPOLI AND A

GREAT-AUNT WHO HE THOUGHT WAS VIRTUALLY THE LAST JEWISH

PERSON RESIDING IN LIBYA).
