SUBJECT: PM ON COHABITATION: "NOT LOOKING GOOD"

Classified By: CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR E. ASHLEY WILLS. REASONS 1.5 B

, D.

1. (C) SUMMARY: PM WICKREMISINGHE TOLD ME LAST NIGHT

(AUGUST 13) THAT COHABITATION TENSIONS REMAIN HIGH AND THAT

PROSPECTS FOR IMPROVEMENT ARE "NOT LOOKING GOOD". HE

PROFESSED TO BE SERIOUSLY CONCERNED ABOUT THE PRESIDENT\'S

MENTAL BALANCE. WHEN TOLD THAT SHE WILL BE COMING TO

JEFFERSON HOUSE TONIGHT FOR DINNER, HE ASKED ME TO CALL HIM

AFTERWARDS TO GIVE HIM MY OPINION OF HER FRAME OF MIND. I

SAID I WOULD DO SO. IN THE LAST FEW DAYS, SEVERAL VIP\'S IN

GOVERNMENT AND BUSINESS HAVE SUGGESTED THAT ONLY WE CAN

BROKER A DEAL BETWEEN THE PM AND PRESIDENT, AN IDEA I HAVE

RESOLUTELY DISMISSED. END SUMMARY.

COHABITATION

------------

2. (C) AT MINISTER GL PEIRIS\'S 56TH BIRTHDAY PARTY LAST

NIGHT (AUGUST 13), I HAD A FIVE-MINUTE CONVERSATION WITH PM

WICKREMISINGHE. IN RESPONSE TO MY QUESTION, HE SAID

COHABITATION WAS NOT GOING WELL AND THAT THE PROSPECTS ARE

"NOT LOOKING GOOD". HE STRESSED TWICE THAT HE IS READY TO

TRY TO MAKE IT WORK BUT DOUBTS CBK FEELS RECIPROCALLY. HE

CONFIRMED THAT FORMER FM KADIRGAMAR HAS BEEN TRYING TO BROKER

A DEAL BETWEEN HIM AND THE PRESIDENT BUT WITHOUT SUCCESS.

KADIRGAMAR TOLD HIM FRIDAY THAT HE WOULD ARRANGE A

FACE-TO-FACE MEETING BETWEEN THE TWO PRINCIPALS BUT HAD SO

FAR FAILED TO DO SO. HE HAD CHECKED WITH KADIRGAMAR TWICE

OVER THE WEEKEND AND AGAIN YESTERDAY AND LEARNED THAT HE

(KADIRGAMAR) HAD NOT EVEN BEEN ABLE TO TALK TO HER YET. SHE

HAS NOT RETURNED EVEN HER FORMER FOREIGN MINISTER\'S CALLS.

3. (C) THE PM PROFESSED TO BE SERIOUSLY WORRIED ABOUT HER

MENTAL BALANCE. AMONG OTHER REASONS FOR HIS WORRY IN THIS

REGARD, HE ASSESSED THAT HER FLIRTING WITH THE MAOIST JVP,

WIDELY REPORTED IN THE LOCAL MEDIA IN THE LAST FEW DAYS, NOT

ONLY WENT AGAINST THE NATION\'S INTEREST BUT HER OWN PARTY\'S

AS WELL. (WE AGREE). GL PEIRIS, WHO WAS STANDING WITH US,

THEN MENTIONED THAT THE PRESIDENT WILL BE COMING TO DINNER

TONIGHT AT JEFFERSON HOUSE. THE PM BRIGHTENED. HE ASKED

WHOM ELSE I HAD INVITED (I TOLD HIM CBK HAD INFORMED ME THAT

I SHOULD INVITE HER BROTHER, HER SISTER AND HER SON, A

20-YEAR OLD VET STUDENT IN THE UK; MY WIFE AND 20-YEAR OLD

DAUGHTER, WHO WRONGLY SUSPECTS A MATCHMAKING ATTEMPT ON HER

FATHER\'S OR THE PRESIDENT\'S PART, AND EMMA -- MY NON-GOLDEN,

NON-RETRIEVER -- WILL JOIN ME ON THE US SIDE, AGAIN A

PRESIDENTIAL STIPULATION). HE THEN ASKED WHETHER I WOULD

CALL HIM AFTER THE DINNER OR TOMORROW WITH MY ASSESSMENT OF

HER MOOD. I SAID I WOULD.

4. (C) AS THE CONVERSATION ENDED, THE PM AND PEIRIS

EXPRESSED CONFIDENCE THAT WHETHER OR NOT THE PRESIDENT GOES

ALONG, THE GOVERNMENT NOW BELIEVES IT HAS ENOUGH VOTES TO

PASS A CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENT LIMITING HER POWERS TO

DISMISS PARLIAMENT. THIS HAS BEEN THE KEY ISSUE IN

COHABITATION FOR THE UNF BECAUSE IT FEARS CBK WILL PROROGUE

PARLIAMENT AT HER EARLIEST CONVENIENCE SOMETIME AFTER

DECEMBER 6. THAT DATE CONSTITUTES THE ONE YEAR ANNIVERSARY

OF THE SITTING OF THIS PARLIAMENT; ACCORDING TO THE SRI

LANKAN CONSTITUTION, THE PRESIDENT CAN DISMISS PARLIAMENT

AFTER IT HAS SAT ONE YEAR REGARDLESS OF WHETHER THE PM, HIS

CABINET OR A MAJORITY OF MP\'S WOULD AGREE. FOR THE SAKE OF

HIS GOVERNMENT\'S STABILITY AND LONGEVITY, RANIL WANTS EITHER

TO GET HER WRITTEN, SIGNED COMMITMENT NOT TO EXERCISE THIS

POWER FOR A CERTAIN PERIOD OR TO AMEND THE CONSTITUTION TO

ELIMINATE HER ABILITY IN THIS AREA EXCEPT IN VERY SPECIAL

CIRCUMSTANCES. OR BOTH. THE MEDIA THIS MORNING ALSO REPORT

THAT THE GOVERNMENT FEELS THAT IT HAS THE VOTES TO PASS THE

AMENDMENT (A TWO-THIRDS MAJORITY WOULD BE NEEDED TO PASS IT

SO THERE WILL HAVE TO BE ROUGHLY 20 DEFECTIONS FROM CBK\'S PA

TO REACH THE MAGIC FIGURE OF 150

VOTES).

US ROLE? NO WAY

----------------

5. (C) OVER THE LAST FEW DAYS, WE HAVE HEARD FROM SEVERAL

IMPORTANT FIGURES IN GOVERNMENT AND BUSINESS THAT A US ROLE

WILL IN THE END BE NECESSARY TO BROKER A DEAL BETWEEN RANIL

AND CHANDRIKA. SUCH IS THE EXASPERATION PEOPLE FEEL WITH

COHABITATION TENSIONS WHEN THE NATION\'S FUTURE IS AT STAKE,

SUCH IS A CERTAIN CULTURAL INCLINATION TO LOOK TO OTHERS TO

SOLVE SRI LANKA\'S PROBLEMS, AND SUCH IS THE CONFIDENCE IN THE

US THAT THIS IDEA HAS ACQUIRED TOO MUCH CURRENCY. TWO UNF

MINISTERS, AN EDITOR AND SEVERAL BUSINESS EXECUTIVES

APPROACHED ME LAST NIGHT ALONG THESE LINES. I RESOLUTELY

DISMISSED THE IDEA. MY LINE IS THAT THIS MAY CONSTITUTE A

"DAMNED IF WE DO, DAMNED IF WE DON\'T" SITUATION, AND IF SO WE

PREFER TO BE DAMNED FOR NOT BUTTING IN TO THAT EXTENT. IT

WOULD BE INTERVENTIONIST, I SAY, AND IT WOULD LEAD

INEVITABLY TO OUR BROKERING EVERY TIME DISAGREEMENTS ERUPT.

BESIDES, I POINT OUT, A DEAL, IF ONE IS TO BE DONE, MUST

ARISE ORGANICALLY FROM THE PRINCIPALS IF IT IS TO LAST. I

NEVERTHELESS REPORT THE FOREGOING BECAUSE IT SHOWS THE

FRUSTRATION AND EVEN DESPERATION MANY PEOPLE FEEL.

COMMENT

-------

SAY I FACE TONIGHT\'S DINNER WITH TREPIDATION. THE PRESIDENT

COULD WELL BE IN A BAD MOOD. AND HER BRAT BROTHER ANURA HAS

BEEN MAKING VERY IRRESPONSIBLE STATEMENTS LATELY, INCLUDING

ONE IN WHICH HE ACCUSED PRESIDENT BUSH OF HAVING "STOLEN"

2000\'S ELECTION. I PREDICT MY SELF-CONTROL WILL BE TESTED.

SO WILL EMMA\'S. SHE SNIFFS OUT INSINCERITY IN A JIFFY. (CAN
