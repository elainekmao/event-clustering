1. (C) SUMMARY: THE ALBANIAN, CROATIAN AND MACEDONIAN FOREIGN

MINISTERS MET BRIEFLY IN DUBROVNIK MARCH 7 TO CONFIRM THE

"ADRIATIC CHARTER" DRAFT TEXT (ALREADY WITH DEPARTMENT),

ISSUE A DECLARATION AND GIVE THE INITIATIVE HIGHER PUBLIC

PROFILE. AMBASSADOR ROSSIN ATTENDED FOR THE USG AT FM

PICULA\'S INVITATION AND PARTICIPATED IN THE PRESS CONFERENCE

AFTERWARD. TEXT OF MINISTERS\' DECLARATION, WITH REASONABLY

FIRM IRAQ LANGUAGE, AT PARA 9 (FAXED TO EUR/SCE). WE CREDIT

ALBANIAN FM META FOR ENSURING THE IRAQ LANGUAGE WAS INCLUDED

IN THE FINAL TEXT. ALTHOUGH THE CROATIANS POSE LESS

RESISTANCE THAN AT PREVIOUS A3 MEETINGS, AMBASSADOR OVERHEARD

THE CROATIAN LEAD OFFICIALS TRYING TO MINIMIZE IT.

2. (C) IN THE CONFERENCE AND AT THE PRESS EVENT, ALL THREE

MINISTERS STRESSED THE IMPORTANCE OF, AND THEIR GRATITUDE

FOR, USG "PARTNERSHIP" IN ADVANCING THEIR NATO ASPIRATIONS,

AND UNDERSCORED THE IMPORTANCE OF THE ADRIATIC CHARTER AS

PROOF POSITIVE OF OUR COMMITMENT. ALL LOOKED FORWARD TO

SIGNING WITH SECRETARY POWELL IN WASHINGTON. PER REF A,

AMBASSADOR TOLD THE MINISTERS THAT A SIGNING CEREMONY IN

MARCH WAS UNLIKELY AND NOTED THAT THE CHARTER TEXT WAS UNDER

REVIEW IN WASHINGTON, WITH SOME WORDING CHANGES POSSIBLE

SOON. THE MINISTERS UNDERSTOOD THAT MARCH WAS HARD TO

PROGRAM BUT URGED THAT SIGNING NOT BE ALLOWED TO SLIP TOO

LONG. PER REF B, DEPARTMENT\'S PROPOSED CHANGES IN CHARTER

WORDING SHOULD BE PROVIDED TO THE THREE WASHINGTON

AMBASSADORS FOR A3 CONSIDERATION. END SUMMARY.

3. (C) THE FOREIGN MINISTERS A3 MEETING MARCH 7 IN DUBROVNIK

LASTED 45 MINUTES, WITH AMBASSADOR ROSSIN INVITED TO JOIN IN

ALL BUT THE FIRST TEN MINUTES. DISCUSSION DURING THE

AMBASSADOR\'S PRESENCE WAS RATHER PERFUNCTORY (THE DECLARATION

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 ZAGREB 00523 01 OF 02 111302Z

AND DRAFT CHARTER HAVING BEEN COMPLETED BEFOREHAND) WITH THE

EXCEPTION OF MINOR TENSION WHEN ALBANIAN FM META COMPLAINED

TO CROATIAN FM PICULA ABOUT TRAVEL RESTRICTIONS ON ALBANIANS

COMING TO CROATIA. PICULA DWELT ON CROATIA\'S NATO AND EU

ASPIRATIONS AND PROGRESS AND STRESSED ITS INTEREST IN

REGIONAL COOPERATION. MACEDONIAN FM MITREVA EMPHASIZED

PRACTICAL COOPERATION, MENTIONED IN THE ADRIATIC CHARTER, ON

ISSUES SUCH AS BORDER SECURITY, TRAFFICKING AND ECONOMIC

COOPERATION. SHE TOUTED THE MAY BORDER CONFERENCE IN OHRID

(SKOPJE 313).

4. (C) META SIMILARLY STRESSED ALBANIA\'S INTEREST IN REGIONAL

COOPERATION BUT ALSO NOTED THE NEED FOR DEVELOPMENT

ASSISTANCE SO THESE COUNTRIES COULD ATTAIN NATO AND EU

STANDARDS IN A REASONABLE TIME. META EMPHASIZED AT LENGTH

ALBANIAN SOLIDARITY WITH THE U.S. ON IRAQ, COMPARING SADDAM\'S

REGIME WITH THE SOCIALIST ISOLATION HIS COUNTRY SUFFERED FOR

50 YEARS. ALL THREE MINISTERS WENT OUT OF THEIR WAY TO

UNDERSCORE THEIR APPRECIATION FOR USG SUPPORT, NOT ONLY ON

THE ADRIATIC CHARTER, THE OPEN DOOR AND THEIR NATO

CANDIDACIES, BUT ALSO ON THE FULL RANGE OF THEIR ASPIRATIONS

AND CHALLENGES.

5. (C) AMBASSADOR EMPHASIZED IN HIS TURN U.S. COMMITMENT TO

THE OPEN DOOR AND TO ASSIST THE THREE COUNTRIES IN MAKING

THEIR NATO ASPIRATIONS REAL THROUGH THEIR MAP PROGRAMS.

WHILE EACH COUNTRY FACED DIFFERENT DEFENSE REFORMS,

INTEROPERABILITY AND CIVIL SOCIETY CHALLENGES TO QUALIFY FOR

INVITATIONS, NATO WAS ALL ABOUT COLLABORATION, AND THEREFORE

WE APPLAUDED THEIR INITIATIVE IN TEAMING TOGETHER THROUGH

THIS ADRIATIC PROCESS, NOT JUST WITH HORTATORY LANGUAGE BUT

CONFIDENTIAL

WITH CONCRETE PLANS. AMBASSADOR TOLD THE MINISTERS THAT THE

DRAFT CHARTER TEXT WAS BEING REVIEWED IN WASHINGTON AND WE

APPLAUDED ITS OVERALL TONE AND PROVISIONS; WE EXPECTED TO

PROPOSE SOME MINOR WORDING CHANGES IN DUE COURSE. ALTHOUGH

WE WERE NOT ABLE TO CONFIRM A WASHINGTON SIGNING DATE YET AND

MARCH LOOKED UNLIKELY, WE WOULD BE IN TOUCH SOON AND REMAINED

COMMITTED TO AN EARLY SIGNING CEREMONY.

6. (C) FINALLY, WHILE NOTING THAT THE USG WOULD WELCOME THE

ADRIATIC INITIATIVE AND DUBROVNIK DECLARATION AS A WHOLE, WE

ESPECIALLY APPRECIATED THE DECLARATION\'S REITERATION OF

IRAQ\'S OBLIGATION TO COMPLY WITH UNSCR 1441 "OR FACE THE

CONSEQUENCES." SUCH SOLIDARITY WAS ESSENTIAL TO MAXIMIZE

PRESSURE ON SADDAM TO COMPLY AND AVOID THE NEED FOR USE OF

FORCE TO DISARM IRAQ.

7. (C) THE MINISTERS UNDERSTOOD THE UNCERTAINTY IN SCHEDULING

THE WASHINGTON SIGNING CEREMONY BUT URGED THAT IT NOT BE

DELAYED TOO LONG. MACEDONIAN FM MITREVA WAS STRONGEST ON

THIS POINT; SHE INDICATED SHE HAD HEARD THAT DELAY UNTIL MAY

WAS BEING CONSIDERED AND SAID THAT WOULD DISSIPATE THE

PROGRESS MADE BY THE THREE. AMBASSADOR ASSURED HER WE WOULD

MAKE EVERY EFFORT TO SCHEDULE THE SIGNING CEREMONY AS SOON AS

POSSIBLE AND DISCOUNTED WHAT SHE HAD HEARD.

AMBASSADOR ECHOED THEMES FROM THE MINISTERIAL; ALL THE

MINISTERS THANKED THE USG PUBLICLY FOR ITS "PARTNERSHIP" IN

THE A3 ENTERPRISE. AS IN PRIVATE, FM META WAS STRONGEST ON

IRAQ. IN TYPICAL CROATIAN FASHION, ONLY ONE JOURNALIST ASKED

A QUESTION, WHICH PICULA HANDLED WELL. IN COMMENTS AFTERWARD

TO CROATIAN JOURNALISTS, PICULA SPUN THE DECLARATION TO

9. (U) TEXT OF DECLARATION:

"DECLARATION OF THE MINISTERS OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS OF THE

REPUBLIC OF ALBANIA, THE REPUBLIC OF CROATIA AND THE REPUBLIC

OF MACEDONIA"

DUBROVNIK, MARCH 7, 2004

WE, THE MINISTERS OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS OF THE REPUBLIC OF

ALBANIA, THE REPUBLIC OF CROATIA, AND THE REPUBLIC OF

MACEDONIA MET TODAY IN DUBROVNIK TO REAFFIRM OUR COMMITMENT

TO ACHIEVE FULL MEMBERSHIP IN NATO. IN THAT REGARD, WE LOOK

FORWARD TO THE SIGNING OF THE CHARTER OF PARTNERSHIP AMONG

THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA AND OUR THREE COUNTRIES.

WE SHARE A COMMON VISION OF A FULLY INTEGRATED EUROPE. WE

AFFIRM OUR COMMITMENT TO THE RULE OF LAW, RESPECT OF HUMAN

AND MINORITY RIGHTS AND THE FREE MARKET. WE ALSO UNDERSCORE

OUR FULL COMMITMENT TO THE PRINCIPLES CONTAINED IN THE

CHARTER OF THE UNITED NATIONS AND RESPECTIVE OSCE DOCUMENTS.

WE WARMLY WELCOME THE DECISION OF THE NORTH ATLANTIC COUNCIL

IN PRAGUE IN NOVEMBER 2002 TO INVITE SEVEN EUROPEAN

DEMOCRACIES, OUR VILNIUS GROUP PARTNERS, TO BEG ACCESSION

TALKS WITH NATO. WE ALSO WELCOME NATO,S ANNOUNCEMENT THAT

THESE INVITEES WILL NOT BE THE LAST AND THAT IT WILL CONTINUE

WITH &OPEN DOOR8 POLICY IN ORDER TO FULLY INTEGRATE

ALBANIA, CROATIA AND MACEDONIA INTO THE EUROPEAN AND

TRANSATLANTIC POLITICAL, ECONOMIC, SECURITY AND DEFENSE

INSTITUTIONS.

WE DECLARE OUR COMMITMENT TO THE FULL IMPLEMENTATION OF MAP

AND OUR RESPECTIVE ANP,S IN ORDER TO BETTER PREPARE

OURSELVES FOR FULL NATO MEMBERSHIP. FURTHERMORE, WE SHALL

INTENSIFY OUR ACTIVITIES FOR INCREASING OUR CAPABILITIES TO

MEET NEW SECURITY CHALLENGES AND FOR MODERNIZING OUR ARMED

FORCES.

WE RECONFIRM OUR COUNTRIES, COMMITMENT TO TAKE ACTIVE PART

IN THE INTERNATIONAL COALITION AGAINST TERRORISM AND OUR

SUPPORT OF ALL SECURITY COUNCIL RESOLUTIONS AND OTHER

RELEVANT INTERNATIONAL DOCUMENTS REFERRING TO THE

DESTRUCTION, CONTROL AND NON-PROLIFERATION OF WEAPONS OF MASS

DESTRUCTION. WE TAKE THIS OPPORTUNITY TO CALL UPON IRAQ TO

COMPLY FULLY AND UNCONDITIONALLY WITH THE REQUIREMENTS OF UN

SECURITY COUNCIL RESOLUTION 1441, OR FACE THE CONSEQUENCES.

FOLLOWING THE COMMITMENTS EXPRESSED BY OUR PRESIDENTS AT THE

TIRANA SUMMIT ON FEBRUARY 12, WE AGREE TO INTENSIFY OUR

REGIONAL CO-OPERATION IN ORDER TO ENHANCE PEACE AND STABILITY

IN EUROPE AND WILL USE ALREADY EXISTING BILATERAL AND

MULTILATERAL MECHANISMS. WE REAFFIRM OUR DETERMINATION TO

FURTHER ENHANCE BILATERAL, REGIONAL AND MULTILATERAL

RELATIONS BETWEEN OURSELVES AND BOSNIA-HERZEGOVINA, SERBIA

AND MONTENEGRO AND WITH OTHER NEIGHBORS.

END TEXT.
