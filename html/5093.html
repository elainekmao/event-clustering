SUBJECT: POPE\'S MESSAGE TO DIPLOMATIC CORPS FOCUSES ON WAR,

PEACE AND SOLIDARITY AMONG NATIONS

-------

SUMMARY

-------

1. IN HIS ANNUAL SPEECH TO THE DIPLOMATIC CORPS JANUARY

13, POPE JOHN PAUL II OFFERED HIS PERSPECTIVE ON THE STATE OF

THE WORLD. CITING THE CURRENT "FEAR THAT OFTEN DWELLS IN THE

HEARTS OF OUR CONTEMPORARIES," HE IDENTIFIED THE CAUSES OF

THIS FEAR AS: "INSIDIOUS TERRORISM, UNRESOLVED PROBLEMS IN

THE MIDDLE EAST INCLUDING THE HOLY LAND AND IRAQ, THE TURMOIL

IN SOUTH AMERICA -- ARGENTINA, COLOMBIA AND VENEZUELA --

FAMINE AND CONFLICTS IN AFRICA, PANDEMICS, AND ENVIRONMENTAL

EXPLOITATION. THE POPE SOUGHT TO STRIKE A NOTE OF OPTIMISM

AMIDST THIS LITANY OF WOES, ASSERTING THAT RESPECT FOR LIFE,

"SINKING INTO THE ABYSS." WHILE THE POPE MADE A STRONG

LAST OPTION WITH THE APPROVAL OF THE UN. CONTRARY TO

HEADLINES IN ONE ITALIAN PAPER, THE HOLY SEE TOLD US THEY ARE

NOT PLANNING ON SENDING ENVOYS TO IRAQ AND BAGDAD AT THIS

TIME.

-------------

WAR WITH IRAQ

-------------

2. AS EXPECTED, THE POPE\'S ANNUAL ADDRESS TO THE

DIPLOMATIC CORPS STRONGLY REJECTED WAR, TERRORISM AND ARMED

VIOLENCE SPECIFICALLY IN ISRAEL/PALESTINIAN TERRITORIES AND

IRAQ. WHILE THE MEDIA FOCUS WILL BE ON THE POPE\'S "NO TO

WAR," THE POPE ALSO MAINTAINED THE LINES OF THE FULL HOLY SEE

POSITION ON IRAQ WHICH RECOGNIZES THAT THE DECISION TO WAGE

WAR TO ENSURE THE COMMON GOOD WAS AN OPTION -- EVEN IF A LAST

ONE AND "IN ACCORDANCE WITH VERY STRICT CONDITIONS" (I.E.,

THE HOLY SEE\'S INTERPRETATION OF THE JUST WAR THEORY).

ACCORDING TO THE POPE, ONLY THE UN HAD THE INTERNATIONAL

AUTHORITY TO MAKE A DECISION TO WAGE WAR. BUT HE

SPECIFICALLY UNDERLINED THAT CONFLICTS CAN BE AVOIDED BY

"GOOD WILL, TRUST IN OTHERS, FIDELITY TO COMMITMENTS AND

COOPERATION BETWEEN RESPONSIBLE PARTNERS." HE ALSO

EMPHASIZED THE IMPORTANCE OF COUNTRIES RESPECTING COMMON AND

------------------------

SOLIDARITY AMONG NATIONS

------------------------

3. THE POPE\'S SAID THE WORLD\'S VARIOUS CRISES COULD BE

AVERTED ONLY WITH RESPECT FOR LIFE, INCLUDING THE REJECTION

OF CLONING AND EUTHANASIA, AS WELL AS THE REJECTION OF WAR;

RESPECT FOR LAW AS A FOUNDATION FOR NATIONAL AND

INTERNATIONAL STABILITY; AND THE DUTY OF SOLIDARITY WITH LESS

FORTUNATE PEOPLES AND NATIONS AS A COUNTER TO VIOLENCE. HE

EXHORTED DEVELOPED NATIONS TO SHARE THEIR TECHNOLOGICAL

KNOW-HOW WITH DEVELOPING NATIONS. HERE, HE SPECIFICALLY

CALLED ON THE DEVELOPED WORLD TO PROVIDE ACCESS TO WATER, AND

TO GENERIC MEDICINES TO FIGHT CURRENT PANDEMICS. (COMMENT:

THE HOLY SEE REMAINS CONCERNED ABOUT THE COLLAPSE OF THE WTO

TALKS IN GENEVA LAST MONTH ON TRIPS, AND THEIR IMPLICATIONS

FOR THE POOREST COUNTRIES. END COMMENT.)

---------------------------------------

CHRISTIAN EUROPE AND RESTRICTIVE RUSSIA

---------------------------------------

4. THE POPE USED HIS ADDRESS TO REPEAT THE HOLY SEE\'S CALL

FOR CHRISTIANITY AND RELIGIOUS TRADITIONS TO BE ACKNOWLEDGED

IN A PROPOSED EUROPEAN CONSTITUTION. ACCORDING TO THE

FROM RUSSIAN REGARDING ITS REFUSALS OF VISAS TO FOREIGN

PRIESTS AND BISHOPS.

--------------------------------------------

PLEA FOR GOOD GOVERNANCE AND THE COMMON GOOD

--------------------------------------------

6. A COMMON THREAD OF THE DISCOURSE WAS THE POPE\'S CALL ON

POLITICAL LEADERS TO "SERVE THE COMMON GOOD." HE STRESSED

THAT MORAL CONVICTION WAS ESSENTIAL TO POLITICAL LEADERSHIP

AND CALLED FOR "GOOD GOVERNANCE" THAT WOULD ENSURE MATERIAL

AND SPIRITUAL WELL BEING, REJECT INJUSTICE AND INSTITUTIONAL

CORRUPTION, PROTECT HUMAN FREEDOM AND RIGHTS, AND ENGAGE IN

PUBLIC SERVICE.

-------

COMMENT

-------

7. WHILE MEDIA ARE LIKELY TO FOCUS ON THE "NO TO WAR"

PHRASE, THE POPE\'S DISCOURSE REMAINED BALANCED ON IRAQ AND

THE MIDDLE EAST, AND AVOIDED DIRECT CRITICISM OF THE U.S. IN

FACT, HIS FOCUS ON RESPECT FOR INTERNATIONAL LAW, WHICH, ON

THE ONE HAND, SEEKS TO ENSURE THE U.S. ACTS WITHIN THE UN

FRAMEWORK, ALSO CLEARLY SUGGESTS THAT THE IRAQ\'S AND NORTH

KOREA\'S OF THE WORLD NEED TO FOLLOW RULES OF CONDUCT AND

ADHERE TO AGREEMENTS ALREADY SIGNED. IN LIGHT OF OUR ONGOING

DIALOGUE WITH THE HOLY SEE ON AFRICAN FAMINE, WE WELCOMED THE

PONTIFF\'S REFERENCES TO FAMINE IN AFRICA, AND HIS OPENING THE

DOOR TO "KNOW HOW" IN RESPONDING TO DEVELOPMENT ISSUES. HIS

BLAST AT CONTINUED VISA PROBLEMS FOR CATHOLIC PRIESTS IN

RUSSIA REFLECTS THE SERIOUSNESS WITH WHICH HE VIEWS THIS

PROBLEM, WHICH HE TERMED A "CRISIS" NOT IN KEEPING WITH

RUSSIA\'S DEMOCRATIC ASPIRATIONS. END COMMENT.
