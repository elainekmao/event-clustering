SUBJECT: ITALY FULLY SUPPORTS DRAFT OPTIONAL PROTOCOL ON

TORTURE

REF: SECSTATE 130158

CLASSIFIED BY: A/POL P. THIEDE FOR REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D)

1. (C) IN RESPONSE TO REFTEL DEMARCHE, MFA OFFICE DIRECTOR

FOR HUMAN RIGHTS EMANUELE PIGNATELLI TOLD POLOFF JULY 23 THAT

THE GOI SHARES THE EU POSITION ON THE DRAFT OPTIONAL PROTOCOL

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 ROME 03647 240902Z

ON TORTURE. STATING THAT THE GOI HAS BEEN TRYING TO GET THE

USG TO SIGN THE DOPT, PIGNATELLI OUTLINED HIS GOVERNMENT\'S

POSITION WITH CONVICTION. THE GOI IS "PROFOUNDLY CONVINCED"

OF THE DOPT\'S NECESSITY. NEGOTIATING THE DOPT TOOK TEN

YEARS; ALL SOLUTIONS AND NATIONAL POSITIONS WERE CONSIDERED.

AN INTERNATIONAL BODY ABLE TO INTERVENE TO PREVENT CASES OF

TORTURE AND TO HELP COUNTRIES DEVELOP LEGAL FRAMEWORKS TO

PREVENT TORTURE IS ESSENTIAL. THE SUCCESS OF THE EUROPEAN

CONVENTION AGAINST TORTURE AND ITS COMMITTEE TASKED WITH THE

TYPE OF RESPONSIBILITIES ENVISIONED BY THE DOPT HAD CONVINCED

THE GOI OF THE VALUE OF EXPANDING THEM TO THE INTERNATIONAL

SYSTEM.

2. (C) PIGNATELLI ACKNOWLEDGED THAT THE DOPT "COULD ALWAYS

BE IMPROVED." HOWEVER, THE DRAFTING COMMITTEE HAD DONE AN

EXCELLENT JOB, AND IT WAS BETTER TO HAVE A SLIGHTLY-IMPERFECT

DOPT THAN NONE AT ALL. WITH REGARD TO THE PROCEDURAL

CONCERNS RAISED BY REFTEL AND PREVIOUS DEMARCHES, PIGNATELLI

SAID THAT CONSENSUS IS CERTAINLY PREFERABLE BUT NOT ALWAYS

POSSIBLE. IN THIS CASE, BEING ABLE TO BRING THE DOPT TO A

VOTE ENABLED THE CHR TO RESOLVE A DILEMMA; I.E., HOW TO PASS

THE DOPT WITHOUT WATERING IT DOWN SO MUCH AS TO BE USELESS.

3. (C) CURRENTLY, THE GOI IS ENGAGED IN AN EFFORT TO

CONVINCE OTHER COUNTRIES WITH GOOD HUMAN RIGHTS RECORDS TO

ACCEDE TO THE DOPT, NOTING THAT ITS AIM IS TO PREVENT

TORTURE, NOT TO JUDGE OR CONDEMN OTHER GOVERNMENTS.

NONETHELESS, THE GOI "FULLY APPRECIATED THE CONSIDERATIONS

THAT THE USG HAS EXPRESSED."
