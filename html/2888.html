SUBJECT: MILITARY BASES IN ITALY: CGIL AND UGL

REPRESENTATION OF DIRECT-HIRE WORKERS

REF: ROME 2198

CLASSIFIED BY: AMBASSADOR MEL SEMBLER. REASON 1.5 (D).

1. (C) IN MEETINGS WITH THE AMBASSADOR APRIL 22 AND 26

(REFTEL), CGIL AND UGL LEADERS BOTH REFERRED TO U.S. FORCE

POLICY THAT EXCLUDES THESE TRADE UNION CONFEDERATIONS FROM

REPRESENTING DIRECT-HIRE WORKERS AT BASES IN ITALY. WHILE

CHARACTERIZING THE UNION\'S RELATIONS WITH THE EMBASSY AS

"EXCELLENT," OUTGOING CGIL SECRETARY GENERAL SERGIO COFFERATI

REFERRED TO ONE "MINOR" PROBLEM THAT, HE SAID, HE WAS SURE

WOULD BE TAKEN UP AND RESOLVED BY HIS SUCCESSOR. ALTHOUGH

COFFERATI DIDN\'T IDENTIFY THIS "MINO" PROBLEM (AND PASSED

OVER IT IN SECONDS), THE RFERENCE TO BASE EXCLUSION WAS

CLEAR.

2. (C) T THE UGL, DEPUTY SECRETARY GENERAL RENATA POLVERIN

ALSO REFERRED TO A "SMALL" PROBLEM AT THE BASES BUT WAS

DIRECT IN RAISING AND LAYING OUT THE ISUE. IT WAS ONE, SHE

SAID, IN WHICH THE UGL (ANDCGIL) HAD BEEN PREVENTED "FOR

IDEOLOGICAL REASON" FROM REPRESENTING DIRECT-HIRE WORKERS.

THE MATTR HAD BEEN RAISED AND "ALMOST RESOLVED" UNDER THE

PREVIOUS AMERICAN AMBASSADOR AND PREVIOUS GOI. PLVERINI

SAID UGL MEMBERS AT THE BASES WOULD LIKETHEIR UNION TO BE

ABLE TO REPRESENT THEM. SHE NTED THAT THEN-LABOR MINISTER

CESARE SALVI (OF THECENTER-LEFT GOI HEADED BY PRIME MINISTER

MASSIMO D\'ALEMA), IN ORDER NOT TO APPEAR PARTISAN, HAD

INTERCEDED WITH THE EMBASSY IN 2000 IN BEHALF OF BOTH THE

LEFT-OF-CENTER CGIL AND RIGHT-OF-CENTER UGL. THE CURRENT

(CENTER-RIGHT) GOI, SHE SUGGESTED, MIGHT INTERVENE FOR

SIMILAR REASONS ON BEHALF OF BOTH CONFEDERATIONS. THE

AMBASSADOR UNDERTOOK TO CONSIDER THE MATTER WHICH, HE

EMPHASIZED, WAS ONE U.S. MILITARY AUTHORITIES WOULD DECIDE.

3. (C) COMMENT: THE ISSUE OF CGIL/UGL EXCLUSION WAS FIRST

RAISED BY THE CGIL IN 1994, BY THE UGL MORE RECENTLY. BOTH

UNIONS HAVE PURSUED THEIR QUEST QUIETLY, THROUGH DIRECT

REPRESENTATIONS TO U.S. FORCES AND THIS MISSION. WHILE THE

UNIONS HAVE DISCUSSED THE MATTER WITH THE GOI MINISTRY OF

LABOR, BOTH HAVE REFRAINED FROM RAISING IT PUBLICLY. PUBLIC

DISCLOSURE - WHICH DID NOT RAISE CONTROVERSY AT THE TIME -

OCCURRED IN JUNE 2000, WHEN COMMUNIST RENEWAL (RC) SENATOR

GIOVANNI RUSSO SPENA FILED A "QUESTION" IN PARLIAMENT SHORTLY

BEFORE ITS ADJOURNMENT.

4. (C) COMMENT CONTINUED: THE CURRENT CONTEXT IS TRANQUIL.

SO FAR AS WE KNOW, BASE LABOR RELATIONS ARE GOOD, AND THE

MISSION\'S RELATIONS WITH ALL FOUR NATIONAL TRADE UNION

CONFEDERATIONS ARE EXCELLENT. MILITARY COOPERATION WITH THE

CURRENT GOI IS ONGOING; OUR MILITARY PRESENCE IN ITALY IS

EXPANDING. NONETHELESS THE CGIL/UGL ISSUE, "DORMANT" OVER

THE LAST SEVERAL MONTHS, HAS AGAIN BEEN RAISED WITH THIS

MISSION AND COULD AGAIN BECOME AN ISSUE IN PARLIAMENT AND BE
