SUBJECT: ITALIAN PARLIAMENT APPROVES ITALY\'S TROOP

CONTRIBUTION TO OEF

CLASSIFIED BY: POL MINCOUNS THOMAS COUNTRYMAN, REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) SUMMARY: THE ITALIAN PARLIAMENT GAVE SUBSTANTIAL --

IF REDUCED FROM LAST NOVEMBER\'S VOTE FOR THE ORIGINAL

DEPLOYMENT -- SUPPORT TO THE GOI PROPOSAL TO SEND UP TO 1,000

ALPINI GROUND TROOPS TO AFGHANISTAN FOR OPERATION ENDURING

FREEDOM. THE OPPOSITION SPLINTERED BADLY AND ATTEMPTED TO

LINK REINFORCEMENT IN AFGHANISTAN TO A PRESUMED U.S. ATTACK

ON IRAQ. BERLUSCONI\'S CENTER-RIGHT COALITION HELD FAST. IN

ITS DISARRAY, THE CENTER-LEFT WAS THE LOSER, WITH ITS

INABILITY TO REACH CONSENSUS A BIGGER HEADLINE THAN THE

IMPENDING DEBATE. END SUMMARY.

2. (U) GOI DEFENSE MINISTER MARTINO PRESENTED THE

GOVERNMENT\'S PLAN TO REINFORCE OEF BY SENDING 800-1,000

ALPINI TROOPS TO BOTH PARLIAMENTARY CHAMBERS OCTOBER 2,

SPEAKING BEFORE THE SENATE AND LEAVING THE TEXT WITH THE

CHAMBER. HE OUTLINED THE MISSION THE ALPINI WOULD PERFORM,

LISTED THE DECISIONS OF INTERNATIONAL ORGANIZATIONS

AUTHORIZING USE OF FORCE AGAINST TERRORISM IN AFGHANISTAN,

AND HIGHLIGHTED THE GOI POSITION THAT THE NOVEMBER 7, 2001,

PARLIAMENTARY AUTHORIZATION FOR ITALIAN PARTICIPATION IN OEF

AUTHORIZED THIS "RECONFIGURATION" OF THE ITALIAN

PARTICIPATION, AS WELL, BUT THAT THE GOI WANTED TO MEET "NEW

EXPECTATIONS" BY CONSULTING PARLIAMENT. MARTINO CALLED ON

THE OPPOSITION TO AGAIN "PUT INSTITUTIONAL DUTY BEFORE

POLITICAL PARTY INTERESTS" AND REPEAT THE ALMOST UNANIMOUS

SUPPORT IT GAVE THE GOVERNMENT LAST YEAR. HE REPEATEDLY

UNDERSCORED THAT ITALIAN SUPPORT TO OEF WAS NOT LINKED IN ANY

WAY TO AN ATTACK ON IRAQ BY EITHER THE U.S. OR AN

INTERNATIONAL COALITION; DEPLOYMENT OF ITALIAN GROUND TROOPS

WOULD NOT "COMPRISE SUBSTITUTION OF U.S. PERSONNEL AND

VEHICLES TO ALLOW THEIR HYPOTHETICAL REDEPLOYMENT IN OTHER

THEATERS." THE WAR AGAINST TERRORISM AND THE STRUGGLE FOR

STABILITY AND SECURITY IN AFGHANISTAN ARE NOT OVER. TO

WITHDRAW ITALIAN SUPPORT FOR THE EFFORT NOW WOULD BE TO VOTE

AGAINST THE INTERNATIONAL TRUSTWORTHINESS OF ITALY.

3. (U) SENATE DEBATE ON MARTINO\'S PRESENTATION BEGAN THE

MORNING OF OCTOBER 3. THE OPPOSITION DID NOT RESPOND WITH

THE SAME NEAR-UNANIMOUS SUPPORT AS LAST NOVEMBER; IN FACT,

THE CENTER-LEFT SPLINTERED. IN COMPLICATED VOTING ON A

SERIES OF SIX RESOLUTIONS, COMMUNIST RENEWAL (RC), THE

GREENS, AND THE COMMUNIST PARTY OF ITALY (PDCI) VOTED

CONSISTENTLY AGAINST THE GOVERNMENT. MOST OF THE DEMOCRATS

OF THE LEFT (DS) ALSO VOTED CONSISTENTLY AGAINST THE GOI, BUT

DS SAW SEVERAL PROMINENT DEFECTIONS. THE SMALL

REPRESENTATION OF THE CENTER-LEFT UNION OF DEMOCRATS FOR

EUROPE (UDEUR) AND ITALIAN SOCIALISTS (SDI) SUPPORTED THE

GOI. THE MORE CENTRIST DAISY AND SEVERAL DS SENATORS (THE

"DISSIDENTS") LEFT THE FLOOR WHEN THE VOTE WAS CALLED ON THE

GOVERNMENT\'S RESOLUTION TO SHOW THEY WERE NOT OPPOSED,

ALTHOUGH THEY COULD NOT "POLITICALLY" VOTE IN FAVOR. DAISY

GROUP LEADER WILLER BORDON SAID BEFORE THE ASSEMBLY THAT THE

DAISY DID NOT INTEND TO EXPRESS OPPOSITION TO THE

CENTER-RIGHT MOTION.

4. (U) DAISY AND THE SDI OFFERED A SEPARATE RESOLUTION,

LARGELY APPROVED WITH THE SUPPORT OF THE GOVERNING COALITION,

IN FAVOR OF SENDING TROOPS TO OEF WITH SOME CONDITIONS.

MARTINO URGED SUPPORT FOR THE PARTS OF THE RESOLUTION

CONFIRMING ITALY\'S COMMITMENT AGAINST TERRORISM AND TO THE

CONSTRUCTION OF DEMOCRACY IN AFGHANISTAN. HE URGED THAT THE

PART OF THE RESOLUTION COMMITTING THE GOI TO SUPPORT

DIPLOMATIC EFFORTS ON IRAQ IN ORDER TO AVOID A UNILATERAL,

PREVENTIVE ARMED INTERVENTION BE DEFEATED SINCE IT

ARBITRARILY LINKED UNRELATED OPERATIONS. THIS PART OF THE

RESOLUTION WAS DEFEATED. ANOTHER RESOLUTION OFFERED BY UDEUR

ALSO URGED THAT IT BE APPROVED) WAS ALSO PASSED. RESOLUTIONS

5. (SBU) DEBATE IN THE CHAMBER BEGAN AT 15:00 LOCAL TIME.

THE RESULTS MIRRORED THOSE IN THE SENATE: THE

MAJORITY-BACKED RESOLUTION PASSED 266 TO 151, WITH 65

ABSTENTIONS, FOLLOWING THE SAME PARTY LINES DESCRIBED ABOVE.

THE DAISY-SDI AND UDEUR RESOLUTIONS ALSO PASSED, WITH THE

EXCEPTION OF THE UNRELATED PART OF THE DAISY MOTION.

REVEALINGLY, DAISY LEADER FRANCESCO RUTELLI SAID HIS PARTY

(N.B. NOT HIS COALITION; RUTELLI REMAINS THE TITULAR HEAD OF

THE OLIVE TREE CENTER-LEFT COALITION) WOULD VOTE IN FAVOR OF

THE GOVERNMENT PROPOSAL OUT OF A SPIRIT OF SERVICE FOR ITALY.

HE REGRETTED THAT IT WAS THE FIRST TIME HE WAS NOT TRYING TO

REPRESENT THE OLIVE TREE COALITION, BUT ONLY HIS OWN DAISY

PARTY. "ONE YEAR AGO, WE SAID THAT WITHOUT THE DEFEAT OF

TERRORISM, THERE WOULD NOT BE PEACE. THIS IS SOMETHING THAT

IS FAR FROM BEING ACHIEVED," RUTELLI NOTED.

7. (C) COMMENT: WHILE THE OUTCOME OF THIS VOTE IS BOTH

HISTORIC (FIRST ITALIAN COMMITMENT OF OFFENSIVE GROUND FORCES

SINCE WORLD WAR II) AND IMPORTANT TO THE U.S. (ITALY WILL

BECOME IN MARCH OUR BIGGEST PARTNER IN OEF), THE DEBATE HAD

ALMOST NOTHING TO DO WITH THE MERITS OF THE DEPLOYMENT.

RATHER, IT WAS THE PRODUCT OF THREE FACTORS:

-- THE OPPOSITION\'S FIXATION ON SCORING POINTS AGAINST PRIME

MINISTER BERLUSCONI WHENEVER THERE IS AN OPPORTUNITY;

-- EVEN MORE IMPORTANT THAN THIS ARE THE LEADERSHIP

STRUGGLES WITHIN THE OPPOSITION, AND PARTICULARLY WITHIN THE

DS;

-- AND MOST IMPORTANT OF ALL, BOTH THE GOI AND THE

OPPOSITION ARE WARMING UP FOR THE EVENTUAL DEBATE ABOUT IRAQ

THEY BOTH EXPECT.

8. (C) COMMENT, CONTINUED. DESPITE THE GOI\'S BEST EFFORTS

TO CLARIFY THAT THE ISSUE OF OEF REINFORCEMENT IS SEPARATE

FROM A POTENTIAL ATTACK ON IRAQ AND THE EMBASSY\'S OWN DIRECT

BRIEFINGS TO THEM, THE OPPOSITION PERSISTED IN LINKING THE

TWO IN AN EFFORT TO LEGITIMIZE THEIR ABOUT-FACE FROM LAST

NOVEMBER\'S VOTE. ITALY\'S CENTER-LEFT (WITH THE POSSIBLE

EXCEPTION OF THE DAISY PARTY) IS SEEKING TO CAPITALIZE ON

STRONG ANTI-WAR PUBLIC SENTIMENT. THEIR EFFORTS LARGELY

FAILED, HOWEVER -- MOST OF TODAY\'S MEDIA HEADLINED THE

"COLLAPSE" OF THE LEFT EVEN MORE THAN THE UPCOMING DEBATE.
