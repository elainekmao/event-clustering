SUBJECT: WFP\'S SURGING FOOD AID REQUIREMENTS FOR 2003 -

REPORT OUT ON WFP\'S RESOURCES CONSULTATION OF FEBRUARY 7,

2003

REF: (A) ROME 000589, (B) 2000 ROME 04690

-------

SUMMARY

-------

1. WFP\'S PROJECTED 2003 NEEDS (ALL PROJECTS AND OPERATIONS)

ARE ESTIMATED AT 4.8 MILLION TONS (MTS), VALUED AT U.S.

DOLLARS 2.4 BILLION (NOT INCLUDING ANY IRAQ RESPONSE IN THE

EVENT OF CONFLICT). WITH A LARGE NUMBER OF HIGH PROFILE AND

NEEDY OPERATIONS, 2003 IS EXPECTED TO PUT CONSIDERABLE

STRAIN ON WFP\'S ABILITY TO ADDRESS THE FOOD AID REQUIREMENTS

PARTICULARLY OF LESS HIGH-PROFILE, YET NO LESS NEEDY

EMERGENCIES. ON A NOTE OF GOOD NEWS, TWELVE EUROPEAN DONORS

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ROME 00643 01 OF 02 181039Z

INCREASED THEIR COMMITMENT TO WFP BY U.S. DOLLARS (USD)

154.24 MILLION, LED BY THE EUROPEAN COMMISSION (PLUS USD 53

MILLION) AND THE UK (PLUS USD 68.1 MILLION). AMBASSADOR HALL

CONFIRMED THE ADMINISTRATION\'S COMMITMENT TO HELP MEET

EMERGENCY NEEDS WORLDWIDE AND SUGGESTED THE ESTABLISHMENT OF

A OECD-CONSTITUTED HUMANITARIAN LIAISON WORKING GROUP (HLWG)

IN ROME TO DISCUSS MATTERS OF COMMON HUMANITARIAN CONCERN.

END SUMMARY.

--------------------------------------------- --------------

WFP RESOURCES CONSULTATION - FEBRUARY 7, 2003 - SNAPSHOT OF

2002 CONTRIBUTIONS

--------------------------------------------- --------------

2. ON THE FINAL DAY OF THE FIRST REGULAR SESSION OF THE WFP

EXECUTIVE BOARD (FEBRUARY 7), EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR JIM MORRIS

PROVIDED A REVIEW FOR THE DONOR COMMUNITY OF 2002

CONTRIBUTIONS AND ANTICIPATED WFP FOOD AID NEEDS FOR 2003.

IN 2002, DONOR CONTRIBUTIONS EQUALED USD 1.805 BILLION,

TOWARDS WHICH THE U.S. CONTRIBUTED USD 928.7 MILLION (51.4

PERCENT). NOTE. IN 2001, THE U.S. CONTRIBUTION, USD 1.2

BILLION, CONSTITUTED 62 PERCENT OF WFP\'S TOTAL

CONTRIBUTIONS. END NOTE.

3. TWELVE EUROPEAN DONORS INCREASED THEIR COMMITMENT TO WFP

BY USD 154.24 MILLION, LED BY THE EUROPEAN COMMISSION (PLUS

USD 53 MILLION) AND THE UK (PLUS USD 68.1 MILLION). THE ONLY

"BLACK SHEEP" WERE FRANCE (MINUS USD 21.7 MILLION) AND SPAIN

(MINUS USD 1.3 MILLION). NOTE: THIS IN US MISSION\'S VIEW

DEMONSTRATES A GREATER EUROPEAN ACCEPTANCE OF BURDEN SHARING

WITHIN THE FOOD AID REALM. END NOTE.

4. AMONG NON-EUROPEAN DONORS, CANADA (PLUS USD 9.3 MILLION);

SAUDI ARABIA (PLUS USD 2.0 MILLION) - SIGNIFICANTLY

WFP\'S 2003 REQUIREMENTS - "AWASH" IN EMERGENCIES

--------------------------------------------- ---

5. EMERGENCY OPERATIONS (EMOPS) WILL TARGET 39.2 MILLION

BENEFICIARIES, AND WILL REQUIRE AN INPUT OF ALMOST 2.4

MILLION TONS. PROJECTED REQUIREMENTS FOR PROTRACTED RELIEF

AND RECOVERY OPERATIONS (PRROS) WILL INCREASE SUBSTANTIALLY,

UP FROM 1.2 MILLION TONS THIS YEAR TO 1.6 MILLION TONS FOR

2003. THIS IS DUE IN PART TO THE EXPANDING PRRO PORTFOLIO,

NOW NUMBERED AT 36 OPERATIONS WORLD-WIDE, AND IS A RESULT OF

DRAMATICALLY INCREASED RELIEF NEEDS IN SELECTED OPERATIONS

SUCH AS ANGOLA, IN THE AFTERMATH OF THE 2002 PEACE ACCORD.

AMBASSADOR HALL\'S AFFIRMATION OF CONTINUING U.S. SUPPORT TO

WFP

--------------------------------------------- --------------

6. AMBASSADOR TONY HALL, IN RESPONDING FOR THE U.S

DELEGATION, AFFIRMED THAT WFP CONTINUES TO BE AN IMPORTANT

PARTNER IN ENSURING THAT MILLIONS OF CHILDREN AROUND THE

WORLD HAVE A MEAL EVERY DAY. HE CONFIRMED THAT THE USAID FY

2003 TITLE II PLEDGE OF U.S. DOLLARS 200 MILLION FOR WFP HAD

BEEN SIGNED ON JANUARY 24 AND U.S. DOLLARS 5 MILLION GRANTED

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ROME 00643 02 OF 02 181039Z

THIS PAST WEEK TO PARTIALLY SUPPORT THE WFP IRAQ EMOP AND

SPECIAL OPERATION (SO). HALL DREW THE BOARD\'S ATTENTION TO

PRESIDENT BUSH\'S FEBRUARY 1 RADIO ADDRESS TO THE NATION

WHERE THE PRESIDENT COMMENTED THAT MORE THAN 60 PERCENT OF

INTERNATIONAL EMERGENCY FOOD AID COMES AS A GIFT FROM THE

PEOPLE OF THE UNITED STATES. HE CITED THE PRESIDENT\'S

ANNOUNCED INTENTION IN 2004 TO REQUEST MORE THAN ONE BILLION

DOLLARS TO MEET EMERGENCY FOOD NEEDS WORLDWIDE, AND A

PROPOSED U.S. DOLLAR 200 MILLION "FAMINE FUND" TO BRING

ASSISTANCE TO AFRICA AND OTHER REGIONS FACING STARVATION.

7. AMBASSADOR HALL ALSO NOTED THAT THERE EXISTS IN BOTH

GENEVA AND NEW YORK AN OECD-CONSTITUTED HUMANITARIAN LIAISON

WORKING GROUP (HLWG) TO DISCUSS MATTERS OF COMMON CONCERN

AMONGST DONORS AND OCCASIONALLY WITH REPRESENTATIVES OF

RELEVANT PARTS OF THE VARIOUS UN SECRETARIATS. IN JULY 2000,

THE NETHERLANDS (REF B), PROPOSED THE ESTABLISHMENT IN ROME

OF AN HLWG TO HAVE A MORE FORMALIZED OPPORTUNITY AMONG THE

MAIN DONORS TO DISCUSS IN A FRANK, OPEN AND CONSTRUCTIVE

ATMOSPHERE ABOUT HUMANITARIAN AND OTHER CROSSCUTTING ISSUES

WITH POTENTIAL IMPLICATIONS ON HUMANITARIAN AFFAIRS. SUCH A

BODY WOULD ALLOW MEMBERS TO CONSULT EACH OTHER ON

HUMANITARIAN POLICIES AND CO-ORDINATE RESPECTIVE ACTION.

HALL PROPOSED THAT THE TIME HAS INDEED COME TO BRING TO

FRUITION THIS TYPE OF ENTITY IN ROME. NOTE: AT PRESENT, ONLY

LIMITED TIME IS DEVOTED TO HUMANITARIAN QUESTIONS - MOSTLY

AT WFP - AND OFTEN IN AN AD HOC MANNER. END NOTE.

8. 2003 WILL BE A YEAR OF MOUNTING HUNGER CRISES. 38 MILLION

PEOPLE IN SUB-SAHARAN AFRICA FACE STARVATION. WFP HAS

RATCHETED UP ITS BUDGETARY NEEDS ESTIMATES BY U.S. DOLLARS

600 MILLION OVER THE 2002 LEVEL (WITHOUT FACTORING IN IRAQ).

CLEARLY, THE NUMBERS ARE OVERWHELMING THE SYSTEM AND

STRETCHING WFP\'S COPING CAPACITY TO SOMEWHERE NEAR THE

BREAKING POINT. HALL
