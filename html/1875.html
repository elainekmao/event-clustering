SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) PRESIDENT MUGABE\'S NATIONAL HEROES\' DAY ADDRESS

TO A LARGE BUT LISTLESS CROWD AT HEROES\' ACRE WAS

DEFIANT IN TONE AND UNCHANGED IN SUBSTANCE. MUGABE

LAMBASTED THE BRITISH FOR THEIR HISTORICAL SINS AND TOOK

AIM AT THE U.S. OVER THE SENATE PASSAGE OF ZDERA. HE

TALLIED UP FIGURES FOR LAND ACQUIRED AND PEOPLED

RESETTLED IN THE FAST TRACK LAND PROGRAM AND WARNED

COMMERCIAL FARMERS NOT TO RESIST. MUGABE CALLED ON

COMMERCIAL FARMERS TO COOPERATE AND IMPLIED TROUBLE IF

THEY DID NOT. IN FOLLOWING CONVERSATIONS, UK HIGH

COMMISSIONER DONNELLY TERMED THE ADDRESS "AN OLIVE

BRANCH WITH THORNS." OTHER WESTERN DIPLOMATS WITH MORE

SEASONING IN ZIMBABWE WONDERED IF THIS WERE NOT AN

OVERLY OPTIMISTIC READ.

--------------------------

THE ANGLO-SAXON CONSPIRACY

--------------------------

2. (SBU) A CROWD ESTIMATED BETWEEN 4,000 (OPPOSITION

PRESS) AND 10,000 (GOVERNMENT PRESS) WERE TREATED TO AN

HOUR AND TWENTY MINUTES OF PRESIDENTIAL RHETORIC ON

AUGUST 11. A HEARTY LOOKING MUGABE, WHO DELIVERED HIS

ADDRESS IN FIRM TONES, BEGAN WITH A CONDEMNATION OF

WESTERN HYPOCRISY. HE ACCUSED THE DEMOCRATIC WORLD OF

NOT RAISING A FINGER IN THE PAST AS THE "BRITISH RACIAL

AUTOCRACY" BRUTALIZED AND TRAUMATIZED BLACK ZIMBABWEANS.

HE ACCUSED THE COLONIALISTS OF PILLAGING THE COUNTRY\'S

MINERAL WEALTH AND FORCING THE ZIMBABWEANS TO WORKS THE

LAND "TO FEED THEIR (BRITISH) CHILDREN." HE ACCUSED UK,

US, AND GERMAN MERCENARIES OF FIGHTING ON THE SIDE OF

THE RHODESIANS DURING THE LIBERATION STRUGGLE, USING

NATO WEAPONS AGAINST THE POPULATION.

3. (SBU) MUGABE DELIVERED A DIATRIBE AGAINST THE

AMERICAN SENATE AND CONGRESS, WHO HE STATED HAD NEVER

CONSIDERED IMPOSING SANCTIONS AGAINST THE UDI

RHODESIANS. INSTEAD, THE U.S. HAD ASKED TO BE EXEMPTED

FROM UN SANCTIONS IN ORDER TO BUY CHROME FROM THE IAN

SMITH REGIME, REFUSING THE OPTION OF BUYING FROM THE

SOVIET UNION, WHICH WAS "A FREE STATE AT THAT TIME." HE

CHARGED THE SAME SENATORS OF WORKING WITH THE OPPOSITION

TODAY ON THE "INFAMOUS ZDERA." HE ASKED RHETORICALLY

WHAT CRIME ZIMBABWE HAD COMMITTED TO CAUSE TALK OF

SANCTIONS, THEN ANSWERED BY SAYING HOW DARE A BLACK

GOVERNMENT WITH A WHITE COMMUNITY CHALLENGE THE KIN OF

THE GOD-CHOSEN ANGLO-SAXONS. THIS WAS SEEN AS

PRACTICING TERRORISM AND AUTOCRACY.

----------------------------

A WALLOW IN COLONIAL HISTORY

----------------------------

4. (SBU) MUGABE NEXT TOOK DIRECT AIM AT THE BRITISH,

NOTING THAT VICE PRESIDENT MUZENDA HAD BEEN TORTURED

WITH NEEDLES WHILE IN INCARCERATION. SPEAKER OF

PARLIAMENT MNANGAGWA WAS HARD OF HEARING DUE TO BEATINGS

"ARTIFICIAL HUNGER."

HOLDING FIRM ON LAND

--------------------

DOMINANCE" BUT NOTED THAT HE HAD HEARD THAT PARTS OF THE

AND WANTED TO JOIN THE GOVERNMENT IN REFORM. THESE WERE

6. (SBU) HE SPOKE DIRECTLY TO RECENT EVENTS IN

CHINHOYI, CHARGING THAT THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS HAD

QUOTING HIMSELF ON RECONCILIATION

---------------------------------

7. (C) MUGABE STRESSED THAT WHAT ANGERS AND DISTURBS

THE CIO, POLICE, AND ZDF MOST IS THE FACT THAT THE

FARMERS NOW PROVOKING INCIDENTS ARE THE SAME ONES WHO

FOUGHT AGAINST THE CURRENT AUTHORITIES IN THE LIBERATION

STRUGGLE. HE OBSERVED THAT IF HE HAD DONE AS THE

MILITANTS HAD WANTED IN 1980, THE (FORMER RHODESIAN

FIGHTERS) WOULD HAVE BEEN "DECAPITATED" AND WOULD NOT

"BE WRIGGLING BACK TO LIFE AGAIN." HE SAID THAT WE

(ZIMBABWEANS) WERE BETTER, MORE MORAL, AND HAD INSTEAD

EXTENDED THE HAND OF FRIENDSHIP AND RECONCILIATION.

MUGABE QUOTED HIMSELF FROM 1980 ON BEATING SWORDS INTO

PLOWSHARES BEFORE CONCLUDING WITH SOME PORK BARREL

COMMENT

-------

8. (C) MUGABE WAS IN FINE FETTLE, AND HIS PERFORMANCE

-- DEMAGOGIC THOUGH IT OFTEN WAS -- PUTS TO REST FOR NOW

TALK THAT HE IS TOTTERING ON THE BRINK OF PHYSICAL

COLLAPSE. WESTERN DIPLOMATS WHO HAVE BEEN TREATED TO

PAST PERFORMANCES NOTED THAT THERE WAS AN UNUSUALLY

LARGE COMPONENT OF UNIFORMED MILITARY IN THE AUDIENCE

THIS YEAR. THE CROWD CHEERED ON CUE, BUT THERE WAS A

NOTABLE LACK OF ENTHUSIASM OVERALL.

9. (C) IN A FOLLOWING CONVERSATION, BRITISH HIGH

COMMISSIONER DONNELLY SPOKE ON THE CHINHOYI SITUATION AS

WELL AS HIS TAKE ON THE SPEECH. HE SAID THAT THE CFU

TEAM NEGOTIATING THE LAND ISSUE WITH THE GOZ HAD

REPORTED A "GOOD" MEETING WITH MUGABE ON AUGUST 19,

ALBEIT ONE THAT HAD BROUGHT NO BREAKTHROUGH. CONNELLY

CALLED THE SPEECH AN "OLIVE BRANCH WITH THORNS."

SEVERAL OF HIS WESTERN COUNTERPARTS WHO HAVE BEEN IN

ZIMBABWE FOR LONGER PERIODS TOLD CHARGE THAT THEY HAD

PERCEIVED MORE THORN THAN OLIVE BRANCH AND FELT THAT

MUGABE\'S POSITION REMAINS UNCHANGED.

10. (C) THE SENATE\'S PASSAGE OF ZDERA HAS CLEARLY

CAUGHT MUGABE\'S ATTENTION. DONNELLY OBSERVED THAT WHILE
