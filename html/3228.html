SUBJECT: PROSPECTS FOR LEADERSHIP STABILITY IN VIETNAM

REASON: 1.5 (D)

2. (C) SUMMARY. A PARTY PLENUM AND NEW NATIONAL

ASSEMBLY WITHIN THE NEXT FEW WEEKS WILL DECIDE ON

SEVERAL NEW KEY LEADERS. WHILE SOME PERSONNEL

JOCKEYING PROBABLY CONTINUES, WE DETECT FEW, IF ANY,

GENUINE DEBATES ON POLICY DIRECTION. WE ANTICIPATE

THAT THE NEW LINE-UP WILL CONTINUE TO BE LARGELY

COLLECTIVE AND PRAGMATIC IN DECISION-MAKING AS WELL AS

GENERALLY SUPPORTIVE OF ECONOMIC REFORM AND DIPLOMATIC

INTEGRATION INTO THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY. WE

BELIEVE IT WILL BE IMPORTANT TO BUILD ON THE POSITIVE

MOMENTUM IN BILATERAL TIES WITH AN ACTIVE SERIES OF

HIGH LEVEL VISITS IN BOTH DIRECTIONS OVER THE NEXT YEAR

AND BEYOND. END SUMMARY.

3. (C) THE EXPECTED SIXTH PLENUM OF THE CENTRAL

COMMITTEE OF THE NINTH CONGRESS OF THE COMMUNIST PARTY

OF VIETNAM (CPV) JULY 4-15 AND THE OPENING OF THE 11TH

NATIONAL ASSEMBLY (NA) ON JULY 19 WILL LEAD TO A NEW

ROUND OF SENIOR LEADERSHIP SHIFTS WITHIN THE GVN

(REFTEL). AMONG THE LIKELY CHANGES WILL BE ONE OR TWO

DEPUTY PRIME MINISTERS, AS MANY AS HALF A DOZEN

MINISTERS, POSSIBLY THE SUPREME PROCURATOR, AND SEVERAL

NA COMMITTEE CHIEFS, INCLUDING FOREIGN AFFAIRS AND

ECONOMIC AND BUDGET AFFAIRS. THE PRESIDENT, PRIME

MINISTER, AND CHAIRMAN OF THE NATIONAL ASSEMBLY ARE

EXPECTED TO WIN RE-ELECTION EASILY.

4. (C) MUCH OF THE BACK-DOOR BARGAINING ON PERSONNEL

SHIFTS HAS PROBABLY ALREADY CONCLUDED, WITH THE PLENUM

EXPECTED TO GIVE ITS FINAL BLESSING IN PRIVATE TO THE

NEW SLATE AND THE NATIONAL ASSEMBLY, AFTER TOKEN

DISCUSSION, TO RATIFY THESE CHOICES OBEDIENTLY. UNLIKE

THE RIVALRY BETWEEN THEN-GENERAL SECRETARY LE KHA PHIEU

AND EVENTUAL GENERAL SECRETARY NONG DUC MANH IN THE

RUN-UP TO THE 2001 NINTH PARTY CONGRESS, HOWEVER, THESE

PERSONNEL DECISIONS APPEAR TO HAVE VERY LITTLE TO DO

WITH IDEOLOGY OR SETTING A DIFFERENT POLICY COURSE FOR

VIETNAM OVER THE NEXT FIVE YEARS.

5. (C) THE NINTH PARTY CONGRESS ESSENTIALLY MADE THE

BOTTOM LINE CHOICES IN FAVOR OF EXPANDED ECONOMIC

REFORM, HIGHER PRIORITY TO THE PRIVATE SECTOR AND

INTERNATIONAL TRADE, MAINTENANCE OF DOMESTIC STABILITY,

AND CONTINUED INTEGRATION INTO THE REGIONAL AND

INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY. WE SEE NO ONE ON THE HORIZON

WITHIN THE CURRENT LEADERSHIP OR POTENTIAL LEADERSHIP

RANKS WHO IS STAKING AN OPPOSITION TO THIS POLICY

FRAMEWORK OR WHO MIGHT WISH TO BACKTRACK ON NINTH PARTY

CONGRESS PRIORITIES. INDEED, SOME OF THE POTENTIAL

WINNERS FROM THE LEADERSHIP SWEEPSTAKES, SUCH AS

CURRENT MINISTER OF TRADE VU KHOAN (A LIKELY DPM) OR HO

CHI MINH CITY PARTY CHIEF NGUYEN MINH TRIET (STILL A

DARK HORSE CANDIDATE AS DPM) MIGHT SEEK FURTHER TO

ENERGIZE THE GVN\'S ECONOMIC REFORM PROCESS ONCE IN

OFFICE. NO ONE IN SIGHT, HOWEVER, APPEARS TO BE

ADVOCATING FOR SIGNIFICANT POLITICAL REFORM, APART FROM

AN ONGOING IF SEEMINGLY STERILE CAMPAIGN TO STRENGTHEN

"GRASSROOTS DEMOCRACY" AND NUMEROUS SUCCESSFUL PROGRAMS

(INCLUDING WITH USG SUPPORT) ON THE DEVELOPMENT OF RULE

OF LAW.

6. (C) THE CPV AND GVN LEADERSHIP HAS WEATHERED

NUMEROUS CHALLENGES SINCE NATIONAL UNIFICATION IN 1975,

PARTICULARLY THE DARK DAYS OF ISOLATION AND ECONOMIC

DISASTER IN THE 1980\'S AND THE FALL-OUT FROM THE 1997

ASIAN FINANCIAL CRISIS. WHAT WE ARE LEFT WITH IS NOT A

GENERATION OF RISK TAKERS OR VISIONARIES, BUT RATHER A

MORE OR LESS GENUINELY COLLECTIVE LEADERSHIP (WITHOUT

ANY HOTSHOTS OR STARS) THAT IS INCREASINGLY PRAGMATIC

RATHER THAN DOGMATIC. THESE INDIVIDUALS APPARENTLY

UNDERSTAND AND AGREE ON THE CRITICAL NEED FOR ECONOMIC

DEVELOPMENT AND JOB CREATION, WHILE NONETHELESS

STRIVING TO MINIMIZE THREATS TO SOCIAL ORDER, DOMESTIC

STABILITY, AND MAINTENANCE OF CPV POLITICAL POWER.

7. (C) EMBASSY BELIEVES THAT THE NEW LEADERSHIP WILL

CONTINUE TO BE COMPOSED OF INDIVIDUALS WITH WHOM THE

USG CAN WORK PRODUCTIVELY. WE LOOK FORWARD TO

COORDINATING WITH WASHINGTON AGENCIES ON AN ACTIVE

SCHEDULE OF VISITS IN BOTH DIRECTIONS OVER THE NEXT

YEAR AND BEYOND IN ORDER TO BUILD ON THE POSITIVE

MOMENTUM IN OUR BILATERAL TIES.
