SUBJECT: TEACHING THE PARTY ABOUT HUMAN RIGHTS

1. SUMMARY. A HUMAN RIGHTS CENTER AT THE MAIN PARTY SCHOOL

HAS FOR THE LAST SEVEN YEARS BEEN UNDERTAKING A UNIQUE

EFFORT TO EDUCATE OFFICIALS ABOUT INTERNATIONAL LEGAL

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 HANOI 00156 01 OF 02 240242Z

CONCEPTS RELATED TO HUMAN RIGHTS. THIS LONG-TERM PROCESS

MERITS OUR ATTENTION AND POSSIBLY SUPPORT, DESPITE ONGOING

DIFFERENCES OF VALUES AND APPROACHES TO THIS DELICATE TOPIC.

END SUMMARY.

2. THE HO CHI MINH POLITICAL ACADEMY -- THE CENTRAL PARTY

SCHOOL OF THE COMMUNIST PARTY OF VIETNAM (CPV) -- HAS SINCE

1994 INCLUDED A RESEARCH CENTER FOR HUMAN RIGHTS. ITS

DIRECTOR, DR. CAO DUC THAI, TOLD POL/C ON JANUARY 23 THAT

ITS MAJOR FUNCTIONS ARE TO:

-- CONDUCT RESEARCH ON THE HISTORY AND THEORY OF HUMAN

RIGHTS, BASED PRIMARILY ON INTERNATIONAL LAW;

-- UNDERTAKE RESEARCH PROJECTS ON HUMAN RIGHTS SITUATIONS

"AROUND THE WORLD," INCLUDING IN THE U.S.;

-- TEACH STUDENTS AT THE ACADEMY -- THE ALREADY ELITE AND

THE UP-AND-COMING OF THE CVP, AT NATIONAL AS WELL AS

PROVINCIAL LEVELS -- ABOUT HUMAN RIGHTS PRINCIPLES AND THEIR

INTERNATIONAL LEGAL FRAMEWORKS;

-- DISSEMINATE HUMAN RIGHTS DOCUMENTS TO TARGETED GROUPS

WITHIN VIETNAM;

-- ADVISE THE GVN AND CPV ON LEGISLATION WITH HUMAN RIGHTS

IMPLICATIONS; AND,

-- STRENGTHEN COOPERATION WITH INTERNATIONAL EXPERTS, NGOS,

AND UNIVERSITIES.

3. THE CENTER CURRENTLY HAS AN IN-HOUSE STAFF OF 16,

INCLUDING FOUR WITH DOCTORATES AND SIX WITH MASTER\'S DEGREE,

MOSTLY IN INTERNATIONAL LAW. (ONE RESEARCHER/LECTURER WHO

SAT IN ON THE MEETING HAD AN LL M -- MASTER\'S IN

INTERNATIONAL HUMAN RIGHTS LAW -- FROM A UNIVERSITY IN

SWEDEN.) ANOTHER TWO DOZEN OR SO RESEARCHERS ARE ON

TEMPORARY LOAN FROM OTHER GVN AGENCIES, INCLUDING THE

MINISTRIES OF JUSTICE, FOREIGN AFFAIRS, AND PUBLIC SECURITY,

AS WELL AS THE NATIONAL ASSEMBLY, DR. THAI SAID.

4. ALL STUDENTS AT THE ACADEMY (USUALLY 2-3,000 IN ANY

GIVEN YEAR) IN PRINCIPLE TAKE ONE COURSE ON HUMAN RIGHTS

THEORY IN INTERNATIONAL LAW, ACCORDING TO DR. THAI (WHO

TEACHES THE COURSE), BUT THE CENTER DOES NOT NOW OFFER A

BROADER CURRICULUM. IT PUBLISHES A REGULAR BULLETIN WITH

ARTICLES BY ITS RESEARCHERS; DR. THAI PROMISED HE WOULD ASK

FOR PERMISSION TO INCLUDE EMBASSY ON ITS DISTRIBUTION. HE

EXPRESSED A HOPE EVENTUALLY TO TRANSFORM THE BULLETIN INTO A

MAINLINE JOURNAL, WHILE ADMITTING THE DIFFICULTY IN

SUSTAINING SUCH AN EFFORT ON A COMMERCIAL BASIS. UNESCO

UNDERWRITES ANOTHER OF THE CENTER\'S PROGRAMS, PUBLISHING

HUMAN RIGHTS DOCUMENTS AIMED AT CHILDREN, HE NOTED.

5. AS AN EXAMPLE OF HOW THE CENTER HAD ADVISED THE GVN AND

CPV ON LEGISLATION, DR. THAI CITED EVOLVING NATIONAL POLICY

ON PEOPLE WITH AIDS. HE NOTED THE REAL PROBLEM OF SOCIETAL

DISCRIMINATION AGAINST THIS POPULATION AND THE NEED FOR

LEGISLATION TO PROTECT THEIR LEGITIMATE AND EQUAL RIGHTS

BASED ON THE CONSTITUTION.

6. DR. THAI CLAIMED THAT THE "SENIOR PARTY LEADERSHIP" WAS

VERY INTERESTED IN THE ISSUE OF HUMAN RIGHTS, HENCE THE

ESTABLISHMENT OF THIS CENTER. HE NOTED SPECIFICALLY THAT

MANY OTHER GVN OFFICIALS AND CPV CADRES DID NOT YET

UNDERSTAND THE ISSUE OR THE LEGAL BASIS -- IN BOTH

INTERNATIONAL AND NATIONAL TERMS -- FOR THESE RIGHTS. THE

CHALLENGE FOR THE CENTER, HE SAID, WAS TO HELP IMPROVE THE

LEVEL OF UNDERSTANDING ABOUT HUMAN RIGHTS GENERALLY AND

SPECIFICALLY IN VIETNAM. HE DESCRIBED THE CENTER AS THE

"UNIQUE AGENCY" IN VIETNAM TO UNDERTAKE THIS TASK. HE

CLAIMED THAT INTEREST AND UNDERSTANDING IN HUMAN RIGHTS

WITHIN VIETNAM WAS "INCREASING" AND ATTITUDES OF OFFICIALS

WERE GRADUALLY MORE "POSITIVE" AND "OPEN." HE ADMITTED,

HOWEVER, DIFFERING LEVELS OF INTEREST AMONG OFFICIALS.

7. WHEN ASKED ABOUT FREQUENT COMPLAINTS FROM VIETNAMESE

ABOUT "INTERFERENCE IN INTERNAL AFFAIRS" WHEN OUTSIDE

OBSERVERS COMMENTED ON ALLEGED HUMAN RIGHTS ABUSES IN

VIETNAM, DR. THAI CITED A CLEAR DISTINCTION BETWEEN RESEARCH

AND REPORTING ON HUMAN RIGHTS, AND ACTUAL INTERVENTION IN A

LOCAL SITUATION. HE CLAIMED TO BE A FAITHFUL READER OF THE

STATE DEPARTMENT\'S HUMAN RIGHTS REPORT ON VIETNAM, AND

PROMISED TO PROVIDE FEEDBACK FROM THIS YEAR\'S REPORT. HE

SAID THAT THE CENTER HAD NO/NO CONTACTS WITH INTERNATIONAL

GROUPS SUCH AS AMNESTY INTERNATIONAL OR HUMAN RIGHTS WATCH,

WHILE UNDERTAKING EXCHANGES WITH GROUPS IN AUSTRALIA,

SWEDEN, AND DENMARK.

8. WHILE ADMITTING THAT US AND VIETNAMESE PERCEPTIONS ON

HUMAN RIGHTS WERE OFTEN AT ODDS, HE NONETHELESS CLAIMED THAT

OUR DIFFERENCES ON THIS ISSUE WERE NOT REALLY SO GREAT. THE

PRIMARY DIFFERENCE RELATED TO LIMITATIONS ON INDIVIDUAL

FREEDOMS, WHICH WERE STILL IMPORTANT TO BOTH PEOPLES. FOR

9. POL/C ENCOURAGED DR. THAI TO INTENSIFY INTERACTION

BETWEEN THE EMBASSY AND THE CENTER AND NOTED AMBASSADOR

BURGHARDT\'S INTEREST IN VISITING THE CENTER AT A LATER DATE.

DR. THAI ACCEPTED WITH ALACRITY AND SAID THAT HE WOULD VALUE

SUCH ENHANCED CONTACTS.

10. COMMENT: THE VIETNAMESE VIEWPOINT ON HUMAN RIGHTS

REMAINS WIDELY DIVERGENT FROM OUR OWN, AND RESPECT FOR CORE

VALUES SADLY INADEQUATE IN BOTH POLICY AND IMPLEMENTATION.

IT IS NONETHELESS STRIKING THAT THERE ARE PEOPLE WITHIN THE

VERY BOWELS OF THE CPV WHO ARE STEADILY IMPLANTING THE

CONCEPT THAT HUMAN RIGHTS DO NOT STEM FROM AN ALIEN-IMPOSED

VALUE SYSTEM, AND THAT RESPECT FOR HUMAN RIGHTS IS DEEPLY

IMBEDDED IN INTERNATIONAL LAW AND IN VIETNAM\'S OWN EXPLICIT

COMMITMENTS TO VARIOUS INTERNATIONAL CONVENTIONS AND ITS OWN

CONSTITUTION. THAT THE CPV ITSELF IS FOSTERING THIS

INTERNAL REVOLUTION OF THINKING PERHAPS REVEALS AN INHERENT

RECOGNITION THAT BLUSTER AND DENIAL OVER HUMAN RIGHTS

ALLEGATIONS ARE NOT IN VIETNAM\'S LONG-TERM INTEREST, AND

THAT ONLY A CAREFUL LONG-TERM EDUCATION CAMPAIGN CAN

ADEQUATELY CHANGE THE CONSCIOUSNESS OF BOTH OFFICIALS AND

CITIZENS THROUGHOUT THE NATION. WHILE THIS SMACKS OF

"INTERNAL PEACEFUL EVOLUTION," IT IS A PROCESS WE SHOULD
