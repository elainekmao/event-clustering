SUBJECT: ITALY COMMUNICATIONS BILATERAL: UPDATE

REFS: A) ROME 5535, B) ROME 5720, C) STATE 253506

1. THIS CABLE CONTAINS AN ACTION REQUEST, PLEASE SEE

PARAGRAPH 10.

2. ECMIN AND ECON SPECIALIST MET WITH COMMUNICATIONS

MINISTRY SENIOR OFFICIALS DECEMBER 16 TO CONVEY INFORMATION

CONTAINED IN REF C. OFFICIALS FROM THE COMMUNICATIONS

AUTHORITY AND THE MINISTRY FOR INNOVATION AND TECHNOLOGY

ALSO ATTENDED THE MEETING.

3. THE AGENDA PROPOSED IN REF C WAS ACCEPTED IN ITS

ENTIRETY BY COMMUNICATIONS MINISTRY OFFICIALS WITH THE

SINGLE ADDITION OF 3G TO BE DISCUSSED IN SESSION VI

(TECHNOLOGY DEVELOPMENTS). IT ALSO WAS AGREED TO END THE

BILATERAL WITH A LUNCHEON ON FRIDAY, TO ALLOW TIME FRIDAY

AFTERNOON FOR A POSSIBLE MEETING BETWEEN THE U.S. DELEGATION

AND ITALIAN COMMUNICATIONS INDUSTRY REPRESENTATIVES.

4. A POSSIBLE MEETING WITH ITALIAN INDUSTRY IS VIEWED

FAVORABLY BY COMMUNICATIONS MINISTRY OFFICIALS, WHO ALSO

FULLY CONCUR THAT THIS SHOULD BE KEPT SEPARATE FROM THE

GOVERNMENT TO GOVERNMENT BILATERAL. COMMUNICATIONS MINISTRY

OFFICIALS INDICATED THAT THEY WISH TO BE KEPT INFORMED AND

ASKED THAT SUCH A MEETING BE OPENED TO AS MANY OPERATORS AS

POSSIBLE, RATHER THAN TO A SELECTED FEW. THE COMMUNICATIONS

UNCLASSIFIED

MINISTRY OFFICIALS INITIALLY OFFERED TO ORGANIZE AND HOST,

SEPARATE FROM THE BILATS, THE MEETING WITH INDUSTRY

REPRESENTATIVES, BUT IN A SUBSEQUENT DISCUSSION INDICATED

THAT IT WOULD BE BEST IF THE NATIONAL ICT PRIVATE SECTOR

ASSOCIATION WERE TO TAKE THE LEAD. (EMBASSY COMMENT: A

MEETING WITH ITALIAN PRIVATE SECTOR IS STILL IN THE WORKS AS

CONTACTS TO DATE HAVE BEEN INFORMAL AND LIMITED. EMBASSY

WILL CONTINUE TO PURSUE THE MATTER AND KEEP WASHINGTON

INFORMED.)

5. PRESS. THE COMMUNICATIONS MINISTRY DOES NOT ENVISION A

PRESS CONFERENCE TO FOLLOW THE BILATERALS. THEY WOULD LIKE

TO ISSUE A PRESS STATEMENT, WHICH CAN BE AGREED UPON

IN ADVANCE AND FINED-TUNED, IF NECESSARY, DURING THE

BILATERAL.

6. REPRESENTATION EVENTS. THE AMERICAN CHAMBER OF COMMERCE

COMMUNICATIONS COMPANIES HAVE AGREED TO COVER MOST (IF NOT

ALL) THE COST OF A U.S.-HOSTED RECEPTION ON THE EVENING OF

MARCH 5. IN EXCHANGE, THEY HAVE ASKED FOR SOME PRIVATE TIME

(ABOUT 1-1/2 HOURS) WITH THE DELEGATION BEFORE THE

RECEPTION. HOSTING A RECEPTION RATHER THAN A DINNER IS MORE

COST EFFECTIVE AND WILL ALLOW AN EXPANDED GUEST LIST TO

INCLUDE ALL EMBASSY ICT CONTACTS. SEPARATELY, THE MINISTRY

OF COMMUNICATIONS WILL HOST A DINNER MARCH 6 PRINCIPALLY FOR

THE TWO DELEGATIONS.

7. LOGISTICS AND INTERPRETATION. WE CONFIRMED THAT ALL

BILATERAL SESSIONS, INCLUDING LUNCHEONS, WILL BE HELD AT THE

MINISTRY OF COMMUNICATIONS. THE MINISTRY WILL ORGANIZE

SIMULTANEOUS INTERPRETATION, WITH COSTS SPLIT (APPROXIMATELY
