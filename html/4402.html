SUBJECT: VATICAN AND QATAR ESTABLISH DIPLOMATIC RELATIONS

1. THE HOLY SEE ANNOUNCED NOVEMBER 18 THAT IT HAS

ESTABLISHED FORMAL DIPLOMATIC RELATIONS WITH QATAR. THE

HOLY SEE WILL BE REPRESENTED AT THE LEVEL OF NUNCIO AND

QATAR BY AN AMBASSADOR. THE HOLY SEE HAS DIPLOMATIC

RELATIONS WITH MOST MOSLEM STATES WITH THE GLARING EXCEPTION

OF SAUDI ARABIA, OMAN, AND THE UNITED ARAB EMIRATES. IT

ALSO HAS DIPLOMATIC RELATIONS WITH THE ARAB LEAGUE AND THE

PALESTINIAN LIBERATION ORGANIZATION.

2. QATAR\'S CATHOLIC POPULATION NUMBERS AROUND 45,000 -

MOSTLY MIGRANT WORKERS FROM THE PHILIPPINES - SUPPORTED BY 8

PRIESTS WHO ALSO WORK IN YEMEN, SAUDI ARABIA, BAHRAIN, THE

UNITED ARAB EMIRATES, AND OMAN.

3. COMMENT: THE HOLY SEE CONTINUES TO SEEK TO INTENSIFY

ITS DIALOGUE WITH ISLAMIC STATES, PRIMARILY TO ENSURE

ADEQUATE PASTORAL CARE FOR RESIDENT CATHOLICS. IN THE WAKE

OF 9/11, HOWEVER, THE DIALOGUE HAS TAKEN ON GREATER URGENCY

AS THE POPE AND HIS COLLABORATORS WORK TO AVOID A "CLASH OF

CIVILIZATIONS" BETWEEN ISLAM AND CHRISTIANITY, AND BETWEEN

THE ARAB WORLD AND THE WEST. END COMMENT.
