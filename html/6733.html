Security/Military

-----------------

1310 Duck and Cover Alert; gas masks donned.

1320 All Clear sounded.

1337 Duck and Cover Alert; gas masks donned.

1345 All Clear sounded.

1346 Duck and Cover Alert; gas masks donned.

1352 All Clear sounded.

1456 Embassy Marine Guard reported that all three

missiles launched from Iraq and precipitating above Duck

and Covers overshot and landed in Arabian Gulf; no

injuries or damage reported.

1647 Duck and Cover Alert; gas masks donned.

1655 All Clear sounded.

1750 ASRO reported missile launched from Iraq and

precipitating above Duck and Cover impacted; no injuries

or damage reported.

2220 Received call from NEA/ARP Kuwait Desk Officer

regarding a CNN news story about a bomb scare

near a Kuwaiti Hotel. RSO established contact

with the Kuwaiti EOD; their investigation

determined that a suspicious vehicle in front

of the Kuwait Marriott was searched and

cleared-no bomb was found. DOS Task Force

informed via telephone; Kuwait Desk Officer

informed via e-mail.

2225 Received word via Embassy staffer of an

explosion near 5th Fleet headquarters in

Bahrain. Fox news subsequently confirmed that

a propane tank exploded near the Fleet; no

injuries were reported; it is not considered a

terrorist attack at this time.

Political

---------

1908 Received telephone call from Enjood Al-Ghanim

on behalf of the Al-Ghanim company in order to

express thanks for American efforts in Iraq and

express condolences for American Prisoners of

War in Iraq.

1944 Received call from Al-Awadi/Al-Anzi families

expressing sorrow over the American soldier killed by his

fellow serviceman. Caller voiced thanks for America\'s

efforts in Iraq; stated "all Kuwaiti people are with

you."

1954 Received call from Kuwaiti citizen expressing thanks

for the American and British forces in Kuwait; caller

stated "Our hearts are with you; we are in a safe place

because the Americans are here."

2117 Received call from Kuwaiti citizen expressing

gratitude to America; caller stated "You\'re freeing so

many people who suffer in the Iraqi regime, and you\'re

doing an extremely important job for the people of Iraq,

and the next generation."

2130 Received call from Kuwaiti citizen speaking on

behalf of entire family; caller expressed appreciation

for America\'s hard work, stating "We don\'t regret

anything. Best of luck, we love you and President Bush,

and Bush senior as well."

2230 Received call from Kuwaiti citizen representing 30

compatriots; caller expressed appreciation for Operation

Iraqi Freedom and condolences for all Coalition soldiers

who have lost their lives in action. Caller stated "We

pray for an easy and quick return of coalition POWs, and

support the Coalition 100% in its actions inside Iraq."

2320 Received call from Kuwaiti citizen expressing deep

gratitude to the Coalition forces and offering to provide

housing and food if necessary.

2335 Received call from Kuwaiti citizen expressing

profound appreciation to the Coalition; offered to help

in any way.

2240 Received call from Kuwaiti citizen expressing

support for the war against suppression of

Iraqis.

1555 Airport Operations: 1) As of today, Kuwait City

International

Airport is open from 0600-1800; this will be

via Dubai (now serving as Lufthansa\'s main
