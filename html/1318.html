1. (C) SUMMARY. IN A BIZARRE OUTBRIEF ON OCTOBER 12,

THE UN/UNDP TEAM SENT TO EXPLORE WAYS TO BRIDGE THE GAP

BETWEEN DONORS AND GOVERNMENT ON LAND REFORM MADE

RECOMMENDATIONS ON HOW TO "IMPROVE" THE FAST TRACK

PROGRAM. THE TEAM STRESSED THAT THEIR MANDATE WAS

TECHNICAL -- NOT POLITICAL. DONOR QUESTIONS WERE

LIMITED BUT THERE WAS A PALPABLE SENSE OF DISBELIEF AND

DISAPPOINTMENT IN THE MISSION. GIVEN THE NARROW MANDATE

OF THE MISSION, THE MORE FUNDAMENTAL ISSUE OF THE RULE

OF LAW WAS NOT ADDRESSED. WE THINK THAT THE PARALLEL UN

INITIATIVE TO EXAMINE THE RULE OF LAW SHOULD BE

ENCOURAGED WHILE PUTTING ON HOLD ANY FURTHER TECHNICAL

MISSIONS TO WORK ON A VIABLE LAND PROGRAM. POST

BELIEVES THAT THE LATTER CAN BE CONDUCTED ON THE GROUND

BY DONORS, WHILE THE UN CAN MOST PRODUCTIVELY HELP

ADDRESS THE RULE OF LAW ISSUE. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) ON OCTOBER 12 UNDP RESIDENT REPRESENTATIVE

VICTOR ANGELO INTRODUCED THE FIVE MEMBERS OF THE UN/UNDP

TEAM WHO BRIEFED HEADS OF DIPLOMATIC AND SELECTED

DEVELOPMENT ASSISTANCE MISSIONS ON RECOMMENDATIONS THAT

WOULD APPEAR IN THEIR FINAL REPORT TO THE UNDP

ADMINISTRATOR AND THE UN SECRETARY GENERAL. (COMMENT:

DONORS WERE SURPRISED AT THE UNDP RESREP\'S INVITATION TO

THE PALESTINE LIBERATION ORGANIZATION REPRESENTATIVE, AS

WELL AS HEADS OF MISSION OF THE SOUTHERN AFRICAN

DEVELOPMENT COMMUNITY AND THE G-77. ABOUT TWO THIRDS OF

THE PARTICIPANTS WERE NEW FACES AND FEW OF THEM

POTENTIAL DONORS. THE EUROPEAN COMMISSION

REPRESENTATIVE WAS CONSPICUOUS BY HIS ABSENCE. END

COMMENT). ANGELO SAID THAT AFTER THREE WEEKS IN COUNTRY

CONSULTING WITH A WIDE RANGE OF STAKEHOLDERS, THE

ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT HAD GIVEN THE TEAM ALL THE

ASSISTANCE THAT IT NEEDED, AND HAD RECEIVED THE TEAM AT

THE HIGHEST LEVEL. ANGELO NOTED THAT PRESIDENT MUGABE

WAS KEPT REGULARLY UPDATED ON THE TEAM\'S FINDINGS.

SPEAKING FOR THE TEAM, CHIEF OF PARTY JACQUES CHONCHOL

FOUND THAT THERE IS BROAD AGREEMENT WITHIN ZIMBABWE

AMONG ALL STAKEHOLDERS THAT:

-- LAND REFORM IN ZIMBABWE IS A POLITICAL, SOCIAL AND

ECONOMIC IMPERATIVE THAT REQUIRES IMMEDIATE ATTENTION;

-- IT IS PRIMARILY THE RESPONSIBILITY OF THE GOVERNMENT

OF ZIMBABWE TO DESIGN AND IMPLEMENT A WORKABLE LAND

REFORM PROGRAM;

4. (C) THE UN/UNDP MISSION CHIEF OF PARTY CONTINUED

WITH THE CAVEAT THAT POLITICAL CONSIDERATIONS FELL

OUTSIDE THE TEAM\'S MANDATE. THE TEAM VISITED VARIOUS

SETTLEMENTS AND NOTICED THAT THE FAST TRACK EXERCISE HAD

RUN INTO DIFFICULTIES: THE GOVERNMENT HAD NO RESOURCES

FOR INFRASTRUCTURE OR AGRICULTURAL INPUTS FOR THOSE

RESETTLED, THERE WAS INADEQUATE INSTITUTIONAL CAPACITY

AS WELL AS A CUMBERSOME STATUTORY AND ADMINISTRATIVE

FRAMEWORK. IN LIGHT OF THESE FACTS, THE TEAM\'S REPORT

WILL MAKE FOUR RECOMMENDATIONS TO IMPROVE THE OVERALL

EFFICIENCY OF THE PROGRAM:

-- ESTABLISHMENT OF A TRUST FUND ADMINISTERED BY UNDP

AND FINANCED BY DONORS TO PURCHASE AND UPGRADE ACQUIRED

LAND, UNDER THE FAST TRACK RESETTLEMENT EXERCISE. IT

SHOULD ENCOMPASS AN ORDERLY PROCESS OF RESETTLEMENT AND

THE GOVERNMENT SHOULD STOP FURTHER OCCUPATIONS. A

COMMITTEE WOULD OVERSEE THE MANAGEMENT OF THE FUND WITH

REPRESENTATIVES FROM THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE, THE

DONORS AND UNDP.

--FOUNDING OF A TRAINING INSTITUTE;

-- SIMPLIFICATION OF THE ADMINISTRATIVE AND LEGAL

PROCEDURES WITH RESPECT TO LAND ACQUISITION AND

DECENTRALIZED; AND

-- USING A PROPORTION OF THE TRUST FUND TO SUPPLEMENT

WHO WOULD IMPLEMENT THE LAND REFORM PROGRAM.

5. (C) KICKING OFF THE QUESTION AND ANSWER SESSION, THE

NEW ZEALAND HIGH COMMISSIONER ASKED WHETHER THE TRAINING

INSTITUTE THE TEAM ADVOCATED WAS TO BE SOMETHING NEW,

BECAUSE THE COUNTRY WAS LITTERED WITH SUCH INSTITUTIONS

AND MANY OF WHOSE GRADUATES WERE QUALIFIED FARMERS WHO

WERE NOT BEING PLACED ON LAND. HE ALSO ASKED WHETHER

THE TRUST FUND RECOMMENDATION HARKED BACK TO THE RESULTS

OF THE 1998 DONORS CONFERENCE. CHONCHOL REPLIED THAT IT

WOULD BE A NEW INSTITUTION ON THE MODEL OF THOSE SET UP

IN OTHER COUNTRIES THAT HAD PROVEN HIGHLY SUCCESSFUL.

ZIMBABWE LACKED CAPACITY TO IMPLEMENT A PROPER LAND

REFORM PROGRAM, IT NEEDED TO TRAIN TECHNICIANS, PEASANT

LEADERS, AND PROPERLY ORGANIZE CO-OPERATIVES, AMONG

OTHER THINGS. REGARDING THE TRUST FUND, CHONCHOL SAID

THAT IT WOULD ALSO BE SOMETHING NEW, ADMINISTERED BY THE

DONORS, GOVERNMENT AND THE UNDP.

6. (C) IN A RUMP SESSION, WORLD BANK, DFID AND USAID

MET TO DISCUSS IMPLICATIONS OF THE MISSION. THE

CONSENSUS WAS THAT THE MEETING WAS BIZARRE AND THE

MISSION FAILED TO ADVANCE THE DIALOGUE ON THE RULE OF

LAW OR CLARIFY THE TECHNICAL ISSUES. RATHER, THE

RECOMMENDATIONS TINKER ON THE EDGES OF A FUNDAMENTALLY

FLAWED APPROACH. THE WORLD BANK REPRESENTATIVE OBSERVED

THAT MOST DONORS PRESENT WERE MORE KNOWLEDGEABLE ABOUT

THE TECHNICAL ISSUES THAN THE TEAM. OVERALL THE CONCERN

WAS THAT THE ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT COULD USE RESULTS OF

THIS MISSION TO JUSTIFY UNLEASHING FURTHER LAWLESSNESS,

BECAUSE DONORS ALMOST CERTAINLY WILL FAIL TO SUPPORT THE

PROPOSED TRUST FUND. THERE IS ALSO WIDESPREAD BELIEF

HERE THAT THE GOZ WILL LOSE THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS\'

UNION\'S COURT CHALLENGE. THE SUPREME COURT LIKELY WILL

RULE THAT THE USE OF PRESIDENTIAL POWERS TO AMEND BOTH

THE CONSTITUTION AND THE LAND ACT WILL BE RULED

UNCONSTITUTIONAL. THE FEAR IS THAT THE GOZ WILL THEN

JUSTIFY FURTHER INVASIONS AND LAND SEIZURES GIVEN THE

DESPERATE ECONOMIC SITUATION AND THE NEED FOR ZANU-PF TO

DELIVER ON SOMETHING TO ITS RURAL CONSTITUENCY BEFORE

THE 2002 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTIONS.

7. (C) COMMENT: AS WE SUSPECTED, THE MAJOR THRUST OF

THE TEAM\'S RECOMMENDATIONS IS TO RATIONALIZE THE

GOVERNMENT\'S FAST TRACK APPROACH TO LAND REFORM AND

RESETTLEMENT. THIS RESULT WAS NOT SURPRISING, GIVEN THE

NARROW AND TECHNICAL TERMS OF REFERENCE UNDER WHICH THE

TEAM OPERATED. THE ATTEMPT TO SEGREGATE THE TECHNICAL

FROM THE POLITICAL WAS AN IMPOSSIBLE TASK BECAUSE LAND

REFORM IN ZIMBABWE IS NOW ESSENTIALLY A POLITICAL

PROCESS. DESPITE THE NEW RESREP\'S BEST EFFORT AT

PUTTING A POSITIVE SPIN ON THE TEAM\'S FINDINGS, THE NET

RESULT IS A POTENTIALLY IMPORTANT LOST OPPORTUNITY TO

BRIDGE THE CURRENT IMPASSE CHARACTERIZING THIS ISSUE.

THE REPORT POTENTIALLY COULD EVEN SERVE AS A SETBACK TO

THE PROCESS OF DIALOGUE, IF IT RESULTS IN RAISING FALSE

GOVERNMENT EXPECTATIONS REGARDING POTENTIAL DONOR

SUPPORT FOR THE FAST TRACK PROGRAM.

8. (C) COMMENT CONTINUED: POST AGREES WITH THE STRATEGY

TO SEPARATE THE THREE MAJOR ISSUES (RULE OF LAW, THE

ECONOMY AND THE LAND PROGRAM). HOWEVER, IN OUR VIEW,

THE UN PUT THE CART BEFORE THE HORSE, BECAUSE THE

TECHNICAL ISSUES ARE NOT THE FUNDAMENTAL PROBLEM HERE.

LAND WITH GOVERNMENT WITH GOOD SUCCESS AT THE 1998
