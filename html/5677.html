SUMMARY

--------

1. (C) THOMAS OSORIO, HEAD OF ICTY\'S ZAGREB OFFICE, TOLD US

THAT OTP SPOKESPERSON FLORENCE HARTMANN\'S FEBRUARY 5

STATEMENT CRITICIZING THE GOC FOR FAILING TO APPREHEND ICTY

INDICTEE ANTE GOTOVINA AND FOR FAILING TO MEET ICTY

INFORMATION REQUESTS REPRESENTS THE OPENING SALVO OF A NEW

OTP BID TO PRESS THE GOC TO TAKE MORE VISIBLE ACTION TO

ARREST THE FUGITIVE. OSORIO SAID THE OTP IS "CONVINCED" THAT

THE GOTOVINA IS HIDING IN CROATIA, BUT WAS UNWILLING (OR

UNABLE) TO OFFER CONCRETE EVIDENCE TO SUBSTANTIATE HARTMANN\'S

HARSH CLAIMS. OSORIO, WHO WAS NOT/NOT CONSULTED BEFORE

HARTMANN\'S PRESS STATEMENT, THOUGHT THAT HER CRITICISM OF GOC

DOCUMENT SHARING LAPSES WAS OVERSTATED.

2. (C) WHILE THE OTP OFTEN HAS GOOD REASON TO BE FRUSTRATED

WITH THE GOC -- IT IS A RELUCTANT AND FAIR WEATHER PARTNER

-- THE OTP URGENTLY NEEDS TO IMPROVE THE MANAGEMENT OF ITS

"CROATIA ACCOUNT." MISSTEPS IN THE NOW-SUSPENDED BOBETKO WAR

CRIMES CASE, PARTICULARLY THE OTP DECISION NOT TO INTERVIEW

THE SUSPECT BEFORE INDICTING HIM (REF C), HELPED FOSTER A

CRISIS THAT WRACKED THE GOC IN FALL 2002, DIMINISHED ICTY\'S

CREDIBILITY HERE AND COMPLICATED CROATIAN EU ASPIRATIONS WITH

NOTHING TO SHOW FOR THE EFFORT. WE SHOULD CONTINUE TO PRESS

THE GOC ON GOTOVINA AND ICTY COOPERATION WRIT LARGE.

3. (C) IN THE STRONGEST LANGUAGE USED BY AN ICTY OFFICIAL

SINCE THE CRISIS OVER GENERAL BOBETKO FLARED IN SEPTEMBER

2002, HARTMANN ACCUSED THE GOC OF FAILING TO APPREHEND

FUGITIVE CROATIAN GENERAL AND ICTY INDICTEE ANTE GOTOVINA.

GOTOVINA, HARTMANN ALLEGED, WAS "TRAVELING FREELY IN

CROATIA." HARTMANN ALSO ACCUSED THE GOC OF "SELECTIVE, SLOW

AND INCOMPLETE COMPLIANCE WITH SPECIFIC REQUESTS FOR

DOCUMENTS."

4. (C) IN LIGHT OF HARTMANN\'S STATEMENT, WE MET FEBRUARY 6

WITH ICTY ZAGREB OFFICE RESREP THOMAS OSORIO (PROTECT

THROUGHOUT). ALTHOUGH HE WOULD NOT -- OR COULD NOT -- GO

INTO DETAILS, OSORIO ASSERTED THAT THE OTP HAS EVIDENCE TO

BACK UP HARTMANN\'S ACCUSATION ON GOTOVINA. THERE ARE "NEW

SIGHTINGS AND NEW INFORMATION," ALTHOUGH HE SAID THAT NONE OF

THE NEW LEADS ARE AS SOLID AS THE REPORTED SFOR SIGHTING OF

GOTOVINA IN HERCEGOVINA IN 2002 (REF D). IN SUM, ICTY NOW IS

CONVINCED THAT GOTOVINA IS MOVING IN AND OUT OF CROATIA AND

THAT THE GOC HAS NOT/NOT BEEN ACTING IN GOOD FAITH TO

APPREHEND HIM. OSORIO NOTED THAT IF THE EU MADE GOTOVINA\'S

CAPTURE A PRECONDITION FOR ACTING ON CROATIA\'S EU MEMBERSHIP

SHOULD THE GOC BALK, DEMONSTRATE CATEGORICALLY THAT CROATIA

IS FAILING TO COMPLY WITH ITS ICTY OBLIGATIONS. ACCORDING

HOMES, INTERVIEW GOTOVINA\'S ATTORNEYS AND ASSOCIATES AND

BELIEVED TO FREQUENT. ICTY STAFF WILL ACTIVELY MONITOR THESE

AND OTHER ACTIVITIES AND MAY SEEK TO ACCOMPANY THE POLICE ON

THEIR RAIDS TO VERIFY GOC COMPLIANCE.

7. (C) OTP FRUSTRATION WITH GOC PASSIVITY ON GOTOVINA IS

UNDERSTANDABLE; THE GOC DREADS THE PROSPECT OF HAVING TO

TRANSFER GOTOVINA TO THE HAGUE AND DESPERATELY DOES NOT WANT

THE GENERAL TO BE IN CROATIA. HOWEVER, OUR EXPERIENCE WITH

MUCH OF WHAT WE KNOW OF THE OTP\'S "EVIDENCE" ON GOTOVINA IS

THAT IT IS BASED ON CIRCULAR REPORTING AND HEARSAY. WHILE

GOTOVINA VERY WELL MAY BE IN THE REGION, WE HAVE SEEN

NOTHING, ASIDE FROM THE SFOR SIGHTING, TO UNDERPIN THE HARSH

OTP STATEMENT THAT GOTOVINA IS "WALKING FREELY" IN CROATIA

(AND WE HAVE BEEN ASKING OSORIO AND THE CROATIANS FOR

MONTHS). PER REF B, OTP POLITICAL ADVISOR JEAN-JAQUES JORIS

TOLD OUR COLLEAGUES IN THE HAGUE THAT "CROATIAN AND FOREIGN

SOURCES" SUPPORTED THE OTP CLAIM THAT GOTOVINA WAS MOVING

DOCUMENT SHARING

----------------

8. (C) OSORIO SAID HE WAS NOT CONSULTED BEFORE HARTMANN

ISSUED HER STATEMENT. IN HIS VIEW CRITICISM OF THE GOC\'S

PERFORMANCE ON INFORMATION SHARING WAS OVERSTATED AND THE

GOC, AS A RULE, WAS RESPONSIVE ON DOCUMENT REQUESTS.

ACCORDING TO OSORIO, THERE ARE THREE OUTSTANDING DOCUMENT

DELIVERY PROBLEMS: 1) THE GOC HAS FAILED TO DELIVER BODY

COUNT AND AUTOPSY RESULTS CONDUCTED BY THE CROATIAN MILITARY

AFTER OPERATION STORM; 2) THE GOC HAS NOT MET A DEADLINE TO

PROVIDE DEMOGRAPHIC DATA WHICH ICTY NEEDS TO HELP DEMONSTRATE

YUGOSLAV ETHNIC CLEANSING FOR THE MILOSEVIC TRIAL; AND 3) THE

GOC HAS MISSED A NUMBER OF DEADLINES ON INFORMATION LINKED TO

INVESTIGATIONS OF CROATIAN WAR CRIMES IN BOSNIA. IN THE CASE

OF THE FIRST TWO DOCUMENT SHARING ISSUES, OSORIO TOLD US THAT

THE GOC FAILURE TO DELIVER THE DOCUMENTS WAS A TECHNICAL,

SHORT-TERM HICCUP. IN FACT, IN THE CASE OF THE BODY COUNT

INFORMATION, OSORIO SAID THAT HE HAD OBTAINED A BOOTLEG COPY

FROM THE HEAD OF THE GOC COMMISSION FOR MISSING PERSONS. IN

THE CASE OF THE OUTSTANDING BIH INFORMATION, THE ISSUE COULD

HAVE BEEN HANDLED "THROUGH REGULAR CHANNELS," OSORIO

ASSERTED.

11. (C) WITH BOBETKO OUT OF THE PICTURE, THE OTP IS

STRUGGLING TO MAKE SENSE OF ITS PROSECUTION STRATEGY FOR THE

DISCUSSED WITH CHIEF PROSECUTOR MLADEN BAJIC THE POSSIBILITY

THAT HE INCLUDE NORAC IN THE LOCAL CROATIAN INDICTMENT OF

FOR ITS EFFORTS TO PROVE THAT THE GOC CONCEPTUALIZED AND

COMMENT

-------

13. (C) THE OTP IS UNDERSTANDABLY FRUSTRATED. THE BOBETKO

CASE, AND YEARS AND YEARS OF WORK, IS SLIPPING AWAY WITHOUT A

DAY IN COURT. JUSTICE IS DENIED AND THE GOC, WHICH EVADED

FOR MONTHS ITS RESPONSIBILITY TO ACT AS ICTY\'S AGENT ON THE

CASE, HAS COME OUT THE WINNER. THE BRITISH, WHO WENT OUT ON

A LIMB TO SUPPORT THE OTP ON BOBETKO BY SUSPENDING CROATIA\'S

STABILIZATION AND ASSOCIATION AGREEMENT, ARE FURIOUS WITH THE

OTP ABOUT THIS. SO ARE WE. IN THE PROCESS OF BACKING DEL

PONTE, WE DAMAGED OUR RELATIONSHIP WITH DEPUTY PRIME MINISTER

GORAN GRANIC, ONE OF THE GOC\'S KEY DECISIONMAKERS. AS A

RESULT OF THE BOBETKO CRISIS, THOSE IN CROATIA -- INCLUDING

THOSE WITHIN THE GOC -- WHO ARGUED THAT CROATIA SHOULD HOLD

FIRM FOR A SOLUTION MORE TO ITS LIKING WERE VINDICATED. THEY

MAY BE EMBOLDENED TO TAKE THE SAME HARD, "WE KNOW BETTER,"

STANCE ON NEW ICTY INDICTMENTS OR ON OTHER THORNY ISSUES SUCH

AS REFUGEE RETURN.

14. (C) THE OTP\'S ACTIONS HAVE ENORMOUS CONSEQUENCES FOR

CROATIA, THE REGION AND US. BOBETKO\'S REAL AND SERIOUS

MEDICAL AILMENTS WERE WELL KNOWN -- A RESPECTED CROATIAN

HUMAN RIGHTS LEADER TOLD THE AMBASSADOR SHORTLY AFTER BOBETKO

WAS INDICTED THAT "NO ONE WHO KNEW BOBETKO\'S MEDICAL

CONDITION WOULD HAVE INDICTED HIM," AND WONDERED WHY DEL

PONTE DECIDED NOT TO DO SO. AS REPORTED REF D, DEL PONTE --

DESPITE GOC REQUESTS AND OTP STAFF INTENT -- PASSED UP AN

OPPORTUNITY TO INTERVIEW THE 83-YEAR OLD GENERAL BEFORE

ISSUING THE INDICTMENT, APPARENTLY FOR NO SOLID REASON.

15. (C) HARTMANN\'S UNSUBSTANTIATED STATEMENT, THE OTP\'S LACK
