THE WITNESS

2. (LOU) AMIR DIDIC (STRICTLY PROTECT), HEREAFTER

REFERRED TO AS THE WITNESS, IS A BOSNIAN MUSLIM FROM

POLANKA, A VILLAGE SOUTHWEST OF BRCKO. HE WAS BORN MAY

1, 1967. HE WAS A CIVILIAN AT THE TIME OF HIS ARREST BY

BOSNIAN SERB FORCES ON MAY 19, 1992.

THE WITNESS\'S ACCOUNT

---------------------

3. (U) AT THE TIME OF HIS ARREST, THE WITNESS WAS DRIVING

WITH HIS AUNT AND A FRIEND. HE AND HIS FRIEND WERE TAKEN

TO THE POLICE STATION IN BRCKO, WHERE THEY WAITED ONE

HOUR BEFORE BEING TAKEN WITH 15 OTHER MEN TO A WAREHOUSE

COMPLEX KNOWN AS LUKA WHICH WAS BEING USED AS A PRISON

CAMP. WHEN THEY ARRIVED, THEY WERE PUT IN A WAREHOUSE

WITH 100-150 OTHER PRISONERS WHO WERE SINGING "CETNIK"

SONGS. THEY WERE SEARCHED, AND THEIR VALUABLES WERE

TAKEN. THEY WERE TAKEN ONE BY ONE FOR INTERROGATION BY

POLICE INSPECTORS. THIS INITIAL INTERROGATION WAS

RELATIVELY NORMAL. BECAUSE WITNESS WAS FROM THE FREE

TERRITORY, HE WAS ASKED ABOUT THE ARMY OF

8. (U) WHEN THE WITNESS ARRIVED AT BATKOVIC THERE WERE

LYING FACE DOWN, THEIR HANDS BEHIND THEIR HEADS AND THEIR

HEADS SHAVED. ALL OF THEM WERE MEN EXCEPT FOR A WOMAN

NAMED SENA FROM BRCKO, WHO HAD INSISTED ON ACCOMPANYING

HER HUSBAND, A ONE-LEGGED MAN WHO HAD BEEN TORTURED.
