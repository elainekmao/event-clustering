SUBJECT: BONGO CANCELS TRIP TO HARARE AT LAST MINUTE

CLASSIFIED BY POLITICAL SECTION CHIEF MATT HARRINGTON. REASONS:

1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (U) DRC PRESIDENT JOSEPH KABILA OFFICIALLY OPENED THE 2001

HARARE AGRICULTURAL SHOW ON AUGUST 29. ACCORDING TO A PRESS

REPORT, GABONESE PRESIDENT OMAR BONGO WAS SCHEDULED TO OPEN THE

EVENT BUT CALLED OFF HIS TRIP AT SHORT NOTICE. GEORGE CHARAMBA, A

SPOKESMAN IN THE PRESIDENT\'S OFFICE, TOLD JOURNALISTS THAT BONGO

CANCELLED HIS PARTICIPATION "AT THE LAST MINUTE...BECAUSE HE WAS

COMMITTED ELSEWHERE."

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 02576 310936Z

2. (C) HENRY MUKONOWESHURO, A DIPLOMAT IN THE FOREIGN MINISTRY\'S

AMERICAS OFFICE, TOLD POLITICAL SECTION CHIEF ON AUGUST 31 THAT

BONGO HAD NEVER COMMITTED TO ATTENDING THE FAIR. IN A WRITTEN

RESPONSE DECLINING THE GOZ INVITATION, BONGO REPORTEDLY CITED

"PRIOR COMMITMENTS OF WHICH HE HAD BEEN UNAWARE."

3. (C) COMMENT: WE SUSPECT THAT BONGO, WHO IS REPORTEDLY

SENSITIVE ABOUT HIS STATURE AS AN AFRICAN STATESMAN AND MEDIATOR,

MAY WANT TO KEEP SOME DISTANCE BETWEEN HIMSELF AND MUGABE, WHOSE

IMAGE CONTINUES TO DECLINE ON THE INTERNATIONAL STAGE. BONGO\'S

"PRIOR COMMITMENT" MAY WELL BE TO HIS OWN IMAGE. WE WOULD WELCOME

EMBASSY LIBREVILLE\'S THINKING ON THIS ISSUE.

ROTH
