SUBJECT: ZAGREB UPDATE: JUNE 24, 1992

SUMMARY

-------

1. THE CROATIAN GOVERNMENT HAS APPEALED FOR FOREIGN

MILITARY INTERVENTION TO HELP STEM THE TIDE OF

CONTINUED SERB/JNA AGGRESSION. STATE COMMITTEE

CHAIRMAN RAMLJAK DISCUSSED UNPROFOR DEVELOPMENTS WITH

FORCE COMMANDER GENERAL NAMBIAR. EC REPRESENTATIVE

WOLTMANN TOLD SIBENIK RESIDENTS THAT THE VANCE PLAN

AMENDMENTS WILL BE BENEFICIAL. THE MOD HAS PROTESTED

RENEWED ATTACKS ON CROATIA, SOME OF WHICH ARE COMING

FROM UN-CONTROLLED AREAS. LATEST OFFICIAL CROATIAN

CASUALTIES INCLUDE 1,773 CIVILIANS AND 1,389 SOLDIERS

KILLED AND 20,127 PERSONS INJURED. TUDJMAN IS

EXPECTED TO ANNOUNCE NEW ELECTIONS FOR AUGUST 2. END

SUMMARY.

GOVERNMENT CALLS FOR FOREIGN MILITARY HELP

------------------------------------------

2. CROATIA ISSUED ANOTHER PROTEST JUNE 22 AGAINST

THE NEW SERB/JNA ATTACKS ON DUBROVNIK WHICH CAUSED

SIGNIFICANT DAMAGE TO HISTORICAL BUILDINGS IN THE OLD

TOWN. THE GOVERNMENT CONDEMNED THE INTENSIFIED

SHELLING OF SIBENIK, ZADAR, SLAVONSKI BROD AND THE

REGION OF POSAVINA, AS WELL AS THE CONTINUOUS ATTACKS

ON SARAJEVO. THE GOVERNMENT MADE AN APPEAL TO THE

INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY FOR A COLLECTIVE, EFFICIENT

"MILITARY INTERVENTION," BECAUSE THE PRESENT

MEASURES, CONDEMNATIONS AND SANCTIONS HAVE PROVED

INADEQUATE. ITS APPEAL WAS MOTIVATED BY THE

CONTINUING ACCUMULATION OF "VICTIMS, FAMINE, SHORTAGE

OF WATER, ELECTRICITY AND MEDICINES." THE GOVERNMENT

STATEMENT STRESSED "THE AGONY OF THE CITIZENS OF

DUBROVNIK CAUSED BY THE PROLONGED SIEGE, ISOLATION,

AND CONTINUOUS EXPOSURE TO SHELLING AND SHOOTING."

EC REPRESENTATIVE WOLTMANN DISCUSSED "PINK ZONE"

--------------------------------------------- ---

4. EC REPRESENTATIVE DIETER WOLTMANN TOLD SIBENIK

COUNTY REPRESENTATIVES JUNE 23 THAT UNPROFOR WILL

TAKE FULL RESPONSIBILITY OVER SECTOR SOUTH BY JUNE

25, IF BOTH SIDES -- CROATIAN AND JNA AUTHORITIES --

AGREE WITH AMENDMENTS TO THE VANCE PLAN WORKED OUT BY

GENERAL NAMBIAR. THESE AMENDMENTS FORESEE WITHDRAWAL

OF CANNONS TO 30 KMS FROM THE COUNTIES\' BORDERS

(UNDER THE CONTROL OF UNPROFOR), OF TANKS AND OTHER

HEAVY ARMAMENT TO 10 KMS, AND FOR INFANTRY TO 5 KMS.

THIS "TERRITORY" WOULD REPRESENT A "DEMARCATION LINE"

OF UNPROFOR CONTROL. THE ACCEPTANCE OF THIS PLAN

DOES NOT UNDERCUT CROATIAN AUTHORITY, ACCORDING TO

WOLTMANN. HE ALSO SUGGESTED A CROATIAN "AMNESTY" FOR

THOSE RESIDENTS IN THE CONTESTED AREAS NOT INVOLVED

IN WAR CRIMES.

--------------------------------------------- -----
