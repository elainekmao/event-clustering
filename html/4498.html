START MEMO PROJ-DESC:

This is a proposal for a 14-day funded voluntary

visitor program for two judges of the Court of

Appeal of Sri Lanka to observe the structure and

function of the American judicial system, including

its federal nature and its role in protecting human

rights; deepen the understanding of court

administration in particular and the criminal

justice system in the U.S. in general. It should

also include caseload management, computerization,

and the training of judges to adapt to new

technology.

START MEMO POST-OBJ:

The Court of Appeal of Sri Lanka is equivalent to

the United States Court of Appeal. The nominees

are senior judges of the Court of Appeal who are

likely to be promoted to the Supreme Court of Sri

Lanka in the very near future. This project is

keeping with the broad Mission Program Plan Goal of

\'Supporting human rights, democracy and the rule of

law in Sri Lanka.\' It also supports \'Improving

understanding of U.S. policy and its underlying

context.\' Post objectives in proposing this project

are to:

-- provide better qualified and knowledgeable judges

to serve justice without delay, thus enhancing the

faith of Sri Lankan citizens in the judicial system

and strengthening an independent judiciary as a key

element of democracy;

-- Ensure that the rule of law as practiced in the

U.S. is better understood by key members of the

judicial branch in Sri Lanka, encouraging them to

apply positive lessons learned from their U.S.

experience in administering justice in Sri Lanka;

-- provide speedier relief to victims of human

rights violations, political prisoners, detainees,

families of the disappeared and torture victims

through more efficient court system.

The two candidates nominated for this program are

well qualified both to participate fully in program

activities and to implement lessons learned upon

their return to Sri Lanka.

END MEMO POST-OBJ:

START MEMO POST-RCMD:

The two judges nominated are judges of the Court of

Appeal of Sri Lanka with wide experience in the

judicial system in Sri Lanka. However, they have

not had any exposure to the U.S. judicial system or

to the American society. Therefore, the program

should focus on the structure and function of the

American judicial system, including its federal

nature and its role in protecting human rights. The

participants should gain exposure to the use of

modern information technologies in administration of

the judicial system, caseload management and the

training of judges to adapt to new technology. The

program should also be designated to deepen their

understanding of court administration in particular

and the criminal justice system in general.

END MEMO POST-RCMD:

START MEMO NOMINATING-JUSTIFICATION:

Mr. Dissanayake has been a judicial officer since

1976. starting from the bottom of the judicial

service as a Magistrate he rose to be a District

Judge and then a High Court Judge prior to his

appointment to the Appeal Court. He is likely to be

appointed to the Supreme Court of Sri Lanka in the

very near future. A voluntary Visitor program on

American judicial system will provide the necessary

exposure for him to understand the American system

and to be familiar with the many legal aspects are

treated in U.S. courts. He could use the additional

knowledge he would acquire through this program to

push for judicial reforms necessary in Sri Lanka.

As stated previously, even after the retirement at

the age of 65 these senior judges are called in to

serve in many important commissions and other

statutory bodies and continue to play a key role in

the judicial process and rule of law.
