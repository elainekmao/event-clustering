THAT ITALY ACCEPT RAVALOMANANA AS PRESIDENT OF MADAGASCAR,

THE GOI PROVIDED A NUANCED STATEMENT INDICATING THAT IT IS

NOT YET CONVINCED THAT RAVALOMANANA CAN BE CONSIDERED THE

CONSTITUTIONALLY-ELECTED PRESIDENT. THE GOI CITES THE FACT

THAT NEITHER THE OAU NOR THE EU HAS YET RECOGNIZED HIM AS

SUCH. AFTER A COMING EU WORKING GROUP MEETING, THE GOI WILL

PROVIDE FURTHER INFORMATION ON THE UNION\'S POSITION, TO WHICH

ITS OWN WILL CONFORM. END SUMMARY.

FOREWORD

THE ITALIAN GOVERNMENT WOULD LIKE TO THANK THE GOVERNMENT OF

THE UNITED STATES FOR ITS JUNE 27TH STATEMENT. WE

ACKNOWLEDGE THE WILLINGNESS OF THE U.S. GOVERNMENT TO

RECOGNIZE PRESIDENT RAVALOMANANA, AS STATED ALSO IN THE

RECENT LETTER BY PRESIDENT BUSH.

AS A GENERAL RULE, ITALY DOES NOT FORMALLY RECOGNIZE

GOVERNMENTS BUT RATHER STATES. MOREOVER, WE DO NOT CONSIDER

THAT THE RECOGNITION STATEMENT ESTABLISHES INTERNATIONAL

LEGAL STATUS.

OAU POSITION

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 ROME 03328 090150Z

WE TOOK NOTE OF THE STATEMENT RECENTLY ISSUED BY THE MAIN OAU

CONFLICT MANAGEMENT BODY WHICH, AFTE MAKING REFERENCE TO THE

AGREEMENTS REACHED DURIN THE TWO DAKAR MEETINGS, EMPHASIZES

THE NEED TO ROCEED WITH NEW PRESIDENTIAL ELECTIONS AND TO

DEELOP TEMPORARY INSTITUTIONS AGREEABLE TO BOTH PARTES. IN

THIS STATEMENT, THE PRINCIPLES OF THE UNTY AND TERRITORIAL

INTEGRITY OF MADAGASCAR ARE RESSERTED, AS WELL AS THE

PRINCIPLE OF NON-ACCEPTACE OF ANY CHANGE OF GOVERNMENT MADE

IN VIOLATIONOF THE CONSTITUTIONAL NORMS OF A COUNTRY.

FINALL, THE STATEMENT ASSERTS THAT THE DECEMBER 16, 2001

ELECTIONS IN MADAGASCAR DID NOT LEAD TO THE ESTABISHMENT OF

A LEGITIMATE GOVERNMENT AND THAT NEITER RAVALOMANANA NOR

RATSIRAKA IS ADMITTED AS THECOUNTRY,S REPRESENTATIVE AT THE

OAU.

ITALIAN OSITION

AS A GENERAL RULE, WE GIVE FULL CONSIDERATION TO OAU

RESOLUTIONS ON MAJOR ISSUES CONCERNING THE AFRICAN CONTINENT.

WE ALSO APPRECIATE THE NECESSITY OF PREVENTING A FURTHER

DETERIORATION OF THE SITUATION AND OF CONTAINING THE VIOLENCE

SPREADING IN MADAGASCAR. HOWEVER, WE BELIEVE THAT THE

PRINCIPLE OF CONSTITUTIONAL LEGITIMACY, WHICH IS THE BASIS OF

CIVIL COEXISTENCE AND ONE OF THE ACCOMPLISHMENTS OF THE OAU

(1999 OAU SUMMIT IN ALGIERS), SHOULD BE SAFEGUARDED AS WELL.

AT THE MADAGASCAR NATIONAL DAY, WHICH TOOK PLACE JUNE 26,

ITALY WAS REPRESENTED BY ITS HONORARY CONSUL GENERAL IN

ANTANANARIVO, IN SPITE OF ITS DECISION NOT TO SEND A

CONGRATULATORY MESSAGE FROM THE PRESIDENT OF THE REPUBLIC.

EUROPEAN UNION POSITION

THE POSITION OF ITALY WILL COMPLY WITH THAT OF THE EUROPEAN

UNION. FOR OUR PART, WE WILL HELP DRAFT THE UNION,S

POSITION DURING THE FORTHCOMING AFRICAN WORKING GROUP

MEETING, WHICH IS SCHEDULED FOR JULY 9 AND 10. AFTER THAT

MEETING, WE WILL INFORM YOU OF ANY EUROPEAN DECISION TAKEN.

END TEXT.
