SUBJECT: VP MSIKA: BLAMES OTHERS FOR VIOLENCE AND

ECONOMIC PROBLEMS, BUT SOME ENCOURAGING WORDS ON LAND

REF: A) HARARE 4695 B) STATE 144224

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR MCDONALD. REASONS: 1.5 (B) AND

(D).

SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) AT THE DEPARTMENT\'S INSTRUCTION, AMBASSADOR

EXPRESSED STRONG U.S. CONCERNS ABOUT THE FARM OCCUPATIONS

AND CONTINUING RURAL VIOLENCE TO VICE PRESIDENT MSIKA ON

AUGUST 25. MSIKA BLAMED COMMERCIAL FARMERS AND THE

OPPOSITION MDC FOR INCITING MUCH OF THE PRE-ELECTION

VIOLENCE AND INSISTED THAT, IN OCCUPYING FARMS, MOST WAR

VETERANS HAD MERELY ENGAGED IN TRESPASSING, WHICH WAS NOT

A SERIOUS CRIME. THE CABINET HAD AGREED THAT NO NEW FARM

OCCUPATIONS WOULD BE PERMITTED, AND THAT WAR VETERANS

SHOULD BE MOVED TO FARMS IDENTIFIED FOR COMPULSORY

ACQUISITION. HE ACKNOWLEDGED, HOWEVER, THAT PRESIDENT

MUGABE WAS NOT IN AGREEMENT THAT VETERANS SHOULD BE

REMOVED FROM CURRENTLY-OCCUPIED FARMS ON AN EXPEDITED

BASIS. MSIKA SAID ZIMBABWE BORE ONLY SOME OF THE

RESPONSIBILITY FOR ITS ECONOMIC PROBLEMS AND SAID THE

GOVERNMENT\'S MILLENIUM ECONOMIC RECOVERY PLAN OUGHT TO

GET THE ECONOMY BACK ON TRACK. IN THE MEANTIME, THE GOZ

HAD DEVALUED THE CURRENCY AND WAS PLANNING TO REDUCE THE

SIZE OF THE CIVIL SERVICE AND CUT GOVERNMENT EXPENDITURES

-------------

U.S. CONCERNS

-------------

2. (C) AMBASSADOR, ACCOMPANIED BY USAID DIRECTOR AND

POLOFF, CALLED ON VICE-PRESIDENT MSIKA AUGUST 25 TO

DELIVER REF B DEMARCHE ON CONTINUING VIOLENCE AND THE

GOVERNMENT\'S LAND RESETTLEMENT PROGRAM, AS PRESIDENT

MUGABE WAS UNAVAILABLE. AFTER PROVIDING MSIKA WITH A

NON-PAPER CONTAINING REFTEL TALKING POINTS, THE

AMBASSADOR UNDERSCORED CONTINUING U.S. CONCERN OVER

VIOLENCE AND FARM OCCUPATIONS, WHICH HAD DAMAGED

ZIMBABWE\'S STANDING IN THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY. HE

DESCRIBED THE EVICTIONS OF WAR VETERANS FROM SEVERAL

FARMS EARLIER IN THE WEEK (REF A) AS A POSITIVE

DEVELOPMENT, BUT NOTED THAT VIOLENCE AND INTIMIDATION

WERE CONTINUING IN MANY AREAS. THE UNITED STATES

RECOGNIZED THE NEED FOR LAND REFORM IN ZIMBABWE AND

WANTED TO BE HELPFUL, BUT COULD NOT UNDER THESE

CONDITIONS.

---------------------

PRE-ELECTION VIOLENCE

---------------------

3. (C) THE PRE-ELECTION VIOLENCE, MSIKA CLAIMED, WAS NOT

PAGE 04 HARARE 04750 01 OF 03 251458Z

MOSTLY OF THE GOVERNMENT\'S MAKING. HE LAID MUCH OF THE

BLAME ON COMMERCIAL FARMERS, WHOM HE SAID HAD RENEGED ON

THEIR COMMITMENTS AT THE SEPTEMBER 1998 DONORS CONFERENCE

ON LAND REFORM, AND SUBSEQUENTLY FORMED MILITIAS TO BLOCK

THE GOVERNMENT\'S RESETTLEMENT EFFORT. HE ALSO BLAMED THE

OPPOSITION MOVEMENT FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE (MDC), WHICH HE

SAID WAS LAUNCHED NOT FROM ZIMBABWE BUT BY "OUTSIDE

FORCES."

4. (C) THE VICE PRESIDENT INSISTED THAT THE FARM

OCCUPATIONS PRIOR TO THE ELECTIONS HAD BEEN MOTIVATED NOT

BY POLITICAL CONSIDERATIONS BUT BY GENUINE ANGER AGAINST

FARMERS AND THE GOVERNMENT FOR THE SLOW PACE OF

RESETTLEMENT. (NB. IN PRIOR CONVERSATIONS WITH THE

AMBASSADOR, MSIKA ACKNOWLEDGED THAT POLITICS HAD PLAYED A

ROLE IN THE OCCUPATIONS. END NOTE) THE DONORS\'

SUSPENSION OF ASSISTANCE IN LIGHT OF THE OCCUPATIONS,

MSIKA CLAIMED, EMBOLDENED THE FARMERS TO "ENGINEER"

VIOLENT CONFRONTATIONS WITH THE WAR VETERANS, EVEN THOUGH

THE VETERANS HAD BEEN TOLD TO BE PEACEFUL AND NOT TO

INTERFERE WITH FARMING OPERATIONS. THE GOVERNMENT WOULD

HAVE BEEN IRRESPONSIBLE IN ORDERING THE POLICE TO EVICT

THE LARGE NUMBERS OF WAR VETERANS, AS THIS WOULD HAVE

RESULTED IN WIDESPREAD BLOODSHED. IN ANY CASE, MSIKA

SAID, MOST OF THE WAR VETERANS WERE MERELY TRESPASSING,

WHICH IN HIS VIEW WAS NOT A SERIOUS CRIME.

5. (C) MSIKA INSISTED THAT HE DOES NOT CONDONE

LAWLESSNESS AND CRITICIZED THOSE WAR VETERANS WHO HAD

INTERFERED WITH FARMING OPERATIONS OR BUILT STRUCTURES ON

FARMS THEY HAD OCCUPIED. MSIKA SAID HE HAD ARGUED IN

CABINET THAT THE VETERANS MUST BE MOVED AS SOON AS

POSSIBLE TO FARMS IDENTIFIED FOR ACQUISITION, AS THE

LONGER THIS PROCESS DRAGS ON, THE MORE DIFFICULT IT WILL

BE TO MOVE THEM. HE ACKNOWLEDGED, HOWEVER, THAT

PRESIDENT MUGABE DID NOT SHARE THE CABINET\'S VIEW.

6. (C) THE AMBASSADOR SAID HE DISAGREED WITH MSIKA\'S

CLAIM THAT THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS HAD ENGINEERED THE

VIOLENCE. ON THE CONTRARY, THE EVIDENCE CONFIRMED THAT

ZANU-PF BORE THE REPONSIBILITY FOR THE VAST MAJORITY OF

PRE-ELECTION VIOLENCE. MSIKA DISAGREED, SAYING THAT

ZANU-PF ONLY HAD RESPONDED TO PROVOCATIONS. TALK OF

FARMER MILITIAS IS NONSENSE. BOTH HE AND MSIKA KNEW, THE

AMBASSADOR SAID, THAT MANY OF THE SO-CALLED WAR VETERANS

WERE TOO YOUNG TO HAVE FOUGHT IN THE LIBERATION STRUGGLE,

AND CLEARLY HAD BEEN SENT TO THE FARMS TO INTIMIDATE AND

BEAT THE FARMERS AND THEIR WORKERS. IF PRESIDENT MUGABE

HAD MADE IT CLEAR FROM THE BEGINNING THAT VIOLENCE AND

INTIMIDATION WERE UNACCEPTABLE AND POLICE HAD BEEN

ALLOWED TO DO THEIR JOB, MOST OF THE LAWLESSNESS COULD

HAVE BEEN AVOIDED.

---------------------

ECONOMIC DIFFICULTIES

---------------------

7. (C) MSIKA CLAIMED THAT ZIMBABWE DID NOT BEAR FULL

RESPONSIBILITY FOR ITS ECONOMIC PROBLEMS. THE GOZ

PERHAPS WAS TO BLAME FOR SPENDING MORE THAN IT SHOULD

HAVE ON RURAL DEVELOPMENT AND ON ITS MILITARY INVOLVEMENT

IN THE DROC, WHICH HE HOPED WOULD END SOON. HOWEVER, HE

CRITICIZED THE UNITED KINGDOM FOR ADVOCATING THE

IMPOSITION OF EU SANCTIONS AGAINST ZIMBABWE, AND FORMER

AF ASSISTANT SECRETARY CHESTER CROCKER FOR DOING THE SAME

THING IN THE UNITED STATES. THE AMBASSADOR NOTED THAT

CROCKER WAS A PRIVATE CITIZEN REFLECTING HIS OWN VIEWS

AND DID NOT SPEAK ON BEHALF OF THE U.S. GOVERNMENT.

HE EMPHASIZED THAT THE USG WAS NOT SEEKING TO PUNISH THE

GOZ, BUT IS VERY CONCERNED ABOUT DEVELOPMENTS HERE.

MSIKA ASKED ABOUT THE ZIMBABWE DEMOCRACY ACT (ZDA), WHICH

HE SAID WOULD PUSH ZIMBABWE IN THE WRONG DIRECTION. THE

AMBASSADOR RESPONDED THAT THE EMBASSY SHARES THE CONCERNS

OF THE U.S. CONGRESS ABOUT FARM OCCUPATIONS AND THE

ACCOMPANYING VIOLENCE.

PRESIDENT REPLIED THAT THE GOZ\'S MILLENIUM ECONOMIC

RECOVERY PLAN SHOULD GET THE ECONOMY BACK ON THE RIGHT

10. (C) THE GOVERNMENT\'S RESETTLEMENT EFFORT, MSIKA

CONTINUED, WOULD BE "QUICKER AND SMOOTHER" WITH OUTSIDE

ASSISTANCE, BUT WOULD BE "ROUGHER AND SLOWER" WITHOUT

SUCH AID. THE DONORS ARE WILLING TO BE INVOLVED, THE

AMBASSADOR REPLIED, BUT EMPHASIZED THAT THE VIOLENCE MUST

END, RULE OF LAW MUST BE RESTORED, AND COURT ORDERS

RELATED TO THE ILLEGAL INVASIONS MUST BE ENFORCED.

ADDITIONALLY, THE PROCESS MUST BE FAIR AND TRANSPARENT,

AND FARMOWNERS MUST BE COMPENSATED FOR THEIR LAND.

ZIMBABWE\'S LAND POLICY IS NOT A "NORMAL" ONE, MSIKA SAID,

RECALLING THAT THE LIBERATION WAR HAD BEEN FOUGHT

PRIMARILY OVER THE CONCENTRATION OF LAND IN THE HANDS OF

A FEW.

11. (C) THE USAID DIRECTOR NOTED THAT DONORS HAVE

DISCUSSED INFORMALLY HOW THEY MIGHT REENGAGE ON LAND

REFORM, AND UNDER WHAT CONDITIONS. AMONG THE HURDLES TO

REENGAGEMENT WAS THE GOZ\'S CUMBERSOME INSTITUTIONAL

FRAMEWORK. WOULD THE GOZ BE WILLING TO SET UP A NEW,

MORE STREAMLINED STRUCTURE TO WORK WITH THE DONORS ON

LAND REFORM? THE VICE-PRESIDENT AGREED TO LOOK INTO THAT

POSSIBILITY.

COMMENT

-------

12. (C) MSIKA\'S COMMENTS LAYING THE PREDOMINANT BLAME ON

OTHERS FOR ZIMBABWE\'S ECONOMIC MESS AND THE PRE-ELECTION

VIOLENCE WERE MERELY THE LATEST IN A COMMON REFRAIN FROM

THIS GOVERNMENT. THESE ARE ALL FROM THE SAME SCRIPT. ON

LAND, WE WERE ENCOURAGED BY MSIKA\'S REPORT OF CABINET

AGREEMENT THAT NO NEW INVASIONS WILL BE PERMITTED AND

THAT WAR VETERANS SHOULD BE REMOVED FROM CURRENTLY-

OCCUPIED FARMS, EXCEPT FROM THOSE WHICH HAVE BEEN LISTED.

PRESIDENT MUGABE IS CLEARLY UNCOMFORTABLE WITH REMOVING

THE VETERANS, HOWEVER, AND HIS DISCOMFORT LIKELY WAS NOT

ASSUAGED BY THE AGGRESSIVE TACTICS EMPLOYED BY POLICE

EARLIER THIS WEEK IN EVICTING SOME VETERANS FROM FARMS ON

HARARE\'S OUTSKIRTS. IN A SEPARATE CONVERSATION WITH THE

AMBASSADOR, INTERNATIONAL TRADE AND INDUSTRY MINISTER

NKOSANA MOYO SURMISED THAT LOCAL POLICE OFFICERS, IN

CAHOOTS WITH THE WAR VETERANS, MIGHT HAVE USED SUCH

TACTICS INTENTIONALLY IN ORDER TO COMPLICATE THE GOZ\'S

ABILITY TO PROCEED WITH ADDITIONAL EVICTIONS.

MEANWHILE, ACCORDING TO THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION

(CFU), THE EVICTIONS ARE CONTINUING. THE CFU WAS TOLD BY

THE DEPUTY POLICE COMMISSIONER ON AUGUST 25 THAT THE

POLICE WOULD CONTINUE TO REMOVE VETERANS FROM OCCUPIED

FARMS UNLESS ORDERED OTHERWISE.

13. (C) MSIKA\'S COMMENTS ON THE ECONOMIC WAY FORWARD

FRANKLY DID NOT INSPIRE MUCH CONFIDENCE THAT THE

GOVERNMENT WILL BE ABLE TO EXTRICATE ITSELF FROM THE
