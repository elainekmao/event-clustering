SUBJECT: JORDAN: EMBASSY SCIENCE FELLOW SELECTION

Ref: A) 2001 SECSTATE 215701 B) AMMAN 598

1. Embassy Amman has reviewed the three candidates for the

Embassy Science Fellow Program that were forwarded to us by

OES and has chosen Dr. Sylvana Li of the USDA/FAS as our

first choice. Dr. Li\'s background suits her well to the

job. Not only is her expertise in water quality and

resource management, she is already working with the USGS

and EPA to showcase the latest technologies in wastewater

reuse in agriculture. Dr. Li also plans to use the contacts

she makes this summer in Amman to develop future projects

involving Jordan, Israel, and the Palestinians, advancing

our regional environment agenda.

2. We understand that Dr. Li would be available to work at

Embassy Amman for the month of August. During her

fellowship she will be working with the Ministries of Water

and Agriculture, offering expertise and technical advice on

water quality issues for agricultural applications.

3. Post has identified housing from the embassy housing

pool to accommodate Dr. Li. Dr. Li will be given an office

in which she will have a telephone and computer with email

access.

4. Complying with the provisions of the Embassy Science

Fellow program (ref A), post will offer Dr. Li access to

post medical services, CLO, APO, check cashing, and the

commissary/recreational association, during her official

stay in Amman.

5. Post point of contact for Dr. Li is Regional

Environment Officer Paul Malik, who can be reached by phone

at 962-6-592-0101 ext 2621, or via email at

malikp(at)state.gov

6. Post looks forward to welcoming Dr. Li to Amman later

this summer.
