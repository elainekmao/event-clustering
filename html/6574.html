SUBJECT: MEDIA REACTION AND PUBLIC OPINION: WAR AGAINST IRAQ

1. SUMMARY: THE INITIATION OF MILITARY ACTION AGAINST IRAQ

ENVELOPED PRINT AND SATELLITE TV COVERAGE IN THE UAE MEDIA. ABU

DHABI TV IS REPORTING THE LATEST DEVELOPMENTS LIVE FROM BAGHDAD,

WASHINGTON, AND OTHER PLACES IN THE REGION. ABU DHABI TV ALSO

CARRIED LIVE SADDAM HUSSEIN\'S MARCH 20TH SPEECH AND PRESIDENT

BUSH\'S MARCH 19TH ADDRESS TO THE NATION EXPLAINING THE ONSET OF

MILITARY ACTION. AN "AL-KHALEEJ" EDITORIAL NOTES THAT IRAQ IS

ONLY THE BEGINNING AND OTHER ARAB CAPITALS WILL FOLLOW.

MEANWHILE, AN "AKHBAR AL-ARAB" EDITORIAL BLAMES SADDAM FOR THE

SUFFERING OF IRAQIS AND THE WAR. END SUMMARY.

2. UNDER THE HEADLINE \'A ROAD MAP IS PENETRATING ARAB CAPITALS,"

SHARJAH-BASED PAN-ARAB DAILY "AL-KHALEEJ" EDITORIALIZED ON 3/20:

"THE QUESTION NOW IS NOT WHETHER OR NOT THE WAR WILL START, BUT

WHAT COMES AFTER IT AND HOW LONG WILL .S. TROOPS STAY IN IRAQ?

THE MOST DANGEROUS QUESTION IS THE LEVEL OF COORDINATION BETWEEN

WASHINGTON AND TEL AVIV IN DRAWING NEW GEOPOLITICAL MAPS AND

RESHAPING THE FUTURE OF THE REGION. IT HAS BECOME KNOWN THAT

ONCE U.S. TROOPS ENTER IRAQ THEY WILL NOT DEPART, WHICH HAS BEEN

CONFIRMED BY NUMEROUS AMERICAN OFFICIALS UNDER THE PRETEXT OF

CEMENTING PEACE AND STABILITY, THE ELIMINATION OF WMD, AND

CURTAILING ANY ETHNIC DISPUTES ... IRAQ WILL ONLY BE THE STARTING

POINT TO RE-MAPPING THE MIDDLE EAST, AND THERE IS NO DOUBT THAT

ISRAEL WILL PLAY A FUNDAMENTAL ROLE IN THIS ... STATEMENTS OF

AMERICAN OFFICIALS CLARIFY THAT THE WAR AGAINST IRAQ IS ONLY THE

FIRST STEP. THE LIST OF NATIONS AND ORGANIZATIONS WHICH THE U.S.

INSISTS ON LABELING AS \'TERRORISTS\' IS EXTREMELY LONG, EXCEPT FOR

ISRAEL. THEREFORE, THE AMERICAN MILITARY PRESENCE WAS NOT MEANT

TO \'LIBERATE\' IRAQIS, BUT TO ACHIEVE A NEW AMERICAN STRATEGY IN

THE REGION..."

3. ABU DHABI-BASED PAN-ARAB DAILY "AKHBAR AL-ARAB" 3/20

EDITORIALIZED:

"...THE U.S. AND A HANDFUL OF ITS ALLIES INSIST ON DECLARING WAR

IN THE NEXT FEW HOURS, WHILE ON THE OTHER SIDE, THE REGIME IN

IRAQ REJECTS SHOULDERING ITS HISTORICAL RESPONSIBILITY BEFORE GOD

AND THE ENTIRE NATION AND INSISTS ON RETAINING POWER UNTIL THE

LAST IRAQI CITIZEN ... WE HAVE TO ADMIT THAT WE ARE FACING A

VICIOUS POWER WHICH HAS ITS OWN AGENDA ... AND THE RIGHT THING TO

DO IN SUCH A SITUATION IS TO SAVE WHAT CAN BE SAVED AND NOT

SACRIFICE THE NATION FOR THE SAKE OF A FEW INDIVIDUALS,

ESPECIALLY WHEN THEY THEMSELVES HAVE PROVIDED A JUSTIFICATION FOR

AN UNPRECEDENTED INTERVENTION IN THE REGION\'S AFFAIRS ..."

PUBLIC OPINION READ-OUT

4. THE GENERAL MOOD IN THE UAE IS SIMILAR TO THAT ELSEWHERE IN

THE REGION. MANY EXPRESS FEELINGS OF FRUSTRATION AND DEEP

SADNESS ABOUT WHAT THEY VIEW AS A TRAGIC ENDING TO THE CRISIS.

MOST OF POST\'S INTERLOCUTORS ARE CONVINCED THAT WAR HAS BECOME

INEVITABLE AS A RESULT OF SADDAM HUSSEIN\'S STUBBORNNESS AND

REFUSAL TO STEP DOWN TO AVERT A DESTRUCTIVE WAR AGAINST IRAQ.

THIS BELIEF IS VERY STRONG IN THE UAE AND REFLECTS THE OFFICIAL

LINE OF THE UAEG FOLLOWING THE INITIATIVE IT PROPOSED DURING THE

SHARM EL-SHEIKH OIC SUMMITS. HOWEVER, THE UAE PUBLIC IS

EXTREMELY CONCERNED ABOUT THE CONSEQUENCES OF THE WAR FOR OTHER

ARAB AND NEIGHBORING NATIONS AND FOR THE REGION AS A WHOLE. THE

MAIN CONCERN HERE IS WORRY FOR THE IRAQI PEOPLE AND POSSIBLE

CIVILIAN CASUALTIES DURING THE WAR.

5. ONE UAE PROFESSOR OF POLITICAL SCIENCE SUMMED UP THE POPULAR

MOOD THUS: "EVERYBODY IS CONFUSED. HOW TO REMOVE SADDAM WITHOUT

WAR? WAS IT RIGHT TO LAUNCH A WAR? NOBODY IS HAPPY WITH THE WAR,

NOBODY. WE ARE THINKING ABOUT THE IRAQI PEOPLE AND HOW TO HELP

THEM. WE HOPE THAT SOMETHING WILL HAPPEN TO SADDAM AT THE LAST

MINUTE. I WAS NOT FOR PROLONGING THE INSPECTIONS, BUT I AM VERY

CONCERNED ABOUT CIVILIAN CASUALTIES. IF THERE ARE NOT TOO MANY OF

THEM, THE US WON\'T MAKE TOO MANY ENEMIES IN THE ARAB WORLD."
