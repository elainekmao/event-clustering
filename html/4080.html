SUBJECT: WFP BOARD DISCUSSION OF BIOTECH FOOD

1. SUMMARY: DURING THE OCTOBER 21, 2002 DISCUSSION OF

BIOTECH ENGINEERED FOOD AID AT THE WORLD FOOD PROGRAM, EU

MEMBERS OF THE EXECUTIVE BOARD ARGUED THAT WORKABLE

ALTERNATIVES TO BIOTECH ENGINEERED FOOD AID MUST BE FOUND

AND THAT THE SOVEREIGN RIGHT OF COUNTRIES TO ACCEPT OR

REJECT BIOTECH FOOD AID MUST BE RESPECTED. THE EU ARGUED

IN FAVOR OF IMPLEMENTING "ADVANCE INFORMED AGREEMENT"

BEFORE THE ENTRY INTO FORCE OF THE CARTAGENA PROTOCOL.

THE UNITED STATES SPOKE MOST FORCEFULLY ON BEHALF OF

THOSE WHO ARE IN DANGER OF STARVING (WITH SUPPORT FROM

CANADA AND JAPAN). THE G-77 COUNTRIES STATED THAT THEY

HAD NOT HAD ADEQUATE TIME TO CONSIDER THIS COMPLEX ISSUE

AND WOULD HAVE TO CONSULT THEIR CAPITALS. THE DUTCH, WHO

HAD REQUESTED THE ITEM BE CONSIDERED BY THE BOARD, PUSHED

FOR A STRONGER DECISION FROM THE BOARD THAN THE ONE

ADOPTED (TO "TAKE NOTE" OF THE PAPER AND "LOOK FORWARD"

TO FOLLOW UP MEASURES.) THE OFFICERS OF THE EXECUTIVE

BOARD WILL LIKELY MEET TO DECIDE WHAT TO DO BEFORE THE

NEXT EXECUTIVE BOARD MEETING IN FEBRUARY. END SUMMARY.

2. AT THE REQUEST OF THE NETHERLANDS DELEGATION, THE WFP

EXECUTIVE BOARD HAD BEFORE IT AN INFORMATION DOCUMENT

OUTLINING WFP POLICY CONCERNING BIOTECH ENGINEERED FOOD

AID. THE ENSUING DISCUSSION ON OCTOBER 21, 2002 REVEALED

SERIOUS CONFUSION AND CONCERNS AMONG BOTH OECD DONOR AND

RECIPIENT COUNTRIES CONCERNING THE HEALTH AND

ENVIRONMENTAL EFFECT OF BIOTECH ENGINEERED FOOD.

3. THE DUTCH, IN THEIR INTRODUCTORY REMARKS,

ACKNOWLEDGED THAT WFP WAS NOT THE PLACE TO DISCUSS FOOD

SAFETY NOR TRADE, BUT ARGUED THAT IT WAS THE FORUM TO

DISCUSS FOOD AID. THEY EMPHASIZED THE RIGHT OF RECIPIENT

COUNTRIES TO ACCEPT OR REFUSE FOOD AID ON THE BASIS OF

"INFORMED CONSENT" AND CALLED FOR PROVIDING WORKABLE

ALTERNATIVES TO BIOTECH FOOD AID. NORWAY AND BELGIUM

ANNOUNCED THEY WOULD RESTRICT CASH CONTRIBUTIONS TO

PURCHASE NON-GMO FOOD ONLY. SWITZERLAND NOTED THAT EVEN

IF FOOD WAS SAFE TO CONSUME, THE EFFECT ON BIODIVERSITY

WAS STILL UNCLEAR AND ADVOCATED MILLING.

4. THE EC REPRESENTATIVE SAID THAT HE REALIZED THAT NON-

ACCEPTANCE POSED LOGISTICAL PROBLEMS SUCH THAT FOOD MIGHT

NOT ARRIVE IN TIME TO SAVE LIVES. HOWEVER, GOVERNMENTS

SHOULD BE ALLOWED TO APPLY THE PRECAUTIONARY PRINCIPLE TO

PROTECT THEMSELVES AGAINST ADVERSE EFFECTS ON THE

ENVIRONMENT FROM BIOTECH MAIZE (PARTICULARLY BULK FOOD

AID BEING USED AS SEEDS WHICH WAS LIKELY TO CONTAMINATE

LOCAL VARIETIES WHICH WERE "BETTER ADAPTED.") THE EC

ASKED DONORS TO GIVE CASH TO FUND LOCAL PROCUREMENT OF

FOOD AID. THEY ALSO CALLED FOR IMPLEMENTATION OF THE

ADVANCE INFORMED AGREEMENT PROVISION OF THE CARTAGENA

PROTOCOL "BEFORE IT ENTERED INTO FORCE." AT THE SAME

TIME, THE EC REPRESENTATIVES ACKNOWLEDGED THAT THE EU

ALLOWS THE IMPORT OF GM MAIZE FOR FOOD AND INTENTIONAL

INTRODUCTION INTO THE ENVIRONMENT, AND DOES NOT BAN THE

IMPORT OF ANIMALS FED ON GM MAIZE.

5. THE U.S. PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVE, AMBASSADOR TONY

HALL, SPOKE FORCEFULLY IN FAVOR OF ACCEPTING BIOTECH

ENGINEERED FOOD AID TO PREVENT IMPENDING DISASTER IN

SOUTHERN AFRICA. HE RECOUNTED THAT HE HAD VISITED THE

SUB-REGION LAST WEEK AND WITNESSED FIRST-HAND HOW

DESPERATE THE SITUATION WAS. AMBASSADOR HALL STATED THAT

BECAUSE INTERNATIONAL BODIES WERE DEBATING GM FOOD IT WAS

CREATING FEAR AND CAUSING DELAYS IN GETTING ASSISTANCE TO

THE HUNGRY AND AS A RESULT, PEOPLE WOULD DIE. AMBASSADOR

HALL POINTED OUT THAT THE UNITED STATES HAD PREDICTED

THERE WOULD BE A CRISIS AND RESPONDED IN A TIMELY WAY

WITH DONATIONS OF TONS OF FOOD THAT IS EATEN BY MILLIONS

EVERY DAY IN THE UNITED STATES AND ELSEWHERE. THIS WAS

FOOD THAT HAD BEEN SUBJECTED TO RIGOROUS TESTING FOR

SAFETY AND WHICH POSED NO KNOWN RISK TO HUMAN HEALTH.

RATHER, BIOTECH FOOD OFFERED HOPE FOR THE FUTURE OF

AGRICULTURE.

7. AMBASSADOR HALL MENTIONED THAT NO ONE HE MET IN

SOUTHERN AFRICA MENTIONED BIOTECH -- NOT NGO\'S, OR

GOVERNMENT OFFICIALS, LET ALONE POTENTIAL BENEFICIARIES.

HE CLOSED BY NOTING THAT BOTH ACTION AND INACTION HAVE

CONSEQUENCES IN SUCH A SITUATION.

8. THE FEW REPRESENTATIVES OF THE G-77 THAT SPOKE VOICED

THEIR FRUSTRATION AT BEING CAUGHT IN THIS DILEMMA. IRAN,

WHO CHAIRS THE G-77, SAID THE RECIPIENTS DON\'T KNOW IF

THERE IS A RISK OR NOT, THAT PEOPLE ARE FACED WITH THE

"CHOICE BETWEEN STARVING NOW OR DYING IN SIX MONTHS TIME

IF THEY CONSUME GMO\'S." HE CALLED ON WFP TO BRING IN

EXPERTS TO INFORM THE MEMBERSHIP. ZIMBABWE, WHO SPEAKS

FOR THE AFRICAN REGIONAL GROUP, SAID THE ISSUE WAS TOO

SENSITIVE TO DECIDE WITHOUT CONSULTING WITH CAPITALS.

9. COMMENT: AS EXPECTED, THE DISCUSSION OF BIOTECH

ENGINEERED FOOD AID DEGENERATED INTO A DISCUSSION OF THE

"RISKS" -- TO HUMAN HEALTH AND THE ENVIRONMENT -- BY A

BODY THAT IS NOT EQUIPPED OR MANDATED TO HANDLE QUESTIONS

OF RISK ANALYSIS AND RISK MANAGEMENT. THE EU COUNTRIES

APPEAR TO BE LINING UP BEHIND THE "SOLUTION" OF

IMPLEMENTATION OF THE CARTAGENA PROTOCOL\'S "ADVANCE

INFORMED AGREEMENT" PROVISIONS PRIOR TO ENTRY INTO FORCE
