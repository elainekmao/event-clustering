1. (C) SUMMARY. THIS COMPANION PIECE TO REF A PROVIDES

ADDITIONAL BACKGROUND, SUGGESTED TALKING POINTS FOR SENIOR

WASHINGTON OFFICIALS, AND POST ACTION STRATEGY IN RESPONSE TO

THE DELIBERATELY MISLEADING WHITE PAPER ON BIOTECHNOLOGY PUT

IN CIRCULATION BY THE ITALIAN MINISTRY OF AGRICULTURE.

AMBASSADOR AND EMBOFFS HAVE STRONGLY CRITICIZED THE WHITE

PAPER AT HIGH LEVELS OF THE GOI AND WILL CONTINUE TO DO SO.

USING TALKING POINTS CONTAINED PARA 15, WE RECOMMEND THAT

SENIOR USG OFFICIALS DIRECTLY CONFRONT KEY GOI LEADERS ON THE

PAPER\'S MOST SERIOUS MISSTATEMENTS -- THAT BIOTECH IS

"POLLUTION," THAT U.S.-ORIGIN SEEDS ARE FLAWED, THAT BIOTECH

MAY CAUSE THE NEXT BSE-TYPE EPIDEMIC, AND MORE. BECAUSE SOME

VARIETY OF POLICY PAPER INEVITABLY WILL BE INTRODUCED IN

PARLIAMENT, THE USG SHOULD TRY TO PERSUADE THE GOI TO DRAFT A

DOCUMENT BASED ON SOUND SCIENCE RATHER THAN SCARE-MONGERING.

2. (SBU) AS DETAILED REF B, THE ITALIAN AGRICULTURE MINISTRY

IS CIRCULATING AN UNDATED AND UNSIGNED WHITE PAPER ENTITLED

"TRANSGENIC ORGANISMS AND AGRICULTURE: A TIME FOR CHOICES"

ULTIMATELY INTENDED TO STIMULATE BIOTECH DEBATE IN

PARLIAMENT. ANNOUNCING ON ITS SECOND OF 37 PAGES THAT THE EU

IS SLOWLY LIBERALIZING RULES FOR USE OF "TRANSGENIC

ORGANISMS," THE PAPER SOUNDS AN ALARM THAT ITALY\'S CONTINUED

DELAY IN DEBATING BIOTECHNOLOGY COULD LEAD BY DEFAULT TO A

PROGRESSIVE PENETRATION OF THE ITALIAN MARKET BY TRANSGENIC

PRODUCTS. THE PAPER URGES IMMEDIATE DEBATE BEFORE THE

QUESTION BECOMES MOOT AND ITALIANS HAVE BEEN DEPRIVED OF

FREEDOM OF CHOICE ON AN ISSUE WITH PROFOUND ETHICAL,

SANITARY, AND ECONOMIC REPERCUSSIONS.

3. (SBU) THE PAPER ALLEGES THAT EFFORTS TO DATE TO CALMLY

EVALUATE THE POTENTIAL RISKS AND BENEFITS OF BIOTECHNOLOGY IN

ITALY HAVE RESULTED ONLY IN IDEOLOGICAL POLARIZATION.

6. (C) THE PAPER WINDS THROUGH A MURKY MIXTURE OF DERIVATIVE

QUESTIONS OFFERED FOR DEBATE AND PUTATIVE SCIENTIFIC AXIOMS

THAT PROGRESSIVELY STEER THE DISCUSSION TOWARDS ANTI-BIOTECH

POLITICAL CONCLUSIONS AND AWAY FROM SCIENCE-BASED DEBATE.

THE SUBSECTION ON THE ITALIAN FOOD SYSTEM IS ILLUSTRATIVE.

THE SUBSECTION FIRST SUGGESTS, RATHER NEUTRALLY, THAT "THE

QUESTION POSED IS WHETHER AND IN WHAT MEASURE THE DECISION TO

IMPROVE QUALITY CAN BE COMPATIBLE WITH USE OF TRANSGENIC

ORGANISMS." IN CASE THE READER HAS DIFFICULTY ANSWERING THE

QUESTION, THE PAPER OFFERS A LESS SUBTLE REPHRASING: "WE MUST

CALMLY EVALUATE WHETHER THE IMAGE AND CREDIBILITY OF OUR FOOD

SYSTEM CAN BE DAMAGED BY THE PRESENCE OF TRANSGENIC ORGANISMS

THAT, LEAVING ASIDE CONSIDERATION OF NEGATIVE AFFECTS ON

HEALTH AND FOOD QUALITY, CARRY A STIGMA OF INDUSTRIAL

ENGINEERING THAT APPEARS TO BE IN COMPLETE CONFLICT WITH THE

WORLD\'S IMAGE OF OUR FOOD PRODUCTS." FOR THE TRULY DENSE,

THE PAPER PROVIDES A SLEDGEHAMMER THIRD FORMULATION: "THIS

EVALUATION MUST, MOREOVER, TAKE ACCOUNT OF THE COLLECTIVE

INTEREST IN PRESERVING THE BIOLOGIC PATRIMONY OF OUR

AGRICULTURE FROM THE INEVITABLE GENETIC POLLUTION DERIVING

7. (C) THE SUBSECTION ON ITALIAN LAND AND ENVIRONMENT ALSO

SHOWS PROGRESSIVE SHRILLNESS AND LOSS OF OBJECTIVITY. THE

SUBSECTION FIRST SUGGESTS THAT "RELATIONS OF THE DIVERSE

FORMS OF AGRICULTURE AMONG THEMSELVES AND BETWEEN THEM AND

NATURAL ENVIRONMENTS ARE TWO ASPECTS TO CONSIDER WITH

PARTICULAR ATTENTION AS WE EVALUATE THE BENEFITS OF USING OR

CONDEMNATION OF "GENETIC POLLUTION" AND WE HAVE: "WE MUST BE

LINES (GMO AND GMO-FREE) AND AVOIDING GENETIC POLLUTION COULD

PRODUCE A SITUATION OF OBVIOUS INCOMPATIBILITY WITH THE

MAINTENANCE OF ACTIVITIES -- SUCH AS ORGANIC FARMING AND

PRODUCTION OF QUALITY PRODUCTS (FOR EXAMPLE PARMIGIANO

CHEESE) -- THAT DO NOT ALLOW EITHER USE OR CONTAMINATION BY

TRANSGENIC PRODUCTS." THE SUBSECTION ENDS BY REMOVING ANY

PRETENSE OF OBJECTIVITY: "IT IS EVIDENT THAT USE OF

TRANSGENIC PRODUCTS COULD PRODUCE POLLUTION THAT COULD MEAN

THE LOSS OF THOSE CHARACTERISTICS THAT TODAY DIFFERENTIATE

OUR AGRICULTURE IN THE MARKETPLACE." PRESUMABLY, PARLIAMENT

IS LEFT TO DEBATE WHETHER ITALY SHOULD ALLOW CHEAP, LOW

QUALITY IMPORTS INTO ITS MARKET IN THE CERTAIN KNOWLEDGE THAT

DOING SO WILL DESTROY ITALIAN AGRICULTURE.

8. (C) THE ITALIAN FOOD SYSTEM SUBSECTION NEVER RETURNS TO

THE LEFT ASIDE DISCUSSION OF POSSIBLE HEALTH AND FOOD QUALITY

EFFECTS OF BIOTECH. IN FACT, SO MARGINALIZED IS SCIENCE IN

THIS DOCUMENT THAT NOWHERE IN ITS 37 PAGES ARE THE SUPPOSEDLY

AXIOMATIC HEATH AND FOOD QUALITY EFFECTS DOCUMENTED.

ALEMANNO\'S EVIDENT FEAR OF SCIENCE (HE IS A HIGH-SCHOOL

GRADUATE WHO CLAIMED NO EXPERTISE IN AGRICULTURE PRIOR TO

LANDING THE JOB AS MINISTER) LEAVES MANY OF THE PAPER\'S

CONCLUSIONS HANGING WITHOUT FOUNDATION AND MAY MAKE EASIER

THE TASK OF OPPOSING THE PROPOSED POLICY. RATHER THAN

SUMMARIZING REASONED SCIENTIFIC CONCERN ABOUT PROSPECTS FOR

MAINTAINING A BIOTECH-FREE PRODUCTION OPTION WHEN

CONVENTIONAL SEEDS ARE PLANTED IN PROXIMITY TO BIOTECH SEEDS,

THE SUBSECTION ON LAND AND THE ENVIRONMENT CONDEMNS ALL

BIOTECH PRODUCTS AS EVENTUALLY AND INEVITABLY POLLUTING

ITALIAN PRODUCTION OR CONTAMINATING THE IMAGE OF ITALIAN

PRODUCTS.

9. (SBU) FOLLOWING IS COMMENTARY ON OTHER PROBLEMATIC ASPECTS

OF THE WHITE PAPER.

-- REJECT U.S. SEEDS. THE PAPER SUGGESTS THAT "THE

PHENOMENON OF UNCONTROLLED CONTAMINATION" BY TRANSGENIC

ORGANISMS CAN BE AVOIDED ONLY BY ENACTING INCENTIVES FOR

ITALIAN SEED SELF-SUFFIENCY AND, IN THE MEANWHILE, BY

DEVELOPING SUPPLY CHANNELS WITH COUNTRIES THAT CAN CERTIFY

SEEDS AS BIOTECH-FREE AND AVOIDING IMPORT OF SEEDS FROM OTHER

SOURCES AND, IN PARTICULAR, THE "UNITED STATES FROM WHERE,

ACCORDING TO THE CHECKS EFFECTED BY THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH,

MOST CONTAMINATED SEEDS ORIGINATE." IN MAKING THIS

DISCRIMINATORY SUGGESTION, THE PAPER MAKES NO MENTION THAT

THE U.S. SEED PRODUCERS CAN, FOR A PREMIUM, PRODUCE CERTIFIED

GMO-FREE SEED.

-- SCIENCE: PERSUASION BY OMISSION. IN THE NAME OF

ELIMINATING "USELESS AND DAMAGING" POLEMICS, THE PAPER SIMPLY

OMITS OPPOSING ARGUMENTS. NOT A SINGLE SCIENTIFIC CITATION

IS INCLUDED IN THE 37 PAGE PAPER, AND THE FIFTEEN YEARS OF EU

FUNDED RESEARCH RECENTLY SUMMARIZED IN A EUROPEAN COMMISSION

REPORT IS NOT MENTIONED. THE ADVANCES IN ITALIAN

BIOTECHNOLOGY, INCLUDING VIRAL AND FUNGAL RESISTANT PRODUCTS

NOW READY FOR FIELD TESTING, ARE IGNORED. THE ENVIRONMENTAL

BENEFITS OF REDUCED USAGE OF HERBICIDES AND PESTICIDES, AS

WELL THE PRODUCTION BENEFITS OF DROUGHT TOLERANCE AND STRESS

RESISTANCE ARE OVERLOOKED. THE POTENTIAL BENEFITS TO

SUBJECT: ITALY\'S WRONG TURN ON BIOTECH: WHAT WE SHOULD DO

-- LATER THIS YEAR, THE EU WILL BEGIN IMPLEMENTING PROCEDURES

FOR APPROVING NEW FOOD PRODUCTS, INCLUDING BIOTECHNOLOGY FOOD

PRODUCTS, FOR COMMERCIALIZATION ACROSS THE EU COMMON MARKET.

THE EUROPEAN COMMISSION WILL CONTINUE TO CONSIDER PROPOSALS

FOR MONITORING BIOTECHNOLOGY FOOD PRODUCTS ALREADY APPROVED.

-- AS A MEMBER STATE, IT IS OF COURSE PERFECTLY APPROPRIATE

THAT ITALY DEVELOP NATIONAL POSITIONS AS EVENTS IN BRUSSELS

GET UNDERWAY.

-- MINISTER ALEMANNO INTENDS TO STIMULATE DISCUSSION LEADING

ULTIMATELY TO A NATIONAL BIOTECHNOLOGY POLICY. AGAIN, THAT

IS OF COURSE PERFECTLY APPROPRIATE.

-- THIS PAPER, HOWEVER, IS OF SERIOUS CONCERN TO MANY IN THE

SCIENTIFIC AND BUSINESS COMMUNITIES. THE PAPER DISMISSES

SCIENCE AS A DISRUPTIVE INFLUENCE IN POLICY DELIBERATIONS AND

WOULD BASE ITALIAN BIOTECHNOLOGY POLICY ON PUBLIC OPINION
