SUBJECT: WORLD FOOD SUMMIT FIVE YEARS LATER: INFORMAL

DISCUSSION OF DRAFT DECLARATION

1. SUMMARY AND ACTION REQUEST: AT THE INSTIGATION OF

THE INDEPENDENT CHAIR OF THE FAO COUNCIL, A GROUP WILL

MEET INFORMALLY MAY 18 TO EXCHANGE VIEWS ON THE DRAFT

DECLARATION FOR THE WORLD FOOD SUMMIT FIVE YEARS LATER.

GUIDANCE REQUESTED NO LATER THAN MAY 16 ON THE DRAFT TEXT

FOR USE DURING THE EXCHANGE OF VIEWS. END SUMMARY.

2. AT A LUNCHEON MEETING OF REPRESENTATIVES OF FAO\'S

SEVEN REGIONAL GROUPS, CHAIRED BY THE INDEPENDENT

CHAIRMAN OF THE FAO COUNCIL, IT WAS DECIDED TO MEET AT

THE FAO ON MAY 18 TO EXCHANGE VIEWS ON THE TEXT OF A

DRAFT DECLARATION FOR THE WORLD FOOD SUMMIT:FIVE YEARS

LATER (WFS5YL). THE STARTING POINT IS THE HEAVILY

BRACKETED DRAFT THAT WAS LEFT UNFINISHED WHEN THE OPEN-

ENDED WORKING GROUP (OEWG) LAST MET IN JUNE, 2001. THE

COMPOSITION OF THE MEETING WILL REFLECT THE COMPOSITION

OF THE OEWG AND THE TWO CO-CHAIRS ARE TO REPRESENT BOTH

THE G-77 AND OECD GROUPS.

3. SEVERAL DELEGATIONS (AND THE DIRECTOR GENERAL) HAVE

PUSHED UNSUCCESSFULLY TO ADVANCE NEGOTIATION OF THE TEXT

OF THE DECLARATION IN ORDER TO CONCLUDE EARLIER THAN HAD

BEEN AGREED LAST NOVEMBER, I.E. BY THE COMMITTEE ON FOOD

SECURITY (CFS) JUNE 6-8. OTHER GROUPS, MOST NOTABLY THE

EUROPEAN REGION, HAVE INSISTED THAT THE TIMETABLE AGREED

TO AT THE LAST COUNCIL BE RESPECTED. SEVERAL REGIONAL

GROUPS HAVE CONSULTED INTERNALLY, BUT TO DATE THERE HAS

BEEN NO EXCHANGE OF VIEWS AMONG THE GROUPS.

4. THE EU HAS EXAMINED THE DECLARATION AT THE EXPERT

LEVEL IN BRUSSELS AND STARTED A SERIES OF CONSULTATIONS

WITH OTHER GROUPS IN ROME, BUT HAS NOT AGREED AMONG

MEMBER STATES ON A FINAL TEXT. THEY MEET AGAIN IN

BRUSSELS MAY 15. THE EU HAS IDENTIFIED PARAS 8, 12, 13,

15, 16, 17, 19 AND 21 AS PROBLEMATIC.

5. PROGRESS IS MIXED ON AMONG THE G-77 GROUPS. THE LATIN

AMERICAN GROUP REPORTEDLY HAS AGREED ON A TEXT, BUT

OTHERS IN THE G-77 ARE STILL STAKING OUT THE GROUND

RULES. THE MIDDLE EAST GROUP PROPOSED DISCUSSION OF

BRACKETED TEXT ONLY, REJECTING PROPOSALS TO INTRODUCE NEW

LANGUAGE. THE CO-CHAIR FROM THE AFRICA GROUP EXPRESSED

THE VIEW THAT THE OEWG IS CLOSE TO AGREEMENT ON MANY

POINTS BUT NEEDS TIME FOR DISCUSSION. THE ASIA GROUP IS

DIVIDED BETWEEN THOSE WHO PREFER TO STICK TO THE AGREED

TIMETABLE AND THOSE PUSHING FOR ADVANCEMENT.

6. WHILE THE EU, US AND AUSTRALIA STRESSED THAT SUCH A

MEETING SHOULD NOT BE CONSIDERED A NEGOTIATION,

REPRESENTATIVES AGREED AN INFORMAL EXCHANGE OF VIEWS

WOULD FACILITATE PREPARATIONS FOR THE COMMITTEE ON FOOD

SECURITY WHICH MEETS JUNE 6-8. ALTHOUGH IT WAS AGREED

THERE SHOULD BE TWO CO-CHAIRS, THE MAIN STICKING POINT

THAT REMAINS IS WHO WILL REPRESENT THE OECD SIDE.

(IRANIAN AMBASSADOR NOORI NAEENI WILL CHAIR FOR THE G-

77). THE IRISH REPRESENTATIVE WHO CHAIRED THE CFS AND

OEWG IS NOT ABLE TO COME TO ROME IN ADVANCE OF JUNE. THE

INDEPENDENT CHAIR SUGGESTED THE CHAIRMAN OF THE PROGRAM

COMMITTEE, WHO IS FROM THE NETHERLANDS, BUT HE WILL NOT

BE AVAILABLE. ANOTHER SUGGESTION WAS THAT THE U.S.

(ACTING PERM REP),AS A MEMBER OF THE CFS BUREAU, CO-CHAIR

THE MEETING.

7. ACTION REQUEST: DEPARTMENT GUIDANCE REQUESTED ON THE

TEXT OF THE DRAFT DECLARATION FOR USE DURING THE INFORMAL

DISCUSSIONS MAY 17. OF PARTICULAR USE WOULD BE:

---INDICATIONS OF WHERE WE WOULD LIKE TO SEE TEXT

INTRODUCING NEW PARAGRAPHS, THE INFORMAL MEETING WILL BE

THE APPROPRIATE TIME TO FLOAT ANY NEW TOPICS WHICH WE MAY

WISH TO SEE COVERED IN THE DECLARATION.
