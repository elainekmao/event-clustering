SUBJECT: READOUT ON SADC MINISTERIAL MEETING IN HARARE

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES AUBREY HOOKS FOR

REASONS: 1.5(B) AND (D).

1. (C) SOUTHERN AFRICAN DEVELOPMENT COMMUNITY (SADC)

MINISTERS MET IN HARARE ON JUNE 11 TO APPROVE A PROTOCOL

THAT WILL RESTRUCTURE SADC\'S ORGAN ON POLITICS, DEFENSE

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 01914 171453Z

AND SECURITY. HOWEVER, BECAUSE OF "DISCREPANCIES" IN

THE TEXT OF THE PROTOCOL, THE APPROVAL WAS DEFERRED

UNTIL THE NEXT SADC MINISTERIAL MEETING IN MASERU,

LESOTHO IN ABOUT TWO WEEKS, ACCORDING TO EDWIN MANGAZA,

THE NEW UNDER SECRETARY FOR AFRICA IN ZIMBABWE\'S

MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS. MANGAZA WOULD NOT

ELABORATE ON THE DISCREPANCIES OR THE SUBSTANCE OF THE

PROTOCOL, BUT EMPHASIZED THAT THERE WERE NO SUBSTANTIVE

DISAGREEMENTS AMONG THE PARTIES ABOUT FORMALIZING THE

AGREEMENT. THE DATE FOR THE MASERU MEETING HAS NOT BEEN

ESTABLISHED YET, BUT MUST TAKE PLACE BEFORE THE JULY 9-

11 OAU SUMMIT, WHICH WILL TAKE UP THE ISSUE OF THE SADC

PROTOCOL, THE UNDER SECRETARY TOLD POLOFF. MANGAZA

REPORTED THAT THE HARARE MEETING INCLUDED THE FOREIGN

MINISTERS OF ZIMBABWE, LESOTHO, MOZAMBIQUE, AND NAMIBIA,

THE DEPUTY FOREIGN MINISTER OF ANGOLA, THE HOME AFFAIRS

MINISTERS OF ZAMBIA AND MALAWI, AND THE MINISTER OF

DEFENSE OF SOUTH AFRICA. TANZANIA AND THE DRC WERE

REPRESENTED BY THEIR LOCAL DIPLOMATS.

2. (U) IN REMARKS OPENING THE HARARE MEETING, PRESIDENT

MUGABE URGED SADC TO TAKE CONCRETE MEASURES AGAINST

ANGOLAN REBEL LEADER JONAS SAVIMBI, ACCORDING TO THE

JUNE 12 EDITION OF THE GOVERNMENT-CONTROLLED "THE

HERALD." THE PRESIDENT SAID THE REGIONAL BODY SHOULD

NOT WAIT FOR THE UN TO CONCLUDE ITS INVESTIGATION ON THE

IMPLEMENTATION OF SANCTIONS AGAINST UNITA. MUGABE ALSO

IMPLORED THE UN TO MOVE SWIFTLY IN DEPLOYING MONUC IN

THE DRC, THE ARTICLE STATED.

3. (C) COMMENT: IN A RESTRUCTURING ON THE AGENDA TO BE

FORMALIZED AT SADC\'S AUGUST 2001 SUMMIT, THE ORGAN ON

POLITICS, DEFENSE AND SECURITY WILL BE MADE SUBORDINATE

TO THE SADC SUMMIT AND HEADS OF STATE. IF THE

RESTRUCTURING IS APPROVED, IT WILL EVENTUALLY RELEASE

THE STRANGLEHOLD THAT PRESIDENT MUGABE -- THE ORGAN\'S

CHAIRMAN -- HAS HAD ON SADC SECURITY ISSUES FOR THE LAST

FIVE YEARS. IT IS UNCLEAR AT THIS POINT WHETHER THE NEW

PROTOCOL WILL FORCE MUGABE TO GIVE UP HIS CHAIR THIS

YEAR OR NEXT. HIS GRANDSTANDING AT THE OPENING OF THE

SADC MEETING SUGGESTS HE MAY NOT HAVE GIVEN UP ON THE

POSSIBILITY OF HOLDING SWAY OVER THE ORGAN FOR ANOTHER

YEAR. WE WILL DEFER TO EMBASSY GABORONE, HOWEVER, ON

WHAT THIS MAY MEAN FOR SADC. END COMMENT.
