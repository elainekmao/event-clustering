SUBJECT: 11TH NATIONAL ASSEMBLY OPENING

REF: A. HANOI 1760 B. HANOI 1432

1. (U) SUMMARY. THE NEW NATIONAL ASSEMBLY WILL BEGIN

MEETING ON JULY 17, BUT HAS YET NOT SCHEDULED THE VOTES ON

THE NEW GVN AND NA LEADERSHIP (REF A). THE NEW NA IS NOT

EXPECTED TO PASS ANY NEW LEGISLATION AT THIS INITIAL SESSION

OTHER THAN THE BUDGET. IT REMAINS POSSIBLE THAT THERE ARE

CONTINUED DISAGREEMENTS OVER SENIOR LEADERSHIP SLOTS. END

SUMMARY.

2. (U) IN A MEETING WITH POL/C ON JULY 15, VICE CHAIRMAN

OF THE OFFICE OF THE NATIONAL ASSEMBLY (ONA) PHAN THANH SON

CONFIRMED THAT THE FIRST SESSION OF THE 11TH NATIONAL

ASSEMBLY WILL CONVENE ON JULY 17, TO BE FOLLOWED BY A FORMAL

OPENING (TO WHICH THE AMBASSADOR HAS BEEN INVITED) ON JULY

19TH. THE FIRST TWO DAYS WILL BE SPENT MOSTLY ON PROCEDURAL

ISSUES, ESPECIALLY IMPORTANT GIVEN THAT 348 OF THE 498

DELEGATES ARE NEWCOMERS (REF B). ONA HAS DISTRIBUTED TO ALL

DELEGATES VOLUMINOUS MATERIALS ON NA PROCEDURES AND

PRACTICES ON WHICH THE DELEGATES CAN BONE UP PRIOR TO THE

OPENING, SON ADDED, WHILE ADMITTING THAT THERE WAS NO TRUE

TRAINING OFFERED TO THE NEW DELEGATES.

3. (U) THE FIRST NA SESSION IS SCHEDULED TO LAST UNTIL

ABOUT AUGUST 10, SON NOTED. ITS MAJOR ORDER OF BUSINESS

WILL BE THE ELECTION OF A NEW LEADERSHIP SLATE, STARTING

WITH THE PRESIDENT OF THE SRV, FOLLOWED BY THE PRIME

MINISTER, DEPUTY PRIME MINISTERS, AND THEN THE CABINET

MINISTERS, THE CHAIRMAN OF THE NATIONAL ASSEMBLY, HEADS OF

OTHER MAJOR GVN ORGANS, THE MEMBERS OF THE NATIONAL ASSEMBLY

STANDING COMMITTEE, AND FINALLY, HEADS OF THE MAJOR NA

COMMITTEES. THE NEW STANDING COMMITTEE WILL MAKE THE FINAL

DECISIONS ON WHICH 125 DELEGATES WILL BE APPOINTED AS "FULL-

TIME" MEMBERS. SON SAID THAT THEY WOULD BE CANVASSED FIRST

AS TO THEIR WILLINGNESS TO ACCEPT THIS JOB (WHICH WILL IN

ALMOST ALL CASES INVOLVE EARLY RETIREMENT FROM OTHER PARTY

OR GOVERNMENTAL JOBS), AND ADMITTED THAT SOME MAY VERY WELL

DECLINE THE HONOR FOR FAMILY OR OTHER REASONS.

4. (U) (SBU) SON DECLINED TO PREDICT EXACTLY WHEN THESE

PERSONNEL "ELECTIONS" BY THE NA WILL TAKE PLACE, AND CLAIMED

THAT THE SIXTH PARTY PLENUM HAD NOT YET FINALIZED ITS

DECISIONS ON RECOMMENDATIONS FOR THESE SLOTS. (SEPARATELY,

A CPV OFFICIAL CONFIRMED TO POL/C ON JULY 15 THAT THE PLENUM

HAD ENDED JULY 15 AS PLANNED, BUT WAS UNABLE TO PROVIDE ANY

INFORMATION AS TO THE PERSONNEL RECOMMENDATIONS REACHED BY

THE PLENUM.) SON REFUSED EVEN TO PREDICT THAT THE PERSONNEL

VOTES MIGHT TAKE PLACE DURING THE WEEK OF JULY 22, NOTING

THE NEED FOR THE NEW NA TO HEAR A NUMBER OF REPORTS FROM THE

PRIME MINISTER AND OTHER SENIOR OFFICIALS ABOUT THEIR WORK

OVER THE PREVIOUS FIVE YEARS AND PLANS FOR THE NEXT FIVE

YEARS.

5. (U) (SBU) SON INDICATED THE POSSIBILITY THAT THERE

COULD BE COMPETITION ESPECIALLY OVER THE JOBS OF PRIME

MINISTER AND CHAIRMAN OF THE NATIONAL ASSEMBLY (SUGGESTING -

- SOMEWHAT IMPROBABLY -- THAT THE PARTY AND STANDING

COMMITTEE MIGHT NOMINATE DIFFERENT CANDIDATES), OR AT LEAST

THAT THE INCUMBENTS MIGHT NOT BE RE-ELECTED (REF A). HE

DECLINED, HOWEVER, TO OFFER ANY PERSONAL OPINIONS ON THIS

PROCESS OR ITS OUTCOME.

6. (U) SON CONFIRMED THAT THE ONLY LEGISLATION THAT WILL

BE CONSIDERED AT THIS INITIAL SESSION WOULD BE THE NATIONAL

BUDGET. HE SPECIFICALLY RESPONDED THAT NO BTA-RELATED

LEGISLATION WILL BE INTRODUCED FOR CONSIDERATION. THE NA

WILL CONVENE AGAIN IN OCTOBER FOR NORMAL LEGISLATIVE DUTIES,

HE NOTED.

7. (SBU) COMMENT: WHILE WE CONTINUE TO EXPECT THAT THE

PARTY WILL CONTROL THE PERSONNEL PROCESS AND THAT THE NA

WILL CONFIRM THE PARTY\'S CHOICES, IT APPEARS INCREASINGLY

LIKELY THAT THE BACKSTAGE DISCUSSIONS OVER LEADERSHIP

CHOICES HAVE BEEN LIVELY AND PERHAPS CONTROVERSIAL. WE DO

NOT EXPECT MUCH OF A TRUE DEBATE OVER NOMINATIONS WITHIN THE

NA, BUT ITS RATIFICATION PROCESS MAY NOT BE AS AUTOMATIC AS

BEEN THE CASE IN SOME PREVIOUS NA SESSIONS. THE DEBATE MAY

IN ANY EVENT OFFER AMPLE OPPORTUNITIES FOR AT LEAST OBLIQUE

CRITICISM OF SENIOR OFFICIALS OF THE CURRENT GOVERNMENT,

ESPECIALLY IN THE PUBLIC SECURITY MINISTRY, ALLEGED TO BE

INVOLVED WITH THE NAM CAM CORRUPTION CASE.
