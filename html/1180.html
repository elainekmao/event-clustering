SUBJECT: CUBAN DOCTORS LIKELY TO BE RETURNED TO

GOROMONZI DETENTION CENTER

REF: HARARE 3397 (NOTAL)

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD FOR REASONS 1.5

B/D.

1. (C) ON JUNE 26 UNHCR REGIONAL REPRESENTATIVE OLUSEYI

BAJULAIYE TOLD CONSUL THAT CUBAN DOCTORS LEONEL CORDOVA

RODRIGUEZ AND NORIS DEL CARMEN PENA MARTINEZ WERE STILL

AT THE RHODESVILLE REMAND CENTER, WHERE THE GOVERNMENT

HAD MOVED THEM ON JUNE 17. HE THOUGHT THAT THE CUBANS

WOULD BE RETURNED TO THE GOROMONZI DETENTION CENTER IN

THE NEXT FEW DAYS, AS THE RHODESVILLE FACILITY WAS ONLY

A TRANSIT CENTER. THE UNHCR REPRESENTATIVE REPORTED

THAT HIS STAFF STILL ENJOYS DAILY ACCESS TO THE CUBANS,

WHO WERE RESIGNED TO THE TRANSFER. NEVERTHELESS,

BAJULAIYE TOLD CONSUL THAT HE INTENDS TO PROTEST THE

CUBANS\' TRANSFER, COMPLAINING THAT GOROMONZI DETENTION

CENTER IS LOCATED TOO FAR FROM HARARE. HE ALSO INTENDED

TO REGISTER HIS CONCERN ABOUT THE CUBANS\' SAFETY AT

GOROMONZI.

2. (C) G. GARRETT GRIBSBY OF SENATOR HELMS\' STAFF ASKED

AMBASSADOR MCDONALD FOR ASSISTANCE IN DELIVERING A "CARE

PACKAGE" TO THE CUBANS, AS HE WAS UNDER INSTRUCTIONS TO

SENATOR STAFFER INTENDS TO MEET WITH BAJULAIYE THE
