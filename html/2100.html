SUBJECT: PARTY OFFICIAL OPTIMISTIC ON BILATERAL RELATIONSHIP

1. (U) SUMMARY: A SENIOR COMMUNIST PARTY OF VIETNAM (CPV)

FOREIGN RELATIONS OFFICIAL, BUI THE GIANG, EMPHASIZED THE

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 HANOI 02812 260838Z

POSITIVE IN THE US-VIETNAM BILATERAL RELATIONSHIP IN AN

OCTOBER 24 COURTESY CALL WITH CHARGE. HE PREDICTED THAT

MORE PEOPLE-TO-PEOPLE EXCHANGES WOULD PROVE EFFECTIVE IN

OVERCOMING LINGERING TENSIONS AND BITTERNESS. HE WARMLY

THANKED CHARGE FOR THE EMBASSY\'S USD 35,000 CONTRIBUTION TO

THE VIETNAM RED CROSS\' FLOOD RELIEF PROGRAM. GIANG

INDICATED THAT PASSAGE OF THE BILATERAL TRADE AGREEMENT WAS

ONE OF TWO TOP PRIORITIES FOR THE NATIONAL ASSEMBLY NEXT

MONTH. END SUMMARY.

2. (U) BUI THE GIANG, DIRECTOR OF THE PEOPLE TO PEOPLE

RELATIONS DEPARTMENT OF THE CPV CENTRAL COMMITTEE\'S

COMMISSION FOR EXTERNAL RELATIONS, MET WITH CHARGE DURING A

COURTESY CALL AT CPV HEADQUARTERS. HE DESCRIBED THE THREE

DIMENSIONS OF VIETNAM\'S FOREIGN RELATIONS AS STATE-TO-STATE,

PEOPLE-TO-PEOPLE, AND PARTY-TO-PARTY, WITH THE MFA, THE

VIETNAM UNION OF FRIENDSHIP ORGANIZATIONS, AND THE PARTY

COMMISSION FOR EXTERNAL RELATIONS RESPECTIVELY RESPONSIBLE.

GIANG SAID THAT HE AND HIS STAFF OF EIGHT, AS

REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONLY PARTY, "ADVISE" AND COORDINATE

THE ACTIVITIES OF PEOPLES\' GROUPS IN THEIR DEALINGS WITH

FOREIGN COUNTERPARTS. ASIDE FROM HANDLING TRADITIONAL

RELATIONS WITH OTHER COMMUNIST PARTIES, GIANG EXPLAINED WHEN

ASKED, THAT THE CPV HAS RELATIONSHIPS WITH RULING PARTIES IN

MANY COUNTRIES, CITING JAPAN\'S LDP AND MALAYSIA\'S UMNO AMONG

OTHERS. HE SAID THAT BOTH MAJOR U.S. PARTIES HAD DECLINED

CPV INQUIRIES ABOUT OPENING RELATIONS ON THE GROUNDS THAT

THEY DO NOT HAVE FORMAL RELATIONS WITH ANY OVERSEAS

POLITICAL PARTIES.

3. (SBU) WHEN ASKED ABOUT BTA RATIFICATION ISSUES, GIANG

ANSWERED THAT RATIFICATION "DEPENDED ON DEVELOPMENTS,"

PRESUMABLY REFERRING PRIMARILY TO THE FATE OF THE VIETNAM

HUMAN RIGHTS ACT IN THE US SENATE. GIANG ECHOED CPV GENERAL

SECRETARY NONG DUC MANH\'S REMARKS EARLIER THIS WEEK THAT THE

SIPDIS

NATIONAL ASSEMBLY\'S TOP TWO PRIORITIES FOR ITS UPCOMING

SESSION ARE THE PROPOSED CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENTS AND THE

BTA. GIANG SAID THAT THE NATIONAL ASSEMBLY WOULD CAREFULLY

STUDY THE BTA SINCE IT IS THE MOST COMPREHENSIVE AGREEMENT

VIETNAM HAS EVER UNDERTAKEN. WITHOUT EXPLICITLY SAYING THAT

THE NATIONAL ASSEMBLY WOULD PASS THE BTA, HE SAID THAT THE

PRESIDENT WOULD AFTERWARD SIGN IT WITHOUT DELAY.

4. (SBU) DIRECTOR GIANG EMPHASIZED THE POSITIVE IN THE

BILATERAL RELATIONSHIP, WARMLY THANKING CHARGE FOR THE

EMBASSY\'S USD 35,000 CONTRIBUTION TO THE VIETNAM RED CROSS

FOR MEKONG RIVER FLOOD VICTIMS EARLIER IN THE DAY. HE

POINTED OUT LANGUAGE IN THE NATIONAL POLICY SETTING REPORT

OF THIS YEAR\'S NINTH NATIONAL COMMUNIST PARTY CONGRESS THAT

"VIETNAM IS READY TO BE A FRIEND AND PARTNER WITH ALL

COUNTRIES," CONTRASTING IT WITH THE SEVENTH CPV CONGRESS\'S

1991 REPORT IN WHICH VIETNAM "WISHED TO BE A FRIEND OF ALL

COUNTRIES." ACCORDING TO GIANG, THIS MEANS THAT VIETNAM IS

MATURE ENOUGH AND PREPARED TO BE A FRIEND AND PARTNER TO

COUNTRIES "INCLUDING THE UNITED STATES." HE EXPRESSED A

BELIEF THAT WHILE THE BILATERAL RELATIONSHIP HAS ITS UPS AND

DOWNS, THE OVERALL TREND IS UPWARD. NOTING HIS PERSONAL

STAKE IN THE BTA, ACQUIRED THROUGH WORKING ON IT FOR SEVERAL

YEARS, HE OFFERED HIS OFFICE AS A POINT OF CONTACT IN TIMES

OF NEED.

5. (SBU) GIANG AND CHARGE DISCUSSED THE LINGERING

BITTERNESS FELT BY SOME ON BOTH SIDES LEFT OVER FROM THE

VIETNAM WAR. NOTING THAT THIS BITTERNESS IS A "CHRONIC

IRRITANT" IN THE BILATERAL RELATIONSHIP, GIANG SAID THAT HE

BELIEVED THAT PEOPLE-TO-PEOPLE EXCHANGES WERE QUITE

EFFECTIVE IN OVERCOMING THESE OLD TENSIONS. HE CITED

INSTANCES INVOLVING BOTH OVERSEAS VIETNAMESE AND "AGENT

ORANGE VICTIMS" IN WHICH PERSONAL CONTACT WITH THEIR FORMER

OPPONENTS FOES BROKE DOWN OLD HATREDS. GIANG CONTRASTED THE

PATH OF U.S. RELATIONS WITH VIETNAM TO THOSE OF POST-WORLD

WAR II GERMANY OR JAPAN, OBSERVING THAT IN THE US-VIETNAM

CASE, PEOPLE TEND TO "CLING TO THE PAST." HOWEVER, HE TOOK

A FAVORABLE VIEW OF US-VIETNAM RELATIONS IN THE LONG-TERM

COMPARED WITH THOSE BETWEEN VIETNAM AND CHINA, CITING WHAT

HE SAID WAS A FAVORITE SAYING IN THE COUNTRYSIDE: "HEY GUYS,

WHEN THE WESTERNERS LEAVE, THE CHINESE WILL COME AGAIN."
