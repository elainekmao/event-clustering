SUBJECT: TFUS01: MEDIA REACTION TERROR ATTACKS IN THE

U. S.; HARARE

1. THE INDEPENDENT WEEKLY "THE STANDARD" DEDICATED

ITS SEPTEMBER 16 EDITORIAL TO THE TERRORIST ATTACKS IN

THE U. S. UNDER HEADLINE "AN ATTACK ON CIVILIZATION,"

THE PAPER COMMENTS:

2. ". . .IN A DRAMATIC SERIES OF WELL-PLANNED,

EXPERTLY COORDINATED AND UNSCRUPULOUSLY EXECUTED

EXPLOSIONS, MODERN CONCEPTIONS AND PERCEPTIONS OF WAR,

SECURITY, CRIMINAL JUSTICE, DEVELOPMENT, TRANSPORT AND

PERHAPS HUMANITY ITSELF, WERE SHATTERED. IN TOTAL

DEFIANCE OF MODERNITY\'S FAITH IN REASON, THE ATTACKERS

SET OUT TO KILL FACELESS MASSES, AND REMAINED FACELESS

KILLERS THEMSELVES. IN A DRAMATIC DISPLAY OF

ALIENATION, SOME OF THE MOST OUTSTANDING BENEFITS OF

MODERN TECHNOLOGY - AIR TRAVEL, INFORMATION AND

COMMUNICATION TECHNOLOGY AND ARCHITECTURE - WERE TURNED

AGAINST THE SOCIETY THAT LEADS THE WORLD IN THEIR

PRODUCTION. THE RISKS OF FLYING AND WORKING IN

SKYSCRAPERS AND MILITARY OFFICES HAVE NEVER BEEN MORE

DRAMATICALLY DEMONSTRATED. . . HERE WAS THE U. S., AT

THE BEGINNING OF THE 21ST CENTURY AND THE SOLE

SUPERPOWER AFTER THE COLD WAR, FINDING ITSELF \'AT WAR\'

WITH NO IMMEDIATELY IDENTIFIABLE ADVERSARY, JUST

SOMETHING CALLED \'TERRORISM.\' AS SOLE SUPERPOWER,

AMERICA WOULD ALSO WANT TO DISMISS \'TERRORISM\' AS AN

ADVERSARY AND INSTEAD CONFRONT IT AS A MERE CRIMINAL

COWARD THAT KILLS INNOCENT AND DEFENSELESS PEOPLE AND

HIDES BEYOND ANONYMITY. INDEED, A TECHNOLOGICALLY

SUPERIOR NUCLEAR SUPERPOWER SUCH AS AMERICA DESERVES A

MORE SUBSTANTIAL ENEMY THAN NAMELESS TERRORIST COWARDS

WHO USE AMERICAN TECHNOLOGY TO FIGHT AMERICANS. AND SO

THE SEARCH MUST PROCEED. THE ADVERSARY MUST BE TRACKED

DOWN, POSITIVELY IDENTIFIED, BROUGHT TO JUSTICE AND

PUNISHED. EVERYONE WHO WAS HORRIFIED BY THE EVENTS OF

SEPTEMBER 11 WOULD LIKE TO SEE SOMEONE ANSWERING FOR

THE FIRST MASSACRE OF THIS CENTURY. FOR SOME, REVENGE

TO INFORMATION SOCIETY AND CUSTOMIZED DESTRUCTION."
