1. (C) SUMMARY: ON JANUARY 23 AMBASSADOR, ACCOMPANIED BY

POL MC COUNTRYMAN, DELIVERED POINTS REF A TO MFA SYG GIUSEPPE

BALDOCCI AND COORDINATOR FOR EU SECURITY SANDRO DE BERNADIN.

HE ALSO PROVIDED THE POINTS IN A NON-PAPER. BALDOCCI SAID

THAT MFA FRATTINI WAS "VERY PLEASED, VERY SATISFIED" WITH THE

"PRODUCTIVE, OPEN DIALOGUE" DURING HIS JANUARY 21 MEETING IN

WASHINGTON WITH SECRETARY POWELL. BALDOCCI HOPED THAT THIS

WOULD BE THE BEGINNING OF FREQUENT "DIRECT CONTACT" BETWEEN

THE MINISTERS. BALDOCCI STRESSED THAT THE GOI, EU, AND USG

HAD THE SAME GOALS ON ALL REF A ISSUES, THOUGH THERE WERE

SLIGHT DISCREPANCIES ON THE MEANS BY WHICH THE GOALS WOULD BE

REALIZED. HE AGREED THAT THE PRESSURE MUST BE KEPT ON

BAGHDAD, AND PROMISED THAT THE GOI WOULD WORK FOR A STRONG,

UNIFIED STATEMENT FROM THE GAERC. THE EU WAS ALSO READY TO

BE HELPFUL IN ANY DIALOGUE WITH THE NORTH KOREANS. THE GREEK

AND ITALIAN PRESIDENCIES WOULD TRY TO "REFOCUS" EU ATTENTION

ON THE BALKANS, AND THE GOI WOULD LIKELY PLAY A MAJOR PART IN

ANY ESDP DEPLOYMENT TO MACEDONIA. THE GOI BELIEVES THAT

THERE IS SUFFICIENT SUPPORT IN THE EU TO MAINTAIN SANCTIONS

AGAINST ZIMBABWE. END SUMMARY

IRAQ

2. (C) BALDOCCI AGREED THAT IRAQ WAS NOT JUST AN AMERICAN

OR TRANSATLANTIC ISSUE, BUT AN INTERNATIONAL THREAT OF GREAT

SERIOUSNESS. HE INSISTED THAT THE EU AND ITS MEMBER STATES

(INCLUDING GERMANY AND FRANCE) UNDERSTAND THIS, AND AGREE

THAT THE ULTIMATE GOAL IS TO DISARM SADDAM, EVEN THOUGH THERE

MAY BE DIFFERING PERCEPTIONS WITHIN THE ORGANIZATION ON HOW

TO IMPLEMENT THAT POLICY.

3. (C) BALDOCCI EMPHASIZED THAT THERE SHOULD BE "NO MESSAGE

OF DIVISION" IN THE GAERC STATEMENT. IT MUST STRESS "UNITY

AND PRESSURE." THE GOI WOULD DO WHAT IT COULD TO UNIFY THE

15. COUNTRYMAN CAUTIONED BALDOCCI THAT UNITY OFTEN MEANT A

"LEAST COMMON DENOMINATOR" STATEMENT, WHICH IN THIS CASE

COULD MEAN A CALL FOR ANOTHER UNSC RESOLUTION TO AUTHORIZE

USE OF FORCE AGAINST IRAQ. A WEAK STATEMENT COULD ALSO BE

INTERPRETED IN BAGHDAD AS IC ACQUIESCENCE TOWARDS ADDITIONAL

MONTHS, OR YEARS, OF CONTINUED IRAQI STALLING ON COMPLIANCE

WITH ITS INTERNATIONAL COMMITMENTS TO DISARM. BALDOCCI SAID

THAT HE WOULD CONVEY TO FRATTINI USG VIEWS THAT WE MUST SPEAK

WITH ONE CLEAR, STRONG VOICE, AND KEEP THE PRESSURE ON

SADDAM. (SEPTEL WILL REPORT ON JAN. 24 DEMARCHE REQUESTED IN

REF B).

-----------

NORTH KOREA

-----------

4. (C) BALDOCCI SAID THAT THE GOI CONCURRED WITH U.S.

POLICY AS PRESENTED IN REF A. FRATTINI WOULD BE MEETING WITH

CHINESE FOREIGN MINISTER TANG JIAXUAN ON JANUARY 24 IN ROME,

AND NORTH KOREA WILL BE ON THE AGENDA. WE NEED A COMMITMENT

FORM NORTH KOREA TO COMPLY WITH ITS COMMITMENTS UNDER

INTERNATIONAL AGREEMENTS, CONTINUED DE BERNADIN. HE NOTED

THAT THE GREEK EU PRESIDENCY SHARED OUR APPROACH TOWARDS

DPRK, AND AGREED ON THE NEED TO KEEP THE PRESSURE ON

PYONGYANG. THE EU WAS READY TO BE HELPFUL IN ANY DIALOGUE

WITH THE NORTH KOREANS, AND WAS NOW CONSIDERING DEPLOYMENT OF

A MISSION TO PYONGYANG. THE ISSUE WILL COME UNDER DISCUSSION

AT THE EU TROIKA-RUSSIA MEETING ON JANUARY 24.

BALKANS

-------

5. (C) DE BERNADIN SAID THAT THE EU CONCURS ON THE

IMPORTANCE OF ICTY COOPERATION FOR GOVERNMENTS THROUGHOUT THE

REGION. THE GREEK AND ITALIAN EU PRESIDENCIES WOULD TRY TO

"REFOCUS" ATTENTION ON THE BALKANS NOW THAT "ENLARGEMENT EAST

WAS IN ITS FINAL STAGES". USG OFFERS TO COOPERATE ON BALKANS

ISSUES, PARTICULARLY ON ORGANIZED CRIME, WERE "MUSIC TO OUR

EARS," SAID BALDOCCI.

ESDP

6. (C) THE DEVIL IS IN THE DETAILS, SAID DE BERNADIN, BUT

ONLY A FEW ISSUES REMAINED TO BE IRONED OUT ON BERLIN PLUS,

THEN THE EU WOULD BE READY TO DEPLOY. HE HOPED THAT

DISCUSSIONS COULD BE COMPLETED AS EARLY AS THE END OF

FEBRUARY. MACEDONIA\'S REQUEST FOR AN ESDP POST-TASK FORCE

FOX MILITARY DEPLOYMENT SHOULD ACT AS A CATALYST FOR THE

PROCESS. INTERNAL GOI DISCUSSIONS ON THE LEVEL OF

PARTICIPATION WERE STILL IN EARLY STAGES, BUT HE WAS

CONFIDENT THAT ITALY WOULD BE WILLING TO "PLAY A MAJOR ROLE."

---------

ZIMBABWE

--------

SUPPORT IN THE EU TO MAINTAIN SANCTIONS. THE ONLY

COMPLICATING FACTOR WAS THE JULY EU-AFRICA SUMMIT IN LISBON.

HE HOPED THAT OTHER AFRICAN COUNTRIES WOULD NOT BOYCOTT THE

SUMMIT IF MUGABE WAS DENIED A VISA. POL MC NOTED THAT IT

WOULD BE THE AFRICANS, NOT THE EU, WHO WOULD LOSE MORE FROM A

BOYCOTT. IF THE EU WANTED TO DEMONSTRATE SERIOUSNESS ABOUT

ZIMBABWE, IT SHOULD BE PREPARED TO STAND FIRM ON THIS POINT.
