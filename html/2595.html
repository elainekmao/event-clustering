SUBJECT: CDC AND GVN LAUNCH NATIONAL PROGRAM TO COMBAT HIV/AIDS

1. THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH (MOH)/NATIONAL AIDS STANDING BUREAU,

IN COLLABORATION WITH AND SUPPORT FROM THE CENTERS FOR DISEASE

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 HANOI 00553 110920Z

CONTROL AND PREVENTION (CDC)/EMBASSY HANOI, HAS LAUNCHED

ITS NATIONAL PROGRAM TO COMBAT THE HIV/AIDS EPIDEMIC WITH A TWO-

DAY WORKING MEETING OF KEY PROVINCIAL LEADERS FROM 40 PROVINCES

ACROSS VIETNAM. ONE MEETING TOOK PLACE IN HANOI, MARCH 6TH AND

7TH, FOR LEADERS FROM THE NORTHERN PROVINCES, AND A SECOND WILL

OCCUR IN HO CHI MINH CITY, MARCH 13TH AND 14TH, FOR LEADERS FROM

THE SOUTHERN PROVINCES.

2. THE MEETINGS ARE DESIGNED TO ORIENT PROVINCIAL LEADERS ON THE

MOH AND CDC COLLABORATION, WITH SPECIAL EMPHASIS ON DEVELOPING

HIV PREVENTION PROGRAMS THAT ADDRESS THE NEEDS OF HIGH-RISK

POPULATIONS, SUCH AS INJECTION DRUG USERS (IDU) AND COMMERCIAL

SEX WORKERS (CSW). DISCUSSIONS FOCUS ON INTEGRATED VOLUNTARY

HIV COUNSELING AND TESTING (VCT), PEER EDUCATION, AND EARLY HIV

INTERVENTION, CARE AND SUPPORT ACTIVITIES. AMONG MANY ISSUES

DISCUSSED IS THE IMPORTANCE OF PROMOTING HIV TESTING SO THAT HIV-

INFECTED PERSONS LEARN THEIR STATUS EARLY. PROVIDING ANONYMOUS

COUNSELING AND TESTING IS ONE MECHANISM FOR EARLY HIV DIAGNOSIS

AND TREATMENT. THE TWO-DAY MEETINGS ALSO PROVIDE

INFORMATION ABOUT PROJECT MANAGEMENT MECHANISMS AND

FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES.

3. IN OCTOBER 2001, THE USG, THROUGH EMBASSY HANOI, SIGNED A

COOPERATIVE AGREEMENT WITH THE MOH ESTABLISHING A FIVE-YEAR, $10

MILLION PROGRAM TO STRENGTHEN AND EXPAND VIETNAM\'S HIV PREVENTION

AND CARE PROGRAM. VIETNAM IS ONE OF 24 COUNTRIES SUPPORTED BY

THE USG\'S GLOBAL COMMITMENT TO CURB THE FURTHER SPREAD OF HIV

DISEASE THROUGH FINANCIAL AND TECHNICAL SUPPORT (GLOBAL AIDS

PROGRAM [GAP]). PROGRAM AREAS SUPPORTED UNDER GAP IN VIETNAM

INCLUDE THE DEVELOPMENT AND EXPANSION OF VCT AND PEER EDUCATION

PROGRAMS, EARLY HIV CLINICAL INTERVENTION PROGRAM, AND

TRAINING OF HEALTH PROFESSIONALS IN MEDICAL AND PUBLIC HEALTH-

RELATED KNOWLEDGE AND SKILLS.

4. OVER THE NEXT FIVE YEARS, CDC WILL WORK CLOSELY WITH THE

VIETNAM GAP-LIFE REPRESENTATIVE OFFICE (MOH\'S OPERATIONAL ARM FOR

THIS COOPERATIVE AGREEMENT) IN CONDUCTING PROVINCIAL SITUATIONAL

AND NEEDS ASSESSMENTS, DEVELOPING INTEGRATED VCT SYSTEMS IN

THE 40 PROVINCES WITH THE HIGHEST HIV PREVALENCE, EXPANDING AND

STRENGTHENING HIV/STD/TB SURVEILLANCE SYSTEMS, AND PROVIDING

TRAINING TO STRENGTHEN THE KNOWLEDGE AND SKILLS OF HEALTH

PROFESSIONALS FOR BETTER CARE AND SUPPORT OF PEOPLE LIVING WITH

HIV OR THOSE WHO ARE AT HIGH RISK FOR CONTACTING THE DISEASE.

5. COMMENT: HIV/AIDS IN VIETNAM HAS RAPIDLY INCREASED OVER THE

LAST 10 YEARS. ALTHOUGH CURRENTLY HIV IN VIETNAM IS STILL

LARGELY IN HIGH-RISK GROUPS INCLUDING IDU AND CSW AND REMAINS LOW

IN THE GENERAL POPULATION, THE EXPLOSIVE EPIDEMICS IN THAILAND

AND CAMBODIA PRESENT A THREAT TO THE LOW NUMBER OF INFECTIONS IN

VIETNAM. CDC\'S GLOBAL AIDS PROGRAM IS ONE THE LARGEST OF SEVERAL

USG PROGRAMS WORKING COOPERATIVELY IN VIETNAM TO HELP PREVENT THE

EPIDEMIC IN VIETNAM FROM BECOMING LIKE THAT OF THAILAND OR

CAMBODIA. THE U.S. AGENCY FOR INTERNATIONAL DEVELOPMENT (USAID)

AND THE US DEPARTMENT OF LABOR ARE ALSO SUPPORTING HIV PREVENTION

PROGRAMS IN VIETNAM, WORKING WITH INTERNATIONAL AND LOCAL NGO\'S

AND VIETNAM\'S MINISTRIES OF HEALTH AND LABOR IN DEVELOPING HIV

INTERVENTIONS TARGETING HIGH-RISK POPULATIONS AND PROGRAMS AIMED

AT REDUCING STIGMA AND DISCRIMINATION IN THE WORKPLACE.
