(B) AND (D)

1. (U) THIS IS THE FIRST OF A TWO-PART SERIES ON THE ITALIAN

BUDGET.

2. (C) SUMMARY: A DIVIDED ITALIAN CABINET, AFTER AN ALL-NIGHT

DEBATE, HAS AGREED UPON AND SENT TO PARLIAMENT THE HIGHLY

CONTROVERSIAL 2003 BUDGET. THE BERLUSCONI GOVERNMENT\'S BUDGET

IS AN ATTEMPT TO PROMOTE ECONOMIC GROWTH AND FULFILL CAMPAIGN

PROMISES, WHILE CUTTING SPENDING WHEREVER POSSIBLE. THE

RESULT IS A BUDGET DIVIDED BETWEEN TARGETED TAX CUTS AND

INFRASTRUCTURE INVESTMENT ON ONE HAND AND SPENDING CUTS AND

CAPS FOR MINISTRIES, REGIONAL AND MUNICIPAL GOVERNMENTS ON

THE OTHER. SENIOR GOI OFFICIALS HAVE ASSURED US THAT DEFENSE

MINISTRY CUTS WILL NOT MEAN CUTS IN PROCUREMENT FROM U.S.

DEFENSE FIRMS. FINANCE MINISTER TREMONTI WAS THE MAIN

ARCHITECT OF THE 2003 BUDGET AND WILL BE ITS KEY DEFENDER IN

PARLIAMENT. IF THE BUDGET DEFICIT IS HIGHER THAN THE 1.5

PERCENT PREDICTED, WHICH MANY ECONOMISTS SAY IS LIKELY, THEN

TREMONTI WILL NEED TO FIND ADDITIONAL RESOURCES OR FURTHER

SPENDING CUTS. ON THIS TIGHTROPE, THE FINANCE MINISTER\'S

REPUTATION -- AND IN NO SMALL MEASURE THAT OF PRIME MINISTER

BERLUSCONI -- LIES IN THE BALANCE.

3. (SBU) PARLIAMENT MUST PASS THE BUDGET BY THE END OF

DECEMBER. THE

CENTER-LEFT IN PARLIAMENT, INDUSTRIALISTS AND UNIONS ARE

FINDING COMMON GROUND IN THEIR OPPOSITION TO THE

BELT-TIGHTENING MEASURES PROPOSED BY THE GOVERNMENT. IN

THE DRAFT 2003 BUDGET

---------------------

4. (SBU) ON OCTOBER 2, THE GOI PRESENTED ITS 2003 BUDGET TO

PARLIAMENT, TOTALING E619 BILLION (47.4 PERCENT OF GDP) IN

SPENDING. THE PLAN INCLUDES E20 BILLION IN PLANNED SPENDING

CUTS, PARTIALLY OFFSET BY TAX CUTS OF E7.5 BILLION TO

ENCOURAGE ECONOMIC GROWTH. AMONG THE REVENUE GENERATING

MEASURES ARE PLANNED SALES OF STATE ASSETS, WITH A POTENTIAL

PAYOFF OF E4 BILLION, AS WELL AS A TAX AMNESTY, WHICH COULD

RAISE AS MUCH AS E8 BILLION.

THE ARCHITECTS

--------------

5. (SBU) PRIME MINISTER BERLUSCONI AND FINANCE MINISTER

TREMONTI AGREED ON THE DIFFICULT FRAMEWORK FOR THE BUDGET:

CUT TAXES AND LAUNCH AN INFRASTRUCTURE INVESTMENT PROGRAM, AS

PROMISED DURING THE 2001 CAMPAIGN; CUT SPENDING BUT WITHOUT

ANGERING KEY CONSTITUENCIES; AND AVOID VIOLATING STABILITY

PACT TARGETS.

6. (SBU) ACHIEVING A BALANCE BETWEEN THESE COMPETING AND

OFTEN CONFLICTING GOALS PROVED CHALLENGING IN THE EXTREME.

FINANCE MINISTER TREMONTI AND HIS ECONOMIC TEAM HAVE CHOSEN A

PATH THAT COMBINES CUTTING AND CAPPING SPENDING, WHERE

POSSIBLE, WITH TAX AMNESTIES TO BRING IN ONE-TIME REVENUES.

FINANCE MINISTRY OFFICIALS ARE COUNTING ON A GLOBAL ECONOMIC

RECOVERY IN LATE 2003 TO RAISE TAX REVENUES, BOLSTER THE

PRICE OF STATE-OWNED ASSETS, AND ENCOURAGE INVESTMENT.

7. (C) COMMENT: WHILE TREMONTI WAS THE ARCHITECT OF THIS

BUDGET, AND OF THE ENTIRE BERLUSCONI ECONOMIC PLAN, HE HAS

SURROUNDED HIMSELF WITH A HAND-PICKED CADRE OF LIEUTENANTS,

MANY OF WHOM REPLACED LONG-TIME SENIOR CIVIL SERVANTS. AMONG

THE COTERIE ARE DOMENICO SINISCALCO, FINANCE MINISTRY

DIRECTOR GENERAL (REPLACED MARIO DRAGHI, WHO HAD HELD THIS

POSITION FOR NEARLY A DECADE); DEPUTY FINANCE MINISTER

ANTONIO BALDASSARI; UNDER SECRETARIES VITO TANZI AND GIUSEPPE

VEGAS; DIRECTOR GENERAL FOR ACCOUNTING VITTORIO GRILLI (THE

GOI EQUIVALENT OF THE OMB DIRECTOR) AND VINCENZO FORTUNATO,

CHIEF OF CABINET. THE SUCCESS OR FAILURE OF THE 2003 BUDGET

-- ECONOMICALLY AND POLITICALLY WILL REFLECT STRONGLY ON THIS

TEAM. END COMMENT

8. (SBU) TAXPAYERS: ON TAX CUTS, THE BUDGET PROPOSES A

REDUCTION IN PERSONAL INCOME TAX THAT WILL BENEFIT LOW-INCOME

FAMILIES. WHILE THIS MAY SATISFY SOME VOTERS, MOST ITALIANS

WILL NOT BENEFIT. DURING THE 2001 CAMPAIGN, THEN-CANDIDATE

BERLUSCONI PROMISED FUNDAMENTAL REFORM OF THE TAX SYSTEM,

INCLUDING ELIMINATION OF THE COMPLEX NETWORK OF OVERLAPPING

TAX LEVIES. TREMONTI HAS NOW DESIGNED A NEW STRUCTURE: A

HIGHER MINIMUM INCOME BEFORE TAXES, AND TWO TAX BRACKETS --

23 PERCENT BELOW E100,000 INCOME AND 33 PERCENT OVER THAT

LEVEL. TOP TAX RATES FOR THE HIGHEST INCOME GROUP WOULD

HAVE FALLEN FROM 42.5 PERCENT TO 33 PERCENT; FOR

MIDDLE-INCOME GROUPS, FROM A MAXIMUM RATE OF 39 PERCENT TO 23

PERCENT. GLOBAL ECONOMIC CONDITIONS FORCED THE GOI TO

POSTPONE THIS REFORM UNTIL 2003. NOW, IF APPROVED BY

PARLIAMENT, THE NEW INCOME TAX RATES WILL NOT KICK IN BEFORE

2004 AT THE EARLIEST.

9. (C) THE MINISTRIES: THE 2003 BUDGET PROPOSES A 10 PERCENT

SPENDING CUT IN DISCRETIONARY FUNDS FOR ALL GOVERNMENT

MINISTRIES. FINANCE DEPUTY SECRETARY BALDASSARI TOLD US THAT

THE GOVERNMENT HOPES TO ACHIEVE SUBSTANTIAL SAVINGS POSSIBLE

BY SWITCHING TO ON-LINE PURCHASING. FINANCE UNDER SECRETARY

TANZI TOLD US ON SEPTEMBER 30 THAT A E35 BILLION

DISCRETIONARY FUND FOR THE NON-BINDING PURCHASE OF CAPITAL

GOODS AND SERVICES, SUCH AS TELECOMMUNICATIONS UPGRADES,

WOULD BE CUT. PUBLIC SECTOR HIRING WILL BE FROZEN. NOT

SURPRISINGLY, MINISTERS ALREADY ARE

JOCKEYING TO ENSURE THAT THEIR MINISTRIES TAKE LESS OF A HIT,

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 ROME 05049 02 OF 04 170811Z

AND THAT FAVORED PROGRAMS ARE PRESERVED AND REMAIN UNDER

THEIR CONTROL. IN THE DAYS BEFORE THE CABINET DEBATE ON THE

BUDGET, MINISTER OF PRODUCTIVE ACTIVITIES (MPA) MARZANO

THREATENED TO RESIGN IF HIS MINISTRY DID NOT RETAIN CONTROL

OVER A SPECIAL FUND FOR THE MEZZOGIORNO, ITALY\'S SOUTHERN

REGION. NEVERTHELESS, UNDER THE 2003 BUDGET, CONTROL OF THIS

FUND, INTENDED TO PROMOTE INDUSTRIAL DEVELOPMENT IN THE

SOUTH, WILL BE GIVEN

TO THE FINANCE MINISTRY, NOT THE MPA. (COMMENT: MARZANO DID

NOT RESIGN BUT TOLD US CANDIDLY THAT FUNDING FOR THE

MEZZOGIORNO WILL FALL SHORT OF WHAT HE WANTED TO SEE. END

COMMENT.)

10. (SBU) THE DRAFT BUDGET PROPOSES LOWER DEFENSE SPENDING IN

REAL TERMS. DESPITE A MODEST 0.3 PERCENT NOMINAL INCREASE IN

DEFENSE

SPENDING, FROM E19.5 BILLION TO E19.6 BILLION, PREDICTED 2003

INFLATION OF 1.9 PERCENT WOULD MEAN A CUT OF ALMOST 1.6

PERCENT IN REAL SPENDING. THE BUDGET FOR "PERSONNEL,

OPERATIONS AND CAPITAL", FOR THE ARMY, NAVY AND AIR FORCE, IS

TO BE CUT BY AN ESTIMATED 1.4 PERCENT. DEFENSE MINISTRY

CONTACTS ARE CONCERNED THAT PERSONNEL COSTS WILL EAT UP A

LARGE PART OF THE BUDGET, AS THE MILITARY MOVES FROM A

CONSCRIPT FORCE TO A PROFESSIONAL AND MORE HIGHLY-PAID FORCE.

DESPITE THE COMPETING DEMANDS, FINANCE UNDER SECRETARY TANZI

TOLD US THAT DEFENSE PROCUREMENT WITH THE U.S. SHOULD NOT BE

HARMED. NOTE: ITALY HAS SIGNED ON FOR A USD ONE BILLION

INVESTMENT IN THE JOINT STRIKE FIGHTER, AND HAS COMMITTED TO

SEVERAL OTHER MAJOR PROCUREMENTS FROM THE U.S. END NOTE.

11. (C) DESPITE TANZI\'S REMARKS, WE HAVE HEARD RAMPANT

12. (SBU) THE UNIONS: THE THREE MAJOR TRADE UNION

CONFEDERATIONS (CGIL, CISL, AND UIL) SET ASIDE THEIR SUMMER

DIFFERENCES (OVER THE GOVERNMENT\'S INTRODUCTION OF MODEST

LABOR FLEXIBILITY IN ANNUAL LABOR AGREEMENTS) TO SLAM THE

DRAFT BUDGET. FOR LABOR, THE BUDGET\'S PRIORITIES ARE

INVERTED. CAPS ON SPENDING FOR LOCAL SERVICES - WHETHER

PUBLIC CHILD-CARE, HEALTH CARE OR EDUCATION -- WILL MORE THAN

OFFSET ANY BENEFITS ORDINARY WORKERS WOULD GLEAN FROM A TAX

CUT, UNIONS LEADERS ARGUE. IN THEIR EYES, THE

REVENUE-GENERATING TAX AMNESTY IS ONLY THE LATEST EXAMPLE OF

A

FLAWED ECONOMIC POLICY, WHICH EMPHASIZES ACCOUNTING GIMMICKS

AND COZY BENEFITS FOR THE GOVERNMENT\'S BUSINESS CONSTITUENCY

OVER LONG-TERM INVESTMENT IN RESEARCH, INFRASTRUCTURE AND

OTHER MEASURES TO DIVERSIFY ITALY\'S ECONOMY. CGIL, THE

LARGEST OF THE THREE CONFEDERATIONS, DECLARED THAT ITS

OCTOBER 18 NATIONWIDE GENERAL STRIKE WILL BE A PROTEST

AGAINST THE DRAFT BUDGET AS WELL AS THE LABOR MARKET

FLEXIBILITY PROVISIONS PROPOSED BY CISL AND UIL. (COMMENT:

THIS DECLARATION IS A BIT DISINGENUOUS, GIVEN THAT THE

OCTOBER 18 STRIKE WAS ANNOUNCED LAST SUMMER, BEFORE THE

BUDGET WAS DRAFTED, BUT IS GOOD POLITICS. END COMMENT.) THE

LEADERS OF THE OTHER TWO CONFEDERATIONS, BEYOND ECHOING

WARNINGS ABOUT WEAK SUPPORT FOR THE SOUTH, WARNED THAT THEY

WOULD JOIN CGIL IN THE STREETS IF LOWER-THAN-PROJECTED

CONFINDUSTRIA, ITALY\'S INDUSTRIALIST ASSOCIATION, BLASTED

17. (SBU) AS THE GOVERNMENT\'S DRAFT BUDGET IS DEBATED IN

PARLIAMENT BETWEEN NOW AND THE END OF THE YEAR, IT IS CLEAR

THAT THE LOSING GROUPS ARE NOT GOING TO ACCEPT THE BUDGET

WITHOUT A FIGHT. INDEED, AN UNLIKELY ALLIANCE BETWEEN

MONTHS AGO OVER ARTICLE 18 OF THE LABOR CODE) CENTER-LEFT

PARLIAMENTARY LEADERS, AND EVEN SOME CABINET MINISTERS ARE

POSSIBLE CHANGES TO THE BUDGET. MOD DIPLOMATIC ADVISOR

TRUPIANO TOLD US THAT THE FIGHT FOR THE MOD BUDGET "ISN\'T

OVER".

18. (SBU) THE FINANCE MINISTER HAS CREATED A BUDGET INTENDED

TO STIMULATE GROWTH, WHILE NOT INCREASING THE BUDGET DEFICIT.

HE HAS LABORED TO STRIKE A PROPER POLICY BALANCE BETWEEN TAX

CUTS AND SPENDING CUTS, ONE-OFF MEASURES AND LONG TERM

INVESTMENT PLANS. A BUDGET THAT PLEASES NO ONE MAY, INDEED,

BE THE RIGHT PRESCRIPTION.

19. (C) THE 2003 BUDGET IS, HOWEVER, SOMETHING OF A HIGH-RISK

GAMBLE. IF THE GLOBAL ECONOMY RECOVERS IN 2003, THIS POLICY

MIX MIGHT BE ENOUGH TO REVIVE ITALY\'S ECONOMY. THE GOI ALSO

WOULD BE WELL-POSITIONED TO MEET STABILITY PACT TARGETS FOR A

BALANCED BUDGET IN 2006. SHOULD ECONOMIC GROWTH IN 2003 PICK

UP, THE GOI ALSO WOULD BE IN POSITION TO TACKLE THE MORE

INTRACTABLE, STRUCTURAL PROBLEMS, SUCH AS LABOR MARKET

INFLEXIBILITY AND A BURDENSOME PENSION SYSTEM, SHOULD IT

SUMMON THE POLITICAL WILL TO DO SO.
