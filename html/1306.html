1. SUMMARY: DONOR COORDINATION AND COLLABORATION IN

ZIMBABWE IS A CORE COMPONENT OF THE U.S. MISSION\'S

RESPONSE TO THE HIV/AIDS CRISIS IN ZIMBABWE. THE

U.S. MISSION, AND ITS E.U. PARTNERS, ARE TAKING THE

LEAD IN THE DONOR COMMUNITY IN MITIGATING THE

HIV/AIDS CRISIS. THE U.S. MISSION PLAYED AN

IMPORTANT ROLE IN MOBILIZING THE DONOR COMMUNITY TO

COORDINATE AND COLLABORATE IN ORDER TO MAXIMIZE THE

IMPACT OF WHAT REMAIN LIMITED DONOR RESOURCES. THE

U.S. MISSION SUPPORTS THE INTENSIFIED COMMITMENT

FOSTERED AT THE QUELUZ SUMMIT TO ADVANCE U.S. - E.U.

COOPERATION ON HIV/AIDS. END SUMMARY

2. BACKGROUND: THE HIV/AIDS EPIDEMIC HAS PROPELLED

ZIMBABWE RAPIDLY TOWARDS A CRISIS AT BOTH THE

HOUSEHOLD AND NATIONAL LEVELS. THE STATISTICS

PORTRAY AN ALARMING SITUATION: UNAIDS ESTIMATES 25%

OF THE SEXUALLY ACTIVE ADULT POPULATION TO BE HIV-

INFECTED AND THERE ARE AN ESTIMATED 500,000 ORPHANS

IN ZIMBABWE. SHOCKING AS THE CURRENT SITUATION IS,

THE FULL IMPACT OF THE DISEASE HAS YET TO BE FELT.

GIVEN THE HIGH PREVALENCE, MORTALITY RATES WILL

CONTINUE TO ESCALATE IN COMING YEARS. THE HIGH

DEATH RATE WILL RESULT IN EVEN MORE ORPHANS -- AN

ESTIMATED 910,000 BY THE YEAR 2005.

3. U.S. EMBASSY RESPONSE: GIVEN THE GRAVITY OF THE

CRISIS, U.S. AMBASSADOR MCDONALD HAS MADE HIV

PREVENTION A TOP PRIORITY FOR THE PROGRAMS SPONSORED

BY THE MISSION. THE AMBASSADOR MOBILIZES OTHER

MEMBERS OF THE DONOR COMMUNITY, INCLUDING

4. USAID PROGRAMMING: USAID PROGRAMMING CONTINUES TO

PLAY A LEADING ROLE IN THE U.S. MISSION\'S HIV/AIDS

ACTIVITIES. USAID\'S BUDGET FOR HIV/AIDS HAS MORE

THAN DOUBLED TO USD $7.2 MILLION THIS YEAR AND

REPRESENTS OVER 50 PERCENT OF USAID/ZIMBABWE\'S

ANNUAL BUDGET. HIV/AIDS CRISIS MITIGATION IS ONE OF

THE USAID MISSION\'S CORE STRATEGIC OBJECTIVES. THIS

PROGRAM IS A DYNAMIC AND INNOVATIVE, FIVE-YEAR, $35

MILLION EFFORT FOCUSED ON BEHAVIOR CHANGE, POLICY

AND ADVOCACY AND SUPPORT TO COMMUNITIES FOR CHILDREN

ORPHANED BY AIDS. FOR SPECIFIC DETAILS OF THE U.S.

MISSION RESPONSE, STRATEGY AND ACTIVITIES SEE REF A.

5. COORDINATION AND COLLABORATION: AN OVERARCHING

COMPONENT TO USAID\'S HIV/AIDS STRATEGIC PLAN IS

COORDINATION AND COLLABORATION WITH DONORS WHOM THE

U.S. MISSION CONSIDERS AS PARTNERS. THE USAID

MISSION HAS TAKEN A LEADERSHIP ROLE IN HIV/AIDS

DONOR COLLABORATION, INCLUDING ESTABLISHING AN

HIV/AIDS TECHNICAL GROUP, WHICH MEETS BI-MONTHLY TO

SHARE TECHNICAL INFORMATION, COORDINATE STRATEGIES

AND DISSEMINATE RESULTS OF PROGRAM ACHIEVEMENTS.

7. EU SUPPORT TO THE FIGHT AGAINST AIDS IN ZIMBABWE:

THE EUROPEAN COMMUNITY IS A RELATIVELY NEW

COOPERATING PARTNER IN THE HEALTH SECTOR IN

ZIMBABWE, APART FROM EXTENSIVE SUPPORT TO LOCAL

FACILITIES THROUGH ITS MICROPROJECTS FUND, SUPPORT

FOR THE HEALTH SECTOR UNDER THE SEVENTH AND SIXTH

EUROPEAN DEVELOPMENT FUND (EDF) WAS MINIMAL. THERE

WAS, HOWEVER SOME SUPPORT FOR AIDS ACTIVITIES UNDER

A REGIONAL PROGRAM. UNDER THE EIGHTH EDF, WHICH

RUNS FROM 1996-01, THE SITUATION IS VERY DIFFERENT.

OF THE TOTAL NATIONAL INDICATIVE PROGRAMME (NIP) OF

EURO 110 MILLION, EURO 24.5 MILLION HAS ALREADY BEEN

COMMITTED FOR THE HEALTH SECTOR SUPPORT PROGRAMME I

(HSSP I). THIS WILL PROVIDE SUPPORT TO

DECENTRALISATION AND THE PUBLIC-PRIVATE

COLLABORATIVE FRAMEWORK. ABOUT 70% OF THE TOTAL

FUNDS WILL BE USED TO CONTRIBUTE TO THE HEALTH

SERVICES FUND (HSF).

A FURTHER EURO 30 MILLION WILL BE MADE AVAILABLE TO

PRESERVE THE HUMAN CAPITAL OF THE COUNTRY IN THE

FACE OF THE SEVERE HIV/AIDS EPIDEMIC INCLUDING;

A. PROVISION OF ESSENTIAL DRUGS AND SUPPLIES FOR

TREATMENT OF SEXUALLY TRANSMITTED INFECTIONS,

INCLUDING HIV/AIDS AND TB. (THE WORLD BANK WAS

SUPPORTING THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE IN THE PROVISION

OF THESE DRUGS UNTIL 1999). THE EU ALSO PROPOSES TO

SUPPORT THE PROPOSED COMMERCIALIZATION OF THE

GOVERNMENT MEDICAL STORES.
