SUBJECT: (S) JORDANIAN OIL NEEDS: UAE READY TO

TRANSFER FUNDS

1. (U) CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR MARCELLE M. WAHBA

FOR REASONS 1.5 (B,D).

2. (S) IN THE COURSE OF A MEETING ON THE

AFTERNOON OF 3/23, MFA MINSTATE SHAYKH HAMDAN BIN

ZAYID STATED THAT HE HAD BEEN CALLED BY JORDANIAN

FM MUASHER THE PREVIOUS EVENING, WHO HAD TOLD HIM

THAT IRAQ HAD CUT ITS OIL PIPELINE SUPPLY TO

JORDAN EARLIER THAT DAY. SHAYKH HAMDAN SAID SAUDI

ARABIA AND KUWAIT WILL SUPPLY JORDAN WITH 75

THOUSAND BARRELS PER DAY (KSA 50,000 AND KUWAIT

25,000). THE UAE IS PROVIDING JORDAN WITH AN

IMMEDIATE CASH INFUSION TO HELP DEFRAY THE COST OF

PURCHASING FUEL FROM OTHER SOURCES. THE MONEY IS

READY NOW AND WILL BE TRANSFERRED TO THE JORDANIAN

CENTRAL BANK AS SOON AS AMMAN PROVIDES THE

APPROPRIATE ACCOUNT NUMBER. THE AMBASSADOR ASKED

IF THE EMIRATI CONTRIBUTION WOULD BE SUFFICIENT TO

COVER WHAT WE UNDERSTOOD HAD BEEN PREVIOUSLY

AGREED -- 25,000 BARRELS PER DAY (BPD). SHAYKH

HAMDAN STATED THE UAE WOULD DO ITS BEST TO PROVIDE

THE CASH EQUIVALENT OF 25 THOUSAND BPD.

WAHBA
