SUBJECT: UNHCR: PRESIDENT MUGABE TO MAKE DECISION ON

RELEASE OF CUBAN DOCTORS

REF: A) HARARE 3231 (NOTAL), B) STATE 113556 (NOTAL)

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD FOR REASONS 1.5

B/D.

1. (C) ON JUNE 15 LUSAKA-BASED UNITED NATIONS HIGH

COMMISSIONER FOR REFUGEES (UNHCR) REGIONAL

REPRESENTATIVE OLUSEYI BAJULAIYE TOLD AMBASSADOR

MCDONALD THAT HE HAD MET WITH A NUMBER OF OFFICIALS IN

HARARE WITH RESPECT TO THE CASES OF CUBAN DOCTORS LEONEL

CORDOVA RODRIGUEZ AND NORIS DEL CARMEN PENA MARTINEZ.

THE REGIONAL UNHCR REPRESENTATIVE REPORTED THAT HE HAD

ATTEMPTED TO DELIVER A MATTRESS AND MAGAZINES TO THE

CUBANS, BUT WAS BARRED FROM DOING SO. HE SAID THE CUBAN

DOCTORS ARE IN A VERY STRESSED EMOTIONAL STATE,

PARTICULARLY DOCTOR PENA. THE TWO HAD BEEN FED ONLY ONE

MEAL A DAY BY THE ZIMBABWEANS, PER THE POLICE

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 03263 151829Z

REGULATIONS FOR PRISONERS IN REMAND STATUS, AND HAD BEEN

USING WHAT LITTLE MONEY THEY HAD TO SUPPLEMENT THEIR

MEAGER DIET. BAJULAIYE SAID HE PROTESTED THEIR

TREATMENT STRENUOUSLY TO THE ACTING PERMANENT SECRETARY

OF THE MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS, DR. NGONI CHIDEYA.

AMBASSADOR MCDONALD REQUESTED THAT BAJULAIYE PUT HIS

COMPLAINTS IN WRITING TO THE GOZ AND THE UNHCR

REPRESENTATIVES SAID THAT HE WOULD DO SO. THE CUBANS

HAVE THREATENED TO GO ON A HUNGER STRIKE TO PROTEST THE

INHUMANE CONDITIONS IN WHICH THEY ARE BEING KEPT,

ACCORDING TO BAJULAIYE.

2. (C) UNHCR ATTORNEY MICHIEL SALOMONS SAID THAT THE

BIG NEWS WAS THAT DR. CHIDEYA AND VICE PRESIDENT JOSEPH

MSIKA WOULD BE BRIEFING PRESIDENT MUGABE THE EVENING OF

JUNE 15 ON THE CASE AND THAT MUGABE WAS LIKELY TO MAKE A

DECISION ON IT THAT NIGHT. BAJULAIYE SAID THAT HE

THOUGHT THAT THE MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS AND VICE

PRESIDENT MSIKA WERE BOTH INCLINED TO RECOMMEND THE

IMMEDIATE RELEASE OF THE CUBANS TO THE UNHCR. IF THE

DECISION WAS A NEGATIVE ONE, THEN BAJULAIYE WOULD

CONTACT UNCHR HEAD OGATA TO TELEPHONE OR WRITE TO

PRESIDENT MUGABE.

3. (C) SALOMONS ALSO INFORMED US THAT HE HAD BEEN IN

TOUCH WITH THE DUTCH COUNSEL TO THE SECRETARY OF STATE

OF THE MINISTRY OF JUSTICE, PETER VAN KRIEKEN, TO

PROPOSE THAT THE NETHERLANDS ACCEPT THE CUBANS IN LIEU

OF CANADA. BAJULAIYE REPORTED THAT THE UNHCR

REPRESENTATIVE IN OTTAWA HAD REPORTED REAL RELUCTANCE BY

CANADA TO TAKE ON THE CASE, AS IT HAD BEEN BURNED IN A

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 04 HARARE 03263 151829Z

SIMILAR SITUATION IN THE PAST (NFI). THE DUTCH

AMBASSADOR IN HARARE THOUGHT THAT THE PROPOSAL WAS A

GOOD ONE AND PROMISED TO CONTACT HER GOVERNMENT WITH A

FAVORABLE RECOMMENDATION. VAN KRIEKEN THOUGHT THAT IT

WOULD NOT BE A PROBLEM TO ACCEPT THE CUBANS FOR A PERIOD

OF THREE MONTHS, ASSUMING THE USG HAD NO PROBLEM WITH

IT.

4. (C) SALOMONS ASKED THAT THE U.S. AMBASSADOR TO THE

NETHERLANDS APPROACH THE DUTCH FOREIGN MINISTRY AND

ASSURE IT THAT THE USG VIEWED FAVORABLY THE CUBANS

REQUESTING TEMPORARY ASYLUM OR EMERGENCY RESETTLEMENT IN

THE NETHERLANDS. IF THE ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT RELEASES

THE CUBANS TO THE CUSTODY OF THE UNHCR, THE PLAN WOULD

BE TO MOVE THE PAIR OUT OF ZIMBABWE ON JUNE 16 TO

NAIROBI, AND FROM NAIROBI ONWARD TO THE NETHERLANDS.

5. (C) ACTION REQUESTED: WE WOULD APPRECIATE DEPARTMENT

REQUESTING AN APPROACH TO THE NETHERLANDS GOVERNMENT

ALONG THE LINES PROPOSED BY SALOMONS.
