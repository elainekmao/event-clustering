1. IN RESPONSE TO REF A POST-SHIPMENT VERIFICATION ON

APPLICATIONS D292692/D293119, ECONOFFS VISITED THE JORDAN

BROMINE COMPANY\'S (JBC\'S) BROMINE MANUFACTURING PLANT IN

GHORE SAFI, SOUTHERN JORDAN, ON JULY 8, 2002 AND MET WITH JBC

MATERIALS MANAGER W.C. (GRAIG) RUSSENHOLT. RUSSENHOLT WARMLY

WELCOMED ECONOFFS AND EXPLAINED THAT REF A EXTRANCHECK

EQUIPMENT, CONSISTING OF 5 HEAT EXCHANGE ELEMENTS, TAG

1140989P24P1, AS WELL AS THE 8 ACRIS BUTTERFLY VALVES, TAG

VF166 AND VF167.3 RESPECTIVELY, HAD ALREADY BEEN INTEGRATED

IN THE ASSORTED BROMINE MANUFACTURING ASSEMBLIES. ECONOFFS

CONSEQUENTLY REQUESTED AND WERE GIVEN A TOUR OF THE

AFOREMENTIONED ASSEMBLIES AS A MEANS TO VERIFY EQUIPMENT

INTEGRATION AND PROPER USE AS STIPULATED BY REF C EXPORT

LICENSE. NO UNTOWARD DEVELOPMENTS AROSE AND REF A

EXTRANCHECK EQUIPMENT WAS VERIFIED TO BE ON SITE AND IN PLACE

AS DESIGNED.

2. AS A WELCOME ASIDE, RUSSENHOLT BRIEFED ECONOFFS IN DETAIL

REGARDING JBC\'S RECENT IMPLEMENTATION OF ADDED SECURITY

MEASURES AT THE PLANT. THESE MEASURES INCLUDED STRENGTHENED

PERMITER SECURITY, ADDITIONAL GUARDS TO SCREEN ACCESS/EGRESS,

ETC., IN RESPONSE TO SUGGESTIONS OFFERED BY SCO AND ECONOFF

DURING REF B VISIT. RUSSENHOLT NOTED THAT ENHANCED SECURITY

INITIATIVES ARE BEING CONSIDERED AND WILL LIKELY BE

IMPLEMENTED BY A PHASE-IN, GRADUAL APPROACH.

3. RUSSENHOLT CLEARLY EXPRESSED HIS APPRECIATION FOR USDOC

AND EMBASSY ASSISTANCE IN SMOOTHING THE DELIVERY OF CRITICAL

U.S.-ORIGIN EQUIPMENT FOR THE PLANT AND NOTED THAT AS A

RESULT, JBC FACILITY CONSTRUCTION IS SLIGHTLY AHEAD OF

SCHEDULE. ALL AVAILABLE INFORMATION INDICATES THAT JBC WILL

USE REF A EQUIPMENT IN COMPLETE COMPLIANCE WITH THE TERMS OF

EXPORT LICENSES D262962 AND D293119, WITHOUT EXCEPTION.

GIVEN THE SIZE AND IMPORTANCE OF THIS UNIQUE JOINT VENTURE,

WE APPRECIATE USDOC\'S DEMONSTRATED EFFORTS TO SPEED THE

EXTRANCHECK PROCESS.
