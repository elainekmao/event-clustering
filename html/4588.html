SUBJECT: WFP LAUNCHES NORTH KOREA EMERGENCY OPERATION FOR

2003 - USD 201.08 MILLION

REF: (A) ROME 5392

-------

SUMMARY

-------

1. ON DECEMBER 2, WORLD FOOD PROGRAM LAUNCHED ITS 2003

EMERGENCY OPERATION (EMOP) FOR NORTH KOREA - ONE YEAR

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ROME 05830 01 OF 03 031555Z

(JANUARY- DECEMBER 2003), VALUED AT USD 201.08 MILLION, TO

ASSIST 6.4 MILLION PEOPLE - MAINLY WOMEN AND CHILDREN - WHO

WILL STILL NEED FOOD ASSISTANCE IN SPITE OF A SLIGHT

INCREASE IN PROJECTED HARVESTS. WFP EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR JAMES

MORRIS VISITED NORTH KOREA, CHINA, SOUTH KOREA AND JAPAN

DURING NOVEMBER 2002, AND IS SERIOUSLY CONCERNED WITH THE

SIZEABLE RATCHETING DOWN OF BENEFICIARY COVERAGE (BY THREE

MILLION) DUE TO LACK OF DONOR SUPPORT. CLEARLY, LACK OF ANY

JAPANESE FOOD ASSISTANCE SUPPORT DURING 2002 (THEY

CONTRIBUTED 320,000 METRIC TONS IN 2001) - HAS HURT THE

PROGRAM. MORRIS\' STATEMENT TO THE UN SECURITY COUNCIL ON

DECEMBER 3 (WHERE HE WILL HIGHLIGHT INTER ALIA CURRENT FOOD

SHORTFALLS IN NORTH KOREA) AND SUBSEQUENT VISIT TO

WASHINGTON ON DECEMBER 5 WILL OFFER OPPORTUNITIES FOR DIRECT

DIALOGUE ON 2003 SUPPORT LEVELS AND THE CRITICAL NEED FOR

INCREASED WFP MONITORING AND ACCESS TO FOOD AID

DISTRIBUTIONS THROUGHOUT NORTH KOREA. END SUMMARY.

----------

BACKGROUND

----------

2. IN 2003, WFP ESTIMATES THAT 6.4 MILLION PEOPLE IN THE DPR

KOREA - MAINLY WOMEN AND CHILDREN - WILL STILL NEED FOOD

ASSISTANCE IN SPITE OF A SLIGHT INCREASE IN PROJECTED

HARVESTS. A JOINT FAO/WFP CROP AND FOOD SUPPLY ASSESSMENT

MISSION, WHICH VISITED THE DPRK IN SEPTEMBER/OCTOBER 2002

HAS FORECAST A 4.9 PERCENT INCREASE IN 2002/03 (NOVEMBER-

OCTOBER) PRODUCTION OVER LAST YEAR\'S PRODUCTION. WHILE THIS

HELPS THE NATIONAL FOOD BALANCE SHEET, DOMESTIC PRODUCTION

WILL AGAIN FALL BELOW MINIMUM FOOD NEEDS AND THE COUNTRY

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 04 ROME 05830 01 OF 03 031555Z

WILL AGAIN HAVE TO DEPEND ON SUBSTANTIAL EXTERNAL FOOD

ASSISTANCE FOR THE NEXT YEAR AS ITS CAPACITY TO IMPORT

COMMERCIALLY IS HIGHLY CONSTRAINED.

3. SINCE 1995 WFP HAS DELIVERED ABOUT 3 MILLION TONNES OF

FOOD AID, MOSTLY CEREALS, TO THE DPRK. FOR 2002, UNDER

EMERGENCY OPERATION 10141.0, THE PROVISION OF 611,000 TONNES

OF FOOD AID WAS APPROVED TO ASSIST 6.4 MILLION PEOPLE AT A

TOTAL COST TO WFP OF USD 215.8 MILLION. HOWEVER, THE

OPERATION REMAINED LARGELY UNDER RESOURCED THROUGHOUT THE

YEAR. BY THE END OF 2002, LESS THAN 450,000 TONNES WILL

HAVE BEEN DISTRIBUTED. WFP FOOD SHORTAGES HAVE BECOME MOST

ACUTE IN THE LAST QUARTER OF THE YEAR AND, WITH FEW PLEDGES

MADE IN LATE 2002 FOR ARRIVAL IN 2003, THERE WILL BE VERY

LIMITED CARRY-OVER STOCKS. BY NOVEMBER, NEARLY 3 MILLION

NURSERY, KINDERGARTEN, PRIMARY AND SECONDARY SCHOOL

CHILDREN, AS WELL AS PREGNANT AND NURSING WOMEN - ALL ON THE

WEST COAST - WERE DENIED WFP RATIONS.

-SUPPORT AGRICULTURAL RECOVERY THROUGH RELIEF AND

5. SECONDARY OBJECTIVES WILL AIM AT REDUCING THE IMPACT OF

NATURAL DISASTERS, PARTICULARLY THOSE ATTRIBUTED TO SEASONAL

TYPHOONS, AND MITIGATING THE POTENTIAL NEGATIVE AND

DISPROPORTIONATE IMPACT OF ECONOMIC REFORMS ON THE FOOD

SECURITY OF CERTAIN SEGMENTS OF THE POPULATION.

6. WFP\'S PROGRAMME WILL CONTINUE TO TARGET SPECIFIC GROUPS

WITHIN THE POPULATION, WHO HAVE SPECIAL DIETARY NEEDS AND/OR

HAVE NO WAY OF MEETING THEIR OWN MINIMUM ENERGY REQUIREMENTS

AND SOME OF THEIR MICRO-NUTRIENT NEEDS. THESE INCLUDE

CHILDREN FROM 6 MONTHS TO 10 YEARS, PREGNANT AND NURSING

WOMEN, THE ELDERLY AND THOSE PARTICULARLY AFFECTED BY

NATURAL DISASTERS AND THE ECONOMIC DOWNTURN. IN 2003, WFP

WILL SHIFT ITS TARGETING MORE TOWARDS THOSE DEPENDENT ON THE

PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION SYSTEM, RESIDING PREDOMINANTLY IN URBAN

AREAS. WITH THE EXCEPTION OF ORPHANS, WFP WILL EXCLUDE

FROM ITS COVERAGE RESIDENTS OF CENTRAL PYONGYANG.

-------------------------

WFP ACCESS AND MONITORING

-------------------------

7. WFP\'S POLICY OF "NO ACCESS, NO FOOD" WILL CONTINUE. THIS

MEANS THAT ONLY THOSE COUNTIES/DISTRICTS WHERE WFP IS

GRANTED ACCESS WILL RECEIVE DISTRIBUTIONS. DUE TO CHANGES

IN THE ADMINISTRATIVE SET-UP OF HAMHUNG CITY ANNOUNCED BY

THE GOVERNMENT IN LATE 2001, SEVEN DISTRICTS WERE MERGED

INTO TWO - HAMHUNG AND HUNGNAM CITIES. THE ACCESSIBLE

COUNTIES AND TOTAL COUNTIES FIGURES WERE THUS ADJUSTED (I.E.

EACH REDUCED BY FIVE) TO REFLECT THIS CHANGE. THEREFORE,

WFP CURRENTLY HAS ACCESS TO 163 OUT OF 206

COUNTIES/DISTRICTS. THIS ACCOUNTS FOR ABOUT 87 PERCENT OF

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 04 ROME 05830 02 OF 03 031556Z

WHAT IS BELIEVED TO BE THE COUNTRY\'S TOTAL CIVILIAN

POPULATION OF 22.6 MILLION.

8. NO NEW COUNTIES WERE OPENED TO WFP DURING 2002. THE MOST

RECENT COUNTY TO BE DECLARED ACCESSIBLE - SAKJU, IN NORTH

PYONGAN PROVINCE - WAS DONE SO IN OCTOBER 2001. WFP REMAINS

HIGHLY CONCERNED FOR THE PLIGHT OF POPULATION IN

INACCESSIBLE COUNTIES, AND IN 2003, WFP WILL CONTINUE ITS

EFFORTS TO GAIN ACCESS TO ADDITIONAL COUNTIES.

9. WFP WILL ALSO CONTINUE TO SEEK WIDER ACCESS TO

INFORMATION ON FARMERS\' MARKETS AND PRICES IN ORDER TO

ACHIEVE A GREATER UNDERSTANDING OF THE HOUSEHOLD FOOD

SECURITY SITUATION.
