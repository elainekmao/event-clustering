SUBJECT: ALL\'S NOT WELL IN WESTERN SLAVONIA

1. CONFIDENTIAL - ENTIRE TEXT.

2. SUMMARY: IN WESTERN SLAVONIA, THE AREA RETAKEN FROM

REBEL SERBS BY CROATIAN FORCES MAY 1-3, SERBS DETAINED BY

THE CROATS AS POSSIBLE CRIMINALS ARE REPORTEDLY FINDING

THAT THE GOC HAS ITS THUMB ON THE SCALES OF JUSTICE --

TRIALS ARE MOVING SLOWLY IF AT ALL, AND THE DETAINEES HAVE

INSUFFICIENT ACCESS TO COUNSEL. SERBS WHO REMAINED IN

THEIR HOMES ENCOUNTER HARASSMENT AND/OR EXPERIENCE

PROBLEMS IN RECLAIMING THEIR PROPERTY. REINTEGRATION OF

THE SERB POPULATION IS STILL FAR FROM REALITY. END

SUMMARY.

-------------------------------------

DETAINEES FACE LACKADAISICAL JUSTICE

-------------------------------------

3. IN A JULY 13 DISCUSSION WITH POLOFF, SENIOR UN CIVIL

AFFAIRS OFFICER IN PAKRAC GRAHAM DAY WAS COMPLETELY

DOWNCAST ABOUT THE SITUATION FOR SERBS IN FORMER SECTOR

WEST, DESCRIBING THEIR TREATMENT AS ONE OF CONSISTENT,

LOW-LEVEL BUREAUCRATIC NAGGING AND EVEN PERSISTENT ABUSE.

BY FAR THE MOST PRESSING PROBLEM, HE SAID, WAS OBTAINING

FUNDS TO PAY PRIVATE LAWYERS FOR THE COMPETENT DEFENSE OF

SERBS STILL INTERNED ON CHARGES OF WAR CRIMES. (NOTE:

SEVERAL OTHER UN OFFICIALS HAVE RECENTLY CRITICIZED TO US

THE PERFORMANCE OF THE PUBLIC DEFENDERS AND JUDGES

ASSIGNED TO THESE CASES. MOST OF THE CROATIAN LAWYERS HAD

NOT INTERVIEWED OR EVEN MET WITH THEIR CLIENTS BEFORE THE

TRIALS STARTED. IF THE LAWYERS ARRIVED AT THE TRIALS AT

ALL, THEY EITHER CAME LATE, OR LEFT EARLY. JUST DAYS

AFTER THE TRIALS STARTED, SEVERAL JUDGES RECESSED FOR A

ONE-MONTH SUMMER VACATION, LEAVING THE ACCUSED TO PONDER

THEIR FATE IN HOT, OVERCROWDED JAILS. 180 MEN HAVE BEEN

INDICTED AND ONLY FOUR LAWYERS WERE ASSIGNED TO DEFEND

THEM. TWENTY CASES HAVE GONE TO TRIAL AT THIS TIME. AN

OFFICIAL AT THE UN CENTER FOR HUMAN RIGHTS (THE

"MAZOWIECKI" COMMISSION) SAID ALL THE CHARGES WERE

ABSTRACT, AND UNCHR WAS ATTEMPTING TO GET THEM DROPPED.

UN OFFICIALS ARE MONITORING THESE TRIALS DAILY.

4. ONLY ONE INDIVIDUAL, MLADEN KULIC, HAS A PRIVATE

LAWYER, LOCATED FOR HIM BY UNOV (UN OFFICE IN VIENNA)

OFFICIAL MICHAEL PLATZER (PROTECT). PLATZER SAID THIS

LAWYER HAD BEEN HARASSED BY SEVERAL PUBLIC DEFENDERS AND

EVEN ONE JUDGE FOR TAKING ON HER CLIENT. AGAINST ALL

INTERNATIONAL STANDARDS, AND CONTRARY TO GOC TREATY

OBLIGATIONS, CROATIAN POLICE INSISTED ON ATTENDING THE

PRIVATE DISCUSSIONS SHE HELD WITH HER CLIENT. THE UN HAS

IDENTIFIED COMPETENT PRIVATE ATTORNEYS WHO ARE WILLING TO

TAKE OTHER CASES. HOWEVER, PRO BONO WORK IS NOT ALLOWED

UNDER CROATIAN LAW. MANY LAWYERS ARE NOT WILLING TO BILL

6. APPROXIMATELY 50 FAMILIES OF WEST SLAVONIAN SERBS NOW

IN BELGRADE HAVE EXPRESSED A DESIRE TO RETURN TO THEIR

HOMES IN CROATIA. THEY HAD LEFT IN MAY AND JUNE WITH THE

DAILY UNHCR CONVOYS TO BANJA LUKA. HOWEVER, BUREAUCRATIC

OBSTACLES IN BELGRADE HAVE PREVENTED THESE PEOPLE FROM

RETURNING. MINISTER MAJDAK\'S OFFICE CLAIMED THAT THESE

PEOPLE WERE WELCOME IN CROATIA, AND THAT NO IMPEDIMENTS

EXISTED ON THE PART OF THE GOC FOR THEIR RETURN.

ANTITHETICALLY, MANY FAMILIES IN THE PAKRAC AREA STILL

EXPRESS INTEREST IN LEAVING. THEY HAVE BEEN IDENTIFIED BY

UNHCR, AND ARE BEING DEALT WITH ON AN INDIVIDUAL BASIS.

DZAKULA AND DAY BOTH CONFIRMED THAT MANY OTHERS TALK OF

7. COMMENT. AN ARTICLE IN THE GOVERNMENT-INFLUENCED

DAILY VJESNIK JULY 14 IS TITLED "PEACEFUL AND STABLE IN

PAKRAC." AT A NEWS CONFERENCE ON JULY 12, PAKRAC POLICE

CHIEF IVKANEC STRESSED A SIMILAR THEME. SUCH IS FAR FROM

THE CASE, HOWEVER. EVENTS IN THE FORMER SECTOR WEST WILL

BE READ AS A TEST OF THE REINTEGRATION OF SERBIAN AND

CROATIAN CIVIL SOCIETIES. EXPERIENCE TO DATE IS NOT

ENCOURAGING. END COMMENT.
