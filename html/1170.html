SUBJECT: DUTCH ON IRAN: SHIRAZ DEFENDENTS, AG MINISTER VISIT

REF: SECSTATE 114655

Classified by A/Polcouns Eric Lundberg, Reasons 1.5 b/d

1.(C) A/Polcouns orally conveyed ref A points to Dutch MFA

PDAS for the Middle East Hans Driesser and Iran Deskoff Maria

Klomp on June 21. Driesser reviewed recent Dutch forays on

the Shiraz defendants, including Foreign Minister Van

Aartsen\'s contentious meeting with Iranian FM Kharazzi in

Tehran on May 29 (ref B). The EU, Driesser noted, had

consistently called for a transparent process and for full

access to defense lawyers for the defendants and had stressed

to Tehran that death sentences would be totally unacceptable.

A Dutch Embassy rep was present in Shiraz for all of the

press conferences and would return for the next press

conference two days prior to the sentencing. While Driesser

pointed out that the EU had not gone as far as demanding the

"release" of the defendants, he agreed that the EU\'s

conditions had not been met. He concurred that the U.S., EU

and other interested parties should engage in immediate

consultations following the verdict to fashion a coordinated

response.

Agriculture Minister to Visit

-----------------------------

2.(U) In other business, Driesser told A/Polcouns that the

announced visit of Dutch Agriculture Minister Brinkhorst -

held off in order to give Van Aartsen primacy of place -

would go forward as announced July 1-4. The combined

government/business delegation will focus on promotion of

sales of technology for the dairy, poultry, floral and

fisheries industries, as well as other areas. We understand

that Trade Minister Ybema is also mulling over a separate

visit, though no date has been set.
