SUBJECT: AMBASSADOR BURNS BRIEFS ITALIANS ON NATO ISSUES

CLASSIFIED BY: DCM WILLIAM POPE FOR REASONS 1.5(B), (D)

1. (C) SUMMARY. US AMBASSADOR TO NATO NICK BURNS DISCUSSED

TASK FORCE FOX, NATO-EU AND NATO-RUSSIA RELATIONS,

ENLARGEMENT AND THE BALKANS IN A SERIES OF MEETINGS WITH THE

ITALIAN DEFENSE MINISTER, OTHER SENIOR ITALIAN OFFICIALS,

JOURNALISTS AND POLITICAL LEADERS WHILE IN ROME FEBRUARY 7

AND 8. BURNS RAISED USG CONCERNS ABOUT THE WIDENING

CAPABILITIES GAP WITH NATO ALLIES, AND ADDRESSED ITALIAN

QUESTIONS REGARDING US UNILATERALISM AND THE FIGHT AGAINST

TERRORISM. DEFMIN ANTONIO MARTINO TOLD BURNS THAT ITALY IS

PREPARED TO TAKE COMMAND OF TFF FOR UP TO SIX MONTHS, SHOULD

THAT BE NECESSARY. HE NOTED THAT EU DEFENSE MINISTERS DO NOT

SHARE THE ENTHUSIASM OF EU FOREIGN MINISTERS FOR A FOLLOW-ON

EU COMMAND FOR TFF. ITALIAN OFFICIALS ALSO EXPLORED THE IDEA

OF TIMING A CELEBRATION OF THE CREATION OF THE NATO-RUSSIA

COUNCIL TO COINCIDE WITH PRESIDENT BUSH\'S TRIP TO MOSCOW IN

LATE MAY. BURNS SUGGESTED THAT POLDIR ARAGONA RAISE THE

ISSUE DURING HIS FEBRUARY 11-12 VISIT TO WASHINGTON. END

SUMMARY.

DEFMIN MARTINO

--------------

2. (C) WHILE IN ROME TO GIVE THE GRADUATION SPEECH AT THE

NATO DEFENSE COLLEGE, USNATO AMBASSADOR BURNS MET WITH

DEFENSE MINISTER MARTINO AND OTHER SENIOR ITALIAN OFFICIALS

FROM THE MFA, MOD, PRIME MINISTER\'S AND PRESIDENT\'S OFFICE,

AS WELL AS JOURNALISTS AND POLITICAL LEADERS, IN A SERIES OF

MEETINGS AND ROUNDTABLE DISCUSSIONS. IN A FEBRUARY 8

MEETING, DEFMIN MARTINO CONFIRMED THAT ITALY IS PREPARED TO

TAKE THE LEAD ON TFF FOR UP TO SIX MONTHS, IF REQUESTED.

MARTINO AGREED WITH BURNS THAT THE SITUATION IN MACEDONIA IS

FLUID AND ADMITTED THAT WORKING WITH THE MACEDONIANS CAN BE

UNPREDICTIBLE. BURNS SAID THE USG WILL SUPPORT AN EXTENSION

OF TFF FOR THREE TO FOUR MONTHS, BUT WOULD NEED TO REVIEW AN

EXTENSION OF SIX MONTHS. THE US IS OPEN TO DISCUSSING A

SHIFT IN COMMAND FROM NATO TO THE EU, SHOULD THE MANDATE NEED

TO BE EXTENDED AGAIN, BUT HAD NOT MADE ANY FIRM DECISIONS,

BURNS SAID. HE POINTED OUT THAT, AT NATO, BRITAIN, GERMANY

AND THE NETHERLANDS ARE RELUCTANT TO SUPPORT AN EU COMMAND

UNTIL ALL THE BERLIN-PLUS ARRANGEMENTS HAVE BEEN MET. MARTINO

NOTED THAT, UNLIKE THE "FOREIGN MINISTERS\' CLUB," THE

"DEFENSE MINISTERS\' CLUB" IS STRONGLY OPPOSED TO SHIFTING

COMMAND FROM NATO TO THE EU.

3. (C) BURNS COMMENTED ON THE DISTURBING CAPABILITIES GAP

BETWEEN THE US AND NATO ALLIES, AND POINTED TO LORD

ROBERTSON\'S SUGGESTION TO PARE DOWN THE DCI\'S 58 SPENDING

PRIORITIES TO A MORE MANAGEABLE FIVE OR SIX. IF THE CORE

NATO ALLIES FOCUSED ON DEVELOPING CAPABILITIES SUCH AS

STRATEGIC LIFT, SPECIAL OPS FORCES, PRECISION GUIDED

MUNITIONS AND SECURE COMMUNICATIONS, NATO WOULD HAVE THE

FLEXIBILITY TO RESPOND TO ASYMMETRICAL THREATS AS WELL AS

CONVENTIONAL ONES. MARTINO, AN ECONOMIST BY TRAINING,

ADMITTED THAT EUROPE HAS HAD A FREE RIDE FROM THE US FOR THE

PAST 50 YEARS. INCREASED DEFENSE SPENDING WILL HAVE TO BE

FINANCED BY INCREASED TAXATION -- NOT A PALATABLE OPTION,

MARTINO SAID. NATO NOT ONLY NEEDS TO REDUCE ITS PRIORITIES,

ITS MEMBERS NEED TO SPEND AVAILABLE FUNDS MORE EFFICIENTLY,

HE SAID. ALONG THESE LINES, MARTINO NOTED THE IMPORTANCE OF

ITALY\'S PARTICIPATION IN JOINT STRIKE FIGHTER, AND ADDED THAT

JSF IS AN OPPORTUNITY ITALY CAN\'T AFFORD TO MISS. HE AGREED

THAT ITALIAN INDUSTRY DEMANDS ARE STALLING COMPLETION OF THE

DEAL, AND SAID NATIONAL ARMAMENTS DIRECTOR ADMIRAL DIPAOLA

MAY MAKE SOME HEADWAY WHILE IN WASHINGTON DURING THE WEEK OF

FEBRUARY 11.

4. (C) MARTINO ADMITTED THAT NATO ENLARGEMENT WILL BE A

CHALLENGE BUT TOLD BURNS THAT BRINGING ROMANIA AND BULGARIA

IN IS IMPORTANT FOR THE BALKANS REGION. THEY WANT TO FEEL A

PART OF THE DEMOCRATIC COMMUNITY AT LARGE, AND IT WILL BE

HARD FOR THEM TO BE LEFT BEHIND IF SLOVENIA AND SLOVAKIA ARE

MAKE THE NECESSARY REFORMS.

POLDIR ARAGONA

--------------

5. (C) AMBASSADOR BURNS HEARD A SIMILAR MESSAGE ON

ENLARGEMENT FROM MFA POLITICAL DIRECTOR GIANCARLO ARAGONA

DURING A FEBRUARY 7 MEETING. ARAGONA SAID ADMITTING TWO OR

SEVEN OF THE NINE ASPIRANTS WOULD BE ACCEPTABLE BUT ADMITTING

FIVE WOULD BE DIFFICULT. STRENGTHENING SE EUROPE IS A

SIGNIFICANT NATIONAL INTEREST FOR ITALY, HE SAID. ALBANIA

AND MACEDONIA ARE NOT READY FOR MEMBERSHIP, BUT BRINGING

ROMANIA AND BULGARIA IN WOULD STABILIZE THE REGION AND HELP

SPUR NECESSARY REFORMS. BURNS NOTED THAT AN INTERAGENCY TEAM

WOULD BE TRAVELING TO THE ASPIRANT COUNTRIES IN FEBRUARY TO

EVALUATE HOW TO HELP THEM TOWARD MEMBERSHIP.

TO CELEBRATE THE CREATION OF THE NATO-RUSSIA COUNCIL AFTER

THE NATO FM\'S MEETING IN REYKJAVIK. ON FEBRUARY 4, DEFMIN

MARTINO HAD REPEATED AN EARLIER ITALIAN INVITATION TO HOST

TRIP TO MOSCOW MAY 23-26, ARAGONA SAID. BURNS NOTED THAT THE

REYKJAVIK FM\'S MEETING MAY 14-16 OR THE FIFTH ANNIVERSARY OF

THE FOUNDING ACT ON MAY 27, BURNS SAID. THE CZECH\'S WERE

LIKELY TO OBJECT TO HOLDING AN EVENT TOO CLOSE TO THE PRAGUE

SUMMIT IN NOVEMBER, HE NOTED.

8. (C) BURNS\' LUNCHEON WITH SENIOR GOI OFFICIALS FEATURED

SIMILAR THEMES. PARTICPIANTS INCLUDED PRESIDENT CIAMPI\'S

SENIOR DIPLOMATIC ADVISOR, PURI PURINI; PM BERLUSCONI\'S

SENIOR MILITARY ADVISOR, GEN. TRICARICO; MOD DIPLOMATIC

ADVISOR GRECO; VICE CHOD CAMPORINI; AND MFA SENIOR SECURITY

ADVISOR PENSA. THERE WAS GENERAL AGREEMENT THAT NATO

COUNTRIES NEED TO INCREASE DEFENSE SPENDING AND SPEND THOSE

FUNDS MORE EFFICIENTLY. THE OFFICIALS ALSO AGREED THAT NATO

NEEDS TO BE ABLE TO RESPOND MORE FLEXIBLY TO UNCONVENTIONAL

THREATS AS WELL AS TO THE CONVENTIONAL THREATS THAT HAVE BEEN

THE BASIS FOR THE ALLIANCE. GENERAL CAMPORINI NOTED THAT

COMMAND STRUCTURE LIMITATIONS MAKE IT DIFFICULT FOR NATO TO

DEPLOY TROOPS QUICKLY. IN DISCUSSING TFF COMMAND, CAMPORINI

FLOATED THE IDEA THAT ITALY COULD ASSUME COMMAND OF TFF UNDER

A NATO FLAG, AND THAT THE TRANSITION TO AN EU MISSION COULD

COME AT THE HALFWAY POINT OF THE ITALIAN COMMAND.

9. (C) THROUGHOUT AMB. BURNS\' MEETINGS, GOI OFFICIALS SENT A

CLEAR AND CONSISTENT MESSAGE OF A CLOSE ALLY THAT IS FULLY

WILLING -- AND AT TIMES EAGER -- TO WORK MORE CLOSELY WITH

THE US ON POL-MIL AFFAIRS. BUILDING UPON ONGOING SUPPORT FOR

OEF AND THE PREVIOUS GOVERNMENT\'S CLOSE COLLABORATION DURING

THE KOSOVO CAMPAIGN, KEY PLAYERS IN BERLUSCONI\'S GOVERNMENT

ARE SEEKING AVENUES OF COOPERATION RANGING FROM MD TO

NATO-RUSSIA TO DEFENSE-INDUSTRIAL COOPERATION. SUCH

OPPORTUNITIES HAVE THEIR LIMITS, AS THE CENTER-RIGHT GOI

PURSUES ITS OWN CONCEPT OF NATIONAL SELF-INTEREST. BUT THEY

ARE REAL. WITH THE GOI\'S DEEPENING SENSE THAT KEY EUROPEAN

"PARTNERS\' ARE UNWILLING TO GIVE ITALY ITS DUE, THE US HAS A

UNIQUE OPPORTUNITY TO FOSTER EVEN CLOSER GOI-USG COOPERATION,

BOTH WITHIN THE ALLIANCE AND BILATERALLY.

10. (U) AMBASSADOR BURNS HAS CLEARED THIS MESSAGE.
