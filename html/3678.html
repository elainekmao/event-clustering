SUBJECT: SHOOTOUT ON COMMERCIAL FARM

Classified By: political section chief Matt Harrington. Reasons: 1.5 (

B) and (D).

1. (C) Late in the morning of September 10, commercial

farmer Ian Cochrane reportedly engaged in a shootout with

three men on his property near Karoi, some 250 km northwest

of Harare. Miraculously, no one was hurt, and only a vehicle

belonging to a nearby farmer who came to help was damaged.

2. (C) The account of the incident was relayed to us by two

other commercial farmers in Karoi -- Chris Shepherd and Alan

Parsons -- who are reliable embassy contacts and received a

report directly from Cochrane. According to our sources,

Cochrane had gone to the aid of his mother, who was besieged

by settlers in her residence, some 400 meters from Cochrane\'s

own house. He forced his way through a group of

demonstrating settlers by firing his shotgun into the ground,

then ran to the back of the house when he found the front

locked. He encountered three men, two holding shotguns and

the other an automatic rifle. One of the men -- Patrick

Maponga -- has played a leading role in recent efforts to

evict commercial farmers in the area and is believed by area

residents to be a major in the Zimbabwean military. Maponga

and his two compatriots, we are told, opened fire on

Cochrane, who responded in kind, and a shootout ensued. We

understand that Cochrane is now safely inside his mother\'s

residence, the three attackers have fled, and police have

responded to the scene.

3. (C) Comment: Although details are still fuzzy, at first

blush, this incident has the definite appearance of a

(potentially deadly) set-up. Although we may never know the

intent of the three armed men, it is clear that the

government will use it to the fullest in its propaganda

campaign against white farmers. We expect Ian Cochrane to

play the prominent role of villain in the

government-controlled press on September 11, and the GOZ

could use this incident to justify an intensified crackdown

on "resistant" commercial farmers.
