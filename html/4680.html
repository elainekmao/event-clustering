1. (C) DRAWING ON POINTS REFTEL, POLOFF ON DECEMBER 11

EMPHASIZED WITH GIOVANNI RICCIULI, DIRECTOR OF THE MFA\'S

OFFICE FOR MEDITERRANEAN AND SOUTHERN EUROPE ISSUES, THE

IMPORTANCE OF KEEPING THE PRESSURE ON ALL SIDES TO AGREE TO A

CYPRUS SETTLEMENT. RICCIULI UNDERLINED THAT THE GOI SHARED

THE USG\'S SENSE OF URGENCY, AND IN FACT FM FRATTINI WOULD BE

MEETING WITH CYPRIOT FM IOANNIS KASOULIDES IN ROME THAT VERY

AFTERNOON. (COMMENT: RICCIULI INTERRUPTED THE MEETING TO

ASK AN ASSISTANT TO HAND CARRY REFTEL POINTS TO FRATTINI\'S

STAFFERS SO THAT THEY COULD BE INCLUDED IN HIS BRIEFING

MATERIALS.)

2. (C) RICCIULI SAID THAT THE DETAILED ANALYSIS PROVIDED

REFTEL WOULD PROVE VALUABLE DURING THE FRATTINI/KASOULIDES

MEETING AND IN DISCUSSIONS LEADING UP TO AND IN COPENHAGEN.

HOWEVER, HE CONTINUED, IF WE HOPED FOR A CYPRUS AGREEMENT IN

TIME FOR THE COPENHAGEN SUMMIT, ALL SIDES WOULD HAVE TO

CONCUR ON THE POLITICAL NECESSITY OF AN AGREEMENT; THE

"POLITICAL WILL" OF BOTH SIDES TO AGREE ON THE OVERALL

CONCEPT OF THE UN PROPOSAL IS NECESSARY. DETAILS WOULD HAVE

TO BE WORKED OUT LATER. UNSYG ANNAN HAD TIMED THIS PROPOSAL

CAREFULLY, TO COINCIDE WITH COPENHAGEN. FRATTINI WOULD

EMPHASIZE TO KASOULIDES THAT THERE WAS "NO TIME TO WAIT UNTIL

AFTER COPENHAGEN" FOR A DEAL, THAT AGREEMENT WOULD REQUIRE A

"MAJOR POLITICAL EFFORT" AND CONCESSIONS, AND THAT NO SIDE

WOULD BE COMPLETELY HAPPY. RICCIULI SAID THAT FRATTINI KNOWS

THAT THE EU, U.S., AND UN MUST KEEP THE PRESSURE ON ALL SIDES.

3. (C) SANDRO DE BERNADIN, IN THE MFA\'S OFFICE OF EUROPEAN

INTEGRATION, TOLD POLOFF DECEMBER 11 THAT THE GOI CAME OUT OF

THE COPENHAGEN MINISTERIAL "HOPEFUL" THAT ENOUGH PROGRESS

COULD BE MADE ON ESDP AND BERLIN-PLUS IN THE COMING MONTHS SO

THAT EUROPE COULD "HONOR ITS DECLARED COMMITMENTS" TO HELP

STABILIZE MACEDONIA. THIS COULD NOT BE DONE WITHOUT

AGREEMENT ON BERLIN-PLUS, AND ITALY SHARES THE BRITISH VIEW

THAT AD-HOC ARRANGEMENTS OR BERLIN-PLUS "WORK AROUNDS" ARE

UNACCEPTABLE.

4. (C) ACCORDING TO DE BERNADIN, MOST MINISTERS AT THE

GAERC WERE SUPPORTIVE OF HIGH REP SOLANA\'S IDEA TO LINK

BERLIN-PLUS TO ONLY THOSE COUNTRIES PARTICIPATING IN

PARTNERSHIP FOR PEACE. THE PROPOSAL CAUGHT GREEK FM

PAPANDREOU OFF GUARD, HE SAID, BUT THE GOI WAS HOPEFUL THAT

THE PROPOSAL ANSWERED GREEK CONCERNS. THE GOI WOULD WORK

HARD AT COPENHAGEN TO DE-LINK THE ESDP AND TURKEY ACCESSION

ISSUES, DESPITE GERMAN FEELINGS TO THE CONTRARY.
