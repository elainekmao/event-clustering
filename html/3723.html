1. (C) SUMMARY. ITALY IS MOVING CLOSER TO WHERE WE WANT OUR

NATO ALLIES TO BE ON IRAQ. PM BERLUSCONI, AND INCREASINGLY

OTHER TOP GOVERNMENT LEADERS, UNDERSTAND THE STRATEGIC

IMPORTANCE OF NEUTRALIZING IRAQ\'S WMD CAPABILITIES. THEY

HOPE WAR CAN BE AVOIDED. HOWEVER, THROUGH PUBLIC REMARKS

AND A FORWARD-LEANING COMMUNICATIONS STRATEGY, THE GOI IS

BEGINNING TO PREPARE THE ITALIAN PEOPLE FOR THE POSSIBILITY

THAT MILITARY ACTION AGAINST BAGHDAD WILL BE TAKEN.

BERLUSCONI AND DEPUTY PM FINI CONTINUE TO STRESS THAT THE USE

OF FORCE TO DEFANG IRAQ, PREFERABLY UNDER UN COVER, IS

WARRANTED IF OTHER APPROACHES FAIL. ACCELERATED HIGH-LEVEL

INTELLIGENCE SHARING, CLOSE CONSULTATIONS, AND FOCUSED PUBLIC

OUTREACH ARE KEY TO ENSURING THE GOI KEEPS ITS MESSAGE ON

TRACK. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) ITALY WILL BE A HELPFUL PARTNER WHEN PRESIDENT BUSH

VISITS EUROPE IN NOVEMBER AND AT THE NATO DEFENSE MINISTERIAL

LATER THIS MONTH. TOP GOVERNMENT OFFICIALS, ESPECIALLY PRIME

MINISTER BERLUSCONI, ARE MOVING IN THE RIGHT DIRECTION ON

IRAQ AND WILL NOT SHY AWAY FROM DISCUSSING IT BENEFICIALLY IN

A NATO CONTEXT. EVEN IN THIS FRIENDLY ENVIRONMENT, HOWEVER,

THE GAME IS NOT OVER.

3. (C) ON THE PUBLIC AFFAIRS FRONT, BERLUSCONI AND HIS

COMMUNICATIONS TEAM ARE DOING A COMMENDABLE JOB OF PREPARING

THE ITALIAN PEOPLE FOR POSSIBLE MILITARY ACTION.

BERLUSCONI\'S LATEST FORAY--A SIGNED FRONT-PAGE ARTICLE IN AN

ITALIAN DAILY SEPTEMBER 11--CONDEMNED IRAQ\'S SYSTEMATIC

VIOLATION OF ITS UN OBLIGATIONS, ARGUED THAT THE USE OF FORCE

TO AFFECT CHANGE SHOULD NOT BE EXCLUDED, AND AVERRED THAT

THERE ARE MOMENTS IN HISTORY WHICH CALL FOR PREVENTIVE

ACTION. "THINGS EITHER CHANGE," HE SAID, "OR IT WILL BE

NECESSARY TO ACT IN A CONCRETE MANNER, WITH EVERY POSSIBLE

DIPLOMATIC AND POLITICAL VEHICLE, AND WITHOUT EXCLUDING THE

MILITARY OPTION."

4. (C) LATER, COMMENTING PUBLICLY ON THE PRESIDENT\'S UN

SPEECH, BERLUSCONI SAID HE WAS SURE THE SECURITY COUNCIL

WOULD NOT HESITATE TO IMPOSE INSPECTIONS ON IRAQ NOR TO

AUTHORIZE THE USE OF MILITARY FORCE IN THE EVENT SADDAM

CONTINUES TO FLOUT ITS RESOLUTIONS. ASKED DIRECTLY WHETHER

ITALY WOULD TAKE PART IF THERE WAS AN ATTACK, BERLUSCONI

HEDGED: "LET\'S WAIT AND SEE," HE SAID. "AT THE RIGHT TIME

ITALY, ALONG WITH ITS EUROPEAN PARTNERS, WILL TAKE THE RIGHT

DECISION." OTHER KEY GOVERNMENT OFFICIALS--WHO HAVE THEIR

OWN POLITICAL ORCHARDS TO TEND--ARE BEGINNING TO ADOPT MORE

FORWARD-LEANING POSITIONS. DEPUTY PM FINI, LEADER OF THE

SECOND LARGEST PARTY IN THE GOVERNING COALITION, SAID IN

PUBLIC REMARKS AT THE AMBASSADOR\'S RESIDENCE ON SEPTEMBER 11

THAT, "IN THE STRUGGLE AGAINST TERRORISM THE USE OF MILITARY

FORCE IS SOMETIMES NECESSARY."

5. (C) PRESIDENT CIAMPI, A KEY OPINION MAKER AND INDEPENDENT

FROM BERLUSCONI\'S GOVERNMENT, HAS HEWED TO A MORE CIRCUMSPECT

PUBLIC POSITION, BUT NEVERTHELESS UNDERSCORED ITALY\'S STRONG

SOLIDARITY WITH THE UNITED STATES IN THE FIGHT AGAINST

TERRORISM IN REMARKS COMMEMORATING SEPTEMBER 11. DURING AN

OFFICE CALL BY THE AMBASSADOR AND DCM PRIOR TO PUBLIC

CEREMONIES ON SEPTEMBER 11, CIAMPI SAID IT WAS NECESSARY TO

FIGHT TERROR BUT ALSO TO ERADICATE IT FROM THE "MINDS AND

HEARTS" OF ITS POTENTIAL RECRUITS. HE EXPLAINED THAT

FIGHTING TERRORISM INCLUDED, IN HIS OPINION, ACTING TO

DESTROY WMD, WHILE WINNING HEARTS AND MINDS MEANT ADDRESSING

POLITICAL AND SOCIAL PROBLEMS IN THE MIDDLE EAST AND

ELSEWHERE. WE MAY HEAR MORE OF THIS TWO-TRACK APPROACH IN

COMING WEEKS AS GOI POLITICAL LEADERS WORK TO BUILD PUBLIC

SUPPORT FOR THE POSSIBLE USE OF FORCE AGAINST IRAQ.

6. (C) THE CENTER-LEFT OPPOSITION IS SPLIT BETWEEN THOSE

OPPOSED TO WAR AT ALL COSTS, AND OTHERS--NOTABLY LEADERS OF

THE LARGEST LEFT-WING PARTY--WHO ACKNOWLEDGE THAT, IF

AUTHORIZED BY THE UN SECURITY COUNCIL, IT CANNOT BE RULED OUT

AS AN EXTREME MEASURE. THE CENTER-LEFT DOES NOT HAVE

SUFFICIENT WEIGHT IN THE PARLIAMENT TO HOLD THE GOVERNMENT

HOSTAGE ON KEY FOREIGN POLICY MATTERS. HOWEVER, ON A MATTER

OF SUCH GRAVITY, BERLUSCONI WILL NOT BE ABLE TO RUN ROUGHSHOD

OVER THE OPPOSITION, NOR IS HE SO INCLINED. ASSISTING THE

GOI BUILD BROAD POLITICAL BACKING FOR OUR IRAQ POLICY WILL

HELP ENSURE THAT ITALIAN SUPPORT IS DELIVERED AND CAN BE

SUSTAINED.

BRIEFING ON IRANIAN AND IRAQI WMD PROGRAMS SEPTEMBER 13 IN

AND, MORE BROADLY, EUROPEAN SECURITY. RECENT POLLING DATA ON

PUBLIC SUPPORT FOR MILITARY ACTION IS CONFLICTING. ONE

SURVEY PUBLISHED SEPTEMBER 12 SHOWED 59 PERCENT AGREED A

"PREVENTIVE INTERVENTION IN IRAQ" WOULD BE JUSTIFIED, WHILE

ANOTHER THE SAME DAY SAID 66 PERCENT OPPOSED MILITARY ACTION.

TWO THINGS, HOWEVER, ARE CLEAR: PUBLIC OPINION IS IN FLUX

AND A WELL-TARGETED, AND, WE DARE SAY, RELENTLESS PA EFFORT

CAN AFFECT IT.

8. (C) COMMENT. THE GOI WILL DO ITS UTMOST TO ENSURE THAT

THE EU ARRIVES AT A UNIFIED POSITION ON IRAQ. IN THE EVENT

THAT PROVES IMPOSSIBLE, OUR ACE IS THAT BERLUSCONI

UNDERSTANDS--AND SHOULD APPRECIATE EVEN MORE AFTER HIS

MEETINGS IN THE UNITED STATES--THE STRATEGIC IMPORTANCE OF

DEFANGING BAGHDAD. MOREOVER, BERLUSCONI HAS DEMONSTRATED A

CAPACITY FOR LEADING PUBLIC OPINION ON FOREIGN POLICY ISSUES

HE KNOWS ARE MAKE OR BREAK FOR US. HE AND OTHER KEY GOI

LEADERS, HOWEVER, REMAIN CONCERNED ABOUT THE DESTABILIZING

POTENTIAL OF WAR IN THE NEAR EAST. THEIR PREFERENCE IS TO

AVOID IT. REASSURING THEM THAT WE HAVE THOUGHT THROUGH THE

IMPLICATIONS AND ARE COMMITTED TO POST-CONFLICT

RECONSTRUCTION--AND PROVIDING RELEVANT POINTS FOR THE GOI TO
