CLASSIFIED BY: POL MINCOUNS THOMAS COUNTRYMAN, REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D)

1. (C) SUMMARY: IN HIS PRESENTATIONS BEFORE PARLIAMENT,

ITALY\'S PRIME MINISTER BERLUSCONI FOCUSED ON THE NEED TO

SUPPORT THE UNITED STATES, UNDERSCORED THE THREAT FROM IRAQ\'S

WEAPONS CAPABILITIES, AND NOTED THAT MULTILATERAL

ORGANIZATIONS MUST BE EFFECTIVE IN ORDER TO KEEP INDIVIDUAL

COUNTRIES FROM ACTING UNILATERALLY. THE GOVERNING COALITION

IS LINED UP SQUARELY BEHIND THE PRIME MINISTER. THE

OPPOSITION MAINTAINS ITS RESERVATIONS, ESPECIALLY THE NEED TO

AVOID UNILATERAL ACTION, WITH PUBLIC STATEMENTS HEAVY ON THE

NEED TO "PREVENT WAR" AND NEGATIVE ON POTENTIAL ITALIAN

INVOLVEMENT. PRIVATELY, THE MORE CENTRIST ELEMENTS OF THE

OPPOSITION ARE PREPARED TO CONCEDE THE POTENTIAL NEED FOR

MILITARY INTERVENTION -- UNDER UN AUSPICES. EMBASSY

CONTINUES TO HIGHLIGHT THE NATURE OF THE THREAT TO CONTACTS

BOTH IN THE GOVERNMENT AND IN THE OPPOSITION. END SUMMARY.

2. (U) PRIME MINISTER BERLUSCONI PRESENTED THE GOI POSITION

ON IRAQ TO THE CHAMBER OF DEPUTIES AT 10:30 A.M. ON SEPTEMBER

25 AND TO THE SENATE AT 4:30 P.M. THE SAME DAY. HIS SPEECH

FOCUSED ON THE NEED TO SUPPORT THE UNITED STATES AND

UNDERSCORED THAT EFFECTIVE MULTILATERAL ACTION COULD PRECLUDE

THE NEED FOR UNILATERAL ACTION BY THE USG. THE PRIME

MINISTER MADE CLEAR THAT THE THREAT POSED BY SADDAM HUSSEIN

AND HIS WMD IS REAL: IT IS "A REGIME THAT THREATENS TO USE

DREADFUL CHEMICAL AND BACTERIOLOGICAL TOOLS THAT COULD LEAD

TO DRAMATIC EVENTS." BERLUSCONI EMPHASIZED THAT ITALY, ON

THE BASIS OF ITS NATIONAL INTERESTS, MUST WORK WITH EUROPE

AND THE UNITED STATES TO ELIMINATE THE IRAQI THREAT AND IRAQI

WEAPONS OF MASS DESTRUCTION AND TO ENSURE A STRONG, DETAILED,

CLEAR UN RESOLUTION DEFINING CONDITIONS FOR THE "MEASURED USE

OF FORCE" IN CASE OF A NEW IRAQI CHALLENGE TO THE

INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY. HE CHARACTERIZED THE HISTORICAL

COST OF INACTION AS "INCALCULABLE." THE ONLY ALTERNATIVE,

THEREFORE, IS THE CONSTRUCTION OF A COALITION CAPABLE OF

IMPOSING ON IRAQ RESPECT FOR A NEW, STRONG, CLEAR AND

PRESSING UN RESOLUTION. REFERRING TO ITALY\'S CONSTITUTION,

BERLUSCONI SAID "ITALY REPUDIATES WAR AS AN OFFENSIVE

INSTRUMENT," BUT ADDED THAT PREVENTION IS SOMETIMES NECESSARY.

3. (C) THE GOVERNING COALITION LINED UP BEHIND THE PRIME

MINISTER. MEETING WITH THE DCM AWAY FROM THE DEBATE, CHAMBER

FOREIGN AFFAIRS COMMITTEE CHAIRMAN GUSTAVO SELVA (NATIONAL

ALLIANCE, AN) SAID HIS DEFENSE COMMITTEE COUNTERPART, LUIGI

RAMPONI (AN), WAS TAKING THE SAME POSITION AS THE PM IN THE

CHAMBER EVEN AS SELVA AND THE DCM MET (REF A). FERDINANDO

ADORNATO, CHAIRMAN OF THE CHAMBER\'S CULTURE AND SCIENCE

COMMITTEE AND FORMER PARTY SPOKESMAN, WOULD SPEAK FOR

BERLUSCONI\'S FORZA ITALIA (FI) PARTY. "WE WILL NOT LEAVE THE

U.S. ALONE," SELVA ASSURED US.

4. (U) OPPOSITION REPRESENTATIVES, NOT SURPRISINGLY,

OPPOSED BERLUSCONI\'S STATEMENT. MARGHERITA (DAISY) PARTY

PRESIDENT FRANCESCO RUTELLI SAID THERE WAS NO SPACE FOR A

UNILATERAL SOLUTION IN EITHER WAR OR PEACE. NOTHING CAN

SUBSTITUTE FOR UN LEGITIMACY, AND A U.S. DECISION CANNOT

AUTOMATICALLY BECOME AN INTERNATIONAL OR ITALIAN ONE. LIKE

DEMOCRATS OF THE LEFT (DS) REPRESENTATIVES, RUTELLI

EMPHASIZED THE NEED FOR ITALY TO WORK WITH ITS EUROPEAN

ALLIES. (RUTELLI ADDED "AND AMERICA.") DS SECRETARY PIERO

FASSINO RHETORICALLY ASKED BERLUSCONI: WHAT CONSEQUENCES

WOULD WAR HAVE FOR RELATIONS BETWEEN WESTERN AND ISLAMIC

SOCIETIES? WHAT REPLY COULD ITALY EXPECT IN THE WAY OF

TERRORIST ACTS? WHAT WOULD BE THE CONSEQUENCES FOR THE

MIDDLE EAST? FASSINO SAID THE PARLIAMENTARY DEBATE SHOWED

THAT ITALY SHOULD DO ALL IN ITS POWER TO AVOID WAR. DS

PRESIDENT MASSIMO D\'ALEMA ACCUSED BERLUSCONI OF BEING NOT AN

ALLY OF, BUT SUBSERVIENT TO, THE BUSH ADMINISTRATION. IN

KOSOVO, HE SAID, THERE WAS THE NEED TO DEFEND CIVILIANS WHO

WERE THREATENED AND EVICTED FROM THEIR HOMES. IN

AFGHANISTAN, THERE WAS EVIDENCE OF A TERRORIST PRESENCE AND

THE WORLD ACTED IN CONFORMITY WITH INTERNATIONAL

LEGITIMIZATION. NOW, HE CONTINUED, ITALY IS CONFRONTED WITH

THE IDEA OF A PREVENTIVE WAR, WITH PROPOSED ACTION OUTSIDE

THE FRAMEWORK OF INTERNATIONAL LEGITIMACY. D\'ALEMA

INACCURATELY ACCUSED BERLUSCONI OF FAILING EVEN TO MENTION

THE EUROPEAN UNION IN HIS REMARKS. THE GREENS, COMMUNIST

RENEWAL, AND THE ITALIAN COMMUNIST PARTY ALL CRITICIZED THE

SPEECH, AS WELL.

INTERNATIONAL AFFAIRS FOR THE DAISY AND DS PARTIES THE

MORNING OF SEPTEMBER 25. BOTH WERE WILLING TO LISTEN TO U.S.

VIEWS AND FULLY CONSCIOUS OF THE NEED TO PREVENT ANTI-WAR

ARGUMENTS FROM BECOMING ANTI-U.S. RHETORIC. BOTH STRONGLY

UNDERSCORED THE IMPERATIVE FOR UN SUPPORT FOR ANY ACTION IN

IRAQ. DAISY REPRESENTATIVE LAPO PISTELLI WAS MORE

FORWARD-LEANING AND ACKNOWLEDGED THAT UN WEAKNESS COULD FORCE

A U.S. REACTION. DS REP MARINA SERENI WAS LESS WILLING TO

CONCEDE THE POTENTIAL NEED FOR MILITARY INTERVENTION AND THE

FACT THAT IRAQ HELD THE KEY FOR PREVENTING IT. EACH MADE

CLEAR THAT BAGHDAD HAD ZERO CREDIBILITY WITH THE ITALIAN

LEFT. HOWEVER, EACH SAID THAT THE USG SEEMED TO BE

UNDERESTIMATING THE NEGATIVE REGIONAL CONSEQUENCES OF U.S.

MILITARY ACTION.

6. (C) THE PRIME MINISTER\'S DEPUTY DIPLOMATIC ADVISER,

PAOLO DIONISI, TOLD DCM AND POLMINCOUNS THE AFTERNOON OF

SEPTEMBER 25 THAT BERLUSCONI WAS VERY SATISFIED WITH HIS

STATEMENT BEFORE PARLIAMENT AND WITH THOSE MADE BY HIS

COALITION PARTNERS. DIONISI THOUGHT OPPOSITION REACTION WAS

PREDICTABLE AND MANAGEABLE.

7. (C) COMMENT: IN HIS SPEECH, BERLUSCONI DID A SOLID JOB

IN CONTINUING TO LAY THE GROUND TO BRING PUBLIC OPINION

BEHIND HIM IF THE U.S. CALLS ON ITALY TO SUPPORT ACTION

AGAINST IRAQ (REF B). HE URGED INTERNATIONAL ACTION, BUT

UNDERSCORED SUCH ACTION MUST BE STRONG, CLEAR, DECISIVE --

AND BACKED BY THE WILLINGNESS TO USE FORCE. HIS COALITION IS

SUPPORTING THE PM IN HIS EFFORTS TO BRING THE ITALIAN PUBLIC

ALONG. OPPOSITION INSISTENCE ON A UN IMPRIMATUR, HOWEVER, IS

ALSO A VERY REAL REFLECTION OF PUBLIC OPINION HERE. EVEN

ITALIANS ALREADY PERSUADED THAT WE MUST ACT AGAINST IRAQ

UNDERSCORE THAT ACTION UNDER A UN UMBRELLA IS FAR PREFERABLE

TO ACTION ALONE OR BY ONLY A FEW. CHAMBER FOREIGN AFFAIRS

COMMITTEE CHAIR SELVA EXPRESSED SATISFACTION FOLLOWING HIS

MEETING WITH THE DCM, SAYING "YOU HAVE REINFORCED JUST THOSE

POINTS I WANTED TO HEAR" WITH REFERENCE TO THE FACT THAT

PRESIDENT BUSH HAS NOT DECIDED ABSOLUTELY IN FAVOR OF

MILITARY ACTION AND THAT THE U.S. WOULD PREFER TO ACT THROUGH

THE UN AND IN CONCERT WITH ITS ALLIES. IF AND WHEN THE TIME

COMES, BERLUSCONI WILL DO HIS UTMOST TO HELP AND SUPPORT THE

USG, BUT HIS ABILITY TO ACT WILL BE CONSIDERABLY RESTRICTED

ABSENT A UN MANDATE, OR AT LEAST A BROAD AND COHESIVE

INTERNATIONAL COALITION.
