SUBJECT: CROATIA SEEKS BILATERAL MEETING WITH SECRETARY

POWELL DURING UNGA

CLASSIFIED BY: DCM PATRICK S. MOON FOR REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D)

1. (SBU) ON JULY 29, DCM MET WITH VICE SKRACIC, HEAD OF THE

MFA\'S UN DEPARTMENT. SKRACIC PRESENTED THE GOC\'S FORMAL

REQUEST FOR A BILATERAL MEETING WITH SECRETARY POWELL FOR

CROATIAN FOREIGN MINISTER TONINO PICULA DURING THIS YEAR\'S

UNGA. SKRACIC SAID THAT THE GOC UNDERSTANDS HOW BUSY THE

SECRETARY WILL BE AND THAT IF A MEETING ON THE MARGINS OF

SIPDIS

UNGA IS NOT POSSIBLE, PICULA IS READY TO TRAVEL TO WASHINGTON

EITHER IMMEDIATELY BEFORE OR AFTER THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY.

PICULA NOW PLANS TO BE IN NEW YORK FROM SEPTEMBER 22 - 26,

BUT WOULD BE READY TO TRAVEL TO WASHINGTON EITHER ON

SEPTEMBER 18 - 19 OR SEPTEMBER 29 - 30 FOR A MEETING WITH THE

SECRETARY. SKRACIC UNDERSTOOD THAT SCHEDULES WILL TAKE TIME

SIPDIS

TO FORMULATE, BUT ASKED FOR AN EARLY RESPONSE SO CROATIA\'S

EMBASSY IN WASHINGTON CAN BEGIN WORK ON A PROGRAM OF OTHER

SUBSTANTIVE MEETINGS FOR THE MINISTER.

2. (SBU) SKRACIC SAID THAT THE MAIN SUBJECTS OF DISCUSSION

WOULD BE CROATIA\'S CONTRIBUTION OF TROOPS TO IRAQ, A POSSIBLE

COMPROMISE FORMULA ON ARTICLE 98/ICC AND COUNTERTERRORISM.

THE DCM TOLD SKRACIC THAT WE WOULD FORWARD THE GOC REQUEST.

HE NOTED THAT DURING A BUSY TIME LIKE THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY,

SENIOR U.S. OFFICIALS OFTEN HELD THEMATIC MEETINGS WITH

GROUPINGS OF REGIONAL LEADERS. IN THIS REGARD, HE ASKED

WHETHER CROATIA\'S PARLIAMENT WOU.LD APPROVE BEFORE PICULA\'S

TRIP A PLANNED DEPLOYMENT OF TROOPS TO IRAQ. SKRACIC SAID HE

WAS CONFIDENT THAT THE PARLIAMENT WOULD APPROVE THE

DEPLOYMENT IN ADVANCE OF THE MINISTER\'S TRIP.

3. (SBU) SKRACIC ASKED THAT WE EXPLORE THE POSSIBILITY OF A

PHOTO OPPORTUNITY FOR CROATIAN PRESIDENT MESIC WITH PRESIDENT

BUSH. MESIC NOW PLANS TO ARRIVE IN NEW YORK ON SEPTEMBER 21

AND INTENDS TO DEPART BY THE END OF THE WEEK.

POST RECOMMENDATION

-------------------

4. (C) WE DO NOT SEE JUSTIFICATION FOR A PHOTO OPPORTUNITY

FOR PRESIDENT MESIC, EXCEPT PERHAPS AS PART OF A GROUP

PHOTOGRAPH (PERHAPS OF ADRIATIC CHARTER COUNTRIES OR OF

COUNTRIES WHICH ARE PROVIDING TROOPS TO IRAQ). LIKEWISE WE

DO SEE ENOUGH JUSTIFICATION FOR A PRIVATE BILATERAL MEETING

WITH FM PICULA, ALTHOUGH WE WOULD SUPPORT HIS PARTICIPATION

IN A MEETING OF REGIONAL LEADERS WITH SENIOR USG OFFICIALS.
