SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) IN A SEPTEMBER 29 MEETING WITH THE AMBASSADOR,

MDC PRESIDENT MORGAN TSVANGIRAI CALLED THE BOMBING OF MDC

HEADQUARTERS AND SUBSEQUENT RAIDS ON ITS OFFICES

"AMATEURISH." THE GOZ HAS NO EVIDENCE AGAINST THE MDC

LEADERSHIP BUT WOULD CONTINUE TO TRY TO PAINT THE PARTY

AS A SUBVERVISE ORGANIZATION. TSVANGIRAI AGREED THAT THE

GOZ WAS TRYING TO USE THE ZIMBABWE DEMOCRACY ACT AS A

SCAPEGOAT, BUT THE MDC WOULD TRY TO KEEP THE FOCUS ON THE

GOVERNMENT\'S POLITICAL AND ECONOMIC MISMANAGEMENT. HE

LAMENTED THAT ZANU-PF MPS HAVE BEEN UNWILLING TO

COOPERATE WITH THE MDC ON ISSUES OF CRITICAL NATIONAL

IMPORTANCE. TSVANGIRAI EXPRESSED CONCERN THAT THE GOZ IS

USING WAR VETERANS AS A RESERVE ARMY, INDICATED BY THEIR

RECENT PLACEMENT UNDER THE AUTHORITY OF THE MINISTRY OF

DEFENSE. THE MDC PRESIDENT REPORTED THAT THE MDC IS

PLANNING A RALLY IN HARARE ON SEPTEMBER 30 TO CELEBRATE

THE PARTY\'S ONE-YEAR ANNIVERSARY. THE PARTY\'S EXECUTIVE

COMMITTEE WOULD MEET SUBSEQUENTLY TO DISCUSS ORGANIZING

MASS ACTION. THE AMBASSADOR URGED TSVANGIRAI TO EXERCISE

RESTRAINT AND CAUTION WHEN ORGANIZING SUCH ACTIVITIES.

END SUMMARY.

2. (C) MDC PRESIDENT MORGAN TSVANGIRAI CALLED ON

AMBASSADOR MCDONALD ON SEPTEMBER 29. HE WAS ACCOMPANIED

BY MDC CHAIRMAN ISAAC MATONGO AND SENIOR ADVISOR GANDI

MUDZINGWA. THE AMBASSADOR WAS JOINED BY THE DCM AND

BOMBING AND RAIDS OF MDC OFFICES

--------------------------------

3. (C) TSVANGIRAI SAID HE HAD REQUESTED THE MEETING

BECAUSE HE WANTED TO TOUCH BASE AND TO RAISE SEVERAL

SPECIFIC POINTS. FIRST, HE SAID, THE RECENT BOMBING OF

MDC HEADQUARTERS AND THE SUBSEQUENT SEARCH OF THE PARTY\'S

OFFICES HAD SIMPLY BEEN A PRETEXT TO GAIN ACCESS TO THE

MDC\'S RECORDS AND STRATEGIES. TSVANGIRAI REPORTED THAT

TWO PEOPLE HAD BEEN CHARGED IN THE CASE (NOTE: ON TWO

COUNTS OF TERRORISM AND SABOTAGE UNDER THE LAW AND ORDER

MAINTENANCE ACT. END NOTE) -- A SERVING POLICE OFFICER

(ZACHARIA NKOMO) AND AN MDC SECURITY OFFICIAL (SOLOMON

CHIKOWERO). NKOMO, WHO ALLEGEDLY THREW THE GRENADE, HAD

PARTICIPATED IN THE MDC\'S YOUTH MOVEMENT BUT HAD NEVER

DONE SECURITY WORK FOR THE PARTY, AS ALLEGED BY THE GOZ.

THE GOZ, TSVANGIRAI INSISTED, WAS TRYING TO FABRICATE A

LINKAGE BETWEEN NKOMO AND CHIKOWERO, WHOM IT HAS ACCUSED

OF GIVING THE GRENADE TO NKOMO. TSVANGIRAI SCOFFED AT

THE CHARGES AGAINST CHIKOWERO, WHO HAS BEEN RELEASED ON

BAIL, SAYING THE GOZ HAD NO EVIDENCE AGAINST HIM AND WAS

TRUMPING UP CHARGES AGAINST HIM TO JUSTIFY FURTHER ACTION

AGAINST THE OPPOSITION PARTY.

4. (C) WHAT STEPS WAS THE GOZ PLANNING TO TAKE NEXT

AGAINST THE MDC, THE AMBASSADOR ASKED? TSVANGIRAI

DESCRIBED THE WHOLE BOMBING INCIDENT AND SUBSEQUENT RAID

AS "AMATEURISH." THE GOZ IS TRYING TO PAINT THE MDC AS A

SUBVERSIVE ORGANIZATION SO THAT IT CAN ARREST THE PARTY

ZIMBABWE DEMOCRACY ACT

----------------------

6. (C) TSVANGIRAI RAISED THE ZIMBABWE DEMOCRACY ACT

(ZDA), NOTING THE GOZ\'S ATTEMPTS TO PORTRAY IT AS A

SANCTIONS BILL WHICH UNDERMINES THE COUNTRY\'S

SOVEREIGNTY. THE AMBASSADOR AGREED THAT GOZ OFFICIALS

HAVE TRIED TO USE THE ZDA AS A SCAPEGOAT FOR PROBLEMS OF

THEIR OWN MAKING. HE POINTED OUT THAT THE ZDA CONVEYS A

VERY IMPORTANT MESSAGE WHICH THE GOZ SHOULD TAKE TO

HEART. ACCORDING TO TSVANGIRAI, THE MDC POSITION IS THAT

THE BILL LAYS OUT, ACCURATELY, THE UNFORTUNATE CURRENT

FACTS OF LIFE IN ZIMBABWE. THE OPPOSITION PARTY, HE

SAID, WOULD CONTINUE TO TRY TO KEEP THE FOCUS ON THE

VIOLENCE IN THE COUNTRY AND THE GOVERNMENT\'S REFUSAL TO

RESTORE AND ENSURE THE RULE OF LAW.

PARLIAMENT

----------

7. (C) TSVANGIRAI NOTED THAT THE MDC MEMBERS OF

PARLIAMENT HAD BEEN "RAISING LOTS OF VOICES," AND HE

APPEALED FOR ASSISTANCE AIMED AT STRENGTHENING PARLIAMENT

AS AN INSTITUTION. THE AMBASSADOR DESCRIBED THE MDC\'S

WINNING OF 57 SEATS IN JUNE AS A VERY POSITIVE

DEVELOPMENT BUT EXPRESSED CONCERN THAT IT MAY BE

DIFFICULT FOR DEMOCRACY TO TAKE HOLD UNDER THE CURRENT

SERIOUS POLITICAL AND ECONOMIC PROBLEMS. ZANU-PF,

TSVANGIRAI REPLIED, HAS NOT ACCEPTED THE ELECTION RESULTS

SIPDIS

AND THAT IS WHY THE RULING PARTY CONTINUES TO BE SO

PAGE 03 HARARE 05581 02 OF 03 291557Z

CONFRONTATIONAL. HE LAMENTED THAT ZANU-PF MPS HAVE

EVINCED NO WILLINGNESS TO ENGAGE IN BIPARTISAN

COOPERATION, EVEN ON ISSUES OF CRITICAL NATIONAL

IMPORTANCE, SUCH AS LAND, ECONOMY, AND RESTORATION OF THE

RULE OF LAW.

WAR VETERANS

------------

8. (C) TSVANGIRAI CLAIMED THAT THE GOZ IS USING WAR

VETERANS AS A RESERVE ARMY BECAUSE PRESIDENT MUGABE DOES

NOT TRUST THE REGULAR ARMY TO DO HIS DIRTY WORK.

THE RECENT PLACEMENT OF THE WAR VETERANS ASSOCIATION

UNDER THE MINISTRY OF DEFENSE, HE SAID, FORMALIZES THEIR

MILITARY ROLE, WHICH WORRIES THE MDC A GREAT DEAL. IN

THE MEANTIME, THE LAWLESSNESS CONTINUES THROUGHOUT THE

COUNTRY AND THE GOZ IS MAKING NO ATTEMPT TO CONTROL IT.

MASS ACTION

-----------

9. (C) THE AMBASSADOR ASKED TSVANGIRAI TO COMMENT ON

REPORTS OF THE MDC\'S INTENTION TO ORGANIZE SOME SORT OF

MASS ACTION. THE PARTY, TSVANGIRAI REPLIED, IS PLANNING

TO HOLD A MID-DAY RALLY IN HARARE\'S RUFARO STADIUM ON

SATURDAY, SEPTEMBER 30 (NOTE: MDC CHAIRMAN ISAAC MATONGO

LATER TOLD DCM AND POLOFF THAT THE PARTY EXPECTS 50-

60,000 PEOPLE FROM AROUND THE COUNTRY TO ATTEND. END

NOTE.) THE PURPOSE OF THE RALLY IS TO CELEBRATE THE ONE-

YEAR ANNIVERSARY OF THE PARTY\'S FOUNDING, TO THANK MDC

SUPPORTERS FOR THEIR COURAGE DURING THE PAST YEAR, AND TO

DISCUSS THE PARTY\'S NEXT STEPS.

10. (C) ASKED WHETHER STAYAWAYS WOULD BE ORGANIZED AFTER

THE RALLY, TSVANGIRAI SAID THEY WERE UNAVOIDABLE BUT THAT

NOTHING HAD BEEN PLANNED. THE AMBASSADOR URGED THE MDC

TO EXERCISE RESTRAINT AND CAUTION IN PLANNING THE NEXT

STEPS. THE OPPOSITION PARTY, HE POINTED OUT, HAS BEEN

SUCCESSFUL BECAUSE IT HAS MAINTAINED PUBLIC FOCUS ON

ISSUES WHICH MATTER TO MOST ZIMBABWEANS, WITHOUT TRYING

TO INFLAME AN ALREADY EXPLOSIVE SITUATION. HE EXPRESSED

HOPE THAT THE PARTY WOULD CONTINUE TO TAKE THIS APPROACH

AND WOULD NOT GIVE THE GOVERNMENT AN EXCUSE TO CRACK

DOWN. TSVANGIRAI ACCEPTED THE POINT, BUT SAID HE WAS IN

A DELICATE POSITION -- PEOPLE ACROSS THE COUNTRY ARE

ANGRY AND ARE DEMANDING ACTION, AS THE CURRENT COURSE OF

EVENTS IN ZIMBABWE IS UNSUSTAINABLE. THE SEPTEMBER 30

RALLY, HE SAID, WOULD SET THE TONE FOR A SUBSEQUENT

EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE MEETING, WHICH WOULD DECIDE ON THE

PARTY\'S FUTURE ACTIONS.

PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION -- IS HE CONSIDERING STEPPING DOWN

OR WOULD HE SERVE OUT HIS TERM. HE HAS SEEN NO

COMMENT

-------

12. (C) TSVANGIRAI WAS SOMEWHAT CALMER THAN THE LAST

TIME WE MET WITH HIM, BUT CLEARLY VERY WORRIED ABOUT

RECENT POLITICAL AND ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENTS. IT WAS

OBVIOUS FROM HIS COMMENTS THAT THE MDC IS FEELING INTENSE

PRESSURE FROM ITS SUPPORTERS NATIONWIDE TO TAKE A FIRM

STAND AGAINST THE GOVERNMENT\'S FAILURE TO TURN THE

ECONOMY AROUND AND TO RESTORE THE RULE OF LAW. HE IS

UNDERSTANDABLY RELUCTANT, HOWEVER, TO ENCOURAGE LARGE-

SCALE PROTESTS FOR FEAR OF UNLEASHING A GOVERNMENT

CRACKDOWN THAT COULD TURN BLOODY. AT THE SAME TIME, THE

MDC RISKS LOSING LEGITIMACY IF IT DOES NOT RESPOND TO THE
