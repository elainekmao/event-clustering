SUBJECT: MFA PROTESTS VIETNAM HUMAN RIGHTS ACT

1. (U) CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES

ROBERT C. PORTER, JR. REASON: 1.5 (D)

2. ( C ) SUMMARY: AN MFA DEMARCHE TO CHARGE

ON SEPTEMBER 10 CHARACTERIZED THE HOUSE

PASSAGE OF THE VIETNAM HUMAN RIGHTS ACT

(VHRA) AS THE MOST SERIOUS STEP SINCE

NORMALIZATION OF RELATIONS AND WARNED OF

UNPREDICTABLE CONSEQUENCES, INCLUDING ON

RATFICATION HERE OF THE BTA. THE OFFICIAL

REACTION IS PREDICTABLE BUT NONETHELESS

CONFIDENTIAL

REFLECTS ONGOING TOP LEVEL SUSPICION OF US

MOTIVES TOWARD VIETNAM. END SUMMARY.

3. ( C ) FOREIGN MINISTRY (MFA) AMERICAS

DEPARTMENT DIRECTOR NGUYEN XUAN PHONG CALLED

CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES TO MFA ON SEPTEMBER 10,

HAVING BEEN UNWILLING TO MEET WITH ANY OTHER

EMBASSY OFFICIAL ON SEPTEMBER 7 IN CHARGE\'S

ABSENCE IN HO CHI MINH CITY. PHONG TOLD

CHARGE THAT HE WISHED TO EXPRESS THE

"FEELING" THAT THE PASSAGE OF THE VIETNAM

HUMAN RIGHTS ACT (VHRA) COULD HAVE A

NEGATIVE IMPACT ON THE DEVELOPMENT OF

BILATERAL RELATIONS AND ON THE WILLINGNESS

OF THE GOVERNMENT AND PEOPLE OF VIETNAM TO

WORK WITH THE USG. HE NOTED THAT THE "HARD

REALITY" OF THE 410-1 VOTE IN THE HOUSE OF

REPRESENTATIVES MADE IT "IMPOSSIBLE TO SAY"

THAT THIS REFLECTED MINORITY OPINION.

4. ( C ) PHONG LABELED THE CONTENT OF THE

VHRA AS A "BLATENT LIE" AND AS "GROSS

INTERFERENCE" IN VIETNAM\'S INTERNAL AFFAIRS.

HE NOTED THAT VIETNAM HAD REPEATEDLY DURING

THE NEGOTIATIONS ON THE BILATERAL TRADE

AGREEMENT URGED THE REVOCATION OF THE

JACKSON-VANICH AMENDMENT AS A "COLD WAR

RELIC." HOWEVER, NOT ONLY DID THE JACKSON-

VANICH AMENDMENT STILL APPLY TO VIETNAM, BUT

CONGRESS WAS NOW SEEKING TO MAKE AN "EVEN

WORSE" LAW (THE VHRA), WHICH WAS AIMED AT

CONFIDENTIAL

OPPOSING THE INDEPENDENCE AND SOVEREIGNTY OF

VIETNAM, AS WELL AS BEING AGAINST THE

INTERESTS OF THE FUTURE OF BILATERAL

RELATIONS.

5. ( C ) PHONG STATED THAT THE GOVERNMENT

AND PEOPLE OF VIETNAM VIEWED THE VHRA AS THE

"MOST BLATANT AND SERIOUS" ACT REGARDING

POLICY TOWARD VIETNAM SINCE THE

NORMALIZATION OF RELATIONS. HE CALLED THE

IMPACT OF ITS POSSIBLE PASSAGE BY THE SENATE

AS "UNPREDICTABLE," ESPECIALLY AS CONCERNED

NATIONAL ASSEMBLY RATIFICATION OF THE BTA.

PHONG SAID THAT THE MFA "SQUARELY OPPOSED"

THE VHRA AND REQUESTED THE STATE DEPARTMENT

TO "DO WHATEVER" TO STOP SENATE APPROVAL, IN

THE INTEREST OF RELATIONS BETWEEN THE TWO

NATIONS.

6. ( C ) PHONG ALSO EXPRESSED CONCERN THAT

THE STATE DEPARTMENT, CONTRARY TO

EXPECTATIONS, HAD NOT FORMALLY OPPOSED HOUSE

PASSAGE OF THE VHRA, AND CALLED ON

ADDITIONAL STEPS BY THE STATE DEPARTMENT

BEFORE SENATE CONSIDERATION. SENATE PASSAGE

COULD HAVE A "VERY, VERY NEGATIVE" IMPACT

HERE AND WOULD BE "VERY DANGEROUS" TO THE

FUTURE OF THE RELATIONSHIP. HE NOTED THAT

IT WAS ALREADY "VERY DIFFICULT" TO EXPLAIN

HOUSE PASSAGE OF THE VHRA TO THE PEOPLE AND

LEADERSHIP OF VIETNAM, SOME OF WHOM WERE

CONFIDENTIAL

CONCERNED ABOUT A PERCEIVED HARDENING OF US

POLICY TOWARD VIETNAM OR EVEN ABOUT A

"SECOND AGENDA" OF SEEKING TO DESTROY

VIETNAM\'S "REVOLUTION, COUNTRY, AND PEOPLE,

IN SPITE OF BEAUTIFUL WORDS." HE NOTED THE

VHRA\'S INCLUSION OF FUNDS FOR GROUPS TO

PROMOTE DEMOCRACY AND FREEDOM, WHICH SOME IN

VIETNAM SAW AS A PRETEXT TO URGE VIETNAMESE

PEOPLE TO "RAISE UP AGAINST THE GOVERNMENT."

7. ( C ) WHILE EMPHASIZING THE POSITIVE

VOTE ON BTA THE SAME DAY BY THE HOUSE,

CHARGE PROMISED TO CONVEY MFA CONCERNS TO

WASHINGTON. HE NOTED THAT HOUSE PASSAGE OF

THE VHRA REFLECTED CONCERN OVER THE HUMAN

RIGHTS SITUATION IN VIETNAM AND URGED

VIETNAM TO CONSIDER STEPS TO IMPROVE THE

HUMAN RIGHTS SITUATION AND ITS INTERNATIONAL

IMAGE, SUCH AS ACCESS BY INDEPENDENT

OBSERVERS TO THICH QUANG DO, AS WELL AS TO

THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS. HE AGREED WITH PHONG

CONFIDENTIAL

THAT THE TWO COUNTRIES NEEDED TO WORK HARD

TO FIND WAYS TO KEEP THE RELATIONSHIP MOVING

IN A POSITIVE DIRECTION, AND AGAIN URGED THE

GVN TO BE MORE RESPONSIVE TO OVERALL HUMAN

RIGHTS CONCERNS.

9. ( C ) COMMENT: MUCH OF THE MFA DEMARCHE

WAS PREDICTABLE, AND PHONG READ CAREFULLY

FROM A SCRIPT THAT ECHOED SIMILAR COMMENTS

BY THE MFA SPOKESMAN AND IN A SEPTEMBER 8

"NHAN DAN" COMMENTARY. THE SHRILL TONE IS

PROBABLY ALSO RELATED TO WHAT APPEARS TO BE

ONGOING HEIGHTENED WORRY AMONG SOME OF THE

LEADERSHIP ABOUT THE USG\'S COMMITMENT TO THE

INADEQUATELY ABLE TO EXPLAIN TO THE

LEADERSHIP THE ABSENCE OF FORMAL STATE
