SUBJECT: TFIZ01: KUWAIT OIL UPDATE -- APRIL 05, 2003

REF: KUWAIT 1232 AND PREVIOUS

Classified By: DCM Frank C. Urbancic for reasons 1.5 (B) and (D)

1. (C) Detailed oil production for Kuwait Oil Company was not

available today, but production likely remains just under 2.4

million barrels.

2. (C) EconChief had a chance to visit the two wells in the

Rumeila field of Southern Iraq that continue burning. One

well is burning crude oil, emitting a plume of thick black

smoke. The KOC firefighting team is on site at the well.

KOC had earlier tried to extinguish the fire using water from

trucks, but ran out of water before the fire was put out.

They have now dug a lagoon which they had filled with water,

and preparations for another attempt were being made as of

the early afternoon. If successful, the fire would be out by

sundown.

3. (C) The other well is burning gas, with a very hot flame

but a much cleaner smoke plume. Boots and Coots (B and C)

officials told EconChief that once KOC had its fire out,

water trucks would focus on filling the lagoon B and C had

dug. Once filled, B and C would then begin efforts to

control the second well. If all goes according to plan, the

two fires could be out by Monday or Tuesday.

4. (U) Local Arabic press today reported that the KOC will

suspend its operations in Iraq after the two fires still

burning in Rumeila are extinguished, leaving any remaining or

new fires to B and C.
