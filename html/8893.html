SUBJECT: U/S BOLTON ENCOURAGES EARLY SIGNING OF AN

ICC ARTICLE 98 AGREEMENT

1. (U) Classified by Ambassador Marcelle M. Wahba

for reasons 1.5 (B) and (D).

2. (C) In the course of a June 18 meeting in Abu

Dhabi with MFA Minister of State Shaykh Hamdan bin

Zayid Al-Nahyan, Under Secretary for Under

Secretary for Arms Control and International

SIPDIS

Security Assistance John R. Bolton urged the UAE to

conclude a bilateral ICC Article 98 agreement (see

septel for other topics). U/S Bolton provided an

overview of USG progress made towards signing ICC

Article 98 agreements, 45 of which have been

concluded. U/S Bolton had signed the most recent

one that morning with Kuwait. He laid out the

President\'s desire to see more agreements

concluded, including one with the UAE, particularly

in light of the potential for the unfair targeting

of Americans and the fact that the ICC has recently

begun operations. Bolton noted the ridiculous

cases brought in Belgium against former President

Bush, Secretary Powell and General Franks.

3. (C) Shaykh Hamdan affirmed the UAE\'s desire for

an agreement and pledged his personal involvement

in signing one as soon as possible. Hamdan said

the U.S. had done the world a huge favor in

removing Saddam Hussein\'s regime in Iraq and was

pursuing a similarly just policy in North Korea and

Iran. In Hamdan\'s view, the U.S. "deserves our

support" and the least the UAE could do was to

swiftly conclude an Article 98 agreement.

4. (C) Post will follow up with the MFA to ensure prompt

implementation of Hamdan\'s promise.

Wahba
