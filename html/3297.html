2. (C) SUMMARY. THE LEADERSHIP TURNOVER AT THE 11TH

NATIONAL ASSEMBLY (NA) COULD BE BROADER THAN EARLIER

EXPECTED AS THE PARTY SEEKS TO DEMONSTRATE ITS

VIGILANCE AGAINST CORRUPTION. UNDER ONE SCENARIO, THE

NA CHAIRMAN COULD BE FORCED OUT AND REPLACED BY CURRENT

PRIME MINISTER KHAI, WHILE HCMC CHIEF TRIET TAKES OVER

THE TOP GOVERNMENT JOB. SENIOR LEVEL CHANGES AT THE

MINISTRY OF PUBLIC SECURITY, INCLUDING POSSIBLY THE 66-

YEAR OLD MINISTER, ARE ALSO LIKELY. NO FACTS ARE YET

AVAILABLE, BUT THE VIVID RUMOR MILL IS A RESULT OF THE

CLOSED NATURE OF THIS SOCIETY AS WELL AS AN INDICATION

OF AT LEAST SOME ELITE DISSATISFACTION WITH PERCEIVED

CORRUPT ACTIVITIES OF SOME SENIOR LEADERS. MAJOR POLICY

SHIFTS THAT WOULD AFFECT US INTERESTS REMAIN UNLIKELY

EVEN UNDER SUCH SCENARIOS. END SUMMARY.

3. (C) ACCORDING TO NOTED PRO-DEMOCRACY AND ANTI-

CORRUPTION ACTIVIST DR. NGUYEN THANH GIANG (PLEASE

PROTECT), THE FALLOUT FROM THE NAM CAM CORRUPTION CASE

CONTINUES TO AFFECT LAST-MINUTE DECISION-MAKING AT THE

ONGOING 6TH PARTY PLENUM IN PREPARATION FOR THE OPENING

OF THE 11TH NATIONAL ASSEMBLY (REFTEL), WHICH HAS NOW

BEEN RESCHEDULED TO BEGIN ON JULY 17. GIANG TOLD POL/C

AND POLOFF ON JULY 12 THAT HE BELIEVES PERSONNEL SHIFTS

COULD BE EVEN MORE EXTENSIVE THAN EARLIER EXPECTED.

HIS SOURCES TELL HIM THAT NATIONAL ASSEMBLY CHAIRMAN

NGUYEN VAN AN HAS RECENTLY BEEN ACCUSED OF ACCEPTING

BRIBES FROM NAM CAM SEVERAL YEARS AGO IN HIS CAPACITY AS CHIEF

OF THE PARTY\'S PERSONNEL AND ORGANIZATION COMMISSION;

AN MAY NOW BE FORCED TO STEP DOWN, CONTRARY TO EARLIER

PREDICTIONS.

4. (C) GIANG SAID THAT, UNDER THIS SCENARIO, PRIME

MINISTER PHAN VAN KHAI -- PREDICTED BY MOST OBSERVERS

TO REMAIN IN OFFICE, BUT ONLY FOR 1-2 MORE YEARS --

WOULD MOVE OVER TO TAKE AN\'S PLACE. GIANG NOTED THAT

KHAI\'S OWN REPUTATION IS INCREASINGLY SOILED BY THE

ACTIVITIES OF KHAI\'S SON, A RUMOR WE HAVE ALSO HEARD

FROM OTHER HANOI RESIDENTS. WIVES OF AT LEAST FIVE

PROMINENT SENIOR PARTY FIGURES, INCLUDING AN, KHAI,

FORMER PRIME MINISTER VO VAN KIET, AND MPS\' HUONG, ARE

ALSO ALLEGED TO HAVE HAD LINKS WITH NAM CAM DIRECTLY OR

INDIRECTLY, GIANG ADDED.

5. (C) IF KHAI MOVES TO THE NA, GIANG PREDICTED THAT

HO CHI MINH CITY PARTY CHIEF NGUYEN MINH TRIET WOULD

TAKE OVER AS PRIME MINISTER. ALTHOUGH MANY OBSERVERS

HAVE SUGGESTED THAT THE NAM CAM CASE WOULD HURT TRIET

GIVEN THE HCMC NEXUS, GIANG STRESSED THAT NAM CAM\'S

MOST NOTORIOUS ACTIVITIES TOOK PLACE BEFORE TRIET MOVED

TO HCMC, AND THAT HIS ARREST ACTUALLY HAPPENED ON

TRIET\'S WATCH. ALLEGATIONS OF CORRUPTION AGAINST THE

OTHER MAIN CONTENDER FOR THE PM SLOT, DEPUTY PRIME

MINISTER NGUYEN TAN DUNG, HAVE HURT THE LATTER\'S

CHANCES, GIANG NOTED.

6. (C) GIANG ALSO EXPRESSED CERTAINTY THAT MINISTER

OF PUBLIC SECURITY LT. GENERAL LE MINH HUONG WOULD ALSO

BE FORCED TO RETIRE DUE TO THE NAM CAM CASE, ALONG WITH

ONE OR TWO DEPUTY MINISTERS. (NOTE: ADDITIONALLY,

GENERAL HUONG MAY HAVE HEALTH PROBLEMS; HE SEEMED

NOTABLY PALSIED DURING AMBASSADOR\'S MAY 14 MEETING WITH

HIM. END NOTE) HE PREDICTED THAT HUONG\'S REPLACEMENT

WOULD BE POLITBURO SECRETARIAT MEMBER TRUONG VINH
