1. (U) This is an action request. See para 6.

2. (C) Embassy received a letter from United States Senator

Bob Graham on June 18 requesting action on behalf of a

constituent regarding a Cuban citizen who had applied for

asylum and was being held in detention in The Netherlands.

Dutch government officials informed PolOff that the asylum

applicant, JOSE MANUEL VARGAS MARTIN, was denied asylum and

will be deported to Cuba on June 30. GoNL officials informed

PolOff that, at any time before his deportation on June 30,

Martin would be allowed to travel to any third country that

either issued him a valid visa or provided official

verification that Martin would be allowed entry to a third

country if transported to a border crossing.

3. (C) Martin applied for a visa at the Embassy of Costa

Rica, where he apparently has family ties. The Costa Rican

Embassy forwarded his application to San Jose on June 20.

PolOff spoke with the Costa Rican ConOff (protect source),

who indicated that San Jose will likely not/not issue a visa

since Martin arrived in The Netherlands without a passport or

other valid photo identification. Martin has not/not applied

for a U.S. visa.

4. (C) UNHCR informed PolOff that UNHRC will not entertain

VARGAS MARTIN\'s refugee application based on the fact that

the GONL determined he did not have a legitimate asylum

claim. UNHCR also told PolOff that the applicant is

represented by legal counsel and lost an appeal of the asylum

decision.

5. (SBU) The letter forwarded to post by Senator\'s Graham\'s

office (on behalf of his stepmother Ada Maria Pastran Vargas)

indicates that VARGAS MARTIN will be persecuted upon return

to Cuba, partially because of his former relationship with

current Miami NBC reporter Joaquin Torres Alvarez. Post has

no additional information regarding a credible fear of

persecution.

------------------

Action Request

------------------

6. (C) Embassy The Hague requests Department guidance on this

case. Does the Department have any additional information to

provide to the GoNL that could influence his case? Please

advise what, if any, alternative recourse may be available in

the United States and whether Department wishes to respond

directly to Senator Graham or have post do so.
