SUBJECT: MAPUTO FOLLOW-UP: KATANGA OFFENSIVE MUST CEASE

REF: STATE 208224

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 06111 301553Z

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD FOR REASONS 1.5

B/D.

1. (C) PER REFTEL INSTRUCTION, AMBASSADOR MCDONALD MET

WITH MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS PERMANENT SECRETARY

WILLARD CHIWEWE, IN THE ABSENCE OF FOREIGN MINISTER

MUDENGE, OCTOBER 30, AND MADE THE POINTS IN PARA 1. THE

AMBASSADOR ALSO LEFT A NON-PAPER WITH THE POINTS FOR

CHIWEWE\'S CONSIDERATION.

2. (C) THE SENIOR ZIMBABWEAN DIPLOMAT RESPONDED, "WE

UNDERSTAND." HE NOTED THAT ZIMBABWE AND THE U.S. HAD

VIRTUALLY NO DIFFERENCES ON PRINCIPLES, BUT HE TOOK

EXCEPTION TO OUR CHARACTERIZATION OF KABILA BEING

RESPONSIBLE FOR THE LATEST OFFENSIVE IN KATANGA. "IT

COULD BE SOLDIERS," HE SHRUGGED. IN ANY EVENT, HE

CONTINUED, THE "SKIRMISH NEEDS TO BE REGRETTED." HE

UNDERSCORED ZIMBABWE\'S POSITION THAT THE SADC STATES ARE

ANXIOUS TO SEE A PERMANENT END TO HOSTILITIES IN THE

CONGO. HE ALSO CALLED FOR THE UNITED STATES TO USE ITS

INFLUENCE TO SECURE THE WITHDRAWAL OF RWANDA AND UGANDA

FROM THE CONGO. AMBASSADOR MCDONALD ASSURED THE

PERMANENT SECRETARY THAT HIS COUNTERPARTS IN KIGALI AND

KAMPALA ARE MAKING SIMILAR POINTS TO THEIR HOST

GOVERNMENTS ON A REGULAR BASIS.

MCDONALD
