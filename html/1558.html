SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) ON FEBRUARY 28, ECONOFF MET WITH COLLIN CLOETE,

VICE-PRESIDENT FOR COMMODITIES OF THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS

UNION (CFU), TO DISCUSS THE UPCOMING MARCH 21 CFU

SPECIAL CONGRESS AND REPORTS OF VEHEMENT DISAGREEMENTS

AT THE UNION\'S TOP LEADERSHIP LEVEL. CLOETE, A KEY

MEMBER OF THE CFU EXECUTIVE, TOLD US THAT THE SPECIAL

CONGRESS WILL CENTER ON CONSIDERING WAYS THAT THE CFU

CAN BROADEN ITS MEMBERSHIP AND INTEGRATE BETTER INTO

ZIMBABWE\'S BLACK MAJORITY SOCIETY, AS WELL AS TO

CONSIDER DIFFERENT WAYS TO ENGAGE THE GOVERNMENT OF

ZIMBABWE (GOZ) ON LAND REFORM. CLOETE CLAIMED, NOT

ENTIRELY CONVINCINGLY, THAT THE CONGRESS WILL NOT

CONSIDER A WHOLESALE CHANGE IN THE CFU LEADERSHIP. HE

STATED THAT THE CFU COUNCIL HAS ALREADY AGREED THAT THE

CURRENT LEADERSHIP WILL REMAIN UNTIL THE ANNUAL CFU

ELECTIONS IN AUGUST, AND ADDED THAT THE SPECIAL CONGRESS

WILL NOT ENDORSE THE GOZ "FAST-TRACK" RESETTLEMENT

PROGRAM. CLOETE INDICATED THAT THE ALLEGED DIVISIONS IN

THE CFU MANAGEMENT THAT WERE RECENTLY COVERED IN THE

PRESS WERE THE RESULT OF A FORMER CFU PRESIDENT, NICK

SWANEPOEL, ATTEMPTING TO CUT A SECRET DEAL WITH THE GOZ

ON THE LAND ISSUE (REF A). END SUMMARY.

-----------------------------

THE NEED TO INTEGRATE THE CFU

-----------------------------

2. (C) CLOETE SAID THAT THE CFU NOW RECOGNIZES THAT IT

WAS A MISTAKE NOT TO HAVE INTEGRATED ITS MEMBERSHIP BY

MERGING WITH THE INDIGENOUS COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION

(ICFU), WHICH REPRESENTS BLACK COMMERCIAL FARMERS. THE

SPECIAL CONGRESS IS GOING TO CONSIDER THE ISSUE OF

FORMALLY APPROACHING THE ICFU TO BEGIN MERGER TALKS.

CLOETE ALSO INDICATED THAT THE SPECIAL CONGRESS WILL

CONSIDER WAYS FOR THE CFU TO BROADEN ITS OUTREACH AND

LINKS TO BLACK ZIMBABWEAN CIVIC ORGANIZATIONS.

--------------------------------------------- -

CHANGING THE LAND DEBATE TO THE COMMUNAL LANDS

--------------------------------------------- -

3. (C) CLOETE INDICATED THAT THE CENTRAL ISSUE OF THE

SPECIAL CONGRESS WILL BE TO BRAINSTORM ON WAYS TO ENGAGE

THE GOZ AND ZIMBABWE\'S BLACK MAJORITY ON THE LAND ISSUE.

THIS EFFORT WILL CENTER ON HOW TO HELP MAKE THE

COUNTRY\'S COMMUNAL FARMING AREAS AND COMMUNAL FARMERS

MORE PRODUCTIVE AND SUCCESSFUL. CLOETE POINTED OUT THAT

COMMUNAL LANDS COMPRISE 42 PERCENT OF ZIMBABWE\'S TOTAL

LAND, AND CONTRARY TO WHAT THE GOZ STATES, THE MAJORITY

OF THIS LAND IS NOT UNSUITABLE FOR AGRICULTURE, CATTLE

OR GAME RANCHING. RATHER, WHAT IS NEEDED IS FOR

COMMUNAL FARMERS TO BE GIVEN THE TRAINING,

INFRASTRUCTURE AND INPUTS THAT ARE NEEDED TO MAKE THESE

LANDS PRODUCTIVE AND TO MOVE THEM FROM SUBSISTENCE TO

COMMERCIAL FARMING. CLOETE BLAMED THE GOZ FOR NOT DOING

THIS IN THE PAST, BECAUSE IT WANTED TO KEEP COMMUNAL

FARMERS POOR AND WHOLLY DEPENDENT ON THE GOVERNMENT.

HOWEVER, HE ALSO ACKNOWLEDGED THAT THE CFU SHOULD HAVE

TAKEN THE LEAD IN THIS ENDEAVOR WHEN THE GOZ ABDICATED

ITS RESPONSIBILITY. THE SPECIAL CONGRESS WILL ALSO

CONSIDER HOW THE CFU CAN TAKE THE LEAD IN ORGANIZING A

COALITION OF NGOS, INTERNATIONAL DONORS, AND HOPEFULLY

THE GOZ, TO WORK ON THE ISSUE OF THE PRODUCTIVITY AND

PRESERVATION OF THE COMMUNAL LANDS.

SWANEPOEL (A FORMER PRESIDENT OF THE CFU), WITHOUT THE

KNOWLEDGE OR APPROVAL OF THE CFU EXECUTIVE, CONTACTED

THE GOZ TO OFFER A DEAL TO BREAK THE IMPASSE ON THE LAND

MEETING, WHICH WAS HELD ON FEBRUARY 13. AT THE MEETING,

GOZ (REF A). THE COUNCIL DISCUSSIONS REGARDING THE

INDEPENDENT AND UNAUTHORIZED APPROACH WERE QUITE WIDE-

THE CFU EXECUTIVE AND THE CHANGING OF THE NAME OF THE

CFU.

5. (C) THIS LED TO A VERY HEATED DISCUSSION, WHERE

CERTAIN COUNCIL MEMBERS, LED BY SWANEPOEL, CRITICIZED

THE CURRENT CFU EXECUTIVE FOR BEING PART OF THE PROBLEM

FARMERS FACE IN REACHING A DEAL WITH THE GOZ TO DEFUSE

THE LAND CRISIS. SWANEPOEL SINGLED OUT CFU PRESIDENT

TIM HENWOOD, DIRECTOR DAVID HASLUCK, AND VICE-PRESIDENT

FOR REGIONS WILLIAM HUGHES AS PEOPLE WHO HAD TO GO.

AFTER AN HOUR OF HEATED DISCUSSIONS BETWEEN AND AMONGST

THE COUNCIL AND EXECUTIVE MEMBERS, THE EXECUTIVE

DECLARED THAT IF THEY WERE PART OF THE PROBLEM, AND DID

NOT HAVE THE SUPPORT OF THE COUNCIL, THEY WOULD ALL

RESIGN. THE EXECUTIVE MEMBERS THAN ALL WALKED OUT, AND,

ACCORDING TO CLOETE, LEFT THE COUNCIL MEMBERS STUNNED.

AN HOUR LATER, THE COUNCIL PHONED ALL THE EXECUTIVE

MEMBERS AND REQUESTED THAT THEY RETURN TO CFU

--------------------------------------------- ----

THE MACHIAVELLIAN MANEUVERS OF A FORMER PRESIDENT

--------------------------------------------- ----

6. (C) CLOETE STATED THAT THE DIVISIONS IN THE CFU WERE

THE RESULT OF THE MACHIAVELLIAN MANEUVERS OF SWANEPOEL.

HIS DEAL WITH THE GOZ WAS INDEPENDENT OF THE PRIVATE

SECTOR INITIATIVE (REF B) THAT THE CFU EXECUTIVE

SUPPORTS, AND ALSO WENT BEYOND THE EXECUTIVE\'S

APPOINTMENT OF A SMALL GROUP OF CFU MEMBERS TO ENGAGE

THE GOZ. CLOETE ALLEGED THAT SWANEPOEL HAS A HISTORY OF

CONNIVING AGAINST CFU EXECUTIVES WITH WHOM HE DOES NOT

AGREE. CLOETE CITED AN INCIDENT WHEN SWANEPOEL

CONVINCED THE COUNCIL TO REMOVE A CFU PRESIDENT SO

SWANEPOEL COULD COMPLETE THE REMAINDER OF THE TERM.

ACCORDING TO CLOETE, SWANEPOEL TOLD THE COUNCIL AT THE

FEBRUARY 13 MEETING THAT HE WAS GOING TO THE UNITED

STATES TO MEET WITH PRESIDENT BUSH OVER HIS DEAL, AND HE

WOULD BE RETURNING WITH US $60 MILLION TO FUND LAND

REFORM. HE ALSO ALLEGEDLY SAID THAT HE WAS AVAILABLE TO

TRAVEL ANYWHERE IN ZIMBABWE TO MEET WITH FARMERS WHO

WANTED TO DISCUSS HIS PLAN BECAUSE HE HAD ACCESS TO THE

HELICOPTER OF ZIMBABWE ARMS DEALER JOHN BREDENKAMP.

CLOETE ASSERTED THAT SWANEPOEL HAS ALWAYS MAINTAINED

CLOSE RELATIONSHIPS WITH ZANU-PF POLITICIANS, SOCIALLY

AND OTHERWISE. WE HAVE HEARD FROM OTHER SOURCES THAT

SWANEPOEL IS PERHAPS THE ONLY COMMERCIAL FARMER WHOM

PRESIDENT MUGABE TRUSTS. ACCORDING TO CLOETE, SWANEPOEL

TOLD THE CFU COUNCIL THAT ONE OF HIS ZANU-PF CONTACTS

HAD TOLD HIM THAT IT WAS TIME TO MAKE A DEAL WITH THE

GOZ ON LAND, OR THERE SOON WOULD BE 300 WHITE FARMERS

ROUNDED UP AND KILLED. SWANEPOEL WOULD NOT TELL THE

COUNCIL WHO HAD TOLD HIM THIS OR WHO WOULD DO THIS, ONLY

THAT HE WAS THE MESSENGER.

COMMENT

-------

7. (C) ALTHOUGH CLOETE TOLD US THAT THE COUNCIL ENDED UP

BACKING THE CURRENT EXECUTIVE, HIS DEMEANOR AND DELIVERY

LEFT US LESS THAN WILLING TO BET THE FARM ON ITS

CONTINUING TO DO SO. WE ASSESS SWANEPOEL\'S EFFORT TO

CUT A SECRET DEAL WITH THE GOZ AS ILL-TIMED AND ILL-

ADVISED AND ONE THAT COULD UNDERMINE THE CFU AS AN

INSTITUTION. THE CFU\'S EFFORTS TO ASSIST AND ALIGN

ITSELF MORE CLOSELY TO THE BLACK FARMING COMMUNITY, BOTH

COMMERCIAL AND COMMUNAL, IS, AT THIS STAGE, LIKELY TOO

8. (C) COMMENT CONTINUED: WE EXPECT THAT MUGABE AND

TO IMPLEMENT A DIVIDE-AND-CONQUER STRATEGY AGAINST THE

UNION. WHILE WE DO NOT DISCOUNT THAT MORE WHITE FARMERS

MAY BE KILLED BEFORE THE RULE OF LAW IS RESTORED IN

ZIMBABWE, WE DO NOT TAKE AS CREDIBLE THE THREAT TO KILL

300 COMMERCIAL FARMERS. WE BELIEVE THE UPCOMING

CONGRESS MAY BE A WATERSHED EVENT, NOT FOR BREAKING THE

IMPASSE WITH MUGABE OVER LAND, BUT IN DETERMINING

WHETHER THE CFU REMAINS A UNITED ORGANIZATION

REPRESENTING COMMERCIAL FARMERS, OR ENDS UP COLLAPSING

INTO DISSENTING FACTIONS THAT WILL TRY TO CUT INDIVIDUAL

DEALS WITH THE GOZ TO SAVE THEMSELVES. END COMMENT.
