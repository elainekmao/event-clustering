SUDAN AND THE FUNDAMENTALIST CHALLENGE

--------------------------------------

2. (C) DURING A JULY 21 COURTESY CALL, SECRETARY OF THE

PONTIFICAL CONGREGATION FOR THE EVANGELIZATION OF THE PEOPLES

ARCHBISHOP ROBERT SARAH OFFERED HIS VIEWS ON A WIDE RANGE OF

ISSUES FACING THE CHURCH IN AFRICA. SARAH, FROM GUINEA, TOLD

US HE HAD JUST RETURNED FROM A BISHOPS\' CONFERENCE MEETING IN

ADDIS ABABA, WHERE THE BISHOPS HAD EXPRESSED STRONG CONCERN

AT THE CONTINUED PROBLEMS OF CONFLICT AND UNDER-DEVELOPMENT

IN EAST AFRICA AND SUDAN. SARAH REPORTED THE SUDANESE

BISHOPS CHARACTERIZED THE SUDAN SITUATION AS GRIM, WITH

ISLAMIC FUNDAMENTALISTS CONTINUING TO DE-HUMANIZE CHRISTIANS

AND OTHER NON-MUSLIM PEOPLES AS "DOGS, GOOD ONLY TO BE

BURNED." HE SAID THE SUDANESE GOVERNMENT IS BITTERLY

ANTAGONISTIC TO CHRISTIANS, AND HE DID NOT SEE ANY HOPE FOR

AN END TO THEIR HOSTILITY. AS A RESULT, HE DIDN\'T SEE ANY

OTHER SOLUTION THAN SEPARATION OF SUDAN INTO TWO COUNTRIES

(NOTE: THIS IS NOT THE OFFICIAL VATICAN POSITION, WHICH

SUPPORTS DIALOGUE AND RECONCILIATION BETWEEN NORTH AND SOUTH

SUDAN. FURTHERMORE, OUR CONVERSATION OCCURRED AFTER THE

AGREEMENT ON THE MACHAKOS PROTOCOL, YET SARAH DID NOT

ACKNOWLEDGE THAT IT PRESENTED A REAL SOLUTION END NOTE.)

3. (C) PICKING UP ON A THEME WE HAVE HEARD FREQUENTLY IN

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 VATICA 03638 01 OF 02 231756Z

PAST MONTHS, SARAH SEE A GROWING CHALLENGE TO THE CHURCH IN

AFRICA FROM ISLAMIC FUNDAMENTALISTS. STARTING IN SUDAN,

YOUNGER, GULF-EDUCATED ISLAMIC ACTIVISTS HAVE BEEN SPREADING

MONEY THROUGHOUT SAHELIAN, EAST AND WEST AFRICA IN RECENT

YEARS. THEIR MESSAGE IS THAT CHRISTIANITY IS A FOREIGN,

HIERARCHICAL, RELIGION AND THAT ISLAM IS AN INDIGENOUS

RELIGION MORE IN TUNE WITH AFRICAN TRADITIONS. SARAH SAID THE

CHURCH DOESN\'T HAVE THE FINANCIAL RESOURCES TO COMPETE WITH

THESE GULF-FINANCED FUNDAMENTALISTS IN PROVIDING CASH AND

SOCIAL SERVICES. THE CHURCH\'S ONLY HOPE TO COUNTER THESE

INFLUENCES WAS THROUGH EDUCATION. THE ISLAMIC

FUNDAMENTALISTS HAVE MADE THEIR GREATEST GAINS AMONG THE

UNEDUCATED, BUT THE CHURCH\'S SCHOOLS, -- THOUGH UNDER-FUNDED

-- WOULD TRY TO HELP PEOPLE DISCERN THE DANGERS INHERENT IN

THE FUNDAMENTALIST PATH. (COMMENT: IN PREVIOUS CONVERSATIONS

WITH SARAH, HE, LIKE OUR OTHER INTERLOCUTORS OF AFRICAN

ORIGIN, HAD BEEN MUCH LESS CONCERNED WITH THE ISLAMIC

CHALLENGE. HE AND THEY HAD FOCUSED ON BASIC PROBLEMS OF

DEVELOPMENT AND CONFLICT RESOLUTION. HE NOW SEEMS TO SHARE

THE SAME CONCERNS AS HIS EUROPEAN CURIAL COLLEAGUES, WHO HAVE

BEEN AVIDLY RE-READING HUNTINGTON\'S CLASH OF CIVILIZATIONS

SINCE 09/11. END COMMENT.)

4. (C) SARAH WAS QUICK TO UNDERSCORE THAT THE CHURCH\'S

CENTURIES LONG RELATIONSHIP WITH AFRICA\'S "INTEGRATED"

ISLAMIC COMMUNITIES HAS BEEN POSITIVE. HE NOTED THE

BASICALLY GOOD RELATIONS BETWEEN THE COMMUNITIES IN

MAJORITY-MUSLIM COUNTRIES SUCH AS SENEGAL AND CHAD. YET, EVEN

THERE HE FEARED FOR A FUTURE IN WHICH WAHABBI-IST ARAB

PROSELYTIZING COULD LEAD TO VIOLENT INTER-COMMUNAL CONFLICT.

PROMOTING RECONCILIATION IN THE GREAT LAKES

-------------------------------------------

5. (SBU) RESPONDING TO A QUERY ABOUT HIS PREVIOUS FOCUS ON

RWANDAN EXCESSES IN THE DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC OF THE CONGO

(DRC), SARAH TOLD US HE HAD MET WITH RWANDAN PRESIDENT KAGAME

IN KIGALI IN LATE MAY. THE ASSOCIATION OF BISHOPS\'

CONFERENCES IN CENTRAL AFRICA (ACEAC IN ITS FRENCH ACRONYM)

ORGANIZED A SERIES OF MEETINGS CULMINATING IN A PLENARY WITH

KAGAME IN LATE MAY. THE ACEAC HAD DECIDED TO CONDUCT A

SERIES OF PUBLIC MEETINGS FOR THE FIRST TIME IN YEARS IN THE

THREE COUNTRIES OF THE ASSOCIATION, TO DEMONSTRATE TO

CITIZENS OF THE GREAT LAKES CONFLICT ZONE THE POSSIBILITY OF

RECONCILIATION. THEY DISCUSSED WITH KAGAME THE NEED FOR

ACTIVE CONFLICT RESOLUTION SO THAT THE PEOPLE WOULD BE ABLE

TO FACE THEIR MAJOR HEALTH AND DEVELOPMENT CHALLENGES. SARAH

REPORTED THAT KAGAME TOLD THE BISHOPS HE WAS READY TO

CONSIDER POSITIVELY THEIR SUGGESTIONS ON HOW TO PROCEED. THE

BISHOPS ARE DRAFTING A DOCUMENT FOR KAGAME THAT SHOULD BE

READY BEFORE SEPTEMBER.

REFORM IS A TWO-WAY STREET

--------------------------

6. (U) TURNING THE DISCUSSION TO AFRICA\'S PROBLEMS OF

REFORM, DEVELOPMENT, AND THE HIV/AIDS CRISIS, SARAH

UNDERLINED THE CHURCH\'S FUNDAMENTAL VIEW THAT THE DEVELOPED

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 VATICA 03638 02 OF 02 231511Z

WORLD MUST ALSO LOOK TO ITS OWN RESPONSIBILITIES. ON

CORRUPTION, FOR EXAMPLE, HE SAID AFRICAN OFFICIALS ARE

WORKING IN A "ZONA SPORCA" OR FILTHY ENVIRONMENT. THEY

WOULDN\'T HAVE CORRUPT MONEY IF EQUALLY CORRUPT BUSINESSMEN

WEREN\'T GIVING IT TO THEM. HE WAS ENCOURAGED TO HEAR OF U.S.

LAWS CONSTRAINING U.S. FIRMS FROM OFFERING BRIBES, AND AGREED

ON THE IMPORTANCE OF ENSURING THAT THE WESTERN NATIONS

DEVELOPED SIMILAR PRACTICES. THE CHURCH\'S OFFICIAL

PUBLICATIONS ON DEVELOPMENT CONDEMN CORRUPTION AND INSIST ON

REFORMS PROMOTING GOOD GOVERNANCE IN RECIPIENT COUNTRIES.

BUT, ACCORDING TO THE DOCTRINE OF "SOCIAL GOODS," SARAH SAID

DEVELOPED COUNTRIES ALSO HAVE A DUTY TO LIVE IN SOLIDARITY

WITH THE DEVELOPING WORLD. THIS MEANS HELPING THEM WITH

RELIEF ASSISTANCE AS NEEDED, HELPING THEM TO BUILD CAPACITY,

AND OFFERING THEM ACCESS TO DEVELOPED MARKETS AND FINANCE.

HE REGARDED SUPPORT FROM DEVELOPED COUNTRIES FOR INCREASED

EDUCATION AND TREATMENT AS ESSENTIAL TO COMBAT HIV/AIDS. BUT

THE MOST URGENT TASK WAS, SARAH INSISTED, TO END THE

CONFLICTS CURRENTLY PLAGUING AFRICA, WITHOUT WHICH LITTLE

PROGRESS WOULD BE POSSIBLE. THE UNITED STATES "HAS A SPECIAL

RESPONSIBILITY, AS THE GREATEST POWER ON EARTH, TO STEP UP TO

THESE CHALLENGES AND LEAD THE DEVELOPED WORLD TO GREATER

SOLIDARITY WITH THE LESS FORTUNATE," HE CONCLUDED.

COMMENT

-------

7. (C) THE CONGREGATION FOR THE EVANGELIZATION OF PEOPLES

IS ONE OF THE HOLY SEE\'S OLDEST AND LARGEST CONGREGATIONS.

IT MANAGES THE CHURCH\'S MISSIONARY ACTIVITIES AROUND THE

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 VATICA 03638 02 OF 02 231511Z

WORLD IN COUNTRIES THAT HAVE NOT HISTORICALLY BEEN CHRISTIAN.

IT ENJOYS STRONG FINANCIAL SUPPORT FROM DIOCESES IN THE

DEVELOPED WORLD, AND THUS IS ONE OF THE "RICHEST" DICASTRIES.

IN PAST CONVERSATIONS OUR INTERLOCUTORS HAVE FOCUSED WITH

EVIDENT PRIDE ON THE LARGE SOCIAL SERVICE PRESENCE OF THE

CHURCH IN AFRICA. MORE THAN ONE HAS SAID THE CHURCH PROVIDES

MORE DEVELOPMENT ASSISTANCE (INCLUDING SCHOOLS AND HOSPITALS)

IN AFRICA THAN DOES THE EUROPEAN UNION. THE CURRENT

PRIORITIES LAID OUT BY SARAH -- CONFRONTING ISLAMIC

FUNDAMENTALISM, PROMOTION REGIONAL RECONCILIATION, AND

ENCOURAGING GOVERNMENTAL REFORM AND HUMAN DEVELOPMENT -- ALL

TRACK CLOSELY WITH U.S. OBJECTIVES IN AFRICA. THEY SUGGEST

THAT WE CAN SECURE IMPORTANT SUPPORT FOR U.S. POLICIES IN

THESE AREAS FROM THE HOLY SEE.
