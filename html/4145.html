1. (C) SUMMARY: IN RESPONSE TO A HUMAN RIGHTS UPDATE, MFA

OFFICE DIRECTOR FOR CENTRAL AMERICAN AND CARIBBEAN AFFAIRS

NICOLO\' GORETTI SHARED INFORMATION ON HOW THE GOI AND OTHER

EU MEMBERS VIEWED CUBA AND THE EU DIALOGUE WITH IT. HE

LOCATED ITALY IN A "PRO-ENGAGEMENT" GROUP ALONG WITH OTHER

"LATIN" MEMBERS. SIMILARLY, HE SUPPORTED CONTINUED DIALOGUE

EVEN THOUGH THE TALKS HAD PRODUCED LITTLE AND WERE UNLIKELY

TO ACCOMPLISH MUCH IN THE FUTURE. FINALLY, GORETTI TOUCHED

ON TRADE ISSUES AND PREVIEWED DIPLOMATIC AND HUMANITARIAN

SUBJECTS LIKELY TO BE RAISED AT THE NOVEMBER 4 EU-CUBA

MEETING. END SUMMARY.

2. (SBU) ON OCTOBER 25 AT THE MFA, POLOFF DELIVERED REF B

DEMARCHE ON HUMAN RIGHTS IN CUBA TO OFFICE DIRECTOR FOR

CENTRAL AMERICAN AND CARIBBEAN AFFAIRS NICOLO\' GORETTI DE

FLAMINI. GORETTI SET THE TONE FOR REMARKS THAT SEEMED

CONTRADICTORY BY OPENING A HALF-HOUR DISCOURSE WITH THE

COMMENT, "I HAVE NOTHING TO SAY. I COMPLETELY AGREE." HE

CONCURRED THAT THE HUMAN RIGHTS SITUATION IS WORSENING WITH,

FOR EXAMPLE, THE HARASSMENT OF THE PROJECT VARELA ORGANIZERS.

GORETTI WAS FAMILIAR WITH ALL OF THE CASES DESCRIBED IN THE

NON-PAPER AND HAD EVEN RECEIVED A COPY OF THE DEMARCHE THAT

AMEMB PARIS HAD DELIVERED. HE PROMISED TO PASS THE NON-PAPER

TO THE EU PRESIDENCY FOR THE UPCOMING MEETING AND NOTED THAT

THE DANISH PRESIDENCY WAS VERY WELL INFORMED BECAUSE, HE

BELIEVED, IT HAD AN AMBASSADOR IN HAVANA.

3. (C) AS NOTED IN REFS C AND D, GORETTI HAD CONCERNS

ABOUT HOW TO SUPPORT PROJECT VARELA. NOTING THAT THE GOI

AGREES WITH VARELA, HE SUGGESTED THAT THE USG NOT "PUSH TOO

MUCH," WHICH COULD DISCREDIT LOCAL ACTIVISTS. CASTRO\'S CLAIM

THAT THE BUSH ADMINISTRATION WAS THE REAL FORCE BEHIND VARELA

WAS INCORRECT BUT AN EASY CRITICISM. INSTEAD, THE

INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY SHOULD FINESSE ITS SUPPORT. FOR

EXAMPLE, FORMER PRESIDENT CARTER COULD MORE CREDIBLY PUSH THE

INITIATIVE THAN THE USG. NOTING THAT THE EU HAD MADE A

STATEMENT IN FAVOR OF IT, GORETTI CALLED VARELA A "USEFUL,

DEMOCRATIC INITIATIVE" THAT UNFORTUNATELY HAS NOT BEEN

APPRECIATED BY THE GOC.

4. (C) ADDRESSING THE EU-CUBA DIALOGUE, GORETTI SAID,

"PERSONALLY I DON\'T THINK IT WILL BE VERY SUCCESSFUL." THE

DIALOGUE HAD "BEEN WITHOUT CONTENT SINCE DECEMBER ... NOT

VERY FRUITFUL OR ADVANCING." NONETHELESS, HE ASSERTED, IT

WAS "GOOD" OR "WORTH A TRY" TO CONTINUE.

5. (C) GORETTI DESCRIBED THE EU MEMBERS AS FALLING INTO

THREE CAMPS: THE UK, WHICH WAS ABSOLUTELY OPPOSED TO

DIALOGUE; SWEDEN, HOLLAND, AND DENMARK, WHICH THOUGHT

DIALOGUE WAS HOPELESS AND INAPPROPRIATE BECAUSE OF HUMAN

RIGHTS ABUSES; AND A "LATIN" GROUP OF ITALY, FRANCE, BELGIUM,

AND TO A CERTAIN EXTENT SPAIN AND PORTUGAL, WHICH FAVORED

DIALOGUE WITH CUBA IN ORDER TO PUSH FOR HUMAN RIGHTS AND

ENCOURAGE A FUTURE ECONOMIC OPENING.

6. (C) FOR ITS PART, THE GOC FAVORED CONTINUING DIALOGUE

WITH THE EU, NOTING THAT IT HAD GOOD BILATERAL RELATIONS WITH

EUROPEAN COUNTRIES BUT A POOR RELATIONSHIP WITH THE EU.

FURTHERMORE, IT WANTED TO JOIN THE AFRICAN, CARIBBEAN,

PACIFIC (ACP) INITIATIVE ESTABLISHED BY THE COTONOU

AGREEMENT. THE LATIN GROUP HAD HELPED CUBA OBTAIN OBSERVER

STATUS AT THE OPENING SESSION OF EU-ACP TRADE NEGOTIATIONS

REDUCING TARIFFS, AS A WAY OF PREPARING IT FOR A POST-CASTRO

ERA. CUBA NEEDED TO BE EDUCATED ABOUT THE IMPORTANCE OF

COMPLYING WITH INTERNATIONAL REGULATIONS INSTEAD OF BEING

ISOLATED. (AS AN EXAMPLE OF THIS, GORETTI CITED CUBA\'S

REFUSAL TO ACCEPT INTERNATIONAL RULES IN THE PARIS CLUB WITH

REGARD TO THE DEBT IT OWES RUSSIA.) TO BECOME A MEMBER,

HOWEVER, THE GOC WOULD HAVE TO IMPLEMENT THE COTONOU CRITERIA

AND SUBMIT AN APPLICATION. THE GOI DOUBTS CUBA COULD EVER

QUALIFY UNDER CASTRO.

7. (C) GORETTI PREVIEWED THREE OTHER ISSUES LIKELY TO COME

UP NOVEMBER 4: RENEWAL OF THE EU COMMON POSITION, A

POTENTIAL UNHRC RESOLUTION, AND CUBA\'S NEED FOR HUMANITARIAN

ASSISTANCE. THE CUBANS WOULD UNDOUBTEDLY ASK FOR THE

DECEMBER NON-RENEWAL OF THE "TOUGH" EUROPEAN COMMON POSITION

THAT CALLS FOR CUBAN DEMOCRATIZATION. IN A DIPLOMATIC DANCE

WHOSE ENDING IS PRE-DETERMINED, THE EU WOULD PROMISE TO TAKE

CUBA\'S VIEWS INTO CONSIDERATION BUT CERTAINLY RENEW THE

POSITION. THE RENEWAL WOULD INFURIATE THE GOC, WHICH WOULD

CLAIM THAT THE EU WAS TOO INFLUENCED BY THE UNITED STATES.

THE GOC WOULD BE SIMILARLY IMPOTENT TO FORESTALL THE ANNUAL

UN HUMAN RIGHTS COMMITTEE CONDEMNATION OF IT, ABOUT WHICH IT

9. (SBU) FINALLY, GORETTI NOTED, TWO HUNDRED AMERICAN
