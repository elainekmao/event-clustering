SUBJECT: GREATER SOPHISTICATION AND THOROUGHNESS

CHARACTERIZE GOVERNMENT CAMPAIGN AGAINST INDEPENDENT

MEDIA

CLASSIFIED BY PAO D. BRUCE WHARTON FOR REASONS 1.5B AND D.

-------

SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) IN ITS INTENSIFYING EFFORT TO CONTROL INFORMATION

AVAILABLE TO THE PUBLIC, THE GOVERNMENT CONTINUED TO

HARDEN ITS APPROACH TOWARD THE INDEPENDENT MEDIA. THE

GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE (GOZ) IS PURSUING A TWO-PRONGED

APPROACH: CAREFUL CONTROL OF THE CONTENT AND "SPIN" OF

NEWS REPORTED BY THE STATE-OWNED MEDIA; AND CONSTANT

CHALLENGES TO THE INDEPENDENT AND FOREIGN MEDIA\'S

ABILITY TO WORK. BEHIND-THE-SCENES EFFORTS BY THE

STATE-OWNED AND THE INDEPENDENT MEDIA CONTINUE AS BOTH

SIDES JOCKEY TO STRENGTHEN THEIR POSITIONS. THE GOZ

CONTINUES TO PURSUE ITS AUTHORITARIAN INCLINATIONS WHILE

THE INDEPENDENT MEDIA CONTINUE TO SHOW COURAGE AND

TENACITY. END SUMMARY.

GOVERNMENT SYMPATHIZER ANNOUNCES NEW DAILY PAPER

--------------------------------------------- ---

2. (C) THE WEEKLY "MIRROR" ANNOUNCED IN ITS FEBRUARY 23

EDITION THAT IT WOULD LAUNCH "THE DAILY MIRROR" ON MAY

1. "THE MIRROR" IS PUBLISHED BY PROFESSOR IBBO MANDAZA,

A ZIMBABWEAN POLITICAL SCIENTIST AND DIRECTOR OF THE

SOUTHERN AFRICAN POLITICAL ECONOMY SERIES (SAPES) TRUST,

A HARARE-BASED POLITICAL RESEARCH INSTITUTION. THE

PUBLISHER IS PRACTICED IN THE ART OF DECEPTION.

ALTHOUGH MANDAZA DENIES THAT "THE MIRROR" SUPPORTS THE

GOVERNMENT AND ZANU-PF, EDITORIALS, THE WEEKLY ANONYMOUS

"SCRUTATOR" COLUMN (WRITTEN BY MANDAZA) AND EDITORIAL

DECISIONS LEAVE LITTLE DOUBT OF THE NEWSPAPER\'S

POLITICAL ORIENTATION. "THE MIRROR\'S" TARGET AUDIENCE

IS EDUCATED, URBAN ZIMBABWEANS. "THE MIRROR" CLAIMS

CIRCULATION OF ABOUT 20,000 PER WEEK (PROBABLY

OPTIMISTIC), AND CONTAINS VERY LITTLE ADVERTISING.

SINCE ITS LAUNCH IN EARLY 1999, ITS ABILITY TO SURVIVE

FINANCIALLY HAS BEEN SOMETHING OF A MYSTERY. THE PAPER

INITIALLY RECEIVED SOME SUPPORT FROM THE SOUTHERN AFRICA

MEDIA DEVELOPMENT FUND (SAMDEF), BUT WAS TURNED DOWN IN

2000 FOR FUNDING FOR A DAILY PAPER. SPECULATION IN

HARARE IS THAT "THE MIRROR\'S" OPERATING COSTS ARE

SUBSIDIZED BY SAPES REVENUES AND THROUGH FINANCIAL

SUPPORT FROM ZANU-PF. IF "THE DAILY MIRROR" DOES APPEAR

TIGHTENING ACCREDITATION OF FOREIGN JOURNALISTS

--------------------------------------------- --

3. (C) THE JOHANNESBURG-BASED REGIONAL CORRESPONDENT FOR

THE "WASHINGTON POST," JON JETER, TOLD US THAT HE

ENCOUNTERED A NEWLY CUMBERSOME PROCESS FOR GAINING A

PRESS CREDENTIAL IN ZIMBABWE. SINCE JUNE 2000 THE

PROCESS HAS BEEN SIMPLE AND FAST, IF SOMEWHAT EXPENSIVE.

IN THE PAST, FOREIGN JOURNALISTS SIMPLY HAD TO GO TO THE

INFORMATION MINISTRY, PRODUCE A PHOTO ID AND LETTER FROM

THEIR EMPLOYER, PAY (U.S.) $100 (IN US CURRENCY,

PLEASE), AND WERE ISSUED A WORK PERMIT ON THE SPOT.

THAT PERMIT WAS GOOD FOR UP TO 3 MONTHS. UPON ARRIVAL

IN HARARE ON FEBRUARY 27, JETER FOUND A MORE COMPLICATED

PROCEDURE. HE WAS ASKED TO PROVIDE DETAILS ABOUT THE

STORIES HE INTENDED TO FILE, THE PEOPLE HE WANTED TO

MEET, AND THE AREAS HE WISHED TO VISIT. HIS PRO-FORMA

LETTER FROM THE "WASHINGTON POST" WAS ALSO QUESTIONED

AND HE WAS TOLD THAT FOR FUTURE VISITS HE NEEDED TO

PROVIDE A MORE SPECIFIC LETTER, ON OFFICIAL COMPANY

LETTERHEAD. JETER WAS GIVEN A CREDENTIAL, BUT IT TOOK

24 HOURS INSTEAD OF THE HERETOFORE-USUAL 30 MINUTES.

JETER WAS ALSO TOLD BY AN OFFICIAL OF THE MINISTRY OF

INFORMATION AND PUBLICITY THAT THE PROCESS WAS LIKELY TO

"DAILY NEWS" CLOSE TO ACQUIRING NEW PRINTING PRESS

--------------------------------------------- -----

4. (C) THE "DAILY NEWS," WHICH HAS HEROICALLY PUBLISHED

AN EDITION EVERY DAY SINCE THE JANUARY BOMBING AND

DESTRUCTION OF ITS PRESSES, IS ON THE VERGE OF SIGNING A

CONTRACT FOR THE PURCHASE OF A NEW (U.S.)$4 MILLION

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 00897 02 OF 02 070831Z

PRINTING PRESS. PRIVATE DONATIONS AND CORPORATE

FINANCIAL RESOURCES HAVE RAISED ABOUT ONE-HALF OF THE

PURCHASE PRICE. THE BALANCE IS BEING PROVIDED VIA A

LOAN FROM THE SOROS FOUNDATION\'S MEDIA DEVELOPMENT ARM,

THE OPEN SOCIETY FOUNDATION. DELIVERY AND SET-UP OF THE

ZIMBABWEAN MEDIA.

ZIMBABWEAN BUSINESSMEN SHORE UP INDEPENDENT MEDIA

--------------------------------------------- ----

5. (C) ZIMBABWE\'S TWO MOST SUCCESSFUL ENTREPRENEURS,

STRIVE MASIYIWA (ECONET WIRELESS) AND NIGEL CHANAKIRA

(KINGDOM FINANCIAL HOLDINGS) HAVE MADE MAJOR INVESTMENTS

IN THE INDEPENDENT MEDIA. THE TWO HAVE CREATED A

HOLDING COMPANY, THE INDEPENDENT MEDIA GROUP (IMG) THAT

HAS BOUGHT CONTROLLING SHARES IN ASSOCIATED NEWSPAPERS

OF ZIMBABWE (ANZ), THE PARENT COMPANY OF THE "DAILY

NEWS." IMG HAS ALSO BOUGHT LARGE, THOUGH NOT

CONTROLLING AMOUNTS, OF STOCK IN THE "ZIMBABWE

INDEPENDENT" AND THE "STANDARD." THESE ARE IMPORTANT

INVESTMENTS AS THEY PROVIDE A MUCH-NEEDED FINANCIAL

CUSHION TO ALL THREE PAPERS AND BRING THEM INTO THE

ORBIT OF ZIMBABWE\'S MOST TALENTED AND SUCCESSFUL

BUSINESSMEN. (NB: BOTH MASIYIWA AND CHANAKIRA WERE

THREATENED BY GOZ-ENGINEERED LAWSUITS IN 2000 WHEN IT

BECAME APPARENT THAT NEITHER OF THE MEN SUPPORTED ZANU-
