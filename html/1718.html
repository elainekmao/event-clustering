1. (C) SUMMARY: HENRY KISSINGER MET WITH VATICAN PRIME

MINISTER AND FOREIGN MINISTER ON MAY 22. THE VATICAN

STRESSED THE NEED FOR AN "INTERNATIONAL FORCE" TO KEEP THE

ISRAELIS AND PALESTINIANS APART. IN A SUBSEQUENT

CONVERSATION, ISRAELI AMBASSADOR TOLD CHARGE THAT THE

VATICAN IS NOW PROPOSING AN INTERNATIONAL FORCE. IN

ADDITION, IT PLANS TO SEND A "SPECIAL ENVOY" TO ISRAEL AND

THE TERRITORIES. JERUSALEM HAS REACTED COOLLY TO BOTH

PROPOSALS. VATICAN RESPONSE TO REFTEL FOCUSED ON NEED FOR

IMMEDIATE STOP TO VIOLENCE LEST "HABITS OF WAR" BECOME SET.

END SUMMARY.

2. (C) FORMER SECRETARY OF STATE HENRY KISSINGER VISITED

VATICAN CITY MAY 22-23. HE WAS IN ITALY IN HIS CAPACITY AS

THE CHAIR OF THE ADVISORY BOARD OF BOOZ/ALLEN, WHICH WAS

HOLDING ITS ANNUAL MEETING. DR. KISSINGER MET VATICAN

PRIME MINISTER SODANO AND FOREIGN MINISTER TAURAN EVENING

OF MAY 22. KISSINGER BRIEFED CHARGE MORNING OF MAY 23.

THE FORMER SECRETARY SAID THAT THE MEETING FOCUSED ON THE

SITUATION IN THE MIDDLE EAST. THE VATICAN REPEATED ITS

CALL FOR INTERNATIONAL GUARANTEES FOR THE HOLY SITES, AND

GAVE DR. KISSINGER A NON-PAPER (FAXED TO EUR/WE MAY 24).

IN ADDITION, SODANO AND TAURAN STRESSED THE NEED FOR AN

"INTERNATIONAL FORCE" TO KEEP THE ISRAELIS AND PALESTINIANS

APART. CHARGE ACCOMPANIED DR. KISSINGER TO A PRESENTATION

TO THE POPE ON THE MORNING OF MAY 23. THE POPE LOOKED

EXTREMELY WEAK, AND APPEARED NOT TO RECOGNIZE DR.

KISSINGER. AFTER THE THREE MINUTE MEETING WITH THE POPE,

DR. KISSINGER REMARKED TO THE CHARGE THAT THE POPE SEEMED

TO BE UNAWARE OF HIS IDENTITY.

3. (C) CHARGE SPOKE TO ISRAELI AMBASSADOR JOSEPH LAMDAN

EVENING OF MAY 23. THE AMBASSADOR USED THIS OPPORTUNITY TO

TELL CHARGE THAT THE VATICAN HAS BEEN RAISING THE IDEA OF

AN "INTERNATIONAL FORCE". LAMDAN SAID THAT HIS GOVERNMENT

RESPONDED VERY "COLDLY" TO THE IDEA. HE ALSO TOLD CHARGE

THAT THE VATICAN TOLD HIM IT WOULD SEND A "SPECIAL ENVOY"

TO ISRAEL AND THE TERRITORIES. AMBASSADOR LAMDAN SAID THAT

AT FIRST, JERUSALEM SAID IT WOULDN\'T RECEIVE HIM, BUT IS

NOW RECONSIDERING.

4. (C) POLOFF SUBSEQUENTLY MET WITH VATICAN MIDDLE EAST

DIRECTOR GIOVANNI D\'ANIELLO MAY 24 TO SHARE REFTEL POINTS

ON US REENGAGEMENT IN THE MEPP AND TO PASS HIM A COPY OF

SUMMARY OF MITCHELL REPORT TAKEN FROM INTERNET. D\'ANIELLO

THANKED US FOR THE PAPER (NOTE: DESPITE THEIR FOCUS ON THE

NEW MILLENNIUM, THE CURIA IS NOT YET FULLY WIRED).

D\'ANIELLO, INVOKING THE POPE\'S SUNDAY HOMILY, SAID IT IS

MOST IMPORTANT NOW FOR THE ISRAELI AND PALESTINIAN LEADERS

TO HAVE THE POLITICAL COURAGE TO STOP THE VIOLENCE. FROM

THAT EVERYTHING ELSE WAS POSSIBLE. D\'ANIELLO NOTED THE

EXISTENCE OF A NUMBER OF PLANS AND INTERNATIONAL

INSTRUMENTS FOR PROGRAMMING CONFIDENCE BUILDING -- BUT CAME

BACK TO THE NEED FOR THE VIOLENCE TO STOP FIRST. IF REAL

PROGRESS IS NOT SHOWN NOW, HE FEARED THAT PEOPLE, BOTH IN

THE REGION AND INTERNATIONALLY, WILL BECOME ACCUSTOMED TO

THE VIOLENCE AND THE CONFLICT WOULD GRIND ON INDEFINITELY.

HE DID UNDERSCORE THE VATICAN\'S POSITION THAT THE HOLY

SITES IN JERUSALEM SHOULD COME UNDER INTERNATIONAL

GUARANTEES, AND HE AVERRED THAT REACHING AN AGREEMENT TO
