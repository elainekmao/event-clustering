1. (U) ACTION REQUEST. SEE PARA 5.

2. (C) ON APRIL 9, BALKAN OFFICE HEAD MAURIZIO MASSARI AND

MFA UNDER SECRETARY ROBERTO ANTONIONE HAD "EXTREMELY

PRODUCTIVE AND ENCOURAGING" MEETINGS IN TIRANA WITH PRIME

MINISTER MAJKO, FOREIGN MINISTER DADE, AND ECONOMIC MINISTER

MEKSI. MASSARI TOLD POLOFF APRIL 10 THAT THE GOI AND GOA

RENEWED A PROTOCOL OF COOPERATION, AND THE GOI PLEDGED 202

MILLION EUROS IN AID THROUGH 2004 FOR, INTER ALIA,

INFRASTRUCTURE IMPROVEMENT, ENTERPRISE DEVELOPMENT, AND

AGRICULTURE. MOST SIGNIFICANT, SAID MASSARI, WAS 30 MILLION

EUROS EARMARKED FOR ENERGY, FOLLOWING UP ON A PLEDGE BY PM

BERLUSCONI TO PM MAJKO DURING THE LATTER\'S VISIT TO ROME LAST

MONTH (REFTEL). THE FUNDS WERE TO BE USED TO PURCHASE

ELECTRICITY TO "KEEP THE LIGHTS ON" FOR THE SHORT TERM, BUT

ALSO TO BUILD A VIABLE ENERGY GENERATION INFRASTRUCTURE FOR

LONG TERM ENERGY PRODUCTION. "WE WERE VERY FRANK" WITH THE

ALBANIANS, SAID MASSARI. THE GOI EXPECTS THE GOA TO PLAN FOR

THE FUTURE. HE SAID THAT ANTONIONE REMINDED THE ALBANIANS

THAT THE CAUSE OF THEIR ENERGY PROBLEMS WAS "NOT ONLY THE

WEATHER." THE GOI WANTS TO SEE ITS AID GO TO PROJECTS THAT

WILL "CORRECT PAST MISTAKES."

3. (C) THE GOA IS HEARING A SIMILAR MESSAGE FROM THE EU

REGARDING OPENING STABILIZATION AND ASSOCIATION (SAA)

NEGOTIATIONS, SAID MASSARI. THE GOI\'S VIEW IS THAT THE

NEGOTIATIONS SHOULD START BEFORE JUNE. HOWEVER, ANTONIONE

REMINDED THE GOA THAT THERE ARE MANY SKEPTICS, IN THE EU AND

OSCE, WHO QUESTION THE GOA\'S ABILITY AND COMMITMENT TO

CHANGE, THEREFORE RAPID PROGRESS ON NEEDED REFORMS WAS

CRITICAL. MASSARI SAID THE MESSAGE WAS RECEIVED AND

ACKNOWLEDGED BY ALL THEIR INTERLOCUTORS.

4. (C) MASSARI REPEATED THE GOI\'S VIEW (REFTEL) THAT THE IC

SHOULD ACKNOWLEDGE GOA REFORMS TO DATE, SUCH AS OVERALL

RELATIVE POLITICAL STABILITY, COOPERATION WITH THE GOI IN

FIGHTING ILLEGAL IMMIGRATION, AND A RECENT BIPARTISAN

PARLIAMENTARY DECISION TO IMPLEMENT ODIHR ELECTORAL SYSTEM

REFORMS RECOMMENDATIONS. POTENTIAL INVESTORS NEED A SIGNAL

FROM THE IC THAT ALBANIA IS MAKING PROGRESS. WE HAVE TO

CONTINUE TO PRESSURE THE GOI TO DO MUCH MORE TO COMBAT

ORGANIZED CRIME AND REFORM THE JUDICIAL SYSTEM, AND IN THE

GOI\'S VIEW STARTING THE SAA NEGOTIATIONS COULD BE PART OF

THOSE MEANS. THE NEGOTIATIONS CAN LAST AS LONG AS NECESSARY,

AND SERVE AS ANOTHER TOOL TO INFLUENCE GOI ACTIONS.

5. (C) MASSARI\'S VIEW IS THAT IT WOULD BE A MISTAKE TO NOT

ACKNOWLEDGE THE GOI\'S PROGRESS THUS FAR, AND COULD MAKE IT

HARDER FOR THE MAJKO GOVERNMENT TO CONVINCE THE ALBANIAN

PEOPLE THAT ECONOMIC REFORMS, SOMETIMES PAINFUL IN THE SHORT

TERM, MUST BE ENACTED FOR THE GOOD OF THE COUNTRY\'S LONG TERM

STABILITY. HE SINGLED OUT THE BRITISH AS AMONG THE MOST

SKEPTICAL OF ITALY\'S EU PARTNERS REGARDING ALBANIA\'S

COMMITMENT TO CHANGE, AND WONDERED IF THE U.S. COULD "PUT IN

A GOOD WORD" WITH THE BRITS. HE ALSO REPEATED HIS REQUEST

FOR A "POSITIVE" U.S. STATEMENT AT THE APRIL 17 FRIENDS OF

ALBANIA MEETING IN VIENNA. ACTION REQUEST: POST WOULD LIKE

TO GIVE A HEADS-UP TO MASSARI ON THE GENERAL TONE OF OUR

INTERVENTION IN VIENNA.
