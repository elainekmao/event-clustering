1. (C) SUMMARY: RECENT EVENTS IN ZIMBABWE ARE BEGINNING

TO UNDERMINE A ZANU-PF THAT HAD BEEN INFUSED WITH NEW

CONFIDENCE SINCE ITS DECEMBER 2000 CONGRESS. THE SUDDEN

DEATH OF PARTY COMMISSAR BORDER GEZI, THE RESIGNATION OF

INDUSTRY AND INTERNATIONAL TRADE MINISTER NKOSANA MOYO,

THE LOSS OF THE MASVINGO MAYORSHIP TO THE OPPOSITION

MDC, AND THE FAILED CAMPAIGN TO BOOST URBAN SUPPORT BY

ATTACKING BUSINESSES AND NGOS, ARE EVENTS THAT HAVE

DULLED THE RULING PARTY\'S PRESIDENTIAL RACE PROSPECTS.

DOUBTS ARE GROWING ABOUT THE PARTY\'S RECENTLY-COMPLETED

RESTRUCTURING EXERCISE, WHICH HAD BEEN LED BY GEZI, IN

WHETHER IT WILL HELP THE PARTY MORE THAN HURT IT IN NEXT

YEAR\'S PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION. FINGER POINTING IS ON THE

UPSWING, AND OLD DIVISIONS ARE REAPPEARING AS CONCERNS

ABOUT PRESIDENT MUGABE\'S CANDIDACY FOR THE ELECTION

RESURFACE. END SUMMARY.

SUDDEN VACANCIES WILL BE HARD TO FILL

-------------------------------------

2. (C) THE APRIL 28 DEATH OF YOUTH DEVELOPMENT

MINISTER, PARTY COMMISSAR, AND BINDURA MP BORDER GEZI IN

A CAR ACCIDENT (REF E) LEFT A YAWNING GAP IN THE ZANU-PF

STRUCTURE. IT WILL BE NEARLY IMPOSSIBLE TO FIND AS

ENTHUSIASTIC A PARTY PROMOTER TO FILL ALL THREE OF

GEZI\'S POSITIONS. BRIAN RAFTOPOULOS, A RESEARCH FELLOW

AT THE UNIVERSITY OF ZIMBABWE, TOLD US THERE IS NO ONE

IN THE PARTY WHO CAN ADEQUATELY REPLACE GEZI. WHEN

ASKED WHETHER GEZI\'S DEATH HAD NOT BEEN AN ACCIDENT,

RAFTOPOULOS DISCOUNTED THE POPULAR RUMOR, SAYING GEZI

HAD BEEN TOO VALUABLE TO THE PARTY FOR ANY RIVAL TO KILL

HIM, AND IN ANY CASE, WAS TOO YOUNG TO BE A SERIOUS

CONTENDER FOR A MAJOR POSITION OF POWER. FRED SHAVA,

ZANU-PF\'S NATIONAL DIRECTOR FOR ADMINISTRATION, TOLD

POLOFF THAT GEZI HAD BEEN A "FANTASTIC ORGANIZER."

SHAVA REFUSED TO SPECULATE ON WHO HIS REPLACEMENT AS

POLITICAL COMMISSAR MIGHT BE, BUT SAID HE WOULD COME

FROM THE PARTY\'S CENTRAL COMMITTEE, IF NOT THE SMALLER

POLITBURO. THE PARTY WILL BE LOOKING FOR SOMEONE WITH

GEZI\'S "AGILITY," THE DIRECTOR STATED. THE MAY 11

EDITION OF THE "ZIMBABWE INDEPENDENT" LISTED CHINHOYI MP

PHILIP CHIYANGWA, BUSINESSMAN JAMES MAKAMBA, AND

MASHONALAND CENTRAL GOVERNOR ELLIOT MANYIKA AS THE

LIKELIEST SUCCESSORS. MINISTER OF STATE FOR NATIONAL

SECURITY NICHOLAS GOCHE IS ALSO A POSSIBILITY AS HE IS

THE ACTING COMMISSAR. AS FOR WHO WOULD BE APPOINTED

YOUTH DEVELOPMENT MINISTER, SHAVA REMARKED THAT THAT IS

PURELY "THE PRESIDENT\'S PREROGATIVE."

HAD NOT EVEN BEEN A PARTY MEMBER. RAFTOPOULOS LIKENED

5. (C) THE REORGANIZATION OF PROVINCIAL PARTY

STRUCTURES AND THE REPLACEMENT OF ELECTED PARTY

EXECUTIVES MAY HURT ZANU-PF MORE THAN HELP IT IN THE

RUNUP TO THE 2002 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION. PARTY

COMMISSAR GEZI HAD LED THE CHARGE TO REENERGIZE THE

PARTY AFTER ITS DEVASTATING LOSSES IN THE JUNE 2000

PARLIAMENTARY ELECTIONS. ACCORDING TO ZVOBGO, GEZI

"MASSACRED" THE PARTY\'S STRUCTURES. NINE OUT OF TEN

LEGITIMATELY ELECTED PROVINCIAL EXECUTIVES WERE

SUMMARILY DISMISSED, AND YOUNGER, MOSTLY INEXPERIENCED

LEADERS WERE IMPOSED BY HARARE. ZVOBGO BELIEVES THIS

PROVINCIAL LEADERSHIPS "DON\'T KNOW WHERE THE PARTY\'S

BEEN OR WHERE IT\'S GOING," WHICH IS A SIGN OF THE

GENERAL DECAY OF THE ZANU-PF MACHINE, THE MASVINGO MP

ADDED.

6. (C) WEBSTER SHAMU, ZANU-PF MP FOR CHEGUTU AND OUSTED

MASHONALAND WEST PARTY CHAIRMAN, SANG ZVOBGO\'S REFRAIN.

WITH THE PARTY RESTRUCTURING EXERCISE, ZANU-PF WAS

MOVING AWAY FROM DEMOCRATIC STRUCTURES TO MORE

DICTATORIAL ONES, SHAMU COMPLAINED. THE PARTY

LEADERSHIP WAS BYPASSING ITS OWN PROCEDURES TO PUT IN

MORE LOYAL EXECUTIVES. THE MAY 14 "DAILY NEWS"

CONTENTIOUS AFFAIR. THE ELECTION BY PARTY CADRES HAD TO

BE POSTPONED AFTER WAR VETERANS DISRUPTED THE MEETING TO

PROTEST CHIYANGWA\'S CANDIDACY. THE WAR VETERANS

REPORTEDLY COMPLAINED THAT CHIYANGWA WAS "TRYING TO

LEAPFROG TO A SENIOR POSITION IN THE PARTY." (COMMENT:

COMMENT.) THE ARTICLE ALSO QUOTED A FORMER ZANU-PF MP

8. (C) ZVOBGO TOLD US THAT THE ONLY WAY TO GET MUGABE

TO STEP DOWN BEFORE THE ELECTION WOULD BE FOR A SERIES

OF "RESPECTABLE" AND INFLUENTIAL PEOPLE IN THE PARTY TO

BEGIN SPEAKING OUT AGAINST MUGABE\'S CANDIDACY. ZVOBGO

OPINED THAT THE FIRST PERSON TO CASTIGATE MUGABE WOULD

FACE A STRONG REBUKE, BUT ONCE A SECOND AND THIRD PERSON

JOINED THE CHORUS, IT WOULD BE DIFFICULT FOR MUGABE TO

DROWN OUT THE RESULTING NOISE, AND HE WOULD BE FORCED TO

RELINQUISH HIS CANDIDACY FOR PRESIDENT. ZVOBGO HINTED

THAT HE WOULD BE ONE OF THE FIRST PEOPLE TO SPEAK OUT

AGAINST MUGABE. THE WILY MP EMPHASIZED HOWEVER, THAT

THE CRITICISM COULD NOT BEGIN TOO EARLY BEFORE THE

ELECTION LEST MUGABE HAVE TIME TO DEVISE COUNTERMEASURES

THAT WOULD QUASH ANY PARTY REVOLT. BRIAN RAFTOPOULOS

THEORIZED THAT AFTER MUGABE WON RE-ELECTION AND

LEGITIMIZED HIS WIN WITH BLESSINGS FROM SADC LEADERS, HE

WOULD FEEL COMFORTABLE STEPPING DOWN WITH THE KNOWLEDGE

THAT LAND REDISTRIBUTION WOULD CONTINUE AND THE MDC WAS

DESTROYED. ZVOBGO DISMISSED THIS THEORY, SAYING WHOEVER

BELIEVES IT DOES NOT KNOW MUGABE AS WELL AS HE DOES.

ZVOBGO THINKS MUGABE INTENDS TO COMPLETE HIS NEXT TERM.

9. (C) THERE ARE NEW SIGNS OF INTERNAL DISSATISFACTION

IN ZANU-PF AND THE GOZ. SENIOR CIVIL SERVANTS ARE

LEAVING AGAINST A BACKDROP OF A PURGE OF PARTY

STRUCTURES THAT HAS LEFT A LOT OF BRUISED PEOPLE BEHIND.

ALTHOUGH OUR CONTACTS DOWNPLAYED NKOSANA MOYO\'S

RESIGNATION, IT HAS THE POTENTIAL TO SET OFF A CHAIN

REACTION OF OTHER HIGH-PROFILE RESIGNATIONS IN

GOVERNMENT OR CABINET. ONE OR TWO MORE WOULD FURTHER

DAMPEN MORALE IN ZANU-PF AS A WHOLE, AND INCREASE THE

LEVEL OF PARANOIA AMONG THE TOP LEADERSHIP. MORE

MINISTERS WOULD COME UNDER SUSPICION OF DISLOYALTY, AND

MORE, SUCH AS FORMER LANDS AND AGRICULTURE MINISTER

KUMBIRAI KANGAI, COULD FIND THEMSELVES ON THE WRONG END

OF A CORRUPTION INVESTIGATION. WAR VETERANS HAVE

ACCUSED REGISTRAR-GENERAL TOBAIWA MUDEDE, A PARTY

LOYALIST AND STALWART, OF RIGGING THE MASVINGO MAYORAL

ELECTION. THE LOSS OF THE BULAWAYO MAYORAL ELECTION --

SCHEDULED FOR JUNE -- AND ADDITIONAL RECRIMINATIONS FROM

THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY -- PARTICULARLY THE EU, IF

IT DECIDES TO IMPOSE SANCTIONS NEXT MONTH -- WOULD WEIGH

HEAVILY ON ZANU-PF LEADERS, ESPECIALLY MUGABE.

UNFORTUNATELY, THE LIKELY REACTION FROM ZANU-PF WILL BE

TO CIRCLE THE WAGONS AND RETURN TO THE TRIED AND TRUE

TACTICS OF VIOLENCE AND INTIMIDATION AGAINST RURAL

RESIDENTS. END COMMENT.
