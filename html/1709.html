1. (C) AS PART OF OUR EFFORT TO PROMOTE GREATER

INTERNATIONAL VISIBILITY FOR BOSNIA\'S CROAT MODERATE

POLITICIANS (REF A), POST HAS TRIED TO CONFIRM THAT THE

HOLY SEE WILL RESPOND POSITIVELY TO THE REQUEST BY

FEDERATION OFFICIALS FOR AN INVITATION TO VISIT THE VATICAN

(REF B). AS YET, WE HAVE BEEN UNABLE TO PIN DOWN OUR

INTERLOCUTORS IN THE SECOND SECRETARIAT (MFA) ON WHETHER OR

WHEN AN INVITATION WILL BE FORTHCOMING. WE HAVE

NEVERTHELESS ASSURED THEM WE BELIEVE IT WOULD BE A GOOD

IDEA TO RESPOND POSITIVELY TO THE REQUEST. IN RESPONSE TO

POLOFF\'S QUERY 5/14, DFM MIGLIORE SAID THAT HE DID NOT KNOW

FOR CERTAIN IF A VISIT BY FEDERATION OFFICIALS WAS IN THE

WORKS, BUT THAT HE HAD HEARD A RUMOR ABOUT SOME BOSNIANS

VISITING. HE THOUGHT THE VATICAN WOULD WANT TO GO SLOWLY,

SINCE THEY WERE NOT SURE OF THE SITUATION IN HERZEGOVINA

AND WOULDN\'T WANT TO DO ANYTHING TO MAKE THINGS WORSE.

2. (C) VATICAN BALKANS DIRECTOR JOSEPH MARINO TOLD POLOFF

5/16 THAT HADN\'T HEARD ABOUT ANY INVITATION TARGETED AT

CROAT MODERATES, BUT HE SHARED WITH CHARGE LATER 5/16 THAT

BOSNIAN AMBASSADOR TO THE HOLY SEE KRALJEVIC (A CROAT

CATHOLIC) HAD INDICATED HE WAS WORKING ON A PROPOSAL FOR A

VISIT BY THE BOSNIAN JOINT PRESIDENCY.

3. (C) COMMENT: FROM WHERE WE SIT AN INVITATION TO THE

JOINT PRESIDENCY LOOKS ONLY SLIGHTLY LESS DESIRABLE THAN

THE MORE CROAT-HEAVY LINEUP FROM A FEDERATION DELEGATION AS

DISCUSSED REF B. WE DEFER TO DEPARTMENT AND EMBASSY

SARAJEVO, HOWEVER, AS TO HOW TO PLAY IT. WE WILL, IN THE
