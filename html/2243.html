SUBJECT: VATICAN CONDEMNS CLONING EXPERIMENT

REFTEL: VATICAN 1745

1. THE VATICAN HAS REITERATED ITS UNEQUIVOCAL CONDEMNATION

OF HUMAN CLONING FOLLOWING THE ANNOUNCEMENT BY ADVANCED CELL

TECHNOLOGY THAT IT HAD CARRIED OUT THE PROCEDURE. THE VATICAN

DECLARED THE EVENT AS A "GRAVE" DEVELOPMENT. THE VATICAN

INSISTS THE CELLS ARE HUMAN LIFE AND THAT SCIENTISTS SHOULD

ACKNOWLEDGE THEY ARE DEALING WITH HUMAN EMBRYOS INSTEAD OF

TALKING ABOUT CLUSTERS OF CELLS.

2. CLAIMS OF THE THERAPEUTIC POTENTIAL OF CLONED HUMAN

EMBRYOS WERE OF PARTICULAR CONCERN FOR THE VATICAN, WHICH

CRITICIZED THE HIERARCHY OF THE VALUE OF HUMAN LIFE APPARENTLY

INTRODUCED BY THE THERAPIES. FOR THE VATICAN EVERY HUMAN LIFE,

FROM CELL ONE TO NATURAL DEATH, HAS EQUAL VALUE. IF ONE

PARTICULAR DEVELOPMENT STAGE IS SINGLED OUT FOR USE BY ANOTHER

MORE MATURE STAGE, IT WARNED OF A NEW FORM OF DISCRIMINATION

BASED ON "STAGE OF HUMAN DEVELOPMENT" - AN EMBRYO BEING WORTH

LESS THAN A FETUS, AN INFANT HAVING LESS VALUE THAN AN ADULT

AND SO ON. THE VATICAN STATEMENT PROMOTED ALTERNATIVE STEM

CELL RESEARCH AND THERAPY USING STEM CELLS FROM ADULTS AND

SPONTANEOUSLY ABORTED FETUSES WHICH IT DECLARED SCIENTIFICALLY

VALID AND MORALLY LICIT.

3. NOTE: POLOFF WAS TOLD BY A SENIOR OFFICIAL OF THE

PONTIFICAL COUNCIL FOR LIFE (BIOETHICAL ISSUES) THAT HE FELT

"BETRAYED" BY THE ADMINISTRATION WHICH HE EXPECTED TO PREVENT

CLONING TAKING PLACE. THE PRESIDENT\'S REMARKS 11/26 DECLARING

CLONING "WRONG" WERE REPORTED BY VATICAN-BASED MEDIA. WE

EXPECT THE VATICAN WILL APPRECIATE THE PRESIDENT\'S REMARKS.

END NOTE.

4. COMMENT: WE DO NOT EXPECT THE VATICAN TO CHANGE ITS

POSITION ON HUMAN CLONING. HOWEVER, SHOULD THE PROCEDURE

EVENTUALLY LEAD TO MATURE HUMAN "CLONES" TAKING THEIR PLACE IN

SOCIETY THEN THE VATICAN WOULD URGE FULL RESPECT AND RIGHTS TO
