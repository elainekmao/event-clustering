SUBJECT: MEDIA REPORT ZDERA; HARARE

1. THE MAINSTREAM PRINT PRESS CONTINUES TO PUBLISH

OPINION PIECES ON THE ZIMBABWE DEMOCRACY AND ECONOMIC

RECOVERY ACT (ZDERA) OF 2001. THIS WEEK THE ACT WAS

INCLUDED ON THE AGENDA AT THE JUST-ENDED FIFTH ZANU PF

NATIONAL PEOPLE\'S CONFERENCE IN VICTORIA FALLS. A QUICK

SAMPLE OF THE OPINION PIECES AND WHAT WAS SAID ABOUT THE

ZDERA AT THE ZANU PF CONGRESS FOLLOW:

2. UNDER HEADLINE "WHY WE SHOULD SUPPORT THE ZIMBABWE

DEMOCRACY BILL," THE DECEMBER 14 EDITION OF THE INDEPENDENT

WEEKLY "ZIMBABWE INDEPENDENT" CARRIED THE FOLLOWING PIECE

BY PAUL MAVHIMA:

". . .THE PASSAGE OF THE BILL IS A GOOD INDICATOR THAT

THE WORLD IS WATCHING ZIMBABWE AND THAT THERE IS NOW

ZERO TOLERANCE EVEN FROM ITS AFRICAN-AMERICAN FRIENDS

WHO COULD HAVE BLOCKED THE BILL IF THEY HAD REALLY

WANTED TO DO SO. IT WOULD BE UNUSUAL TO FIND A

SITUATION WHERE THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY WOULD NOT

TAKE STEPS IN CONDEMNING A GOVERNMENT THAT IS KILLING

ITS OWN PEOPLE. . . ."

3. UNDER HEADLINE "DEMOCRACY BILL: U.S.\'S WAY OF TESTING

THE TURBULENCE," THE DECEMBER 15 EDITION OF THE GOVERNMENT-

CONTROLLED DAILY "CHRONICLE" RAN THE FOLLOWING FEATURE

ARTICLE BY KENNEDY MAVHUMAHASHA:

"DETRACTORS OF ZIMBABWE\'S LAND REFORMS EXPECT U.S.

PRESIDENT GEORGE BUSH TO SIGN THE ZDERA 2001 INTO LAW

SOON REGARDLESS OF STRONG REPRESENTATIONS FROM AFRICA

AGAINST THE PIECE OF LEGISLATION. ANALYSTS SAID THAT

THE FACT THAT THE ACT, CRITICIZED WIDELY FOR BEING

\'DRACONIAN AND RACIST,\' EASILY SAILED THROUGH THE

AMERICAN SENATE AND CONGRESS REFLECTED THE HARD-LINE

RESOLVE IN THE BUSH ADMINISTRATION TO HAVE ITS WAY

AGAINST ZIMBABWE. . . ."

4. THE DECEMBER 17 EDITION OF THE GOVERNMENT-CONTROLLED

DAILY "THE HERALD" PRINTED CRITICISM OF ZDERA FROM

CONGRESSWOMAN CYNTHIA MCKINNEY AND CHIEF ELDER OSIRIS

AKKEBALA. THE PAPER PRINTED THEIR REMARKS ON THE OPINION

PAGE UNDER HEADLINES "BILL PERPETUATES PRIVILEGES," AND

"AMERICA STICKS IMPERIALIST HANDS IN SOVEREIGN AFFAIRS OF

ZIMBABWE," RESPECTIVELY.

5. THE DECEMBER 16 EDITION OF THE GOVERNMENT-CONTROLLED

WEEKLY "THE SUNDAY MAIL" CARRIED AN OPEN LETTER TO

CONGRESSMAN PAYNE SIGNED BY FRIENDS OF ZIMBABWE, BROOKLYN,

NY, UNDER HEADLINE "HANDS OFF ZIMBABWE" [NB: WE NOTE THAT

THE ANONYMOUS AUTHOR OF THIS LETTER USES DISTINCTLY NON-

AMERICAN RHETORIC AND VOCABULARY]:

"AS ONE OF 40,000,000 BLACK PEOPLE WHOM THE

CONGRESSIONAL BLACK CAUCUS IS SUPPOSED TO REPRESENT, I

WAS APPALLED TO LEARN THAT YOU HAVE CO-SPONSORED A

VERSION OF THE ZDERA OF 2001. IN WHATEVER LANGUAGE IT

IS COUCHED, THIS ACT IS STILL NOTHING BUT A SANCTIONS

BILL AGAINST THE SECOND LONGEST STABLE DEMOCRACY ON THE

AFRICAN CONTINENT. ITS ALLEGED CONCERN ABOUT THE \'RULE

OF DEMOCRACY\' CONCEALS ITS REAL GOAL, WHICH IS TO

PUNISH AN AFRICAN LEADERSHIP, WHICH HAS HAD THE COURAGE

TO ACT ON THE RIGHT OF AFRICAN PEOPLE TO RECLAIM LAND

WHICH WAS STOLEN FROM THEM. . . I ALSO CANNOT

CAN NOW ADVOCATE THEM AGAINST ZIMBABWE, A COUNTRY WHOSE

ECONOMY IS ALREADY DEVASTATED. THE CAMPAIGNS AGAINST

IRAQ AND CUBA HAVE PROVEN THAT SANCTIONS DON\'T WORK. .

."

6. UNDER HEADLINE "U.S., U.K. GOVERNMENTS WARNED," THE

DECEMBER 15 EDITION OF THE GOVERNMENT-CONTROLLED DAILY "THE

HERALD" CARRIED THE FOLLOWING ARTICLE BY PHILLIP MAGWAZA:

"THE ZIMBABWE NATIONAL LIBERATION WAR VETERANS

ASSOCIATION YESTERDAY (DECEMBER 14) WARNED THE AMERICAN

AND BRITISH GOVERNMENTS THAT THERE WILL BE NO PEACE IN

THE COUNTRY ONCE THE ZIMBABWE SANCTIONS BILL IS PASSED

INTO LAW BY U.S. PRESIDENT GEORGE BUSH. . .\' ONCE THE

BILL IS PASSED, THERE SHOULD BE NOTHING THAT WILL STOP

US FROM TAKING ACTION. WE ARE GOING TO FIGHT ON THE

DAY THE BILL IS PASSED. THERE WILL BE NO PEACE IN THIS

COUNTRY. . . .,\' WAR VETERANS ACTING CHAIRMAN PATRICK

NYARUWATA IS QUOTED AS SAYING. . . ."
