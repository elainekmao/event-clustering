REASONS 1.5 (D AND E)

SUMMARY

-------

1. (SBU) POST FEEDBACK ON REFTEL WTO BIOTECH MEDIA STRATEGY

IS SHAPED BY OUR ACTIVE INVOLVEMENT IN THE ISSUE AND

CROATIA\'S STATUS AS AN EU ASPIRANT WHICH PLANS TO SUBMIT AN

APPLICATION FOR ACCESSION NEXT MONTH. IN CROATIA, THERE IS

LITTLE PUBLIC UNDERSTANDING OF TRADE ISSUES, EVEN LESS OF

INTERNATIONAL ENVIRONMENTAL OR DEVELOPMENT ISSUES. THUS OUR

CAMPAIGN WOULD FOCUS ON ISSUES DEAR TO CROATIANS -- THE FOOD

THEY EAT AND THEIR DESIRE TO KNOW WHAT IT IS THEY ARE EATING.

END SUMMARY.

REACTIONS TO SUGGESTED MESSAGES

-------------------------------

2. (SBU) POST GENERALLY AGREES WITH THE THEMES OFFERED

REFTEL. TO TAILOR THE MESSAGE, AFTER MAKING THE STRONG POINT

THAT THE WTO DISPUTE IS NOT ABOUT WHETHER THE BIOTECH IS SAFE

OR NOT -- THE EU CONCEDES IT IS -- BUT RATHER THE FAILURE OF

THE EU TO FOLLOW THROUGH ON INTERNATIONALLY ACCEPTED RULES,

WE NEED TO ADDRESS VIGOROUSLY THE PUBLIC PERCEPTION THAT

BIOTECH FOODS ARE SUSPECT AND THAT THE US DOES NOT RESPECT

CROATIANS\' RIGHT TO CHOOSE. IF WE DO NOT OVERCOME THAT

PERCEPTION, WE COULD WIN THE TRADE BATTLE BUT LOSE THE

MARKETING WAR.

3. (C) FOLLOWING COMMENTS ARE KEYED TO REFTEL:

-- THEMES A AND B (WTO COMMITMENTS, RULES-BASED APPROVAL

PROCESS): WE NEED TO MAKE CLEAR THAT THE EU HAS A PROCESS TO

REVIEW APPLICATIONS FOR THE BIOTECH PRODUCTS, HAS DETERMINED

THEY ARE SAFE, AND IS STALLING NONE THE LESS. WHILE A

DETAILED ANALYSIS OF THE TRADE ISSUES MAY BE USEFUL FOR OUR

GOVERNMENT INTERLOCUTOR, WE WILL NEED TO KEEP IT SIMPLE FOR

THE PRESS AND THE PUBLIC.

-- THEME C (MISINFORMATION AGGRAVATING FAMINE IN AFRICA): TO

DATE, THE CROATIAN PUBLIC HAS NOT BEEN SEIZED WITH THIS

ISSUE. ITS RECEPTIVITY TO THIS MESSAGE IS UNCERTAIN.

- THEME D (BIOTECH SAFE, NO SCIENTIFIC BASE FOR MORATORIUM):

THIS IS AN IMPORTANT THEME TO PURSUE IN CROATIA.

UNFORTUNATELY, FOR EVERY SCIENTIFIC STUDY WE CAN OFFER UP,

THE OPPONENTS OF BIOTECH ARE ABLE TO OFFER UP ANOTHER STUDY,

WHICH WHILE OF MORE-THAN-DUBIOUS QUALITY, OFTEN REDUCE OUR

IMPACT. CITING SOURCES WHICH HAVE CONCLUDED THAT BIOTECH IS

SAFE, SUCH AS THE EU ITSELF AND THE WHO, WOULD BE VALUABLE,

AS THEY ARE CONSIDERED MORE NEUTRAL THAN THE USG.

-- THEME E (BIOTECH WILL HELP DEVELOPING COUNTRIES):

CROATIANS ARE NOT OVERLY CONCERNED WITH ECONOMIC GROWTH IN

DEVELOPING COUNTRIES; WHAT THEY WORRY ABOUT IS ERODING A

NICHE THEY HOPE TO EXPLOIT AS AN UNSPOILED, "NATURAL"

MEDITERRANEAN PARADISE FOR EUROPEAN TOURISTS. THEY ARE ALSO

WORRIED THAT IF THEY PLANT BIOTECH FOODS, THEY MAY ENDANGER

THEIR EXPORT PROSPECTS TO THE EU.

-- THEME F (BIOTECH ENVIRONMENTALLY BENEFICIAL): CROATIANS

ARE NOT INTELLECTUALLY CONSISTENT ON THE ENVIRONMENT. THE

MAJORITY OF CROATIANS REGARD CROATIA AS ENVIRONMENTALLY PURE

AND ARE PERFECTLY WILLING TO THINK OF GMO-FREE FOODS AS

"HEALTHY FOODS," NO MATTER HOW MUCH PESTICIDE WAS USED TO

GROW THEM. NEVERTHELESS, IT IS A MESSAGE WORTH PUTTING OUT,

HOPEFULLY APPEALING TO SOME MORE ENVIRONMENTALLY SENSITIVE

PEOPLE AT THE MARGINS AND POINTING UP THE FALLACY THAT

GMO-FREE EQUALS "ENVIRONMENTAL." FIGURES SHOWING HOW MUCH

PESTICIDE IS USED ON AN AMERICAN CORNFIELD VERSUS ITS

EUROPEAN COUNTERPART COULD BE ILLUMINATING.

-- THEME G (CONSUMER CHOICE): THIS COULD BE A POWERFUL

ARGUMENT HERE. BUT IT IS A DOUBLE-EDGED SWORD, SINCE THE

CROATIAN PUBLIC BELIEVES CONSUMER CHOICE MUST BE ENSURED BY

LABELING. A RECENT US SPEAKER TRIED TO MAKE THE ARGUMENT

THAT LABELING OF BIOTECH FOODS WOULD REDUCE CONSUMER CHOICE.

GROCERY MANUFACTURERS WOULD REFUSE TO USE BIOTECH SOURCES

SINCE CONSUMERS WOULD INEVITABLY REJECT BIOTECH FOODS LABELED

AS SUCH. THIS ARGUMENT DID NOT FIND RESONANCE HERE. EVEN

OUR STAUNCHEST ALLIES -- THE REPUTABLE, SERIOUS SCIENTIFIC

COMMUNITY, AGRONOMISTS, TRADE AND REGULATORY OFFICIALS --

THINK THAT MANDATORY LABELING IS AN INDISPENSABLE FIRST STEP

TOWARD DISPELLING UNFOUNDED CONSUMER FEARS.

4. (C) WE MUST BE PREPARED TO ANSWER THE QUESTION "GIVEN

CROATIA\'S DESIRE TO EXPORT TO THE EU, WEAK REGULATORY

STRUCTURE AND INSTITUTIONS, AND SMALL AREA, DOES THIS ACTION

AGAINST THE EU MEAN THAT THE US WILL NOT ALLOW CROATIA THE

TIME TO PUT INTO PLACE ALL THE STRUCTURES WE NEED TO CONTROL

BIOTECH CROPS AND TO PROVIDE THE MONITORING OUR POPULACE

DEMANDS OF OUR FOOD STOCKS?"

ALLIES IN A PUBLIC DIPLOMACY CAMPAIGN

-------------------------------------

5. (SBU) OUR BEST ALLIES IN THE PRO-BIOTECH CAMPAIGN HAVE

BEEN SCIENTISTS, MAINLY THOSE WHO THEMSELVES WORK IN THE AREA

OF BIOTECHNOLOGY. MUCH OF THE FOOD AND AGRICULTURAL

INDUSTRIES SEE BIOTECH AS EITHER A DIRECT COMPETITIVE THREAT,

OR A RISK FACTOR FOR FUTURE EXPORTS TO THE EU, SO HAVE NOT

BEEN PUBLIC SUPPORTERS OF BIOTECH. ADDITIONALLY, THEY HAVE

BEEN COWED BY A VOCAL, LOCAL ENVIRONMENTAL NGO FROM EVEN

THINKING ABOUT SOURCING BIOTECH INPUTS. WE DO NOT KNOW OF

ANY JOURNALISTS WHO COULD BE CALLED "ADVOCATES" FOR BIOTECH,

BUT A NUMBER ARE WILLING TO PRESENT BOTH SIDES OF THE STORY.

SPECIFICS OF LOCAL REACTIONS TO A WTO-CASE

------------------------------------------

6. (C) MANY CROATIANS, INCLUDING GOVERNMENT OFFICIALS AND

PARLIAMENTARIANS, CLAIM THE US APPLIES A HARSHER STANDARD

AGAINST CROATIA THAN OTHER COUNTRIES. THEY HAVE ACCUSED US

OF THREATENING CROATIA WITH A WTO CASE WHEN THE GOVERNMENT

CONSIDERED INTRODUCING A TEMPORARY BAN ON THE IMPORT OF GMOS

(A DISTORTION OF THE TRUE STORY), WHILE ALLEGEDLY ALLOWING

NEW ZEALAND AND THE EU TO LIMIT THE IMPORT OF THESE PRODUCTS.

ALSO, A DE FACTO BAN ON SOME PRODUCTS IS IN PLACE WHILE THE

CROATIAN GOVERNMENT GLACIALLY DEVELOPS LEGISLATION AND

GUIDELINES FOR EVALUATING AND APPROVING BIOTECH PRODUCTS. A

WTO CASE WOULD SERVE NOTICE THAT WE WILL NOT ACCEPT

INDEFINITE DELAYS. HOWEVER, IT RISKS ALSO HARDENING ALREADY

ENTRENCHED NEGATIVE FEELINGS ABOUT THE PRODUCTS THAT WE HOPE

TO SELL.

THE MESSAGE

------------

7. (C) IN CROATIA, THE MOST EFFECTIVE MESSAGE WOULD

EMPHASIZE THAT THE EU ITSELF HAS FOUND THESE FOODS TO BE NO

MORE RISKY THAN TRADITIONAL FOODS. THE EU HAS HAD BIOTECH

CROPS (SOY AND CORN) ON THE MARKET FOR YEARS WITH NO ILL

EFFECTS, AND DESPITE THIS IT IS RAISING ARTIFICIAL BARRIERS.

WE ARE NOT ASKING THAT THE EU DISPENSE WITH ITS APPROVAL

PROCESS, BUT RATHER ASKING IT TO START USING IT.
