CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD REASONS 1.5 B/D.

1. (C) ON JUNE 26 AMBASSADOR MCDONALD DELIVERED REFTEL

DEMARCHE TO DR. MACHIVENYIKA MAPURANGA, PERMANENT

SECRETARY OF THE MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS. AS THE

SIPDIS

CONVERSATION EVOLVED IT BECAME EVIDENT THAT THE DEMARCHE

WAS A NON-STARTER. MAPURANGA REPORTED THAT DURING A

MINI SUMMIT IN HARARE JUNE 25 NAMIBIAN PRESIDENT SAM

NUJOMA AND PRESIDENT MUGABE (ANGOLA\'S DOS SANTOS DID NOT

COME AS REPORTED IN REF B) BOTH PRESSED CONGOLESE

PRESIDENT LAURENT KABILA TO RECONSIDER HIS POSITION ON

SIR KETUMILE MASIRE AS FACILITATOR OF CONGOLESE NATIONAL

DIALOGUE. THE CONGOLESE PRESIDENT THANKED HIS ALLIES

FOR THEIR OPINIONS, BUT STATED THAT HE HAD ALREADY MADE

UP HIS MIND. WHILE STRONGLY COMMITTED TO THE NATIONAL

DIALOGUE KABILA SAID HE WAS DONE WITH MASIRE. THE SADC

ALLIES FAILED TO CONVINCE KABILA AND MUGABE NOW REGARDS

IT AS A DEAD ISSUE. KABILA SAID HE HAD ALREADY WRITTEN

2. (C) THE PRESIDENTS ALSO DISCUSSED THE AMOUNT OF

TERRITORY THAT JEAN-PIERRE BEMBA OF THE MLC HAD SEIZED

OTHER WESTERN COUNTRIES WERE NOT APPLYING SUFFICIENT
