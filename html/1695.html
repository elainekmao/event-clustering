1. (C) THE VIOLENCE REPORTED REFTEL HAS CONTINUED

UNABATED IN MASVINGO TOWN IN THE RUNUP TO THE MAYORAL

ELECTION MAY 12-13, WITH CONTINUING SKIRMISHES BETWEEN

SUPPORTERS OF THE RULING ZANU-PF AND THOSE OF THE

OPPOSITION MOVEMENT FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE (MDC).

ACCORDING TO THE INDEPENDENT "DAILY NEWS," ZANU-PF

MEMBER OF PARLIAMENT AND WAR VETERAN LEADER CHENJERAI

"HITLER" HUNZVI IS CIRCULATING WITH AN AK-47 RIFLE,

ACCOMPANIED BY OTHER ARMED WAR VETERANS, AS HE

SUPERVISES THE LATEST CHAPTER IN THE GOVERNMENT\'S

ONGOING TERROR CAMPAIGN AGAINST OPPOSITION SUPPORTERS.

GANDI MUDZINGWA, PERSONAL ASSISTANT TO MDC PRESIDENT

MORGAN TSVANGIRAI, TOLD POLITICAL SECTION CHIEF ON MAY

11 THAT POLICE HAVE ESTABLISHED A HEAVY PRESENCE ON

ALL ROADS INTO MASVINGO, AND ARE SEARCHING ALL

VEHICLES CARRYING MORE THAN FIVE PASSENGERS. A SENIOR

POLICE OFFICIAL IN MASVINGO CONFIRMED TO JOURNALISTS

THAT SOME POLICE OFFICERS WERE BEING INVESTIGATED FOR

PARTICIPATING ACTIVELY IN ZANU-PF\'S "CAMPAIGN TEAMS."

2. (U) ZANU-PF SUPPORTERS REPORTEDLY ORDERED ALL

BUSINESSES IN TOWN TO CLOSE ON MAY 10 SO THAT WORKERS

COULD ATTEND CAMPAIGN RALLIES ADDRESSED BY VICE-

PRESIDENTS SIMON MUZENDA AND JOSEPH MSIKA, AND OTHER

SENIOR ZANU-PF OFFICIALS. ONE BUSINESSMAN CONDEMNED

THE ACTION, SAYING "IT IS IRRATIONAL FOR A PARTY TO

ORDER THE CLOSURE OF ALL BUSINESSES WHEN THE ECONOMY

OF THE COUNTRY IS AILING." ANOTHER SAID HE HAD BEEN

THREATENED BY WAR VETERANS WITH DEATH IF HE DID NOT

CLOSE SO HAD HAD NO OTHER CHOICE. MEANWHILE, 18 MDC

SUPPORTERS APPEARED IN COURT ON MAY 11 ON CHARGES OF

INCITING VIOLENCE AND WERE RELEASED ON BAIL, WHILE

FOUR REMAINED IN DETENTION BECAUSE THEY HAD VIOLATED

PREVIOUS BAIL RESTRICTIONS. ACCORDING TO PRESS

REPORTS, NOT A SINGLE ZANU-PF SUPPORTER OR WAR VETERAN

HAS BEEN ARRESTED OR CHARGED IN CONNECTION WITH THE

VIOLENCE WHICH HAS MARRED THE LAST TWO WEEKS OF THE

MAYORAL CAMPAIGN, ALTHOUGH FOLLOWERS OF BOTH OF

ZIMBABWE\'S LARGEST PARTIES HAVE BEEN INVOLVED IN

SKIRMISHES.

3. (C) LONGTIME ZANU-PF POLITICIAN EDDISON ZVOBGO --

WHO HAILS FROM MASVINGO PROVINCE AND RETAINS

SIGNIFICANT INFLUENCE THERE -- PREDICTED TO CHARGE ON

MAY 10 THAT NEITHER ZANU-PF NOR THE MDC WOULD WIN THE

MAYORAL ELECTION. RATHER, INDEPENDENT FEMIAS

CHAKABUDA WAS THE BEST-KNOWN CANDIDATE IN MASVINGO AND

WOULD LIKELY BE THE VICTOR. CURRENTLY HEAD OF THE

CITY\'S TAXPAYERS\' ASSOCIATION, CHAKABUDA HAD

LONGSTANDING TIES TO ZANU-PF AND HAD SERVED ON THE

CITY COUNCIL FOR TWENTY YEARS, BUT LEFT THE RULING

PARTY IN DISGUST A YEAR AGO TO JOIN THE MDC. HE HAD

INTENDED TO RUN ON THE MDC TICKET FOR MAYOR, BUT HIS

CANDIDACY WAS NIXED BY THE PARTY\'S NATIONAL

LEADERSHIP, WHICH DISTRUSTED HIM BECAUSE OF HIS

PREVIOUS POLITICAL AFFILIATION. CHAKABUDA THEN

DECIDED TO STAND AS AN INDEPENDENT.

4. (C) ZVOBGO TOLD CHARGE THAT HE HAD TURNED DOWN A

REQUEST FROM VICE-PRESIDENT MSIKA TO CAMPAIGN FOR THE

ZANU-PF CANDIDATE. HE POINTED OUT THAT HE HAD BEEN

ACTIVELY INVOLVED IN EVERY ELECTION IN MASVINGO SINCE

INDEPENDENCE, SO HIS STANDING ON THE SIDELINES IN THIS

CASE WAS IN ITSELF A TELLING STATEMENT. A ZANU-PF

LOSS IN THE MAYORAL ELECTION, ZVOBGO ANTICIPATED,

WOULD SEND A POWERFUL MESSAGE TO THE RULING PARTY\'S

OLD GUARD THAT THEY CAN NO LONGER "IMPOSE" CANDIDATES

AND LEADERS ON THE ZIMBABWEAN PUBLIC. THE PEOPLE OF

MASVINGO, ZVOBGO SAID, ARE "ENORMOUSLY DISENCHANTED"

WITH PRESIDENT MUGABE, WHO COULD LOSE THE PROVINCE IN

THE 2002 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION.

COMMENT

-------

5. (C) WE ARE NOT SURPRISED BY THE FERVOR WITH WHICH

ZANU-PF AND THE MDC ARE CONTESTING THIS MUNICIPAL

ELECTION. THE POLITICAL STAKES ARE VERY HIGH IN

MASVINGO, WHICH MOST OBSERVERS CONSIDER THE KEY

BATTLEGROUND PROVINCE IN NEXT YEAR\'S PRESIDENTIAL

ELECTION. AS WE INDICATED IN REFTEL, ZANU-PF WANTS TO
