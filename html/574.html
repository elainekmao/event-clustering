SUBJECT: CGYOOL: PRES. TUDJMAN SAYS HE IS UNWILLING

- TO EXTEND UNPROFOR WHILE IT IS UNSUCCESSFUL;

- EXPLAINS BOBETKO/MLADIC MEETING

-------

SUMMARY

-------

1. AT HIS MONTHLY PRESS CONFERENCE PRESIDENT TUDJMAN

SAID IT IS TOO EARLY TO DISCUSS EXTENDING UNPROFOR\'S

MANDATE. HE EXPLAINED GENERAL BOBETKO\'S MEETING WITH

GENERAL MLADIC AS AIMED AT STOPPING ATTACKS ON CROATIA

AND DENYING BELGRADE\'S ALLEGATIONS THAT CROATIA HAD

18 OR 19 BRIGADES IN B-H. HE SAID IT WAS GENERAL

MORILLON\'S FAULT THAT, CONTRARY TO CROATIA\'S

EXPECTATIONS, HVO AND B-H GOV\'T FORCE LEADERS HAD NOT

ATTENDED THE TALKS. HE DENIED THAT AN INCREASED ALERT

-------------------

ON UNPROFOR AND WAR

-------------------

.

2. AT HIS DECEMBER 1 PRESS CONFERENCE, PRESIDENT .

TUDJMAN SAID IT IS TOO EARLY TO DISCUSS PROLONGATION

OF THE UNPROFOR MANDATE. "WE HAVE PUT FORTH OUR

REQUESTS (TO UNPROFOR). ...IT IS STILL TOO EARLY TO

DISCUSS WHAT WILL HAPPEN AFTER THE EXPIRATION OF

UNPROFOR\'S MANDATE. IN ANY CASE, CROATIA IS NOT

WILLING TO PROLONG UNPROFOR\'S MANDATE WHILE IT IS NOT

FULFILLING ITS TASKS." HE ACKNOWLEDGED THAT HE HAD

MET CONFIDENTIALLY WITH GENEVA NEGOTIATORS VANCE AND

OWEN IN CROATIA OVER THE NOV 28-29 WEEKEND TO DISCUSS

UNPROFOR AND THE CONTINUING CRISIS IN THE FORMER

YUGOSLAVIA, BUT HE PROVIDED NO DETAILS. LATER, HE

SAID VANCE AND OWEN HAD TOLD THE SERBS IN SECTOR EAST

AND KNIN THAT THE SO-CALLED KRAJINA IS PART OF

CROATIA AND THAT SOLUTIONS TO THE QUESTION OF SERBIAN

MINORITIES MUST BE FOUND WITHIN THE FRAMEWORK OF

CROATIAN LAW. REPRESENTATIVES OF UNPROFOR, ACCORDING

TO THE PRESIDENT, HAVE CONCLUDED THAT "THE VANCE PLAN

HAS BEEN CALLED INTO QUESTION" BY SERBIAN REFUSAL TO

COOPERATE AND THAT "STEPS SHOULD BE TAKEN TO HAVE

UNPROFOR IMPLEMENT IT."

BIGGER UNPROFOR, AIR STRIKES, CARROT/STICKS

-------------------------------------------

5. THE PRESIDENT\'S PRESCRIPTION FOR RESOLVING THE

CONFLICTS IN CROATIA AND B-H WAS A COMBINATION OF

CONTINUED INTERNATIONAL HELP AND DOMESTIC MEASURES.

HE SAID THE MOST THAT COULD BE EXPECTED FROM THE

INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY WAS THE SECURITY COUNCIL\'S

SANCTIONING DEPLOYMENT OF A LARGER PEACEKEEPING FORCE

"WITH THE PURPOSE OF PREVENTING FURTHER AGGRESSION,

ALONG WITH THE USE OF AIR FORCES." HE ADDED THAT HE

AGREED WITH THOSE WHO ARGUED THAT AIR STRIKES ON "THE

AGGRESSOR\'S VITAL INSTALLATIONS WOULD SUFFICE TO

TERMINATE FURTHER AGGRESSION."

6. TUDJMAN SAID CROATIA IS TRYING TO RESOLVE ITS

PROBLEMS WITH THE SERBIA MINORITY BY CONSTITUTIONAL

MEANS, BY RESPECTING HUMAN RIGHTS STANDARDS, CREATING

TWO ADMINISTRATIVE DISTRICTS FOR SERBS (GLINA AND

KNIN), AND PASSING AN AMNESTY LAW FOR THOSE NOT

ACCUSED OF WAR CRIMES. THE PRESIDENT SAID THAT, ON

THE OTHER HAND, THE COUNTRY\'S NEW ANTI-TERRORIST LAW

WOULD BE USED AGAINST THOSE WHO CONTINUED SUBVERSIVE
