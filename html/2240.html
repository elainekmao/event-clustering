SUBJECT: NIGERIA: ALLEGATIONS OF DOS CONTRACTOR FRAUD FOR

OPERATION FOCUS RELIEF

SENSITIVE BUT UNCLASSIFIED - PROTECT ACCORDINGLY

1. (U) This is an action request. Please see para 5.

2. (SBU/NF) A Pacific Architects and Engineers (PA&E)

contract employee has alleged significant contract fraud on

the part of PA&E contract employees in Nigeria. PA&E is the

Department of State contractor for Operation Focus Relief

logistics.

3. (SBU/NF) According to the employee, both expatriate and

host nation employees are involved in significant contract

fraud. Specifically, the PA&E in-country headquarters

(Abuja) accountant, a Nigerian national, and her brother, are

alleged to have colluded with other Nigerian employees of

PA&E, as well as expatriate employees, to defraud the U.S.

government. This is achieved by: overcharging for services

and equipment and pocketing the surplus; arranging for the

accountant,s extended family to be awarded contracts for

services and equipment in order to effect the overcharges;

ordering the destruction of receipts; and, threatening

expatriate employees with bodily harm if they blow the

whistle.

4. (SBU/NF) The person who made the above allegations has

been hurriedly removed as a PA&E employee and has left the

country. Other PA&E expatriate employees will leave over the

next three weeks.

5. (SBU/NF) ACTION REQUEST: Embassy requests that the

Department quickly send a joint PA&E/Department of State

audit team to investigate the matter. Time is of the

essence. Operation Focus Relief ends December 15. PA&E

Nigeria has begun shutting down operations and transferring

personnel. The nature of the allegations, given the

extensive relationship between PA&E and the department, makes

this a serious issue. END ACTION REQUEST.
