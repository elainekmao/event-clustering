SUBJECT: DUTCH ON BELARUS DEMARCHE

REF: STATE 194808

Classified By: CLASSIFIED BY POLITICAL COUNSELOR MARY DALY FOR REASONS

1.5 (B AND D).

(C) POLOFF MADE REFTEL POINTS TO DUTCH MFA DESK OFFICER FOR

UKRAINE AND BELARUS DANIEL VINKELES MELCHERS ON JULY 7.

VINKELES MELCHERS ADVISED THAT THE DUTCH GOVERNMENT SHARES

U.S. CONCERNS AND IS PARTICULARLY FOCUSED ON A POSSIBLE

REFERENDUM INTENDED TO EXTEND LUKASHENKO\'S TERM IN OFFICE.

THE GONL THINKS THAT THE EU SHOULD NOT ACCEPT SUCH A STEP.

THE GONL BELIEVES THE TIME IS RIGHT TO TAKE A STRONG STAND ON

BELARUS. VINKELES MELCHERS NOTED THAT BELARUS IS ON THE

AGENDA OF THE PSC MEETING IN BRUSSELS ON JULY 8 AND THAT VISA

AND ECONOMIC SANCTIONS ARE AMONG THE MEASURES TO BE DISCUSSED

IN THE POLICY PAPER FOR THAT MEETING. THAT SAID, HE NOTED

THAT AS CURRENT OSCE CHAIRMAN-IN-OFFICE THE NETHERLANDS MAY

BE SOMEWHAT RESTRAINED IN VOICING ITS IDEAS TOO STRONGLY IN

EU COUNCILS.
