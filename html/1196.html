SUBJECT: ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT CONTINUES TO DEFER

RELEASE OF CUBAN DOCTORS

REF: HARARE 3485

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD FOR REASONS 1.5

B/D.

1. (C) IN INTRODUCING CONSUL TO DOMINIC BARTSCH, UNHCR

SENIOR DURABLE SOLUTIONS OFFICER (THE UN OFFICER

REPLACING ATTORNEY MICHIEL SALOMONS), UNHCR REGIONAL

REPRESENTATIVE OLUSEYI BAJULAIYE SAID THAT MOVEMENT ON

THE RELEASE OF THE CUBAN DOCTORS HAD GROUND TO A HALT.

HE TOLD US THAT THERE HAD BEEN NO PROGRESS EITHER BY THE

GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE OR UNHCR IN FINDING A SOLUTION TO

THE CASE OF CUBAN DOCTORS LEONEL CORDOVA RODRIGUEZ AND

NORIS DEL CARMEN PENA MARTINEZ. BAJULAIYE SAID THAT

ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT OFFICIALS HAD INFORMED HIM THAT

PRESIDENT MUGABE IS "MUCH TOO BUSY" TO MAKE A DECISION

REGARDING THE CUBANS. INDEED, WE CAN AVER THAT MUGABE

HAS BEEN CONSUMED WITH DEALING WITH THE AFTERMATH AND

PUTTING TOGETHER A CABINET FOLLOWING THE RECENT

PARLIAMENTARY ELECTION IN WHICH HIS PARTY NARROWLY WON A

MAJORITY.

2. (C) BAJULAIYE REPORTED TO CONSUL THAT THE LETTER HE

HAD DRAFTED FOR MRS. OGATA TO SEND TO PRESIDENT MUGABE

WAS STILL ON THE DESK OF THE UN HIGH COMMISSIONER FOR

REFUGEES. HE OPINED THAT THE HIGH COMMISSIONER MAY BE

WAITING FOR THE RETURN TO GENEVA OF DEPUTY HIGH

COMMISSIONER SOREN JESSEN-PETERSEN, WITH WHOM SHE WOULD

LIKE TO CONSULT PRIOR TO TAKING ANY ACTION. JESSEN-

PETERSEN\'S LETTER TO VICE PRESIDENT JOSEPH MSIKA REMAINS

UNANSWERED, ACCORDING TO THE REGIONAL UNHCR

REPRESENTATIVE. BAJULAIYE ALSO REVEALED THAT DOCTORS

CORDOVA AND PENA HAD WRITTEN LETTERS TO THE ZIMBABWEAN

GOVERNMENT IN WHICH THEY PLEADED FOR THE RIGHT TO BE

RESETTLED IN ONE OF TWO COUNTRIES THAT HAVE ACCEPTED

THEM AS REFUGEES. THE UNHCR REPRESENTATIVE STATED THAT

NO MATTER WHAT HAPPENED, HE WOULD RETURN TO LUSAKA ON

SATURDAY, JULY 1, AND THAT BARTSCH WOULD BE LEAVING ON

SUNDAY, JULY 2. THE CASE WOULD THEN PASS INTO THE HANDS

OF LOCAL UNHCR REPRESENTATIVE JOHN ADU, WHO WILL BE

RETURNING AFTER A VACATION FROM LONDON ON JULY 2.

3. (C) CONSUL GAVE TWO TOILETRY KITS TO BAJULAIYE TO

DELIVER TO THE CUBAN DOCTORS. SENATE FOREIGN RELATIONS

COMMITTEE STAFF MEMBER G. GARRETT GRIBSBY BROUGHT THE

KITS, BUT WAS UNABLE TO HAND THEM OVER PERSONALLY,

HAVING BEEN DENIED ACCESS TO THE DOCTORS. THE REGIONAL

UNHCR REPRESENTATIVE REPORTED THAT HIS STAFF HAD

DELIVERED THE KITS TO THE DESPONDENT CUBANS ON JUNE 29.

A FRUSTRATED BAJULAIYE SAID THAT HE WOULD SPEAK ONE MORE

TIME TO THE PERMANENT SECRETARIES OF THE MINISTRIES OF

FOREIGN AFFAIRS AND HOME AFFAIRS AS WELL AS THE CHIEF OF

IMMIGRATION TO INFORM THEM OF HIS INTENTION TO RETURN TO

LUSAKA BECAUSE OF LACK OF PROGRESS IN THE CASE. HE

SUGGESTED THAT LEGAL INTERVENTION (NOT BY UNHCR) MAY BE

A FINAL OPTION TO SECURE THE RELEASE OF THE CUBANS, AS

IT WAS "ILLEGAL" FOR THE ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT TO

CONTINUE TO KEEP THEM IN JAIL WHEN THEY HAD COMMITTED NO

CRIME IN ZIMBABWE.

4. (C) SWEDISH AMBASSADOR LENNARTH HJELMAKER TOLD DCM

ON JUNE 29 THAT SWEDEN IS PREPARED TO HELP RESETTLE THE

CUBANS, BUT AT PRESENT IS UNSURE OF WHAT HIS COUNTRY\'S

ROLE IN THE CASE SHOULD BE. THE SWEDES ARE OCCASIONALLY

IN TOUCH WITH UNHCR IN HARARE, BUT HAVE NOT HEARD FROM

THEM IN OVER A WEEK. THE SWEDISH AMBASSADOR SPECULATED

THAT THE ZIMBABWEANS SUSPECT THAT SWEDEN IS MERELY AN

ENTREPOT FROM WHICH THE CUBAN DOCTORS WILL PROCEED

DIRECTLY TO THE UNITED STATES. HE CONTINUED THAT HE HAD

NOT DISCUSSED THE CASE WITH THE ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT.

SWEDEN HAD BEEN VERY CRITICAL OF PRESIDENT MUGABE\'S USE

OF VIOLENCE AND INTIMIDATION IN THE RECENTLY CONCLUDED

PARLIAMENTARY ELECTION CAMPAIGN, A REASON THE SWEDISH

AMBASSADOR SPECULATED THE FORMER WOULD NOT WANT SWEDEN

TO BE PART OF THE SOLUTION. CANADIAN DEPUTY HIGH

COMMISSIONER RON WILLSON TOLD DCM ON JUNE 29 THAT CANADA

STANDS READY TO RECEIVE THE CUBANS, BUT REMARKED THAT

HIS GOVERNMENT HAD NOT BEEN APPROACHED SINCE INITIAL

DISCUSSIONS BY UNHCR IN THIS CONNECTION SOME THREE WEEKS
