SUBJECT: AMERICAN FARMER IN ZIMBABWE PROVIDES GLOOMY

SNAPSHOT OF COUNTRY\'S COMMERCIAL AGRICULTURE

REF: HARARE 3401

CLASSIFIED BY POLOFF TODD C. FAULK FOR REASONS: 1.5 (B)

AND (D).

1. (C) ON NOVEMBER 15, THE EMBASSY RECEIVED AN URGENT

MESSAGE FROM AMERICAN FARMER MITZI CARRUTHERS, WHO, WITH

HER ZIMBABWEAN HUSBAND BRUCE, WORKS A SIZABLE FARM 20 KM

OUTSIDE OF BINDURA TOWN. THEY DO NOT OWN THE PROPERTY,

BUT LEASE IT FROM A ZIMBABWEAN WHO HAS SINCE RELOCATED

TO AUSTRALIA. THE FARM HAD BEEN VISITED THAT DAY BY A

TECHNICAL TEAM FROM THE AGRITEX PARASTATAL BELONGING TO

THE MINISTRY OF LANDS AND AGRICULTURE. AS NEITHER SHE

NOR HER HUSBAND WERE AT HOME, THE FARM MANAGER CONVINCED

THE TEAM TO RETURN THE NEXT DAY. FEARING THEY WERE

ABOUT TO BE SERVED A SECTION 8 EVICTION NOTICE, MRS.

CARRUTHERS, AN EMBASSY WARDEN, CONTACTED THE EMBASSY

(SEE REFTEL FOR DETAILS ON SECTION 8 OF THE LAND

ACQUISITION ACT).

2. (C) ON THE MORNING OF NOVEMBER 16, POLOFF JOINED

MRS. CARRUTHERS AT HER FARM TO AWAIT THE RETURN OF THE

AGRITEX GROUP AND OBSERVE THE PROCEEDINGS. THE GROUP,

COMPRISING TWO YOUNG WOMEN AND A MAN, SHOWED UP MORE

THAN AN HOUR LATE AND EXPLAINED THEY WERE JUST A

TECHNICAL TEAM THERE TO TAKE SOIL SAMPLES, MARK THE

PROPERTY BOUNDARY, AND ASSESS THE EXTENT OF THE FARM\'S

INFRASTRUCTURE. (NOTE: THE FARM OWNERS SOME TIME AGO

HAD BEEN SERVED A SECTION 5 NOTICE, WHICH NOTIFIES THE

OWNERS OF THE GOVERNMENT\'S INTENTION TO ACQUIRE THE

PROPERTY. SURVEYING THE PROPERTY IS PERMISSIBLE UNDER

SECTION 5 OF THE LAND ACQUISITION ACT. END NOTE.) THE

AGRITEX PEOPLE WERE LOW-KEY AND NON-CONFRONTATIONAL, AND

HAD ALL THE APPEARANCE AND DEMEANOR OF SUMMER INTERNS.

WHEN ASKED IF A SECTION 8 EVICTION NOTICE WAS SOON TO

FOLLOW, THE TEAM LEADER RESPONDED THAT A DIFFERENT

MINISTRY DEPARTMENT WAS RESPONSIBLE FOR THAT AND SHE DID

NOT KNOW WHAT THE DEPARTMENT INTENDED TO DO. THE TEAM

CARRIED OUT ITS ONE-HOUR SURVEY WITHOUT INCIDENT.

3. (C) MRS. CARRUTHERS TOLD POLOFF THAT THIS WAS THE

FIRST TIME HER FARM HAD BEEN VISITED BY THE LANDS

MINISTRY. SHE AND HER HUSBAND HAVE BEEN VERY LUCKY AND

HAVE NOT HAD SQUATTERS OR WAR VETERANS ON THE FARM SINCE

THE TROUBLE BEGAN LAST YEAR. SHE NOTED, HOWEVER, THAT A

FEW MONTHS AGO THE MAYOR OF BINDURA APPEARED ON THE FARM

IN AN APPARENT SCOUTING MISSION FOR A PROPERTY THAT WAS

CLOSER TO TOWN. THE MAYOR\'S FARM NEAR SHAMVA WAS TOO

FAR AWAY, HE REPORTEDLY TOLD THE FARM MANAGER. MRS.

CARRUTHERS REPORTED THAT HER NEIGHBORS HAVE NOT BEEN SO

FORTUNATE. MOST OF THEM HAVE OCCUPIERS WHO HAVE DENUDED

SURROUNDING FORESTS AND BUILT HUTS ON THE PROPERTIES

(POLOFF OBSERVED SUBSTANTIAL EVIDENCE OF THIS ON THE WAY

TO THE CARRUTHERS\' FARM). THE BINDURA POLICE

COMMISSIONER EVEN BUILT A HUGE HOUSE IN THE MIDDLE OF A

NEIGHBOR\'S IRRIGATED FIELD, MRS. CARRUTHERS STATED.

4. (C) ALTHOUGH RELIEVED THAT THEY HAVE NOT BEEN SERVED

A SECTION 8 NOTICE YET, THE CARRUTHERS STATED THEY ARE

WORRIED ABOUT THE FUTURE. THEY ALREADY HAVE ZIM$1.5

MILLION (APPROXIMATELY US$5,000) WORTH OF FERTILIZERS ON

FIELDS THAT ARE INTENDED TO GROW SOYBEANS. THEY HAVE

PURCHASED THE SEED, THEIR WORKERS HAVE PLOWED THE

FIELDS, AND AN EXPENSIVE IRRIGATION SYSTEM IS INSTALLED

AND READY FOR USE, THEY REPORTED. THEY ARE APPREHENSIVE

ABOUT PLANTING -- FEARFUL THAT THEIR CROPS WILL BE

PLOWED OVER BY SQUATTERS OR UNSCRUPULOUS LOCAL OFFICIALS

OR THAT THEY WON\'T EVEN BE AROUND TO HARVEST IN A FEW

MONTHS. ON THE OTHER HAND, IF THEY DON\'T PLANT SOON,

THERE IS NO DOUBT THEY WILL HAVE WASTED MILLIONS OF

ZIMBABWE DOLLARS AND WON\'T HAVE ANY WAY TO RECOVER IT.

IN A FOLLOW-UP CONVERSATION NOVEMBER 19, BRUCE

CARRUTHERS REPORTED THAT THE AGRITEX PEOPLE HAD NOT

RETURNED TO THE FARM AS PROMISED. HE AND HIS WIFE ARE

PLANNING TO JOIN A COLLECTIVE APPEAL BY LOCAL FARMERS TO

THE DISTRICT ADMINISTRATOR AND PROVINCIAL GOVERNOR TO

SAVE THEIR LAND. INDIVIDUAL APPEALS HAVE HAD LITTLE

EFFECT IN THE PAST, HE SAID.

5. (C) COMMENT: IT IS PROBABLY NOT A COINCIDENCE THAT

THE LANDS MINISTRY DECIDED TO SURVEY THE CARRUTHERS FARM

LESS THAN A WEEK AFTER PRESIDENT MUGABE AMENDED THE LAND

ACQUISITION ACT TO PERMIT THE OUTRIGHT NATIONALIZATION

OF ZIMBABWE\'S COMMERCIAL FARMS. THE GOVERNMENT IS

EVIDENTLY REDOUBLING ITS EFFORTS TO SEIZE MOST WHITE-

OWNED PROPERTY BEFORE THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION DUE BY

MARCH 2002. GIVEN THE NEW LEGISLATION AND THE BINDURA
