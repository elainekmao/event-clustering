SUBJECT: THE LIMITS OF VATICAN PERSUASION ON BISHOPS\'

POLITICAL VIEWS

REF: 2001 VATICAN 0196

1. (SBU) SUMMARY: DESPITE THE PERCEPTION OF THE HOLY SEE AS

A HIERARCHICAL ORGANIZATION, IT RARELY GIVES "MARCHING

ORDERS" TO CATHOLIC BISHOPS ON SECULAR POLITICAL MATTERS.

IN THE RARE CASES WHEN IT DOES DECIDE TO PRESS ITS POLITICAL

VIEWS, IT IS LARGELY LIMITED TO THE POWER OF PAPAL

PERSUASION - WHICH IS COMPELLING, BUT NOT ALWAYS ENOUGH TO

CHANGE BISHOPS\' INDIVIDUAL CONVICTIONS. THE POPE\'S VIEWS ON

POLITICAL MATTERS ARE CONVEYED BY THE SECRETARIAT OF STATE

THROUGH THE LOCAL NUNCIO. HOWEVER, THE HOLY SEE GENERALLY

SEEKS TO AVOID PUBLIC DISPUTES, BOTH TO PRESERVE THE UNITY

OF THE CHURCH AND TO PROTECT THE PHYSICAL SECURITY OF ITS

ADHERENTS IN COUNTRIES WHERE CATHOLICS ARE A THREATENED

MINORITY. WHEN LOCAL BISHOPS\' VIEWS RUN COUNTER TO U.S.

INTERESTS, DIRECT CONTACT WITH THE RESIDENT NUNCIO AND THE

BISHOPS THEMSELVES OFFER THE BEST MEANS TO ADDRESS ISSUES.

EMBASSY VATICAN WOULD ALSO BE PREPARED TO RAISE ISSUES OF

PRIORITY INTEREST WITH THE SECRETARIAT OF STATE. END

SUMMARY.

--------------------

BISHOPS AND POLITICS

--------------------

2. (SBU) EMBASSY VATICAN OCCASIONALLY RECEIVES QUERIES FROM

POSTS AS TO WHETHER THE HOLY SEE MIGHT INTERVENE TO CHANGE

THE POLITICAL BEHAVIOR OF A BISHOP. RECENT INQUIRIES, FOR

EXAMPLE, HAVE FOCUSED ON HARD LINE CROAT-NATIONALIST CLERGY

IN CROATIA AND BOSNIA-HERZEGOVINA AND BISHOPS\' LETTERS

SEEMING TO SUPPORT BASQUE SEPARATISM. IN THE PAST, BISHOPS\'

SUPPORT FOR LIBERATION THEOLOGY IN CENTRAL AMERICA OR

REPRESSIVE REGIMES IN THE SOUTHERN CONE OF LATIN AMERICA

HAVE EVOKED CONSIDERABLE PUBLIC DISCUSSION AND CRITICISM.

UNLIKE OTHER GOVERNMENTS WE MIGHT APPROACH TO REIN IN AN

OFFICIAL, THE HOLY SEE IS CONSTRAINED ON POLITICAL ISSUES

(UNLIKE ISSUES OF CHURCH DOCTRINE) BY CHURCH STRUCTURES AND

TRADITIONS FROM COMPELLING ADHERENCE TO VIEWS FAVORED BY THE

CURIA.

------------------------------

THE LIMITS TO PAPAL PERSUASION

------------------------------

3. (U) THE HOLY SEE HAS LIMITED OPTIONS FOR INFLUENCING

LOCAL BISHOPS\' POLITICAL VIEWS. USUALLY IT RELIES ON THE

POWER OF PERSUASION VIA THE LOCAL NUNCIO. A BISHOP IS

BROADLY AUTONOMOUS IN HIS DIOCESE, HOWEVER, AS THE CATHOLIC

CHURCH FOLLOWS THE PRINCIPLE OF SUBSIDIARITY -- THE

REQUIREMENT THAT LOCAL ELEMENTS OF THE CHURCH MANAGE THEIR

OWN AFFAIRS. THE HOLY SEE MAY INVOKE THE FEALTY OF A BISHOP

TO HIS POPE, BUT IT HAS LIMITED DISCIPLINARY RECOURSE IN THE

EVENT THE BISHOP REFUSES TO ALTER OR TONE DOWN HIS VIEWS.

WERE THE POPE TO ATTEMPT TO REPLACE OR RECALL THE BISHOP ON

THE BASIS OF POLITICAL VIEWS AGAINST THE WILL OF HIS FLOCK,

HE WOULD RISK PROVOKING A SCHISM, WITH NO GUARANTEE THAT THE

POPE\'S VIEWS WOULD PREVAIL. THE VATICAN SEEKS TO AVOID SUCH

AN INJURY TO THE "FAMILY" OF THE UNIVERSAL CHURCH AT ALMOST

ANY COST.

4. (SBU) IN RECENT HISTORY, THE POPE HAS EXCOMMUNICATED A

BISHOP ONLY FOR DOCTRINAL DIFFERENCES, NOT POLITICAL ONES.

FOR EXAMPLE, IN 1988 ULTRA-CONSERVATIVE FRENCH PRELATE,

MARCEL LEFEBVRE WAS EXCOMMUNICATED "AUTOMATICALLY" FOR

ORDAINING NEW BISHOPS FOR HIS BREAK-AWAY GROUP OF CATHOLICS

WITHOUT THE REQUIRED PERMISSION OF THE POPE. ANOTHER

EXAMPLE OF CHURCH DISCIPLINE WAS THE BREAKUP OF THE SAO

PAOLO DIOCESE IN 1989 INTO FOUR SEPARATE DIOCESES. THE POPE

AND HIS CURIA UNILATERALLY DECLARED THE BREAKUP AND

INSTALLATION OF THREE ADDITIONAL (AND MORE CONSERVATIVE)

ABILITY IN CHANGING POLITICAL POSITIONS OF ITS BISHOPS. IN

VATICAN\'S BROADER INTEREST IN REGIONAL RECONCILIATION AND

STABILITY. THE NUNCIO IN ZAGREB WAS CHARGED WITH CONVEYING

6. (SBU) COMMENT: WHEN CONFRONTED WITH A BISHOP WHOSE VIEWS

GO AGAINST THE GRAIN OF THE HOLY SEE\'S VIEWS ON SECULAR

POLITICAL ISSUES, THE HOLY SEE WILL ALWAYS ASSESS ITS OWN

BIG PICTURE OF FAITH, MORALS, DOCTRINE AND THE WELL-BEING OF

ITS FAITHFUL IN JUDGING WHETHER AND HOW TO INTERVENE. THE

HOLY SEE WILL ALMOST ALWAYS CHOOSE TO ACT BEHIND THE SCENES,

APPROACHING AN OUTSPOKEN BISHOP THROUGH HIS COLLEAGUES IN

THE LOCAL BISHOPS\' CONFERENCE AND/OR THE LOCAL NUNCIO. IN
