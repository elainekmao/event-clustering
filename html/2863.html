SUBJECT: JAPANESE PRIME MINISTER AFFIRMS COOPERATION IN

-- SECURITY AND COUNTERTERRORISM

1. (SBU) SUMMARY. PRIME MINISTER JUNICHIRO KOIZUMI

AFFIRMED JAPAN\'S SUPPORT FOR BILATERAL COOPERATION IN

SECURITY AS PART OF AN ENHANCED JAPAN-ASEAN RELATIONSHIP AND

OFFERED NON-MILITARY COOPERATION ON COUNTERTERRORISM DURING

AN APRIL 27-28 VISIT TO HANOI. PRIME MINISTER PHAN VAN KHAI

ASKED FOR INCREASED AID AND A BILATERAL TRADE AGREEMENT, BUT

KOIZUMI DID NOT RESPOND POSITIVELY TO EITHER REQUEST. WHILE

NO NEW AGREEMENTS WERE SIGNED, THE TWO COUNTRIES AGREED TO

ENHANCE BILATERAL TIES THROUGH CONTINUED ECONOMIC AND

POLITICAL DIALOGUE AND ACTIVITIES. END SUMMARY.

---------------------------------------

BILATERAL COOPERATION INCLUDES SECURITY

---------------------------------------

2. (U) IN HANOI, PM KOIZUMI PAID COURTESY CALLS ON

PRESIDENT TRAN DUC LUONG AND GENERAL SECRETARY OF THE

COMMUNIST PARTY OF VIETNAM NONG DUC MANH, AS WELL AS HELD A

MORE SUBSTANTIVE MEETING WITH HIS COUNTERPART, PM KHAI.

ACCORDING TO JAPANESE EMBOFFS, THE TWO PRIME MINISTERS

DISCUSSED BILATERAL COOPERATION IN A NUMBER OF AREAS,

LARGELY AS A BILATERAL APPLICATION OF AND FOLLOW-UP TO PM

KOIZUMI\'S FIVE-POINT INITIATIVE FOR GREATER JAPAN-ASEAN

COOPERATION FIRST ANNOUNCED LAST JANUARY IN SINGAPORE. THE

ELEMENTS OF THIS COOPERATION INCLUDE (1) A COMPREHENSIVE

ECONOMIC PARTNERSHIP; (2) DESIGNATION OF THE YEAR 2003 AS

THE YEAR OF JAPAN-ASEAN EXCHANGES, ESPECIALLY IN LIGHT OF

THE 30TH ANNIVERSARY OF JAPAN-VIETNAM RELATIONS; (3) THE

INITIATIVE TO DEVELOP EAST ASIA (IDEA); (4) COOPERATION IN

EDUCATION AND HUMAN RESOURCES DEVELOPMENT; AND (5)

COOPERATION ON TRANSNATIONAL ISSUES SUCH AS SECURITY,

PIRACY, DISEASE, ENVIRONMENT, NARCOTICS, AND HUMAN

TRAFFICKING. KOIZUMI PLEDGED TO HOST RELEVANT MEETINGS AND

WORKSHOPS IN THESE AREAS, EMBOFFS SAID.

3. (SBU) PM KOIZUMI ALSO TOLD PM KHAI THAT JAPAN COULD NOT

PROVIDE MILITARY COOPERATION AGAINST TERRORISM DUE TO

CONSTITUTIONAL CONSTRAINTS, BUT COULD HELP WITH BUDGET

ASSISTANCE AND INFORMATION SHARING, AREAS JAPAN ALSO

"CONSIDERS IMPORTANT." PM KOIZUMI EXPRESSED APPRECIATION

FOR AND "ENCOURAGED" VIETNAM\'S CONTINUED PARTICIPATION IN

AFGHAN RECONSTRUCTION, NOTING ESPECIALLY VIETNAM\'S

ATTENDANCE AT THE TOKYO PLEDGING CONFERENCE WHERE VIETNAM

PROMISED USD 300,000 OF MATERIEL ASSISTANCE. PM KOIZUMI

ALSO THANKED VIETNAM FOR ALLOWING THE JAPAN DEFENSE FORCES

TO FLY OVER VIETNAM EN ROUTE TO PAKISTAN.

4. (SBU) THE TWO SIDES AGREED TO MAINTAIN AND POSSIBLY

"STRENGTHEN" A BILATERAL POLITICAL DIALOGUE. AFTER A HIATUS

OF SEVERAL YEARS, BILATERAL DISCUSSIONS AT THE VICE FOREIGN

MINISTERIAL LEVEL TOOK PLACE IN APRIL 2002 IN HANOI.

ACCORDING TO EMBOFFS, BOTH SIDES ARE NOW DETERMINED TO

5. (U) ASIDE FROM SECURITY AND COUNTERTERRORISM, PM

KOIZUMI\'S MEETINGS WITH THE GVN LEADERSHIP ALSO TOUCHED ON

ECONOMIC AND ODA ISSUES. ACCOUNTS FROM EMBOFFS AND PRESS

REPORTS INDICATED THAT THE GVN LEADERSHIP IS "VERY KEEN" TO

CONCLUDE A BILATERAL TRADE AGREEMENT, WHILE JAPAN IS NOT

PARTICULARLY INTERESTED BECAUSE JAPAN ALREADY GRANTS VIETNAM

MOST FAVORED NATION TREATMENT. FURTHER, THE GOJ IS

CONCERNED THAT VIETNAM IS MOSTLY INTERESTED IN EXPORTING

MORE AGRICULTURAL PRODUCTS TO JAPAN, A SENSITIVE DOMESTIC

POLITICAL ISSUE, ACCORDING TO EMBOFF. PM KOIZUMI REPORTEDLY

AGREED WITH PM KHAI TO PUSH TO CONCLUDE A BILATERAL

INVESTMENT PROTECTION AGREEMENT, HOPEFULLY "BY THE END OF

THE YEAR." (NOTE: THERE HAD BEEN RUMORS THIS AGREEMENT

MIGHT BE A DELIVERABLE FOR THIS VISIT. END NOTE)
