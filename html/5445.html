1.5 (D) AND (E).

1. (U) PA ITALY HAS FOCUSED CONSIDERABLE RESOURCES ON

BIOTECH ISSUES OVER THE PAST YEAR, SPONSORING SPEAKER

PROGRAMS, DVCS, ROUNDTABLES, SEMINARS, INTERNATIONAL AND

VOLUNTARY VISITOR PROJECTS, PRESS INTERVIEWS, OP-ED PIECES,

AND OTHER PUBLIC DIPLOMACY ACTIVITIES DESIGNED TO PROMOTE

U.S. BIOTECH INTERESTS IN ITALY. THIS IS ONE OF OUR HIGHEST

PRIORITIES, AND WE HAVE BEEN SUCCESSFUL IN TRANSMITTING OUR

MESSAGE TO ITALIAN POLICY MAKERS AND OPINION LEADERS.

2. (C) WE BELIEVE, HOWEVER, THAT GIVEN THE CURRENT CRISIS IN

IRAQ, IT WILL BE UNTIMELY TO MOUNT A HIGH PROFILE PUBLIC

DIPLOMACY CAMPAIGN IN ITALY TO PROMOTE A WTO CASE AGAINST THE

EUROPEAN UNION OVER ITS MORATORIUM ON APPROVALS OF BIOTECH

FOOD PRODUCTS. THE BULK OF OUR PUBLIC DIPLOMACY EFFORT IN

THE WEEKS AND MONTHS TO COME MUST FOCUS ON PROMOTING U.S.

POLICY IN IRAQ AND GAINING ITALIAN PUBLIC SUPPORT FOR IT. WE

ARE CONCERNED THAT IF WE ARE ENGAGED IN THIS EFFORT AT THE

SAME TIME THAT WE ARE PUBLICLY PROMOTING LEGAL ACTION AGAINST

THE EUROPEAN UNION OVER BIOTECH FOOD PRODUCTS (A VERY

SENSITIVE ISSUE IN ITALY), WE COULD JEOPARDIZE OUR ABILITY TO

INCREASE ITALIAN PUBLIC SUPPORT FOR OUR ACTIONS IN IRAQ.

WHILE U.S. IRAQ POLICY AND U.S. BIOTECH FOOD POLICY ARE NOT

LOGICALLY RELATED, OUR EXPERIENCE IS THAT THE ITALIAN PRESS

AND PUBLIC WILL MAKE A CONNECTION THAT COULD BE TO THE

DETRIMENT OF OUR EFFORTS AT THIS CRITICAL TIME. WE SHOULD

SEEK TO SLAY ONLY ONE DRAGON AT A TIME.

3. (C) WE SHOULD THEREFORE PROCEED INCREMENTALLY IN OUR

PUBLIC DIPLOMACY CAMPAIGN TO SUPPORT THE USG CASE IN THE WTO

AGAINST THE EUROPEAN UNION OVER THE MORATORIUM; WE SHOULD

TARGET SPECIFIC POLICY MAKERS AND OPINION LEADERS FIRST,

RATHER THAN THE GENERAL PUBLIC. THE MOST EFFECTIVE PUBLIC

DIPLOMACY CAMPAIGN FOR SUCH A TARGETED AUDIENCE WOULD FOCUS

AS CLOSELY AS POSSIBLE ON THE LEGAL BASIS OF THE USG CASE,

I.E., ON THE ILLEGAL NATURE OF THE MORATORIUM. THUS THE

FIRST TWO OF THE SEVEN BROAD MESSAGES OUTLINED IN REFTEL

WOULD BE THE MOST USEFUL IN ITALY IN THE NEAR TERM: THAT THE

MORATORIUM ON APPROVALS OF BIOTECH FOOD PRODUCTS VIOLATES

COMMITMENTS MADE BY THE EU IN THE WTO; AND THAT THE U.S. IS

SIMPLY SEEKING SCIENTIFIC, RULES-BASED REVIEW OF

APPLICATIONS, NOT FORCING ACCEPTANCE OF BIOTECH FOODS ON

EUROPEAN CONSUMERS. WE KNOW ITALIAN POLICY MAKERS AND

OPINION LEADERS ("VALIDATORS") WHO WOULD SUPPORT US IN THESE

ARGUMENTS.

4. (C) THE REMAINING FIVE BROAD MESSAGES (PARAGRAPH 3C-G IN

REFTEL), FOCUSING ON WHAT SOME ITALIAN OPINION LEADERS REGARD

AS SUBSIDIARY ISSUES, WOULD BE MUCH LESS EFFECTIVE WITH THAT

AUDIENCE. WE WOULD HAVE DIFFICULTY IDENTIFYING INFLUENTIAL

ITALIAN "VALIDATORS" TO SUPPORT THEM AND WE ARE THEREFORE

INCLINED TO USE THEM SPARINGLY IN OUR TARGETED PUBLIC

DIPLOMACY CAMPAIGN. WHEN THE HEAT IS OFF THE IRAQ CRISIS AND

WE CAN USEFULLY RESUME A BROADER-BASED PUBLIC DIPLOMACY

CAMPAIGN, ISSUE C (FAMINE IN AFRICA) HAS PARTICULAR RESONANCE

WITH ITALIAN POLITICAL LEADERS AND THE PUBLIC. PM BERLUSCONI

HAS EXPRESSED PERSONAL INTEREST IN THIS THEME TO OUR

AMBASSADORS TO ITALY, THE VATICAN, AND THE FAO.

5. (U) WE ARE CURRENTLY IDENTIFYING ITALIAN PARLIAMENTARIANS

FOR A BIOTECH VOLUNTARY VISITOR PROGRAM AND WOULD WELCOME

FUNDING FROM THE DEPARTMENT OF APPROXIMATELY $25,000 TO ALLOW

TEN PARLIAMENTARIANS TO PARTICIPATE IN THIS PROGRAM. WE WILL

ALSO ORGANIZE WITHIN TWO MONTHS U.S. SPEAKER PROGRAMS IN

MILAN, NAPLES, AND ROME IN SUPPORT OF U.S. BIOTECH POLICY.

WE WOULD EAGERLY ACCEPT DVCS WITH HIGH-LEVEL USG OFFICIALS

(SUCH AS USTR ZOELLICK AND U/S LARSON) AS SOON AS A DECISION

IS MADE TO PROCEED WITH THE WTO CASE.
