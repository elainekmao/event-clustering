SUBJECT: ABUJA II: COMMONWEALTH COMMITTEE MEETING

NEITHER BREAKS DOWN NOR BREAKS THROUGH

REF: ABUJA 2253

CLASSIFIED BY DCM REWHITEHEAD DUE TO 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) SUMMARY. THE OCTOBER 25 TO 27 VISIT BY

REPRESENTATIVES OF SEVEN COMMONWEALTH (CW) COUNTRIES AND

THE CW SECRETARY-GENERAL REVIEWED PROGRESS ON

COMMITMENTS AGREED TO BY THE GOZ ON LAND REFORM, RULE OF

LAW, AND ATTENDANT ISSUES AS OUTLINED IN THE ABUJA

AGREEMENT OF SEPTEMBER 6 (REFTEL). OUR GLEANINGS FROM

DIPLOMATIC CONTACTS, AND SPECIFICALLY HARARE BASED CW

DIPLOMATS WHO WERE INVOLVED IN THE VISIT, REVEAL GENERAL

SKEPTICISM AMONG THE CW DELEGATION THAT THE GOZ HAS

HONORED ITS ABUJA COMMITMENTS. GOZ ATTEMPTS TO STAGE-

MANGE THE VISIT DID NOT SUCCEED, AND THE CW COMMITTEE

HEARD THE VIEWS OF A BROAD RANGE OF ZIMBABWEANS. THE

VISIT HAD SEVERAL CONTENTIOUS MOMENTS, THE WORST DURING

DIFFICULT NEGOTIATIONS OVER THE FINAL COMMUNIQUE. THE

COMPROMISE RESULTED IN A DOCUMENT THAT PLEASED NO ONE

BUT ALLOWED BOTH SIDES TO CLAIM SOME DEGREE OF SUCCESS

AND THUS KEEP THE ABUJA PROCESS ALIVE.

2. (C) POSITIVE OUTCOMES FOR THE CW INCLUDE CONTINUING

THE DIALOGUE WITH THE GOZ AND VISIBLE ENGAGEMENT OF ITS

OPPONENTS, RULE OF LAW ISSUES STILL ON THE AGENDA, AND

AGREEMENT TO AN IMPENDING UNDP ASSESSMENT OF LAND REFORM

AS A MEANS OF MONITORING ABUJA COMPLIANCE. FOR ITS

PART, THE GOZ AVOIDED FURTHER ISOLATION FROM THE

INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY, RESISTED EFFORTS TO PIN IT DOWN

ON POLITICAL/ELECTORAL ISSUES, AND MAINTAINED ITS

DUBIOUS INTERPRETATION THAT THE GOZ OWNS LAND ONCE THE

LAND IS LISTED AND THEREFORE IS OPERATING WITHIN THE

RULE OF LAW. FROM THE U.S. PERSPECTIVE, THE THREE-DAY

WALTZ AROUND ABUJA FELL SHORT IN A NUMBER OF AREAS,

INCLUDING THE CRUCIAL ISSUE OF ELECTION OBSERVATION. WE

RECOMMEND THAT THE DEPARTMENT FOLLOW UP WITH NIGERIAN

PRESIDENT OBASANJO, THE GODFATHER OF THE ABUJA

AGREEMENT, IN WASHINGTON THIS WEEK ABOUT OUR CONCERNS

THAT ILLEGAL LAND INVASIONS, VIOLENCE AND HUMAN RIGHTS

VIOLATIONS, AND A VIABLE ELECTORAL ENVIRONMENT HAVE NOT

BEEN SUCCESSFULLY ADDRESSED. END SUMMARY.

3. (C) CANADIAN DEPUTY HIGH COMMISSIONER RON WILLSON

AND UK COUNTERPART DIANNE CORNER DEBRIEFED G-8 DEPUTIES

OCTOBER 29 ON THE ABUJA II MEETING IN HARARE. THEY

DESCRIBED A DISORGANIZED AND SOMETIMES CONTENTIOUS

VISIT. WILLSON, WHO ADMITTED THAT CANADIAN DEPUTY

MINISTER KILGOUR HAD TAKEN THE HARDEST LINE WITH GOZ,

TERMED THE MEETING "NOT A RESOUNDING SUCCESS" WITH

"MINIMAL RESULTS," A CHARACTERIZATION THAT CORNER

SECONDED. WILLSON SAID THAT THE VISIT ALMOST FOUNDERED

IN ITS FINAL STAGES WHEN THERE WAS A NEAR BREAKDOWN ON

THE NEGOTIATED COMMUNIQUE OVER SEVERAL ISSUES. AS A

RESULT OF THIS THE CW DID NOT GET THE FOLLOWING:

-- A MENTION IN THE COMMUNIQUE OF ELECTIONS OBSERVERS,

7. (C) CORNER AND WILLSON OBSERVED THAT THE GOVERNMENT

SOUGHT TO MANIPULATE THE PROGRAM BY MONOPOLIZING THE

GROUP\'S TIME AND THROUGH PURPOSEFUL DISORGANIZATION OF

THE ENTIRE EVENT. THE MFA DID NOT PRESENT THE CW

EMBASSIES INVOLVED A PROPOSED SCHEDULE OF MEETINGS UNTIL

WEDNESDAY EVENING, JUST BEFORE INITIAL CALLS ON GOZ

OFFICIALS WERE TO BEGIN. ONLY FIVE OF THE MORE THAN A

DOZEN NON-GOVERNMENTAL GROUPS REQUESTED BY THE CW

FIGURED ON THE LIST, LEADING TO A FLURRY OF LAST-MINUTE

NEGOTIATIONS AND CAPITULATION OF THE GOZ TO MEETING WITH

CIVIL SOCIETY AND VISITS TO A CFU-CHOSEN FARM. IN THE

GOZ REPRESENTATIVES. CORNER SAID THAT FOREIGN MINISTER

8. (C) WILLSON DESCRIBED A SECOND GOZ COMPROMISE ON

SITE VISITS TO FARMS OUTSIDE OF HARARE. THE INITIAL GOZ

PLAN TO HELICOPTER ONLY THE HEADS OF VISITING

DELEGATIONS TO FARMS OF THE GOZ\'S CHOICE WAS ALTERED TO

ALLOW A VISIT BY HEADS OF DELEGATION TO ONE FARM OF GOZ

CHOICE AND A SECOND CHOSEN BY THE CW COMMITTEE ITSELF.

WILLSON SAID THAT THE VISIT TO THE FIRST FARM IN

MASVINGO WAS PROBABLY INTENDED BY THE GOZ TO SHOWCASE

PEACEFUL CO-EXISTENCE BETWEEN COMMERCIAL FARMERS AND

THOSE RESETTLED FROM COMMUNAL LANDS. THE FARMER IN

QUESTION, DESCRIBED BY HIS NEIGHBORS AS A GENTLE

INDIVIDUAL OVERWHELMED BY THE OCCUPATION AND DESTRUCTION

OF HIS FARM, HAD REPORTEDLY BEEN WARNED IN ADVANCE BY

THE GOZ TO TAKE A SOFT LINE. HE DID, BUT THEN ASKED TO

YIELD THE FLOOR TO A NEIGHBOR CHOSEN AS SPOKESMAN BY THE

LOCAL COMMERCIAL FARMER COMMUNITY. MUDENGE AND LANDS

MINISTER MADE OBJECTED STRONGLY TO THIS, LEADING TO AN

EXCHANGE WITH CANADIAN DEPUTY MINISTER KILGOUR AND

PLACING NIGERIAN FOREIGN MINISTER LAMIDO, CHAIRMAN OF

THE CW COMMITTEE, IN AN AWKWARD SPOT.

9. (C) IF POSSIBLE, WILLSON RELATED, THE SECOND FARM

ISIT WAS EVEN WORSE FROM THE GOZ PERSPECTIVE. WHEN THE

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 03240 03 OF 04 291644Z

CW HEADS OF DELEGATION FINALLY HELICOPTERED IN, OTHER

MEMBERS OF THE DELEGATIONS HAD ALREADY BEEN THERE FOR

TWO HOURS. THEY HAD ALREADY TOURED THE BURNT-OUT HOUSES

OF FARM WORKERS, VISITED THE VANDALIZED HOME OF A FARM

FAMILY NOW LIVING IN HARARE, AND HEARD THE RESETTLED

FARMER OCCUPIERS ADMIT THAT TWO OF THEIR NUMBERS

REPORTEDLY KILLED DURING THE TAKEOVER HAD ACTUALLY

PERISHED WHEN THEY ROLLED OVER A PURLOINED TRACTOR. UK

JUNIOR MINISTER BARONESS AMOS SUMMED UP THE VISIT BY

STATING THAT SHE FOUND IT STRANGE THAT 82 SETTLERS HAD

DISPLACED 265 FARM WORKERS.

10. (C) WILLSON SAID THAT THE FARM VISITS HAD REVEALED

THE STARTLING FACT THAT THE GOZ BELIEVES THAT FARMS

GAZETTED UNDER SECTION 5 OF THE FAST TRACK RESETTLEMENT

PLAN COME UNDER IMMEDIATE GOZ OWNERSHIP. IN RESPONSE,

THE CANADIAN HIGH COMMISSIONER READ DIRECTLY FROM A GOZ

POLICY DOCUMENT DESCRIBING THE LAND REFORM PLAN THAT

STATES: "RESETTLEMENT CANNOT TAKE PLACE AFTER A SECTION

5 ORDER." MUDENGE AND MADE INSISTED THAT THIS LANGUAGE

IS MINISTRY OF INFORMATION PHRASEOLOGY AND HAS NO LEGAL

STANDING.

11. (C) CORNER DISCOUNTED PRESS REPORTS IN THE GOZ

MEDIA THAT THE CW DELEGATION HAD SPLIT ALONG

AFRICAN/NON-AFRICAN LINES. SHE OBSERVED THAT SOME OF

THE GOZ-FRONT CIVIL SOCIETY GROUPS HAD MADE "LOOPY"

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 04 HARARE 03240 03 OF 04 291644Z

STATEMENTS ABOUT REDRESSING HISTORICAL INEQUITIES THAT

SEEMED TO STRIKE A RESONANT NOTE WITH THE AFRICAN

DELEGATIONS. DESPITE THIS, NEGOTIATIONS OVER THE

COMMUNIQUE HAD REVEALED A GENERALLY UNITED FRONT, WITH

THE CW DELEGATES PUSHING THE ZIMBABWEANS ON ELECTION

OBSERVATION, LAWFUL LAND REFORM AND SIMILAR POINTS.

WILLSON OBSERVED THAT THE NIGERIANS, WHO HAD THE MOST

FACE TO LOSE IF THE VISIT FAILED, HAD SUBMITTED AN

INITIAL COMMUNIQUE THAT WAS TOO SOFT AND SEEMED TO

RATCHET BACK REQUIRED GOZ ACTIONS AS AGREED TO AT THE

ABUJA MEETING. HE POINTED OUT THAT THE FINAL COMMUNIQUE

WAS SOMEWHAT STRONGER, BUT NOT TO THE POINT THAT THE GOZ

HAD REFUSED TO ACCEPT THE TEXT. THE CANADIAN AND

BRITISH HIGH COMMISSIONERS WERE SATISFIED THAT THE

COMMUNIQUE HAD HIGHLIGHTED THE CRITICAL DIFFERENCES OVER

WHAT IS RULE OF LAW WITH RESPECT TO LAND OCCUPATION

ISSUES, ALTHOUGH NO RESOLUTION OF DIFFERENCES WAS

REACHED.

IMPLEMENTED. GIVEN THE GOZ RECORD AT BACKTRACKING AND

POLITICAL WILL TO COOPERATE WITH THE UNDP\'S EFFORT TO

MONITOR THE PROVISIONS OF THE AGREEMENT. AT THIS POINT,

LEVERAGE OVER MUGABE AND THE GOZ. WE THUS RECOMMEND

THAT THE DEPARTMENT TAKE ADVANTAGE OF HIS VISIT TO

WASHINGTON THIS WEEK TO STRESS THAT THE PROBLEMS OF

UNLAWFUL LAND SEIZURE, CONTINUED LAND AND PRE-ELECTION

VIOLENCE, AND STONEWALLING ON INTERNATIONAL ELECTION

OBSERVERS MUST BE DEALT WITH CONCRETELY IF WE ARE TO

MITIGATE THE GROWING CRISIS IN ZIMBABWE.
