SUBJECT: FOLLOW UP ON RJ FLIGHTS TO IRAQ: FONMIN TALKS TO

RJ PRESIDENT, MFA SAYS U.S. MESSAGE LOUD AND CLEAR

REF: A. STATE 112154

B. AMMAN 3130

C. USUN 1531

Classified By: PolCouns Doug Silliman for reasons 1.5 (B) and (D)

1. (C) Following up on our June 11 demarche on the

frequency and regularity of Royal Jordanian (RJ) flights to

Iraq (ref b), PolCouns met June 20 with Ali al-Ayed, Personal

Assistant to FonMin Muasher, and Samer Naber, MFA Legal

Advisor. Ayed said that, since the June 11 demarche, the

Jordanian Embassy in Baghdad had issued a circular diplomatic

note retracting its earlier note (which had advertised RJ

flights to Amman and offered diplomatic discounts). He said

the issue of RJ flights to Iraq was being discussed "at a

very high level," noting that FonMin Muasher had spoken to RJ

President Samer Majali about it earlier this week. Ayed said

the GOJ had heard the U.S. message "loud and clear" and had

no intention of violating the UN sanctions regime on Iraq.

2. (C) PolCouns reminded Ayed and Naber that it was not

only the advertising of the flights, but their regularity and

frequency, that had raised concerns in Washington. He hoped

that the GOJ would take these USG concerns into consideration

in their internal deliberations.
