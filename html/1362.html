SUBJECT: WAR VETERANS INVADE SUPREME COURT

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 06578 241456Z

CLASSIFIED BY DCM EARL M. IRVING FOR REASONS 1.5 (B) AND

(D).

1. (C) SUPREME COURT CHIEF JUSTICE ANTHONY GUBBAY TOLD

USAID HARARE DIRECTOR DEPP THAT AT 11:00 AM NOVEMBER 24

SEVERAL MEMBERS OF THE NATIONAL LIBERATION WAR VETERANS

ASSOCIATION BROKE INTO THE SUPREME COURT\'S HEARING

CHAMBER AND STAGED AN UNRULY DEMONSTRATION AGAINST THE

COURT. THE JUSTICES WERE SCHEDULED TO RENDER A

JUDGMENT, OSTENSIBLY ON THE CONTINUING ILLEGAL LAND

OCCUPATIONS, THAT MORNING. THE VETERANS KNOCKED DOWN A

SECURITY OFFICER AND SENT A WOMAN EMPLOYEE OF THE COURT

FLYING AS THEY FOUGHT TO GAIN ACCESS TO THE CHAMBER. WE

LEARNED FROM MOVEMENT FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE ADVISOR CLIVE

PUZEY THAT SENIOR COMMERCIAL FARMERS\' UNION OFFICIAL DAVID

HASLUCK WAS FORCED TO FLEE THE SCENE THROUGH A KITCHEN

WINDOW.

2. (C) THE SUPREME COURT JUDGES WERE IN THE ROBING

CHAMBER AT THE TIME THE DISTURBANCE BEGAN. HAD THE

DEMONSTRATORS WAITED A FEW MORE MINUTES THEY WOULD HAVE

FOUND THE JUDGES SEATED AND HEARING ARGUMENTS. IT TOOK

FIFTEEN MINUTES FOR POLICE TO ARRIVE AND DISPERSE THE

DEMONSTRATORS, ACCORDING TO GUBBAY. NONE OF THE JUSTICES

WERE HARMED AND THE SESSION WAS IMMEDIATELY CANCELED. THE

COURT CLOSED ITS DOORS AND A TENSE CALM PREVAILED AROUND

THE COURTHOUSE AT MID-AFTERNOON. CHIEF JUSTICE GUBBAY,

DETERMINED NOT TO YIELD TO INTIMIDATION, APPEARED ON TIME

FOR A 2:00 PM SPEECH OUTSIDE THE COURT, WHICH HE DELIVERED

SEEMINGLY UNRUFFLED.
