SUBJECT: RWANDA AND UGANDA TO BE INVITED TO THE

WINDHOEK SADC SUMMIT

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) ON AUGUST 3 MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS UNDER

SECRETARY FOR AFRICA DAVID HAMADZIRIPI TOLD S/E HOWARD

SIPDIS

WOLPE THAT, IN THE COURSE OF A CONVERSATION BETWEEN

SOUTH AFRICAN PRESIDENT THABO MBEKI AND PRESIDENT MUGABE

THE PREVIOUS DAY, THE IDEA OF INVITING RWANDA AND UGANDA

FOR MEETINGS ON THE MARGINS OF THE SADC SUMMIT IN

WINDHOEK EMERGED. MUGABE REPORTEDLY WAS RECEPTIVE TO

THE INITIATIVE, THE ZIMBABWEAN DIPLOMAT STATED. S/E

WOLPE WELCOMED THE DEVELOPMENT, NOTING THAT IT WOULD BE

A GOOD CONFIDENCE BUILDING STEP.

2. (C) SOUTH AFRICAN DEPUTY HIGH COMMISSIONER VICTOR

MDITSHWA TOLD CHARGE LATER IN THE DAY THAT MBEKI PRESSED

MUGABE TO SPEED UP THE LUSAKA PEACE PROCESS. MDITSHWA

ALSO EMPHASIZED THAT THE CONGO CONFLICT WILL HAVE A

PROMINENT POSITION ON THE SADC SUMMIT AGENDA. IN THIS

CONNECTION, MBEKI TOLD THE ZIMBABWEAN PRESIDENT THAT HE

CONFIDENTIAL

HAD BEEN IN TOUCH WITH RWANDAN PRESIDENT KAGAME AND UGANDAN

PRESIDENT MUSEVENI ON THE SUBJECT OF THEIR ATTENDANCE IN

WINDHOEK FOR THE SUMMIT. THE SOUTH AFRICAN DEPUTY HIGH

COMMISSIONER DID NOT CONFIRM THAT AN INVITATION HAD BEEN

EXTENDED TO THEM, HOWEVER.

3. (U) S/E WOLPE DID NOT CLEAR THIS MESSAGE PRIOR TO

HIS DEPARTURE.
