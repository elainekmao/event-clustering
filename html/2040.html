SUBJECT: IMPACT OF VHRA DEBATE ON PROTESTANTS

1. (U) CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES ROBERT C.

PORTER, REASON: 1.5 (D)

2. (C) SUMMARY. REACTION IN VIETNAM TO THE VIETNAM

HUMAN RIGHTS ACT MAY HAVE CREATED A NEW OBSTACLE FOR

POSSIBLE LEGAL RECOGNITION OF AN UNDERGROUND BAPTIST

GROUP. THE ATMOSPHERE ALSO APPARENTLY REMAINS TOO

"SENSITIVE" EVEN FOR THE OFFICIALLY RECOGNIZED TIN LANH

ASSOCIATION TO AGREE TO MEET WITH EMBOFFS. END

SUMMARY.

3. (C ) IN A MEETING WITH POL/C ON OCTOBER 6, THE

REVEREND NGUYEN NGOC HIEN (STRICTLY PROTECT) OF THE

VIETNAM BAPTIST GENERAL CONFERENCE DESCRIBED HIS

CHURCH\'S EFFORTS TO OBTAIN LEGAL RECOGNITION FROM THE

SRV. REVEREND HIEN, WHO SAID HE HAD IN 1991 LEFT THE

SOUTHERN EVANGELICAL CHURCH IN WHICH HIS FATHER AND TWO

BROTHERS ARE PASTORS, CLAIMED TO HAVE BEEN ORDAINED BY

KOREAN PRESBYTERIANS. HE SAID HE HAD SWITCHED THE

AFFILIATION OF HIS UNDERGROUND CHURCHES TO THE BAPTISTS

IN 1997, FOLLOWING CONTACTS WITH VIETNAMESE-AMERICAN

BAPTISTS. HE CLAIMED TO HAVE BEGUN WITH ONLY 10

FOLLOWERS BUT NOW TO HAVE APPROXIMATELY 8,000 MEMBERS

NATIONWIDE, WITH ABOUT 100 HOUSE CHURCHES IN 25

PROVINCES (14 IN THE NORTH, 4 IN THE CENTRAL REGION,

AND 7 IN THE SOUTH). HE DESCRIBED MOST MEMBERS AS

WORKERS, FARMERS, AND RETIRED PEOPLE; A FEW ARE ACTIVE

OR FORMER PARTY MEMBERS, HE ADDED. TRAINED CLERGY IS A

PROBLEM; HE SAID HIS CHURCH HAS ONLY ABOUT TEN ACTUAL

MINISTERS TO SERVICE THE MANY FOLLOWERS. HIS FOLLOWERS

AND MINISTERS FACE REGULAR HARASSMENT, INCLUDING FINES

AND OCCASION BEATINGS, HE CLAIMED.

4. (C ) ACCORDING TO REVEREND HIEN, HE HELD THREE

FORMAL MEETINGS WITH THE GOVERNMENT COMMITTEE ON

RELIGION SINCE JUNE 2001 IN ORDER TO OBTAIN A LEGAL

STATUS FOR HIS CHURCH. IN THIS PROCESS, HE COMPLETED

AND SUBMITTED NUMEROUS FORMS REGARDING THE HISTORY OF

HIS GROUP, ITS PURPOSE, ACTIVITIES, AND MEMBERS. HE

HAD BEEN PROMISED A RESPONSE BY THE END OF AUGUST BUT

DID NOT RECEIVE ONE, HE SAID.

5. (C ) REVEREND HIEN CLAIMED THAT THE PASSAGE BY THE

HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE VIETNAM HUMAN RIGHTS

ACT (VHRA) HAD DRAMATICALLY CHANGED THE ATMOSPHERE FOR

DISCUSSION. BETWEEN JUNE AND AUGUST, HIS CONTACTS AT

THE GOVERNMENT OFFICE ON RELIGION HAD BEEN POSITIVE AND

"VERY HAPPY," BUT SINCE SEPTEMBER 6 THEIR ATTITUDES

CLEARLY DEMONSTRATED HOW UPSET THEY WERE WITH THE VHRA,

HE STRESSED. HE NOTED THAT HIS CONTACTS APPEARED

MARGINALLY MORE POSITIVE SINCE THE SENATE RATIFICATION

OF THE BILATERAL TRADE AGREEMENT, BUT HE WAS UNWILLING

TO SPECULATE WHAT WOULD HAPPEN TO HIS APPLICATION

SHOULD THE SENATE ALSO PASS THE VHRA IN SOME FORM OR

ANOTHER.

6. (C ) SEPARATELY, THE REVEREND AU QUANG VINH, THE

ACTING CHAIRMAN OF THE HANOI ASSOCIATION OF PROTESTANTS

(TIN LANH), THE GOVERNMENT-RECOGNIZED ORGANIZATION TO

SUPERVISE PROTESTANTS IN THE NORTH, SENT A LETTER TO

POL/C INDIRECTLY BY FOREIGN CONTACTS TO REGRET THAT HE

COULD NOT AGREE TO MEET POL/C OFFICIALLY DUE TO CURRENT

"SENSITIVE TIME IN VIETNAM-US RELATIONS." HE CLAIMED

THAT THE TIN LANH CHURCH AND HE PERSONALLY MIGHT

ENCOUNTER "COMPLICATIONS" WITH THE GOVERNMENT IF HE

AGREED TO MEET NOW. (NOTE: POL/C HAD PREVIOUSLY

WRITTEN TO THE HEADS OF ALL THE MAJOR RELIGIOUS

ORGANIZATIONS IN HANOI REQUESTING COURTESY CALLS.

REVEREND VINH APPARENTLY CHOSE TO REPLY AVOIDING THE

POST OFFICE DUE TO HIS INCLUSION OF COPIES OF SEVERAL

OFFICIAL JUDGMENTS LEVYING FINES AGAINST HIM SEVERAL

TIMES SINCE 1994 FOR HAVING PROVIDED INFORMATION TO

VARIOUS FOREIGNERS. END NOTE)

7. (C ) COMMENT: THE CAUSAL RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN THE

VHRA AND DELAY IN FURTHER DISCUSSIONS OVER THE

BAPTISTS\' STATUS MAY BE LESS GENUINE THAN PERCEIVED BY

REVEREND HIEN. HOWEVER, OVERALL VIETNAMESE SENSITIVITY

TO PERCEIVED INTERFERENCE BY THE US ON HUMAN RIGHTS AND

RELIGIOUS LIBERTIES ISSUES DOES SEEM TO HAVE BEEN

HEIGHTENED BY THE HOUSE VOTE ON VHRA. PASSAGE BY THE

SENATE WOULD FURTHER EXACERBATE THIS PERCEPTION. IF

SO, THERE MAY BE INCREASED OFFICIAL RELUCTANCE TO TAKE

FURTHER POSITIVE STEPS ON HUMAN RIGHTS THAT COULD

INTERPRETED BY DOMESTIC CRITICS AS HAVING BEEN ADOPTED

IN RESPONSE TO US PRESSURE.
