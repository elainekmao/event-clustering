SUBJECT: MEDIA REPORTS RUMORS OF GADHAFI HEART ATTACK

1. ON AUGUST 14 TWO LEADING NEWSPAPERS, "REPUBBLICA"

AND "CORRIERE DELLA SERA", REPORTED RUMORS THAT

MUHAMMAR GADHAFI HAD SUFFERED A RECENT HEART ATTACK.

THE PAPERS, CITING UNNAMED DIPLOMATIC SOURCES AND

BUSINESSMEN, REPORTED THAT A LIBYAN ARMED FORCES

ATTACK AGAINST ISLAMIC MILITANTS AND GADHAFI\'S

DISAPPEARANCE FROM THE LIBYAN PRESS HAD LED TO RUMORS

OF HIS BEING TOO SICK TO EXERCISE HIS FUNCTIONS.

HOWEVER, AN ANONYMOUS OFFICER OF THE LIBYAN SECRET

SERVICES REPORTEDLY CLAIMED THAT GADHAFI WOULD MAKE A

PUBIC APPEARANCE ON THE SEPTEMBER 1 ANNIVERSARY OF

HIS TAKING POWER IN 1969.

2. OFFICIALS AT THE MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS WERE

UNABLE EITHER TO CONFIRM THE PRESS REPORTS OF

GADHAFI\'S HEART ATTACK OR SHED ANY FURTHER LIGHT ON

THEM.
