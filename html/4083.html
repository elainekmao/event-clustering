SUBJECT: DEEPENING HUMANITARIAN CRISIS IN COTE

D\'IVOIRE - UN AND WFP LAUNCH EMERGENCY APPEAL

-------

SUMMARY

-------

1. ON FRIDAY, OCTOBER 25, THE U.N. WORLD FOOD

PROGRAMS PLANS TO LAUNCH A REGIONAL EMERGENCY

OPERATION (EMOP) TO ADDRESS THE CURRENT AND POSSIBLE

FUTURE NEEDS DEVELOPING AS A RESULT OF THE DEEPENING

HUMANITARIAN CRISIS IN COTE D\'IVOIRE. THE EMOP WILL

COVER THE PERIOD NOVEMBER 1, 2002 THROUGH JANUARY 31,

2003, AT A COST OF APPROXIMATELY USD 3 MILLION.

4,145 MT FOOD IS REQUESTED FOR 94,000 BENEFICIARIES.

WELL AS ADDRESS NEW DEVELOPMENTS IN A TIMELY FASHION.

LAUNCH OF NEW REGIONAL EMOP

---------------------------

2. EVEN THOUGH A CEASE-FIRE HAS BEEN SIGNED IN COTE

D\'IVOIRE BETWEEN THE GOVERNMENT AND THE REBELS

(HALTING A MONTH-LONG INSURRECTION) AND A FRAGILE

PEACE IS HOLDING IN THE COUNTRY, THE U.N. IS

LAUNCHING A FLASH APPEAL THIS WEEKEND FOR THE

UNFOLDING CRISIS IN COTE D\'IVOIRE AND SURROUNDING

COUNTRIES. AS PART OF THAT DOCUMENT, WFP IS ISSUING

A SMALL REGIONAL EMOP THAT WILL COVER THE NEXT THREE

MONTHS (NOVEMBER 1 THROUGH JANUARY 31). THE COST IS

APPROXIMATELY USD 3 MILLION AND 4,145 MT FOOD IS

REQUESTED FOR 94,000 BENEFICIARIES.

3. AS PART OF THE WFP EXECUTIVE BOARD (EB) MEETING

THIS PAST WEEK, WFP WEST AFRICA REGIONAL DIRECTOR

MANUEL ARANDA DA SILVA PROVIDED THE EB MEMBERS AN

OVERVIEW OF THE PROBLEMS IN COTE D\'IVOIRE AND

OUTLINED WFP\'S RESPONSE THUS FAR. THIS CABLE

PROVIDES A SUMMARY OF WFP\'S CURRENT WORK, AS WELL AS

NIGER. MOST OF THESE WERE IMMIGRANTS LIVING AND

5. WFP IS QUITE CONCERNED ABOUT THE REGIONAL IMPACT

OF A WIDENING CRISIS IN COTE D\'IVOIRE, ESPECIALLY

GIVEN THE GROWING PROBLEMS OF DROUGHT IN THE SAHEL.

ABIDJAN SERVES AS THE PORT OF ENTRY FOR MANY OF THE

SAHEL COUNTRIES. HIGH PRICES FOR BASIC CEREALS

CAUSED BY THE DROUGHT WILL BE FURTHER EXACERBATED BY

TRANSPORT BLOCKAGES. CONVERSELY, EXPORTS FROM THE

SAHEL THAT TRADITIONALLY TRANSIT THROUGH ABIDJAN HAVE

ALREADY SLOWED OR STOPPED. LASTLY, MANY PEOPLE IN

THE SAHEL COUNTRIES RELY ON REMITTANCES FROM

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ROME 05236 02 OF 04 251424Z

RELATIVES IN COTE D\'IVOIRE. IT IS HIGHLY PROBABLE

THAT REMITTANCE FLOWS WILL SUBSTANTIALLY DIMINISH,

THEREBY ADDING TO THE FINANCIAL DIFFICULTIES OF COTE

D\'IVOIRE\'S NEIGHBORS.

--------------------------------------------- --------

ADDRESSING CURRENT NEEDS AND PREPARING FOR THE WORST

--------------------------------------------- --------

6. WFP\'S REGIONAL EMOP IS BASED ON THE ASSUMPTION

THAT THE CURRENT POLITICAL STALEMATE WILL CONTINUE

WITH THE DE FACTO PARTITION REMAINING (I.E. THE

REBELS NOW CONTROL THE NORTHERN REGION, ROUGHLY HALF

OF THE COUNTRY). IT IS EXPECTED THAT THIS WILL

ENGENDER CONTINUED POPULATION DISPLACEMENT BOTH

INSIDE COTE D\'IVOIRE AND OUTFLOWS TO BURKINA FASO,

MALI, GHANA, AND LIBERIA.

7. ALTHOUGH WFP AND ITS U.N. AGENCY PARTNERS ARE

HOPING FOR A POSITIVE OUTCOME TO THE CRISIS, WFP IS

TAKING A REALISTIC LOOK AT THE POSSIBILITIES FOR

FURTHER DETERIORATION AND THE IMPACT AS PART OF ITS

CONTINGENCY READINESS. EVEN SO, IF THERE ARE SWIFT,

MASSIVE MOVEMENTS ACROSS BORDERS, NO AGENCY WILL BE

PREPARED OVERNIGHT TO RESPOND, BUT VICE SPENDING A

LOT OF MONEY ON ESTABLISHING RESPONSE MECHANISMS THAT

MAY NOT BE NEEDED, WFP IS TAKING THE NECESSARY STEPS

NOW TO REVIEW WHAT WOULD BE REQUIRED TO RESPOND AS

QUICKLY AS POSSIBLE.

CRISIS SUPPORT UNIT

-------------------

8. WFP\'S CURRENT COTE D\'IVOIRE COUNTRY DIRECTOR

LEAVES ABIDJAN SUNDAY, OCTOBER 27, DUE TO UNSECORD\'S

DECISION TO PASS ABIDJAN TO PHASE III SECURITY WHICH

ALLOWS NO DEPENDENTS. TO ENSURE AN EFFECTIVE AND

EFFICIENT IMPLEMENTATION OF WFP\'S REGIONAL EMOP, WFP

IS ESTABLISHING A CRISIS SUPPORT UNIT IN ABIDJAN.

BECAUSE THE MAJORITY OF THE COUNTRIES SURROUNDING

COTE D\'IVOIRE ARE COUNTRIES WITH DEVELOPMENT PROJECTS

RATHER THAN EMERGENCIES, NO STRUCTURE NOR TRADITION

OF EMERGENCY OPERATIONS EXISTS. THE CRISIS SUPPORT

UNIT WILL THUS SERVE TO PROVIDE THIS EXPERTISE.

9. THE UNIT WILL OVERSEE COORDINATION AND MANAGEMENT

OF WFP\'S RESPONSE OPERATIONS. WFP IS TEMPORARILY

TRANSFERRING ITS GUINEA COUNTRY DIRECTOR, GEMMO

LODESANI, A VERY EXPERIENCED EMERGENCY WFP MANAGER,

WFP\'S ACTION THUS FAR

---------------------

11. IN ITS INITIAL RESPONSE, WFP HAS BORROWED FOOD

FROM ITS DEVELOPMENT STOCKS, AND THE JAPANESE

GOVERNMENT HAS AGREED TO CONTRIBUTE AN INITIAL 79 MT

RICE TO THE EMERGENCY OPERATION FROM ITS BILATERAL

STOCKS. A TOTAL OF ABOUT 13,250 PEOPLE INSIDE COTE

D\'IVOIRE ARE CURRENTLY RECEIVING EMERGENCY ASSISTANCE

FROM WFP. THE NUMBERS BREAK DOWN AS FOLLOWS:

12. IN BOUAKE, 3,000 VULNERABLE PEOPLE ARE CURRENTLY

BEING SUPPORTED THROUGH HEALTH AND SOCIAL STRUCTURES

WITH 100 MT RICE VIA THE INTERNATIONAL COMMITTEE OF

THE RED CROSS (ICRC). COMPLEMENTARY RATIONS, SUCH AS

FISH, OIL AND SALT, ARE BEING PREPARED FOR TRANSFER.

13. AMONG THE TENS OF THOUSANDS OF CIVILIANS WHO

HAVE FLED BOUAKE, ABOUT 2,000 ARE TEMPORARILY SETTLED

IN TRANSIT SITES IN YAMOUSSOUKRO, DIDIEVI AND

SAKASSOU, WHILE WAITING FOR ONWARD TRANSPORT. WFP IS

PROVIDING THESE POPULATIONS, VIA THE CATHOLIC CHURCH

AND THE NON-GOVERNMENTAL ORGANIZATION (NGO) CROIX DE

LA PAIX INTERNATIONAL, WITH HIGH-ENERGY BISCUITS

(HEBS) AND 15 MT RICE FOR TWO WEEKS.

14. A TOTAL OF 2,700 IDPS IN THE LOCALITIES OF TIE

N\'DIEKO, MOLONOU, AND GARE KAN, NORTH OF BOUAKE, WILL

BE PROVIDED WITH WFP FOOD ASSISTANCE VIA CARE. AN

INITIAL DISTRIBUTION OF 2 MT OF HEBS HAS ALREADY

TYPES OF INTERVENTIONS

----------------------

17. THE PRIMARY EMERGENCY INTERVENTION WILL BE TO

PROVIDE A GENERAL RATION TO ALL THOSE AFFECTED BY

FOOD INSECURITY DUE TO DISPLACEMENT. A RATION OF

2,100 KILO-CALORIES (KC) PER DAY WILL BE GIVEN TO

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ROME 05236 04 OF 04 251424Z

THOSE BENEFICIARIES ENTIRELY RELIANT ON FOOD

ASSISTANCE AND WHO DO NOT HAVE ANY OTHER MEANS OF

COPING.

18. A RATION OF 1,850 KC/DAY WILL BE PROVIDED TO

THOSE WHO HAVE LIMITED MEANS OF COPING WITH FOOD

INSECURITY (E.G. THOSE WHO ARE RETURNING TO THEIR

COUNTRY OF ORIGIN) FOR A PERIOD OF TWO MONTHS. THESE

BENEFICIARIES WILL GO THROUGH TRANSIT CENTERS WHERE

WFP PLANS TO GIVE THEM HEBS. THE EMOP PROJECTS

160,000 RATIONS OF HEBS MAY BE NEEDED IN THE TRANSIT

CENTERS. EACH BENEFICIARY WOULD RECEIVE 500 GRAMS

(2,250 KC/DAY) OF HEBS WHILE IN THE TRANSIT CENTER.

19. THE EMOP ALSO PLANS FOR SPECIAL NUTRITIONAL

SUPPLEMENTARY AND THERAPEUTIC FEEDING INTERVENTIONS.

RESPONSE ACCOUNT (IRA) DEMONSTRATES WFP\'S COMMITMENT

NOT TO BE "OVERTAKEN BY EVENTS" IN THIS LINCHPIN WEST

AFRICAN NATION.
