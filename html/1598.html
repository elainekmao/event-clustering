1. (C) SUMMARY: ON MARCH 20 THE IMF RESREP, GERRY

JOHNSON, AND THE IMF TEAM LEADER, PAULO NEUHAUS, GAVE

THE DONOR COMMUNITY AN HOUR-LONG OUTBRIEF ON THE

RESULS OF ITS 12 DAY VISIT TO ZIMBABWE. NEUHAUS

PROVIDED NO NEW SURPRISES OR BOMBSHELLS, BUT DID

REVEAL, AS ANTICIPATED, THAT "THE CURRENT ECONOMIC

SITUATION IS VERY BAD AND WORRISOME, AND THE OUTLOOK

IS NEGATIVE." COMMENTING THAT PROGRESS BY THE GOZ ON

ECONOMIC COMMITMENTS MADE AFTER THE SEPTEMBER 2000

VISIT HAS BEEN FROZEN SINCE OCTOBER, NEUHAUS

DESCRIBED THE VISIT AS ANOTHER OPPORTUNITY TO CHART

OUT A RECOMMENDED PATH FORWARD. THE FUND\'S MINIMUM

CRITERIA FOR RE-ENGAGEMENT (THE FIRST STEP OF WHICH

WOULD BE A STAFF MONITORED PROGRAM, OR SMP) ARE:

DEVALUATION AND A CREDIBLE FOREIGN EXCHANGE POLICY;

DRASTIC MONETARY POLICY TIGHTENING; THE RAISING OF

PARASTATAL TARIFFS (ELECTRICITY, FUEL ETC.) TO STEM

OPERATING LOSSES; AND STRICT ADHERENCE TO THE

SPENDING LIMITS CONTAINED IN THE 2001 BUDGET. THIS

VISIT WAS A FOLLOW-UP TO THE ARTICLE IV BOARD REVIEW

OF ZIMBABWE IN DECEMBER 2000, FINANCE MINISTER

MAKONI\'S VISIT TO WASHINGTON LAST MONTH, AND THE

RECENT KOHLER/WOLFENSOHN/MUGABE MEETING IN DAR ES

SALAM. LOOKING AHEAD, THE IMF WILL OBSERVE WHAT

POSITIVE STEPS ZIMBABWE TAKES, AND WAIT FOR IT TO

SUBMIT AN ACTION PLAN, HOPEFULLY BEFORE THE SPRING

IMF/WB MEETINGS IN WASHINGTON.

2. (C) OTHER ECONOMIC DATA SHARED ARE THE FUND\'S

ESTIMATE THAT EVEN WITH THE FULL IMPLEMENTATION OF

BEST POLICIES AND PRACTICES IMMEDIATELY, REAL GDP

WILL SHRINK BY AT LEAST FIVE PERCENT THIS YEAR. IF

ZIMBABWE STAYS WITH CURRENT POLICIES, THE FUND

BELIEVES THAT THE ECONOMY WILL CONTRACT SHARPLY, WITH

15 TO 20 PERCENT SHRINKAGE HIGHLY POSSIBLE. "A

HUMANITARIAN DISASTER IS LOOMING," NEUHAUS TOLD THE

AUDIENCE, "AND WE SPEAK HONESTLY TO THEM ABOUT THE

DANGERS." AFTER THE PUBLIC SESSION NEUHAUS CONFIDED

TO US THAT ZIMBABWE WAS IN ARREARS TO THE FUND ON ITS

PAYMENTS, WITH A LITTLE OVER U.S. 18 MILLION OVERDUE

BY ABOUT 20 DAYS. NEUHAUS EXPECTS THE ARREARS TO

GROW, AS SPENDING FOR ENERGY TAKES PRECEDENCE OVER

LOAN REPAYMENTS. "IF THE WILL IS THERE WE WILL WORK

TOGETHER," NEUHAUS SAID, "BUT ZIMBABWE\'S POLITICS

MUST DECIDE WHAT THE FUTURE WILL BRING." END

SUMMARY.

----------------

FACING THE ABYSS

----------------

3. (C) THE IMF TEAM LEADER, PAULO NEUHAUS, PRESENTED

A HARSH ASSESSMENT OF ZIMBABWE\'S FAILURE TO EXECUTE

EVEN BASIC PRINCIPLES OF GOOD ECONOMIC STEWARDSHIP.

HIS TICKLIST OF BAD BEHAVIOR INCLUDED VERY POOR AND

EVEN DAMAGING MONETARY POLICIES (FOR EXAMPLE THE

RECENT ENGINEERED INTEREST SLIDE), THE CONTINUED

FREEZE OF THE PEGGED FOREIGN EXCHANGE RATE, THE

FAILURE TO INCREASE FUEL PRICES, THE UNREVEALED COSTS

OF THE CONGO MILITARY PRESENCE, AND THE ONGOING LAND

RESETTLEMENT MOVES WHICH HAVE EXTINGUISHED INVESTOR

CONFIDENCE AND FROZEN OUT THE DONORS. ANOTHER

EXAMPLE OF THE COUNTER-PRODUCTIVE STEPS THEY HAVE

TAKEN, HE EXPLAINED, IS THE RECENT PROGRAM TO LEND

STATUTORY RESERVES AT 15 PERCENT TO POTENTIAL

EXPORTERS, A FACT THAT ENCOURAGES THEM TO HIDE EXPORT

RECEIPTS EXTERNALLY, AT LEAST TILL DEVALUATION OCCURS

AND INFLATION SLOWS DOWN. WHILE THE PEOPLE WE SPOKE

WITH ARE NOT IN DENIAL," SAID NEUHAUS, "WHAT THEY CAN

CONVINCE THEIR SUPERIORS TO DO IS VERY PROBLEMATIC

AND UNKNOWN." THEIR ACTIONS OVER THE COMING MONTHS

WILL BE KEY IN DETERMINING IF THE RELATIONSHIP AWAKES

FROM "ITS PRESENT DORMANCY."

THAT ONLY IF A SOUND MACROECONOMIC PROGRAM IS LAID

OUT, COULD A STAFF MONITORED PROGRAM WITH QUARTERLY

TARGETS BE INITIATED. IF THE RESULTS ARE GOOD (AFTER

A MINIMUM OF PROBABLY THREE QUARTERS OF MONITORING),

THEN A FUNDING ARRANGEMENT COULD BE BROUGHT TO THE

BOARD. IF TARGETS AND CONDITIONS CONTINUE TO BE MET,

THEN CONSIDERATION COULD BE GIVEN TO TABLING ZIMBABWE

AT THE PARIS CLUB FOR RESCHEDULING TALKS. GIVEN

POLITICS HERE, THIS IS A PATH STUDDED WITH HIGH

OBSTACLES.
