2. (C) CECCARELLI COMMENTED ON REF A POINTS AS FOLLOWS:

-- CUBA: ITALY FAVORS HAVING A RESOLUTION PRESENTED.

CECCARELLI REFERRED TO EU VIEW THAT RESOLUTION SHOULD INCLUDE

REFERENCE TO CUBA\'S "ECONOMIC SITUATION" (I.E., HE DID

NOT/NOT USE THE WORD "EMBARGO" -- A WORD ABSENCE WE ATTRIBUTE

TO DIPLOMATIC DELICACY, NOT A CHANGE IN POLICY).

-- MIDDLE EAST: CECCARELLI AGREED RESOLUTIONS SHOULD BE

BALANCED. (DURING THEIR 3/26 MEETING, MFA SECRETARY GENERAL

BALDOCCI TOLD THE AMBASSADOR HE WOULD REVIEW OUR TALKING

POINTS AND GET BACK TO US. WE SUGGESTED ITALY MIGHT WISH TO

RECONSIDER ITS TRADITIONAL POLICY OF GOING ALONG WITH THE EU

CONSENSUS, WHATEVER THAT MIGHT BE, AND TAKE THE LEAD ON THIS

OCCASION TO CALL FOR A VOTE ON ONE OR MORE OF THE UNBALANCED

RESOLUTIONS.)

-- SUDAN: ONE OF THE "GRAVEST SITUATIONS," ACCORDING TO

CECCARELLI. NONETHELESS, HE SAID, THE KEY TO PROGRESS LIES

IN OPENING A DIALOGUE DOOR WITH THE SUDANESE.

-- IRAQ: CECCARELLI OBSERVED WE ARE PASSING FROM A SITUATION

OF POTENTIAL PERIL TO REAL PERIL.

-- IRAN: CECCARELLI RETURNED AGAIN TO THEME "DIALOGUE." THE

EU IS FOLLOWING A DOUBLE TRACK, HE SAID, CRITICIZING HUMAN

RIGHTS VIOLATIONS WHILE, AT THE SAME TIME, TRYING TO

RECOGNIZE AND FAVOR POSITIVE DEVELOPMENTS.

-- PARTS OF SOUTH EASTERN EUROPE: EU MEMBERS HAVE NOT YET

SEEN A RESOLUTION DRAFT TEXT.

-- AFGHANISTAN: THE GOI, HE SAID, IS KEEPING THE SITUATION IN

AFGHANISTAN UNDER REVIEW.

-- CHECHNYA: THE EU IS EXAMINING ELEMENTS FOR DISCUSSION AND

A POSSIBLE EVENTUAL CHAIRMAN\'S STATEMENT.

-- ZIMBABWE: THIS YEAR\'S "BLACK SHEEP;" THE DEVELOPING

SITUATION LEAVES THE GOI MUCH DISTRESSED.

-- ECONOMIC, SOCIAL AND CULTURAL RIGHTS: CECCARELLI REFERRED

TO U.S./EU DIFFERENCES BUT DISMISSED THEM AS "NO BIG DEAL."

-- DEATH PENALTY: ON THE OTHER HAND, THIS IS A POINT OF

"COMPLETE ACCORD" IN THE EU (I.E., AND THEREFORE OF COMPLETE

DISACCORD WITH THE U.S.).

-- OPTIONAL PROTOCOL TO THE TORTURE CONVENTION: CECCARELLI

OBSERVED THAT ITALY EXPECTS TO ADOPT A LAW ON TORTURE.

3. (C) COMMENT: RESPONDING TO POLOFF\'S QUESTION, CECCARELLI

SAID HE HAS SEEN NO OBVIOUS CHANGES IN GOI APPROACHES TO

UNCHR ISSUES. THE CENTER-RIGHT BERLUSCONI GOVERNMENT, HE

SAID, IS PURSUING THE SAME OBJECTIVES, AND EMPLOYING THE SAME

TACTICS, AS ITS CENTER-LEFT PREDECESSORS. NONETHELESS, WE

BELIEVE SOME ROOM EXISTS FOR ENCOURAGING NEW GOI THINKING.

THE GOI UNDER BERLUSCONI RETAINS A EUROPEAN OUTLOOK, BUT WITH

A DIFFERENCE. IT CONSIDERS THE ASSERTION OF NATIONAL

FOR EXAMPLE, FOR GERMANY AND FRANCE. AT LEAST FOR NOW,
