SUBJECT: NEED FOR PD NETWORK

REF: STATE 134398

POST HAS NOTED SOME DISCUSSION THAT SEEMS TO INDICATE

THAT OPENNET PLUS WOULD AND SHOULD REPLACE PDNET

OVERSEAS. WE WOULD LIKE TO CLARIFY THAT DESPITE THE

PROJECTED ARRIVAL OF OPENNET PLUS AT SOME TIME IN THE

FUTURE, WE WILL CONTINUE TO NEED A SEPARATE PDNET IN

ORDER TO MEET ALL OUR INTERACTIVE REQUIREMENTS.

ALTHOUGH OPENNET PLUS WILL PROVIDE WIDER ACCESS TO THE

INTERNET THROUGHOUT THE EMBASSY, AS AN SBU SYSTEM IT IS

UNLIKELY TO PROVIDE THE OPEN INTERACTIVE ENVIRONMENT

NEEDED FOR PUBLIC INTERACTION. AS WE CONTINUE OUR

EFFORTS TO STAY ON THE CUTTING EDGE OF TECHNOLOGY IN

BOTH OUTREACH AND IN INFORMATION GATHERING, WE WILL

CONTINUE TO NEED THE ABILITY TO EXPERIMENT WITH NEW

APPLICATIONS AND NEW DOWNLOADS FROM NON-GOVERNMEENT

SITES, TO ACCESS INFORMATION INCLUDING PHOTO, VIDEO,

SOUND, AND EXECUTABLE FILES FROM A VARIETY OF SOURCES,

AND TO ENCOURAGE INTERACTION WITH A WIDE PUBLIC

AUDIENCE. THESE NEEDS ARE PARTICULAR TO PD WORK AND

NONE ARE LIKELY TO BE MET THROUGH AN OPENNET THAT WOULD

STILL PROVIDE APPROPRIATE SECURITY FOR THE EMBASSY AS A

WHOLE.

IN OUR VIEW, OPENNET PLUS AND PDNET ARE NOT

INTERCHANGEABLE. DISCONTINUING PDNET WOULD HAVE A

DEMONSTRABLY NEGATIVE IMPACT ON OUR ABILITY TO HARDNESS

INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY FOR PUBLIC DIPLOMACY PURPOSES.

GNEHM
