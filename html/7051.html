SUBJECT: TFIZ01: KUWAIT OIL UPDATE -- APRIL 02, 2003

REF: KUWAIT 1195 AND PREVIOUS

Classified By: DCM Frank C. Urbancic for reasons 1.5 (A), (B) and (D)

1. (S/NF) Post has investigated rumors of an explosion and

fire at the Rawdatain oil field in northern Kuwait, and found

them to be untrue. KPC and U.S. military sources confirm

that an apparent Iraqi missile did recently land in an

unpopulated area south of the Rawdatain and west of the

Sabriya fields, but the impact had no effect on oil

facilities or operations.

2. (C) Post is also investigating unconfirmed reports of an

unscheduled shutdown of Gathering Center 20 in the Wafra

field (shared with Saudi Arabia). There is nothing to

suggest at this point that the shutdown is serious or long

lasting.

2.(C) Explanatory Comments:

Wafra Joint Operations refer to onshore concessions in

Saudi-Kuwait divided zone operated by Saudi Arabian Texaco

(SAT). R/B refers to the two producing reservoirs -- Ratawi

and Burgan.

KOC is Kuwait Oil Company

KNPC is Kuwait National Petroleum Corporation

KGOC is the Kuwaiti successor to Arabian Oil Company (AOC),

which previously operated a concession in the offshore

portions of the Saudi-Kuwait divided zone. AOC still

operates the fields under a service agreement.

KEC refers to Kuwait Export Crude, the country\'s export

blend. Salt PTB refers to pounds of salt per thousand

barrels of crude oil.
