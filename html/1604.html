SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE FLOOD STATUS UPDATE, NUMBER 3

1. THOUGH RAINS CONTINUED TO FALL OVER THE ZAMBEZI RIVER

BASIN, RAINFALL AMOUNTS EASED SLIGHTLY DURING THE PAST

SEVEN DAYS AND FLOODING IN THE ZAMBEZI VALLEY SUBSIDED.

HOWEVER, THE NORTHERN AREAS OF MASHONALAND CENTRAL, WHERE

LARGE AREAS OF CROPLAND HAVE BEEN DESTROYED, REMAIN AT RISK

FROM FLOODING FROM THE ZAMBEZI RIVER AND BACK-FLOW FROM THE

CAHORA BASSA DAM IN MOZAMBIQUE. THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE

(GOZ) CIVIL PROTECTION DEPARTMENT (CPD) IS STILL ASSESSING

DAMAGE IN THE AFFECTED AREAS. THE FOLLOWING ARE THE

PROVINCES AND DISTRICTS AFFECTED ACCORDING TO THE CIVIL

PROTECTION DEPARTMENT (CPD) WEEKLY REPORT OF MARCH 13:

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 HARARE 01108 231036Z

= MASHONALAND CENTRAL: MUZARABANI DISTRICT: THOUGH

FLOODING HAS MADE MOST AREAS INACCESSIBLE, ASSESSMENT IS

STILL GOING ON IN OTHER AREAS AND THE NUMBER OF PEOPLE

AFFECTED HAS RISEN FROM 5700 TO 10,500 FAMILIES. THERE ARE

3 CASES OF VILLAGERS WHO HAVE DROWNED AND THERE ARE EARLY

REPORTS OF WIDESPREAD DESTRUCTION OF CROPS. AN IMMEDIATE

REQUIREMENTS IS FOOD, WHICH IS CURRENTLY BEING TRANSPORTED

BY THE AIR FORCE OF ZIMBABWE. THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH HAS

PROVIDE 25,000 WATER TREATMENT TABLETS AND ORGANIZATIONS

SUCH AS THE RED CROSS, SCF/UK, COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION,

FARMERS WORLD, COTTCO AND LOCAL BUSINESSMEN HAVE DONATED

ADDITIONAL FOOD STUFFS.

= GURUVE DISTRICT: A TOTAL NUMBER OF 527 HOUSEHOLDS HAVE

BEEN AFFECTED AND A LIMITED NUMBER OF PEOPLE WERE AIRLIFTED

TO HIGHER GROUND DURING THE EARLY PART OF THE REPORTING

PERIOD. THERE ARE NO REPORTS OF DISEASE OUTBREAK.

= MASHONALAND WEST: THERE ARE REPORTS OF ROADS AND

BRIDGES IN FLOOD. AT CHAKARI MINE, 4 PEOPLE WERE SWEPT

AWAY BY A FLOODED STREAM. AREAS WITH LOW-LYING BRIDGES,

EARTH AND GRAVEL ROADS ARE IMPASSABLE. LAKE KARIBA\'S WATER

LEVEL HAS GONE BEYOND ITS SAFETY LEVEL AND TWO FLOODGATES

REMAIN OPEN RESULTING IN HIGH WATER LEVELS IN THE

DOWNSTREAM ZAMBEZI RIVER.

= MIDLANDS PROVINCE: SOME SECONDARY ROADS IN THE GOKWE

DISTRICT ARE REPORTED IMPASSABLE AND SLIPPERY DUE TO

INCESSANT RAINS, MAKING SURVEY AND RESCUE TEAM MOVEMENTS

DIFFICULT. A LOCAL COMMUNITY SCHOOL WAS CLOSED, BUT HAS

SINCE REOPENED.

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 04 HARARE 01108 231036Z

= MATABELELAND NORTH: TSHOLOTSHO DISTRICT: VILLAGERS

WHOSE HOMES WERE WASHED AWAY WHEN THE GWAAI RIVER BURST ITS

BANKS ARE STILL CAMPED AT A LOCAL HOSPITAL. ISOLATED

RESCUE OPERATIONS CONTINUE.

2. ASSESSMENT EXERCISES CARRIED OUT SO FAR REVEAL THAT

ABOUT 30,000 PEOPLE NATIONWIDE NEED SOME LEVEL OF

ASSISTANCE. THIS NUMBER INCLUDES ESTIMATES OF AREAS NOT

YET PHYSICALLY SURVEYED IN MASHONALAND CENTRAL,

MATABELELAND NORTH AND MIDLANDS. SOME OF THE PROBLEMS

BEING FACED BY THE SURVEY AND RESCUE TEAMS IN CARRYING OUT

ASSESSMENTS ARE IMPASSABLE ROADS AND PROBLEMS OBTAINING

FUEL FROM EITHER THE OPEN MARKET OR GOVERNMENT FUEL

LOCATIONS, BOTH OF WHICH ARE DRY. AS THE FLOODS CONTINUE

IN MOST AREAS, THERE REMAINS THE FEAR OF AN OUTBREAK OF

WATER BORNE DISEASES.

3. ASSISTANCE TO FLOOD VICTIMS IS BEING RENDERED BY THE

GOZ CIVIL PROTECTION DEPARTMENT. WE WILL CONTINUE TO

MONITOR THEIR WEEKLY STATUS REPORTS AS WELL AS REPORTS FROM

CPD, AGRITEX, FEWS/SDC AND OTHER SOURCES OF INFORMATION ON

FLOOD CONDITIONS AND RESULTANT DAMAGE IN ZIMBABWE.
