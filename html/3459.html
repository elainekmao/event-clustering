SUBJECT: IRAQ: MARTINO CAUSES STIR OVER ITALIAN SUPPORT

FOR U.S. MILITARY INTERVENTION

REF: A) ROME 3760 B) ROME 3795

CLASSIFIED BY: ACTING DCM THOMAS COUNTRYMAN FOR REASONS 1.5(B) AND (D)

1. (U) SUMMARY: ITALIAN DEFENSE MINISTER MARTINO CAUSED A

STIR AMONG ITALIAN POLITICAL LEADERS AND COMMENTATORS BY

SUGGESTING THAT ITALY WOULD "AUTOMATICALLY" SUPPORT U.S.

MILITARY INTERVENTION IN IRAQ. HE ADDED, HOWEVER, THAT ITALY

WOULD ONLY PARTICIPATE IN THE MILITARY EFFORT IF THERE IS

"PROVEN EVIDENCE" OF IRAQ\'S CAPABILITY TO PRODUCE WEAPONS OF

MASS DESTRUCTION (WMD). OPPOSITION FORCES REACTED WITH

OUTRAGE, WHILE OTHER GOI OFFICIALS HASTENED TO TONE DOWN

MARTINO\'S MESSAGE. END SUMMARY.

2. (U) IN AN INTERVIEW PUBLISHED IN THE AUGUST 8 ISSUE OF

THE CENTRIST NEWS WEEKLY "PANORAMA," ITALIAN MOD ANTONIO

MARTINO SAID THAT, AT A RECENT NATO DEFENSE MINISTERS MEETING

IN BRUSSELS, HE AND HIS EUROPEAN COLLEAGUES DISCUSSED WITH

SECRETARY RUMSFELD THE PROBABLE EXISTENCE OF WMD PLANTS IN

SIPDIS

IRAQ. MARTINO SAID THAT IT WOULD BE "IRRESPONSIBLE" NOT TO

TAKE ACTION IF THERE IS POSITIVE EVIDENCE THAT SUCH

PRODUCTION FACILITIES EXIST.

3. (SBU) CAUTIOUS AS USUAL WHEN IT COMES TO COMMITTING

ITALIAN MILITARY PARTICIPATION, HOWEVER, MARTINO REMARKED

THAT THE "LITMUS TEST" ON WHETHER OR NOT TO ENGAGE IN

MILITARY ACTIVITY AGAINST IRAQ IS THE ISSUE OF UN INSPECTORS.

IF SADDAM HUSSEIN ALLOWS THE UNCONDITIONAL RETURN OF THE

INSPECTORS, HE ARGUED, THERE WILL BE NO NEED FOR MILITARY

INTERVENTION. IF, INSTEAD, THE IRAQI LEADER CONTINUES TO

BLOCK THEIR RETURN, MARTINO INFERRED THAT THIS WOULD BE

JUSTIFICATION FOR MILITARY ACTION.

4. (U) ACCORDING TO PRESS STATEMENTS, MARTINO BELIEVES THE

U.S. IS RIGHT IN NOT WANTING TO WAIT INDEFINITELY BEFORE

STRIKING, GIVEN THE POSSIBLE CHEMICAL AND BIOLOGICAL THREAT

FROM IRAQ. STILL, HE DOES NOT BELIEVE U.S. ACTION IS

IMMINENT, SINCE WASHINGTON WILL FIRST WANT TO BUILD THE

WIDEST POSSIBLE INTERNATIONAL COALITION -- POSSIBLY WIDER

THAN DESERT STORM IN 1991, AND INCLUDING THE RUSSIAN

FEDERATION. MARTINO SAID THAT, IF THE U.S. EVENTUALLY

DECIDES TO ATTACK, ITALY, AS A CLOSE U.S. ALLY, WOULD

"AUTOMATICALLY" MAKE ITS AIR SPACE AVAILABLE. THE

PARTICIPATION OF ITALIAN TROOPS WOULD DEPEND, HOWEVER, "ON

THE PROVEN EVIDENCE OF IRAQ\'S WAR PLANS."

5. (U) RESPONDING TO MARTINO\'S STATEMENTS AND RESULTING

OPPOSITION CRITICISM, ALFREDO MANTICA, MFA UNDER SECRETARY

FOR THE MIDDLE EAST, AND MARGHERITA BONIVER, MFA UNDER

SECRETARY FOR ASIA, TOLD THE PRESS THAT MILITARY INTERVENTION

SIPDIS

BY AN INTERNATIONAL ALLIANCE WOULD REQUIRE A UN RESOLUTION.

THEY ALSO SAID ITALY WOULD MAKE ITS DECISION ON WHETHER TO

PARTICIPATE IN CONJUNCTION WITH ITS EUROPEAN PARTNERS,

ALTHOUGH BONIVER OPINED THAT ITALY WOULD ULTIMATELY BACK THE

U.S. IF ASKED BY PRESIDENT BUSH. ONE RULING COALITION MEMBER

IN THE SENATE CAUTIONED THAT ANY DECISION INVOLVING AN

ITALIAN MILITARY COMMITMENT MUST GO BEFORE PARLIAMENT.

6. (U) THEIR STATEMENTS DID LITTLE TO PACIFY THE OPPOSITION,

WHICH PROTESTED MARTINO\'S "DISCONCERTING" STATEMENTS AND

"SUBMISSIVE" ATTITUDE TOWARD THE UNITED STATES. OVER 70

LEADERS OF THE LEFT AND CENTER-LEFT SIGNED A PETITION

OPPOSING MILITARY INTERVENTION IN IRAQ. MANY COMMENTATORS

POINT TO THE DANGERS OF AN ATTACK ON IRAQ ON MIDDLE EAST

STABILITY AND THE GLOBAL ECONOMY, WHILE OPPOSITION FORCES ARE

CALLING FOR AN END TO THE UN EMBARGO AGAINST IRAQ AND ARE

APPEALING TO OTHER LEFTIST FORCES IN EUROPE TO TAKE POLITICAL

ACTION TO MAKE CLEAR EUROPE\'S OPPOSITION TO AN EXTENSION OF

THE CONFLICT.

7. (U) SOME IN ITALY SPECULATE THAT THE U.S. REQUEST FOR

MORE ITALIAN TROOPS IN OEF IS AN EFFORT TO FREE UP U.S.

TROOPS AND THUS A SIGNAL OF U.S. INTENTIONS TO ATTACK IRAQ.

ALTHOUGH BONIVER CALLED THIS INTERPRETATION A "CREDIBLE

HYPOTHESIS," MARTINO DENIED IT, SAYING THAT ITALIAN SOLDIERS

WOULD REPLACE BRITISH, NOT AMERICAN, TROOPS AS PART OF AN

ON-GOING SIX-MONTH ROTATION.

8. (C) COMMENT. THE GOI UNDER PM BERLUSCONI HAS BEEN A

STRONG SUPPORTER OF U.S. MILITARY POLICY. AT THE SAME TIME,

HOWEVER, IT IS EQUALLY COMMITTED TO A COMMON EUROPEAN DEFENSE

POLICY AND THEREFORE RELUCTANT TO GUARANTEE ACTIVE MILITARY

PARTICIPATION IN IRAQ -- IF AND WHEN IT BECOMES NECESSARY --

IN THE ABSENCE OF AN EU CONSENSUS. DESPITE GOI (AND OUR OWN)
