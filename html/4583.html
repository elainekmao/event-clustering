SUBJECT: WFP PROPOSES PARTNERSHIP WITH U.S. PEACE CORPS -

REQUEST FOR 1,000 VOLUNTEERS TO SUPPORT WORLD FOOD PROGRAM

ACTIVITIES BY 2004

REF: (A) 01 ROME 5868, (B) 01 ROME 3755

-------

SUMMARY

-------

1. WFP EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR JAMES MORRIS HAS REQUESTED UNITED

STATES PEACE CORPS DIRECTOR GADDI H. VASQUEZ TO INVOLVE

1,000 PEACE CORPS VOLUNTEERS IN WORLD FOOD PROGRAM

ACTIVITIES BY 2004. WFP IS RESPONSIBLE FOR HANDLING MORE

THAN ONE THIRD OF GLOBAL FOOD AID FLOWS. THE UNITED STATES

IS WFP\'S LEADING DONOR, HAVING CONTRIBUTED USD 6.38 BILLION

IN SUPPORT TO WFP OVER THE DECADE 1992-2001. WFP BELIEVES

(AND THE USMISSION STRONGLY AGREES) THAT A STRENGTHENED

PARTNERSHIP WITH PEACE CORPS WILL INCREASE THE EFFECTIVENESS

AND EFFICIENCY OF FOOD AID WORLDWIDE AS WELL AS HELPING TO

ENSURE THAT LONG-RANGE WFP WORK FORCE PLANNING PROVIDES

EQUITABLE US REPRESENTATION. END SUMMARY.

BACKGROUND

----------

2. USMISSION RECEIVED COPY OF A LETTER FROM WFP EXECUTIVE

DIRECTOR JAMES MORRIS TO UNITED STATES PEACE CORPS DIRECTOR

GADDI H. VASQUEZ DATED NOVEMBER 15, 2002, WHICH PROPOSES A

PARTNERSHIP TO INVOLVE 1,000 PEACE CORPS VOLUNTEERS IN WORLD

FOOD PROGRAM ACTIVITIES WORLDWIDE BY 2004. AS PER REF A, WFP

HAS BEEN ACTIVE IN EFFORTS TO INCREASE THE NUMBER OF

AMERICANS WORKING IN THE UN-SYSTEM, PARTICULARLY WFP. IN

PART THIS EFFORT FLOWS FROM A SENATOR HELMS COMMISSIONED GAO

REPORT (JULY 2001) ENTITLED "UNITED NATIONS: TARGETED

STRATEGIES COULD HELP BOOST US REPRESENTATION." WFP BELIEVES

THAT A FORMAL ASSOCIATION WITH THE PEACE CORPS, IN ADDITION

TO STRENGTHENING THE EFFICIENCY AND EFFECTIVENESS OF ITS

FIELD OPERATIONS, CAN ALSO ADDRESS AMERICAN UNDER-

REPRESENTATION BY OPENING AN ENTRY-LEVEL "WINDOW" FOR

RECRUITMENT OF U.S. CITIZENS.
