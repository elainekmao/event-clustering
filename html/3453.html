1. (U) THIS IS AN ACTION REQUEST. PLEASE SEE PARAGRAPH 5.

2. (C) SUMMARY. U/S BOLTON DISCUSSED HIS RECENT MOSCOW

VISIT, NON-PROLIFERATION AND ARMS COUNTROL ISSUES, MISSILE

DEFENSE, THE GLOBAL PARTNERSHIP PROGRAM, CHINA AND THE

INTERNATIONAL CRIMINAL COURT (REF A) AT AN AUGUST 5 WORKING

LUNCH WITH SENIOR ITALIAN MFA AND MOD OFFICIALS. MFA

DIRECTOR GENERAL GIANCARLO ARAGONA EMPHASIZED THAT MISSILE

DEFENSE AND NON-PROLIFERATION EFFORTS SHOULD NOT WORK TO

DESTABILIZE THE EXISTING BALANCE WITH RUSSIA OR WITH CHINA.

HE SAID THAT ITALY IS READY TO PARTICIPATE IN THE MD PROGRAM

TO THE MAXIMUM OF ITS TECHNICAL CAPABILITIES AND IS LOOKING

TO PROCEED WITH COOPERATIVE PROJECTS. HE ALSO CONFIRMED

ITALY\'S COMMITMENT TO CONTRIBUTE EURO 1 BILLION TO THE 10 10

OVER 10 PLAN AND ASKED FOR USG SUGGESTIONS ON PROJECTS WORTH

FUNDING. END SUMMARY.

RUSSIA - IRAN

-------------

3. (C) ARAGONA, A FORMER AMBASSADOR TO MOSCOW, WAS

PARTICULARLY INTERESTED IN THE IMPACT OF RUSSIA\'S RECENT

ANNOUNCEMENT OF ADDITIONAL SUPPORT FOR IRAN\'S NUCLEAR REACTOR

PROGRAM. THE ANNOUNCEMENT, WHICH CAME AS THE USG WAS LOOKING

TO BUILD ON THE ENCOURAGING RAPPORT BETWEEN PUTIN AND

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 ROME 03855 01 OF 03 061129Z

PRESIDENT BUSH, COULD INSTEAD HAVE A CHILLING EFFECT ON

US-RUSSIAN RELATIONS, U/S BOLTON STRESSED. IT PUT A DEFINITE

PALL OVER PLANNED TALKS ON RUSSIAN NON-PROLIFERATION EFFORTS,

HE SAID, AND CAME AS AN UNPLEASANT SURPRISE TO US AND MANY

RUSSIAN OFFICIALS ALIKE. PUTIN HIMSELF HAD STATED PREVIOUSLY

THAT A NUCLEAR CAPABLE IRAN IS NOT IN RUSSIA\'S INTEREST, HE

SAID. NOTING THAT THERE CONTINUES TO BE AN INCREASE IN

IRAN\'S CAPABILITY TO IMPROVE ITS COVERT NUCLEAR PROGRAM, U/S

BOLTON OUTLINED USG CONCERNS WITH RUSSIA\'S SUPPORT FOR IRAN\'S

BUSHEHR NUCLEAR REACTOR: 1) A FUNCTIONING REACTOR -- EVEN ONE

ALLEGEDLY FOR CIVIL POWER PURPOSES -- OFFERS GREATER TRAINING

OPPORTUNITIES AND EXPERIENCE THAN A RESEARCH REACTOR, 2)

RUSSIAN SCIENTISTS AND TECHNICIANS ENTERING IRAN TO ASSIST

WITH BUSHEHR PROVIDE IDEAL COVER FOR OTHERS ENTERING FOR

COVERT PURPOSES, AND 3) FUEL RODS FROM BUSHEHR COULD BE USED

BY IRAN FOR ILLEGITIMATE PURPOSES UNLESS A STRICT CONTROL

SYSTEM IS ESTABLISHED AND ENFORCED. RUSSIAN ASSISTANCE TO

BUILD FIVE NEW REACTORS WILL ONLY MAGNIFY THESE CONCERNS, U/S

BOLTON SAID. AS IT IS, THE USG CONSENSUS IS THAT IRAN IS

FIVE TO EIGHT YEARS AWAY FROM HAVING NUCLEAR WEAPONS, HE

WARNED.

4. (C) MFA COORDINATOR FOR INTERNATIONAL SECURITY AFFAIRS

CARLO TREZZA ASKED IF ENHANCED SAFEGUARDS WOULD ADDRESS USG

CONCERNS AND STILL ALLOW IRAN TO PROCEED WITH A REACTOR TO

MEET ITS ELECTRICITY NEEDS. U/S BOLTON AGREED THAT ENHANCED

SAFEGUARDS ARE A PREREQUISITE, BUT NOTED THAT BUSHEHR SHOULD

NOT BE VIEWED IN ISOLATION. HE URGED THE ITALIANS TO

CONSIDER WHY AN OIL-RICH STATE SUCH AS IRAN NEEDS TO DEVELOP

NUCLEAR POWER CAPABILITY OR MINE URANIUM. THE ITALIANS

SUGGESTED THAT BETTER INTEL SHARING AMONG ALLIES, BILATERALLY

IN GENERAL. WE MAY DIFFER ON HOW TO RESPOND, ARAGONA SAID,

CONCERNS ABOUT RUSSIAN COOPERATION AND EXPORTS TO ASSIST

- AS YOU TOLD US AFTER HIS VISIT TO RUSSIA IN APRIL, THE

WAS WORRIED THAT THE TRANSFER OF SENSITIVE TECHNOLOGIES FROM

RUSSIA TO IRAN WAS AIDING IRAN\'S ATTEMPTS TO BUILD NUCLEAR

RUSSIA

------

6. (C) ARAGONA STRESSED PM BERLUSCONI\'S COMMITMENT TO

BUILDING A NEW RELATIONSHIP WITH RUSSIA, BILATERALLY AND

MULTILATERALLY. HE POINTED OUT THAT THE RUSSIANS ARE NERVOUS

ABOUT IMPROVED US MILITARY CAPABILITIES BECAUSE THEIR OWN

CAPABILITIES ARE DECLINING RAPIDLY, BUT ARE UNCLEAR HOW TO

PROCEED. WHILE THE US STRATEGIC RELATIONSHIP WITH RUSSIA IS

A BILATERAL CONCERN, ARAGONA WARNED THAT A SEVERE IMBALANCE

BETWEEN RUSSIAN AND US CAPABILITIES, OR UNCERTAINTY ON

RUSSIA\'S PART, COULD IMPACT THE OVERALL SECURITY SITUATION.

U/S BOLTON RESPONDED THAT COOPERATION ON MD WITHIN NATO AND

THE NATO-RUSSIA COUNCIL SHOULD REASSURE RUSSIA, AND SHOULD

ENCOURAGE RUSSIA TO MOVE TOWARD GREATER COOPERATION WITH THE

WEST.

7. (C) U/S BOLTON UNDERLINED THE INCREASING COMMON GROUND

BETWEEN THE US AND RUSSIA, WHICH THE RECENT ANNOUNCEMENT ON

IRAN THREATENS TO UPSET. UNDER THE NEW STRATEGIC FRAMEWORK,

THE US RELATIONSHIP WITH RUSSIA IS FOCUSED ON STRATEGIC

DEFENSIVE ISSUES, STRATEGIC OFFENSIVE ISSUES AND

NON-PROLIFERATION, HE EXPLAINED. THE TERMINATION OF THE ABM

TREATY MAKES IT POSSIBLE TO SHARE SOME MISSILE TECHNOLOGY

WITH ALLIES AND RUSSIA, INCREASING THE LIKELIHOOD OF

US-RUSSIA COOPERATION ON MISSILE DEFENSE. TO ASSUAGE RUSSIAN

9. (C) FRATICELLI RAISED THE POSSIBILITY OF USING THE RUSSIAN

S300 SYSTEM AS PART OF AN MD PROGRAM. HE SAID THE RUSSIANS

HAVE PUSHED TO HAVE THEIR SYSTEM, WHICH ALREADY EXISTS, USED

AS THE FRAMEWORK FOR INCORPORATING OTHER SYSTEMS. THIS

WOULD, AT THE VERY LEAST, INVOLVE OVERCOMING RUSSIAN CONCERNS

ABOUT THE US ACQUIRING RUSSIAN TECHNOLOGY, U/S BOLTON POINTED

OUT, BUT IN GENERAL THE US SEES NO LIMITS TO RUSSIAN

PARTICIPATION. THE RECENTLY CREATED CONSULTATIVE GROUP ON

STRATEGIC STABILITY, CONSISTING OF RUSSIAN AND US FOREIGN AND

DEFENSE MINISTERS, WOULD HOLD ITS FIRST MEETING ON SEPTEMBER

20 TO LOOK AT AREAS FOR MD COOPERATION, HE SAID.

GLOBAL PARTNERSHIP

------------------

10. (C) ARAGONA NOTED GOOD PROGRESS WITH THE RUSSIAN\'S ON THE

GLOBAL PARTNERSHIP PLAN. MINISTER TREZZA PLANS TO MEET WITH

THE RUSSIAN DIRECTOR OF ARMS CONTROL AFTER THE AUGUST BREAK

TO DISCUSS CONCRETE PROJECTS, SUCH AS IN THE CHEMICAL,

NUCLEAR AND SUBMARINE AREAS, HE SAID. ARAGONA CONFIRMED THAT

THE GOI IS READY TO COMMIT UP TO EURO 1 BILLION TO THE 10 10

OVER 10 PLAN -- BOTH TO FURTHER ARMS CONTROL OBJECTIVES AND

TO HELP ITALIAN INDUSTRY. ITALY WOULD WELCOME US SUGGESTIONS

ON SOLID PROJECTS WORTHY OF FUNDING, HE ADDED, AS WELL AS

WAYS TO JOIN FORCES AND POOL RESOURCES WITH PARTNERS. U/S

CHINA

-----

11. (C) FRATICELLI RAISED THE CHINA CARD, NOTING THAT USG

CONCERNS AND EXPORT CONTROLS HAD LED TO EXPENSIVE PROBLEMS

FOR ITALIAN INDUSTRIES. U/S BOLTON SAID THE USG IS REVIEWING

ITS LICENSING PROGRAM IN GENERAL TO MAKE IT MORE EFFECTIVE.

WITH REGARD TO CHINA, HE EXPLAINED THAT THE US IS STILL

WAITING FOR CHINA TO PROMULGATE AND ENFORCE A SYSTEM OF

EXPORT CONTROLS FOR BALLISTIC MISSILES, AS CALLED FOR UNDER A

NOVEMBER 2000 AGREEMENT. CHINA SAYS IT AGREES THAT

PROLIFERATION IS A PROBLEM AT THE SAME TIME THAT IT IS

SELLING SENSITIVE ITEMS TO PAKISTAN, IRAN, IRAQ AND NORTH

KOREA. THE MEETING BETWEEN SECRETARY POWELL AND THE CHINESE

FM MAY GIVE SOME INSIGHT INTO CHINA\'S ACTIONS, U/S BOLTON

SAID. HE ASSURED THE ITALIANS THAT THE US IS EAGER TO

DISCUSS USG CONCERNS ABOUT CHINA IN MORE DETAIL, EITHER IN

ROME OR IN WASHINGTON. ARAGONA AGREED THAT SUCH

CONSULTATIONS SHOULD TAKE PLACE BEFORE THE END OF THE YEAR,

WITH REPRESENTATIVES FROM MFA, MOD AND OTHER RELEVANT

MINISTRIES.

12. (C) PROMPTED BY A QUESTION FROM MINISTER ARAGONA, U/S

BOLTON EXPLAINED THAT NONCOMPLIANCE WAS THE PRIMARY CONCERN

BEHIND THE BUSH ADMINISTRATION\'S DECISION NOT TO SUPPORT THE

BIOLOGICAL WEAPONS CONVENTION PROTOCOL. EVERY AGENCY

CONSULTED DURING AN INTERAGENCY REVIEW LAST YEAR HAD

CONCLUDED THAT IT WASN\'T IN THE US INTEREST TO ADOPT THE

PROTOCOL. IN ADDITION, THE NATURE OF BW RESEARCH AND

DEVELOPMENT MADE IT EASY TO FRUSTRATE INSPECTIONS, AND

INFORMATION GATHERED DURING INSPECTIONS COULD THEN BE USED TO

DEFEAT US DEFENSES, U/S BOLTON NOTED. THE US RESPONSE ALSO

REFLECTED CONCERNS EXPRESSED BY US BIOTECH AND PHARMOLOGICAL

COMPANIES WORRIED ABOUT VULNERABLE INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

RIGHTS. FINALLY, PROVISIONS IN THE PROTOCOL COULD HAVE

WEAKENED THE AUSTRALIA GROUP AND OTHER MULTILATERAL EFFORTS

TO PREVENT THE EXPORT OF DUAL USE EQUIPMENT. THE USG

PRESENTED ALTERNATIVES TO THE PROTOCOL LAST FALL, HE ADDED,

MANY OF WHICH HAVE GARNERED INTERNATIONAL SUPPORT.
