SUBJECT: VATICAN CHARITY COMBATS HARSH CONDITIONS IN NORTH

KOREA

REF: 01 VATICAN 1244

-------

SUMMARY

-------

1. VATICAN NGO, CARITAS HONG KONG, COORDINATES THE

VATICAN\'S RELIEF EFFORTS IN NORTH KOREA. THE ORGANIZATION

HAS A SOLID WORKING RELATIONSHIP WITH THE DPRK GOVERNMENT.

CARITAS REPORTS IMPROVEMENTS IN THE FOOD SITUATION AFTER

MASSIVE INPUTS FROM INTERNATIONAL ORGANIZATIONS, INCLUDING

THE WORLD FOOD PROGRAM. OVER THE PAST FIVE YEARS, CARITAS

PROVIDED MORE THAN 20 MILLION DOLLARS OF FOOD, AGRICULTURAL

AND DEVELOPMENT AID. ACCORDING TO CARITAS, NORTH KOREA\'S

GOVERNMENT HAS PULLED BACK SOMEWHAT FROM ENGAGEMENT WITH THE

OUTSIDE WORLD, IN PARTICULAR THE U.S. AND SOUTH KOREA, AND

HAS REDUCED ITS INTERNAL PROPAGANDA RHETORIC. CARITAS IS

COMMITTED TO MAINTAINING AN ACTIVE PRESENCE IN NORTH KOREA.

END SUMMARY.

-----------------

SITUATION UP-DATE

-----------------

2. EMBASSY OFFICERS RECEIVED A SITUATION UPDATE JANUARY 10

FROM CARITAS\' NORTH KOREAN AID COORDINATOR KATHI ZELLWEGER

WHO LAST VISITED THE DPRK IN NOVEMBER 2001, HER FIFTH ENTRY

FOR THAT YEAR. ACCORDING TO ZELLWEGER, "PEOPLE ARE LIVING ON

THE EDGE" - THE POPULATION HAS ENOUGH FOOD TO SURVIVE BUT

NOT ENOUGH TO LEAD A HEALTHY LIFE. FOOD SHORTAGES ARE

PARTICULARLY ACUTE IN THE EASTERN COASTAL DISTRICTS WHERE

ARABLE LAND IS AT A MINIMUM AND URBAN-BASED NORTH KOREANS DO

NOT HAVE ACCESS TO PLOTS OF LAND FOR SUBSISTENCE GARDENING.

CARITAS HONG KONG COORDINATES THE PROVISION OF FOOD INPUTS

AND BASIC HEALTH CARE AT THE GRASS ROOTS LEVEL. (NOTE:

CARITAS IS PART OF A FOOD AID LIAISON UNIT OPERATING OUT OF

PYONGYANG. THE UNIT ALSO INCLUDES THE WORLD FOOD PROGRAM.

END NOTE). ZELLWEGER NOTED THAT NORTH KOREAN CHILDREN ARE

STUNTED - A CLEAR SIGN OF CHRONIC MALNUTRITION. ZELLWEGER

SAYS MASSIVE FOOD AID - ONE MILLION TONS IN 2001 - HAS HAD

AN IMPACT FOR THE BETTER AND NO DEVASTATING FAMINE IS

ENVISAGED.

----------------------

ON THE POLITICAL SCENE

----------------------

3. ON THE POLITICAL SCENE, ZELLWEGER HAS NOTICED A

REDUCTION IN PROPAGANDA RHETORIC COMING FROM THE GOVERNMENT.

SHE EXPECTS THE SIXTIETH BIRTHDAY OF THE "BELOVED LEADER" TO

BE CELEBRATED BY AN INCREASE IN FOOD RATIONS AND PERHAPS BY

AN AMNESTY FOR CERTAIN CATEGORIES OF PRISONERS. ZELLWEGER

OPINED THAT EXPECTATIONS RESULTING FROM THE "SUNSHINE

SUMMIT" WERE OVER OPTIMISTIC. SHE BELIEVES FAMILY REUNIONS

NEED TO BE REVIVED AS THE OLDER GENERATION IS DYING OUT AND

YOUNGER SOUTH KOREANS ARE LOSING THEIR "LINKS" TO THE DPRK.

ACCORDING TO ZELLWEGER, THE DPRK IS "BACKING AWAY" FROM

SOUTH KOREA AND THE U.S. SHE CONSIDERS THE UP-COMING WORLD

SOCCER CUP MEDIA COVERAGE MAY HAVE A POSITIVE SPIN OFF

REFOCUSING PUBLIC ATTENTION TO NORTH KOREA\'S FOOD SHORTAGES

AND RELATED ISSUES. ZELLWEGER SAYS PROTRACTED RELIEF

OPERATIONS ARE NOT ATTRACTIVE TO POTENTIAL DONORS.

----------------

CARITAS PROJECTS

----------------

4. CARITAS SPONSORED PROJECTS IN NORTH KOREA INCLUDE A

CHILDREN\'S CLOTHING FACTORY PROVIDING WORK FOR WOMEN WHOSE

SALARIES ARE PAID IN CASH AND FOOD. CARITAS ALSO SUPPORTS

FISH FARMING - WORKING TO ENCOURAGE PRODUCTION INCREASES AND

COOPERATIVE FARMS. CARITAS PROVIDES "KITS" TO FARMS

CONTAINING TOOLS, SEEDS, MECHANICAL PARTS, FERTILIZERS AND

PLASTIC SHEETING. ZELLWEGER SAID SOME OF THE COOPERATIVES

HAVE A "FREEDOM" TO PLANT WHAT THEY WANT AND SO PRODUCE CASH

CROPS SUCH AS TOBACCO. THE MONEY RAISED FROM CASH CROPS IS

USED TO BUY OTHER ESSENTIAL REQUIREMENTS. AS WELL AS

PROVIDING FOOD AID TO THE MOST VULNERABLE - WOMEN, CHILDREN,

THE ELDERLY - CARITAS PROVIDES BASIC HEALTH CARE KITS.

ZELLWEGER SAID THE HEALTH SYSTEM IS "DISMAL" AND THE ACUTE

LACK OF SUPPLIES IS FRUSTRATING MEDICAL PERSONNEL. THE

CARITAS HEALTH CARE KITS PROVIDE BASIC EQUIPMENT TO VILLAGE

HOSPITALS AND CLINICS AS WELL AS A STABLE SUPPLY OF DRUGS -

AT LEAST TWELVE MONTHS. (NOTE: ZELLWEGER SAID THE WHO HAS AN

OFFICE IN PYONGYANG BUT THAT IT IS "DESPERATELY UNDER-

FUNDED." END NOTE).

5. AN INDEPENDENT FIVE-YEAR REVIEW OF THEIR OPERATIONS

CONDUCTED IN NOVEMBER 2001 RESULTED IN CARITAS COMMITTING TO

STAY IN NORTH KOREA FOR THE LONG HAUL, HAVING ALREADY

PROVIDED SOME 23 MILLION DOLLARS OF AID. FUTURE AID

STRATEGIES WILL BUILD ON THE EXPERIENCE OF THE PAST SIX

YEARS WITH FOOD AID, AGRICULTURE AND HEALTH AS THE MAIN

SECTORS. THE MAIN GEOGRAPHICAL FOCUS WILL BE ON THE EAST

COAST PROVINCES.

-------

COMMENT

-------

6. ZELLWEGER IS A USEFUL EMBASSY CONTACT AS HER REGULAR

SOJOURNS INTO NORTH KOREA PROVIDE US WITH A GOOD ASSESSMENT

OF THE HUMANITARIAN AND, TO A LESSER EXTENT, POLITICAL

SITUATION IN THE COUNTRY. ZELLWEGER WILL BE IN WASHINGTON IN

THE SPRING AND POST HOPES TO FACILITATE MEETINGS FOR HER

WITH STATE AND NGO OFFICIALS. SHE EXPRESSED INTEREST IN

MEETING WITH CATHOLIC RELIEF SERVICES, CARITAS\' U.S. BRANCH.

END COMMENT.
