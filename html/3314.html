SUBJECT: UN TO SEEK COMMON POLICY ON GMO FOOD AID?

1. (SBU) SUMMARY: FODAG HAS LEARNED FROM THE WORLD FOOD

PROGRAM (PLEASE PROTECT) THAT THE UN SECRETARY GENERAL HAS

ASKED HIS UNDER-SECRETARY GENERAL FOR HUMANITARIAN AFFAIRS

TO "TAKE THE LEAD IN FACILITATING THE DEVELOPMENT AND

ARTICULATION OF A COHERENT SYSTEM-WIDE POSITION ON THE ISSUE

OF IMPORTATION OF FOOD AID CONTAINING GENETICALLY MODIFIED

ORGANISMS." WE BELIEVE THAT A MORE APPROPRIATE ROLE FOR THE

UN WOULD BE TO FACILITATE THE PROVISION OF INFORMATION TO

COUNTRIES CONCERNING CURRENT NATIONAL AND INTERNATIONAL

POLICIES. END SUMMARY.

2. (SBU) THE WORLD FOOD PROGRAM (PLEASE PROTECT) PROVIDED

FODAG WITH CORRESPONDENCE BETWEEN THE UN UNDERSECRETARY

GENERAL FOR HUMANITARIAN AFFAIRS AND THE HEADS OF THE ROME

FOOD AGENCIES IN WHICH UNDERSECRETARY OSHIMA STATES THAT THE

UN SECRETARY GENERAL, "CONCERNED BY THE HUMANITARIAN

SITUATION IN ZIMBABWE, AND PARTICULARLY THE CONCERNS

EXPRESSED BY THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE ABOUT THE

IMPORTATION OF FOOD AID CONTAINING GENETICALLY MODIFIED

ORGANISMS," HAS ASKED UNDERSECRETARY OSHIMA TO "TAKE THE

LEAD IN FACILITATING THE DEVELOPMENT AND ARTICULATION OF A

COHERENT SYSTEM-WIDE POSITION ON THE ISSUE OF IMPORTATION OF

FOOD AID CONTAINING GENETICALLY MODIFIED ORGANISMS."

3. (U) THE ROME UN BODIES WITH RELEVANT POLICIES ARE: THE

UN FOOD AND AGRICULTURE ORGANIZATION\'S (FAO) CODEX

ALIMENTARIUS COMMISSION (IS IT SAFE TO EAT?) AND THE WFP

(CAN IT BE USED AS FOOD AID?). WE DO NOT KNOW IF

UNDERSECRETARY OSHIMA HAS QUERIED OTHER UN BODIES.

4. (SBU) THE WFP RESPONSE TO OSHIMA\'S REQUEST STATES THAT

WFP ACCEPTS ONLY FOOD AID WHICH MEETS FOOD SAFETY

REGULATIONS OF THE DONOR AND RECIPIENT COUNTRY AND WHICH

MEETS CODEX STANDARDS. THE LETTER FURTHER STATES THAT "IT IS

PROBABLY NOT A GOOD IDEA FOR A NONTECHNICAL BODY LIKE OCHA

TO GET INVOLVED IN SETTING POLICY IN THIS AREA, OR WE COULD

SOON BE AT ODDS WITH WTO, THE CODEX ALIMENTARIUS AND MAJOR

DONORS."

5. (SBU) THE FAO RESPONSE NOTES THAT NATIONAL POLICIES

DIFFER ON THE SUBJECT, BUT STATES THAT THE FAO\'S "POSITION"

IS THAT THE CARTAGENA PROTOCOL SHOULD BE FOLLOWED ON THE

QUESTION OF ENVIRONMENTAL SAFETY. (COMMENT: HOWEVER, THE FAO

RESPONSE INCORRECTLY CHARACTERIZES SEVERAL ASPECTS OF THE

REQUIREMENTS OF THE CARTAGENA PROTOCOL, INCLUDING IMPLYING

THAT THE ADVANCE INFORMED AGREEMENT PROVISIONS FOR LIVING

MODIFIED ORGANISMS FOR INTENTIONAL INTRODUCTION INTO THE

ENVIRONMENT WOULD APPLY TO FOOD AND STATING THAT THE

PROTOCOL STIPULATES THAT GRAIN SHIPMENTS SHOULD BE MILLED TO

ENSURE GRAIN FOR FOOD WILL NOT BE PLANTED. END COMMENT.)

6. (SBU) CONCERNING FOOD SAFETY, THE FAO RESPONSE REFERS TO

THE SCIENCE-BASED STANDARDS OF THE FAO/WHO CODEX

ALIMENTARIUS COMMISSION AND NOTES THAT THE CODEX AD HOC

INTERGOVERNMENTAL TASK FORCE ON FOODS DERIVED FROM

BIOTECHNOLOGY HAS DEVELOPED PRINCIPLES FOR THE SAFETY

ASSESSMENT OF FOODS DERIVED FROM GENETICALLY MODIFIED

SOURCES AND GUIDELINES FOR SAFETY ASSESSMENTS OF FOODS

DERIVED FROM GENETICALLY MODIFIED PLANTS. (COMMENT: THE

FAO RESPONSE DOES NOT MENTION THAT CODEX DOES NOT REQUIRE

LABELING FOR GMO PRODUCTS. END COMMENT.)

6. (SBU) THE FAO RESPONSE CONCLUDES: "FAO BELIEVES THAT

GOVERNMENTS MUST CONSIDER THE CONSEQUENCES OF STARVATION AND

MALNUTRITION FOR THE HEALTH OF THEIR POPULATIONS...AND

WHETHER THESE OUTWEIGH THE REMOTE POSSIBILITY OF INCREASED

RISKS TO HUMAN HEALTH FROM CONSUMING FOODS DERIVED FROM

MODERN BIOTECHNOLOGY AND THE ADDITIONAL RISK TO THE

ENVIRONMENT."
