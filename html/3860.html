2. (SBU) MISOFF SHARED INSTRUCTIONS CONCERNING EUROPEAN

COMMISSION (EC) MEMBERSHIP IN THE UN FOOD AND AGRICULTURE

ORGANIZATION\'S (FAO) CODEX ALIMENTARIUS COMMISSION WITH

CANADIAN DEPUTY PERM REP (DPR). CANADA CURRENTLY

REPRESENTS NORTH AMERICA ON THE FAO\'S COMMITTEE ON

CONSTITUTIONAL AND LEGAL MATTERS WHERE THIS ISSUE WILL BE

DISCUSSED OCTOBER 2-3, 2002. CANADIAN DPR STATED THAT HIS

OWN INSTRUCTIONS WERE DIFFERENT FROM U.S. INSTRUCTIONS

AND PROMISED TO CONSULT OTTAWA IMMEDIATELY (CONTACT IN

OTTAWA IS CHRIS MORAN, TEL 944-4847).

3. (SBU) ISSUE ONE: EC MEMBER STATES DEVELOPING OR

SUPPORTING THE POSITION OF THE EC WITHIN ITS COMPETENCE:

CANADA IS PREPARED TO ACCEPT. PER REFTEL, U.S. WOULD NOT

SUPPORT, BUT CONSIDER A "SPECIAL PROVISION." SUCH A

PROVISION MIGHT ALLOW MEMBER STATE REPRESENTATIVES TO

SPEAK AS PART OF THE EC DELEGATION WITHIN EXISTING FAO

RULES, PARTICULARLY IN CLARIFYING COMPLICATED TECHNICAL

MATTERS.

RECOMMENDATION: SHOULD BE DISCUSSED WITH OTTAWA TO COME

TO COMMON POSITION ALONG THE LINES OF U.S. POSITION.

U.S. POSITION RESPECTS PRINCIPLE OF "NO ADDITIONALITY"

WHICH IS THE BASIS OF CURRENT FAO PRACTICE AS REGARDS THE

EC.

4. (SBU) ISSUE TWO: EC PRESENCE DURING VOTE: CANADA IS

PREPARED TO ACCEPT EC MEMBER ABSENCE FROM ROOM DURING EC

VOTE.

RECOMMENDATION: CCLM PAPER PREPARED BY FAO LEGAL OFFICE

RECOMMENDS THAT THIS ISSUE BE DECIDED IN THE CODEX

COMMISSION ITSELF; U.S. SUPPORTS THIS ADVICE. IN

DISCUSSION WITH CANADIAN DPR, CANADA APPEARS WILLING TO

ACCEPT DEFERRING A DECISION ON THIS TO THE COMMISSION.

THIS NEEDS TO BE CONFIRMED WITH OTTAWA.

5. (SBU) ISSUE THREE: EC PARTICIPATION IN THE CODEX

EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE. CANADA IS PREPARED TO SUPPORT

ALLOWING EC TO PARTICIPATE IN THE COMMITTEE CONSISTENT

WITH CURRENT FAO PRACTICE. U.S. PROPOSAL WOULD REQUIRE

THAT THE EC REP BE RECOGNIZED TO SPEAK AS A MEMBER OF

WHATEVER EU DELEGATION WAS A MEMBER OF THE EXECUTIVE

COMMITTEE.

RECOMMENDATION: THE FAO PROPOSAL IS IN LINE WITH CURRENT

PRACTICE AT THE FAO, WHERE THE EC PARTICIPATES IN THE FAO

COUNCIL AND IN OTHER LIMITED MEMBERSHIP BODIES SUCH AS

FAO DRAFTING COMMITTEES. IN THESE INSTANCES THE EC IS

NOT CONSIDERED AN "ELECTED MEMBER" AND IS NOT ELIGIBLE TO

SERVE ON BUREAU POSITIONS; IT IS, HOWEVER, RECOGNIZED AS

THE EC AND GIVEN THE FLOOR ON ITEMS WHERE IT HAS DECLARED

COMPETENCE. IN THESE SITUATIONS THE MEMBER STATE WHICH

IS ALSO A MEMBER OF THE LIMITED MEMBERSQ BODY DOES NOT

SPEAK ON THE SAME ISSUE. THE CCLM IS LIKELY TO CONCLUDE

THAT THE U.S. PROPOSAL IS A DEVIATION FROM THE CURRENT

FAO PRACTICE. IN DISCUSSING WITH CANADIANS, THE U.S.

MIGHT SUGGEST THAT A STALEMATE AT THE CCLM IS A PREFERRED

OUTCOME (WITH THE ISSUE BEING SENT BACK TO THE CODEX

COMMISSION FOR DISCUSSION AND/OR DECISION.)

6. (SBU) COMMENT: WE RECOGNIZE THAT THE ACTIONS OF THE

CODEX COMMISSION, AS A STANDARD SETTING BODY RECOGNIZED

BY THE WORLDQADE ORGANIZATION, ARE OF A HIGHER LEVEL OF

IMPORTANCE THAN MOST OF THE DECISIONS TAKEN BY THE FAO

GOVERNING BODIES. NONETHELESS, FODAG\'S EXPERIENCE ON THE

GROUND IS THAT THE CURRENT FAO PRACTICE RESPECTS THE

PRINCIPLE OF "NO ADDITIONALITY" AND THAT EU MEMBER STATES

THEMSELVES ARE AN EFFECTIVE WATCHDOG IN ENSURING THAT THE

EC DOES NOT STRAY BEYOND ITS AREA OF COMPETENCE. HALL
