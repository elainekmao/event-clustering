2. (C) PRESIDENT TRAN DUC LUONG\'S VISIT TO THE

REPUBLIC OF KOREA RECEIVED EXTENSIVE AND USUALLY FRONT

PAGE COVERAGE IN THE VIETNAM PRESS, ADDING TO THE

GENERALLY FEEL-GOOD EMPHASIS IN BILATERAL RELATIONS

THAT HAS BEEN INCREASINGLY EVIDENT OVER THE PAST HALF-

DECADE. REF A PROVIDED AN EXCELLENT ACCOUNT OF HIS

MAJOR ACTIVITIES, AS WELL AS THE TEXT OF THE JOINT

STATEMENT.

3. (C ) ROK EMBASSY POLITICAL COUNSELOR LEE YONG JOON

PROVIDED POL/C WITH SOME ADDITIONAL INSIGHTS INTO THE

PLANNING AND OBJECTIVES OF THE VISIT ON AUGUST 24. HE

CONFIRMED THAT THE VISIT WAS MORE AIMED AT PROTOCOL

THAN SUBSTANCE. PERHAPS THE TRICKIEST PART OF THE

PLANNING, HE SAID, WAS THE REFERENCE TO A

"COMPREHENSIVE PARTNERSHIP." VIETNAMESE OFFICIALS

INITIALLY WERE NERVOUS THAT THIS TERM WOULD ALIENATE

THE NORTH KOREANS AND COMPLICATE RELATIONS WITH

PYONGYANG, BUT EVENTUALLY CONCLUDED IT WAS WORTH THE

RISK. LEE CLAIMED THAT KIM DAE JUNG\'S EXPRESSION OF

"CONDOLENCES" FOR LOSS OF LIFE DURING THE WAR WAS

KIM\'S OWN INITIATIVE, OUT OF A MISREADING THAT THE

VIETNAMESE SOUGHT SUCH AN EXPRESSION OF REMORSE.

(COMMENT: THIS IS NOT THE FIRST TIME THE KOREANS HAVE

MADE A "SPONTANEOUS" AND "UNILATERAL" STATEMENT

REGARDING THE WAR. END COMMENT) HIS OWN

INTERPRETATION FROM HIS PLANNING DISCUSSIONS HERE IS

THAT THE VIETNAMESE SIDE TRULY WISHED TO SIDESTEP THE

HISTORICAL ISSUE ENTIRELY, PROBABLY OUT OF CONCERN

THAT ITS OWN TREATMENT OF PRISONERS-OF-WAR COULD BE

USED AGAINST IT. HE NOTED THAT PRESIDENT LUONG HAD

NOT EVEN RESPONDED TO KIM\'S STATEMENT.

4. ( C ) LUONG\'S DELEGATION WAS SOMEWHAT UNUSUAL

GIVEN ITS INCLUSION OF A LARGE NUMBER OF VIETNAMESE

BUSINESS REPRESENTATIVES; THEIR PARTICIPATION ONLY

CAME UP SOMEWHAT LATE IN THE PREPARATORY PROCESS, LEE

SAID. THEIR PRESENCE AND THE NUMBER OF BUSINESS-

RELATED EVENTS ON HIS SCHEDULE WERE A CLEAR INDICATION

OF THE IMPORTANCE OF THE COMMERCIAL ASPECTS OF THE

RELATIONSHIP. LEE NOTED THAT KOREAN COMPANIES

INCREASINGLY VIEWED VIETNAM AS THE MOST IMPORTANT

ASIAN SITE FOR OFFSHORE INVESTMENT FOR KOREA\'S

ECONOMIC FUTURE, IN PARTICULAR TO COMPETE OVER THE

NEXT FIFTY YEARS WITH CHINA. HE CLAIMED THAT KOREAN

FIRMS REALIZE THAT SHORT-TERM PROFITS MAY BE NON-

EXISTENT OR FEW BUT HAD DECIDED THAT IT WAS ESSENTIAL

FOR KOREAN BUSINESSES TO GET IN ON THE GROUND FLOOR.
