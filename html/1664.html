SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) THE QUIET ATTEMPT BY THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION

(CFU) TO RE-ENGAGE WITH THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE (GOZ)

ON LAND REFORM (REFTEL) HAS REACHED AN IMPASSE. THE GOZ

IS INSISTING AS A PRECONDITION FOR FURTHER TALKS THAT

THE CFU WITHDRAW ITS LAWSUIT CHALLENGING THE

CONSTITUTIONALITY OF "FAST-TRACK", EVEN THOUGH THE

SUPREME COURT HAS ALREADY RULED IN FAVOR OF THE CFU. AT

THE SAME TIME, THE GOZ, WITH THE INVOLVEMENT OF THE AIR

FORCE, ARMY, POLICE AND THE CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE

ORGANIZATION (CIO), HAS BEGUN A NEW PUSH TO QUICKLY GET

ADDITIONAL SQUATTERS ONTO COMMERCIAL FARMS NATIONWIDE.

ON APRIL 25, THE GOZ-CONTROLLED "THE HERALD" NEWSPAPER

REPORTED THAT THE GOZ PLANS TO SUBMIT A BILL TO

PARLIAMENT THAT WILL LEGALIZE THE STATUS OF FARM

INVADERS AND PREVENT THEIR EVICTION UNTIL THE GOZ IS

ABLE TO RESETTLE THEM. LARGE-SCALE WHITE COMMERCIAL

FARMERS, WHO GROW MORE THAN 80 PERCENT OF ZIMBABWE\'S

WHEAT CROP, HAVE NOT RECEIVED GUARANTEES FROM THE GOZ

THAT THEY WILL BE ALLOWED TO HARVEST IF THEY PLANT AND

THEREFORE HAVE DELAYED PLANTING A CROP. CONTRARY TO

FOR IMPROVEMENTS TO THE LAND ON ACQUIRED FARMS, THE CFU

AND INDIVIDUAL FARMERS WE HAVE SPOKEN TO INSIST THAT TO

FARMS IN A DISTRICT SOUTHWEST OF HARARE INDICATES THAT

THE GOZ IS PROVIDING LITTLE OR NO ASSISTANCE TO THE

RESETTLED FARMERS. END SUMMARY.

--------------------------------------------- --

CFU - GOZ QUIET TALKS ON LAND REFORM DEADLOCKED

--------------------------------------------- --

2. (C) ON APRIL 27, ECONOFF SPOKE WITH TIM HENWOOD

(PROTECT), PRESIDENT OF THE CFU, AT THE ZIMBABWE

INTERNATIONAL TRADE FAIR IN BULAWAYO. HENWOOD SAID THAT

THE QUIET TALKS INITIATED BY THE CFU WITH THE GOZ TO TRY

AND FIND A WAY FORWARD ON LAND REFORM HAVE REACHED AN

IMPASSE. THE GOZ IS INSISTING THAT THE CFU MUST

WITHDRAW ITS LITIGATION AGAINST THE GOZ OVER THE "FAST-

TRACK" RESETTLEMENT PROGRAM BEFORE THE GOZ WILL TALK

FURTHER WITH THE CFU. THE CFU NEGOTIATING TEAM WAS TOLD

BY GOZ OFFICIALS THAT THE LAWSUIT, AND THE SUPREME COURT

JUDGEMENT IN THE CFU\'S FAVOR ON THE SUIT, IS VERY

EMBARRASSING TO PRESIDENT MUGABE. HOWEVER, HENWOOD SAID

THAT THE CFU CANNOT WITHDRAW A LAWSUIT UPON WHICH A

COURT HAS ALREADY RENDERED JUDGEMENT.

A GOZ BILL TO LEGALIZE THE FARM INVADERS

----------------------------------------

4. (C) ON APRIL 25, THE GOZ-CONTROLLED "THE HERALD"

NEWSPAPER REPORTED THAT THE GOZ IS GOING TO SUBMIT A

BILL TO PARLIAMENT THAT WILL LEGALIZE THE STATUS OF

ILLEGAL OCCUPIERS ON COMMERCIAL FARMS. THE BILL WILL

NULLIFY SUPREME COURT AND HIGH COURT RULINGS REQUIRING

THAT ILLEGAL OCCUPIERS BE IMMEDIATELY EVICTED FROM

COMMERCIAL FARMS AND OTHER OCCUPIED LANDS. THE BILL

INCLUDES SPECIFIC PROVISIONS REGARDING WHEN INDIVIDUALS

MUST HAVE OCCUPIED FARMS, AND ENTITLES THEM TO LEGAL

PROTECTION FROM EVICTION FOR SPECIFIC PERIODS UNTIL THE

GOZ IS ABLE TO RESETTLE THEM. THE BILL GIVES PRESIDENT

MUGABE, ALL CABINET MINISTERS, AND ANY OTHER INDIVIDUALS

DESIGNATED BY THE PRESIDENT, AUTHORITY TO CERTIFY ANY

FARM INVADERS AS "PROTECTED PERSONS" WHO CANNOT BE

EVICTED FROM THE FARMS THEY OCCUPY. (COMMENT: RECENT

GOZ PRACTICE INDICATES THAT THE RULING PARTY WILL FAST-

TRACK THE BILL THROUGH PARLIAMENT, WITH LITTLE OR NO

DEBATE AND NO AMENDMENTS, AND ITS LEGAL PROVISIONS WILL

THEN BE INTERPRETED LIBERALLY BY THE GOZ. END COMMENT.)

COMMERCIAL FARMERS NOT PLANTING WHEAT

-------------------------------------

5. (C) AS OF APRIL 27, COMMERCIAL FARMERS WHO NORMALLY

GROW OVER EIGHTY PERCENT OF ZIMBABWE\'S WHEAT AND PLANT

AT THE END OF APRIL, HAD NOT BEGUN PLANTING THIS YEAR\'S

CROP. CFU PRESIDENT HENWOOD TOLD ECONOFF THAT THE

FARMERS HAD STILL NOT RECEIVED ANY GUARANTEE FROM THE

GOZ THAT THEY WILL BE ABLE TO HARVEST A WHEAT CROP IF

PLANTED. WITHOUT SUCH A GUARANTEE, ZIMBABWE\'S BANKS

WILL NOT LOAN MONEY FOR WHEAT PLANTING, AND THE FARMERS

THEMSELVES WILL NOT RISK THEIR PERSONAL CAPITAL ON

PLANTING A CROP THEY MAY NOT BE ALLOWED TO HARVEST AND

SELL. IN ADDITION, HENWOOD SAID THAT MOST OF ZIMBABWE\'S

LARGEST WHEAT GROWERS ARE IN THE THREE MASHONALAND

PROVINCES, WHERE WAR VETS HAVE RECENTLY BECOME MORE

AGGRESSIVE IN INTERFERING WITH FARM OPERATIONS.

ZIMBABWE ONLY HAS AN ESTIMATED TWO-MONTH SUPPLY OF WHEAT

REMAINING, AND IF THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS ARE NOT ALLOWED

TO PLANT WHEAT IN THE NEXT TWO WEEKS, ZIMBABWE WILL BE

REQUIRED TO IMPORT TENS OF THOUSANDS OF ADDITIONAL TONS

OF WHEAT OVER ITS NORMAL IMPORT REQUIREMENTS AT A TIME

WHEN THE GOZ HAS NO HARD CURRENCY. WHEAT IS AN

IRRIGATED CROP IN ZIMBABWE, AND THE MAJORITY OF COMMUNAL

FARMERS DO NOT HAVE THE IRRIGATION SYSTEMS REQUIRED FOR

LEFT IF COMMERCIAL FARMERS DO NOT PLANT.

GOZ NOT PAYING FARMERS FOR FARMS ALREADY ACQUIRED

FINANCIALLY HIGH-AND-DRY. IN ONE EXAMPLE, A CHEGUTU

PIECES OF LAND, LET ALONE PAYMENT FOR IMPROVEMENTS TO

THE LAND, WHEN ECONOFF VISITED HIM ON APRIL 10. IN

ADDITION, THE MAIN PIECE OF LAND THAT IS THE HEART OF

RESETTLED ZIMBABWEANS ARE LEFT BLOWING IN THE WIND

--------------------------------------------- -----

7. (C) ON APRIL 10, ECONOFF TOURED CHEGUTU DISTRICT

SOUTHWEST OF HARARE WITH ED GUNDRY (PROTECT), THE

CHAIRMAN OF THE CFU MASHONALAND WEST - SOUTH REGION

(WITHIN WHICH CHEGUTU DISTRICT FALLS), AND BEN FREETH

(PROTECT), THE REGIONAL EXECUTIVE OFFICER OF THIS SAME

DISTRICT. IN ADDITION TO VISITING TWO COMMERCIAL FARMS

WHERE TOBACCO IS THE PRINCIPAL CROP AND CATTLE RANCHING

IS A SIDE VENTURE, ECONOFF VISITED TWO FARMS IN CHEGUTU

THAT HAVE BEEN "RESETTLED" BY THE GOZ -- ONE DURING

1998, AND THE OTHER SINCE "FAST-TRACK" BEGAN IN JUNE OF

LAST YEAR. BOTH OF THESE FARMS WERE FORMERLY LARGE-

SCALE TOBACCO AND CATTLE RANCHING OPERATIONS. THE SOIL

ON THESE FARMS IS VERY SANDY, MAKING IT IDEAL FOR

GROWING TOBACCO, AND SUITABLE FOR CATTLE RANCHING GIVEN

SUFFICIENT GRAZING AREA. THIS SOIL IS NOT WELL SUITED

FOR GROWING MAIZE OR OTHER CROPS WITHOUT EXTENSIVE USE

OF FERTILIZER AS WELL AS CROP ROTATION.

8. (C) THE FARM THAT WAS RESETTLED IN JUNE LAST YEAR WAS

NEARLY DESERTED. ACCORDING TO FREETH, A YEAR EARLIER IT

HAD BEEN A THRIVING TOBACCO FARM WITH A LARGE CATTLE

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 01492 03 OF 04 021426Z

HERD. NOW VISIBLE WERE OCCASIONAL THATCH-ROOFED MUD

HUTS, THE MAJORITY OF WHICH APPEARED UNOCCUPIED.

ACCORDING TO FREETH, MOST OF THOSE RESETTLED LEFT

SHORTLY AFTER BUILDING THE HUTS WHEN THEY REALIZED HOW

POOR THE SOIL WAS FOR GROWING MAIZE AND THAT THEY WERE

NOT GOING TO GET ASSISTANCE FROM THE GOZ. IN EVIDENCE

WERE A FEW SMALL PATCHES OF STUNTED MAIZE CROPS, NONE OF

WHICH WERE GOING TO PRODUCE EDIBLE MAIZE. WEEDS WERE

GROWING IN THE FORMER TOBACCO FIELDS AND THE GRAZING

AREAS WERE IN THE PROCESS OF BEING OVERTAKEN BY SCRUB.

9. (C) THE FARM RESETTLED IN 1998 WAS MARGINALLY

DIFFERENT IN CONDITION FROM THE ONE RESETTLED AFTER

"FAST-TRACK" LAST JUNE. THIS PROPERTY HAD MORE FAMILIES

VISIBLY LIVING ON SITE IN THEIR MUD HUTS, WITH MORE

PLOTS OF STUNTED MAIZE, AS WELL AS COTTON, MOST OF WHICH

HAD NOT FLOWERED TO PRODUCE A CROP. THERE WAS ALSO THE

OCCASIONAL COW VISIBLE. THE FORMER TOBACCO FIELDS,

EXCEPT FOR THE SCATTERED PLOTS OF STUNTED MAIZE AND

COTTON, WERE COMPLETELY OVERGROWN WITH WEEDS. THERE WAS

NO INDICATION THAT THE GOZ HAD PROVIDED ANY

INFRASTRUCTURE ASSISTANCE, SUCH AS DAMS, IRRIGATION

SYSTEMS OR BUILDINGS IN THE MORE THAN TWO YEARS SINCE

RESETTLEMENT. WHILE THE GOZ MAY HAVE PROVIDED SEED

PACKS AND FERTILIZER TO THOSE RESETTLED, THIS WAS

CLEARLY NOT SUFFICIENT ASSISTANCE EVEN TO MAKE THEM

SUCCESSFUL SUBSISTENCE FARMERS.
