SUBJECT: IMPLEMENTATION OF USAID POLICIES AND PROGRAMS ON

HIV/AIDS AND TRAFFICKING

REF: 02 STATE 267675

1. SUMMARY: IN RESPONSE TO REFTEL, USAID/CROATIA IS

CURRENTLY FUNDING TWO ACTIVITIES FOR TRAFFICKING IN PERSONS

(TIP) AND ONE FOR HIV/AIDS AND PLANS TO FUND TWO ADDITIONAL

HIV/AIDS ACTIVITIES IN MID-MARCH. ALL PROGRAMS AND

PUBLICATIONS ASSOCIATED WITH THESE CONTRACTS APPROPRIATELY

REFLECT USG POLICIES NOTED IN REFTEL. END SUMMARY.

2. USAID/CROATIA IS FUNDING ONE HIV/AIDS ACTIVITY WHICH

FOCUSES ON PUBLIC EDUCATION AND OUTREACH IN THE SEAPORT CITY

OF SPLIT. IMPLEMENTATION IS THROUGH THE AMERICAN

INTERNATIONAL HEALTH ALLIANCE (AIHA) IN PARTNERSHIP WITH THE

CITY OF SPLIT AND A LOCAL NGO. THE HIV/AIDS PROJECT

INCLUDES THREE INTERVENTION AREAS: PEER-EDUCATION;

DEVELOPMENT OF COMMUNITY AWARENESS MATERIALS; AND A MASS

MEDIA CAMPAIGN. TWO ADDITIONAL ACTIVITIES WITH NGOS WILL BE

FUNDED IN MID-MARCH, BOTH OF WHICH WILL WORK ON COUNSELING,

PUBLIC EDUCATION AND YOUTH AT RISK.

3. USAID/CROATIA IS FUNDING TWO ANTI-TRAFFICKING ACTIVITIES,

ONE WITH THE INTERNATIONAL CATHOLIC MIGRATION COMMISSION

(ICMC) IN COOPERATION WITH THE ROSA CENTER (A LOCAL NGO),

AND ONE WITH THE INTERNATIONAL ORGANIZATION FOR MIGRATION

(IOM). BOTH ACTIVITIES FOCUS ON RAISING AWARENESS AMONG THE

PUBLIC, THE MEDIA, THE LEGAL SECTOR AND POLICY MAKERS

THROUGH TARGETED CAMPAIGNS AND TRAINING. IOM\'S AWARENESS

CAMPAIGN IS AIMED AT EDUCATING THE PUBLIC, THE MEDIA, NGO

COMMUNITY AND GOVERNMENTAL OFFICIALS - TO UNDERSTAND THAT

TRAFFICKED PERSONS ARE VICTIMS AND TRAFFICKING IN HUMAN

BEINGS CONSTITUTES A GROSS VIOLATION OF HUMAN RIGHTS.

TRAINING FOR JOURNALISTS THROUGH THE IOM\'S PROJECT WILL

ENSURE ACCURATE AND NON-BIASED REPORTING ON TIP ISSUES. THE

OVERALL GOAL OF THE ICMC--ROSA CENTER ACTIVITY IS TO BUILD

CAPACITY OF LOCAL NGOS AND GOVERNMENTAL INSTITUTIONS THROUGH

TRAININGS INCLUDING SUB-ACTIVITIES SUCH AS INFORMATION AND

AWARENESS CAMPAIGNS. IN ADDITION THIS ACTIVITY WILL PUBLISH

A QUARTERLY NEWSLETTER. RECENTLY, ICMC AND THE ROSA CENTER

ORGANIZED A SEMINAR FOR CROATIAN JUDGES AND ATTORNEYS FOR

THE PURPOSE OF INCREASING AWARENESS OF THE LEGISLATIVE AND

JUDICIAL ISSUES OF RECOGNIZING AND PROSECUTING TRAFFICKERS.

4. USAID/CROATIA REVIEWS ALL DRAFT PUBLICATIONS AND MEDIA

PLANS IN BOTH TRAFFICKING AND HIV/AIDS ACTIVITIES PRIOR TO

PUBLICATION AND BROADCAST TO ENSURE THEY ARE CONSISTENT WITH

U.S. LAWS AND ADMINISTRATION POLICIES.

5. USAID/CROATIA DOES NOT FULLY OR PARTIALLY FUND ANY

WEBSITE IN ITS GRANTS TO COMBAT HIV/AIDS OR TRAFFICKING.

HOWEVER, WE HAVE REVIEWED OUR OWN WEBSITE AND THOSE OF THE

CURRENT IMPLEMENTERS AND CONFIRM THAT THE MATERIAL IS
