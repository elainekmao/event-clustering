SUBJECT: TERRORIST FINANCE: INITIAL TURKISH RESPONSE ON

CHECHENS, HEKMATYAR, ANSAR AL-ISLAM, LASHKAR

REF: A. SECSTATE 46849

B. SECSTATE 44222

C. SECSTATE 39386

D. SECSTATE 26891

(U) Classified by Economic Counselor Scot Marciel for reasons

1.5 (b), (d).

1. (S) On February 18 and 21, MFA Department Head for

Intelligence and Research Nihat Ersen and MFA

counter-terrorism officer Firat Sunel responded to our

demarches on four terrorist asset freeze requests (reftels).

Two of the four involved pre-notifications of upcoming UN

designations (Chechen groups and Ansar al-Islam), and we

stressed the need to act quickly on both requests.

2. (S) Ersen and Sunel said the names on the four demarches

would be circulated interagency to GOT intelligence agencies,

Treasury, banking regulatory board and Central Bank to

research possible financial links to Turkey. If there were

any assets under these names in Turkey, they would be blocked

informally. However, formal, legal blocking of assets

required a Council of Ministers decree, and GOT practice was

to include names on such decrees only after they have been

designated by the UN Counter-Terrorism Committee. The MFA

was now preparing a draft decree and would include on it

about 20 names recently designated by the UN (including

Laashkar i Jhangvi).

3. (U) Sunel updated us on the reaction to the last Council

of Ministers decree, issued in October 2002, which included

the Nasreddin Holding company in Istanbul. This company\'s

assets had been blocked, and Sunel said he would find out the

dollar amount involved and inform us. The MFA had recently

received a letter from the company\'s Turkish lawyers

threatening a lawsuit if the assets were not unblocked.
