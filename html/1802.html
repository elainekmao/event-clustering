SUBJECT: HANOI UPDATE #13

1. (U) FLOODING IN NORTHERN VIETNAM: RAINY SEASON IS UPON

US. TYPHOON NO. 2 (DURIAN) WAS DOWNGRADED TO A TROPICAL

DEPRESSION JUST BEFORE IT HIT THE MOUNTAINOUS REGIONS OF

NORTHERN VIETNAM JULY 2-4 CAUSING FLOODING AND MUDSLIDES,

KILLING AT LEAST 30 PEOPLE AND DAMAGING NEARLY OVER 30,000

HOMES. HARDEST HIT, BY WHAT IS BEING CALLED THE WORST

FLOODING TO HIT NORTHERN VIETNAM IN YEARS, ARE THE REGIONS

BORDERING THE THAI NGUYEN, CAU AND LO RIVERS, AND AT LEAST

SIX PROVINCES HAVE BEEN SERIOUSLY AFFECTED. LOCAL DISASTER

RELIEF EFFORTS ARE REACHING OUT TO THE VICTIMS. THE GVN HAS

NOT ISSUED A CALL FOR INTERNATIONAL DISASTER RELIEF. BUT,

TYPHOON NO. 3 (UTOR), WHICH WREAKED HAVOC IN THE PHILIPPINES

AND TAIWAN, HIT CHINA LAST FRIDAY, AND ALTHOUGH IT TOO WAS

DOWNGRADED TO A TROPICAL DEPRESSION, IT APPEARS TO BE HEADED

TO HIT NORTHERN VIETNAM SHORTLY. THE GOVERNMENT HAS WARNED

PEOPLE TO BE PREPARED. THE QUESTION REMAINS JUST HOW MUCH

MORE RAIN THE WATER-LOGGED HILLSIDES AND SWOLLEN RIVERS CAN

MANAGE WITHOUT CAUSING A MORE WIDESPREAD DISASTER. HYDRO-

METEOROLOGY OFFICE PERSONNEL TELL US, HOWEVER, THERE IS NO

IMMEDIATE THREAT IN THE RED RIVER (HANOI AREA) DESPITE THE

GENERALLY RISING WATERS IN NORTHERN RIVERS. POST WILL

CONTINUE TO FOLLOW THE SITUATION CLOSELY.

2. (U) SON LA HYDROELECTRIC FACILITY APPROVED: AFTER YEARS

OF DEBATE, VIETNAM IS COMING CLOSER TO CONSTRUCTING A HUGE

NEW HYDROELECTRIC FACILITY IN SON LAW PROVINCE. IN THE

CLOSING DAYS ITS MOST RECENT SESSION, VIETNAM\'S NATIONAL

ASSEMBLY OFFICIALLY "APPROVED IN PRINCIPLE" CONSTRUCTION OF

THE MULTI-BILLION DOLLAR SON LA HYDROELECTRIC POWER PROJECT,

WHILE STILL RESERVING ON HOW BIG THE DAM PROJECT WILL

ACTUALLY BE. TWO WEEKS BEFORE, THE POLITBURO HAD APPROVED

THE PROJECT. AND DESPITE VOCAL OBJECTIONS FROM A NUMBER OF

ASSEMBLY DELEGATES, VIETNAM\'S POLITICAL LEADERSHIP PRESSED

FORWARD WITH THE VOTE. THE DAM AND POWER FACILITY ARE TO BE

LOCATED ON THE DA RIVER UPSTREAM FROM THE HOA BINH

HYDROELECTRIC DAM. THREE OPTIONS HAVE BEEN PROPOSED, THE

LARGEST OF WHICH WOULD INUNDATE 440 SQ. KILOMETERS, DISPLACE

UP TO 100,000 OF THE MOSTLY INDIGENOUS LOCAL POPULATION, AND

COST UP TO US$ 4 BILLION. THE GOVERNMENT WILL NOW PRODUCE

ADDITIONAL INFORMATION ON EACH OF THE OPTIONS, AND THE

NATIONAL ASSEMBLY WILL HOLD A FINAL VOTE DURING ITS FALL

SESSION. AT PRESENT, PLANS ARE TO COMPLETE THE PROJECT BY

2016.

3. (U) SON LA DAM CON\'T: SUPPORTERS ARGUE THAT VIETNAM

URGENTLY NEEDS EXPANDED ELECTRICITY GENERATING CAPACITY AND

SHOULD NOT RELY ONLY ON GAS-FIRED POWER FACILITIES WHICH

HAVE BEEN OR WILL BE CONSTRUCTED IN THE VUNG TAU-BA RIA AND

WILL USE GAS FROM THE HUGE NAM CON SON GAS FIELD. IN

ADDITION, THEY ARGUE THAT THIS DAM WILL BE USED FOR

IRRIGATION IN THE SURROUNDING PROVINCES AND TO CONTROL

FLOODING DOWNSTREAM ON THE RED RIVER AND THAI BINH DELTAS

DURING THE RAINY SEASON.

4. (U) SON LA DAM CON\'T: BUT THE DELEGATES IN OPPOSITION

VOICED MANY OF THE SAME OBJECTIONS THAT HAVE BEEN VOICED FOR

YEARS. FIRST, IT IS ARGUED THERE WILL BE VAST ENVIRONMENTAL

DAMAGE IN THE CATCHMENT AREAS SURROUNDING THE DAM DUE TO

FLOODING AND TREE LOSS AS WELL AS DOWNSTREAM. SAFETY

CONCERNS ARE PERHAPS EVEN MORE PRESSING. THE DAM WOULD BE

LOCATED IN AN EARTHQUAKE PRONE REGION AND IT IS FEARED THAT

A BREACH IN THE DAM WOULD CAUSE THE HOA BINH DAM TO

COLLAPSE, INUNDATING HANOI IN UP TO FIVE STORIES OF WATER IN

A MATTER OF HOURS. THESE ARGUMENTS GAINED GREATER CREDENCE

EARLIER THIS YEAR AFTER NEAR-BY LAI CHAU PROVINCE WAS HIT

WITH AN EARTHQUAKE MEASURING 5.3 ON THE RICHTER SCALE AND

CAUSING SEVERAL MILLION DOLLARS WORTH OF STRUCTURAL DAMAGE.

5. (U) SON LA DAM CON\'T: OTHERS ARGUE THAT THERE HAS NOT

BEEN SUFFICIENT THOUGHT GIVEN TO THE LOGISTICS AND FINANCES

REQUIRED TO RELOCATE ALL THOSE THAT WILL BE DISPLACED.

SPECIFICALLY, MANY WORRY THAT THE CURRENT PLAN TO SEND THE

DISPLACED POPULATION TO THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS WILL FURTHER

DESTABILIZE THIS REGION WHICH WAS ROCKED BY PROTESTS ON THE

PART OF SOME OF THE REGION\'S INDIGENOUS POPULATION IN

FEBRUARY.

6. (U) SON LA DAM CON\'T: FINALLY, THERE ARE SERIOUS

QUESTIONS ABOUT HOW VIETNAM WILL FINANCE THE PROJECT. IN

THE PAST, THE VIETNAMESE GOVERNMENT HAS INDICATED IT WILL

FINANCE THE PROJECT OUT OF THE STATE BUDGET. HOWEVER, MORE

RECENTLY VIETNAM NEWS QUOTED ONE OFFICIAL AS SAYING THAT 30

PERCENT OF THE FUNDING WOULD COME FROM FOREIGN LOANS.

HOWEVER, WORLD BANK AND OTHER DONORS WILL BE UNLIKELY TO

PROVIDE FUNDING GIVEN CONCERNS ABOUT SAFETY, THE ENVIRONMENT

AND DISPLACED PERSONS. AND FOREIGN INVESTMENT IS UNLIKELY

TO BE AN OPTION, EVEN IF THE GVN IS WILLING TO CONSIDER

PERMITTING IT, GIVEN THE PROBLEMS VIETNAM HAS HAD IN THE

PAST COMING TO AGREEMENT WITH PRIVATE INVESTORS ON OTHER

ELECTRICITY GENERATION PROJECTS.

7. (U) SON LA DAM - COMMENT: FOR NOW AT LEAST, THE

VIETNAMESE LEADERSHIP SEEMS DETERMINED TO IGNORE THE

QUESTIONS AND MOVE FORWARD. BUT EVENTUALLY, THESE CONCERNS

WILL HAVE TO BE ADDRESSED. ONE HOPES THAT THIS OCCURS

SOONER RATHER THAN LATER WHEN CONSTRUCTION IS STARTED,

PEOPLE ARE MOVING AND FUNDS BEING SPENT. COMMENT
