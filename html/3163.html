SUBJECT: DRC PRESIDENT KABILA SUPPORTS NEW REWARDS FOR

1. (C) SUMMARY. DRC PRESIDENT JOSEPH KABILA SAID THE NEW

REWARDS FOR JUSTICE IN RWANDA PROGRAM (REFTEL) HAS 100

PERCENT OF HIS PERSONAL SUPPORT, AS WELL AS THAT OF THE

GOVERNMENT, BUT HE ADDED THAT THE PROGRAM MUST LEAD TO

RWANDA\'S WITHDRAWAL FROM THE DRC OR IT WON\'T SUCCEED. END

SUMMARY.

2. (C) WHILE IN ROME FOR THE FAO SUMMIT, KABILA MET WITH

AMBASSADOR-AT-LARGE FOR WAR CRIMES PIERRE PROSPER AND DR.

JENDAYI FRAZIER (NSC) ON JUNE 13 TO DISCUSS THE RECENTLY

LAUNCHED REWARDS FOR JUSTICE IN RWANDA PROGRAM. AMBASSADOR

PROSPER URGED KABILA TO SUPPORT THE INITIATIVE, WHICH FOCUSES

ON NINE TO FIFTEEN KEY SUSPECTS INDICTED FOR GENOCIDE BY THE

ICTR, AS A MEANS OF REMOVING RWANDA\'S EXCUSE FOR OCCUPYING

PARTS OF THE DRC. AFTER OFFERING HIS FULL SUPPORT, KABILA

CAUTIONED THAT HIS PEOPLE MAY NOT TAKE THE IDEA SERIOUSLY.

KABILA STATED THAT HIS PEOPLE WOULD ALSO WANT TO SEE

ACCOUNTABILITY FOR THE MILLIONS OF CONGOLESE DEATHS OVER THE

PAST FEW YEARS IN THE EASTERN CONGO, INCLUDING THOSE WHO DIED

IN KISANGANI ONLY WEEKS AGO. HE ENCOURAGED AMB. PROSPER TO

BE PREPARED TO ANSWER THE TOUGH QUESTIONS HE WOULD ENCOUNTER

FROM AVERAGE CITIZENS OR THE PROGRAM WOULD LOSE CREDIBILITY.

OUR PRIORITY IS TO SEE RWANDA OUT OF OUR COUNTRY, KABILA

STRESSED.

3. (C) BOTH AMB. PROSPER AND DR. FRAZIER EMPHASIZED THAT THE

PROGRAM IS ONE PART OF A MULTIFACETED APPROACH TO RESTORING

STABILITY IN THE REGION. IF THIS LEADERSHIP GROUP CAN BE

REMOVED FROM THE EQUATION, RWANDA WILL NO LONGER BE ABLE TO

CLAIM THAT A SECURITY THREAT EXISTS THAT WARRANTS ITS

OCCUPATION OF PARTS OF THE DRC. THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY

CAN THEN PRESSURE RWANDA TO WITHDRAW. KABILA COMMENTED THAT

HE WOULD PREFER MILITARY PRESSURE TO POLITICAL PRESSURE FOR

SUCH A COMPLICATED PROBLEM, BUT REAFFIRMED HIS SUPPORT FOR

THE REWARDS PROGRAM. HE INVITED AMB. PROSPER TO PRESENT THE

PROGRAM IN KINSHASA THE SECOND WEEK IN JULY. AMB. PROSPER

ADDED THAT HE HAD MET WITH RWANDAN PRESIDENT KAGAME ABOUT THE

PROGRAM AND HAD EXPLAINED THAT A RWANDAN WITHDRAWAL WAS A

PRINCIPAL OBJECTIVE.

4. (C) KABILA ASKED HOW THE INITIATIVE WOULD BE COORDINATED

WITH THE ICTR. AMB. PROSPER EXPLAINED THAT HE HAD MET WITH

CARLA DEL PONTE AND INVESTIGATOR WALPIN, AND HAD SET UP A

TASK FORCE IN KENYA TO RESPOND TO LEADS ON ONE OF THE

PRINCIPAL INDICTEES, KABUGA. A SIMILAR SET-UP WAS ENVISIONED

FOR KINSHASA, WITH AN AMERICAN LINKED TO ARUSHA AND WORKING

WITH THE CONGOLESE TO FOLLOW UP ON TIPS.

5. (C) KABILA SAID A SOLUTION WAS NEEDED FOR THOSE RWANDESE

BEING HELD IN KAMINA AND THE FOOT SOLDIERS. AMB. PROSPER

OFFERED THAT THE REWARDS PROGRAM WOULD HELP HERE TOO BY

"CUTTING OFF THE HEAD OF THE SNAKE," LEAVING LOWER-LEVEL

SOLDIERS. AMB. PROSPER NOTED THAT RWANDAN OFFICIALS HAVE

SAID THAT RWANDA WILL ACCEPT THE REPATRIATION OF THESE FOOT

SOLDIERS ONCE THE LEADERS INDICTED BY THE ICTR HAVE BEEN

APPREHENDED. WHEN PRESSED BY KABILA AS TO HOW LONG IT WOULD

TAKE, AMB. PROSPER REPLIED THAT KABILA\'S HELP WITH

APPREHENDING THE INDICTEES WAS CRUCIAL; WORKING TOGETHER,

WITH THE ICTR, MONUS AND OTHER ENTITIES, TO TAKE THEM INTO

CUSTODY WOULD TAKE A "REASONABLE AMOUNT OF TIME, NOT YEARS,"

AMB. PROSPER ESTIMATED. HE STRESSED, HOWEVER, THAT THE

ACTUAL APPREHENSION WOULD HAVE TO BE DONE BY THE DRC.

APPREHENDING THE LEADERS WOULD NOT RESULT IN RWANDA\'S

WITHDRAWAL FROM THE DRC THE NEXT DAY, DR. FRAZIER ADMITTED,

BUT IT WOULD BE A KEY ELEMENT IN THE OVERALL EFFORT TO END

THE CONFLICT AND WOULD BRING TREMENDOUS INTERNATIONAL

PRESSURE ON RWANDA TO WITHDRAW FROM THE DRC.

6. (C) IN TALKING WITH MEMBERS OF THE PRESS FOLLOWING THE

MEETING, AMB. PROSPER NOTED KABILA\'S SUPPORT FOR THE REWARDS

PROGRAM, AND STRESSED THE NEED TO ENSURE THESE EFFORTS LEAD

TO A RWANDAN WITHDRAWAL FROM THE DRC. HE MENTIONED KAGAME

HAD ALSO EXPRESSED SUPPORT FOR THE INITIATIVE, AND EMPHASIZED

THAT IT WOULD TAKE A TEAM EFFORT TO SUCCEED IN BRINGING THE

INDICTEES TO JUSTICE. IN RESPONSE TO A QUESTION ABOUT MONUC,

KABILA TOLD THE PRESS THAT THE REUNIFICATION OF THE DRC IS

THE PRIORITY - ANYTHING THAT ISN\'T MOVING TOWARD THAT IS A

WASTE OF TIME AND MONEY.

7. (U) THIS CABLE HAS BEEN CLEARED BY AMBASSADOR PROSPER.
