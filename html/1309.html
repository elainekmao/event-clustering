SUBJECT: GOVERNMENT FLOUTS LAW AND SHUTS DOWN NEW RADIO

STATION

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD FOR REASONS 1.5

B/D.

1. (C) SUMMARY: DURING THE EVENING OF OCTOBER 4, THE

GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE WILLFULLY IGNORED A COURT ORDER

AND SHUT DOWN THE RECENTLY LAUNCHED PRIVATE BROADCASTER

CAPITAL RADIO. HEAVILY ARMED POLICE (SEVERAL WERE

CARRYING AK47 ASSAULT RIFLES) APPEARED AT THE RADIO\'S

MAKESHIFT STUDIO IN THE 5-STAR CROWNE PLAZA HOTEL IN

DOWNTOWN HARARE AT ABOUT 1600 ON OCTOBER 4 AND DEMANDED

THAT THE STATION CEASE BROADCASTING. LAWYERS FOR THE

RADIO STATION WERE ABLE TO DELAY SHUTDOWN OF THE RADIO

FOR SEVERAL HOURS, AND OBTAINED A COURT ORDER THAT

BARRED THE POLICE FROM SEIZING THE STATION\'S EQUIPMENT.

THE POLICE INITIALLY INDICATED THAT THEY WOULD COMPLY

WITH THE COURT ORDER, BUT RETURNED AT ABOUT 2300, BROKE

DOWN THE DOOR TO THE STUDIO, AND SEIZED THE EQUIPMENT.

DURING THE SAME PERIOD OF TIME, UNIFORMED POLICE AND

UNIDENTIFIED CIVILIAN GOVERNMENT OFFICIALS (PROBABLY CIO

AGENTS) APPEARED AT THE HOMES OF CAPITAL RADIO\'S MAJOR

PLAYERS. THEY ENTERED AND SEARCHED THE HOME OF STATION

CO-OWNER GERRY JACKSON, AND ESTABLISHED PRESENCE AT, BUT

DID NOT ENTER, THE HOMES OF OTHER PEOPLE NOW OR

PREVIOUSLY ASSOCIATED WITH THE STATION. THE TIMING OF

THE POLICE ACTION AGAINST CAPITAL RADIO\'S BROADCAST

FACILITIES AND KEY PERSONNEL COINCIDES WITH PRESIDENT

MUGABE\'S SIGNING OF A NEW SET OF REGULATIONS GOVERNING

COMMERCIAL BROADCASTING IN ZIMBABWE. THE ELEMENTS OF

THESE NEW REGULATIONS ARE REPORTED IN A SEPARATE

TELEGRAM. CAPITAL RADIO IS NOW OFF THE AIR AND FACES A

FUTURE OF LEGAL BATTLES. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) ACCORDING TO CAPITAL RADIO CO-OWNER GERRY

JACKSON AND PRESS ACCOUNTS, A POLICE "SUPPORT UNIT"

ARMED WITH AK47 ASSAULT RIFLES ARRIVED AT THE RADIO

STATION\'S MAKESHIFT STUDIOS ON THE 16TH FLOOR OF THE 5-

STAR CROWNE PLAZA HOTEL IN DOWNTOWN HARARE AT ABOUT 1600

ON OCTOBER 4. THE POLICE WERE ACCOMPANIED BY MEMBERS OF

ZIMBABWE\'S CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE ORGANIZATION (CIO).

DISCUSSIONS BETWEEN THE POLICE, LED BY ASSISTANT

COMMISSIONER LIBERMAN NDLOVU, AND CAPITAL RADIO\'S

LAWYERS ANTHONY BROOKS AND JONATHAN SAMKANGE, DELAYED

POLICE ACTION FOR SEVERAL HOURS. NDLOVU INITIALLY TOLD

BROOKS THAT HE WAS "WAITING FOR A SEARCH WARRANT." THIS

GAVE THE STATION\'S LAWYERS TIME TO OBTAIN AN ORDER FROM

HIGH COURT JUDGE ESHMAIL CHATIKOBO BARRING POLICE

SEIZURE OF EQUIPMENT. THAT ORDER EXPIRES AT 1600 ON

OCTOBER 5. ASSISTANT COMMISSIONER NDLOVU INITIALLY SAID

THAT HE WOULD RESPECT THE ORDER. ACCORDING TO JACKSON,

NDLOVU THEN RECEIVED A CALL FROM INFORMATION MINISTER

JONATHAN MOYO, AFTER WHICH HE TOLD THE RADIO\'S LAWYERS

THAT HE WOULD NOT RESPECT THE COURT ORDER. NONETHELESS,

POLICE LEFT THE HOTEL AT ABOUT 2230 WITHOUT HAVING

ENTERED THE HOTEL ROOM SERVING AS THE STATION\'S STUDIO.

AT THAT POINT, THE RADIO\'S LAWYERS ALSO LEFT THE HOTEL.

ABOUT 30 MINUTES LATER, AT 2300, HOTEL MANAGEMENT CALLED

JACKSON TO INFORM HER THAT THE POLICE HAD RETURNED, WERE

BREAKING DOWN THE DOOR TO THE STUDIO AND SEIZING THE

EQUIPMENT. THE STUDIO WAS AUTOMATED AND NO STATION

STAFF WERE PRESENT AT THE TIME OF THE SEIZURE. CAPITAL

RADIO DOES NOT KNOW WHERE ITS EQUIPMENT IS NOW, BUT
