SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) ZIMBABWE\'S COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION (CFU)

ANNOUNCED FEBRUARY 15 THAT IT WILL HOLD A SPECIAL

CONGRESS ON MARCH 21 TO EXAMINE WAYS TO RE-ENGAGE THE

GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE (GOZ) ON THE LAND ISSUE. AN

INDEPENDENT APPROACH TO THE GOZ ON THE LAND ISSUE BY A

FORMER CFU PRESIDENT WAS SLAPPED BACK WITH HARDLY

MEASURED DISDAIN. THE GOZ RESPONSE IN THE PRESS IS THAT

IT WOULD ONLY TALK TO INDIVIDUAL COMMERCIAL FARMERS, NOT

THE CFU, WHOSE ONLY GOAL IS TO FRUSTRATE THE "FAST-

TRACK" LAND REFORM AND RESETTLEMENT PROGRAM. THE

MINISTER OF LANDS, AGRICULTURE AND RURAL RESETTLEMENT,

JOSEPH MADE, RECENTLY ANNOUNCED THAT THE GOZ HAD

GAZETTED ANOTHER 33 COMMERCIAL FARMS FOR COMPULSORY

ACQUISITION, AND THAT THE 5 MILLION HECTARES OF LAND

PREVIOUSLY TARGETED UNDER "FAST-TRACK" IS ONLY A

"MINIMUM" QUANTITY NEEDED FOR THE PROGRAM, ALTHOUGH NO

NEW TARGET HAS BEEN ANNOUNCED. MEANWHILE, TENSIONS

CONTINUE TO SIMMER ON ZIMBABWE\'S OCCUPIED COMMERCIAL

FARMS, WITH TWO INCIDENTS IN THE LAST WEEK INVOLVING THE

SHOOTING OF OCCUPIERS BY FARM GUARDS. END SUMMARY.

-------------------------------

CFU TO CONVENE SPECIAL CONGRESS

-------------------------------

2. (U) THE PRESIDENT OF ZIMBABWE\'S COMMERCIAL FARMERS

UNION (CFU), TIM HENWOOD, ANNOUNCED ON FEBRUARY 15 THAT

THE CFU WOULD HOLD A SPECIAL CONGRESS ON MARCH 21 TO TRY

AND FIND A WAY TO RE-ENGAGE THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE

(GOZ) ON LAND REFORM. THE CONGRESS WILL ALSO DECIDE ON

POSSIBLE CHANGES IN THE CFU\'S NATIONAL LEADERSHIP. THIS

ANNOUNCEMENT FOLLOWED A CONTENTIOUS MEETING OF THE CFU\'S

25-MEMBER COUNCIL ON FEBRUARY 13, AT WHICH DIVISIONS

EMERGED REGARDING A CONSENSUS APPROACH TO THE GOZ\'S

"FAST-TRACK" RESETTLEMENT PROGRAM. THE GOZ-CONTROLLED

"THE HERALD" NEWSPAPER REPORTED ON FEBRUARY 17 THAT SOME

COUNCIL MEMBERS, INCLUDING PAST PRESIDENTS OF THE CFU,

BLAMED THE CURRENT CFU NATIONAL LEADERSHIP FOR THE

BREAKDOWN IN DIALOGUE WITH THE GOZ. THESE COUNCIL

MEMBERS REPORTEDLY WANT THE CFU TO ENDORSE THE "FAST-

TRACK" PROGRAM AND WORK WITH THE GOZ TO SEE THAT THE

PROGRAM SUCCEEDS.

3. (C) ON FEBRUARY 21, ECONOFF SPOKE WITH RICHARD AMYOT,

CFU DEPUTY DIRECTOR FOR COMMODITIES, REGARDING THE

UPCOMING SPECIAL CONGRESS. AMYOT SAID THAT THE GOZ-

CONTROLLED PRESS ACCOUNT OF A STORMY CFU COUNCIL MEETING

WAS AN EXAGGERATION. ACCORDING TO AMYOT, THERE WAS NO

"BLOOD ON THE WALLS" AFTERWARDS. AMYOT SAID THE COUNCIL

MEETING WAS TRIGGERED BY BEHIND-THE-SCENES PRESSURE FROM

GOZ MINISTERS INVOLVED IN "FAST-TRACK" (MINISTER OF

LOCAL GOVERNMENT, PUBLIC WORKS AND NATIONAL HOUSING,

IGNATIUS CHOMBO, MINISTER OF LANDS, AGRICULTURE AND

RURAL RESETTLEMENT, JOSEPH MADE, AND MINISTER OF RURAL

RESOURCES AND WATER DEVELOPMENT, JOYCE MUJURU), AND

PRESIDENT MUGABE HIMSELF, TO REMOVE CFU PRESIDENT TIM

SURE OF THE BENEFITS OF A SPECIAL CONGRESS, SINCE THE

GOZ AND ZANU-PF ARE TREATING LAND AS A POLITICAL ISSUE

AND DO NOT APPEAR INTERESTED IN ACHIEVING A

COMPREHENSIVE SOLUTION THROUGH A REASONED DIALOGUE AMONG

ALL STAKEHOLDERS.

7. (U) HENWOOD REITERATED IN HIS FEBRUARY 15 PRESS

DISTRICTS AND PROVINCES.

ZIMBABWEANS FOR LAND UNDER PHASE II OF "FAST-TRACK",

HAVE THE MAJORITY OF PEOPLE FURTHER INDICATING THAT THEY

UNDERUTILIZED LAND WHICH WE THINK IS IN EXCESS OF FIVE

MILLION HECTARES." (COMMENT: MADE\'S STATEMENT THAT 5

MILLION HECTARES IS NOW ONLY THE MINIMUM AMOUNT THAT THE

GOZ REQUIRES FOR RESETTLEMENT TRACKS WITH OTHER SOURCES

CLAIMS THAT THE 5 MILLION HECTARE TARGET HAS BEEN

INCREASED. WE BELIEVE THIS PROVIDES FURTHER

CONFIRMATION THAT THE GOZ INTENDS TO USE PHASE II OF

"FAST-TRACK" FOR POLITICAL PURPOSES. THE SCENARIO IS

THAT THE GOZ WILL PROMISE LAND FOR COMMERCIAL FARMING TO

THE ZIMBABWE MASSES IN RETURN FOR THEIR VOTES FOR MUGABE

IN THE UPCOMING PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION. AFTER THE

ELECTION, WE EXPECT THE GOZ TO CONVENIENTLY DETERMINE

THAT MOST PEOPLE WHO APPLIED UNDER PHASE II WILL NOT

QUALIFY FOR LAND. END COMMENT)

9. (U) TENSIONS CONTINUE TO SIMMER ON OCCUPIED

COMMERCIAL FARMS, AS REVEALED BY TWO RECENT INCIDENTS IN

MASVINGO PROVINCE. ON FEBRUARY 13, FARM GUARDS OPENED

FIRE ON A GROUP OF SQUATTERS, WOUNDING SEVEN. TWO DAYS

LATER, FARM GUARDS ON A DIFFERENT FARM OPENED FIRE ON

SQUATTERS, WOUNDING THREE. ECONOFF ASKED AMYOT, OF THE

CFU, WHETHER HE THOUGHT THAT THESE WERE ISOLATED EVENTS

OR INDICATED A TREND. HE REPLIED THAT TENSIONS WERE

HIGH ON OCCUPIED FARMS, AND THAT FARM OCCUPIERS IN

MASVINGO PROVINCE, ESPECIALLY THE "WAR VETS", HAVE BEEN

PARTICULARLY AGGRESSIVE BECAUSE OF THE ENCOURAGEMENT

THEY HAVE RECEIVED FROM THE PROVINCIAL GOVERNOR JOSIAH

HUNGWE. ACCORDING TO AMYOT, THE FARM GUARDS HAD

PROBABLY JUST REACHED THE BREAKING POINT AND DECIDED TO

RESPOND TO THE UNENDING INTIMIDATION WITH FORCE.

COMMENT

-------

10. (C) AT FIRST GLANCE, A CHANGE IN CFU LEADERSHIP AND

DIRECTION WOULD APPEAR TO PROVIDE THE GOZ WITH SOME ROOM

TO MANEUVER OUT OF THE CORNER IT HAS PLACED ITSELF IN

WITH THE "FAST-TRACK" PROGRAM. HOWEVER, WE ARE

SKEPTICAL THAT A CHANGE IN THE CFU NATIONAL LEADERSHIP,

OR AN OFFER BY THE CFU TO WORK WITH THE GOZ TO MAKE

"FAST-TRACK" SUCCESSFUL, WILL END THE CURRENT IMPASSE.

IT IS CLEAR THAT THE GOZ AND ZANU-PF ARE MANIPULATING

THE LAND ISSUE FOR POLITICAL ENDS, AND ARE NOT SERIOUSLY

INTERESTED IN ADDRESSING THE INEQUITABLE OWNERSHIP OF

LAND IN ZIMBABWE. THE EXCHANGE OF LETTERS BETWEEN

RESONATE WITH ZIMBABWE\'S POOR MASSES AND INCREASE THE
