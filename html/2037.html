SUBJECT: ABUJA AGREEMENT MINISTERS SEEK A MEETING IN

HARARE NEXT WEEK

CLASSIFIED BY DCM REWHITEHEAD DUE TO 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) BRITISH HIGH COMMISSIONER DONNELLY INFORMED DCM

ON OCTOBER 5 THAT THE COMMONWEALTH MINISTERS PRESENT AT

THE MEETING THAT PRODUCED THE ABUJA AGREEMENT ARE

SEEKING A FOLLOW-UP ENCOUNTER IN HARARE SOMETIME THE

WEEK OF OCTOBER 7. HE SAID THAT THE INTENT WAS TO KEEP

THE ABUJA PROCESS MOVING IN SPITE OF THE POSTPONEMENT OF

THE CHOGM IN BRISBANE, WHERE GOZ ADHERENCE TO ABUJA WAS

TO HAVE FIGURED HIGH ON THE AGENDA. DONNELLY SAID THAT

THUS FAR THERE HAS BEEN NO POSITIVE RESPONSE FROM THE

GOZ ON THE PROPOSED MINISTERIAL. IF THE MEETING COMES

OFF -- AND THIS REMAINS UNSURE -- BARONESS AMOS WILL

REPRESENT THE UK. OTHER PARTICIPANTS WOULD INCLUDE

NIGERIA\'S LAMIDO, THE KENYAN FONMIN, AND CANADIAN DEPUTY

MINISTER KILGORE. DONNELLY DOES NOT EXPECT JAMAICA TO

PARTICIPATE.

SULLIVAN
