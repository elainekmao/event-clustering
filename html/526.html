SUMMARY

-------

1. LOCAL PRESS REPORTS IT IS ALL QUIET TODAY ON THE

DRNIS FRONT, BUT UNPROFOR HAS POSTPONED ITS TAKEOVER

OF SECTOR SOUTH UNTIL THE CEASEFIRE IS RESPECTED.

PRESIDENT TUDJMAN TOLD THE FRENCH PAPER "LE FIGARO"

THAT CROATIA WILL NOT IGNORE THE PERIL OF CROATIANS

IN BOSNIA-HERCEGOVINA BUT CROATIAN ARMY FORCES ARE

NOT NOW ENGAGED. MILITARY COURT OFFICIALS IN OSIJEK

ARE BUSY WITH PROSECUTIONS OF PROPERTY CRIMES AND

INCREASED VIOLENCE. THE NATIONAL BANK PLANS A TIGHT

MONETARY POLICY IT HOPES WILL CAP QUARTERLY INFLATION

AT 52 PERCENT. END SUMMARY.

ALL QUIET ON THE DRNIS FRONT

-----------------------------

2. NO FURTHER ACTION HAS BEEN REPORTED IN THE ZAGREB

PRESS FROM THE BATTLEFIELD AROUND SIBENIK, INCLUDING

THE MILJEVCI PLATEAU. THE LATTER AREA, PART OF THE

"PINK ZONE" SOME 15 KMS FROM SIBENIK, WAS THE SITE OF

THE CROATIAN MILITARY ADVANCE EARLIER THIS WEEK WHICH

PUT INTO JEOPARDY UNPROFOR\'S EFFORTS TO TAKE OVER

SECTOR SOUTH.

UNPROFOR DELAYS TAKEOVER OF SECTOR SOUTH

---------------------------------------

3. AT A JUNE 25 MEETING IN ZADAR, UNPROFOR

COORDINATOR FOR CIVILIAN QUESTIONS, VICTOR ANDREJEV,

INFORMED THE REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ZADAR, GRACAC,

OBROVAC, BENKOVAC, BIOGRAD, SINJ, AND KNIN COUNTIES

THAT UNPROFOR HAS POSTPONED THE TAKEOVER OF FULL

CONTROL OVER SECTOR SOUTH. ANDREJEV SAID UNPROFOR

WILL TAKE

CONTROL OF SECTOR SOUTH WHEN THE CEASEFIRE IS

"RESPECTED."

TUDJMAN\'S "LE FIGARO" INTERVIEW

---------------------------------

5. PRESIDENT FRANJO TUDJMAN TOLD THE PARIS "LE

FIGARO" THAT "IF WAR CONTINUES IN BOSNIA AND

HERCEGOVINA, CROATIA WILL HAVE TO INTERVENE."

ACCORDING TO TUDJMAN, CROATIA IS FOR A PEACEFUL

SOLUTION IF THIS IS POSSIBLE. IN CASE A MILITARY

INTERVENTION WOULD BE NECESSARY TO RESTORE THE PEACE,

THE PRESIDENT IS FOR MORE EFFICACIOUS MEASURES SUCH

AS AN AIR AND NAVAL BLOCKADE BY ALLIED AND/OR NATO

FORCES. THE MAIN PART OF TUDJMAN\'S INTERVIEW WAS

DEDICATED TO CROATIAN-BOSNIAN RELATIONS. HE STRESSED

AGAIN THAT NO MILITARY PACT HAS BEEN SIGNED BETWEEN

CROATIA AND BOSNIA-HERCEGOVINA. HE POINTED OUT THAT

THE GOAL OF CROATS AND MUSLIMS IS TO ESTABLISH

MILITARY COOPERATION WHICH WOULD ENABLE THEM TO

DEFEND THEMSELVES FROM AGGRESSION IN B-H. IF CROATS

IN NEIGHBORING B-H WOULD BE THREATENED WITH

ANNIHILATION, CROATIA WILL BE OBLIGED "TO DO

SOMETHING TO HELP THEM." TUDJMAN ASSERTED THAT AT

THE MOMENT NO REGULAR CROATIAN ARMY IS FIGHTING IN

B-H. (EMBASSY PARIS HAS PROVIDED A FULLER VERSION OF

THIS INTERVIEW IN REFTEL.)
