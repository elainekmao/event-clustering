SUBJECT: AFGHANISTAN\'S FUTURE - AND UBL SPEECH

REF: A. STATE 192153 B. STATE 190913

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR RICHARD H. JONES,

REASON: 1.5 (B) AND (D).

-------

SUMMARY

-------

1. (S) USAMA BIN LADEN\'S NOVEMBER 3 STATEMENT WAS

THE SPEECH OF A DESPERATE MAN DISAPPOINTED THAT

HIS PREVIOUS STATEMENTS HAD NOT GENERATED GREATER

SUPPORT IN THE MUSLIM WORLD, KUWAIT MFA

UNDERSECRETARY AL-JARALLAH TOLD THE AMBASSADOR

NOVEMBER 4. AL-JARALLAH DID NOT EXPRESS CONCERN

ABOUT THE PROSPECTS OF MILITARY OPERATIONS

CONTINUING DURING RAMADAN, NOTING THAT THE

AFGHANIS THEMSELVES HAD NOT RESPECTED RAMADAN

WHEN FIGHTING AMONG THEMSELVES. HE REAFFIRMED

KUWAITI - AND GCC - SUPPORT FOR REACHING OUR

COMMON GOAL -- THE END FOR THE TALABAN, UBL, AND

AL-QAIDA.

----------------------------------------

AFGHANISTAN: GCC COUNTRIES STAND WITH US

----------------------------------------

2. (S) THE AMBASSADOR DREW EXTENSIVELY FROM REF A

IN A DISCUSSION ON AFGHANISTAN NOVEMBER 4 WITH

MINISTER OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS UNDERSECRETARY KHALID

AL-JARALLAH. AL-JARALLAH, EXPRESSING

APPRECIATION FOR THE BRIEFING, AGREED THAT THE

SITUATION IN AFGHANISTAN PRESENTED DIFFICULT

CHALLENGES. IT POSED A THREAT NOT ONLY FOR

WESTERN COUNTRIES, HE SAID, BUT ALSO COUNTRIES IN

THIS REGION. THE COUNTRIES OF THE GCC, HE ADDED,

ARE READY TO WORK TOGETHER WITH THE U.S., THE

U.K., AND THE COALITION TO REACH OUR COMMON GOAL

- THE END FOR THE TALABAN, USAMA BIN LADEN, AND

AL-QAIDA.

-------------------------------

UBL SPEECH: SIGN OF DESPERATION

-------------------------------

3. (S) AL-JARALLAH RAISED THE SPEECH BY USAMA BIN

LADEN BROADCAST THE EVENING BEFORE. IT WAS A VERY

STRANGE SPEECH, HE SAID. IN HIS ASSESSMENT, IT

WAS A SPEECH OF A DESPERATE MAN. WHEN UBL USED

THE EXPRESSION "WA ISLAMA" (AN EXPRESSION USED BY

MUSLIMS NEEDING HELP IN TOUGH CIRCUMSTANCES), IT

INDICATED THAT HE WAS FEELING DEEPLY DEPRESSED.

4. (S) ELABORATING, AL-JARALLAH SAID THAT HE

THOUGHT UBL HAD EXPECTED THAT HIS FIRST SPEECH

AFTER THE BOMBING BEGAN (WITH AL-QAIDA SPOKESMAN

BU GAITH AT HIS SIDE) WOULD CREATE A STRONG

SPIRIT OF SUPPORT IN THE ISLAMIC WORLD. IN FACT,

THERE HAS BEEN VERY LITTLE REACTION. THERE HAVE

BEEN SOME DEMONSTRATIONS IN PAKISTAN, BUT THEY

HAVE BEEN SMALLER THAN EXPECTED AND APPARENTLY

DECREASING, AND THERE HAVE BEEN SOME POPULAR

EXPRESSIONS OF SUPPORT IN PLACES LIKE INDONESIA.

OVERALL, HOWEVER, THE REACTION HAS BEEN MINIMAL.

BECAUSE OF THIS, UBL IS DISAPPOINTED AND

DESPERATE, AL-JARALLAH SAID.

5. (S) THE AMBASSADOR THANKED AL-JARALLAH FOR HIS

INSIGHT AND NOTED THAT PUBLIC STATEMENTS BY

PROMINENT KUWAITIS -- EITHER FROM INSIDE THE

GOVERNMENT, THE EDUCATION AND RELIGIOUS

ESTABLISHMENTS, OR THE PRIVATE SECTOR -- REFUTING

UBL\'S ALLEGATIONS WOULD BE EXTREMELY HELPFUL IN

OUR EFFORTS TO REFUTE UBL PROPAGANDA.

----------------------------------------

RAMADAN OPERATIONS: KUWAIT NOT CONCERNED

----------------------------------------

5. (S) AL-JARALLAH RESPONDED CALMLY TO THE POINT

REGARDING THE HIGH PROBABILITY THAT THE MILITARY

CAMPAIGN WOULD CONTINUE THROUGH RAMADAN. HE DID

NOT THINK THIS SHOULD BE A SOURCE OF UNDUE

CONCERN. OBSERVING THAT SOME 30,000 AFGHANIS HAD

DIED IN AFGHAN INTERNAL FIGHTING, HE NOTED THAT

THE AFGHANIS HAD NOT RESPECTED RAMADAN WHEN THEY

WERE FIGHTING AMONG THEMSELVES.

--------------------------------

NORTHERN ALLIANCE READY TO MOVE?

--------------------------------

6. (S) AL-JARALLAH NOTED NEWS REPORTS OF B-52

BOMBING NEAR NORTHERN ALLIANCE FRONT LINES OVER

THE PAST TWO-THREE DAYS, AND THOUGHT THERE MIGHT

BE A GOOD CHANCE THAT THE NORTHERN ALLIANCE WOULD

BE ABLE TO MOVE FORWARD SOON AND "ACHIEVE

SOMETHING." THE AMBASSADOR AGREED RECENT NEWS

REPORTS WERE MORE ENCOURAGING BUT STRESSED IT WAS

TOO SOON TO MAKE PREDICTIONS.
