SUBJECT: HOLY SEE NOT SUPPORTIVE OF SEPARATE OSCE

ANTI-SEMITISM MEETING

REF: A) STATE 228101 B) USOSCE 1433 C) STATE 239512

1. (SBU) POLOFF REVIEWED REFS (A) AND (B) DISCUSSION OF A

SPECIAL SESSION ON ANTI-SEMITISM NOVEMBER 16 WITH VATICAN

OSCE DIRECTOR MONS. ETTORE BALESTRERO, FOCUSING ON THE NEED

FOR A STAND-ALONE MEETING TO DISCUSS ANTI-SEMITISM.

BALESTRERO SAID THE HOLY SEE SHARED THE USG\'S CONCERNS AND

INDICATED HE WOULD UNDERTAKE TO SEE HOW THE HOLY SEE OSCE

DELEGATION MIGHT BE ABLE TO WORK WITH THE U.S.

2. (SBU) DCM FOLLOWED UP WITH VATICAN DFM CELESTINO MIGLIORE

NOVEMBER 25, DEPLOYING REF (C) POINTS AND EMPHASIZING THE

IMPORTANCE THE USG ATTACHED TO HAVING A SEPARATE OSCE MEETING

DEVOTED TO ANTI-SEMITISM. MIGLIORE RESPONDED THAT THE HOLY

SEE WAS "HESITANT" TO ACCEPT THIS PROPOSAL, "NOT BECAUSE WE

ARE NOT CONVINCED OF THE NEED TO COMBAT ANTI-SEMITISM."

RATHER, THEY WERE CONCERNED THAT IF THE OSCE WERE TO HOLD A

SPECIAL SESSION ON ANTI-SEMITISM, THE OSCE WOULD HAVE TO

EXPLAIN WHY IT WAS NOT HOLDING SPECIAL SESSIONS ON THE ROMA,

SINTI, AND OTHER GROUPS FACING RACISM OR XENOPHOBIA. HE FELT

THIS WOULD DILUTE THE STATEMENT CONDEMNING DISCRIMINATION

THAT WOULD COME OUT OF OPORTO. THE PROBLEM, MIGLIORE

REITERATED, WAS THAT "SOMEONE WILL ALWAYS SAY THAT THEIR

SPECIFIC PROBLEMS HAVE BEEN LEFT OUT, AND WE WILL INEVITABLY

DISAPPOINT SOME GROUP."

3. (SBU) DCM RESPONDED THAT THE USG REGARDS ANTI-SEMITISM AS

A UNIQUE HISTORICAL PROBLEM THAT MERITS STAND-ALONE

TREATMENT. MOREOVER, WE BELIEVE THAT SUCH TREATMENT WOULD

STRENGTHEN THE OSCE\'S ABILITY TO DEAL WITH OTHER FORMS OF

RACISM AND XENOPHOBIA. MIGLIORE RESPONDED THAT HE UNDERSTOOD

THAT THE OPPOSITION TO A SPECIAL SESSION FOR ANTI-SEMITISM

WAS WIDELY SHARED BY MANY DELEGATIONS, AND THAT HE FOUND IT

WELL-GROUNDED. HE REITERATED HIS VIEW THAT THE OPORTO

STATEMENT WOULD BE STRONGER IF IT REMAINED ON A MORE GENERAL

LEVEL.

4. (SBU) DCM REPEATED THAT THE U.S. STRONGLY BELIEVED THE

ISSUE SHOULD BE SPECIFICALLY RAISED IN OPORTO AND STRONGLY

FAVORED A SUBSEQUENT SEPARATE MEETING. HE URGED MIGLIORE TO

KEEP AN OPEN MIND, AND TO WORK WITH OUR DELEGATION IN VIENNA

AND OPORTO TO ACHIEVE AGREEMENT.
