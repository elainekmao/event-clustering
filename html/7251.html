1. (U) ROME\'S SANT\'EGIDIO COMMUNITY, A VATICAN-ENDORSED LAY

CATHOLIC NGO WITH GLOBAL REACH, WHICH SUCCESSFULLY MEDIATED

AN END TO MOZAMBIQUE\'S CIVIL WAR IN 1991, HAS LAUNCHED AN

AMBITIOUS PROGRAM TO COMBAT HIV/AIDS IN MOZAMBIQUE.

SANT\'EGIDIO, WHICH HAS RETAINED ITS COMMITMENT TO

MOZAMBIQUE\'S DEMOCRATIC DEVELOPMENT, REGARDS HIV/AIDS AS

THE BIGGEST THREAT TO MOZAMBIQUE\'S FUTURE AND HAS LAUNCHED

A DRUG RESOURCE ENHANCEMENT AGAINST AIDS (DREAM) PROJECT IN

MOZAMBIQUE TO PROVIDE HIGHLY ACTIVE ANTI-RETROVIRAL

TREATMENT TO PREGNANT WOMEN AND LACTATING MOTHERS. WITH

ADEQUATE FUNDING, IT INTENDS TO EXPAND THE PROGRAM TO OTHER

KEY AT-RISK SECTORS IN THE COUNTRY. A 12-MONTH CLINICAL

TRIAL HAS PROVEN HIGHLY SUCCESSFUL, REDUCING TRANSMISSION

RATES BY UP TO 40 PERCENT AND SAVING THOUSANDS OF NEWBORNS

FROM HIV INFECTION. THE DREAM PROJECT HAS ALREADY SECURED

SUPPORT FROM MULTINATIONAL DRUG COMPANIES, AND MOZAMBIQUE\'S

MINISTER OF HEALTH, FRANCISCO SOGNANE EXTOLLED THE BENEFITS

OF THE SANT\'EGIDIO PROJECT FOR MOZAMBIQUE\'S PEOPLE AND

URGED THAT IT BE ENLARGED TO ENCOMPASS A GREATER NUMBER OF

PEOPLE INFECTED WITH HIV/AIDS. ADDITIONAL FUNDING WILL

ALLOW THE PROJECT TO BE EXPANDED THROUGHOUT MOZAMBIQUE, AND

POTENTIALLY BEYOND. EMBASSY BELIEVES THAT DREAM FITS LIKE A

GLOVE WITH THE PRESIDENT\'S INTERNATIONAL MOTHER AND CHILD

HIV PREVENTION INITIATIVE AND RECOMMENDS THAT THE WHITE

HOUSE, USAID, AND EMBASSY MAPUTO WORK WITH SANT\'EGIDIO TO

EXPAND THIS PROMISING PROJECT ADMINISTERED BY A GROUP WITH

A PROVEN TRACK RECORD. END SUMMARY AND ACTION REQUEST.

SANT\'EGIDIO: AFTER PEACE, TAKES AIM AT HIV/AIDS

--------------------------------------------- --

2. (U) THE SANT\'EGIDIO COMMUNITY, A VATICAN-APPROVED LAY

MOVEMENT -- NOT LIMITED TO CATHOLICS -- WITH MORE THAN

40,000 MEMBERS DEDICATED TO CHARITY, HUMANITARIAN, AND

CONFLICT RESOLUTION EFFORTS IN MORE THAN 60 COUNTRIES, WAS

INSTRUMENTAL IN MEDIATING AN END TO MOZAMBIQUE\'S

DEVASTATING CIVIL WAR IN 1991. THE COMMUNITY\'S COMMITMENT

TO MOZAMBIQUE HAS NOT ABATED SINCE THEN, AND IT IS NOW

ACTIVELY ENGAGED IN ENSURING THAT THE HARD-WON PEACE LEADS

TO BETTER ECONOMIC AND SOCIAL CONDITIONS FOR THE PEOPLE OF

MOZAMBIQUE. TODAY, SANT\'EGIDIO RECOGNIZES THAT THE

INCREASINGLY HIGH INCIDENCE OF HIV/AIDS IN MOZAMBIQUE IS

NOW THE BIGGEST THREAT TO THE NATION\'S FUTURE, DESTROYING

FAMILIES AND UNDERMINING ECONOMIC PROGRESS. AS A RESULT,

ANDREA RICCARDI, THE KINETIC FOUNDER AND HEAD OF THE

COMMUNITY HAS DEVELOPED AN INITIATIVE TOGETHER WITH THE

SIZEABLE SANT\'EGIDIO COMMUNITY IN MOZAMBIQUE AIMED AT

REVERSING THE SPREAD OF HIV/AIDS THROUGH ANTI-RETROVIRAL

TREATMENTS.

3. (U) ACCORDING TO SANT\'EGIDIO, THE FIGHT AGAINST HIV/AIDS

IN AFRICA HAS BEEN BASED PREDOMINANTLY ON PREVENTING MORE

AFRICANS FROM CONTRACTING THE VIRUS. SANT\'EGIDIO BELIEVES

THIS HAS NOT WORKED, EVEN WHILE THEY SUPPORT CONTINUED

EFFORTS AT EDUCATION AND PREVENTION. THEY POINT OUT THAT

NO EPIDEMIC HAS BEEN OVERCOME BY PREVENTION ALONE.

MOREOVER, THEY BELIEVE THE LACK OF ACCESS TO TREATMENT

SIGNIFICANTLY REDUCES THE EFFECTIVENESS OF PREVENTION, AS

PEOPLE FEAR BEING TESTED AND FOUND TO BE POSITIVE BECAUSE

DREAM: TREATMENT THAT WORKS

----------------------------

4. (U) SANT\'EGIDIO\'S DRUG RESOURCE ENHANCEMENT AGAINST AIDS

IN MOZAMBIQUE (DREAM) PROJECT IS OFFERING ACCESSIBLE

TREATMENT TO PEOPLE WITH HIV/AIDS, BEGINNING WITH PREGNANT

AND LACTATING MOTHERS. ANTI-RETROVIRAL DRUGS ARE

ADMINISTERED TO PREGNANT WOMEN TO SAVE BOTH MOTHER AND

CHILD, THEREBY PREVENTING A MOTHER\'S OTHER CHILDREN FROM

BECOMING ORPHANS. CONDUCTED IN PARTNERSHIP WITH LOCAL

HEALTH PROFESSIONALS AND VOLUNTEERS, AND WITH EXISTING

HEALTH DEPARTMENT FACILITIES AND STAFF, DREAM USES HIGHLY

ACTIVE ANTI-RETROVIRAL THERAPY (HAART) TO PREVENT VERTICAL

(MOTHER-FETUS) TRANSMISSION OF THE HIV VIRUS. TREATMENT IS

OFFERED FREE OF CHARGE.

FIRST RESULTS PROMISING

-----------------------

6. (U) THE FIRST RESULTS OF DREAM WERE PRESENTED AT THE

TENTH INTERNATIONAL CONFERENCE ON RETROVIRUS AND

OPPORTUNISTIC INFECTIONS HELD IN BOSTON IN FEBRUARY. FOUR-

HUNDRED FORTY HIV POSITIVE PATIENTS, PRIMARILY PREGNANT

WOMEN, PARTICIPATED IN THE INITIAL DREAM PROGRAM IN ORDER

TO GAUGE THE EFFECTS OF THERAPY ON MOTHER-FETAL

TRANSMISSION. OF THESE, 240 FOLLOWED THE STANDARD THERAPY

USED IN WESTERN COUNTRIES. MORTALITY RATES WERE LOWERED;

THE QUALITY OF THE PATIENTS\' LIFE IMPROVED MARKEDLY, AND

THE AMOUNT OF THE VIRUS IN THE BLOOD DECREASED NOTICEABLY.

ABOVE ALL, THE PROGRAM REVEALED A HIGH CONTINUING

PARTICIPATION RATE -- A POINT OF PARTICULAR SIGNIFICANCE IN

A DEVELOPING COUNTRY LIKE MOZAMBIQUE. THE RESULTS SHOWED

THAT EVEN IN NATIONS WITH A BASIC HEALTH SYSTEM IT IS

POSSIBLE TO USE A THERAPEUTIC MODEL THAT IMPACTS

SIGNIFICANTLY ON THE RATE OF HIV INFECTION. THE RESULTS OF

THE DREAM TRIAL ARE TO BE PUBLISHED IN AN UP-COMING EDITION

OF THE PRESTIGIOUS BRITISH MEDICAL JOURNAL LANCET. CLINICAL

RESULTS OF THE TRIAL ARE AVAILABLE ON THE INTERNET:

WWW.RETROCONFERENCE.ORG/2003/ABSTRACT/WORDHTM L/2127.HTM

7. (U) THE SANT\'EGIDIO COMMUNITY FURTHER ELABORATED THESE

RESULTS AND PRESENTED ITS PROJECT AT A RECENT GATHERING IN

ROME ATTENDED BY EMBASSY OFFICERS. IT WAS CLEAR THAT THE

DREAM PROJECT HAS ALREADY EARNED THE WHOLEHEARTED SUPPORT

OF A VARIED GROUP OF ENTITIES RANGING FROM MULTINATIONAL

DRUG COMPANIES TO THE CATHOLIC CHURCH IN GERMANY.

MOZAMBIQUE\'S MINISTER OF HEALTH, FRANCISCO SOGNANE,

DREAM AND USG OBJECTIVES

INTERNATIONAL MOTHER AND CHILD HIV PREVENTION INITIATIVE

FOCUSED ON INCREASING THE AVAILABILITY OF PREVENTIVE CARE,

INCLUDING DRUG TREATMENTS, AND BUILDING HEALTHCARE DELIVERY

SYSTEMS TO REACH AS MANY WOMEN AS POSSIBLE WITH THE CARE

THEY NEED. IN FACT, AFTER DCM SHARED THE PRESIDENT\'S

JANUARY ANNOUNCEMENT OF HIS NEW HIV/AIDS INITIATIVE WITH

THE PRESIDENT WAS READING FROM THEIR PROPOSAL BECAUSE OF

THE COMMON FOCUS ON ANTI-RETROVIRAL DRUGS, MOZAMBIQUE,

PREVENTING MOTH-CHILD TRANSMISSION, AND PRESERVING FAMILY

UNITS. THE PRESIDENT\'S ANNOUNCEMENT STATED THAT THE

ADMINISTRATION IS LOOKING TO WORK WITH OTHER GOVERNMENTS,

9. (U) EMBASSY BELIEVES IT WOULD BE HARD TO FIND A FAITH-

BASED INITIATIVE WITH BETTER PROSPECTS THAN THE SANT\'EGIDIO

DREAM PROJECT. WE NOTE THAT THE COMMUNITY OF SANT\'EGIDIO,

FOUNDED IN ROME IN 1968, IS VATICAN-ENDORSED CATHOLIC LAY

MOVEMENT WITH MORE THAN 40,000 MEMBERS (NOT ALL CATHOLIC)

DEDICATED TO CHARITY AND HUMANITARIAN EFFORTS IN MORE THAN

60 COUNTRIES. THE COMMUNITIES ARE COMMITTED TO SOLIDARITY

WITH THE POOR AND SUFFERING INCLUDING PHYSICALLY AND

MENTALLY DISABLED, HOMELESS, FOREIGN IMMIGRANTS, PEOPLE

WITH HIV/AIDS, TERMINALLY ILL PEOPLE, PRISONERS, ELDERLY,

ROMA AND SINTI, AND REFUGEES. IT IS ALSO A LEADING FORCE

FOR ECUMENICAL DIALOGUE AS A WAY TO PROMOTE PEACE AND CO-

OPERATION AMONG RELIGIONS, AND TO RESOLVE ARMED CONFLICTS.

COMMUNITY MEMBERS HAVE CONTRIBUTED TO RESOLVING THE

CONFLICTS IN MOZAMBIQUE AND GUATEMALA, AND HAVE HELPED

MEDIATE PEACE EFFORTS IN AFRICA\'S GREAT LAKES REGION AND

KOSOVO. EMBASSY VATICAN HAS PREVIOUSLY FUNDED A

SANT\'EGIDIO PROJECT IN KOSOVO THAT SEEKS TO RECONCILE SERBS

AND ETHNIC ALBANIANS AT THE ELEMENTARY SCHOOL LEVEL. WE

MAINTAIN AN ACTIVE DIALOGUE WITH SANT\'EGIDIO ON A BROAD

RANGE OF AFRICAN ISSUES, AND THEY CONSULT REGULARLY WITH

SENIOR USG OFFICIALS.
