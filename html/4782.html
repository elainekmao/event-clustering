SUBJECT: ITALIAN TRANSPORT MINISTER DISCUSSES GALILEO WITH

DE PALACIO

1. (U) THIS MESSAGE HAS BEEN CLASSIFIED BY ECONOMIC MINISTER

COUNSELOR SCOTT KILNER. REASONS 1.5B AND D.

2. (C) SUMMARY. ON 4 DECEMBER DCM MET WITH MINISTRY OF

TRANSPORT INTERNATIONAL AFFAIRS COUNSELOR EMILIO MARAINI AND

OUTLINED USG VIEWS ON THE PROPOSED OVERLAY OF GPS M-CODE BY

THE PUBLIC REGULATED SERVICE (PRS) OF THE GALILEO SATELLITE

NAVIGATION SYSTEM. DCM ALSO DELIVERED A LETTER FROM

AMBASSADOR TO MINISTER OF TRANSPORT LUNARDI. IN A DEC 12

CONVERSATION WITH MARAINI, POST LEARNED THAT LUNARDI HAD USED

THE LETTER TO OPEN A PERSONAL DISCUSSION ON M-CODE WITH

TRANSPORT COMMISSIONER DE PALACIO. HOWEVER, DE PALACIO\'S

ASSISTANT ILBRECHT INSISTED THAT THE OVERLAY PROBLEM COULD BE

SOLVED BY MODULATION OF THE PRS SIGNAL RATHER THAN MOVING PRS

TO A SEPARATE SEGMENT OF SPECTRUM. END SUMMARY.

3. (SBU) DCM CALLED ON MARAINI ON THE EVE OF HIS DEPARTURE

FOR THE DEC.5 TRANSPORT COUNCIL TO STRESS THE IMPORTANCE THE

USG ATTACHES TO MOVING GALILEO\'S PROPOSED PRS TO A FREQUENCY

WHICH WOULD NOT OVERLAY THAT OF U.S. M-CODE. DCM STRESSED

THAT THE USG WAS NOT OPPOSED TO THE EUROPEAN SYSTEM, IN FACT

SAW POSSIBLE BENEFITS FOR USERS OF GALILEO AND GPS, BUT

SIMPLY COULD NOT ACCEPT THE RISKS TO U.S. AND NATO MILITARY

OPERATIONS AND PERSONNEL THAT WOULD RESULT FROM THE OVERLAY

OF VITAL M-CODE BY GALILEO\'S PROPOSED PRS. DCM UNDERLINED

THAT NO TECHNICAL FIX WAS POSSIBLE, AND THAT THE EU NEEDED TO

TAKE THE POLITICAL DECISION TO ABANDON THE IDEA OF FREQUENCY

OVERLAYS. HE CONCLUDED BY POINTING TO OUR LONG STANDING

POLITICAL AND INDUSTRIAL BILATERAL COOPERATION AS AN

INDICATION THAT A SOLUTION COULD BE FOUND.

4. (SBU) MARAINI-- IN HIS JOB JUST A FEW MONTHS--WAS AWARE OF

USG VIEWS, BUT SAID THAT THE TRANPORT MINISTERS LACKED THE

COMPETENCE TO ENTER INTO THE MILITARY ASPECTS OF GALILEO,

SINCE THE COMMISSION MANDATE WAS CIVILIAN AND COMMERCIAL. HE

EXPECTED THE MINISTERS TO INDICATED THE NEED FOR THE

COMMISSION TO STUDY SERIOUSLY POSSIBLE SOLUTIONS IN CLOSE

COOPERATION WITH THE U.S. HE PROMISED TO DELIVER THE

AMBASSADOR\'S LETTER TO MINISTER LUNARDI, AND TO DISCUSS THE

ISSUES WITH THE MINISTER EN ROUTE TO BRUSSELS.

5. (C) POST LEARNED ON DECEMBER 12 FROM MARAINI THAT MINISTER

LUNARDI HAD RAISED USG CONCERNS WITH TRANSPORT COMMISSIONER

DE PALACIO ON THE MARGINS OF THE COUNCIL. ACCORDING TO

MARAINI, LUNARDI STRESSED THE NEED TO ADDRESS USG SECURITY

CONCERNS RESPONSIVELY. IN HER REPLY THE COMMISSIONER CALLED

ON HER ASSISTANT ILBRECHT, WHO ARGUED THAT THE OVERLAY

PROBLEMS RAISED BY THE THE U.S. COULD BE RESOLVED BY

MODULATION OF PRS SIGNAL WITHOUT THE NEED TO MOVE PRS TO A

SEPARTE FREQUENCY.

6. (C) COMMENT. EMBASSY BELIEVES THAT ITALY, BECAUSE OF ITS

HIGH POLITICAL AND ECONOMIC STAKES IN GALILEO, WILL SUPPORT A

RESOLUTION OF THE OVERLAY ISSUE IN ORDER TO MOVE FORWARD WITH

TENDERING THE PROJECT, WHICH COULD HAPPEN AS EARLY AS NEXT

JUNE. HOWEVER, THE MINISTRY OF TRANSPORT IS NOT FULLY

CONVINCED THAT THE TECHNICAL SOLUTIONS OFFERED UP BY THE

COMMISSION ARE INFEASIBLE. INDEED, MARAINI TOLD US THAT THE

U.S. STYLE-- IN HIS WORDS, NEGOTIATING BY ULTIMATUM--DID NOT

SIT WELL WITH EITHER ITALY OR THE COMMISSION. WE EXPECT THE

ITALIANS WILL WANT A CLEAR TECHNICAL EXPLANATION OF WHY

SOLUTIONS PROPOSED BY THE COMMISSION WILL NOT WORK.
