CLASSIFIED BY A/POLCOUNS E. LUNDBERG; REASONS 1.5 (B,D).

1.(U) ACTION REQUEST - SEE PARA. 4.

2.(C) SUMMARY: AT FIRST GLANCE, THE DUTCH "WELCOME" THE

DTSI AND SEE IT AS A "POSITIVE STEP IN TRANSATLANTIC DEFENSE

TRADE." NEVERTHELESS, THEY ARE RESERVING OVERALL JUDGMENT ON

THE PROPOSALS UNTIL THEY LEARN MORE ABOUT THEM. THEY ARE ALSO

EAGER TO LEARN MORE ABOUT HOW THE DTSI AND THE EXISTING

GLOBALIZATION TALKS RELATE TO EACH OTHER. END SUMMARY.

3.(C) EMBASSY POLITICAL AND ODC REPS MET ON 6/13 WITH MFA

AND MOD OFFICIALS TO DISCUSS INITIAL DUTCH REACTIONS TO THE

DEFENSE TRADE SECURITY INITIATIVE. DUTCH ECONOMICS MINISTRY

OFFICIALS PREVIOUSLY HAD ATTENDED A DUTCH INTERNAL MEETING

AND THEIR COMMENTS WERE PASSED ON BY MFA OFFICIALS.

CAVEATING THAT THEIR REACTIONS WERE ONLY "INITIAL AND NOT YET

AUTHORITATIVE," DUTCH DEPUTY SECURITY POLICY PDAS GAIUS

SCHELTEMA AND DAS FOR EXPORT CONTROLS JOHAN COERT "WELCOMED

THE DTSI" AS AN IMPORTANT STEP FORWARD IN THE TRANSATLANTIC

TRADE MARKETPLACE. SCHELTEMA SAID THAT THE "SPECIFIC LINK TO

THE DCI IS ALSO POSITIVE AND IMPORTANT." COERT ADDED THE

DUTCH RECOGNIZED THAT MANY OF THE DTSI ELEMENTS WERE KEYED TO

MOU GROUP LONG-STANDING TOPICS. ASKED BY POLOFF, COERT ADDED

THAT INFORMAL DISCUSSION AMONG MOU GROUP MEMBERS IN

WASHINGTON WAS ALSO "POSITIVE AND DEEMED A REAL STEP FORWARD."

COERT ALSO ADDED THAT THE DUTCH ECONOMICS MINISTRY AEROSPACE

DEPARTMENT DIRECTOR HAD JUST REPORTED TO HIS MFA AND MOD

COLLEAGUES THAT DUTCH DEFENSE-RELATED COMPANIES WERE

IMPRESSED BY THE INITIATIVE AND GENUINELY SENSED NEW

POTENTIAL MARKET ACCESS AND TEAMING OPPORTUNITIES AS A RESULT

OF THE DTSI.

4.(C) DUTCH MOD REP H.J. JANSEN EXPRESSED A KEEN INTEREST,

SECONDED BY THE MFA, IN LEARNING HOW THE EXISTING OSD-LED

GLOBALIZATION DIALOGUE RELATED TO THE DTSI. POLOFF EXPLAINED

THAT DTSI PRIMARILY RELATED TO THE EXPORT-CONTROL AND

SENSITIVE TECHNOLOGY/INFORMATION SAFEGUARDING ELEMENTS ON THE

GLOBALIZATION PILLAR STRUCTURE, BUT THAT THE TWO PROCESSES

WERE STILL DIFFERENT. THE DUTCH NOTED THAT DASD VOLKMAN

ALREADY HAD INVITED MOD\'S DIRECTOR FOR PLANS AND

INTERNATIONAL MATERIEL RELATIONS, BGEN TEUSSINK, TO JULY 11

TALKS IN WASHINGTON TO FURTHER EXPLORE A POSSIBLE

U.S.-NETHERLANDS DECLARATION OF PRINCIPLES FOR DEFENSE

EQUIPMENT AND INDUSTRIAL COOPERATION (DOP). THEY SAID THAT

THEY HOPED TO HEAR MORE DETAILS THEN ON WHEN INDIVIDUAL

ELEMENTS OF THE DTSI WOULD BECOME OFFICIAL, HOW THEY WOULD

RELATE TO THE DOP PROCESS AND WHAT SPECIFICALLY WAS EXPECTED

OF DUTCH TO COMPLY WITH ASPECTS OF THE OPPORTUNITIES OUTLINED

IN THE DOP AND DTSI DIALOGUES. (EMBASSY COMMENT; THIS WOULD

BE AN EXCELLENT OPPORTUNITY FOR PM PDAS SUCHAN TO BRIEF DUTCH

OFFICIALS ON THE DTSI, AS NOTED IN REF.B. END COMMENT.) BOTH

MOD AND MFA INTERLOCUTORS ALSO SUGGESTED THE U.S. USE

COMBINATIONS OF A WEBSITE, REGULAR MOU GROUP BRIEFINGS, AND

BOTH BACKGROUND NATO COMMITTEE AND POLITICAL LEVEL GATHERINGS

TO REPORT PROGRESS. THEREBY, THEY INDICATED, THE ALLIANCE

COULD USE PEER PRESSURE AS A CATALYST FOR BUILDING AND

MAINTAINING MOMENTUM FOR THE PROGRAM.

5.(C) EMBASSY OFFICIALS THEN REVIEWED THE DTSI ELEMENTS, WIT

THE DUTCH PROBING TO DETERMINE WHAT WOULD BE REQUIRED OF THE

DUTCH GOVERNMENT, EITHER IN POLITICAL COMMITMENTS, REGULATORY

ADJUSTMENTS OR NEW LEGISLATION, TO RESPOND TO THE U.S.

OVERTURES. WE EXPLAINED THAT THEY SHOULD RECONSIDER THEIR

PAST REFUSAL TO SIGN ONTO A BLANKET ASSURANCES AND ADVANCE

CONSENT FOR THIRD COUNTRY TRANSFER OF USG-SOLD OR GRANTED

DEFENSE ARTICLES STATEMENT. THE MFA SECURITY POLICY LAWYERS

WERE NON-COMMITTAL IN RESPONSE, BUT HEARD OUT OUR ARGUMENTS

ABOUT HOW IT WOULD STREAMLINE LICENSING, REDUCE RISKS FOR

CAPITAL INVESTORS IN DEFENSE PROGRAMS AND BE A CATALYST FOR

MORE PREDICTABLE TRANSATLANTIC MARKETING ARRANGEMENTS, ALL

CATALYSTS TO BETTER NATO DEFENSE INDUSTRIAL COOPERATION.

(NOTE: THE DUTCH CHAMPIONED MOU GROUP REVIEW OF THIS MEASURE

SEVERAL YEARS AGO ONLY TO REFUSE TO SIGN ONTO SUCH LANGUAGE

AT THE CONCLUSION OF THE DIALOGUE WITH THE USG. END NOTE).

WE ALSO NOTED THAT THEY ALREADY OPERATE A SPECIAL EMBASSY

LICENSING PROGRAM. THE DUTCH SEEMED MOST INTERESTED IN

LEARNING MORE ABOUT THE MAJOR PROJECT AND GLOBAL COOPERATIVE

PROGRAM AUTHORIZATIONS AS WELL AS WHAT ROLE THEY MIGHT PLAY

IN FACILITATING DUTCH BUSINESSES PARTICIPATING IN THE TECH

DATA EXPORT AUTHORIZATION.

6.(C) SEPARATELY, DUTCH DEPUTY CHOD VAN BAAL DISCUSSED THE

F-16 MNCP POSSIBLE JOINT ACQUISITION OF U.S. PGMS WITH POLOFF

ON 6/14, NOTING IT COULD BE WORTH UP TO USD 400 MILLION IN

EVENTUAL PURCHASES. HE SOUGHT ASSURANCES THAT THE DTSI COULD

ACCOMMODATE THE LIKELY NECESSARY WAREHOUSING AND DISTRIBUTION

PERMISSIONS AND MULTIPLE DESTINATION LICENSES. POLOFF

EXPLAINED THAT THE ITEMS WERE HLSG-LISTED PRIORITIES AND THUS

A KEY FOCUS OF THE DTSI. WHILE OFFERING NO SPECIFIC, BINDING

ASSURANCE, POLOFF NOTED THE SORT OF "JOINT BUY" PROPOSED ON

THE NATO DEFMIN MARGINS WOULD BE THOSE WE HOPE TO EXPEDITE

WITH THE FMS REFORMS. VAN BAAL SEEMED CONTENT WITH ANSWER

AND INDICATED THAT HE MIGHT SUGGEST TO BGEN TEUSSINK THAT

THIS ISSUE BE ADDRESSED IN THE JULY 11 MEETINGS WITH DASD

VOLKMAN. VAN BAAL ALSO NOTED THAT, WHILE THE DANES STILL

NEEDED MORE TIME TO EXAMINE SOME OF THE "VIRTUAL WAREHOUSING"

PROPOSALS AND COST FIGURES, HE FORESAW AN EARLY FALL 2000

TIMEFRAME FOR THE MNCP COUNTRIES TO FINALIZE THEIR PROPOSAL

FOR THE JOINT PGM ACQUISITION. HE ADDED THAT THE FIRST

WORKING GROUP IMPLEMENTATION MEETING WOULD BE "NEXT WEEK" IN

LEUVEN BELGIUM. HE ALSO ELABORATED THAT THE MNCP COUNTRIES

WERE ALL SEEKING LANTIRN AND WOULD FOCUS THEIR VIRTUAL

WAREHOUSING ACQUISITIONS ALMOST EXCLUSIVELY ON THE NEW

GPS-GUIDED FAMILY OF PGM\'S. "THIS WAY WE CAN BUY INTO THE

U.S. WAR STOCKS FOR CRISIS SITUATIONS," HE SUMMARIZED," AND

YOU CAN BE REASSURED THAT WE WILL NOT BUY THESE SORTS OF

BOMBS ELSEWHERE."
