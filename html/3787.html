SUMMARY

2. (C) POLMINCOUNS DELIVERED TO MFA ASSISTANT

SECRETARY-EQUIVALENT CABRAS REF A DEMARCHE ENJOINING THE EU

SIPDIS

NOT TO INVITE ZIMBABWE TO ITS FALL MEETING WITH THE SOUTHERN

AFRICA DEVELOPMENT COMMUNITY (SADC). NOTING THAT THE EU WAS

CONSIDERING CHANGING VENUES AS A COMPROMISE, CABRAS TOOK THE

OPPORTUNITY TO OPEN A DISCUSSION ON HUMAN RIGHTS POLICY,

USING ERITREA AS AN EXAMPLE. THE GOI WOULD LIKE WASHINGTON\'S

INPUT ON HOW TO DEAL WITH DICTATORSHIPS LIKE ASMARA. CABRAS

APPEARS TO BELIEVE THAT SANCTIONS AND CRITICISM LEAD TO

CONFRONTATION, WHICH CAN BREAK DOWN COMMUNICATION. END

SUMMARY.

--------------------------------------------- --------

THE AFRICAN CONTEXT: SANCTIONS LEAD TO CONFRONTATION

--------------------------------------------- --------

3. (C) ON SEPTEMBER 18, POLMINCOUNS DELIVERED REF A

DEMARCHE ASKING THE EU TO EXCLUDE ZIMBABWE FROM ITS NOVEMBER

MEETING WITH SADC TO BRUNO CABRAS, MFA DIRECTOR GENERAL FOR

SUB-SAHARAN AFRICAN AFFAIRS. THIS EXCHANGE FOLLOWED A JULY

17 DISCUSSION ON THE SAME SUBJECT BETWEEN POLOFF AND MFA

OFFICE DIRECTOR MARIO BOLOGNA (REF B). LIKE BOLOGNA, CABRAS

MADE NO PROMISES.

4. (C) AFTER STATING THAT THE EU MIGHT HOLD THE MEETING IN

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 ROME 04613 01 OF 02 250026Z

MAPUTO INSTEAD OF EUROPE IN ORDER TO PREVENT MUGABE FROM

USING IT TO LEGITIMIZE HIMSELF IN THE WEST, CABRAS ARGUED

THAT ZIMBABWE "SHOULD BE SEEN IN THE AFRICAN CONTEXT." HE

NOTED THAT THE ZIMBABWE CRISIS HAS SPARKED AN AFRICAN

CONFRONTATION WITH THE WEST IN MULTILATERAL FORA. CABRAS

CITED GABONESE PRESIDENT BONGO\'S 9/17 UNGA STATEMENT CALLING

FOR THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY NOT TO IMPOSE SANCTIONS ON

COUNTRIES NOT YET ABLE TO LIVE UP TO THE PRINCIPLES OF THE

NEW PARTNERSHIP FOR AFRICA\'S DEVELOPMENT (NEPAD). CABRAS

SAID THAT NOT INVITING ZIMBABWE TO THE SADC MEETING WOULD BE

VIEWED AS A SANCTION, AND THAT AFRICAN COUNTRIES WORRIED THAT

SANCTIONS WOULD ESTABLISH A PRECEDENT THAT COULD BE LATER

APPLIED TO THEM. UNDER THE EU-ACP COTONOU AGREEMENT,

DEMOCRACY, GOOD GOVERNANCE, AND HUMAN RIGHTS WERE BASIC

AGREED VALUES, BUT TO IMPOSE IMMEDIATE COMPLIANCE WOULD BE

DIFFICULT. AFRICANS SEE SANCTIONS AS THE WRONG APPROACH AND

TEND TO RALLY AROUND SANCTIONED COUNTRIES.

--------------------------------------------- ----------

"DO WE GO ABOUT BASHING ALL THE DICTATORS ON THE HEAD?"

--------------------------------------------- ----------

5. (C) CABRAS THEN OPENED A BROAD-RANGING DISCUSSION ON

HUMAN RIGHTS POLICY. HE ASKED, PERHAPS RHETORICALLY, "SHOULD

WE MOVE TO OPEN OTHER FRONTS (SIC)? SHOULD WE SANCTION OTHER

COUNTRIES? DO WE GO ABOUT BASHING ALL THE DICTATORS ON THE

HEAD? THIS IS THE POINT TO CONVEY TO WASHINGTON." HE WANTED

TO KNOW IF WASHINGTON WOULD SUPPORT SIMILAR "SANCTIONS" ON A

COUNTRY LIKE ERITREA, WHICH HAS NOT HELD EVEN PARLIAMENTARY

ELECTIONS IN TEN YEARS AND IMPRISONS ITS POLITICAL OPPONENTS.

ON THE OTHER HAND, THE GOI DOES NOT WANT TO JEOPARDIZE THE

CONFIDENTIAL

PEACE PROCESS WITH ETHIOPIA AND, ACCORDING TO CNN, THERE ARE

TWO AL-QAEDA BASES IN ERITREA THAT HAD TO BE CONSIDERED

"BEFORE YOU START TO DESTABILIZE."

6. (C) POLMINCOUNS REPLIED THAT THE USG BELIEVES THAT

COMMUNICATION WITH A GOVERNMENT SHOULD BE CUT OFF ONLY IN

EXTREME CASES IN WHICH OTHER MEANS HAVE BEEN EXHAUSTED AND WE

MUST SHOW PEOPLE THAT THEIR LEADER HAS NO INTERNATIONAL

LEGITIMACY. CALLING A REFUSAL TO MEET WITH A GOVERNMENT A

SANCTION IS INACCURATE. THE OAU AND SADC ARE INCLINED TO

CHALLENGE MOVES LIKE THIS, BUT IT IS NOT UNREASONABLE TO HOLD

SADC MEMBERS TO THEIR STATED VALUES. FINALLY, IT IS

SYMBOLICALLY IMPORTANT FOR THE EU TO EXCLUDE ZIMBABWE, AND

THERE ARE SEVERAL ALTERNATIVE WAYS TO GET THE WORK DONE.

7. (C) CABRAS CONTINUED UNFAZED: MUGABE HAS BEEN

REPRESSIVE FOR 22 YEARS. IF WE EXCLUDE ZIMBABWE NOW, PEOPLE

WILL ASK WHY. POLMINCOUNS AGREED THAT IT IS IMPOSSIBLE TO BE

COMPLETELY EQUITABLE. CABRAS REITERATED THAT HE WANTED

"IDEAS ABOUT ERITREA FROM YOUR ADMINISTRATION." THE COUNTRY

IS STRATEGICALLY LOCATED AND COOPERATIVE IN THE PEACE PROCESS

BUT REACTS NEGATIVELY TO PRESSURE FOR HUMAN RIGHTS AND

DEMOCRACY. POLMINCOUNS SAID THAT HE HAD NOT SEEN ANY

INDICATION THAT THE USG HAD THE SAME LEVEL OF CONCERN ABOUT

THE HUMANITARIAN SITUATION AND HUMAN RIGHTS ABUSES IN ERITREA

COMMENT: WHY ERITREA?

8. (C) ERITREA WOULD HARDLY APPEAR TO BE THE SECOND-WORST

HUMAN RIGHTS ABUSER ON THE CONTINENT, BUT CABRAS\' RESPONSE

APPEARS BOTH A DEFLECTION OF AN UNWELCOME DEMARCHE AND AN

ATTEMPT TO OBTAIN INFORMATION ON ERITREA POLICY. AS A FORMER

COLONY OF ITALY, ERITREA IS MORE ON THE RADAR SCREEN IN ROME

THAN IS ZIMBABWE. IN ADDITION, THE GOI IS PEEVED BECAUSE IN

OCTOBER 2001, THE GOE EXPELLED THE GOI AMBASSADOR IN ASMARA

AFTER HE PROTESTED (ON BEHALF OF THE EU) A NUMBER OF

POLITICALLY-MOTIVATED ARRESTS. THE GOE CLAIMED HE WAS

INVOLVED IN ESPIONAGE. TEN DAYS LATER, WHEN AMEMBASSY ASMARA

ISSUED ITS OWN CRITICISM OF THE ARRESTS, TWO LOCAL U.S.

EMBASSY EMPLOYEES WERE ARRESTED ON SIMILAR CHARGES. END

COMMENT.

9. (C) ACTION REQUEST: PLEASE PROVIDE UPDATE WE CAN SHARE

WITH CABRAS ON THE USG APPROACH TOWARD ERITREA, PARTICULARLY

WITH REGARD TO HUMAN RIGHTS AND DEMOCRACY.
