SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) DURING HIS DECEMBER 10-11 VISIT TO HARARE, AF

ASSISTANT SECRETARY MET WITH A RANGE OF INDIVIDUALS OUTSIDE

GOVERNMENT, INCLUDING MDC LEADER MORGAN TSVANGIRAI, SUPREME

COURT JUSTICE WILSON SANDURA, UNDP RESREP VICTOR ANGELO,

COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION (CFU) OFFICIALS, AND

REPRESENTATIVES OF HUMAN RIGHTS GROUPS AND OTHER NGOS.

KANSTEINER HEARD A CONSISTENT REFRAIN THAT ZIMBABWE\'S

POLITICAL CRISIS AND ECONOMIC MESS WILL CONTINUE TO WORSEN

IN THE RUN-UP TO THE ELECTION AND THAT THE GOZ/ZANU-PF ARE

EMPLOYING A RANGE OF TACTICS TO INCREASE THEIR PROSPECTS IN

NEXT YEAR\'S PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION AND LIMIT INTERNATIONAL

SCRUTINY OF THEIR ACTIONS. ON ZDERA, TSVANGIRAI RECOGNIZED

THE THREAT OF IMPOSING RESTRICTIONS ON THE RULING ELITE

COULD BE MORE EFFECTIVE THAN THE ACTION, BUT HE HOPED THE

USG WOULD IMPLEMENT AT LEAST ONE RESTRICTION TO DEMONSTRATE

THAT IT MEANS BUSINESS. A PROMINENT HUMAN RIGHTS ACTIVIST

ASSERTED THAT, SINCE THE JUDICIARY HAS BEEN COMPROMISED,

ZIMBABWEANS NOW MUST COUNT ON THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY

TO HOLD THE ZANU-PF LEADERSHIP ACCOUNTABLE FOR ITS ACTIONS.

UNDP RESREP REPORTED THAT WFP IS READY TO MOVE FORWARD WITH

HUMANITARIAN ASSISTANCE, BUT THE GOZ HAS NOT SIGNED THE

LETTER OF UNDERSTANDING BECAUSE IT DISAGREES WITH WFP\'S

ANALYSIS OF THE CAUSES OF THE FOOD CRISIS, AND BECAUSE THE

TWO SIDES HAVE NOT AGREED ON THE DELIVERY MECHANISM. (NOTE:

THE LETTER OF UNDERSTANDING WAS SIGNED SUBSEQUENT TO THIS

MEETING.) END SUMMARY.

MOVEMENT FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE (MDC)

DEPUTY SECRETARY-GENERAL GIFT CHIMANIKIRE AND ELECTIONS

IS UNABLE TO CAMPAIGN IN CERTAIN AREAS OF THE COUNTRY,

PARTICULARLY MASHONALAND CENTRAL AND PARTS OF MASHONALAND

WEST. ASKED HOW THE MDC GOT ITS MESSAGE TO THE RESIDENTS

OF THOSE AREAS, TSVANGIRAI SAID SUPPORTERS GO IN UNDER THE

COVER OF NIGHT TO DISTRIBUTE PAMPHLETS, NOT, HE POINTED

OUT, AN APPROACH ONE WOULD EXPECT IN A DEMOCRATIC SYSTEM.

IN JUNE 2000 AND NEXT YEAR\'S PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION HAS

ENABLED THE PARTY TO PENETRATE AREAS FORMERLY CLOSED TO IT,

AND GIVEN THEM TIME TO CONVINCE ZIMBABWEANS THAT THE ONLY

WAY TO STOP THE VIOLENCE IS TO VOTE OUT THE CURRENT

GOVERNMENT. TSVANGIRAI PREDICTED THAT THE MDC WOULD HAVE

LOST IF THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION HAD BEEN HELD IN JUNE

2000. THE PARTY\'S VICTORY IN THE CHEGUTU MAYORAL RACE IS A

GOOD EXAMPLE OF THE BENEFITS OF THAT BREAK IN THE ACTION,

AND PROVIDED A HUGE PSYCHOLOGICAL BOOST TO THE PARTY AND

JUSTICE SANDURA

12. (C) SUPREME COURT JUSTICE SANDURA IS ZIMBABWE\'S SENIOR

BLACK JUDGE AND HAS SERVED ON THE SUPREME COURT SINCE 1998.

HE WAS WIDELY CONSIDERED THE LEADING CANDIDATE TO REPLACE

FORMER CHIEF JUSTICE GUBBAY BEFORE THE GOVERNMENT\'S MOVE TO

STACK THE COUNTRY\'S HIGHEST BENCH WITH SYMPATHETIC JURISTS.

SANDURA WAS CONTEMPTUOUS OF HIS NEW COLLEAGUES\' DECEMBER 3

RULING ENDORSING THE CONSTITUTIONALITY OF THE FAST TRACK

RESETTLEMENT PROGRAM AND AGREEING THAT THE RULE OF LAW HAD

BEEN RESTORED ON COMMERCIAL FARMS (REF C). THE RULE OF

LAW, HE SAID, PREVAILS NOWHERE IN THIS COUNTRY, NOT ON

COMMERCIAL FARMS AND NOT IN URBAN AREAS. CHIEF JUSTICE

CHIDYAUSIKU\'S JUDGMENT IN THE CASE WAS MERELY A POLITICAL

STATEMENT WHICH IGNORED THE LEGAL ISSUES. IN ORDER TO

CLAIM THAT IT WAS ABIDING BY THE RULE OF LAW, THE GOZ HAD

LEGALIZED THE OCCUPATION OF FARMS. "HOW CAN YOU PASS A LAW

SAYING THAT A TRESPASSER WHO COMES INTO YOUR HOUSE CAN STAY

THERE UNTIL YOU FIND SOMEWHERE ELSE TO PUT HIM?" THE

PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION IS WHAT IS DRIVING FAST TRACK,

SANDURA SAID, AS GIVING LAND TO THE PEOPLE IS THE ONLY

ISSUE MUGABE HAS TO TALK ABOUT.
