SUBJECT: GON PROPOSES BILATERAL CONSULTATIONS MECHANISM

REF: A. (A) KATHMANDU 0402

B. (B) STATE 72411

1. This is an action request. Please see para 5.

2. The Embassy has received a diplomatic note from the

Ministry of Foreign Affairs proposing the establishment of

regular bilateral consultations, a topic raised by Foreign

Secretary Madhu Raman Acharya during his April 3 meetings in

SIPDIS

the Department (Ref B). The text of the Diplomatic Note and

the accompanying proposal have been faxed to SA/INS.

3. Begin text of diplomatic note:

The Ministry of the Foreign Affairs, His Majesty\'s Government

of Nepal, presents its compliments to the Embassy of the

United States of America in Kathmandu and has the honor to

refer to the recent visit to the United States of America of

Foreign Secretary Mr. Madhu Raman Acharya. During the visit,

discussions were held on various aspects of Nepal-US

relations including an agreement on Consultation Mechanism

between Nepal and USA at the Foreign Ministry level for the

promotion of bilateral relations. It is expected that such

(a) consultation mechanism would provide a regular and

institutionalized framework to review and chart a future

course of action on the entire gamut of bilateral relations

in a broad perspective.

The Ministry has the honor to enclose a draft agreement for

discussion and further consideration in this respect.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs, His Majesty\'s Government of

Nepal, avails itself of this opportunity to renew to the

Embassy of the United States of America in Kathmandu the

assurances of its highest consideration.

Shital Niwas, April 29, 2002

End text of diplomatic note.

4. The draft agreement proposes consultations "preferably at

least once a year alternately in the capital of either

country at the Foreign Ministry level" to include "all

aspects of bilateral relations as well as regional and

international issues of mutual interest."

5. Action Request: Embassy reqeuests Department\'s guidance

on how to respond to the MFA\'s note.

MALINOWSKI
