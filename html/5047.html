SUBJECT: SUPPORTING OAS SYG GAVIRIAS\' MISSION: VATICAN

SEEKS TO AVOID POLARIZATION AND VIOLENCE

REF: A) 02STATE 268741 B) 02VATICAN 5607

CLASSIFIED BY: POLOFF GEORGE FROWICK, REASON EO 12958 1.5 B,D

1. (C) SUMMARY: VATICAN COUNTRY DIRECTOR FOR CARIBBEAN

STATES MONS. GIORGIO LINGUA TOLD POLOFF JANUARY 7 THAT THE

HOLY SEE\'S MAIN CONCERN IN VENEZUELA IS TO SEE TENSIONS

REDUCED AND THE PARTIES WORKING TOWARDS AN AUGUST 2003

REFERENDUM, AS PERMITTED BY THE VENEZUELAN CONSTITUTION. THE

VENEZUELAN BISHOPS CONFERENCE\'S DECEMBER 2002 STATEMENT

SUGGESTS THAT THE CHURCH IN VENEZUELA, AFTER THE SHOOTINGS ON

PLAYA FRANCIA DE ALTAMYRA, IS PULLING BACK ITS PREVIOUS

STRONG SUPPORT FOR THE OPPOSITION IN ORDER TO HELP AVOID A

VIOLENT DENOUEMENT. THE HOLY SEE IS OPEN TO A "FRIENDS OF

VENEZUELA" PROCESS ONLY IF IT IS MADE A PART OF GAVIRIAS\'

BRIEF (ASSUMING THAT LULA DA SILVA WOULD HAVE A MODERATING

EFFECT ON CHAVEZ). LINGUA WAS UNSURE WHETHER THE HOLY SEE

WOULD URGE THE PARTIES TO ASK GAVIRIAS TO TABLE A CONCRETE

PROPOSAL. WHILE THE HOLY SEE APPEARS TO BE IN CLOSE CONTACT

WITH BOTH THE VENEZUELAN BISHOPS AND ITS NUNCIO, ITS ABILITY

TO EXERCISE CONTROL OVER THEIR POSITIONS IS LIMITED. EMBASSY

CARACAS MAY WISH TO WORK WITH PAPAL NUNCIO DUPUY IN ADVANCE

OF HIS ANNUAL GREETING TO PRESIDENT CHAVEZ AS DEAN OF THE

DIPLOMATIC CORPS, TO MAKE SURE HE IS ON MESSAGE. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) POLOFF SHARED REF A POINTS ON THE VENEZUELAN CRISIS

JANUARY 8 WITH VATICAN CARIBBEAN AFFAIRS DIRECTOR MONS.

GIORGIO LINGUA. LINGUA SAID THE HOLY SEE\'S WAS VERY

CONCERNED AT THE INCREASE IN CIVIL STRIFE SINCE WE LAST SPOKE

(REF B). HE INDICATED THE VENEZUELAN BISHOPS CONFERENCE\'S

DECEMBER 2002 STATEMENT SHOWED THAT THE CHURCH, AFTER THE

SHOOTINGS ON PLAYA FRANCIA DE ALTAMYRA, WAS BACKING AWAY FROM

ITS PREVIOUS STRONG AND OPEN SUPPORT FOR THE OPPOSITION.

LANGUAGE IN THAT STATEMENT WHICH HIGHLIGHTED THE CONTINUED

SUFFERING OF VENEZUELA\'S POOREST, HOWEVER, WAS NOT AN ATTEMPT

TO BUILD BRIDGES TO CHAVEZ. RATHER, ACCORDING TO LINGUA, IT

INDICATED THE BISHOPS WERE TRYING TO ATTRACT CHAVEZ

SUPPORTERS AWAY FROM THE PRESIDENT. INDEED THE STATEMENT

CALLED ON MEDIA, THE GOVERNMENT AND SECURITY FORCES TO AVOID

INFLAMING THE SITUATION. IT EXHORTED THE GOVERNMENT TO

ENSURE HUMAN RIGHTS AND ESCHEW "STANDING ON LEGALISMS AND

POLITICAL STRATEGIES" THAT THREATEN THE SOCIAL FABRIC. THE

HOLY SEE REMAINS FEARFUL THAT, IN THE BISHOPS\' WORDS, A

VIOLENT "SOCIAL FRACTURE" REMAINS A GREATER POSSIBILITY TODAY

THAN IT WAS BEFORE THE GENERAL STRIKE. YET THE HOLY SEE HAS

NO PLANS AT THIS TIME ACTIVELY TO ENGAGE IN THE DIALOGUE.

LINGUA REITERATED HIS MESSAGE FROM REF B, THAT THE HOLY SEE

HOPED ALL PARTIES WOULD CALM DOWN AND ORGANIZE POLITICALLY TO

CONTEST A REFERENDUM ON THE CHAVEZ PRESIDENCY IN AUGUST OF

THIS YEAR, AS IS PERMITTED IN THE VENEZUELAN CONSTITUTION.

LINGUA OPINED THAT CHAVEZ WAS LOSING SUPPORT AS THE STRIKE

PROCEEDED (EVEN AS THE STRIKE APPEARED TO BE WINDING DOWN),

AND THAT OVER THE TIME BETWEEN NOW AND AUGUST THE

OPPOSITION\'S ABILITY TO CARRY THE REFERENDUM WOULD GROW.

3. (C) LINGUA SAID THE VATICAN WAS OPEN TO A "FRIENDS OF

VENEZUELA" PROCESS ONLY IF IT WAS MADE A PART OF GAVIRIAS\'

BRIEF, BUT WOULD NOT WISH TO SEE A FRIENDS GROUP IF IT

COMPETED (AND THUS DISTRACTED) FROM THE OAS ENVOY\'S WORK. HE

BELIEVED THAT BRAZILIAN PRESIDENT LULA DA SILVA MIGHT BE ABLE

TO EXERCISE A MODERATING INFLUENCE ON CHAVEZ. LINGUA WAS

UNSURE WHETHER THE HOLY SEE WOULD ISSUE A PUBLIC CALL TO

SUPPORT GAVIRIAS\' WORK. HE WAS ALSO NONCOMMITTAL ABOUT HOLY

SEE ENGAGEMENT IN THE PLAN TO HAVE GAVIRIAS TABLE A CONCRETE

PLAN AROUND WHICH THE PARTIES COULD BEGIN DIALOGUE.

4. (SBU) ASKED WHETHER PAPAL NUNCIO DUPUY WOULD GREET

PRESIDENT CHAVEZ AGAIN THIS YEAR AS DEAN OF THE DIPLOMATIC

CORPS, LINGUA SAID HE HADN\'T YET HEARD. HE SPECULATED THAT

THE ANNUAL MEETING MIGHT BE POSTPONED THIS YEAR, GIVEN THE

SITUATION ON THE STREETS. LINGUA WRYLY NOTED THAT IF THE

NUNCIO TRULY SPOKE HIS MIND AGAIN HE MIGHT BE PNG\'D. (NOTE:

SOME MAY RECALL THAT DUPUY\'S URGING OF CHAVEZ IN REMARKS LAST

JANUARY TO MAKE SPACE FOR THE OPPOSITION\'S DEMANDS PROMPTED

FROM THE LATTER A VITUPERATIVE ATTACK ON THE CHURCH AS A

"PARASITE ON VENEZUELAN SOCIETY.") LINGUA TOLD US THAT

NUNCIOS USUALLY DRAFT THEIR ANNUAL REMARKS WITHOUT CLEARING

THEM WITH THE HOLY SEE, AND THAT THEY WOULD GENERALLY AIM FOR

REMARKS THAT REFLECT THE VIEWS OF THE DIPLOMATIC CORPS AS

OPPOSED TO THOSE OF THE HOLY SEE.

5. (C) COMMENT: THE HOLY SEE IS FEARFUL THAT IF IT WERE TO

ENGAGE MORE ACTIVELY IN BRINGING THE VENEZUELAN PARTIES

TOGETHER, ITS OWN POOR RELATIONSHIP WITH THE CHAVEZ

ADMINISTRATION, EXEMPLIFIED IN CHAVEZ\' REACTION TO THE

NUNCIO\'S JANUARY 2002 COMMENTS, WOULD BE MADE EVEN WORSE.

THIS COULD HAVE UNPREDICTABLE CONSEQUENCES FOR THOSE CLERGY

AND FAITHFUL WHO SUPPORT THE OPPOSITION. LINGUA\'S REACTIONS

SEEMED TO INDICATE THAT THE HOLY SEE IS NOT FULLY IN CONTROL

OF THE VENEZUELAN BISHOPS, NOR OF ITS OWN NUNCIO. WE WOULD

BE INTERESTED TO HEAR FROM EMBASSY CARACAS WHETHER THEY ARE

CONSULTING WITH DUPUY ON HIS REMARKS, IF ANY, AT THIS YEAR\'S
