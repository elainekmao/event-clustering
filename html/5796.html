SUBJECT: TURKISH REQUEST FOR SMALLPOX/ANTHRAX VACCINES

REF: A. ANKARA 944

B. 02 STATE 256701

C. 02 STATE 256691

(U) Classified by DCM Robert Deutsch for reasons 1.5 (B) and

(D).

1. (C) MFA\'s International Political Organizations Department

Head Feza Ozturk reiterated GOT\'s acceptance of our offer to

provide access to smallpox and anthrax vaccines at meetings

on 2/17 with EconCouns and 2/18 with EconOff, Post Medical

Officer and the Team Leader of the Department\'s Smallpox

Vaccination Program. The GOT request (Ref A) is to receive

vaccines for 30,000 persons (30,000 doses) for smallpox and

for 70,000 persons (420,000 doses) for anthrax. Once we

advise that the vaccines have been allocated to Turkey, MFA

will arrange for our expert team to meet with the Ministry of

Health and Turkish General Staff regarding training, vaccine

handling and other logistics.

2. (C) ACTION AND COMMENT. Demarche instructions (Ref B)

refer to U.S. willingness to provide access to vaccines and

to the offer as foreign assistance. They do not mention

costs or payments. The Turks understood this to mean "no

cost." We offered the vaccines to the Turks and they

accepted our offer. Embassy requests confirmation that

vaccines are to be delivered at no-cost to GOT. Embassy also

requests confirmation as soon as possible that we can meet

GOT request for specified quantities and receive information

on how these vaccines will be made available.
