SUBJECT: HOLY SEE FEARS FAILURE ON TRIPS IN WTO TALKS WOULD

POSE SERIOUS HUMANITARIAN REPERCUSSIONS; URGES COMPROMISE

REF: DEILY-HARDT TELCON 12/19

1. (U) THE HOLY SEE CONTACTED THE EMBASSY DECEMBER 19 TO

CONVEY ITS URGENT CONCERNS ABOUT THE POTENTIAL FAILURE OF

ONGOING NEGOTIATIONS AT THE WTO GENERAL COUNCIL IN REGARD TO

TRIPS AND PUBLIC HEALTH. IN A NON-PAPER DELIVERED TO THE

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 VATICA 06064 191322Z

EMBASSY (TEXT BELOW), THE HOLY SEE CHARACTERIZED THE U.S. AS

BEING ALONE IN OPPOSITION TO THE CURRENT CHAIRMAN\'S TEXT, AND

SUGGESTED THAT THIS "BLOCKAGE" WOULD "RESULT IN SERIOUS HARM"

TO MILLIONS IN DEVELOPING COUNTRIES AT RISK FROM HIV/AIDS,

MALARIA, TUBERCULOSIS, AND OTHER PUBLIC HEALTH CRISES, WHILE

ALSO DAMAGING A RULES-BASED TRADE SYSTEM. EMBASSY

UNDERSTANDS THAT HOLY SEE PERMANENT OBSERVER TO THE WTO,

DIARMUD MARTIN RAISED THIS CONCERN WITH THE SECRETARIAT OF

STATE AND REQUESTED THEIR INTERVENTION WITH THE EMBASSY.

AMBASSADOR HAS ALSO RECEIVED A REQUEST FROM VATICAN-BASED

MEDIA TO COMMENT ON THE U.S. POSITION IN THESE NEGOTIATIONS.

PER REF DISCUSSION, EMBASSY WILL DEFER PUBLIC COMMENT UNTIL

AFTER THE CONCLUSION OF CURRENT ROUND OF TALKS DECEMBER 20.

WE WILL ALSO CONVEY TO THE HOLY SEE OUR STRONG DESIRE TO

ENSURE LIBERAL LICENSING ARRANGEMENTS FOR THOSE MEDICINES

RELATING TO HIV/AIDS, TUBERCULOSIS, MALARIA AND OTHER

INFECTIOUS EPIDEMICS OF SIMILAR GRAVITY CITED BY THE HOLY SEE

IN ITS NON-PAPER. AS THE NON-PAPER CONCLUDES, THE HOLY SEE

WOULD VIEW A FAILURE OF THE TALKS AS "A MATTER OF SERIOUS

ETHICAL AND HUMANITARIAN CONCERN."

2. (U) THE FULL TEXT OF THE HOLY SEE\'S NON-PAPER FOLLOWS:

BEGIN TEXT:

THE HOLY SEE HAS LEARNED WITH CONCERN OF THE BLOCKAGE OF

NEGOTIATIONS AT THE GENERAL COUNCIL OF THE WORLD TRADE

ORGANIZATION (WTO) ON THE QUESTION OF TRIP\'S AND PUBLIC

HEALTH.

THE DOHA DECLARATION ON THIS ISSUE WAS HAILED AT THE TIME BY

ALL SIDES AS AN IMPORTANT STEP FORWARD IN RECONCILING THE

PUBLIC HEALTH NEEDS OF THE POOREST COUNTRIES AND THE

IMPORTANT BENEFITS WHICH CAN COME FROM A RULES-BASED

BEEN PATIENTLY NEGOTIATED BY THE CHAIRMAN OF THE TRIP\'S

COUNCIL.

A FAILURE TO COME TO AN AGREEMENT BY THE TIME-LIMIT INDICATED

RUNS THE SERIOUS RISK THAT ENTIRE NEGOTIATION WOULD COLLAPSE.

IT WOULD BE A DIFFICULT TO "FREEZE" THE CURRENT AGREED TEXT

FOR ANY SIGNIFICANT PERIOD INTO THE NEW YEAR.

THE FAILURE TO REACH A CONSENSUS WOULD RESULT IN SERIOUS

HARM, ESPECIALLY TO MILLIONS OF PERSONS IN DEVELOPING

COUNTRIES WHO ARE MOST EXPOSED TO HIV/AIDS, TUBERCULOSIS AND

MALARIA, AND TO OTHER POSSIBLE SERIOUS PUBLIC HEALTH CRISIS.

SUCH A FAILURE, IN THE PRESENT CIRCUMSTANCES, WOULD RESULT

INEVITABLY IN DAMAGE TO A RULES-BASED INTERNATIONAL TRADE

SYSTEM, SOMETHING WHICH IS OF PARTICULAR INTEREST TO THE

UNITED STATES.

THE HOLY SEE URGENTLY INVITES THE UNITED STATES TO EXAMINE

ALL THE POSSIBLE WAYS WHICH ARE STILL OPEN TO ADDRESS
