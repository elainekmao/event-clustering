SUBJECT: AMBASSADOR NICHOLSON\'S MEETING ON BIOTECH WITH

HOLY SEE PM EQUIVALENT LEONARDO SANDRI

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 VATICA 05625 01 OF 02 191610Z

1. (U) SUMMARY: AMBASSADOR CALLED ON HOLY SEE DEPUTY

PRIME MINISTER EQUIVALENT LEONARDO SANDRI NOVEMBER 16 TO

EXPRESS U.S. CONCERN OVER THE LACK OF HOLY SEE

INVOLVEMENT REGARDING THE GROWING THREAT OF HUNGER AND

STARVATION IN SOUTHERN AFRICA AND, SPECIFICALLY, THE

ZAMBIAN GOVERNMENT\'S REJECTION OF U.S. FOOD ASSISTANCE

BECAUSE OF ITS BIO-TECH CONTENT. AFTER HEARING THE

DETAILS OF THE SITUATION IN SOUTHERN AFRICA, AND IN

RESPONSE TO THE AMBASSADORS\' FORCEFUL PLEA FOR THE HOLY

SEE TO STEP FORWARD IN RESPONSE TO THIS CLEAR MORAL

ISSUE, SANDRI SAID HE WOULD DISCUSS THE ISSUE WITH PRIME

MINISTER EQUIVALENT SODANO AND FOREIGN MINISTER TAURAN

AND GET BACK TO THE AMBASSADOR THIS WEEK WITH A RESPONSE.

TO INCREASE THE PRESSURE ON THE HOLY SEE, THE AMBASSADOR

ALSO PUBLISHED AN OP-ED IN ITALY\'S LARGEST CATHOLIC DAILY

NEWSPAPER, AVVENIRE, OUTLINING THE MORAL DIMENSIONS OF

THE ISSUE AND URGING THE WORLD COMMUNITY TO FEED THE

HUNGRY FOOD RATHER THAN FEAR.

2. (U) IN A NOVEMBER 16 MEETING WITH DEPUTY PRIME

MINISTER EQUIVALENT ARCHBISHOP LEONARDO SANDRI,

AMBASSADOR OUTLINED U.S. CONCERNS OVER THE MOUNTING FOOD

CRISIS IN SOUTHERN AFRICA AND THE BAFFLING DECISION OF

THE ZAMBIAN GOVERNMENT TO REJECT U.S. FOOD ASSISTANCE OUT

UNJUSTIFIED FEARS OF BIOTECH FOODS. THE AMBASSADOR

POINTED OUT THAT, IN THE FACE OF THE GRAVE HUMANITARIAN

SITUATION IT CURRENTLY FACES, THE GOVERNMENT OF ZAMBIA

HAD ASKED THAT THE WORLD FOOD PROGRAM REMOVE U.S. MAIZE

FROM THE COUNTRY EVEN THOUGH MILLING THE GRAIN WOULD

COMPLETELY ADDRESS THE CONCERNS THEY HAVE PUBLICLY CITED

FOR NOT ACCEPTING THE GRAIN. THE AMBASSADOR POINTED OUT

PAGE 03 VATICA 05625 01 OF 02 191610Z

THE UNCONSTRUCTIVE, POTENTIALLY LIFE-THREATENING ROLE

PLAYED BY THE JESUIT CENTER FOR THEOLOGICAL REFLECTION IN

ZAMBIA, WHICH HAD ENCOURAGED THE GOVERNMENT TO REJECT

BIOTECH FOODS, AND ALSO NOTED THAT ZAMBIA\'S CATHOLIC

BISHOPS IN ZAMBIA HAVE BEEN LARGELY SILENT IN THE FACE OF

THIS CRISIS. WITH LIVES AT RISK, THE AMBASSADOR

EMPHASIZED, THE U.S. BELIEVED THE HOLY SHOULD FOLLOW THE

UN, THE WHO, AND THE EUROPEAN COMMISSION IN ISSUING

PUBLIC STATEMENTS THAT ACKNOWLEDGE THE SAFETY FOR HUMAN

CONSUMPTION OF THE U.S. MAIZE AND THE IMPORTANCE OF

FEEDING THE HUNGRY, ADDING THAT THE HOLY SEE\'S STRONG

MORAL VOICE ON THIS ISSUE COULD POTENTIALLY HELP SAVE

THOUSANDS OF LIVES.

3. (U) IN RESPONSE, SANDRI ASKED IF THE U.S. WAS

PROVIDING THE FOOD FREE OR SELLING THE FOOD. THE

AMBASSADOR SAID IT WAS PROVIDED TO THE WORLD FOOD PROGRAM

FREE OF CHARGE, ADDING THAT WE PROVIDE NEARLY 70 PERCENT

OF THE FOOD TO THE WORLD FOOD PROGRAM FOR SOUTHERN

AFRICA. WITH THE HUMANITARIAN SITUATION IN SOUTHERN

AFRICA WORSENING AND DEMANDS ON DONORS INCREASING,

DECISIONS TO WITHHOLD U.S.-SUPPLIED FOOD RELIEF WOULD

HAVE A POTENTIALLY DEVASTATING EFFECT, THE AMBASSADOR

NOTED.

4. (SBU) COMPARING THE HOLY SEE\'S AND SOUTHERN AFRICAN

BISHOPS SILENCE WITH THE CLEAR STATEMENTS OF OTHER

ORGANIZATIONS AND COUNTRIES, THE AMBASSADOR POINTED OUT

THAT "EVEN GREENPEACE HAD CAVED IN AND SAID, IN LIGHT OF

THE CIRCUMSTANCES WITH PEOPLE STARVING THEY COULD NO

LONGER SUSTAIN THEIR POSITION AGAINST FEEDING PEOPLE

BIOTECH FOODS." WITH EVEN GREENPEACE SHOWING THEIR

COMPASSION IN THIS SITUATION, THE AMBASSADOR ASKED,

"WHERE IS THE CHURCH"?

5. (SBU) SANDRI RESPONDED BY ASSERTING THAT THE EUROPEAN

UNION WAS AGAINST BIOTECH FOODS AND THAT WAS

PROBLEMATICAL FOR THE HOLY SEE. THE AMBASSADOR CLARIFIED

THAT THE EUROPEAN COMMISSION HAD APPROVED FOR

COMMERCIALIZATION A NUMBER OF GM PRODUCTS, AND NOTED THAT

OBJECTIONS RELATED MORE TO HOW TO STRUCTURE

COMMERCIALIZATION RATHER THAN CONCERN OVER THE SAFETY OF

BIOTECH FOODS. HE ALSO POINTED OUT THAT THE HOLY SEE\'S

SCIENTIFIC ADVISORS, THE PONTIFICAL ACADEMY OF SCIENCE,

HAVE TOLD US THEY FOUND NOTHING WRONG WITH THIS FOOD, AND

HAVE ISSUED REPORTS TO THAT EFFECT. IN CLOSING, THE

AMBASSADOR SAID HE COULD NOT BELIEVE THAT THE POPE, WITH

HIS EMPHASIS ON THE VALUE OF HUMAN LIFE AND HIS

OPPOSITION TO EUTHANASIA AND ABORTION, KNOWS OF THIS

SERIOUS SITUATION AND IS NOT EQUALLY OPPOSED TO
