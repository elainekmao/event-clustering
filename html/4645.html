SUBJECT: RSA AMBASSADOR SHEILA SISULU NAMED WFP DEPUTY

EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR EFFECTIVE FEBRUARY 1, 2003

1. WFP HAS ANNOUNCED THE APPOINTMENT OF AMBASSADOR SHEILA

SISULU AS DEPUTY EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR. AMBASSADOR SISULU WILL

ASSUME HER NEW DUTIES ON FEBRUARY 1, 2003.

2. A NATIONAL OF SOUTH AFRICA, AMBASSADOR SISULU BRINGS TO

WFP AN EXTENSIVE BACKGROUND AS AN EDUCATION REFORMER AND

ANTI-APARTHEID ACTIVIST. OVER A TEN YEAR PERIOD, AMBASSADOR

SISULU HELD VARIOUS SENIOR POSITIONS AT THE SOUTH AFRICAN

COMMITTEE FOR HIGHER EDUCATION. SHE HAS ALSO HELD POSITIONS

AS THE NATIONAL EDUCATION COORDINATOR AT THE EQUAL

OPPORTUNITIES COUNCIL AND AT THE SOUTH AFRICAN COUNCIL OF

CHURCHES. AT THE END OF APARTHEID, AMBASSADOR SISULU WAS

APPOINTED AS A SPECIAL ADVISOR TO THE MINISTER OF EDUCATION.

IN 1997 SHE WAS ASKED BY THE GOVERNMENT TO SERVE AS CONSUL-

GENERAL AT THE SOUTH AFRICAN CONSULATE IN NEW YORK. TWO

YEARS LATER SHE WAS APPOINTED AS SOUTH AFRICA\'S AMBASSADOR

TO THE UNITED STATES AND PRESENTLY OCCUPIES THAT POST.

3. IN A RECENT EVENT IN WASHINGTON ON DECEMBER 2, MARKING

AIDS DAY 2002, AMBASSADOR SISULU, COMMENTED THAT THE DROUGHT

IN SOUTHERN AFRICA WAS "CONSPIRING WITH AIDS TO CREATE A

COMMENT

-------

4. WITH THE APPOINTMENT OF AMBASSADOR SISULU, WFP REAFFIRMS

ITS COMMITMENT TO RECRUITMENT AND RETENTION OF STAFF FROM

DEVELOPING COUNTRIES, INCLUDING AT THE SENIOR EXECUTIVE

LEVEL. AND AS WAR AND DROUGHT (PARTICULARLY IN SUB-SAHARAN

AFRICA) CONTINUE TO DRASTICALLY RATCHET UP HUMANITARIAN FOOD

AID NEEDS, WFP ALSO SIGNALS ITS DESIRE TO HAVE A STRONG

ADVOCATE FOR AFRICA AT THE HIGHEST RUNG OF THE ORGANIZATION.

U.S. MISSION IS PLEASED WITH THE APPOINTMENT AND LOOKS

FORWARD TO WORKING CLOSELY WITH AMBASSADOR SISULU. HALL
