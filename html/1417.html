SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE PARLIAMENT RATIFIES OTTAWA CONVENTION

BANNING ANTI-PERSONNEL MINES

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 06956 270833Z

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD FOR REASONS: 1.5

(B) AND (D).

1. (U) ON DECEMBER 5, ZIMBABWE\'S PARLIAMENT PASSED THE

ANTI-PERSONNEL MINES (PROHIBITION) BILL, WHICH RATIFIES

THE OTTAWA CONVENTION BANNING ANTI-PERSONNEL LANDMINES.

PARTIES TO THE CONVENTION ARE PROHIBITED FROM USING,

STOCKPILING, TRANSFERRING, AND PRODUCING ANTI-PERSONNEL

MINES. ON NOVEMBER 16, THE ZIMBABWE NATIONAL ARMY (ZNA)

DESTROYED ITS STOCKPILE OF APPROXIMATELY 4500 LANDMINES

IN A CEREMONY ATTENDED BY WESTERN MILITARY AND

DIPLOMATIC OBSERVERS. AT THE CEREMONY, DEFENSE MINISTER

MOVEN MAHACHI DENIED RUMORS THAT ZIMBABWE WAS USING

ANTI-PERSONNEL LANDMINES IN THE DRC.

2. (C) COMMENT: IN ADDITION TO SUBSCRIBING TO A

WORTHWHILE ENDEAVOR, THE PASSING OF THE ANTI-LANDMINE

BILL DEMONSTRATES THAT THE BITTERLY DIVIDED POLITICAL

PARTIES IN THE ZIMBABWE PARLIAMENT CAN WORK TOGETHER ON

ISSUES OF GLOBAL IMPORTANCE. THE RATIFICATION MEASURE

GOES TO PRESIDENT MUGABE FOR HIS SIGNATURE, AND, GIVEN

THE ZNA\'S VISIBLE BACKING, WE EXPECT HIM TO ENACT IT

INTO LAW. END COMMENT.

MCDONALD
