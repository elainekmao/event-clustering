SUBJECT: UNDP RESREP BRIEFS DONORS ON UN/UNDP

MISSION TO REVIEW ZIMBABWE\'S LAND REFORM PROGRAM

REF: (A). STATE 186563, (B) HARARE 5201

CLASSIFIED BY DCM EARL M. IRVING FOR REASONS 1.5 (B) AND

(D).

1. (SBU) UNDP RESIDENT REPRESENTATIVE AND UN RESIDENT

COORDINATOR IN ZIMBABWE VICTOR ANGELO BRIEFED DONORS ON

SEPTEMBER 28 ON THE TERMS OF REFERENCE OF A FIVE-PERSON

MISSION SENT TO LOOK FOR POSSIBLE AREAS OF ENGAGEMENT ON

ZIMBABWE\'S LAND REFORM PROGRAM. THIS MISSION IS A

FOLLOW-UP TO THE RECENT "MINI-LAND SUMMIT" AT UNGA IN

NEW YORK, WHERE IT WAS SUGGESTED THAT THE UN MIGHT PLAY

A "FOCAL ROLE" IN ASSISTING TO RESOLVE THIS ISSUE (REF

B). PRINCIPALLY THE TEAM WILL DETERMINE WHETHER A SOUND

TECHNICAL PROCESS CAN BE ESTABLISHED GUIDED BY AND

CONSISTENT WITH THE PRINCIPLES AGREED AT THE 1998 LAND

DONORS\' CONFERENCE. THE IMPRESSIVELY QUALIFIED TEAM,

WHICH ARRIVED ON SEPTEMBER 26 AND WILL DEPART ON OCTOBER

13, CONSISTS OF DR. JACQUES CHONCHOL (CHILE), AN EXPERT

ON LAND REFORM; PLINIO SAMPAIO (BRAZIL) AN EXPERT IN

ANALYSIS AND APPRAISAL OF LAND REFORM AND AGRICULTURE;

PROFESSOR H.W.O. OKOTH-OGENDO (KENYA) AN EXPERT IN LAND

LAW AND LEGISLATION; KENNETH ALLISON (JAMAICA), AN

A SENIOR UNDP ECONOMIST. THE UNDP RESIDENT COORDINATOR

INVITED THE HEAVILY ATTENDED MEETING PARTICIPANTS TO

RETURN OCTOBER 12 FOR AN OUT-BRIEFING BY THE TEAM ON ITS

FINDINGS.

2. (SBU) THE UNDP RESREP REPORTED THAT THE TEAM\'S FIRST

MEETING HAD BEEN WITH NEWLY INSTALLED MINISTRY OF

FOREIGN AFFAIRS PERMANENT SECRETARY WILLARD CHIWEWE, WHO

WILL BE THE TEAM\'S GOVERNMENT POINT OF CONTACT, AND WILL

CHAIR ALL MEETINGS ON THE GOVERNMENT SIDE. WHILE

CALLING THE MISSION\'S VISIT "A NEW BEGINNING," CHIWEWE

IMMEDIATELY LAID DOWN A MARKER: THE GOVERNMENT\'S "FAST

TRACK" APPROACH TO LAND REFORM WAS "NON-NEGOTIABLE" AND

COULD NOT BE CHALLENGED. ANGELO SUGGESTED THAT TO

OVERCOME THE ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT\'S INITIAL HARD-LINE

POSITION THE TEAM WOULD PROBABLY PROPOSE THREE

SCENARIOS, STARTING WITH A SHORT-TERM ONE (LEAST

FEASIBLE) AND PROCEEDING TO A LONG-TERM (MOST FEASIBLE)

ONE. THE SENIOR ZIMBABWEAN DIPLOMAT HOPED THE MISSION

WOULD BREAK NEW GROUND IN FINDING A WAY OF RELAUNCHING

THE DIALOGUE ON LAND. ANGELO CONTINUED THAT THE UNITED

NATIONS WOULD DEPLOY A "MIRROR MISSION" TO LOOK INTO THE

RULE OF LAW, WHICH HE SAID WAS A SINE QUA NON FOR A

TECHNICALLY SOUND LAND REFORM PROCESS. THE UNDP RESREP

TOOK PAINS TO ASSURE HIS DONOR LISTENERS THAT THE TEAM

WOULD CONSULT AS BROADLY AS POSSIBLY WITH ALL

STAKEHOLDERS. A SUBTEXT OF ITS ENGAGEMENT WITH THE

GOVERNMENT WAS THE NEED TO REACH DOMESTIC CONSENSUS ON A

MUTUALLY ACCEPTABLE PLAN OF ACTION, AND THUS IT WOULD AT

EVERY OPPORTUNITY REMIND ITS GOVERNMENT INTERLOCUTORS TO

IS ITS IMPACT ON CONSIDERATIONS OF SOCIAL JUSTICE,

TAKEN TO ASSIGN LAND TO THOSE WHO WERE CAPABLE OF

DIPLOMAT\'S QUESTION OF WHAT WOULD BE THE NEXT STEP

FOLLOWING SUBMISSION OF THE TEAM\'S REPORT, VICTOR ANGELO

TECHNICAL PROPOSALS.

4. (C) COMMENT: OUR COLLEAGUES WERE FOR THE MOST PART

THE VERY MODEL OF OLD-FASHIONED DIPLOMATS FACED WITH A

PROPOSITION ABOUT WHICH THEY WERE LESS THAN

ENTHUSIASTIC: OBLIQUE, POLITE, AND NON-COMMITTAL. THOSE

WHO SPOKE CLEARLY, HOWEVER, MADE IT PLAIN THAT RULE OF

LAW IS THE ISSUE FOR THE DONOR COMMUNITY, AND IF FAST

TRACK IS THE ISSUE FOR THE GOVERNMENT, THEN POTENTIALLY

SERIOUS PROBLEMS LIE AHEAD. FOR OUR PART, WHILE WE ARE

IMPRESSED WITH THE ASSEMBLED TEAM, WE QUESTION HOW WE

CAN GET SOMETHING USEFUL OUT OF SUCH A SHORT TDY,

PARTICULARLY IN ZIMBABWE\'S POLARIZED POLITICAL CLIMATE.

RESREP ANGELO, FOR HIS PART, HAS WALKED INTO A TIGHT

SPOT. IF HE IS SEEN AS TOO ACCOMMODATING OF THE

GOVERNMENT, HE RISKS A CONTENTIOUS TENURE HERE. END

COMMENT.
