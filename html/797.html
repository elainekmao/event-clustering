SUBJECT: CROATIAN COURTS CONVICT "RSK" SERBS OF WAR

CRIMES

1. (U) CLASSIFIED BY ROBERT P. FINN, CHARGE, 1.5 (D).

2. (C) IN THE PAST WEEK, CIVILIAN AND MILITARY COURTS IN

CROATIA HAVE SENTENCED MANY SERBS TO PRISON FOR ARMED

REBELLION AND WAR CRIMES COMMITTED DURING THE PAST FOUR

YEARS. UNCRO IS ORGANIZING A TASK FORCE WITH OTHER UN

AGENCIES AND INTERESTED INTERNATIONAL ORGANIZATIONS TO

OBSERVE THESE TRIALS AND MONITOR THE LEGAL AND HUMAN

RIGHTS OF THE DETAINED. POLOFF WILL PARTICIPATE IN THE

MONITORING. MANY SERBS ON TRIAL ARE NOT REPRESENTED BY A

LAWYER, AND THOSE WITH COURT-APPOINTED COUNSEL HAVE

USUALLY MET THEIR LAWYERS ONLY ONCE OR TWICE, OFTEN ONLY

AT THE COURT INVESTIGATIVE HEARINGS. UNCRO HAS REQUESTED

THAT THE GOC EXTEND THE GENERAL AMNESTY FOR ARMED

REBELLION FROM THE CURRENT LIMIT OF MAY 10, 1995 TO COVER

THE AUGUST EVENTS. UN OFFICIALS TELL US THAT THE GOC HAS

BEEN UNRESPONSIVE SO FAR. THE PARLIAMENT RECENTLY

EXTENDED THE QUESTIONING PERIOD UNDER WHICH MEN CAN BE

DETAINED WITHOUT CHARGES FROM THREE TO SIX MONTHS, I.E. TO

FEBRUARY, 1996. AMONG OTHER PROBLEMS, THIS EXTENSION

MEANS THAT THESE INDIVIDUALS CANNOT FILE TO RECOVER THEIR

PROPERTY, WHICH THEY MUST DO BY DECEMBER 4 UNDER THE

CURRENT LAW.

3. (U) ON NOVEMBER 14, THE PARISH COURT IN OSIJEK

CONVICTED, IN ABSENTIA, EIGHT SERBS FROM TENJE (IN EASTERN

SLAVONIA) OF "GENOCIDE," ISSUING SENTENCES BETWEEN 10 AND

15 YEARS IN LENGTH. THE SERBS WERE ACCUSED OF COMMITTING

CRIMES AGAINST THE CROAT AND OTHER MINORITY CIVILIAN

POPULATION OF TENJE IN 1991-1992. THE LIST OF CRIMES

INCLUDED ORGANIZING AND IMPLEMENTING TERROR CAMPAIGNS AND

FORCED LABOR BRIGADES, LIMITING CIVILIAN FREEDOM OF

MOVEMENT, MAKING DEATH THREATS, LOOTING, AND MINING AND

DESTROYING PROPERTY. THE COURT CLAIMED THAT, AS A RESULT

OF THE ACTIVITIES OF THESE EIGHT SERBS, 24 CROATS WERE

KILLED AND 98 WERE EXPELLED FROM THE TERRITORY. GORAN

HADZIC (A NEGOTIATOR IN THE EASTERN SLAVONIA PEACE

AGREEMENT), WAS TO BE TRIED WITH THIS GROUP OF EIGHT, BUT

THE JUDGE DECIDED TO TRY HIS CASE SEPARATELY AT THE LAST

MINUTE.

KRAJINA: THE KNIN GROUP

-----------------------

4. (U) ON NOVEMBER 15, THE KARLOVAC MILITARY COURT LN

GOSPIC CONVICTED ELEVEN KRAJINA SERBS OF ARMED REBELLION

AGAINST THE CROATIAN STATE, SENTENCING THEM TO 2.5 TO 4

YEARS IN PRISON. TWO INDIVIDUALS WERE ACQUITTED AND

RELEASED. ALTHOUGH DETAINED SINCE LAST AUGUST, THE 13

WERE CHARGED AND LEGAL PROCEEDINGS HAD STARTED ONLY TWO

DAYS PREVIOUSLY.

5. (U) ON NOVEMBER 16, THE SAME COURT SENTENCED A SECOND

GROUP OF TWELVE DETAINEES TO 3 TO 6,#QV IN PRISON FOR

SIMILAR CHARGES. TWO HAD JOINED THE REBEL FORCES IN 1991

AND HAD FOUGHT IN THE "ARSK" THROUGHOUT THE WAR. THE

OTHER 10 HAD LEFT THE OCCUPIED AREAS BETWEEN 1991-1993 FOR

SERBIA, HAD BEEN MOBILIZED IN SERBIA DURING THE PAST

SUMMER, AND WERE RETURNED TO SECTORS NORTH AND SOUTH TO

FIGHT. ALL 12 WERE BORN IN CROATIA. ANOTHER 47 SERBS

REMAIN IN THE GOSPIC JAIL UNDER "INVESTIGATIVE

PROCEEDINGS." THEY WILL BE CHARGED AND TRIED NEXT WEEK.

6. (U) ON NOVEMBER 16, THE PARISH COURT IN ZADAR STARTED

TRIAL PROCEEDINGS AGAINST 25 INDIVIDUALS FOR WAR CRIMES

COMMITTED IN SKABRNJA IN 1991. ONLY ONE MAN IS IN

CUSTODY. THE OTHER 24 ARE BEING TRIED IN ABSENTIA. AT

LEAST TWO WOMEN ARE AMONG THE GROUP. A 19-YEAR-OLD

WITNESS TESTIFIED THAT ON NOVEMBER 19, 1991, YUGOSLAV ARMY

(JNA) SERBS SHOT AND SLAUGHTERED SEVERAL OF HIS RELATIVES

AND NEIGHBORS AFTER THEIR BASEMENT HIDING PLACE HAD BEEN

DISCOVERED. HE AND OTHERS WERE TAKEN TO NEARBY VILLAGES

WHERE THEY WERE HELD IN A FREEZER AND TORTURED UNTIL SOME

NON-SERB JNA SOLDIERS SAVED THEIR LIVES.

ARRESTEES FROM WESTERN SLAVONIA

-------------------------------

7. (C) THE SISAK PARISH COURT STARTED TRIAL PROCEEDINGS

AGAINST FIVE SERBS NOVEMBER 16 ACCUSED OF COMMITTING

CRIMES AGAINST HUMANITY AND INTERNATIONAL LAW. SPECIFIC

CHARGES INCLUDE MURDER, LOOTING, DESTRUCTION OF PROPERTY,

AND ORDERING EXPULSIONS. ALL FIVE WERE ARRESTED DURING

THE MAY OFFENSIVE ON SECTOR WEST. (COMMENT: ONE SOURCE

WHO CLAIMS TO HAVE SPOKEN TO SOME OF THESE FIVE MEN SAYS

THEY ARE "GUILTY AS HELL" AND EVEN ADMIT IT TO

INTERLOCUTORS THEY TRUST. THE SAME CONTACT, HOWEVER,

DISTINGUISHES THESE FIVE FROM THE KNIN DETAINEES WHOSE

"ONLY CRIME WAS JUST BEING IN THE RSK AND OF MILITARY

AGE." END COMMENT.)

8. (U) MORE THAN SIX MONTHS AFTER THE CROATIAN OFFENSIVE

IN WESTERN SLAVONIA, UNCRO OFFICIALS KNOW OF 66 CASES OF

SERBS FROM SECTOR WEST WHO ARE STILL UNDER "INVESTIGATIVE

PROCEEDINGS" AND WAITING TO BE CHARGED WITH A CRIME. MOST

OF THESE MEN ARE LOCATED IN POZEGA, BJELOVAR, AND

VARAZDIN. THE OFFICIALS EXPRESSED CONCERN FOR THE MANNER

IN WHICH THE PROCEEDINGS AND TRIALS ARE BEING CONDUCTED.

THEY ESTIMATE THAT TWO-THIRDS OF THE 120-ODD KRAJINA SERBS

DETAINED BY THE CROATIAN POLICE DO NOT HAVE ADEQUATE LEGAL
