1. (C) RULING PARTY CANDIDATE AND MASHONALAND CENTRAL

GOVERNOR ELLIOT MANYIKA SOUNDLY DEFEATED OPPOSITION

CANDIDATE ELLIOT PFEBVE IN THE PARLIAMENTARY BY-ELECTION

HELD JULY 28-29 IN BINDURA DISTRICT. THE RULING ZANU-PF

WIDENED ITS MARGIN OF VICTORY CONSIDERABLY OVER LAST

JUNE\'S PARLIAMENTARY ELECTION, IN WHICH THE MDC LOST BY

JUST OVER 2,000 VOTES. MANYIKA POLLED 15,864 VOTES

AGAINST 9,456 FOR PFEBVE, AN INCREASE OF 4,300 VOTES IN

THE RULING ZANU-PF\'S MARGIN OF VICTORY OVER LAST YEAR.

THE BY-ELECTION WAS HELD TO REPLACE YOUTH DEVELOPMENT

MINISTER AND ZANU-PF COMMISSAR BORDER GEZI, WHO DIED IN

AN APRIL CAR ACCIDENT. THE TURNOUT OF 25,849 REGISTERED

VOTERS WAS VIRTUALLY THE SAME AS LAST JUNE. THE

DOMESTIC MONITORING ORGANIZATION ZIMBABWE ELECTION

SUPPORT NETWORK (ZESN) TOLD US THAT 56,651 VOTERS WERE

REGISTERED IN THE DISTRICT FOR THE ELECTION, A 6.5

PERCENT INCREASE OVER THE 53,191 REGISTERED LAST JUNE.

RINDAI CHIPFUNDE, COORDINATOR OF ZESN, SAID THE SIZE OF

THE INCREASE IN REGISTERED VOTERS RAISED EYEBROWS. SHE

SPECULATED THAT MOST OF THE NEWLY-REGISTERED VOTERS WERE

RECENTLY-RESETTLED PEASANT FARMERS, MOST OF WHOM SUPPORT

ZANU-PF. (COMMENT: THE INCREASE COULD ALSO BE

ATTRIBUTED TO REGISTRATION CAMPAIGNS BY BOTH ZANU-PF AND

MDC, BUT NOT TO DEMOGRAPHIC PATTERNS OR ECONOMIC GROWTH

IN THE REGION. END COMMENT.)

2. (C) THE POLLING DAYS WERE MARRED BY A NUMBER OF

VIOLENT INCIDENTS. THE INDEPENDENT "DAILY NEWS"

REPORTED THAT ON 28 JULY, 21 MDC SUPPORTERS WERE

ABDUCTED BY ZANU-PF SUPPORTERS, AN MDC VEHICLE WAS

STONED, AND FOUR OTHER SUPPORTERS WERE BEATEN AND

HOSPITALIZED. THE PRESS REPORTED THAT ON 29 JULY,

POLICE DETAINED MDC CANDIDATE PFEBVE AND 25 OTHERS FOR

ALLEGEDLY GETTING TOO CLOSE TO A POLLING STATION. MDC

PRESIDENT MORGAN TSVANGIRAI TOLD US JULY 31 THAT ZANU-PF

BUSED LARGE NUMBERS OF PEOPLE INTO BINDURA CONSTITUENCY,

ESTABLISHED BASES THAT WERE USED BY WAR VETERANS AND

PARTY THUGS TO BEAT AND INTIMIDATE VOTERS, AND POSTED

200 ZANU-PF YOUTHS OUTSIDE EVERY POLLING CENTER, A

PRESENCE THAT WAS INEVITABLY INTIMIDATING TO VOTERS. HE

ADDED THAT IN LIGHT OF THESE EFFORTS, IT WOULD HAVE BEEN

NAIVE TO EXPECT THE ELECTION TO GO ANY OTHER WAY. THE

MDC HAD EXPECTED TO RECEIVE ABOUT 40 PERCENT OF THE

VOTES (ITS CANDIDATE RECEIVED 36 PERCENT), AND

TSVANGIRAI SAID THE MDC WOULD NOT CONTEST THE RESULT.

SIPDIS

MDC ELECTIONS DIRECTOR PAUL THEMBA NYATHI TOLD US

EARLIER THAT AN OPPOSITION WIN IN BINDURA COULD HAVE

SPARKED A MORE HARSH AND WIDESPREAD CRACKDOWN ON THE

MDC, OR HAVE CONVINCED ZANU-PF NOT TO HOLD MORE

ELECTIONS AS THEY COULD NOT WIN THEM. THE SEVERAL

MONTHS PRECEDING THE ELECTION WERE BADLY MARRED BY

POLITICAL VIOLENCE AND INTIMIDATION, MAINLY AT THE

EXPENSE OF MDC SUPPORTERS (REF B). HUNDREDS OF ZANU-PF

SUPPORTERS ATTACKED TSVANGIRAI\'S MOTORCADE ON 22 JULY AS

HE TRIED TO CAMPAIGN FOR PFEBVE (REF A).

3. (C) COMMENT: THE BINDURA VOTE, THE FIRST

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 04 HARARE 02311 01 OF 02 011401Z

PARLIAMENTARY BY-ELECTION IN NEARLY SEVEN MONTHS, WAS A

CRUCIAL TEST FOR ZIMBABWE\'S TWO MAIN PARTIES. THE

RESULT DEMONSTRATED THAT THERE ARE STILL A LARGE NUMBER

OF RURAL RESIDENTS WILLING TO VOTE FOR ZANU-PF; A HEAVY

INVESTMENT IN PATRONAGE AND GOVERNMENT HANDOUTS BY

BINDURA\'S FORMER MP, BORDER GEZI, CERTAINLY HELPED

MAINTAIN SUPPORT AMONG THE RULING PARTY\'S CORE CADRE IN

THE DISTRICT. DIRTY TRICKS, SUCH AS REGISTERING NON-

RESIDENT VOTERS AND TURNING AWAY SUSPECTED MDC

SUPPORTERS FROM THE POLLING BOOTH, PROBABLY PLAYED A

ROLE AS WELL. WE SUSPECT THAT VIOLENCE AND INTIMIDATION

WERE ALSO SIGNIFICANT FACTORS. MORE THAN HALF OF ALL

REGISTERED VOTERS STAYED HOME, AND THE NUMBER OF MDC

VOTES FELL BY 16 PERCENT FROM LAST YEAR. ZANU-PF\'S

ABILITY TO CONCENTRATE ITS INTIMIDATION MACHINERY ON A

SINGLE DISTRICT ACCOUNTS FOR MUCH OF THE SWING IN VOTES

FROM LAST YEAR. THE MDC LIKELY WILL NOT CONSIDER THE

BINDURA RESULT A SIGNIFICANT BLOW TO ITS PRESIDENTIAL

PROSPECTS, GIVEN THAT MASHONALAND HAS LONG BEEN A ZANU-

PF STRONGHOLD. MDC SECRETARY-GENERAL WELSHMAN NCUBE

TOLD US RECENTLY THAT THE OPPOSITION PARTY NEEDS TO

GARNER 40 PERCENT OF THE VOTES IN MASHONALAND TO WIN THE

PRESIDENCY.
