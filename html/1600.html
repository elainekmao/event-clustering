ZIMBABWE

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) ZIMBABWEAN MINISTER OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS

STANISLAUS MUDENGE SUMMONED COMMONWEALTH AND SADC HEADS

OF MISSION IN HARARE ON MARCH 21 TO DELIVER A TWO-HOUR

HARANGUE ON THE COMMONWEALTH MINISTERIAL ACTION GROUP

(CMAG) STATEMENT ISSUED THE DAY BEFORE IN LONDON.

"NOTHING WHICH HAS HAPPENED IN ZIMBABWE CONSTITUTES A

VIOLATION OF THE HARARE DECLARATION," THE MINISTER SAID

IN A STATEMENT RELEASED FOLLOWING THE MEETING. CANADIAN

HIGH COMMISSIONER JAMES WALL TOLD CHARGE THAT MUDENGE

INSISTED THAT ONLY "PERSISTENT, CONSISTENT VIOLATIONS OF

THE HARARE DECLARATION" WARRANTED CONSIDERATION BY THE

CMAG, THUS ITS DISCUSSION OF ZIMBABWE UNDER "ANY OTHER

BUSINESS" AND DECISION TO SEND A DELEGATION TO ZIMBABWE

WAS NOT ONLY IRREGULAR BUT ALSO ILLEGAL. HE RULED OUT

ANY CMAG MISSION. ACCORDING TO THE BRITISH DEPUTY HIGH

COMMISSIONER IN HARARE, IAN HAY-CAMPBELL, THE CMAG\'S

DECISION TO ISSUE THE STATEMENT AND SEND THE TEAM WAS

GALVANIZED BY MUDENGE TELEPHONING COMMONWEALTH SECRETARY

GENERAL MCKINNON DURING THE DISCUSSION AND ISSUING

THREATS IN INTEMPERATE LANGUAGE, THAT WERE SUBSEQUENTLY

REPORTED TO THE CMAG MEMBERS. ANY FENCE-SITTERS WERE

IMMEDIATELY CONVINCED OF THE CORRECTNESS OF THE

RECOMMENDATION FOR A MISSION AND STATEMENT.

2. (C) ACCORDINQ TO THE CANADIAN HIQH COMMISSIONER,

MUDENGE GENERALIZED THAT ANY CONCERN IN ZIMBABWE BOILED

DOWN TO LAND. THE FOREIQN MINISTER SAID HIS GOVERNMENT

HAD NOT MOVED AS FAST AS IT SHOULD HAVE TO RESOLVE THE

LAND QUESTION IN THE 1980S AND BY 1997 WAS DESPERATE.

IT WAS GLAD FOR THE EUROPEAN UNION OFFER TO HOST A

DONORS\' CONFERENCE. HE WAVED AWAY THE UTILITY OF THE

CONFERENCE SINCE THE DONORS HAD DONE "NOTHING," AND IN

ANY CASE, THE PROBLEM WAS ATTRIBUTABLE TO BRITAIN\'S

STRING OF BROKEN PROMISES. HE TOLD THE GATHERED HEADS

OF MISSION THAT "INTERNATIONAL CONCERN WAS MISPLACED,"

SINCE THE CURRENT DISPUTE WAS A BILATERAL ONE BETWEEN

THE UNITED KINGDOM AND ZIMBABWE. "DON\'T GANG UP ON

ZIMBABWE," HE PLEADED, "FOR GOD\'S SAKE DON\'T TAKE

SIDES." HE ALSO WARNED THEM NOT TO INTERFERE IN

ZIMBABWEAN DOMESTIC POLITICS (COMMENT: PRESUMABLY A

REFERENCE TO EXTERNAL SUPPORT OF THE OPPOSITION MOVEMENT

FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE. END COMMENT). OTHERWISE, THE

PEOPLE "WILL ACT VERY STRONQLY."

3. (C) DURINQ THE QUESTION AND ANSWER TIME THE MINISTER

REDUCED THE PROBLEMS WITH THE JUDICIARY TO A MISHANDLING

OF CHIEF JUSTICE GUBBAY\'S RESIGNATION. HE CALLED THE

GOVERNMENT\'S CRITICISM OF THE JUDICIARY "A HEALTHY

DEMOCRATIC EXERCISE," CITING FREQUENT CRITICISM OF

JUDICIAL RULINQS AND IMPEACHMENT OF JUDGES BY

LEGISLATURES IN THE U.S. HE ALSO CAST ASPERSIONS

PERSONALLY ON GUBBAY, WHO COMPARED UNFAVORABLY WITH

PREVIOUS CHIEF JUSTICES WHO WERE MUCH BETTER AT HANDLING

CRITICISM. HE ACKNOWLEDGED THAT THE SUPREME COURT\'S

RULINGS ON LAND WERE CORRECT LEGALLY, BUT NOT MORALLY.

HE POOH-POOHED CRITICISMS OF THE GOVERNMENT\'S APPROACH

TOWARD THE MEDIA, SAYING ZIMBABWE HAS "EXTREME PRESS

FREEDOM," AND REMARKED (ERRONEOUSLY) THAT TWO FOREIGN

JOURNALISTS HAD RECENTLY BEEN EXPELLED BECAUSE THEIR

WORK PERMITS HAD EXPIRED. REGARDING THE BOMBING OF THE

"DAILY NEWS" THE MINISTER SAID, "SO WHAT?" AND NOTED

THAT THE GOVERNMENT-CONTROLLED DAILY "THE HERALD"

OFFERED TO PRINT THE PAPER THE FOLLOWING DAY. HE

CONTINUED THAT LAND REFORM WAS IRREVERSIBLE AND

ACKNOWLEDGED THAT THE CURRENT PROCESS WAS COMPLETELY

FLAWED. HE WENT ON TO DRAW AN ANALOGY ABOUT PLANE CRASH

VICTIMS EATINQ HUMAN FLESH TO SURVIVE IN AN UNFORTUNATE

ATTEMPT TO EXPLAIN THAT HIS GOVERNMENT HAS BEEN DRIVEN

TO EXTREME MEASURES TO SATISFY THE PEOPLE\'S HUNGER FOR

LAND. HE ENDED THE MEETING BY STATING THAT "WE [IN THE

ZANU-PF- LED GOVERNMENT] ARE REVOLUTIONARIES" WHO

BELIEVE THAT "WHAT WE ARE DOINQ IS RIQHT" AND WOULD

RATHER BE DEFEATED THAN BACK DOWN.
