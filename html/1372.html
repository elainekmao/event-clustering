SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE SUPREME COURT OVERRULES HIGH COURT ON

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD FOR REASONS: 1.5

(B) AND (D).

1. (U) ON NOVEMBER 24, ZIMBABWE\'S SUPREME COURT

OVERRULED THE NOVEMBER 20 HIGH COURT DECISION TO GIVE

"INTERIM RELIEF" TO FARM SQUATTERS FACING EVICTION FROM

OCCUPIED FARMS. IN RESPONDING TO A CLASS ACTION LAWSUIT

ON BEHALF OF RURAL PEASANT FARMERS (REF B), THE HIGH

COURT\'S JUDGE PRESIDENT, GODFREY CHIDYASIKU, IN EFFECT

TRIED TO SUSPEND THE SUPREME COURT\'S CONSENT DECREE,

WHICH ORDERED POLICE TO REMOVE ILLEGALLY SETTLED

SQUATTERS FROM COMMERCIAL FARMS. ACCORDING TO THE

NOVEMBER 25 "THE HERALD," THE SUPREME COURT UNANIMOUSLY

RULED THAT THE HIGH COURT DECISION "IS INVALID AND OF NO

FORCE AND EFFECT" AND THAT THE HIGH COURT HAS NO

AUTHORITY TO OVERRULE SUPREME COURT DECISIONS. SINCE

THE CLASS ACTION WAS MADE AS A CHALLENGE ON

CONSTITUTIONAL GROUNDS, CHIEF JUSTICE ANTHONY GUBBAY,

ASKED THE CLAIMANT RHETORICALLY WHY THE CASE WAS NOT

BROUGHT BEFORE THE SUPREME COURT, WHICH IS THE ONLY

COURT EMPOWERED TO HEAR CONSTITUTIONAL CASES.

2. (C) IN SPEAKING TO DCM AND POLOFF NOVEMBER 28, BRIAN

RAFTOPOULOS, RESEARCH FELLOW AT THE UNIVERSITY OF

ZIMBABWE\'S INSTITUTE OF DEVELOPMENT STUDIES, STATED THAT

THE HIGH COURT JUDGE PRESIDENT IS TRYING TO CREATE

CLEAVAGES IN THE JUDICIARY. THE PROCESS TO REMOVE

JUSTICES IS TOO SLOW FOR PRESIDENT MUGABE, RAFTOPOULOS

CONTINUED, SO HE IS LOOKING FOR OTHER WAYS TO UNDERMINE

THE JUDICIARY. OTHER INTERLOCUTORS HAVE TOLD US THAT

JUSTICE CHIDYASIKU IS ONE OF SEVERAL JUSTICES ON THE

HIGH COURT LOYAL TO ZANU-PF. RAFTOPOULOS BELIEVES THAT

JUSTICES SYMPATHETIC TO THE RULING PARTY MAY IN THE

FUTURE TRY TO EXPLOIT AMBIGUITIES IN THE LAW TO FURTHER

ITS CAUSE. THE ATTORNEY REPRESENTING THE CLASS ACTION

CLAIMANTS HAD ARGUED TO THE SUPREME COURT THAT THE HIGH

COURT HAD "INHERENT JURISDICTION AS A COURT OF JUSTICE

TO PREVENT A MISCARRIAGE OF JUSTICE."

3. (C) COMMENT: THE JUDGE PRESIDENT\'S ATTEMPT TO

OVERRULE THE SUPREME COURT, AND THE WAR VETERANS\' BRIEF

INVASION OF THE SUPREME COURT BUILDING ON NOVEMBER 24

(REF A) COULD PRESAGE AN INTENSIFIED ZANU-PF CAMPAIGN

AGAINST THE JUDICIARY. DESPITE THE BLATANT ATTEMPT TO

INTIMIDATE THE SUPREME COURT JUSTICES BEFORE THEIR

RULING, THE JUSTICES QUICKLY REGROUPED AND DECISIVELY

STRUCK DOWN A HIGH COURT RULING THAT HAD LITTLE LEGAL

MERIT. IN SOME CASES, POLICE HAVE BEGUN ISSUING

EVICTION ORDERS TO FARM SQUATTERS, BUT NO LARGE-SCALE

ACTION IS BEING TAKEN TO IMPLEMENT THE SUPREME COURT\'S

CONSENT ORDER TO REMOVE THE SQUATTERS.
