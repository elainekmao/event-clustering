ATTACKS ON THE JUDICIARY

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\' AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS 1.5 (B/D).

REFS: A. (00) HARARE 7286, B. (00) HARARE 6547, C. (00)

HARARE 6330, D. (00) HARARE 5469, E. (00) HARARE 5337,

1. (C) SUMMARY: AS OF JANUARY 11, THERE IS STILL NO

OFFICIAL GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE (GOZ) STATEMENT OR

REACTION TO THE SUPREME COURT RULING OF DECEMBER 21 IN

FAVOR OF THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION (CFU) IN THEIR

LAWSUIT ON THE CONSTITUTIONALITY OF THE "FAST-TRACK"

LAND RESETTLEMENT PROGRAM (REF A). THERE HAVE, HOWEVER,

BEEN NUMEROUS VERBAL ATTACKS ON THE JUDICIARY BY HIGH-

LEVEL GOZ OFFICIALS. AT THE OPENING OF THE HIGH COURT

IN BULAWAYO ON JANUARY 8, THE JUDGE PRESIDENT OF THE

HIGH COURT, GODFREY CHIDYAUSIKU, AN ARDENT MUGABE AND

ZANU-PF SUPPORTER, LAUNCHED INTO A DIATRIBE AGAINST THE

SUPREME COURT AND ITS POSITION ON THE LAND REFORM ISSUE,

STATING THAT THE COURT HAD EFFECTIVELY INVITED THE CFU

TO SUE THE GOZ OVER THE LAND PROGRAM BY THE COURT\'S

PUBLIC STATEMENTS ON THE PROGRAM\'S CONSTITIONALITY. TWO

DAYS LATER ON JANUARY 10, AT A CAMPAIGN APPEARANCE FOR A

PARLIAMENTARY BY-ELECTION, VICE PRESIDENT MUZENDA

STRONGLY CRITICIZED THE SUPREME COURT FOR UPHOLDING

COLONIAL-ERA LAWS THAT DEPRIVE BLACK ZIMBABWEANS OF

THEIR RIGHTFUL HERITAGE OF THE LAND. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) ON JANUARY 9 THE GOZ-CONTROLLED "THE HERALD"

NEWSPAPER REPORTED THAT ON THE OCCASION OF OFFICIAL

ANNUAL OPENING OF THE HIGH COURT IN BULAWAYO ON JANUARY

8, THE JUDGE PRESIDENT OF THE HIGH COURT, GODFREY

CHIDYAUSIKU, LASHED OUT AT THE SUPREME COURT FOR ITS

ROLE IN THE LAND REFORM PROCESS IN ZIMBABWE. (COMMENT:

THE JUDGE PRESIDENT IS CONSIDERED BY MOST OBSERVERS TO

BE AN ARDENT SUPPORTER OF PRESIDENT MUGABE AND ZANU-PF,

AND THERE IS SPECULATION THAT WITH THE TIRELESS LOBBYING

OF HIS FRIEND, MINISTER OF STATE FOR INFORMATION AND

PUBLICITY JONATHAN MOYO, HE WILL BE NAMED CHIEF JUSTICE

OF THE SUPREME COURT BY MUGABE WHEN THE CURRENT CHIEF

JUSTICE RETIRES IN 2002. END COMMENT.) CHIDYAUSIKU DID

NOT SPECIFICALLY MENTION THE DECEMBER 21 SUPREME COURT

RULING. INSTEAD, HE ALLEGED THAT SUPREME COURT CHIEF

JUSTICE ANTONY GUBBAY GAVE COMMERCIAL FARMERS THE GREEN

LIGHT TO SUE WHEN, IN JANUARY 1991, HE CRITICIZED

PROVISIONS OF A CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENT BILL THAT

SOUGHT TO GIVE THE GO THE POWER TO COMPULSORILY ACQUIRE

LAND FOR RESETTLEMENT WITHOUT PAYING COMPENSATION. HE

ADDED THAT GUBBAY\'S COMMENTS ASSURED THE FARMERS THAT

THEIR LEGAL ACTIONS WOULD BE SUCCESSFUL. IN ADDITION,

CHIDYAUSIKU CRITICIZED THE SUPREME COURT\'S RULING

OVERTURNING HIS OWN RULING THAT GRANTED A STAY OF

EXECUTION OF THE NOVEMBER 10 SUPREME COURT CONSENT ORDER

REQUIRING THE GOZ TO REMOVE ILLEGAL OCCUPIERS FROM FARMS

WITH ALL DUE SPEED (REF B). WHILE ACKNOWLEDGING THAT

THE RULING WAS BINDING ON THE HIGH COURT, HE ADDED THAT

HE HAD DIFFICULTY ACCEPTING THE CORRECTNESS OF THE

ATTACKS ON THE JUDICIARY

HELD IN BIKITA WEST FOR THE UPCOMING PARLIAMENTARY BY-

ELECTION. MUZENDA CRITICIZED THE JUDICIARY FOR GIVING

FAVORABLE JUDGEMENTS TO COMMERCIAL FARMERS AT THE

EXPENSE OF THE MAJORITY OF BLACKS THAT REMAIN LANDLESS

AND IMPOVERISHED. MUZENDA STATED THAT HE FAILED "TO

UNDERSTAND JUDGES WHO, IN THEIR JUDGEMENT, ACKNOWLEDGE

THAT ZIMBABWEANS WERE COLONIZED...DEPRIVED OF THEIR

LAND...AND THEIR RIGHTS BY WHITE COLONIZERS. YET THEY

PROCEED, WITHIN THE SAME JUDGEMENT, TO INSIST ON THE

SANCTITY OF THE PROPERTY RIGHTS OF THE SAME WHITE MAN

WHO TODAY THRIVES ON ILL-GOTTEN WEALTH."

5. (C) COMMENT: THE ABSENCE OF AN OFFICIAL GOZ COMMENT

ON THE DECEMBER 21 SUPREME COURT RULING, WHILE GOZ

OFFICIALS MAKE SCURRILOUS ATTACKS ON THE JUDICIARY,

INDICATES TO US THAT PRESIDENT MUGABE MAY STILL BE

CONSIDERING HIS OPTIONS ON LAND REFORM. THE SUPREME

COURT RULING DID LEAVE MUGABE AND THE GOZ SOME WIGGLE

ROOM ON ITS LAND RESETTLEMENT PROGRAM, AND THEY CONTINUE

TO HOLD OUT HOPE THAT DONORS WILL RETURN TO ZIMBABWE

WITH MONEY FOR LAND REFORM. END COMMENT.
