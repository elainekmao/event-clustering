SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE\'S COMMERCIAL FARMERS BEING BACKED TO THE

WALL

CLASSIFIED BY ECON/COMM OFFICER MARK PROKOP, REASON 1.5 (B),

(D)

1. (C) ON AUGUST 28 THREE COMMERCIAL FARMERS REPRESENTING

THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION (CFU) PAID A CALL ON THE DCM AND

ECONOFF TO DESCRIBE THE SERIOUSNESS OF THEIR COLLECTIVE

PLIGHT AND TO ADVISE US THAT IN THEIR VIEW TIME WAS RUNNING

OUT TO HALT MUGABE\'S ACTIONS THAT ARE DESTROYING ZIMBABWE\'S

ECONOMIC FOUNDATION. THEY ALSO REQUESTED THE USG VIEW OF THE

SITUATION, AND ADVISED US THAT THEY WERE MAKING SIMILAR CALLS

AT OTHER PRINCIPAL MISSIONS INCLUDING THE UK, GERMANY, THE

EU, THE BELGIANS AND SOME OF THE NORDIC COUNTRIES. THE

VISITORS WERE WILLIAM HUGHES, CFU VICE PRESIDENT FOR REGIONS;

MACK CRAWFORD, CFU REPRESENTATIVE FOR MATABELELAND; AND DAVE

DUBARE, A MATABELELAND FARMER AND CFU MEMBER. THEY BELIEVE

FIRMLY THAT PRESIDENTS MBEKI, MOGAE AND CHISSANO HOLD THE KEY

TO CHANGING MUGABE\'S PATH, AS THEIR COUNTERPARTS DID YEARS

AGO WHEN THEIR CONCERTED ACTION FORCED IAN SMITH FROM POWER.

2. (C) THE CORE MESSAGE THEY DELIVERED WAS A SIMPLE ONE:

AFTER 18 MONTHS OF HARASSMENT AND EXTREME FINANCIAL AND

PSYCHOLOGICAL PRESSURE, THE COUNTRY\'S COMMERCIAL FARMERS ARE

CLOSE TO THE POINT OF THROWING IN THE TOWEL, EITHER

COLLECTIVELY OR INDIVIDUALLY. THE PRESSURE HAS RISEN

CONSIDERABLY IN RECENT WEEKS, AS EVENTS IN CHINHOYI, HWEDZA,

DHOMA AND MATABELELAND INDICATE. THE FARMERS DESCRIBED A

NUMBER OF DIFFERENT TACTICS BEING USED IN VARIOUS LOCATIONS

BY THE GOZ TO DRIVE THEM OUT OF BUSINESS OR OFF THEIR FARMS.

FOR EXAMPLE, IN THE WESTERN DISTRICTS AND IN THE AREA BETWEEN

HARARE AND MUTARE, WIDESPREAD BURNING OF WINTER CATTLE

GRAZING PASTURES HAS BEEN CARRIED OUT TO DESTROY THE

VIABILITY OF BEEF OPERATIONS (THE FOOT AND MOUTH DISEASE

OUTBREAK HAS SHUT DOWN ALL CATTLE TRANSPORT - SO RELIEF BY

HERD RELOCATION IS RULED OUT). AT THE SAME TIME FIRES HAVE

BEEN SET IN ADJOINING COMMUNAL AREAS TO DRIVE PEASANTS ONTO

THE RANCHERS\' PROPERTIES, WHERE THEY CONSIDER THE STOCK TO BE

THERE FOR THE TAKING. ANOTHER TACTIC WAS MAKING SURE THAT AN

INVADER\'S SETTLEMENT IS ON EACH AND EVERY COMMERCIAL FARM,

AND THAT AT LEAST ONE AUTOMATIC WEAPON IS AT EACH SETTLEMENT

TO BE SHOWN TO FARM WORKERS TO REMIND THEM OF THEIR FATE

SHOULD THEY INTERFERE WITH THE INVADERS OR VOTE WRONGLY IN

ANY UPCOMING ELECTIONS. THE FARMERS POINTED OUT THAT THE

GOVERNMENT\'S ARRAY OF EVOLVING TACTICS WAS CLEARLY DESIGNED

AND EXECUTED TO SKATE BELOW THE RADAR SCREEN OF INTERNATIONAL

ATTENTION BROUGHT ON BY VIOLENCE, AND ALL AGREED THAT THE GOZ

HAD BEEN FAIRLY SUCCESSFUL OF LATE IN THIS REGARD.

3. (C) THE FARMERS TOLD US THAT OUT OF FEAR, EXASPERATION

AND SIMPLE FATIGUE, A COMMUNITY OF FARMERS IN THE VIRGINIA

AREA (ABOUT 100 K EAST OF HARARE AND AN AREA THAT SAW MUCH

VIOLENCE, INTIMIDATION AND A NUMBER OF MURDERS IN THE RUNUP

TO PARLIAMENTARY ELECTIONS LAST YEAR) HAD CUT AN INDEPENDENT

DEAL WITH THE INVADERS, GIVING UP 30 TO 40 PERCENT OF THEIR

LAND JUST TO BE LEFT ALONE AND IN PEACE. THIS WAS THE FIRST

WE HAD HEARD OF SUCH A DEVELOPMENT, AND THE FARMERS

REPRESENTING THE UNION CLEARLY UNDERSTOOD THAT SUCH ACTIONS

UNDERMINED NATIONAL COLLECTIVE EFFORTS AND PLAYED INTO THE

WISHES OF THE RULING PARTY. AS ONE OF THEM QUIPPED ON THEIR

WAY OUT, "UNITED WE NEGOTIATE, DIVIDED WE BEG."

4. (C) THE FARMERS ALSO POINTED OUT THAT THE RAPIDLY

DETERIORATING AND HYPERINFLATIONARY ECONOMIC ENVIRONMENT,

SPURRED BY THE RAPIDLY DECLINING ZIMDOLLAR, MAY ALSO PUT ANY

OF THOSE FARMERS WHO REMAIN OUT OF BUSINESS. "WELL OVER HALF

OF OUR INPUTS ARE IMPORTED, AND WE JUST CANNOT PAY 700, 1000

OR 1500 ZIMDOLLARS (THE LATTER A YEAR END RATE PREDICTION) TO

THE U.S. DOLLAR AND STAY IN BUSINESS." THEY CLOSED WITH THE

POINT THAT THEY AND THEIR COLLEAGUES ARE BEING PUSHED TO THE

BRINK, AND WITH A PLEA THAT THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY, WHEN

THE POLITICAL ISSUES ARE FINALLY DECIDED, BE PREPARED TO AID

AGRICULTURE\'S RESUSCITATION VIA A SIZABLE HARD CURRENCY

FACILITY PRICED AT REASONABLE RATES TO GET THE INDUSTRY BACK

ON ITS FEET.

5. (C) COMMENT: WE DIFFER WITH NONE OF THE ANALYSIS OR

CONCLUSIONS REACHED BY THE FARMERS. THEIR RECOGNITION OF THE

GUILE AND TACTICS ARRAYED AGAINST THEM IS NOT FLAWED, AND

THEIR COMMERCIALLY-BASED ECONOMIC SUGGESTIONS ARE REASONABLE

ONES. WE SKETCHED OUT USG CONCERNS AND INTERESTS, AND SOME

OF THE POSSIBLE PATHS FORWARD. OUR CONTACTS WITH THIS GROUP

WILL CONTINUE, AS BOTH SIDES FOUND IT WORTHWHILE, EVEN IN THE

CONTEXT OF A VERY BLEAK OUTLOOK.
