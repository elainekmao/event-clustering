SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE WILL ADHERE TO DRC DISENGAGEMENT PLANS

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 01028 151337Z

REFS: A) STATE 45916, B) HARARE 1019

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS: 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) ON MARCH 15, POST DELIVERED REFTEL DEMARCHE TO

BERNARD TAKAVARASHA, THE UNDER SECRETARY FOR AFRICA IN

ZIMBABWE\'S MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS. POLOFF STATED

USG CONCERNS THAT IT IS MORE IMPORTANT THAN EVER FOR THE

BELLIGERENTS TO AVOID CONFLICT WHILE THE DISENGAGEMENT

OF FORCES BEGINS AND TO ADHERE TO THE DISENGAGEMENT

SCHEDULE. TAKAVARASHA AGREED, AND STATED THAT ZIMBABWE

"WILL DO WHAT IT IS COMMITTED TO DO." HE NOTED THAT IF

ANY DELAYS OCCUR IN ZIMBABWE\'S WITHDRAWAL, IT WILL BE

BECAUSE OF FIGHTING INITIATED BY THE OTHER SIDE.

"ZIMBABWE HAS NOT BEEN THE PROBLEM," HE ARGUED; "WE HAVE

BEEN TRYING TO GET OUT OF THE DRC FOR A LONG TIME."

2. (C) POLOFF ASKED THE UNDER SECRETARY TO COMMENT ON

FOREIGN MINISTER MUDENGE\'S STATEMENT, REPORTED IN THE

MARCH 15 EDITION OF "THE DAILY NEWS," THAT ZIMBABWE WILL

BEGIN PULLING BACK ITS FORCES FROM THE FRONTLINE BY 15

KM. ON MARCH 15. TAKAVARASHA RESPONDED THAT HE WAS

UNAWARE OF THE SITUATION ON THE GROUND AT THE MOMENT BUT

REITERATED THAT THE GOZ WOULD FOLLOW THROUGH ON ITS

COMMITMENTS.

3. (C) COMMENT: TAKAVARASHA, RELATIVELY NEW TO HIS

POSITION, DOES NOT APPEAR AS WELL-INFORMED ON DRC

MATTERS AS SOME OF HIS MORE JUNIOR COLLEAGUES.

PRINCIPAL ADMINISTRATIVE OFFICER FOR THE DRC, MAXWELL

CONFIDENTIAL
