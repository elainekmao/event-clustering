SUBJECT: TIME REPORTER ON FREE VIETNAM MOVEMENT

1. CLASSIFIED BY ROBERT C. PORTER, JR., CHARGE

D\'AFFAIRES, A.I., REASON 1.5(D).

2. (C) A TIME MAGAZINE CORRESPONDENT INFORMED CHARGE

ON NOVEMBER 19 THAT SHE RECENTLY INTERVIEWED BOTH VO

VAN DUC AND NGUYEN HUU CHANH. DURING THE COURSE OF THE

INTERVIEWS, BOTH MEN REPORTEDLY DISCUSSED THE

OBJECTIVES AND METHODS OF THEIR ORGANIZATION, THE FREE

VIETNAM MOVEMENT (FVM). THE CORRESPONDENT TOOK

EXTENSIVE NOTES AND RECORDED MUCH OF THE INTERVIEW.

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HANOI 03049 200944Z

SHE INDICATED THAT IF U.S. LEGAL AUTHORITIES WERE

INTERESTED IN LISTENING TO THE TAPE OF THE INTERVIEWS,

THEY SHOULD CONTACT THE LEGAL DEPARTMENT OF TIME

MAGAZINE. (SHE ADMITTED THAT SHE WASN\'T CERTAIN THAT

ACCESS TO THE MATERIALS WOULD BE GRANTED.)

2. (C) IN ADDITION, THE CORRESPONDENT SAID THAT DUC

OR CHANH GAVE HER A COPY OF A FVM VIDEO TAPE THAT

REPORTEDLY DESCRIBES AND DEPICTS THE ACTIVITIES OF THE

ORGANIZATION. SHE SAID THAT SHE WOULD BE WILLING TO

PROVIDE THE TAPE TO USG INVESTIGATORS IF THEY WERE

INTERESTED.

3. (C) FINALLY, THE CORRESPONDENT RECALLED THAT

EITHER DUC OR CHANH OR BOTH WERE ACTIVE IN PHNOM PENH

AND ELSEWHERE IN CAMBODIA IN THE EARLY TO MID-1990\'S.

IN 1994, THEY REPORTEDLY RAN A VOCATIONAL TRAINING

SCHOOL IN PHNOM PENH (PACIFIC TECHNICAL SCHOOL) WHICH,

ACCORDING TO THE CORRESPONDENT, THEY ADMITTED WAS A

COVER FOR RECRUITMENT AND TRAINING OF FVM SUPPORTERS

FOR POSSIBLE INFILTRATION INTO VIETNAM.

4. (C) PLEASE PROTECT THE SOURCE OF THIS INFORMATION.

PORTER
