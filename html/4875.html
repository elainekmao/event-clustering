SUBJECT: AMBASSADOR PRESSES VATICAN DIPLOMATIC COLLEAGUES

FOR SUPPORT ON AFRICAN FAMINE AND BIOTECH FEARS

1. SUMMARY: AMBASSADOR HOSTED A MEETING ON THE AFRICA

FAMINE AND U.S. BIOTECH RELIEF FOOD DECEMBER 18 FOR THIRTY

HOLY SEE DIPLOMATIC COLLEAGUES FROM EUROPE, AFRICA, ASIA,

AND LATIN AMERICA. WORLD FOOD PROGRAM DEPUTY EXECUTIVE

DIRECTOR JEAN JACQUES GRAISSE, U.S. AMBASSADOR TO FODAG

TONY HALL, AND ITALIAN GENETICIST FROM THE ITALIAN NATIONAL

RESEARCH CENTER IN NAPLES DR. ROBERTO DEFEZ BRIEFED THE

HOLY SEE AMBASSADORS ON THE STATE OF THE AFRICAN FAMINE AND

URGED THEM TO WORK BOTH WITH THEIR CAPITALS AND WITH THE

HOLY SEE TO DEVELOP MORE HUMANITARIAN RELIEF. ALL

PRESENTERS, INCLUDING AMBASSADOR NICHOLSON, OFFERED CLEAR

AND COMPELLING COMMENTARY ON THE NEED TO DO MORE TO RESPOND

TO THIS CRISIS AND EMPHASIZED THE SAFETY OF US FOOD. END

SUMMARY.

2. WFP DEPUTY EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR JEAN JACQUES GRAISSE LAID

OUT THE FOOD NEEDS FOR AFRICA OVER THE COMING MONTHS,

FOCUSING ON THE FOLLY OF REFUSING TO ACCEPT "COMPLETELY

SAFE" U.S. FOOD. HE TERMED THIS "THE MOST ABSURD

SITUATION" HE HAD SEEN IN A CAREER OF RELIEF WORK.

AMBASSADOR HALL REVIEWED HIS RECENT MISSION TO AFRICA AND

URGED THE AMBASSADORS TO GET THEIR GOVERNMENTS TO STEP UP

THEIR ASSISTANCE LEVELS, BOTH OF COMMODITIES AND OF CASH.

HE UNDERSCORED THE MORAL ISSUES AT HAND, AND NOTED THAT HOW

POLITICAL LEADERS RESPOND -- BOTH IN FAMINE-STRUCK

COUNTRIES AND DONOR COUNTRIES, WILL BE HELD IN JUDGMENT BY

THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY. DR. DEFEZ\' PRESENTATION

UNDERSCORED BOTH THE RELATIVE AND ABSOLUTE SAFETY OF

BIOTECH FOODS WHEN COMPARED TO CONVENTIONAL AND ORGANIC

FOODS. DEFEZ ALSO EXPLAINED THE ENVIRONMENTAL

INNOCUOUSNESS OF BIOTECH CROPS, WHICH HE DESCRIBED AS "WEAK

COMPETITORS IN A NATURAL ENVIRONMENT."

3. THE HOLY SEE AMBASSADORS\' COMMENTS WERE GENERALLY

POSITIVE, WITH THE GERMAN AMBASSADOR NOTING THAT THE

AFRICAN FAMINE HAS SEIZED THE ATTENTION OF THE EU, WHICH

WILL DEVOTE OVER 100 MILLION EURO TO IT IN COMING MONTHS.

THE GREEK AMBASSADOR QUESTIONED WHETHER AFRICAN OPPOSITION

TO US FOOD WAS "POLITICAL" (WE TOLD HIM IT WAS ROOTED IN

FEAR OF EU REACTIONS, NOT FEAR THAT THE FOOD WAS AMERICAN).

CANADIAN AMBASSADOR LICARI, AN OLD AFRICA HAND, EXPRESSED

HIS COUNTRY\'S FRUSTRATION WITH THE ZAMBIAN GOVERNMENT\'S

DECISION TO REFUSE US FOOD ASSISTANCE, BUT TOLD THE

ASSEMBLED GUESTS THAT MORE AFRICAN VOICES NEEDED TO BE

HEARD. HE SAID THE US NEEDED TO ENGAGE IN A MORE EFFECTIVE

"INFORMATION CAMPAIGN" TO EDUCATE AFRICAN LEADERS ABOUT US

FOOD. THE DUTCH AMBASSADOR\'S WIFE LATER REPORTED TO MRS.

NICHOLSON THAT HER HUSBAND THOUGHT THE MEETING WAS THE MOST

MEANINGFUL TWO HOURS HE HAD SPENT IN HIS ENTIRE DIPLOMATIC

CAREER.

4. COMMENT: IN BRINGING TOGETHER THE DIPLOMATIC CORPS, WE

HAVE SOUGHT TO STIMULATE OUR DIPLOMATIC COLLEAGUES TO

GREATER AWARENESS AND ACTIVITY VIS-A-VIS THE AFRICA FAMINE

AND STEP UP INTERNATIONAL PRESSURE ON THE HOLY SEE TO

ABANDON THE CAUTION THAT HAS CHARACTERIZED ITS POSITION ON

THE SAFETY OF US FOOD ASSISTANCE TO DATE. MOST OF THE

AMBASSADORS/CHARGES TOOK DETAILED NOTES, AND MANY INDICATED

THEY WOULD REPORT U.S. CONCERNS BACK TO CAPITALS. WE HAVE

PRESSED AND WILL CONTINUE TO PRESS THE DEAN OF THE VATICAN

DIPLOMATIC CORPS (FROM SAN MARINO) TO MAKE THIS ISSUE A

THEME OF HIS ANNUAL GREETING TO THE POPE ON JANUARY 13.
