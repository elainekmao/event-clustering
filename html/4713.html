SUBJECT: STEEL: ITALY ON UPCOMING OECD HIGH-LEVEL STEEL

MEETING

1. (U) ON DECEMBER 13, 2002 ECONOFF SPOKE WITH GIUSEPPE

LORENZINI, OFFICE III DIRECTOR, DIRECTORATE GENERAL FOR

COMMERCIAL AGREEMENTS, MINISTRY OF PRODUCTIVE ACTIVITIES

(WHICH INCLUDES THE FOREIGN TRADE SUB-MINISTRY). OFFICE III

HAS THE GOI LEAD ON THE ISSUE OF STEEL RESTRUCTURING.

2. (SBU) LORENZINI TOLD US THAT ITALY, LIKE THE EU, WAS

GENERALLY IN AGREEMENT WITH THE U.S. ON THE NEED TO ADDRESS

SUBSIDIES (LORENZINI WAS IN BRUSSELS EARLIER THIS WEEK TO

COORDINATE THE EU POSITION IN ADVANCE OF NEXT WEEK,S OECD

MEETINGS). HOWEVER, FOLLOWING THE EU LINE, HE TOLD US HE DID

NOT BELIEVE PROGRESS IN THE TALKS ON REDUCING OR ELIMINATING

SUBSIDIES COULD BE ACHIEVED WITHOUT ADDRESSING OTHER ISSUES,

PARTICULARLY TRADE REMEDIES. LORENZINI NOTED ITALY\'S CONCERN

THAT MORE ATTENTION SHOULD BE PAID TO REGIONAL AND LOCAL

SUBSIDIES, IN ADDITION TO THOSE AT THE NATIONAL LEVEL. IN

TERMS OF REDUCING CAPACITY, ITALY AGREES WITH THE GOAL, BUT

POLITICAL REALITIES MAKE THE GOI PARTICULARLY EAGER FOR THE

DEVELOPMENT OF A MECHANISM TO PROVIDE FINANCIAL INCENTIVES

FOR CLOSING UNECONOMICAL FACILITIES (THOUGH LIKE MANY OF ITS

FELLOW EU MEMBERS, GOVERNMENT BUDGET CONSTRAINTS WOULD

INHIBIT ANY POTENTIAL GOI-FUNDED INITIATIVES).

3. (SBU) THE GOI FORESEES THE OECD REMAINING THE "HOME" OF

STEEL TALKS INTO 2003, DESPITE THE EU\'S HOPE TO HAVE THE

DECEMBER HLG MEETING THE LAST OF ITS KIND WITHIN THE OECD.

LORENZINI WAS CAUTIOUSLY OPTIMISTIC THAT AGREEMENT (PERHAPS

INFORMALLY) TO SOME SORT OF PROPOSAL ON STEEL SUBSIDIES COULD
