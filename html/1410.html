SUBJECT: SENIOR FOREIGN MINISTRY OFFICIAL COMMENTS ON

SIGNING OF DISENGAGEMENT AND REDEPLOYMENT OF FORCES

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 06907 270831Z

AGREEMENT

REF: LUSAKA 5410

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD FOR REASONS 1.5

B/D.

1. (C) ON DECEMBER 7 DCM HAD BREAKFAST WITH A VERY

TIRED DAVID HAMADZIRIPI, A SENIOR OFFICIAL OF THE

MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS CURRENTLY POSTED IN

KINSHASA. THE ZIMBABWEAN DIPLOMAT, WHO ATTENDED THE DRC

DISENGAGEMENT MEETING IN HARARE, REPORTED THAT HIS

GOVERNMENT EXPECTED THE SIGNING CEREMONY TO BE NON-

CONTROVERSIAL, BUT NEGOTIATIONS THAT BEGAN ON DECEMBER 5

STRETCHED INTO THE EARLY HOURS OF THE NEXT MORNING. HE

CONTINUED THAT SOME MEMBERS OF THE CONGOLESE REBEL

MOVEMENTS, PARTICULARLY THE MOVEMENT FOR THE LIBERATION

OF THE CONGO (MLC), OBJECTED TO THE JOINT POLITICAL

COMMITTEE\'S ADOPTION OF THE SUB-PLANS FOR THE

DISENGAGEMENT AND REDEPLOYMENT OF FORCES IN THE FOUR

MILITARY SECTORS OF THE DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC OF THE CONGO

(DRC)(REFTEL). THE MLC, WHICH WAS REPRESENTED AT THE

MINISTERIAL-LEVEL MEETING BY ITS RELATIVELY JUNIOR

MEMBER ON THE JOINT MILITARY COMMISSION, REFUSED TO SIGN

CLAIMING HIS MOVEMENT WOULD ONLY DO SO WHEN THE INTERNAL

DIALOGUE IN THE DRC HAD BEGUN. HAMADZIRIPI THOUGHT THAT

IT WAS A PLAY FOR TIME, AS JEAN-PIERRE BEMBA PROBABLY

HAD NOT GIVEN HIS DELEGATE A MANDATE TO JOIN THE

CONSENSUS.

2. (C) HAMADZIRIPI MADE A STRONG PLEA FOR A SIX-MONTH

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 06907 270831Z

EXTENSION OF THE MONUC MANDATE AND AN EARLY DEPLOYMENT

OF THE UNITED NATIONS\' PEACEKEEPING BATTALION. "WITHOUT

IT THESE AGREEMENTS ARE A DEAD LETTER," HE SUMMED UP.

HE HOPED THE USG WOULD SUPPORT THE HARARE EFFORT AT THE

WORLD BODY. THE SIGNING OF THE DISENGAGEMENT PLAN

SHOULD BE SEEN AS A SIGN THAT THE DRC PLAYERS ARE

SERIOUS ABOUT A PEACEFUL RESOLUTION, HE DECLARED. HE

ADDED THAT ZIMBABWE HAD BROUGHT HOME TWO BATTALIONS FROM

THE CONGO, WHICH IT DID NOT INTEND TO REPLACE. THE

FIGHTING NEAR PWETO SHOULD NOT DETER MONUC DEPLOYMENT

BECAUSE MONUC COULD EASILY DEPLOY TO ANY OTHER AREA OF

THE FRONT, WHICH IS MOSTLY QUIET. WHEN ASKED WHETHER

THE REPORTED TAKING OF PWETO BY REBEL FORCES POSED A

THREAT TO THE AGREEMENT, HAMADZIRIPI STATED

CATEGORICALLY, "NO."

3. (C) COMMENT: HAMADZIRIPI, A RELIABLE CONTACT ON DRC

MATTERS, WAS UNUSUALLY KEEN ON RECEIVING US SUPPORT FOR

THIS LATEST DIPLOMATIC EFFORT TO DISENTANGLE THE WARRING

PARTIES IN THE DRC. PRESIDENT MUGABE, WHO ADDRESSED

LOCAL DEFENSE ATTACHES ATTENDING THE SIGNING CEREMONY,

URGED THEIR GOVERNMENTS TO GET BEHIND THE DISENGAGEMENT

PLAN. THIS EXTRAORDINARY DIPLOMATIC PLEADING COULD BE

AN INDICATION THAT THE DRC ALLIES ARE INCREASINGLY

DESPERATE TO STOP THE FIGHTING BEFORE REBELS MAKE ANY

MORE ADVANCES. ALTHOUGH ZIMBABWE MAY HAVE INTENDED TO

CONTINUE A GRADUAL WITHDRAWAL, RECENT ADVANCES BY THE

REBELS MAY FORCE THE ZIMBABWE DEFENSE FORCES TO REDEPLOY

FORCES TO THE CONGO. THE ALLIES COULD FEAR THAT THEY

ARE LOSING MOMENTUM TO THE REBELS, PARTICULARLY IN LIGHT

OF REPORTS OF A HASTY RETREAT BY DRC ALLIES FROM PWETO.
