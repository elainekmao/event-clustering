SUBJECT: LIFE IN TEHRAN

1. CONFIDENTIAL - ENTIRE TEXT.

2. TEHRAN REMAINS ECONOMICALLY DEPRESSED AND

DEPRESSING ACCORDING TO UAE MINISTER OF HEALTH

HAMAD AL-MIDFA. DURING AMBASSADOR\'S FAREWELL CALL

OCTOBER 3 (DCM ACCOMPANYING), MIDFA COMMENTED ON

HIS RECENT VISIT TO TEHRAN AS PART OF A REGIONAL

CONFERENCE ON PUBLIC HEALTH. HE SAID HE FOUND THE

CITY IN A POOR STATE OF MAINTENANCE. HE STAYED AT THE

FORMER HILTON; THE SHEETS WERE YEARS OLD, NO HOT

WATER WAS AVAILABLE FOR TEA AND THE PLUMBING WORKED

BADLY. HE SPENT MUCH OF HIS TIME DRIVING AROUND THE

CITY. HE FOUND PEOPLE PERSONALLY FRIENDLY BUT

COMPLAINING ABOUT THE LACK OF ECONOMIC PROGRESS. A

FEW INDICATED REGRET FOR PASSING OF THE SHAH\'S REGIME.

LINES OF WOMEN IN BLACK CHADORS WERE WAITING OUTSIDE

VARIOUS SHOPS. THERE WAS NO EVIDENCE OF NEW

CONSTRUCTION OR REPAIR.

3. THE MEETING ITSELF, SAID AL-MIDFA, WAS TOTALLY

DISORGANIZED AND CHAOTIC. IN WHAT MAY BE A TEA-LEAVES

GAUGE OF POLITICAL RELATIONS, THE UAE WAS

ASSIGNED FORTH PLACE IN PROTOCOL ORDER AFTER KUWAIT,

OMAN AND QATAR. NO OTHER GCC STATES ATTENDED THE

MEETING. AL-MIDFA NOTED THAT BAHRAIN HAD PLANNED TO

ATTEND BUT HAD BEEN DISSUADED BY SAUDI ARABIA.
