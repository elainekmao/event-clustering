SUBJECT: IRAQ/UNSC SANCTIONS: DEMARCHE ON ROYAL JORDANIAN

FLIGHTS DELIVERED, MESSAGE HEARD

REF: STATE 112154

Classified By: A/DCM Doug Silliman for reasons 1.5 (B) and (D)

1. (C) A/DCM covered ref points on Royal Jordanian airlines

(RJ) flights to Baghdad June 11 with Ali al-Ayed, Personal

Assistant to Foreign Minister Muasher. A/DCM stressed that

RJ has conducted 4-times-weekly flights to Baghdad since

January, and 5-times-weekly flights since April, and that the

frequency and regularity of the flights makes them appear to

be regular commercial flights, not/not humanitarian flights.

In addition, the recent circular diplomatic note from the

Jordanian Embassy in Baghdad advertising the flights and

offering diplomatic discounts raised particular concern in

Washington. A/DCM urged the GOJ restrict the frequency and

regularity of RJ operations to and from Baghdad, end

advertising and discounts, and ensure that all RJ flights are

truly humanitarian and in keeping with UN sanctions.

2. (C) Ayed took careful notes, and -- in the absence of

FonMin Muasher -- promised to convey the demarche to the

Acting FonMin. He expected that the issue would go to the

Prime Minister for a decision, and that GOJ officials would

speak to RJ about its traffic to and from Baghdad.

3. (C) COMMENT: Ayed clearly understood the importance of

the demarche, and was visibly disturbed when he heard that

the Jordanian Embassy in Baghdad had sent a circular note

advertising the flights.
