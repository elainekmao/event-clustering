SUBJECT: JORDAN: AIR MARSHALS PROGRAM, ACTION REQUEST

REF: STATE 78755

SENSITIVE BUT UNCLASSIFIED.

1. Action Request: Post received via a diplomatic note from

the Jordanian Ministry of Foreign Affairs the following

letter regarding new U.S. procedures pertaining to foreign

air marshals programs. The note and letter formally request

U.S. government permission to allow Royal Jordanian Airlines

(RJ) to operate flights to the United States with armed law

enforcement officers on board. We understand from the

Jordanian Civil Aviation Authority(CAA) that RJ and CAA have

separately discussed such procedures with Civil Aviation

Security Liaison Officer (CASLO) in Rome. Please advise on

next steps.

2. Begin text of letter.

The Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan has used air marshals to

provide extra security aboard all Royal Jordanian (RJ)

flights for a number of years (since 1971). Therefore, Air

Marshal policies and procedures pertaining to unlawful

interference and security of passengers, crew and the

aircraft on international routes are well established by the

Jordan Civil Aviation Authority who had delegated this role

to the Jordan Armed forces/Special Aviation Security and

protection unit.

We strongly believe and based on our experience for over 30

years that our Air Marshal program is well organized and no

violations were reported by any officials at International

Airports on this program including USG agencies and TSA.

Due to the new regulatory requirements for an air marshal

program set by the USG after September 11, we would like to

inform you that it is the intention of the Government of

Jordan to use armed air marshals and we certify that our

armed air marshals are duly trained and authorized law

enforcement officers.

The Government of Jordan kindly request the USG permission

for our National Carrier (RJ) to operate direct flights into

the United States with armed law Enforcement Officers (LEO)

onboard.

Thank you in advance for your cooperation.

End text.
