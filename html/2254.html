SUBJECT: UNDP TEAM FINDS LITTLE TO APPLAUD IN ABUJA

COMPLIANCE REVIEW

REF: HARARE 3518

CLASSIFIED BY JOSEPH G. SULLIVAN FOR REASONS 1.5 B/D

1. (C) SUMMARY: THE UNDP-LED MISSION TO EVALUATE

COMPLIANCE WITH, AND SUGGEST WAYS FORWARD ON THE ABUJA

ACCORD ON LAND REFORM HAS FOUND MOSTLY FRUSTRATIONS AND

ONLY OCCASIONAL BRIGHT SPOTS DURING THEIR EXTENDED

MISSION IN ZIMBABWE. PATTERNS VARY WIDELY ACROSS THE

COUNTRY, BUT THE DISRUPTION PROVOKED BY LACK OF CLEAR

DIRECTION IS CAUSING WIDESPREAD DIFFICULTIES IN

PLANTINGS FOR BOTH ORIGINAL FARMERS AND FOR NEW

SETTLERS. SOME GOZ MINISTERS HAVE SOUGHT TO BULLY THE

UNDP-LED MISSION INTO ACCEPTING THE CHAOTIC PROCESS, BUT

THE TEAM SEEMS FIXED ON PRESENTING AN HONEST, IF NON-

CONFRONTATIONAL REPORT. THE GOZ IS SUPPOSED TO SEND A

DELEGATION TO NY IN ANOTHER WEEK TO MEET WITH MARK

MALLOCH BROWN AND SEE WHAT FOLLOW-UP CAN BE GIVEN TO

TEAM RECOMMENDATIONS. MOST UNDP-LED TEAM MEMBERS SEE

LITTLE PROSPECT FOR PROGRESS UNTIL AFTER ZIMBABWE\'S

ELECTIONS, BUT THEY WISH TO KEEP THE DOOR OPEN FOR

FUTURE ENGAGEMENT BY PRESENTING A SERIES OF

RECOMMENDATIONS FOR FUTURE STEPS. END SUMMARY

2. (C) DURING A NOVEMBER 29 LUNCHEON WITH THE UNDP-LED

TEAM, KEY GOZ MINISTERS AND SOME POTENTIAL DONORS,

EXCLUDING THE UK, THE AMBASSADOR HAD THE OPPORTUNITY TO

SOUND OUT PROGRESS OF THE PROLONGED MISSION. TEAM

LEADER ABDULLAH JANNE TOLD US THAT THE TEAM HAD FOUND

MANY PROBLEMS AND ONLY A FEW BRIGHT SPOTS. HE SAID THE

TEAM HAD TRAVELLED THROUGHOUT THE COUNTRY AND HAD WIDE-

RANGING CONVERSATIONS TO EVALUATE THE STATE OF THE ABUJA

AGREEMENT ON LAND AND PROSPECTS FOR INTERNATIONAL

ENGAGEMENT ON LAND REFORM. JANNE SAID THE TEAM WOULD

DEPART THE MIDDLE OF DEC 2 WEEK AND ISSUE A REPORT OF

OBSERVATIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS WHICH WOULD IDENTIFY

POSITIVE AREAS TO FOLLOW THROUGH ON AND PROBLEMS WHICH

NEEDED TO BE ADDRESSED. A GOZ TEAM LED BY AGRICULTURE

MINISTER MADE WOULD THEN TRAVEL TO NY FOR ENGAGMENT WITH

THE UNDP.

3. (C) OTHER TEAM MEMBERS, INCLUDING WORLD BANK, IMF AND

EU REPRESENTATIVES, TOLD US THAT THE PROGRESS OF

GOVERNMENT LAND REFORM EFFORTS VARIED WIDELY WITH THE

ABSENCE OF CLEAR RULES AND DISRUPTION OF PRODUCTION MOST

PREVALENT. A POSITIVE EXCEPTION WAS MIDLANDS PROVINCE

WHERE THE PROVINCIAL GOVERNOR, SENIOR GOVERNMENT

MINISTERS COMMERCIAL FARMERS AND SETTLERS ALL WORKING

COLLABORATIVELY TO OVERCOME MOST PROBLEMS THAT HAVE

EMERGED IN THE IMPLEMENTATION OF LAND REDISTRIBUTION.

AS A RESULT, IN MIDLANDS TILLING IS PROCEEDING ON BOTH

COMMERCIAL AND SETTLED FARMS. TEAM MEMBERS GAINED THE

IMPRESSION OF CONSIDERABLE DIFFERENCES WITHIN THE

GOVERNMENT ITSELF. FOR EXAMPLE, THEY WERE TOLD THAT THE

AGRICULTURE MINISTER\'S RECENT DECLARATION OF LOW LIMITS

ON MAXIMUM FARM SIZE TO BE ENFORCED BY GOVERNMENT LAND

ACQUISITION WOULD NEVER BE APPROVED BY THE PARLIAMENT.

4. (C) AT THE NOV 29 LUNCHEON, AGRICULTURE MINISTER MADE

WAS HIS USUALLY AGGRESSIVE SELF TOWARD TEAM LEADER JANNE

IN FRONT OF A TABLE OF ABOUT EIGHT OTHER TEAM MEMBERS

AND DONORS. MADE ACCUSED JANNE\'S TEAM OF ASKING

INTRUSIVE QUESTIONS IN THEIR FARM VISITS WHICH SHOWED

LACK OF RESPECT FOR THE GOVERNMENT\'S REPORT OF THE

STATUS OF THE RESETTLEMENT PROCESS. JANNE, A NATIVE OF

SIERRA LEONE, KEPT HIS COOL, BUT WE UNDERSTAND THAT

UNSYG KOFI ANNAN WAS VERY UPSET WITH GOZ TREATMENT OF

HIS MISSION.

VICE PRESIDENT MSIKA. MUCHENA, WHO HAS WORKED CLOSELY
