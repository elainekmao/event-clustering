SUBJECT: (U) CRIMES IN KRAJINA -- REPORT ON ARRESTS,

CHARGES

1. (U) CLASSIFIED BY ROBERT P. FINN, DCM, PER 1(5)(B).

2. (C) SUMMARY: AFTER CONSIDERABLE PRODDING, THE GOC

HAS PROVIDED US A REPORT INDICATING THAT AS OF OCTOBER

31, A TOTAL OF 19 PERSONS ARE UNDER CRIMINAL

INVESTIGATION FOR MURDERS COMMITTED IN THE "KRAJINA."

1260 PERSONS ARE BEING PROCESSED IN 779 CASES OF

LOOTING, AND ELEVEN PERSONS ARE UNDER SUSPECION OF

ARSON (THOUGH THERE MAY BE MANY MORE). END SUMMARY.

3. (C) ON NOVEMBER 7, A CONTACT AT THE GOC MINISTRY OF

INTERIOR PROVIDED US (AFTER TWO WEEKS\' DELAY) A REPORT

OFFERING DETAILS BEHIND SOME OF THE NUMBERS ANNOUNCED

EARLIER FOR JUDICIAL PROCEEDINGS INVOLVING CRIMES IN

FORMER SECTORS NORTH AND SOUTH. AS THERE WAS

APPARENTLY SOME HESITANCY ON THE PART OF OUR CONTACT IN

DIVULGING THIS MATERIAL, AND IT IS NOT CLEAR WHETHER IT

HAS BEEN MADE PUBLIC, IT SHOULD BE CONSIDERED FOR USG

BACKGROUND USE ONLY.

4. (C) ACCORDING TO THE REPORT, 19 PERSONS HAVE SO FAR

BEEN TURNED OVER TO INVESTIGATIVE COURT FOR POSSIBLE

CHARGES OF MURDER. (NOTE: THE ACTUAL TOTAL APPEARS TO

BE 26 BECAUSE SOME INDIVIDUALS WERE INVOLVED IN MORE

THAN ONE OF THE CRIMES.) OTHER CASES ARE STILL UNDER

"INTENSIVE INVESTIGATION." SPECIFICALLY, THE CASES

INVOLVE MURDERS IN:

5. (C) THE MOI FURTHER STATES THAT AT LEAST 2787 CASES

OF "FIRES, MAINLY IN RESIDENCES AND COMMERCIAL

BUILDINGS," HAVE BEEN REPORTED, BUT THAT BECAUSE OF THE

TIME REQUIRED TO DETERMINE WHETHER THE FIRES WERE

CAUSED BY ARSON OR WAR DAMAGE, ONLY 11 PERSONS HAVE SO

FAR BEEN CHARGED. THE RECORD IS BETTER ON FELONY THEFT

(LOOTING), WHERE 1054 CASES ARE UNDER REVIEW, OF WHICH

779, INVOLVING 1260 SUSPECTED INDIVIDUALS, HAVE BEEN

TURNED OVER FOR CRIMINAL PROCESSING (I.E.,

INVESTIGATION).
