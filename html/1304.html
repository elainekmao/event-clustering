SUBJECT: MBEKI TO CONVENE MEETING OF EXTERNAL

SIGNATORIES ON DROC PEACE PROCESS

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 05602 031242Z

REFS: A) STATE 186856, B) HARARE 5584

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD. REASONS: 1.5 (B)

AND (D).

1. (C) AMBASSADOR REACHED FOREIGN AFFAIRS MINISTER

STANISLAUS MUDENGE BY PHONE ON OCTOBER 2 TO FOLLOW UP ON

REF A DEMARCHE. MUDENGE HAD BEEN UNAVAILABLE WHEN

DEMARCHE WAS DELIVERED TO MFA PERMANENT SECRETARY

WILLARD CHIWEWE SEPTEMBER 29 (REF B). AMBASSADOR URGED

MUDENGE TO USE ZIMBABWE\'S INFLUENCE WITH KABILA TO

PERMIT THE FULL MOVEMENT OF MONUC FORCES IN THE DROC AND

TO ABIDE BY THE LUSAKA ACCORDS. MUDENGE REPORTED THAT

SOUTH AFRICAN PRESIDENT MBEKI WILL CONVENE SHORTLY A

MEETING OF THE HEADS OF STATE OF THE EXTERNAL SIGNATORY

COUNTRIES TO ASSESS THE CURRENT STATUS OF IMPLEMENTATION

OF THE LUSAKA ACCORDS AND TO URGE KABILA TO ABIDE BY

LUSAKA AND IMPLEMENT THE PEACE PROCESS. THE MEETING WAS

ORIGINALLY TO HAVE TAKEN PLACE OVER THE SEPTEMBER 30-

OCTOBER 1 WEEKEND, BUT MBEKI POSTPONED IT. MUDENGE

REPORTED THAT PRESIDENT MUGABE SUPPORTS THE IDEA OF SUCH

A MEETING. AMBASSADOR CONVEYED U.S. SUPPORT FOR

DISCUSSIONS THAT WOULD FURTHER THE PEACE PROCESS WITHOUT

COMPROMISING THE LUSAKA AGREEMENTS.

2. (C) COMMENT: MUDENGE HAS BEEN LARGELY INACCESSIBLE

SINCE RETURNING FROM WASHINGTON LAST MONTH AS HIS WIFE

HAS BEEN QUITE ILL. MUDENGE DID NOT STATE THE NATURE OF

HER ILLNESS, BUT SAID THAT SHE WOULD REMAIN IN HARARE

FOR TREATMENT. ALTHOUGH THE MFA HAS FOR SOME TIME BEEN
