SUBJECT: AGRICULTURE MINISTER ORDERS SEED

CERTIFICATION AGENCY TO QUANTIFY GMO-FREE

SOURCES FOR SOY AND CORN SEED FOR PLANTING.

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 ROME 00042 031430Z

1. SUMMARY. THE ITALIAN MINISTER OF

AGRICULTURE ORDERED THE NATIONAL SEED

CERTIFICATION ENTITY (ENSE) TO PROVIDE BY

JANUARY 12 ASSESSMENT OF THE AVAILABILITY OF

GMO-FREE SOY AND CORN SEEDS FOR PLANTING. TO

CONSIDER AN EXPANSION OF THE POTENTIAL

SOURCES OF NON-GM SEEDS FOR THE 2002 PLANTING

SEASON, THE MINISTER CONTEMPLATES RESCINDING

EXISTING MINIMUM STANDARDS ON SEED

GERMINATION FOR THESE TWO SEED TYPES.

MINISTER ALEMANNO WILL DECLARE A TOLERANCE

FOR ADVENTITIOUS PRESENCE OF GM MATERIAL IN

SEEDS FOR PLANTING BASED ON THE FINDINGS OF

THE ENSE STUDY. END SUMMARY.

2. ITALIAN MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE GIOVANNI

ALEMANNO ISSUED A DECREE DECEMBER 28, 2001

REQUIRING A SURVEY ON AVAILABILITY OF SOY AND

CORN SEED WHICH ARE FREE FROM ADVENTITIOUS

PRESENCE OF GENETICALLY MODIFIED MATERIAL.

THE SEED CERTIFICATION INSTITUTE WHICH WILL

CARRY OUT THE SURVEY IS ENTE NAZIONALE

SEMENTI ELETTE, ENSE, A TECHNICAL OFFICE

FUNDED BY THE MINISTRY OF AGRICULTURE AND

DIRECTED BY A BOARD APPOINTED BY THE

MINISTER. IN THE DEC 28 DECREE ALEMANNO

PRESUMED A CONSENSUS AMONG ITALIAN PRODUCERS

TO KEEP THE ITALIAN FOOD CHAIN FREE OF GMOS.

3. THE DECREE STATES THAT IN ORDER TO

CONSIDER SOURCES OF CORN AND SOYBEAN SEEDS

FOR PLANTING WHICH ARE ABLE TO COMPLY WITH A

ZERO TOLERANCE FOR GM MATERIAL, CURRENT

MINIMUM STANDARDS FOR SEED GERMINATION RATES

MAY BE SUSPENDED. IN ORDER TO BE MARKETED IN

ITALY SEED CORN IS CURRENTLY REQUIRED TO HAVE

A GERMINATION RATE OF AT LEAST 90 PERCENT AND

SOYBEAN SEED MUST GERMINATE AT 80 PERCENT.

4. DATA ON SEED SALES FOR 2000 SHOW THAT THE

UNITED STATES SUPPLIED HALF, BY VALUE, OF

ITALY\'S CORN SEED IMPORTS. THE UNITED STATES

SUPPLIED ABOUT EIGHTY FOUR PERCENT OF ITALY\'S

IMPORTS OF SOYBEAN SEED FOR PLANTING IN THE

SAME YEAR. THE VALUE OF UNITED STATES SALES

OF THE TWO TYPES OF SEED WAS APPROXIMATELY

DOLS 32 MILLION.

5. COMMENT: THIS DECREE IS NOT ONLY

UNEXPECTED BUT IT ALSO APPEARS TO CONTRADICT

WHAT ALEMANNO TOLD SECRETARY VENEMAN IN

NOVEMBER 2001. DURING SECRETARY VENEMAN\'S

MEETING WITH ALEMANNO HE VOLUNTEERED THAT

ZERO TOLERANCE FOR GMOS WAS UNATTAINABLE. WE

HAVE SEEN SIGNALS FROM ALEMANNO THAT HE IS

BECOMING MORE ANTI-GMO SINCE THAT TIME. THIS

POST\'S SOUNDING OF THE SKELETAL EPIPHANY WEEK

GOI REVEALS NO INDICATION THAT THE DECREE IS

ANYTHING BUT AN ALEMANNO MINISTRY OF

AGRICULTURE INITIATIVE. WE ARE DEVELOPING

INTERVENTION RECOMMENDATIONS. END COMMENT.

6. SELECTED TEXT OF ALEMANNO DECREE FOLLOWS:

- A. BY JANUARY 12,2002, ENSE WILL CARRY OUT

A MARKET SURVEY IN ORDER TO DETERMINE THE

QUANTITIES OF CONVENTIONAL CORN AND SOY SEED

WITH ZERO TOLERANCE THRESHOLD FOR GMO

PRESENCE AVAILABLE OR ACQUIRABLE FOR THE

ITALIAN MARKET FOR THE 2002 PLANTING SEASON,

AND ALSO WITH REFERENCE TO THE QUANTITIES OF

SUCH SEEDS MEETING THE PURITY THRESHOLD BUT

WITH GERMINATION RATE BELOW THE THRESHOLD

DETERMINED BY NATIONAL NORMS IN FORCE.

- B. THE MINISTRY OF AGRICULTURAL POLICY

WILL OBTAIN, BY THE SAME DATE, THE

EVALUATIONS OF THE COMPETENT REGIONAL

COMMISSION FOR AGRICULTURE.

- C. A FUTURE DECREE, TO BE ISSUED BY

JANUARY 16, 2002, WILL DETERMINE THE

TOLERANCE THRESHOLDS FOR THE PRESENCE OF GMOS

FOR THE 2002 PLANTING SEASON. SEMBLER
