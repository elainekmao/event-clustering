SUBJ: SADC\'S FUTURE

REF: A. WINDHOEK 2413, B. STATE 261031 (NOTAL)

1. (C) ZIMBABWE SEES THE CREATION OF THE NEW SOUTHERN

AFRICAN DEVELOPMENT COMMUNITY (SADC) AS A FIRST STEP

TOWARD AN EFFECTIVE REGIONAL TRADING BLOC. MFA

ASSISTANT SECRETARY FOR INTERNATIONAL ORGANIZATIONS

NELSON CHICHAYA TOLD POLOFF THAT ZIMBABWE RECOGNIZES

THAT SOUTH AFRICA MUST BE INCLUDED IN THE SADC FOR THE

REGIONAL GROUP TO FLOURISH.

2. (C) CHICHAYA SAID THAT PRESIDENTS MASIRE AND MUGABE

DID NOT AND WOULD NOT SUPPORT ANY MERGER WITH THE

PREFERENTIAL TRADE AREA FOR EASTERN AND SOUTHERN AFRICA

(PTA). THEY BELIEVE THE PTA IS TOO LARGE AND HAS TOO

MANY UNSTABLE MEMBERS TO BE AN EFFECTIVE ORGANIZATION.

MFA REGIONAL ORGANIZATIONS OFFICER MANGWANA TOLD THE AID

DIRECTOR THAT THE SADC SUMMIT COMMISSIONED A STUDY ON

"HARMONIZING" SADC-PTA RELATIONS, EXPLICITLY RECOGNIZING

THAT BOTH WILL CONTINUE TO EXIST SO THEY NEED TO STUDY

HOW TO AVOID DUPLICATION. THE GOZ ENVISIONS AN AFRICAN

ECONOMIC COMMUNITY WHICH WOULD BE COMPOSED OF FOUR OR

FIVE REGIONAL TRADING ORGANIZATIONS.

3. (LOU) MISSION OFFICERS HAVE REQUESTED APPOINTMENTS

WITH OTHER SENIOR GOZ OFFICIALS TO DISCUSS THE FUTURE OF

THE SADC. WE WILL NOT BE ABLE TO SEE THEM UNTIL THE END

OF AUGUST AFTER THE ZIMBABWEAN DELEGATION HAS PREPARED

ITS REPORT FOR THE CABINET.

4. (LOU) THE MISSION CONGRATULATES WINDHOEK ON ITS

REPORT ON THE SADC SUMMIT. WE LOOK FORWARD TO RECEIVING

COPIES OF THE CONFERENCE DOCUMENTS.
