SUMMARY

---------

1. USAID STAFF FROM THE U.S. MISSION IN ROME AND

FOOD FOR PEACE/WASHINGTON TRAVELED TO GUINEA,

LIBERIA, AND SENEGAL SEPTEMBER 29 THROUGH OCTOBER 9

TO REVIEW THE FOOD SECURITY SITUATION FOR REFUGEES,

INTERNALLY DISPLACED PERSONS (IDPS), AND RESIDENTS

WHOSE COMMUNITIES HAVE BEEN AFFECTED BY INFLUXES OF

EITHER GROUP. THIS REPORT FOCUSES ON GUINEA SOLELY.

LIBERIA WAS REPORTED VIA REF TEL.

2. KOUANKAN CAMP, LOCATED IN N\'ZEREKORE PREFECTURE

OF THE FOREST REGION OF GUINEA AND BUILT TO

ACCOMMODATE 20,000 PERSONS, HAS NOW SWELLED TO A

POPULATION OF 34,000. SERIOUS SECURITY CONCERNS

SURROUND THE SIZE OF THIS CAMP AND THE U.N. AGENCIES

HAVE AGREED THAT ITS INHABITANTS SHOULD BE

TRANSFERRED FURTHER NORTH TO THE ALBADARIAH REGION.

LACK OF GUINEAN GOVERNMENT WRITTEN AUTHORIZATION AND

THE U.N. HIGH COMMISSIONER FOR REFUGEES\'S (UNHCR)

EXTRAORDINARY PRICE TAG FOR THE TRANSFER HAVE SLOWED

THE PROCESS. WFP SAYS IT WILL STOP FOOD

DISTRIBUTIONS BY THE END OF THE YEAR, IF THE TRANSFER

IS NOT UNDERWAY.

3. THE NEW LAINE CAMP, BUILT TO ACCOMMODATE THE

GROWING NUMBER OF LIBERIAN REFUGEES, IS ALREADY FULL.

41,000 SIERRA LEONEAN REFUGEES REMAIN IN THE

ALBADARIAH CAMPS, BUT MORE THAN HALF PLAN ON

RETURNING AFTER THE HARVEST. UNTIL THE END OF THE

YEAR, UNHCR/GUINEA HAS HALTED THE ORGANIZED

REPATRIATION OF REFUGEES DUE TO THE UNHCR/SIERRA

LEONE FUNDING CRISIS. REFUGEES WERE GIVEN LAND,

SEEDS, AND TOOLS FOR PLANTING AND THE HARVEST LOOKS

TO BE QUITE PROMISING. THE EMERGENCY UNIT OF THE

THE EXPANDING KOUANKAN REFUGEE CAMP

--------------------------------------

6. THE KOUANKAN REFUGEE CAMP, LOCATED IN N\'ZEREKORE

PREFECTURE, WAS CREATED FOR 20,000 PERSONS AND IS

CURRENTLY EXCEEDING ITS CAPACITY BY 14,000. THE

POPULATION SIZE BEGAN EXPANDING FROM 13,500 LAST

SPRING WHEN LIBERIANS STARTED CROSSING THE GUINEA

BORDER IN LARGE NUMBERS. IN AUGUST, UNEASE MOUNTED

ABOUT THE COMPOSITION OF THE REFUGEES, WITH STRONG

SUSPICIONS THAT INCREASING NUMBERS OF THE REBEL

MOVEMENT LIBERIA UNITED FOR RECONCILIATION AND

DEMOCRACY (LURD) WERE PRESENT IN THE CAMP. WFP

RAISED CONCERNS ABOUT THIS, AS IT WAS FAIRLY CERTAIN

THAT LURD OR AT A MINIMUM, LURD FAMILIES, WERE

RECEIVING WFP FOOD. ON AUGUST 22, THE U.N.

HUMANITARIAN CONTACT GROUP MET IN CONAKRY TO DISCUSS

MANAGEMENT OF THE NEW INFLUX INTO KOUANKAN, AS WELL

AS THE REFUGEES\' SECURITY. THE GROUP DECIDED THE

TO THE ALBADARIAH AREA. SINCE MANY OF THE SIERRA

VERIFYING NUMBERS

12. UNHCR CONDUCTED A VERIFICATION EXERCISE IN

AUGUST TO DETERMINE THE NUMBER OF REFUGEES IN

KOUANKAN, AS THE FIGURE OF CAMP INHABITANTS HAD RISEN

UNCLASSIFIED

TO 33,532. WHEN UNHCR ANNOUNCED IT WAS GOING TO
