SUBJECT: WFP\'S WEST AFRICA REGIONAL AIRCRAFT EXPANDS

ITS ROUTING TO INCLUDE COTE D\'IVOIRE BUT SOON MAY

HAVE TO SHUT DOWN OPERATIONS DUE TO FUNDING

SHORTFALLS

REFS: A) CONAKRY 1042 B) ROME 5454

---------

SUMMARY

---------

1. THE U.N. WORLD FOOD PROGRAM (WFP) HAS ISSUED A NEW

SPECIAL OPERATION (SO) FOR ITS WEST AFRICA PASSENGER

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ROME 05788 01 OF 03 021338Z

AIRCRAFT SERVICE AND LAST WEEK ISSUED A DONOR\'S ALERT

FOR THE SO. THE NEW SO REQUESTS 1.2 MILLION USD FOR

TWELVE MONTHS OF SERVICE. SINCE EARLY NOVEMBER, WFP

HAS EXPANDED THE FLIGHT SCHEDULE TO INCLUDE COTE

D\'IVOIRE IN ADDITION TO GUINEA, SIERRA LEONE, AND

LIBERIA. IN THE LAST YEAR, A TOTAL OF 5,481

PASSENGERS FROM MORE THAN 152 ORGANIZATIONS USED THE

PLANE. IN ADDITION, 17 MEDICAL EVACUATIONS WERE

FLOWN. CURRENT FUNDING FOR THE SO WILL SUPPORT THE

AIR OPERATION ONLY THROUGH MID-DECEMBER. IF NO NEW

FUNDING IS PLEDGED WITHIN THE COMING DAYS, WFP WILL

BE FORCED TO SHUT DOWN THE OPERATION IN DECEMBER.

SEE FINAL PARA FOR RECOMMENDATION. END SUMMARY

I CANNOT GET RID OF THIS STUPID LINE. CAN YOU??

-------------

BACKGROUND

-------------

2. AS REPORTED IN REF TEL A, IN OCTOBER 2001 WFP

LAUNCHED A SPECIAL OPERATION (SO) FOR PASSENGER

AIRCRAFT SERVICE TO GUINEA, SIERRA LEONE, AND LIBERIA

TO SUPPORT HUMANITARIAN OPERATIONS. THE COST FOR THE

FIRST YEAR WAS BUDGETED AT 1.2 MILLION USD (10/07/01-

10/06/02). IN FISCAL YEAR 2002, STATE/PRM PROVIDED

400,000 USD AND USAID/DCHA/OFDA GAVE 225,000 USD TO

THE SO. THE IRISH AND SWISS ALSO CONTRIBUTED

$108,070 AND $134,230 RESPECTIVELY. THE FLIGHT

OPERATION IS MANAGED BY THE WFP/GUINEA COUNTRY OFFICE

AND IS BASED IN CONAKRY.

3. IN LATE SEPTEMBER, 2002, WFP ISSUED A FOLLOW-ON

SO TO BEGIN ON OCTOBER 7 FOR ANOTHER YEAR. THE

NEW ROUTING

---------------

FLYING INTERNALLY TO SITES IN GUINEA FOUR DAYS A WEEK

AND ONCE A WEEK MADE A REGIONAL ROTATION TO LIBERIA,

SIERRA LEONE, AND GUINEA. ON TUESDAY, NOVEMBER 5,

WFP ADDED FLIGHTS TO COTE D\'IVOIRE, SERVICING ABIDJAN

AND YAMOUSSOUKRO, AND MAY SOON ADD BOUAKE, IF NEEDED.

5. UNDER THE CURRENT SCHEDULE, THE PLANE FLIES ON

MONDAYS FROM CONAKRY TO KISSIDOUGOU AND N\'ZEREKORE IN

THE FOREST REGION OF GUINEA. FROM N\'ZEREKORE IT GOES

TO YAMOUSSOUKRO AND ABIDJAN, WHERE IT BEDS FOR THE

NIGHT. ON TUESDAYS IT FLIES THE SAME SCHEDULE IN

6. WFP/GUINEA DISTRIBUTES THE SCHEDULE WIDELY AND

PRIDES ITSELF IN MAINTAINING THE ANNOUNCED SCHEDULE.

BUT AWARE OF THE CHANGING NEEDS IN THE REGION, WFP IS

RESPONSIVE TO EMERGING NEEDS.

------------------------------

PASSENGER INFORMATION

------------------------------

7. IN THE LAST YEAR (OCTOBER 2001 TO OCTOBER 2002),

A TOTAL OF 5,481 PASSENGERS FROM MORE THAN 152

ORGANIZATIONS USED THE PLANE FOR A TOTAL OF 981

FLIGHT HOURS. 2,520 PASSENGERS WERE FROM

INTERNATIONAL NGOS AND 2,223 FROM U.N. AGENCIES,

INCLUDING 882 FROM UNHCR, 849 FROM WFP AND 195 FROM

UNICEF. IN JULY, THE PLANE TRANSPORTED 648

PASSENGERS: ITS HIGHEST NUMBER OF PASSENGERS IN ONE

MONTH. IN THE LAST SIX MONTHS, THE MONTHLY PASSENGER

AVERAGE WAS 572.

8. WFP OPERATES A LET-410 THAT IS REGISTERED IN

BULGARIA AND IS FLOWN BY BULGARIAN PILOTS. THE

AIRCRAFT HAS A 17-SEAT CAPACITY, HOWEVER FOR SAFETY

REASONS RELATED TO THE TEMPERATURE AND ATMOSPHERIC

CONDITIONS IN WEST AFRICA, WFP ALLOWS ONLY 12

PASSENGERS PER FLIGHT.

9. WFP\'S SPECIAL OPERATION COVERS PASSENGER COSTS SO

THERE IS NO CHARGE TO PASSENGERS. WFP MAINTAINS

LEGITIMATE HUMANITARIAN-RELATED PERSONNEL AND

12. THE INTERNATIONAL COMMITTEE OF THE RED CROSS

(ICRC) ALSO FLIES A REGIONAL AIRCRAFT. ICRC HAS TOLD

WFP STAFF THAT THEY MAY USE IT IN EXTRAORDINARY

CIRCUMSTANCES SUCH AS MEDICAL EVACUATION, BUT IT IS

NOT AVAILABLE TO NGOS AND OTHER U.N. AGENCIES. THE

ICRC PLANE (10-PASSENGER CAPACITY) IS BASED IN

FREETOWN, AND FLIES TO THE FOREST REGION OF GUINEA

TWICE A WEEK.

------------------------------------------

COSTS AND POSSIBLE SHUT-DOWN OF SERVICE

------------------------------------------

13. THE COSTS OF MAINTAINING AND FLYING THE WFP

AIRCRAFT ARE 100,000 USD PER MONTH OR 1.2 MILLION USD

FOR 12 MONTHS, AS WFP\'S NEW FLIGHT SCHEDULE INCREASES

THE FLIGHT HOURS TO 100 HOURS PER MONTH. IN THE LAST

YEAR, WFP AVERAGED 80 FLIGHT HOURS PER MONTH OR

APPROXIMATELY 75,000-80,000 USD PER MONTH, WHICH

ALLOWED WFP TO STRETCH ITS FUNDING (OF APPROXIMATELY

867,300 USD) TO COVER THE 12 MONTHS.

14. BESIDES THE DONATIONS IT RECEIVED, WFP BORROWED

DURING THE SUMMER SOME FUNDS FROM OTHER OPERATIONS IN

ORDER TO COVER GAPS IN FUNDING AND CONTINUE THE

SERVICE FOR A FEW MONTHS. WFP HAS NOW REACHED A

POINT WHERE IT EITHER MUST RECEIVE ADDITIONAL

DONATIONS OR PLEDGES BY DECEMBER 7 OR SHUT DOWN THE

OPERATION.

COMMENT

----------

15. THE WFP REGIONAL AIRCRAFT SUPPLIES A VALUED

SERVICE TO THE REGION\'S HUMANITARIAN COMMUNITY. ITS

DISCONTINUANCE WOULD SEVERELY HAMPER HUMANITARIAN

OPERATIONS, AS HUMANITARIAN WORKERS AND DONORS WOULD

BE FORCED TO DRIVE LONG DISTANCES. (THE DISTANCE

FROM CONAKRY TO N\'ZEREKORE IS 950 KILOMETERS AND

TAKES 15 HOURS TO TRAVEL, ONE WAY, BY VEHICLE.)

16. AS PROBLEMS IN THE WEST AFRICA REGION CONTINUE

TO GENERATE REFUGEES AND INTERNALLY DISPLACED

PERSONS, THE USEFULNESS OF THE PLANE IN REACHING AND

SERVICING THESE POPULATIONS IS CLEAR. U.S.

MISSION/ROME SUGGESTS THAT STATE/PRM AND USAID/DCHA

STRONGLY CONSIDER ADDITIONAL FUNDING FOR THIS SO IN

FY03.

17. MINIMIZE CONSIDERED. HALL
