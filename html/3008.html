SUBJECT: JORDAN: IMF MISSION UPDATE

Senstive but Unclassified.

1. (sbu) The IMF mission is leaving Amman for Washington

Thursday (May 23) evening. According to a mission member,

they are taking with them a draft "letter of intent" for a

new 2-year stand-by program. The letter, however, contains

some "empty paragraphs" that need to be filled in by the

Jordanians before a draft program could obtain the full

clearance of IMF staff and management in Washington for

submission to the Executive Board for approval.

2. (sbu) The empty paragraphs relate to structural reforms

in the areas of tax and pension policy. The Jordanians were

simply unprepared this week to give the detail the IMF would

need for its internal reviews. The Jordanians are working on

the paragraphs and hope to be able to send them to Washington

very soon. The macro/financial framework of the proposed

program remains as it has been for some time. There is a

financing gap that remains to be filled, the mission member

said.

3. (sbu) On timing, the mission member said the Jordanians

would have to provide the missing info very soon in order for

a program to be approved by the executive board in time for a

Paris Club negotiation in July, especially since the July

Paris Club meetings begin July 8. Failing that, he thought

Jordan should be ready for the Paris Club meetings that begin

September 9.
