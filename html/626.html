SUMMARY

-------

1. IN A JAN 5 ZAGREB BRIEFING CHAIRED BY UNSYG SPECIAL

REPRESENTATIVE AKASHI, UNPROFOR FORCE COMMANDER GENERAL

COT BRIEFED AMBASSADORS ALBRIGHT AND GALBRAITH, ASSTSEC

SHATTUCK, AND OTHER MEMBERS OF THE ALBRIGHT DELEGATION ON

LIMITED OFFICIAL USE

THE SITUATION ON THE GROUND IN CROATIA AND BOSNIA. COT

EMPHASIZED THAT WHILE PEACKEEPING IS THE MANDATE IN

CROATIA, PROTECTION OF HUMANITARIAN AID OPERATIONS IS THE

TASK IN B-H. WHILE THERE IS AT LEAST A TEMPORARY

CEASEFIRE WORKING IN CROATIA, GENERAL WARFARE AND

TARGETING OF HUMANITARIAN AID CONVOYS IN B-H CONTINUE

UNABATED. AMB. ALBRIGHT STRESSED THE NEED TO KEEP BOTH

DISCUSSION OF UNPROFOR AND CRITICISM OF UN RESOLUTIONS

AFFECTING ITS OPERATIONS WITHIN "THE BOSOM OF THE

FAMILY," AND OUT OF PUBLIC FORA.

END SUMMARY.

2. AFTER A BRIEF INTRODUCTION FROM AKASHI AND A SHORT

PRESENTATION ON UNPROFOR CIVIL AND HUMANITARIAN AFFAIRS

PROJECTS, GENERAL COT OPENED THE FLOOR TO QUESTIONS.

AMB. ALBRIGHT ASKED FOR AN ASSESSMENT OF THE CURRENT

COMBAT LEVELS IN CROATIA AND BOSNIA AND THEIR EFFECT ON

PEACE PROSPECTS AND HUMANITARIAN AID OPERATIONS.

-------

CROATIA

-------

3. COT SAID THAT PEACEKEEPING IS THE UNPROFOR MANDATE IN

CROATIA. SINCE DECEMBER 23 THERE HAD BEEN A GENERALLY-

RESPECTED, UNPROFOR-BROKERED PROVISIONAL CEASEFIRE

BETWEEN CROATIAN AND KRAJINA SERB FORCES. UNPROFOR IS

WORKING TO PERSUADE THE SIDES TO EXTEND THE CEASEFIRE

BEYOND ITS CURRENT JAN 15 TERMINATION DATE. THIS PERIOD

OF RELATIVE STABILITY MADE POSSIBLE THROUGH UNPROFOR

EFFORTS IS "THE SORT OF GOOD NEWS NOT OFTEN REPORTED ON

CNN," COT ADDED.

------

BOSNIA

------

4. COT SAID UNPROFOR\'S MANDATE TO PROTECT HUMANITARIAN

AID OPERATIONS IN BOSNIA WAS SEVERELY HAMPERED BY THE

HIGHLY DANGEROUS SITUATION ON THE GROUND, ESPECIALLY IN

CENTRAL BOSNIA. BOTH THE GENERAL LEVEL OF COMBAT AND

TARGETING OF HUMANITARIAN CONVOYS WERE INCREASING. COT

PARTICULARLY NOTED THE RECENT HEAVY FIGHTING IN THE VITEZ

AREA. AT PRESENT, SERB FORCES THERE HAD BECOME

"INTERESTED SPECTATORS" TO THE RAGING CONFLICT BETWEEN

BOSNIAN CROAT (HVO) AND MAINLY MUSLIM GOVERNMENT FORCES,

BUT COT PREDICTED NEW SERB OFFENSIVES SOON.

5. A/S SHATTUCK ASKED COT TO CHARACTERIZE UNPROFOR

TACTICS FOR PROTECTING AID OPERATIONS IN BOSNIA,

PARTICULARLY UNPROFOR\'S VIEW ON WHETHER A MORE "RIGOROUS"

POSTURE IN DEFENDING RELIEF FLOWS WOULD BE WISE. COT

RESPONDED THAT UNPROFOR AND UNHCR OPERATIONS ARE "WHOLLY

INTERTWINED," DESPITE UNHCR\'S "TRADITIONAL PHILOSOPHY OF

AVOIDING PEOPLE IN KHAKI." COT RESISTED ANY IMPLICATION

THAT UNPROFOR WAS LESS THAN RIGOROUS IN ITS RELIEF

PROTECTION EFFORTS. HE NOTED THAT A FRENCH UNPROFOR

SOLDIER IN SARAJEVO HAD RECENTLY BEEN WOUNDED, AND

POSSIBLY PARALYZED, WHEN HE DROVE A UNHCR RELIEF DELIVERY

INTO A SECTOR CONSIDERED TOO DANGEROUS FOR UNHCR DRIVERS.

UNPROFOR REGULARLY MAKES DIRECT DELIVERI

ZONES IN CENTRAL BOSNIA.

--------

SARAJEVO

--------

6. AMB. ALBRIGHT ASKED COT FOR HIS ASSESSMENT OF THE

SITUATION IN SARAJEVO. COT RESPONDED THAT UNHCR COULD

OFFER A MORE GENERAL BRIEFING, BUT HE COULD ADDRESS THE

MILITARY ENVIRONMENT. COT OBSERVED THAT THE FIGHTING IN

SARAJEVO IS TERRIBLE, BUT DISTRIBUTION THERE OF

HUMANITARIAN AID WAS, IN MANY WAYS, THE BEST IN BOSNIA.

HE FURTHER STRESSED THAT SOME HIGHLY VISIBLE ASPECTS OF

THE SIEGE, NAMELY THE CONSTANT SERB ARTILLERY AND SNIPER

FIRE, MAY OBSCURE THE REAL MILITARY EQUATION.

APOLOGIZING FOR ENGAGING IN "A MACABRE ARITHMETIC," COT

STRESSED THAT THE NUMBER OF KILLED AND WOUNDED IN

SARAJEVO WAS GENERALLY LOWER THAN WHAT MIGHT BE ASSUMED

ON THE BASIS OF THE DAILY COUNT OF ARTILLERY IMPACTS.

MOREOVER, ESPECIALLY HEAVY SERB ARTILLERY BARRAGES IN

RECENT DAYS WERE IN PART RESPONSES TO INCREASINGLY STRONG

INFANTRY CHALLENGES BY BOSNIAN GOVERNMENT FORCES IN THE

MOUNT ZUK AREA OF THE CITY AND ELSEWHERE.

ATROCITIES

----------

7. AMB. ALBRIGHT AND A/S SHATTUCK BOTH STRESSED THE

DELEGATION\'S CONCERN THAT UNPROFOR TAKE AN ACTIVE ROLE IN

KEEPING THE INTERNATIONAL WAR CRIMES COMMISSION AND OTHER

BODIES INFORMED ABOUT ATROCITIES IN CROATIA AND BOSNIA.

COT NOTED THAT THE RECENT MASSACRES IN THE MEDAK POCKET

(IN CROATIA) AT STUPNI DOL (IN CENTRAL B-H) HAD BEEN

THOROUGHLY DOCUMENTED BY UNPROFOR MILITARY AND CIVILAN

POLICE ELEMENTS AND REPORTED TO THE COMMISSION AND OTHER

APPROPRIATE ENTITIES. HE THEN OPINED, IN PASSIONATE

TERMS, THAT "ALTHOUGH WE IN UNPROFOR ARE NOT ALWAYS SURE

WHY WE ARE STILL HERE, WE DO KNOW THAT, WERE WE NOT HERE

AS WITNESSES, THERE WOULD BE MANY MORE ATROCITIES."

REFERRING LATER TO THIS POINT, AMB. ALBRIGHT ACKNOWLEDGED

THAT UNPROFOR\'S PRESENCE MUST INDEED HAVE PREVENTED

RESOLUTIONS

-----------

8. IN A CONCLUDING DISCUSSION, AMBASSADOR ALBRIGHT

REFLECTED ON THE EXTRAORDINARY LEVEL OF ACTIVITY IN THE

LIMITED OFFICIAL USE

LIMITED OFFICIAL USE

PAGE 03 ZAGREB 00082 03 OF 04 071632Z

UN SECURITY COUNCIL IN THE PAST TWO YEARS, NOTING THAT

MORE RESOLUTIONS HAD PASSED THERE IN THAT TIME THAN IN

THE INSTITUTION\'S ENTIRE PREVIOUS HISTORY. ONE REASON

WAS SIMPLY THAT IT WAS EASIER IN A POST-COLD WAR

ENVIRONMENT TO PASS RESOLUTIONS. BEYOND THIS FACT,

HOWEVER, THERE WERE EXTREMELY HIGH EXPECTATIONS FOR UN

PEACEKEEPING IN THE WAKE OF THE COLD WAR. SHE WENT ON TO

OBSERVE THAT GENERAL COT HAD SOMETIMES CRITICIZED THE

COUNCIL\'S RESOLUTIONS IN THEIR APPLICATION TO THE FORMER

YUGOSLAVIA, AND ASKED IF HE COULD ELABORATE ON HIS

PUBLICLY-STATED VIEWS.

9. COT SAID THAT, AS A SOLDIER, HE WAS ACCUSTOMED TO

EXPRESSING HIMSELF IN DIRECT AND SIMPLE FASHION. PLANS

CONCEIVED UNDER SOME UN RESOLUTIONS SEEMED TO HIM

"INCONCRETE." HE CALLED THE ORIGINAL RESOLUTION AND

IMPLEMENTATION OF THE VANCE PLAN FOR CROATIA "A SWINDLE,"

IN THE SENSE THAT THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY HAD NEVER

MADE AVAILABLE THE FORCES AND AUTHORITIY TO ACCOMPLISH

THE MISSION. WALKING TO A THEATER MAP ON THE CONFERENCE

ROOM WALL, COT POINTED OUT THAT HE HAD HAD TO "SCRAPE UP

EVERY SOLDIER HE COULD LOCATE IN CROATIA AND B-H COMMAND"

TO STEM AN AUTUMN OUTBREAK OF FIGHTING BETWEEN CROATS AND

KRAJINA SERBS IN CROATIA. SUCH A SCRAMBLE POINTED UP THE

WEAKNESSES OF UNPROFOR ASSETS, AND WAS A HUMILIATING

EXERCISE FOR A GENERAL IN COMMAND OF INTERNATIONALLY-

SUPPORTED PEACEKEEPING FORCES. OPERATIONAL LIMITATIONS

ON UNPROFOR IN BOSNIA MADE THE UN\'S PROTECTION EFFORT

THERE ANOTHER "SWINDLE," COT SAID. OF THE 7,500

ADDITIONAL TROOPS CALLED FOR TO IMPLEMENT "SAFE AREAS,"

ONLY 2,000 HAD BEEN FORTHCOMING.

10. AMB. ALBRIGHT RESPONDED THAT SHE UNDERSTOOD THESE

FRUSTRATIONS, AND THAT THE UN NOW FINDS ITSELF WORKING IN

"A NEURALGIC TIME" IN TRYING TO MAINTAIN PEACEKEEPING

OPERATIONS AS PUBLIC CONCERN ABOUT THE POLITICAL, FISCAL,

AND HUMAN COSTS OF SUCH OPERATIONS INCREASES IN MANY

NATIONS, INCLUDING THE UN

ITED STATES. EMPHASIZING THE

NECESSITY OF LEARNING FROM MISTAKES, AMB. ALBRIGHT SAID

SHE WAS NONETHELESS TROUBLED WHEN CRITICISMS OF THE UN BY

OTHER ELEMENTS OF THE INSTITUTION APPEARED IN PUBLIC

STATEMENTS. THE SECURITY COUNCIL MAY NEED TO LOOK EVEN

MORE REALISTICALLY AT FUTURE RESOLUTIONS, BUT COMMANDERS

IN THE FIELD SHOULD ADDRESS THEIR CONCERNS FIRST "IN THE

BOSOM OF THE FAMILY" AT THE UN, AND NOT IN PUBLIC FORA.

GENERAL COT DID NOT RESPOND. IN CONCLUSION, BOTH

AMBASSADORS GALBRAITH AND ALBRIGHT EMPHASIZED THEIR

APPRECIATION FOR THE COMMITMENT AND COURAGE OF UNPROFOR
