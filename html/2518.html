SUBJECT: ITALIAN DEFMIN\'S COMMENTS SPARK ANOTHER ROUND OF

"EURO-SKEPTICISM" CHARGES

1. ITALIAN DEFENSE MINISTER ANTONIO MARTINO REVIVED

OPPOSITION PARTY ACCUSATIONS THAT HE IS A "EURO-SKEPTIC" BY

SUGGESTING THAT THE EU HAD RUSHED INTO CREATING ITS OWN

MILITARY IDENTITY WITHOUT THINKING IT THROUGH. THE NUMBER OF

TASKS APPROPRIATE FOR THE EU\'S RAPID REACTION FORCE (RRF)

SHOULD BE REDUCED TO "VERY FEW," MARTINO TOLD THE BRITISH

"DAILY TELEGRAPH" IN A FEBRUARY 21 INTERVIEW. NATO HAS

PROVEN TO BE THE BEST VEHICLE TO PERFORM MULTINATIONAL

OPERATIONS OF BOTH AN OFFENSIVE AND PEACE-KEEPING NATURE,

MARTINO SAID. IT HAS A COMMAND AND CONTROL STRUCTURE THAT

WORKS -- SOMETHING THE RRF LACKS AT THIS POINT. "NATO

WORKS," MARTINO STATED, ADDING, "IF IT AIN\'T BROKE, DON\'T FIX

IT."

2. MARTINO ASKED THE SPANISH EU PRESIDENCY TO CALL A MEETING

OF EU DEFENSE MINISTERS TO RE-EXAMINE THE SCOPE OF THE

PETERSBURG TASKS AND DECIDE WHICH ONES ARE FEASIBLE FOR THE

RRF, ACCORDING TO THE INTERVIEW AND OTHER PRESS REPORTS.

MARTINO PREDICTED THAT THERE WOULD NOT BE MANY MISSIONS THE

RRF COULD CARRY OUT WITHOUT THERE BEING SOME DISAGREEMENT

AMONG THE EU MEMBERS. HE CAUTIONED AGAINST CREATING

"DIVISIVE SITUATIONS" AMONG MEMBERS.

3. ITALY\'S CENTER-LEFT OPPOSITION QUICKLY JUMPED ON MARTINO\'S

STATEMENTS AS ANOTHER SIGN OF HIS -- AND THE BERLUSCONI

ADMINISTRATION\'S -- EURO-SKEPTICISM. COMING NOT LONG AFTER

HIS PUBLIC DOUBTS ABOUT THE WISDOM OF A SINGLE EUROPEAN

CURRENCY AND HIS VOCAL REFUSAL TO SUPPORT ITALY\'S

PARTICIPATION IN THE AIRBUS A400M MILITARY TRANSPORT PLANE,

MARTINO\'S COMMENTS ADD MORE FUEL TO THE FIRE. GIOVANNI

FORCIERI, VICE PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE DEFENSE COMMITTEE AND

OPPOSITION MEMBER, SAID MARTINO\'S STATEMENTS WERE "SERIOUS"

AND NOT USEFUL TO THE US OR NATO. GIUSEPPE MOLINARI,

OPPOSITION LEADER ON THE CHAMBER OF DEPUTIES\' DEFENSE

COMMITTEE, CHARGED THAT ITALY IS BECOMING "FLATTENED OUT ON

DEVOTIONAL ATLANTICSM," AND CALLED FOR MARTINO TO APPEAR

BEFORE PARLIAMENT. CENTER-LEFT PARTY LEADER PIERO FASSINO

NOTED THAT BERLUSCONI CLAIMED TO BE DEFENDING ITALY\'S

NATIONAL INTERESTS, WHICH FASSINO SAID USED TO MEAN BEING A

PART OF EUROPE. OPPOSITION MEMBERS ALSO OBJECTED TO

MARTINO\'S REQUEST FOR AN EU DEFMIN MEETING WITHOUT FIRST

HAVING CONSULTED WITH PARLIAMENT.

4. COMMENT. MARTINO\'S COMMENTS ON THE RRF ARE FURTHER

EVIDENCE THAT HE IS LESS DOGMATIC ABOUT WHAT IT MEANS TO BE

"PRO-EU" THAN MANY ITALIANS, PARTICULARLY THOSE IN THE

OPPOSITION. HE IS AN ADVOCATE FOR EUROPEAN MILITARIES WITH

RESTRUCTURED ARMED FORCES AND BETTER ARMAMENTS TO MEET GLOBAL

SECURITY NEEDS, AND ARGUES THAT IF EUROPEANS HAD ALLOTTED

MORE FUNDS FOR DEFENSE OVER THE YEARS, EUROPE WOULD HAVE

GREATER INFLUENCE IN WASHINGTON. HE IS NOT THE ONLY MINISTER

WILLING TO TAKE A PRACTICAL, AND OFTEN CONTROVERSIAL, LOOK AT

EU INSTITUTIONS, BUT HIS RRF COMMENTS MAY BE A LITTLE OUT IN

FRONT OF HIS PRIME MINISTER\'S POSITION. IN A RECENT

INTERVIEW IN "THE SUNDAY TIMES," BERLUSCONI SAID THAT THE RRF

SHOULD WORK IN COOPERATION WITH NATO BUT BE COMPLETELY

AUTONOMOUS. MARTINO MAY HEAR MORE ON THE SUBJECT FROM HIS EU

COUNTERPARTS AT THE INFORMAL DEFENSE MINISTERS\' MEETING

SCHEDULED FOR MARCH 22-23 IN SARAGOSA. END COMMENT.
