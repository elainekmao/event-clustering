SUBJECT: ADM. JOHNSON/AMB. NICHOLSON BALKAN TRIP FOCUSES ON

INTER-RELIGIOUS, INTERETHNIC RECONCILIATION

REF: SIDEREAS/FROWICK EMAIL DECEMBER 23

1. SUMMARY: AT THE INVITATION OF AFSOUTH CINC ADMIRAL

JOHNSON, AMBASSADOR NICHOLSON TRAVELED TO THE BALKANS TO

MEET WITH RELIGIOUS LEADERS IN BOSNIA AND KOSOVO AND WITH

AMBASSADOR BUTLER IN SKOPJE, TO PROMOTE RELIGIOUS

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 VATICA 06156 01 OF 02 301354Z

RECONCILIATION EFFORTS IN THE REGION. IN BOSNIA THE

DELEGATION MET WITH THE CATHOLIC, MUSLIM AND JEWISH MEMBERS

OF THE B-H INTER-RELIGIOUS COUNCIL (IRC) WHO ADMITTED

CONTINUING CHALLENGES IN FORGING RECONCILIATION IN BOSNIA;

CATHOLIC CARDINAL PULJIC FOCUSED ON NEED FOR REFUGEES TO BE

ABLE TO RETURN "WITH DIGNITY." AMBASSADOR NICHOLSON ALSO

BROKE AWAY FOR A BRIEF MEETING WITH FRANCISCAN FRIAR DZOLAN

TO DISCUSS THE FRANCISCANS\' ATTITUDE TOWARD RECONCILIATION.

IN KOSOVO, THE DELEGATION VISITED AN EMBASSY VATICAN-

SPONSORED PROJECT FOR INTER-ETHNIC RECONCILIATION RUN BY THE

SANT\'EGIDIO COMMUNITY. CHILDREN STUDYING IN THE "SCHOOL OF

PEACE" AT THE PRIZREN ALBANIAN NORMAL SCHOOL DEMONSTRATED AN

UNDERSTANDING OF HISTORICAL PHENOMENA SUCH AS THE HOLOCAUST

AND THE US CIVIL RIGHTS MOVEMENT AND CLEARLY ENJOYED EACH

OTHER\'S COMPANY. THE NEXT STEP IN PRIZREN SHOULD BE TO

INCORPORATE SERB CHILDREN IN THE PROGRAM ALONG WITH THE

KOSOVAR ALBANIANS AND TURKS AND THE BOSNIACS CURRENTLY

INVOLVED. THE TRIP CONCLUDED WITH A LUNCH HOSTED BY COMKFOR

LTG MINI, WHO HAS URGED THE CATHOLIC BISHOP OF PRIZREN TO

PROMOTE SERB RETURNS AND ASKED EMBASSY VATICAN TO WORK WITH

THE USG TO PUT PRESSURE ON THE BISHOP TO DO SO. END SUMMARY.

--------------------------------------------- ---

BOSNIAN INTER-RELIGIOUS COUNCIL: MORE WORK TO DO

--------------------------------------------- ---

2. CINC AFSOUTH ADMIRAL JOHNSON INVITED U.S. AMBASSADOR TO

THE HOLY SEE JIM NICHOLSON TO ACCOMPANY HIM ON A TRIP TO THE

UNCLASSIFIED

BALKANS DECEMBER 19-20, TO MEET WITH RELIGIOUS LEADERS

WORKING FOR RECONCILIATION IN BOSNIA, AND TO VISIT AN

EMBASSY VATICAN-SPONSORED RECONCILIATION PROJECT IN PRIZREN,

YUGOSLAVIA. IN SARAJEVO AMBASSADOR BOND AND EMBASSY

SARAJEVO TEAM BRIEFED THE DELEGATION, NOTING THE GOOD

PROGRESS THE RELIGIOUS GROUPS HAD MADE IN DRAFTING A LAW ON

RELIGIONS (NOTE: OUR VATICAN CONTACTS BELIEVE THIS DRAFT LAW

PROVIDES "EXCELLENT GUARANTEES"). BRIEFERS NOTED THAT

PROBLEMS AMONG THE MOSTLY CATHOLIC CROATS CONTINUE, WITH THE

VATICAN RELUCTANT TO TAKE ON THE MORE NATIONALISTIC ELEMENTS

OF ITS OWN CLERGY IN BOSNIA-HERZEGOVINA. THE CROAT

COMMUNITY HAS DEVELOPED A "MINORITY COMPLEX" AND THROWS UP

ITS OWN OBSTACLES TO CROAT REFUGEE RETURNS BY INSISTING THAT

ALL MUNICIPAL SERVICES BE REBUILT BEFORE RETURNS CAN OCCUR.

CARDINAL PULJIC HAS GREAT CREDIBILITY FROM WEATHERING THE

WAR IN SARAJEVO, BUT HE SHARES THIS "COMPLEX." THE CLERGY

NEED TO LET DOWN THEIR GUARD AND TAKE THE LEAD IN PROMOTING

INTER-RELIGIOUS RECONCILIATION. KEY MEASURES TO TAKE EARLY

ON WOULD BE PROMOTING VISITS TO EACH OTHER\'S PLACES OF

WORSHIP, AND WORKING TOGETHER ON A SCHOOL CURRICULUM.

3. AMBASSADOR BOND, ADMIRAL JOHNSON, AMBASSADOR NICHOLSON

AND STAFF THEN MET WITH THREE OF THE FOUR RELIGIOUS LEADERS

IN SARAJEVO -- CATHOLIC CARDINAL PULIC, MUSLIM MUFTI CERIC,

AND JEWISH COMMUNITY LEADER DR. JAKOB FINCI. THE SERB

ORTHODOX COMMUNITY WAS NOT REPRESENTED BECAUSE METROPOLITAN

NIKOLAJ HAD OTHER COMMITMENTS. CERIC EXPRESSED THE GROUP\'S

GRATITUDE TO THE US FOR HELPING END THE WAR AND BRINGING

SECURITY TO BOSNIA. HE WAS POSITIVE ON THE DRAFT LAW ON

RELIGIONS, NOTING IT WAS NOW BEFORE THE B-H ASSEMBLY. BOTH

CERIC AND FINCI SPOKE FRANKLY ABOUT THE CHALLENGES STILL

FACING THE IRC, IN PARTICULAR DIFFICULTIES RESTORING

PROPERTIES AND DEVELOPING A COMMON PROPOSAL FOR RELIGIOUS

EDUCATION IN PUBLIC SCHOOLS. BOTH NOTED BOSNIA\'S LONG

TRADITION OF RELIGIOUS TOLERANCE AS A SOURCE OF HOPE FOR THE

FUTURE EVEN AS THEY DECRIED BOSNIAN POLITICAL LEADERS\'

DEMAGOGUERY ON RELIGIOUS IDENTIFICATION. PULJIC SAID THERE

WAS NO ALTERNATIVE TO DIALOGUE AND ADMITTED THERE WAS MUCH

WORK YET TO BE DONE BEFORE BOSNIA COULD EMBARK ON A PATH

TOWARD "EUROPE." PULIC EMPHASIZED, AS PREDICTED, THE NEED

FOR ECONOMIC OPPORTUNITIES AND FOR REFUGEES TO BE ABLE TO

RETURN TO THEIR ORIGINAL ABODES "NOT AS PAUPERS, BUT WITH

DIGNITY." ADMIRAL JOHNSON AND AMBASSADOR BOND AND

AMBASSADOR NICHOLSON URGED THE RELIGIOUS LEADERS TO EXERCISE

LEADERSHIP. ALL THREE FOCUSED ON THE NEED FOR THE IRC TO DO

MORE OUTSIDE OF SARAJEVO AND TO SHOW THE WORLD AN EXAMPLE OF

INTERFAITH RECONCILIATION AND COOPERATION.

RECORD OF PROMOTING INTER-ETHNIC UNITY IN BOSNIA. HE

BRIEFED US ON HIS LATEST PROJECT -- A COMMUNITY HOUSE WHERE

KOSOVO: RECONCILIATION WITH THE WRONG PARTNERS

--------------------------------------------- -

5. IN KOSOVO, THE DELEGATION VISITED AN EMBASSY VATICAN-

SPONSORED PROJECT FOR INTER-ETHNIC RECONCILIATION RUN BY THE

SANT\'EGIDIO COMMUNITY. WITH A USD 60,000 GRANT FROM PD/ECA,

SANT\'EGIDIO (A LAY CATHOLIC COMMUNITY THAT FOCUSES ON

DEVELOPMENT AND RECONCILIATION IN AFRICA AND THE BALKANS)

DEVELOPED AN ELEMENTARY SCHOOL CURRICULUM THAT TRAINS

TEACHERS AND PROVIDES MATERIALS TO TEACH RECONCILIATION

TECHNIQUES. THE CHILDREN STUDYING IN THE "SCHOOL OF PEACE"

AT THE PRIZREN NORMAL SCHOOL SANG AND READ THEIR OWN TEXTS,

DEMONSTRATING AN UNDERSTANDING OF HISTORICAL PHENOMENA SUCH

AS THE HOLOCAUST AND THE U.S. CIVIL RIGHTS MOVEMENT. THE

CHILDREN CLEARLY ENJOYED EACH OTHER\'S COMPANY, OFFERING HOPE

FOR THE YOUNG GENERATION T OVERCOME THEIR PARENTS\'

HOSTILITY. WE NOTED, HOWEVER, THE ABSENCE OF SERB CHILDREN.

PER REF EMAIL, THE NEXT STEP IN PRIZREN SHOULD BE TO

INCORPORATE SERB CHILDREN IN THE PROGRAM ALONG WITH THE

ALBANIANS, TURKS AND THE BOSNIACS CURRENTLY ENROLLED IN THE

PRIZREN NORMAL SCHOOL.

6. THE TRIP CONCLUDED WITH A LUNCH HOSTED BY COMKFOR LTG

FABIO MINI FOR ADMIRAL JOHNSON, AMBASSADOR NICHOLSON, AND

NATIONAL COMMANDERS FROM THE ITALIAN, FRENCH AND GERMAN KFOR

CONTINGENTS. GENERAL MINI HAD VISITED WITH CATHOLIC BISHOP

MARKO SOPI (WHOSE APOSTOLIC ADMINISTRATION IS CENTERED ON

PRIZREN). GENERAL MINI TOLD US HE HAD URGED BISHOP SOPI TO

INVOLVE HIMSELF MORE PUBLICLY IN PROMOTING SERB RETURNS.

MINI HOPED THAT SOPI WOULD VISIT A SERB ORTHODOX CHURCH OR

COMMENT

-------

7. AMBASSADOR NICHOLSON/EMBASSY VATICAN WOULD LIKE TO

CONVEY OUR GRATITUDE FOR THE OUTSTANDING BRIEFINGS AND

SUPPORT OFFERED BY AMBASSADORS BOND AND BUTLER AND THEIR

STAFFS, AND BY THE STAFF AT USOP. WE WILL FOLLOW UP WITH

THE HOLY SEE TO URGE GREATER ENGAGEMENT TO PROMOTE

RECONCILIATION IN BOTH BOSNIA AND KOSOVO. POPE JOHN PAUL II

IS PREOCCUPIED WITH A POSSIBLE CLASH OF CIVILIZATIONS

DEVELOPING FROM THE U.S.-LED WAR ON TERRORISM, THUS THE HOLY

SEE WILL WANT TO DO EVERYTHING IN ITS POWER TO ENSURE THAT

RECONCILIATION MAKES PROGRESS IN THE BALKANS AS A POSITIVE

EXAMPLE TO THE WORLD. AS DISCUSSED WITH EMBASSY SARAJEVO

AND USOP, WE WILL BROACH WITH THE VATICAN THE POSSIBILITY OF

AN OFFICIAL VATICAN DELEGATION TO BOSNIA ATTEND THE OPENING

OF A REBUILT MOSQUE AS WELL AS MEANS TO ENERGIZE BISHOP SOPI

TO PROMOTE SERB RETURNS IN KOSOVO.

NICHOLSON
