ECMM - NO MILITARY FLIGHTS FROM CROATIA

---------------------------------------

2. AT A NOVEMBER 24 PRESS CONFERENCE EC MONITOR

MISSION SPOKESMAN CHRIS POOLE SAID THAT NO

MILITARY PLANES HAD TAKEN OFF FROM THE FOUR

AIRPORTS UNDER OBSERVATION IN CROATIA. HE

CLARIFIED REMARKS REPORTEDLY MADE BY CEDRIC

THORNBERRY IN BELGRADE THAT MILITARY PLANES "FROM

CROATIA" WERE ALSO FLYING INTO B-H. POOLE SAID

THORNBERRY HAD IN FACT BEEN REFERRING TO MILITARY

AIRCRAFT TAKEOFFS FROM THE SERBIAN-CONTROLLED

AIRPORT IN UDBINA (SECTOR SOUTH) OF CROATIA.

--------------------------------

86,709 RESIDENCY PERMITS GRANTED

--------------------------------

3. MINISTER OF THE INTERIOR IVAN JARNJAK TOLD A

PRESS CONFERENCE THAT THE MINISTRY OF THE INTERIOR

SO FAR THIS YEAR HAD RECEIVED 122,825 CITIZENSHIP

REQUESTS. 86,709 WERE GRANTED AND 6,810

REJECTED. A STAFF OF 2,000 EMPLOYEES PROCESSES

2,000-2,500 REQUESTS DAILY. JARNJAK ACKNOWLEDGED

THAT THERE HAD BEEN CASES IN SEVERAL TOWNS WHERE

RESIDENCE PERMITS HAD NOT BEEN PROPERLY PROCESSED.

DURING THE FIRST NINE MONTHS OF 1992. THE MUP HAS

ALREADY REGISTERED 19,449 CRIMINAL ACTS, WHICH IS

MORE THAN REPORTED IN ALL OF 1991. THE POLICE

DETAINED 42,095 SUSPECTS, INCLUDING MANY

RECIDIVISTS AND MINORS. THERE HAVE BEEN 2,753

BOMBINGS AND 837 ARSON FIRES, A 70.3 PERCENT

INCREASE OVER 1991. THESE CRIMINAL ACTS DAMAGED

OR DESTROYED 2,917 HOUSES, 457 CARS, 240 SHOPS AND

RESTAURANTS, 182 FARMS, AND 78 MONUMENTS.

HNS ROUNDTABLE CRITICIZES ZUPANIJA PLANS

----------------------------------------

5. THE GOVERNMENT\'S PLANS FOR THE NEW "ZUPANIJA"

AND LOCAL GOVERNMENT REORGANIZATION WERE

CRITICIZED AT A NOVEMBER 24 MULTI-PARTY ROUNDTABLE

SPONSORED BY THE CROATIAN NATIONAL PARTY (HNS).

HNS LEADER SAVKA DABCEVIC-KUCAR SAID THE LAW WAS

HURRIED. SHE CRITICIZED THE RE-DISTRICTING PLANS

AS BEING AN EFFORT TOWARD "STATE CENTRALISM."

ADDING MORE BUREAUCRATIC LAYERS WILL ALSO MEAN

MORE EXPENSE, SHE ARGUED. FORMER COMMUNIST PARTY

SDPH (SOCIAL DEMOCRATIC PARTY OF CROATIA) LEADER

ZDRAVKO TOMAC SAID CROATIA IS OUT OF STEP WITH

INTERNATIONAL TRENDS TOWARD MAKING FEWER SMALL

ADMINISTRATIVE UNITS. HSS (CROATIAN

PEASANTS\'PATRY) LEADER DRAGO STIPAC CALLED THE

PROPOSED ZUPANIJA LAW "TOTAL IMPROVISATION IN AN

AWKWARD MOMENT."

8. FROM THEIR BASE IN MUSAPSTAN AND CRNO IN THE

ZADAR REGION (PINK ZONE OF SECTOR SOUTH), SERBS

OPENED FIRE AT THE CROATIAN DEFENSE LINE OF ZADAR

CITY. ONE CIVILIAN WAS INJURED. SERBIAN UNITS

OPENED FIRE FROM OCCUPIED PODGRADINE AT

POSEDARJE. THREE CROATIAN HOUSES WERE ALSO SET ON

FIRE IN CRNO.

9. THE MILITARY DISTRICT COURT IN SPLIT OPENED

LEGAL PROCEEDINGS AGAINST 14 MEMBERS OF THE

SERBIAN DEMOCRATIC PARTY IN DUBROVNIK. THEY ARE
