SUBJECT: HOLY SEE: FM TAURAN REPLY TO SECRETARY\'S LETTER ON

BIOTECH FOOD SAFETY AND SOUTHERN AFRICAN FOOD CRISIS

REF: A) VATICAN 4361 B) STATE 177956

1. (SBU) IN HIS REPLY TO SECRETARY POWELL\'S LETTER

REQUESTING A STRONG HOLY SEE STATEMENT REGARDING THE SAFETY

OF U.S. BIOTECH FOOD ASSISTANCE IN SOUTHERN AFRICA (REF B),

HOLY SEE FOREIGN MINISTER EQUIVALENT ARCHBISHOP JEAN-LOUIS

TAURAN GAVE NO INDICATION THAT THE HOLY SEE WOULD OFFER A

STRONG PUBLIC STATEMENT IN SUPPORT OF U.S. FOOD ASSISTANCE,

BUT REAFFIRMED THAT THE VATICAN IS OPEN TO FULL ACCEPTANCE

OF BIOTECH FOOD TECHNOLOGY AFTER "PROPER" SCIENTIFIC STUDY.

FURTHERMORE, HE AGREED THAT BIOTECH FOODS SHOULD BE SHARED

WITH LESS DEVELOPED COUNTRIES. TAURAN\'S DEPUTY TOLD DCM

OCTOBER 3 THAT AS A RESULT OF THE LETTER, PAPAL NUNCIO\'S IN

THE REGION HAD BEEN PROVIDED THE CONCLUSIONS OF A 1998 STUDY

GROUP DOCUMENT OF THE PONTIFICAL ACADEMY FOR LIFE (WHICH WAS

GENERALLY POSITIVE TOWARD CONSUMPTION OF BIOTECH FOODS) AND

ASKED THAT THE NUNCIOS SHARE THIS WITH THE BISHOPS IN THE

REGION. DESPITE THE CAUTIOUS TENOR OF THE LETTER AND ITS

SUPPORT FOR LABELING OF BIOTECH FOODS, POST REGARDS THIS

LETTER AS A SMALL STEP FORWARD IN OBTAINING THE HOLY SEE\'S

ACKNOWLEDGEMENT THAT THERE IS NO REASON FOR REJECTION OF

BIOTECH FOODS.

2. (SBU) THE RESPONSE, COUCHED WITH CHARACTERISTIC VATICAN

CAUTION, CONVEYS THE HOLY SEE\'S APPRECIATION OF THE U.S.

ROLE IN OFFERING ASSISTANCE TO ALLEVIATE FURTHER HUNGER AND

STARVATION. WHILE ASSERTING THAT THE HOLY SEE IS "NOT IN A

POSITION" TO EVALUATE WHETHER COUNTRIES SHOULD OR SHOULD NOT

USE SPECIFIC FOODS, IT OFFERS A NUMBER OF GENERALLY POSITIVE

"CONSIDERATIONS" LEADING TO THE VIEW THAT THERE IS NO REASON

FOR REJECTION OF BIOTECH FOODS, WHICH "SHOULD BE AVAILABLE

TO POPULATIONS IN DEVELOPING COUNTRIES." AT THE SAME TIME,

THE HOLY SEE INSISTED THAT THE USE OF BIOTECH FOODS SHOULD

BE ACCOMPANIED BY A "RIGOROUS AND PROPORTIONED CAUTION" AND

SHOULD BE CLEARLY IDENTIFIED FOR THE PUBLIC.

3. (U) THE 1998 STUDY OF THE PONTIFICAL ACADEMY FOR LIFE TO

WHICH TAURAN REFERS PROVIDED A GENERALLY POSITIVE ASSESSMENT

OF THE POSSIBILITIES OF BIOTECH FOODS. FOR EXAMPLE, IT

CONCEDED THAT BIOTECHNOLOGY WAS "WITHOUT DOUBT A FORMIDABLE

TOOL FOR PRODUCING MORE AND BETTER FOOD." BUT IT ALSO SAID

THAT THE TECHNOLOGY "MUST BE ACCOMPANIED BY A RESPECT FOR

THE ENVIRONMENT," AND, "DEVELOPED COUNTRIES MUST FIND WAYS

TO SHARE BIOTECHNOLOGICAL CAPACITY WITH DEVELOPING

COUNTRIES." THE REPORT CONCLUDES WITH NORMAN BORLAUG\'S

FAMOUS QUIP: "WE CAN\'T TURN BACK THE HANDS OF TIME AND ONLY

USE TECHNOLOGIES APPROPRIATE FOR A MUCH LESS POPULATED

WORLD." WE ARE ACTIVELY WORKING WITH THE ACADEMY TO MOVE

THEIR ASSESSMENT BEYOND THIS STUDY BY DEVELOPING

OPPORTUNITIES TO EXPOSE ITS STAFF AND MANAGEMENT TO VISITING

BIOTECH SCIENTISTS AND BIO-ETHICISTS. WE WILL WORK WITH ITS

SISTER ACADEMY - THE PONTIFICAL ACADEMY OF THE SCIENCES - TO

SUPPORT A PROPOSED "STUDY WEEK" ON BIOTECHNOLOGY BY

COORDINATING THE PARTICIPATION OF RESPECTED US SCIENTISTS.

4. (U) IN CONNECTION WITH OUR PUSH TO HAVE THE HOLY SEE

ACKNOWLEDGE THE STRONG MORAL ARGUMENT THAT U.S. FOOD

ASSISTANCE IS SAFE AND SHOULD BE MADE AVAILABLE TO HUNGRY

PEOPLE, THE AMBASSADOR TOOK ADVANTAGE OF AN OCTOBER 4

CONFERENCE ON THE ETHICS OF GMO TECHNOLOGIES FOR THE

PHILIPPINES, ORGANIZED ON THE MARGINS OF THE CANONIZATION OF

JOSE MARIA ESCRIVA, FOUNDER OF THE OPUS DEI PRELATURE. THE

AUDIENCE, CONSISTING OF PHILIPPINE BISHOPS AND SELECTED

BUSINESSMEN, WAS TREATED TO A GOOD PROGRAM OF EXPERTS IN

BIOETHICS AND BIOENGINEERING FROM VARIOUS PONTIFICAL

ACADEMIES IN SUPPORT OF GMO TECHNOLOGIES FOR THE

PHILIPPINES. THE AUDIENCE WAS RECEPTIVE TO AMBASSADOR\'S

STRONG ARGUMENT IN SUPPORT OF THE MORALITY OF PROVIDING SAFE

U.S. FOOD ASSISTANCE TO THE HUNGRY IN SOUTHERN AFRICA. THE

ONLY SOUR NOTE WAS WHEN A VATICAN DESK OFFICER REPEATEDLY

POSITION ON BIOTECH FOODS. SUBSEQUENT TO THE PRESENTATION,

A GROUP OF PHILIPPINE BISHOPS TOLD THE AMBASSADOR THEY FULLY

SUPPORTED HIS ARGUMENTS, AND BELIEVED THE PHILIPPINES SHOULD

MOVE AHEAD MORE AGGRESSIVELY TO DEVELOP APPROPRIATE BIOTECH

FOODS.

FROM THE VATICAN, OCTOBER 2, 2002

YOUR EXCELLENCY,

I AM PLEASED TO ACKNOWLEDGE RECEIPT OF YOUR LETTER

CONCERNING BIOTECH FOODS THAT THE UNITED STATES EMBASSY TO

THE HOLY SEE FORWARDED TO ME ON SEPTEMBER 16, 2002.

THE HOLY SEE IS AWARE OF THE HUMANITARIAN CRISIS IN

SOUTHERN AFRICA AND APPRECIATES THE ROLE OF THE UNITED

STATES IN OFFERING ASSISTANCE, INCLUDING BIOTECH FOODS, IN

ORDER TO ALLEVIATE FURTHER HUNGER AND STARVATION IN THE

REGION.

THE PONTIFICAL ACADEMY FOR LIFE HAS EXAMINED THE

QUESTION OF BIOTECH FOODS AND IN 1998 PUBLISHED THE WRITINGS

OF A STUDY GROUP IN A BOOK IN ITALIAN ENTITLED

"BIOTECNOLOGIE ANIMALI E VEGETALI. NUOVE FRONTIERE E NUOVE

RESPONSIBILIT."

WHILE THE HOLY SEE IS NOT IN A POSITION TO MAKE ANY

TYPE OF EVALUATION ON SINGLE PRODUCTS OR WHETHER SINGLE

COUNTRIES SHOULD OR SHOULD NOT USE SUCH FOODS, IT DOES

NONETHELESS OFFER THESE CONSIDERATIONS ON THE MATTER:

A) THERE IS NOT AN ABSOLUTE REJECTION OF BIOTECH FOODS.

THEY CAN BE JUSTIFIED INASMUCH AS THEY CAN EXIST FOR THE

GOOD OF THE HUMAN PERSON. GOD HAS DESTINED ANIMALS AND

VEGETABLES FOR THE GOOD OF THE HUMAN PERSON AND HAS PLACED

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 VATICA 04909 02 OF 02 081513Z

THEM UNDER HIS OR HER CARE.

B) IN THE NAME OF THE GOOD OF EVERY HUMAN PERSON, IT IS

MORALLY REQUIRED THAT ALL MEASURE BE TAKEN TO ENSURE THAT

THERE ARE NO RISKS TO THE HEALTH OF HUMAN BEINGS. HERE THE

PRINCIPLE OF PRECAUTION IS FOLLOWED, WHICH DOES NOT INCLUDE

AN ABSOLUTE REJECTION, BUT RATHER A RIGOROUS AND

PROPORTIONED CAUTION ANALOGOUS TO THAT USED FOR

PHARMACEUTICAL GOODS.

C) IT IS NECESSARY THAT BIOTECH FOODS, ONCE THEY ARE

AVAILABLE FOR PUBLIC CONSUMPTION, BE IDENTIFIED AS SUCH WITH

AN APPROPRIATE LABEL, EVEN IF THEY ARE NOT DANGEROUS. THIS

PROCEDURE WOULD BE IN LINE WITH THE RIGHT OF THE CONSUMER TO

APPROPRIATE INFORMATION.

D) SUCH FOODS SHOULD ALSO BE AVAILABLE TO POPULATIONS IN

DEVELOPING COUNTRIES, SUBJECT TO PREVIOUS VERIFICATIONS OF

THE ETHICAL CONDITIONS AND IN LINE WITH THE PRINCIPLES OF

JUSTICE AND SOLIDARITY WITH REFERENCE TO COMMERCIAL AND

ECONOMIC QUESTIONS.

I AVAIL MYSELF OF THE OCCASION TO EXTEND TO YOUR EXCELLENCY

THE ASSURANCES OF MY HIGHEST CONSIDERATION.

JEAN-LOUIS TAURAN

END TEXT.

NICHOLSON
