ASSITANCE ON INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS

1. OVER THE PAST FEW YEARS VIETNAM HAS PUT IN PLACE THE

LEGAL FRAMEWORK NECESSARY TO PROVIDE BASIC PROTECTION FOR

INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS. HOWEVER, AT THE SAME TIME, IT

HAS DEMONSTRATED A SERIOUS LACK OF ABILITY TO ENFORCE THE

REGULATIONS IT HAS IN PLACE. INTERNATIONAL DONORS HAVE

RECOGNIZED VIETNAM\'S LOW LEVEL OF CAPACITY IN THIS AREA AND

HAVE RESPONDED WITH SIGNIFICANT TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE

PROGRAMS. THE UNITED STATES, JAPAN AND SWITZERLAND ARE

PROVIDING THE MOST SIGNIFICANT TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE TO

VIETNAM IN THE AREA OF IPR. THIS CABLE IS INTENDED TO

PROVIDE READERS WITH A BROAD PICTURE OF THE LEVEL AND TYPES

OF ASSISTANCE THE GVN IS RECEIVING TO ENHANCE ITS ABILITY TO

PROTECT INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS.

--------------

USG ASSISTANCE

--------------

2. THE USG IS PROVIDING SIGNIFICANT TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE TO

HELP THE GVN IMPLEMENT THE MEASURES NECESSARY TO COMPLY WITH

THE GVN\'S OBLIGATIONS UNDER BOTH THE 1997 COPYRIGHT

AGREEMENT WITH THE U.S. AND THE U.S. - VIETNAM BILATERAL

TRADE AGREEMENT (WHICH CAME INTO FORCE DECEMBER 10, 2001).

THE USG IS PROVIDING ASSISTANCE THROUGH A VARIETY OF SOURCES

INCLUDING THE SUPPORT FOR TRADE ACCELERATION (STAR) PROJECT

(THE USAID-FUNDED TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE PROGRAM TO ASSIST THE

GVN IMPLEMENT THE BTA), THE U.S. VIETNAM TRADE COUNCIL

(USVTC), AND THE PUBLIC AFFAIRS SECTION (PAS) OF THE

EMBASSY.

3. THE STAR PROJECT, A THREE-YEAR, USD 8 MILLION DOLLAR

PROGRAM WILL SPEND AN ESTIMATED ONE-FIFTH TO ONE-FOURTH OF

ITS RESOURCES OVER THE LIFE OF THE PROJECT TO ASSIST THE GVN

REFORM ITS IPR REGIME. ONE OF STAR\'S TOP IPR TECHNICAL

ASSISTANCE PRIORITIES IS TO ASSIST THE GVN REVISE TWO KEY

PIECES OF LEGISLATION: THE CIVIL CODE (PART IV ESTABLISHES

THE LEGAL BASIS FOR IP PROTECTION IN VIETNAM) AND THE CIVIL

PROCEDURE CODE (WHICH AFFECTS ADMINISTERING OF TRIPS AND ALL

OTHER IP REQUIREMENTS). STAR\'S OTHER PRINCIPLE OBJECTIVE IS

TO HELP THE GVN PREPARE TO ACCEDE TO SEVERAL INTERNATIONAL

IPR CONVENTIONS. THE FOLLOWING ARE IPR-RELATED PROJECTS

THAT STAR HAS COMPLETED RECENTLY OR WILL COMPLETE IN THE

COMING YEAR IN VIETNAM:

-- IN AUGUST 2002, STAR SUPPORTED A ONE-DAY WORKSHOP HOSTED

BY THE SUPREME PEOPLE\'S COURT (SPC) TO REVIEW IMPLICATIONS

OF THE BTA AND WTO FOR THE DEVELOPMENT OF THE NEW CIVIL

PROCEDURE CODE. THE IP CHAPTER OF THE BTA ESTABLISHES A

NUMBER OF STANDARDS FOR COURT PROCEDURES THAT WILL NEED TO

BE INCLUDED IN THE NEW CIVIL PROCEDURE CODE.

-- IN OCTOBER 2002, STAR SUPPORTED THE SPC IN ORGANIZING AN

IP-TRAINING PROGRAM IN HO CHI MINH CITY (HCMC) FOR AROUND 80

JUDGES AND SENIOR LEGAL OFFICIALS.

-- IN OCTOBER 2002, STAR AND THE INTERNATIONAL INTELLECTUAL

PROPERTY INSTITUTE (IIPI), IN CONJUNCTION WITH NATIONAL

OFFICE OF INDUSTRIAL PROPERTY (NOIP), PART OF THE MINISTRY

OF SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY (MOST), HOSTED MAJOR IP SEMINARS

IN HANOI AND HCMC WHICH PROVIDED A THOROUGH OVERVIEW OF THE

4. THE U.S. VIETNAM TRADE COUNCIL HAS A USD 745,000 ONE-YEAR

GRANT FROM USAID TO PROVIDE TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE IN SUPPORT

OF VIETNAM\'S IMPLEMENTATION OF THE BILATERAL TRADE

AGREEMENT. IN 2003, APPROXIMATELY ONE-SIXTH OF THOSE FUNDS

WILL SUPPORT PROGRAMS RELATED TO IPR. USVTC IPR-RELATED

PROGRAMS INCLUDE:

-- FROM 1997 TO 2001 USVTC SPONSORED SEVERAL GVN OFFICIALS

ATTENDANCE AT THE U.S. PATENT AND TRADEMARK OFFICE\'S ANNUAL

VISITING SCHOLARS AND INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ENFORCEMENT

PROGRAMS IN WASHINGTON, D.C.

-- IN APRIL 2003, USVTC WILL SPONSOR A STUDY MISSION TO THE

U.S., FOCUSING ON ENFORCEMENT OF KEY INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

RIGHTS. PARTICIPANTS IN THE STUDY MISSION WILL INCLUDE

OFFICIALS FROM COV, NOIP, CUSTOMS AND THE OFFICE OF THE

GOVERNMENT. THE MAIN OBJECTIVES OF THE MISSION WILL BE TO

STUDY THE ENFORCEMENT RIGHTS CREATED IN THE TRIPS AGREEMENT

(AND RELATED IP TREATIES) AND TO FOSTER A BILATERAL DIALOGUE

ON INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS ISSUE.

-- USVTC HAS ALSO PROPOSED SENDING OFFICIALS THAT

PARTICIPATE IN THE IPR STUDY MISSION TO THE U.S. ON FOLLOW-

JAPAN

-----

6. THE JAPANESE, THROUGH THE JAPAN INTERNATIONAL COOPERATION

AGENCY (JICA), HAVE A THREE-YEAR TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE

PROGRAM CALLED THE MODERNIZATION OF INDUSTRIAL PROPERTY

PROJECT (MOIPA) THAT BEGAN IN APRIL 2000 AND IS SCHEDULED TO

CONCLUDE IN MARCH 2003. THE OBJECTIVE OF THE PROJECT IS TO

IMPROVE NOIP\'S ABILITY TO PROCESS INDUSTRIAL PROPERTY

APPLICATIONS THROUGH ALL STAGES -- FROM THE FILING OF THE

APPLICATION TO ISSUANCE OF THE FINAL DECISION TO GRANT

PROTECTION TITLES. NOIP CURRENTLY PROCESSES APPROXIMATELY

5000 IP (PATENT, TRADEMARK AND OTHER) APPLICATIONS A YEAR

AND THE DEMAND IS GROWING ABOUT 10 PERCENT A YEAR.

7. THE INITIAL STAGES OF THE PROJECT WERE DEDICATED TO

IMPROVING NOIP\'S TECHNOLOGICAL CAPABILITIES. ABOUT 40

PERCENT OF THE PROJECT\'S BUDGET HAS BEEN DEDICATED TO

HARDWARE PURCHASES AND SOFTWARE DEVELOPMENT. JICA HAS

PROVIDED NOIP WITH 56 COMPUTERS, 5 SERVERS, 56 BARCODE

READERS, 7 SCANNERS, 35 PRINTERS, A GENERATOR AND OTHER

HARDWARE. THE TOTAL COST OF THE EQUIPMENT PROVIDED IN FY

2000 AND 2001 WAS SLIGHTLY MORE THAN USD 1 MILLION. (N.B.

PRIOR TO INITIATION OF THE JICA PROJECT, NOIP HAD A FEW OLD

STAND-ALONE COMPUTERS IN WHICH THEY ENTERED ALL INDUSTRIAL

PROPERTY APPLICATION DATA). ACCORDING TO JICA, ALL OF THE

COMPUTER EQUIPMENT WAS PURCHASED IN VIETNAM AND THE SOFTWARE

IS ALSO BEING DEVELOPED DOMESTICALLY.

8. JICA HAS FOUR LONG-TERM RESIDENT ADVISORS FROM THE

JAPANESE PATENT OFFICE IN NOIP OVERSEEING IMPLEMENTATION OF

THE PROJECT AND PROVIDING TECHNICAL SUPPORT, BOTH IN

ADMINISTERING THE NEW SYSTEM AND IN UNDERSTANDING THE NEW

TECHNOLOGIES. JICA ALSO PERIODICALLY SENDS ADDITIONAL SHORT-

TERM ADVISORS TO VIETNAM. FINALLY, JICA SENDS AT LEAST 4-

5 NOIP OFFICIALS TO JAPAN FOR TRAINING EACH YEAR.

ASSITANCE ON INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS

ENTITLED SPECIAL PROGRAM OF COOPERATION IN THE FIELD OF

INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY. THE SWISS STATE SECRETARIAT FOR

ECONOMIC AFFAIRS (SECO) OVERSEES THE PROJECT, WHICH IS BEING

IMPLEMENTED BY THE SWISS FEDERAL INSTITUTE OF INTELLECTUAL

PROPERTY WITH NOIP. THIS IS THE FIRST TIME THAT SWITZERLAND

HAS SUPPORTED A PARTNER COUNTRY WITH A COMPREHENSIVE PROGRAM

SPECIFICALLY IN THE FIELD OF INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ON A

BILATERAL BASIS. THE THREE SPECIFIC OBJECTIVES OF THE

PROJECT ARE: 1) TO ASSIST THE GVN MODERNIZE ITS IP SYSTEM

INCLUDING THE LEGAL FRAMEWORK, THE ENFORCEMENT SYSTEM, AND

THE CAPACITIES OF NATIONAL IP INFORMATION SYSTEMS; 2) TO

PROVIDE TRAINING, SEMINARS AND STUDY TOURS TO IMPROVE THE

SKILLS OF OFFICIALS WORKING ON IP; AND 3) TO PROMOTE

AWARENESS AND RESPECT FOR IP AMONG LOCAL BUSINESSES AND

CONSUMERS.

10. THE SWISS HAVE COMPLETED OR PLAN TO UNDERTAKE THE

FOLLOWING TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE PROJECTS:

-- SECO HAS TRANSLATED THE VARIOUS INTERNATIONAL IP

CONVENTIONS AND TREATIES INTO VIETNAMESE. THEY PUBLISHED

ONE BOUND VERSION THAT INCLUDES ALL THE RELEVANT TREATIES

AND ONE THAT CONTAINS THE EXECUTIVE SUMMARY OF EACH TREATY.

-- SECO CONDUCTED SEMINARS IN 2001 AND 2002 ON THE VARIOUS

INTERNATIONAL IPR TREATIES AND CONVENTIONS IN HANOI AND

HCMC.

-- IN 2002, SECO CONDUCTED A STUDY TOUR FOR 10 NOIP

PAGE 03 HANOI 02893 03 OF 04 041113Z

OFFICIALS TO SWITZERLAND TO STUDY UNFAIR COMPETITION, TRADE

NAMES AND GEOGRAPHICAL INDICATORS.

-- SECO ESTABLISHED A LIBRARY WITH VARIOUS IP-RELATED

MATERIALS AT NOIP. WILL CONTINUE TO ADD MATERIALS TO THE

LIBRARY THROUGHOUT THE LIFE OF THE PROJECT.

-- IN OCTOBER-NOVEMBER 2002 A SWISS EXPERT TRAVELED TO

VIETNAM ON A FACT-FINDING MISSION TO SUPPORT THE PROPOSAL TO

ESTABLISH NOIP BRANCH OFFICES IN THE PROVINCES.

-- SECO PLANS TO PROVIDE SPECIFIC IP-RELATED TRAINING FOR

-- SECO WILL SOON CONDUCT AN EXPERT MISSION TO VIETNAM TO

AGREEMENTS AND CONVENTIONS.

EUROPEAN UNION

--------------

11. THE EUROPEAN UNION PROVIDES TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE ON IPR

ON A REGIONAL BASIS THROUGH THE EC-ASEAN INTELLECTUAL

PROPERTY RIGHTS CO-OPERATION PROGRAMME (ECAP II). THE EU

HAS PUT TOGETHER A FRAMEWORK OF PROGRAM OBJECTIVES AND

ACTIVITIES FOR THE VIETNAM COMPONENT OF ECAP II, BUT

IMPLEMENTATION HAS NOT YET BEGUN. EU HEADQUARTERS IN

BRUSSELS HAS ASKED THE EU MISSION IN HANOI TO RE-EVALUATE,

AND POSSIBLY REDESIGN, THE PROJECT TO ENSURE PARITY WITH

HANOI HAS ASKED BRUSSELS TO RECONSIDER THAT DECISION SO THAT

THEY CAN SOON BEGIN IMPLEMENTATION OF THE PROJECT.

12. IF THE ECAP II PROGRAMS IN VIETNAM MOVE FORWARD AS

PLANNED, THE PRINCIPLE ACTIVITIES WILL INCLUDE:

-- APPRAISAL OF IP-RELATED LEGISLATION AND REGULATIONS;
