2. REF A\'S RE-DRAFT OF THE CROATIA HUMAN RIGHTS

REPORT CONSTITUTES, IN OUR VIEW, A MAJOR AND AS YET

UNSUBSTANTIATED REORIENTATION OF THE DRAFT REPORT WE

SUBMITTED IN REF B.

3. IN LIGHT OF ALL THAT HAS HAPPENED IN CROATIA IN

1992, IT MIGHT BE POSSIBLE BY PICKING AND CHOOSING TO

"DOCUMENT" VIRTUALLY ANY CASE ONE WERE INCLINED TO

MAKE REGARDING THE HUMAN RIGHTS PERFORMANCE OF THE

CROATIAN GOVERNMENT. OVERALL BALANCE WILL BE CRITICAL

TO THE CREDIBILITY OF THIS YEAR\'S REPORT. IN OUR

DRAFT WE SOUGHT CAREFULLY TO STRIKE A BALANCE WHICH

ACCURATELY REFLECTS THE HUMAN RIGHTS SITUATION IN

THIS COUNTRY OVER THE PAST YEAR.

4. IN PARAGRAPH 11 OF REF A WE ARE ADVISED TO MAKE A

"SPECIAL EFFORT" TO DOCUMENT HUMAN RIGHTS ABUSES BY

THE CROATS -- OTHERWISE THE STRONG U.S. INDICTMENT OF

SERBIAN ABUSES IN SERBIA AND BOSNIA-HERCEGOVINA WILL

SEEM LESS CREDIBLE. IT IS POSSIBLE THAT WE

MISUNDERSTAND THE INTENT OF THIS REQUEST, BUT, FOR

THE RECORD, WE WOULD BE EXTREMELY UNCOMFORTABLE

TAKING SUCH A CALCULATED APPROACH TO DESCRIBING

CROATIA\'S HUMAN RIGHTS PERFORMANCE. MORE PLAINLY

PUT, WE DON\'T THINK CROATIA\'S AND SERBIA\'S 1992 HUMAN

RIGHTS REPORTS SHOULD BE DRAFTED OR CONSIDERED "IN

TANDEM."

5. THIS FORMERLY EVACUATED MISSION HAD ALMOST NO

FILES FROM THE FIRST HALF OF 1992 AND EXPERIENCED AN

ALMOST TOTAL CHANGEOVER OF PERSONNEL LAST SUMMER.

YET WE FULLY ACKNOWLEDGED, AND WE INCLUDED IN OUR
