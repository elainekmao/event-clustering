SUBJECT: IMMIGRATION: EASTERN MEDITERRANEAN INTERIOR

1. NOT FOR INTERNET DISTRIBUTION.

2.(U) INTERIOR MINISTERS FOR THE "ADRIATIC-IONIAN" REGION

ANNOUNCED A NEW ACTION PLAN TO COMBAT CLANDESTINE

IMMIGRATION AND HUMAN TRAFFICKING AT THE END OF A ONE-DAY

MINISTERIAL IN LECCE NOVEMBER 13. THE ACTION PLAN WAS

SIGNED BY MINISTERS FROM ITALY, ALBANIA, BOSNIA, CROATIA,

THE FRY, GREECE AND SLOVENIA; THE MACEDONIAN AND BULGARIAN

MINISTERS DID NOT SIGN BUT ASSOCIATED THEMSELVES WITH THE

PLAN\'S INITIATIVES. REPRESENTATIVES FROM THE COMMISSION AND

EU PRESIDENT DENMARK ALSO ATTENDED. TURKEY WAS, IN THE VIEW

OF THE ITALIAN MEDIA, "NOTICEABLY ABSENT," BUT INTERIOR VICE-

MINISTER MANTOVANO COUNTERED BY POINTING TO THE RECENT

TURKISH ELECTIONS AND ABSENCE OF A NEW CABINET. MANTOVANO

UNDERSCORED THE GOOD COOPERATION ITALY HAD ENJOYED FROM

TURKEY ON CLANDESTINE IMMIGRATION UP TO DATE.

3.(U) UNDER THE "ALERT AND REACTION" PLAN, THE SIGNATORY

COUNTRIES COMMITTED TO:

-- CONDUCT JOINT BORDER AND MARITIME INTERDICTION PATROLS

(IN ACCORDANCE WITH RESPECTIVE NATIONAL LEGISLATION),

CONCENTRATING ON AREAS OF HIGH TRAFFIC;

-- EXCHANGE BORDER POLICE AND OTHER PERSONNEL AT PORTS AND

AIRPORTS HEAVILY USED BY CLANDESTINE IMMIGRANTS AS

EMBARKATION OR TRANSIT POINTS;

-- ESTABLISH PROCEDURES TO INTERDICT AND RETURN VESSELS

ENGAGED IN TRAFFICKING AND CLANDESTINE IMMIGRATION TO THEIR

PORTS OF ORIGIN

-- ADOPT MEASURES TO ACCELERATE IDENTIFICATION PROCEDURES

FOR UNDOCUMENTED ILLEGALS IN ORDER TO SPEED THEIR

REPATRIATION FROM DESTINATION COUNTRIES. ALTHOUGH THE

PROCEDURES TO BE EMPLOYED WERE NOT SPECIFIED, ITALIAN

INTERIOR MINISTER PISANU SUGGESTED FINGERPRINTING EXPELLEES,

A PROPOSAL THAT HAS GENERATED STRONG OPPOSITION FROM THE

ITALIAN LEFT.

-- PERIODICALLY ASSESS, VIA THE ADRIATIC-IONIAN INITIATIVE\'S

ROTATING PRESIDENCY, SIGNATORIES COMMITMENT TO IMPLEMENTING

THE VARIOUS MEASURES.

4.(U) THE MINISTERS ALSO PLEDGED TO PROMPTLY IMPLEMENT

EXISTING ANTI-TRAFFICKING AND LAW-ENFORCEMENT COOPERATION

ACCORDS. PERHAPS MORE IMPORTANTLY, THE MINISTERS AGREED TO

IMPLEMENT EXISTING READMISSION ACCORDS (UNDER WHICH

EMBARKATION COUNTRIES AGREE TO TAKE BACK SOME OR ALL OF

THEIR NATIONALS EXPELLED FOR ILLEGAL IMMIGRATION) AND, WHERE

CURRENTLY LACKING, TO NEGOTIATE NEW MEASURES WITH OTHER

SIGNATORIES AND "NEIGHBORING THIRD COUNTRIES." PISANU

POINTED TO ITALY\'S ACCORD WITH ALBANIA AS A MODEL, NOTING

THAT IN THE LAST THREE MONTHS "NOT ONE DINGHY, NOT ONE

PROSTITUTE" HAD ARRIVED IN ITALY FROM ALBANIA. INSTEAD,

ITALY NOW RECEIVES ONLY WORKERS OR BUSINESSMEN VISITING FOR

LEGITIMATE TRADE.

5.(U) THE ACTION PLAN MARKED A NEW AND MORE ADVANCED PHASE

OF COOPERATION AMONG THE COUNTRIES IN THE ADRIATIC-IONIAN

AREA, PISANU ASSERTED, ONE THAT SHOULD SWIFTLY IMPROVE

INTERDICTION OF CLANDESTINES EN ROUTE AND, FOR THOSE

SUBSEQUENTLY CAUGHT IN DESTINATION COUNTRIES, SPEED

6.(U) NOT EVERYONE AGREED WITH PISANU\'S ASSESSMENT. A

MODEST GROUP OF PROTESTORS (ESTIMATES RANGED FROM 3,000 AND

5,000) VIED FOR MEDIA ATTENTION BY ORGANIZING A

\'COUNTERSUMMIT\' TO PROTEST THE BERLUSCONI GOVERNMENT\'S

IMMIGRATION POLICY, CONCENTRATING ON THE RECENTLY-ENACTED

BOSSI-FINI LEGISLATION (REF A). "UNDER BOSSI-FINI, WE ARE

ALL CLANDESTINE IMMIGRANTS," ASSERTED ONE BANNER DISPLAYED

OUTSIDE THE MINISTERIAL VENUE.

7.(SBU) THE LECCE ACCORD EMPHASIZES THE BERLUSCONI

GOVERNMENT\'S PREFERRED TOOLS FOR COMBATTING ILLEGAL

IMMIGRATION: TOUGH INTERDICTION AND ENFORCEMENT MEASURES

COUPLED WITH A LEGAL IMMIGRATION SYSTEM CENTERED PRIMARILY

ON EMPLOYMENT. A JUST-CONCLUDED "REGULARIZATION," UNDER

WHICH EMPLOYERS WERE ENCOURAGED TO REGISTER THEIR ILLEGAL

WORKERS, MOVED SOME 700,000 ILLEGAL IMMIGRANTS (OR SOME

FORTY-SIX PERCENT OF THE ESTIMATED 1.5 MILLION ILLEGALS IN

ITALY) INTO LEGAL STATUS. ITALY HAS ALREADY CONCLUDED OVER

TWENTY READMISSION ACCORDS (INCLUDING WITH VIRTUALLY EVERY

COUNTRY REPRESENTED IN LECCE), OFFERING THESE COUNTRIES

HIGHER QUOTAS FOR LEGAL IMMIGRANTS AS A REWARD FOR THEIR

COOPERATION IN PREVENTING CLANDESTINE DEPARTURES AND IN

ACCEPTING REPATRIATED ILLEGALS. LABOR MINISTRY CONTACTS TELL

US THEY ARE DISCUSSING A SIMILAR ACCORD WITH THE

PHILIPPINES, SOURCE OF MANY DOMESTIC CARE-GIVERS. THEY ARE

8.(SBU) COMMENT: THE ACCORD SHOULD HELP THE BERLUSCONI

GOVERNMENT FRAME ILLEGAL IMMIGRATION AS ONE OF THE

CENTERPIECES OF ITS EU PRESIDENCY NEXT YEAR. ITALIAN

OFFICIALS HAVE LONG LAMENTED WHAT THEY PERCEIVE AS THE

RELUCTANCE OF NORTHERN MEMBER-STATES (FAR REMOVED, IN THEIR

EYES, FROM THE IMMEDIACY AND MAGNITUDE OF THE PHENOMENON) TO

SANCTION TOUGHER ENFORCEMENT AGAINST CLANDESTINE

IMMIGRATION. THIS ACCORD LAYS OUT, IN PLAIN VIEW FOR THE

WIDER EU TO SEE, THE ITALIANS\' PREFERRED TOOLS FOR

ADDRESSING THE PROBLEM. WE EXPECT TO SEE THE BERLUSCONI

GOVERNMENT REACHING INTO THE SAME TOOLBOX NEXT YEAR AS IT

ATTEMPTS TO PUSH THE EU TO ADOPT A TOUGHER, MORE

COMPREHENSIVE \'SOLUTION\' TO THE PHENOMENON.
