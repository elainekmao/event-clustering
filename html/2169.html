SUBJECT: VIETNAM RATES ASEAN MEETINGS "FULLY SUCCESSFUL"

REF: BANDAR SERI BEGAWAN 00716

1. (SBU) SUMMARY: VIETNAM WAS FULLY SATISFIED WITH THE

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 HANOI 02946 130325Z

RESULTS OF THE ASEAN SUMMIT, ASEAN+3, AND ASEAN+1 MEETINGS

IN BRUNEI NOVEMBER 5-6. MFA OFFICIALS CLAIMED THAT PM PHAN

VAN KHAI TOOK THE INITIATIVE IN GETTING THE STRONG

ENDORSEMENT OF EARLY RATIFICATION BY ALL MEMBERS OF THE 12

CONVENTIONS AGAINST TERRORISM; TERRORISM IN GENERAL WAS ONE

OF THE TWO MAJOR THEMES IDENTIFIED BY MFA AS DOMINATING THE

TALKS. THE OTHER KEY TOPIC FROM VIETNAM\'S PERSPECTIVE WAS

HOW TO MINIMIZE THE EFFECT ON ASEAN\'S FUTURE OF DIVERGENT

ECONOMIC LEVELS AND GROWTH AMONG THE "OLD" AND "NEW"

MEMBERS. VIETNAM RATED BRUNEI\'S ORGANIZATION AND LEADERSHIP

AS GOOD. END SUMMARY.

2. (SBU) MFA ASEAN DEPARTMENT DEPTUTY DIRECTOR-GENERAL

PHAM HUU CHI MET WITH POL/C AND POLOFFS ON NOVEMBER 7

FOLLOWING HIS RETURN FROM THE ASEAN MEETINGS IN BRUNEI. CHI

CONFIRMED THAT MUCH OF THE SUMMIT\'S DISCUSSIONS WERE

CENTERED ON INTERNATIONAL TERRORISM AND HOW TO COPE WITH IT.

CHI SAID THAT HE HAD BEEN IMPRESSED THAT ASEAN, AN

ORGANIZATION THAT HAD HISTORICALLY BEEN FAIRLY WED TO A PRE-

DETERMINED AGENDA PLANNED MONTHS IN ADVANCE, HAD BEEN ABLE

SO QUICKLY TO INCORPORATE A FOCUS ON THIS TOPIC AFTER THE

EVENTS OF SEPTEMBER 11. HE CLAIMED THAT PM KHAI MADE A PUSH

TO URGE ALL MEMBERS TO RATIFY ALL 12 CONVENTIONS AGAINST

TERRORISM AS SOON AS POSSIBLE, AND WAS ALSO INSTRUMENTAL IN

PUTTING FORWARD THE SUCCESSFUL PROPOSAL THAT ASEAN CONVENE

MINISTERIAL CONFERENCES AT THE FOREIGN MINISTER AND INTERIOR

MINISTER LEVELS, PROBABLY IN FEBRUARY AND APRIL

RESPECTIVELY, TO DISCUSS THE CURRENT THREAT AND CONCRETE

MEASURES TO COUNTER TERRORISM. HE ADDED THAT PM KHAI ALSO

PROPOSED CREATION OF AN ASEAN COORDINATION CENTER IN ORDER

TO BE ABLE TO IMPROVE LIAISON WITH EACH OTHER AND WITH OTHER

UNCLASSIFIED

LAW ENFORCEMENT AGENCIES SUCH AS INTERPOL. EVENTUALLY,

MEMBER STATES MIGHT ASSIGN A LIAISON OFFICER TO THEIR

OVERSEAS EMBASSIES IN EACH OTHERS\' COUNTRIES, HE DESCRIBED.

3. (SBU) CHI CLAIMED THAT THE LEADERS DID NOT/NOT DISCUSS

THE ISSUE OF COALITION MILITARY ACTION IN AFGHANISTAN, AND

THAT NONE OF THE LEADERS HAD ADVOCATED A STATEMENT OF

SUPPORT FOR ONGOING ACTIONS BY THE US AND THE INTERNATIONAL

COALITION. HE SAID THAT THE LEADERS FOCUSED ON COUNTER-

MEASURES AGAINST TERRORISM IN THE REGION INSTEAD.

4. (SBU) ACCORDING TO CHI, LEADERS DISCUSSED CONCERNS ABOUT

ASEAN\'S FUTURE GIVEN DEVELOPMENTAL GAPS BETWEEN THE "OLD"

AND "NEW" MEMBERS. CHI STATED THAT ASEAN COULD NOT "MOVE

FORWARD IF NEIGHBORS ARE TOO POOR." HE CITED THE DISPARITY

BETWEEN THAILAND AND MYANMAR AS A CASE IN POINT. CHI NOTED

PARTICULAR GAPS WITHIN ASEAN REGARDING INFRASTRUCTURE

(INCLUDING INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY) AND HUMAN RESOURCES

DEVELOPMENT. HE SAID THE LEADERS HAD ALSO DISCUSSED THE

NEED TO PROMOTE INTRA-ASIAN TRADE ESPECIALLY GIVEN THE

GLOBAL ECONOMIC SLOWDOWN. HE POINTED TO CAMBODIAN PRIME

MINISTER HUN SEN\'S PROPOSAL TO CREATE ANOTHER GROWTH

TRIANGLE FOR THE MEKONG REGION AS AN EXAMPLE. LEADERS ALSO

DISCUSSED THE NEED TO COMPLETE SINGAPORE-KUNMING RAIL LINKS

(VIA CAMBODIA AND VIETNAM).

5. (U) CHI NOTED VIETNAM\'S SATISFACTION WITH THE PROPOSAL

TO BRING CHINA INTO THE ASEAN FREE TRADE AREA BUT NOTED THAT

IT COULD TAKE UP TO TEN YEARS TO ACCOMPLISH. MINISTRIES OF

TRADE WILL BE EXPLORING THE MODALITIES OF THIS PROPOSAL, HE

SAID.

6. (SBU) CHI EXPLAINED THAT LEADERS HAD DISCUSSED, WITHOUT

CONCLUSION, THE IDEA OF AN ASEAN SUMMIT WITH INDIA, ON AN

ANNUAL OR BIANNUAL BASIS. HE SAID THAT SOUTH KOREA, WITH

SUPPORT FROM JAPAN AND MALAYSIA, HAD SOUGHT TO STRENGTHEN

THE ASEAN+3 PROCESS (MUCH ALONG THE LINES OF THE EARLIER

EAEC CONCEPT), WHILE SINGAPORE REMAINED OPPOSED TO SUCH

ACTION. HE CLAIMED THAT THE ONLY DISCUSSION OF THE

SPRATLEYS DURING THE ASEAN+3 MEETING CAME WHEN CHINA AND THE

PHILIPPINES SEPARATELY EXPRESSED HOPES FOR THE CONCLUSION OF

A CODE OF CONDUCT FOR THE REGION. CHI ALSO SAID THAT IN

ORDER TO EXPAND ASEAN\'S EXTERNAL RELATIONS FURTHER, THE

"MYANMAR PROBLEM" WILL HAVE TO BE RESOLVED.

8. (SBU) CHI RATED THE MEETINGS AS "FULLY SUCCESSFUL" AND

NOTED THAT VIETNAM WAS "FULLY SATISFIED." HE GAVE GOOD

MARKS TO BRUNEI\'S ORGANIZATION AND LEADERSHIP. THE ONLY

GLITCH CAME DURING THE LAO PRIME MINISTER\'S REMARKS, MOST OF

WHICH FOR SOME REASON WERE NOT TRANSLATED SO THE OTHER

LEADERS COULD NOT UNDERSTAND HIM.
