SUBJECT: MEPI ELECTION ASSISTANCE FOR JORDAN

REF: ZIADEH/SILLIMAN EMAIL (3-26); AMMAN 01650

1. (SBU) IN THE COURSE OF A COURTESY CALL BY THE NEW USAID

DIRECTOR AND THE AMBASSADOR ON MARCH 27, THE AMBASSADOR

DISCUSSED WITH PLANNING MINISTER BASSAM AWADALLAH THE

PROPOSED $400,000 MIDDLE EAST PARTNERSHIP INITIATIVE (MEPI)

ELECTION ASSISTANCE. THE PROJECT IS TO BE CARRIED OUT IN

COOPERATION WITH THE CONSORTIUM FOR ELECTORAL AND POLITICAL

PROCESS STRENGTHENING (CEPPS). THE MINISTER, WHOSE SUPPORT

IS ESSENTIAL FOR THIS PROJECT BECAUSE OF ITS POTENTIAL

POLITICAL SENSITIVITY IN THIS DIFFICULT PERIOD, AGREED WITH

THE PROPOSAL IN PRINCIPLE, BUT EMPHASIZED THE IMPORTANCE OF

CONSULTING CLOSELY WITH THE GOJ ON ITS IMPLEMENTATION.

2. (SBU) THE AMBASSADOR AND THE MINISTER AGREED THAT THEY

WOULD MEMORIALIZE THEIR AGREEMENT BY COUNTER SIGNED

IMPLEMENTATION LETTER EARLY NEXT WEEK TO ENSURE THAT THE

MISSION AND THE PLANNING MINISTRY ARE KEPT FULLY APPRISED OF

THE DESIGN AND IMPLEMENTATION OF THE ELECTION PROJECT. THE

PROJECT WILL CONSIST OF TRAINING FOR CANDIDATES AND CAMPAIGN

MANAGERS, PARTICULARLY FOR FEMALE CANDIDATES, AS WELL AS THE

STRENGTHENING OF POLITICAL PARTIES AND NGO\'S INVOLVED IN

ADVOCATING ISSUES AND OTHER ASPECTS OF THE ELECTION PROCESS.

3. (SBU) THE MISSION LOOKS FORWARD TO WORKING CLOSELY WITH

NEA AND CEPPS, THE GRANTEE, IN THE DESIGN AND IMPLEMENTATION

OF THIS PROJECT. WE SUGGEST THAT IF THE $400,000 IS

APPROVED, THE NEXT STEP WOULD BE FOR PUBLIC AFFAIRS AMMAN,

IN COORDINATION WITH THE MISSION\'S INTERAGENCY MEPI

COMMITTEE, TO WORK WITH THE GRANTEE CEPPS TO DRAW UP A

WORKPLAN LEADING UP TO THE JUNE ELECTIONS.

2. WE LOOK FORWARD TO WORKING WITH NEA ON THIS IMPORTANT

FORM OF SUPPORT FOR JORDAN\'S ELECTIONS, POSSIBLY THE FIRST

MAJOR ELECTIONS IN THE REGION AFTER THE LIBERATION OF IRAQ

AND AN IMPORTANT BENCHMARK FOR THE MEPI INITIATIVE.
