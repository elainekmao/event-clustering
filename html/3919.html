SUBJECT: GOI ON TURKEY\'S EU CANDIDACY

REF: STATE 188078

CLASSIFIED BY: POL MC TOM COUNTRYMAN. REASON:1.5 (B)(C).

1. (C) ON OCTOBER 4 THE MFA\'S DIRECTOR GENERAL FOR EUROPEAN

AFFAIRS PAOLO PUCCI TOLD POL MINISTER COUNSELOR THAT WHILE

ITALY RECOGNIZED THE REFORMS PUT IN PLACE RECENTLY BY THE

TURKISH LEGISLATURE, IT WOULD HAVE LIKED TO SEE MORE

PROGRESS, ESPECIALLY IN THE AREA OF RELIGIOUS FREEDOM. IN

RESPONSE TO USG VIEW (REFTEL), HE SAID THAT THE GOI IS

ENCOURAGED BY WHAT IT SEES AS PROGRESS ON GREEK-TURKISH

RELATIONS. HE SAID THAT THE GOI WOULD DO WHAT IT COULD TO

PROMOTE TURKEY\'S EU CANDIDACY, BUT THAT THE REALITY WAS THAT

THERE WERE OTHERS IN THE EUROPEAN COMMUNITY WHO WERE LESS

ENTHUSIASTIC ABOUT TURKEY\'S CANDIDACY.

2. (C) PUCCI\'S VIEW IS THAT THE BEST TURKEY COULD HOPE FOR

PRIOR TO THE COPENHAGEN SUMMIT WOULD BE A STATEMENT

RECOGNIZING PROGRESS TO DATE AND URGING THE GOT TO IMPLEMENT

THE REFORMS LEGISLATED. THIS APPROACH WOULD BE MOST

EFFECTIVE, CONTINUED PUCCI, WITHOUT THE PRESSURE OF A

DEADLINE TO MEET EU STANDARDS.

3. (C) POL-MC HAD EARLIER DELIVERED THE SAME POINTS ON TURKEY

TO PM (AND ACTING FM) BERLUSCONI\'S DEPUTY DIPLOMATIC ADVISER,

CESARE RAGAGLINI, WHO REPLIED THAT THE GOI APPRECIATED THE US

ARGUMENTS MORE THAN MOST IN THE EU AND WANTED TO PROMOTE

TURKEY\'S CANDIDACY. HE NOTED THAT GERMANY MAY BE A BIGGER

OBSTACLE THAN GREECE IN MOVING FORWARD.
