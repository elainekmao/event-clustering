SUBJECT: GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE ENCOURAGED BY LUSAKA

SUMMIT

REFTEL: LUSAKA 724

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 00762 161016Z

REASONS: 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE IS VERY ENCOURAGED BY

THE RESULTS OF THE SUMMIT HELD AMONG THE LUSAKA

SIGNATORIES IN LUSAKA ON FEBRUARY 15 (REFTEL). BERNARD

TAKAVARASHA, THE UNDERSECRETARY FOR AFRICA IN ZIMBABWE\'S

MFA, TOLD POLOFF FEBRUARY 16 THAT THE SUMMIT OUTCOME WAS

"VERY POSITIVE" AND "BETTER THAN EXPECTED." TAKAVARASHA

STATED THE MOST IMPORTANT DEVELOPMENT WAS THAT THE

"PEOPLE OF THE CONGO" (GOVERNMENT AND REBEL FACTIONS)

HAD AGREED TO START THE INTER-CONGOLESE DIALOGUE.

ZIMBABWE VIEWS THE DIALOGUE AS CRITICAL TO ANY

MEANINGFUL PEACE SETTLEMENT IN DRC, THE MFA OFFICIAL

NOTED. ONCE STARTED, THE DIALOGUE COULD SIDELINE THE

EXTERNAL STATE ACTORS, AND RIGHTFULLY SO, TAKAVARASHA

STATED.

2. (C) WHEN ASKED ABOUT THE GOVERNMENT OF RWANDA\'S

FAILURE TO SEND A REPRESENTATIVE TO THE SUMMIT, THE

UNDERSECRETARY SAID IT WAS REGRETTABLE, BUT HE DID NOT

DWELL ON IT. HE NOTED THAT PRESIDENT MUSEVENI DID NOT

ATTEND EITHER, BUT THAT HE HAD SENT REPRESENTATIVES WHO

OFFERED APOLOGIES FOR THE UGANDAN PRESIDENT\'S ABSENCE.

POLOFF ASKED TAKAVARASHA TO COMMENT ON PRESIDENT

MUGABE\'S STATEMENT THAT ZIMBABWE WOULD WITHDRAW ITS

FORCES ONLY AFTER UGANDA AND RWANDA DID, TO WHICH HE

RESPONDED THAT THAT HAS BEEN ZIMBABWE\'S POSITION ALL

ALONG. THE MFA OFFICIAL JOKINGLY REMARKED THAT ZIMBABWE

IS TOO POOR TO ESTABLISH PERMANENT BASES IN THE DRC AS

THE MUCH WEALTHIER U.S. HAS DONE AROUND THE WORLD.

3. (C) COMMENT: THE ZIMBABWEAN MFA OFFICIAL SEEMED

GENUINELY PLEASED WITH THE RESULTS OF THE LUSAKA SUMMIT,

ALMOST TO THE POINT OF GIDDINESS, WHICH IS NOT AN MFA

TRAIT. THE MFA\'S COMMENTS UNDERSCORE ZIMBABWE\'S DESIRE

FOR THE RAPID IMPLEMENTATION OF PEACE IN THE DRC SO THAT

IT CAN BRING HOME AT LEAST SOME OF ITS TROOPS BEFORE

NEXT YEAR\'S PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION. END COMMENT.
