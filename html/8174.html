SUBJECT: MEDIA REACTION ON MIDDLE EAST

Summary

--Lead story in all papers today, May 20, focuses on

the series of suicide bombings that took place

yesterday in Israel. Front-page stories continue to

focus on preparations for the Jordanian parliamentary

elections.

Editorial Commentary

-- "Who is responsible for the bloodshed?"

Center-left, influential Arabic daily Al-Dustour

(05/20) editorializes: "No one has to look very hard

for the real cause for this recent escalation of

violence on both the Palestinian and the Israeli

fronts. It is Sharon and his extremist right-wing

government. They have closed off the road for peace

and destroyed the accords and they continue their war

of settlements, colonization and invasions.. It is

truly sad that the world stands watching the Israeli

arrogance. The Israeli government commits crimes on

daily basis against the Palestinians without any

condemnation or denunciation.. We call on the United

States and the international community to intervene

strongly in order to salvage the peace plan and to put

an end to the suffering of the Palestinian people. We

also look to the people in Palestine to pull in their

ranks, to put their differences aside, and to

formulate a unified strategy that would enable the

people to confront the challenges leading to their

freedom and independence."

-- "The terrorism that serves the enemies"

Columnist Yaqoub Jaber writes on the op-ed page of

center-left, influential Arabic daily Al-Dustour

(05/20): "It is probably too early to say that Al-

Qa\'eda organization was responsible for the bombings

in Riyadh and Casablanca. Yet, we can safely say that

such bombings against targets related to the foreign

and American presence marks a reaction against the war

that the United States launched against Iraq..

According to these groups, terrorism is the only means

of revenge, since armies have failed to protect

national independence. But they do not realize that

their push for terrorist actions out of revenge is

actually harming their own people and their own

countries and the image of Arabs and Muslims in the

world. It is our duty to warn that terrorists may

take advantage of the fall of Baghdad to spread death

and destruction and to continue to defame Arabs and

Muslims. We would not blame the governments if they

deal harshly with those who chose terrorism as a means

of self-expression, a means that serves only the

objectives of the enemies and the adversaries."
