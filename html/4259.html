SUBJECT: PUERTO RICO\'S ASSOCIATE MEMBERSHIP WITH FAO

1. SUMMARY: THE UN FOOD AND AGRICULTURE ORGANIZATION

(FAO) HAS INFORMED FODAG THAT PUERTO RICO SEEKS TO REGAIN

ASSOCIATE MEMBER STATUS WITH THE FAO (THIS STATUS HAVING

LAPSED DUE TO PUERTO RICO\'S INABILITY TO MEET ITS

FINANCIAL OBLIGATIONS.) ACCORDING TO THE FAO STATUTES,

ASSOCIATE MEMBER STATUS MUST BE REQUESTED BY THE MEMBER

NATION RESPONSIBLE FOR THE TERRITORY\'S INTERNATIONAL

RELATIONS. THE MEMBER NATION ALSO UNDERTAKES TO ENSURE

THAT THE ASSOCIATE MEMBER COMPLIES WITH ITS OBLIGATIONS

(INCLUDING FINANCIAL OBLIGATIONS). THIS CABLE PROVIDES

BACKGROUND ON PUERTO RICO\'S ASSOCIATE MEMBERSHIP. END

SUMMARY.

2. THE FAO CONSTITUTION (ARTICLE II.11) ALLOWS FOR

ASSOCIATE MEMBERSHIP FOR TERRITORIES THAT ARE NOT

RESPONSIBLE FOR THE CONDUCT OF THEIR INTERNATIONAL

RELATIONS. THE MEMBER NATION HAVING AUTHORITY FOR THE

TERRITORY\'S INTERNATIONAL RELATIONS APPLIES ON THE

TERRITORY\'S BEHALF AND ASSUMES CERTAIN OBLIGATIONS (THE

MOST IMPORTANT BEING ASSUMING RESPONSIBILITY FOR ENSURING

THAT THE ASSOCIATE MEMBER FULFILLS ITS FINANCIAL

OBLIGATIONS.)

3. THE UNITED STATES (JOHN BOLTON, ASSISTANT SECRETARY

OF STATE FOR INTERNATIONAL ORGANIZATION AFFAIRS) APPLIED

ON BEHALF OF PUERTO RICO OCTOBER 9, 1991 AND ACCEPTED THE

OBLIGATIONS OF FAO\'S CONSTITUTION ON PUERTO RICO\'S

BEHALF. IN 1993 PUERTO RICO REPORTED TO THE FAO THAT IT

COULD NOT MEET ITS FINANCIAL OBLIGATIONS AND THAT IT

WISHED TO WITHDRAW FROM THE FAO.

4. SINCE MEMBERS (INCLUDING ASSOCIATE MEMBERS) MUST WAIT

FOUR YEARS AFTER JOINING TO WITHDRAW, AND SINCE THE

WITHDRAWAL COULD ONLY BE MADE BY THE UNITED STATES (ON

PUERTO RICO\'S BEHALF), PUERTO RICO DID NOT EFFECTIVELY

GIVE NOTICE TO WITHDRAW UNTIL DECEMBER 20, 1995. AS THE

FAO CONSTITUTION STIPULATES THAT WITHDRAWAL TAKES EFFECT

ONE YEAR AFTER NOTICE HAS BEEN GIVEN (WITH FINANCIAL

OBLIGATIONS CONTINUING UP TO THAT TIME), PUERTO RICO OWED

USD 75,424.80 IN ARREARS BY THE TIME ITS WITHDRAWAL

BECAME EFFECTIVE.

5. CONCERNING THE U.S. OBLIGATION TO ENSURE THAT THE

ASSOCIATE MEMBER MEETS ITS FINANCIAL OBLIGATIONS, THE

1995 U.S. WITHDRAWAL NOTICE (FROM THE ACTING ASSISTANT

SECRETARY OF STATE FOR INTERNATIONAL ORGANIZATION AFFAIRS

SIPDIS

GEORGE WARD) STATED THAT THE U.S. WAS "INFORMED" OF THE

OUTSTANDING OBLIGATIONS AND THAT THE UNITED STATES HAD

"CONVEYED THIS INFORMATION TO THE GOVERNMENT OF PUERTO

RICO WHICH HAS CONFIRMED THAT IT ACCEPTS THE OBLIGATIONS

AND INTENDS TO MAKE PAYMENT BEFORE DECEMBER 31, 1996."

6. THE SECRETARY OF STATE OF PUERTO RICO HAS WRITTEN TO

THE FAO REQUESTING TO BE RE-ADMITTED AS AN ASSOCIATE

MEMBER. THE FAO HAS RESPONDED THAT THIS NEEDS TO BE

HANDLED BY THE MEMBER NATION (THE U.S.). IN THIS

CONTEXT, PUERTO RICO HAS BEEN ADVISED AGAIN OF ITS

OUTSTANDING ARREARS WHICH WOULD NEED TO BE PAID OFF

BEFORE BECOMING A MEMBER AGAIN.

7. 1991 AND 1995 LETTER OF ACCESSION AND WITHDRAWAL AND

OTHER SUPPORTING DOCUMENTATION HAVE BEEN FAXED TO IO/EDA

AND USDA/FAS. FYI, THERE ARE NO OTHER ASSOCIATE MEMBERS

AT THE FAO.

8. COMMENT: THIS INFORMATION IS PROVIDED FOR BACKGROUND

ONLY, SINCE, TO OUR KNOWLEDGE, PUERTO RICO HAS NOT

APPROACHED THE SECRETARY OF STATE REQUESTING THAT THE

UNITED STATES APPLY FOR ASSOCIATE MEMBER STATUS FOR
