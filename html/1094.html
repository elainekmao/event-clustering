SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE\'S ARREARS TO THE WORLD BANK

CLASSIFIED BY ECON/COMM OFFICER MARK PROKOP, REASONS

1.5 (B), (D)

REF: HARARE 2817

1. (C) ECON OFFICER RECENTLY LEARNED FROM THE WORLD

BANK\'S RESIDENT REPRESENTATIVE THAT ZIMBABWE\'S

ARREARS TO THE IBRD AS OF MAY 29 TOTALED

APPROXIMATELY U.S.$18 MILLION. INSTALLMENT PAYMENTS

FROM ZIMBABWE TO THE BANK ARE DUE FORTNIGHTLY, AND

ARE USUALLY IN THE U.S. $4 TO $5 MILLION RANGE,

THOUGH SOME PAYMENTS CLIMB TO $8 OR $9 MILLION,

DEPENDING ON THE REPAYMENT SCHEDULES OF PREVIOUS

LOANS. ANOTHER PAYMENT WAS DUE ON MAY 30, WHICH

WOULD BRING THE NOW 75 DAYS IN ARREARS TOTAL TO

APPROXIMATELY $23 MILLION.

2. (C) DUE TO THE PLEDGING OF A LARGE SHARE OF THE

TOBACCO SALES EXPORT RECEIPTS FOR FUEL AND ENERGY

PAST-DUE BILLS (REFTEL), THE HARD CURRENCY SHORTAGE

PERSISTS AT NEAR CRISIS LEVELS AND IT IS NOT EXPECTED

TO EASE ANYTIME SOON. THEREFORE, WE ANTICIPATE THAT

THE GOZ WILL HAVE DIFFICULTY MAKING THESE PAYMENTS,

THE MORE SO AS THE BALANCE INCREASES. ACCORDING TO

THE WORLD BANK RESREP, GOVERNMENT OFFICIALS SEEM

RELATIVELY NONPLUSSED ABOUT THE ARREARAGE, WITH THE

RESERVE BANK GOVERNOR STATING THAT THOUGH HE IS DOING

HIS BEST, EVEN HE CANNOT GET BLOOD FROM A TURNIP.

3. (C) WHEN ASKED WHAT THE OUTLOOK WAS FOR

RESUMPTION OF LENDING AND FURTHER FINANCIAL SUPPORT,

THE RESIDENT REPRESENTATIVE STATED THAT AFTER THE

ELECTIONS DIALOGUE WOULD HAVE TO START AFRESH WITH A

CABINET WITH NEW MEMBERS, AND A DETERMINATION MADE

THEN ON WHETHER OR NOT THE TWO BRETTON WOODS

INSTITUTIONS WOULD EVEN WANT TO REENTER THE MARKET

HERE. "A LOT OF BAD THINGS CAN HAPPEN BETWEEN NOW

AND THEN, MATE" HE CONFIDED (THE RESREP IS AN

AUSTRALIAN), "AND THE NEW GOVERNMENT CAN CONTINUE TO

BE NON-COOPERATIVE." HE CONTINUED THAT AS A

NECESSARY FIRST STEP THE IMF WOULD HAVE TO GET GOZ

AGREEMENT TO START A SHADOW PROGRAM, THAT WOULD NEED

TO LAST AT A MINIMUM FOUR OR FIVE MONTHS. TO SAVE

TIME, THIS SAME PERIOD COULD BE USED TO WORK OUT THE

TERMS AND PERFORMANCE CRITERIA OF A NEW IMF FACILITY.

4. (C) ONCE THE IMF GIVES THE GREEN LIGHT, THEN THE

ALREADY-APPROVED AFRICAN DEVELOPMENT BANK FUNDS COULD

BE RELEASED (APPROXIMATELY U.S. $45 MILLION), HE

EXPLAINED, AND AT THE SAME TIME THE WORLD BANK AND

THE EU COULD RESUME DISCUSSIONS ON THE PATH FORWARD.

HOWEVER, HE POINTED OUT, IF THE ARREARS SITUATION

CONTINUES OR WORSENS, THEIR HANDS WILL BE TIED. THE

WORLD BANK PLANS TO HOST A QUARTERLY ALL-DAY DONORS

DISCUSSION ON MAY 31, AT WHICH THE FOREGOING TOPICS

AND A HOST OF OTHER ISSUES WILL BE DISCUSSED IN

DEPTH. THE MISSION WILL BE REPRESENTED AT THAT

GATHERING BY THE USAID DIRECTOR.

MCDONALD
