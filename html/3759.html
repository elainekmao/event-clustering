SUBJECT: FEAR AND LOATHING AT MINISTRY OF DEFENSE

CONFIDENTIAL

REF: A. HANOI 2272 B. HANOI 1189

1. (U) CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR RAYMOND BURGHARDT,

REASON: 1.5 (D)

2. (C) IN A DISCUSSION WITH AMBASSADOR AND POL/C ON

SEPTEMBER 18, COLONEL TRAN NHUNG (PROTECT), DIRECTOR OF

THE INTERNATIONAL DEPARTMENT OF QUAN DOI NHAN DAN (THE

"PEOPLE\'S ARMY" NEWSPAPER), CONFIRMED REF A SPECULATION

THAT MINISTER OF NATIONAL DEFENSE GENERAL TRA WOULD NOT

TRAVEL TO THE U.S. IN 2002. HE CLAIMED THAT THE

MILITARY LEADERSHIP HAD DECIDED THAT GENERAL TRA WAS

"TOO BUSY" TO GO THIS YEAR, ESPECIALLY GIVEN ONGOING

"PROBLEMS" IN THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS. WHEN ASKED

WHETHER THE MINISTRY OF PUBLIC SECURITY DID NOT PLAY

THE PREDOMINANT SECURITY ROLE IN THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS,

COLONEL NHUNG INSISTED THAT MND RETAINED A "VERY

IMPORTANT ROLE" IN THAT TROUBLED REGION. COLONEL NHUNG

ADDED, HOWEVER, THAT MINISTER TRA WOULD PROBABLY

NONETHELESS VISIT THE U.S. SOMETIME IN 2003. HE NOTED

THAT MINISTER TRA WAS ONLY EXPECTED TO REMAIN IN HIS

POSITION FOR TWO YEARS, DESPITE HIS RE-ELECTION AT THE

RECENT 11TH NATIONAL ASSEMBLY INAUGURAL MEETING. TRA

AND PRIME MINISTER PHAN VAN KHAI WOULD BOTH RETIRE IN

2004, HE PREDICTED. (NOTE: MANY OTHER INFORMED

OBSERVERS HAVE MADE THE SAME ASSESSMENT. END NOTE)

3. (C) COLONEL NHUNG DESCRIBED ONE OF THE MAJOR

PROBLEMS IN US-VIETNAM RELATIONS AS "FEAR" OF THE U.S.

BY SENIOR MND OFFICIALS. HE CLAIMED THAT "WHENEVER YOU

SEE A POSITIVE EDITORIAL ON THE U.S., THAT

REFLECTS MY VIEWS; WHENEVER IT IS NEGATIVE, THAT COMES

FROM THE MINISTRY\'S LEADERSHIP." HE ADMITTED THAT THE

MND WOULD NEVER ALLOW PUBLICATION IN QDND OF AN OP ED

PIECE WRITTEN BY THE AMBASSADOR. (NOTE: QDND EDITOR-

IN-CHIEF TOLD AMBASSADOR MAY 9 THAT HIS NEWSPAPER WOULD

WELCOME PAS-PRODUCED MATERIALS, AS LONG AS THEY FIT

GENERAL EDITORIAL POLICY -- REF B.) ONE MAJOR REASON

FOR ANTI-U.S. SENTIMENT WAS CONCERN ABOUT THE

DIMINISHING ROLE OF THE MND, HE SAID. HE CLAIMED THAT

MND LEADERS BELIEVED THAT CONTINUED ECONOMIC

DEVELOPMENT -- AND CORRESPONDINGLY HIGHER LEVELS OF

TRADE AND INVESTMENT WITH THE U.S. -- WOULD FURTHER

WEAKEN MND\'S INFLUENCE WITHIN THE GVN. HE DISMISSED AS

IRRELEVANT COMPARISONS WITH THE PRC, WHERE DEFENSE

OFFICIALS HAD OVER THE PAST DECADE PARLAYED GREATER

LEVELS OF NATIONAL PROSPERITY INTO HIGHER BUDGETS FOR

NATIONAL DEFENSE AND ACQUISITION OF MORE SOPHISTICATED

WEAPONS SYSTEMS.

4. (C) COMMENT: AN OFTEN FRANK INTERLOCUTOR OUTSIDE

THE EARSHOT OF HIS COLLEAGUES, COLONEL NHUNG

UNWITTINGLY ECHOED VICE FOREIGN MINISTER LE VAN BANG\'S

REMARKS TO AMBASSADOR (REF A) THAT BOTH SIDES NEED TO

MAKE SURE THEIR RESPECTIVE MILITARIES WERE ON BOARD AS

DIPLOMATS SOUGHT FURTHER TO IMPROVE BILATERAL TIES. IT

IS NO SURPRISE THAT SUSPICION AND CONSERVATISM REMAIN

THE HALLMARKS OF MANY SENIOR MND OFFICIALS, DESPITE

WHAT HAS BECOME ROUTINELY GOOD COOPERATION WITH THE US

MILITARY ON THE SEARCH FOR MISSING-IN-ACTION, MEDICAL

RESEARCH, AND DEMINING. INTERESTINGLY, COLONEL NHUNG

DID NOT CITE WAR MEMORIES AS ONE OF THE FACTORS IN MND

NEGATIVISM VIS-.-VIS THE U.S., BUT THIS LIKELY REMAINS
