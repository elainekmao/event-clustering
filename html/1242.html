1. (C) SUMMARY: THE AUGUST 2 ZIMBABWE/SOUTH AFRICA REGULAR

BIANNUAL SUMMIT FOCUSED LARGELY ON BILATERAL ECONOMIC

ISSUES, MBEKI\'S AFRICAN RENAISSANCE PLAN AND ISSUES OF LAND AND

LAWLESSNESS. THE ZIMBABWEAN PRESIDENT EXPLAINED THAT THE

WAR VETERANS\' ACTIONS CONSTITUTED A DEMONSTRATION, BUT

OCCUPIED FARMS WILL NOT AUTOMATICALLY BE ALLOCATED TO THEM.

INSTEAD THE GOZ HAS STRUCTURES AND A PLAN, ACCORDING TO

PRESIDENT MUGABE, AIMED AT ENSURING THAT THE LAND ACQUISITION

PROCESS IS AS DIVERSE AND EQUITABLE AS POSSIBLE. IN THE NEAR

FUTURE THE WAR VETS WILL BE REMOVED FROM FARMS THAT ARE NOT

LISTED, MUGABE TOLD HIS SOUTH AFRICAN COUNTERPART. HE

APOLOGIZED TO MBEKI FOR THE LOSS OF LIFE DURING THE PRE-ELECTION

PERIOD ON THE FARMS, NOTING THAT BOTH SIDES SHARED BLAME FOR

IT. THE SOUTH AFRICANS SEEMED PLEASED WITH THEIR MEETINGS HERE,

AND CONSCIOUS OF NOT APPEARING TO BE THE REGIONAL HEAVY. THEY

TOOK THE ZIMBABWEANS EXPLANATIONS AND RATIONALIZATIONS FOR

THE LAWLESSNESS, AT LEAST PUBLICLY, AT FACE VALUE. END

SUMMARY

2. (C) ON AUGUST 3 THE CHARGE CALLED ON VICTOR MDITSHWA, SOUTH

AFRICAN DEPUTY HIGH COMMISSIONER, TO GET A READOUT ON THE VISIT

ON AUGUST 2 OF A SENIOR GOVERNMENT DELEGATION FROM SOUTH

AFRICA, LED BY PRESIDENT MBEKI. THE GROUP INCLUDED THE

MINISTERS OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS, FINANCE, TRADE, AGRICULTURE, AND

MINERALS AND ENERGY, AS WELL AS THE GOVERNOR GENERAL OF THE

SOUTH AFRICAN RESERVE BANK, THE DIRECTOR GENERAL OF THE

MINISTRY OF FINANCE, THE DEPUTY DIRECTOR GENERAL OF THE

MINISTRY OF TRADE, AND DIRECTOR GENERAL OF THE PRESIDENT\'S

OFFICE CHIKANE. THE ZIMBABWEAN AND SOUTH AFRICAN HEADS OF

3. (C) WE WERE TOLD THAT THE TWO LEADERS HAD OVER A TWO-HOUR

ONE-ON-ONE DISCUSSION COVERING A RANGE OF TOPICS, ALTHOUGH

NONE IN GREAT DEPTH. TOPICS INCLUDED THE FUEL CRISIS, THE

ELECTRICITY BILL AND THE GENERAL STATUS OF THE ECONOMY

PARTICULARLY THE MILLENIUM ECONOMIC RECOVERY PROGRAM. THEY

REFLECTED ON REGIONAL, CONTINENTAL AND GLOBAL ISSUES. MBEKI

VIGOROUSLY FLOGGED HIS AFRICAN RECOVERY AND DEVELOPMENT

PLAN, STATING THAT ZIMBABWE AND THE SADC COUNTRIES WERE ALL

STRATEGIC PARTNERS IN ITS EXECUTION. THE MINISTERS ALSO MET

THEIR COUNTERPARTS IN SIMILARLY RESTRICTED CIRCUMSTANCES. THE

TALKS WERE A PRELIMINARY ENCOUNTER AND THE MINISTERIAL TEAMS

(TO WHICH THE TWO GOVERNMENTS ARE REFERRING AS THE STANDING

OMMITTEE) WOULD BE LOOKING TO MEET AGAIN SOON AFTE THIS

COMING WEEKEND\'S WINDHOEK SUMMIT PROVIDINGTHE CHANCE TO RE-

ENGAGE IN MORE DETAIL.

4. C) THE SOUTH AFRICANS\' THEME HAD BEEN THAT THE TWOCOUNTRIES

MUST EITHER SWIM TOGETHER IN THE REGIONOR SINK TOGETHER. THEY

WERE STRUCK BY A MORE FLEIBLE AND POSITIVE APPROACH BY THE

POST-ELECTION ANU-PF GOVERNMENT. ACCORDING TO MDITSHWA

THERE PPEARED TO BE A WILLINGNESS (AT LAST) TO FACE UP T

MUGABE INTIMATED THAT THE WAR VETS WERE A TOOL TO APPLY
