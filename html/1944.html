SUBJECT: CONTINUED PRESSURE ON REFORM ACTIVISTS

REF: HANOI 02197

1. (U) CLASSIFIED BY A/DCM DAVID MONK, REASON 1.5 (D)

2. (C) SUMMARY. NO ADDITIONAL NEWS IS AVAILABLE ABOUT

THE STATUS OF REFORM ACTIVISTS HOANG MINH CHINH AND

PHAM QUE DUONG (REFTEL) EXCEPT THAT THEY ARE BELIEVED

TO HAVE REPORTED AS REQUESTED TO THE HANOI POLICE

STATION ON SEPTEMBER 6. POLICE APPARENTLY

"INTERVIEWED" AT LEAST 19 ACTIVISTS ON SEPTEMBER 5 IN

HANOI; TWO FROM HO CHI MINH CITY APPEAR TO HAVE BEEN

SENT BACK THERE THE SAME EVENING. AUTHORITIES SEEM TO

HAVE RETALIATED BY CUTTING OFF THE PHONE SERVICE OF AT

LEAST SOME OF THESE ACTIVISTS, WHO HAVE CLAIMED

PRIVATELY THESE MOVES MAY REPRESENT A SERIOUS

ESCALATION OF PRESSURE AGAINST THEM. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) REFORM ACTIVIST NGUYEN THANH GIANG TOLD POLOFF

ON SEPTEMBER 7 THAT HE STILL DID NOT KNOW THE CURRENT

SITUATION OF FELLOW ACTIVISTS HOANG MINH CHINH AND PHAM

QUE DUONG (REFTEL). GIANG SAID THAT HE AND HIS

ASSOCIATES "DID NOT DARE" ANY MORE TO GO TO CHINH\'S AND

DUONG\'S HOMES TO CHECK ON THEIR WELFARE, NOR COULD THEY

CONTACT NOW THEM BY TELEPHONE. GIANG SAID THAT HE HAD

MET CHINH\'S WIFE AT A FUNERAL ON THE AFTERNOON OF

SEPTEMBER 6, DURING WHICH SHE CONFIRMED THAT CHINH HAD

RETURNED TO THE POLICE STATION THAT DAY FOR FURTHER

"INTERVIEWS" BUT THAT CHINH WAS CONTINUING THE HUNGER

STRIKE HE HAD BEGAN ON SEPTEMBER 5. GIANG ADDED THAT

HE BELIEVED DUONG HAD ALSO RETURNED TO THE POLICE

STATION ON SEPTEMBER 6.

3. (C) ACCORDING TO GIANG, POLICE IN HANOI

"INTERVIEWED" A TOTAL OF 19 REFORM ACTIVISTS (INCLUDING

HIMSELF) AT VARIOUS POLICE STATIONS ON SEPTEMBER 5. HE

RECOUNTED HOW POLICE HAD VISITED SEVEN ACTIVISTS AT

CONFIDENTIAL

THEIR HOMES AND "INVITED" THEM (WITH APPROPRIATE LEGAL

"INVITATION PAPERS") TO GO TO THE POLICE STATIONS "TO

WORK WITH THEM." TEN OTHER ACTIVISTS WERE SIMILARLY

"INVITED" TO GO TO POLICE STATIONS AFTER HAVING VISITED

THE HOME OF EITHER CHINH OR DUONG ON SEPTEMBER 5.

GIANG MAINTAINED THAT THE "INTERVIEWS" WERE PRIMARILY

RELATED TO A REQUEST SENT BY REGISTERED MAIL TO THE

GOVERNMENT ON SEPTEMBER 3 TO ESTABLISH AN ANTI-

CORRUPTION NGO (REFTEL). HE ADDED THAT NOT ALL OF THE

19 HAD EVEN KNOWN ABOUT THIS INITIATIVE, ALTHOUGH ALL

HAD AT SOME POINT BEEN ASSOCIATED WITH GIANG IN

PROMOTING "DEMOCRATIC REFORM." THEY INCLUDE: HIMSELF,

CHINH, DUONG, TRAN KHUE (WITH DUONG, CO-AUTHOR OF THE

REQUEST), NGUYEN THI THANH XUAN, HOANG TIEN, NGUYEN DAU

KINH, TRAN DUNG TIEN, TRAN BA, LE CHE QUANG, DAU QUY

HA, DUOI HUY, NGUYEN THU, VU BINH, NGUYEN DOAI, DAO

QUONG TIEN, V. THINH, TRAN DAI SON, AND DUOI SON.

4. (C) GIANG SAID THAT CO-AUTHOR KHUE AND XUAN (WHOM

GIANG DESCRIBED AS KHUE\'S FRIEND AND A DEMOCRATIC

REFORM SUPPORTER, BUT NOT INVOLVED WITH THE PROPOSAL)

WERE SENT BACK TO HO CHI MINH CITY, WHERE THEY RESIDE,

ON THE NIGHT OF SEPTEMBER 5. THEY HAD BEEN IN HANOI

FOR THE PAST MONTH WHILE KHUE WORKED ON THE REQUEST.

GIANG SAID THAT HE DID NOT KNOW WHAT HAD HAPPENED TO

THESE TWO SINCE THEY LEFT HANOI.

5. (C) GIANG TOLD POLOFF THAT HE, ALL OF HIS FELLOW

ACTIVISTS, AND THE OWNER OF THE HOME WHERE KHUE HAD

STAYED, HAD THEIR HOME TELEPHONE SERVICE CUT. HIS OWN

CELL PHONE SERVICE HAS ALSO BEEN CUT, AS POLOFF

DISCOVERED ON SEPTEMBER 6. GIANG SAID THAT WHEN HE

REALIZED THAT HIS CELL PHONE WAS NOT WORKING, HE BOUGHT

A NEW SIM CARD ON THE MORNING OF SEPTEMBER 7 TO

REACTIVATE HIS PHONE WITH A NEW AND DIFFERENT NUMBER.

HE CLAIMED THAT, WITHIN TWO HOURS, THE NEW NUMBER HAD

AGAIN STOPPED WORKING. GIANG MENTIONED THAT SOME OF

HIS FELLOW ACTIVISTS ALSO HAVE CELL PHONES, BUT THAT HE

DOES NOT YET KNOW IF THEIRS ARE STILL IN SERVICE.

6. (C) GIANG OPINED THAT THE "INTERVIEWS" AND

TELEPHONE DISRUPTIONS ARE THE STRONGEST ACTION THE SRV

HAS TAKEN AGAINST DEMOCRACY REFORMERS IN SOME TIME. HE

SAID THAT HE BELIEVES THAT THIS ACTION MAY BE A WARNING

BY THE REMAINING "HARD-LINE" LEADERSHIP TO NONG DUC

MANH AND OTHER NEW LEADERS. ALTERNATIVELY, GIANG

HYPOTHESIZED, SINCE HE AND HIS FELLOW ACTIVISTS PROPOSE

SETTING UP AN ORGANIZATION TO FIGHT CORRUPTION, THOSE

WITHIN THE LEADERSHIP OF WHATEVER POLITICAL TENDENCY

WHO BENEFIT FROM CORRUPTION WOULD OPPOSE SUCH EFFORTS.

EITHER WAY, GIANG GUESSED, "THE PEOPLE" WOULD BE QUITE

UPSET IF THEY LEARNED ABOUT THE POLICE REACTION TO THIS

PROPOSAL TO SET UP AN ANTI-CORRUPTION NGO, THUS MAKING

IT MORE DIFFICULT FOR THE GOVERNMENT TO CONTINUE TO

GOVERN.

7. ( U ) ACCORDING TO PRESS REPORTS, THE FOREIGN

MINISTRY SPOKESPERSON ON SEPTEMBER 6 DENIED THAT ANY

ARRESTS HAD TAKEN PLACE AGAINST INDIVIDUALS ATTEMPTING

TO ESTABLISH AN ANTI-CORRUPTION NGO.

8. ( C ) IN A SEPARATE MEETING ON SEPTEMBER 7

(SEPTEL), THE CHAIRMAN OF THE VIETNAM LAWYERS\'

ASSOCIATION CONFIRMED TO POL/C AND POLOFF THAT, IN

LEGAL PRINCIPLE, VIETNAMESE CITIZENS CLEARLY HAVE THE

RIGHT TO SET UP AN ASSOCIATION TO FIGHT CORRUPTION OR

UNDERTAKE OTHER ACTIVITIES NOT PROHIBITED BY LAW. HE

CLAIMED TO HAVE NO KNOWLEDGE OF THIS NEW INITIATIVE OR

POLICE REACTION.

9. (C) COMMENT. GIANG\'S DESCRIPTION OF THE SEVERITY

OF THIS ROUND OF ACTIONS MAY BE INFLUENCED BY HIS OWN

INVOLVEMENT. INASMUCH AS EMBASSY CAN DETERMINE, POLICE

REACTION TO THIS INITIATIVE, WHILE UNHELPFUL, HAS NOT

BEEN NOTABLY HEAVY-HANDED AND MAY NOT EVEN LEAD TO

ACTUAL ARRESTS OR IMPRISONMENT -- AT LEAST, IN THE

ABSENCE OF FURTHER PERCEIVED PROVOCATIONS. END COMMENT
