SUBJECT: HOLY SEE CALLS IN AMBASSADOR TO CONVEY CONCERNS ON

MIDDLE EAST AND BETHLEHEM

CLASSIFIED BY: AMBASSADOR JIM NICHOLSON. REASONS: 1.5 (B) AND (D).

SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) HOLY SEE FOREIGN MINISTER ARCHBISHOP JEAN-LOUIS

TAURAN CALLED IN AMBASSADOR NOVEMBER 27 TO CONVEY THE POPE\'S

CONCERNS ABOUT THE SITUATION IN THE MIDDLE EAST, PARTICULARLY

THE ISRAELI REOCCUPATION OF BETHLEHEM. WITH THE CHRISTMAS

SEASON APPROACHING, TAURAN SAID THE CURRENT CURFEW IN

BETHLEHEM -- PROJECTED TO LAST THROUGH DECEMBER 30 BY SOME

ISRAELI AUTHORITIES -- WOULD HAVE VERY NEGATIVE CONSEQUENCES.

HE INDICATED THAT IF THE CURFEW WAS NOT LIFTED AND ACCESS TO

THE HOLY SITES GUARANTEED, THE POPE WOULD BE COMPELLED TO

MAKE A STATEMENT SHARPLY CRITICAL OF ISRAEL FOR RESTRICTING

THE FREEDOM OF RELIGION. TAURAN WELCOMED THE PROPOSAL FOR A

QUARTET MEETING IN WASHINGTON DECEMBER 20, ADDING THAT IT WAS

TIME TO MOVE FROM ANALYSIS TO CONCRETE STEPS ON THE GROUND.

HE ALSO APPLAUDED PRESIDENT BUSH\'S DECISION NOT TO MOVE THE

U.S. EMBASSY TO JERUSALEM, WHICH HE SAID WOULD HAVE BEEN A

CAUSE OF "GREAT DISTURBANCE." ON IRAQ, TAURAN TOLD THE

AMBASSADOR THE HOLY SEE HAD SENT A MESSAGE TO THE IRAQI

GOVERNMENT REITERATING THEIR BELIEF THAT SADDAM MUST MEET HIS

UN COMMITMENTS. END SUMMARY.

--------------------------------------------- -------

POPE WANTS END TO CURFEW, ENSURE CHRISTMAS ACCESS TO HOLY

SITES

--------------------------------------------- -------

2. (C) ARCHBISHOP TAURAN TOLD THE AMBASSADOR NOVEMBER 27 THAT

THE POPE WAS PERSONALLY DEEPLY CONCERNED ABOUT THE MOST

RECENT ISRAELI INCURSION INTO BETHLEHEM AND THE IMPLICATIONS

OF THE ONGOING CURFEW THERE FOR THE UPCOMING CHRISTMAS

SEASON. WHILE RECOGNIZING THAT ISRAEL HAD PULLED BACK ITS

TANKS FROM AROUND THE CHURCH OF THE NATIVITY, TAURAN SAID

BETHLEHEM REMAINED UNDER A STRICT CURFEW THAT HAD SERIOUS

IMPLICATIONS FOR TRADITIONAL CHRISTMAS EVENTS AT THE HOLY

SITES. HE UNDERSTOOD THAT SOME ISRAELI AUTHORITIES HAD

SUGGESTED THE CURFEW COULD LAST UNTIL DECEMBER 30.

RHETORICALLY ASKING, "WHAT ABOUT CHRISTMAS," TAURAN SAID THAT

IF THE CURFEW WERE TO CONTINUE THROUGH THAT TIME, THE POPE

WOULD FEEL COMPELLED TO ISSUE A STATEMENT CRITICAL OF THE

ISRAELIS FOR "RESTRICTING FREEDOM OF RELIGION." THE HOLY SEE

RECOGNIZED THAT SUCH A STATEMENT WOULD BE LIKELY TO PROVOKE A

POINTED ISRAELI REACTION, AND WOULD STRONGLY PREFER THAT

STEPS BE TAKEN TO AVOID THIS SCENARIO. TAURAN SAID HE HOPED

THE U.S. COULD WORK WITH ISRAEL TO AVOID ANY PROBLEMS DURING

THIS CHRISTMAS SEASON.

3. (C) THE AMBASSADOR AGREED TO CONVEY TAURAN\'S CONCERNS TO

WASHINGTON. HE NOTED THAT ISRAEL WAS CERTAINLY SEEKING TO

AVOID A REOCCURRENCE OF THE OCCUPATION OF THE CHURCH OF THE

NATIVITY, AND ASKED TAURAN WHETHER THEY PERCEIVED A RISK THAT

THE PALESTINIANS MIGHT SEEK TO CREATE TROUBLE IN THE HOLY

SITES. TAURAN SAID HE DOUBTED THE PALESTINIANS WOULD OCCUPY

THE CHURCH AT THIS TIME, BECAUSE THE INTERNATIONAL BACKLASH

WOULD BE TOO GREAT.

---------------------------------

QUARTET: TIME FOR CONCRETE STEPS

---------------------------------

4. (C) THE AMBASSADOR TOLD TAURAN THAT THE QUARTET WOULD BE

MEETING IN WASHINGTON DECEMBER 20 TO CONTINUE WORKING ON A

ROADMAP TOWARD ACHIEVING THE PRESIDENT\'S VISION OF A

TWO-STATE SOLUTION. HE NOTED THAT CONTINUED VIOLENCE

THREATENED TO DERAIL THESE EFFORTS, AND THAT PALESTINIAN AND

ISRAELI RESTRAINT WAS NEEDED TO IMPROVE THE SECURITY

SITUATION, ACHIEVE REFORMS, AND ALLEVIATE THE HUMANITARIAN

SITUATION. TAURAN WELCOMED THE CONTINUED ENGAGEMENT OF THE

QUARTET, BUT SAID "THE TIME HAD ARRIVED TO MOVE FROM ANALYSIS

TO CONCRETE STEPS." HE ALSO REPEATED HIS LONG-STANDING VIEW

THAT AN INTERNATIONAL PEACEKEEPING OR MONITORING PRESENCE ON

THE GROUND IS NEEDED TO END THE SPIRAL OF VIOLENCE. ONLY

SUCH A PRESENCE, HE EXPLAINED, WOULD BE ABLE TO DETERMINE WHO

WAS VIOLATING AGREEMENTS. PRESSED ON THE COMPOSITION OF SUCH

A FORCE, TAURAN SAID THAT WAS NOT A MATTER ON WHICH THE HOLY

SEE COULD COMMENT. TAURAN ALSO EXPRESSED CONCERN OVER A

RECENT STATEMENT OF PM SHARON DENYING THE POTENTIAL FOR TWO

STATES.

IRAQ: HOLY SEE TELLS SADDAM TO FLY RIGHT

-----------------------------------------

6. (C) TURNING TO IRAQ, TAURAN SAID THE HOLY SEE WAS VERY

PLEASED THAT WAR APPEARED TO HAVE BEEN AVOIDED FOR THE TIME

BEING. RECOGNIZING THAT THE NEXT STEP WAS UP TO IRAQ, THE

HOLY SEE HAD RECENTLY SENT A MESSAGE TO IRAQ MAKING CLEAR

THEIR BELIEF THAT SADDAM NEEDED TO ABIDE BY THE UN

RESOLUTIONS AND ALLOW THE INSPECTORS FULL ACCESS TO POTENTIAL

WMD SITES. THE AMBASSADOR ASSURED TAURAN THAT THE U.S. DID

NOT WANT WAR, BUT THAT IT WAS UP TO IRAQ TO ASSURE THE WORLD

THAT IT HAD DISARMED AS REQUIRED. TAURAN ACKNOWLEDGED THIS,

BUT STRESSED THAT WAR WOULD HAVE NEGATIVE CONSEQUENCES FOR

THE REGION, AND WOULD LIKELY INCREASE THE "APPETITE FOR

TERROR" AMONG RADICALS IN THE REGION.

COMMENT

-------

7. (C) FOLLOWING THE DISASTROUS OCCUPATION OF THE CHURCH OF

THE NATIVITY LAST SPRING, THE HOLY SEE IS DETERMINED TO

ENSURE THAT THE CHURCH DOES NOT AGAIN BECOME CAUGHT IN THE

ISRAELI-PALESTINIAN CROSS-FIRE. EVER CONSCIOUS OF THE

SYMBOLIC IMPORTANCE OF THE HOLY SITES, THE POPE WOULD SEE

ISRAELI RESTRICTIONS THIS CHRISTMAS SEASON AS A SYMBOL OF

CONTINUED HIGH-HANDEDNESS AND CAN BE EXPECTED TO BE SHARPLY

CRITICAL IF THE CURFEW WOULD MAR THE HOLY SEASON. ANY

ASSURANCE WE COULD PROVIDE THE HOLY SEE THAT WE ARE

ENCOURAGING ISRAEL TO ENSURE A PEACEFUL CHRISTMAS IN
