SUBJECT: LIBERIA: WFP ADDRESSES A GROWING NUMBER OF

INTERNALLY DISPLACED PERSONS

---------

SUMMARY

---------

1. USAID STAFF FROM THE U.S. MISSION IN ROME AND

FOOD FOR PEACE/WASHINGTON TRAVELED TO GUINEA,

LIBERIA, AND SENEGAL SEPTEMBER 29 THROUGH OCTOBER 9

TO REVIEW THE FOOD SECURITY SITUATION FOR REFUGEES,

INTERNALLY DISPLACED PERSONS (IDPS), AND RESIDENTS

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ROME 05421 01 OF 09 061632Z

WHOSE COMMUNITIES HAVE BEEN AFFECTED BY INFLUXES OF

EITHER GROUP. THIS REPORT FOCUSES ON LIBERIA SOLELY.

GUINEA WILL BE REPORTED VIA SEP TEL.

2. THE U.N. WORLD FOOD PROGRAM (WFP) IS CURRENTLY

PROVIDING FOOD RATIONS TO 184,027 IDPS IN LIBERIA.

THE NUMBER OF IDPS HAS MORE THAN DOUBLED IN THE LAST

SIX MONTHS. THE REGISTRATION PROCESS IN THE CAMPS

MUST BE IMPROVED. WFP HAS MADE SIGNIFICANT STRIDES

IN TIGHTENING THE FOOD DISTRIBUTION PROCESS, MAKING

IT MORE EFFICIENT AND TIMELY, BUT IT REQUIRES

CONSTANT VIGILANCE. WFP HAS ALSO PUT INTO PLACE

MEASURES TO GUARD AGAINST SEXUAL EXPLOITATION. THE

RATION SIZE GIVEN TO IDPS NEEDS TO BE REVIEWED. IDPS

HAVE BEEN GIVEN LAND FOR PLANTING AND THOSE WHO WERE

ABLE TO PLANT LAST SPRING/SUMMER ARE EXPECTING A GOOD

HARVEST. THE NUTRITIONAL STATUS OF THE IDPS IS

APPROXIMATELY THE SAME AS THE LOCAL POPULATION AND

CURRENTLY AT ACCEPTABLE LEVELS. THE GROWING BURDEN

PLACED ON HOST FAMILIES MAY SOON HAVE TO BE

ADDRESSED. THE MORALE OF THE U.N. FAMILY IS MUCH

IMPROVED FROM SIX MONTHS AGO. APPROXIMATELY 22,500

SIERRA LEONEAN REFUGEES REMAIN IN LIBERIA. SEE PARA

36 FOR RECOMMENDATIONS. END SUMMARY

CLEANING UP IDP REGISTRATION

-------------------------------

9. THE REGISTRATION PROCESS IN THE IDP CAMPS IS

SOMETHING THAT CONTINUES TO BE PERFECTED. THE

GOVERNMENT\'S LIBERIA REFUGEE, REPATRIATION AND

RESETTLEMENT COMMISSION (LRRRC) WAS, UNTIL RECENTLY,

SOLELY IN CHARGE OF THE REGISTRATION, BUT THERE WERE

MANY PROBLEMS AND WFP WAS VERY UNCOMFORTABLE WITH THE

PROCESS. LRRRC WAS RELYING ON CAMP MANAGERS TO

COMPOSE THE LISTS OF BENEFICIARIES. IN AN EFFORT TO

VERIFY THAT POTENTIAL BENEFICIARIES WERE TRULY

DISPLACED, LRRRC HAD REQUIRED A FAMILY TO BUILD ITS

HOUSE BEFORE IT RECEIVED A RATION CARD. DUE TO THE

VULNERABILITY OF SOME HEADS OF HOUSEHOLDS, THIS

PRACTICE LED TO A LONG DELAY FOR SOME BENEFICIARIES

IN RECEIVING ANY FOOD ASSISTANCE.

RE-VAMPING FOOD DISTRIBUTIONS

---------------------------------

13. AFTER THE SECURITY PROBLEMS OF LAST MAY AND

JUNE, WFP STOPPED STORING COMMODITIES IN LOCAL

WAREHOUSES UP-COUNTRY. EACH DAY THE TRUCKS FILLED

WITH WFP COMMODITIES DROVE UP TO BONG COUNTY AND WFP

DISTRIBUTED FOOD DIRECTLY FROM THE TRUCKS. ALTHOUGH

THIS METHOD PREVENTED LOSS OF STOCKS, THE

DISTRIBUTIONS WERE TAKING ALMOST THE ENTIRE MONTH TO

COMPLETE. BAGIRISHYA AND HIS TEAM HAVE SINCE CUT

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 04 ROME 05421 03 OF 09 061633Z

MONTHLY DISTRIBUTION TIME DOWN TO SEVEN DAYS BY

REOPENING THE WAREHOUSE IN PHEBE (BONG COUNTY) WITH

THE ADDED PRECAUTION OF STORING FOOD FOR ONLY ONE DAY

PRIOR TO DISTRIBUTION, AND LEAVING IT PRACTICALLY

EMPTY THE REST OF THE MONTH. WFP INTENDS TO

ULTIMATELY CUT THE DISTRIBUTION TIME DOWN TO THREE OR

FOUR DAYS.

14. BAGIRISHYA ALSO DISCOVERED THAT MANY OF THE FOOD

MONITORS HAD BECOME OFFICE-BOUND AND DISPATCHED THEM

ALL TO THE FIELD. THE MONTSERRADO CAMP MONITORS

VISIT THE CAMPS EVERY DAY; THE UP-COUNTRY MONITORS

VISIT TWO TO THREE TIMES A WEEK. THE MONITORS

OVERSEE FOOD DISTRIBUTIONS, ADDRESS PROBLEMS THAT

ARISE, AND WORK WITH WFP\'S IMPLEMENTING PARTNER TO

INCREASE EFFICIENCY. THE MONITORS WILL ALSO SOON BE

TRAINED IN POST-DISTRIBUTION MONITORING, SO THAT WFP

WILL HAVE A BETTER IDEA OF HOW LONG THE FOOD IS

LASTING AND HOW IT IS USED.

RATION SIZES

--------------------

21. WFP IS CURRENTLY PROVIDING IDPS A RATION EQUAL

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ROME 05421 05 OF 09 061633Z

TO 1,800 KILOCALORIES (KC) PER DAY, WHICH IS 300 KC

SHORT OF THE INTERNATIONAL STANDARD OF 2,100 KC PER

DAY. UNDER THE ASSUMPTION THAT THOSE LIVING NEARER

TO MONROVIA WOULD HAVE GREATER ACCESS TO ADDITIONAL

COPING MECHANISMS, WFP\'S NEW PRRO (WHICH BEGINS IN

JANUARY) PLANS TO PROVIDE THE IDPS IN MONTSERRADO

WITH 1,800 KILOCALORIES (KC), WHILE THE RATION FOR

AFTER VIEWING THE AGRICULTURE PRODUCTION OUTSIDE SOME

OF THE BONG COUNTY CAMPS, HOWEVER, THE USAID TEAM

RECOMMENDS THE DECISION REGARDING RATION SIZE SHOULD

BE BASED ON VULNERABILITY AND/OR NUTRITIONAL

ASSESSMENT RATHER THAN A NOTIONAL DETERMINATION OF

COPING MECHANISMS AND URGED WFP TO MAKE DECISIONS

REGARDING RATION SIZE AFTER CONDUCTING ASSESSMENTS.

AGRICULTURAL ACTIVITY AROUND THE CAMPS

------------------------------------------

22. THE GOOD NEWS IS THAT IDPS ARE BEING PROVIDED

LAND FOR PLANTING AROUND THE CAMPS IN BONG, AS WELL

AS IN MONTSERRADO. WHEREAS IN FEBRUARY, THE AMOUNTS

OF LAND PROVIDED TO THE IDPS WERE VERY SMALL AND IDP

MOVEMENTS WERE GREATLY RESTRICTED OUTSIDE THE CAMP,

TODAY THE SITUATION IS MUCH IMPROVED. THERE SEEMS TO

BE NO PROBLEM NOW WITH IDPS FREELY MOVING IN THE

AREA, AND THE FIELDS WITNESSED BY THE TEAM WERE TRULY

IMPRESSIVE. THE FOOD AND AGRICULTURAL ORGANIZATION

OF THE U.N. (FAO) PROVIDED SEEDS LAST SPRING, AND THE

HARVEST WILL BE GOOD FOR THOSE WHO WERE ABLE TO

UNCLASSIFIED

23. THE IDPS FROM LOFA ARE AN INDUSTRIOUS BUNCH AND

WANT TO CONTINUE THEIR ACTIVITIES. DURING A

DISCUSSION WITH THE USAID TEAM, THEY ASKED FOR

VOCATIONAL SCHOOLS, AS WELL AS MORE AGRICULTURAL

SUPPORT. AS BONG AND LOFA COUNTIES ARE CONSIDERED

THE BREAD BASKETS FOR THE COUNTRY, IT IS CRITICALLY

IMPORTANT THAT IDPS FROM THESE COUNTIES BE ABLE TO

CONTINUE AGRICULTURAL ACTIVITY IN THEIR TEMPORARY

LOCATIONS.

BEEN GIVEN 189 ACRES OF LAND FOR PLANTING, AND ARE

HOPING TO GET MORE TO ACCOMMODATE ITS 16,383

INHABITANTS. PLOTS, WHICH ARE SHARED BY TWO

NUTRITIONAL STATUS

---------------------

25. ACTION CONTRE LA FAIM (ACF) IS ADDRESSING

NUTRITIONAL PROBLEMS IN LIBERIA AND TOLD THE USAID

TEAM THAT THE NUTRITIONAL SITUATION WAS PRECARIOUS,

BUT FAR FROM A DISASTER. MALNUTRITION RATES HAD

PEAKED IN EARLY SEPTEMBER, THE END OF THE HUNGRY

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ROME 05421 06 OF 09 061634Z

SEASON BETWEEN MAY AND SEPTEMBER.

26. ACF REPORTED THE NUMBER OF SEVERELY MALNOURISHED

BENEFICIARIES (LESS THAN 70 PERCENT WEIGHT FOR

HEIGHT) IN THE THERAPEUTIC FEEDING CENTERS (TFCS) WAS

ALREADY BEGINNING TO DECLINE SINCE THE BEGINNING OF

SEPTEMBER. THERE WERE 180 IN THE TFC IN THE VOA CAMP

(MONTSERRADO COUNTY) AND 40 PERCENT OF THOSE WERE

FROM THE LOCAL POPULATION. ACF REPORTED THE SAME

TREND IN ITS TFC IN PHEBE IN BONG COUNTY. OUT OF THE

80 TFC BENEFICIARIES IN BONG, 45-50 PERCENT WERE FROM

THE LOCAL POPULATION. ACF ATTRIBUTED THIS TREND TO

THE INCREASED BURDEN PUT ON HOUSEHOLDS ACTING AS HOST

FAMILIES TO IDPS, AS WELL AS THE DISRUPTION IN

AGRICULTURE CAUSED BY THE UPROOTING OF OVER 200,000

PEOPLE. SUCH A HIGH PERCENTAGE OF SEVERELY

MALNOURISHED BENEFICIARIES FROM THE LOCAL POPULATION

IS WORRISOME AND MERITS CLOSE MONITORING, EVEN THOUGH

THE OVERALL NUMBERS ARE STILL SMALL.

27. ACF SAID THE AVERAGE LENGTH OF STAY FOR

MODERATELY MALNOURISHED IN THE SUPPLEMENTARY FEEDING

CENTERS (SFCS) WAS THREE TO FOUR MONTHS. THE

RECOVERY TIME IS LONGER THAN NORMAL BECAUSE THE SFC

RATION IS BEING SHARED WITH THE FAMILY OR

SOLD/BARTERED BECAUSE OF THE INCREASED NEEDS.

NUMBERS IN THE SFCS IN MONTSERRADO COUNTY WERE

BETWEEN 2,000 AND 2,500, WHEREAS NUMBERS IN THE SFCS

UP-COUNTRY TOTALED APPROXIMATELY 1,000.

NEEDS OF HOST FAMILIES

28. AS STATED ABOVE, THE LOCAL POPULATIONS ARE

FROM THE NORTH. THE BURDEN THAT SUCH EXTRA FAMILY

MEMBERS (SOMETIMES AS MANY AS 10 OR MORE) CREATES ON

AN ALREADY POOR FAMILY IS SIGNIFICANT. AS A RESULT,

MANY IN THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY HAVE NOTICED AN

INCREASE IN THE LEVEL OF DESPERATION AND IN THE

NUMBER OF PEOPLE BEGGING.
