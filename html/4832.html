SUBJECT: GOI INQUIRY ON HELICOPTER PARTS SALE TO IRAN

CLASSIFIED BY: ACTING DEPUTY CHIEF OF MISSION JOHN DWYER

FOR REASONS 1.5 (B) (D)

1. (C) AT THE END OF A DECEMBER 13 MEETING WITH A/DCM ON A

SEPARATE ISSUE, BERLUSCONI FOREIGN POLICY ADVISOR GIANPAOLO

SCARANTE REQUESTED "INFORMAL" USG VIEWS ON THE POSSIBLE

RENEWAL OF A LONG-FROZEN SALE OF AGUSTA HELICOPTER PARTS TO

IRAN, CURRENTLY UNDER REVIEW WITHIN THE GOI.

2. (C) ACCORDING TO SCARANTE, IN 1993 THE ITALIAN

GOVERNMENT FROZE AGUSTA\'S $25 MILLION CONTRACT TO DELIVER

HELICOPTER SPARE PARTS TO THE IRANIAN GOVERNMENT. THE LEGAL

BASIS FOR THAT ACTION, HE SAID, WAS THE UNCHR RESOLUTION ON

IRAN IN EFFECT AT THAT TIME. IN THE YEARS THAT FOLLOWED, AS

LONG AS SUCH A RESOLUTION REMAINED IN EFFECT, THE GOI WAS

ABLE TO MAINTAIN ITS FREEZE ON THE SALE. WITHOUT A UNCHR

RESOLUTION ON IRAN THIS YEAR, HOWEVER, SCARANTE SAID THE GOI

HAD TO REVIEW WHETHER IT COULD CONTINUE TO HOLD UP DELIVERY

OF THE SPARE PARTS. IN THIS CONTEXT, HE SAID HE WOULD

APPRECIATE A "LOW-KEYED, INFORMAL" USG VIEW ON ITALY\'S

LETTING THIS SALE GO FORWARD.

3. (C) ON DECEMBER 18, ECMIN PHONED SCARANTE TO CONVEY THAT

THE USG WOULD NOT/NOT FAVOR ITALY\'S ALLOWING THE PARTS TO BE

DELIVERED. HE REMINDED SCARANTE THAT THE LONG-STANDING U.S.

EMBARGO OF MOST ECONOMIC AND FINANCIAL TRANSACTIONS WITH IRAN

REFLECTS OUR CONTINUED STRONG CONCERN WITH IRAN\'S PURSUIT OF

WMD AND BALLISTIC MISSILES, CONTINUING HUMAN RIGHTS

VIOLATIONS, SUPPORT FOR INTERNATIONAL TERRORISM, AND

UNHELPFUL ACTIONS VIS-A-VIS AFGHANISTAN AND MIDDLE EAST

PEACE. WE LIKEWISE STRONGLY ENCOURAGED OTHER COUNTRIES TO

LIMIT THEIR TRADE WITH IRAN. ECMIN ALSO URGED THE GOI TO

EXAMINE THE SALE CLOSELY IN THE CONTEXT OF ITALY\'S

OBLIGATIONS UNDER THE WASSENAAR ARRANGEMENT.

4. (C) SCARANTE, WITHOUT ARGUING ANY OF THE ABOVE POINTS,

REITERATED THAT THE AGUSTA SALE WAS SIMPLY UNDER GOI REVIEW

AT THE PRESENT TIME, AND THAT USG VIEWS WOULD BE "AN

IMPORTANT FACTOR" IN THAT REVIEW. ECMIN EXPRESSED

APPRECIATION FOR SCARANTE\'S HAVING CONTACTED THE U.S.

EMBASSY, AND URGED THAT THE GOI OFFER US THE OPPORTUNITY TO

CONSULT MORE FORMALLY IF THERE WAS ANY SERIOUS CHANCE THAT
