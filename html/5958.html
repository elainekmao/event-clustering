SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) DURING A FEBRUARY 22 PRIVATE AUDIENCE WITH THE POPE

AND SUBSEQUENT MEETING WITH VATICAN SECRETARY OF STATE

SODANO, UK PRIME MINISTER BLAIR OUTLINED TO THE HOLY SEE HIS

VIEWS ON THE NEED FOR THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY TO DISARM

SADDAM HUSSEIN. THE POPE AND HIS SENIOR ADVISORS REPORTEDLY

CONVEYED THEIR VIEW THAT DISARMAMENT CAN BE ACHIEVED WITHOUT

RECOURSE TO MILITARY ACTION. BOTH SIDES AGREED ON THE

CENTRALITY OF THE UN\'S ROLE, THE IMPORTANCE OF RESOLVING THE

ISSUE OF IRAQ\'S POSSESSION OF WMD, AND THE NEED TO ENSURE

THAT THIS ISSUE DOES NOT LEAD TO NEW DIVISIONS IN THE

INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY. END SUMMARY.

-----------------------------

PRIVATE MEETING TURNED PUBLIC

-----------------------------

2. (C) PRIME MINISTER TONY BLAIR MEET WITH THE POPE FOR A

HALF HOUR IN A PRIVATE AUDIENCE FEBRUARY 22, FOLLOWED BY AN

HOUR-LONG MEETING WITH VATICAN SECRETARY OF STATE SODANO AND

FOREIGN MINISTER TAURAN. BLAIR\'S PRIVATE MEETING WITH THE

POPE WAS NOT ORIGINALLY INTENDED TO BE SEEN AS A POLITICAL

MEETING, BUT RATHER A PRIVATE FAMILY VISIT. (BLAIR\'S WIFE

AND CHILDREN ARE CATHOLIC, AND HAD WANTED TO MEET THE POPE.)

THE TIMING WAS DETERMINED NOT BY THE PRESSURE OVER IRAQ, BUT

BY THE COINCIDENCE OF A PREVIOUSLY SCHEDULED MEETING IN ROME

WITH ITALIAN PM BERLUSCONI (REF C) AND THE BLAIR CHILDREN\'S

SCHOOL HOLIDAYS. UK AMBASSADOR COLVIN (PROTECT) SAID THE UK

IRAQ: AVOID DIVISIONS; KEEP FOCUS ON UN

----------------------------------------

3. (C) ACCORDING TO THE VATICAN PRESS SUMMARY AND THE UK

AMBASSADOR, WHO PARTICIPATED IN THE SECOND MEETING, BUT NOT

THE PAPAL AUDIENCE, THE THREE AGREED SUBJECTS WERE IRAQ, THE

ISRAELI-PALESTINIAN CONFLICT, AND THE EU CONSTITUTION. ON

IRAQ, THE AMBASSADOR SAID PM BLAIR LAID OUT HIS VIEWS --

WHICH SHE SAID WOULD BE VERY FAMILIAR TO OUR EMBASSY IN

LONDON -- ON THE NEED TO DISARM SADDAM AND TO ENSURE THE UN

MET ITS RESPONSIBILITIES. THE POPE AND HIS COLLABORATORS IN

TURN OUTLINED THE HOLY SEE\'S VIEW THAT "ALL INTERESTED

PARTIES IN THE IRAQI CRISIS NEED TO COLLABORATE WITH THE

ORGANIZATION OF THE UN AND KNOW HOW TO USE THE RESOURCES

OFFERED BY INTERNATIONAL LAW IN ORDER TO AVERT THE TRAGEDY OF

WAR," WHICH THE HOLY SEE CONTINUES TO BELIEVE IS AVOIDABLE.

BOTH SIDES AGREED THAT THE UN ROLE SHOULD BE CENTRAL, BUT

DIFFERED OVER THE POTENTIAL NEED FOR RECOURSE TO FORCE IF

SADDAM FAILED TO COMPLY. THE HOLY SEE ALSO EMPHASIZED TO

BLAIR ITS STANDARD VIEW THAT "SPECIAL CONSIDERATION" BE GIVEN

TO THE HUMANITARIAN SITUATION OF THE IRAQI PEOPLE, "ALREADY

SO SEVERELY TRIED BY THE LONG YEARS OF EMBARGO."

THE ISSUE FOLLOWING THE MEETINGS WITH BLAIR WAS ITS CLEAR

THE VATICAN\'S CONCERN BOTH FOR POTENTIAL DIVISIONS BETWEEN

THE MUSLIM AND CHRISTIAN WORLDS, AS WELL AS DIVISIONS WITHIN

THE WEST ITSELF.

THE CHURCH IN THE EU CONSTITUTION

---------------------------------

6. (C) REGARDING THE EU CONSTITUTION -- AN ISSUE THE POPE

RAISES WITH ALL EU MEMBERS AND PROSPECTIVE MEMBERS -- THE

POPE REITERATED HIS STRONG DESIRE THAT EXPLICIT RECOGNITION

BE GIVEN IN THE CONSTITUTION TO CHURCHES AND COMMUNITIES OF

BELIEVERS, AND THAT THE EU MAKE AN EXPLICIT COMMITMENT TO

MAINTAIN A STRUCTURED DIALOGUE WITH CHURCHES.

--------------------------------------------- ----

COMMENT: VATICAN STEPPING UP THE DIPLOMATIC PACE

--------------------------------------------- ----

7. (C) DESPITE THE PRIVATE ORIGINS AND NATURE OF THE VISIT,

THE MEDIA HAS SEIZED ON THE VISIT -- WITH ENCOURAGEMENT FROM

THE HOLY SEE -- AS A REFLECTION OF THE POPE\'S CONTINUING

CENTRAL ROLE AS A SOURCE OF MORAL INFLUENCE ON THE CURRENT

INTERNATIONAL DEBATE OVER IRAQI DISARMAMENT. BLAIR\'S VISIT

FOLLOWED CLOSE ON THE HEELS OF PAPAL AUDIENCES WITH KOFI

ANNAN, TARIQ AZIZ (REF B), AND JOSCHKA FISCHER, AS WELL AS

CARDINAL ETCHEGARAY\'S MEETING WITH SADDAM (REF A), AND IT

PRECEDES VISITS THIS WEEK OF SPANISH PRIME MINISTER AZNAR AND

IRAN\'S PARLIAMENTARY SPEAKER MOHAMMED REZA KHATAMI. THE

POPE\'S TOP OFFICIALS ALSO CLOSETED WITH ITALIAN PRESIDENT

CIAMPI AND PM BERLUSCONI FEBRUARY 17 IN AN ANNUAL MEETING IN

WHICH IRAQ WAS A MAIN TOPIC OF CONVERSATION. AS THEY HAVE

POINTED OUT TO US, (REFS A AND B), THE VATICAN ACKNOWLEDGES

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 VATICA 00806 02 OF 02 261603Z

THE RIGHTNESS OF OUR CAUSE IN DEALING WITH SADDAM\'S WMD AND

ACKNOWLEDGES THEIR DANGER, BUT CANNOT YET BRING ITSELF TO

ACCEPT THAT RESORT TO FORCE IS THE ONLY WAY FORWARD. THIS

REFLECTS BOTH A WIDELY ACCEPTED VIEW WITHIN SOME CHURCH

CIRCLES THAT WAR IS AN IMMORAL AND INEFFECTIVE MEANS TO

RESOLVE INTERNATIONAL PROBLEMS, AND MORE PRAGMATIC CONCERNS

FOR THE IMPACT OF WAR ON CHRISTIAN-MUSLIM RELATIONS IN

GENERAL AND ON CATHOLIC POPULATIONS IN MUSLIM COUNTRIES IN

PARTICULAR.

8. (C) IN PURSUING ITS ACTIVIST DIPLOMATIC AGENDA, THE

VATICAN IS SEEKING TO USE THE FULL FORCE OF ITS MORAL

MEGAPHONE TO ADVANCE ITS VIEW THAT IRAQ\'S THREATS CAN BE

ADDRESSED SHORT OF WAR. BLAIR\'S MEETING AND THAT OF AZNAR

FEBRUARY 27 WILL HELP TO EMPHASIZE THE EXTENT TO WHICH THE

HOLY SEE SHARES OUR CONCERNS ABOUT THE THREAT POSED BY

SADDAM, WHILE ALSO MAKING CLEAR THE NEED FOR THE UN TO MEET

ITS RESPONSIBILITIES. IN THE WAKE OF THIS HIGH-LEVEL

DIPLOMATIC ACTIVITY, WE HAVE NOTED INCREASING CONCERNS BEING

EXPRESSED ABOUT THE U.S. "NEGLECTING" OR "UNDERVALUING" THE

MORAL INFLUENCE OF THE POPE. WE CONTINUE TO BELIEVE THAT A

SENIOR LEVEL MEETING WITH THE HOLY SEE WOULD ALLOW US TO

OVERCOME THE WIDESPREAD MEDIA IMAGE OF THE U.S. AND HOLY SEE

AT ODDS OVER IRAQ BY EMPHASIZING OUR SHARED CONCERN OVER

SADDAM\'S POSSESSION OF WMD, OUR SHARED COMMITMENT TO WORKING

WITH AND SUPPORTING THE UN, AND OUR SHARED CONCERN FOR THE

IRAQI PEOPLE.
