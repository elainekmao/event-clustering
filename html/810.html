l.SUMMARY: ON DECEMBER 14 AMBASSADOR GALBRAITH PAID A

COURTESY CALL ON THE NEW PRESIDENT OF THE HOUSE OF

REPRESENTATIVES VLATKO PAVLETIC. DISCUSSION FOCUSED ON

THE BALKANS PEACE AGREEMENT AND THE DIFFICULTIES OF

IMPLEMENTATION. PAVLETIC SAID THAT THE AGREEMENT OFFERS

THE BASIS FOR ALL THREE ETHNIC GROUPS TO RESOLVE THEIR

DIFFERENCES. BECAUSE THERE COULD HAVE BEEN NO PEACE

WITHOUT THE U.S. INITIATIVE, PAVLETIC STRESSED THAT IT IS

VITAL FOR CONTINUED U.S. ASSISTANCE IN IMPLEMENTATION.

THE AMBASSADOR AGREED THAT THERE IS MUCH UNFINISHED

BUSINESS, INCLUDING CREATING A CIVIL SOCIETY, THE RETURN

OF A MULTI-ETHNIC SOCIETY, AND A RESOLUTION OF THE PROBLEM

OF THOSE WHO HAVE DISAPPEARED. THE AMBASSADOR STRESSED

THAT PEACE WILL NOT BE REAL AND HEALING CANNOT BEGIN UNTIL

A FINAL ACCOUNTING IS DONE AND THOSE RESPONSIBLE FOR WAR

CRIMES -- ON ALL SIDES -- HAVE BEEN BROUGHT TO JUSTICE.

END SUMMARY.

2.0N DECEMBER 14 AMBASSADOR GALBRAITH PAID A COURTESY

CALL ON THE NEW PRESIDENT OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES

VLATKO PAVLETIC (CROATIAN DEMOCRATIC UNION -- HDZ).

PAVLETIC FOCUSED HIS COMMENTS ON THE SIGNING OF THE

BALKANS PEACE AGREEMENT IN PARIS AND NOTED THAT, AS

DIFFICULT AS THE NEGOTIATING PROCESS WAS, IMPLEMENTATION

WILL BE HARDER. HE SAID THAT THE CRUEL WAR OF THE LAST

FIVE YEARS HAS BROUGHT A PEACE THAT THAT IS ALMOST AS

CRUEL TO ITS VICTIMS. HE LAMENTED THAT WHILE THE VIOLENCE

HAS ENDED, HATRED AND INTOLERANCE PERSIST. A DIFFICULT

JOB LIES AHEAD TO CREATE THE CONDITIONS FOR CROATS, SERBS,

AND MUSLIMS TO LIVE TOGETHER AGAIN. HE NOTED, HOWEVER,

THAT FINDING A FORMULA TO BUILD TRUST WILL BE MORE

DIFFICULT THAN FINDING THE FINANCIAL RESOURCES TO REBUILD

VUKOVAR.

3.PAVLETIC ADDED THAT THE PEACE AGREEMENT OFFERS THE

BASIS FOR ALL THREE ETHNIC GROUPS TO RESOLVE THEIR

DIFFERENCES. HOWEVER, THE ENTIRE FORMULA COULD PROVE

UNWORKABLE IF ONE PARTY VIOLATES IT. HE SAID THIS WAS A

SPECIFIC REFERENCE TO LEADERS IN SECTOR EAST WHO HAVE

PUBLICLY INTERPRETED THE AGREEMENT DIFFERENTLY THAN THE

GOC (NOTE: LEADERS IN EASTERN SLAVONIA HAVE SAID THAT

CROATIAN DISPLACED PERSONS WILL ONLY BE ALLOWED TO RETURN

WHEN SERBS BEGIN TO COME BACK TO FORMER SECTORS NORTH AND

SOUTH). PAVLETIC WARNED THAT IF SUCH ATTITUDES PERSIST,

THE GOVERNMENT AND HDZ WILL NOT BE ABLE TO MAINTAIN

CONTINUED PUBLIC OR PARLIAMENTARY SUPPORT FOR THE

AGREEMENT. (PAVLETIC ALSO UNDERLINED THAT THE OPPOSITION

PARTIES\' WERE SEEKING AN IDEAL SOLUTION AND SAID THAT ONLY

THE HDZ, WITH A REALISTIC VIEW OF THE PROBLEM AT HAND,

CARRIED THE VOTE IN FAVOR OF THE AGREEMENT.) EVEN WITHOUT

THE UNHELPFUL STANCE OF SOME EASTERN SLAVONIAN LEADERS,

IMPLEMENTATION WILL NOT BE EASY OR SWIFT. THERE COULD

HAVE BEEN NO PEACE WITHOUT THE U.S. INITIATIVE AND BECAUSE

OF ITS INFLUENCE, PAVLETIC STRESSED, IT IS VITAL FOR

CONTINUED U.S. ASSISTANCE IN IMPLEMENTATION. PAVLETIC

SAID IT WILL BE DIFFICULT TO REINTRODUCE LAW AND ORDER,

SINCE FORMER INSTITUTIONS HAVE VANISHED WITH THE MASS

MOVEMENT OF PEOPLE AND LOSS OF PROPERTY.

4.THE AMBASSADOR AGREED THAT THERE IS MUCH UNFINISHED

BUSINESS, INCLUDING IMPLEMENTATION OF THE AGREEMENT,

CREATION OF CIVIL SOCIETY, THE RETURN OF MULTI-ETHNIC

WHILE IT DID NOT GIVE CROATIA EVERYTHING IT WANTED,

TERRITORIAL INTEGRITY WITHIN ITS INTERNATIONALLY

AGREEMENT THAT FULLY SATISFIES ONE SIDE, THAT PEACE

REQUIRES COMPROMISES. EVEN THOUGH THE CROATS OF BOSANSKA

RIGHT TO RETURN, RECOVER PROPERTY, AND VOTE. BY VOTING
