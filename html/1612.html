SUBJECT: UNDP RESIDENT REPRESENTATIVE CONSULTS WITH

DONORS ON GOZ RESPONSE TO MALLOCH-BROWN LETTER

REF: HARARE 1016

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS 1.5 B/D.

1. (C) SUMMARY: UNITED NATIONS DEVELOPMENT PROGRAM

(UNDP) RESIDENT REPRESENTATIVE VICTOR ANGELO CONVENED

A MEETING OF DIPLOMATIC HEADS OF MISSION MARCH 28 TO

DISCUSS THE ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT\'S RESPONSE TO THE

MALLOCH-BROWN LETTER (REFTEL). MOST OF THE DIPLOMATS

SAID THAT THE ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT RESPONSE GAVE THE

DONOR COMMUNITY PRECIOUS LITTLE WITH WHICH TO WORK,

ALTHOUGH SOME SAW MERIT IN THE FACT THAT IT ANSWERED

AT ALL. IN GENERAL, HOWEVER, THE DONORS WERE

UNSYMPATHETIC WITH THE GOVERNMENT\'S PLEA FOR

ASSISTANCE ON THE FAST TRACK RESETTLEMENT PROGRAM, A

NON-NEGOTIABLE ITEM. THE LETTER\'S FAILURE TO ADDRESS

THE ISSUE OF RULE OF LAW IN THE BROADEST SENSE WAS THE

PRINCIPAL CONCERN OF MOST DONORS. THEY ASKED ANGELO

TO CONTINUE ENGAGING THE GOVERNMENT, PUTTING ACROSS

THEIR CONCERNS AND ASKING FOR CLARIFICATION OF SOME

THE TERMS USED IN THE LETTER. ALTHOUGH THE MALLOCH-

BROWN INITIATIVE APPEARS TO BE COLLAPSING, THE VICTIM

OF THE UNDP\'S MISBEGOTTEN ROLE OF TACKLING THE LAND

QUESTION INSTEAD OF RULE OF LAW, WE CONTINUE TO

BELIEVE IN THE UTILITY OF DIALOGUE WITH THE

GOVERNMENT. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) UNDP RESIDENT REPRESENTATIVE VICTOR ANGELO

CONVENED DIPLOMATIC HEADS OF MISSION ON MARCH 28 FOR A

DISCUSSION ON THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE\'S RESPONSE TO

UNDP ADMINISTRATOR MALLOCH-BROWN\'S DECEMBER 15 LETTER

TO PRESIDENT MUGABE (REFTEL). THERE WERE BASICALLY

TWO GROUPS: THOSE WHO WERE VERY NEGATIVE (UK, GERMANY,

NETHERLANDS, AND CANADA), AND THOSE WHO WERE LOOKING

FOR SOMETHING POSITIVE IN THE RESPONSE (ITALY, FRANCE,

BELGIUM AND ARGENTINA). (COMMENT: THE LIKELY DONORS

ARE IN THE FORMER, NOT THE LATTER GROUP. END COMMENT.)

3. (C) POSITIVE ASPECTS, AS PITCHED BY ANGELO, WERE

THAT THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE RESPONDED (RATHER THAN

IGNORED THE LETTER), USED DIPLOMATIC LANGUAGE, AND

ENDORSED CONTINUING DIALOGUE WITH THE DONORS, BUT

WITHOUT SPECIFYING MODALITIES. SOME DONORS WELCOMED

THE GOVERNMENT\'S ACCEPTANCE OF A TRUST FUND (WHICH IS

PROVIDED FOR IN THE CONSTITUTION) AND ESTABLISHING A

TECHNICAL UNIT WITHIN THE OFFICES OF THE UNDP, BUT NO

ONE INDICATED THEY ARE READY TO OFFER FUNDING.

(COMMENT: NEITHER THE BRITISH NOR WE ARE PREPARED TO

SUPPORT A TECHNICAL UNIT WITHIN UNDP AT THIS TIME

BECAUSE IT IS IN THE WRONG PLACE: IT ESTABLISHES DONOR

AND NOT ZIMBABWEAN CAPACITY. MOREOVER, ANY TECHNICAL

SUPPORT UNIT AT THIS TIME WOULD SUPPORT THE FAST-TRACK

RESETTLEMENT PROGRAM. END COMMENT.) THE GOVERNMENT\'S

APPEAL TO CONTINUED DIALOGUE WAS UNANIMOUSLY VIEWED AS

A POSITIVE DEVELOPMENT, ALTHOUGH THERE IS NO DEARTH OF

THOSE WHO CONSIDER IT LITTLE MORE THAN AND A COMBINED

PUBLIC RELATIONS EFFORT AND DELAYING TACTIC. ON THE

SUBJECT: UNDP RESIDENT REPRESENTATIVE CONSULTS WITH

DONORS ON GOZ RESPONSE TO MALLOCH-BROWN LETTER

TO ACKNOWLEDGE THAT FIVE DONORS ACTUALLY DELIVERED

SOMETHING TO ADVANCE THE CAUSE OF LAND REFORM IN

ZIMBABWE. MANY HEADS OF MISSION HAD DIFFICULTY

VIEWING FAVORABLY THE ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT\'S REQUEST

FOR ASSISTANCE TO CLEAN UP THE MESS CREATED BY ITS

FAST-TRACK RESETTLEMENT EXERCISE, ABOUT WHICH THE

DONORS HAD WARNED IT.

5. (C) THERE WAS CONSENSUS THAT IT WAS PREMATURE TO

BEGIN ASSISTING THE GOZ\'S LAND REFORM PROGRAM,

PARTICULARLY AS THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE HAS NOT

ADDRESSED THE DONORS\' CONCERNS WITH THE RESPECT TO THE

RULE OF LAW. NEVERTHELESS, ALL DONORS EXPRESSED THE

DESIRE TO FOSTER DIALOGUE WITHIN A SPECIFIED TIME

FRAME WITH BOTH THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE AND THE

NATIONAL ECONOMIC CONSULTATIVE FORUM, A POSSIBLE

VEHICLE FOR SUCH ENGAGEMENT. THE DONORS REQUESTED

VICTOR ANGELO TO SEEK CLARIFICATION ON THE

RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN THE NECF AND THE CABINET LAND

COMMITTEE. REGARDING HUMANITARIAN ASSISTANCE, THE

DONORS INSISTED THAT A CLEAR DISTINCTION HAD TO BE

MADE BETWEEN THAT FOR THE RESETTLEMENT PROGRAM (I.E.,

THE MAN-MADE CRISIS) AND THAT FOR NATURAL DISASTERS

(I.E., DROUGHTS AND FLOODS).

6. (C) ANGELO AGREED TO CONSULT WITH THE ZIMBABWEAN

GOVERNMENT BEGINNING MARCH 30 AND PUT ACROSS THE

DONORS\' VIEWS. HE WILL EXPLORE AMBIGUITIES IN THE

LETTER, INCLUDING WHAT PRECISELY THE ZIMBABWEAN

GOVERNMENT MEANS BY ITS LABELING OF THE CHAOTIC

PROCESS AS A "LEGAL (HOW IT UNDERSTANDS THE RULE OF

LAW), TRANSPARENT AND EQUITABLE (BENEFICIARY

SELECTION) LAND REFORM PROGRAM." THE UNDP RESREP

UNDERSTOOD THAT HE WOULD HAVE TO PACKAGE HIS POINTS IN

SUCH A WAY THAT THE GOVERNMENT DOES NOT SLAM THE DOOR

IN HIS FACE.

7. (C) COMMENT: WE HAVE NUMEROUS PROBLEMS WITH THE

UNDP\'S APPROACH TO THIS ENTERPRISE. RATHER THAN

TRYING TO FIND A SOLUTION TO THE LAND QUESTION, IT

SHOULD HAVE TACKLED THE OVER-ARCHING ISSUE OF THE RULE

OF LAW THAT WILL TRIP UP ANY DONOR HEADQUARTERS AND

MOST LEGISLATIVE BRANCHES. THE UNITED NATIONS IS THE

BEST PLACED, IF NOT THE ONLY, ORGANIZATION ABLE TO DO

SO. THE UNITED NATIONS WAS TRAPPED INTO TAKING ON THE

LAND ISSUE, A FACT THAT HAS PLAYED INTO THE HANDS OF

THE GOZ, WHICH HAS MADE A RHETORICAL DISTINCTION

BETWEEN THE RULE OF LAW ON THE FARMS AND THE RULE OF

LAW IN GENERAL. THIS IS A NON-STARTER, AND ANGELO HAS

NOT BEEN ABLE TO QUASH THIS CYNICAL DIFFERENTIATION.

WE BELIEVE THAT LAND REFORM IS AN INTERNAL ZIMBABWEAN

MATTER, AND THAT THERE SHOULD BE A FRANK AND OPEN

DIALOGUE BETWEEN ALL STAKEHOLDERS. WE SEE NO

PERCENTAGE IN GETTING IN THE MIDDLE OF A GOZ-CFU

DIALOGUE, FOR EXAMPLE. THE MALLOCH-BROWN INITIATIVE

APPEARS TO BE COLLAPSING, ALTHOUGH WE CONTINUE TO

BELIEVE IN THE UTILITY OF GENUINE DIALOGUE. END

COMMENT.
