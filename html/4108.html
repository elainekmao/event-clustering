SUBJECT: ADDITIONAL 2002 AMNESTIES

REF: HANOI 2596

1. (U) ON OCTOBER 18, VIETNAMESE STATE PRESIDENT TRAN DUC

LUONG OFFICIALLY GRANTED AMNESTY TO 3,069 INMATES FROM

PRISONS UNDER THE MANAGEMENT OF EITHER THE MINISTRY OF

PUBLIC SECURITY OR THE MINISTRY OF NATIONAL DEFENSE. THIS

IS IN ADDITION TO THE AMNESTIES FOR 6,223 OTHER PRISONERS

(INCLUDING FOURTEEN FOREIGNERS, ONE OF WHOM WAS AN AMERICAN

CITIZEN) ON THE OCCASION OF VIETNAM\'S NATIONAL DAY ON

SEPTEMBER 2 OF THIS YEAR. WHEN ASKED AT A PRESS CONFERENCE

WHETHER ANY OF THE AMNESTIES RELATED TO POLITICAL CRIMES,

VICE FOREIGN MINISTER NGUYEN PHU BINH PREDICTABLY ASSERTED

THAT "THERE ARE NO POLITICAL PRISONERS IN VIETNAM. THOSE WHO

ARE REHABILITATED AT CAMPS HAD BROKEN THE LAW AND BEEN TRIED

AT COURT." BETWEEN 1990 AND 2001, THE GVN GRANTED AMNESTY

TO A REPORTED TOTAL OF 35,259 INMATES, OF WHOM MORE THAN

23,000 WERE RELEASED IN 2000 ON THE OCCASION OF THE 55TH

ANNIVERSARY OF THE FOUNDING OF THE SRV.

2. (U) ACCORDING TO LOCAL MEDIA, TO QUALIFY FOR AMNESTIES,

INMATES MUST HAVE MANAGED TO PAY ALL OF THEIR "BRACKET

PENALTIES," WHICH INCLUDE FINES AS WELL AS ANY COMPENSATION

FOR THE DAMAGE CAUSED BY THEIR CRIMES. THEY MUST ALSO MEET

OTHER CRITERIA OF ELIGIBILITY, SUCH AS GOOD BEHAVIOR AND

POLITICAL EDUCATION. A SPECIAL "CENTRAL COMMISSION,"

CONSISTING OF REPRESENTATIVES FROM THE MINISTRIES OF PUBLIC

SECURITY, JUSTICE, AND FOREIGN AFFAIRS AS WELL AS FROM THE

OFFICE OF THE PRESIDENT, THE OFFICE OF THE GOVERNMENT, THE

SUPREME PEOPLE\'S PROCURACY, AND THE SUPREME PEOPLE\'S COURT,

IS RESPONSIBLE FOR REVIEWING THE PROPOSED LIST OF INMATES

CONSIDERED ELIGIBLE FOR AMNESTY AND SUBMITTING ITS

RECOMMENDATIONS TO THE STATE PRESIDENT FOR APPROVAL.

3. (U) THERE HAVE BEEN UNCONFIRMED REPORTS OF CORRUPTION

IN THIS YEAR\'S PROCESS. WHILE IN THEORY ALL INFORMATION

ABOUT POSSIBLE ELIGIBILITY FOR AMNESTY SHOULD BE TREATED AS

CLASSIFIED, ALLEGATIONS HAVE SUGGESTED THAT PRISON OFFICIALS

SOMETIMES DISCLOSE THE INFORMATION ABOUT "POSSIBLE"

ELIGIBILITY TO SPECIFIC INMATES, ESPECIALLY THOSE CHARGED OF

DRUG-RELATED OR ECONOMIC CRIMES (WHO ARE DEEMED MORE

"FINANCIALLY CAPABLE"), AND SOLICIT BRIBES TO ENSURE THAT

THESE APPLICATIONS WILL BE TREATED IN A MORE FAVORABLE

MANNER. ACCORDING TO A REPORT FROM INVESTIGATORS AT THE

SUPREME PEOPLE\'S PROCURACY, ONE INMATE CONVICTED OF A DRUG-

RELATED CRIME RECENTLY COMMITTED SUICIDE IN A PRISON IN

NORTHERN VIETNAM DUE TO HIS APPARENT DISAPPOINTMENT THAT HIS

FAMILY HAD NOT MANAGED TO PAY TO SETTLE HIS "BRACKET

PENALTIES," AND HENCE HE WAS STILL NOT ELIGIBLE FOR AMNESTY.

INTERESTINGLY, THE NOT USUALLY VERY INVESTIGATIVE LOCAL

MEDIA HAS ITSELF RAISED THE QUESTION ABOUT HOW THAT INMATE

LEARNED ABOUT HIS POSSIBLE "ELIGIBILITY" IN THE FIRST PLACE.
