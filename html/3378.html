SUBJECT: ITALY WILL GET OUT THE VOTE IN ECOSOC ON CHR CUBA

RESOLUTION

REF: SECSTATE 139472

CLASSIFIED BY: A/POL P. THIEDE FOR REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D)

1. (C) ON JULY 23, IN SEPARATE MEETINGS AT THE MFA, POLOFF

PRESENTED REFTEL DEMARCHE TO NICOLO\' GORETTI DE FLAMINI AND

EMANUELE PIGNATELLI, RESPECTIVELY OFFICE DIRECTORS FOR

CENTRAL AMERICA AND FOR HUMAN RIGHTS. PIGNATELLI CONFIRMED

THE GOI\'S INTENTION TO VOTE FOR THE RESOLUTION AGAIN AND TO

CONFIDENTIAL

LOBBY OTHERS TO SUPPORT IT AS WELL. THE GOI\'S LEADERSHIP

WOULD BE IMPORTANT, PIGNATELLI IMPLIED, SINCE IT WAS

REPRESENTING THE EU PRESIDENCY DUE TO THE ABSENCE OF DENMARK

FROM ECOSOC.

2. (C) PIGNATELLI CONTINUED THAT THE GOI PREFERED

"RECIPROCAL ENCOURAGEMENT" OF, NOT CONFRONTATION WITH, STATES

WITH GOVERNANCE SHORTCOMINGS. THE GOC HAD "MADE SOME

IMPROVEMENTS BUT NEEDS TO GO FURTHER." PIGNATELLI CITED

BETTER CONDITIONS OF DETENTION AS AN EXAMPLE. (HOWEVER,

GORETTI AGREED WITH THE DEMARCHE THAT THE NUMBER OF POLITICAL

PRISONERS HAS INCREASED AND RESPECT FOR HUMAN RIGHTS HAS

DETERIORATED.)

3. (C) PIGNATELLI EXPRESSED CONCERN ABOUT PROCEDURAL

ISSUES AT ECOSOC. HE ASKED WHETHER THE USG WOULD CALL FOR A

VOTE ON THE SEPARATE RESOLUTION ON RACISM. HE HOPED THAT WE

WOULD DO SO, STATING THAT THE GOI WAS AS CONCERNED BY THE

RACISM RESOLUTION AS THE USG WAS BY THE CUBA ONE. ASKED WHY

THE GOI PREFERRED THAT WE TAKE THE LEAD ON THE RACISM

RESOLUTION, PIGNATELLI ASSERTED THAT WE HAD CALLED FOR THE

VOTE IN THE UNCHR, SO IT WAS ONLY LOGICAL THAT WE SHOULD DO

SO AGAIN. HE ALSO ASKED WHETHER THE USG WOULD CALL FOR A

VOTE ON THE CUBA RESOLUTION, IF FOR ANY REASON PERU DID NOT

DO SO. POLOFF REPLIED THAT SHE HAD NO INFORMATION ON THIS

BUT UNDERSCORED THAT THE USG WAS FULLY COMMITTED TO THE

PASSAGE OF THE CUBA RESOLUTION.
