SUBJECT: CROATIA APPROVES USE OF AIRSPACE, AIRFIELDS FOR

CIVAIR TRANSIT OF TROOPS TO MIDEAST

CLASSIFIED BY: AMBASSADOR LAWRENCE ROSSIN FOR REASONS 1.5 (B.D)

1. (C) ON FEBRUARY 26, THE CROATIAN CABINET PASSED A FORMAL

DECISION (TEXT AT PARA 5 BELOW) TO AGREE TO A USG REQUEST FOR

THE USE OF CROATIAN AIRSPACE AND AIRFIELDS BY COMMERCIAL

AIRCRAFT FOR REFUELING AND STOPOVER IN SUPPORT OF MILITARY

OPERATIONS IN THE MIDDLE EAST AGAINST IRAQ. THE DECISION

CREATES AN INTER-AGENCY WORKING GROUP, TO BE CHAIRED BY AN

ASSISTANT MINISTER OF DEFENSE, WHICH WILL IMPLEMENT AND

MONITOR ACTIVITIES UNDER THE DECISION. ALTHOUGH THE DECISION

GOES INTO EFFECT ON FEBRUARY 26, IT MAY TAKE AS LONG AS TWO

WEEKS FOR THE WORKING GROUP TO BE FORMALLY ESTABLISHED;

CROATIA WILL NOT BE ABLE TO RESPOND TO SPECIFIC REQUESTS FOR

FLIGHTS UNTIL THAT TAKES PLACE.

2. (C) THIS DECISION WAS PRESENTED TO THE PARLIAMENT

PRECISELY AS PM RACAN TOLD THE AMBASSADOR ON FEBRUARY 24 IT

WOULD BE (REFTEL). AT THE SABOR\'S VERSION OF "QUESTION TIME"

ON FEBRUARY 26, RACAN TOLD MP\'S THAT THE DECISION WAS TAKEN

"WITH THE UNDERSTANDING" OF EUROPEAN GOVERNMENTS WHICH ARE

DECIDING ON CROATIA\'S EU MEMBERSHIP APPLICATION. RACAN ALSO

SAID THAT CROATIA\'S OFFER OF LOGISTICS SUPPORT WAS A

MINIMALIST POSITION. "SOME COUNTRIES WHICH ARE AGAINST THE

APPROACH OF THE UNITED STATES ARE PROVIDING MORE LOGISTICS

SUPPORT THAN WE ARE OFFERING," RACAN EXPLAINED. RACAN

INSISTED THAT THE DECISION TAKEN BY THE GOVERNMENT IS WITHIN

THE COMPETENCE OF THE CABINET, BUT ANY REQUEST FOR ADDITIONAL

SUPPORT WOULD BE PRESENTED TO THE PARLIAMENT, AS REQUIRED BY

CROATIAN LAW.

3. (C) IN A FEBRUARY 25 MEETING WITH THE AMBASSADOR,

PRESIDENT MESIC WAS MORE SUPPORTIVE OF U.S. POLICIES/REQUESTS

ON IRAQ THAN WE EXPECTED, CONSIDERING SOME OF HIS RECENT

STATEMENTS TO THE LOCAL PRESS. MESIC SAID THAT HE SUPPORTS

THE GOC DECISION TO AGREE TO THE USG REQUEST FOR LANDING

RIGHTS IN CROATIA. HE SAID THAT HE RAISED THE ISSUE DURING

HIS JANUARY 24 MEETING IN PARIS WITH PRESIDENT CHIRAC, WHO

CHARACTERIZED CROATIA\'S DECISION TO PROVIDE FLOW-THROUGH

ACCESS AS APPROPRIATE.

4. (C) IN A FEBRUARY 25 MEETING WITH THE AMBASSADOR, DEFMIN

ANTUNOVIC SAID THAT THE GOVERNMENT DECISION WOULD APPROVE OUR

REQUEST WITH VERY NARROW PARAMETERS; ANY REQUEST BEYOND

AIRSPACE, LANDING AND REFUELING WOULD HAVE TO BE TAKEN TO THE

PARLIAMENT. CROATIA\'S DEFENSE LAW REQUIRES SABOR APPROVAL

BEFORE THE ENTRY OF ANY FOREIGN TROOPS INTO CROATIA. THE GOC

INTERPRETATION IS THAT THE SABOR WOULD HAVE TO APPROVE IN

ADVANCE -- WITH A TWO-THIRDS MAJORITY -- IF A U.S. SOLDIER IS

TO SET FOOT ON CROATIAN SOIL. UNDER THE CURRENT DECISION,

TROOPS WILL HAVE TO REMAIN ON BOARD THE AIRCRAFT DURING

REFUELING. IN THE CASE OF AN AIRCRAFT BREAKDOWN, THE GOC

COULD OFFER OVERNIGHT STAYS WITHOUT OVERSTEPPING ITS LEGAL

BOUNDARIES. SUCH A CASE WOULD BE DECLARED AN EMERGENCY STOP

UNDER ICAO RULES AND THE GOC WOULD BE LEGALLY COVERED.

BASED ON THE ARTICLE 30 SECTION 2 OF THE LAW ON THE REPUBLIC

OF CROATIA (OFFICIAL GAZETTE, NO 101/98, 15/200 AND

117/2001), GOVERNMENT OF CROATIA AT ITS SESSION ON 26

FEBRUARY 2003, REACHED THE FOLLOWING

DECISION ON THE USE OF AIRSPACE AND AIRPORTS OF THE REPUBLIC

OF CROATIA FOR THE NEEDS OF COALITION FORCES LED BY THE

UNITED STATES OF AMERICA

I. BASED ON REPUBLIC OF CROATIA\'S DETERMINATION TO COMBAT

INTERNATIONAL TERRORISM AND PREVENT PROLIFERATION OF WEAPONS

OF MASS DESTRUCTION, REPEATEDLY DECLARED BY CROATIA\'S

GOVERNMENT, PARLIAMENT AND THE PRESIDENT OF THE REPUBLIC,

CROATIA\'S GOVERNMENT WILL WITHIN ITS ABILITY, MEET THE

REQUEST TO EXTEND ITS SERVICES TO COALITION FORCES LED BY THE

UNITED STATES IN CONDUCTING OPERATIONS AIMED AT DISARMING

IRAQ, AS IT IS DETERMINED IN UN SECURITY RESOLUTION 1441.

II. IN EVENT THAT THERE IS A REQUEST AS STATED IN THE ITEM I

OF THIS DECISION, CROATIAN GOVERNMENT APPROVES THE USE OF

CROATIA\'S AIRSPACE FOR OVER-FLIGHTS, LANDING AND REFUELING

CIVIL TRANSPORTATION AIRCRAFT AT CROATIAN AIRPORTS.
