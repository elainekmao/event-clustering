SUBJECT: FM MUDENGE LECTURES THE DIPLOMATIC CORPS

CLASSIFIED BY CDA RICHARD ROTH FOR REASONS 1.5 (B) AND

(D).

1. (C) SUMMARY AND COMMENT: IN AN EXCRUCIATING TWO-

HOUR LECTURE TO THE FULL DIPLOMATIC CORPS SEPTEMBER 20

ZIMBABWE FOREIGN MINISTER MUDENGE EXTOLLED THE GREAT

SUCCESS OF THE ABUJA AGREEMENT BECAUSE "EVERYONE

AGREED ON THE CENTRALITY OF THE LAND ISSUE", PRAISED

THE SADC HEADS OF STATE MEETING (SEPTEMBER 10-11)

BECAUSE THEY AFFIRMED THE ABUJA AGREEMENT ON LAND

REFORM, AND LAID DOWN A MARKER ON THE NEED FOR FOREIGN

GOVERNMENTS TO "HONOR AND RESPECT" ZIMBABWE\'S LAW

PROHIBITING THE FOREIGN FINANCING OF POLITICAL

PARTIES. HE ALSO DEVOTED A PORTION OF HIS

PRESENTATION ON THE RECENT KIGALI POLITICAL COMMITTEE

MEETING ON THE DRC (SEE SEPTEL). WHILE TRYING TO

REMAIN WITHIN HIS ROLE AS FOREIGN MINISTER MUDENGE

FREQUENTLY DONNED HIS FAR MORE PARTISAN HAT AS A ZANU-

PF MEMBER OF PARLIAMENT (MP), CONVENIENTLY SWEEPING

ASIDE THE OTHER SUBSTANTIVE ASPECTS OF THE ABUJA AND

SADC MEETINGS ON RULE OF LAW, CESSATION OF

INTIMIDATION AND GOVERNMENT-CONDONED VIOLENCE. IT

WILL BE VERY HARD TO LEVEL THIS PLAYING FIELD IF

MUDENGE ACCURATELY REFLECTS THE POLICY INTENTIONS OF

MUGABE AND HIS INNER CIRCLE, WHICH WE BELIEVE HE DOES.

END SUMMARY AND COMMENT.

2. (C) IN DESCRIBING THE SEPTEMBER 6 ABUJA MEETING,

MUDENGE SAID THAT INITIALLY IT WAS HEADED FOR A

DISASTER BECAUSE OF THE TOTALLY INCOMPATIBLE

PERSPECTIVES OF THE GOZ WITH THE OTHER PARTICIPANTS.

IN HIS VIEW, IT WAS THROUGH THE EFFORTS OF NIGERIAN

PRESIDENT OBASANJO THAT THE MEETING WAS "SAVED"

BECAUSE HE WAS ABLE TO "DOWNPLAY" THE OTHER

"EXTRANEOUS" ISSUES THAT THE OTHER DELEGATES WANTED TO

DISCUSS AND PLACE THE FOCUS ON THE LAND REFORM

QUESTION. MUDENGE CLAIMED THAT THE OTHER DELEGATES

AGREED IN THE END TO THE "CENTRALITY" OF THE LAND

ISSUE, BUT EXPRESSED THEIR CONCERNS ABOUT HOW IT WAS

BEING IMPLEMENTED. HE MENTIONED RULE OF LAW, VIOLENCE

AND THE IMPACT ON THE ECONOMY AS BEING THE PRINCIPLE

CONCERNS OF THE OTHER DELEGATES. IN THE END THE

COMPROMISE WAS ACCEPTED OUTLINING THE PRINCIPLES OF

WHAT HE DESCRIBED AS A "PACKAGE DEAL" WITH THE

COMMITMENTS OF EACH PARTY CLEARLY DEFINED.

3. (C) MUDENGE ASSERTED THAT THE GOZ INTENDS TO HONOR

THE SPIRIT AND THE LETTER OF THE ABUJA AGREEMENT, BUT

WILL DO SO IN A VERY DELIBERATE FASHION. HE

ACKNOWLEDGED THAT THE GOVERNMENT HAD CONDUCTED A 12-

HOUR INTERNAL MEETING OF THE POLITBURO TO DISCUSS THE

IMPLICATIONS OF ABUJA AND HOW TO IMPLEMENT IT. HE

CLAIMED THAT THE GOVERNMENT WAS ACCUSED BY SOME

POLITBURO MEMBERS OF SELLING OUT. NEVERTHELESS, THE

GOZ WILL PUT IN PLACE A MECHANISM TO IMPLEMENT THE

ABUJA AGREEMENT NEXT WEEK. HE FRANKLY ACKNOWLEDGED

THAT THERE HAVE BEEN INCIDENTS OF VIOLENCE UP UNTIL

NOW, BUT ASCRIBED THIS TO THE FACT THAT THERE HAD NOT

BEEN AN IMPLEMENTING MECHANISM IN PLACE. HE STATED

FLATLY THAT THE GOVERNMENT AND ZANU-PF WILL GREATLY

REDUCE VIOLENT INCIDENTS AND BRING ABOUT A RETURN TO

NORMALCY. HE ALSO SAID THAT THE GOZ WILL STOP ANY NEW

OCCUPATIONS.

4. (C) MUDENGE ACKNOWLEDGED THAT THE GOVERNMENT HAD

NOT YET HAD A SERIOUS MEETING WITH THE COMMERCIAL

FARMERS, SOME OF WHOM BELIEVE THERE WILL BE "FULL

COMPENSATION" FOR THEIR LAND. BUT, HE CLAIMED, MANY

WILL NOT TAKE COMFORT FROM THIS BECAUSE THE ISSUE IS

NOT MONEY, BUT LAND, AND MANY COMMERCIAL FARMERS WILL

TRY TO ENSURE THAT ABUJA WILL NOT SUCCEED. "WE INTEND

TO FULFILL OUR COMMITMENTS AND WE HOPE THAT THE UK,

AUSTRALIA AND CANADA WILL FULFILL THEIRS". HE ALSO

EXPRESSED THE HOPE THAT OTHERS WOULD ASSOCIATE

THEMSELVES WITH THIS COMMITMENT, I.E. BE PREPARED TO

CONTRIBUTE MONEY FOR LAND REFORM.

6. (C) AFTER AN IMPASSIONED PLEA FOR UN SECURITY

COUNCIL PERM-5 MEMBERS TO URGE THEIR CAPITOLS TO

SUPPORT AN INSERTION OF A LARGE UN PEACEKEEPING FORCE

IN THE DRC TO REPLACE THE SMALL UN OBSERVER FORCE,

MUDENGE ADDED AN UNANNOUNCED ITEM TO HIS AGENDA.

TELLING THE ASSEMBLED DIP CORPS THAT HE WAS APPEALING

TO THEM AS A MINISTER AND NOT AS A ZANU-PF MP, HE

LAUNCHED INTO A NONE TOO THINLY VEILED WARNING THAT

THEIR GOVERNMENTS MUST "HONOR AND RESPECT" THE

RECENTLY PASSED GOZ LAW PROHIBITING THE FOREIGN

FINANCING OF POLITICAL PARTIES. HE SAID BLUNTLY THAT

THIS WOULD MEAN THAT DIRECT FOREIGN SUPPORT OF THE MDC

IS FORBIDDEN. AS FOREIGN MINISTER HE HOPED THAT

ACCREDITED GOVERNMENTS WOULD SEE ADHERENCE TO THIS LAW

PAGE 03 HARARE 02787 02 OF 02 211042Z

IN THE CONTEXT OF GOOD RELATIONS. TO STRENGTHEN HIS

POINT MUDENGE CLAIMED THAT THE GOZ HAD "COPIED FROM

SOME OF YOUR LAWS" IN WRITING ITS OWN SIMILAR

LEGISLATION. "THIS LAW IS IMPORTANT FOR OUR

SOVEREIGNTY, INDEPENDENCE AND SELF-RESPECT. LET US

MAKE OUR OWN MISTAKES ACCORDING TO OUR NATIONAL

CHARACTER. WE HAVE OUR OWN POLITICAL CULTURE, PLEASE

LET US FOLLOW IT ON OUR OWN."

7. (C) SHEDDING HIS MINISTERIAL CLOAK, MUDENGE

INFORMED THE AUDIENCE THAT ZANU-PF INTENDS TO FIGHT

VERY STRONGLY AGAINST EXTERNAL INTERFERENCE. AS

FOREIGN MINISTER HE HOPED THAT HE WOULD NOT HAVE TO

CALL IN ANY OF THE AMBASSADORS OR CHARGES TO CONFRONT

THEM WITH VIOLATIONS BY THEIR GOVERNMENTS OF

ZIMBABWE\'S LAWS. HOWEVER, HE ADDED, HE WOULD NOT

HESITATE TO DO SO IF THE GOZ\'S INFORMATION WAS

SUFFICIENT TO "PROVE" THAT FOREIGN GOVERNMENTS WERE

BREAKING THE GOZ\'S LAWS.

ADDITIONAL COMMENT:

-------------------

8. (C) AS ONE OF THE HARDLINERS WITHIN THE GOZ AND

ZANU-PF, MUDENGE GAVE A VERY FRANK OUTLINE OF THE

GOVERNMENT\'S STRATEGY: PORTRAY RECENT DEVELOPMENTS AS

SUPPORTING THE GOZ\'S POLICIES ON LAND REFORM, DOWNPLAY

ANY EFFORTS TO FOCUS ON RULE OF LAW, HUMAN RIGHTS, AND

INTIMIDATION ISSUES, AND CAST THE UPCOMING ELECTION AS

A "VICTORY" FOR ZANU-PF IN FINALLY GETTING BACK THE

LAND FOR THE MAJORITY OF ZIMBABWE\'S CITIZENS. A

BACKGROUND THEME WILL BE TO BLAME ANY LACK OF SUCCESS

ON EXTERNAL INTERFERENCE AND TO TRY AND LIMIT ANY

SUPPORT TO THE MDC TO THE BAREST MINIMUM. IN THE

CONTEXT OF THIS MEETING THERE WASN\'T EVEN A HINT AT

THE POSSIBILITY OF ELECTION MONITORS/OBSERVERS OR

PARTICIPATION BY EXTERNAL ACTORS. GIVEN MUDENGE\'S

MARKER ON ASSISTANCE TO POLITICAL PARTIES, WE STRONGLY

BELIEVE THAT GETTING INDEPENDENT ELECTORAL PROGRAMS IN

HERE WILL BE A VERY TOUGH CHALLENGE AND HIGHLY

UNWELCOMED BY THE MUGABE GOVERNMENT.
