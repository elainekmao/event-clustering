1. SUMMARY. REACTION TO OMB ADMINISTRATOR JOHN GRAHAM\'S

MESSAGE ON REGULATORY REFORM IN THE U.S. AND EUROPE RANGED

FROM A LIVELY GIVE-AND-TAKE AMONG SENIOR GOI OFFICIALS,

INCLUDING A NOTED EU PARLIAMENTARIAN, TO POLITE CURIOSITY

AMONG BUSINESS LEADERS. WHILE BOTH AUDIENCES ACKNOWLEDGED

THE UTILITY OF DOING STUDIES ON THE IMPACT OF PROPOSED

REGULATIONS, THERE WAS NO CONSENSUS WHETHER THE EUROPEAN

COMMISSION SHOULD MAKE SUCH STUDIES MANDATORY BEFORE ISSUING

REGULATIONS. GOI OFFICIALS REPRESENT A POTENTIAL BASE OF

SUPPORT FOR THE USG AS WE MAKE OUR CASE BEFORE THE COMMISSION

ON THE EU\'S PLANNED REGULATORY REFORM. GETTING ITALIAN

BUSINESSES ENGAGED IN THE ISSUE WILL BE A MORE DIFFICULT

TASK. END SUMMARY.

2. OMB ADMINISTRATOR JOHN GRAHAM CAME TO ROME ON NOVEMBER 13

AS PART OF A PUBLIC-AFFAIRS SPONSORED PROGRAM, WHICH INCLUDED

STOPS IN BERLIN, COPENHAGEN, BRUSSELS AND PARIS. THE EMBASSY

HOSTED A ROUNDTABLE WITH NOTABLE ITALIAN GOVERNMENT

OFFICIALS, INCLUDING: GIUSEPPE GARGANI, EU PARLIAMENTARIAN

AND CHAIR OF THE EU PARLIAMENTARY COMMITTEE ON LEGAL AFFAIRS

AND THE INTERNAL MARKET; ALESSANDRO PALANZA, VICE SECRETARY

GENERAL, CHAMBER OF DEPUTIES; MADDALENA FILIPPI, JUDICIAL

ADVISOR TO THE MINISTER OF PUBLIC ADMINISTRATION AND A MEMBER

OF THE OECD WORKING GROUP ON REGULATORY REFORM; STEFANO

RODOTA, PRESIDENT, ITALIAN DATA PRIVACY COMMISSION AND CHAIR

OF THE EU WORKING GROUP ON DATA PRIVACY; AND CLAUDIO

TUCCIARELLI, DIRECTOR GENERAL, REFORMS MINISTRY AND ADVISOR

TO THE PRIME MINISTER OF REGULATORY REFORM. AN AFTERNOON

SESSION WAS HOSTED BY CONFINDUSTRIA, ITALY\'S LARGEST

EMPLOYERS ASSOCIATION, WITH INVITED MEMBERS OF THE ITALIAN

3. DURING THE EMBASSY\'S ROUNDTABLE, QUESTIONS FOR GRAHAM

FOCUSED ON SEVERAL KEY ASPECTS: WHAT TYPE OF COST-BENEFIT

ANALYSIS IS DONE? WHAT IS THE BALANCE OF EXPERTISE IN OMB

AMONG ECONOMISTS, TECHNICAL SPECIALISTS, SOCIAL POLICY

EXPERTS? WHAT IS THE METHODOLOGY USED? WHAT IS OMB\'S

(GRAHAM\'S OFFICE) RELATIONSHIP WITH STATE AND LOCAL

LEGISLATORS, AUTHORITIES AND REGULATORS? WHAT GUIDELINES ARE

USED TO DETERMINE WHEN A REGULATION IS NEEDED? WHAT IS OMB\'S

RELATIONSHIP WITH INDEPENDENT REGULATORY AGENCIES, SUCH AS

THE SEC AND THE FEDERAL RESERVE BANK? WHAT ARE THE PRIVACY

CONSIDERATIONS? WHAT REVIEW, IF ANY, DOES OMB CONDUCT OF

LEGISLATION PASSED BY CONGRESS THAT IN EFFECT, ARE REGULATORY

IN NATURE? GRAHAM HIGHLIGHTED MANY ASPECTS OF U.S. POLICIES

AND PROCEDURES: COST BENEFIT ANALYSES INCLUDE QUANTITATIVE

ECONOMIC MEASUREMENTS AS WELL AS CONSIDERATION OF FAIRNESS

AND EQUITY ISSUES; HIS 50-PERSON STAFF REPRESENTS ALL THE

SOCIAL SCIENCES; GUIDELINES/METHODOLOGY ARE ON OMB\'S WEBPAGE.

HE NOTED THAT OMB HAS NO REGULATORY OVERSIGHT OVER THE SEC

AND THE FED, AND CAN ONLY PROVIDE ADVISORY OPINIONS ON DRAFT

LEGISLATION.

4. OMB\'S POLICY TO CONDUCT REGULATORY IMPACT STUDIES OF THE

MOST IMPORTANT DRAFT REGULATIONS GENERATED THE GREATEST

INTEREST. PALANZA OF THE CHAMBER OF DEPUTIES AND JUDICIAL

ADVISOR FILIPPI SEEMED TO ACKNOWLEDGED THE DESIRABILITY, IF

NOT NECESSITY, OF DOING SUCH STUDIES BEFORE REGULATIONS WERE

ISSUED. PALANZA ACKNOWLEDGED THE UTILITY OF THE OECD\'S

WORKING GROUP ON REGULATIONS AND REGULATORY REFORM, AND

QUESTIONED WHETHER THE EU COULD ADOPT SIMILAR PROCESSES.

FILIPPI ALSO EXPRESSED INTEREST IN EX-POST-FACTO REVIEW OF

REGULATIONS ONCE IMPLEMENTED.

5. THE EU\'S PLAN FOR REGULATORY REFORM ALSO PROMPTED A

GIVE-AND-TAKE DISCUSSION. GRAHAM RAISED THE QUESTIONS ABOUT

WHOM IN THE COMMISSION WILL BE RESPONSIBLE FOR QUALITY

CONTROL AND PEER REVIEW. IN RESPONSE, GARGANI NOTED THAT

EUROPE HAS NO STANDARD FOR REGULATORY IMPACT STUDIES; THERE\'S

NO UNIVERSAL TRADITION OF DOING SO. HE EXPRESSED THAT AMONG

HIS COMMITTEE MEMBERS, THERE\'S SOME SKEPTICISM THAT SUCH

STUDIES SHOULD BE DONE; HE SEEMED TO HEDGE HIS PERSONAL

OPINION. HE QUESTIONED HOW THE U.S. DEFINES EFFICIENCY AND

FAIRNESS IN ITS COST-BENEFIT ANALYSES, WHICH GRAHAM HAD

UNDERSCORED ARE PART OF THE U.S. REVIEW ALONG WITH A PURE

QUANTITATIVE IMPACT ANALYSIS. GARGANI ALSO NOTED, SUBTLETY,

THAT COSTS AND EFFICIENCY ARE NOT ALWAYS THE DECIDING FACTORS

BEHIND REGULATIONS, PARTICULARLY IN CASES OF LEGISLATION

PASSED BY PARLIAMENTS THAT IN EFFECT, CREATE REGULATIONS.

ACKNOWLEDGING THIS IS SOMETIMES THE CASE IN THE UNITED

STATES, GRAHAM ARGUED, HOWEVER, THIS IS NOT A REASON TO

IGNORE REGULATORY IMPACT ANALYSES.

6. DESPITE THE DIFFERENCES IN OPINIONS AMONG THE ROUNDTABLE

PARTICIPANTS, ALL WERE EAGER TO READ THE OFFICIAL U.S.

COMMENTS ON THE COMMISSION\'S REGULATORY REFORM ACTION PLAN.

THEY EXPRESSED APPRECIATION OF ACTIVE U.S. ENGAGEMENT IN THIS

ISSUE.

7. IN CONTRAST TO THE LIVELY DEBATE OF THE ROUNDTABLE, THE

SESSION HOSTED BY CONFINDUSTRIA DEMONSTRATED ITALIAN

BUSINESSES\' MINIMAL ENGAGEMENT IN THE ISSUE OF REGULATORY

REFORM. WITH THE EXCEPTION OF THE REPRESENTATIVE FROM THE

ITALIAN CHEMICAL MANUFACTURERS ASSOCIATION, THE PARTICIPANTS

LISTENED TO GRAHAM WITH POLITE ATTENTION. IN CONTRAST, THE

CHEMICAL MANUFACTURING REPRESENTATIVE SPOKE PASSIONATELY AND

INTELLIGENTLY ABOUT THE RISK OF DISENGAGEMENT, USING THE

COMMISSION\'S WHITE PAPER ON CHEMICAL PRODUCTS AS AN EXAMPLE.

GRAHAM ALSO NOTED THE FLAWS IN THIS WHITE PAPER, AND

QUESTIONED HOW THE COMMISSION COULD IMPOSE THE SAME TOXICITY

TESTS FOR CHEMICALS THAT COULD PRESENT WIDESPREAD EXPOSURE

RISK AND THOSE WITH MINIMAL EXPOSURE RISKS. THE CHEMICAL

MANUFACTURERS\' REPRESENTATIVE UNDERSCORED THE DESIRABILITY OF

HAVING INPUT FROM THIRD PARTIES, INCLUDING THE UNITED STATES,

ON REGULATORY REFORM ISSUES. IN RESPONSE TO GRAHAM\'S INQUIRY

ABOUT HOW ITALIAN BUSINESSES LOBBY ON ISSUES IN BRUSSELS,

PANELISTS ACKNOWLEDGE THE DIFFICULTY IN BEING EFFECTIVE AND

IN BEING "LISTENED TO", THEY REMARKED.

8. COMMENT: POST BELIEVES ADMINISTRATOR GRAHAM\'S VISIT COULD

HELP GENERATE A MORE IN-DEPTH EXCHANGE WITH GOI OFFICIALS,

ESPECIALLY THOSE WHO HAVE HANDS-ON INVOLVEMENT WITH

REGULATORY REFORM, ON THE COMMISSION\'S PLANS. WE APPRECIATE

WASHINGTON AND USEU\'S SUPPORT FOR THIS VISIT.
