SUBJECT: IFES TEAM ASKED TO LEAVE ZIMBABWE

CLASSIFIED BY POLITICAL SECTION CHIEF MATT HARRINGTON. REASONS:

1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) A WEEK AFTER THEIR ARRIVAL IN ZIMBABWE, A TWO-MEMBER

ADVANCE TEAM FROM THE INTERNATIONAL FOUNDATION FOR ELECTION

SYSTEMS (IFES) WAS ASKED BY THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE TO LEAVE

THE COUNTRY. IFES AFRICA PROGRAM DIRECTOR TOM BAYER AND SENIOR

PROGRAM OFFICER LAURIE COOPER ARRIVED IN ZIMBABWE ON SEPTEMBER 5

TO CONDUCT AN INITIAL ASSESSMENT OF THE POLITICAL SITUATION AND

ELECTION PREPARATIONS, AND TO CLARIFY THE PARAMETERS OF IFES\'S

PROPOSED ELECTION OBSERVATION EFFORT. THEY HAD PLANNED TO REMAIN

IN ZIMBABWE UNTIL SEPTEMBER 15. DESPITE THEIR BEST EFFORTS TO

MEET WITH THE KEY GOZ OFFICIALS RESPONSIBLE FOR OVERSEEING THE

PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION, THE IFES TEAM RAN INTO A BRICK WALL. WHEN

BAYER SUBMITTED A LETTER SEEKING AN APPOINTMENT WITH REGISTRAR-

GENERAL TOBAIWA MUDEDE, HE RECEIVED A CURT WRITTEN RESPONSE FROM

AN UNDERLING DECLINING THE REQUEST TO MEET AND ADDING THAT "WE

...WILL NOT NEED YOUR TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE ON ELECTION MATTERS,

PARTICULARLY IN VIEW OF YOUR LAST U.S. PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION WHICH

DEMONSTRATED THE CONTRARY. WE HAVE NOT SEEN AFRICANS INVOLVED IN

THE OBSERVATION OF EUROPEAN/AMERICAN ELECTIONS...ZIMBABWE WILL

SATISFACTORILY MANAGE THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION WITHOUT YOUR

ASSISTANCE." IN A SUBSEQUENT CHANCE MEETING, MUDEDE BERATED

COOPER, TELLING HER THAT THE GOZ WAS FULLY CAPABLE OF RUNNING ITS

ELECTIONS WITHOUT ASSISTANCE.

2. (C) ON SEPTEMBER 11, BAYER WAS VISITED AT HIS HOTEL BY THREE

MEN WHO IDENTIFIED THEMSELVES AS DEPARTMENT OF IMMIGRATION

OFFICERS, ONE OF WHOM PRESENTED VERIFYING IDENTIFICATION. THEY

ASKED WHAT THE TEAM WAS DOING HERE, WITH WHOM THEY WERE MEETING,

AND WHY BAYER AND COOPER THOUGHT IFES ASSISTANCE WAS NEEDED. THE

GOZ OFFICIALS REQUESTED THAT BAYER AND COOPER PRESENT THEMSELVES

LATER IN THE DAY AT THE DEPARTMENT OF IMMIGRATION\'S HEADQUARTERS.

AT THE AFTERNOON MEETING, THE IMMIGRATION OFFICERS CITED "WORRY"

AND "SKEPTICISM" ABOUT IFES\'S ACTIVITIES, PLUS IFES\'S ALLEGED LACK

OF IMMIGRATION DOCUMENTATION AND FAILURE TO PROVIDE SUFFICIENT

NOTICE OF THE TEAM\'S ARRIVAL TO RELEVANT GOVERNMENT OFFICIALS.

BAYER AND COOPER WERE TOLD TO LEAVE THE COUNTRY AND APPLY FOR A

WORK PERMIT UPON THEIR RETURN TO THE UNITED STATES. ASKED WHETHER

THE TWO WERE BEING DEPORTED, THE RANKING IMMIGRATION OFFICER SAID

"NO." WHEN BAYER INQUIRED WHETHER HE AND COOPER WOULD BE

PERMITTED TO RETURN TO ZIMBABWE, THE GOZ OFFICIAL SAID THEY WOULD

BE WELCOME TO COME BACK "ON HOLIDAY."

3. (C) BAYER TOLD POLITICAL SECTION CHIEF ON SEPTEMBER 13 THAT HE

AND COOPER PLANNED TO DEPART ZIMBABWE LATER THAT DAY. IFES, HE

SAID, WOULD TAKE THE NEXT WEEK TO ASSESS THE IMPACT OF THE TEAM\'S

DIFFICULTIES HERE AND SUBSEQUENTLY TOUCH BASE WITH THE DEPARTMENT

AND EMBASSY HARARE TO DISCUSS NEXT STEPS. BAYER ACKNOWLEDGED THAT

IFES HAD INITIALLY PREFERRED TO MAINTAIN A CERTAIN DISTANCE FROM

THE EMBASSY WHILE CONDUCTING ITS ACTIVITIES HERE. HE THOUGHT,

HOWEVER, THAT A CHANGE IN TACTICS MIGHT NOW BE NECESSARY, AND THAT

THE EMBASSY MIGHT IN THE FUTURE HAVE TO PLAY A MORE ACTIVE LIAISON

ROLE BETWEEN IFES AND THE GOZ.

COMMENT

-------

4. (C) IT IS NOT CLEAR WHETHER THIS IS THE END OF THE ROAD HERE

FOR IFES AS AN ORGANIZATION OR THESE TWO INDIVIDUALS AS IFES

REPRESENTATIVES. THE GOZ APPEARS TO HAVE DECIDED THAT IT DOES NOT

WANT OUTSIDERS (AT LEAST FROM THE WEST) OBSERVING THE RUN-UP TO

ELECTIONS, AND WE EXPECT THE GOZ TO TRY TO STYMIE IFES AT EVERY

TURN. (N.B. WE DO NOT KNOW IF THIS DECISION IS IRREVOCABLE OR

SUBJECT TO ADDITIONAL REGIONAL AND NON-REGIONAL PRESSURES.) WE

AGREE WITH BAYER THAT THE EMBASSY WILL NEED TO PLAY A MORE VISIBLE

ROLE IN SUPPORTING IFES IN-COUNTRY, INCLUDING KEEPING THE

GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE INFORMED OF THE TEAM\'S PROPOSED ACTIVITIES,

IF THEY ARE TO HAVE ANY CHANCE AT SUCCESS. AS A FIRST STEP, WE

PLAN TO SEND A TEMPERATE DIPLOMATIC NOTE TO THE GOZ EXPRESSING OUR

CONCERN ABOUT THE IFES TEAM\'S DE FACTO EXPULSION, EMPHASIZING THAT

THEY ARE A HIGHLY-RESPECTED, INTERNATIONALLY RECOGNIZED

ORGANIZATION AND NOT PART OF THE USG. WE INTEND TO EXPRESS OUR

STRONG SUPPORT FOR THEIR WORK HERE, AND SEEK CLARIFICATION OF THE

PROCEDURES IFES MUST FOLLOW IN ORDER TO CONTINUE WITH THEIR

ACTIVITIES. WE RECOMMEND HOLDING ANY OTHER ACTIONS IN ABEYANCE

UNTIL WE RECEIVE AN OFFICIAL GOVERNMENT RESPONSE, THE SUBSTANCE

(AND TONE) OF WHICH SHOULD GUIDE OUR NEXT STEPS. WE NOTE FOR THE

RECORD THAT MUDEDE HAS ALSO REFUSED TO MEET WITH THE EU OFFICIAL

SENT HERE TO PREPARE A LONG-TERM MONITORING INITIATIVE SIMILAR TO

THE ONE ENVISAGED BY IFES.
