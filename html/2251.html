SUBJECT: PEACEFUL PROTEST OUTSIDE NATIONAL ASSEMBLY

1. (U) A FEW DOZEN INDIVIDUALS TOOK TO THE STREETS OF

HANOI FOR SEVERAL HOURS ON DECEMBER 3 FOR WHAT APPEARED TO

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 HANOI 03173 031036Z

BE A PEACEFUL PROTEST AIMED AT MEMBERS OF THE NATIONAL

ASSEMBLY DURING ITS CURRENT SESSION. BECAUSE THE STREETS

LEADING TO THE NA BUILDING HAVE BEEN BLOCKED SINCE THE NA

OPENING, THE PROTESTERS CONGREGATED AS CLOSE AS THEY COULD,

DIRECTLY IN FRONT OF THE FOREIGN MINISTRY AND NEAR THE HO

CHI MINH MAUSOLEUM.

2. (U) PROTESTERS CAME AT LEAST FROM KHANH HOA, KIEN

GIANG, VINH LONG, AND DONG THAP PROVINCES, JUDGING FROM

SIGNS AND BANNERS THEY HELD AS THEY SAT QUIETLY. SEVERAL

SIGNS READ "SOS TO THE STANDING COMMITTEE OF THE NATIONAL

ASSEMBLY." OTHER SIGNS VARIOUSLY COMPLAINED ABOUT THE

PLIGHT OF FARMERS AND LAND SEIZURES, INSUFFICIENT DEMOCRACY

IN VIETNAM, AND ILLEGAL SALES OF PUBLIC PROPERTY BY

OFFICIALS.

3. (U) AT LEAST FORTY UNIFORMED POLICE OBSERVED THE

PROTESTERS FROM NEARBY. THERE WERE NO INDICATIONS THAT

POLICE MADE ANY ARRESTS OR TOOK PROTESTERS INTO DETENTION.

THE PROTESTERS APPARENTLY DISPERSED ONCE THE DAILY NA

SESSION CONCLUDED. IT IS NOT CLEAR WHETHER THE PROTESTERS

HAD OBTAINED PRIOR PERMISSION FOR THE RALLY.

4. (U) COMMENT: THIS INCIDENT IS SIMILAR TO EVEN LARGER

PEACEFUL PROTESTS, ALSO MOSTLY BY SOUTHERNERS, THAT TOOK

PLACE DURING THE NATIONAL ASSEMBLY SESSION IN JUNE. IT IS

NOTABLE THAT THE GVN HAS PERMITTED THESE EXPRESSIONS OF

DISSATISFACTION AND HAS NOT RESPONDED WITH FORCE. IT IS

UNLIKELY THAT THE LOCAL MEDIA WILL COVER THESE EVENTS, OR

THAT THE PROTESTS WILL BE ALLOWED TO GROW TOO LARGE OR TOO

VOCAL.
