SUBJECT: HOMELAND SECURITY COORDINATOR

REF: SECSTATE 126082

Classified By: EDWARD W. GNEHM FOR REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D)

1. (C) In response to Reftel, counterterrorism and homeland

security responsibilities fall to a number of agencies in

Jordan. The General Intelligence Directorate (GID) is

responsible for internal security and most counterterrorism

operations in Jordan, and as such has disrupted several

terrorist cells in Jordan in the past few years. The

Protective Security Directorate (PSD), in close cooperation

with the GID, is responsible for maintaining security at all

diplomatic facilities in Jordan. The Jordanian Army, which

patrols Jordan\'s borders, has intercepted several groups of

terrorists trying to infilitrate the West Bank and Israel

with weapons and explosives. The JAF\'s Director of Military

Security also conducts counterterrorism and internal

dissident surveillance operations against civilians in close

cooperation with the GID and PSD. As responsibilities are

split between the agencies, there is no one "national

coordinator" for counterterrorism. Said Kheir, as Director

of the GID, is the highest-ranking official with

counterterrorism responsibilities. The Director of the PSD,

Tayseen Shurdom, is also an important point-of-contact on

counterterrorism issues.
