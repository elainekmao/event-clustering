SUBJECT: SEN. DANFORTH, ITALIAN MFA AGREE ON SUDAN APPROACH

CLASSIFIED BY: POLMINCOUNS T. COUNTRYMAN FOR REASON 1.5 (B) AND (D)

1. (C) SUMMARY: IN A JANUARY 11 MEETING AT THE MFA,

SENATOR DANFORTH AND THE MFA DIRECTOR GENERAL FOR AFRICA,

BRUNO CABRAS, SPOKE OF THE HISTORIC OPPORTUNITY THE SUDANESE

NOW HAVE TO MAKE PEACE WITH THE SUPPORT OF THE INTERNATIONAL

COMMUNITY. CABRAS REVEALED THAT THE GOI WOULD PROVIDE AN

ADDITIONAL THREE SPECIALISTS TO HELP IN THE NEGOTIATIONS AND

MONITORING PROCESSES. THE TWO MEN TOUCHED ON ASSUAGING

EGYPTIAN FEARS, MAINTAINING INTERNATIONAL PRESSURE THROUGH

THE IMPLEMENTATION PHASE, AND SUPPORTING THE GOOD EFFORTS OF

KENYA AND IGAD. THE MEETING CONFIRMED THAT USG AND GOI

THINKING ON SUDAN ARE COMPATIBLE. END SUMMARY.

---------------------------------------------

MFA: GOI KEENLY INTERESTED IN SUDANESE PEACE

---------------------------------------------

2. (U) AT THE REQUEST OF SPECIAL ENVOY FOR PEACE IN SUDAN

JOHN DANFORTH, CABRAS AND HE MET AT THE MFA FOR A SATURDAY

EXCHANGE OF VIEWS. ALSO PRESENT AT THE MEETING WERE NSC

AFRICA DIRECTOR MATT MCLEAN, POLMC, POLOFF (NOTETAKER), AND

MFA EAST AFRICA OFFICE DIRECTOR PAOLA IMPERIALE.

3. (C) CABRAS UNDERLINED THE GOI\'S INTEREST IN THE PEACE

PROCESS AND REVEALED THAT NEW GOI PERSONNEL WOULD BE SENT TO

ASSIST. THERE WAS "KEEN INTEREST IN THE VATICAN, PRESIDENCY,

AND MFA" IN SUDAN, BOTH FOR HISTORICAL AND MORAL REASONS AND

BECAUSE THERE WERE A LOT OF ITALIAN RELIGIOUS WORKERS IN THE

SOUTH OF SUDAN. "WE KNOW WE HAVE TO DO MORE," CABRAS SAID,

PERHAPS REFLECTING MULTIPLE DEMARCHES POST DELIVERED LAST

YEAR REQUESTING FUNDING AND PERSONNEL FOR THE NUBA MOUNTAINS

CEASE-FIRE MONITORING UNIT. HE PROVIDED A DOCUMENT

COMMITTING A BRIGADIER GENERAL TO MACHAKOS FOR PEACE

NEGOTIATIONS AND A COLONEL AND MAJOR FOR THE NUBA MOUNTAINS

TO SERVE, RESPECTIVELY, AS FIELD COMMANDER AND

LIAISON/OBSERVER. THIS WAS IN ADDITION TO THE GOI DIPLOMATIC

LIAISON OFFICER ALREADY IN PLACE. ASKED WHETHER REPORTS OF

MILITARY ENGAGEMENT ARE INVESTIGATED BY MONITORS, CABRAS SAID

THAT ALL REPORTS OF CEASE-FIRE VIOLATIONS ARE INVESTIGATED,

--------------------------------------------- --

KEEPING CAIRO ENGAGED AND THE PROCESS ADVANCING

--------------------------------------------- --

4. (C) CABRAS SAID THAT EGYPT WAS "ALMOST TERRORIZED BY

THE PEACE PROCESS, WITH ITS POSSIBILITY OF AN INDEPENDENT

SOUTHERN SUDAN." IT WOULD BE IMPORTANT TO ENSURE THAT EGYPT

DID NOT OPPOSE THE PROCESS. IN RESPONSE TO DANFORTH\'S

REQUEST FOR ADVICE FOR HIS MEETINGS IN EGYPT, CABRAS

SUGGESTED HE REITERATE THAT THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY DOES

NOT WANT SOUTHERN SECESSION BUT AT THIS POINT THE SPLM NEEDS

A BACKUP PLAN IN CASE THE AGREEMENT IS NOT IMPLEMENTED. HE

ALSO ADVISED THAT WE KEEP THE NEW ARAB LEAGUE SPECIAL ENVOY

IN THE LOOP TO ASSUAGE EGYPTIAN CONCERNS.

5. (C) IN RESPONSE TO DANFORTH\'S STATEMENT THAT HE HAS

TRIED TO BE AS EVEN-HANDED AS POSSIBLE, EVEN THOUGH THE U.S.

VIEW IS THAT THE SOUTH IS THE VICTIM, CABRAS SAID IT WAS

IMPORTANT TO CONVEY PRESIDENT BUSH\'S CLEAR MESSAGE THAT THE

SPLM WILL NOT BENEFIT BY GOING SLOWLY. FOR THE TIME BEING,

THE GOI DID NOT VIEW THE CURRENT DELAY IN NEGOTIATIONS AS A

BAD SIGN. HOWEVER, THE GOS WAS "VERY NERVOUS" ABOUT THE

SUDAN PEACE ACT, WHICH IT PERCEIVED AS AIMED AGAINST IT, AND

NEEDED TO BE REASSURED THAT IT APPLIES TO BOTH SIDES.

---------------------------------------------

SUPPORTING THE GOOD EFFORTS OF KENYA AND IGAD

---------------------------------------------

6. (C) CABRAS SAID THE GOI WAS THINKING OF PROVIDING

FUNDING FOR STRUCTURAL SUPPORT TO THE INTER-GOVERNMENTAL

AUTHORITY FOR DEVELOPMENT (IGAD) IN ADDITION TO CURRENT

HUMANITARIAN ASSISTANCE AND PEACE PROCESS AID TO IGAD. HE

PRAISED IGAD\'S WORK AS MEDIATOR AND KENYAN GEN. SUMBEIYWO\'S

EFFORTS AS KENYA\'S SPECIAL REPRESENTATIVE TO THE TALKS. THE

GOI VIEWED THE SUDAN PEACE ACT AS A USEFUL STICK AND WOULD

OFFER THE GOS THE PROMISE OF AN EU AID PACKAGE, TO BE

PROVIDED IMMEDIATELY UPON THE CONCLUSION OF AN ACCORD, AS A

CARROT. THE GOI WOULD ENSURE THAT THE IMF WAS GIVING THE GOS

THE SAME MESSAGE. MCLEAN SUGGESTED THAT AID DISBURSEMENTS BE

TIED TO PROGRESS IN IMPLEMENTATION, AND CABRAS AGREED, THOUGH

HE ADDED THAT TIME WOULD TEND TO CONSOLIDATE THE AGREEMENT IN

PEOPLE\'S MINDS. CABRAS NOTED THAT THE CONCLUSION OF THE

AGREEMENT MIGHT WELL BE ON THE GOI WATCH AS EU PRESIDENT

(JULY TO DECEMBER 2003).
