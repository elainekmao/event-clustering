SUBJECT: ICTY: READ-OUT ON RS PRIME MINISTER DODIK\'S

MEETING WITH DEL PONTE

1. (U) CLASSIFIED BY: ALLEN S. WEINER, LEGAL COUNSELOR.

REASONS: 1.5(B), (D).

2. (C) SUMMARY: REPUBLIKA SRPSKA (RS) PRIME MINISTER

DODIK\'S MAY 29 WITH THE CHIEF PROSECUTOR OF THE INTERNATIONAL

CRIMINAL TRIBUNAL FOR THE FORMER YUGOSLAVIA (ICTY) WAS USEFUL

FROM A SYMBOLIC POINT OF VIEW INSOFAR AS IT DEMONSTRATED

"GOODWILL" ON THE PART OF THE RS. IN THE ICTY\'S VIEW,

HOWEVER, NOTHING OF GREAT SUBSTANCE EMERGED FROM THE MEETING.

DODIK HAS PLEDGED IMPROVED COOPERATION IN CERTAIN AREAS,

INCLUDING ACCOMMODATING CONCERNS OF THE ICTY\'S OFFICE OF THE

PROSECUTOR (OTP) ON THE DRAFT LAW ON RS COOPERATION WITH THE

ICTY. DODIK AND DEL PONTE ALSO DISCUSSED APPREHENSION ISSUES

AND THE QUESTION OF PROVISIONAL RELEASE OF BOSNIAN SERB

INDICTEES. END SUMMARY.

3. (C) ON JUNE 7, ICTY DEPUTY PROSECUTOR GRAHAM BLEWITT

PROVIDED EMBASSY LEGAL COUNSELOR WITH A READ-OUT OF RS PRIME

MINISTER DODIK\'S MAY 29 MEETING WITH ICTY CHIEF PROSECUTOR

CARLA DEL PONTE. ACCORDING TO BLEWITT, THE MEETING, WHICH

WAS HASTILY ARRANGED, WAS BRIEF AND WAS "IN THE NATURE OF A

COURTESY CALL." THAT SAID, HE THOUGHT THE VISIT WAS

SIGNIFICANT IN THAT IT DEMONSTRATED GOODWILL ON THE PART OF

THE RS, AND A NUMBER OF SPECIFIC AREAS OF COOPERATION WERE

DISCUSSED. BLEWITT NOTED THAT FORMER RS MINISTER OF JUSTICE

TRBOJEVIC, WHO WAS FIRED SHORTLY AFTER HIS VISIT TO THE ICTY

EARLIER THIS YEAR, ACCOMPANIED DODIK. ALTHOUGH TRBOJEVIC NO

LONGER HAS A FORMAL POSITION IN THE RS GOVERNMENT, DODIK

INDICATED THAT TRBOJEVIC REMAINS ONE OF HIS CLOSE ADVISORS

AND WILL CONTINUE TO WORK ON RS-ICTY COOPERATION ISSUES.

4. (C) DURING THE MEETING, DEL PONTE REPORTED THAT THE

ICTY\'S BANJA LUKA FIELD OFFICE WAS NOW OPEN, AND THAT THE

OFFICE OF THE PROSECUTOR (OTP) WAS SATISFIED WITH THE LEVEL

OF COOPERATION IT WAS RECEIVING FROM THE RS AT THE "LIAISON

LEVEL."

5. (C) ON THE ISSUE OF PROVISIONAL RELEASE OF BOSNIAN SERB

INDICTEES, DEL PONTE EXPRESSED HER OPPOSITION TO, AND

DISAPPOINTMENT WITH, THE TRIAL CHAMBER\'S DECISION TO GRANT

PROVISIONAL RELEASE TO MIROSLAV TADIC AND SIMO ZARIC, TWO OF

THE INDICTEES IN THE BOSANSKI SAMAC CASE. (NOTE: A THIRD

INDICTEE, MILAN SIMIC, HAS SUBSEQUENTLY BEEN GRANTED

PROVISIONAL RELEASE.) SHE SAID THAT SHE WOULD CONTINUE TO

OPPOSE PROVISIONAL RELEASE TO THE RS AS LONG AS ICTY

INDICTEES REMAINED AT LARGE THERE.

6. (S) BLEWITT SAID DEL PONTE EXPRESSED DISPLEASURE WITH

THE RS\'S FAILURE TO APPREHEND TWO INDIVIDUALS COVERED BY A

SEALED INDICTMENT. BY WAY OF BACKGROUND, BLEWITT EXPLAINED

THAT ALTHOUGH THE INDICTMENT IN QUESTION REMAINS UNDER SEAL,

THE OTP HAD REASON TO BELIEVE IT HAD BEEN COMPROMISED.

ACCORDINGLY, OTP HAD SERVED THE INDICTMENT AND ARREST

WARRANTS ON THE RS AS A TEST CASE OF THE RS\'S COMMITMENT TO

IMPROVE COOPERATION IN THIS AREA. DODIK REPORTED THAT HIS

GOVERNMENT\'S INFORMATION IS THAT THE TWO INDIVIDUALS IN

QUESTION HAVE FLED TO SERBIA. HE SAID THE RS HAD APPROACHED

THE SERBIAN MUP FOR HELP IN SECURING CUSTODY OF THE TWO, BUT

HAD RECEIVED NO RESPONSE. DEL PONTE SAID SHE WOULD PUBLICLY

DISCLOSE THE INDICTMENT IF THERE IS NO PROGRESS ON

APPREHENDING THE INDICTEES.

7. (S) WITH RESPECT TO RADOVAN KARADZIC, DODIK SAID HE

WOULD BE WILLING TO SHARE INFORMATION ON KARADZIC\'S

WHEREABOUTS, BUT SAID THAT THE RS INTELLIGENCE SERVICES "WERE

EFFECTIVE ONLY TO A POINT" ON THIS SCORE. HE URGED DEL PONTE

TO CONTINUE PRESSING SFOR TO ARREST KARADZIC. HE OBSERVED,

HOWEVER, THAT IF KARADZIC WERE ARRESTED JUST BEFORE UPCOMING

ELECTIONS, IT COULD "AFFECT HIS ABILITY TO REMAIN IN POWER."

8. (C) THE OTP ALSO RAISED THE ISSUE OF ACCESS TO RS

WITNESSES. THE OTP, ACTING PURSUANT TO ITS AUTHORITY TO

SUMMON WITNESSES UNDER RULE 39 OF ITS RULES OF PROCEDURE AND

EVIDENCE, HAS OVER THE PAST FEW MONTHS BEEN INTERVIEWING

REPUBLIKA SRPSKA ARMY (VRS) OFFICERS IN BANJA LUKA IN

CONNECTION WITH THE KRSTIC TRIAL. RS COOPERATION HAS

GENERALLY BEEN GOOD, BUT THERE HAVE BEEN A NUMBER OF "NO

SHOWS" RECENTLY. DODIK PROMISED FULL COOPERATION AND SAID HE

WOULD USE HIS "INFLUENCE" TO ATTEMPT TO ENSURE THAT ALL

SUMMONED VRS OFFICERS MET WITH OTP INVESTIGATORS.

9. (C) ON THE DRAFT RS LAW ON COOPERATION WITH ICTY, THE

OTP NOTED THAT THEY HAD NOT BEEN SATISFIED WITH THE INITIAL

DRAFT OF THE LAW PROVIDED TO THEM, AND HAD PROVIDED THE RS

WITH COMMENTS. DODIK REPORTED THAT THE LAW HAS BEEN REVISED

TO INCORPORATE THE OTP\'S COMMENTS. HE ALSO SAID THAT THE LAW

WILL BE INTRODUCED AT THE NEXT SESSION OF THE RS PARLIAMENT.

IN RESPONSE TO LEGAL COUNSELOR\'S QUESTION, BLEWITT SAID THAT

THE OTP HAS NOT YET SEEN THE REVISED LAW. (COMMENT: IT

WOULD BE HELPFUL IF EMBASSY SARAJEVO COULD OBTAIN A COPY OF

THE CURRENT DRAFT OF THE LAW FOR US TO SHARE WITH THE OTP TO

ENSURE THAT OTP CONCERNS HAVE BEEN SATISFACTORILY ADDRESSED.)

10. (S) DODIK ALSO SAID THAT IT WOULD HELP OVERCOME THE

WIDESPREAD VIEW IN THE RS THAT THE ICTY IS BIASED AGAINST THE

SERBS IF THE OTP ISSUED INDICTMENTS AGAINST NON-SERBS IN

BOSNIA. DEL PONTE SAID ONLY THAT THE OTP WAS WORKING HARD IN

THIS REGARD.
