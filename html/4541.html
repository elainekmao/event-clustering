SUBJECT: DISCUSSING RELIGIOUS FREEDOM WITH FM NIEN

REF: A. STATE 235694 (NOTAL) B. STATE 241039

- C. HANFORD/BURGHARDT E-MAIL D. HANOI 2694

1. (U) SUMMARY. AMBASSADOR EXPRESSED CONCERN TO FM NIEN

ABOUT RECENT REPORTS OVER ABUSES OF RELIGIOUS FREEDOMS IN

THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS IN PARTICULAR, AND WARNED ABOUT A

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 HANOI 02862 01 OF 02 291001Z

POSSIBLE DESIGNATION OF VIETNAM AS A "COUNTRY OF PARTICULAR

CONCERN" UNDER THE INTERNATIONAL RELIGIOUS FREEDOM ACT. FM

NIEN CLAIMED SUCH REPORTS WERE "WRONG" BUT PROMISED TO TRY

TO FIND OUT MORE. THE GVN PERSPECTIVE ON THIS ISSUE IS SO

DIFFERENT FROM THAT OF THE USG THAT GVN OFFICIALS MAY

LITERALLY NOT UNDERSTAND THE SOURCE OF USG CONCERN, AND MAY

BE INCLINED TO INFER SINISTER MOTIVES. END SUMMARY.

2. (U) AMBASSADOR STRESSED TO FOREIGN MINISTER NGUYEN DY

NIEN IN A MEETING ON NOVEMBER 27 (SEPTELS) THE NEED FOR

IMPROVEMENTS IN THE CONDITIONS FOR RELIGIOUS FREEDOM IN

VIETNAM, AS DESCRIBED MOST RECENTLY BY AMBASSADOR-AT-LARGE

FOR INTERNATIONAL RELIGIOUS FREEDOM JOHN HANFORD AND OTHER

USG OFFICIALS DURING THE NOVEMBER 8 HUMAN RIGHTS DIALOGUE IN

WASHINGTON (REFS A AND B). HE NOTED THAT HE PLANNED TO

DISCUSS THIS ISSUE IN MORE DEPTH WITH DEPUTY FOREIGN

MINISTER LE VAN BANG AND GOVERNMENT COMMITTEE ON RELIGION LE

QUANG VINH IN THE NEAR FUTURE.

3. (U) AMBASSADOR HIGHLIGHTED RECENT REPORTS OF REPRESSION

OF PROTESTANTS IN THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS. HE NOTED THAT MANY

OFFICIALS IN DAK LAK AND GIA LAI PROVINCES IN PARTICULAR

APPEARED TO ASSOCIATE ALL PROTESTANTS WITH THE DEGA

SEPARATISTS, ALTHOUGH THE LATTER COMPOSE PERHAPS ONLY ONE

PERCENT OF PROTESTANTS IN THIS AREA. HE EXPRESSED CONCERN

THAT AT LEAST SOME OFFICIALS TREATED ALL PROTESTANTS AS

SUBVERSIVES AND TRIED TO CLAMP DOWN ON THEM. HE REITERATED

THE IMPORTANCE WITH WHICH THE U.S. VIEWED THE ISSUE OF

RELIGIOUS FREEDOM, AND PLEDGED THAT WE WOULD CONTINUE TO

MONITOR THE SITUATION CAREFULLY, INCLUDING FREQUENT VISITS

BY OUR CONSULATE GENERAL AND EMBASSY STAFFS. HE NOTED

AMBASSADOR HANFORD\'S WARNING THAT, ABSENT POSITIVE

DEVELOPMENTS SOON, VIETNAM WOULD LIKELY BE DESIGNATED A

"COUNTRY OF PARTICULAR CONCERN" UNDER THE INTERNATIONAL

RELIGIOUS FREEDOM ACT, WHICH COULD HAVE SERIOUS NEGATIVE

REPERCUSSIONS ON US ASSISTANCE AND INVESTMENT.

4. (U) FM NIEN CLAIMED THAT US CONCERNS APPEARED TO BE

BASED ON "WRONG INFORMATION" BUT PROMISED TO "FIND OUT." HE

ADMITTED THAT HE DID NOT KNOW EXACTLY HOW TO GO ABOUT

"PROVING" THAT THE INFORMATION WAS WRONG, HOWEVER. HE

DESCRIBED OVERALL CONDITIONS IN THE CENTRAL HIGHLAND AS

"MUCH IMPROVED," NOTING THAT HE HAD CONFIRMED THIS

IMPRESSION WITH MANY RECENT VISITORS TO THE CENTRAL

HIGHLANDS AS WELL AS WITH FELLOW NATIONAL ASSEMBLY DELEGATES

FROM THAT REGION. HE EMPHASIZED THAT SOME IN THE CENTRAL

HIGHLANDS, HOWEVER, CONTINUED TO FOLLOW THE DEGA

SEPARATISTS, AND THAT "MANY" IN VIETNAM DID "NOT UNDERSTAND

THE US POSITION" ON THE DEGAS.

5. (U) AMBASSADOR REITERATED WHAT HE HAD TOLD MINISTER FOR

ETHNIC MINORITIES KSOR PHUOC (REF D): THE U.S. HAD NO

INTEREST IN THE SEPARATION OF A DEGA STATE AND INSTEAD

SUPPORTED -- AND RECOGNIZED -- A UNIFIED VIETNAM. HE

STRESSED THAT THERE WAS "NO TRUTH" IN REPORTS OF ANY USG

SUPPORT IN ANY WAY TO OVERTHROW THE GVN OR TO SET UP A

SEPARATE STATE FOR THE DEGAS. HE NOTED THAT EAP DAS DALEY

HAD ALSO MADE THIS POINT DIRECTLY TO DEGA LEADER KOK KSOR.

HE EXPRESSED CONCERN, HOWEVER, OVER REPORTS THAT SOME HOUSE

CHURCHES IN THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS HAD NOT BEEN ALLOWED TO

JOIN THE SOUTHERN EVANGELICAL CHURCH OF VIETNAM (SECV),

DESPITE PENDING APPLICATIONS, AND THAT MANY CHURCHES,

PASTORS, AND BELIEVERS WERE SUBJECT TO UNDUE PRESSURES DUE

TO THEIR "ILLEGAL" STATUS.

6. (U) FM NIEN POINTED TO THE CONSTITUTIONAL RIGHT TO

FREEDOM OF WORSHIP IN VIETNAM, BUT ADMITTED THAT CHURCHES

LINKED WITH THE DEGA MOVEMENT WOULD INDEED NOT BE "ALLOWED."

HE CLAIMED THAT ALL VIETNAMESE CITIZENS WERE FREE TO CHOOSE

THEIR RELIGION AND SHOULD FACE "NO PROBLEM AT ALL." HE SAID

THAT THOSE CHURCHES THAT WISHED FORMAL TIES WITH THE SECV

SHOULD APPLY THROUGH LOCAL OFFICIALS AND GET APPROPRIATE

PERMISSION.

7. (U) COMMENT: THE FOREIGN MINISTER SEEMED TO HAVE A

POOR GRASP OF THE ISSUE. AT ONE POINT, HE DISCUSSED THE

MATTER OF REGISTRATION OF A CHURCH AS IF IT WERE THE SAME AS

THEY WORK DIRECTLY ON RELIGION POLICY, PROBABLY NEVER THINK
