SUMMARY

--------

1. (C) AT A CLOSED SESSION ON MAY 8, THE CROATIAN

CABINET FORMALLY DECIDED TO ACCEPT FUNDS FROZEN IN THE

U.S. UNDER MILOSEVIC-ERA SANCTIONS. ON MAY 7

WE MET WITH MFA SENIOR LEGAL ADVISOR ANDREJA METELKO-

ZGOMBIC AND AMERICA\'S DESK DIRECTOR MIROSLAV KOVACIC TO

PRESS FOR CROATIAN ACCEPTANCE. THIS ISSUE WAS ALSO

RAISED DURING THE SECRETARY\'S MEETING WITH FM PICULA ON

THE MARGINS OF THE SIGNING OF THE ADRIATIC CHARTER ON MAY

2.

2. (SBU) ON MAY 9 WE CONFIRMED WITH METELKO-ZGOMBIC THAT

THE CABINET HAD DECIDED TO ACCEPT THE FUNDS, WHILE NOT

PREJUDICING THE PARLIAMENT\'S DECISION ON WHETHER TO

RATIFY THE AGREEMENT ON SUCCESSION ISSUES (ASI). IN

COMMENTS TO THE PRESS AFTER THE CABINET SESSION,

GOVERNMENT OFFICIALS ALSO ANNOUNCED IT WOULD SEEK LEGAL

ACTION AGAINST ONE OF THE JOINT VENTURE BANKS IN GERMANY,

WHERE DEPOSITS MYSTERIOUSLY ERODED. METELKO-ZGOMBIC

STATED THAT THE PURPOSE OF THE LEGAL ACTION WOULD BE TO

TRY TO FORCE DISCLOSURE OF THE HISTORY OF THE ACCOUNTS,

THOUGH IT WAS POSSIBLE THAT SOME SORT OF RESTITUTION

MIGHT BE SOUGHT. END SUMMARY.

3. (SBU) ON MAY 8, IN A CLOSED CABINET SESSION, THE

GOVERNMENT DECIDED TO ACCEPT THE APPROXIMATELY $56

MILLION TRANSFER FROM THE USG. DEPUTY FOREIGN MINISTER

IVAN SIMONOVIC WAS QUOTED AS SAYING THAT THE GOVERNMENT

WAS MIXING "PRINCIPLES WITH PRAGMATISM," STRESSING THAT

ACCEPTING THE MONEY DID NOT MEAN THAT CROATIA WOULD

RATIFY THE ASI. SIMULTANEOUSLY, OFFICIALS ANNOUNCED THE

DECISION TO FILE A LAWSUIT AGAINST THE LHB BANK IN

GERMANY, APPARENTLY PARTIALLY OWNED BY SLOVENIA\'S

LJUBLJANSKA BANKA. IN RESPONSE TO OUR QUERY, METELKO-

ZGOMBIC CLARIFIED THAT THE PURPOSE OF THE LAWSUIT WOULD

BE TO EXTRACT INFORMATION ABOUT THE DISAPPEARING

DEPOSITS, THOUGH SHE DID NOT EXCLUDE THE POSSIBILITY OF

SEEKING SOME SORT OF COMPENSATION FROM THE BANK.

4. (SBU) THE DAY BEFORE THE DECISION, WE MET WITH MFA

SENIOR LEGAL ADVISOR ANDREJA METELKO-ZGOMBIC AND

AMERICA\'S DESK DIRECTOR MIROSLAV KOVACIC TO PRESS FOR

CROATIAN ACCEPTANCE OF THE FUNDS FROZEN IN THE U.S. UNDER

MILOSEVIC-ERA SANCTIONS. WE STRESSED THAT WE SAW THE

DISBURSEMENT OF THE FUNDS IN THE U.S. AS SEPARATE FROM

THE ISSUE OF THE ASI, ALTHOUGH RESOLUTION OF THE LATTER

WOULD CERTAINLY BE IN CROATIA\'S INTEREST.

5. (C) METELKO-ZGOMBIC ACKNOWLEDGED THE RESOLUTION OF

SUCCESSION ISSUES WAS IN CROATIA\'S INTERESTS, BUT THE

PUBLIC AND THE PARLIAMENT WERE DEMANDING ANSWERS. SHE

REJECTED THE ARGUMENT THAT "EVERYONE IN VIENNA KNEW" THAT

THE NUMBERS IN THE ASI WERE NOTIONAL; WHILE ADMITTING THE

LIMITS OF HER KNOWLEDGE OF BANKING, SHE INSISTED THAT

"ANY VILLAGE BANK" COULD COME UP WITH A FIGURE FOR ITS

DEPOSITS. WHEN CROATIA WENT TO THE OVERSEAS JOINT

VENTURE BANKS TO ASK FOR AN ACCOUNTING, IT WAS REBUFFED

AND SENT TO THE NATIONAL BANK OF YUGOSLAVIA. METELKO-

ZGOMBIC INSISTED THAT THE NBY WAS EVADING ITS

RESPONSIBILITY, AS LAID OUT IN APPENDIX 1 TO ANNEX C OF

THE ASI, TO FACILITATE THE RELEASE OF INFORMATION FROM

THE JOINT VENTURE BANKS TO THE SUCCESSOR STATES. WE

WONDERED ALOUD WHETHER A FULL ACCOUNTING WOULD REALLY BE

ENOUGH TO CONVINCE THE PARLIAMENT TO SWALLOW AND RATIFY;

METELKO-ZGOMBIC RETORTED IT WAS THE LEAST THE GOVERNMENT

COULD DO.

6. (C) TURNING TO THE DISBURSEMENT OF FUNDS FROM THE

FEDERAL RESERVE BANK OF NEW YORK, METELKO-ZGOMBIC SAID

SHE DID NOT UNDERSTAND THE RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN THE

UNFREEZING OF THE FUNDS, AS WE HAD PROMISED TO DO, AND

THE DECISION TO DIVIDE AND DISBURSE THE FUNDS, WHICH HAD

COME AS A COMPLETE SURPRISE, AND THROUGH A RATHER UNUSUAL

CHANNEL -- THE U.S. BANK COMMUNICATION TO THE CROATIAN

NATIONAL BANK. WE EMPHASIZED THAT THE USG HAD DECIDED

THE QUICKEST AND MOST EFFICIENT WAY TO GET THE SUCCESSOR

STATES BACK THEIR OWN MONEY, UN-ERODED BY LAWSUITS, WAS

THROUGH THIS MECHANISM.

7. (C) WHILE REFUSING TO PREJUDGE THE FINAL GOVERNMENT

DECISION, METELKO-ZGOMBIC SAID THE GOVERNMENT WOULD TRY

TO FIND A WAY TO ACCEPT THE FUNDS WITHOUT PREJUDICING THE
