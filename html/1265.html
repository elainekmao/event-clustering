SUBJECT: DROC FOREIGN MINISTER VISITS HARARE

CLASSIFIED BY AMB. TOM MCDONALD. REASONS: 1.5 (B) AND

(D).

1. (C) SUMMARY: DROC FOREIGN MINISTER YERODIA PAID A

BRIEF VISIT TO ZIMBABWE ON AUGUST 29 TO ASSUAGE

HARARE\'S CONCERNS AFTER PRESIDENT KABILA SAID HE WOULD

SEEK TO REVISE THE LUSAKA AGREEMENT. AMBASSADOR

MCDONALD OBTAINED A READOUT ON YERODIA\'S VISIT FOM

NEW MFA PERMANENT SECRETARY AND THE UNDERSECRTARY FOR

AFRICA. BOTH ITERATED THAT THE LUSAKA AGREEMENT IS

STILL ON TRACK. THE UNDERSECRETARY SID ZIMBABWE IS

OPEN TO HEARING ANY PROPOSALS TO EVISE THE AGREEMENT,

BUT IT HAS NOT FORMED A POSTION ON KABILA\'S DESIRE

THAT UGANDA, RWANDA, ANDBURUNDI SHOULD BE NAMED AS

AGGRESSORS. HOWEVER, ANY REVISION PROCESS SHOULD NOT

BE A HINDRANCE TOTHE MONUC PHASE II DEPLOYMENT,

ACCORDING TO THE IMBABWEAN MFA. END SUMMARY.

2. (U) AFTER AN AGUST 29 MEETING IN HARARE WITH

PRESIDENT MUGABE,DROC FOREIGN MINISTER YERODIA TOLD

REPORTERS THAT THE LUSAKA PROCESS WAS STILL ON TRACK

BUT SHOULDBE REVISED TO NAME RWANDA, UGANDA, AND

BURUNDI A AGGRESSORS. CURRENT FACILITATOR MASIRE WAS

A "OMPLICATOR" AND COULD NOT BE TRUSTED. IN HIS

PULIC COMMENTS, YERODIA SAID THAT, ALTHOUGH THE DROC

DID NOT THINK A FACILITATOR WAS NECESSARY, IT HAD

PROPOSED POSSIBLE ALTERNATIVES IN AN EFFORT TO B

FORTHCOMING, INCLUDING KENNETH KAUNDA, FREDERIKDE

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 04849 01 OF 02 311700Z

KLERK, AND CYRIL RAMAPHOSA. ZIMBABWEAN FOREIN

MINISTER MUDENGE TOLD JOURNALISTS THAT THE LUSAKA

ACCORDS HAD NOT BEEN "WRITTEN IN STONE," AND SID THE

DROC\'S DESIRE TO REVISE THE LUSAKA ACCORD WOULD BE

CONSIDERED.

3. (C) IN AN AUGUST 30MEETING ON A DIFFERENT TOPIC

WITH MFA PERMSEC WILARD CHIWEWE, THE AMBASSADOR ASKED

FOR A READOUT OF YERODIA\'S VISIT. CHIWEWE NOTED THAT

HE HAD ATENDED PRESIDENT MUGABE\'S MEETING WITH

YERODIA, AD HAD NOT COME AWAY WITH THE SENSE THAT THE

CONGLESE GOVERNMENT WAS AN OBSTACLE TO FORWARD

MOVEMNT ON LUSAKA. MASIRE HAD NOT BEEN DISCUSSED,

BUT HE EMPHASIZED THAT THE PHASE II MONUC DEPLOYMENT

MUST TAKE INTO ACCOUNT KABILA\'S CONCERNS ABOUT WHEE

THE TROOPS WILL BE DEPLOYED. THE AMBASSADOR AKED

WHETHER YERODIA HAD INSISTED THAT LUSAKA BE EVISED.

THE USG, HE SAID, IS STRONGLY CONCERNED THAT OPENING

UP THE LUSAKA ACCORDS TO REVISION WOUD BE A SERIOUS

SETBACK TO THE PEACE PROCESS. TH ZIMBABWEAN

GOVERNMENT, CHIWEWE REPLIED, BELIEVE WE HAVE COME

THIS FAR BECAUSE OF LUSAKA. ANY REQUEST FOR REVISION,

HE SAID, WOULD REQUIRE A STRONG JUSTIFICATION, BUT THE

DROC GOVERNMENT HAD NOT YET ENGAGED IN THE FORMAL

AMENDMENT PROCESS PERMITTED UNDER ARTICLE 26. ALL

INDICATIONS, CHIWEWE THOUGHT, ARE THAT LUSAKA IS ON

COURSE, PROVIDED THAT MONUC II DEPLOYS QUICKLY.

4. (C) THE AMBASSADOR AND POLOFFS MET SEPARATELY ON

AUGUST 31 WITH MFA UNDERSECRETARY FOR AFRICA DAVID

HAMADZIRIPI ABOUT YERODIA\'S DISCUSSIONS HERE.

HAMADZIRIPI SAID THE MEETING WAS INTENDED TO BE A

CONSULTATION AMONG THE ALLIES TO ENSURE THEY WERE ON

THE SAME TRACK AND TO DISCUSS ACCELERATING

IMPLEMENTATION OF THE LUSAKA AGREEMENTS. DUE TO

SCHEDULING CONFLICTS, HOWEVER, ANGOLA WAS UNABLE TO

SEND ANYONE AND NAMIBIA WAS REPRESENTED BY ITS

AMBASSADOR HERE.

5. (C) YERODIA, HAMADZIRIPI SAID, HAD AGREED THAT

LUSAKA REMAINS VALID, BUT BELIEVED THERE WAS A NEED TO

REVIEW THE AGREEMENTS NOW THAT RWANDA, BURUNDI, AND

UGANDA HAVE BEEN DESCRIBED AS AGGRESSOR NATIONS IN

UNSCR 1304, AND BECAUSE SOME OF THE ORIGINAL REBEL

SIGNATORIES HAVE NOW WITHDRAWN FROM THE CONFLICT.

HAMADZIRIPI INDICATED THAT ZIMBABWE IS OPEN TO

REVIEWING ANY PROPOSALS FOR AMENDMENTS, AS PERMITTED

BY ARTICLE 26, BUT HAD NOT YET TAKEN A POSITION ON THE

SUBSTANCE OF POSSIBLE CHANGES. HAMADZIRIPI DID NOT

SEE A FORMAL REVIEW OF LUSAKA AS AN OBSTACLE TO THE

MONUC PHASE II DEPLOYMENT. PARTICIPANTS IN THE

DISCUSSIONS WELCOMED RWANDA\'S PROPOSAL TO WITHDRAW 200

KM, AND DEFENSE MINISTERS OF DROC\'S ALLIES PLAN TO

MEET SOON TO DISCUSS THE MODALITIES OF DISENGAGEMENT.

6. (C) ACCORDING TO HAMADZIRIPI, MASIRE\'S ROLE AS

PEACE FACILITATOR DID NOT COME UP IN THE PRIVATE

DISCUSSIONS. THIS IS A SENSITIVE ISSUE FOR ZIMBABWE,

HE POINTED OUT, BECAUSE THE GOZ WANTS TO IMPLEMENT

LUSAKA BUT DOES NOT WANT TO OFFEND BOTSWANA BY

UNCEREMONIOUSLY DUMPING THE WELL-RESPECTED MASIRE.

PRESIDENT KABILA IS ABSOLUTELY OPPOSED TO MASIRE, SO

ZIMBABWE MUST HELP COME UP WITH AN ALTERNATIVE PLAN

THAT DOES NOT DENIGRATE MASIRE. THE GOZ, HAMADZIRIPI

RECALLED, HAD ACCEPTED THE NAMIBIAN PROPOSAL THAT TWO

EMINENT PERSONS BE ASKED TO WORK WITH MASIRE. DESPITE

THE OPPOSITION TO THE PROPOSAL BY THE DROC AND OTHERS,

HAMADZIRIPI WAS CONFIDENT A WAY OUT COULD BE FOUND, AS

THE DROC IS COMMITTED TO THE FACILITATION PROCESS.

7. (C) EVINCING A BIT OF FRUSTRATION, HAMADZIRIPI

SAID THE COUNTRIES OF THE REGION HAD WORKED HARD TO

KEEP THE LUSAKA PROCESS ALIVE, AND HE CRITICIZED THE

UN FOR "LETTING US DOWN." THE AMBASSADOR REMINDED

HAMADZIRIPI THAT PRESIDENT KABILA BORE MOST OF THE

BLAME FOR THE SLOW PACE OF PROGRESS, AND SAID MONUC II

COULD NOT PROCEED UNTIL KABILA PROVIDES THE NECESSARY

SECURITY GUARANTEES AND ALLOWS DEPLOYMENT IN AREAS HIS

GOVERNMENT CONTROLS. HAMADZIRIPI ACKNOWLEDGED THE

POINT, AND SAID THE GOZ WOULD DO ALL IT COULD TO

ENSURE THE CONDITIONS WERE RIGHT FOR THE PHASE II

DEPLOYMENT. ZIMBABWE\'S INVOLVEMENT IN THE DROC,

HAMADZIRIPI INSISTED, WAS MOTIVATED GENUINELY BY THE

PRINCIPLE OF PROTECTING A NATION\'S SOVEREIGN BORDERS,

NOT BY ECONOMIC INTERESTS, AND THE GOZ WOULD CONTINUE

TO REMAIN ENGAGED IN THIS PROCESS.

8. (C) COMMENT: HAMADZIRIPI IS THE MOST SENIOR MFA

OFFICIAL ENGAGED ON A DAILY BASIS ON DROC POLICY, AND

SO IS MUCH MORE THOROUGHLY BRIEFED ON DROC THAN

CHIWEWE, WHO ASSUMED HIS CURRENT POSITION ONLY ON

AUGUST 28. HAMADZIRIPI, HOWEVER, IS SOMETIMES OVERLY

OPTIMISTIC AND FAILS TO ACKNOWLEDGE ZIMBABWE\'S CLEAR

ECONOMIC INTERESTS IN THE DROC. LIKE THE REST OF THE

MFA, HE DID NOT APPEAR PHASED BY KABILA\'S LATEST

WRANGLINGS, AND REMAINED HOPEFUL AFTER YERODIA\'S VISIT

THAT THE DROC WOULD STICK TO LUSAKA AND THAT THE MONUC

DEPLOYMENT WOULD GO FORWARD. DESPITE ZIMBABWE\'S

CONTINUING AND INTENSIFYING DIFFICULTIES WITH KABILA,

HE IS STILL KEY TO ENDING THE GOZ\'S MILITARY

ENTANGLEMENT THERE.
