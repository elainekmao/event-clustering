1. (C) SUMMARY. OCM THANKED ZIMBABWEAN MINISTRY OF

FOREIGN AFFAIRS REPRESENTATIVE FOR HELPFUL ZIMBABWEAN

ACTIONS IN KAMPALA. OURING THE COURSE OF THE

CONVERSATION, THE MFA REPRESENTATIVE MADE HELPFUL NOISES

ABOUT FACILITATING THE UPCOMING VISIT OF SPECIAL

REPRESENTATIVE WOLPE, BUT WAS OUT OF THE LOOP ON DETAILS

OF THE TRIP AND ON THE PROPOSED MAPUTO MEETING OF

CONCERNED HEADS OF STATE. HOWEVER, THE DETAILS OF S/E

WOLPE\'S VISIT TO HARARE ARE BEING WORKED OUT AND ARE WELL

IN HAND. END SUMMARY.

----------------------------

ZIMBABWE HOPES FOR PROGRESS

----------------------------

2. (C) DCM AND TDY POLOFF (NOTETAKER) CALLED ON

AMBASSADOR GODFREY MWEDZI, GOZ\'S DIRECTOR FOR AFRICAN

AFFAIRS, APRIL 13, TO CONVEY USG APPRECIATION FOR GOZ ROLE

IN KAMPALA TALKS (REF B). MAXWELL CHIKOROWONDA, GOZ

CONGO DESK OFFICER, SERVED AS NOTETAKER FOR THE

ZIMBABWEANS.

3. (C) MWEDZI RECEIVED OUR THANKS WITH FEW COMMENTS,

OBSERVING THAT IT IS ENCOURAGING THAT THERE HAS BEEN

PROGRESS IN A DELICATE PROCESS. HE SAID THE GOZ APPRECIATES

U.S. SUPPORT, WHICH HELPS BOOST CONFIDENCE IN THE PROCESSES

HE EXPECTS WILL FOLLOW FROM CURRENT DEVELOPMENTS.

MWEDZI EMPHASIZED THE JMC NEEDS TO BE CLOSELY FOCUSED

UPON. HE ALSO RECEIVED WITHOUT COMMENT OUR UPDATE ON

THE VISIT OF PRIME MINISTER BARAK (REF C).

4. (C) WHEN DCM INFORMED MWEDZI THAT SPECIAL REP WOLPE

PAGE 03 HARARE 02100 141117Z

WILL BE ARRIVING NEXT WEEK, MWEDZI SAID HE HAD NOT BEEN

INFORMED OF THE VISIT THROUGH HIS CHANNELS, BUT EXPRESSED

A GENERAL WELCOME FOR THE VISIT. HE THOUGHT IT WAS

IMPORTANT THAT THE VISIT COME AFTER THE APRIL 14, WHEN THE

SITUATION IN DROC SHOULD BE CLEARER, AND PRACTICAL

ASPECTS OF IMPLEMENTATION CAN BE SEEN.

5. (C) WHEN QUERIED BY DCM IF HE HAD ANY FURTHER

INFORMATION ON THE PROPOSED MEETING IN MAPUTO THAT

UGANDAN PRESIDENT MUSEVENI MENTIONED TO A/S RICE (REF A),

MWEDZI SAID HE HAD NOT HEARD OF CONCRETE PLANS FOR IT. HE

SAID HE WILL LET US KNOW AS HE GETS INFORMATION. HE

MENTIONED THAT HE HAD HEARD FROM THE GOZ WASHINGTON

EMBASSY ABOUT INQUIRIES FROM A/S RICE.

------------------------

WANT TO SEE COOPERATION

-------------------------

6. (C) MWEDZI OBSERVED THAT WHAT IS NOTABLE ABOUT THE

KAMPALA MEETING AND CURRENT DISENGAGEMENT PLANS IS

THAT THE POSITIONS ON THE GROUND OF THE VARIOUS SIDES HAVE

CHANGED CONSIDERABLY IN THE NINE MONTHS SINCE THE SIGNING

OF THE LUSAKA ACCORD. HE REPEATED SEVERAL TIMES THAT JMC

AND MONUC NEED TO COOPERATE, AND THAT, IN FACT, WHAT HE

CALLED "POINTLESS SQUABBLING" CAN WEAKEN THE JMC

SYSTEMS. HE ADDED THAT GOZ WANTS THE UN DEPLOYMENT TO

GO FORTH AS QUICKLY AS POSSIBLE AND INTENDS TO KEEP THE

PROCESS MOVING.

7. (C) MWEDZI CLOSED THE VISIT WITH AN INQUIRY ABOUT OUR

READOUT OF THE DROC NATIONAL DIALOGUE AND THE VISIT OF

NATIONAL DIALOGUE COORDINATOR MASIRE TO KINSHASA.

MWEDZI AGREED WITH DCM THAT THE PROCESS APPEARS TO BE

STUCK IN NEUTRAL, AND ADDED THAT HE THINKS THE PROBLEMS

MASIRE EXPERIENCED DURING HIS VISIT HAVE GIVEN HIM A CLEAR

VIEW OF THE STATE OF PLAY IN DROC.

----------

COMMENT

---------

8. (C) MWEDZI WAS PLEASANT, BUT AS ILL-INFORMED ABOUT HIS

GOVERNMENT\'S ACTIVITIES AND INTENTIONS AS ALWAYS. WE

WILL FOLLOW UP TO ENSURE ARRANGEMENTS FOR SPECIAL REP.

WOLPE\'S VISIT ARE ON TRACK. ON A POSITIVE NOTE, WHILE

MWEDZI IS NOT GOING TO REVEAL ANYTHING OF HIS OWN

THINKING TO US, HIS ASSESSMENT THAT DEVELOPMENTS IN THE

DAYS AFTER APRIL 14 WILL BE TELLING AND HIS ASSERTION THAT

THE GOZ IS EAGER TO PUSH THE PROCESS FORWARD PROBABLY

REFLECT THINKING HERE AT THE HIGHEST LEVELS. END COMMENT.
