SUBJECT: SADC-PF ISSUES HARD-HITTING ASSESSMENT OF ZIMBABWEAN

ELECTION PROCESS, WHILE SOUTH AFRICAN TEAM ENDORSES MUGABE VICTORY

CLASSIFIED BY POLITICAL SECTION CHIEF MATT HARRINGTON. REASONS:

1.5 (B) AND (D).

SUMMARY

-------

1. (U) ON MARCH 13, THE SADC PARLIAMENTARY FORUM ISSUED A TOUGH

CRITIQUE OF THE ZIMBABWEAN ELECTION PROCESS, SAYING THE ELECTION

CAMPAIGN WAS MARRED BY INCIDENTS OF VIOLENCE IN ALL PROVINCES OF

THE COUNTRY, PREDOMINANTLY AGAINST MDC SUPPORTERS. THE SADC-PF

CONCLUDED THAT THE CLIMATE OF "INSECURITY" PREVAILING IN ZIMBABWE

SINCE THE JUNE 2000 PARLIAMENTARY ELECTIONS PREVENTED AN ASSESSMENT

THAT THE ELECTORAL PROCESS HAD COMPLIED WITH SADC\'S ELECTORAL NORMS

AND STANDARDS. THE SOUTH AFRICAN OBSERVER MISSION, ON THE OTHER

HAND, DESCRIBED THE ELECTION OUTCOME AS A LEGITIMATE EXPRESSION OF

THE WILL OF THE ZIMBABWEAN PEOPLE AND CALLED ON THE INTERNATIONAL

COMMUNITY TO ACCEPT THE RESULT. END SUMMARY.

SADC PARLIAMENTARY FORUM

------------------------

2. (U) THE SADC PARLIAMENTARY FORUM\'S 70-MEMBER ELECTION

OBSERVATION MISSION ISSUED ON MARCH 13 A HARD-HITTING STATEMENT ON

THE ZIMBABWEAN ELECTORAL PROCESS (FAXED TO AF/S). THEIR KEY

FINDINGS ARE LISTED BELOW:

--THE ELECTION PERIOD WAS CHARACTERIZED BY HIGH LEVELS OF

POLARIZATION AND POLITICAL INTOLERANCE, LACK OF COMMUNICATION

AGAINST STAKEHOLDERS AND LACK OF FREE FLOW OF INFORMATION TO THE

ELECTORATE, WHICH ARE NECESSARY CONDITIONS FOR DEMOCRACY TO

PREVAIL;

--THE ELECTION CAMPAIGN WAS MARRED BY INCIDENTS OF VIOLENCE IN ALL

PROVINCES OF THE COUNTRY, PERPETRATED PRIMARILY AGAINST MDC

SUPPORTERS OR PERCEIVED OPPONENTS OF THE GOVERNMENT;

--THERE WERE NUMEROUS CASES OF ALLEGED TORTURE, ARSON, ASSAULT, AND

INCIDENCES OF FALSE IMPRISONMENT, INCLUDING ABDUCTION OF MDC

POLLING AGENTS. IN ONE SUCH INCIDENT, A SADC-PF OBSERVER TEAM

INTERVENED WHEN POLICE IN MASHONALAND CENTRAL DETAINED 24 MDC

ELECTION AGENTS WHO WERE ON THEIR WAY TO HARARE TO VOTE;

--ACTS OF VIOLENCE APPEARED TO BE SYSTEMATICALLY EMPLOYED BY YOUTH

AND WAR VETERANS WITH CAMPS DOTTED AROUND THE COUNTRY;

--THE USE OF RIOT SQUADS TO DISPERSE POTENTIAL VOTERS IN HARARE

RAISED QUESTIONS ABOUT THE IMPARTIALITY OF THE POLICE;

--THE RULING PARTY\'S CAMPAIGN WAS RELATIVELY UNINTERRUPTED WHILE

SOME OPPOSITION PARTY MEETINGS WERE CANCELLED OR INTERRUPTED;

--THE REDUCTION OF POLLING STATIONS IN URBAN AREAS HAD A MAJOR

IMPACT, RESULTING IN CONGESTION WITH SOME PEOPLE SPENDING MORE THAN

48 HOURS IN QUEUES BECAUSE OF THEIR SHEER DETERMINATION TO VOTE. A

SIGNIFICANT NUMBER OF THE ELECTORATE WAS UNABLE TO VOTE AS A RESULT

OF LOGISTICAL, ADMINISTRATIVE AND OTHER IMPEDIMENTS;

--FREE MOVEMENT OF PARTY AGENTS WITH BALLOT BOXES WAS COMPROMISED

BY INTIMIDATION AND REPORTED ABDUCTIONS IN SOME PROVINCES;

TENSE Q & A SESSION

-------------------

5. (U) THE Q & A SESSION WHICH FOLLOWED BORDERED ON HOSTILE, AS

BOTH LOCAL AND FOREIGN JOURNALISTS SUBJECTED MOTSUENYANE AND THE

ACCOMPANYING MEMBERS OF HIS TEAM TO RIGOROUS QUESTIONING,

SOMETIMES LAUGHING OUT LOUD AT THEIR RESPONSES. AN AUSTRALIAN

JOURNALIST NOTED THAT A RANGE OF WELL-RESPECTED CIVIL SOCIETY

ORGANIZATIONS HAD PROVIDED AMPLE DOCUMENTATION OF THE SERIOUSLY

FLAWED ELECTION PROCESS, ASKED WHETHER THE SOUTH AFRICAN DELEGATION

HAD MET WITH THEM AND, IF SO, HAD THOSE ORGANIZATIONS BEEN LYING OR

HALLUCINATING? ONE QUESTIONER ASKED MOTSUENYANE TO ELABORATE ON

THE "ADMINISTRATIVE OVERSIGHTS" THAT HAD PREVENTED VOTERS IN HARARE

AND CHITUNGWIZA FROM CASTING BALLOTS, WHILE ANOTHER INQUIRED

PEOPLE WELL. AN INTERNATIONAL TELEVISION REPORTER DECLARED THAT

THE SOUTH AFRICAN MISSION APPEARED TO BE ENDORSING THE EXISTENCE OF

LOWER STANDARDS FOR DEMOCRACY IN AFRICA. ASKED DIRECTLY WHO WAS

RESPONSIBLE FOR MOST OF THE VIOLENCE, MOTSUENYANE SIMPLY REFUSED TO

BE DRAWN ON THAT QUESTION.

COMMENT

-------

6. (C) THE SADC-PF ASSESSMENT WAS APPROPRIATELY CRITICAL AND

PROVIDED AMPLE DETAILS ON THE NUMEROUS FLAWS IN THE ELECTION

PROCESS, WHILE CAREFULLY AVOIDING CONFERRING ANY LEGITIMACY ON THE

OUTCOME. THEY DID NOT PULL ANY PUNCHES AND STATED EXPLICITLY THAT

MDC SUPPORTERS WERE THE PREDOMINANT VICTIMS -- NOT THE PERPETRATORS

-- OF POLITICAL VIOLENCE. WE EXPECT THE GOZ WILL IGNORE THE

INDICTMENT FROM REGIONAL PARLIAMENTARIANS AND, INSTEAD, FOCUS ON

THE "ENDORSEMENT" FROM THE SOUTH AFRICAN TEAM, WHOSE PERFORMANCE,

FRANKLY, WE FOUND EMBARRASSING. MANY ZIMBABWEANS ARE PROFOUNDLY

DISAPPOINTED -- BUT NOT SURPRISED -- BY THE STATEMENT FROM THE

SOUTH AFRICANS, WHOM THEY HAD VIEWED AS THE ONLY REGIONAL COUNTRY

CAPABLE OF PROTECTING THEM FROM THEIR OWN GOVERNMENT. A SOUTH

AFRICAN CIVIC ACTIVIST WHO WORKS CLOSELY WITH AMNESTY INTERNATIONAL

IN THE REGION TOLD ME AFTERWARD THAT HE WAS DEEPLY EMBARRASSED BY

THE SOUTH AFRICAN TEAM\'S ASSESSMENT. MOTSUENYANE EMPHASIZED

SEVERAL TIMES THAT HIS DELEGATION\'S REPORT WAS ONLY "INTERIM," BUT
