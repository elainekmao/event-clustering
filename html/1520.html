2. (C) SUMMARY: REPORTS FROM THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS

PROVINCES OF GIA LAI AND DAKLAK SUGGEST LARGE-SCALE

DEMONSTRATIONS HAVE ENDED FOLLOWING THE ARRESTS OF AT LEAST

20 PROTESTERS. THOUSANDS OF VIETNAM\'S ETHNIC MINORITIES

PROTESTED THIS PAST WEEK IN FRONT OF GOVERNMENT AND

COMMUNIST PARTY BUILDINGS IN PROVINCIAL CAPITALS.

INJURIES, BUT NO DEATHS, HAVE BEEN REPORTED AS SECURITY

FORCES HAVE ACTED TO STEM THE ARREST. THE MFA DESCRIBED

THOSE ARRESTED AS "PROVOCATIVE PEOPLE" WHO DESTROYED

PROPERTY AND CAUSED INSTABILITY, BUT THE ROOTS OF THE

PROTESTS ARE VARIED. THE PROTESTS APPEAR TO HAVE BEEN

TRIGGERED MOST DIRECTLY BY THE ARREST JANUARY 29 OF TWO

ETHNIC MINORITY MEN WHO APPEAR TO HAVE BEEN LEADERS IN

PROTESTANT HOUSE CHURCHES IN THE REGION. THE TWO MEN WERE

PROMPTLY RELEASED AFTER SIGNING A STATEMENT BEFORE

GOVERNMENT AUTHORITIES. HOWEVER, TENSION HAS BEEN BUILDING

FOR A VARIETY OF REASONS: SMOLDERING RESENTMENT BY

MINORITIES OVER LARGE-SCALE ETHNIC VIETNAMESE MIGRATION

INTO THE AREA; SUBSEQUENT LOSS OF THEIR TRADITIONAL LAND TO

THE MIGRANTS; AND THE EXTREME IMPOVERISHMENT OF MINORITIES,

WHICH HAS BEEN EXACERBATED BY THE DROP OF COFFEE PRICES TO

A THIRD OF THEIR PREVIOUS VALUE.

4. (U) THE DEMONSTRATIONS BEGAN IN PLEIKU, CAPITAL OF GIA

LAI PROVINCE, ON FEBRUARY 2. RESIDENTS OF THE TOWN TOLD

FOREIGN JOURNALISTS THAT THREE TO FOUR THOUSAND PEOPLE, ALL

MEMBERS OF INDIGENOUS MINORITY GROUPS, MARCHED IN WELL-

ORGANIZED GROUPS ALONG ROADS LEADING INTO PLEIKU. "THE

MOOD OF THE DEMONSTRATION WAS STRIKINGLY PEACEFUL,"

ACCORDING TO A BUSINESSMAN WHO WITNESSED THE EVENT. ON THE

FOLLOWING DAY, AND FOR THE NEXT TWO DAYS, OCCASIONAL

VIOLENCE MARRED THE DEMONSTRATIONS, AND SEVERAL POLICEMEN

AND PROTESTORS WERE INJURED IN CLASHES, ACCORDING TO PRESS

ACCOUNTS.

5. (U) ACCORDING TO A REPORT BY THE STATE-RUN VIETNAM NEW

AGENCY, THE DEMONSTRATIONS WERE PROVOKED BY "BAD ELEMENTS"

AND "EXTREMISTS" WHO WHIPPED UP POPULAR MISUNDERSTANDING

OVER THE ARREST OF TWO ETHNIC MINORITY MEN ON JANUARY 29.

THE VNA REPORT SAID THE PROTESTS BEGAN IN PLEIKU WHEN LARGE
