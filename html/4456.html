START MEMO NOMINATING-JUSTIFICATION:

Nissanka Weerasekera (42 Years) is currently the

Managing Director of Nextventures, a leading venture

capital company in Sri Lanka. In 1999/00 he served

as president of the venture capital association of

Sri Lanka. He is one of the leading financial

professionals in Sri Lanka and has an excellent rack

record. Nissanka\'s farsighted thinking made

Nextventures (previously known as People\'s Venture

Investment) invest in seed capital of Millennium IT

(MIT) at a time when most banks were reluctant to

invest in IT sector, as software development was

still new to Sri Lanka. This investment has paid

rich dividends with MIT today ranked as a world

class company winning contracts in Sri Lanka and

abroad. Nextventures has many other successes under

Nissanka\'s management. Recently, the company also

took equity in Textcentric, which is a US-SL joint

venture to promote e-learning.

Nissanka had his entire education in Sri Lanka. His

qualifications include a MA in Economics from the

University of Colombo and a B.Sc. in Physics from

the University of Peradeniya. He also has

professional qualifications in accounting being a

Fellow of the Chartered Institute of Management

Accountants (CIMA, UK). In addition, he is enrolled

in Chartered Financial Analysts (CFA) Program.

Previously. he has followed two short training

courses in the U.S. at George Washington University

(1993) and USAID/Stanford Research Institute, 1991.

Nissanka continues to be a useful contact for us in

Sri Lanka\'s business sector. He has excellent

presentation skills and leadership skills. His

professional background, strong personality and

leadership skills made him an ideal candidate for

higher positions within the government (such as BOI,

PERC, Bank of Ceylon or People\'s Bank, Finance

Ministry) or in the private sector. We expect the

IV program on Electronic Commerce in the Global

Economy to help Nissanka to gain knowledge of e-

commerce as his company is investing in equity in IT

startups.
