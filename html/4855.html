2. (C) THE MFA WILL DEVELOP AND COORDINATE ITALY\'S POSITIONS

ON CHR RESOLUTIONS AND RELATED ISSUES; BUT IT WILL FOLLOW

OVERARCHING PARAMETERS AND, IN SOME CASES, SPECIFIC POSITIONS

FORMULATED BY PRIME MINISTER BERLUSCONI AND HIS ADVISORS.

BERLUSCONI RECENTLY NAMED A NEW FOREIGN MINISTER, FRANCO

FRATTINI, AFTER DUAL-HATTING HIMSELF AS ACTING FOREIGN

MINISTER FOR ELEVEN MONTHS, BUT HE CLEARLY INTENDS TO KEEP AN

ACTIVE HAND IN FOREIGN POLICY. WITHIN THE MFA, HUMAN RIGHTS

OFFICE DIRECTOR COUNSELOR GIUSEPPE CALVETTA AND HIS STAFF

COORDINATE ITALIAN POSITIONS AND SHOULD SERVE AS TRAFFIC COPS

BETWEEN ROME AND THE ITALIAN DELEGATIONS IN BRUSSELS AND

GENEVA. THE COUNTRY DESKS AND DIRECTORATES ARE EXPECTED TO

TAKE THE LEAD IN DEVELOPING POSITIONS ON KEY COUNTRY

RESOLUTIONS. MULTILATERAL AFFAIRS DIRECTOR GENERAL

(A/S-EQUIVALENT) GIANCARLO ARAGONA HAS SHOWN A PROPENSITY

(MOST RECENTLY REGARDING LIBYA\'S NOMINATION AS CHR-59 CHAIR)

TO WADE INTO KEY ISSUES AND CALL COUNTERPARTS DIRECTLY.

4. ITALY IS NOT A MEMBER OF CHR-59, BUT ITS MEMBERSHIP ON THE

EU TROIKA (AS THE NEXT EU PRESIDENT FOLLOWING GREECE) SHOULD

GIVE IT SUBSTANTIAL, IF INDIRECT, INFLUENCE OVER COMMON EU

POSITIONS ON SPECIFIC CHR TOPICS THAT WOULD THEN BE DEPLOYED

BY THOSE MEMBER-STATES SERVING ON THE COMMISSION. ITALY\'S

PRINCIPAL GOALS FOR THE 2003 COMMISSION ARE TO MAINTAIN AND

IMPROVE THE COMMISSION\'S EFFECTIVENESS AND TO REINFORCE FOR

ITS OWN PROSPECTS FOR RETURNING TO THE COMMISSION IN 2004.

(ITALY WITHDREW ITS CANDIDACY WITHIN THE WEOG FOR CHR-59 IN

ORDER TO CLEAR THE WAY FOR RENEWED U.S. MEMBERSHIP.)

5. POSSIBLE LEVERAGE: IN EXCHANGE FOR POSTPONING THEIR OWN

RETURN TO THE CHR, THE ITALIANS EXPECTED ENHANCED

CONSULTATIONS WITH THE U.S. ON HUMAN RIGHTS ISSUES. COUCHED

UNDER THIS RUBRIC, ADDITIONAL BILATERAL EXCHANGES COULD BE AN

ESPECIALLY USEFUL MECHANISM TO INFLUENCE THEIR THINKING IN

ADVANCE OF INTERNAL EU DELIBERATIONS. THE ITALIANS ARE

FAITHFUL, GENERALLY ENTHUSIASTIC PARTICIPANTS IN THE EU\'S

COORDINATION PROCESS THAT PRODUCES COMMON UNION-WIDE

POSITIONS. HOWEVER, THE BERLUSCONI GOVERNMENT HAS SHOWN AN

INCREASED WILLINGNESS TO RESIST REFLEXIVELY ACCEPTING AN EU

CONSENSUS POSITION TO THE DETRIMENT OF ITS NATIONAL INTERESTS

(AN APPROACH THAT UNFORTUNATELY HAS NOT WORKED TO OUR

ADVANTAGE IN THE LIBYAN CHAIRMANSHIP DEBATE). WE MAY BE ABLE

TO USE THIS WILLINGNESS TO INFLUENCE THE ITALIAN POSITION ON

SPECIFIC CHR TOPICS OF ESPECIALLY HIGH IMPORTANCE -- BUT WE

WOULD NEED TO CHOOSE CAREFULLY. LIKE OUR EMBASSIES IN OTHER

EU CAPITALS, WE CANNOT STRESS ENOUGH THE IMPORTANCE OF EARLY

ENGAGEMENT, BEFORE EU DELIBERATIONS BEGIN TO COALESCE AROUND

A COMMON POSITION. AS THE DISCUSSIONS OVER LIBYAN

CHAIRMANSHIP HAVE SHOWN, ITALY HAS A FAIRLY UNIQUE POSITION

ON LIBYA GIVEN THE HISTORICAL RELATIONSHIP, COMMERCIAL TIES

AND GENERAL PHILOSOPHY OF ENGAGEMENT. PM BERLUSCONI\'S

OCTOBER VISIT TO TRIPOLI REINFORCED THEIR INVESTMENT IN

"REHABILITATING" AND LEFT THE MFA WITH LITTLE ROOM FOR

MANEUVER. LIKE MANY EUROPEAN FOREIGN MINISTRIES, THE ITALIANS

ARE IN MANY CASES SPREAD FAIRLY THIN. THE MFA WELCOMES

DETAILED U.S. ASSESSMENTS AND INPUT ON INDIVIDUAL ISSUES.
