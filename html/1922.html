SUBJECT: STAFFDEL MEETING AND ATTEMPTED MEETING WITH RELIGIOUS FIGURES IN HCMC

CONFIDENTIAL

REF: A. HCMC 0699 AND PREVIOUS B. HCMC 0302

1. (U) CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES ROBERT PORTER.

REASON: 1.5 (B) AND (D).

2. (C) SUMMARY: ON AUGUST 14, STAFFDEL REES MET IN HCMC

WITH HOA HAO RELIGIOUS ELDER TRAN HUU DUYEN WHO PASSED A

LIST OF 20 HOA HAO PRISONERS, SOME OF WHOM MISSION

PREVIOUSLY HAD BEEN UNAWARE. ON THE SAME DAY, THE STAFFDEL

ATTEMPTED UNSUCCESSFULLY TO MEET WITH BUDDHIST MONK THICH

QUANG DO WHO REMAINS UNDER ADMINISTRATIVE DETENTION,

CONFINED TO HIS LIVING QUARTERS AND NOT PERMITTED TO

RECEIVE VISITORS. END SUMMARY.

ATTEMPTED VISIT TO THICH QUANG DO

---------------------------------

3. (C) ON AUGUST 14, JOSEPH REES (COUNSEL TO THE HOUSE

COMMITTEE ON INTERNATIONAL RELATIONS) AND BRIAN GUNDERSON

(CHIEF OF STAFF TO HOUSE MAJORITY LEADER) WENT

UNACCOMPANIED BY TAXI TO THE THANH MINH ZEN MONASTERY,

RESIDENCE OF BUDDHIST MONK THICH QUANG DO. QUANG DO HAS

BEEN UNDER ADMINISTRATIVE DETENTION SINCE EARLY JUNE,

CONFINED TO HIS LIVING QUARTERS. HE IS NOT PERMITTED TO

RECEIVE VISITORS, AND THE PHONE LINES TO THE MONASTERY HAVE

BEEN CUT (SEE REF A). UPON THEIR RETURN FROM THE

MONASTERY, REES AND GUNDERSON RECOUNTED TO CONGENOFF WHAT

TRANSPIRED THERE.

4. (C) REES STATED THAT THE TAXI DROPPED HIM AND GUNDERSON

OFF ON THE NARROW STREET IN FRONT OF THE MONASTERY, AND

THEY WALKED THROUGH THE OPEN GATE LEADING INTO THE

MONASTERY\'S ENTRANCEWAY. IN FRONT OF THEM WAS A PAGODA,

AND TO THE LEFT WAS AN ADJACENT BUILDING, THE SECOND FLOOR

OF WHICH IS THICH QUANG DO\'S LIVING QUARTERS. ON ENTERING

THE COMPOUND, REES AND GUNDERSON ENCOUNTERED AN ELDERLY

BUDDHIST NUN AND A SMALL GIRL WHOM REES SAID HE JUDGED TO

BE 10 OR 12 YEARS OLD. REES SAID HE TOLD THE ELDERLY NUN

THEY HAD COME TO VISIT THICH QUANG DO. REES SAID THE NUN

APPEARED TAKEN ABACK AND NERVOUS. SHE TOLD THEM IN ENGLISH

THAT NO ONE WAS ALLOWED TO VISIT THICH QUANG DO. REES

STATED THAT THE SMALL GIRL THEN EXITED THROUGH THE GATE.

HE SAID HE EXPECTED HER TO RETURN WITH THE POLICE. HE

NOTED THAT UP TO THAT POINT, HE HAD BEEN SURPRISED TO SEE

NO APPARENT POLICE PRESENCE EITHER INSIDE THE MONASTERY

COMPOUND OR OUTSIDE THE GATE.

5. (C) REES SAID HE AND GUNDERSON LOITERED IN FRONT OF THE

PAGODA FOR SOME MINUTES. THE ELDERLY NUN THEN INVITED THEM

INTO THE VESTIBULE OF THE PAGODA, SAT THEM DOWN AND SERVED

THEM TEA. HE SAID THEY WAITED AROUND FOR AT LEAST 20

MINUTES TAKING NOTE OF THE UNCOMPLETED RENOVATIONS AT THE

PAGODA. THERE WAS NO ACTIVITY, NO SIGN OF THE LITTLE GIRL

OR THE POLICE. REES AND GUNDERSON ASKED THE ELDERLY NUN

ONE MORE TIME IF THEY COULD GO UP TO VISIT THICH QUANG DO.

THE NUN WAS APOLOGETIC, ACCORDING TO REES, BUT ANSWERED

THAT THICH QUANG DO WAS NOT ALLOWED TO RECEIVE VISITORS.

REES SAID HE AND GUNDERSON DECIDED NOT TO PRESS THE POINT,

TOOK THEIR LEAVE, AND RETURNED VIA TAXI TO THEIR HOTEL.

MEETING WITH HOA HAO ELDER TRAN HUU DUYEN

6. (C) EARLIER THE SAME DAY, REES AND GUNDERSON,

ACCOMPANIED BY CONGENOFF, VISITED HOA HAO ELDER TRAN HUU

DUYEN AT HIS SMALL OFFICE IN HCMC. DUYEN THANKED REES FOR

VISITING HIS FAMILY IN HCMC IN 1997 WHILE DUYEN WAS IN

PRISON. HE ALSO SAID HE BELIEVED REES\'S EFFORTS WORKING

WITH HOA HAO LEADERS RESIDENT IN THE U.S. HELPED PERSUADE

THE GVN TO RELEASE HIM IN A PRISONER AMNESTY IN 1998.

DUYEN THEN PASSED THE STAFFDEL A LIST OF 20 HOA HAO

PRISONERS AND PERSONS UNDER ADMINISTRATIVE DETENTION, FIVE

OF THE NAMES OF WHOM MISSION PREVIOUSLY HAD BEEN UNAWARE.

FOLLOWING ARE THE NEW NAMES AND TRANSLATIONS OF DUYEN\'S

HANDWRITTEN ANNOTATIONS:

LE VAN TINH (IMPRISONED AROUND 1990; Z30 PRISON, XUAN LOC)

LE VAN SON (IMPRISONED AROUND 1985; BADLY ILL; Z30 PRISON)

NGUYEN VAN DAO (IMPRISONED SOMETIME BEFORE 1999, BADLY ILL)

MS. PHAN THI TIEM (ARRESTED JULY 24, 2001 IN AN GIANG

PROVINCE FOR INVOLVEMENT IN MS. THU\'S SELF-IMMOLATION)

MS. TRAN THI DUYEN (ARRESTED JULY 24, 2001 IN AN GIANG

PROVINCE FOR INVOLVEMENT IN MS. THU\'S SELF-IMMOLATION)

(NOTE: MS. NGUYEN THI THU, A MEMBER OF A DISSIDENT HOA HAO

ORGANIZATION, KILLED HERSELF BY IMMOLATION ON MARCH 19,

2001 (SEE REF B). MISSION HAS YET TO CONFIRM THE

INFORMATION PROVIDED BY DUYEN WITH ANY OTHER SOURCES. END

NOTE.)

7. (C) DUYEN TOLD THE STAFFDEL THAT THE SITUATION FOR HOA

HAO BELIEVERS IN VIETNAM HAD IMPROVED SINCE THE GVN FORMED

A HOA HAO ADMINISTRATIVE COMMITTEE AND LEGALLY RECOGNIZED

THE HOA HAO RELIGION IN 1999. THE IMPROVEMENTS HE NOTED

INCLUDED THE ABILITY OF BELIEVERS TO CELEBRATE OPENLY THE

HOA HAO FOUNDING DAY AND THE FOUNDER\'S BIRTHDAY. DUYEN

EMPHASIZED, HOWEVER, THAT SERIOUS RESTRICTIONS REMAINED,

INCLUDING PROHIBITIONS ON DISPLAYING THE HOA HAO FLAG AND A

BAN ON ORGANIZED COMMEMORATIONS OF THE FOUNDER\'S DEATH

ANNIVERSARY. HE ADDED THAT THE ELEVEN-MEMBER HOA HAO

ADMINISTRATIVE COMMITTEE WAS NOT CHOSEN FREELY BY HOA HAO

BELIEVERS AND DOES NOT REPRESENT THE INTERESTS OF THE HOA

HAO COMMUNITY. HE ADMITTED THAT ALL ELEVEN MEMBERS OF THE

COMMITTEE ARE HOA HAO FOLLOWERS, BUT CLAIMED THAT NINE OF

THEM ARE ALSO COMMUNIST PARTY MEMBERS. "DECISIONS BY THE

COMMITTEE REPRESENT GOVERNMENT, RATHER THAN HOA HAO

INTERESTS," HE CLAIMED.

8. (C) DESPITE HIS STATED LACK OF CONFIDENCE IN THE

GOVERNMENT-SANCTIONED HOA HAO ADMINISTRATIVE COMMITTEE,

DUYEN DECLARED THAT HE DOES NOT SUPPORT THE DISSIDENT HOA
