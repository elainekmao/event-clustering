SUBJECT: SUPREME COURT GRANTS INTERIM RULING ON

COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION COURT CASE.

CLASSIFIED BY DCM EARL IRVING. REASONS: 1.5 (B) AND

(D).

REFS: HARARE 5337

1. (U) IN AN INTERIM RESPONSE TO THE LAWSUIT FILED

RECENTLY BY THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION (CFU), THE

SUPREME COURT ON SEPTEMBER 26 RULED THAT FARMERS MUST BE

NOTIFIED 90 DAYS PRIOR TO THEIR EVICTIONS FROM ACQUIRED

FARMS. IN ADDITION, THE COURT ALSO SCHEDULED A FULL

HEARING OF THE SUIT FOR NOVEMBER 6, 7, AND 8. THE COURT

RULING DOES NOT HALT THE GOZ\'S "FAST TRACK" PROGRAM, BUT

IT SHOULD SLOW DOWN THE RESETTLEMENT PROCESS SOMEWHAT,

CERTAINLY UNTIL THE SUPREME COURT HEARS THE CFU\'S

CONSTITUTIONAL CHALLENGE IN NOVEMBER.

2. (C) RICHARD AMIOT OF THE CFU TOLD ECONOFF ON

SEPTEMBER 27 THAT THIS INTERIM RULING IS IMPORTANT FOR

TWO REASONS. IT LIFTS THE IMMINENT THREAT OF EVICTION

THAT SOME FARMERS WERE FACING AT THE END OF THIS MONTH,

AND IT INDICATES THAT THE COURT IS GOING TO REQUIRE THAT

THE GOZ\'S "FAST TRACK" PROGRAM BE CONSISTENT WITH

ZIMBABWEAN LAW.

3. (C) COMMENT: THIS INTERIM COURT RULING COULD BUY SOME

TIME FOR THE CFU AND THE GOZ TO TRY AND REACH AGREEMENT
