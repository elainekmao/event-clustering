SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE REGIME\'S ATTITUDE TOWARD ELECTION

MONITORS (C-NA1-00977)

CLASSIFIED BY POLITICAL OFFICER TODD FAULK FOR REASONS:

1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) BELOW ARE EMBASSY HARARE\'S RESPONSES TO REFTEL

TASKING QUESTIONS:

A. (C) HIGH-LEVEL GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE (GOZ)

OFFICIALS HAVE EXPRESSED TO US OVER THE LAST SEVERAL

MONTHS THE GOZ\'S INTENTION TO PERMIT OFFICIAL GOVERNMENT

OBSERVERS OF THE 2002 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION. THIS

INCLUDES US, UN, EU, SADC, AND COMMONWEALTH/UK

OBSERVERS, ALTHOUGH WE DO NOT EXPECT THE GOZ TO

SPECIFICALLY INVITE ANY OBSERVERS, WITH THE POSSIBLE

EXCEPTION OF SADC. WE DO NOT EXPECT THIS OFFICIAL

POSITION TO CHANGE SUBSTANTIALLY, EVEN WITH A FURTHER

DETERIORATION OF ZIMBABWE\'S RELATIONS WITH THE WEST,

BECAUSE THE GOZ VALUES THE STAMP OF LEGITIMACY WESTERN

OBSERVERS COULD CONFER ON AN ELECTION OUTCOME.

B. (C) ASSUMING THE ZIMBABWE DEMOCRACY AND ECONOMIC

RECOVERY ACT (ZDERA) HAS NOT YET PASSED THE US CONGRESS,

THE GOZ IS LIKELY TO ALLOW AT LEAST ONE GROUP OF

OBSERVERS INTO ZIMBABWE ANYTIME BETWEEN NOW AND THE END

OF 2001. WHETHER ADDITIONAL VISITS ARE PERMITTED MAY

DEPEND ON THE PASSAGE OF THE ZDERA AS WELL AS THE

PUBLICITY AND NATURE OF THE FINDINGS OF THE INITIAL

VISIT. WE DEEM IT LIKELY THAT ONE OF THE GOZ\'S

RESPONSES TO ZDERA PASSAGE, OR THE IMPOSITION OF

PERSONAL SANCTIONS TARGETING PRESIDENT MUGABE AND KEY

ADVISORS, WILL BE TO PROHIBIT US-SPONSORED CAMPAIGN

OBSERVERS FROM ENTERING ZIMBABWE BEFORE THE ELECTION.

(NOTE: THE GOZ IS STILL LIKELY TO ADMIT OFFICIAL USG

OBSERVERS INTO ZIMBABWE FOR THE ACTUAL POLLING DAYS

THEMSELVES, GIVEN THAT ZIMBABWEAN NATIONAL ELECTIONS

HISTORICALLY HAVE BEEN PEACEFUL. END NOTE.) IN

ADDITION, IF THE INITIAL GROUP\'S, OR SUBSEQUENT GROUPS\',

FINDINGS ARE NEGATIVE AND WIDELY PUBLICIZED, THE GOZ IS

LIKELY TO BAN FUTURE VISITS BY OBSERVERS FROM THE SAME

ORGANIZATION.

C. (C) CAMPAIGN OBSERVERS, I.E. GROUPS ADMITTED WELL

BEFORE THE ELECTION, WILL ENCOUNTER FEWER GOZ OBSTACLES

THAN OBSERVERS ADMITTED TO MONITOR THE PRESIDENTIAL

ELECTION ITSELF. ONCE CAMPAIGN OBSERVERS ARE ADMITTED,

THEY WILL LIKELY BE PERMITTED TO TRAVEL FREELY ABOUT THE

COUNTRY; HOWEVER, CONSTANT SURVEILLANCE BY INTELLIGENCE

ORGANIZATIONS CANNOT BE RULED OUT. THE EXCEPTION TO

THIS WILL OCCUR IF CAMPAIGN OBSERVERS ARE IN COUNTRY

IMMEDIATELY BEFORE OR DURING OTHER ELECTIONS, SUCH AS

THE BULAWAYO MAYORAL ELECTION AND MAKONI WEST BY-

ELECTION SCHEDULED FOR 8-9 SEPTEMBER, AND THE CHIKOMBA

BY-ELECTION SCHEDULED FOR 22-23 SEPTEMBER. IF THIS IS

THE CASE, CAMPAIGN OBSERVERS ARE MORE LIKELY TO

ENCOUNTER OBSTACLES SUCH AS POLICE CORDONS AND "NO-GO"

AREAS IN THE ELECTION DISTRICTS, POSSIBLE HARASSMENT BY

ZANU-PF SUPPORTERS, AND GOZ REFUSAL TO ACCREDIT

OBSERVERS (WHICH WOULD PROHIBIT THEM FROM GOING WITHIN

100 METERS OF ANY POLLING PLACE). ELECTION OBSERVERS

DESIGNATED TO OBSERVE THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION WILL

LIKELY ENCOUNTER SIGNIFICANT OBSTACLES SUCH AS FREQUENT

CHANGES AND DELIBERATE CONFUSION OVER ACCREDITATION AND

OBSERVATION RULES, GOZ REFUSAL TO ACCREDIT UNTIL THE

LAST MINUTE, AND HIGH GOVERNMENT FEES. ONCE IN COUNTRY,

THEY MAY BE TURNED AWAY AT POLLING PLACES DESPITE

ACCREDITATION OR MAY ENCOUNTER HARASSMENT BY ZANU-PF

SUPPORTERS.

D. (C) WE HAVE NO INDICATION THAT THE MOVEMENT FOR

DEMOCRATIC CHANGE (MDC) PLANS TO INFLUENCE THE REPORTING

OF CAMPAIGN OBSERVERS OR ELECTION MONITORS, ALTHOUGH IT

IS CERTAINLY IN THE PARTY\'S INTEREST TO EXAGGERATE

REPORTS OF VIOLENCE AND INTIMIDATION.

2. (C) COMMENT: ALTHOUGH THE GOZ HAS INFORMED US OF ITS

INTENTION TO ALLOW ELECTION OBSERVERS, THERE IS NO

UNDERSTANDING WHAT THE TERMS OF REFERENCE OF THESE

"OBSERVERS" MIGHT BE NOR WHETHER THE GOZ DRAWS A CLEAR

DISTINCTION BETWEEN "OBSERVERS" AND "MONITORS." IN ANY

CASE, WE STRONGLY BELIEVE THAT FOR POLICY REASONS WE

MUST TEST THE LIMITS THAT THE GOZ AND ZANU-PF MIGHT TRY

TO IMPOSE ON OUTSIDE MONITORS AS THE ONLY POTENTIALLY

CREDIBLE WAY TO DETERMINE THE DEGREE OF FAIRNESS AND

TRANSPARENCY OF THE UPCOMING ELECTION. END COMMENT.
