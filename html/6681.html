-----------------

Security/Military

-----------------

In the past twelve hours, we have had two "duck-and-

cover" alerts in response to reported launches of

surface-to-surface missiles from Iraq. The most recent

was at approximately 2326. No/no reports of damage

or casualties, nor any indication of chemical or

biological warheads were reported.

---------

Political

---------

The Kuwaiti MFA contacted the Ops Center to confirm the legitimacy

of visa applications being made in their London Embassy on behalf

of ORHA. Working with Kuwait Desk Officer and ORHA team members

in Kuwaiti, Ops Officer was able to confirm the applications were

legitimate and pass this information to the MFA.

--------

Consular

--------

The remaining stranded Amcit passengers reported earlier have

departed Kuwait on Kuwait Airways for Chicago via London this

afternoon.

Fourteen Amcits have requested Embassy assistance to depart from

Kuwait. While the majority have requested assistance in obtaining

flight reservations, four have requested assistance with Saudi

transit visas. Embassy Consular officers will be assisting these

Amcits tomorrow a.m. Embassy Consular officer released Warden

Notice number 11, which reinforced all previous warnings to

American citizens living in Kuwait to be vigilant and practice

good personal safety and security measures. Kuwait International

Airport remains open, although most carriers have suspended or

restricted service. However, Kuwait Airways is flying at close to

normal operations.

An Embassy Consular officer and FSN met with Congen Dhahran and

Kuwaiti border officials at the Kuwaiti/Saudi border today. The

Kuwaiti border officials reported that no Americans citizens have

tried to cross the border since the start of hostilities between

Iraq and U.S. Kuwaiti authorities claim that during the past few

days, approximately 5,000 Arabs/Kuwaitis have crossed the

Kuwaiti/Saudi border, which is down from a normal of 12,000 people

on the weekend. The Consular officer and Congen confirmed that

the Saudi Embassy in Kuwait will issue gratis "emergency transit

visas" to American citizens in Kuwait, if the situation

deteriorated.

----------

Management

----------

At the request of TFIZ01, we confirmed through OMC-K that, as of

1655 only 9 oil fires had been set by Iraqi forces at the Rumalya

oil/gas separation facility; 2 of the 9 were extinguished by

civilian contractors moving with the 1st Marine Expeditionary

Force (1MEF) in Southern Iraq. This contradicts a CNN report that

fifteen oil fires were burning in Rumalya.
