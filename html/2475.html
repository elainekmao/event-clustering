SUBJECT: ITALIAN SUPPORT FOR SOMALI PEACE INITIATIVES

REF: ROME 331

CLASSIFIED BY: POL M C THOMAS COUNTRYMAN FOR REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D)

1. (C) SUMMARY AND COMMENT: BECAUSE OF ITALY\'S HISTORIC

TIES WITH SOMALIA AND ETHIOPIA, THE GOI IS PLAYING A STRONG

ROLE IN THE SOMALI PEACE PROCESS. IT APPEARS TO SEE ITS ROLE

AS BOTH ENCOURAGING ALLIED STATES TO SUPPORT THE MOI-LED

RECONCILIATION EFFORT AND RECONCILING SOMALI-ETHIOPIAN

CONFIDENTIAL

TENSIONS. THE MFA HAS INDICATED ITS CONCERN (REF A) THAT

ETHIOPIA COULD SPONSOR AGGRESSION AND FALSELY CLAIM TO BE

ACTING AT U.S. BEHEST. REPORTS OF A GOI-SPONSORED PEACE

CONFERENCE ON FEB. 15 ARE SPURIOUS. END SUMMARY AND COMMENT.

2. (C) AT A MEETING FEB. 5 WITH MINISTER BRUNO CABRAS,

DIRECTOR GENERAL FOR SUB-SAHARAN AFRICAN AFFAIRS AT THE MFA,

POST INQUIRED ABOUT A REPORT IN THE ITALIAN MEDIA OF A FEB. 8

SOMALI OPPOSITION MEETING IN ROME, TO BE FOLLOWED BY A PEACE

CONFERENCE AMONG FACTION REPRESENTATIVES HERE FEB. 15.

CABRAS STATED THAT ANY SUCH MEETINGS WERE PRIVATE INITIATIVES

OF WHICH HE WAS NOT AWARE. HE SUGGESTED THAT THE PRESS HAD

INCORRECTLY DESCRIBED AN EVENT ACTUALLY PLANNED FOR FEB. 15:

AN MFA ROUNDTABLE ON SOMALIA FOR EUROPEAN EXPERTS AND

ACADEMICS, NOT SOMALI OPPOSITION FIGURES.

3. (C) CABRAS INFORMED EMBOFFS THAT THE GOI WAS PLANNING A

SOMALIA PEACE INITIATIVE, BUT THAT IT WOULD BE COMPLEMENTARY

TO THE PLANNED NAIROBI PEACE CONFERENCE AND COORDINATED WITH

RELEVANT PLAYERS, INCLUDING THE USG. THE GOI WOULD NOT DO

ANYTHING TO HARM THE NAIROBI PROCESS.

4. (C) ASKED ABOUT A RECENT ROUNDTABLE ON SOMALIA IN WHICH

TWO FORMER GOI OFFICIALS CALLED FOR A SOMALI PEACE CONFERENCE

TO BE HELD IN ROME INSTEAD OF NAIROBI, CABRAS STATED THAT

THESE INDIVIDUALS SIDED WITH A PRO-ETHIOPIAN FACTION. THIS

GROUP PREFERRED A MEETING THAT WOULD DISCUSS ONLY TERRORISM,

THEREBY DISCREDITING SOMALIA\'S TRANSITIONAL NATIONAL

GOVERNMENT. CABRAS SAID HE HAD SHARED THE MFA\'S VIEW--THAT

THIS WAS A MISUSE OF THE TERRORISM ISSUE--WITH DEPARTMENT

OFFICIALS RECENTLY.

5. (U) A FEB. 7 MFA PRESS RELEASE REPORTED THAT MFA UNDER

SECRETARY ALFREDO MANTICA HAD MET THAT DAY WITH A SOMALI

SIPDIS

DELEGATION HEADED BY OSMAN JAMA, PRESIDENT OF THE MIXED

COMMISSION (ADMINISTRATION-PARLIAMENT) FOR NATIONAL

RECONCILIATION. THE PURPOSE OF THE ENCOUNTER WAS TO DISCUSS

THE SOMALI PEACE PROCESS AND ITALY\'S ROLE IN IT. "THIS

MEETING INITIATES AN INTENSE SERIES OF MEETINGS THROUGH WHICH

THE ITALIAN GOVERNMENT WILL MAINTAIN CONTACT WITH THE VARIOUS

SOMALI PARTIES INTERESTED IN CONTRIBUTING TO THE PEACE

PROCESS," MANTICA SAID. "ITALY\'S INITIATIVE, ALTHOUGH

INDEPENDENT, COMPLEMENTS THE IGAD PROCESS ... IN ORDER TO

SUPPORT IT AND ENSURE ITS SUCCESS.... ITALY IS READY TO

PROVIDE THE POLITICAL SUPPORT NECESSARY TO ENSURE THE

REALIZATION OF INTRA-SOMALI DIALOGUE." ECHOING POINTS MADE

IN PREVIOUS POST DISCUSSIONS WITH MFA, THE PRESS RELEASE ALSO

NOTED, "WE CONSIDER INDISPENSABLE THE WIDEST POSSIBLE

PARTICIPATION OF ALL SOMALI FACTIONS AT THE UPCOMING

CONFERENCE IN NAIROBI, AND WE HOPE THAT THE TRANSITIONAL

NATIONAL GOVERNMENT AND ALL RELEVANT PARTIES COLLABORATE

CLOSELY WITH KENYAN PRESIDENT MOI IN HIS COORDINATION EFFORTS

..."

6. (U) A FEB. 5 ARTICLE IN THE ITALIAN DAILY LA STAMPA

STATED THAT MANTICA HAD JUST RETURNED FROM A VISIT TO THE

HORN OF AFRICA, WHERE HE MET MOI AND THE REPRESENTATIVES OF

THE EIGHT WARRING SOMALI FACTIONS. MANTICA IS QUOTED AS

SAYING THAT SOMALIA SHOULD SEIZE THE OPPORTUNITY THE PEACE

CONFERENCE PRESENTS BEFORE IT IS TOO LATE, BECAUSE IT RISKS

BECOMING ANOTHER AFGHANISTAN. MANTICA EMPHASIZED THE GOI\'S

READINESS TO WORK WITH ETHIOPIA, WITH WHOM IT SHARES HISTORIC
