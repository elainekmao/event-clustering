1. (SBU) SUMMARY: BECAUSE OF THE VATICAN\'S MORAL

AUTHORITY, IT CAN INFLUENCE GOVERNMENTS\' SOCIAL AND

DEVELOPMENTAL POLICIES. PERSUADING THE VATICAN TO COME OUT

MORE FORTHRIGHTLY IN FAVOR OF GMOS IN THE CONTEXT OF

CONFIDENTIAL

AGRICULTURE IN THE DEVELOPING WORLD COULD INFLUENCE DECISION

MAKERS IN DEVELOPING COUNTRIES AND WOULD UNDERCUT ANTI-GMO

EUROPEAN ACTIVISTS\' ARGUMENTS THAT HAVE CONTRIBUTED TO

GMO-HOSTILE EU AND EU-MEMBER STATES\' POLICIES. VATICAN

DECISION MAKERS BELIEVE BIOTECHNOLOGY IS PROMISING, BUT THEY

COUNSEL CAUTION AND PATIENCE BECAUSE OF UNKNOWN RISKS. KEY

TO MOVING THE VATICAN POSITION ON BIOTECHNOLOGY AND GMOS IS

UNDERSTANDING ITS PEOPLE AND ITS FOREIGN POLICY LEITMOTIF:

"THE TRUE GOOD OF THE HUMAN PERSON." BEFORE VATICAN DECISION

MAKERS WILL PROMOTE BIOTECH, THEY WILL WANT TO SEE

ENVIRONMENTAL RISKS, AND SOCIAL AND CONSUMER CONCERNS

ADDRESSED. THE VATICAN NEEDS CONVINCING THAT BIOTECHNOLOGY

AND GMOS ARE FOR TODAY, AND THAT THE BEST SCIENCE DOVETAILS

WITH ITS LEITMOTIF.

2. (SBU) WORKING WITH EMBASSY ROME BIOTECH ACTION GROUP,

EMBASSY VATICAN IS MAKING THIS ISSUE A FOCUSED PRIORITY OF

OUR ACTIVITIES DURING THE COMING MONTHS. BUILDING ON OUR

PREVIOUS EFFORTS (E.G. AMBASSADOR HOSTED DINNER FOR GMO

EXPERT C.C. PRAKASH), WE WILL FOCUS ON BRINGING IN U.S.

EXPERTS FOR CONSULTATIONS, NOMINATING A VATICAN FOREIGN

MINISTRY OFFICIAL FOR THE INTERNATIONAL VISITORS PROGRAM,

PROPOSING ACTIVITIES ON THE MARGINS OF THE WORLD FOOD SUMMIT

IN JUNE, AND HOSTING A VISIT TO INTERNATIONAL AGRICULTURAL

RESEARCH STATIONS FOR VATICAN EXPERTS LATER IN 2002. WE HAVE

A GOOD WINDOW OF OPPORTUNITY TO PRESS THE VATICAN ON THIS

WITH THE NEW CONVERGENCE BETWEEN IT AND THE USG ON THE FLIP

SIDE OF AGRICULTURAL BIOTECH -- HUMAN CLONING. END SUMMARY.

3. (U) ARTICLES IN "LA CIVILITA CATTOLICA," A SEMI-OFFICIAL

VATICAN PUBLICATION, ARE VETTED BY THE VATICAN\'S SECRETARIAT

OF STATE BEFORE PUBLISHING. THEY TEND TO REFLECT CURRENT

VATICAN POLICY AND PREOCCUPATIONS. JESUIT PRIEST AND FORMER

PROFESSOR OF HUMAN GENETICS IN THE FACULTY OF MEDICINE AT

GEMELLI CLINIC, ANGELO SERRA, RECENTLY WROTE AN ASSESSMENT OF

PLANT AND ANIMAL BIOTECHNOLOGY FOR "LA CIVILTA CATTOLICA."

SERRA\'S ARTICLE OFFERS INSIGHTS ON VATICAN THINKING ON

SEVERAL ASPECTS OF BIOTECHNOLOGY AND ITS APPLICATION, AND

FORMULATES "NEW OBLIGATIONS" FOR SCIENCE, TECHNOLOGY AND

SOCIETY.

4. (U) THE ARTICLE LAUDS COLLABORATION BETWEEN SCIENTISTS,

REGULATORS, FARMERS AND ENVIRONMENTALISTS (PARTICULARLY IN

THE U.S.) AS A SOURCE OF CONSUMER CONFIDENCE IN GMO\'S, SERRA

DEDICATES THE GREATER PART OF HIS ARTICLE TO THE "RISKS" OF

BIOTECHNOLOGY. FIRSTLY, FOOD AND HEALTH SECURITY RISKS: THE

INCREASE IN ALLERGIES, RESISTANCE TO ANTIBIOTICS AND OF MOST

CONCERN, ECOLOGICAL DAMAGE. THE U.S. IS SINGLED OUT FOR BEING

TOO BLITHE TOWARDS RISK ASSESSMENT, BUT SERRA ALSO CRITICIZES

THE SOMETIMES-IRRATIONAL OPPOSITION OF ANTI-GM ORGANIZATIONS.

SECONDLY, SERRA SOLICITS GREATER CONSIDERATION OF THE RISKS

TO BIODIVERSITY, GIVING EXAMPLES OF COUNTRIES LIKE ITALY AND

INDIA THAT PRODUCE MOST OF THEIR FOOD FROM 10 OR SO STRAINS

OF RICE, GRAIN AND CORN. SERRA CALLS FOR ATTENTIVE AND

DETAILED OBSERVATION OF AREAS WHERE GMO ORGANISMS HAVE BEEN

INTRODUCED TO AVOID THREATS TO BIODIVERSITY THAT MAY IN TURN

ADVERSELY AFFECT HUMAN BEINGS.

CONFIDENTIAL

5. (U) SERRA\'S THIRD "RISK" DEALS WITH THE SOCIAL

REPERCUSSIONS OF BIOTECHNOLOGY, PARTICULARLY ECONOMICS AND

EQUITABLE DISTRIBUTION. HE QUOTES FIGURES THAT PUT 81 PERCENT

OF THE GLOBAL AGROCHEMICAL MARKET AND 37 PERCENT OF THE

GLOBAL SEED MARKET IN THE HANDS OF TEN COMPANIES. SERRA

LAMENTS THE PRODUCTION OF STERILE PATENTED SEEDS BECAUSE IT

CONCENTRATES GREAT WEALTH IN A FEW HANDS AND THREATENS THE

SURVIVAL OF THE POOREST. HE CALLS FOR STRATEGIES THAT ENSURE

AN EQUITABLE ECONOMIC "EVENING OUT" THAT FAVOR THE POOREST.

6. (U) SERRA CONCLUDES BY DESCRIBING PLANT BIOTECHNOLOGY\'S

GOAL AS "THE PERFECT", THE MEANS USED BY GENETIC ENGINEERING

TO REACH THIS GOAL AS "EXTRAORDINARY," THE RESULTS AS

"PROMISING," BUT THAT ITS CONSEQUENCES NEED ACCURATE ETHICAL

REFLECTION. (NOTE: SERRA\'S VIEWS TRACK VERY CLOSELY WITH THE

VIEWS WE HAVE HEARD BOTH PUBLICLY AND PRIVATELY FROM KEY

VATICAN INTERLOCUTORS IN THIS DISCUSSION, E.G., BISHOP

SGRECCIA AND ARCHBISHOP MARCHETTO END NOTE.)
