SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE SUPPORTS DRC "MINI-SUMMIT" ON MARGINS

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 04753 281218Z

OF MILLENIUM SUMMIT

REF: A) STATE 164098 B) HARARE 4749

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR MCDONALD, REASON 1.5 (B), (D)

1. (C) PER REF A INSTRUCTION, POLOFF SPOKE ON AUGUST 28

WITH MFA UNDER SECRETARY FOR AFRICA DAVID HAMADZIRIPI TO

SOLICIT THE GOZ\'S VIEWS ON A POSSIBLE DRC "MINI-SUMMIT"

ON THE MARGINS OF THE MILLENIUM SUMMIT. HAMADZIRIPI WAS

UNAWARE OF PRESIDENT CHILUBA\'S CURRENT EFFORTS BUT SAID

THE GOZ WOULD BE SUPPORTIVE OF SUCH A MEETING. THE

MILLENIUM SUMMIT WOULD PROVIDE A GOOD OCCASION FOR THE

APPROPRIATE HEADS OF STATE TO CONSULT AND DETERMINE HOW

BEST TO MOVE THE PROCESS FORWARD.

2. (C) HAMADZIRIPI SAID THE GOZ HAD RECEIVED NOTHING

OFFICIAL FROM THE DRC ON ITS ANNOUNCED SUSPENSION OF

IMPLEMENTATION OF THE LUSAKA ACCORDS. HIS UNDERSTANDING

FROM MEDIA REPORTS, HOWEVER, IS THAT THE DRC HAS NOT

SUSPENDED LUSAKA BUT, RATHER, EXPRESSED ITS VIEW THAT

RECENT DEVELOPMENTS MAY REQUIRE A "REVISION" OF THE

AGREEMENTS. HAMADZIRIPI NOTED THAT ARTICLE 26, CHAPTER 3

OF THE LUSAKA ACCORDS DOES ALLOW REVISIONS, BUT IT WAS

NOT YET CLEAR PRECISELY WHAT CHANGES THE DRC WAS SEEKING.

IN THE MEANTIME, AS FAR AS THE GOZ IS CONCERNED, "LUSAKA

REMAINS VERY MUCH ALIVE."

3. (C) ASKED ABOUT THE DRC\'S INITIATIVES WITH REGARD TO

A QUADRIPARTITE SUMMIT OF DROC, RWANDA, UGANDA, AND

BURUNDI UNDER OAU AUSPICES, HAMADZIRIPI SAID THE GOZ

CONFIDENTIAL

WELCOMED ANY MEETING INTENDED TO CONTRIBUTE TO

IMPLEMENTATION OF THE LUSAKA ACCORDS. HAMADZIRIPI WAS

MORE CAUTIOUS ABOUT KABILA\'S APPARENT WILLINGNESS

TO ACCOMMODATE THE UN MONUC PHASE II DEPLOYMENT. IT WAS

NOT YET CLEAR, HE SAID, THAT ALL OF KABILA\'S AND THE UN\'S

OBJECTIONS HAD BEEN ADEQUATELY ADDRESSED SO THAT THE

DEPLOYMENT COULD MOVE FORWARD.

COMMENT

-------

4. (C) HAMADZIRIPI\'S MORE CAUTIOUS ASSESSMENT ON THE

MONUC DEPLOYMENT DIFFERED FROM THAT PROVIDED ON AUGUST 25

BY FORMER INDUSTRY AND COMMERCE MINISTER NATHAN

SHAMUYARIRA, WHO CONVEYED TO US IN REF B MUGABE\'S

UNDERSTANDING THAT KABILA WAS WILLING TO ALLOW THE

DEPLOYMENT TO GO FORWARD. THE DIVERGENCE IN THEIR

UNDERSTANDINGS OF WHAT KABILA PLANS TO DO NEXT IS, IN OUR

VIEW, NOT SURPRISING, GIVEN THE OFTEN CONFLICTING SIGNALS

COMING OUT OF KINSHASA.

5. DAVID HAMADZIRIPI IS THE MFA\'S MOST SENIOR OFFICIAL

REGULARLY AND ACTIVELY ENGAGED ON DRC-RELATED ISSUES, AND

HE IS A FREQUENT AND RELIABLE MISSION CONTACT. MFA

CURRENTLY HAS NO DEPUTY MINISTER AND WE HAD HOPED TO

DELIVER REF A INSTRUCTION TO THE RECENTLY-APPOINTED MFA

PERMANENT SECRETARY -- WILLARD CHIWEWE -- BUT HE

APPARENTLY IS NOT YET FULLY ON BOARD. THE AMBASSADOR

EXPECTS TO MEET SHORTLY WITH CHIWEWE, WHO MOST RECENTLY

SERVED AS PERMANENT SECRETARY AT THE INFORMATION

MINISTRY.
