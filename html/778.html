2. SUMMARY: PRESIDENT FRANJO TUDJMAN AND HIS TEAM GOT AN

ALMOST UNANIMOUS MANDATE FROM PARTY DELEGATES AT A LAVISH

TWO-DAY PARTY CONGRESS IN A ZAGREB CONCERT HALL OCTOBER

14-15. TUDJMAN REAFFIRMED HIS CONSERVATIVE NATIONALIST

POLICIES, BUT ANATHEMATIZED NATIONALIST EXTREMIST

TOMISLAV MERCEP FROM THE CONGRESS ON THE SECOND DAY.

REPEATING CALLS FOR LAW AND ORDER IN THE KRAJINA, TUDJMAN

SAID CROATIA WOULD NOT GET IN THE WAY OF INTERNATIONAL

NEGOTIATING EFFORTS TO BRING ABOUT THE PEACEFUL

REINTEGRATION OF SERB-HELD EASTERN SLAVONIA, BUT

REITERATED THAT CROATIA IS PREPARED TO SOLVE THE PROBLEM

BY ITSELF. END SUMMARY.

3. TUDJMAN TOLD THE APPRECIATIVE AUDIENCE OCTOBER 14

THAT THERE WOULD BE NO DEVIATION FROM THE CENTRIST

POLICIES THAT HAD CHARACTERIZED HIS GOVERNMENT. HE MADE

A STRONG PLEA FOR LAW AND ORDER, CONDEMNING THE "CRIMINAL

ELEMENTS" WHICH HAD VIOLATED THE LAW IN THE KRAJINA.

TUDJMAN NOTED THAT CROATIA HAS BECOME A REGIONAL POWER,

CREATING AN ATMOSPHERE IN WHICH THE UNITED STATES\' PEACE

INITIATIVE COULD OPERATE. TUDJMAN SAID CROATIA WAS

WILLING TO COOPERATE WITH OTHER NATIONS AS AN INDEPENDENT

STATE.

4. ON THE SECOND DAY OF THE CONGRESS, TUDJMAN WAS

RETURNED TO THE PARTY PRESIDENCY WITH 1,199 OF 1294 VOTES

CAST. FONMIN GRANIC AND DEFMIN SUSAK WERE ELECTED AS

PARTY VICE PRESIDENTS, ALONG WITH THREE OTHERS. THE

CONGRESS ALSO ELECTED AN 80-MEMBER CENTRAL COMMITTEE.

BOSNIA-HERZEGOVINA FEDERATION PRESIDENT ZUBAK SPOKE ABOUT

THE STRATEGIC IMPORTANCE OF THE FEDERATION FOR BOSNIA-

5. TUDJMAN ANNOUNCED THAT THE HDZ LEADERSHIP HAD NO ROOM

FOR THE LIKES OF MERCEP, IN SPITE OF HIS VALUABLE WARTIME

SERVICES. MERCEP THEN WALKED OUT OF THE PARTY CONGRESS.

PLAYING THE CENTRIST THEME AGAIN, TUDJMAN SAID MERCEP\'S

PARTY PAPER WAS NO MORE ACCEPTABLE THAN THE "FERAL

TRIBUNE." COMMENT: MERCEP WAS THE COMMANDER OF A LOCAL

DEFENSE UNIT DEFENDING VUKOVAR IN 1991; AFTER VUKOVAR

FELL, HE WAS TRANSFERRED TO OGULIN, WHERE UNITS UNDER HIS

COMMAND ARE WIDELY BELIEVED TO HAVE COMMITTED WAR CRIMES.

"FERAL TRIBUNE" IS A SATIRICAL WEEKLY WELL KNOWN FOR

TAKING POTSHOTS AT TUDJMAN. END COMMENT.

6. LA BELLE JOUR: THE MOOD AT THE HDZ CONVENTION WAS

UPBEAT AND FESTIVE. SECURITY WAS POLITE AND EFFICIENT;

WELL-DRESSED YOUNG MEN AND WOMEN SERVED AS CONTROLS AND

ESCORTS. INITIAL SPEECHES WERE MADE BY PARLIAMENTARIANS

FROM NEIGHBORING COUNTRIES, WITH PRIDE OF PLACE GIVEN TO

THE GERMAN CHRISTIAN DEMOCRATIC UNION--READ BAVARIA--AND

CHRISTIAN SOCIALIST UNION, WHOSE REMARKS IN GERMAN WERE

WIDELY UNDERSTOOD BY THE AUDIENCE. THE OCTOBER 14

MORNING SESSION WAS FOLLOWED BY A GOURMET BUFFET WITH

MUSIC AND TWO KINDS OF WINE. ABOUT THE ONLY JARRING NOTE

WAS THE IRRITATION OF THE BELGIAN AMBASSADOR, WHOSE

PARKED CAR WAS REMOVED FROM THE ROADWAY AND PLACED ABOUT

FIFTY FEET AWAY BY ONE OF THE ZAGREB TRAFFIC "PINCERS."
