SUBJECT: MEDIA REACTION: DEMOCRACY AND WOMEN\'S PARTICIPATION IN

UAE POLITICS MEPI IRAQ NORTH KOREA AFGHANISTAN

1. SUMMARY: DEMOCRACY AND THE PARTICIPATION OF WOMEN IN

POLITICAL LIFE WERE THE DOMINANT ISSUES IN THE UAE MEDIA. WHILE

NOTING THE UAE PEOPLE ARE ENTHUSIASTIC AND INTERESTED IN

DEMOCRACY, UAE COLUMINST MOHAMMED AL HAMADI ARGUED IN "AL

ITTIHAD" THAT THERE IS A NEED TO LEARN DEMOCRACY BEFORE

PRACTICING IT. AS FURTHER SUPPORT, A RECENT POLL BY THE "AL

KHALEEJ" SHOWED 83% OF NATIONALS SUPPORT INCREASED PARTICIPATION

FOR WOMEN IN UAE POLITICAL LIFE, AND 96% AGREE ON THE ENTRY OF

WOMEN IN THE LABOR MARKET.

2. AT THE SAME TIME, IN AN "AL KHALEEJ" EDITORIAL, NAEEM QADAH

DESCRIBED MEPI AS A CONSPIRACY TO ACHIEVE ISRAELI AMBITIONS.

COMPARING THE MOVE TOWARDS WAR WITH IRAQ WHILE NEGOTIATING WITH

NORTH KOREA, AN "AL BAYAN" EDITORAL ASKED THE U.S. TO DEAL WITH

IRAQ LIKE NORTH KOREA AND DEMONSTRATE JUSTICE AND FAIRNESS IN ITS

INTERNATIONAL RELATIONS. FINALLY, IN A GULF NEWS FEATURE ARTICLE

ENTITLED "HELPING AFGHAN WOMEN," KAVITHA DANIEL REVIEWS THE

EFFORTS OF WOMEN FOR WOMEN, AN AMERICAN VOLUNTEER ORGANIZATION,

TO HELP AFGHAN WOMEN FIND A BETTER FUTURE FOR THEMSELVES DESPITE

THE LACK OF INTERNATIONAL FUNDING FOR RECONSTRUCTION. END

SUMMARY.

3. REFERRING TO DIFFERENT OPINIONS ON MEPI, UAE COLUMNIST

MOHAMMED AL HAMADI WROTE IN ABU DHABI-BASED SEMI-OFFICIAL "AL

ITTIHAD" ON 1/8:

"... THE UAE PEOPLE ARE ENTHUSIASTIC AND INTERESTED IN DEMOCRACY,

THE SAME AS OTHER PEOPLE IN THE WORLD ... I BELIEVE THERE IS A

BIG GAP BETWEEN THE PRACTICE OF DEMOCRACY ON THE GROUND AND THE

DESIRE TO DO SO. IN THE UAE AND OTHER GULF COUNTRIES, APPLYING

DEMOCRACY SUDDENLY AND IMMEDIATELY IS NOT THE CORRECT CHOICE.

BESIDES, IT IS A DANGEROUS STEP, ESPECIALLY WHEN IT IS NOT THE

REAL DESIRE OF THE MAJORITY OF PEOPLE AT THE MOMENT, THOUGH THERE

ARE MANY WHO INSIST IT IS. THIS DOES NOT MEAN PEOPLE DO NOT WANT

DEMOCRACY, BUT BECAUSE MANY ARE NOT FAMILIAR WITH IT."

4. SHARJAH-BASED PAN-ARAB "AL KHALEEJ" PUBLISHED THE RESULTS OF

A POLL BY ITS STUDIES UNIT ON THE PARTICIPATION OF WOMEN IN

POLITICAL LIFE ON 1/8. THE POLL SURVEYED 700 NATIONALS AND

SHOWED 83% AGREE ON THE PARTICIPATION OF WOMEN IN POLITICS:

"... 7 OUT OF 10 NATIONALS BELIEVE TRADITIONS AND CUSTOMS ARE THE

BIGGEST OBSTACLES TO WOMEN FOR MEMBERSHIP IN THE NATIONAL COUNCIL

OR A MINISTERIAL POSITION ... 20% OF NATIONALS BELIEVE THERE IS

DISCRIMINATION BETWEEN MEN AND WOMEN ... THE UAE SOCIETY TODAY IS

MORE WILLING TO ACCEPT THE EXPANSION OF THE ROLE OF WOMEN IN

POLITICAL LIFE; ONE-THIRD OF NATIONALS BELIEVE STRONGLY THAT

WOMEN MUST TAKE A LEADING POLITICAL POSITION WHERE AS HALF OF

NATIONALS BELIEVE WOMEN MUST HAVE A PRESENCE IN POLITICS ... 68%

OF NATIONALS AGREE ON WOMEN TAKING A MINISTERIAL POSITION ... 81%

OF NATIONALS SUPPORT THE MEMBERSHIP OF WOMEN IN THE NATIONAL

COUNCIL. 59% ALSO AGREE ON WOMEN TAKING A LEADING POSITION IN

DIPLOMACY ... 65% OF NATIONALS DO NOT AGREE ON WOMEN BEING

JUDGES, 28% AGREE AND 13% STRONGLY AGREE ... THE ENTIRE UAE

POPULATION AGREES WOMEN SHOULD JOIN THE LABOR MARKET WITH ONLY A

MINOR EXCEPTION. 53% STRONGLY AGREE ON WOMEN\'S WORK, 43% AGREE

IN PRINCIPLE, AND ONLY 4% DISAGREE, I.E., 96% OF NATIONALS AGREE

ON THIS ISSUE ... THE ENTRY OF WOMEN INTO THE LABOR MARKET IS ONE

OF THE MOST IMPORTANT SOLUTIONS TO THE CURRENT DEMOGRAPHIC

IMBALANCE. IT WILL REDUCE THE NEGATIVE IMPACT OF THE HIGH RATE

OF FOREIGN LABOUR IN VITAL AND SENSITIVE SECTORS OF THE NATIONAL

ECONOMY."

5. UNDER THE HEADLINE "THE U.S. INITIATIVE IS A CONSPIRACY TO

ACHIEVE ZIONIST AMBITIONS," NAEEM QADAH WROTE IN "AL KHALEEJ" ON

1/7:

"... THE WORLD DOES NOT HATE THE U.S. IT HATES CONTRADICTION,

DOUBLE STANDARDS, AND AN IMMORAL BIAS ... LINKING POWELL\'S

PROJECT OF POLITICAL AND ECONOMIC REFORMS IN THE ARAB WORLD WITH

REFURBISHING THE U.S. IMAGE IN THE REGION MEANS THE SUCCESS OF

THE PROJECT IS LINKED WITH A BETTER IMAGE. FACTS INDICATE THIS

IS IMPOSSIBLE AS LONG AS U.S. POLICY IN THE REGION IS SUBJECT TO

ISRAELI POLICY ... ALL EVIDENCES AND INDICATIONS FROM WASHINGTON

IMPLY THE U.S. WILL NEVER CONSIDER ANY SUGGESTION OR COMMENT

AFFECTING ISRAELI INTERESTS. IF THE U.S. IS REALLY KEEN ON

PROMOTING REAL DEMOCRACY, IT WILL WORK TO REMOVE OBSTACLES TO

PEACE ... ONE QUESTION MUST BE ADDRESSED BY COLIN POWELL: WHO

WILL BE CONVINCED OF WHAT YOU SAY, SECRETARY, WHEN THEY SEE THE

DAILY ORGANIZED KILLING OF PALESTINIAN CHILDREN, WOMEN, AND

ELDERS DURING THEIR SLEEP, OR WHEN THEY SEE THE U.S. AND U.K.

MILITARY BUILD-UP PLANNING TO CONSUME IRAQI OIL AND RE-OCCUPY THE

REGION IN 19TH-CENTURY STYLE?"

6. COMMENTING ON U.S. DOUBLE STANDARDS TOWARDS NORTH KOREA AND

IRAQ, DUBAI-BASED BUSINESS ORIENTED "AL BAYAN" EDITORIALIZED ON

1/8:

"... NOBODY WANTS THE AMERICANS TO TREAT NORTH KOREANS IN THE

SAME HORRIBLE MANNER THEY DO IN IRAQ. WE ASK AMERICANS TO TREAT

IRAQIS IN RECIPROCAL WAY (LIKE NORTH KOREA). THIS IS THE MINIMUM

AMOUNT OF JUSTICE AND FAIRNESS WE CAN ASK FOR INTERNATIONAL

RELATIONS..."

7. ASSESSING THE SUCCESS OF AMERICAN AND INTERNATIONAL

RECONSTRUCTION EFFORTS IN AFGHANISTAN, A SPECIAL FEATURE ENTITLED

"HELPING AFGHAN WOMEN" BY KAVITHA DANIEL IN THE ENGLISH DAILY

GULF NEWS COVERS THE WORK OF WOMEN FOR WOMEN, AN AMERICAN

VOLUNTEER ORGANIZATION, TO TRAIN AFGHAN WOMEN TOWARDS SELF-

SUSTAINABILITY WHILE COMMENTING ON THE LACK OF IMPROVEMENT IN

AFGHANISTAN. EXCERPTS INCLUDE:

"...IN ITS INTERACTION WITH THE 1,200 WOMEN RECRUITED OFF THE

STREETS OF KABUL, THE GROUP FOUND THESE ILLITERATE, UNSKILLED,

AND SOMETIMES DISPLACED, WOMEN WILLING TO BE TRAINED FOR ANY

ACTIVITY TO EARN MONEY, SAYS (LINA) ABIRAFEH (COUNTRY DIRECTOR OF

WOMEN FOR WOMEN), WHO IS IN THE UAE TO MEET DONORS AND

PHILANTHROPISTS TO RAISE AWARENESS AND FUNDS..."

"...ACCORDING TO HER (ABIRAFEH), DESPITE A YEAR OF

RECONSTRUCTION, THERE ARE NO VISIBLE SIGNS OF IMPROVEMENT IN

AFGHANISTAN. INCREASED RATES OF DOMESTIC VIOLENCE IN HOUSEHOLDS

HAS BEEN A FALLOUT OF HIGH UNEMPLOYMENT LEVELS..."

"A HOST OF OTHER WOMEN\'S ORGANIZATIONS ARE ALSO WORKING TO

IMPROVE THE CONDITIONS, BUT LACK OF FUNDS HAS FORCED THEM TO WORK

FROM PROJECT TO PROJECT, SAYS ABIRAFEH."
