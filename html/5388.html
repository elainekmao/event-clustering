SUMMARY

-------

1. WORLD FOOD PROGRAM (WFP) PRESENTED "NOTES FOR A

CONSULTATION ON STRATEGIC ISSUES" IN ROME ON JANUARY 14,

2003. WFP REITERATED ITS INTENT TO INTENSIFY ITS FOCUS IN

COUNTRIES WITH THE GREATEST NEED - WHICH IT DEFINED AS THOSE

WITH A PER CAPITA GROSS DOMESTIC PRODUCT (GDI) OF LESS THAN

USD 885 AND A RATE OF UNDER-5 CHILD STUNTING GREATER THAN 30

PERCENT. THE TARGET WOULD BE TO DEDICATE 75 PERCENT OF WFP\'S

DEVELOPMENT RESOURCES TO THESE 29 COUNTRIES BY THE END OF

THE 2004-2005 BIENNIUM. WFP FURTHER PROPOSED FOUR PROGRAM

WORK OBJECTIVES: A) SUPPORTING UNIVERSAL PRIMARY EDUCATION

AND REDUCING GENDER DISPARITY IN ACCESS TO EDUCATION; B)

SUPPORTING IMPROVED HEALTH OF CHILDREN AND VULNERABLE GROUPS

THROUGH NUTRITION EDUCATION; C) SAVING LIVES IN CRISIS

SITUATIONS; AND D) PRESERVING LIVELIHOODS IN CRISIS

BACKGROUND

----------

2. THESE NOTES FORM PART OF A WORK IN PROCESS LEADING UP TO

A STRATEGIC FRAMEWORK TO BE SUBMITTED FOR EXECUTIVE BOARD

APPROVAL IN MAY 2003. THE NOTES REFLECT THE WFP

HAS PRECIPITOUSLY FALLEN OVER THE PAST DECADE - FROM ALMOST

U.S. DOLLARS 1 BILLION A DECADE AGO TO U.S. DOLLARS 273

MILLION THIS PAST YEAR, WITH THE CLEAR IMPROBABILITY THAT

THIS TREND WILL BE REVERSED IN THE FUTURE. DESPITE THE

ISSUANCE OF THE WFP FOOD FOR DEVELOPMENT POLICY (FAAD) IN

1999, THE REALITY IS THAT A NUMBER OF MAJOR DONORS (EC,

SWEDEN, JAPAN, THE NETHERLANDS) HAVE MADE A POLICY DECISION

NOT TO CONTRIBUTE TO WFP\'S DEVELOPMENT PROGRAM. MOREOVER,

THE UNITED STATES HAS REPEATEDLY ASKED THAT BOTH WFP AND THE

EXECUTIVE BOARD REFOCUS ON THE KEY RECOMMENDATION OF THE

1994 TRIPARTITE EVALUATION OF WFP (NORWAY, THE NETHERLANDS,

AND CANADA) WHICH SUGGESTED THAT WFP REDUCE THE NUMBER OF

COUNTRIES IN WHICH IT FUNCTIONS TO ABOUT 50.

IN HOW MANY COUNTRIES SHOULD WFP WORK?

--------------------------------------

6. WFP FLOATED A "BALLON D\'ESSAI" ("TRIAL BALLOON") TO FOCUS

ITS DEVELOPMENT RESOURCES ON 29 COUNTRIES WHOSE PER CAPITA

GROSS DOMESTIC PRODUCT (GDI) IS LESS THAN U.S. DOLLARS 885

AND WHOSE RATE OF UNDER-5 CHILD STUNTING IS GREATER THAN 30

PERCENT. THE TARGET WOULD BE TO DEDICATE 75 PERCENT OF WFP\'S

DEVELOPMENT RESOURCES TO THESE 29 COUNTRIES BY THE END OF

THE 2004-2005 BIENNIUM. NOTE: WFP PRESENTLY IMPLEMENTS

DEVELOPMENT ACTIVITIES IN 54 COUNTRIES. END NOTE. THERE WAS

CONSIDERABLE REACTION TO THIS PROPOSAL, PARTICULARLY FROM

ASIAN AND LATIN AMERICAN PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES PRESENT.

CONTINUING DISCUSSIONS ARE NECESSARY NOT ONLY TO REACH

CONSENSUS ON THIS DE FACTO "DOWNSIZING" CRITERIA, BUT ALSO

TO DEVELOP SPECIFIC COUNTRY PHASE OUT TIMETABLES. IT WAS

INCREASING RESOURCES TO SUPPORT WFP\'S WORK

------------------------------------------

7. WFP\'S EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR WANTS TO INCREASE WFP\'S REVENUE

BASE FROM ABOUT 1.9 BILLION DOLLARS TO 3 BILLION DOLLARS PER

YEAR. THIS REQUIRES GREATLY EXPANDED TRADITIONAL DONOR

SUPPORT, REACHING OUT CREATIVELY TO NON-TRADITIONAL DONORS,

ENGAGING THE PRIVATE SECTOR, AND IMPROVING LEVERAGE OF

RESOURCES. FOR EXAMPLE, INDIA, AN EMERGING DONOR, HAS

AGREED TO DONATE 1 MILLION TONS OF WHEAT FOR AFGHANISTAN.

OTHER EMERGING DONORS INCLUDE ALGERIA, VIET NAM, THAILAND,

THE REPUBLIC OF SOUTH AFRICA (RSA), THE RUSSIAN FEDERATION

AND PAKISTAN. IN ADDITION, WFP IS BEGINNING TO SEEK OUT

PRIVATE SECTOR PARTNERSHIPS, COGNIZANT THAT UNICEF RECEIVES

MORE THAN 30 PERCENT OF ITS REVENUES FROM PRIVATE DONATIONS.

8. SPECIFICALLY, WFP WILL:

-ASK CASH DONORS TO ALLOW WFP TO "TWIN" THEIR CASH WITH THE

COMMODITY CONTRIBUTIONS FROM EMERGING DONORS (SEE REF A);

-ASK THOSE DONORS THAT PREFER TO HAVE THEIR CASH DONATIONS

USED TO PURCHASE FOOD TO ALLOW WFP TO PURCHASE THAT FOOD

FROM EMERGING DONORS, WHERE SUCH A TRANSACTION MEETS THE

TEST OF TIMELINESS OF DELIVERY, QUALITY OF PRODUCT AND COST

COMPETITIVENESS;

-ASK DONORS TO ESTABLISH A FUND (OR OTHER FINANCIAL

COMMENT

-------

9. SIMPLY, WAR AND DROUGHT (PARTICULARLY IN SUB-SAHARAN

AFRICA) HAVE DRASTICALLY RATCHETED UP HUMANITARIAN FOOD AID

NEEDS AND OVERWHELMED WFP\'S RESOURCING CAPACITY. THE

STRATEGIC NOTES PAPER RECOGNIZES THAT THE ORGANIZATION\'S

ACTUAL AND ANTICIPATED DEVELOPMENT RESOURCES ARE ABOUT HALF

OF WHAT ARE NEEDED FOR APPROVED COUNTRY PROGRAMS. WFP IS

INTENT ON FINDING WAYS OF MAXIMIZING INPUTS FROM EMERGING

DONORS, BUT THIS RUNS INTO THE CONVENTIONAL FULL COST
