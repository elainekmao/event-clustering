SUBJECT: COMMERCIAL FARMER ABDUCTED BY WAR VETERANS AND

TAKEN TO STATE HOUSE IN EFFORT TO FORCE AN AUDIENCE WITH

PRESIDENT MUGABE

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 06910 270923Z

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD FOR REASONS 1.5

(B) AND (D).

1. (U) THE DECEMBER 7 EDITION OF THE INDEPENDENT

NEWSPAPER "THE DAILY NEWS" CARRIED A FRONT PAGE STORY

THAT A COMMERCIAL FARMER, LANCE KENNEDY, HAD BEEN

FORCIBLY ABDUCTED BY A GROUP OF TWENTY WAR VETERANS WHO

WERE OCCUPYING HIS SIGARO FARM, AND FORCED TO DRIVE SOME

OF HIS ABDUCTORS INTO HARARE TO STATE HOUSE IN AN

ATTEMPT TO GAIN AN AUDIENCE WITH PRESIDENT MUGABE. THIS

ABDUCTION OCCURRED AFTER A DISPUTE BETWEEN MR. KENNEDY

AND THE WAR VETERANS OVER THE RIGHT TO PLANT ON A PIECE

OF LAND ON KENNEDY\'S FARM. THE WAR VETERANS WERE

REFUSED ENTRANCE TO STATE HOUSE BY POLICE GUARDS. THEY

THEN REMAINED OUTSIDE STATE HOUSE IN KENNEDY\'S PARKED

CAR FOR ANOTHER TWO HOURS, BEFORE THE POLICE FORCED THEM

TO LEAVE. THEY THEN RETURNED TO THE SIGARO FARM, WHERE

MR. KENNEDY WAS RELEASED UNHURT.

2. (C) ECONOFF CONTACTED JERRY DAVISON, THE COMMERCIAL

FARMERS UNION EXECUTIVE OFFICER FOR WATER AND

ENVIRONMENTAL AFFAIRS TO GET ADDITIONAL DETAIL ON THIS

STORY. DAVISON TOLD ECONOFF THAT KENNEDY\'S FARM IS ONLY

ABOUT TEN MILES NORTH OF HARARE. THE WAR VETERANS

ABDUCTED MR. KENNEDY AND BROUGHT HIM TO STATE HOUSE,

SEEKING AN AUDIENCE WITH MUGABE IN ORDER TO RESOLVE

THEIR DISPUTE. NORMALLY THESE DISPUTES ARE RESOLVED IN

THE DISTRICT SEAT IN WHICH A FARM IS LOCATED. IN THIS

CASE, HOWEVER, BECAUSE THIS WOULD HAVE BEEN A LONGER

TRIP, WITH THE DISTRICT SEAT LOCATED ABOUT THIRTY MILES

NORTH OF THE FARM, THE WAR VETERANS DECIDED TO SAVE TIME

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 06910 270923Z

AND GO RIGHT TO THE TOP AND HAVE MUGABE SETTLE THE

DISPUTE. DAVISON CONFIRMED THAT WHILE KENNEDY WAS

FORCIBLY ABDUCTED, HE WAS NOT PHYSICALLY ASSAULTED BY

THE WAR VETERANS, AND HE WAS RELEASED UNHARMED AT HIS

FARM FOLLOWING THE FAILURE OF THE WAR VETS TO GAIN THEIR

DESIRED AUDIENCE WITH MUGABE.

3. (C) COMMENT: WHILE THERE IS NO QUESTION THAT

PRESIDENT MUGABE HAS USED THE WAR VETERANS AS ENFORCERS

FOR ZANU-PF DURING THE ELECTIONS, AS WELL AS SWAT TEAMS

IN THE FARM INVASIONS, THIS STORY AGAIN RAISES THE

QUESTION OF HOW MUCH DAY-TO-DAY CONTROL MUGABE AND THE

GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE HAVE OVER THE INCREASINGLY

AUDACIOUS WAR VETERANS. CLEARLY, THIS IS AN

EMBARRASSING INCIDENT FOR MUGABE, AND HE WOULD NOT HAVE

ALLOWED IT TO OCCUR WERE THERE AN ESTABLISHED COMMAND-

AND-CONTROL STRUCTURE IN PLACE OVER THE WAR VETS. END

COMMENT.

MCDONALD
