1. (C) SUMMARY: S/E DANFORTH AND DELEGATION

DISCUSSED STRATEGIES FOR MOVING FORWARD SUDAN PEACE

PROCESS 12/20 WITH SANT\'EGIDIO COMMUNITY AFRICA

SPECIALISTS FABIO RICCARDI AND DON MATTEO ZUPPI.

RICCARDI PROPOSED CONFIDENTIAL TALKS BETWEEN

TRUSTED SENIOR GOS AND SPLM REPRESENTATIVES AT A

LOCATION OUT OF THE LIMELIGHT, PERHAPS AT

SANT\'EGIDIO\'S HEADQUARTERS. DANFORTH AND GROUP

TOOK SUGGESTION ON BOARD WITHOUT MAKING ANY

COMMITMENTS. LATER MEETING WITH VATICAN FM TAURAN,

S/E BRIEFED ON HIS SUDAN MISSION, AND TAURAN

EXPLAINED VATICAN VIEW OF SUDAN AS THREAT TO

CHRISTIANITY IN AFRICA. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) S/E DANFORTH, ACCOMPANIED BY AMBASSADOR

ROBERT OAKLEY, AF/E JEFF MILLINGTON AND SUDAN DESK

OFFICER RAAD VISITED SANT\'EGIDIO HEADQUARTERS IN

ROME 12/20 ACCOMPANIED BY AMBASSADOR NICHOLSON AND

EMBASSY VATICAN OFFICERS. S/E DANFORTH BRIEFED THE

SANT\'EGIDIO REPS ON HIS RECENT VISIT TO THE REGION,

UNDERSCORING THE FOUR POINTS HE HAD PROPOSED AS

CONFIDENCE BUILDING MEASURES. HE SAID HE WAS

STRUCK THAT PEOPLE IN THE GOS ARE ALMOST

PATHOLOGICALLY DISHONEST. AS IN REFTEL,

SANT\'EGIDIO\'S SUDAN SPECIALIST, FABIO RICCARDI,

PRESENTED HIS IDEA OF A CONFIDENTIAL OFF-SITE

MEDIATION TO MOVE THE SUDAN PEACE PROCESS FORWARD.

3. (C) RICCARDI AGREED WITH S/E DANFORTH THAT THE

GOS TOO OFTEN WILL MAKE ASSURANCES THAT IT HAS NO

INTENTION OF KEEPING. HE SAID THAT THE U.S. NOW

HAS STRONG CARDS, HOWEVER, BECAUSE THE GOS WISHES

AFTER SEPTEMBER 11 TO PROVE IT\'S ANTI-TERRORIST

BONAFIDES AND, MOREOVER, USE ITS PROSPECTIVE OIL

WEALTH TO BRING THE COUNTRY MORE FULLY INTO THE

COMMUNITY OF NATIONS. THEY WOULDN\'T BE ABLE TO DO

THAT SO LONG AS THEY HAVE AN ACTIVE INSURGENCY TO

WHICH THEY REACT BY BOMBING CIVILIANS. RICCARDI

SAID GARANG IS PERPETUATING THE CONFLICT BY

SUSTAINING MILITARY PRESSURE ON THE GOS. HE

WONDERED WHETHER GARANG HAS IT IN HIMSELF TO STOP

THE FIGHTING. HE SHARED MILLINGTON\'S FEAR THAT

GARANG, LIVING COMFORTABLY OUTSIDE OF SUDAN, MAY BE

HAPPY TO SEE THE FIGHTING CONTINUE INDEFINITELY.

4. (C) RICCARDI CONTINUED THAT DURING HIS RECENT

KHARTOUM VISIT HE HAD DISCUSSED HIS IDEA OF DIRECT

AND CONFIDENTIAL TALKS AT SANT\'EGIDIO\'S ROME

HEADQUARTERS WITH SENIOR MEMBERS OF THE GOS, WHO

ENDORSED IT IN PRINCIPAL. HE SOUGHT USG HELP TO

PERSUADE SPLM LEADER GARANG TO ACCEPT THE PROPOSAL.

IN RESPONSE TO MILLINGTON\'S CONCERN THAT THE

PARTIES MIGHT USE SUCH A PROCESS TO CUT A DEAL

BETWEEN THEMSELVES THAT WOULDN\'T RESPECT THE

PRINCIPLES SUCH AS RELIGIOUS FREEDOM INHERENT IN

IGAD, RICCARDI SAID THAT THAT IS WHY THERE WAS NEED

FOR A MEDIATOR. SANT\'EGIDIO COULD BE COUNTED ON TO

ENSURE AT THE RIGHT MOMENT THAT THOSE PRINCIPLES

ARE MADE PART OF THE MEDIATION. IN RESPONSE TO S/E

DANFORTH\'S QUERY, RICCARDI CLARIFIED THAT HE WAS

PROPOSING BACK-CHANNEL TALKS TOWARDS A POLITICAL

ARRANGEMENT FOR GARANG AND THE SPLM/SPLA TO HAVE A

ROLE IN A POST-CONFLICT GOS.

5. (C) DON MATTEO INTERJECTED THAT WHAT WAS

NEEDED WAS A FRAMEWORK BOTH TO ENGAGE GARANG AND TO

PROTECT HIM FROM BECOMING CO-OPTED BY THE GOS

WITHOUT OBTAINING ANY REAL FREEDOMS FOR HIS PEOPLE.

IGAD COULD BE THE FRAMEWORK FOR A FINAL AGREEMENT,

BUT A MEDIATED CONFIDENTIAL NEGOTIATION WOULD BE

NECESSARY TO ELICIT CONCRETE ELEMENTS OF THE

COMPROMISE BETWEEN THE WARRING SIDES. THE TALKS

SHOULD NOT BE BETWEEN AL-BASHIR AND GARANG, BUT

BETWEEN TRUSTED SENIOR LIEUTENANTS -- ASSUMING WE

CAN OVERCOME GARANG\'S MISTRUST OF HIS LIEUTENANTS.

WE SHOULDN\'T EXPECT QUICK RESULTS EITHER; THE

MOZAMBIQUE TALKS WENT ON FOR OVER TWO YEARS, THE

FIRST YEAR OF WHICH WAS IMPORTANT FOR THE TWO SIDES

TO BEGIN TO BUILD TRUST.

PARTICIPATE IF, IN THE END, NOBODY ELSE COULD BE

IDENTIFIED TO NEGOTIATE FOR THE SPLM. RICCARDI

SAID THAT FOR THIS FORMULA TO WORK, GARANG MUST BE

PERSUADED THAT MOVING FORWARD DOESN\'T THREATEN HIM.

7. (C) S/E DANFORTH THANKED RICCARDI FOR HIS VIEWS

AND PROMISED TO KEEP THEM IN MIND AS HE DISCUSSED

THE PEACE PROCESS WITH OTHER PLAYERS. HE

UNDERSCORED THAT THE USG WAS NOT IN A POSITION TO

SUPPORT ANOTHER PEACE INITIATIVE BECAUSE A

PROLIFERATION OF INITIATIVES WOULD PROVIDE AN

IRRESISTIBLE TEMPTATION FOR THE PARTIES TO GO

"INITIATIVE SHOPPING" WITHOUT MOVING TOWARDS PEACE.

NEVERTHELESS, RICCARDI HAD SOME INTERESTING IDEAS

THAT COULD WELL BE INCORPORATED IF CONDITIONS

ALLOWED.

8. (C) SUBSEQUENTLY S/E DANFORTH BRIEFED VATICAN

FM ON HIS RECENT VISIT TO THE REGION AND EFFORTS TO

PROMOTE PEACE. TAURAN, WHO IS NORMALLY THE

DEFINITION OF TACITURNITY, WAS MOST ANIMATED IN

AGREEING WITH DANFORTH ON THE PROBLEM OF PINNING

GOS INTERLOCUTORS TO A TRUTHFUL STATEMENT. TAURAN

SAID THE GOS WAS NOT TO BE TRUSTED -- IT THREATENED

FREEDOM OF RELIGION AND HUMAN RIGHTS. MOREOVER, HE

IMPLIED, THE CONFLICT IN SUDAN WAS EMBLEMATIC OF A

BROADER STRUGGLE BETWEEN ISLAM AND CHRISTIANITY

ACROSS THE AFRICAN CONTINENT. (NOTE: A SIMILAR

VIEW WAS EXPRESSED TO AMBASSADOR EARLIER IN THE

WEEK, DURING A VISIT WITH CARDINAL SEPE, PRESIDENT

OF THE PONTIFICAL COUNCIL FOR THE PROPAGATION OF

THE FAITH -- THE VATICAN\'S MISSIONARY

ORGANIZATION).
