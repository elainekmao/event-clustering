2. (SBU) TRI-MISSION BIOTECH IWG: POLOFF AND ASSISTANT

ATTENDED 02/15 TRI-MISSION IWG (EMBASSY ROME, FODAG AND

EMBASSY VATICAN) LED BY EMBASSY ROME AGCOUNS, TO DEVELOP

APPROACH ON AGRICULTURAL BIOTECHNOLOGY ISSUES. EMBASSY

VATICAN WILL LOOK FOR OPPORTUNITIES (EXPERT SPEAKERS, DVC\'S

INVITATIONAL TRAVEL, ETC.) TO CARRY THE USG MESSAGE ON

BIOTECHNOLOGY TO VATICAN DECISION-MAKERS IN COORDINATION WITH

THE OTHER U.S. MISSIONS IN ROME.

3. (C) SANT\'EGIDIO DINNER IHO AMBASSADOR: SANT\'EGIDIO

FOUNDER ANDREA RICCARDI HOSTED AMBASSADOR AND EMBOFFS TO

RECOGNIZE CLOSE COOPERATION WITH THE USG. KEY EMBASSY

INTERLOCUTORS INCLUDED DON VICENZO PAGLIA, BALKANS EXPERT AND

BISHOP OF TERNI, AND AFRICA HAND DON MATTEO ZUPPI. RICCARDI

AND ZUPPI RELATED RECENT AND PAST SANT\'EGIDIO INTERACTIONS

WITH FIDEL CASTRO.

DISCRETE CONTACTS WITH THE CIRCA 25 PRC SEMINARIANS SPONSORED

BY THE VATICAN FOR CLERICAL TRAINING IN ROME (REFTEL).

8. (U) CATHOLIC U IN TIRANA? DCM AND POLOFF WERE TOLD 02/27

BY BALKANS DIRECTOR MARINO THAT THE GOA HAD DONATED, FOR $1,

A LARGE PLOT OF LAND IN ALBANIA OUTSIDE TIRANA FOR THE SITE

OF A NEW CATHOLIC UNIVERSITY. MARINO SAID THE VATICAN

SUPPORTS THIS PROJECT, AND THAT THE NEXT PHASE IS FOR THE

TIRANA DIOCESE TO ORGANIZE AN ENDOWMENT FOR FUNDING

CONSTRUCTION AND OPERATIONS. THE HOLY SEE ITSELF WOULD ONLY

PROVIDE VERY LIMITED RESOURCES IN THAT REGARD. HE INQUIRED

WHETHER PD/ECA HAD ANY PROGRAMS TO SUPPORT UNIVERSITY
