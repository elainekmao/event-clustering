USG-VIETNAM INCREASE ENGAGEMENT ON HEALTH ISSUES

--------------------------------------------- ---

1. IN A SIGNIFICANT BROADENING OF USG-GVN ENGAGEMENT ON

HEALTH ISSUES, THE CHARGE AND VICE MINISTER OF HEALTH TRAN

CHI LIEM SIGNED AN AGREEMENT ON OCTOBER 23 TO PROVIDE $2

MILLION TO THE HEALTH MINISTRY TO EXPAND ITS HIV/AIDS

PREVENTION, CARE AND TREATMENT PROGRAMS. FROM THE CDC GLOBAL

AIDS PROGRAM, THE FUNDS COVER THE FIRST YEAR OF A PROJECT

THAT WILL CONTINUE FOR FIVE YEARS, TOTALING MORE THAN $10

MILLION. THE FUNDING WILL GO TO SUPPORT A WIDE RANGE OF

HIV/AIDS PROGRAMS AND ACTIVITIES INCLUDING PEER EDUCATION,

VOLUNTARY COUNSELING AND TESTING, PREVENTION AND TREATMENT OF

OTHER SEXUALLY TRANSMITTED INFECTIONS, TUBERCULOSIS DIAGNOSIS

AND TREATMENT, AND RELATED TRAINING. CDC HAS ALSO ASSIGNED

TECHNICAL ADVISORS TO ASSIST THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH IN

PLANNING, IMPLEMENTING AND EVALUATING THE PROGRAM. THE

EXPANDED PROGRAM BUILDS ON THE TRAINING AND OPERATIONAL

RESEARCH THAT CDC HAS BEEN PROVIDING TO THE VIETNAM AIDS

PROGRAM SINCE 1999.

2. TWENTY-FOUR COUNTRIES IN ASIA, AFRICA, AND THE AMERICAS

ARE NOW ELIGIBLE FOR THE CDC GLOBAL AIDS PROGRAM, INCLUDING

VIETNAM, WHICH BECAME ELIGIBLE IN 2001, ALONG WITH THAILAND

AND CAMBODIA.

3. IN ADDITION TO CDC/HHS SUPPORT FOR THE COMBAT AGAINST

HIV/AIDS, USAID ALSO CARRIES OUT AN EXTENSIVE ANTI-HIV/AIDS

EFFORT. USAID HAS BEEN FUNDING HIV/AIDS EDUCATION AND

PREVENTION ACTIVITIES THROUGH ITS NGO PARTNERS OVER THE PAST

FOUR YEARS. USAID\'S CURRENT PROGRAM TOTALS APPROXIMATELY $2

MILLION PER YEAR FOR A VARIETY OF ACTIVITIES WITH A NUMBER OF

NGO PARTNERS. USAID IS FUNDING FAMILY HEALTH INTERNATIONAL

(FHI) TO PROVIDE COMPREHENSIVE HIV/AIDS PREVENTION PROGRAMS

IN FOUR PROVINCES INCLUDING DROP-IN CENTERS FOR INJECTION

DRUG USERS (IDUS) AND COMMERCIAL SEX WORKERS AND EXTENSIVE

BEHAVIOR CHANGE COMMUNICATION CAMPAIGNS. IN ADDITION, FHI IS

IMPLEMENTING BEHAVIORAL SURVEILLANCE SURVEYS IN FIVE

PROVINCES TO TRACK BEHAVIOR CHANGES IN HIGH-RISK POPULATIONS

SUCH AS IDUS AND SEX WORKERS. USAID FUNDS CONDOM SOCIAL

MARKETING THROUGH DKT INTERNATIONAL IN SIX PROVINCES. USAID

HAS INITIATED A GRANT TO THE NGO NETWORKS FOR HEALTH PROJECT

TO BEGIN AN HIV/AIDS PREVENTION INITIATIVE IN LAO CAI

PROVINCE WITH THE ETHNIC MINORITY POPULATION. THIS ACTIVITY

FOCUSES ON YOUTH WORKERS IN TOURISM AND MINING. FINALLY,

USAID IS ABOUT TO BEGIN TWO NEW ACTIVITIES: A $300,000 STUDY

ON STIGMA AND DISCRIMINATION TO BE CARRIED OUT BY THE

INTERNATIONAL CENTER FOR RESEARCH ON WOMEN (ICRW), AND A

POLICY DEVELOPMENT ACTIVITY FOCUSING ON PEOPLE LIVING WITH

AIDS, THROUGH THE FUTURES GROUP\'S POLICY PROJECT.

4. IN EARLY OCTOBER, MORE THAN 100 VIETNAMESE PARTICIPATED

IN THE SIXTH INTERNATIONAL CONGRESS ON AIDS AND THE PACIFIC

(ICAAP) IN MELBOURNE. THE VIETNAMESE DELEGATION INCLUDED THE

MINISTER OF HEALTH, 25 PARTICIPANTS SPONSORED BY USAID, AND

10 SPONSORED BY CDC.

HIV/AIDS IN VIETNAM

-------------------

5. ACCORDING TO A RECENT GOVERNMENT PRESS RELEASE, VIETNAM

HAS DETECTED 39,845 HIV INFECTED PEOPLE AS OF OCTOBER 1.

HOWEVER, IT IS ESTIMATED THAT 107,000 PEOPLE ARE LIVING WITH

HIV/AIDS. AMONG THEM, 5,955 HAVE DEVELOPED FULL-BLOWN AIDS

AND 3,247 DIED. THE LARGEST NUMBERS OF HIV-INFECTED PERSONS

AND AIDS PATIENTS ARE FOUND IN QUANG NINH PROVINCE, AND THE

CITIES OF HAIPHONG, HANOI, AND HO CHI MINH CITY. ALL 61

PROVINCES HAVE REPORTED HIV/AIDS CASES.

-------

COMMENT

-------

6. VIETNAM HAS EXPERIENCED A MARKED INCREASE IN HIV

INFECTION. DRUG INJECTION, LONG A PROBLEM IN VIETNAM, HAS

CONTRIBUTED TO THE EXPLOSIVE EPIDEMIC GROWTH. HIV INFECTION

AMONG IDUS NOW EXCEEDS 50% IN HO CHI MINH CITY AND HAIPHONG.

THERE ARE ALARMING LINKAGES BETWEEN DRUG USERS AND OTHER

POPULATIONS, PARTICULARLY SEX WORKERS. NEARLY A QUARTER OF

IDUS IN HANOI AND OVER A FIFTH IN DA NANG SAID THAT THEY HAD

BOUGHT SEX IN THE PAST YEAR, AND MOST DID NOT USE CONDOMS.

THIS CLEARLY REPRESENTS A WORRYING POTENTIAL FOR THE WIDER

SPREAD OF HIV TO SEX WORKERS AND THEIR CLIENTS.

7. THERE ARE POSITIVE SIGNS THAT THE VIETNAMESE DELEGATION\'S

ATTENDANCE AT THE ICAAP IN MELBOURNE HAS HELPED STRENGTHEN

THE HAND OF THOSE IN THE GVN WHO ARGUE THAT HARM REDUCTION

EFFORTS (SUCH AS NEEDLE EXCHANGE PROGRAMS) AND OTHER PUBLIC

HEALTH APPROACHES ARE MORE EFFECTIVE IN REDUCING HIV/AIDS

THAN THE CURRENT OFFICIAL GVN MENTALITY, WHICH TREATS

HIV/AIDS AS A "SOCIAL EVIL" AND HAS HAMPERED THE FIGHT

AGAINST THE DISEASE.
