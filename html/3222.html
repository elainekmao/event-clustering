SUBJECT: RESEARCHING RELIGION AT THE PARTY SCHOOL

REF: HANOI 156

1. (U) SUMMARY. A SEVEN YEAR-OLD CENTER AT THE MAIN PARTY

SCHOOL FOCUSES ON THE ROLE OF RELIGION IN VIETNAMESE HISTORY

AND PRESENT, AND HELPS TO EDUCATE UP-AND-COMING PARTY

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 HANOI 01653 01 OF 02 011003Z

MEMBERS AND GOVERNMENT OFFICIALS ABOUT THE "POSITIVE" ROLE

RELIGION CAN PLAY IN VIETNAM\'S DEVELOPMENT. ITS OFFICIALS

HAVE PROMISED LATER THIS SUMMER TO PROVIDE DETAILED FEEDBACK

ON THE VIETNAM CHAPTER OF THE INTERNATIONAL RELIGIOUS

FREEDOM REPORT. END SUMMARY.

2. (U) LIKE ITS COUNTERPART TO ANALYZE HUMAN RIGHTS

(REFTEL), THE "CENTER FOR SCIENCE OF BELIEF AND RELIGION"

("TRUNG TAM KHOA HOC VE TIN NGUONG VA TON GIAO," SOMETIMES

TRANSLATED IN OFFICIAL DOCUMENTS AS THE CENTER FOR RELIGIOUS

STUDIES) OF THE HO CHI MINH NATIONAL POLITICAL ACADEMY

DEMONSTRATES THE RECOGNITION OF THE COMMUNIST PARTY OF

VIETNAM (CPV) THAT IT MUST FACE SENSITIVE ISSUES HEAD ON AND

MUST ALSO TRAIN ITS "BEST AND BRIGHTEST" IN HOW TO IMPLEMENT

OFFICIAL POLICY. POL/C MET ON JUNE 28 WITH THE CENTER\'S

DIRECTOR, DR. NGUYEN DUC LU, IN THE EMBASSY\'S FIRST CONTACT

WITH THIS CENTER.

3. (U) ACCORDING TO DR. LU, THE ACADEMY ESTABLISHED THE

CENTER IN 1995, AND TASKED IT WITH UNDERTAKING BOTH RESEARCH

AND TRAINING. THE CENTER\'S FOURTEEN RESEARCH STAFFERS

CURRENTLY INCLUDE EIGHT WITH DOCTORATES. NONE OF THEM IS A

PRACTITIONER OF ANY MAJOR RELIGION, DR. LU ADMITTED, WHILE

STRESSING THAT SOME OF THEM, LIKE MOST VIETNAMESE, HAVE

"BELIEF" SYSTEMS SUCH AS ANCESTOR WORSHIP (WHICH HE STRESSED

WAS NOT SUPERSTITIOUS). DR. LU CLAIMED THAT CPV POLICY DID

NOT AT ALL PROHIBIT PARTY MEMBERS FROM BEING RELIGIOUS, BUT

AGREED THAT RELIGIOUS CPV MEMBERS WERE "VERY FEW."

4. (U) DR. LU NOTED THAT CPV POLICY TOWARD RELIGION HAD

BECOME MUCH MORE FAVORABLE IN 1990 WITH THE ISSUANCE OF

UNCLASSIFIED

RESOLUTION 24, WHICH NOTED THAT RELIGION WAS A "LONG-

STANDING PROBLEM" IN VIETNAM, ASSESSED THAT RELIGION COULD

BE A "POSITIVE FORCE" IN BUILDING A "NEW SOCIETY," AND

CONCLUDED THAT RELIGION WAS A "SPIRITUAL NEED" OF SOME

VIETNAMESE CITIZENS. SINCE THAT TIME, CPV POLICY HAS BEEN

TO "RESPECT" RELIGION AND TO UNDERTAKE APPROPRIATE

"MEASURES" TO ENSURE THAT RELIGIOUS BELIEVERS HAD THE

NECESSARY FREEDOM TO WORSHIP. DR. LU STRESSED THAT THE

CPV\'S POSITION WAS THAT -- "IF HANDLED WELL" -- RELIGION

COULD BE GOOD FOR SOCIETY AND THE NATION.

5. (U) DR. LU CONFIRMED REPORTS THAT THE CPV WAS TINKERING

WITH ITS POLICY ON RELIGION, BUT DECLINED TO PROVIDE ANY

DETAILS. HE ADMITTED ONLY THAT THE UPDATED POLICY, WHICH

WILL PROBABLY LEAD TO NEW REGULATIONS RATHER THAN A NEW LAW,

WOULD BE "BETTER" AND "MORE CORRECT" THAN EXISTING

LEGISLATION.

6. (U) WHILE NOT OFFERING TO SHARE COPIES OF ANY RECENT

RESEARCH, DR. LU RELUCTANTLY DESCRIBED SOME RECENT TOPICS:

-- HO CHI MINH\'S VIEWS ON RELIGION (GENERALLY FAVORABLE,

DR. LU SAID);

-- COMPARATIVE BUDDHISM (I.E., BETWEEN VIETNAM AND OTHER

NATIONS IN SOUTHEAST ASIA);

-- PRACTICES OF ANCESTOR WORSHIP IN VIETNAM;

-- THE NEXUS BETWEEN BUDDHISM AND CULTURE IN VIETNAMESE

HISTORY (HE NOTED THAT THIS LINK WAS NOWADAYS NOT VERY

STRONG);

-- ORGANIZATION AND PRECEPTS OF THE SIX MAJOR RELIGIOUS

GROUPS IN VIETNAM;

-- MARXIST/LENINIST ASSESSMENTS OF THE ROLE OF RELIGION;

-- THE "HUMANITARIAN VALUE" OF RELIGION;

-- RELIGIOUS PRACTICES OF ETHNIC MINORITIES (DR. LU

ADMITTED THAT THERE HAS BEEN VERY LITTLE DONE SO FAR ON THIS

ISSUE, CLAIMING THAT SUCH RESEARCH WAS "DIFFICULT").

7. (U) MOST OF THE TRAINING GIVEN BY THE CENTER FOR

STUDENTS (MID- TO SENIOR-LEVEL PARTY AND GOVERNMENT

OFFICIALS) WAS "PRETTY BASIC," DR. LU SAID. STAFF FROM THE

CENTER USUALLY ONLY LECTURED AS PART OF OTHER, BROADER

COURSES, RATHER THAN OFFERING SPECIALIZED COURSES OF STUDY.

THEIR FOCUS IS ON EXPLAINING THE HISTORY OF CPV VIEWS ON

RELIGION AND THE CURRENT IMPLEMENTATION OF RELIGIOUS POLICY.

HE ADMITTED THAT IT WAS "VERY NECESSARY" FOR ALL CADRES TO

HAVE A GOOD UNDERSTANDING ABOUT THE "PROBLEM" OF RELIGION,

AND THAT MANY DID NOT KNOW ENOUGH ABOUT CPV POLICY. (ONE

BUDDHIST MONK AFFILIATED WITH THE CENTRAL BUDDHIST SANGHA IN

HANOI PREVIOUSLY NOTED TO POL/C THAT HE IS OCCASIONALLY

INVITED TO SPEAK TO STUDENTS AT THE ACADEMY ON THE HISTORY

AND ROLE OF BUDDHISM IN VIETNAM; EMBASSY IS NOT CERTAIN IF

OTHER RELIGIOUS LEADERS ALSO LECTURE OCCASIONALLY.)
