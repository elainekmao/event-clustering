CLASSIFIED BY: POLITICAL MINISTER COUNSELOR THOMAS COUNTRYMAN FOR REASO

NS 1.5 (B) AND (D)

1. (C) ON JAN. 9, CHARGE AND POL MC CALLED ON CESARE

RAGAGLINI, FM FRATINI\'S CHEF DE CABINET, RAISED REFTEL WITH

HIM BRIEFLY, AND ADVISED HIM WE WOULD RAISE IT IN MORE DETAIL

WITH THE MFA\'S MIDDLE EAST BUREAU.

2. (C) IN THE ABSENCE OF AMBASSADOR ANTONIO BADINI, THE MFA\'S

DG FOR MIDDLE EASTERN AFFAIRS AND THE POC ON THIS LIBYAN

ISSUE, POL MC CALLED JAN. 9 ON ANTONIO BELLAVIA, THE MFA\'S

OFFICE DIRECTOR FOR LIBYA AND THE MAGHREB COUNTRIES. POL MC

DELIVERED REFTEL DEMARCHE, EMPHASIZED THE REASONS THAT COMPEL

THE USG TO CALL FOR A VOTE, AND UNDERLINED THAT OUR INTENTION

IS TO ENHANCE THE CREDIBILITY OF THE COMMISSION BOTH

INTERNATIONALLY AND WITHIN THE U.S., NOT TO DISRUPT ITS WORK

OR SET AN UNHELPFUL PRECEDENT.

3. (S) BELLAVIA APPRECIATED BEING INFORMED OF THE USG

DECISION IN ADVANCE OF THE JAN. 20 VOTE. HE SAID THAT THE EU

CAUCUS IN GENEVA JAN. 8 AGREED TO PROPOSE TO THE U.S. A WEOG

STATEMENT (IN LIEU OF A VOTE) STATING THAT THE WEOG IS UNABLE

TO ACCEPT THE LIBYAN CANDIDACY BY ACCLAMATION.

4. (C) LABOR COUNSELOR ALSO CALLED ON GIUSEPPE CALVETTA, THE

MFA\'S HUMAN RIGHTS OFFICE DIRECTOR, AND DELIVERED REFTEL

DEMARCHE. CALVETTA SIMPLY NOTED THAT ITALY WOULD HAVE

PREFERRED A NO VOTE AND WILL NOW WORK ON MINIMIZING ANY

DISRUPTION TO THE WORK OF THE COMMISSION CAUSED BY THE VOTE.
