SUMMARY

-------

1. USMISSION AMBASSADOR TONY HALL HAS KNOWN MATRA GABRE-

TSADICK AND HER HUSBAND DEME WHO RUN A NON-GOVERNMENTAL

SIPDIS

ORGANIZATION CALLED PROJECT MERCY IN THE SOUTHERN REGION

(SNNPR) FOR MANY YEARS AND CONSIDERS THEM OUTSTANDING

HUMANITARIANS. HE HAS VISITED THEIR PROJECT NEAR BUTAJIRA,

IN THE GURAGE ZONE. AS ETHIOPIA IS AGAIN IN THE MIDST OF A

SERIOUS CRISIS, THEIR NGO NEEDS ADDITIONAL SUPPORT IN ORDER

TO ENHANCE ITS OUTREACH. EMBASSY/USAID/UN AGENCIES ARE ASKED

TO ONCE AGAIN CONSIDER PROJECT MERCY AS A VIABLE

IMPLEMENTING PARTNER. END SUMMARY.

BACKGROUND

----------

2. AS NOTED REFS (A)(B), THE SITUATION IN ETHIOPIA IS AGAIN

CRITICAL. THE SERIOUS DROUGHT HAS LEAD TO WIDESPREAD CROP

FAILURE AND DECLARATIONS OF DISASTER BY THE GOVERNMENT OF

THE FEDERAL DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC OF ETHIOPIA (GFDRE) AND US

EMBASSY ADDIS. PRIME MINISTER MELES ZENAWI HAS STATED

PUBLICLY THAT THE SITUATION IS WORSE THAN THE GREAT FAMINE

OF 1984-5.

3. BOTH USAID AND THE UN AGENCIES INCLUDING THE WORLD FOOD

PROGRAM (WFP) ARE RESPONDING TO THE SITUATION. THE US HAS

ANNOUNCED INITIAL PLEDGES AND HAS REQUESTED OTHER DONORS TO

CONTRIBUTE SWIFTLY AND GENEROUSLY (REF C). BOTH

INTERNATIONAL AND LOCAL NGO\'S ARE REQUIRED AS IMPLEMENTING

PARTNERS IN ORDER TO REACH AFFECTED COMMUNITIES THROUGHOUT

THE COUNTRY.

4. PROJECT MERCY IS A US-REGISTERED CHARITABLE GROUP THAT

OPERATES IN THE SOUTHERN REGION OF ETHIOPIA. PERSONAL

KNOWLEDGE OF THEIR OPERATIONS AND A SITE VISIT BY THEN-

CONGRESSMAN HALL IN AUGUST 2000 SHOWED PROJECT MERCY RUNNING

AN EXTREMELY EFFECTIVE OPERATION WHICH INCLUDED A NUMBER OF

COMPREHENSIVE DEVELOPMENT PROJECTS.

5. IN ADDITION TO BEING A REGISTERED PRIVATE VOLUNTARY

ORGANIZATION (PVO) WITH USAID, THEY RECEIVED ASSISTANCE FROM

BOTH USAID AND WFP IN AUGUST 2000 (A TOTAL OF 847 METRIC

TONS OF FOOD AID). THEY HAVE BEEN OPERATING IN THE REGION

FOR ALMOST A DECADE AND ENJOY THE SUPPORT OF THE LOCAL

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 04 ROME 05771 291212Z

COMMUNITY AND GOVERNMENT.

6. THEY ARE NOW PROPOSING TO EXPAND THEIR OPERATIONS GIVEN

THE GROWING EMERGENCY SITUATION, AS THEY DID DURING THE

DROUGHT OF 1999-2000. THEY WANT TO ESTABLISH BOTH A

SUPPLEMENTAL FEEDING PROGRAM AND FOOD DISTRIBUTIONS IN SEVEN

DISTRICTS OF THE GURAGE ZONE IN THE SOUTHERN REGION (SNNPR).

THEY ESTIMATE THE NEED TO BE ALMOST 53,000 PEOPLE, INCLUDING

13,000 CHILDREN.

7. THEIR OWN ASSESSMENTS SHOW THE NEED FOR A SUPPLEMENTAL

FEEDING PROGRAM IN ORDER TO REACH THE SEVERELY MALNOURISHED

AND A FOOD DISTRIBUTION FOR THE REST OF THE POPULATION.

THEY HAVE DEVELOPED A FORTIFIED PORRIDGE CALLED ATMIT, THAT

IS BOTH NUTRITIONALLY SOUND AND LOCALLY ACCEPTED. THEY

REQUIRE APPROXIMATELY 6,000 TONS OF ROLLED OATS, NON-FAT DRY

MILK, SUGAR AND FORTIFICATION MIX. FOR THE GENERAL FOOD

DISTRIBUTION, WHEAT FLOUR (OR WHEAT FLOUR BLENDS) AND

VEGETABLE OIL ARE NEEDED.

8. US MISSION/ROME WILL CONTINUE TO HIGHLIGHT THE NEED IN

THE REGION AND WILL WORK WITH OTHER DONORS TO INCREASE THEIR

DONATIONS. ANY POSSIBLE SUPPORT FOR PROJECT MERCY FROM

EMBASSY/USAID/UN AGENCIES IN-COUNTRY IS APPRECIATED.
