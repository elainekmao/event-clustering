1. (C) SUMMARY: SPECIAL ENVOY TO THE GREAT LAKES HOWARD

WOLPE, ACCOMPANIED BY SPECIAL ADVISOR JOHN PRENDERGAST

AND POLOFFS, MET WITH NEWLY REAPPOINTED FOREIGN MINISTER

STANISLAUS MUDENGE AND UNDERSECRETARY FOR AFRICA DAVID

HAMADZIRIPI ON 19 JULY. WOLPE AND PRENDERGAST EXPRESSED

USG CONCERNS ABOUT THE CONTINUED VIABILITY OF THE LUSAKA

ACCORDS IF THE SIGNATORIES WERE TO ACQUIESCE TO KABILA\'S

REJECTION OF SIR KETUMILE MASIRE AS FACILITATOR. IN

ADDITION, WOLPE HIGHLIGHTED RECENT ACTION IN THE

EQUATEUR REGION AND OBSTACLES PLACED BY DRC PRESIDENT

LAURENT KABILA TO MONUC DEPLOYMENT AS ADDITIONAL

WORRYING FACTORS. HE PRESSED THE FM ON THE NEED FOR

ZIMBABWE TO EXERT ALL POSSIBLE LEVERAGE ON KABILA TO

ABANDON HIS REJECTION OF MASIRE, AND OFFERED SOME

SUGGESTIONS ON HOW THAT MIGHT BE ACCOMPLISHED. MUDENGE,

WHILE AFFIRMING THE NEED FOR MASIRE TO BE RETAINED AS

FACILITATOR, FAULTED MASIRE\'S APPROACH, AND PROPOSED

THAT MASIRE CONSULT CLOSELY, AND REGULARLY, WITH THE

LUSAKA SIGNATORY MINISTERS MOST DIRECTLY INVOLVED IN

NEGOTIATING THE LUSAKA AGREEMENT. THE FM ALSO BLAMED

THE FIGHTING IN EQUATEUR ON THE UGANDANS, AND SAID IT

WAS PROVING EASIER TO WORK WITH PAUL KAGAME NOW THAT HE

IS PRESIDENT. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) SPECIAL ENVOY TO THE GREAT LAKES HOWARD WOLPE

MET JULY 19 WITH FOREIGN MINISTER STANISLAUS MUDENGE.

WOLPE WAS ACCOMPANIED BY AF SPECIAL ADVISOR JOHN

PRENDERGAST AND POLOFFS; MUDENGE WAS ACCOMPANIED BY

UNDERSECRETARY FOR AFRICA DAVID HAMADZIRIPI. WOLPE

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 04061 01 OF 02 241510Z

EXPRESSED ANXIETY ABOUT THE LUSAKA PROCESS, WHICH IS

"HANGING BY A THREAD." HIS MISSION, HE STATED, WAS TO

GET THE PULSE OF THE AFRICAN LEADERS ON HOW TO WORK

TOGETHER TO REVIVE THE PROCESS AND TO PRESENT SOME IDEAS

FOR MOVING THE BALL FORWARD. THE FOREIGN MINISTER,

CLEARLY TIRED FROM THE PAST MONTH\'S PARLIAMENTARY

ELECTIONS IN ZIMBABWE AND HIS RECENT TRIP TO LOME FOR

THE OAU SUMMIT, SAID HE\'D BEEN CONCENTRATING ON "OTHER

THINGS" AND ASKED FOR A BRIEF RECAPITULATION OF THE

CURRENT SITUATION OF DROC AND THE STATUS OF MASIRE,

WHICH WOLPE PROVIDED.

VIEWS ON MASIRE

---------------

3. (C) WOLPE EXPRESSED CONCERN ABOUT REPORTS THAT

NAMIBIAN PRESIDENT SAM NUJOMA HAD TOLD SEVERAL U.S.

MEMBERS OF CONGRESS THAT MASIRE NO LONGER HAS THE

SUPPORT OF KABILA\'S SOUTHERN ALLIES, AND REITERATED THE

USG STANCE THAT KABILA\'S OPPOSITION TO MASIRE IS A FIG

LEAF FOR HIS RESISTANCE TO THE INTER-CONGOLESE DIALOGUE.

TO ACQUIESCE TO KABILA\'S REJECTION OF MASIRE, HE

CONTINUED, WOULD BE TO EFFECTIVELY ABANDON LUSAKA AND TO

RESTART THE WAR. HE DESCRIBED MASIRE\'S EFFORTS TO PULL

TOGETHER A SUPPORT TEAM MADE UP OF FRANCOPHONES WITH

EXPERTISE IN THE CONGO. WOLPE ALSO NOTED THAT THE OAU

HAD RECENTLY ISSUED A STRONG STATEMENT OF SUPPORT FOR

MASIRE. THIS, HE SAID, COULD INCREASE THE LEGITIMACY OF

THE FACILITATION EFFORT AND OFFER KABILA A WAY TO ACCEPT

MASIRE WITHOUT LOSING FACE.

4. (C) MUDENGE CLAIMED THAT THE CONCEPT OF A

"FACILITATOR" IN THE LUSAKA PROCESS HAD BEEN HIS OWN

IDEA, BUT NOTED THE DIFFICULTY OF MASIRE\'S TASK. HE

ACKNOWLEDGED THAT MASIRE IS A "VERY WISE MAN," BUT SAID

THAT THIS "RIVER (THE LUSAKA PROCESS) IS FULL OF

CROCODILES." WHILE PROFESSING "TREMENDOUS ADMIRATION"

FOR MASIRE, MUDENGE EXPRESSED A NUMBER OF RESERVATIONS

ABOUT THE WAY MASIRE AND HIS STAFF HAVE CONDUCTED

THEMSELVES THUS FAR. HE ASSERTED THAT THEY HAD FAILED

TO SEEK ADVICE FROM THE VARIOUS PARTIES THAT WERE

INTIMATELY INVOLVED IN CREATING THE LUSAKA ACCORDS, IN

PARTICULAR ZAMBIAN PRESIDENT CHILUBA. IT IS ONLY

THROUGH DISCUSSIONS WITH THESE PEOPLE, WHO UNDERSTAND

HOW HARD IT WAS TO GET THE COMPROMISES WROUGHT ON PAPER

AND WHO HAD TO "FINESSE" THEIR WAY THROUGH EACH PARTY\'S

SENSITIVITIES, THAT MASIRE AND HIS TEAM CAN HOPE FOR

SUCCESS. MUDENGE REPEATED SEVERAL TIMES THAT MASIRE

MUST MAKE CONTACT WITH BOTH SIDES OF THE DROC CONFLICT,

AN EFFORT THAT\'S "BEEN MISSING."

5. (C) MUDENGE ALSO LAMENTED OTHER FACTORS PREVENTING

THE SUCCESS OF THE FACILITATOR; AS AN EXAMPLE, HE SAID

THAT MASIRE HAS BROUGHT IN A ZAMBIAN ASSOCIATED WITH

FORMER PRESIDENT KAUNDA. THIS IS VERY DISTURBING,

SUBJECT: S/E WOLPE MEETS WITH FM MUDENGE

CONSIDERING THE IMPORTANCE OF THE ZAMBIANS\' ROLE. AS

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 04061 02 OF 02 241511Z

FOR KABILA\'S INCREASING RELUCTANCE TO ACKNOWLEDGE ANY OF

THE LUSAKA TENETS, MUDENGE ASSERTED THAT KABILA HAD HAD

NO REAL UNDERSTANDING OF THE ACCORDS WHEN THEY WERE

FIRST SIGNED; HE SAID THAT THE ONLY REASON KABILA HAD

AGREED TO LUSAKA WAS THAT AN AIDE HAD GIVEN HIM AN

INACCURATE INTERPRETATION OF ITS PROVISIONS. NOW, WITH

HIS POSITION STRENGTHENED BY HIS ALLIES, KABILA FEELS NO

COMPULSION TO ALLOW MONUC TROOPS INTO HIS COUNTRY.

FIGHTING IN EQUATEUR

--------------------

6. (C) WOLPE EXPRESSED USG CONCERN ABOUT CONTINUED

MOVEMENT BY BOTH MLC\'S AND KABILA\'S TROOPS IN THE

EQUATEUR REGION -- IN VIOLATION OF THE KAMPALA CEASEFIRE

LINE - WHICH IS EXACERBATING THE STRAINS ON THE LUSAKA

PROCESS. MUDENGE, ASSERTING THAT SOME UGANDAN OFFICERS

HAD BEEN CAPTURED IN THE EARLIER EQUATEUR FIGHTING, SAID

THAT THE GOZ IS NOT WILLING TO ENTERTAIN THE NOTION THAT

THE MLC, AND ITS LEADER, BEMBA, ARE ANYTHING BUT THE

"MOUTHPIECE" OF THE UGANDANS. WOLPE NOTED THAT THE USG

HAD EARLIER COME DOWN HARD ON MUSEVENI AS WELL AS BEMBA,

BUT THAT NOW IT WAS THE GOVERNMENT FORCES THAT WERE IN

VIOLATION OF THE KAMPALA AGREEMENT.

7. (C) THE FOREIGN MINISTER SAID HE SHARED WOLPE\'S

CONCERNS ABOUT THE EQUATEUR SITUATION. HE ADDED THAT

"OTHER PARTIES FOR VARIOUS REASONS DON\'T WANT PEACE TO

GO FORWARD," SPECIFICALLY CRITICIZING THE GOU FOR BEING

"EVASIVE" ABOUT ITS COMMITMENT TO LUSAKA, AND

QUESTIONING WHAT THE UGANDANS ARE UP TO. IN CONTRAST,

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 04061 02 OF 02 241511Z

HE AVERRED, THE GOZ HAS MADE IT CLEAR THAT IT IS "NOT

PREPARED TO COUNTENANCE ANY VIOLATION OF KAMPALA."

MONUC

-----

8. (C) WOLPE NOTED WITH CONCERN THAT KABILA CONTINUES

TO DENY MONUC FULL ACCESS, BY, FOR, EXAMPLE, INSISTING

ON CLEARING ALL FLIGHTS. STRESSING THAT BOTH THE MASIRE

AND THE EQUATEUR ISSUES NEED TO BE RESOLVED QUICKLY,

WOLPE SAID THAT THERE IS LITTLE IF ANY HOPE FOR THE FULL

DEPLOYMENT OF MONUC AND WITHDRAWAL OF CONGOLESE FORCES,

IF THE GOVERNMENT IN KINSHASA IS NOT WILLING TO COMPLY

WITH ALL ASPECTS OF LUSAKA DISARMAMENT. THE CONGOLESE,

HE SAID, HAVE LESS ENTHUSIASM FOR MONUC THAN THE

EXTERNAL SIGNATORIES, ADDING THAT MONUC CANNOT CONTINUE

TO BE DENIED FULL ACCESS TO THE CONGO. WOLPE TOLD

MUDENGE THAT AN EARLY MONUC DEPLOYMENT IN KISANGANI IS

UNDER CONSIDERATION IN AN EFFORT NOT ONLY TO STABILIZE

THE SITUATION IN THAT CITY, BUT ALSO AS A MEANS TO

STRENGTHEN EXTERNAL SIGNATORY CONFIDENCE IN MONUC\'S

CAPABILITY AND SERIOUSNESS. PRENDERGAST ADDED THAT THE

USG GOAL IS TO MAKE THE SITUATION AS CONDUCIVE AS

POSSIBLE TO MONUC DEPLOYMENT, SAYING THAT, GIVEN THE

DEBACLE IN SIERRA LEONE, THE UN WANTS TO BE CERTAIN THAT

THERE IS AN EFFECTIVE CEASE-FIRE IN PLACE, AND THAT

MONUC OBSERVERS WILL BE UNIMPEDED IN THEIR EXECUTION OF

THEIR RESPONSIBILITIES.

9. (C) MUDENGE CONCURRED ABOUT THE IMPORTANCE OF

DEPLOYING MONUC, BUT NOTED THAT KABILA IS BECOMING LESS

PAGE 04 HARARE 04061 02 OF 02 241511Z

CONVINCED THAT THIS IS IN HIS INTEREST. WOLPE RESPONDED

THAT THIS IS WHY THE GOZ\'S ROLE IS SO IMPORTANT. "YOUR

LEVERAGE IS ABSOLUTELY VITAL," HE SAID. MUDENGE

RESPONDED THAT "LEVERAGE IS A DIMINISHING RESOURCE."

STILL, WOLPE COUNTERED, KABILA IS ULTIMATELY DEPENDENT

ON THE ZIMBABWEANS. WOLPE CLOSED THE MEETING BY ONCE

MORE URGING MUDENGE AND THE GOZ TO EXERT ALL POSSIBLE

PRESSURE ON KABILA TO PERMIT LUSAKA TO GO FORWARD, AS IT

IS IN THE COMMON INTEREST OF ALL THE EXTERNAL STATES TO

END THIS DEVASTATING CONFLICT.

COMMENT

-------

10. (C) MUDENGE\'S RESPONSE TO WOLPE\'S ASSERTION THAT

THE GOZ COULD TRULY INFLUENCE KABILA SINCE KABILA IS

STILL TOTALLY DEPENDENT ON THE GOZ WAS A COY LAUGH. HE

SAID THEY WOULD BE DISCUSSING COURSE CORRECTIONS AND

LEVERAGE AT THE UPCOMING POLITICAL COMMITTEE MEETING.

HOWEVER, MUDENGE RECOGNIZED THAT ACQUIESCENCE TO

KABILA\'S REJECTION OF MASIRE COULD SOUND THE DEATH KNELL

OF LUSAKA.
