NS 1.5 (B) AND (D)

1. (C) SUMMARY: AS PART OF OUR EFFORT TO GET THE GOI TO

RECONSIDER ITS POSITION ON LIBYA\'S CANDIDACY FOR THE CHR-59

CHAIRMANSHIP, POL MC REITERATED THE USG RESERVATIONS ABOUT A

LIBYAN CHR CHAIRMANSHIP, URGED ITALY TO JOIN THE WEOG

CONSENSUS TO ISSUE A LETTER OF CONCERN ABOUT LIBYA\'S

CANDIDACY, AND STRESSED TO MFA OFFICIALS THE NEED TO WORK

CLOSELY WITH US TO AVOID A WORST-CASE OUTCOME IN GENEVA ON

JAN 20. THE ITALIANS UNDERSTOOD OUR CONCERNS WELL AND

PROMISED TO FACTOR OUR MESSAGE INTO ITS CONSIDERATION OF THE

WEOG\'S APPROACH. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) ON DEC 19, POL MC AND POLOFF MET WITH FRANCESCO

CERULLI, THE MFA\'S DEPUTY DG FOR MIDDLE EASTERN AFFAIRS. POL

MC FIRST REITERATED THE USG CONCERNS ABOUT THE CHAIRMANSHIP

OF A COUNTRY LIKE LIBYA, A SERIOUS VIOLATOR OF UN

RESOLUTIONS, TO THE CHR-59 AND HOW SUCH A CHAIRMANSHIP WOULD

FURTHER TARNISH THE CREDIBILITY OF THE CHR. POL MC ADDED

THAT THE ELECTION OF LIBYA TO SUCH A POSITION IS ALSO A

DOMESTIC CONCERN OF OURS SINCE IT WOULD SET BACK THE STEADILY

INCREASING LEVEL OF CONGRESSIONAL SUPPORT FOR THE UN SYSTEM.

3. (C) POL MC THEN EXPRESSED DISAPPOINTMENT WITH THE LETTER

SENT LAST WEEK BY THE WEOG TO THE AFRICAN GROUP REGARDING THE

LIBYAN CANDIDACY AND DESCRIBED IT AS "LOWER THAN THE LOWEST

COMMON DENOMINATOR." (NOTE: THE LETTER ONLY STATED THAT

THERE WAS NO CONSENSUS IN THE WEOG AND THAT THE CANDIDACY

ISSUE WAS STILL BEING DISCUSSED. END NOTE.) POL MC THEN

URGED CERULLI TO WORK MORE CONSTRUCTIVELY WITHIN THE WEOG TO

MAKE A PUBLIC STATEMENT EXPRESSING THE CONCERNS WE HAVE ABOUT

LIBYA\'S CHAIRMANSHIP OF THE CHR-59.

4. (C) POL MC PRESENTED CERULLI WITH THE "LOSE, LOSE, LOSE

SITUATION" WE SHOULD ALL TRY TO AVOID: NO EXPRESSION OF

CONCERN FROM THE WEOG (WHICH THE USG DOES NOT WANT,) A VOTE

(WHICH THE GOI DOES NOT WANT) AND HARMING THE US DOMESTIC

SUPPORT NEEDED TO COLLABORATE FULLY WITH THE GOI ON OTHER

MORE IMPORTANT MATTERS RELATING TO THE UN AND LIBYA SUCH AS,

FOR INSTANCE, THE LIBYAN UNSC CANDIDACY (WHICH NEITHER THE

USG NOR THE GOI WOULD WANT.) POL MC FINALLY URGED CERULLI TO

WORK TOGETHER TO REACH A SITUATION WHICH, EVEN IF NOT PERFECT

FOR ALL SIDES, WOULD BETTER SERVE THE CREDIBILITY OF THE CHR,

AND THE INTERESTS OF BOTH THE USG AND THE GOI.

5. (C) COMMENT: THE ITALIANS UNDERSTAND WELL OUR RESERVATIONS

ABOUT A LIBYAN CHR-59 CHAIRMANSHIP. HOWEVER, THE INCLUSION

OF THE CHR CHAIR IN A PACKAGE OF "REGULARIZATION" MEASURES

OFFERED DURING PM BERLUSCONI\'S OCTOBER VISIT TO TRIPLOI

SEVERELY LIMITS ITALIAN ROOM FOR MANEUVER. CERULLI HAD

NOTHING NEW TO SAY, BUT WE WILL KEEP PUSHING FOR THE GOI TO

SHOW MORE FLEXIBILITY IN GENEVA. END COMMENT.
