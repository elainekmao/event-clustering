START MEMO NOMINATING-JUSTIFICATION:

Mr. Athas is a very senior journalist in the Sri Lankan

print media who has specialized as a defence

correspondent. He regularly contributes to the widely

read defence column in "The Sunday Times" newspaper.

In addition to being the defence correspondent, he is

also the Consultant Editor of this paper.

During the prolonged ethnic strife in Sri Lanka he

received threats from certain elements in the Armed

Services who did not take too kind to the revelations

in his column.

Recognizing his contributions in the field of

journalism New York-based Committee to Protect

Journalists (CPJ) awarded him the prestigious Press

Freedom Award in 1994. The Editors\' Guild of Sri Lanka

awarded him the \'Journalist of the Year 1998\', and the

\'Columnist of the Year 2001\'. He is the only Sri

Lankan journalist invited as a member in the

International Consortium of Investigative Journalists

(ICIJ).

Mr. Athas is visiting the U.S. to attend the

Investigative Reporters and Editors Annual Conference

to be held in Washington from June 5-8 at the National

Press Club. As a target of opportunity, post proposes

a 10-day funded Volvis program for this highly

respected Sri Lankan journalist on governmental affairs

and security issues. Post believes that this proposed

Volvis program would provide him with a wide exposure

to U.S. government and its policies especially in

defense, national security, foreign policy, and anti-

terrorism. This will result in multiplier effect as he

will use the newly acquired knowledge in his widely

read column. If this proposal is accepted, Mr. Athas

will be available for programming in Washington from

June 9.

END MEMO NOMINATING-JUSTIFICATION:
