1. (C) ON JANUARY 27, POLOFF DELIVERED POINTS REFTEL AS

NON-PAPER TO FERNANDO GENTILINI, DIRECTOR OF MFA\'S BALKANS

ISSUES OFFICE. AFTER CONSULTING WITH A NUMBER OF MFA OFFICES

AND SUPERIORS, HE INFORMED POLOFF THAT THE GOI WAS "IN LINE"

WITH THE U.S. ON THE SUBSTANCE OF ALL AGENDA ITEMS FOR THE

JANUARY 29-30 PEACE IMPLEMENTATION COUNCIL (PIC) STEERING

BOARD MEETING. ITALY ALSO AGREED THAT THE ISSUE OF

STRENGTHENING THE BOSNIAN GOVERNMENT\'S LEGAL AUTHORITIES FOR

COMBATING TERRORISM SHOULD BE ADDED TO THE PIC MEETING AGENDA.

2. (C) GENTILINI SAID THE ONE AREA WHERE THE GOI AND USG

MIGHT DIFFER, SLIGHTLY, WAS ON THE TIMING OF TRANSITIONING

CERTAIN RESPONSIBILITIES TO LOCAL AUTHORITIES. FOR INSTANCE,

THE GOI AGREES THAT THE REFUGEE AND RECONSTRUCTION TASK FORCE

(RRTF) HANDOVER PLAN COULD BE COMPLETED BY DECEMBER 2003.

HOWEVER, THE GOI WOULD LIKE THE PIC STEERING BOARD TO MONITOR

CLOSELY AND REPORT ON LOCAL AUTHORITIES\' PROGRESS IN THE

RUN-UP TO HANDOVER.

3. (C) THE GOI ALSO CONCURS WITH REFTEL POINTS URGING THE

OHR TO ACCELERATE PREPARATIONS FOR A SPECIAL WAR CRIMES

CHAMBER WITHIN THE STATE COURT OF BIH. ONE GOI CONCERN IS

THE REQUIREMENT FOR IC SUPPORT, (FUNDING, SECONDMENT OF

PROSECUTORS AND LAWYERS) TO GET THE CHAMBER UP AND RUNNING.

THE GOI MAY RAISE SUCH CONCERNS AT THE MEETING.
