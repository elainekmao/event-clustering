SUBJECT: ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT CONFIRMS POLITICAL

COMMITTEE TO MEET IN LUSAKA ON JULY 28

REF: LUSAKA 3300

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS UNDER SECRETARY FOR

AFRICA DAVID HAMADZIRIPI TOLD CHARGE JULY 25 THAT THE

POLITICAL COMMITTEE WILL MEET IN LUSAKA ON JULY 28

(REFTEL), AND THAT HE AND HIS MINISTER PLANNED TO

ATTEND. AFTER INQUIRING ABOUT KABILA\'S RECENT THREAT TO

PULL THE PLUG ON MONUC, THE ZIMBABWEAN DIPLOMAT

COMPLAINED THAT THE POLITICAL COMMITTEE SEEMED TO BE

DISCUSSING THE SAME ISSUE OVER AND OVER AGAIN.

2. (C) WHEN CHARGE TOLD HAMADZIRIPI THAT THERE WAS AN

EFFORT UNDER WAY TO GET THE OAU MORE INVOLVED IN THE

CONGO PEACE PROCESS, THE LATTER REMARKED THAT HE HOPED

THE CONTINENTAL BODY WOULD SEND A MORE SENIOR PERSON TO

THE LUSAKA MEETING. TO DATE THEY HAVE BEEN SENDING ONLY

CONFIDENTIAL

"VERY JUNIOR OFFICERS," WHO HAVE BEEN UNABLE TO MAKE ANY

PROGRESS VIS A VIS THE CHAIRMANSHIP OF THE JOINT

MILITARY COMMISSION (JMC). JMC CHAIRMAN LALLALI HAD NOT

BEEN IN LUSAKA SINCE FEBRUARY AND ZAMBIAN BRIGADIER

KAZEMBE WAS UNABLE TO SHOULDER THE ENTIRE BURDEN OF

RUNNING THE COMMITTEE HIMSELF, AS HE HAD DUTIES TO

ATTEND WITH THE ZAMBIAN ARMY. THE ZIMBABWEANS DO NOT

SEE HOW THE POLITICAL COMMITTEE CAN ACHIEVE MUCH WITH A

PART-TIME JMC IN PLACE, HAMADZIRIPI CONCLUDED.
