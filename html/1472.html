SUBJECT: VIOLENCE AND INTIMIDATION CONTINUE IN ZIMBABWE

BY-ELECTION DISTRICT

REFTEL: HARARE 7164

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS: 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) SUMMARY: THE LEVEL OF VIOLENCE IN BIKITA WEST--

WHERE A SPECIAL PARLIAMENTARY BY-ELECTION WILL BE HELD

JANUARY 13-14 TO FILL A SEAT LEFT VACANT BY THE DEATH OF

THE INCUMBENT--HAS CONTINUED IN RECENT WEEKS AS ZANU-PF

SUPPORTERS STEP UP THEIR INTIMIDATION CAMPAIGN. WAR

VETERANS AND ZANU-PF YOUTH CONTINUE TO BEAT UP MDC

SUPPORTERS, WHEREAS THE MDC HAS DEPLOYED ITS OWN YOUTH

TO "PROTECT" ITS CAMPAIGNERS. THE GOVERNMENT OF

ZIMBABWE AND ZANU-PF ARE DOLING OUT LARGESSE IN AN

UNABASHED ATTEMPT TO BUY VOTES. BOTH PARTIES ARE

BRINGING IN HEAVY-HITTERS FROM HARARE TO HOLD RALLIES IN

THE MASVINGO DISTRICT, ACCORDING TO AN MDC ADVISOR.

GIVEN ZANU-PF\'S HAM-FISTED TACTICS, AND THE WIDESPREAD

FEAR AMONG THE ELECTORATE, WE WOULD BE SURPRISED IF THE

MDC PULLED OUT A VICTORY HERE. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) OUR CONTACTS HAVE INFORMED US OF NEW INCIDENTS

OF VIOLENCE AND INTIMIDATION IN BIKITA WEST. AN ADVISOR

A REMOTE LOCATION WHERE HE WAS BEATEN WITH CLUBS.

CAST THEIR BALLOT FOR THE MDC ON ELECTION DAY.

MUPA, THE LOCAL YOUTH ARE RECEIVING BETWEEN Z$1,000 AND

Z$10,000 EACH TO DO EVERYTHING FROM VOTING FOR ZANU-PF

TO BEATING UP MDC SUPPORTERS. MUPA CLAIMED THAT THE

GOVERNMENT HAS ESTABLISHED A NEW RESETTLEMENT AREA IN

THE SAVE CONSERVANCY--WHICH EXTENDS INTO BIKITA--IN

ORDER TO DISTRIBUTE LAND TO THOSE PROMISING TO VOTE FOR

ZANU-PF. HE STATED THAT ZANU-PF IS DRAWING UP A

"SUPPLEMENTAL" VOTERS\' ROLL OF APPROXIMATELY 3,400 NEW

VOTERS--MOSTLY FROM AREAS OUTSIDE BIKITA WEST--WITH THE

COOPERATION OF THE ELECTORAL SUPERVISORY COMMISSION

(ESC). THE ESC ALSO FAILED TO NOTIFY THE MDC OF AN

EARLY JANUARY MEETING WHERE IT ASSIGNED MOSTLY LOCAL

CIVIL SERVANTS, POLICE AND CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE

ORGANIZAITON OFFICERS AS PRESIDING OFFICERS TO OVERSEE

THE POLLING STATIONS, ACCORDING TO MUPA.

5. (C) DESPITE THE VIOLENCE AND LEVEL OF FEAR AMONG THE

POPULACE, BOTH ZANU-PF AND MDC ARE BRINGING IN THEIR

HEAVY-HITTERS TO CAMPAIGN IN THE DISTRICT. ZANU-PF HAS

BROUGHT IN FOREIGN MINISTER MUDENGE, LOCAL GOVERNMENT

MINISTER CHOMBO, MASVINGO GOVERNOR JOSIAH HUNGWE, YOUTH

DEVELOPMENT MINISTER BORDER GEZI AND OTHERS TO CONDUCT

UP TO FIVE RALLIES A DAY IN THE DISTRICT. MUPA CLAIMED

THAT THE ZANU-PF RALLIES ARE DRAWING FEWER THAN 200

PEOPLE EACH, AND MOST OF THEM ARE ELDERLY WOMEN--

TRADITIONAL ZANU-PF SUPPORTERS. THE MDC, FOR ITS PART,

HAS SENT VICE-PRESIDENT SIBANDA, SECRETARY-GENERAL

NCUBE, NATIONAL CHAIRMAN MATONGO, 10 MPS AND OTHER

MEMBERS OF THE NATIONAL EXECUTIVE, IN ADDITION TO

TSVANGIRAI\'S APPEARANCE LAST WEEKEND. THE MDC HAS HELD

FOUR RALLIES ACROSS THE CONSTITUENCY, ACCORDING TO THE

PARTY\'S E-MAIL UPDATE. MUPA CLAIMED THAT ITS RALLIES

HAVE ATTRACTED BETWEEN 500 AND 2,000 PEOPLE EACH.

ACCORDING TO THE MDC\'S INFORMAL SURVEYS OF LIKELY

VOTERS, THE MDC WILL WIN THE ELECTION BY 2,500 VOTES

(7,500 FOR MDC AND 5,000 FOR ZANU-PF).

6. (C) GANDI MUDZINGWA, TSVANGIRAI\'S ADVISOR, SAID THE

DEPLOYMENT OF MDC YOUTH HAS RESTORED A BIT OF STABILITY

TO THE AREA. THESE YOUTH, HOWEVER, HAVE FOCUSED ON

PROTECTING THE MDC CANDIDATE AND THOSE CAMPAIGNING FOR

HIM, LEADING WAR VETERANS AND ZANU-PF SUPPORTERS TO

FOCUS THEIR INTIMIDATION CAMPAIGN ON ORDINARY CIVILIANS.

MUDZINGWA WAS DUBIOUS ABOUT WHETHER DEPLOYING MDC YOUTHS

WAS A SUSTAINABLE LONG-TERM STRATEGY, AS IT REQUIRED

"COLOSSAL" RESOURCES, AND THE OPPOSITION PARTY WAS

CURRENTLY IN DESPERATE FINANCIAL STRAITS.

7. (C) COMMENT: GIVEN ZANU-PF\'S WIDE RANGE OF HAM-

FISTED INTIMIDATION AND MANIPULATION TACTICS, AND OUR

CONTACTS\' REPORTED LEVEL OF PERVASIVE FEAR AMONG VOTERS,

THE ODDS ARE AGAINST THE MDC HOLDING ONTO ITS BIKITA

WEST SEAT IN PARLIAMENT. IT WOULD TAKE A LARGE VOTER

TURNOUT FOR THE MDC TO WIN, AND THAT APPEARS UNLIKELY IN

THE CURRENT ELECTION CLIMATE. THE MDC\'S PROJECTIONS OF

VICTORY ARE A BIT OVEROPTIMISTIC, IN OUR ASSESSMENT,

ESPECIALLY IF IT BELIEVES ONLY 12,500 OUT OF 40,000

ELIGIBLE VOTERS WILL TURN OUT ON ELECTION DAY. THE

MARONDERA WEST BY-ELECTION AND THE JUNE PARLIAMENTARY
