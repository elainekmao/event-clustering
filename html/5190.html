SUBJECT: WFP REPORTS ON GENEVA MEETING ON IRAQ AND

DISCUSSES SECURITY CONCERNS

REF: ROME 30

CLASSIFIED BY: AMBASSADOR TONY HALL FOR REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D).

--------

SUMMARY

---------

1. (S) U.N. HUMANITARIAN COORDINATOR IN IRAQ, RAMIRO LOPES

DA SILVA, REPORTS THAT THE FRAGILITY OF THE IRAQI SOCIETY IS

EXTREME AND MILITARY ACTION THAT DISRUPTS THE CURRENT SYSTEMS

WILL CREATE A DISASTROUS SITUATION. THE U.N. IS PLANNING ON

THE ASSUMPTION THAT THE NORTH WILL BE SHIELDED FROM MILITARY

ACTION AND THAT THE SOUTH/CENTRAL REGIONS WILL BEAR THE BRUNT

OF ANY ATTACKS. SOME PROGRESS IS BEING MADE IN U.N.

PREPARATIONS, BUT LACK OF RESOURCES ARE A STUMBLING BLOCK.

IF WE WANT WFP TO ASSIST EARLY ON IN RESURRECTING OR

REJUVENATING THE CURRENT FOOD DISTRIBUTION SYSTEM, SOME

DIALOGUE WITH THE U.N. SECURITY COORDINATION OFFICE

(UNSECOORD) WOULD BE HELPFUL. WFP CONTINUES TO BE CONCERNED

ABOUT POSSIBLE BIOLOGICAL AND/OR CHEMICAL SPILLS AND LEAKS TO

ITS STAFF AND/OR FOOD COMMODITIES. WFP REQUESTS FEEDBACK ON

ASSUMPTIONS MADE BY THE U.N. COUNTRY TEAM IN IRAQ AND ITS

PLANNING. SEE PARA 11. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) SENIOR EMERGENCY COORDINATOR (SEC) R. DAVIS IN THE

U.S. MISSION/ROME MET WITH WFP STAFF ON JANUARY 14 TO DISCUSS

THE IRAQ PLANNING MEETING THAT WAS HELD ON JANUARY 12/13 IN

GENEVA AMONG THE U.N. AGENCIES AND OFFICES, INTERNATIONAL

COMMITTEE OF THE RED CROSS (ICRC), INTERNATIONAL FEDERATION

OF THE RED CRESCENT (IFRC), AND NON-GOVERNEMNTAL ORGANIZATION

(NGO) GROUPS, I.E. ICVA AND INTERACTION. THE U.N.

HUMANITARIAN COORDINATOR IN IRAQ, RAMIRO LOPES DA SILVA, AND

THE U.N. RESIDENT COORDINATORS ALSO ATTENDED.

3. (S) ACCORDING TO WFP, LOPES DA SILVA REPORTED TO THE

GROUP THAT THE FRAGILITY OF THE IRAQI SOCIETY IS EXTREME AND

MILITARY ACTION THAT DISRUPTS THE CURRENT SYSTEMS WILL CREATE

A DISASTROUS SITUATION. THE U.N. IS ASSUMING THAT THE NORTH

WILL BE SHIELDED FROM MILITARY ACTION, HOWEVER IT SHOULD BE

NOTED THAT ALL FOOD DISTRIBUTED IN THE NORTH FUNNELS THROUGH

THE SOUTH/CENTRAL REGIONS (UNDER THE DIRECTION OF THE GOI\'S

MINISTRY OF TRANSPORT) BEFORE IT REACHES THE WAREHOUSES IN

MOSUL OR KIRKUK, WHERE WFP TAKES OVER THE TRUCKING

RESPONSIBILITIES. THEREFORE, LOPES DA SILVA POINTED OUT IT

PROBABLY WOULD BE NECESSARY TO MAKE ALTERNATE LOGISTICAL

ARRANGEMENTS FOR PROVIDING FOOD TO THE NORTH, ALONG WITH

CREATING ALTERNATIVE SUPPLY LINES FROM SYRIA, TURKEY, OR IRAN.

4. (S) LOPES DA SILVA RELAYED THAT THE U.N. IS ASSUMING THAT

THE CENTRAL/SOUTH REGIONS WOULD BE THE POINT OF MAIN MILITARY

ACTION, POSSIBLY CAUSING THE 5.6 MILLION INHABITANTS OF

BAGHDAD TO BE CUT OFF FROM ELECTRICITY, WATER, SEWAGE, AND

FUEL. MINES ARE A POTENTIAL PROBLEM IN MOVEMENTS FROM THE

CENTRAL REGION TO THE NORTH. LOPES DA SILVA ALSO POINTED OUT
