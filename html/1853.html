3. ON JULY 26, ON THE MARGINS OF THE ASEAN REGIONAL

FORUM, EAP ASSISTANT SECRETARY JAMES KELLY MET WITH

DR. N. HASSAN WIRAJUDA, SPECIAL ENVOY OF THE

INDONESIAN FOREIGN MINISTER. WIRAJUDA WAS FUNCTIONING

AS THE HEAD OF THE INDONESIAN DELEGATION IN VIETNAM

FOLLOWING THE DECISION BY THE INDONESIAN FOREIGN

MINISTER NOT TO TRAVEL TO HANOI DUE TO THE SITUATION

IN JAKARTA.

4. WIRAJUDA BEGAN BY EXPRESSING HIS HOPE FOR A

PEACEFUL SOLUTION TO THE CURRENT POLITICAL CRISIS IN

JAKARTA, NOTING THAT INSTABILITY IN JAKARTA HAS THE

POTENTIAL TO LEAD TO CLASHES IN THE ISLANDS. HE SAID

HE HOPED THAT THE NEW GOVERNMENT WOULD RESOLVE THE

CRISIS PEACEFULLY. WIRAJUDA ALSO INDICATED HIS

INTENTION TO TRAVEL TO WASHINGTON DC IN THE NEAR

FUTURE.

5. KELLY BROUGHT UP THE SUBJECT OF ACEH, DECLARING USG

INTEREST IN A PEACEFUL RESOLUTION AND INQUIRING ABOUT

JAKARTA\'S PLANS FOR AUTONOMY FOR THE PROVINCE. KELLY

NOTED THAT, WHILE THE INSURGENCY MAY NECESSITATE SOME

MILITARY RESPONSE, MAINTAINING INDONESIAN UNITY COULD

BEST BE DONE BY CALMING DOWN THE SITUATION AND NOT

"FEEDING REVOLUTIONARIES." KELLY REMINDED WIRAJUDA

THAT INDONESIA-U.S. RELATIONS COULD NOT STAND "ANOTHER

TIMOR," AND ASSERTED USG READINESS TO HELP.

6. WIRAJUDA RESPONDED THAT HE MUST SEE HOW THE NEW

GOVERNMENT MOVED FORWARD ON THIS ISSUE, BUT THAT THEN-

VICE PRESIDENT MEGAWATI SUKARNOPUTRI HAD APPEARED

"IMPATIENT" WITH THE SLOW PEACE PROCESS IN ACEH.

WIRAJUDA SAID JAKARTA AGREED ON "SPECIAL AUTONOMY" FOR

ACEH IN CONCEPT BUT THE IMPLEMENTATION HAD NOT YET

BEEN RESOLVED. BOTH SIDES HAD TAKEN A "WAIT AND SEE"

STANCE FOLLOWING THEIR LAST MEETING, IN JULY, BUT

WIRAJUDA ANTICIPATED FORWARD MOVEMENT IN THEIR NEXT

MEETING IN SEPTEMBER.

7. KELLY RESPONDED THAT AGREEMENT WOULD BE WONDERFUL

NEWS, NOTING THE NEED FOR INDONESIA TO RETURN TO

STRENGTH SO IT COULD RESUME PLAYING ITS LEADERSHIP

ROLE IN ASEAN. WIRAJUDA REMARKED THAT THE CURRENT

POLITICAL CRISIS MEANT THAT INDONESIA COULD CONTRIBUTE

LESS TO ASEAN THAN IN THE PAST. KELLY COMMENTED ON

THE ECONOMIC SIDE OF THE POLITICAL TURMOIL, NOTING

THAT PEACEFUL RESOLUTION OF THE DIFFICULTIES FACING

JAKARTA WOULD HAVE AN IMPORTANT PSYCHOLOGICAL IMPACT

ON U.S. BUSINESSES AND THUS ON THE OVERALL INDONESIAN

ECONOMY.

8. IN PARTING, A/S KELLY HANDED WIRAJUDA A NON-PAPER

ON M. DEBORAH WYNES, THE USG CANDIDATE FOR THE UN\'S

JOINT INSPECTION UNIT.
