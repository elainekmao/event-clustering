SUBJECT: EVANGELICAL PASTOR\'S VIEWS ON HOUSE CHURCHES AND RELIGIOUS

FREEDOM

THIS IS AN AMCONGEN HO CHI MINH CITY CABLE. CLASSIFIED BY EMI

YAMAUCHI, CONSUL GENERAL, REASON 1.5 (D).

1. (C) PASTOR NGUYEN HONG QUANG, CHAIRMAN OF THE MENNONITE CHURCH AND

MEMBER OF THE VIETNAM EVANGELICAL FELLOWSHIP OF HOUSE CHURCHES (VEF),

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HANOI 01454 060431Z

RECENTLY VISITED THE CONGEN. THE VEF IS NOT REGISTERED WITH THE GVN

AND IS THEREFORE NOT A RECOGNIZED RELIGION. QUANG, WHO IS A RELATIVELY

NEW BUT SEEMINGLY RELIABLE RELIGIOUS ACTIVIST, BROUGHT ALONG PHOTOS AND

"TESTIMONIES" OF TWO HOUSE CHURCH PASTORS WHO HAD BEEN SUBJECTED TO

HARASSMENT AND/OR BEATINGS IN HO CHI MINH CITY AND KON TUM PROVINCE IN

THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS. POST IS IN THE PROCESS OF

TRANSLATING/SUMMARIZING THE 25-PLUS PAGES OF DOCUMENTS, POLICE

COMPLAINTS, ETC.

2. (C) QUANG IS PERSONALLY ACQUAINTED WITH THE TWO VICTIMS. THE HCMC

VICTIM IS A HOUSE CHURCH PASTOR WHOSE HOME WAS RAIDED BY POLICE.

ACCORDING TO QUANG, THE HCMC PASTOR WAS THREATENED BUT NOT HARMED. THE

POLICE INSTEAD WALKED OFF WITH HIS TELEVISION SET, RADIO, AND OTHER

HOUSEHOLD EFFECTS. (POST NOTE: POST HAS HEARD SIMILAR STORIES IN THE

PAST ABOUT CONFISCATED TELEVISIONS, BICYCLES, ETC. IN THE PROVINCES,

BUT SUCH OCCURRENCES ARE SOMEWHAT UNUSUAL IN HCMC.) HE WAS WARNED TO

STOP ILLEGAL RELIGIOUS MEETINGS.

3. (C) THE KON TUM PROVINCE VICTIM WAS A HOUSE CHURCH PASTOR WHOSE

MOTORCYCLE WAS RUN OFF THE ROAD, ALLEGEDLY BY PLAINCLOTHES POLICEMEN.

THE PASTOR WAS TAKEN TO JAIL FOR "CAUSING" THE ACCIDENT, AND WAS BEATEN

BADLY ENOUGH TO BE ADMITTED TO A HOSPITAL, ACCORDING TO QUANG.

ENCLOSED WITH THE KON TUM PROVINCE VICTIM\'S TESTIMONY WERE PHOTOS,

WHICH CLEARLY SHOWED CUTS AND BRUISES ON THE HEAD AND NECK, AND TORN

CLOTHING.

4. (C) IN RESPONSE TO QUESTIONS FROM THE CG, QUANG RESPONDED:

-- HE HIMSELF HAS HAD MEETINGS WITH GVN AUTHORITIES FROM THE MINISTRY

OF PUBLIC SECURITY (MPS), THE NATIONAL COMMITTEE ON RELIGIOUS AFFAIRS,

NATIONAL ASSEMBLY MEMBERS, ETC. HE BELIEVES THAT AT THE NATIONAL

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HANOI 01454 060431Z

LEVEL, THE GVN IS MAKING AN EFFORT TO UNDERSTAND THE HOUSE CHURCH

EVANGELICAL MOVEMENT AND TO IMPROVE TREATMENT OF "NON-REGISTERED

RELIGIOUS BELIEVERS."

-- IN SPITE OF THE ABOVE CASE OF HARASSMENT IN HCMC, QUANG BELIEVES THE

LEVEL AND FREQUENCY OF SUCH INCIDENTS HAVE DECLINED GRADUALLY THIS

YEAR. AS PROOF, HE SHOWED US HIS NEW "HOUSEHOLD REGISTRATION" BOOKLET,

WHICH MAKES HIM AN OFFICIAL HCMC RESIDENT. THIS WOULD NOT HAVE BEEN

POSSIBLE, HE NOTED, WITHOUT THE SUPPORT OF THE HCMC RELIGIOUS AFFAIRS

COMMITTEE.

-- IN THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS, HE BELIEVES THERE HAS NOT BEEN MUCH

CHANGE. HARASSMENT AND BEATINGS OCCUR WHEN POLICE DISCOVER "ILLEGAL

MEETINGS" HOSTED BY UNREGISTERED HOUSE CHURCH LEADERS.

5. (C) QUANG DID NOT WISH US TO RAISE THESE SPECIFIC CASES WITH

VIETNAMESE AUTHORITIES. HE SAID IT WAS ENOUGH THAT THE USG WAS "AWARE

OF THESE INCIDENTS" AND THAT WE WOULD "RECORD THESE PROBLEMS" FOR THE

ANNUAL HUMAN RIGHTS AND RELIGIOUS FREEDOM REPORTS.

6. (C) IN RESPONSE TO CG\'S QUESTION ABOUT ANOTHER VISIT TO THE CENTRAL

HIGHLANDS, QUANG INDICATED THAT THE FELLOWSHIP OF HOUSE CHURCHES WAS

PLANNING A CONFERENCE IN THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS IN LATE JUNE. IF THE

CONFERENCE WENT SMOOTHLY, THE FELLOWSHIP\'S EXECUTIVE BOARD WOULD SEEK

TO MEET WITH PROVINCIAL OFFICIALS. IN THE MEANTIME, FOR HIS

FELLOWSHIP\'S OWN PURPOSES, HE DID NOT BELIEVE CALLING INCREASED

ATTENTION TO THE HIGHLANDS WAS HELPFUL. HE SEEMED CERTAIN THAT THE KON

TUM PASTOR WOULD WELCOME A VISIT FROM CONGENOFFS.

7. (C) COMMENT: QUANG, WHO MET WITH THE COMMISSION ON INTERNATIONAL

RELIGIOUS FREEDOM THIS PAST FEBRUARY, HAS IMPRESSED US AS A SERIOUS,

DETERMINED CRUSADER FOR RELIGIOUS FREEDOM. THIS WAS, HOWEVER, OUR

FIRST MEETING WITH HIM ALONE (USUALLY HE IS WITH TWO OR THREE OTHER

PASTORS OF OTHER PROTESTANT DENOMINATIONS). HE DREW A CLEAR LINE

BETWEEN THE GVN CENTRAL GOVERNMENT AND LOCAL AUTHORITIES - WHETHER THEY

WERE OFFICIALS OF THE RELIGIOUS AFFAIRS COMMITTEES, MINISTRY OF PUBLIC

SECURITY, PEOPLE\'S COMMITTEES, OR POLICE DEPARTMENT. HE ACKNOWLEDGED

THERE HAD BEEN SOME MODEST PROGRESS IN MUTUAL UNDERSTANDING WITH GVN

OFFICIALS, AND SHOWED US PHOTOS OF SEVERAL HOUSE CHURCH LEADERS AT

MEETINGS WITH MPS, PEOPLE\'S COMMITTEES, AND RELIGIOUS AFFAIRS

COMMITTEES IN HANOI. QUANG IS FINDING IT JUST AS DIFFICULT AS WE DO,

HOWEVER, TO FIGURE OUT WHO EXACTLY CONTROLS EACH OF THE PROVINCIAL,

DISTRICT, VILLAGE, AND HAMLET PEOPLE\'S COMMITTEES/COUNCILS, POLICE

DEPARTMENTS, AND RELIGIOUS AFFAIRS COMMITTEES -- AND HOW THE GVN CAN

COMMUNICATE ITS OWN INCREMENTAL ATTITUDE CHANGES EFFECTIVELY TO THEM.
