SUBJECT: EU SPECIAL REPRESENTATIVE AJELLO HAS POSITIVE

MEETING WITH PRESIDENT MUGABE

REF: HARARE 4064 (NOTAL)

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING REASONS

1.5 B/D.

1. (C) BRITISH DEPUTY HIGH COMMISSIONER IAN HAY-

CAMPBELL SHARED HIS REPORT ON EU SPECIAL REPRESENTATIVE

ALDO AJELLO\'S VISIT TO HARARE ON JULY 25-26, WHICH WE

REPRODUCE HERE IN FULL.

2. (C) BEGIN TEXT.

THE EU PRESIDENCY (FRANCE) ARRANGED A BRIEFING FOR EU

MISSIONS BY SPECIAL REPRESENTATIVE AJELLO ON JULY 26.

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 04145 271028Z

AJELLO HAD BEEN IN HARARE FROM JULY 25-26 DURING WHICH

TIME HE HAD MET PRESIDENT MUGABE, FOREIGN MINISTER

MUDENGE AND MONUC OFFICIALS. AJELLO SAID MUGABE LARGELY

SHARED HIS ANALYSIS OF THE PRESENT SITUATION. THE

MEETING HAD TAKEN PLACE IN A GOOD ATMOSPHERE. KABILA

APPEARED TO BE LOOKING TO A MILITARY SOLUTION IN THE

DRC. MUGABE AGREED THAT THIS WOULD BE A "NO WIN"

STRATEGY AND THAT THE LUSAKA AGREEMENT HAD TO BE

RESPECTED. MUGABE WAS INCLINED TO DISCOUNT YERODIA\'S

RECENT REMARKS ABOUT LUSAKA.

AJELLO HAD RAISED THE PROBLEMS OF THE MASIRE MISSION AND

THE IDEA OF ATTACHING FRANCOPHONE MEMBERS TO THE TEAM AS

A WAY OF MOVING THE PROCESS FORWARD. MUGABE APPEARED TO

ACCEPT THIS. THE TWO ALSO DISCUSSED THE PLAN FOR THE

NON CONGOLESE BELLIGERENTS TO MEET. AJELLO THOUGHT THE

PROSPECTS FAIR FOR THIS TO BE DISCUSSED AT THE

FORTHCOMING SADC SUMMIT IN WINDHOEK. IF AGREEMENT WAS

REACHED, IT SHOULD BE POSSIBLE TO SET UP SUCH A MEETING

QUITE QUICKLY. MUGABE HAD EXPRESSED THE USUAL

DISAPPOINTMENT OVER THE SLOWNESS OF MONUC DEPLOYMENT.

THIS SUBJECT HAD BEEN RAISED MUCH MORE AGGRESSIVELY BY

MUDENGE IN THEIR MEETING, MUDENGE ACCUSING THE UN OF

EFFECTIVELY UNDERMINING LUSAKA BY NOT YET DEPLOYING AND

THEREBY BEARING THE RESPONSIBILITY. HE WAS NOT

RECEPTIVE TO AJELLO\'S EXPLANATIONS OF THE DIFFICULTIES

PUT IN MONUC\'S WAY BY THE DRC.

WITH THE MONUC OFFICERS, AJELLO HAD HEARD OF THE

SLOWNESS OF UGANDAN DISENGAGEMENT FROM KISANGANI,

ATTRIBUTABLE, IN PART, TO A LACK OF LOGISTICS.

CONFIDENTIAL

AJELLO WAS ENCOURAGED BY MUGABE\'S SIMILAR APPRECIATION

OF DEVELOPMENTS IN THE DRC. HE ALSO CONFIRMED WHAT DHC

HAD LEARNED EARLIER IN THE DAY FROM DAVID HAMADZIRIPI,

MFA UNDER SECRETARY FOR AFRICA, THAT FOREIGN MINISTER

MUDENGE WAS HAVING A MEETING AT VICTORIA FALLS WITH HIS

CONGOLESE COUNTERPARTS ON JULY 27 BEFORE FLYING ON TO

LUSAKA FOR A MEETING OF THE POLITICAL COMMITTEE. THE

CONGOLESE HAD TOLD MUDENGE THEY WOULD ACCOMPANY HIM TO

LUSAKA IN THE SAME AIRCRAFT.

END TEXT.
