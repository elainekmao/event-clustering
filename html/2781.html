SUBJECT: U/S GROSSMAN DELEGATION CONSULTS WITH ITALIAN

OFFICIALS ON PRAGUE SUMMIT

CLASSIFIED BY: ACTING DCM MARGARET DEAN FOR REASONS 1.5(B), (D)

1. (C) SUMMARY. THE GOI AGREES THAT THE PRAGUE SUMMIT SHOULD

BE A SUBSTANTIVE MEETING THAT COVERS MORE THAN JUST NATO

ENLARGEMENT. MEETING WITH U/S GROSSMAN AND HIS DELEGATION IN

ROME ON APRIL 16, SENIOR ITALIAN OFFICIALS ENDORSED THE IDEA

OF REDEFINING THE DEFENSE CAPABILITIES INITIATIVE TO FOCUS ON

A FEW ACHIEVABLE PRIORITIES, EXPRESSED INTEREST IN THE

UNIFIED COMMAND PLAN\'S IMPACT ON NATO, AGREED THAT PRAGUE

WOULD PRESENT AN OPPORTUNITY TO CONSIDER HIGH READINESS FORCE

HEADQUARTERS DETAILS, AND DISCUSSED NATO-RUSSIA RELATIONS.

END SUMMARY.

NEW CAPABILITIES

----------------

2. (C) U/S GROSSMAN\'S DELEGATION REVIEWED U.S. GOALS FOR THE

NATO SUMMIT IN PRAGUE WITH SENIOR MFA AND MOD OFFICIALS IN

ROME ON APRIL 16. THE ITALIAN DELEGATION, HEADED BY MFA

SECRETARY GENERAL BALDOCCI, INCLUDED MFA POLITICAL DIRECTOR

SIPDIS

ARAGONA, VICE CHOD CAMPORINI, MOD DIPLOMATIC ADVISOR GRECO,

MFA MINISTER FOR SECURITY AFFAIRS PENSA, AND MFA NATO OFFICE

DIRECTOR BRAUZZI. ON NEW CAPABILITIES, BALDOCCI, ARAGONA,

AND GEN. CAMPORINI AGREED THAT INTRODUCING A NEW, MORE

FOCUSED CAPABILITIES INITIATIVE AT PRAGUE WOULD BE

CONSTRUCTIVE. CAMPORINI NOTED THAT ITALY\'S LIMITED DEFENSE

FUNDS HAVE BEEN WISELY INVESTED IN PROGRAMS THAT ARE PAYING

OFF, SUCH AS THE ADDITION OF LIFT CAPACITY WITH THE PURCHASE

OF 24 C130JS. HE SUGGESTED THAT NATO EXAMINE THE CAPABILITIES

OF A NEW COMMUNICATIONS SATELLITE PROJECT THAT COULD MEET

SOME ALLIED REQUIREMENTS WITHOUT EXCESSIVE COST (LESS THAN

$500 MILLION FROM DESIGN TO OPERATION).

3. (C) CAMPORINI EXPRESSED SOME DOUBTS ABOUT POOLING FORCES

AND RESOURCES UNTIL THE POLITICAL ISSUES HAVE BEEN RESOLVED.

SHARING CAPABILITIES BETWEEN NATO AND THE EU IS AN IMPORTANT

PREREQUISITE TO ANY COOPERATIVE POOLING EFFORT, HE SAID.

ESDP MUST COMPLEMENT NATO, AND NATO FORCES MUST BE AVAILABLE

FOR ALL POSSIBLE REQUIREMENTS. U/S GROSSMAN EMPHASIZED THAT

THE US WANTS ESDP TO SUCCEED BUT IS CONCERNED THAT THE EU MAY

FAIL TO FULFILL THE HEADLINE GOAL BY 2003. U/S GROSSMAN

URGED THE EU TO RESOLVE THE DIFFERENCES BETWEEN GREECE AND

TURKEY AND COMPLETE THE BERLIN PLUS PROCESS SO THAT ESDP CAN

MOVE FORWARD. BEFORE THE QUESTION OF NATO\'S FUTURE ROLE CAN

TRULY BE ADDRESSED, THE ALLIANCE NEEDS TO BE MORE BALANCED IN

TERMS OF MILITARY CAPABILITIES, U/S GROSSMAN SAID.

4. (C) DISCUSSION IN PRAGUE OF THE HRF HEADQUARTERS WOULD BE

USEFUL, ACCORDING TO CAMPORINI. THE HRF CANDIDACY PROCESS,

WHICH PITTED NATO COUNTRIES AGAINST ONE ANOTHER IN AN

EXPENSIVE EXERCISE, WAS NOT CONSTRUCTIVE, HE ADDED. NOW THAT

WE HAVE SIX NATO ELEMENTS, WE NEED TO DECIDE WHAT TO DO WITH

THEM. HE ENDORSED A RECENT BRITISH IDEA OF PAIRING UP HRF

COMMANDS. WHILE THIS MAY RESULT IN A REDUNDANCY OF

RESOURCES, CAMPORINI SAID IT COULD BE AN ACCEPTABLE SOLUTION.

ITALY SUPPORTS A MORE RATIONAL APPROACH TO CAOC\'S AND JOC\'S,

CAMPORINI SAID, ALTHOUGH THE GOI IS NOT IN A POSITION TO

START THE DISCUSSION ON RATIONALIZING THESE COMMANDS. ONCE

THE DISCUSSION IS UNDERWAY, ITALY IS WILLING TO PARTICIPATE,

AND IS EVEN PREPARED TO MAKE SACRIFICES THAT WOULD IMPROVE

THE EFFICIENCY OF THE ALLIANCE.

5. (C) BALDOCCI AND OTHER KEY DELEGATION MEMBERS EXPRESSED

PARTICULAR INTEREST IN THE FUTURE OF SACLANT AND SACEUR UNDER

THE NEW UCP. THEY URGED THAT MAJOR NATO COMMANDERS REMAIN

DOUBLE-HATTED, WITH BOTH A NATO AND A SIGNIFICANT US COMMAND.

FROM THE ALLIES\' POINT OF VIEW, HE SAID, THIS IS AN

IMPORTANT LINK THAT SHOULD BE PRESERVED BECAUSE OF THE STRONG

MESSAGE IT SENDS.

NEW MEMBERS

-----------

6. (C) THE ITALIANS AGREED THAT ALBANIA AND MACEDONIA ARE NOT

READY FOR NATO MEMBERSHIP BUT SHOULD FEEL THE ENLARGEMENT

PROCESS IS FAIR. BALDOCCI NOTED THAT POLITICAL CIRCUMSTANCES

MAY IMPACT ENLARGEMENT BUT REITERATED THAT A GEOSTRATEGIC

BALANCE IS AN ESSENTIAL PREREQUISITE FOR ITALY. PRIME

MINISTER BERLUSCONI WILL PUBLICLY EXPRESS ITALY\'S SUPPORT FOR

MEMBERSHIP FOR BULGARIA AND ROMANIA DURING HIS STOPS IN SOFIA

NEED TO CREATE A MECHANISM TO SUSPEND OR EXPEL NATO MEMBERS

SOMEWHAT CONTROVERSIAL IN EUROPEAN DEBATES, ARAGONA SAID, BUT

IS AN IDEA ITALY COULD SUPPORT.

8. (C) WHEN ASKED ABOUT RUSSIA\'S POSITION ON MEMBERSHIP FOR

THE BALTICS, U/S GROSSMAN EMPHASIZED THAT ONE REASON FOR

HOLDING A NATO-RUSSIA SUMMIT IN ROME IN LATE MAY IS TO HELP

PRESIDENT PUTIN NEUTRALIZE OPPOSITION TO ENLARGEMENT. ITALY,

ALONG WITH OTHER ALLIES, WILL NEED TO ENSURE THAT THE MAY

SUMMIT IN ROME RESOLVES RUSSIA\'S PUBLIC OPINION PROBLEMS SO

THAT THESE WILL NOT INTERFERE WITH THE PRAGUE SUMMIT. DAS

BRADTKE SUGGESTED THAT ENCOURAGING THE BALTICS TO STATE THAT
