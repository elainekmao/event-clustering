SUBJECT: KUWAIT TO FURTHER POSTPONE ICCP IMPLEMENTATION

REF: KUWAIT 00392 AND PREVIOUS

1. (U) During a February 1, 2003, meeting with Salah Abdul

Redha A. Khourshid, Kuwait\'s Minister for Commerce and

Industry (MOCI), the Ambassador requested that the GOK

further delay implementation of its proposed International

Conformity Certification Program (ICCP) to allow WTO members

additional time to reply (reftel). After consulting with his

advisors, Khourshid agreed, stating that the GOK now plans to

postpone the ICCP start date until at least March 17.

2. (U) Khourshid was joined in the meeting by four MOCI

officials, including three officials from the Public

Authority for Industry (PAI); the Ambassador was joined by

EconOff (note taker) and a senior local staff member. MOCI

Acting Under Secretary Bader Abdul Rahim stated that in

addition to the technical reasons cited (reftel), the GOK\'s

commercial attache is presently involved in negotiations on

technical aspects of the program with the WTO Secretariat.

He added that the WTO has asked Kuwait to submit an

explanatory paper before it moves forward with implementation.
