1. (C) SUMMARY: MFA EAST AFRICA DIRECTOR PAOLA IMPERIALE

AND NSC AFRICA DIRECTOR MATTHEW MCLEAN EXCHANGED VIEWS ON A

BROAD RANGE OF EAST AFRICA ISSUES. THE TWO DISCUSSED SUPPORT

FOR THE NEW GOVERNMENT IN KENYA, POLICY REGARDING SOMALILAND,

THE ROLES OF DJIBOUTI AND ERITREA IN THE GWOT, AND THE

REGIONAL FOOD CRISIS. END SUMMARY.

-------------------------------------

KENYA: SUPPORTING THE NEW GOVERNMENT

-------------------------------------

2. (C) AT THE REQUEST OF MCLEAN, IMPERIALE AND HE MET

SATURDAY, JANUARY 11 FOR AN INFORMAL DISCUSSION OF EAST

AFRICA POLICY. ON KENYA, IMPERIALE MENTIONED THAT THE

ITALIAN PARLIAMENT HAS A PROJECT TO PROVIDE TECHNICAL ADVICE

TO THE KENYAN PARLIAMENT. MCLEAN AGREED THAT TECHNICAL

ASSISTANCE, GOOD GOK POLICY, AND PROGRESS AGAINST CORRUPTION

SHOULD PRECEDE A FULL AID PACKAGE.

--------------------------------------------- --

SOMALILAND: "AUTONOMY WOULD BE VERY DANGEROUS"

--------------------------------------------- --

3. (C) DISCUSSING HOW TO HELP SOMALILAND ABSENT PROGRESS

IN THE SOUTH, IMPERIALE EXPLAINED THE GOI VIEW THAT

OFFICIALLY RECOGNIZING SOMALILAND AUTONOMY "WOULD BE VERY

DANGEROUS." MOST IMPORTANTLY, SOMALILAND HAD "VOTED AWAY"

ITS RIGHT TO SOVEREIGNTY, AND UNDOING THE REFERENDUM WITHOUT

THE AGREEMENT OF A RECOGNIZED CENTRAL GOVERNMENT WOULD SET A

DANGEROUS PRECEDENT. FORMAL RECOGNITION WOULD CREATE

DIFFICULTIES IN THE SUDANESE PEACE PROCESS, BECAUSE ARAB

STATES WOULD FEAR THAT THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY WOULD

THEREFORE APPROVE SOUTHERN SUDAN\'S SECESSION OR AUTONOMY.

FURTHERMORE, SOMALIA HAD HUGE DEBTS. WHO WOULD BE

RESPONSIBLE FOR THEM IN THE EVENT OF FINANCIAL AUTONOMY? IN

ANY CASE, SOMALILAND WAS NOT AS DEMOCRATIC AS IT MIGHT AT

FIRST APPEAR. NEVERTHELESS, IMPERIALE CONCLUDED, THE

INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY\'S FAILURE TO PUSH SOMALILAND TO

DJIBOUTI AND ERITREA: MINOR PARTNERS IN THE GWOT

--------------------------------------------- ----

4. (C) IMPERIALE SAID DJIBOUTI\'S PRESIDENT WANTED TO BE

INVITED TO ROME BUT IMPLIED IT WAS A LOW PRIORITY FOR THE

GOI. MCLEAN SUGGESTED THE PRESIDENT STOP IN ROME ON THE WAY

TO HIS UPCOMING VISIT TO WASHINGTON. IMPERIALE ASKED WHETHER

THE USG WAS PLANNING TO INCREASE ITS SECURITY PRESENCE IN

DJIBOUTI. MCLEAN SAID THAT DJIBOUTI DID NOT HAVE THE

CAPACITY TO ABSORB A MUCH GREATER PRESENCE.

5. (C) IMPERIALE NOTED THAT ALTHOUGH ONLY ERITREA

IMPRISONED JOURNALISTS, MAKING IT LOOK MORE OPPRESSIVE THAN

ETHIOPIA, THERE WAS NO MORE PRESS FREEDOM IN ETHIOPIA BECAUSE

THE PRESS THERE WAS ENTIRELY GOVERNMENT OWNED. MCLEAN SAID

THAT BECAUSE OF THE IMPRISONED FSNS, THE USG WANTED TO GO

SLOWLY ON BUILDING TIES TO ASMARA BUT ALSO HAD TO CONSIDER

THE POSSIBILITY THAT ERITREAN ASSETS WOULD BE OF VALUE IN THE

GWOT.

--------------------------------------------- --

AFRICA FOOD CRISIS: GOI FOCUSES ON EAST AFRICA

--------------------------------------------- --

6. (C) IMPERIALE SAID THAT THE GOI WAS TRYING TO RAISE THE

PROFILE OF THE EAST AFRICAN FOOD CRISIS TO THE LEVEL OF

ATTENTION THE SOUTHERN AFRICAN FOOD CRISIS HAD RECEIVED.

ETHIOPIA\'S ONE-PARTY STATE AND GOVERNMENT OWNERSHIP OF LAND

DISCOURAGED PRIVATE INVESTMENT, AND MCLEAN AND SHE AGREED ON

THE NEED FOR A LONG-TERM SOLUTION: AGRICULTURE REFORM IN

ETHIOPIA. IMPERIALE SAID THAT WHEN MELES CAME TO ROME IN

LATE FEBRUARY OR MARCH, THE GOI WOULD TRY TO GET HIM TO "GIVE

UP HIS COMMUNIST IDEAS."

7. (C) WITH REGARD TO SECURITY ISSUES, IMPERIALE NOTED THAT

THE COUNTRIES OF THE REGION DID NOT SHARE INTELLIGENCE

BECAUSE OF RACIAL SUSPICIONS. ("ETHIOPIANS CONSIDER

THEMSELVES HISTORICALLY SUPERIOR," SHE SAID.) FINALLY, THE

ETHIOPIANS "ATTACH GREAT IMPORTANCE TO AMERICAN INVOLVEMENT

IN THE ELDORET PEACE PROCESS FOR THE SECURITY OF THE REGION."

8. (U) NSC DIRECTOR MCLEAN DID NOT HAVE THE OPPORTUNITY TO
