SUBJECT: CGYOOL-ICRC REPORTS RELEASE OF 87 DETAINEES.

LACK OF SPACE PREVENTS FURTHER RELEASES

1. ICRC-ZAGREB REPORTS THE RELEASE, ON NOVEMBER 3,

OF 87 PRISONERS FROM B-H CAMPS. SIXTY-ONE PRISONERS

HELD BY SERBIAN FORCES IN KAMENICA (NEAR DRVAR) HAVE

BEEN RELEASED AND TRANSFERED BY ICRC TO THE TRANSIT

CENTER IN KARLOVAC (REPUBLIC OF CROATIA) RUN BY

UNHCR. ANOTHER GROUP OF 25 SERBIAN PRISONERS, HELD

BY MUSLIM FORCES IN BIHAC AND CAZIN, HAVE BEEN

RELEASED AND TRANSFERED BY ICRC VIA CROATIA TO OSIJEK

AND FROM THERE TO THE FRY. ONE FREED DETAINEE CHOSE

TO STAY IN THE BIHAC AREA.

2. ICRC-ZAGREB ALSO REPORTS THAT THE PLANNED RELEASE

OF OVER 3,000 PRISONERS AT THE MANJACA CAMP HAS BEEN

INDEFINITELY POSTPONED, IN PART BECAUSE UNHCR HAS

BEEN UNABLE TO FIND PLACES FOR THE RELEASED DETAINEES

3. THE TRANSIT FACILITY AT KARLOVAC IS PRESENTLY

FULL. ORIGINALLY DESIGNED TO HOUSE 2,000 DETAINEES,

500 PLACES HAVE BEEN LOST BECAUSE OF THE DANGER POSED

BY A SEVERELY DAMAGED ORTHODOX CHURCH WHICH IS

ADJACENT TO THE FACILITY AND COULD COLLAPSE AT ANY

MOMENT. ICRC HOPES THAT THE CROATIAN GOVERNMENT WILL

AT LEAST PERMIT UNHCR TO REPLACE THESE 500 PLACES

WHICH HAVE BEEN LOST.

4. THE LACK OF INSTRUCTIONS FROM RADOVAN KARADZIC TO

THE SERBIAN AUTHORITIES RUNNING THE MANJACA CAMP ARE

REPORTEDLY ANOTHER ELEMENT COMPLICATING THE RELEASE

OF THE PRISONERS. DURING A RECENT VISIT TO MANJACA,

OFDA CONTRACTOR TOM BRENNAN RECEIVED THE IMPRESSION

THAT THE SERBS RUNNING THE CAMP WOULD BE MORE THAN

HAPPY TO BE RID OF THE PRISONERS IF THEY WERE ONLY TO

RECEIVE INSTRUCTIONS TO DO SO. ICRC-ZAGREB OFFICIALS

CONFIRMED THESE IMPRESSIONS, ADDING THAT UNSC

RESOLUTION 780 CREATING A WAR CRIMES COMMISSION

APPEARS TO HAVE HAD A VISIBLE IMPACT ON THE ATTITUDES

OF THE CAMP AUTHORITIES.
