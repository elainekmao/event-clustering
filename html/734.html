SUBJECT: GRANIC SAYS CROATIAN ARMY OUT OF SECTOR

SOUTH ZOS; COMPLETE WITHDRAWAL BY 5 PM

REF: ZAGREB 001867

1. FOREIGN MINISTER GRANIC CALLED AT 12 NOON,

MAY 16, TO SAY THAT CROATIAN ARMY FORCES HAD

WITHDRAWN COMPLETELY FROM THE POSITIONS IN THE

ZONE OF SEPARATION (ZOS) IN SECTOR SOUTH THAT

HAD BEEN OCCUPIED SINCE MAY 1. GRANIC AGAIN

ASSURED ME THAT ALL FORCES WOULD BE BACK TO THE

PRE-MAY 1 POSITIONS IN THE SECTORS NORTH AND

EAST ZOS BY 5:00 P.M. LOCAL TIME, MAY 16

(REFTEL).

-

2. BOTH THE CROATIAN ARMY AND THE RSK HAD

FORCES IN THE ZOS BEFORE MAY 1. THIS PRESENCE,

WHICH RELATES TO DIFFERING INTERPRETATIONS OF

THE MAPS FOR THE MARCH 29, 1994 CEASE-FIRE

AGREEMENT AS WELL AS SOME PRE-EXISTING WILLFUL

VIOLATIONS, IS NOT AFFECTED BY CROATIA\'S

WITHDRAWAL PROMISE. THUS THERE WILL CONTINUE TO

BE VIOLATIONS OF THE ZOS AFTER THE DEADLINE.

THE PRE-EXISTING VIOLATIONS ARE LESS DANGEROUS

THAN THOSE THAT OCCURRED THIS MONTH, AND UNCRO

PRESUMABLY WILL BE REASONABLY SATISFIED IF IT

CAN ACHIEVE THE PRE-MAY STATUS QUO IN THE ZOS.

3. UN REPORTING OFTEN RUNS EIGHT TO TWELVE

HOURS BEHIND THE EVENT. THEREFORE THE UN MAY

NOT BE IN A POSITION TO CONFIRM THE CROATIAN

WITHDRAWAL (ASSUMING THERE IS A FULL WITHDRAWAL)

UNTIL MAY 17.

4. GRANIC TOLD ME THAT 180 OF THE 1400 SERB

DETAINEES FROM WESTERN SLAVONIA ARE UNDER

INVESTIGATION FOR POSSIBLE WAR CRIMES. FORTY

SEVEN WERE AMNESTIED YESTERDAY AND HE SAID THE

GOC WOULD TAKE STEPS TO REDUCE FURTHER THE

NUMBER BEING HELD FOR POSSIBLE TRIAL.
