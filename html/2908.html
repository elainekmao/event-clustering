1. (C) SUMMARY. ITALY AND IRAN HAVE BROKEN OFF NEGOTIATIONS

THAT HAD BEEN AIMED AT LENDING ITALIAN KNOW-HOW TO VACCINE

PRODUCTION EFFORTS AT IRAN\'S PASTEUR INSTITUTE. THE ITALIAN

MFA REPORTS THAT IRAN BALKED AT THE STRINGENT INSPECTION

PROVISIONS ITALY INSISTED BE INCORPORATED INTO ANY GOI-GOI

AGREEMENT ON POINT. MFA HAS SEEN NO SIGN THAT IRAN IS

INCLINED TO RECONSIDER ITS REJECTION OF THE PROVISIONS, AND

ITALY THEREFORE CONSIDERS THE MATTER CLOSED. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) INSPIRED BY SWITZERLAND\'S CONCLUSION LAST YEAR OF A

MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING WITH IRAN (REF A) CONTEMPLATING

SWISS EXPORTS AND TECH-TRANSFERS TO IRAN\'S PASTEUR INSTITUTE

( IPI), ITALY UNDERTOOK TO NEGOTIATE ITS OWN MOU WITH IRAN,

MFA NONPROLIFERATION COORDINATOR UGO DE MOHR TOLD ECONOFF ON

MAY 8. DE MOHR SAID THE NEGOTIATIONS DIDN\'T GET FAR BECAUSE,

EVEN THOUGH THERE WAS "100 PERCENT CERTAINTY" THAT IPI WOULD

PRODUCE ONLY BONA FIDE VACCINES, ITALY INSISTED ON INCLUSION

OF VERY RIGOROUS GUARANTEES, INCLUDING RANDOM INSPECTIONS OF

ALL FACILITIES BY ITALIAN EXPERTS, AND IRAN BALKED. WHEN

IRAN COUNTERED WITH A PROPOSAL FOR A LESS INVASIVE SET OF

GUARANTEES, ITALY REPLIED, PER DE MOHR, THAT ITS ORIGINAL

DEMANDS WERE NON-NEGOTIABLE, AND THE TALKS BROKE OFF.

3. (C) HAD THE MOU TALKS SUCCEEDED, DE MOHR SAID, THE TWO

GOVERNMENTS WOULD HAVE MOVED ON TO NEGOTIATION OF AN MOU

ANNEX DETAILING THE FACILITIES INVOLVED AND INSPECTION
