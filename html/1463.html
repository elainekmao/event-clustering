SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) IN HIS HOUR-LONG END OF YEAR MESSAGE DECEMBER

27, PRESIDENT MUGABE GAVE HIS VERSION OF EVENTS IN THE

YEAR 2000. HE ATTRIBUTED THE COUNTRY\'S DIFFICULT

ECONOMIC PATCH TO FALLING COMMODITY AND RISING INPUT

PRICES AS WELL AS RESERVE BANK MONETARY POLICY. HE

REHEARSED THE REASONS FOR THE FAST-TRACK LAND REFORM

PROGRAM AND BLAMED THE GREED OF THE "ANGLO-SAXON"

COMMERCIAL FARMERS FOR HAMPERING PROGRESS. HE CONDEMNED

THE OPPOSITION MOVEMENT FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE FOR

ATTEMPTING TO COME TO POWER THROUGH VIOLENT MEANS AND

PURSUING A SEDITIOUS POLITICAL AGENDA. HE SAID THE YEAR

HAD BEEN A DIFFICULT ONE AND THANKED PEOPLE FOR THEIR

FORBEARANCE WITH THE SHORTAGES OF FUEL. THE SPEECH,

RIDDLED WITH FACTUAL INACCURACIES, LOGICAL

INCONSISTENCIES AND NOT-SO-VEILED THREATS, SUCCEEDED IN

EMBARRASSING ALL BUT THE MOST ARDENT ZANU-PF SUPPORTERS

PRESENT. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) PRESIDENT MUGABE DELIVERED HIS END-OF-YEAR

MESSAGE TO AN ESTIMATED 500 GUESTS AT A STATE HOUSE

RECEPTION DECEMBER 27. THE PRESIDENT, WHO SPOKE FOR AN

HOUR, OFTEN DEPARTED FROM HIS PREPARED TEXT. THE SPEECH

WAS AN EMBARRASSMENT; IT WAS BREATHTAKING IN ITS

INTELLECTUAL DISHONESTY, LOGICAL INCONSISTENCY AND NOT-

SO-VEILED THREATS. AUDIENCE REACTION WAS MUTED WITH THE

EXCEPTION OF THE OCCASIONAL "HEAR, HEAR," SHOUTED BY

SOME OF THE MORE ZEALOUS OF THE PARTY FAITHFUL.

MINISTER OF INDUSTRY AND INTERNATIONAL TRADE NKOSANA

MOYO CONFIDED TO THE AUSTRALIAN HIGH COMMISSIONER HERE

THAT HE FELT "SHATTERED" FOLLOWING THE PRESIDENT\'S SPEECH.

3. (SBU) MUGABE LED OFF BY MAKING A POINT OF GREETING

THOSE IN ATTENDANCE FROM THE PRIVATE SECTOR AND THE

PARASTATALS. HE SEEMED TO BE ALIGNING GOVERNMENT WITH

THESE TWO GROUPS AND MADE MENTION OF THE FACT THAT THEY

HAD STOOD TOGETHER IN SOLIDARITY TO COMBAT THE FORCES

FROM OUTSIDE THE COUNTRY. HE THEN CALLED THE

INDEPENDENT "DAILY NEWS" NEWSPAPER THE "DAILY LIARS,"

AND SAID THAT THEY AND THE OPPOSITION MOVEMENT FOR

DEMOCRATIC CHANGE (MDC) DID NOT KNOW THE DIFFERENCE

BETWEEN A BANQUET AND A RECEPTION. (COMMENT: THIS WAS A

JAB AT THE MDC FOR BOYCOTTING THE EVENT. THE "DAILY

NEWS" REPORTED ON THE DECEMBER 27 THAT THE OPPOSITION

PARTY SAID IT COULD NOT ATTEND SUCH A LAVISH EVENT WITH

THE SUFFERING GOING ON OVER THIS LAST YEAR. END

COMMENT).

THE ECONOMY IS NOT OUR FAULT

----------------------------

4. (SBU) THE PRESIDENT ADMITTED THAT IT HAD BEEN A

DIFFICULT YEAR FOR EVERYONE. HE SAID THE MAIN CAUSE WAS
