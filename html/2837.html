---------

SUMMARY

---------

1. SENIOR EMERGENCY COORDINATOR (SEC) IN THE U.S.

MISSION IN ROME TO THE U.N. AGENCIES TRAVELED TO ANGOLA

APRIL 13--20 TO REVIEW THE OPERATIONS OF THE U.N. WORLD

FOOD PROGRAM (WFP) AND THE EMERGENCY COORDINATION UNIT

OF THE FOOD AND AGRICULTURAL ORGANIZATION OF THE U.N.

(FAO/ECU) AND TO LOOK AT THE COMPLEMENTARITY BETWEEN THE

TWO. PROGRESS OF FAO/ECU WILL BE REPORTED IN SEPTEL.

2. ALTHOUGH THE POSSIBILITY FOR REAL PEACE HAS FINALLY

COME TO ANGOLA, THE IMMEDIATE HUMANITARIAN NEEDS ARE

IMMENSE AND CRITICAL IN THE NEAR TERM. THE U.N. HAS

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ROME 02065 01 OF 10 291503Z

IDENTIFIED 36 INACCESSIBLE AREAS THAT MAY CONTAIN

500,000 PEOPLE IN NEED, IN ADDITION THOUSANDS OF

VULNERABLE PEOPLE ARE WALKING INTO PROVINCIAL CAPITALS

SEEKING ASSISTANCE (PROJECTED 20,000-30,000 PER MONTH

FOR FOUR MONTHS), THE NUMBERS IN THE NUTRITIONAL FEEDING

CENTERS CONTINUE TO RISE (POSSIBLY RISING TO 25,000-

30,000 IN FOUR MONTHS\' TIME), REFUGEES WILL BEGIN

RETURNING HOME, AND THE GOVERNMENT OF THE REPUBLIC OF

ANGOLA (GRA) HAS IDENTIFIED 38 QUARTERING AREAS FOR

UNITA EX-COMBATANTS AND THEIR FAMILIES WHO WILL BE IN

NEED OF ASSISTANCE (POSSIBLE 355,000). FOOD IS A

CRITICAL NEED OF ALL THESE GROUPS. AT THE CURRENT TIME,

THE GRA IS SAYING THAT IT WILL PROVIDE FOOD FOR THE

QUARTERING AREAS. ALREADY PROVIDING FOOD TO

APPROXIMATELY ONE MILLION BENEFICIARIES, ENORMOUS

PRESSURE IS GROWING ON THE U.N. WORLD FOOD PROGRAM (WFP)

TO SIGNIFICANTLY EXPAND ITS PROGRAM IN ORDER TO PROVIDE

FOOD FOR THESE ADDITIONAL GROUPS, AND THERE IS CONCERN

THAT THEIR EFFORTS ARE LAGGING BEHIND. WFP\'S NEW

PROTRACTED RELIEF AND RECOVERY OPERATION (PRRO) FOR

ANGOLA WILL BE PRESENTED AT THE WFP EXECUTIVE BOARD

MEETING THIS MAY. THE PRRO WAS PREPARED BEFORE THE WAR

ENDED AND ADJUSTMENTS WILL LIKELY BE MADE-PRIMARILY TO

THE BENEFICIARY NUMBERS AND THE REQUIRED TONNAGES. AT

THE SAME TIME, THE DONORS AND THE U.N. MUST KEEP

UNRELENTING PRESSURE ON THE GRA TO TAKE RESPONSIBILITY

FOR THE NEEDS OF ITS POPULATION. SEVERAL

RECOMMENDATIONS WERE GIVEN TO WFP\'S PROGRAMMATIC SECTION

IN AN EFFORT TO MORE ACCURATELY TARGET BENEFICIARIES AND

ACCOUNT FOR ITS COMMODITIES. THE CURRENT PIPELINE IS IN

SERIOUS JEOPARDY OF BREAKING IN LATE JUNE/EARLY JULY.

AWARE OF THE POTENTIAL OF THIS SCENARIO BECOMING THE

REALITY, THE U.N. IS MOVING FORWARD WITH CONTINGENCY

PLANS FOR PROVIDING ASSISTANCE TO THE QAS IN

ANTICIPATION THAT IT WILL SOON BE ASKED BY THE GRA TO

6. IN PARALLEL WITH THIS EFFORT, THE HUMANITARIAN

COMMUNITY IS ATTEMPTING TO MEET THE GROWING HUMANITARIAN

NEEDS IN THE COUNTRY. HUNDREDS, AND IN SOME CASES

THOUSANDS, OF PEOPLE ARE WALKING INTO PROVINCIAL

CAPITALS FROM THE BUSH IN SEARCH OF ASSISTANCE. MANY OF

THEM HAVE WALKED HUNDREDS OF KILOMETERS AND ARRIVE WITH

PRACTICALLY NOTHING. SOME DON\'T EVEN HAVE CLOTHES.

OTHERS ARE SO WEAK THEY ARE BEING AIRLIFTED IN

HELICOPTERS OR BEING TRANSPORTED IN TRUCKS BY THE ARMED

FORCES OF ANGOLA (FAA). REPORTEDLY EVEN SOME OF THE

ELITE UNITA SOLDIERS THAT PROTECTED SAVIMBI WERE

MALNOURISHED BY THE WAR\'S END IN FEBRUARY. IN LUENA

(MOXICO PROVINCE), 7,000 TO 8,000 PERSONS HAVE BEEN

ARRIVING EACH MONTH SINCE LAST DECEMBER. WFP EXPECTS

11,000 NEW INTERNALLY DISPLACED PERSONS (IDPS) IN BIE

PROVINCE IN THE MONTH OF MAY. LARGE NUMBERS OF

BENEFICIARIES ALSO EXIST IN HUAMBO, MALANGE, AND HUILA

PROVINCES. OCHA AND WFP ESTIMATE THAT 20,000 TO 30,000

NEW IDPS COULD SURFACE EACH MONTH FOR THE NEXT FOUR

MONTHS.

7. ON APRIL 18, THE U.N. BEGAN LEADING RAPID

ASSESSMENTS (RAS) TO 36 INACCESSIBLE AREAS (IAS) THAT

HAVE BEEN IDENTIFIED AS AREAS WHERE POPULATIONS IN POOR

INACCESSIBLE BECAUSE OF INSECURITY, SOMETIMES COUPLED

WITH LACK OF PHYSICAL ACCESS DUE TO DESTROYED

INFRASTRUCTURE, SUCH AS BRIDGES. THE U.N. OFFICE FOR

THE COORDINATION OF HUMANITARIAN AFFAIRS (OCHA) HAS BEEN

COLLECTING INFORMATION ON THESE AREAS FOR MORE THAN A

YEAR. THE INFORMATION HAS COME FROM VARIOUS SOURCES,

ORGANIZATIONS (NGOS), AND INTERNALLY DISPLACED PERSONS

(IDPS) THEMSELVES. OCHA ANTICIPATES THAT THE

ASSESSMENTS WILL TAKE APPROXIMATELY THREE WEEKS TO

COMPLETE AND MAY YIELD AN ADDITIONAL 500,000 PEOPLE

NEEDING ASSISTANCE.

REPORTED A DIRE SITUATION AND BUNJEI WAS PLACED AT THE

TOP OF THE LIST TO BE VISITED ONCE THE RAS COMMENCED.

THE RA TEAM VISITED APRIL 18. MEMBERS OF THE TEAM

9. WITHIN A PERIOD OF TWO DAYS (APRIL 15/16), 578

FAMILIES (AVERAGE SIZE IS FIVE) ARRIVED IN MUACANHICA

IDP CAMP OUTSIDE LUENA TOWN, A CAMP THAT OPENED ON

FEBRUARY 17. MOST HAD WALKED IN ON THEIR OWN, BUT SOME

HAD BEEN BROUGHT VIA FAA HELICOPTER. THE CAMP IS

MANAGED BY LUTHERAN WORLD FEDERATION (LWF) AND ITS

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ROME 02065 03 OF 10 291504Z

POPULATION ON APRIL 17 WAS 1,504 FAMILIES. A LARGE

PERCENTAGE OF THE POPULATION IS CHILDREN AND SOME ARE

UNACCOMPANIED. LWF REPORTS THAT MOST OF THE CAMP

INHABITANTS ARE UNITA FAMILIES.

10. LWF STATED THE MORTALITY RATE HAS BEEN CONSTANT IN

MUACANHICA CAMP DURING ITS TWO MONTHS OF EXISTENCE, WITH

AT LEAST ONE DEATH PER DAY, MANY BEING ADULTS. IN THE

PREVIOUS WEEK, SIX ADULTS AND ONE CHILD HAD DIED. THE

MOST COMMON REASONS FOR DEATH ARE DIARRHEA AND MALARIA.

THE SEC WAS TOLD THAT IN THE BEGINNING OF THE FORMATION

OF THE CAMP CASKETS WERE BEING MADE FOR THE DEAD, BUT

AFTER AWHILE THE TOLL ON THE TREES BECAME TOO GREAT AND

THE EFFORT WAS HALTED. THE WOOD WAS NEEDED FOR

FIREWOOD. LWF REPORTS THAT MANY OF THE WOMEN ARE HEAD

OF HOUSEHOLDS AND ARRIVE TOO WEAK TO CONSTRUCT A SHELTER

OUT OF PLASTIC SHEETING. ON-SITE OBSERVATION CONFIRMS

THIS STATEMENT. THE SEC TALKED WITH ONE MIDDLE-AGED

SICK WOMAN WHO WAS TAKING CARE OF NINE CHILDREN (SOME OF

WHICH BELONGED TO HER BROTHER WHO HAD GONE TO ZAMBIA)

AND WHOSE HUSBAND HAD DIED A COUPLE WEEKS BEFORE. SHE

WAS TOO WEAK TO PROPERLY CARE FOR HER CHILDREN OR COOK.

DURING THE CONVERSATION, A MOTHER INSIDE THE NEXT TENT

WAS WAILING OVER THE LOSS OF HER 12-YEAR OLD DAUGHTER TO

MALARIA. THE UTTER DESPAIR OF THE SITUATION WAS

STRIKING.

13. ALREADY PROVIDING FOOD TO APPROXIMATELY ONE MILLION

BENEFICIARIES, ENORMOUS PRESSURE IS BUILDING ON

WFP/ANGOLA TO SIGNIFICANTLY EXPAND ITS PROGRAM IN ORDER

TO MEET THE EMERGING NEW NEEDS THAT ARE PRESENTING

THEMSELVES. DURING THE SEC\'S VISIT, WFP/ANGOLA SENIOR

MANAGEMENT AND THE WFP REGIONAL DIRECTOR (WHOSE VISIT TO

ANGOLA OVERLAPPED WITH THAT OF THE SEC\'S) DID NOT SEEM

TO HAVE A TRUE APPRECIATION OF THE URGENCY OF THE

MOMENT. THIS CONCERN WAS SHARED BY THE U.S. MISSION IN

ANGOLA, OCHA, AND THE UNDP RESIDENT

REPRESENTATIVE/HUMANITARIAN COORDINATOR (PLEASE

PROTECT). GIVEN THE INCREASE IN THE NUMBER OF NEEDY

ANGOLANS AT THIS MOMENT AND THE LIKELIHOOD THAT THE

NUMBERS WILL SIGNIFICANTLY INCREASE SOON, WFP\'S

READINESS AND RAPID RESPONSE CAPACITY IS LAGGING BEHIND.

THE WFP LOGISTICS STAFF IS BEING PROACTIVE IN PLANNING

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ROME 02065 04 OF 10 291504Z

FOR THE NEW ENVIRONMENT, BUT THE PROGRAM SECTION HAS NOT

EFFECTIVELY PLANNED AND PREPARED, ALTHOUGH IT IS NOW IN

A CATCH-UP MODE.

14. ON HER FIRST DAY IN LUANDA, THE SEC RECEIVED A WFP

PIPELINE REPORT THAT ANTICIPATED NO INCREASE IN

BENEFICIARY LEVELS IN THE COMING MONTHS. BY THE END OF

THE WEEK, WFP HAD CONDUCTED A PLANNING EXERCISE THAT

REFLECTED A POSSIBLE INCREASE OF 140,000 NEW

BENEFICIARIES IN MAY, RISING STEADILY TO A POSSIBLE

650,000 BY SEPTEMBER (THIS CASELOAD WOULD BE IN ADDITION

TO THE NUMBERS IN THE NEW PRRO). SUCH A FIGURE

ANTICIPATES A SHORT-TERM INCREASE IN THE NUMBER OF

SPONTANEOUS ARRIVALS IN THE PROVINCIAL CAPITALS,

REACHING THE VULNERABLE PEOPLE LOCATED IN THE 36 IAS, A

NEAR-TERM INCREASE IN THE NEED FOR NUTRITIONAL PROGRAMS,

REFUGEES RETURNING HOME, AND PROVIDING FOOD FOR UNITA

FAMILIES IN 32 OF THE 38 QUARTERING AREAS. IN APRIL,

WFP PLANS TO PROVIDE FOOD TO APPROXIMATELY 961,000

PERSONS, 95 PERCENT OF WHICH IS EITHER FOR EMERGENCY

GENERAL DISTRIBUTIONS OR NUTRITIONAL FEEDING PROGRAMS.

IT IS CONCEIVABLE THAT WFP\'S CASELOAD COULD REACH 1.7

MILLION BY LATE SUMMER. WITH THE MAJOR PLANTING SEASON

IN SEPTEMBER/OCTOBER, IT IS VERY LIKELY THERE WILL BE

SIGNIFICANT FOOD NEEDS IN ANGOLA UNTIL THE HARVEST THIS

TIME NEXT YEAR.

15. OVER THE LAST FIVE MONTHS, WFP/ANGOLA HAS BEEN

THINKING ABOUT THE CHANGING ENVIRONMENT.
