THE MONTH. COVERED IN THIS CABLE ARE:

-- AMBASSADOR DELIVERS GLOBALIZATION SPEECH

-- AMBASSADOR/FM TAURAN ON MEPP

-- PRESENTING IRF REPORT

-- AMBASSADOR HOSTS LIBRARY OF CONGRESS AND VATICAN MUSEUMS

2. (U) AMBASSADOR DELIVERS GLOBALIZATION TALK: AMBASSADOR

PARTICIPATED IN 04/05 PANEL DISCUSSION OF THE ETHICS OF

GLOBALIZATION AT SEMINAR SPONSORED BY PONTIFICAL ACADEMY FOR

SOCIAL SCIENCES, ORGANIZED BY CANADA AMB. LICARI AND POLAND

AMB. SOCHOWSKI. AMBASSADOR\'S PRESENTATION TRACKED CLOSELY

WITH THEMES ENUNCIATED BY THE PRESIDENT AND U/S LARSON IN

LEAD-UP TO MONTERREY.

3. (C) AMBASSADOR WAS CALLED IN 04/02 BY VATICAN FM TAURAN

AFTER PALESTINIAN GUNMEN OCCUPIED THE CHURCH OF THE NATIVITY

IN BETHLEHEM. TAURAN EARLIER HAD CALLED IN ISRAELI

AMBASSADOR LAMDAN. BOTH MEETINGS WERE WIDELY REPORTED IN

VATICAN AND ITALIAN PRESS.

4. (U) PRESENTING THE IRF REPORT: AMBASSADOR HOSTED LUNCH

FOR PONTIFICAL COMMISSION FOR INTER-RELIGIOUS DIALOGUE

ARCHBISHOP MICHAEL FITZGERALD AND TWO OF HIS STAFF. THE

LUNCH WAS TO PRESENT BOUND COPIES OF THE INTERNATIONAL

RELIGIOUS FREEDOM REPORT FOR 2001, AND TO EXPLORE POSSIBLE

FUTURE CONTACTS WITH THE INCOMING AMBASSADOR AT LARGE FOR

INTERNATIONAL RELIGIOUS FREEDOM (SEPTEL). FITZGERALD,

NORMALLY FRIENDLY AND OPEN, MADE A POINT OF COMPLAINING ABOUT

US POLICY IN THE MIDDLE EAST.

5. (C) AMBASSADOR DISCUSSED VATICAN SECURITY EFFORTS WITH

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 VATICA 02146 01 OF 02 031513Z

AMCIT CARDINAL EDMUND CASIMIR SZOKA, PRESIDENT OF THE

GOVERNATORATO OF VATICAN CITY, FAMILIARLY CONSIDERED THE

MAYOR-EQUIVALENT. AMBASSADOR BROACHED POSSIBILITY OF USG

MATERIAL ASSISTANCE.

6. (U) AMBASSADOR AND STAFF MET WITH U.S. BISHOP THOMAS

WENSKI, HEAD OF REFUGEE SERVICES FOR THE U.S. CATHOLIC

BISHOPS CONFERENCE, WHO ENTHUSIASTICALLY ENDORSED THE

ANTI-TRAFFICKING CONFERENCE POST IS PLANNING FOR MAY.

7. (U) AMBASSADOR HOSTED A WORKING LUNCH 4/15 FOR JORGE

MEJIA, THE ARGENTINE CARDINAL AND HEAD OF THE VATICAN

MUSEUMS, AND LIBRARY OF CONGRESS OFFICIALS, CONTINUING

DISCUSSIONS FROM A MORNING MEETING ATTENDED BY POL/ADMINOFF.

THE LOC DELEGATION, HEADED BY CHIEF OF STAFF JO ANN C.

JENKINS, HAD MET WITH CARDINAL MEJIA AND DON RAFFAELE FARINA,

PREFECT OF THE VATICAN LIBRARY IN REGARD TO LOC OFFER TO

ASSIST THE VATICAN WITH ACCELERATED DIGITALIZATION AND

INTERNET DIFFUSION OF VATICAN HOLDINGS. THE VATICAN WELCOMED

THE INITIATIVE YET NOTED IT MUST EXPLORE CURRENT CONTRACTUAL

OBLIGATIONS THAT APPEAR TO OFFER EXCLUSIVE U.S. INTERNET

RIGHTS TO A COMMERCIAL FIRM FOR UP TO FIFTEEN YEARS.

8. (U) DCM MEETINGS:

-- ON APRIL 8 DCM ATTENDED A PRESENTATION ON "THE RIGHTS OF

MAN IN THE TEACHINGS OF THE CHURCH", HOSTED BY CARDINAL VAN

THUAN (PRESIDENT OF JUSTICE AND PEACE). MAJOR THEME WAS THAT

THE CHURCH IS THE PRIME DEFENDER OF HUMAN RIGHTS AROUND THE

WORLD.

-- DCM ATTENDED BEATIFICATION CEREMONY APRIL 14. IT WAS A

STATE EVENT LASTING THREE HOURS. THE POPE APPEARED WEAKER

THAN HE DID AT EASTER SERVICES, BUT DID TAKE AN ACTIVE ROLE

IN THE CEREMONY. BEATIFIED WERE SIX INDIVIDUALS, INCLUDING A

NICARAGUAN. IN ATTENDANCE WERE THE PRESIDENTS OF BOTH COSTA

RICA AND NICARAGUA.

9. (C) POLITICAL SECTION MEETINGS:

-- POLOFF MET 04/15 WITH VATICAN MADAGASCAR DESK MONSIGNOR

CAVALIERE, AT REQUEST OF AF/E DIRECTOR (REFTEL). CAVALIERE

WAS NOT WELL BRIEFED ON DEVELOPMENTS IN MADAGASCAR AND SEEMED

SURPRISINGLY UNINTERESTED.
