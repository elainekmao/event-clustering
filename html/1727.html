SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE COUP PLOT REPORT TOUCHES A RAW NERVE

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) SUMMARY: THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE HAS

VIGOROUSLY DISPUTED A MAY 29 ARTICLE IN THE BRITISH

"GUARDIAN" NEWSPAPER THAT STATES THAT ZIMBABWEAN

MILITARY OFFICERS ARE PLANNING A COUP AGAINST PRESIDENT

MUGABE. WE HAVE BEEN UNABLE TO CORROBORATE THE

SUBSTANCE OF THE REPORT. THE GOZ\'S INITIAL DENIALS

RELIED EXCLUSIVELY ON COMMENTS BY SOUTH AFRICAN

OFFICIALS DISTANCING THEMSELVES FROM THE STORY, FOLLOWED

ONLY A SECOND DAY BY THE ZIMBABWEAN DEFENSE FORCE

COMMANDER AWKWARDLY DISMISSING THE REPORT. THE

"GUARDIAN\'S" HARARE STRINGER INFORMED US THAT THE OFF-

THE-RECORD SOURCE FOR THE STORY WAS PRESIDENT THABO

MBEKI. THE VEHEMENCE OF THE ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT\'S

REACTION REFLECTS THE INCREASING PARANOIA WITHIN THE

RULING PARTY FOLLOWING THE RECENT DEATHS OF TWO

MINISTERS IN CAR ACCIDENTS -- HISTORICALLY A FAVORITE

ZANU-PF METHOD OF ASSASSINATION -- AND GROWING CONCERNS

ABOUT THE LOYALTY OF THE MILITARY RANK-AND-FILE. END

SUMMARY.

2. (U) AN ARTICLE WRITTEN BY HUGO YOUNG IN THE MAY 29

EDITION OF THE "GUARDIAN" NEWSPAPER REPORTING THAT

SENIOR ZIMBABWE MILITARY OFFICERS WILL LAUNCH A COUP

AGAINST PRESIDENT MUGABE HAS STRUCK A RAW NERVE IN

HARARE. THE ARTICLE ALLEGES THAT ZIMBABWEAN MILITARY

COMMANDERS INFORMED THE SOUTH AFRICAN GOVERNMENT THAT

THEY EXPECT A FOOD CRISIS LATER THIS YEAR WILL PROVE A

FLASH POINT THAT COULD RESULT IN FOOD RIOTS. THE ARMY

WOULD BE FORCED TO INTERVENE IN SUPPORT OF THE POLICE,

BUT WOULD NOT USE FORCE AGAINST ORDINARY ZIMBABWEANS,

THE ARTICLE CONTINUES. IF THE MUGABE GOVERNMENT ORDERS

THE MILITARY TO SHOOT, IT WOULD INSTEAD OPT TO TAKE

POWER, THE "GUARDIAN" STORY ALLEGES, AND SOUTH AFRICAN

OFFICIALS BELIEVE THAT SENIOR ZIMBABWEAN MILITARY

OFFICERS ALREADY HAVE MADE PLANS TO DO SO. THE BRITISH

NEWSPAPER CONTINUED THAT, ACCORDING TO SOUTH AFRICAN

INTELLIGENCE, THE COUP LEADER WOULD BE THE COMMANDER OF

THE AIR FORCE, AIR MARSHAL PERENCE SHIRI.

3. (C) HARARE STRINGER FOR THE "GUARDIAN" NEWSPAPER,

ANDREW MELDRUM, TOLD CHARGE ON MAY 30 THAT YOUNG HAD

NONE OTHER THAN THABO MBEKI AS HIS OFF-THE-RECORD

SOURCE. DIANE CORNER, THE BRITISH DEPUTY HIGH

COMMISSIONER IN HARARE, DESCRIBED YOUNG AS A LEADING

POLITICAL COMMENTATOR AND, INDEED, ONE OF THE MOST

INFLUENTIAL JOURNALISTS IN BRITAIN. SHE CONTINUED THAT

WHILE FOREIGN AFFAIRS IS NOT YOUNG\'S USUAL BEAT, THE

"GUARDIAN" FELT IT HAD TO SEND ONE OF ITS TOP PEOPLE TO

SECURE AN INTERVIEW WITH MBEKI PRIOR TO THE SOUTH

AFRICAN PRESIDENT\'S VISIT TO LONDON IN JUNE. WE HAVE

CHECKED OUT THE STORY WITH CONTACTS IN THE ZIMBABWEAN

MILITARY, INTELLIGENCE SERVICES AND IN THE SOUTH AFRICAN

HIGH COMMISSION, BUT HAVE BEEN UNABLE TO CORROBORATE THE

SUBSTANCE OF THE REPORTS. MOREOVER, THE MAY 30 EDITION

SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE COUP PLOT REPORT TOUCHES A RAW NERVE

REACTION TO THE "GUARDIAN" STORY (MAY 30) RELIED SOLELY

UPON SOUTH AFRICAN DENIALS OF THE ALLEGED COUP PLOT.

THE "HERALD" REPORTED NO REACTION FROM THE ZIMBABWEAN

MILITARY UNTIL THE MAY 31 EDITION.

5. (C) COMMENT: THE "GUARDIAN" STORY APPEARED AT A TIME

WHEN SENIOR MEMBERS OF THE ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT ARE

FEELING MORE PARANOID THAN EVER. IN THE SPACE OF ONE

MONTH, TWO CABINET MINISTERS HAVE.PERISHED IN CAR

ACCIDENTS, A POLITICAL ASSASSINATION METHOD THAT HAS

BEEN USED BY ZANU-PF IN THE PAST. MANY ZIMBABWEANS WITH

WHOM WE SPEAK ASSUME THAT THE TWO MINISTERS WERE VICTIMS

OF A HIT. THE MOST COMMON SPECULATION IS THAT 36 YEAR-

OLD YOUTH DEVELOPMENT, GENDER, AND EMPLOYMENT CREATION

MINISTER BORDER GEZI WAS KILLED BY ORDER OF THE RULING

PARTY\'S OLD GUARD, WHO BELIEVED HE HAD ACQUIRED TOO MUCH

INFLUENCE TOO QUICKLY. IN RETURN, ACCORDING TO THIS

LINE OF SPECULATION, GEZI\'S SUPPORTERS DECIDED TO SEND A

MESSAGE BY PLANNING THE MURDER OF MAHACHI, A LEADING

MEMBER OF THE OLD GUARD. WE HAVE NO EVIDENCE, HOWEVER,

THAT WOULD INDICATE THAT THE DEATHS OF THE TWO MINISTERS

WERE ANYTHING OTHER THAN TRAGIC ROAD ACCIDENTS.

6. (C) COMMENT CONTINUED: THE GOVERNMENT\'S RABID

RESPONSE TWO DAYS RUNNING TO THE COUP STORY REVEALS ITS

VULNERABILITY AND PERCEPTION THAT IT IS UNDER SIEGE.

THE VEHEMENT DENIALS MIGHT ALSO REFLECT A CONCERN ABOUT

THE LOYALTY OF THE MILITARY\'S RANK-AND-FILE. IN

ADDITION, THE MILITARY IS SOMETHING OF AN OFF-LIMITS

TOPIC FOR THE PRESS HERE. IN JANUARY 1999, AFTER THE

INDEPENDENT "THE STANDARD" NEWSPAPER RAN A STORY ABOUT A

COUP PLOT, THE ARMY ABDUCTED THE NEWSPAPER\'S EDITOR AND

THE STORY\'S WRITER, HOLDING THEM INCOMMUNICADO FOR A

WEEK DURING WHICH TIME SOLDIERS TORTURED THEM. IN MARCH

1999 POLICE ARRESTED THE PUBLISHER AND REPORTER OF "THE

MIRROR," AN OSTENSIBLY INDEPENDENT NEWSPAPER, FOR A

STORY IT RAN ON A HEADLESS BODY OF SOLDIER HAVING BEEN

RETURNED FROM THE CONGO. THE BACKLASH FROM THE HARARE

DIPLOMATIC CORPS AND WORLD PRESS FRATERNITY MADE THE

GOVERNMENT HERE LOOK RIDICULOUS AND TAUGHT IT TO TREAD

LIGHTLY WITH THE SCRIBES. RATHER THAN REMAIN IN

SECLUSION AND MOURNING FOR HIS DEAD COMRADE IN ARMS, THE

LATE DEFENSE MINISTER MOVEN MAHACHI, PRESIDENT MUGABE

BARELY BURIED THE MAN BEFORE JETTING OFF TO JAKARTA FOR

THE.G-15 MEETING, AS IF TO SHOW HIS CONTEMPT FOR THE

"GUARDIAN\'S" ACCOUNT. WE ALSO TAKE MUGABE\'S DEPARTURE

FOR JAKARTA AS A POSITIVE SIGN: THE PRESIDENT\'S

CONTINUED ENGAGEMENT WITH THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY IS

BETTER NEWS FOR ZIMBABWE THAN HIS WITHDRAWAL INTO A

FRIGHTENED AND PARANOID COCOON HERE IN HARARE. END

COMMENT.
