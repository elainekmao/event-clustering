SUBJECT: UNDP HOLDS FOLLOW-UP MEETING WITH THE DONOR

COMMUNITY ON THE LAND REFORM PROCESS IN ZIMBABWE.

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD FOR REASONS 1.5 (B)

AND (D).

1. (C) SUMMARY: THE UN RESIDENT COORDINATOR IN HARARE,

VICTOR ANGELO, HOSTED A FOLLOW-UP MEETING OF THE LOCAL

DONOR COMMUNITY ON NOVEMBER 9. THE PURPOSE WAS TO ALLOW

THE DONOR COMMUNITY TO RESPOND TO THE DRAFT UNDP MISSION

REPORT ON THE LAND REFORM PROCESS IN ZIMBABWE, AS WELL

AS TO DISCUSS HOW THE UNDP AND DONOR COMMUNITY COULD

MOVE THE DIALOGUE WITH THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE (GOZ)

AND OTHER STAKEHOLDERS ON THE LAND REFORM PROCESS IN A

POSITIVE DIRECTION WITHOUT REWARDING LAWLESSNESS. THE

RESIDENT COORDINATOR ACKNOWLEDGED THAT MUCH OF THE

CRITICISM OF THE MISSION REPORT REGARDING HISTORICAL

INACCURACY AND A BIAS AGAINST THE ROLE OF THE DONOR

COMMUNITY IN THE LAND REFORM PROCESS IN ZIMBABWE WAS AN

ACCURATE CRITIQUE OF THE REPORT. A MAJORITY OF THE

DONOR REPRESENTATIVES AGAIN EMPHASIZED THAT THEIR

GOVERNMENTS WILL NOT SUPPORT THE LAND REFORM PROCESS IN

ZIMBABWE UNTIL THE GOZ RESPECTS THE RULE OF LAW AND ENDS

THE ILLEGAL OCCUPATION OF COMMERCIAL FARMS. THE DANISH

AMBASSADOR TOLD THE GROUP THAT THE DANISH MINISTER FOR

DEVELOPMENT COOPERATION HAD MADE CLEAR TO PRESIDENT

MUGABE THAT THERE WAS NO DONOR CONSPIRACY AGAINST THE

GOZ, BUT THAT A RETURN TO THE RULE OF LAW AND A

TRANSPARENT LAND REFORM PROCESS WERE PREREQUISITES FOR

DANISH ASSISTANCE TO THE GOZ. AMBASSADOR MCDONALD

PROPOSED THAT A POSSIBLE WAY TO MOVE FORWARD ON LAND

REFORM WOULD BE FOR THE DONOR COMMUNITY TO SEND AN

INFORMAL DELEGATION TO VISIT THE PROVINCE OF MIDLANDS

WHERE THE GOVERNOR AND COMMERCIAL FARMERS ARE OPEN TO

DIALOGUE AND LOCAL SOLUTIONS THAT MIGHT MORE CLOSELY

FOLLOW THE 1998 DONORS\' CONFERENCE GUIDELINES ARE BEING

CONSIDERED. WHILE SOME RESERVATIONS WERE EXPRESSED, THE

DONORS AS A WHOLE AGREED THAT THIS WAS A WORTHY

INITIATIVE TO FOLLOW. END SUMMARY.

----------------------------------

UNDP HOSTS DONOR COMMUNITY MEETING

----------------------------------

2. (C) THE UN RESIDENT COORDINATOR VICTOR ANGELO HOSTED

A DONOR COMMUNITY MEETING ON NOVEMBER 9 FOR THE

AMBASSADORS OF 22 DIPLOMATIC MISSIONS, THE DIRECTOR OF

USAID, THE COUNSELOR OF THE UNITED KINGDOM\'S DEPARTMENT

FOR INTERNATIONAL DEVELOPMENT (DFID), THE EUROPEAN UNION

REPRESENTED BY THEIR LEAD TECHNICAL OFFICER, AND THE

WORLD BANK DEPUTY RESREP. THE PURPOSE OF THE MEETING

WAS FOR RESREP ANGELO TO RECEIVE DONORS\' REACTIONS TO

THE DRAFT UNDP MISSION REPORT ON THE LAND REFORM PROCESS

IN ZIMBABWE, AS WELL AS TO DISCUSS HOW THE UNDP AND

DONOR COMMUNITY COULD MOVE DISCUSSIONS WITH THE GOZ OVER

THE LAND REFORM PROCESS IN A POSITIVE DIRECTION. THE

RESIDENT COORDINATOR ALSO INFORMED THE DONORS THAT HE

HAD MET WITH HIGH LEVEL OFFICIALS OF THE GOZ REGARDING

THE UNDP DRAFT REPORT AND HE HAD EMPHASIZED TO THEM THAT

FOR THE UNDP TO SUPPORT THE GOZ LAND REFORM PROGRAM, IT

WOULD HAVE TO BE MADE LEGAL, CREDIBLE, AND

IMPLEMENTABLE. WHEN ASKED BY THE ASSEMBLED DONORS WHAT

HE MEANT BY THESE WORDS, THE RESIDENT COORDINATOR

ANSWERED THAT "LEGAL" MEANT LEGAL, "CREDIBLE" MEANT THAT

IT HAS TO BE BASED ON A WIDE NATIONAL CONSENSUS, AND

"IMPLEMENTABLE" MEANT THAT WHAT IS OCCURRING NOW IS NOT

IMPLEMENTABLE.

DONOR CRITICISM OF THE UNDP DRAFT REPORT

----------------------------------------

3. (C) A NUMBER OF ATTENDEES SPOKE STRONGLY ABOUT THE

HISTORICAL INACCURACY OF THE DRAFT REPORT REGARDING THE

ROLE OF THE DONOR COMMUNITY ON LAND REFORM IN ZIMBABWE,

AS WELL AS THE APPARENT BIAS IN THE REPORT AGAINST THE

DONOR COMMUNITY AS A POSITIVE FORCE IN THE LAND REFORM

PROCESS IN ZIMBABWE. THE COUNSELOR FOR DFID STRESSED

THAT THE REPORT MADE NO ACKNOWLEDGEMENT OF ALL THE

SUPPORT THE UNITED KINGDOM HAS PROVIDED TO LAND REFORM

AND RESETTLEMENT SINCE ZIMBABWE\'S INDEPENDENCE. THE

WORLD BANK DEPUTY RESREP SAID WHAT WAS VERY DISTURBING

ABOUT THE REPORT WAS THAT IT LACKED THE PERSPECTIVE OF

STAKEHOLDERS, AND THAT IT PORTRAYED THE GOZ AND THE

DONOR COMMUNITY AS BEING IN OPPOSITION TO ONE ANOTHER

DONOR PREREQUISITES FOR MOVING FORWARD ON LAND REFORM

--------------------------------------------- ---------

4. (C) RESIDENT COORDINATOR ANGELO QUERIED THE ASSEMBLED

GATHERING IF ANYONE HAD CONSTRUCTIVE IDEAS ON HOW TO

MOVE THE DISCUSSION ON LAND REFORM WITH THE GOZ IN A

POSITIVE DIRECTION. A NUMBER OF AMBASSADORS AND HIGH

COMMISSIONERS SPOKE ON THE REQUIREMENT THAT THE GOZ

RETURN TO THE RULE OF LAW AND A TRANSPARENT AND

EQUITABLE LAND REFORM PROCESS THAT ADHERES TO THE TENETS

OF THE 1998 DONORS\' CONFERENCE AGREEMENT. THE GERMAN

AMBASSADOR AND THE AUSTRALIAN HIGH COMMISSIONER BOTH

SPOKE FORCEFULLY ON THE IMPORTANCE OF THESE

PREREQUISITES, BEFORE THEIR RESPECTIVE GOVERNMENTS COULD

AGAIN PROVIDE SUPPORT TO THE LAND REFORM PROCESS IN

ZIMBABWE.

5. (C) THE DANISH AMBASSADOR TOLD THE GROUP THAT AT THE

NOVEMBER 7 MEETING BETWEEN THE DANISH MINISTER FOR

DEVELOPMENT COOPERATION, JAN TROJBORG, AND PRESIDENT

MUGABE, THE MINISTER HAD TRIED TO MAKE CLEAR TO THE

PRESIDENT THAT THERE WAS NO DONOR CONSPIRACY AGAINST THE

GOZ AND LAND REFORM IN ZIMBABWE, AND THE DANISH

GOVERNMENT WAS WILLING TO ASSIST ZIMBABWE ON LAND

REFORM. HOWEVER, HE ALSO STRESSED THAT THE RETURN TO

THE RULE OF LAW ON OCCUPIED FARMS AND A TRANSPARENT LAND

REFORM PROCESS BASED ON THE 1998 DONORS\' CONFERENCE WERE

PREREQUISITES FOR DANISH ASSISTANCE.

AMBASSADOR MCDONALD PROPOSES A WAY FORWARD

------------------------------------------

6. (C) AMBASSADOR MCDONALD THEN PROPOSED THAT A WAY TO

BREAK THE CURRENT IMPASSE AND MOVE THE DISCUSSIONS ON

LAND REFORM FORWARD WOULD BE TO VISIT SITES IN MIDLANDS

PROVINCE WHERE THE AMBASSADOR IN AN EARLIER TRIP AND

USAID OFFICIALS IN JUST THE LAST FEW WEEKS HAD VISITED

WITH GOVERNOR CEPHAS MSIPA AND COMMERCIAL FARMERS WHO

HAD BEEN WORKING TOGETHER CONSTRUCTIVELY ON LAND REFORM

IDEAS. THE GENERAL ASSESSMENT OF THE USAID OFFICIALS

WHO VISITED THIS PROVINCE IS THAT THERE IS A REASONABLE

SET OF ACTORS OF GOODWILL, INCLUDING THE GOVERNOR, WHO

MIGHT DEVELOP THEIR OWN COMMUNITY INITIATIVES. THE

AMBASSADOR SUGGESTED THAT AN INFORMAL DELEGATION FROM

DONOR EMBASSIES AND THE UNDP VISIT THESE SITES IN

MIDLANDS AND SEE FIRSTHAND WHETHER THERE ARE

COMPLEMENTARY NON-GOVERNMENTAL APPROACHES TO LAND REFORM

IN THIS PROVINCE THAT THE DONOR COMMUNITY COULD CONSIDER

SUPPORTING. THE AMBASSADOR MADE CLEAR THAT THIS WOULD

BE A LOOK SEE MISSION WITH NO COMMITMENTS BEING MADE AND

THAT THE UNDERTAKING WOULD BE UNDP LED. WHILE THERE

WERE SOME RESERVATIONS RAISED BY MEMBERS OF THE

ASSEMBLED DONOR COMMUNITY CONCERNING THE GOZ\'S

WILLINGNESS TO ALLOW SUCH APPROACHES, A CONSENSUS WAS

REACHED TO SEND A SMALL DELEGATION TO MIDLANDS PROVINCE

AND REPORT BACK TO THE UNDP AND THE DONOR COMMUNITY AT A

FUTURE MEETING.

7. (C) COMMENT: IT WAS CLEAR FROM THIS MEETING THAT

THERE REMAINS A GREAT DEAL OF SKEPTICISM AND CONCERN

WITHIN THE DONOR COMMUNITY REGARDING THE FINAL VERSION

OF THE UNDP MISSION REPORT ON THE LAND REFORM PROCESS IN

ZIMBABWE. THERE ALSO REMAINS A STRONG CONSENSUS AMONG

THE DONORS, THAT UNTIL THE GOZ UPHOLDS THE RULE OF LAW

AND SHOWS READINESS TO RETURN TO THE PRINCIPLES OF THE

1998 DONOR CONFERENCE, THERE IS LITTLE OR NO ROOM TO

MANEUVER ON THIS ISSUE. JUDGING BY THE DONOR

REPRESENTATIVES RESPONSES, WE ARE ENCOURAGED THAT

AMBASSADOR MCDONALD\'S PROPOSAL MAY OFFER A WAY FOR THE

UNDP TO TAKE THE LEAD ON FINDING A MECHANISM FOR

REENGAGING PRESIDENT MUGABE AND THE GOZ ON THE LAND

REFORM ISSUE. END COMMENT.
