1. SUMMARY: THE FRIENDS OF THE CHAIR ON UN FOOD AND

AGRICULTURE ORGANIZATION (FAO) DIRECTOR GENERAL TERM LIMITS

HELD ITS FIFTH AND FINAL SESSION MARCH 29. THE GROUP AGREED

AD REFERENDUM TO A PROPOSAL TO LIMIT TO TWO THE NUMBER OF

TERMS THAT A DIRECTOR GENERAL COULD SERVE, WITH THE FIRST

TERM BEING SIX YEARS, AND ANY SECOND TERM BEING FOUR YEARS.

THE GROUP AGREED THAT THE PROPOSAL WOULD BE SILENT

CONCERNING THE INCUMBENT. ONCE APPROVED BY THE JUNE SESSION

OF THE FAO COUNCIL, THE PROPOSAL WILL GO FORWARD TO THE

NOVEMBER 2003 FAO CONFERENCE FOR FINAL ADOPTION. AS THE

PROPOSAL IS NOT RETROACTIVE, THERE IS NO LEGAL BAR TO THE

CURRENT DIRECTOR GENERAL RUNNING AGAIN. HOWEVER, THE FIVE

SESSIONS OF THE FRIENDS OF THE CHAIR PROVIDED AMPLE

OPPORTUNITY FOR THE MAJOR DONORS TO MAKE CLEAR THAT, WERE HE

TO DO SO, HE WOULD NOT ENJOY THE SUPPORT OF THOSE COUNTRIES

WHICH FUND THE LION\'S SHARE OF THE FAO\'S BUDGET. IT ALSO

SENT A CLEAR SIGNAL TO OTHER REGIONS WITH AN INTEREST IN THE

POSITION THAT NEW LEADERSHIP IS AVIDLY SOUGHT. END SUMMARY.

2. THE FOURTH AND FIFTH SESSIONS OF THE FRIENDS OF THE

CHAIR ON FAO DIRECTOR GENERAL (DG) TERM LIMITS MET MARCH 17

AND MARCH 29. THE INDEPENDENT CHAIR OF FAO COUNCIL,

AMBASSADOR MEKOUAR (MOROCCO), CHAIRED THE FOURTH SESSION BY

VIDEO-CONFERENCE, AND THE FIFTH AND FINAL SESSION IN PERSON.

3. THE FOURTH SESSION WAS DEVOTED TO INCONCLUSIVE

DISCUSSION OF LENGTH OF TERM AND THE QUESTION OF TRANSITION

MEASURES. THE G-77 RALLIED AROUND A SIX-YEAR TERM AND THE

OECD MEMBERS REITERATED SUPPORT FOR A FOUR-YEAR TERM. ON

TRANSITION MEASURES, THE U.S., NEW ZEALAND AND AUSTRALIA

ARGUED FOR A SPECIFIC PROHIBITION ON THE INCUMBENT RUNNING

AGAIN. THE EU PUT FORWARD ITS PROPOSAL THAT THE DG BE

ALLOWED TO RUN FOR ONE MORE TERM ONLY. THE AFRICAN GROUP

RESPONDED INDIGNANTLY THAT THEY WOULD NOT "NEGOTIATE THE

RIGHTS OF THE DG" TO RUN AGAIN.

4. BRAZIL STATED SPECIFICALLY THAT THERE SHOULD BE NO

REFERENCE TO THE INCUMBENT IN THE PROPOSAL. THE FAO LEGAL

ADVISER RECONFIRMED THAT SILENCE ON WHAT HAD BEEN CALLED

"TRANSITION" MEASURES WOULD BE UNDERSTOOD TO MEAN THAT THE

CURRENT DIRECTOR GENERAL WOULD NOT BE AFFECTED RETROACTIVELY

BY THE AMENDMENT TO THE BASIC TEXTS, AND THEREFORE COULD RUN

AGAIN AND BE TREATED ON THE SAME BASIS AS OTHER CANDIDATES.

AMBASSADOR MEKOUAR SUMMARIZED THAT IF THE CHANGE TO THE

BASIC TEXTS WAS "NOT DIRECTED AT THE CURRENT INCUMBENT" THEN

PERHAPS THE CURRENT INCUMBENT SHOULD NOT BE MENTIONED AT

ALL.

FIFTH SESSION

-------------

5. THE FIFTH AND FINAL SESSION OF THE FRIENDS OF THE CHAIR

MET SATURDAY, MARCH 27. ALL MEMBERS AGREED THAT THERE

SHOULD BE A TWO TERM MAXIMUM. ON LENGTH OF TERM, MEMBERS

RESTATED POSITIONS: G-77: 6 PLUS 6, OECD: 4 PLUS 4. THE

U.S., CANADA AND THE REPUBLIC OF KOREA STATED THAT THEY

COULD ALSO CONSIDER A 10 YEAR MAXIMUM. THE AFRICAN GROUP

LEADER STATED CATEGORICALLY THAT SHE "WOULD NOT TAKE BACK

THE FOUR YEAR OPTION" TO HER GROUP. BRAZIL STRONGLY URGED

6. LEAVING THE LENGTH OF TERM ASIDE, AMBASSADOR MEKOUAR

TURNED THE GROUP TO DISCUSSION OF "TRANSITION MEASURES."

THE EU RESTATED ITS POSITION OF ALLOWING THE INCUMBENT TO

RUN FOR ONE TERM ONLY, BUT ADDED THAT IT COULD ALSO SUPPORT

SILENCE ON TRANSITION MEASURES. OTHER OECD MEMBERS STATED

THAT THEY COULD ACCEPT SILENCE AND THE G-77 AGREED AS WELL,

NOTING THAT THE IMPORTANT ISSUE WAS NOT TO "DISCRIMINATE

AMONG CANDIDATES IN ANY WAY."

7. TURNING BACK TO LENGTH OF TERM, AMBASSADOR MEKOUAR ASKED

IF MEMBERS COULD SUPPORT TWO TERMS, THE FIRST BEING SIX

YEARS, AND THE SECOND FOUR. THE GROUP AGREED TO THE

PROPOSAL, WITH THE EXCEPTION OF AUSTRALIA, WHO NOTED THAT

ON TO COUNCIL

-------------

8. THE REPORT OF THE FRIENDS WILL BE FORWARDED TO THE 124TH

SESSION OF COUNCIL (JUNE 24-28). IF APPROVED IT WILL BE

SENT TO THE FAO COMMITTEE ON CONSTITUTIONAL AND LEGAL

MATTERS FOR A LEGAL SCRUB AND THEN ON TO THE NOVEMBER 2003

CONFERENCE. ONCE ADOPTED BY CONFERENCE, IT WILL BE "APPLIED

FROM THE THIRTY-THIRD SESSION OF THE CONFERENCE IN 2005"

(PER THE 123RD COUNCIL DECISION). THE ELECTIONS FOR

DIRECTOR GENERAL WILL BE AT THE CONFERENCE SESSION IN

NOVEMBER 2005.

COMMENT

-------

9. THE OUTCOME DOES NOT BAR THE CURRENT DG FROM RUNNING

AGAIN, OR LIMIT ANY RUN TO JUST ONE TERM (AS THE EU HAD

INITIALLY HOPED). ON THE OTHER HAND, WE SUCCESSFULLY

AVOIDED ANY ENDORSEMENT, IMPLICIT OR OTHERWISE, OF THE DG\'S

CANDIDACY SHOULD HE RUN AGAIN. IN ADDITION, THE FIVE

SESSIONS OF THE FRIENDS OF THE CHAIR PROVIDED AMPLE

OPPORTUNITY FOR THE MAJOR DONORS TO MAKE CLEAR THAT, WERE

THE DIRECTOR GENERAL RUN AGAIN, HE WOULD NOT ENJOY THE

SUPPORT OF THOSE COUNTRIES WHICH FUND THE LION\'S SHARE OF

THE FAO\'S BUDGET. IT ALSO SENT A CLEAR SIGNAL TO OTHER

REGIONS WITH AN INTEREST IN THE POSITION THAT NEW LEADERSHIP

IS AVIDLY SOUGHT. HALL
