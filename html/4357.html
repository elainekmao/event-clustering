SUBJECT: ARAB CIVITAS NETWORK ANNOUNCED IN AMMAN

1. SUMMARY: 25 REPRESENTATIVES FROM 8 ARAB COUNTRIES

GATHERED AT JORDAN\'S DEAD SEA MOVENPICK, NOVEMBER 1-4, 2002

TO ANNOUNCE THE ESTABLISHMENT OF A REGIONAL ARAB CIVITAS

NETWORK TO BE BASED IN AMMAN. THE WORKSHOP, MADE POSSIBLE

THROUGH AN AID WASHINGTON GRANT TO THE CENTER FOR CIVIC

EDUCATION AND WITH PA AMMAN\'S TECHNICAL AND ADMINISTRATIVE

SUPPORT, SIGNALED THE PARTICIPANTS\' BELIEF IN THE IMPORTANCE

OF DEMOCRATIC VALUES IN BUILDING SOCIETIES AND THE NEED FOR

CREATING RESPONSIBLE CITIZENS EDUCATED IN THE FIELDS OF

HUMAN RIGHTS, CIVIC EDUCATION AND CIVIL SOCIETY. WITH

FURTHER FUNDING FROM USAID, ARAB CIVITAS AIMS TO LEAD CIVIC

EDUCATION INITIATIVES IN THE ARAB WORLD. QUEEN RANIA

EXTENDED HER PATRONAGE TO THE CONFERENCE AND WAS REPRESENTED

BY THE MINISTER OF EDUCATION, DR. KHALED TOUQAN. AMBASSADOR

GNEHM GAVE WELCOMING REMARKS. END SUMMARY.

2. OVER A PERIOD OF THREE DAYS, NOVEMBER 1-4, 2002,

REPRESENTATIVES DRAWN FROM GOVERNMENTAL AND NON-GOVERNMENTAL

ORGANIZATIONS IN JORDAN, EGYPT, MOROCCO, TUNISIA, BAHRAIN,

YEMEN, LEBANON, THE WEST BANK AND EGYPT (GOVERNMENT AND

NGO\'S) WORKING WITH 6 AMERICAN EXPERTS FROM THE CENTER FOR

CIVIC EDUCATION, DELIBERATED ON THE OBJECTIVES AND CRITERIA

FOR AN ARAB CIVITAS NETWORK. DISCUSSIONS CENTERED ON FUTURE

PROJECTS, TECHNICAL AND ADMINISTRATIVE SUPPORT AND LINKAGES

WITH EXISTING CIVIC EDUCATION ENTITIES IN EACH COUNTRY.

PARTICIPANTS CONCLUDED THAT ARAB CIVITAS WILL BE BASED IN

AMMAN AND WILL HAVE A BOARD OF TRUSTEES AND AN

ADMINISTRATIVE BOARD. ITS MEMBERSHIP WILL BE OPEN TO ALL

ARAB COUNTRIES. MS. MUNA DARWISH SHAMI OF JORDAN AND DR.

KAMAL MUGEETH OF EGYPT WERE ASSIGNED WITH THE RESPONSIBILITY

OF PUTTING TOGETHER THE FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS OF THE

WORKSHOP AND DRAFTING THE FINAL BY-LAWS AND OBJECTIVES OF

ARAB CIVITAS IN A PERIOD NOT TO EXCEED 30 DAYS.

3. PA SEES THE ESTABLISHMENT OF AN ARAB CIVITAS AS A CONDUIT

TO INCREASED PROGRAMMING IN THE FIELDS OF CIVIC EDUCATION

AND CIVIL SOCIETY. THE CENTER FOR CIVIC EDUCATION, WHICH

PLAYED AN INSTRUMENTAL ROLE IN STARTING THIS PROJECT, HAS

EXPRESSED KEEN INTEREST IN SUPPORTING ARAB CIVITAS IN THE

FUTURE AND PLANS TO SUBMIT A FIVE YEAR FUNDING PROPOSAL TO

USAID WASHINGTON FOR THIS PURPOSE. PA AND EMBASSY HIGHLY

SUPPORT THIS REQUEST AND UNDERSCORE THE IMPORTANCE AND

TIMELINESS OF THIS INITIATIVE IN PROMOTING U.S. GOVERNMENT

INITIATIVES ON CIVIL SOCIETY IN THE ARAB WORLD.

4. IN A RELATED DEVELOPMENT, REPRESENTATIVES OF CHILDREN\'S

RESOURCE INTERNATIONAL, A NON-PROFIT ORGANIZATION

ESTABLISHED TO PROMOTE QUALITY EDUCATIONAL PRACTICES IN THE

U.S AND ABROAD, PRESENTED A PROPOSAL TO IMPLEMENT A NEW

ACTIVE TEACHING METHOD SUPPORTING FAMILY AND COMMUNITY

INVOLVEMENT IN PRESCHOOL (KG) AND LATER IN PRIMARY GRADES.

JORDAN IS JUST CONSIDERING INTRODUCING KG AS PART OF ITS

"MANDATORY" EDUCIONAL SYSTEM AND AS OF NEXT YEAR IT IS HOPED

THAT IT WILL BE APPLIED IN ALL PUBLIC SCHOOLS. OTHER

COUNTRIES IN THE REGION ARE LOOKING IN THIS DIRECTION AS

WELL.

5. PA BELIEVES THAT THIS INITIATIVE COULD BE IMPLEMENTED AS

PART OF THE ARAB CIVITAS NETWORK ACTIVITIES THROUGH SPECIAL

FUNDING. PA WILL REVIEW MATERIALS REPRESENTED BY CHILDREN\'S

RESOURCES INTERNATIONAL AND WILL CONFER WITH JORDANIAN

MINISTER OF EDUCATION DR. KHALED TOUQAN, WHO HAS GIVEN HIS

SUPPORT TO ARAB CIVITAS BY ATTENDING THE OPENING SESSION

WITH THE AMBASSADOR, GIVING A PRESENTATION AND BY ATTENDING

MOST OF THE DELIBERATIONS ON THE FIRST DAY. DR. TOUQAN IS

KEEN TO PROMOTE EDUCATION REFORM EFFORTS INCLUDING THIS

INITIATIVE.

6. PA WILL CONTINUE TO PLAY AN IMPORTANT ROLE IN SUPPORTING

AND PROMOTING CIVIC EDUCATION IN JORDAN THROUGH ARAB CIVITAS

AND OTHER PROJECTS THAT WILL EMERGE AS A RESULT OF THIS

MILESTONE ACCOMPLISHMENT. GNEHM
