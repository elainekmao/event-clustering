SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) HOLY SEE FOREIGN MINISTER TAURAN, CARDINAL STAFFORD,

THE AMERICAN HEADS OF THE COUNCIL FOR LAITY, AND ITALIAN

CARDINAL RUINI, WHO IS THE VICAR OF ROME AND HEAD OF THE

ITALIAN BISHOPS CONFERENCE, TOLD UNDER SECRETARY BOLTON APRIL

9 THAT THEY ARE LOOKING TO IRAQ\'S FUTURE AND ARE FOCUSED ON

HUMANITARIAN RELIEF AND A BETTER FUTURE FOR THE IRAQI PEOPLE.

THEY CONVEYED THEIR "RESPECT" FOR THE DIFFICULT DECISION THE

PRESIDENT HAD MADE, AND THEIR APPRECIATION FOR U.S. EFFORTS

TO AVOID CIVILIAN CASUALTIES. TAURAN OFFERED THE ACTIVE

COOPERATION OF THE CATHOLIC CHURCH IN IRAQ TO FACILITATE FOOD

AND MEDICINE DISTRIBUTION AND PREVENT LOCAL MAFIAS FROM

PROFITING ON SUCH DISTRIBUTION, AND BOLTON ACCEPTED HIS

OFFER. TAURAN AND OTHERS URGED THAT THE UN BE GIVEN AN

IMPORTANT ROLE IN IRAQI RECONSTRUCTION, AND CALLED ON THE

U.S. TO INVOLVE AS MANY COUNTRIES AS POSSIBLE IN THE PROCESS

OF REBUILDING IRAQ AND IN DEALING WITH CONTINUING WMD THREATS

FROM NORTH KOREA, IRAN AND SYRIA. TAURAN WELCOMED BOLTON\'S

AFFIRMATION OF THE U.S. DESIRE TO INTENSIFY OUR EFFORTS TO

OVERCOME THE CURRENT ARAB-ISRAELI IMPASSE. RUINI EXPRESSED

HIS CONFIDENCE THAT SCENES OF JUBILANT IRAQI\'S WOULD

DRAMATICALLY SHIFT EUROPEAN OPINION ABOUT THE WAR. "IF THE

FUTURE GOES WELL," HE SAID, "THE PAST WILL LOSE ITS

IMPORTANCE." END SUMMARY.

VATICAN RESPECTS WAR DECISION, LOOKS TO FUTURE

--------------------------------------------- -

2. (C) IN MEETINGS APRIL 9 WITH HOLY SEE FOREIGN MINISTER

JEAN LOUIS TAURAN, VICAR OF ROME AND HEAD OF THE ITALIAN

CONFERENCE OF BISHOPS CARDINAL CAMILLO RUINI, AND SENIOR

AMERICAN CARDINAL JAMES FRANCIS STAFFORD, UNDER SECRETARY

BOLTON EMPHASIZED THE PRESIDENT\'S DESIRE TO CONTINUE A CLOSE

DIALOGUE WITH THE HOLY SEE ON IRAQ AS WE MOVE FORWARD TO

ADDRESS HUMANITARIAN PRIORITIES AND BUILD A PEACEFUL,

DEMOCRATIC FUTURE FOR THE IRAQI PEOPLE. TAURAN AND HIS

COLLEAGUES MADE CLEAR THAT THEY WERE NOT INTERESTED IN

CONTINUING A DEBATE ON THE MERITS OF WAR, BUT WERE LOOKING

AHEAD AT HUMANITARIAN NEEDS AND IRAQ\'S FUTURE. "THE DECISION

WAS TAKEN. THAT\'S DONE, AND WE RESPECT THE DECISION THE

PRESIDENT HAS TAKEN," TAURAN SAID. RUINI LIKEWISE RECOGNIZED

THAT THE PRESIDENT HAD WEIGHED THE DECISION CAREFULLY AND

THAT IT WAS CLEAR THE UNITED STATES WAS DOING EVERYTHING

POSSIBLE TO PREVENT CIVILIAN CASUALTIES IN THE CONDUCT OF THE

WAR.

-------------------------------------------

OFFER TO FACILITATE HUMANITARIAN ASSISTANCE

-------------------------------------------

3. (C) IN LIGHT OF THE HOLY SEE\'S PREOCCUPATION WITH THE

HUMANITARIAN SITUATION, TAURAN TOLD UNDER SECRETARY BOLTON

THAT THE CHALDEAN CATHOLIC CHURCH IN IRAQ HAD AN EFFECTIVE,

WELL-ORGANIZED NETWORK IN KEY IRAQI CITIES THAT WOULD BE ABLE

TO FACILITATE THE DISTRIBUTION OF FOODS AND MEDICINES. THE

REPORTED TO TAURAN THAT, IN THE ABSENCE OF NEUTRAL POINTS OF

DISTRIBUTION, LOCAL MAFIAS WERE SPRINGING UP, TAKING THE

DRAMATIC AND IN NEED OF SUPPORT.

THE BROADER WMD THREAT

----------------------

5. (C) WITH ALL OF HIS VATICAN INTERLOCUTORS, BOLTON REVIEWED

THE BROADER WMD THREAT, STRESSING THAT WEAPONS OF MASS

DESTRUCTION ARE NOT MILITARY WEAPONS, AND CAN ONLY BE USED TO

KILL CIVILIANS, AS SADDAM HUSSEIN HAD WITH HIS OWN PEOPLE.

ALL VATICAN INTERLOCUTORS EXPRESSED CONCERN WITH WHAT HAPPENS

NEXT, NOTING FEARS THAT THE WAR ON IRAQ COULD BE FOLLOWED

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 VATICA 01570 02 OF 03 131014Z

WITH OTHER ACTIONS AGAINST IRAN, SYRIA, OR NORTH KOREA.

BOLTON MADE CLEAR THAT IRAQ WAS NOT A MODEL FOR NORTH KOREA,

IRAN OR SYRIA, AND THAT THERE IS NO \'COOKIE CUTTER\' APPROACH

TO RESOLVING THESE ISSUES. HE POINTED OUT THAT WE WERE

ACTIVELY PURSUING DIPLOMATIC INITIATIVES IN ALL OF THESE

CASES, AND THAT WE HOPED THESE COUNTRIES WOULD DRAW THE RIGHT

LESSON FROM THE IRAQ EXAMPLE: THAT IT WAS NOT WORTH IT FOR

THEM TO CONTINUE PURSUING WMD. ALL THREE VATICAN OFFICIALS

EXPRESSED THEIR BELIEF THAT THE U.S. SHOULD SEEK TO WORK WITH

OTHER COUNTRIES IN A MULTILATERAL EFFORT TO DEAL WITH THESE

THREATS TO THE EXTENT POSSIBLE.

6. (C) TURNING TO SPECIFIC COUNTRIES, TAURAN FOCUSED FIRST

ON NORTH KOREA, ABOUT WHICH HE ACKNOWLEDGED THE HOLY SEE HAD

LIMITED UNDERSTANDING. BOLTON REVIEWED THE BACKGROUND TO THE

DISCOVERY OF THE NORTH KOREAN PROGRAM AND OUR EFFORTS TO

PERSUADE NORTH KOREA TO ABANDON ITS PROGRAM. HE EMPHASIZED

THAT WE WERE ACTIVELY TRYING TO ENGAGE CHINA AND RUSSIA TO

BECOME MORE INVOLVED, WHILE WORKING WITH JAPAN AND SOUTH

KOREA. HE POINTED OUT THE HIGH STAKES INVOLVED, GIVEN

SUPPORT IN JAPAN FOR A NUCLEAR PROGRAM TO COUNTER A NORTH

KOREAN THREAT AND THE SUBSEQUENT POTENTIAL FOR AN ASIAN ARMS

RACE BETWEEN JAPAN AND CHINA.

7. (C) ON IRAN, BOLTON OUTLINED LONG-STANDING CONCERNS THAT

IRAN WAS PURSUING A COMPLETE ENRICHMENT PROGRAM THAT WOULD

GIVE THE IRANIANS THE CAPACITY TO MAKE NUCLEAR WEAPONS. HE

REVIEWED OUR EFFORTS TO ENCOURAGE RUSSIA TO CUT OFF

ASSISTANCE, AND THE FINDINGS FROM THE RECENT VISIT OF THE

IAEA INSPECTORS, WHICH SURPRISED MANY IN THE IAEA. TAURAN

ASKED WHETHER WE FOUND RUSSIA TO BE A RELIABLE INTERLOCUTOR,

RUSSIAN LEADERSHIP, BUT THAT THERE WAS NO FOLLOW THROUGH FROM

AVOIDING A CLASH OF CIVILIZATIONS

---------------------------------

9. (C) BOTH CARDINALS SPOKE AT LENGTH ABOUT THE IMPORTANCE OF

AVOIDING PERCEPTIONS OF A CLASH OF CIVILIZATIONS BETWEEN THE

MUSLIM WORLD AND THE WEST -- A RECENT PREOCCUPATION OF THE

HOLY SEE (SEPTEL). CARDINAL STAFFORD RECOUNTED AN EXPERIENCE

CHAIRING A MEETING IN LEBANON WHERE HE WAS SHARPLY CRITICIZED

BECAUSE OF HIS AMERICAN NATIONALITY, AND SAID THE DEPTH OF

HOSTILITY TOWARD THE UNITED STATES WAS DEEPLY TROUBLING TO

HIM. WHILE RECOGNIZING THAT CRITICISM CAME WITH THE

TERRITORY OF BEING THE ONLY SUPERPOWER, HE ALSO BELIEVED THAT

THE U.S. NEEDED TO ACT WITH HUMILITY AND DO A BETTER JOB OF

DEMONSTRATING OUR CONCERN FOR THE PRIORITIES OF OTHER

COUNTRIES.

---------------------------------

OVERCOMING TRANSATLANTIC TENSIONS

---------------------------------

10. (C) CARDINAL RUINI, ONE OF THE POPE\'S CLOSEST

COLLABORATORS AND SOMEONE CONSIDERED A POTENTIAL SUCCESSOR TO

JOHN PAUL II, EXPRESSED HIS CONCERN FOR THE WEAKENING OF NATO

AND THE UN AS A RESULT OF THE IRAQ CRISIS, AND URGED THAT WE

ENSURE BOTH INSTITUTIONS ARE REPAIRED. AS PRESIDENT OF THE

ITALIAN BISHOPS CONFERENCE, RUINI IS PLUGGED INTO THE

DOMESTIC ITALIAN POLITICAL SCENE, AND CITED HIS CONCERN THAT

ITALY -- WHICH USED TO BE PRO-U.S. -- WAS LOSING THIS

ORIENTATION. HE CITED A SIMILAR WORRYING TREND IN GERMANY.

HE NOTED A DISTRUST OF FRANCE, WHICH HE NOTED HARBORED GLOBAL

PAGE 03 VATICA 01570 03 OF 03 131014Z

AMBITIONS THAT WERE ALIEN TO ITALY AND GERMANY AFTER WWII.

ALTHOUGH MOST ITALIANS REMAINED WARY OF THE FRENCH, RUINI

WORRIED THAT THE FRENCH APPROACH COULD BECOME DOMINANT IN

EUROPE UNLESS CURRENT DIVISIONS WERE REPAIRED. THE KEY TO

DOING SO, ACCORDING TO RUINI, WAS TO "MAKE CLEAR THE U.S WAS

FOR PEACE." IN THIS REGARD, HE OBSERVED THAT IMAGES OF

IRAQIS WARMLY WELCOMING AMERICAN TROOPS IN THE DAYS AHEAD

WOULD HAVE A HUGE IMPACT ON EUROPEAN PUBLIC OPINION. "IF THE

FUTURE GOES WELL," HE CONCLUDED, "THE PAST WILL LOSE ITS

IMPORTANCE." BOLTON ASSURED RUINI THAT THE WORLD WOULD SEE

THAT THE U.S. MOTIVES WERE EXACTLY WHAT WE HAD SAID THEY WERE

-- ELIMINATE THE WMD THREAT, END A BRUTAL DICTATORSHIP, AND

GIVE THE IRAQI PEOPLE A CHANCE FOR A BETTER LIFE.

COMMENT

-------

11. (C) COMING ON THE MORNING OF THE COLLAPSE OF BAGHDAD,

BOLTON\'S VISIT SERVED TO PUT AN END TO THE PRE-WAR PERIOD OF

DIVISION OVER THE NECESSITY OF RESORT TO FORCE AND TO REFOCUS

THE BILATERAL RELATIONSHIP ON THE FUTURE FOR THE IRAQI PEOPLE

AND OUR BROADER AGENDA, INCLUDING THE ISRAELI-PALESTINIAN

CONFLICT. A PRESS CONFERENCE AFTER THE MEETINGS WITH OVER

THIRTY MEMBERS OF INTERNATIONAL, CATHOLIC, AND ITALIAN MEDIA

EVOKED ALMOST UNIVERSALLY POSITIVE COVERAGE OF WARMING

RELATIONS BETWEEN THE U.S. AND VATICAN, WHILE HIGHLIGHTING

U.S. APPRECIATION FOR THE HOLY SEE\'S WILLINGNESS TO COOPERATE

IN HUMANITARIAN RELIEF EFFORTS. THE HOLY SEE IS CLEARLY

READY TO MOVE ON FROM THE PERCEPTION OF DIVISION GENERATED BY

THE RUN-UP TO WAR AND TO MOVE AHEAD WITH HUMANITARIAN RELIEF,

A REINVIGORATED MIDDLE EAST PEACE EFFORT, AND CONTINUED

EFFORTS TO BUILD BRIDGES BETWEEN THE CHRISTIAN AND MUSLIM

WORLDS. IN THIS REGARD, AN APRIL 10 PRESS RELEASE FROM THE

VATICAN CALLED THE EVENTS IN IRAQ "AN IMPORTANT DEVELOPMENT

AND A SIGNIFICANT OPPORTUNITY FOR THE FUTURE OF THE
