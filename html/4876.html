REFUGEE POPULATIONS IN UGANDA

-----------------------------

13. ALTHOUGH NOT A MAJOR FOCUS OF THIS VISIT, WFP HAS A

CASELOAD OF BOTH SUDANESE (165,930 PERSONS LIVING IN 62

SETTLEMENTS IN FIVE DISTRICTS) AND RWANDESE/CONGOLESE

(27,055 PERSONS LIVING IN FOUR SETTLEMENTS IN THREE

DISTRICTS). THE ONLY DURABLE SOLUTION TO THE REFUGEE PROBLEM

IN UGANDA IS CESSATION OF HOSTILITIES IN COUNTRIES OF ORIGIN

(SOUTHERN SUDAN, RWANDA AND DRC), REPATRIATION AND

RESETTLEMENT. THE INTERMEDIATE SOLUTION FOR REFUGEE SELF-

RELIANCE IN UGANDA RELATES TO THE QUALITY AND QUANTITY OF

LAND ALLOCATED TO REFUGEES, INCOME ACQUISITION STRATEGIES

AND OTHER COPING MECHANISMS.

14. THERE IS A DEEPENING HUMANITARIAN CRISIS IN NORTHERN

SUMMARY

-------

1. USMISSION/ROME HUMANITARIAN ATTACHE VISITED UGANDA

DECEMBER 18-23, AND TRAVELED IN GULU DISTRICT IN THE

NORTHERN REGION. SERIOUS INSECURITY THROUGHOUT THE REGION

(GULU, KITGUM, LIRA AND PADER DISTRICTS) HAS LED TO THE

DISPLACEMENT OF APPROXIMATELY 735,000 PEOPLE INTO CONGESTED

SETTLEMENTS. THESE INTERNALLY DISPLACED PERSONS (IDPS) ARE

LARGELY DEPENDENT ON HUMANITARIAN AGENCIES (PARTICULARLY WFP

AND A HANDFUL OF NGOS) FOR A MAJOR PORTION OF THEIR FOOD AND

BASIC SERVICES. THERE IS GROWING CONCERN THAT CONTINUING

INSECURITY WILL NOT PERMIT IDPS ACCESS TO THEIR HOMES AND

FIELDS TO CULTIVATE FOOD IN THE MAJOR CROPPING SEASON WHICH

BEGINS IN MARCH 2003. END SUMMARY.

UNEXPECTEDLY PROMPTED THE ENTRY OF AN ESTIMATED 2000-3000

LRA FORCES INTO UGANDA FROM SOUTHERN SUDAN, RESULTING IN

INTENSIFIED ATTACKS ON THE CIVIL POPULATIONS IN THE NORTHERN

REGION. AT PRESENT, PADER DISTRICT IS VIRTUALLY INACCESSIBLE

ECONOMIC COST TO THE NATION OF THE CONFLICT IN THE NORTH IS

4. THE THREE DISTRICTS OF GULU, KITGUM AND PADER ARE HOME TO

MOST OF THE ACHOLI PEOPLE OF UGANDA. "PROTECTED VILLAGES"

WERE CREATED BY THE GOVERNMENT OF UGANDA (GOU) AS A MEANS OF

ISOLATING THE CIVILIAN POPULATION FROM THE LRA WHILE

REDUCING THE ABILITY OF THE REBEL FORCE TO STRENGTHEN ITSELF

THROUGH LOOTING AND ABDUCTIONS AND PREVENTING THE LRA FROM

RECEIVING ACTIVE ASSISTANCE FROM THE POPULATION.

FOLLOWED A DECISION BY THE MILITARY AUTHORITIES IN 1996. IN

KITGUM/PADER, THE VILLAGES WERE MORE OFTEN ESTABLISHED AS A

RESULT OF THE FLIGHT BY RURAL RESIDENTS FOLLOWING LRA

ATTACKS IN 1995-97.
