SUBJECT: TRIAL OF ANOTHER ACTIVIST?

REF: HANOI 2718

1. (U) IN RESPONSE TO MEDIA REPORTS THAT ACTIVIST NGUYEN

KHAC TOAN WOULD GO ON TRIAL ON DECEMBER 20, EMBASSY FORMALLY

REQUESTED PERMISSION ON DECEMBER 15 FOR AN OFFICER TO ATTEND

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 HANOI 03034 200713Z

THE TRIAL. (TOAN HAD BEEN ARRESTED IN JANUARY 2002, AND IS

REPORTEDLY CHARGED WITH "CONDUCTING PROPAGANDA" AGAINST THE

SOCIALIST STATE, "SPREADING MISINFORMATION" ABOUT HE

GOVERNMENT, AND "SPYING" FOR AN UNIDENTIFIED FOREIGN

COUNTRY.) A DEPUTY CHIEF JUSTICE OF THE HANOI PEOPLE\'S

COURT INFORMED POL FSN ON DECEMBER 19, HOWEVER, THAT SUCH

ATTENDANCE BY A FOREIGNER WOULD BE "UNPRECEDENTED" AND

IMPOSSIBLE.

2. (U) AT THE HANOI COURT ON THE MORNING OF DECEMBER 20,

NUMEROUS PLAINCLOTHES AND UNIFORMED POLICE WERE PRESENT, AND

IT APPEARED THAT PEOPLE ENTERING THE COURT BUILDING WERE

REQUIRED TO SHOW PERMISSION LETTERS. PEOPLE WITH OTHER

BUSINESS WERE ASKED TO RETURN ON DECEMBER 23. EMBOFF WAS

NOT ABLE TO CONFIRM THAT TOAN\'S TRIAL WAS ONGOING OR, IF SO,

WHETHER FAMILY MEMBERS AND A LAWYER WERE PRESENT.

3. (U) COMMENT: THE UNUSUAL SECURITY PRECAUTIONS IN PLACE

MAKE IT HIGHLY POSSIBLE THAT TOAN\'S TRIAL IS UNDERWAY. IF

LE CHI QUANG\'S TRIAL (REFTEL) IS ANY INDICATOR, SENTENCING

IS APT TO BE QUICK, AND, IN THIS CASE, THE SPYING CHARGES

MAY LEAD TO AN EVEN HEAVIER SENTENCE.
