SUBJECT: CLARIFICATION OF ITALIAN POSITION ON SECURITY

COUNCIL REFORM

B. ROME 3384

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 ROME 05106 181537Z

CLASSIFIED BY: POLMINCOUNS T. COUNTRYMAN FOR REASON 1.5(D)

1. (C) POST READ WITH INTEREST REF A ON ITALY\'S PIQUE ABOUT

THE U.S. ENDORSEMENT OF A PERMANENT UN SECURITY COUNCIL SEAT

FOR GERMANY. REF A\'S COMMENT NOTED THAT THE GOI PERMREP

"REFRAINED FROM MENTIONING ITALY\'S HOPES FOR A PERMANENT

SECURITY COUNCIL SEAT." HOWEVER, ACCORDING TO THE STATEMENT

ON UNSC REFORM THAT IS POSTED ON THE MFA WEBSITE, "THE

ITALIAN PROPOSAL WOULD LEAVE THE CURRENT FIVE PERMANENT AND

TEN NON-PERMANENT SEATS UNCHANGED." AS REPORTED IN PARA 3

REF B, ITALY\'S UNSC REFORM PROPOSAL IS TO ADD EIGHT TO TEN

REGIONAL, NON-PERMANENT SEATS THAT WOULD BE SHARED BY

COUNTRIES NOT REPRESENTED IN OTHER SLOTS ACCORDING TO THEIR

U.N. CONTRIBUTIONS. IN OTHER WORDS, OUR UNDERSTANDING IS

THAT ITALY IS NOT SEEKING A PERMANENT UNSC SEAT FOR ITSELF.

2. (C) COMMENT: UNDER THE PREVIOUS, CENTER-LEFT GOVERNMENT,

SECURITY COUNCIL REFORM WAS PRACTICALLY AN OBSESSION. WHILE

PM SILVIO BERLUSCONI HAS NOT CHANGED THE GOI POSITION ON THIS

ISSUE, THE CURRENT GOVERNMENT HAS NOT RAISED IT AT A

BILATERAL LEVEL. END COMMENT.
