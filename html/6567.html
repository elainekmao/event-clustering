SUBJECT: ITALY TO OPPOSE SPECIAL UNGA/IRAQ SESSION

REF: A) STATE 70678

CLASSIFIED BY: POL MC THOMAS COUNTRYMAN FOR REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D)

1. (C) POLMIN DELIVERED REF A POINTS TO CLAUDIO BISOGNIERO,

THE DEPUTY DIRECTOR GENERAL FOR THE MFA\'S BUREAU FOR

MULTILATERAL AFFAIRS AND HUMAN RIGHTS. POLMIN ALSO NOTED

WITH APPRECIATION RECENT POSITIVE STATEMENTS BY PM BERLUSCONI

AND FM FRATTINI REAFFIRMING ITALY\'S FRIENDSHIP WITH THE

UNITED STATES AND OPENING ITALIAN BASES AND AIRSPACE TO THE

USG.

2. (C) BISOGNIERO CALLED POLMIL BACK LATE 18 MARCH. AFTER

CONSULTATIONS WITHIN THE GOI BISOGNIERO STATED HE HAD ISSUED

CLEAR INSTRUCTIONS TO THE ITALIAN UN DELEGATION TO OPPOSE ANY

MOVES TO USE UNGA OR OTHER UN FORA FOR MISCHIEF MAKING ON

IRAQ. HE NOTED THERE HAD BEEN NO INDICATIONS FROM THE

ITALIAN DELEGATION IN NEW YORK ABOUT EFFORTS TO RAISE THE

IRAQ ISSUE OUTSIDE THE UNSC.

3. (C) SEPARATELY, HUMAN RIGHTS OFFICE DIRECTOR GIUSEPPE

CALVETTA (WHO PREVIOUSLY SERVED IN GENEVA) NOTED THE

COMPOSITION OF CHR-59 WOULD MAKE IT DIFFICULT TO PREVENT A

SPECIAL SEATING/DISCUSSION IF ARAB STATES AND THE AFRICA

GROUP ALL SUPPORTED ONE. CALVETTA SUGGESTED WE ISSUE A

"DECLARATION," IN GENEVA OR WASHINGTON, REAFFIRMING OUR

COMMITMENT TO RESPECT NONCOMBATANTS\' HUMAN RIGHTS AND

DETAILING THE STEPS WE WOULD TAKE TO DO SO. WE NOTED THAT

SENIOR U.S. OFFICIALS, INCLUDING CJCS MYERS, REPEATEDLY HAVE

ADDRESSED THIS ISSUE PUBLICLY. NONETHELESS, CALVETTA

OBSERVED, THE DYNAMICS WITHIN THE CHR SUGGESTED TO HIM THAT A

PROACTIVE U.S. STATEMENT WOULD SAP MUCH OF THE MOMENTUM FROM

EFFORTS TO ORGANIZE A SPECIAL SEATING.
