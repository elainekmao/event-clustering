SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) IN A MEETING WITH THE MFA\'S ACTING OFFICE DIRECTOR

FOR UN AFFAIRS, MARIO BALDI, POLOFF CONVEYED MESSAGES ON U.S.

GOALS FOR THE 57TH UN GENERAL ASSEMBLY (UNGA) (REF D) AND ON

THE CANDIDACY OF RUTH WEDGWOOD FOR THE UN HUMAN RIGHTS

COMMITTEE (REF A). BALDI STATED THAT THE GOI SHARED USG

GOALS FOR THE SESSION, EXCEPT THAT ON THE MIDDLE EAST WE HAD

DIVERGENT MEANS FOR ACCOMPLISHING THOSE GOALS. THE GOI

ADVOCATES AN INTERNATIONAL CONFERENCE TO END THE CONFLICT IN

THE MIDDLE EAST, USING AFGHANISTAN AS A MODEL. HE EXPLAINED

THE DILEMMA THE GOI FACED AS A RESULT OF THE U.S. POSITION ON

THE INTERNATIONAL CRIMINAL COURT AND ARTICLE 98 BUT PROMISED

THAT ITALY WOULD ADVOCATE FLEXIBILITY AS THE EU DEVELOPS A

COMMON POSITION. THE GOI REQUESTED USG SUPPORT FOR ITS

FUTURE BIDS FOR THE UN COMMISSION ON HUMAN RIGHTS (UNCHR) AND

THE UN SECURITY COUNCIL. ITALY WANTS TO INCREASE HUMAN

RIGHTS PROTECTION AND ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT AROUND THE WORLD

AS A MEANS OF FIGHTING TERRORISM. END SUMMARY.

---------------------------------------

"JOIN THE INTERNATIONAL CRIMINAL COURT"

---------------------------------------

2. (C) POLOFF MET WITH MARIO BALDI, MFA ACTING OFFICE

DIRECTOR FOR UN AFFAIRS, ON AUGUST 7 TO TRANSMIT MESSAGES ON

U.S. GOALS FOR THE 57TH UN GENERAL ASSEMBLY (REF D) AND THE

CANDIDACY OF RUTH WEDGWOOD FOR THE UN HUMAN RIGHTS COMMITTEE

(REF A). BALDI GLANCED AT THE UNGA TALKING POINTS, SAID THAT

THE GOI SHARED OUR GOALS, SMILED, AND SAID, "NOW WHEN ARE YOU

GOING TO SIGN THE ROME ACCORD (FOUNDING STATUTE OF THE

INTERNATIONAL CRIMINAL COURT)?" HE CITED THREE RECENT

HIGH-LEVEL MEETINGS BETWEEN GOI AND USG OFFICIALS ON THE

SUBJECT (REFS B AND C), THE PLAN TO DEVELOP A COMMON EU

POSITION ON ARTICLE 98 AGREEMENTS IN SEPTEMBER, AND -- AS THE

BIRTHPLACE OF ITS FOUNDING STATUTE -- ITALY\'S SPECIAL

COMMITMENT TO THE ICC. ITALY WAS IN THE DIFFICULT POSITION

OF TRYING BOTH TO ACCOMMODATE THE U.S. REQUEST FOR AN ARTICLE

98 AGREEMENT AND TO PURSUE ITS OWN INTERESTS. HE ASKED FOR

CONFIRMATION, WHICH POLOFF PROVIDED, THAT ONLY ROMANIA AND

ISRAEL HAVE YET CONCLUDED SUCH AGREEMENTS. HE PROMISED THAT

ITALY WOULD WORK FOR A PRAGMATIC SOLUTION IN BRUSSELS BUT

SAID IT WAS "VERY IMPORTANT THAT THE UNITED STATES NOT IMPEDE

OTHER COUNTRIES FROM RATIFYING."

3. (C) COMMENT: IN AN AUGUST 1 CONVERSATION, A/POLCOUNS HAD

ASSURED BALDI THAT WE SOUGHT ONLY TO PROTECT OUR INTERESTS

WITHIN THE PARAMETERS ALLOWED BY THE ROME TREATY AND NOT TO

CONVINCE OTHERS NOT TO SIGN. WHILE BALDI\'S COMMENTS

REGARDING THE ICC REFLECT THE GOI\'S INSTITUTIONAL REACTION,

IT IS LIKELY THAT HE IS NOT FULLY INFORMED ABOUT EMBASSY

DISCUSSIONS WITH HIGH-LEVEL OFFICIALS AT THE MFA AND PM\'S

OFFICE. THE FINAL DECISION AS TO ITALY\'S SIGNING OF AN

ARTICLE 98 ACCORD WILL BE MADE AT THE HIGHEST POLITICAL

LEVELS OF THE GOI. IT IS CLEAR, HOWEVER, THAT DISCUSSION IS

TAKING PLACE THROUGHOUT THE BUREAUCRACY. END COMMENT.

4. (C) "WE SHARE THE SAME GOALS BUT DON\'T ALWAYS AGREE ON

MEANS," BALDI SAID, CITING ISRAEL/PALESTINE AND IRAQ AS

EXAMPLES. HE ASSERTED THAT THE LATEST UNGA RESOLUTION ON THE

MIDDLE EAST WAS BALANCED, BECAUSE IT DEPLORED BOTH SIDES\'

FAILINGS. WHEN QUESTIONED ON THIS POINT, HE ASSERTED THAT

THE RESOLUTION CRITICIZED THE "PALESTINIAN AUTHORITY\'S USE OF

CIVILIANS AS HUMAN SHIELDS" AS WELL AS "ISRAEL\'S EXCESSIVE

USE OF FORCE" (SIC). ON IRAQ, HE ASKED WHAT THE LEGAL

JUSTIFICATION FOR USE OF FORCE WOULD BE AND OPINED THAT A UN

SECURITY COUNCIL RESOLUTION WOULD BE UNOBTAINABLE. TOLD THAT

NO PLANS HAVE BEEN MADE AND THAT THE PRESIDENT WOULD CONSULT

WITH ALLIES LIKE ITALY IN THE EVENT OF ANY BEING MADE, HE

REPLIED, "WE HOPE SO."

5. (C) DRAWING ON THE EXAMPLE OF THE INTERNATIONAL

CONFERENCE THAT CREATED THE INTERIM GOVERNMENT IN

AFGHANISTAN, BALDI SUGGESTED A "BONN CONFERENCE" ON THE

MIDDLE EAST THAT WOULD INCLUDE EVERYONE WITH A ROLE TO PLAY

IN RESOLVING THE CONFLICT. THIS CONFERENCE WOULD COVER

SECURITY, JUSTICE, LAND, AND DEVELOPMENT. IT WOULD EMPHASIZE

THAT THERE IS NO CLASH OF CIVILIZATIONS (I.E., BETWEEN THE

MUSLIM WORLD AND THE WEST) BUT ONLY A CLSH BETWEEN THOSE WHO

WANT PEACE AND THOSE WHO WAT ONLY DESTRUCTION. BALDI NOTED

6. (C) BALDI REMINDED POLOFF THAT (ALONG WITH SPAIN) THE

GOI\'S DECISION TO WITHDRAW ITS CANDIDACY FOR THE UNCHR

PROVIDED A CLEAN SLATE FOR THE U.S. CANDIDACY. THE GOI WANTS

A SEAT IN 2004-06 AND "EXPECTS" OUR SUPPORT. ITALY WILL ALSO

RUN IN 2006 FOR THE 2007-08 PERIOD FOR THE UNSC NON-PERMANENT

SEAT THAT IT FAILED TO WIN IN 2000 AND WOULD LIKE OUR SUPPORT

ON THIS, AS WELL. BY BEGINNING ITS CAMPAIGN EARLY, THE GOI

EXPECTS TO WIN THE UNSC SEAT. ITS GOALS WOULD BE UNSC REFORM

AND BETTER COOPERATION BETWEEN THE UN AND EU ON POLITICAL AND

MILITARY LEVELS. (NOTE: ITALY\'S PROPOSAL FOR UNSC REFORM IS

TO ADD EIGHT-TEN REGIONAL, NON-PERMANENT SEATS THAT WOULD BE

SHARED AMONG COUNTRIES -- ESPECIALLY THOSE THAT CONTRIBUTE

GOI PRIORITIES: DRAINING THE WATER TERRORISTS SWIM IN

--------------------------------------------- ---------

8. (C) ASKED WHAT THE GOI\'S PRIORITIES WERE FOR UNGA, BALDI

EXPRESSED ENTHUSIASM FOR AN ACTIVIST AGENDA. USING AN

ITALIAN METAPHOR ABOUT CATCHING A FISH BY DRAINING THE WATER

IT SWIMS IN, BALDI SAID THAT THE GOI PRIORITIZED HUMAN RIGHTS

PROTECTION AND ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT AS TOOLS FOR REDUCING

TERRORISM. "WE MUST FIGHT TERRORISM NOT ONLY THROUGH

REPRESSION BUT ALSO BY FIGHTING THE SENSE OF DESPERATION.

INDOCTRINATION BY TERRORIST GROUPS SUCCEEDS IN AN ATMOSPHERE

OF POVERTY, IGNORANCE, AND LACK OF HOPE FOR A BETTER FUTURE."

BALDI CALLED FOR MILITARY ACTION, POLITICAL RECONCILIATION,

AND ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT MEASURES TO BE IMPLEMENTED TOGETHER,

AS HAS BEEN DONE IN AFGHANISTAN.

9. (SBU) POLOFF NOTED THAT THE GOI HAS BEEN VERY SUPPORTIVE

OF GLOBAL ANTI-TERRORISM EFFORTS. BALDI MENTIONED THAT ITALY

HAS APPROVED TEN OF THE TWELVE INTERNATIONAL TERRORISM

CONVENTIONS. HE EXPECTED PARLIAMENT TO APPROVE THE FINAL TWO

SHORTLY AFTER REOPENING IN SEPTEMBER.

10. (SBU) LIKE THE UNITED STATES, ITALY WOULD EMPHASIZE

GLOBAL DEVELOPMENT, THE NEW ECONOMIC PARTNERSHIP FOR AFRICAN

DEVELOPMENT, DEBT RELIEF, AND OVERSEAS DEVELOPMENT ASSISTANCE

(ODA). THE GOI AGREES WITH PRIORITIZING GOOD GOVERNANCE AND

EDUCATION IN THE DEVELOPING WORLD. IT IS COMMITTED TO

INCREASING ITS ODA IN LINE WITH ITS EU OBLIGATIONS AND HAS

PLACED SPECIAL EMPHASIS ON E-GOVERNMENT-RELATED ASSISTANCE.

ITALY HAS HOSTED TWO CONFERENCES ON E-GOVERNMENT FOR THE

DEVELOPING WORLD, THE MOST RECENT IN PALERMO IN APRIL.
