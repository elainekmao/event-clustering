SUBJECT: JORDAN: MEDIA REACTION ON KING\'S WASHINGTON

VISIT, IRAQ, OCCUPIED TERRITORIES

SUMMARY

NEWS REPORTS IN JORDAN\'S DAILIES COVERED THE ISRAELI

BOMBING OF A RESIDENTIAL AREA IN GAZA, MUASHER\'S

COMMENTS ON KING ABDULLAH\'S FORTHCOMING VISIT TO

WASHINGTON, THE SIGNING OF A JORDANIAN-IRAQI TRADE

PROTOCOL, AND THE COMPLETION OF NEW AMERICAN MILITARY

CAMPS IN KUWAIT AND QATAR.

EDITORIAL COLUMNISTS WROTE LARGELY ON THE ANNIVERSARY

OF THE EGYPTIAN REVOLUTION AND THE SETTLEMENT OF THE

SPANISH-MOROCCAN TENSION. ONE EDITORIALIST COMMENTED

ON THE OPTIMISM OF THE ARAB FOREIGN MINISTERS AND

ANOTHER ABOUT RADIO SAWA.

NEWS REPORTS

THE LEAD STORY IN JORDANIAN DAILIES ON JULY 23 REPORTED

THE "NEW ISRAELI MASSACRE IN GAZA" IN WHICH "12 MARTYRS

AND 150 WOUNDED FELL AS AN F-16 BOMBED A RESIDENTIAL

AREA."

ALL JORDANIAN DAILIES REPORTED FOREIGN MINISTER MARWAN

MU\'ASHER\'S STATEMENT THAT KING ABDULLAH\'S FORTHCOMING

VISIT TO PARIS AND WASHINGTON RELATES TO "HE

PREPARATION OF A CLEAR PLAN TO END THE OCCUPATION AND

ESTABLISH A PALESTINIAN STATE BY MID-2005."

ALL JORDANIAN DAILIES REPORTED ON THEIR FRONT PAGES THE

SIGNING OF A JORDANIAN-IRAQI PROTOCOL TO EXPAND TRADE

EXCHANGES AND ECONOMIC COOPERATION.

ALL DAILIES ALSO REPORTED ON THEIR FRONT PAGES THAT THE

U.S. HAS COMPLETED THE CONSTRUCTION OF ADDITIONAL

MILITARY CAMPS IN KUWAIT AND QATAR.

EDITORIAL COMMENTARIES

-- THE OPTIMISM OF ARAB MINISTERS OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS

PROMINENT COLUMNIST DR. FAHED AL-FANEK WROTE IN SEMI-

OFFICIAL INFLUENTIAL ARABIC DAILY AL-RAI ON JULY 23:

"REPORTS SAY THAT THE ARAB MINISTERS OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS

CAME BACK FROM THEIR MEETING WITH THEIR AMERICAN

COUNTERPART HAPPY, BECAUSE THE SITUATION, AS IT

APPEARED TO THEM, WAS NOT AS BAD AS THEY EXPECTED AFTER

BUSH\'S SPEECH OF JUNE 24. CLEARLY THE THREE MINISTERS

LOOKED FOR SOMETHING POSITIVE AND FOUND SOME, ALBEIT

OBSCURE ONES DEVOID OF MECHANISMS FOR THEIR

IMPLEMENTATION, AND AKIN TO WISHFUL THINKING. THE ARAB

MINISTERS HAVE COME BACK EMPTY HANDED REGARDING

PALESTINE AND WITH EVEN LES REGARDING IRAQ. WASHINGTON

STILL INSISTS ON STRIKING IRAQ AND CHANGING ITS REGIME

TO PROTECT ITS NEIGHBORS DESPITE THEMSELVES, THEREBY

LAUNCHING ITS FIRST WAR THAT IS NOT PRECEDED BY ANY

PROVOCATION, WITHOUT ALLIES, AND AGAINST AN ENEMY THAT

HAS NOT TOUCHED ITS INTERESTS."

-- SUCCESS BY IMITATION

NABIL AL-SHARIF, CHIEF EDITOR OF CENTER-LEFT,

INFLUENTIAL ARABIC DAILY AL-DUSTOUR WROTE ON JULY 23:

"THE LAST BASTION OF ARAB MEDIA, THE YOUTH AUDIENCE,

HAS NOW FALLEN. ANYONE WHO DOUBTS THE ENORMITY OF WHAT

HAS HAPPENED NEEDS ONLY TO ASK THE YOUTH WHICH IS THEIR

FAVORITE RADIO STATION, AND HE WILL DISCOVER THAT IT IS

THE AMERICAN RADIO SAWA. BEFORE WE BREAK INTO WAILS OF

LAMENTATION, WE SHOULD ACKNOWLEDGE THAT THIS STATION

HAS SUCCEEDED IN ATTRACTING ARAB YOUTH THROUGH ITS HIGH

PROFESSIONALISM AND ITS SENSITIVITY TO WHAT THE YOUTH

WANT. WE SHOULD ALSO ADMIT THAT THIS ATTESTS TO THE

FAILURE OF THE ARAB MEDIA. IT IS EASY TO POINT THE

FINGER OF BLAME AND CHARGE THAT THIS STATION SEEKS TO

BRAINWASH OUR YOUTH. IT WOULD BE BETTER TO ASK: `WHAT

DO WE OFFER TO THE YOUTH AS AN ALTERNATIVE?\'

FURTHERMORE, SINCE WE CANNOT SUCCEED BY INNOVATION,

SHOULD WE NOT TRY TO ACHIEVE SUCCESS BY IMITATION?"
