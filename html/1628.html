SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE FOREIGN MINISTER THREATENS STATE OF

EMERGENCY IF SANCTIONS COME TO PASS

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS: 1.5 (B) AND (D).

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 01259 060935Z

1. (U) FOREIGN MINISTER STANISLAUS MUDENGE, IN AN

ADDRESS TO PARLIAMENT ON APRIL 4, THREATENED THAT THE

GOZ COULD DECLARE A STATE OF EMERGENCY IF INTERNATIONAL

SANCTIONS WERE IMPOSED ON ZIMBABWE, ACCORDING TO BOTH OF

HARARE\'S DAILY NEWSPAPERS ON APRIL 6. MUDENGE

REPORTEDLY STATED THAT "IN MOST CASES, DUE TO THE

DEMANDS FOR NATIONAL SURVIVAL, COUNTRIES UNDER SANCTIONS

USUALLY RESORT TO DECLARING STATES OF EMERGENCY. I DO

NOT SAY ZIMBABWE WILL NECESSARILY FOLLOW THIS EXAMPLE BY

DECLARING A STATE OF EMERGENCY, BUT ONE CANNOT RULE IT

OUT." THE FOREIGN MINISTER WENT ON TO SAY THAT RULE BY

DECREE "NEITHER PROMOTES DEMOCRACY NOR ECONOMIC RECOVERY

IN ZIMBABWE, THE PURPORTED OBJECTIVES OF THE ZIMBABWE

DEMOCRACY AND ECONOMIC RECOVERY BILL BEFORE THE USA

SENATE."

2. (C) COMMENT: IT IS UNCLEAR WHAT SPARKED THIS

OUTBURST BY THE FOREIGN MINISTER, WHO RARELY SPEAKS IN

PARLIAMENT. HIS REMARKS, HOWEVER, REFLECT FRUSTRATION

AT ZIMBABWE\'S GROWING ISOLATION AND AT THE MOVES BY SOME

-- INCLUDING THE U.S. CONGRESS, THE COMMONWEALTH, AND

THE EUROPEAN PARLIAMENT -- TO TURN UP THE HEAT ON THE

MUGABE REGIME. MUDENGE, ONE OF THE REGIME\'S MORE HARD-

LINE FIGURES, IS KNOWN FOR MAKING BLUSTERY STATEMENTS

THAT REFLECT THE GOVERNMENT\'S GROWING DESPERATION. WE

SUSPECT WE WILL HEAR A LOT MORE NOISE IN THIS CONNECTION

AND ALREADY HAVE SEEN SOME HAM-HANDED ATTEMPTS BY

GOVERNMENT TO ABRIDGE CONSTITUTIONALLY-GUARANTEED

FREEDOMS. WE ALSO HAVE HEARD THIS SORT OF TALK BEFORE.

THE DIFFERENCE THIS TIME IS THAT MUDENGE WENT STRAIGHT
