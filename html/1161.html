SUBJECT: HANDING OVER CUBAN DOCTORS\' PASSPORTS TO UNHCR

REF: A). STATE 115930 (NOTAL)

B). HARARE 3277

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD FOR REASONS 1.5

B/D.

1. (U) THIS IS AN ACTION REQUESTED MESSAGE. PLEASE SEE

PARAGRAPH 6.

2. (C) DCM AND CONSUL MET WITH UNHCR ATTORNEY MICHIEL

SALOMONS THE MORNING OF JUNE 20 IN RESPONSE TO HIS

REQUEST THAT THE PASSPORTS OF CUBAN DOCTORS\' LEONEL

CORDOVA RODRIGUEZ AND NORIS DEL CARMEN PENA MARTINEZ BE

GIVEN TO UNHCR. SALOMONS NEEDED THESE TO OBTAIN VISAS

THAT AN UNNAMED COUNTRY WOULD GRANT FOR CONDITIONAL

RESETTLEMENT. THE UNHCR ATTORNEY APOLOGIZED FOR NOT

BEING ABLE TO IDENTIFY THE COUNTRY, STATING THAT IN

DOING SO HE WAS ONLY FOLLOWING THE ORDERS OF HIS BOSS

(UNHCR REGIONAL REPRESENTATIVE OLUSEYI BAJULAIYE).

3. (C) ACCORDING TO SALOMONS, THERE ARE A NUMBER OF

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 03323 01 OF 02 201125Z

ELEMENTS IN THE CASE PREVENTING THE UNHCR FROM ISSUING

THEM TRAVEL DOCUMENTS. THEY INCLUDE THE STRONG PROTEST

MADE BY THE UNHCR ON THE GOZ\'S DENIAL OF ACCESS TO

PROPER AND LEGAL REFUGEE PROCEDURES, THE RULING BY THE

ZIMBABWE ELIGIBILITY COMMITTEE THAT IT COULD NEITHER

ACCEPT NOR REJECT THE CUBANS CLAIM FOR REFUGEE STATUS,

AND THE COMMITTEE\'S REQUEST THAT UNHCR FIND A DURABLE

SOLUTION HAS ONLY FURTHER COMPLICATED THE PROCESS.

SALOMONS WAS PROUD TO CLAIM THAT THE UNHCR HAS NOW COME

UP WITH A "SOLUTION", HAVING LOCATED A COUNTRY WILLING

TO ACCEPT THE CUBANS ON A TEMPORARY BASIS. THE UNHCR

HAS NEGOTIATED A WRITTEN AGREEMENT WITH THIS UNSPECIFIED

COUNTRY.

4. (C) SALOMONS SAID THE RESETTLEMENT COUNTRY HAS

STIPULATED THAT THE PASSPORTS OF THE CUBAN DOCTORS MUST

BE GIVEN TO THEM FOR VISA ISSUANCE. SALOMONS AGAIN

REITERATED THAT THE UNHCR WAS LEGALLY PROHIBITED FROM

ISSUING TRAVEL DOCUMENTS TO THE TWO CUBANS AS IT

CONSIDERS THEM TO BE ASYLUM SEEKERS OR "DE FACTO"

REFUGEES. THEREFORE, THE VISAS WOULD HAVE TO BE PUT IN

THE CUBANS PASSPORTS. SALOMONS ALSO OPINED THAT

REGARDLESS OF THE LEGALITY OF HIS ORGANIZATION ISSUING

TRAVEL DOCUMENTS, IT WOULD NOT DO SO, DUE TO "POLITICAL"

CONSIDERATIONS. SALOMONS ALSO ADVISED US THAT HE

BELIEVES THAT THE GOZ WILL NOT RELEASE THE CUBANS TO THE

UNHCR FOR RESETTLEMENT TO THE UNNAMED COUNTRY EVEN WITH

THE WRITTEN CONSENT OF THE LATTER.

5. (C) OF INTEREST, AND IN DIRECT CONTRADICTION TO WHAT

WE WERE TOLD ON JUNE 19, SALOMONS ADMITTED THAT

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 04 HARARE 03323 01 OF 02 201125Z

CONDITIONS AT THE NEW LOCATION WHERE THE CUBANS ARE

BEING HELD ARE SIGNIFICANTLY WORSE THAN AT THE GOROMONZI

DETENTION CENTER. DUE TO THIS, THE UNHCR IS NOW

REQUESTING THAT THE CUBANS TO MOVED TO YET ANOTHER,

UNSPECIFIED, THIRD LOCATION. OUR SURPRISE AT THIS

TURNAROUND WAS EVIDENT, HAVING RECEIVED ASSURANCES FROM

BOTH BAJULAIYE AND SALOMONS ONLY 24 HOURS AGO THAT THEY

HAD INSPECTED THE NEW REMAND FACILITIES AND FOUND IT TO

BE FAR BETTER THAN GOROMONZI. UNHCR HAD NEGOTIATED

AMENITIES FOR THE CUBANS AT GORONMONZI TO INCLUDE FIVE

BLANKETS, A DAILY SHOWER AND THREE MEALS A DAY ALL OF

WHICH WAS BEING DENIED TO THEM AT THE NEW FACILITIES.

DOCTOR CORDOVA RODRIGUEZ HAD BEEN PLACED IN A CROWDED

CELL WITH EIGHT OTHER PRISONERS, TWO OF WHOM IMMEDIATELY

BEGAN BEATING HIM. THE POLICE REACTED AND REMOVED THE

CUBAN DOCTOR FROM THE CELL AND PLACED HIM IN A SINGLE

CELL. BOTH CUBANS ARE SCHEDULED TO HAVE MEDICAL

EXAMINATIONS SOMETIME TODAY.

6. (C) ACTION REQUESTED: SINCE UNHCR IS IN FULL CONTROL

OF THIS PROCESS, POST ASSUMES THAT IT SHOULD RELEASE THE

PASSPORTS OF THE CUBAN DOCTORS TO THE UNHCR

REPRESENTATIVE. HOWEVER, POST WOULD APPRECIATE

INSTRUCTIONS ON THIS POINT.
