SUBJECT: LIBERALIZING SOCIAL CONDITIONS IN TEHRAN

1. CONFIDENTIAL - ENTIRE TEXT.

2. SUMMARY: SINCE KHOMEINI\'S DEATH THE ENFORCEMENT

OF DRESS CODES AND RULES REGARDING THE APPEARANCE OF

WOMEN HAS DECREASED DRAMATICALLY, ACCORDING TO SOURCES

WHO HAVE TRAVELLED TO IRAN IN RECENT MONTHS. REVGUARD

AND KOMITEH PRESENCE ON STREETS HAS DIMINISHED NOTICEABLY

AND AS A RESULT PROSTITUTES ARE OPENLY PLYING THEIR TRADE

ON THE STREETS OF TEHRAN, ONCE AGAIN. END SUMMARY.

3. ACCORDING TO RECENT TRAVELLERS TO IRAN, BOTH IRANIAN

RESIDENTS OF DUBAI, AND ONE UAE NATIONAL, WOMEN ARE

OPENLY WEARING MAKEUP ON THE STREET IN ALL NEIGHBORHOODS

OF TEHRAN, AND NOT ONLY IN THE WEALTHY NEIGHBORHOODS OF

THE NORTH OF THE CITY, BUT ALSO IN THE SOUTH WHERE SUCH

AN OPEN CHALLENGE TO THE DRESS CODE WOULD NEVER HAVE

BEEN TOLERATED IN THE PAST. AT THE SAME TIME, SEVERAL

SOURCES REPORT A BIG CHANGE IN THE STYLE OF CLOTHES

IRANIAN WOMEN ARE WEARING. CHADORS ARE LESS PREVALENT,

AND THE STANDARD BLACK OR BEIGE RAINCOAT IS BEING

REPLACED BY FASHIONABLY STYLED, COLORED RAINCOATS. MANY

WOMEN\'S HEJABS (HEAD SCARVES) ARE BRIGHTLY COLORED AND

ARE BEING WORN FURTHER AND FURTHER BACK ON THE HEAD,

OFTEN EXPOSING DYED HAIR. ONE SOURCE REPORTS HAVING

SEEN WOMEN WEARING LONG-SLEEVED LONG DRESSES WITHOUT

CHADOR OR RAINCOAT COVERING. ONLY ONE SOURCE, A DUBAI

BASED IRANIAN, REPORTS HAVING SEEN WOMEN IN THE NORTH

OF TEHRAN WEARING NO SCARF AT ALL ON THE STREET. ONE

WOMAN GOVERNMENT EMPLOYEE WHILE AGREEING THAT THE

ENFORCEMENT OF DRESS CODES FOR WOMEN IS BEING EASED ON

THE STREETS OF TEHRAN, SAID THAT THERE HAD BEEN NO

SIMILAR EASING OF THE DRESS CODE FOR WOMEN WORKING IN

GOVERNMENT OFFICES.

4. REVGUARD AND KOMITEH CONTINGENTS HAVE VIRTUALLY

DISAPPEARED FROM THE STREETS OF TEHRAN, ACCORDING TO

ALL SOURCES. ACCORDING TO ONE SOURCE, THE KOMITEH IS

BEING INTEGRATED INTO THE GENDARMERIE AND WILL SOON

CEASE TO EXIST AS AN INDEPENDENT ENTITY. ONE

CONSEQUENCE OF THE DECREASED PRESENCE OF THESE TWO

GROUPS IS A VISIBLE INCREASE IN THE NUMBER OF

PROSTITUTES ON THE STREETS OF TEHRAN. ACCORDING TO A

UAE NATIONAL WHO HAS TRAVELLED TO IRAN SEVERAL TIMES IN

THE PAST YEAR, AND WHO WAS THERE IN THE FIRST WEEK OF

SEPTEMBER, HEAVILY MADE UP WOMEN ARE STANDING ON STREET

CORNERS THROUGHOUT TEHRAN. THEY BOLDLY APPROACH CARS

STUCK IN TRAFFIC TO SOLICIT CUSTOMERS. SOURCE RELATED

ONE INCIDENT HE WITNESSED WHILE BEING DRIVEN INTO TEHRAN

FROM THE AIRPORT. A YOUNG WOMAN OF ABOUT SEVENTEEN

APPROACHED HIS CAR AND ASKED FOR A RIDE. WHEN HIS

DRIVER ASKED WHERE SHE WANTED TO GO, SHE ANSWERED

"ANYWHERE". THE DRIVER ASKED IF THAT MEANT SHE WANTED

TO GO WHEREVER THEY WERE GOING, SHE ANSWERED

AFFIRMATIVELY. SHE THEN CONFESSED THAT SHE WAS "NEW"

TO THE BUSINESS AND DID NOT KNOW WHAT TO CHARGE OR

WHETHER TO CHARGE IN RIALS OR DOLLARS.

5. UAE SOURCE RECOUNTED ONE STORY WHICH WE REPORT FOR

WHATEVER VALUE IT MAY HAVE; THE STORY HAS BEEN CONFIRMED

BY THREE IRANIAN RESIDENTS OF DUBAI WHO CLAIM TO HAVE

HEARD IT FROM PEOPLE WHO VISITED KHOMEINI\'S TOMB IN THE

PAST MONTH. SOURCE CLAIMS THAT THE GOVERNMENT OF IRAN

HAS BUILT A BARRIER AROUND THE GRAVE OF KHOMEINI TO STOP

PEOPLE FROM THROWING MONEY ON HIS GRAVE. ACCORDING TO

SOURCE, SHIITE PILGRIMS TRADITIONALLY THROW MONEY

WRAPPED IN PAPER OR FABRIC ON THE GRAVES OF THEIR HOLY

MEN, AND THE MONEY IS COLLECTED AND USED TO KEEP UP THE

SHRINE. SOURCE CLAIMS THAT THE BARRIER WAS SET UP AT

KHOMEINI\'S TOMB TO STOP THE PRACTICE BECAUSE THE SMELL

WAS GETTING TOO STRONG: MANY PEOPLE WERE THROWING

EXCREMENT WRAPPED IN PAPER ONTO THE IMAM\'S GRAVE.
