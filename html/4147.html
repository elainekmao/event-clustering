SUBJECT: WESTERN SAHARAN REFUGEES IMPERILED BY LACK OF FOOD

AID - BRIEFING BY AMBASSADOR WILLIAM SWING, SPECIAL

REPRESENTATIVE OF THE UN SECRETARY-GENERAL FOR MINURSO

REF: (A) ROME 5324, (B) ROME 5308

-------

SUMMARY

-------

1. AMBASSADOR WILLIAM SWING, SPECIAL REPRESENTATIVE OF THE

UN SECRETARY-GENERAL FOR MINURSO (UN MISSION FOR THE

REFERENDUM IN WESTERN SAHARA) ADDRESSED WFP\'S DONORS ON

OCTOBER 25. HE UNDERLINED THAT THE LACK OF FOOD AID AND

INSUFFICIENT FUNDING PRESENTLY THREATEN SOME 155,000 WESTERN

SAHARAN (SAHRAOUI) REFUGEES LIVING IN FOUR ISOLATED QUARTER-

CENTURY OLD CAMPS IN THE INHOSPITABLE TINDOUF REGION, SOME

2,000 KILOMETERS SOUTH OF ALGERIA\'S MEDITERRANEAN COAST.

THESE REFUGEES HAVE FEW OTHER SOURCES OF FOOD AND

OPPORTUNITIES TO EARN AN INCOME ARE SCARCE. WFP\'S FOOD

PIPELINE IS EXPECTED TO BREAK IN FEBRUARY 2003, WHEN ONLY 31

PERCENT OF THE FOOD BASKET WILL BE AVAILABLE, WHICH WOULD

PROVIDE THE REFUGEES WITH ONLY 650 KILO-CALORIES/DAY.

AMBASSADOR SWING HIGHLIGHTED THAT FOOD SECURITY IS VITAL TO

BACKGROUND

----------

2. AMBASSADOR WILLIAM L. SWING, SPECIAL REPRESENTATIVE OF

THE UN SECRETARY-GENERAL FOR MINURSO ADDRESSED DONORS ON

OCTOBER 25 AT WFP HEADQUARTERS. HE DESCRIBED A DRAMATIC

SITUATION WHERE SOME 155,000 REFUGEES FROM WESTERN SAHARA,

LIVING IN FOUR ISOLATED QUARTER-CENTURY OLD CAMPS IN THE

INHOSPITABLE TINDOUF REGION, RELY HEAVILY ON WFP FOOD

ASSISTANCE. THEY HAVE ACCESS TO FEW OTHER SOURCES OF FOOD.

THESE CAMPS ARE IN EXCEEDINGLY ARID ZONES WHERE LITTLE

FLOURISHES AND OPPORTUNITIES TO EARN AN INCOME ARE SCARCE.

THE STALLED UN-SPONSORED SETTLEMENT PLAN HAS QUASHED HOPES

FOR A SPEEDY RESOLUTION OF THEIR PLIGHT.

3. AMBASSADOR SWING COMMENTED THAT ERRATIC FOOD DELIVERIES

AND INCOMPLETE FOOD BASKETS HAVE DRAMATIC EFFECTS ON THE

NUTRITIONAL STATUS OF VULNERABLE BENEFICIARIES, PARTICULARLY

PREGNANT AND NURSING WOMEN. CALORIC INTAKE AMONG

BENEFICIARIES DROPS AS LOW AS 1,288 KILOCALORIES WHEN

RESOURCES ARE INSUFFICIENT - SUCH AS OCCURRED IN JANUARY

2002. NOTE: A JOINT WFP/UNHCR NUTRITIONAL SURVEY IS BEING

CONDUCTED TO DOCUMENT LEVELS OF MALNUTRITION AND VITAMIN

DEFICIENCIES AMONG THE REFUGEE POPULATION. THE SURVEY REPORT

IS EXPECTED TO BE RELEASED IN DECEMBER 2002. END NOTE.

4. IT WAS ALSO POINTED OUT THAT WFP HAS SPONSORED DONORS\'

VISITS TO THE TINDOUF CAMPS, IN FEBRUARY 2001 AND AGAIN IN

APRIL 2002, WITH A TOTAL PARTICIPATION OF EIGHTEEN EMBASSIES

AND THE EUROPEAN COMMUNITY\'S HUMANITARIAN OFFICE (ECHO). AND

WFP RECENTLY OPENED A SUB-OFFICE IN TINDOUF. NOTE: THE

UNITED STATES HAS SUPPORTED A STEPPED-UP UNHCR/WFP PRESENCE

IN TINDOUF, INCLUDING POST-DISTRIBUTION MONITORING TO ENSURE

THAT WFP FOOD AID REMAINS WITH THE REFUGEES AND IS USED

STRICTLY FOR HUMANITARIAN PURPOSES. AN ENHANCED UN PRESENCE

WILL ALSO FURNISH THE REFUGEE POPULATIONS WITH A BETTER

UNDERSTANDING OF THEIR RIGHTS AND THEIR OPTIONS FOR DURABLE

SOLUTIONS. END NOTE.

5. IN ADDITION TO UNHCR AND WFP, THERE ARE SOME 19 NON-

GOVERNMENTAL ORGANIZATIONS (NGOS) - TWO ALGERIAN, 16

EUROPEAN AND ONE FROM THE UNITED STATES - OPERATING

COMPLEMENTARY ASSISTANCE PROGRAMS IN THE CAMPS.

7. APPROXIMATELY 12,200 METRIC TONS ARE REQUIRED TO MEET THE

REFUGEES\' FOOD NEEDS BETWEEN NOW AND JUNE 2003. MONTHLY

REQUIREMENTS ARE ABOUT 2,800 METRIC TONS (TO PROVIDE 2,144

KILOCALORIES PER PERSON PER DAY). THIS COSTS USD 1.25

MILLION/MONTH. NOTE: THE TWO-YEAR WFP PROTRACTED RELIEF AND

RECOVERY OPERATION (PRRO) IS VALUED AT USD 29.7 MILLION

COVERING THE PERIOD SEPTEMBER 1, 2002 - AUGUST 31, 2004.

ECHO IS ALSO PROVIDING APPROXIMATELY USD 14 MILLION OVER A

15-MONTH PERIOD IN NON-FOOD AND MICRO-PROJECT SUPPORT. END

NOTE.

8. GIVEN LACK OF PLEDGES, THE FOOD PIPELINE IS EXPECTED TO

BREAK IN FEBRUARY 2003. THE CEREALS SHORTFALL IS EXPECTED TO

STAND AT 73 PERCENT; SUPPLIES OF OIL, SUGAR AND SALT WILL BE

COMPLETELY EXHAUSTED. IN TOTAL, ONLY 31 PERCENT OF THE FOOD

BASKET WILL BE AVAILABLE, WHICH WILL PROVIDE THE REFUGEES

WITH ONLY 650 KCALS/DAY. CONFIRMATION OF CONTRIBUTIONS NOW

WOULD ENABLE THE WFP COUNTRY OFFICE TO BORROW FROM BUFFER

STOCKS IN THE COUNTRY TO AVOID A COMPLETE BREAKDOWN OF THE

COMMENT

-------

9. AMBASSADOR BILL SWING HAS HAD A LONG AND DISTINGUISHED

FOREIGN SERVICE CAREER AND IS UNDOUBTEDLY ONE OF OUR HANDFUL

OF "WISE MEN" ON AFRICA. HE CLEARLY VIEWS A SUFFICIENTLY

SUBSCRIBED AND PREDICTABLE SUPPLY OF WFP FOOD AID AS A VITAL

ELEMENT IN MOVING THE POLITICAL PROCESS FORWARD - AS IT

DEMONSTRATES TO THE RESPECTIVE PARTIES THE INTERNATIONAL

COMMUNITY\'S UNWAVERING COMMITMENT TO A PERMANENT POLITICAL

SOLUTION. IN THIS REGARD HE NOTED THAT SECURITY COUNCIL

RESOLUTION 1429 (JULY 30, 2002) CALLED INTER ALIA FOR

SUPPORT TO WFP\'S FOOD EFFORTS. HE URGED SOME PROMINENT EX-

DONORS (BELGIUM AND JAPAN) TO RE-JOIN THE FOOD AID EFFORT.

HE UNDERLINED THAT FOOD SHORTAGES ACT AS A DE-STABILIZING

FACTOR IN THE REFUGEE CAMPS AND IMPEDE CONFIDENCE BUILDING

MEASURES WITH THE CONCERNED PARTIES, BY LIMITING UN

LEVERAGE.
