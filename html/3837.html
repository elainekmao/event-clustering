1. SUMMARY: AMBASSADOR SEMBLER RECOGNIZED THE INTERDICTION

AND SAFETY EFFORTS OF ITALIAN SHIPPERS AT THE ANNUAL AMVER

AWARDS CEREMONY ON SEPTEMBER 19. THE EVENT WAS WELL-COVERED

IN THE ITALIAN PRESS. SOME PARTICIPANTS TOOK THE OPPORTUNITY

TO SHARE THEIR CONCERNS REGARDING THE CONTAINER SECURITY

INITIATIVE. POST IS LOOKING INTO COORDINATING A COMBINED

CEREMONY IN 2003 WITH THE ITALIAN EQUIVALENT OF AMVER. END

SUMMARY.

2. THE AMBASSADOR HOSTED AN AMVER (AUTOMATED MUTUAL

ASSISTANCE VESSEL EMERGENCY RESPONSE) AWARDS CEREMONY ON

SEPTEMBER 19, ATTENDED BY LEADING ITALIAN MARITIME INDUSTRY,

UNION AND GOVERNMENT LEADERS, INCLUDING SEVEN OF THE TWELVE

AWARD RECIPIENTS FROM ROME, NAPLES, GENOA AND SICILY.

3. DR. LUIGI PERISSICH, DIRECTOR GENERAL OF THE ITALIAN

SHIPOWNERS ASSOCIATION, TOOK ADVANTAGE OF THE OCCASION TO

EXPRESS SUPPORT FOR THE SIGNING AND IMPLEMENTATION OF A

CONTAINER SECURITY INITIATIVES (CSI) AGREEMENT BETWEEN THE

U.S. AND ITALY. DR. PERISSICH ALSO VOICED AN INTEREST IN A

FOLLOW ON MEETING WITH THE EMBASSY TO DISCUSS SHIPOWNERS

QUESTIONS ON CSI. (CUSTOMS AND STATE OFFICIALS WILL HOST

SUCH A MEETING ON OCTOBER 1.)

4. ADMIRAL SERGIO DE STEFANO, DEPUTY CHIEF OF THE ITALIAN

COAST GUARD, TOLD THE AMBASSADOR THAT THE ITALIAN COAST GUARD

5. THE ITALIAN PRESS WAS WELL REPRESENTED AT THE AMVER
