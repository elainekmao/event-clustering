SUBJECT: (S) UAE TO KEEP IRAQI MISSIONS OPEN, BUT

FULLY BOXED-IN

REF: STATE 75557

1. (U) CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR MARCELLE M. WAHBA

FOR REASONS 1.5 (B,D).

2. (S) AMBASSADOR DELIVERED REFTEL DEMARCHE ON

THE AFTERNOON OF 3/23 TO MFA MINSTATE SHAYKH

HAMDAN BIN ZAYID (OTHER TOPICS SEPTEL, NOTAL).

SHAYKH HAMDAN REPEATED WHAT HE HAS TOLD US BEFORE

-- NAMELY, THAT THE UAEG IS RELUCTANT TO CLOSE

IRAQ\'S DIPLOMATIC MISSIONS HERE BECAUSE OF THE

"GREAT VALUE" BEING DERIVED FROM THE UAE MISSION

IN BAGHDAD. DESIROUS OF AVOIDING A TIT-FOR-TAT

CLOSURE, THE UAEG HAS MADE CLEAR TO THE IRAQI

DIPLOMATS HERE THAT THEY ARE TO BEHAVE THEMSELVES.

THE AMBASSADOR NOTED IN PARTICULAR THE NEED TO

KEEP TABS ON IRAQI INTELLIGENCE SERVICE

OPERATIVES. SHAYKH HAMDAN REASSURED THE

AMBASSADOR THAT SURVEILLANCE OF THE OFFICIAL

IRAQIS IS CONSTANT AND INTRUSIVELY OBVIOUS. HE

BELIEVED THEY HAD GOTTEN THE MESSAGE.

3. (S/NF) SHAYKH HAMDAN NOTED THAT HE GETS CALLS

3-4 TIMES PER DAY FROM THE UAE CHARGE IN BAGHDAD

(USING A THURAYYA SATELLITE TELEPHONE). THE

EMIRATI MISSION IS ABLE TO GLEAN USEFUL

INFORMATION FROM ITS CONTACTS ABOUT DEVELOPMENTS

INSIDE IRAQ (INDEED, DURING THE MEETING THE

EMIRATI CHARGE IN BAGHDAD CALLED SHAYKH HAMDAN TO

REPORT ON 20 EXPLOSIONS IN WESTERN BAGHDAD AROUND

THE TAJI MILITARY COMPLEX AND A NUMBER OF OTHER

SITES). AS AN EXAMPLE OF THE INFORMATION THE UAEG

IS ABLE TO OBTAIN, THE MFA MINSTATE CONFIDED THAT

IRAQI SOURCES HAVE TOLD THE EMIRATIS IN BAGHDAD

THAT ALI HASSAN AL-MAJID (AKA "CHEMICAL ALI) IS

DEAD.

4. (S/NF) COMMENT: THE UAE CLEARLY BELIEVES

THAT THE THREAT POSED BY ALLOWING IRAQI MISSIONS

TO REMAIN OPEN HERE HAS BEEN EFFECTIVELY MITIGATED

BY AGGRESSIVE SURVEILLANCE, STRICT WARNINGS TO

IRAQI DIPLOMATS, AND THE COOPTION OF KEY PERSONNEL

(MOST NOTABLY THE IRAQI AMBASSADOR). WE WOULD

NOTE THAT ORCA CHIEF HERE IS OF A SIMILAR VIEW.

GIVEN THE IMPORTANCE THE UAEG ATTACHES TO KEEPING

TABS ON DEVELOPMENTS IN IRAQ, AND THE RELATIVELY

EXTENSIVE NETWORK OF CONTACTS WHICH THE UAEG

ENJOYS IN BAGHDAD AND BEYOND, WE DO NOT BELIEVE

THE UAEG IS LIKELY TO CHANGE ITS VIEW THAT CLOSING

IRAQ\'S DIPLOMATIC MISSIONS HERE IS NOT IN THE

INTEREST OF THE EMIRATES.

WAHBA
