SUMMARY

-------

2. BENKOVAC FELL TO HV FORCES AT 17:55 LOCAL.

PRESIDENTIAL CHIEF OF STAFF HRVOJE SARINIC EXPRESSED

CONDITIONAL GOC INTEREST IN STOPPING ALL MILITARY

OPERATIONS AND CONDUCTING POLITICAL NEGOTIATIONS WITH SERB

INTERLOCUTORS, INCLUDING BABIC. HE SAID THAT THE GOC

CONSIDERED SERBS ATTACKS ON DUBROVNIK AND EASTERN SLAVONIA

TO BE ONLY PROVOCATIONS, AND THAT THE HV WOULD NOT BE

DRAWN INTO OPENING ANOTHER FRONT WITH THE SERBS. SERB

FORCES WERE PRESENTING MOST RESISTANCE IN SECTOR NORTH,

AROUND SLUNJ, AND THE HV EXPECTS TO HAVE THIS TERRITORY

FINISHED OVER EIGHTY PERCENT OF ITS OBJECTIVE BY 1800

LOCAL TIME. GENERAL CERMAK NAMED BY TUDJMAN TO BE THE

END SUMMARY.

KNIN FALLS

----------

3. SARINIC CONFIRMED TO THE AMBASSADOR THAT THE HV NOW

HAD COMPLETE CONTROL OVER KNIN. HE CLAIMED THAT THE KNIN

CIVIL AUTHORITIES HAD FLED THE CITY IN A PANIC AND HAD NO

TIME TO CARRY WITH THEM OR DESTROY GOVERNMENT DOCUMENTS

AND RECORDS, ALTHOUGH HE SAID IT WAS TOO EARLY TO SAY WHAT

REMAINED. SERB RESISTANCE WAS STILL HOLDING OUT IN THE

TOWNS OF BENKOVAC AND OBROVAC IN WESTERN PART OF SECTOR

SOUTH. HOWEVER, HE CONFIRMED THAT HV FORCES HAD

PENETRATED TO THE CROATIAN INTERNATIONAL BORDER AND HAD

LINKED UP WITH ABH FIFTH CORPS TROOPS AT KOSTAJNICA.

(NOTE: THE HV COMMANDER IN OGULIN SAID THAT HIS FORCES

PLANNED TO LINK UP WITH FIFTH CORPS NEAR RAKOVICA ON

AUGUST 6.) EARLIER IN THE DAY, SARINIC SAID HE HAD

PROMISED SRSG AKASHI AND UNPF COMMANDER JANVIER THAT UNCRO

WOULD BE ALLOWED TO PATROL KNIN AND CONTINUE OPERATIONS

THERE, BUT ONLY AFTER SAFETY CONDITIONS ALLOWED UNCRO TO

RETURN. HE INSISTED THAT IT WOULD BE PSYCHOLOGICALLY

IMPORTANT TO THE REMAINING SERBS FOR THE INTERNATIONAL

COMMUNITY TO MAINTAIN A PRESENCE IN THE TOWN.

THE ABDIC FACTOR

----------------

4. SARINIC CLAIMED THAT OVER SIX HUNDRED TROOPS LOYAL TO

RENEGADE MUSLIM LEADER FIKRET ABDIC HAD QUICKLY SHIFTED

LOYALTIES TO THE HV AND THE ABH 5TH CORPS AS THESE TWO

ARMIES CONSOLIDATED THEIR GAINS IN SECTOR NORTH AND THE

BIHAC POCKET. ABDIC, SARINIC OPINED, WAS A "NORMAL GUY,

NOT A FUNDAMENTALIST," DESPITE HIS OPPORTUNISTIC

ASSOCIATION WITH MARTIC. HE POSED NO PROBLEMS FOR THE

GOC, PER SE. YET THE GOC COULD NOT RISK ITS RELATIONSHIP

WITH IZETBEGOVIC ON ABDIC\'S ACCOUNT. HOWEVER, IN RECENT

CONVERSATIONS WITH TUDJMAN, SARINIC WAS SURPRISED THAT

IZETBEGOVIC APPEARED TO BE FLEXIBLE CONCERNING ABDIC.

8. THE SERBS CONTINUED TO MOUNT THE STRONGEST RESISTANCE

IN SECTOR NORTH, HE SAID, BETWEEN GLINA AND PETRINJA. HE

EXPECTED OPERATIONS THERE TO CONTINUE ANOTHER 24-48 HOURS.

ONE DOZEN TANKS STILL HELD OUT IN THE AREA. THEY WERE

UNABLE TO MOVE DUE TO MECHANICAL FAILURES, YET THEIR

SECTOR EAST PROVOCATIONS

------------------------

15. SARINIC REMAINED CONCERNED ABOUT "ARSK" SHELLING ON

OSIJEK AND VINKOVCI. HE INSISTED THAT THE HV CONSIDERED

THESE ATTACKS TO BE ONLY PROVOCATIONS, AND WOULD NOT

RETALIATE. HE CLAIMED THAT OVER 600 SHELLS HAD FALLEN ON

VINKOVCI AND 6 ON OSIJEK SINCE THE START OF THE HV\'S

ACTION. HE SHOWED US AN UNCONFIRMED AND UNRELIABLE MEDIA

REPORT FROM BANJA LUKA AS OF 1300 LOCAL ON AUGUST 5 THAT

THE "ARSK" HAD GAINED CONTROL OF NUSTAR, SIX KILOMETERS

NORTH OF VINKOVCI.
