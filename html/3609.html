SUBJECT: VATICAN PUTS PEOPLE FIRST AT SUSTAINABLE

DEVELOPMENT SUMMIT

1. "PEOPLE COME FIRST" WILL BE THE LEITMOTIF FOR THE VATICAN

DELEGATION AT THE WORLD SUMMIT OF SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT.

IN AN AUGUST 25 MESSAGE FROM HIS SUMMER RETREAT AT

CASTELGANDOLFO, POPE JOHN PAUL II WISHED SUMMIT DELEGATES

UNCLASSIFIED

SUCCESS "IN FINDING EFFECTIVE WAYS FOR AN INTEGRAL HUMAN

DEVELOPMENT, KEEPING IN MIND THE ECONOMIC, SOCIAL AND

ENVIRONMENTAL DIMENSION. THE POPE DESCRIBED THE WORLD AS

"INCREASINGLY INTERDEPENDENT" AND SAID WE HAVE A DIVINE CALL

TO CULTIVATE AND PROTECT THE EARTH, ITS PEOPLE AND

RESOURCES. HE EMPHASIZED THAT PEACE, JUSTICE AND THE

PROTECTION OF THE ENVIRONMENT CAN ONLY BE THE FRUIT OF

COMMON EFFORT BY ALL HUMANITY - RICH AND POOR.

2. THE HOLY SEE DELEGATION WILL BE LED BY ARCHBISHOP RENATO

MARTINO, THE HOLY SEE\'S PERMANENT OBSERVER TO THE UNITED

NATIONS. ARCHBISHOP MARTINO TOLD REPORTERS AUGUST 26 THAT

THE FIRST PRINCIPLE OF THE RIO DECLARATION -- HUMAN BEINGS

ARE AT THE CENTER OF SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT CONCERNS --

WOULD BE THE BASIS FOR HIS DELEGATION\'S PARTICIPATION IN

JOHANNESBURG. THE RIO DOCUMENT\'S INSISTENCE ON THE PRIMACY

OF THE PERSON IN DEVELOPMENT ISSUES WAS, ACCORDING TO

MARTINO, A KEY HOLY SEE OBJECTIVE AT THAT MEETING. (COMMENT:

IN PRACTICAL TERMS FOR JOHANNESBURG THIS IMPLIES THAT THE

HOLY SEE WILL NOT BE WEDDED TO ANY PARTICULAR STRATEGY,

PARTNERSHIP, BENCHMARK OR DEADLINE UNLESS IT PROMOTES THE

VATICAN\'S TRADITIONAL PRIORITY OF THE VALUE OF THE HUMAN

PERSON, FAMILY, AND SOCIETY. END COMMENT).

3. IN A PAPER PREPARED FOR THE PREPARATORY MEETINGS LEADING

UP TO THE WSSD, THE HOLY SEE HIGHLIGHTED AS ITS PRIORITIES

POVERTY ERADICATION, EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITIES, EDUCATION,

BASIC HEALTH CARE, AND ADEQUATE SHELTER. THEY ALSO SUPPORT

"NEW PATTERNS OF CONSUMPTION AND PRODUCTION" BASED ON

ENVIRONMENTAL STEWARDSHIP IN WHICH THE HUMAN PERSON

RECOGNIZES HIS OR HER RESPONSIBILITY TO HELP ORDER THE

WORLD. MARTINO SIGNALED VATICAN CONCERN FOR THE DEVELOPMENT

OF A MORE RESPONSIBLE APPROACH TO MANAGING THE WORLD\'S WATER

RESOURCES, AND WILL SUPPORT INITIATIVES AIMED AT ENSURING

ADEQUATE SUPPLIES OF DRINKING WATER.

4. THE HOLY SEE\'S VIEWS ON PROMOTING GOOD GOVERNANCE TRACK

CLOSELY WITH U.S. PERSPECTIVES. THEIR PREPARATORY PAPER

HIGHLIGHTS THE IMPORTANCE OF GOOD GOVERNANCE, PARTICULARLY

IN REGARD TO FIGHTING CORRUPTION, PROMOTING GREATER

DEMOCRATIC PARTICIPATION, ESTABLISHING WELL-FUNCTIONING

BUREAUCRACIES AND REGULATORY SYSTEMS, ENFORCING LAWS, AND

PROTECTING HUMAN RIGHTS. THEY CONCLUDE THAT "THE LACK OF

STABLE INSTITUTIONS AND SOUND POLICIES ARE OFTEN A CAUSE OF

POVERTY, ONES THAT EVEN INCREASED LEVELS OF DEVELOPMENT

ASSISTANCE CANNOT OVERCOME. CORRUPTION MUST BE CONSIDERED A

SCANDAL AND A MAJOR IMPEDIMENT TO DEVELOPMENT."

NICHOLSON
