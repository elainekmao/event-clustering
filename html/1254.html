SUBJECT: US-ASEAN DIALOGUE PARTNERSHIP WITH VIETNAM

1. THIS IS AN ACTION CABLE. SEE PARAS 6-7.

2. POLCOUNS AND POLOFF CALLED ON MFA ASEAN DEPARTMENT

DEPUTY DIRECTOR PHAM HUU CHI AUGUST 15 TO DISCUSS VIETNAM\'S

DESIGNATION AS THE U.S\' CURRENT ASEAN DIALOGUE PARTNER AND

EXPLORE PRODUCTIVE DIALOGUE AREAS. CHI SAID THAT, AS

FOREIGN MINISTER NGUYEN DY NIEN EXPRESSED AT THE RECENT

BANGKOK POST-MINISTERIAL CONFERENCE, VIETNAM WANTS TO BE A

SERIOUS DIALOGUE PARTNER WITH THE U.S. AND MAKE THE NEXT

THREE YEARS PRODUCTIVE. VIETNAM WANTS TO HAVE OPEN EARS,

AND WELCOMES ANY SUGGESTIONS FROM THE U.S. ON MOVING THE

DIALOGUE FORWARD. VIETNAM, CHI ADDED, IS IN AN ESPECIALLY

GOOD POSITION THIS YEAR AS ASEAN STANDING COMMITTEE AND

ASEAN REGIONAL FORUM CHAIR TO PROMOTE THE DIALOGUE

PARTNERSHIP.

3. VIETNAM AND THE U.S. HAVE CONSIDERABLE COMMON GROUND ON

AREAS SUCH AS POVERTY REDUCTION, ECONOMIC REFORM,

TRANSNATIONAL ISSUES, AND REGIONAL SECURITY, CHI SAID, WHICH

WOULD BE PRODUCTIVE DIALOGUE AREAS:

-- VIETNAM BELIEVES U.S. ASSISTANCE TO ASEAN ON

POVERTY REDUCTION WOULD BE ESPECIALLY VALUABLE, PARTICULARLY

IMPLEMENTATION OF HANOI PLAN OF ACTION (HPA) PROGRAMS WITH

THIS AIM.

-- VIETNAM CONSIDERS U.S.-ASEAN TRADE TIES A PRIORITY,

AND HOPES THAT THE RECENT CONCLUSION OF THE BILATERAL TRADE

AGREEMENT REPRESENTS A TREND OF BROADENING TIES THAT WILL

CONTINUE. ALONG THESE LINES, CHI NOTED THAT VIETNAM ALSO

APPRECIATES U.S. SUPPORT FOR THE APPLICATIONS OF NON-WTO

ASEAN MEMBERS (INCLUDING VIETNAM) TO JOIN THE WTO.

-- VIETNAM BELIEVES DISCUSSION OF TRANSNATIONAL ISSUES

IS IMPORTANT, AND HOPES THAT COUNTER-NARCOTICS COOPERATION

BETWEEN THE U.S. AND ASEAN CAN EXPAND. A BILATERAL

COUNTERNARCOTICS AGREEMENT WOULD HELP IN THIS REGARD.

4. CHI MENTIONED THAT IT WOULD HELP VIETNAM GREATLY AS

COORDINATOR OF THE U.S.-ASEAN DIALOGUE IF FOREIGN MINISTER

NIEN COULD MEET WITH SECRETARY ALBRIGHT DURING HIS TRAVEL TO

THE U.S. FOR THE UNGA IN SEPTEMBER.

5. IN A SEPARATE AUGUST 16 MEETING WITH POLOFF, MFA ASEAN

DEPARTMENT DEPUTY DIRECTOR NGUYEN HOANG AN ECHOED THE

REQUEST FOR A NIEN MEETING WITH THE SECRETARY IN SEPTEMBER.

AN ALSO LAID OUT SPECIFIC REQUESTS FOR U.S.

INVOLVEMENT/ASSISTANCE AS PART OF ITS DIALOGUE WITH ASEAN:

-- U.S. CONTRIBUTION TO AN ASEAN HUMAN RESOURCES

DEVELOPMENT FUND

-- CONSIDERATION OF U.S. SCHOLARSHIPS FOR ASEAN

STUDENTS

-- OTHER TRAINING PROJECTS/OPPORTUNITIES

-- U.S. PARTICIPATION IN HPA INITIATIVES TO DEVELOP THE

MEKONG BASIN AND THE EAST-WEST CORRIDOR, WHICH WOULD AFFECT

VIETNAM AS WELL AS SEVERAL OTHER OF THE NEWER ASEAN MEMBERS.

PRIORITY PROJECTS ARE JOB CREATION, JOB TRAINING, AND

TECHNOLOGY TRANSFER.

6. ACTION REQUEST: REQUEST DEPARTMENT FEEDBACK ON PARAS 3

AND 5 SUGGESTIONS FOR EMBASSY TO PASS BACK TO THE MFA.

7. ACTION REQUEST CONTINUED: CHI PASSED POLCOUNS A COPY

OF A DOCUMENT, FAXED FROM THE SRV EMBASSY IN WASHINGTON,

ENTITLED "DISCUSSION PAPER FOR USTR-ASEAN TRADE AND

INVESTMENT MEETING." THE DOCUMENT, THE AGENCY ORIGIN OF

WHICH IS UNCLEAR, PROPOSES A U.S.-ASEAN DIALOGUE MEETING

WITH AN AGENDA INCLUDING WTO, APEC, REGIONAL TRADE

ARRANGEMENTS, INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY, AND BIOTECHNOLOGY, AND

SOLICITS VIETNAM\'S VIEWS ON A DATE AND VENUE FOR SUCH A

MEETING. EMBASSY WAS COMPLETELY UNAWARE OF THIS PROPOSAL

UNTIL IT WAS PASSED TO US BY OUR VIETNAMESE COUNTERPARTS.

REQUEST DEPARTMENT KEEP US INFORMED OF WASHINGTON PLANS AND

OBJECTIVES FOR THE DIALOGUE, SO THAT WE CAN BE EFFECTIVE

COORDINATORS.
