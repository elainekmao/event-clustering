SUBJECT: IRAQ: TURKEY AGREES TO US-TURKEY-KDP/PUK-ITF TALKS

- PROVIDED THEY ARE PRECEDED BY US-TURKEY BILATS; TALKS

WITH LARGER OPPOSITION LATER

REF: A. STATE 61835

B. ANKARA 1487

Classified by DCM Robert Deutsch. Reasons 1.5 b and d.

1. (C) MFA Director General for Middle Eastern Affairs Tahsin

Burcuoglu called in DCM March 10 with response to US Ref A

demarche suggesting a US-Turkey-Iraqi opposition meeting.

Burcuoglu said Turkey was agreeable to holding a round of

US-Turkey-KDP/PUK-ITF talks toward the end of this week (when

MFA Deputy U/S Ali Tuygan would be available) in order to

finalize understandings of the joint US-Turkey operations in

northern Iraq, should they end up taking place. Turkey\'s

position, he said, was that such a meeting must be preceded

by a US-Turkey bilateral to fix the structure, format and

agenda of the larger meeting. Turkey\'s reaction had been

coordinated with TGS J-3 LTG Karabay.

2. (C) Burcuoglu said the bilateral was "most important" and

would require a full day. The other parties must know, he

said, that they are not coming to convince Turkey of the

virtues of the Salahadeen meeting. They should, he said,

come prepared to agree on what they hear from the US and

Turkey. There was already a foundation for US and Turkish

presentations in the military MOU that remained on the table,

he said. Burcuoglu said Turkey needed to see the KDP, PUK

and ITF reaction to the MOU. It would be very unfortunate,

he added, if there was no agreement at the end of such a

meeting.

3. (C) Burcuoglu noted that there was already a history of

meeting with this group of five, and these were the key

parties who needed to be on board to avoid misunderstandings

in nothern Iraq. To get other groups involved, he said,

would be feasible at some point, but not for the time being.

That would be, he said, "another thing for another time." He

noted that Turkey would have its own ideas about other groups

that it would want to attend such a meeting in the future.

4. (C) As for the prospective five-way meeting, he emphasized

that Turkey wanted to be sure it would be smooth,

business-like and results-oriented. He said he looked

forward to hearing details from the US side about scheduling

both the full-day bilat and the subsequent five-way meeting.
