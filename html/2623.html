SUBJECT: COMMONWEALTH OBSERVER GROUP GIVES

THUMBS-DOWN ON ZIMBABWEAN ELECTION

CLASSIFIED BY DCM REWHITEHEAD DUE TO 1.5 (B) AND (D).

REF: HARARE 665

1. (U) AFTER A ONE-HOUR DELAY, COMMONWEALTH OBSERVER

GROUP (COG) HEAD OF MISSION ABDULSALAMI ABUBAKAR READ TO

THE ASSEMBLED PRESS AND DIPLOMATIC CORPS IN INTERIM

STATEMENT ON THE ZIMBABWEAN ELECTION THAT WAS SHORT BUT

CERTAINLY NOT SWEET TO GOZ EARS. ABUBAKAR REMARKED ON

THE COMMONWEALTH\'S ENGAGEMENT IN TRYING TO RESOLVE TO

THE ZIMBABWEAN "CRISIS" SINCE ABUJA AND SAID THAT THE

COG TERMS OF REFERENCE REQUIRED THAT IT PRONOUNCE ON THE

ELECTORAL PROCESS AND WHETHER OR NOT THE ELECTION

ALLOWED THE FREE EXPRESSION OF THE VOTERS\' WILL.

2. (U) ON THE FIRST POINT, HE SAID THAT COG HAD BEEN

DEEPLY IMPRESSED BY THE DETERMINATION OF ZIMBABWEANS TO

EXERCISE THEIR DEMOCRATIC RIGHTS UNDER "DIFFICULT

CONDITIONS." HE CONTINUED THAT A HIGH LEVEL OF

POLITICALLY MOTIVATED VIOLENCE MARRED THE PRE-ELECTION

PERIOD, MOST OF IT PERPETRATED BY SUPPORTERS/MEMBERS OF

THE RULING PARTY AGAINST SUPPORTERS/MEMBERS OF THE

OPPOSITION. HE SPECIFICALLY CITED YOUTH MILITIA

ORGANIZED BY THE GOZ OSTENSIBLY AS A TRAINING PROGRAM

THAT HAD CARRIED OUT A "SYSTEMATIC CAMPAIGN OF

INTIMIDATION AGAINST THE MDC." THIS HAD CREATED A

"CLIMATE OF FEAR AND SUSPICION."

3. (U) ABUBAKAR SAID THAT POLICE HAD NOT TAKEN ACTION

AGAINST OR INVESTIGATED INCIDENTS OF VIOLENCE AND BEEN

"HIGH-HANDED" WITH THE MDC AND "LENIENT" TO ZANU-PF.

THIS HAD ADVERSELY AFFECTED THE RULE OF LAW IN ZIMBABWE.

4. (U) HE NEXT STATED THAT THE LEGISLATIVE FRAMEWORK

FOR THE ELECTION WAS BASICALLY FLAWED. THE PUBLIC

OORDER AND SECURITY ACT IN PARTICULAR HAD PREVENTED FREE

MOVEMENT AND FREEDOM OF SPEECH AND HAD NOT ALLOWED THE

OPPOSITION TO CAMPAIGN. THE STATEMENT CITED OTHER

REGULATIONS HAD KEPT CIVIL SOCIETY FROM PLAYING ITS

NORMAL ROLE, INCLUDING UNNECESSARY RESTRICTIONS THAT

PREVENTED DEPLOYMENT OF DOMESTIC OBSERVERS. THERE WAS A

LACK OF TRANSPARENCY IN THE REGISTRATION PROCESS. THE

STATEMENT CRITICIZED THE WIDE DISCRETION GIVEN THE

REGISTRAR GENERAL AND SAID THAT THE INCUMBENT PARTY HAD

USED STATE RESOURCES FOR ITS CAMPAIGN, IN PARTICULAR THE

GOVERNMENT\'S NEAR MONOPOLY ON THE BROADCAST MEDIA.

5. (U) THE STATEMENT CONTINUED THAT MANY COULD NOT VOTE

BECAUSE OF THE REDUCTION OF POLLING STATIONS IN URBAN

AREAS. SUCH A PHENOMENON DID NOT EXIST IN RURAL AREAS.

ABUBAKAR CITED "INEXPLICABLE" DELAYS IN HONORING A HIGH

COURT RULING TO OPEN POLLING STATION FOR A THIRD DAY OF

VOTING IN HARARE AND CHITUNGWIZA. THE REPORT CONCLUDED

THAT "CONDITIONS IN ZIMBABWE DID NOT ADEQUATELY ALLOW

FREE EXPRESSION OF THE WILL OF THE ELECTORS."

6. (C) THE AUSTRALIAN HIGH COMMISSIONER AND AN

AUSTRALIAN MEMBER OF COG TOLD US THAT THE HARD-HITTING

STATEMENT HAD BEEN THE FOCUS OF INTENSE WRANGLING, WITH

NAMIBIAN AND KENYAN DELEGATES (AND TO A LESSER DEGREE

THE SOUTH AFRICANS) ALIGNED AGAINST ALL OTHER MEMBERS OF

THE DELEGATION, INCLUDING OTHER AFRICANS. THEIR

ATTEMPTS TO DELETE LANGUAGE CRITICAL OF THE GOZ FAILED

WHEN THE OTHERS THREATENED TO BRING THE ISSUE TO A VOTE

AND FORCE THEM TO WALK OUT. THE AUSTRALIAN OBSERVER

SAID THAT HE DID NOT THINK THAT IT HAD ULTIMATELY COME

TO THAT, BUT THAT CONSENSUS WAS BY NO MEANS UNIVERSAL.

HE ADDED THAT THERE WOULD BE AN EVEN MORE BRUISING

STRUGGLE OVER THE DRAFT OF THE FINAL REPORT, WHICH WILL

CONTAIN A RECOMMENDATION ABOUT WHETHER OR NOT ZIMBABWE

SHOULD BE SUSPENDED FROM THE COMMONWEALTH.

7. (C) COMMENT. DESPITE ITS INTERNAL RIFTS, THE

COMMONWEALTH HAS JOINED RANKS WITH SADC-PF AND THE

NORWEGIANS IN PUBLICLY CONDEMNING THE CONDITIONS UNDER

WHICH THE ELECTION TOOK PLACE (REFTEL), AND BY EXTENSION

CALLING INTO QUESTION ITS LEGITIMACY. THIS OUTCOME

GENERATED CONSIDERABLE EMOTION. THE AUSTRALIAN OBSERVER

FUMED THAT HE FOUND IT INCONCEIVABLE THAT SOME OF THE

AFRICAN OBSERVERS WOULD ARGUE THAT AFRICAN ELECTIONS

SHOULD BE JUDGED BY MORE LENIENT STANDARDS THAN

ELECTIONS HELD ELSEWHERE IN THE WORLD.
