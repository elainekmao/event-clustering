SUBJECT: MEDIA REACTION U. N. RACE CONFERENCE; HARARE

1. LENGTHY EDITORIALS IN TWO GOVERNMENT-CONTROLLED

NEWSPAPERS FOCUS ON THE U. N. CONFERENCE ON RACISM,

XENOPHOBIA AND INTOLERANCE TAKING PLACE IN DURBAN,

SOUTH AFRICA. EDITORIAL EXCERPTS FOLLOW:

2. UNDER HEADLINE "RESOLVE EVILS OF RACISM AT U. N.

TALKS," THE SEPTEMBER 4 EDITION OF THE DAILY "HERALD"

COMMENTS:

"THE U. N. CONFERENCE ON RACISM HAS LED. . .TO MUCH

HEAT AND SOME REGRETTABLE ACTIONS, INCLUDING

YESTERDAY\'S WITHDRAWAL BY THE U. S. AND ISRAEL. THE

TWO ITEMS THAT HAVE RAISED THE GREATEST EMOTIONS ARE

THE SLAVE TRADE FROM AFRICA THAT ENDED LAST CENTURY

AND THE ACTIONS OF ISRAEL IN THE OCCUPIED PALESTINIAN

TERRITORIES. . .THERE ARE TWO ASPECTS THAT NEED TO BE

ADDRESSED. THE FIRST IS THE FORMAL APOLOGY FROM THOSE

COUNTRIES THAT PRACTICED THE TRADE. . .THE SECOND

ASPECT IS TRYING TO PUT RIGHT SOME OF THE LINGERING

EFFECTS OF THE TRADE. WHETHER THESE EFFORTS ARE

CALLED \'REPARATIONS\' OR WHETHER THEY ARE CALLED \'EXTRA

DEVELOPMENT AID\' IS LESS IMPORTANT THAN A REALISTIC

EFFORT TO END THE UNDERDEVELOPMENT OF AFRICA. THE

GENERAL FEELING THAT ISRAEL IS PRACTICING A FORM OF

APARTHEID IN PALESTINE HAS TO BE HONESTLY

ADDRESSED. . .ALL FORMS OF DISCRIMINATION BASED ON

RACE, RELIGION, CULTURE, NATIONALITY AND THE LIKE ARE

EVIL. ALL MUST BE CONDEMNED. . .SURELY THE MOST VITAL

PART OF THE U. N. CONFERENCE IS FACING DOWN HORRORS

THAT HAVE DONE SO MUCH HARM TO PEOPLE, BOTH TO THE

VICTIMS AND, MORALLY, TO THE VICTIMIZERS. AND SURELY

SUCCESS AT THE CONFERENCE IS TO RECOGNIZE THE EVILS OF

RACISM AND XENOPHOBIA IN ALL THEIR FORMS, AND TO

AGAIN BE TOLERATED. . . ."

3. UNDER HEADLINE "U. S. ATTITUDE SMACKS OF DIPLOMATIC

ARROGANCE," THE SEPTEMBER 2 EDITION OF THE WEEKLY

"SUNDAY MAIL" COMMENTS:

"NOT SO LONG AGO, AMERICAN SECRETARY OF STATE, GENERAL

COLLIN (SIC) POWELL WAS IN SOUTHERN AFRICA,

PONTIFICATING ABOUT DEMOCRACY, TRANSPARENCY AND MANY

OTHER \'FREEDOMS.\' HE WAS FETED IN SOME QUARTERS WHERE

WHAT HE SAID WAS TAKEN AS A FITTING LECTURE TO

AFRICA\'S \'WAYWARD LEADERS.\' WE, AMONG OTHERS,

DISMISSED HIM AS AN \'UNCLE TOM. . .\' NOW HE HAS

FAILED TO SHOW UP IN DURBAN FOR THE WORLD CONFERENCE

ON RACISM, XENOPHOBIA AND INTOLERANCE. THIS WAS

CONFIRMATION THAT NEITHER POWELL NOR THE UNITED STATES

GOVERNMENT IS FIT TO GIVE AFRICA ANY LECTURE ON

DEMOCRACY AND TOLERANCE, WHEN THEY CANNOT TOLERATE

DIVERGENT VIEWS. IN FUTURE DEALINGS WITH DEVELOPING

COUNTRIES IN GENERAL AND AFRICA IN PARTICULAR, THE

UNITED STATES AND SPECIFICALLY POWELL, WILL FACE A

SERIOUS CREDIBILITY CRISIS. IT HAS BECOME OBVIOUS

THAT THEY PREACH THINGS THAT THEY DO NOT PRACTICE

THEMSELVES. THE CREDIBILITY CRISIS WILL ALSO EXTEND

TO U. N. SECRETARY GENERAL, KOFFI ANNAN. ALREADY

DELEGATES TO THE CONFERENCE ARE QUESTIONING IF THEIR

RESOLUTIONS WILL BE TAKEN SERIOUSLY BY THE U. N. GIVEN

THE EXTENT TO WHICH IT PANDERS TO AMERICAN FOREIGN

POLICY AND

INTERESTS. . .THE DEMONSTRATIONS GOING ON IN DURBAN

ARE ALSO A SHARP INDICTMENT ON THE FOREIGN POLICIES OF

THE U. S. AND ITS ALLIES. WE NOW WONDER HOW THE U. S.

PLANS TO DEAL WITH THE ISSUE OF SLAVERY AND

COLONIALISM WHEN IT SENDS A JUNIOR DELEGATION THAT IS

ONLY MATCHED BY THAT OF ISRAEL. THE ISSUE OF ZIONISM

BEING EQUATED TO RACISM, WHICH HAS CAUSED SUCH A

FURORE, IS AN ISSUE THAT WILL FOR LONG CONTINUE TO BE

DEBATED AT VARIOUS FORUMS, WITH OR WITHOUT THE CONSENT

OF THE UNITED STATES. WE DO NOT SEE WHY IT SHOULD NOT

REMAIN ON THE AGENDA IN DURBAN. IT IS DEBATABLE. .

.BUT THE FACT THAT THE U. S. CAN DECREE WHAT THE WORLD

SHOULD AND SHOULD NOT DISCUSS IS REALLY THE HEIGHT OF

DIPLOMATIC ARROGANCE. . . ."
