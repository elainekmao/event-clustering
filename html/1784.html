1. (U) AMBASSADOR HOOKS ATTENDED A DINNER ON JUNE 26 HOSTED BY

HIS SWEDISH COUNTERPART, LENNARTH HJELMAKER, IN HONOR OF VISITING

EU GREAT LAKES ENVOY ALDO AJELLO. ALSO IN ATTENDANCE WERE UNDP

RESIDENT REPRESENTATIVE VICTOR ANGELO AND THE CHIEFS OF MISSION

FROM AUSTRALIA, DENMARK, NORWAY, AND CANADA, AS WELL AS AJELLO\'S

TWO ASSISTANTS.

ZIMBABWE

--------

2. (C) AMBASSADOR HJELMAKER INFORMED THE GROUP THAT AJELLO AND HE

HAD BEEN INSTRUCTED TO DELIVER TO THE GOZ A LETTER FROM THE EU

GENERAL AFFAIRS COUNCIL EXPRESSING UNHAPPINESS OVER THE LACK OF

PROGRESS IN THE ARTICLE 8 DIALOGUE (REFTELS). THE SWEDISH

AMBASSADOR UNDERSCORED THE FEELING OF DEEP FRUSTRATION FELT BY EU

MINISTERS GATHERED IN BRUSSELS, AMONG WHOM THERE HAD BEEN COMPLETE

UNANIMITY ON THE NEED TO SEND THE LETTER AND TO IMPOSE A TWO-MONTH

DEADLINE FOR ACHIEVEMENT OF SATISFACTORY PROGRESS. THE GOVERNMENT

OF ZIMBABWE, HE SAID, MUST MEET THE CONDITIONS IN THE LETTER

WITHIN TWO MONTHS OR THE EU WILL ALMOST CERTAINLY MOVE TO ARTICLE

96. HE NOTED THAT EU AMBASSADORS IN HARARE PREFER A MORE MEASURED

APPROACH, BUT THE MINISTERS ARE DETERMINED TO TAKE A TOUGHER TACK.

HJELMAKER AND AJELLO PLANNED TO PRESENT THE LETTER TO PRESIDENT

MUGABE THE FOLLOWING DAY, ON JUNE 27.

DRC

---

3. (C) AJELLO REPORTED THAT HE HAD MET ON JUNE 25 WITH FM

MUDENGE, WHO FOCUSED ON DEVELOPMENTS IN THE DRC AND AVOIDED

DISCUSSION OF ZIMBABWE\'S INTERNAL SITUATION. MUDENGE CRITICIZED

THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY FOR LETTING DOWN THE DRC AND DISMISSED

EFFORTS TO ARRANGE THE NATIONAL DIALOGUE, SAYING THEY WOULD NOT

SUCCEED. WHEN AJELLO INQUIRED ABOUT KETUMILE MASIRE\'S RECENT

VISIT, MUDENGE BURST OUT LAUGHING. ASKED WHY HE WAS LAUGHING,

MUDENGE CONTINUED GUFFAWING, BUT FINALLY COMPOSED HIMSELF ENOUGH

TO COMMENT THAT MASIRE DOES NOT KNOW HOW TO MANAGE THE NATIONAL

DIALOGUE, AND IT WOULD FAIL. MUDENGE INSISTED THAT ZIMBABWE IS

NOT REQUIRED TO WITHDRAW ITS TROOPS BECAUSE THEY WERE INVITED INTO

THE DRC. AJELLO HAD BEEN TOLD HE COULD MEET ON JUNE 26 WITH

PRESIDENT MUGABE BUT WAS KEPT WAITING ALL DAY. THE MUGABE MEETING

WAS RESCHEDULED FOR THE MORNING OF JUNE 27.

4. (C) SHARING HIS OWN VIEWS ON THE DRC, AJELLO SAID KABILA AND

HIS ALLIES ARE DELIBERATELY PUSHING NEGATIVE FORCES (RWANDAN EX-

FAR/INTERAHAMWE) TO THE EAST. HE REJECTED MUDENGE\'S CONTENTION

REGARDING THE WITHDRAWAL OF ZIMBABWEAN TROOPS, SAYING THE FOREIGN

MINISTER SEEMED TO HAVE OVERLOOKED THE FACT THAT HIS GOVERNMENT

SIGNED THE LUSAKA ACCORDS. REGARDING THE NATIONAL DIALOGUE, THE EU

ENVOY DECLARED THAT MASIRE IS GOING ABOUT IT THE WRONG WAY AND

REFUSES TO ACCEPT ANY ADVICE. HE LAMENTED THAT THE PROCESS COULD

DRAG ON FOR YEARS. TWO FUNDAMENTAL OBSTACLES TO THE NATIONAL

DIALOGUE, ACCORDING TO AJELLO, ARE THAT THE REBELS WILL NOT ACCEPT

KABILA AS PRESIDENT, AND KABILA WILL NOT COMMIT POLITICAL SUICIDE

BY ENGAGING TOO SERIOUSLY.

COMMENT

-------

5. (C) MUDENGE\'S REPORTED CLAIM THAT ZIMBABWE IS NOT REQUIRED TO

WITHDRAW ITS TROOPS IS TROUBLING AND REFLECTS A DELIBERATE

MISINTERPRETATION OF THE LUSAKA ACCORDS. HIS REMARK, HOWEVER, IS

CONSISTENT WITH RECENT COMMENTS WE HAVE HEARD FROM OTHER GOZ

OFFICIALS, INCLUDING SENIOR DEFENSE FORCE LEADERS. THEIR REMARKS

SUGGEST A DETERMINATION TO LEAVE BEHIND AT LEAST A SMALL MILITARY

CONTINGENT TO PROTECT ZIMBABWEAN ECONOMIC INTERESTS, IRRESPECTIVE

OF THE WITHDRAWAL OF THE OTHER ALLIED FORCES OR THE LANGUAGE

CONTAINED IN THE LUSAKA ACCORDS. ZIMBABWE IS ALSO RELUCTANT TO

GIVE UP ITS INFLUENCE OVER THE NEW KABILA REGIME, SOMETHING ITS

TROOP PRESENCE ENSURES. THAT SAID, WE REMAIN CONVINCED THAT THE

GOZ HOPES TO BRING HOME MOST OF ITS TROOPS PRIOR TO THE

PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION EXPECTED IN EARLY 2002. AJELLO WAS

CONTEMPTUOUS OF MASIRE\'S APPROACH TO THE NATIONAL DIALOGUE AND HAD

NO EXPECTATION THAT HIS EFFORTS WOULD BEAR FRUIT.
