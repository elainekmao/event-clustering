SUBJECT: IRAQI OPPOSITION TALKS IN ANAKARA: TURKS ON BOARD

FOR THREE SETS OF TALKS

REF: A. STATE 66506

B. ANKARA 1512

C. ANKARA 1487

(U) Classified by DCM Robert S. Deutsch. Reasons 1.5(b) and

(d).

1. This is an action request. Please see paragraph 5.

2. (S) On March 13, DCM met MFA Director General for the

Middle East Burcuoglu and delivered Ref A points. Burcuoglu

agreed to the following schedule:

-- US-TU bilaterals, with Turkey represented by Deputy U/S

Tuygan, at 17:00 on March 14, followed by a March 15 session

from 10:00 to 13:00. A TGS representative, as well as other

Turkish representatives, are TBD. DCM committed to informing

the Turks as soon as possible regarding the identity of

representatives from OVP and CENTCOM.

-- US-TU-KDP-PUK-ITF talks on March 18. The GOT proposes

that it inform the KDP, PUK, and ITF to prepare to meet in

Ankara on that day. However, the Turks intend to await the

outcome of the bilats before issuing formal invitations to

the other three parties.

-- Talks with the wider Iraqi opposition late on March 18 or,

more probably, on March 19. As with the earlier March 18

five-party meeting, the GOT intends to inform the wider

opposition of the possibility of a meeting, but will refrain

from issuing formal invitations until the bilats are

underway, at the earliest.

3. (S) Burcuoglu questioned whether the KDP, PUK, and ITF

would be prepared to meet with the wider opposition, and

suggested that we discuss this issue further at the bilats.

The GOT is not willing to present the meeting with the wider

Iraqi opposition as a meeting with the "Leadership Committee"

unless an ITF representative has become a committee member.

Burcuoglu said that if the ITF were represented on the

Leadership Committee by March 18, it would "facilitate

everything.8

4. (S) DCM forcefully reminded Burcuoglu that, in the

absence of Turkish parliamentary permission for a US troop

deployment in Turkey, we cannot present positions to the

Kurds based on the military MOU or the economic and political

joint statements. Moreover, DCM reminded Burcuoglu of the

USG,s firm opposition to a unilateral Turkish deployment in

northern Iraq. He stressed that in the meetings we would be

focused on ending provocations and avoiding conflict.

Burcuoglu responded that the GOT would tell the Kurds that if

Turkish troops were to enter Iraq, it would only be on the

basis of coordination with the USG. He acknowledged that the

GOT fully understands the USG position on Turkish troops in

northern Iraq.

5. (S) Action Requested: Please advise on the remaining

composition of the delegation, including an authoritative

CENTCOM participant. We will also need resolution of the ITF

membership in the Leadership Committee issue now.

PEARSON
