1. (C) SUMMARY: ACCORDING TO THE ZIMBABWEAN FOREIGN

MINISTER, THE APRIL 21 VICTORIA FALLS MEETING WAS

DESIGNED AS A SUMMIT OF THE HEADS OF STATE OF THE

EXTERNAL STATE ACTORS TO DISCUSS WAYS OF EXTRICATING

THEMSELVES FROM THE CONGO. THE CONGOLESE WERE NOT

INVITED. AT THE LAST MOMENT, DOS SANTOS COULD NOT

ATTEND. BECAUSE OF HIS PENDING INAUGURATION, KAGAME

WAS ALSO ABSENT BUT HAD SENT BOTH HIS FOREIGN

MINISTER AND EMMANUEL GASANA. THE ZIMBABWEAN FOREIGN

MINISTER DESCRIBED THE MEETINGS AS REMARKABLY FRANK

AND FRIENDLY. HE REPORTED THAT THE DISCUSSION

CENTERED ON TWO ISSUES THAT WERE VIEWED AS CENTRAL TO

A RESOLUTION OF THE DRC CONFLICT: THE DISARMAMENT OF

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 02200 01 OF 05 221924Z

THE EX-FAR/INTERHAMWE, AND THE MOUNTING OF THE

SUCCESSFUL MASIRE-FACILITATED INTER-CONGOLESE

DIALOGUE. THE VIC FALLS PARTICIPANTS ALSO DISCUSSED

WAYS OF SELF-ENFORCING THE KAMPALA CEASE-FIRE

AGREEMENT PENDING SECOND PHASE MONUC DEPLOYMENT AND,

IN PARTICULAR, OF INSURING THAT NEITHER STATE ARMIES

NOR NON-SIGNATORY FORCES WOULD VIOLATE THE 30

KILOMETER DEMILITARIZED ZONE. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) ON APRIL 22,2000, S/E WOLPE, ACCOMPANIED BY

LT. COL. GADDIS, SPECIAL ADVISOR PRENDERGAST, THE

AMBASSADOR AND ECONOFF (NOTETAKER), MET FOR TWO HOURS

WITH ZIMBABWEAN FOREIGN MINISTER STAN MUDENGE TO

REVIEW RECENT DEVELOPMENTS IN THE LUSAKA PEACE

PROCESS AND THE RECENT POLITICAL DETERIORATION IN

ZIMBABWE. THE EXTENDED AND AT TIMES EMOTIONAL

EXCHANGE ON ZIMBABWE\'S DOMESTIC SITUATION IS REPORTED

SEPTEL.

WOLPE: LOCKING IN THE KAMPALA AGREEMENT

---------------------------------------

PURPOSE OF HIS CURRENT MISSION, MAKING THE FOLLOWING

POINTS:

BRINGING ABOUT THE RECENT KAMPALA CEASE-

THE TRUE STORY OF VICTORIA FALLS

--------------------------------

3. (C) FOLLOWING S/E WOLPE\'S EXPLANATION OF THE

PURPOSE OF HIS CURRENT MISSION TO REGIONAL CAPITALS

-- TO STRESS THE IMPORTANCE OF FULL COMPLIANCE WITH

THE RECENT CEASE-FIRE/DISENGAGEMENT AGREEMENTS

REACHED AT KAMPALA, AND TO EXPLORE WITH REGIONAL

LEADERS WAYS OF ACCELERATING THE FULL IMPLEMENTATION

OF THE LUSAKA ACCORD -- MUDENGE GAVE A READ-OUT OF

THE PREVIOUS DAY\'S VICTORIA FALLS MEETING. MUDENGE

SAID THAT THE VIC FALLS GATHERING WAS THE OUTGROWTH

OF CONVERSATIONS THAT HAD OCCURRED ON THE MARGINS OF

THE CAIRO MEETING BETWEEN CHISSANO, DOS SANTOS,

MUSEVENI, AND MUGABE. ALL AGREED IT WAS TIME FOR THE

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 02200 02 OF 05 221924Z

EXTERNAL PARTIES TO SIT DOWN AND DISCUSS WAYS OF

ENDING THE WAR AND GETTING OUT OF THE DRC. ALL OF

THE EXTERNAL LUSAKA SIGNATORIES -- BUT NO CONGOLESE -

- WERE INVITED TO VICTORIA FALLS. UNFORTUNATELY, AT

THE LAST MINUTE DOS SANTOS COULD NOT COME. KAGAME,

WHO WAS DEALING WITH HIS INAUGURAL PREPARATIONS,

APOLOGIZED FOR HIS ABSENCE, AND HAD SENT BOTH HIS

FOREIGN MINISTER AND EMMANUEL GASANA. KAGAME HAD IN

FACT INDICATED HIS AVAILABILITY FOR AN EXTERNAL HEADS

OF STATE MEETING IF IT WERE HELD ON A DIFFERENT DAY,

BUT IT WAS DECIDED TO GO AHEAD AS SCHEDULED. IT WAS

MOZAMBICAN PRESIDENT CHISSANO (CURRENTLY THE HEAD OF

SADC) WHO FACILITATED THE VIC FALLS DISCUSSION.

4. (C) MUDENGE SAID THE MEETING HAD NO AGENDA OTHER

THAN A DISCUSSION OF WHAT WAS REQUIRED FOR ALL OF THE

EXTERNAL PARTIES TO GET OUT OF THE CONGO. HE

DESCRIBED THE MEETING AS EXCEPTIONALLY FRANK AND

FRIENDLY -- THE FRIENDLIEST SUCH GATHERING HE HAD

EVER OBSERVED. HE REGRETTED KAGAME\'S ABSENCE --

WHICH HE EMPHASIZED WAS FOR "VERY UNDERSTANDABLE

REASONS" -- BECAUSE HE WOULD HAVE LIKED THE RWANDAN

PRESIDENT TO PERSONALLY EXPERIENCE THE REMARKABLE

CANDOR AND POSITIVE ATMOSPHERICS OF THE SESSION.

ACCORDING TO MUDENGE, ALL APPEARED INTENT ON BEING AS

HONEST AS POSSIBLE, ON LAYING OUT THEIR BOTTOM LINES,

ON FINDING A WAY OUT OF THE CONGO.

RWANDA SHOULD BE CONSIDERED. THE KEY, HE SAID, WAS

THE NEED TO "SMASH THE SUPPORTIVE STRUCTURES" OF THE

EX-FAR/INTERAHAMWE, SOMETHING WITH WHICH HE AND

PATRICK MAZIMHAKA HAD AGREED AT THE TIME OF THE

SIGNING OF THE LUSAKA AGREEMENT.

10. (C) THE FOREIGN MINISTER SAID THAT MUCH

CONVERSATION CENTERED ON HOW TO IMPLEMENT THE KAMPALA

DECISION THAT THE COMBATANTS WOULD PULL BACK FIFTEEN

KILOMETERS ON EITHER SIDE, SO AS TO ESTABLISH A

THIRTY KILOMETER DEMILITARIZED ZONE. IT WAS AGREED

THAT THIS ZONE HAD TO BE TOTALLY FREE NOT ONLY OF

STATE MILITARY FORCES BUT ALSO OF THE EX-

FAR/INTERAHAMWE AND OTHER SUCH ELEMENTS.. EVERY

PARTY HAD TO ASSUME RESPONSIBILITY THROUGH MECHANISMS

SUCH AS THE RJMC\'S OR JOINT PATROLS, FOR WHICH

RESOURCES ARE NEEDED -- PARTICULARLY IF MONUC PHASE

TWO DEPLOYMENT SHOULD BE FURTHER DELAYED. MUDENGE

NOTED THAT IT WAS STILL NECESSARY TO SECURE KABILA\'S

COOPERATION WITH THIS SELF-POLICING EFFORT.

11. (C) MUDENGE OBSERVED THAT THIS TASK OF SELF-

POLICING WAS MADE MORE DIFFICULT BY THE CONTINUING

REFUSAL OF RWANDA AND RCD-GOMA TO PARTICIPATE IN THE

REGIONAL JMC\'S. ACCORDING TO MUDENGE, RWANDA AND THE

RCD-GOMA HAVE BLAMED A LACK OF RESOURCES FOR THEIR

NON-PARTICIPATION, AS WELL AS THEIR LACK OF FAITH IN

SELF-MONITORING MECHANISMS. BUT, WHATEVER THE

REASON, IT WAS CAUSING DIFFICULTIES ON THE GROUND.

PRENDERGAST AND WOLPE UNDERTOOK TO DISCUSS THIS

SUBJECT IN KIGALI.

12. (C) MUDENGE SAID THAT ALL OF THE LEADERS AT

VICTORIA FALLS PROFESSED CURRENT SATISFACTION WITH

THE CEASE-FIRE OBSERVANCE. ACCORDING TO MUDENGE, THE

ONLY NEGATIVE NOTE WAS SOUNDED BY ZIMBABWEAN DEFENSE

MINISTER MAHACHI WHO CLAIMED THAT THERE HAD BEEN AN

ATTACK ON IKELA AFTER THE APRIL 14 CEASE-FIRE

IMPLEMENTATION DATE. MUSEVENI SAID HE THOUGHT

MAHACHI MAY HAVE BEEN REFERRING TO AN ATTACK THAT HAD

OCCURRED PRIOR TO THE CEASE-FIRE DEADLINE. MAHACHI,

HOWEVER, INSISTED THAT THERE WAS A LATER ATTACK THAT

WAS A VIOLATION OF THE CEASE-FIRE AGREEMENT.
