2. DURING A MEETING WITH PRESIDENT TUDJMAN, DEFMIN

SUSAK, AND DEPUTY FM SANADER, ON OTHER SUBJECTS

(SEPTEL), AMB. GALBRAITH MADE TWO OTHER POINTS:

-----------------------------------------

CRIMINAL ACTS OF CROATIAN OCCUPYING TROOPS

-----------------------------------------

3. AMB. GALBRAITH SAID THAT THE DESECRATION OF GRAVES

HE HAD WITNESSED IN KASIC (REFTEL), EVIDENTLY COMMITTED

BY CROATIAN TROOPS WHO OCCUPIED THE VILLAGE FOR 14

MONTHS, WAS HORRENDOUS AND UNACCEPTABLE. BOTH CROATIAN

OFFICIALS SOUGHT TO DENY CROATIAN RESPONSIBILITY, THEN

TO ARGUE THAT SERBS HAD DONE MUCH WORSE THINGS AND ON A

LARGER SCALE, TO CROATS. THE AMBASSADOR RESPONDED THAT

THIS SORT OF DEFENSE WAS LIKE COMPARING THE ACTIONS OF

A LEGITIMATE POLICE FORCE TO THE MAFIA; AS A WESTERN

DEMOCRATIC COUNTRY, CROATIA MUST EXPECT TO BE HELD TO A

HIGH STANDARD. MOREOVER, SUCH ACTIONS, AS A PRACTICAL

MATTER, CREATED ILL WILL THAT WOULD MAKE REINTEGRATION

MORE DIFFICULT WHEN THE TIME CAME. GALBRAITH SUGGESTED

THAT SUSAK CONSIDER GIVING SPECIAL TRAINING TO CROATIAN

TROOPS TO SENSITIZE THEM ON THESE ISSUES.

--------------------

HANDLING OF REFUGEES

--------------------

4. AMB. GALBRAITH ALSO INQUIRED ABOUT REPORTS THAT

SOME 450 REFUGEES FROM THE BANJA LUKA AREA OF BOSNIA-

HERZEGOVINA HAD BEEN HELD AT THE CONFRONTATION LINE (IN

UNPA SECTOR WEST), REFUSED CROATIAN PERMISSION TO

ENTER. NONE OF THE CROATIAN OFFICIALS PRESENT WAS

FAMILIAR WITH THE CASE. PRES. TUDJMAN MADE A PHONE

CALL AND REPORTED THAT THOSE REFUGEES FROM THE GROUP

WHO HAD MEDICAL PROBLEMS HAD BEEN ADMITTED IMMEDIATELY.

THE OTHERS WERE BEING HELD TEMPORARILY, PENDING FINDING

PLACES FOR THEM TO GO. THIS WAS NECESSARY BECAUSE THE

CONTINUING HEAVY FLOW OF REFUGEES HAD STRAINED

CROATIA\'S CAPACITY TO ABSORB THEM. NEVERTHELESS,

TUDJMAN ASSURED THE AMBASSADOR THE CROATIAN GOVERNMENT

HAD NO INTENTION OF NOT ADMITTING THESE PEOPLE. "HELP

US END THIS WAR," HE SAID, SO THAT SUCH PROBLEMS WOULD

NOT OCCUR.
