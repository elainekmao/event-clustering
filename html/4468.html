SUBJECT: EMBASSY EFFORTS TO STOP TURKISH ATROPINE EXPORTS

TO IRAQ (S)

REF: A. STATE 222715

B. STATE 172843

C. ANKARA 8314

D. ANKARA 6489

Classified by DCM Robert Deutsch. Reasons 1.5 B and D.

1. (S) Post has expended substantial and sustained efforts to

encourage the GOT to shut off potential for Iraqi acquisition

of atropine from Turkey. We offer the following chronology

of our efforts:

(S) May 2002 - Regional Affaires (RA) Office begins work on

issue with GOT interlocutors. Information exchange on-going

to present time.

(S) August 28 ) U/S Grossman raises with U/S Ziyal in

Washington.

(S) First week of Sept. ) DCM raises with MFA DG Kurttekin.

(S) Sept. 10 ) Political Military Affairs (PMA) officer

raises with MFA Iraq Desk, passes points including name and

details of suspect Turkish firm per State 172843. MFA says

it will investigate. ECON also delivers State 172843 points

to MFA DG for Bilateral Economic Affairs.

(S) Between Sept. 10 and Nov. 7 PMA made at least three

follow-up calls to MFA. No further information was provided.

(S) Oct. 30 ) DCM raises with MFA DG Burcuoglu who, holding

up his daily to-do list, says the issue is at top of his

agenda.

(S) Nov. 7 ) PMA Raises again with MFA DDG for Middle East

per State 222715. Gets no information but promise that

results of ongoing investigation will be passed to us. ECON

raises with Acting DG for Agreements at Undersecretariat of

Foreign Trade. Gets no information.

(U) Nov. 12 ) NYT runs story.

(S) Nov. 14 ) PMA raises with MFA Disarmament Department

Head. He too was unable to provide any information.

(S) Nov. 15 - PMA called into the MFA by Iraq Department

Head, who provides info contained Ref C confirming that the

first week of August, 44,000 atropine autoinjectors were sent

from Turkey to Iraq, and stating no shipments had been made

afterwards. MFA stated that the effort to move the atropine

had stopped completely after the MFA took action in late

August/early September. If there had been any attempt

afterward, word had not reached the MFA.

2. (S) Post intends to continue pressing the GOT for more

information on this issue, using any available information.

PEARSON
