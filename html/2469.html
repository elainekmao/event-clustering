SUBJECT: FIFTYFOURTH CASE OF BSE IN ITALY

1. ON FEBRUARY 5, ITALY\'S HEALTH AUTHORITIES

REACTED TO THE FIRST SUSPECTED CASE OF THE

HUMAN VERSION OF MAD COW DISEASE, NEW VARIANT

CREUTZFELDT-JAKOB DISEASE (NVCJD). THE

PATIENT IS A 25 YEAR OLD SICILIAN WOMAN FROM

TRAPANI. ITALY\'S MINISTER OF HEALTH GIROLAMO

SIRCHIA AND THE MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE

GIANNI ALEMANNO BOTH APPEARED ON TELEVISION

NEWS WITH ASSERTIONS THAT MEAT IN ITALY IS

SAFE NOW, THAT THE SYSTEMS TO CHECK BSE ARE

IN PLACE AND ADEQUATE, AND THAT THIS INCIDENT

IS A CASE THAT HAS HAD A LONG INCUBATION

PERIOD. THE QUESTION OF THE DAY IS HOW THE

ITALIAN PUBLIC WILL RECEIVE THESE MESSAGES.

ITALIANS\' LEVEL OF TRUST IN GOVERNMENT AND

GOI EFFORTS TO ASSURE THE PUBLIC THAT IT IS

SAFEGUARDING HEALTH, MAY SUFFER FROM THIS

DEVELOPMENT.

2. THERE CERTAINLY IS THE POSSIBILITY THAT

THE AFFLICTED WOMAN CONTRACTED THE DISEASE

FROM MEAT CONSUMED IN ITALY. GOI STATEMENTS

FOCUS ON THE FACT THAT THE CONTAGION MIGHT

HAVE BEEN PRESENT IN ITALY AT A TIME WHEN NEW

VARIANT CREUTZFELDT-JAKOB WAS UNKNOWN AND

PRECAUTIONS NOW TAKEN TO CONTROL THE DISEASE

IN CATTLE HAD NOT YET BEEN TAKEN. THE FIRST

ITALIAN COW TESTING POSITIVE TO BOVINE

SPONGIFORM ENCEPHALOPATHY (BSE) WAS

DISCOVERED IN JANUARY, 2001. TESTING IN

ITALY HAS BEEN OBLIGATORY SINCE THEN.

3. THE AFFLICTED WOMAN SUSPECTED AS ITALY\'S

FIRST HUMAN CASE OF NVCJD WAS ADMITTED TO THE

HOSPITAL ABOUT ONE MONTH AGO. HER DIAGNOSIS

WAS CONFIRMED BY TESTS CONDUCTED AT THE

LONDON COLLEGE OF MEDICINE, BUT ONLY POST

MORTEM TESTS ARE CONCLUSIVE IN CASES OF

NVCJD, WHICH IS CONSIDERED FATAL. IF THE

ITALIAN PATIENT SUCCUMBS TO WHAT TURNS OUT TO

BE NVCJD, THIS WILL BE THE FIRST CONFIRMED

HUMAN CASE TO HAVE OCCURRED IN ITALY.

ALTHOUGH OTHER SUSPECTED CASES HAVE BEEN

REPORTED PREVIOUSLY IN THE ITALIAN PRESS,

NONE HAVE BEEN CONFIRMED. SINCE ITS

DISCOVERY IN 1986, APPROXIMATELY 100 DEATHS,

MORE THAN NINETY FIVE PERCENT OF WHICH HAVE

OCCURRED IN THE UNITED KINGDOM, HAVE BEEN

CONFIRMED FROM THE DISEASE.

4. SCIENTIFIC EVIDENCE SUPPORTS A CAUSAL

RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN BSE AND NVCJD BUT LITTLE

IS KNOWN ABOUT THE INCUBATION PERIOD BETWEEN

EXPOSURE TO THE INFECTIVE AGENT AND THE

EMERGENCE OF SYMPTOMS. PARTICULARLY IN ITALY,

MISCONCEPTIONS ABOUT BSE AND CONFUSION OF THE

DISEASE WITH THE UNRELATED FOOT AND MOUTH

DISEASE (FMD) IN CATTLE, CONTRIBUTE TO PUBLIC

SUSCEPTIBILITY TO SENSATIONAL JOURNALISM ON

ANIMAL DISEASE AND ITS CONNECTIONS TO HUMAN

HEALTH. ITALIANS DO NOT HAVE A WELL GROUNDED

TRUST IN THEIR AUTHORITIES AND THEY ARE NOT

INCLINED TO TURN TO THE GOI TO BRING THEM

RELIABLE INFORMATION. BY WAY OF CONTRAST, IN

THE U.S. RECENT SCIENTIFIC INFORMATION

INCLUDING THE CONCLUSIONS OF THE HARVARD RISK

ASSESSMENT ON BSE, IS PUBLISHED ON THE

WEBSITES OF THE CENTER FOR DISEASE CONTROL,

THE FOOD AND DRUG ADMINISTRATION, AND THE US

DEPARTMENT OF AGRICULTURE, ALL OF WHICH ARE

FREQUENTLY USED BY THE PUBLIC.
