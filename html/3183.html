SUBJECT: ITALIAN RESPONSE OF GOVERNMENT-LEVEL CONSULTATIONS

ON THE INTERNATIONAL SPACE STATION PROGRAM

REF: STATE 104344

1. MISSION DISCUSSED REFTEL TALKING POINTS WITH COUNSELOR

GUICCIARDI OF THE ITALIAN MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS

DEPARTMENT OF SCIENTIFIC COOPERATION. ALTHOUGH GUICCIARDI

DEMONSTRATED A GENERAL UNDERSTANDING OF THE EUROPEAN

PARTNER\'S REQUEST FOR A GOVERNMENT-LEVEL MEETING IN THE

UNITED STATES ON THE INTERNATIONAL SPACE STATION (ISS), HE

CLAIMED THAT THE ISSUE IS BEING HANDLED ENTIRELY BY THE

ITALIAN EMBASSY IN WASHINGTON. THEREFORE, ALL INQUIRIES

REGARDING THE TIMING OF SUCH A MEETING SHOULD BE PRESENTED TO

THE ITALIAN EMBASSY OR TO THE GERMAN AMBASSADOR TO THE UNITED

STATES, WHO IS ACTING AS THE COORDINATOR FOR THE MEMBERS OF

THE EUROPEAN PARTNER GROUP. AS A COURTESY, ESTOFFS ALSO

BRIEFED PROFESSOR SERGIO VETRELLA, PRESIDENT OF THE ITALIAN

SPACE AGENCY (ASI) DURING A MEETING ON JUNE 6, 2002.

VETRELLA DECLARED HIS BELIEF THAT THE PROPOSED MEETING WOULD

BE A "WASTE OF TIME" SINCE HE PERCEIVES THAT THE POLITICAL

CLIMATE IN THE UNITED STATES WOULD PREVENT ANY REAL MOVEMENT

ON THE ISSUE OF ISS FINANCING.
