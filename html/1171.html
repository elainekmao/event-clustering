SUBJECT: CLIMATE CHANGE: ENVIRONMENT MINISTRY FUNDS

PROJECTS ABROAD

REF: THE HAGUE 1144

1. DUTCH ENVIRONMENT MINISTER PRONK TOLD THE DUTCH

PARLIAMENT ON JUNE 20 THAT THE GOVERNMENT HAD DECIDED

THAT HIS MINISTRY, RATHER THAN THE DEVELOPMENT

COOPERATION MINISTRY, SHOULD FINANCE CLIMATE CHANGE

PROJECTS IN DEVELOPING COUNTRIES. ACCORDING TO PRONK,

THIS REQUIRES A CHANGE IN THE GOVERNMENT COALITION

ACCORD OF 1998.

2. THE 1998 COALITION ACCORD STIPULATED THAT THE

NETHERLANDS MUST REALIZE HALF OF ITS GHG REDUCTIONS

THROUGH USE OF FLEXIBLE INSTRUMENTS ABROAD. FOR CLEAN

DEVELOPMENT MECHANISM (CDM) PROJECTS, THE GOVERNMENT

HAD SET ASIDE SOME 250 MILLION DOLLARS FOR 2001 AND

2002, WHICH HAD TO COME FROM THE DEVELOPMENT ASSISTANCE

BUDGET.

3. PRONK, WHO AT THE TIME OF THE COALITION AGREEMENT

WAS MINISTER FOR DEVELOPMENT COOPERATION, SHARPLY

OPPOSED USING AID FUNDS FOR ENVIRONMENTAL PROJECTS. SO

DID ALL EU MEMBER-STATES.

4. IT NOW SEEMS THAT, AFTER TWO YEARS, THE GOVERNMENT

HAS GIVEN IN TO NATIONAL AND INTERNATIONAL CRITICISM.

DUTCH FINANCE MINISTER ZALM WILL GIVE PRONK AN EXTRA

250 MILLION DOLLARS, TO BE FINANCED FROM CURRENT DUTCH

BUDGET SURPLUSES.

5. DUTCH NEWSPAPERS COMMENT THAT THE MEASURE WAS TAKEN

TO ENHANCE PRONK\'S CREDIBILITY AS CHAIRMAN OF COP-6.

PRONK TOLD PARLIAMENT THAT HE WANTS TO KEEP "ALL CARDS

ON THE TABLE AND ALL BALLS IN THE AIR" AT THE

CONFERENCE. HE SAID THAT HE WANTS AS MANY COUNTRIES AS

POSSIBLE TO AGREE ON CONCRETE MEASURES TO PROTECT THE

CLIMATE, AND, ALSO, HE SEEKS US SUPPORT FOR STRONG

MEASURES TO LIMIT GHG EMISSIONS.

SCHNEIDER
