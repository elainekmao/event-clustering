SUBJECT: VISIT TO MUTARE AND MANICALAND

CLASSIFIED BY JOSEPH G. SULLIVAN FOR REASONS 1.5B/D

1. C) SUMMARY: A BRIEF JANUARY 10-11 VISIT TO MUTARE IN

MANICALAND PROVINCE CONFIRMED THE CONVENTIONAL WISDOM

THAT ZANU/PF IS FACING DIFFICULTIES IN THE CITY OF

MUTARE AND SURROUNDING AREAS AND IS VULNERABLE IN ALL

BUT THE MOST RURAL AREAS. THE AMBASSADOR MET WITH ACTING

PROVINCIAL ADMINISTRATOR MBETSA IN THE ABSENCE OF THE

GOVERNOR, MAYOR LAWRENCE MUDEHWE, A GROUP OF COMMERCIAL

FARMERS AND BISHOPS FROM THE CATHOLIC, ANGLICAN,

EVANGELICAL AND PRESBYTERIAN CHURCHES. HE ALSO VISITED A

USAID FUNDED VOLUNTARY COUNSELING AND TESTING CENTER AND

A PRIMARY SCHOOL RECONSTRUCTED FROM DEVASTATING

HURRICANE ELAINE WITH DOD HUMANITARIAN ASSISTANCE FUNDS.

THE COUNTRY\'S ECONOMIC DECLINE, PROVOKED BY BAD ECONOMIC

POLICIES AND LAND INSTABILITY, WAS MUCH IN EVIDENCE.

POLITICAL VIOLENCE, ORGANIZED BY THE GOVERNMENT, WAS A

MAJOR CONCERN OF CHURCH LEADERS. BUT MOST INTERLOCUTORS

THOUGHT THAT ECONOMIC HARD TIMES AND ZANU-PF INATTENTION

TO MANICALAND MADE PRESIDENT MUGABE\'S ELECTION PROSPECTS

IN THE PROVINCE POOR, A PERCEPTION WHICH THE OPPOSITION

MDC SHARES. END SUMMARY

2. (C) MUTARE AND MANICALAND PROVINCE ARE FEELING THE

IMPACT OF ZIMBABWE\'S ECONOMIC DOWNTURN, WITH TIMBERLAND,

AGRO-INDUSTRY AND COMMERCIAL FARMING NEGATIVELY AFFECTED

BY LAND INSTABILITY AND OCCUPATIONS. CLOSE PROXIMITY TO

THE MOZAMBIQUE BORDER HAS ALSO SPAWNED A FLOURISHING

CLANDESTINE TRADE OF PRICE-CONTROLLED ITEMS FROM

ZIMBABWE TO MOZAMBIQUE WHERE TRADERS ARE ABLE TO REALIZE

A HANDSOME PROFIT. CONSUMERS IN ZIMBABWE FEEL THE

EFFECT IN SHORTAGES OF THESE BASIC COMMODITIES. CHURCH

LEADERS SAID THEIR POOREST CONSTITUENTS ARE FACING REAL

HUNGER BUT THAT GOVERNMENT DELAYS IN RECOGNIZING

SHORTAGES MEANT THAT THE CHURCHES DID NOT APPEAL TO

SUPPORTING INTERNATIONAL ORGANIZATIONS IN A TIMELY

FASHION. PROVINCIAL AUTHORITIES CLAIMED THAT THE IMPACT

OF LAND REDISTRIBUTION WAS MINIMAL ON TIMBER, COFFEE AND

FRUIT AGRO-INDUSTRY, A CLAIM CONTESTED BY PRIVATE

FARMERS. ALL AGREED THAT THE EFFECT WAS FELT MOST IN

SMALL AND MID-SIZED COMMERCIAL FARMS. AFFECTED

COMMERCIAL FARMERS TOLD THE AMBASSADOR THEY HAD ALL BEEN

AFFECTED IN VARIOUS WAYS. THE FARMER WHO HAD QUICKLY

SURRENDERED ONE OF HIS TWO FARMS WITHOUT CONTESTATION

FARED BEST, WHILE THE INDIVIDUAL WHO MADE THE MOST NOISE

AND WAS CONTESTING IN COURT WAS SUFFERING THE MOST. BUT

IN EVERY CASE, PRODUCTIVITY WAS SUFFERING GREATLY WITH

CROPS OR DAIRY PRODUCTION ON COMMERCIAL FARMS REDUCED

SUBSTANTIALLY AND FARM OCCUPIERS WITH LITTLE KNOW-HOW

AND FEW INPUTS, PRODUCING AT MOST A FEW SUBSISTENCE

CROPS. IN MANY CASES, THE BENEFICIARIES OF THE A-2

COMMERCIAL FARM DISTRIBUTION PROGRAM HAVE BEEN URBAN

DWELLERS PICKING UP A PIECE OF PROPERTY FOR THE WEEKEND

WITH LITTLE EXPERIENCE OR PROSPECTS IN SUCCESSFUL

FARMING. MUTARE MAYOR MUDEHWE WAS A PLEASANT EXCEPTION

TO THE TENDENCY TO TAKE ADVANTAGE OF THE LAND

DISTRIBUTION PROGRAM WHILE THE GETTING WAS GOOD.

DESPITE THE FACT THAT HIS OWN LAND IN THE COMMUNAL AREAS

HAS INSUFFICIENT WATER FOR PRODUCTIVE FARMING, HE DID

NOT APPLY FOR A FARM IN THE A-2 PROGRAM TO AVOID THE

APPEARANCE OF CONFLICT OF INTEREST.

3. (C) THE VISIT TO THE DOD-REHABILITATED SCHOOL IN

ROWA, JUST 20 MINUTES OUTSIDE MUTARE IN A COMMUNAL AREA,

WAS INSTRUCTIVE. THE SCHOOLED WAS BASIC BUT

SERVICEABLE. THE PRIMARY SCHOOL STUDENTS, PRESENT DURING

THE SCHOOL HOLIDAYS PERFORMING THEIR MANDATORY GROUNDS

PROTESTANT CLERGYMEN COMPLAINED OF FREQUENT POLITICAL

VIOLENCE ORCHESTRATED BY ZANU/PF AND THE GOVERNMENT.

HAD DISTORTED THEIR RECENT MEETING WITH PRESIDENT MUGABE

LAND REFORM. THEY HAD PUT OUT SUBSEQUENT STATEMENTS

VIOLENCE. THEY SAID THAT ALL RELIGIOUS LEADERS IN THE

PROVINCE WERE UNITED IN THIS APPEAL. THEY DID NOT

BELIEVE THAT GOVERNMENT VIOLENCE WOULD BE EFFECTIVE IN

MUTARE TWICE, NOTWITHSTANDING THE OPPOSITION OF HIS OWN

MANICALAND. HE NOTED THE POOR REPRESENTATION OF

MANICALAND IN THE ZANU-PF LEADERSHIP AND THE TENDENCY OF

HARARE PARTY HEADQUARTERS TO SEEK TO IMPOSE ITS

DECISIONS ON LOCAL PARTY STRUCTURES. HE BELIEVED THAT

POOR ECONOMIC CONDITIONS AND COUNTER-PRODUCTIVE ECONOMIC

POLICIES WOULD HAVE A NEGATIVE EFFECT AT THE POLLS AND

THAT ZANU-PF WOULD NOT DO WELL IN MANICALAND PROVINCE.

HIV-AIDS: A GROWING COMMUNITY FOCUS

-----------------------------------

6. (U) IN MEETINGS WITH CHURCH BISHOPS, HIV-AIDS WAS A

MAJOR CONCERN. THE CATHOLIC BISHOP, WHO HAS HAD

SUBSTANTIAL SUPPORT IN HIS OUTREACH PROGRAMS FROM THE

CATHOLIC RELIEF SERVICE, SAID THAT HE NO LONGER NEEDED

TO PLEA FOR SUPPORT FROM COMMUNITY LEADERS. EVERYONE

HAS BEEN TOUCHED BY THE DISEASE, EVEN IN THE MOST RURAL

PARTS OF HIS DIOCESE. VILLAGE CHIEFS WERE ANXIOUS TO

HELP AND TO HAVE THE CHURCH\'S ASSISTANCE IN CONFRONTING

THE PROBLEM. BISHOP MUTUME SAID THAT THE CHURCH HAS

BUT THEN TO HELP TRANSPORT THE ILL TO THEIR RURAL
