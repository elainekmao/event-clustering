SUBJECT: DANISH DEVELOPMENT COOPERATION MISSION

CONDITIONS FUTURE AGRICULTURE AID ON POSITIVE RESPONSE

TO MALLOCH-BROWN LETTER

REF: HARARE 488 AND PREVIOUS (NOTAL)

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) THE DANISH UNDER SECRETARY FOR BILATERAL

COOPERATION, AMBASSADOR CARSTEN STRAU, TOLD CHARGE

D\'AFFAIRES ON JANUARY 31 THAT DENMARK\'S CONTINUED

ASSISTANCE IN THE FIELD OF AGRICULTURE DEPENDED ON THE

ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT\'S RESPONSE TO THE LETTER FROM

UNITED NATIONS DEVELOPMENT PROGRAM ADMINISTRATOR MARK

MALLOCH-BROWN TO PRESIDENT MUGABE (REFTEL). (COMMENT:

THE RESPONSE TO THE MALLOCH-BROWN LETTER IS SEEN AS A

KEY INDICATOR OF FUTURE DONOR SUPPORT -- WHETHER FOR

LAND OR GENERAL DEVELOPMENT. THE ZIMBABWEAN

GOVERNMENT\'S RESPONSE, NOT TO MENTION ITS TONE AND

TENOR, WILL PROVIDE A SIGNAL OF INTENT ON ITS "FAST-

TRACK" LAND REFORM PROGRAM AND WHAT SORT OF ASSISTANCE

IT WOULD REQUEST OF DONORS. AN IN-YOUR-FACE RESPONSE

WILL LEAD TO A HARDER LINE BY DONORS. IN THE CURRENT

ENVIRONMENT, EVEN A POSITIVE, CONCILIATORY RESPONSE

LIKELY WILL BE MET WITH SKEPTICISM AND A "SHOW-ME"

ATTITUDE. NO DONOR EXPECTS A ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT

ABOUT-FACE. END COMMENT).

2. (C) THE DANISH DIPLOMAT SAID HE PUT THAT MESSAGE

ACROSS TO MINISTER OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS STANISLAUS MUDENGE

EARLIER THAT DAY. THE DANISH PARLIAMENT RECENTLY VOTED

NO NEW AID FOR ZIMBABWE, AND THE PARTY THAT AMBASSADOR

STRAU IS LEADING SEEKS TO LOOK FOR WAYS TO LEVERAGE AID

THAT IS STILL IN THE PIPELINE, DESPITE REDUCED LEVELS.

DENMARK NO LONGER FUNDS ZIMBABWE\'S MINISTRY OF

AGRICULTURE; IT USES MONIES REMAINING FROM THAT PROGRAM

TO SUPPORT "COMPLEMENTARY APPROACHES" TO THE ZIMBABWEAN

GOVERNMENT\'S LAND REFORM PROGRAM. ALL OTHER DANISH AID

NOW GOES TO NON-GOVERNMENTAL ORGANIZATIONS, BUT FUNDS

FOR HIV/AIDS AND OTHER HEALTH PROJECT ASSISTANCE REMAIN

AT NORMAL LEVELS.

3. (C) STRAU TOLD US THAT MUDENGE DID NOT RECITE THE

HISTORY OF THE LAND QUESTION IN ZIMBABWE OR TAKE

ADVANTAGE OF THE OPPORTUNITY TO REVILE THE BRITISH,

SOMEWHAT TO HIS SURPRISE. RATHER, THE ZIMBABWEAN

FOREIGN MINISTER REASONABLY PROBED THE EXTENT OF THE

DANISH AID CUTBACK AND CONDITIONS FOR ITS RETURN TO

PREVIOUS LEVELS. THE DANISH DIPLOMAT STRESSED TO THE

FOREIGN MINISTER THAT THAT WOULD BE CONTINGENT ON

WHETHER VIOLENCE WAS EMPLOYED IN THE UPCOMING

PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION CAMPAIGN AND THE SORTS OF TACTICS

EMPLOYED IN THE FAST-TRACK LAND REDISTRIBUTION EXERCISE.

THERE WAS PROVISION TO SCALE BACK AID LEVELS EVEN

FURTHER. HE URGED THE MINISTER TO ENTER INTO A ROBUST

DIALOGUE WITH ALL STAKEHOLDERS ON LAND REFORM,

PARTICULARLY THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION, IN ORDER TO

SECURE A SETTLEMENT THAT IS ACCEPTABLE TO ALL. DENMARK,

HE NOTED, HAD PLACED ZIMBABWE IN A CATEGORY OF COUNTRIES

NO LONGER ELIGIBLE FOR ITS STANDARD FIVE-YEAR AID

PROGRAM. STRAU\'S GOVERNMENT WILL MONITOR PROGRESS IN

ZIMBABWE OVER THE NEXT TWO YEARS BEFORE CONSIDERING

RESUMING NORMAL ASSISTANCE LEVELS.

4. (C) COMMENT: THE DANES HAVE A LARGE BILATERAL

ASSISTANCE PROGRAM IN ZIMBABWE AND, AS A RESULT, ARE

DISPROPORTIONATELY INFLUENTIAL WITH ITS GOVERNMENT. WE

HAVE HEARD THAT MUDENGE PRIVATELY EXPRESSED BITTERNESS

AT THE DANES\' CHANGE IN ATTITUDE TOWARD HIS GOVERNMENT,

GIVEN THEIR TRACK RECORD OF GENEROSITY AND POLITICAL

SUPPORT FOR THE RULING ZANU-PF WHEN IT WAS A LIBERATION

MOVEMENT. THE DANES HAVE SAID ALL THE RIGHT THINGS AND

THEIR VIEWS ON THE ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT\'S CONDUCT OF

THE LAND REFORM AND RESETTLEMENT EXERCISE HEW CLOSELY TO

OUR OWN. WHETHER STRAU\'S MESSAGE WILL HAVE AN IMPACT ON

PRESIDENT MUGABE\'S FINAL RESPONSE TO THE INTERNATIONAL

COMMUNITY\'S CONDITIONS FOR HELPING ON LAND REFORM

REMAINS TO BE SEEN. NEVERTHELESS, THE GOVERNMENT HERE

COULD NOT FAIL TO UNDERSTAND THE DANES\' CLARITY AND

BLUNTNESS. ALL INDICATORS ARE THAT OTHER EUROPEAN

COUNTRIES WILL FOLLOW SUIT -- LED BY SWEDEN, THE CURRENT

HEAD OF THE 15-NATION EUROPEAN UNION. END COMMENT.
