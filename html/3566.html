1. ON AUGUST 1, 2002 UN FOOD AND AGRICULTURE

ORGANIZATION (FAO) ASSISTANT DIRECTOR GENERAL HENRI

CARSALADE SIGNED THE AGREEMENT FOR A USD 400,000 TWO-YEAR

U.S. TRUST FUND PROJECT "FISHCODE: SUPPORT FOR THE

IMPLEMENTATION OF THE INTERNATIONAL PLAN OF ACTION TO

PREVENT, DETER AND ELIMINATE ILLEGAL, UNREPORTED AND

UNREGULATED FISHING." THE SIGNED COPY WAS MAILED DIRECTLY

TO MICHAEL DAVIS, GRANTS OFFICER, OFFICE OF ACQUISITION

MANAGEMENT, INTERNATIONAL PROGRAM DIVISION, DEPARTMENT OF

STATE.

2. ILLEGAL, UNREPORTED AND UNREGULATED (IUU) FISHING HAS

BEEN IDENTIFIED IN A WIDE RANGE OF INTERNATIONAL FORA AS

BEING A MAJOR CONSTRAINT TO THE ACHIEVEMENT OF

RESPONSIBLE AND SUSTAINABLE FISHERIES. THE PROJECT WILL

ADDRESS SOME OF THE MOST SERIOUS COMPONENTS OF IUU

FISHING, INCLUDING DEVELOPMENT COUNTRY ENFORCEMENT AND

THE FLAG AND PORT OF CONVENIENCE PROBLEM.

3. THE GRANT COMMENCED AUGUST 1, 2002 AND WILL EXPIRE

MAY 19, 2004. THE FAO WILL PROVIDE THE DEPARTMENT OF

STATE (COLIN MCIFF IN OES/OMC) PROGRESS REPORTS EVERY SIX

MONTHS USING STANDARD FAO FORMAT AS WELL AS ANY FINAL

REPORT IT PREPARES.

4. WITH THIS GRANT, MISSION RECORDS SHOW THAT THE USG

VOLUNTARY CONTRIBUTIONS TO FAO TOTALED USD 10,058,421 IN

FY 2002. HEILEMAN
