1. (U) SUMMARY. ROUTE ONE IS BEAUTIFUL DRIVE BUT A

SURPRISINGLY POOR NATIONAL ARTERY LINKING THE VARIOUS

REGIONS OF THE COUNTRY. THERE IS LITTLE INDICATION OF MUCH

USE OF LAND OR RAIL TRANSPORT FOR INTER-PROVINCIAL TRADE.

SMALL-SCALE PRIVATE ENTERPRISE SEEMS TO THRIVE. RICE IS

CLEARLY KING AMONG AGRICULTURAL PRODUCTS. APART FROM SOME

RANCOR AGAINST THE VIETNAM HUMAN RIGHTS ACT, PEOPLE

UNIFORMLY SEEMED BULLISH ON THE US-VIETNAM RELATIONSHIP AND

READY TO PUT THE WAR HISTORY BEHIND US. END SUMMARY.

2. (U) NATIONAL HIGHWAY ROUTE ONE IS THE ARTERY LINKING

VIETNAM\'S THREE MAJOR REGIONS, STRETCHING FROM CA MAU

PROVINCE ON THE GULF OF THAILAND TO LANG SON PROVINCE ALONG

THE CHINESE BORDER. MOST OF ITS LENGTH SKIMS VIETNAM\'S

DESERVEDLY FAMOUS COASTLINE, OFFERING SPECTACULAR VIEW AFTER

SPECTACULAR VIEW. TRAVELERS -- E.G., POL/C AND POL FSN

DURING THEIR OCTOBER 25-NOVEMBER 2 TRIP BETWEEN HO CHI MINH

CITY AND HANOI -- SOON GROW FATIGUED BY THE CONSTANT

REPETITION OF "HOW BEAUTIFUL."

3. (U) NO OTHER ROADS NOW LINK THE COUNTRY, ALTHOUGH WORK

IS UNDERWAY ON TRANSFORMING PARTS OF THE ONE-TIME HO CHI

MINH TRAIL INTO ANOTHER HIGHWAY. TWENTY-SIX YEARS AFTER

"LIBERATION" OF THE SOUTH AND NATIONAL "REUNIFICATION," ONE

MIGHT HAVE EXPECTED THAT THIS NATIONAL HIGHWAY HAD RECEIVED

CENTRAL GOVERNMENT LARGESSE OVER THE YEARS TO FACILITATE

INTER-REGIONAL AND INTER-PROVINCIAL COMMUNICATION. MAYBE IT

HAS HAD, BUT IF SO, THE RESULTS ARE DISAPPOINTING. A FEW

MILES NORTH OF HO CHI MINH CITY, THE HIGHWAY TURNS INTO A

TWO LANE ROAD, MORE OFTEN THAN NOT WITHOUT CENTER OR SIDE

MARKINGS. IN MANY SPOTS FROM NINH THUAN ALL THE WAY THROUGH

TO QUANG BINH PROVINCE, FLOODS HAVE TAKEN THEIR TOLL EVEN ON

THIS BASIC INFRASTRUCTURE. (IN LATE OCTOBER, TRAFFIC ON

ROUTE ONE WAS SHUT DOWN ALTOGETHER AT TAM KY IN QUANG NAM

FOR MORE THAN A DAY DUE TO HIGH WATERS.) THE RESULT IS THAT

STRETCHES OF THIS NATIONAL ROUTE ARE LITTLE MORE THAN DIRT

OR MUDDY PATCHES. CONCERTED EFFORTS SEEM TO BE UNDERWAY TO

IMPROVE BRIDGES, ESPECIALLY SMALL ONES OVER DIKES AND

CREEKS, BUT IN THE MEANTIME ONGOING CONSTRUCTION OFTEN MEANS

ONE-WAY TRAFFIC. (NOTE: ACCORDING TO PRESS REPORTS,

JAPANESE OFFICIAL DEVELOPMENT ASSISTANCE AND/OR LOANS, WORTH

WELL OVER USD 130 MILLION, ARE FUNDING THE WORK ON 71

BRIDGES OVER SEVERAL YEARS. END NOTE) ONCE ACROSS THE

FORMER DMZ, IN QUANG BINH PROVINCE, HOWEVER, THE ROAD

BECOMES DRAMATICALLY BETTER PAVED AND MAINTAINED, ALTHOUGH

IT CONTINUES TO BE TWO LANES FOR MOST OF THE REMAINING

JOURNEY INTO HANOI.

4. (U) TRAFFIC IS REMARKABLY LIGHT, WITH RELATIVELY FEW

TRANSPORT TRUCKS. IT IS POSSIBLE TO GO FOR MILES AT A TIME

WITHOUT PASSING ANOTHER SEDAN OR TRUCK, ALTHOUGH THERE IS A

STEADY FLOOD OF MOTORCYCLES AND BICYCLES AS WELL AS THE

OCCASIONAL CATTLE- OR HORSE-DRAWN CART. FLOODS OF PRIMARY

SCHOOLCHILDREN HEADING HOME ON FOOT OR BICYCLE AFTER THEIR

HALF-DAY SESSIONS COMPOSE ONE OF THE FEW PERSISTENT "TRAFFIC

JAMS."

5. (U) IT MIGHT BE TEMPTING TO ASSUME THAT MUCH FREIGHT

MUST BE GOING BY TRAIN FROM THE TWO ENDS OF THE COUNTRY.

THE SOLE MAJOR NORTH-SOUTH RAILWAY LINE RUNS PRETTY MUCH IN

TANDEM WITH ROUTE ONE, CRISS-CROSSING REGULARLY AND OFTEN

WITHIN SIGHT OF EACH OTHER. TRAFFIC ON THE HIGHWAY MUST

COME TO A HALT AT MANY CROSSINGS, WHERE THERE ARE ALWAYS A

SMALL BEVY OF PETTY MERCHANTS WITH LIFE\'S DAILY NECESSITIES

-- CANDIES, CIGARETTES, FRUIT, ETC. IN EIGHT DAYS OF

TRAVEL, WE SAW NO MORE THAN 3-4 TRAINS A DAY, VIRTUALLY ALL

OF THEM PASSENGER TRAINS. EITHER INTER-PROVINCIAL TRADE IS

EXTREMELY LIMITED, OR MUCH OF IT IS GOING BY BOAT.

6. (U) SIGNS OF A VITAL IF LARGELY SMALL-SCALE PRIVATE

SERVICE SECTOR ARE EVERYWHERE. DUSTY ROADSIDE RESTAURANTS

POSITION EMPLOYEES IN THE STREET TO WAVE IN WOULD-BE

CUSTOMERS WITH THEIR HANDS AND SMILES. BEACHSIDE BEER HALLS

DO A BOOMING BUSINESS, AS DO UBIQUITOUS COFFEE/TEA

RESTAURANTS, SOME OF THEM QUITE ELABORATE. AN ENTHUSIASTIC

LEISURE CLASS CLEARLY EXISTS ALONG ROUTE ONE, ALTHOUGH THEY

SEEM TO HEAD HOME FAIRLY EARLY. EVEN NHA TRANG\'S HOPPING

DISCO SCENE APPARENTLY CLOSES DOWN AROUND MIDNIGHT. NOT TO

WORRY, THOUGH: AMOROUS COUPLES CAN STILL HEAD TO THE

BEACHES AFTER HOURS. OTHER BEACH-SIDE HUTS EVEN IN A

BACKWATER RESORT LIKE NHAT LE (QUANG BINH PROVINCE)

APPARENTLY OFFER MORE THAN SEAFOOD SNACKS. THEIR YOUNG AND

HEAVILY MADE-UP FEMALE STAFF GAVE EVERY INDICATION OF

BECOMING MORE AND MORE FRIENDLY AS THE DAY WORE ON (WE ONLY

STOPPED FOR LUNCH, WHICH SEEMED TO THROW THEM INTO A GOOD

DEAL OF CONFUSION: "SOMEONE ACTUALLY WANTS TO EAT HERE?").

7. (U) APART FROM A FEW STRETCHES OF RUBBER PLANTATIONS,

THE UTTER DOMINANCE OF RICE CULTURE WAS STRIKING, ALBEIT

WITH CONSIDERABLE VARIATION ON MATURITY AND FERTILITY AMONG

THE REGIONS. THE FARTHER SOUTH, THE MORE IMPRESSIVE. SANDY

STRETCHES IN PHU YEN AND BINH DINH WERE MORE BLEAK, IN

PLACES WITH EXTENSIVE SALT FIELDS. HA TINH, NGHE AN AND

THANH HOA IN THE NORTH WERE AGAIN RELATIVELY MORE

PROSPEROUS, WITH SOMEWHAT MORE VARIETY OF CROPS EVIDENT.

HOUSING STANDARDS FOLLOWED THESE GENERAL PATTERNS. HOUSES

IN THE VARIOUS SOUTHERN PROVINCES OFTEN SPORTED THE DATE OF

CONSTRUCTION, WITH A SURPRISING NUMBER HAVING BEEN BUILT IN

THE 1970\'S, BOTH PRE- AND POST-1975. RELATIVELY FEW SEEM TO

HAVE GONE UP IN THE 1980\'S, WITH ANOTHER SURGE IN THE

1990\'S. FOR FARMHOUSES, THERE WAS LITTLE TO DISTINGUISH

THOSE FROM THIRTY YEARS AGO FROM MORE RECENT ONES, BUT EVEN

VILLAGES AND TOWNS TYPICALLY HAD AT LEAST A COUPLE OF

VIRTUAL MANSIONS IN THE TYPICAL TALL AND THIN FASHION

FAVORED IN VIETNAM.

8. (U) REGIONAL DIFFERENCES IN ACCENT REMAIN SHARP, WITH

ACCENTS IN CENTRAL PROVINCES LIKE QUANG NAM AND QUANG TRI,

AS WELL AS THE MORE SOPHISTICATED CITY OF DANANG, DISTINCT

CHALLENGES AT LEAST FOR ONE FSI-TRAINED DIPLOMAT. NATIONAL

EDUCATIONAL SYSTEMS, AND STATE-RUN TELEVISION AND RADIO

BROADCASTS APPEAR TO HAVE DONE LITTLE TO DISPEL SUCH

REGIONAL VARIATIONS.

9. (U) WHILE THE ISSUE OF THE VIETNAM HUMAN RIGHTS ACT

INEVITABLY CAME UP DURING OFFICIAL MEETINGS (REF A), ALONG

WITH OCCASIONAL COMPLAINTS FROM OFFICIALS IN PROVINCES

NEAREST THE OLD DMZ ABOUT DOUBLE-STANDARD LECTURES FROM THE

U.S. ABOUT HUMAN RIGHTS PROBLEMS DESPITE DAMAGE CAUSED

DURING THE WAR, ANECDOTAL IMPRESSIONS AND UNSCIENTIFIC

SOUNDINGS WITH OFFICIAL AND NON-OFFICIAL INTERLOCUTORS ALIKE

INDICATED AN ALMOST ACROSS-THE-BOARD INTEREST IN THE U.S.,

FAVORABLE DISPOSITION TOWARD THE U.S., AND GENUINE EAGERNESS

TO SEE THE US-VIETNAM RELATIONSHIP MOVE FORWARD. A GUIDE IN

A QUANG TRI WAR MEMORIAL -- ON A HISTORICAL SITE WHERE IT

WAS ALLEGED THAT THE US BOMBS DROPPED THERE HAD SEVEN TIMES

THE DESTRUCTIVE POWER OF THE ATOMIC BOMB ON HIROSHIMA --

WENT THROUGH HIS RATHER HORRIFIC SPIEL WITH A STRAIGHT FACE

AND CALM DEMEANOR. WHEN ASKED SUBSEQUENTLY IF HE OR OTHER

LOCAL PEOPLE REMAINED ANGRY AT THE U.S. OR RESENTED THE

CURRENT DEVELOPMENT OF BILATERAL TIES, HE SMILED AND

ANSWERED "NO, THAT\'S ALL IN THE PAST. NO ONE WANTS TO THINK

ABOUT THAT ANY MORE." IT\'S NOT A BAD SUMMARY OF POPULAR

ATTITUDES ALONG ROUTE ONE.
