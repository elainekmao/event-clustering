1. (U) THIS IS THE FIRST IN A SERIES OF REPORTS OF

INTERVIEWS OF WAR CRIMES VICTIMS CONDUCTED IN LATE

1993 IN MAOCA, BOSNIA-HERZEGOVINA, BY A DART/ZAGREB

FIELD REP AND AN AMCIT IRC REP WHILE THEY WERE IN THE

AREA ASSESSING HUMANITARIAN ASSISTANCE NEEDS. THIS

REPORT NAMES VICTIMS AND, IN SOME CASES, PERPETRATORS

AND DETAILS SPECIFIC CRIMES. THE PERSONS INTERVIEWED

FOR THESE REPORTS SEEMED TO THE AMCIT INTERVIEWERS TO

BE A REPRESENTATIVE SAMPLE OF HUNDREDS OF VICTIMS AND

EYEWITNESSES NOW LIVING IN THE TUZLA POCKET, MANY OF

WHOM ALREADY HAVE SWORN STATEMENTS ON FILE WITH LOCAL

WAR CRIMES COMMISSIONS.

2. (LOU) MEHMED ARNAVTOVIC (STRICTLY PROTECT),

8. (U) THE NEXT DAY WAS MUCH THE SAME ROUTINE, BUT

REFRIGERATOR TRUCKS OR TO THROW THEM INTO THE SAVA

9. (U) ONE PRISONER, MIRSAD MUJAGIC, WHO WAS A

POLICEMAN BEFORE THE WAR, WAS BEATEN BY TWELVE

BLACK-CLAD SERBS WITH BASEBALL BATS. THEY

THEN, RANKO CESIC, ONE OF THE MAIN SERBS FROM BRCKO,

KILLED MUGAJIC WITH A PISTOL. WITH CESIC WAS MIODRAG
