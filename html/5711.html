SUBJECT: ANTHRAX AND SMALLPOX PROGRAM FOR KUWAITI MILITARY

Classified By: CDA FRANK C. URBANCIC, REASON: 1.5 (B) AND (D)

1. (U) This is an action request. See para 5.

2. (C) The United States and Kuwait are standing shoulder to

shoulder in the international effort to disarm Iraq. As a

result of Kuwait\'s geographic location next to Iraq, and the

political decision by the GoK to support very large numbers

of US troops in the country, Kuwait is the first and most

likely target of any future Iraqi bio-terror attack in the

region.

3. (C) All US troops are fully vaccinated against both

anthrax and smallpox. As part of its own efforts to provide

equal protections to its troops who will be standing in the

same terrain as their US counterparts, the GoK has requested

enough smallpox and anthrax vaccine to inoculate 15,000 of

its own personnel. The US response has been disappointing,

and our hosts have negatively noted it. In the LoA which we

have been instructed to provide to the Kuwaitis, the US has

offered only enough anthrax vaccine for 2750 people, and it

has offered to sell both the anthrax and smallpox vaccines,

vice provide it as aid in kind. Thus, we are not only not

providing quantities sufficient to protect these coalition

partners, we are asking them to pay for these relatively

small and insufficient amounts.

4. (C) This slap at the Kuwaitis has arrived at exactly the

same moment that the Embassy is engaged in intensive efforts

with the GoK to secure their approval (not required under the

terms of the current DCA) to provide additional millions of

dollars-worth of jet fuel, access to ports, and vast amounts

of other supplies to US forces as aid in kind from the GoK to

the coalition forces. Embassy views this situation as

degrading to our overall relationship, potentially

jeopardizing Kuwaiti willingness to approve future aid in

kind requests from the United States.

5. (C) Action Request: That the Department revisit this

issue with DoD urgently to secure sufficient supplies of

smallpox and anthrax vaccine for the full 15,000 personnel

requests by the GoK, and that it be provided as aid in kind

from the United States.
