SUBJECT: MEETING OF UNIDROIT SUB-FINANCE COMMITTEE

REF: A) STATE 68933 B) ROME 945

1. SUMMARY: THE FINANCE SUB-COMMITTEE OF THE

INTERNATIONAL INSTITUTE FOR THE UNIFICATION OF PRIVATE

LAW (UNIDROIT) MET ON MARCH 17 TO CONSIDER PRELIMINARY

2004 BUDGET ESTIMATES FOR THE ORGANIZATION. GIVEN THE

NUMBER OF QUESTIONS THAT WERE RAISED OVER BUDGET

UNCERTAINTIES REGARDING BOTH INCOME AND EXPENDITURES, NO

REAL EFFORT WAS MADE TO FIND CONSENSUS. THE SUB-

COMMITTEE AGREED TO MEET AGAIN WHEN THE PICTURE BECOMES

CLEARER. END SUMMARY.

2. U.S. MISSION REP ATTENDED THE 3/17 MEETING. PER REF

A) GUIDANCE, U.S. MADE THE FOLLOWING POINTS: OUR BUDGET

POLICY FOR ALL INTERNATIONAL ORGANIZATIONS IS ZERO

NOMINAL GROWTH; WE RECOGNIZE THE WORK UNIDROIT IS DOING

AND CAN SUPPORT A MODEST INCREASE BECAUSE OF THE

IMPORTANCE OF THE CAPETOWN CONVENTION DEPOSITARY FUNCTION

AND FOR NECESSARY INCREASES IN STAFF EXPENSES; THIS DOES

NOT REPRESENT A CHANGE IN U.S. POLICY BUT IS A ONE-TIME

RECOGNITION OF THESE HIGH-PRIORITY ITEMS; AND WE WILL NOT

SUPPORT AN INCREASE IN THE LEGAL COOPERATION PROGRAM BUT

INSTEAD BELIEVE THE PROGRAM SHOULD BE SUPPORTED WITH

EXTRA-BUDGETARY CONTRIBUTIONS FROM THOSE COUNTRIES WHO

FEEL STRONGLY ABOUT IT.

3. CANADA AND GERMANY JOINED THE U.S. IN SUPPORTING A

POLICY OF ZNG. SEVERAL QUESTIONS WERE RAISED AND MORE

DETAILS REQUESTED REGARDING STAFF SALARIES AND TRAVEL.

INCOME UNCERTAINTIES REVOLVE AROUND TWO ISSUES: THE

ANNUAL CONTRIBUTION OF THE ITALIAN GOVERNMENT AND THE

POSSIBLE ACCESSION OF A NEW MEMBER (PANAMA), WHICH WOULD

BRING IN NEW MONIES. THE ITALIAN DELEGATE SAID THE GOI

CONTRIBUTION MAY BE RESOLVED AFTER A MEETING BETWEEN

UNIDROIT AND THE GOI MINISTRIES OF THE ECONOMY AND

FOREIGN AFFAIRS, TO BE SCHEDULED IN THE NEXT FEW WEEKS.

THE SYG ADDED THAT MORE SHOULD ALSO BE KNOWN IN THE NEXT

SEVERAL WEEKS ABOUT THE ACCESSION OF PANAMA.

4. GIVEN THE REQUESTS FOR ADDITIONAL BUDGET DETAILS AND

THE UNCERTAINTIES REGARDING CONTRIBUTIONS FROM THE GOI

AND PANAMA, THE SUB-COMMITTEE AGREED TO MEET AGAIN

PROBABLY IN TWO OR THREE MONTHS RATHER THAN TRYING TO

REACH CONSENSUS NOW.
