1. (C) THE FOLLOWING ELECTION RELATED ACTIVITIES IN

ZIMBABWE ARE NOW IN THE PLANNING STAGES OR UNDERWAY.

THE GOZ HAS NOT BEEN CONSULTED ON THESE ACTIVITIES,

WHICH HAVE BEEN UNDERTAKEN BY NGOS AND/OR CIVIL SOCIETY,

BUT IT WILL PROBABLY BE NECESSARY TO INFORM THE GOZ OF

CERTAIN ASPECTS OF SOME OF THE PROPOSED ESF-FUNDED

ACTIVITIES.

ECONOMIC SUPPORT FUNDS - ZIMBABWE WAS THE LARGEST

RECIPIENT OF ECONOMIC SUPPORT FUNDS IN SUB-SAHARAN AFRICA

IN FISCAL YEAR (FY) 2001; $3 MILLION WAS PROVIDED AND AN

ADDITIONAL $1 MILLION WAS CARRIED OVER TO FY 2002. THERE

IS A CONGRESSIONAL NOTIFICATION ISSUE CONCERNING THE

ROLL-OVER FUNDS; HOWEVER, USAID IS PREPARING PRE-OBLIGATION

DOCUMENTS FOR THESE ACTIVITIES IN ANTICIPATION OF ITS

RESOLUTION. THE FOLLOWING ACTIVITIES HAVE BEEN APPROVED:

-- CIVIL SOCIETY SUPPORT - THE COUNTRY TEAM REVIEWED TWO

UNSOLICITED PROPOSALS AND IRI WAS UNANIMOUSLY SELECTED TO

IMPLEMENT THIS ACTIVITY. TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE AND TRAINING

WILL BE OFFERED TO A BROAD RANGE OF CIVIL SOCIETY GROUPS

INCLUDING POLITICAL PARTIES. ($1.0 MILLION)

-- INTERNATIONAL OBSERVERS - IFES WAS SELECTED BY THE CEPPS

PARTNERS TO IMPLEMENT THIS ACTIVITY. THE SCOPE OF WORKS

CALLS FOR SMALL TEAMS TO MAKE SHORT, PERIODIC VISITS TO

DOCUMENT THE ELECTORAL ENVIRONMENT. DURING THE ACTUAL

POLLING DAYS, A LARGER OBSERVER MISSION WILL BE IN COUNTRY.

BECAUSE OF DIFFICULTIES WITH ZIMBABWEAN AUTHORITIES, IFES

HAS NOT BEEN ABLE TO MAKE ALL THE PLANNED VISITS.

REPROGRAMMING OR CHANGES IN THE SCOPES OF WORK MAY BE

NECESSARY. ($800,000)

-- REGIONAL MONITORS - FUNDS WOULD SUPPORT POSSIBLE TRAVEL

AND OTHER RELATED COSTS OF THE SADC PARLIAMENTARY FORUM TO

OBSERVE THE PRE-ELECTION PERIOD AND THE VOTING DAYS. THE

CEPPS MECHANISM IS LIKELY TO BE USED TO COMMIT FUNDS.

($100,000)

-- DOMESTIC MONITORS AND POLLING AGENTS - FUNDS WILL BE

USED TO SUPPORT A COMBINATION OF ACTIVITIES, INCLUDING

PER DIEM AND TRAVEL COSTS FOR DOMESTIC MONITORS AND

TRAINING COSTS FOR DOMESTIC MONITORS AND PARTY POLLING

AGENTS. A COMBINATION OF THE CEPPS MECHANISM AND DIRECT

GRANTS TO LOCAL NGOS IS LIKELY TO BE USED TO COMMIT

FUNDS. ($400,000)

-- ELECTION SUPPORT NETWORK "DESK" - BASED ON A SUCCESSFUL

ACTIVITY IN THE 2000 PARLIAMENTARY ELECTIONS, FUNDS WILL

BE USED TO ESTABLISH A SUPPORT DESK FOR THE MORE THAN

30-MEMBER NGO NETWORK -- THE ZIMBABWE ELECTIONS SUPPORT

NETWORK (ZESN). THE PURPOSE IS TO PROVIDE A SPACE LARGE

ENOUGH FROM WHICH ALL MEMBERS CAN COORDINATE THEIR

MONITORING ACTIVITIES DURING THE POLLING PERIOD. IF

FUNDING PERMITS, SMALLER SUPPORT DESKS WILL BE SET UP IN

PROVINCIAL CAPITALS. ($75,000)

-- VOTER EDUCATION MATERIALS - VOTER EDUCATION MATERIALS,

INCLUDING PAMPHLETS, COMICS, POSTERS, PRINT ADVERTISEMENTS,

WILL BE DEVELOPED FOR NATIONWIDE DISSEMINATION. THIS

ACTIVITY WILL ALSO INCLUDE PREPARATION OF A BROCHURE

CONTAINING ABBREVIATED MATERIAL FROM THE SADC NORMS AND

STANDARDS FOR ELECTIONS IN BOTH SHONA AND SINDEBELE. A

LOCAL NGO THAT CONDUCTED A CREDIBLE VOTER EDUCATION

TO BE USED TO COMMIT FUNDS. ($100,000)

-- VOTERS ROLLS - THIS ACTIVITY WILL FUND PURCHASE,

PROPOSAL HAS BEEN RECEIVED FROM ZESN FOR THIS ACTIVITY.

-- GOZ ELECTORAL ADMINISTRATION - THERE ARE SEVERAL

-- VICTIMS OF TORTURE - THIS ACTIVITY IS IMPLEMENTED

UNFUNDED NEEDS

-- NEWSPRINT - THE ESCALATING COST OF NEWSPRINT IS

AFFECTING THE ABILITY OF THE INDEPENDENT PRESS TO KEEP

THE COVER PRICE OF THEIR PUBLICATIONS AFFORDABLE TO THE

GENERAL POPULATION. THIS IS BECAUSE THEY MUST BUY

NEWSPRINT THAT INCLUDES ABOUT A 20% IMPORT CONTENT.

BECAUSE FOREIGN EXCHANGE IS NOT AVAILABLE IN THE MARKET

AT THE OFFICIAL RATE, THE SUPPLIER IS FORCED TO PURCHASE

FOREIGN EXCHANGE AT THE PARALLEL RATE. A GRANT TO COVER

THE FOREIGN EXCHANGE COMPONENT OF NEWSPRINT BETWEEN NOW

AND JUNE OF 2002 WOULD COST IN THE RANGE OF $1.5 TO $2.0

MILLION. THE NEWS ORGANIZATIONS WOULD PURCHASE THE

NEWSPRINT AND LOCAL CURRENCY PROCEEDS WOULD BE

PROGRAMMED FOR HUMANITARIAN EFFORTS - POSSIBLY DISPLACED

FARM WORKERS.

-- VOTER EDUCATION MATERIAL AND HUMANITARIAN ASSISTANCE

MONITORING - GIVEN THE CURRENT HUMANITARIAN CRISIS, THE

POSSIBLE USE OF ANY DONOR ASSISTANCE FOR PARTISAN

PURPOSES SEVERAL ACTIVITIES COULD BE CONSIDERED. FOR

EXAMPLE, VOTER EDUCATION MATERIALS MIGHT BE DISTRIBUTED

WITH FOOD RELIEF. SIMILARLY, AN INDEPENDENT

INTERNATIONAL NGO WITH HUMAN RIGHTS EXPERIENCE MIGHT BE

ENGAGED TO MONITOR THE DISTRIBUTION OF RELIEF SUPPLIES

AND REPORT ON ABUSES.
