SUBJECT: NGHE AN: DEVELOPING, BUT A LONG WAY TO GO

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 HANOI 02028 01 OF 03 160853Z

REF: 01 HANOI 3262

1. (U) SUMMARY. NGHE AN PROVINCIAL OFFICIALS ARE HOPEFUL

THAT A STRATEGIC LOCATION AND IMPROVING (THOUGH STILL WEAK)

INFRASTRUCTURE WILL IMPROVE DEVELOPMENT AND THE PROVINCE\'S

ROLE AS AN OUTLET TO THE SEA FOR LAOS AND NORTHEASTERN

THAILAND. THE GVN IS MOVING AHEAD WITH PLANS FOR A NEW

NATIONAL BORDER CROSSING WITH LAOS, BUT THE ROAD TO THE

BORDER, WHILE IMPROVING, IS POOR. SEPTELS WILL REPORT ON

NARCOTICS AND TERRORISM ISSUES. END SUMMARY.

2. (U) POLOFF AND POL FSN VISITED NGHE AN PROVINCE AUGUST

13-15. VINH, THE PROVINCIAL CAPITAL, HAS ABOUT 200,000

PEOPLE AND IS LOCATED ABOUT 300 KILOMETERS DOWN NATIONAL

HIGHWAY 1 FROM HANOI. IN A GENERAL DESCRIPTION OF THE

PROVINCE, NGUYEN SY CUONG, VICE CHIEF OF STAFF FOR FOREIGN

AFFAIRS, NGHE AN PEOPLE\'S COMMITTEE, SAID THAT NGHE AN, WITH

ABOUT 16,500 SQUARE KILOMETERS, IS VIETNAM\'S THIRD LARGEST

PROVINCE AND SHARES A LONG BORDER WITH LAOS TO THE WEST.

THERE IS ONE INTERNATIONAL BORDER CROSSING, LOCATED AT NAM

CAN, ABOUT 280 MOSTLY BUMPY KILOMETERS UP HIGHWAY 7 FROM

VINH (REFTEL). A NEW NATIONAL BORDER CROSSING IS UNDER

CONSTRUCTION AT THANH THUY, LOCATED ABOUT 90 KILOMETERS UP

HIGHWAY 46 FROM VINH. (NOTE: THE NEW BORDER CROSSING DOES

NOT YET APPEAR ON EMBASSY HANOI ADMINISTRATIVE MAPS, BUT IS

INCLUDED IN PROVINCIAL MINISTRY OF PLANNING AND INVESTMENT

(MPI) DOCUMENTS. END NOTE.) NGHE AN HAS A PORT AT CUA LO,

ABOUT 15 KILOMETERS NORTHEAST OF VINH. NATIONAL HIGHWAY 1

AND THE MAIN NORTH-SOUTH RAILWAY LINE BOTH TRAVERSE THE

PROVINCE. IN ADDITION, THERE IS ALSO AN AIRPORT AT VINH

WITH REGULAR FLIGHTS TO HANOI AND HO CHI MINH CITY. CUONG

SAID THAT NGHE AN\'S LOCATION, ABOUT HALFWAY BETWEEN HANOI

AND HUE, IS A PLUS, AS WELL AS HAVING THE MAJOR ROAD, RAIL,

AND AIR LINKS, "THESE ALL MAKE NGHE AN AN ATTRACTIVE

PROVINCE FOR FOREIGN INVESTMENT, CUONG CLAIMED."

3. (U) NGHE AN HAS A POPULATION OF APPROXIMATELY THREE

MILLION, MAKING IT THE THIRD MOST POPULOUS PROVINCE IN THE

COUNTRY. HILLS AND MOUNTAINS COVER ABOUT 80 PERCENT OF THE

PROVINCE. ON THE ARABLE AREAS, FARMERS GROW MAINLY RICE,

CORN, SWEET POTATOES, AND PEANUTS. IN HIGHER ELEVATIONS,

COFFEE, TEA, RUBBER TREES, AND PINEAPPLE ARE CULTIVATED.

IMPORTANT INDUSTRIES INCLUDE CEMENT (THREE FACTORIES),

TEXTILES, AND SEA PRODUCTS. THE PER CAPITA INCOME IS ABOUT

USD 300 PER YEAR, ABOUT 25 PERCENT UNDER THE NATIONAL

AVERAGE OF ABOUT USD 400 PER YEAR. CUONG SAID THE GDP

GROWTH RATE IS "STRONG," AT ABOUT NINE PERCENT THE LAST TWO

YEARS, BUT A MAJOR PRIORITY OF PROVINCIAL AUTHORITIES IS TO

"SEE THIS REFLECTED IN THE PER CAPITA INCOME."

---------------------------------------------

NEW BORDER CROSSING - BRING YOUR LAND CRUISER

---------------------------------------------

4. (U) CUONG SAID THAT WORK IS ONGOING FOR A NEW "NATIONAL

BORDER CROSSING" AT THANH THUY, ABOUT 90 KILOMETERS UP

HIGHWAY 46 FROM VINH. HE SAID PROVINCIAL AUTHORITIES HOPE

THE NEW BORDER CROSSING WILL IMPROVE TRADE FROM LAOS AND

NORTHEAST THAILAND, SINCE IT IS MUCH CLOSER TO THE CUA LO

PORT THAN THE INTERNATIONAL BORDER CROSSING AT NAM CAN

(REFTEL). ACCORDING TO CUONG, THE GVN PLANS TO EVENTUALLY

MAKE THIS AN INTERNATIONAL BORDER CROSSING AND ESTABLISH A

PAGE 04 HANOI 02028 01 OF 03 160853Z

"SPECIAL BORDER ECONOMIC ZONE IN THE AREA," TO INCLUDE

BUSINESSES, A CUSTOMS OFFICE, BANKS, AND OTHER ACTIVITIES.

HE PREDICTED THIS WOULD BE POSSIBLE "WITHIN A COUPLE YEARS."

CUONG SAID THESE DEVELOPMENTS ARE IMPORTANT FOR VIETNAM\'S

"REGIONAL INTEGRATION." CURRENTLY, MORE CROSS BORDER

TRAFFIC "IS PROBABLY" COMING THROUGH HA TINH PROVINCE,

LOCATED JUST SOUTH OF NGHE AN, BECAUSE THERE IS ALSO A PORT,

AN ESTABLISHED BORDER CROSSING, AND A "RELATIVELY GOOD

ROAD." (HIGHWAY 8).

5. (U) EMBOFFS\' TRIP TO THE PROPOSED BORDER CROSSING

PROVED AN AMBITIOUS TASK. HIGHWAY 46 IS ONLY PAVED ABOUT

HALF THE DISTANCE. THE FIRST 25 KILOMETERS FROM VINH ARE

UNDERGOING IMPROVEMENT AND IT APPEARS THAT SECTION WILL BE

RELATIVELY GOOD WITHIN SIX MONTHS TO A YEAR. AFTER PASSING

THROUGH THE SMALL TOWN OF DUNG, ABOUT 40 KILOMETERS FROM

VINH, THE ROAD IS UNPAVED AND GRADUALLY BECOMES QUITE BUMPY

AND RUTTED. IN ADDITION, THERE ARE NUMEROUS TURNS TO GET TO

THANH THUY, NONE OF WHICH IS SIGNPOSTED. EVEN AN

EXPERIENCED EMBASSY DRIVER HAD TO STOP SEVERAL TIMES TO

CONFIRM DIRECTIONS. THE TRIP ALSO INCLUDED FORDING A STREAM

WHERE THERE WAS NO BRIDGE. A LOCAL PERSON ENCOUNTERED ON

THE BANK SAID THE STREAM EXISTS ONLY DURING THE RAINY SEASON

AND IT IS "NOT USUALLY A PROBLEM TO CROSS, UNLESS THERE HAS

BEEN VERY HEAVY RAIN."

6. (U) EMBOFFS MANAGED TO GET TO WITHIN EIGHT KILOMETERS

OF THE NEW BORDER CROSSING, WHICH IS ACTUALLY ABOUT 17

KILOMETERS BEYOND THE VILLAGE OF THANH THUY. HOWEVER,

FURTHER PROGRESS WAS IMPOSSIBLE, WITH THE ROAD BLOCKED DUE

TO ONGOING CONSTRUCTION OVER A GULLY. A LOCAL WOMAN

BRINGING VEGETABLES TO CONSTRUCTION WORKERS ON HER

MOTORBIKE, EXPLAINED THAT THE BORDER ROAD HAD BEEN CLOSED

FOR "ABOUT ONE WEEK." SHE SAID SHE HEARD IT WOULD BE OPEN

IN ABOUT ONE MONTH. PRIOR TO THE CONSTRUCTION, LAO AND

VIETNAMESE CROSSED BACK AND FORTH "REGULARLY." WITH A

TRAVEL PERMIT, IT IS ALSO POSSIBLE TO STAY OVERNIGHT. SHE

HAD HEARD AN OCCASIONAL FOREIGNER CROSSED, BUT SUSPECTED

THESE WERE PROBABLY PEOPLE WORKING FOR AID ORGANIZATIONS OR

THE UNITED NATIONS-NOT TOURISTS. THE VENDOR SAID SHE HOPED

THE NEW BORDER CROSSING AND ROAD IMPROVEMENT WOULD INCREASE

CROSS BORDER TRADE, BUT NOTED, "WE ARE POOR ON THIS SIDE AND

THE LAO ARE EVEN POORER ON THE OTHER SIDE."

--------------------------------------------

TRADE DEVELOPMENT: SLOW, BUT WE\'RE LEARNING

--------------------------------------------

7. (U) CUONG AND DOAN NAM, VICE DIRECTOR OF THE PROVINCIAL

MPI, SAID SEPARATELY THAT THEY ARE OPTIMISTIC REGARDING

IMPROVED TRADE AND INVESTMENT WITH THE U.S. IN THE WAKE OF

THE U.S.-VIETNAM BILATERAL TRADE AGREEMENT (BTA). NAM SAID

THAT THERE HAS NOT BEEN "A SIGNIFICANT" INCREASE IN INTEREST

FROM US INVESTORS, BUT HE EXPECTS IT WILL COME "IN TIME."

ONE MAJOR BENEFIT HAS BEEN THE OPPORTUNITY FOR VIETNAMESE

OFFICIALS AND BUSINESS PEOPLE TO TRAVEL TO THE U.S. TO LEARN

MORE ABOUT THE MARKETS THERE. IN ADDITION, SEMINARS

ORGANIZED BY GVN OFFICIALS HAVE HELPED THE LOCAL BUSINESS

COMMUNITY BETTER UNDERSTAND THE BTA AND WHAT NEEDS TO BE

DONE TO COMPETE IN THE US MARKET. CUONG OF THE PEOPLE\'S

COMMITTEE SAID THAT MANY VIETNAMESE BUSINESS PEOPLE DO NOT

YET UNDERSTAND HOW TO DO BUSINESS WITH THE U.S., BUT IN THE

LONG RUN, THE BTA "WILL BENEFIT EVERYBODY." CUONG SAID

BASED ON FACTORS SUCH AS THE NGHE AN\'S "CENTRAL LOCATION,"

THE RAILWAY, HIGHWAY 1, THE AIRPORT AT VINH, AND THE PORT,

HE IS OPTIMISTIC.

-----------------------------

LACK OF INFRASTRUCTURE INLAND

-----------------------------

8. (U) THE MPI\'S NAM SAID THAT WHILE CONDITIONS ARE

GENERALLY IMPROVING IN NGHE AN, DEVELOPMENT IS STILL

HINDERED BY A LACK OF INFRASTRUCTURE. SPECIFICALLY, HE

NOTED THAT COMMUNICATIONS ARE POOR, WITH "SOME RURAL AREAS"

HAVING NO PHONE SERVICE AND NO INFRASTRUCTURE FOR MOBILE

TELEPHONES. IN ADDITION, THE INLAND ROAD SYSTEM IS "NOT

WELL DEVELOPED." CUONG SAID SEPARATELY, "YOU HAVE SEEN

HIGHWAY 7 (REFTEL), SO YOU KNOW THE PROBLEMS WE FACE AS AN

OUTLET TO THE SEA FOR OUR NEIGHBORS." CUONG ALSO MENTIONED

THE NEED TO UPGRADE INDUSTRIAL TECHNOLOGY. HE CITED ONE

SHOE FACTORY JOINT VENTURE WITH GERMANY THAT WAS FORCED TO

CLOSE LAST YEAR BECAUSE GERMANY "DID NOT SEEM INTERESTED" IN

PROVIDING UP-TO-DATE TECHNOLOGY. AS A RESULT, CUONG SAID

THE FACTORY COULD NOT MEET QUALITY STANDARDS FOR EXPORT.

9. (U) LE DINH NHAM, VICE GENERAL MANAGER OF THE NGHE TINH

PORT, LOCATED AT CUA LO (ABOUT 15 KILOMETERS NORTHEAST OF

VINH) SAID THAT HIS ROLE IS TO "HELP THE PROVINCE DEVELOP."

THE PORT, WHILE NOT NEARLY AS LARGE AS DANANG\'S, CAN HANDLE

SHIPS UP TO 10,000 TONS AND CURRENTLY HAS 550 METERS OF

BERTHS. MOST SHIPS COMING INTO PORT ARE FROM INDIA,

MALAYSIA, ROK, PHILIPPINES, JAPAN, AND "STILL A FEW" FROM

RUSSIA. THE PORT HANDLES BOTH DOMESTIC AND INTERNATIONAL

CARGO AND IS FULLY STAFFED WITH CUSTOMS, HEALTH, AND POLICE

OFFICES. APPROXIMATELY 800,000 TONS OF CARGO PASSED THROUGH

THE PORT IN 2001. NHAM ESTIMATED ABOUT 15 PERCENT CAME FROM

LAOS, MOSTLY PROCESSED FORESTRY PRODUCTS GOING OUT AND

MANUFACTURED GOODS GOING IN. HE PREDICTED THAT THE AMOUNT

TO/FROM LAOS "MAY DECLINE" BECAUSE HE HEARD THE GVN HAS AN

AGREEMENT WITH THE LAO GOVERNMENT TO ENCOURAGE USING VUNG

ANG PORT IN HA TINH PROVINCE, IMMEDIATELY TO THE SOUTH, AND

PLANS TO BUILD A NEW ROAD PARALLEL TO HIGHWAY 8. (NOTE:

ACCORDING TO THE MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS, VIETNAM AND

LAOS OFFICIALS REACHED A "VERBAL AGREEMENT" ON USING VUNG

ANG PORT AT THE 24TH SESSION OF THE VIETNAM-LAOS

INTERGOVERNMENTAL COMMITTEE ON ECONOMIC, CULTURAL,

SCIENTIFIC, AND TECHNICAL COOPERATION, HELD IN JANUARY 2001.

END NOTE.) ON THE OTHER HAND, BUSINESS AT NGHE TIN MAY

PICK UP, ESPECIALLY WITH THE NEW BORDER CROSSING AND

COMMENT

-------

10. (U) IT WAS NOT SURPRISING TO HEAR OPTIMISM FROM THE

PEOPLE\'S COMMITTEE AND THE MPI, BUT PROVINCIAL OFFICIALS

APPEARED RELATIVELY REALISTIC IN THEIR ASSESSMENTS. WHILE

THE TRANSPORTATION FACILITIES IN AND OUT OF THE PROVINCIAL

CAPITAL ARE A PLUS, THINGS GO DOWNHILL FAST AS ONE MOVES

INLAND TOWARDS THE LAO BORDER. BASED ON TWO EMBASSY TRIPS

TO NGHE AN OVER THE PAST 9 MONTHS, IT SEEMS CLEAR THAT

GENERAL DEVELOPMENT, AS WELL AS PROGRESS IN BECOMING MORE

PROMINENT AS AN OUTLET TO SEA FOR ITS NEIGHBORS, IS STILL A

WORK IN PROGRESS. PORTER
