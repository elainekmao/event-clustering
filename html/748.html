SUBJECT: FAMILIES OF THE MISSING REQUEST ASSISTANCE

1. THIS IS AN ACTION MESSAGE -- SEE PARA 3.

2. ON JUNE 28 AMBASSADOR GALBRAITH MET WITH ZELENKA

FARKAS, REPRESENTING THE CENTER FOR PROTECTION OF THE

HUMAN RIGHTS OF IMPRISONED AND MISSING CROATIAN CITIZENS

AND THEIR FAMILIES. FARKAS SOUGHT EMBASSY ASSISTANCE IN

RESOLVING THE FATE OF 2,865 PEOPLE STILL MISSING FROM THE

WAR, AND PARTICULARLY THE JNA SIEGE OF VUKOVAR IN 1991.

THE CENTER HAS COMPILED LISTS OF MISSING PERSONS,

INCLUDING DATES AND LOCATION OF CAPTURE, AND HAS SUBMITTED

THE DATA TO THE UN CENTER FOR HUMAN RIGHTS IN GENEVA.

FARKAS SAID, HOWEVER, THAT SO FAR THEIR APPEALS TO THE UN

FOR ASSISTANCE HAVE NOT BEEN ANSWERED. FARKAS\' GROUP HAS

ALSO SENT AN APPEAL TO SERBIAN PRESIDENT MILOSEVIC

REQUESTING THAT FRY AUTHORITIES PROVIDE INFORMATION ON

WHETHER INDIVIDUALS ARE ALIVE OR DEAD. THE AMBASSADOR

STRESSED THAT THERE IS LITTLE THE EMBASSY CAN DO BEYOND

PROVIDING MORAL SUPPORT, BUT SAID THAT WE ARE STILL

WORKING FOR UN ACCESS TO SUSPECTED MASS GRAVE SITES IN

SECTOR EAST AND CONTINUE TO PRESS AUTHORITIES IN KNIN FOR

INFORMATION. HE SAID THAT IT WAS UNLIKELY THAT OFFICIALS

IN SECTOR EAST OR BELGRADE WOULD COME FORWARD WITH AN

ACCOUNTING OF THE MISSING BECAUSE THEY WOULD IMPLICATE

THEMSELVES IN WAR CRIMES.

3. FOR USUN: POST REQUESTS THAT USUN CONSIDER RAISING THE

ISSUE OF THE MISSING IN CROATIA AND BOSNIA AND

HERCEGOVINA, PERHAPS IN A UNSC PRESIDENTIAL STATEMENT.

THIS WOULD FOCUS ATTENTION ON A COMPELLING HUMANITARIAN

PROBLEM AND BE OF COMFORT TO FAMILIES WHO FEEL THEIR

CONCERNS ARE FORGOTTEN. IT WOULD ALSO KEEP ATTENTION

FOCUSED ON WAR CRIMES, WITH SOME PRESUMED DETERRENT

BENEFIT.

GALBRAITH
