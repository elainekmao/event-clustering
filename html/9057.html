SUBJECT: (U) UAE SUPPORT FOR THE POLISH-LED

MULTINATIONAL DIVISION IN IRAQ

REF: SECDEF 251715Z

1. (U) Classified by Ambassador Marcelle M. Wahba

for reasons 1.5 (B) and (D).

2. (C) The Ambassador raised the issue of UAE

support for the Polish-led multinational division

with UAE Armed Forces Chief of Staff Muhammad bin

Zayid Al-Nahyan (MbZ) via letter and a follow-up

phone call on June 28. Attached to the letter was

the list of countries requiring transportation

assistance detailed reftel. MbZ\'s Special

Assistant, Yousef Al-Otaiba, telephoned Polchief

June 30 with the UAE\'s official response. The UAE

has agreed to provide transportation assistance to

the eight European countries listed, i.e. Poland,

Ukraine, Bulgaria, Hungary, Lithuania, Slovakia,

Latvia and Romania. Al-Otaiba said the UAE was

prepared to offer two "roll-on, roll-off" ships and

one hundred hours of an Aleutian 124 aircraft to

ferry the troops and their equipment to Iraq. He

indicated that UAE GHQ Director of Operations

Brigadier Ali Al-Kaabi would be in touch with the

Embassy\'s Acting USLO Chief for specific

arrangements.

3. (C) On the approach to Dubai Crown Prince and

UAE Defense Minister Muhammad bin Rashid Al-Maktoum

(MbR) regarding vehicles for Iraq and Afghanistan,

our Acting CG in Dubai has a meeting scheduled with

Sayanta Basu of The Investment Office on Tuesday,

July 1. We will report on the details of that

meeting as soon as they are available.

4. (C) Comment: Given the UAE\'s finite military

transportation resources, we believe their pledge

to support the mobilization of the Polish-led

division based out of Europe is appropriate and,

indeed, quite generous. The UAE military would

have been unable, in our opinion, to support the

transportation needs of troops from some of the

more far-flung areas of the globe.

WAHBA
