FROM AMBASSADOR TONY P. HALL ON THE PRESENT CRISIS IN THE

1. FOLLOWING IS TEXT OF A LETTER TO U/SYG KENZO OSHIMA, UNDP

ADMINISTRATOR MALLOCH BROWN AND UNICEF EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR

CAROL BELLAMY DATED FEBRUARY 27, 2003 FROM TONY P. HALL,

AMBASSADOR, U.S. MISSION TO THE UN AGENCIES, ROME. BEGIN

LETTER:

THE HONORABLE KENZO OSHIMA, U/SYG, UN OFFICE FOR THE

COORDINATION OF HUMANITARIAN AFFAIRS (OCHA)

MARK MALLOCH BROWN, UNDP ADMINISTRATOR

CAROL BELLAMY, EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR, UNICEF

UNITED NATIONS HEADQUARTERS

NEW YORK CITY, NEW YORK 10017-3505

DEAR UN COLLEAGUES,

I HAVE JUST RETURNED FROM A TRIP TO ETHIOPIA (FEBRUARY

15-21), A COUNTRY I VISITED FOR THE FIRST TIME IN 1984

DURING A DEVASTATING FAMINE THAT ULTIMATELY SAW OVER ONE

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ROME 00832 01 OF 02 271638Z

MILLION PEOPLE DIE OF STARVATION AND RELATED CAUSES. I HAD

THE GOOD FORTUNE THIS TIME TO BE ACCOMPANIED BY USAID

ASSISTANT ADMINISTRATOR ROGER WINTER (WHO FOR MANY YEARS

ABLY MANAGED THE U.S. COMMITTEE FOR REFUGEES), AND WE WERE

ABLE TO VISIT A NUMBER OF RURAL SITES IN EAST SHEWA, OMOMIYA

AND SOUTHERN NATIONS (SNNPR).

AS PER THE U.S. CENTER FOR DISEASE CONTROL (CDC)

(PRESENTLY ASSESSING THE NUTRITIONAL AND CONTAGIOUS DISEASE

STATUS IN FOUR ETHIOPIAN GEOGRAPHIC ZONES), THEY ESTIMATE

(FEBRUARY-JUNE 2003) 32,000 EXCESS DEATHS DUE TO DROUGHT

WILL OCCUR IN THE REVIEWED AREAS. IF THIS TREND IS PROJECTED

NATIONALLY, UNLESS VIGOROUS ACTION IS TAKEN NOW WITH THE UN

AGENCIES IN A LEADING ROLE, EXCESS DEATHS COULD REACH AS

HIGH AS 300,000 TO 500,000 PEOPLE BY THE END OF 2003.

I MUST BRING TO YOUR ATTENTION THAT WE REPEATEDLY HEARD

THAT IN THIS CRISIS, EXCEPTING WFP, THE REST OF THE UN

ORGANIZATIONS IN ETHIOPIA "DO NOT HAVE A SENSE OF THE DEPTH

OF THE EMERGENCY AND ARE NOT DOING ENOUGH." THE TRUTH IS

POSSIBLY THAT IN ETHIOPIA THE UN AGENCIES ARE GEARED FAR

MORE TOWARD DEVELOPMENT THAN EMERGENCY RESPONSE. GIVEN THE

WIDESPREAD DETERIORATION IN THE HUMANITARIAN SITUATION,

HOWEVER, THIS NEEDS TO BE ADJUSTED.

SPECIFICALLY, I WOULD ASK YOU TO SERIOUSLY CONSIDER:

- NAMING A RESIDENT HUMANITARIAN COORDINATOR TO FOCUS ON

IMPROVING THE EFFICIENCY AND OVERALL EFFECTIVENESS OF UN

OPERATIONS IN RESPONDING TO THE PRESENT CRISIS IN BOTH

ETHIOPIA AND ERITREA;

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 04 ROME 00832 01 OF 02 271638Z

- PRIORITIZING ASSISTANCE TO THE ESTABLISHMENT OF A

NATIONWIDE NUTRITION SURVEILLANCE SYSTEM. WE ARE SIMPLY NOT

GETTING THE "WARNING" DATA WE NEED TO RESPOND AS REQUIRED;

- THE UN (AND THEIR PARTNER NGOS) SHOULD TAKE ADVANTAGE OF

FOOD DELIVERIES (WHERE LARGE NUMBERS OF BENEFICIARIES OFTEN

WAIT FOR HOURS TO RECEIVE THEIR RATION) TO IMPART MOTHER-

CHILD CARE HEALTH TRAINING AND NUTRITION EDUCATION MESSAGES.

IN PARTICULAR, FEEDING SITES SHOULD SERVE AS FOCAL POINTS

FOR IMMUNIZATIONS AGAINST MEASLES AND OTHER CONTAGIOUS

DISEASES SUCH AS TYPHUS, RELAPSING FEVER, MENINGITIS.

IN MY MANY YEARS IN CONGRESS, I FULLY ENDORSED

APPROPRIATIONS FOR UN AGENCIES AND AM HONORED TO BE

REPRESENTING MY COUNTRY TO THE UN AGENCIES ON FOOD AND

AGRICULTURE HERE IN ROME. FOR ME, ETHIOPIA IS UNIQUE AMONG

AFRICAN COUNTRIES FOR HAVING NEVER BEEN COLONIZED. IT IS

TRULY BLESSED WITH A RICH CULTURE, HISTORY AND TRADITIONAL

KNOWLEDGE. BUT THIS PRESENT CRISIS, UNLESS DEFTLY HANDLED,

COULD SETBACK YEARS OF DEVELOPMENT GAINS. WE ALL NEED TO

INTENSIFY OUR EFFORTS.

UNCLASSIFIED
