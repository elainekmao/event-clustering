BACK UNFICYP EXTENSION, DUTCH TROOPS LEAVING IN 2001

--------------------------------------------- -------

1.(SBU) THE DUTCH SUPPORT AN EXTENSION OF UNFICYP\'S MANDATE

AND RECOGNIZE THE NEED TO CONTINUE INDICATING STRONG

INTERNATIONAL SUPPORT FOR COMPREHENSIVE SETTLEMENT TALKS

(REF.A).

2.(C) AS FOR UNFICYP\'S COMPOSITION, DUTCH MFA UN AFFAIRS

OFFICIALS NOTED TO POLOFF THAT THEIR 99-MARINE UNFICYP

CONTINGENT WILL ROTATE OUT OF CYPRUS NO LATER THAN JUNE 2001.

THEY SAID THAT THEY ARE REMINDING THE UK OF THIS PLANNED STEP

AND RECALLED THAT THEIR COMMITMENT TO UNFICYP ALWAYS HAS BEEN

LIMITED TO A THREE-YEAR TERM. THEY SAID THAT THIS MOVE DID

NOT REPRESENT A DIMUNITION OF THEIR INTEREST IN CYPRUS, BUT

RATHER MARKED THE END OF THE SUSTAINABLE DEPLOYMENT PERIOD

FOR THE AD HOC MARINE TASK FORCE NOW INCORPORATED INTO THE

UK\'S UNFICYP FORCE.

UK DRAFT ON OIL-FOR-FOOD OK, BUT STILL HAS PROBLEMS

--------------------------------------------- ------

3.(C) THE DUTCH PLAN TO WORK OFF THE UK DRAFT OIL-FOR-FOOD

RESOLUTION, PER. REF.B, BUT CONSIDER THAT IT DOES NOT GO FAR

ENOUGH IN STREAMLINING ITEMS THAT NEED SANCTIONS COMMITTEE

ATTENTION. DUTCH MFA UN AFFAIRS DIRECTOR PIM DUMORE SAID

THAT DUTCH VIEWS ON THESE SANCTION ISSUES ARE "WELL KNOWN IN

WASHINGTON AND IN NEW YORK." POLOFF NOTED THAT THE U.S.

VALUED OUR DIALOGUE WITH AMBASSADOR VAN WALSUM ON SANCTIONS

QUESTIONS AND THOUGHT THAT THE UK DRAFT INCORPORATED MANY OF

VAN WALSUM\'S SUGGESTIONS FOR IMPROVING THE OIL-FOR-FOOD

PROGRAM.

4.(C) DUMORE SAID THAT HE RECOGNIZED THAT THE UK DRAFT IS AN

IMPROVEMENT ON THE STATUS QUO, BUT CAUTIONED THAT, AS

CURRENTLY DRAFTED, IT STILL LEAVES "TOO MANY UNNECESSARY

ITEMS ON THE SANCTIONS REVIEW LISTS, UNDERMINING THE

POLITICAL RESOLVE SUPPORTING CONTINUED MONITORING OF

TRANSFERS TO IRAQ OF KEY DUAL-USE ITEMS THAT MIGHT SERVE WMD

RECONSTITUTION PURPOSES." HE WARNED THAT, UNLESS FURTHER

REFINED TO ANSWER "THE HUMANITARIAN SYMPATHIES CHAMPIONED IN

THE FRENCH DRAFT AND POPULAR WITH EUROPEAN PARLIAMENTS AND

PUBLICS," THE POLITICAL SUPPORT FOR AN OIL-FOR-FOOD PROGRAM

THAT STILLS RETAINS PROPER SAFEGUARDS ON DUAL-USE ITEMS WILL

FURTHER SUFFER.
