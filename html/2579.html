SUBJECT: MARCH 7 MEETING WITH NIGERIAN ELECTION

DELEGATION

CLASSIFIED BY JOSEPH G. SULLIVAN FOR REASONS 1.5B/D

1. (C) THE AMBASSADOR MET MARCH 8 WITH THE HEAD OF

THE NIGERIAN ELECTION OBSERVER DELEGATION CHIEF

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 00618 081132Z

SHONEKAN, THE NIGERIAN ELECTION COMMISSION

REPRESENTATIVE AND HIGH COMMISSIONER JUTA. SHONEKAN

HAD EARLIER BEEN PRESIDENT OBASSANJO\'S SPECIAL

EMISSARY TO ZIMBABWE FOR THE ABUJA PROCESS AND

ACCOMPANIED OBASSANJO ON HIS LATE JANUARY VISIT TO

ZIMBABWE TO MEET WITH PRESIDENT MUGABE AND MDC

LEADER TSVANGIRAI. SHONEKAN, WHO SAID HE WAS

SURPRISED THAT OBASSANJO HAD SELECTED HIM FOR THIS

ELECTION MISSION, PUT HIS EMPHASIS ON THE NEED FOR

ZIMBABWE\'S ECONOMIC RECOVERY UNDER WHICHEVER

CANDIDATE WAS ELECTED. HE RELATED THE COMMITMENTS

TO END VIOLENCE AND RUN A FAIR ELECTION MADE BY

PRESIDENT MUGABE DURING HIS JANUARY VISIT. BUT,

SHONEKAN NOT SEEM TO DRAW THE APPROPRIATE

CONCLUSIONS ABOUT MUGABE\'S LACK OF GOOD FAITH WHEN

THE AMBASSADOR OUTLINED THE GOVERNMENT-ORGANIZED

CAMPAIGN OF TARGETED VIOLENCE AND INTIMIDATION WHICH

WAS CONTINUING AND THE PROCEDURAL MANIPULATIONS AT

WORK. SHONEKAN WAS PLEASED WITH HIS MEETING WITH

MDC LEADER TSVANGIRAI, AND PARTICULARLY HIS

WILLINGNESS TO FORM A NATIONAL UNITY GOVERNMENT, BUT

WAS NOT DISTRESSED ABOUT THE ABSENCE OF INDICATIONS

OF CONCILIATORY SIGNALS FROM ZANU-PF.

2.(C) THE NIGERIAN ELECTION COMMISSIONER (WE DID NOT GET

HIS NAME.) AND THE NIGERIAN HIGH COMMISSIONER WERE MORE

ENGAGED. THEY EXPRESSED PARTICULAR DISTRESS ABOUT THE

REPORT THAT TENS OF THOUSANDS OF ARMED FORCES MEMBERS

HAD VOTED ABSENTEE. THE ELECTION REGISTRAR HAD

CONFIRMED TO THEM THAT THIS WAS SO, BUT SAID HE DID NOT

KNOW UNDER WHAT CIRCUMSTANCES THEY HAD VOTED. THE

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 00618 081132Z

AMBASSADOR CONFIRMED THAT OUR INFORMATION SHOWED THAT

THEY HAD VOTED UNDER THE GUIDANCE OF THEIR COMMANDING

OFFICER. THE ELECTION COMMISSIONER EXPRESSED CONCERN

OVER THE LAST-MINUTE CHANGES IN ELECTION PROCEDURES AND

THE HIGH COMMISSIONER EXPRESSED CONCERN WHEN HE RECEIVED

A CALL FROM HIS ELECTION OBSERVERS IN MUTARE REPORTING

THAT ZANU-PF ACTIVISTS HAD BEEN GIVEN ELECTION OBSERVER

BADGES IDENTICAL TO THOSE OF HIS DELEGATION.

3. (C) CHIEF SHONEKAN\'S LACK OF ENGAGEMENT IN THE KEY

ELECTION ISSUES WAS NOTABLE AND WE HAVE LITTLE

CONFIDENCE IN WHAT RECOMMENDATIONS HE WILL MAKE TO

PRESIDENT OBASSANJO FOLLOWING THE ELECTIONS. HE EVEN

SEEMED TO SHRUG OFF OUR COMMENT THAT PRESIDENTS

OBASSANJO AND MBEKI HAD THE GREATEST CAPACITY TO

CONVINCE PRESIDENT MUGABE TO ACCEPT A PEACEFUL

DEMOCRATIC TRANSITION. HC JUTA, ON THE OTHER HAND, WAS

MORE ENGAGED THAN WE HAVE EVER SEEN HIM AND HE MADE A

POINT OF SUGGESTING THAT WE BE IN CONTACT ON MONDAY TO

COMPARE NOTES. WE KNOW THAT JUTA WAS RECENTLY CALLED

HOME TO RECOMMEND HOW PRESIDENT OBASSANJO COULD BE

HELPFUL AND HE SEEMS TO BE TAKING THE RESPONSIBILITY

SERIOUSLY.

SULLIVAN
