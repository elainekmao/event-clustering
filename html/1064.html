SUBJECT: PROPOSAL FOR POTUS TO ENGAGE MUGABE BY

TELEPHONE ON CURRENT DISTURBANCES IN ZIMBABWE

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD FOR REASON 1.5

(D).

1. (C) RECENT, PRESIDENTIALLY DIRECTED EVENTS HAVE

TAKEN ZIMBABWE TO THE VERGE OF CRISIS. WE BELIEVE A

STRONG AND HIGH-LEVEL MESSAGE APPEALING FOR CALM,

RESTRAINT AND A RETURN TO DEMOCRACY AND RESPECT FOR THE

RULE OF LAW AND HUMAN RIGHTS IS CRITICAL. IN THIS

CONNECTION, I STRONGLY URGE THAT PRESIDENT DELIVER SUCH

A MESSAGE VIA A TELEPHONE CALL AT THE EARLIEST

OPPORTUNITY TO PRESIDENT ROBERT MUGABE.

2. (C) WITH THE PROBABILITY OF BEING DRIVEN FROM OFFICE

BY THE FIRST VIABLE OPPOSITION CHALLENGE IN TWENTY

YEARS, ROBERT MUGABE HAS UNLEASHED A REIGN OF TERROR

AGAINST THE RIVAL MOVEMENT FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE (MDC)

AND THIS COUNTRY\'S WHITE FARMERS. THE CHIEF EXECUTIVE

BELIEVES THE LATTER TO BE BANKROLLING THE OPPOSITION

PARTY AND HELPING TO RECRUIT AND ORGANIZE ITS RURAL

CONSTITUENCY. MUGABE HAS USED MEMBERS OF THE NATIONAL

LIBERATION WAR VETERANS\' ASSOCIATION AND THE STATE

SECURITY APPARATUS TO PERPETRATE THE VIOLENCE. IT IS A

HARDLY SPONTANEOUS OR NATIONAL MOVEMENT; MOST RIGHT-

THINKING ZIMBABWEANS ARE REPELLED BY THE FARM

OCCUPATIONS. WITHIN THE LAST SEVEN DAYS TWO MDC PARTY

OPERATIVES WERE KILLED IN A FIREBOMB ATTACK ON THEIR

VEHICLE, TWO WHITE FARMERS WERE FATALLY SHOT, A SCORE

WERE BEATEN TO WITHIN AN INCH OF THEIR LIVES, AND

OSTENSIBLE WAR VETERANS RAPED A PAIR OF WHITE WOMEN IN

THEIR FARMHOUSE NEAR HARARE INTERNATIONAL AIRPORT. THE

"WAR VETERANS" HAVE BATTERED HUNDREDS OF BLACK FARM

WORKERS; STOLEN OR DESTROYED THEIR PROPERTY, AND KILLED

AN UNKNOWN NUMBER. TO THEIR CREDIT, THE COMMERCIAL

FARMERS UNION HAS INSTRUCTED ITS MEMBERS NOT TO RESORT

TO VIOLENCE, TAKING SOME OF THE POTENTIAL FOR EXPLOSION

OUT OF THE OPERATION, AND MAKING IT POSSIBLE FOR THE

OBJECTIVE VIEWER TO DECIDE WHO IS AT FAULT. THE POLICE,

AT MUGABE\'S INSTRUCTION, HAVE LARGELY STOOD ON THE

SIDELINES AND PASSIVELY WATCHED THE SPECTACLE OF INJURY

AND DESTRUCTION.

3. (C) WHILE MUGABE AND THE LEADERS OF THE RULING ZANU-

PF PUT THE VIOLENCE IN THE CONTEXT OF REMOVING THE LAST

AND MOST UNJUST VESTIGE OF COLONIAL RULE, THE OWNERSHIP

OF THE LAND, IN OUR JUDGMENT THE STRUGGLE IS ALL ABOUT

POWER: WHO GETS IT AND WHO KEEPS IT. THE PRESIDENT

STANDS TO LOSE SOME OF IT IN THE STILL UNSCHEDULED

PARLIAMENTARY ELECTION. IF THE RULING PARTY LOSES

ENOUGH SEATS, SAY OVER 50 PERCENT, MDC LEADER MORGAN

TSVANGIRAI TELLS US THAT MUGABE WOULD STEP DOWN,

ACT AS A REAL CHECK TO UNTRAMMELED EXECUTIVE POWER.

MUGABE\'S VIEW OF POWER IS MANICHEAN: EITHER YOU HAVE IT

ALL OR YOU HAVE NOTHING. MOREOVER, THE ZIMBABWEAN

PRESIDENT MUST KNOW THAT THERE ARE MORE THAN ENOUGH

PEOPLE IN ZIMBABWE WITH A GOOD REASON TO KILL HIM, AND

THAT AS AN EX-PRESIDENT HE WOULD NOT LAST LONG, AT LEAST

POLITICAL NOSE TELLS HIM THAT CONDITIONS DO NOT EXIST

FOR THAT OUTCOME TO OCCUR WITHIN THE TIME FRAME

PRESCRIBED BY THE CONSTITUTION, THERE IS SUFFICIENT

TURMOIL IN THE COUNTRY FOR HIM TO DECLARE MARTIAL LAW OR

A STATE OF EMERGENCY, WHICH HE COULD USE TO SCRUB THE

4. (C) WE HAVE MADE A NUMBER OF DEMARCHES TO MINISTERS

AND EVEN TO PRESIDENT MUGABE HIMSELF TO ENFORCE THE LAW.

I HAVE PURSUED OUR DONOR COLLEAGUES WITH VIGOR TO WEIGH

HAVE HEARD NOTHING BUT DEMURRALS, TEMPORIZING, AND EVEN

LEAD. I THINK THAT THE TIME HAS COME FOR US TO RATCHET

UP THE PRESSURE UNILATERALLY. THE NEXT STEP, IN MY

VIEW, IS TO REQUEST THE PRESIDENT TO MAKE A TELEPHONE

5. (C) THE PRINCIPAL PITFALL IN A TELEPHONE CALL IS

THAT MUGABE WILL MAKE AN EFFORT TO DRAW THE PRESIDENT

INTO A DEBATE ON THE LAND ISSUE IN ZIMBABWE, AND THE

USG\'S OSTENSIBLE OBLIGATIONS IN THAT REGARD. WE WOULD

URGE THAT THE PRESIDENT STEER AWAY FROM THE TOPIC TO THE

EXTENT POSSIBLE, AND FOCUS ON THE INTERNATIONAL

COMMUNITY\'S VIEW OF RECENT EVENTS IN ZIMBABWE AND WHAT

IT COULD MEAN DOWN THE ROAD. WITHOUT MINCING WORDS, THE

BEST TACTICAL APPROACH IN THIS CONVERSATION WOULD BE TO

EMPHASIZE HISTORY\'S VIEW OF LEADERS: THE LAURELS FOR

THOSE WHO ARE LARGE ENOUGH TO RISE TO THE DEMANDS OF A

SITUATION, VERSUS BRICKBATS OR OBLIVION FOR THOSE WHO

HAVE BEEN BLINDED BY SHORT-TERM CIRCUMSTANCE. MUGABE

VALUES HIS LEGACY AND MAY WELL PROVE MOST RECEPTIVE TO

AN APPROACH ALONG THESE LINES. THE PRESIDENT SHOULD

STRESS TO MUGABE THAT:

-- WHILE NOT PULLING PUNCHES WITH REGARDS TO U.S.

CONCERNS ABOUT SUCH ISSUES AS THE GOZ\'S FAILURE TO

UPHOLD THE RULE OF LAW AND MUGABE\'S PUBLIC STATEMENTS

THAT CAN BE READ AS AN INCITEMENT TO RACIAL OR POLITICAL

VIOLENCE, WE HAVE MADE CLEAR OUR DESIRE TO WORK WITH

ZIMBABWE BOTH TO ADDRESS THE STILL-UNRESOLVED LAND

ISSUE, AND TO HELP HALT THE COUNTRY\'S DOWNWARD ECONOMIC

AND POLITICAL SPIRAL;

-- AS CHIEF EXECUTIVE OF THE REPUBLIC OF ZIMBABWE,

MUGABE\'S FIRST RESPONSIBILITY IS TO ENSURE THAT THE RULE

OF LAW IS UPHELD;

-- THE U.S. VIEWS ZIMBABWE AS A FELLOW DEMOCRACY, AND

THAT IN DEMOCRACIES PEOPLE HAVE THE RIGHT AND FREEDOM TO

CHANGE THEIR GOVERNMENT VIA THE BALLOT BOX; AND

-- THERE IS AN URGENT NEED TO ADDRESS THE ECONOMIC

MORASS, SINCE FARMERS, ESPECIALLY NEW ONES, NEED PEACE,

PREDICTABILITY AND BANK LINES TO PLOW THEIR FIELDS AND

REAP THE FRUITS OF THEIR EFFORTS.
