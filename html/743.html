SUBJECT: SERB LEADER DZAKULA SUGGESTS MEASURES FOR

CONFIDENCE-BUILDING AND ENCOURAGING SERB RETURN

1. CONFIDENTIAL - ENTIRE TEXT.

2. POLOFFS MET WITH VELJKO DZAKULA AT HIS REQUEST JUNE 6.

HE WANTED TO SUGGEST CONCRETE MEASURES TO BEGIN THE

SERIOUS REINTEGRATION OF THE REMAINING SERB AND RETURNING

CROAT COMMUNITIES OF WESTERN SLAVONIA. DZAKULA ESTIMATED

THAT BETWEEN 800 TO 1000 SERBS WERE STILL LIVING IN PAKRAC

COUNTY AND ANOTHER 200 REMAINED IN OKUCANI. HE EMPHASIZED

THAT THIS REMAINING SERB POPULATION WAS ATTEMPTING TO

REORGANIZE THEIR LIVES UNDER A CROAT-CONTROLLED SYSTEM,

BUT THE CROATIANS HAD SHOWED LITTLE GOODWILL SO FAR TOWARD

KEEPING THE SERBIAN POPULATION IN THE AREA. HIGH-LEVEL

GOC OFFICIALS HAD PROMISED DZAKULA TO WORK TOWARDS

ENCOURAGING SERBS TO RETURN FROM BOSNIA AND SECTOR EAST,

HE SAID, BUT LITTLE HAD COME OF THESE TALKS. THE MINISTRY

OF INTERNAL AFFAIRS (MUP) WAS MAKING IT DIFFICULT FOR

MILITARY-AGE MEN TO GET CITIZENSHIP PAPERS (DOMOVNICA),

ALTHOUGH WOMEN AND ELDERLY HAD RECEIVED THEM. DZAKULA HAD

BEEN OFFERED A DOMOVNICA, BUT REFUSED TO ACCEPT ONE UNTIL

EVERY SERB IN THE COUNTY RECEIVED HIS. DAILY CIVIL LIFE

WAS STILL A PROBLEM. ABOUT 180 MEN WERE STILL JAILED

AROUND THE COUNTRY FOR ARMED REBELLION EVEN THOUGH AN

AMNESTY HAD BEEN PROCLAIMED AND GOC OFFICIALS HAD PROMISED

TO RELEASE THEM. EVEN MUNDANE THINGS SUCH AS GATHERING

WOOD FOR FUEL HAD BECOME A PROBLEM.

3. DZAKULA IDENTIFIED THREE PRECONDITIONS WHICH WOULD

THE AMNESTY DECLARED BY THE GOC HAD TO BE COMPLIED WITH

AND EVEN BROADENED TO INCLUDE NOT ONLY ARMED REBELLION,

BUT ALSO ORGANIZING, SALE OF WEAPONS, AND OTHER SIMILAR

CROAT JAILS HAD TO BE RELEASED IMMEDIATELY IF THERE WERE

NO PENDING WAR CRIMES CHARGES AGAINST THEM. SECOND, A

MORATORIUM MUST BE IMPOSED ON THE DRAFTING AND

MOBILIZATION INTO THE CROATIAN ARMY OF SERBS FROM WEST

SLAVONIA. SERBS DISPLACED TO BANJA LUKA AND ELSEWHERE

MUST BE MADE TO FEEL THEY WOULD RETURN FROM AN AREA OF WAR

TO AN AREA OF PEACE IN WESTERN SLAVONIA.

4. THIRD, THE POLITICAL COMPOSITION OF THE LOCAL

GOVERNMENT NEEDED TO BE WORKED OUT TO BALANCE THE TWO

POPULATIONS. THE CONTINUED PRESENCE OF SO MANY POLICE TO

PROTECT THE POPULATION WAS ENCOURAGING BUT THE POLICE

FORCE NEEDED TO BE REORGANIZED ACCORDING TO THE NATIONAL

CENSUS OF 1991. WHILE DZAKULA ACKNOWLEDGED THE SMALL

REMAINING POPULATION OF SERBS IN THE AREA MADE

REPRESENTATION PROPORTIONAL TO THE 1991 CENSUS FIGURES

IMPOSSIBLE IN THE IMMEDIATE FUTURE, HE CLAIMED THAT THERE

WERE SEVERAL QUALIFIED SERBIAN POLICEMEN IN THE REGION WHO

WISHED TO RETURN TO THEIR PREVIOUS JOBS. ON JUNE 7, THE

GOC HAD ANNOUNCED THE CREATION OF A COMMISSION TO MONITOR

THE PEACEFUL REINTEGRATION OF THE PAKRAC AND LIPIK

COMMUNITIES. THIS COMMITTEE WOULD CONSIST OF FIVE

MEMBERS, TWO OF WHICH WOULD ALWAYS BE SERBS, INCLUDING THE

COMMITTEE\'S PRESIDENT. ALTHOUGH SUPPORTIVE OF CREATING

JOINT LOCAL GOVERNMENT AUTHORITIES, DZAKULA INSISTED THAT

75,000 FIGURE IS FOR SERBS DISPLACED IN 1991 AND SINCE.)

THE PROBLEMS OF ALL THESE PEOPLE. ONLY THEN WOULD THE

FLEEING SERB POPULATION BEGIN TO RETURN.
