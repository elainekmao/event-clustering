CLASSIFIED BY DCM REWHITEHEAD DUE TO 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) SUMMARY. THE TWO TOP OFFICIALS OF ZIMBABWE\'S

ELECTORAL SUPERVISORY COMMISSION (ESC) BRIEFED

INTERNATIONAL ELECTION OBSERVERS ON THE MORNING OF MARCH

6 ABOUT THE STATUS OF THE MARCH 9 AND 10 VOTE. IN A

SURREAL NINETY-MINUTE SPEECH, THE RECENTLY APPOINTED

NUMBER TWO OF THE ESC, A PRO-ZANU-PF BRIGADIER, PAINTED

A ROSY PICTURE OF ELECTION READINESS AND DUCKED KEY

QUESTIONS ABOUT THE LOCATION OF POLLING PLACES, WHAT

LOCAL OBSERVERS WILL BE ACCREDITED, AND EXACTLY WHAT

ELECTORAL RULES ARE IN FORCE. WITH LESS THAN SEVENTY-

TWO HOURS BEFORE THE POLLS OPEN, THE GOZ STALL STRATEGY

HAS EFFECTIVELY ENSURED THAT ELECTION-DAY

DISORGANIZATION AND UNCERTAINTY WILL REIGN. END

SUMMARY.

ELECTION COMMISSIONER: "IT\'S NOT OUR FAULT!"

---------------------------------------------

2. (SBU) ESC COMMISSIONER GULA-NDEBELE KICKED OFF THE

PRE-ELECTION BRIEFING WITH A SHORT SPEECH THAT STRESSED

THAT THE ESC ONLY SUPERVISES ELECTIONS, AND THAT THE

REGISTRAR GENERAL IS FULLY RESPONSIBLE FOR THE SMOOTH

COMPORTMENT OF THE VOTE. WITH THIS HOT-POTATO IN THE

AIR, GULA-NDEBELE MADE SOME VAGUE COMMENTS ABOUT HOW

POLITICAL PARTIES CONTESTING THE ELECTION "PERCEIVE" --

HE EMPHASIZED THAT THIS WAS NOT NECESSARILY SO -- THAT

NGOS ARE PARTISAN. THIS HAD FORCED THE ESC TO SEARCH

FOR NON-PARTISAN GROUPS TO ACT AS OBSERVERS. HE

CONCLUDED THAT SINCE ALL THE POLITICAL PARTIES MISTRUST

THE BONA FIDES OF CIVIL SERVANTS, THIS MAKES THEM THE

LOGICAL CHOICE TO SERVE AS MONITORS.

THE BRIGADIER DECLAIMS

----------------------

3. (SBU) NEXT IN LINE WAS THE ESC DEPUTY COMMISSIONER

NYIKAYARAMBA, AN ERSTWHILE ZDF BRIGADIER WHO RETIRED TWO

MONTHS PAST TO ASSUME HIS PRESENT ROLE. THE BRIGADIER

GOT OFF ON THE WRONG FOOT WITH SEVERAL DELEGATIONS BY

ANNOUNCING THAT ALL AFRICAN CHIEFS OF MISSION AND THE

HEADS OF OBSERVER GROUPS, AFRICAN ONLY, WERE INVITED TO

A FOLLOW-ON SESSION IN AN ADJACENT MEETING HALL AS SOON

AS THE CURRENT MEETING HAD ENDED.

4. (C) NYIKAYARAMBA PRAISED OBSERVERS AS THE "EYES AND

EARS" OF THE ESC, AND THEN PROCEEDED TO EXPLAIN WHY THE

ESC WILL BE LARGELY BLIND AND DEAF ON ELECTION DAY. HE

STRESSED THAT THE CENTRAL ISSUE OF THE ELECTION WAS LAND

INEQUITY AND THEN DESCRIBED AT LENGTH ELEVEN COMPETING

PARTIES, INCLUDING SEVERAL OF WHICH WE WERE UNAWARE.

THE BRIGADIER THEN MADE A NUMBER OF STATEMENTS THAT WE

WOULD CHARACTERIZE AS INACCURATE, FOR WANT OF A BETTER

WORD. TO WIT:

-- THE TOTAL NUMBER OF POLLING STATIONS HAS EXPANDED

FROM 3,904 FOR THE 2000 PARLIAMENTARY ELECTIONS TO 4,548

IN 2002. NYIKAYARAMBA DID NOT ADDRESS WHY PRO-MDC

(OPPOSITION) URBAN AREAS HAVE LOST LARGE NUMBERS OF

POLLING STATIONS, ALTHOUGH AN ESCA HANDOUT DID STATE

THAT PRO-ZANU-PF (MUGABE) RURAL AREAS HAVE RECEIVED

ADDITIONAL STATIONS "TO SHORTEN WALKING DISTANCES." IT

HAS BEEN DIFFICULT TO CONFIRM THE ACTUAL NUMBERS, SINCE

-- NYIKAYARAMBA SAID THAT THE ESC HAS ALREADY SUCCEEDED

OPTIMISTIC FIGURE OF ONE ISSUED PER MINUTE. IN A

CLASSIC CATCH 22, THE ZESN FOLK MAY EITHER WAIT IN

HARARE FOR CREDENTIALS, AND MISS THE ELECTIONS, OR

DEPLOY TO THE PROVINCES WITHOUT CREDENTIALS NEEDED TO

LET THEM OBSERVE EFFECTIVELY. END NOTE.)

-- NYIKAYARAMBA GOT HIS BIGGEST TITTER WHEN HE ANNOUNCED

THAT THE ESC DEPLORES POLITICALLY MOTIVATED VIOLENCE AND

IS "CARRYING OUT INVESTIGATIONS OF ISOLATED INCIDENTS OF

VIOLENCE."

-- NYIKAYARAMBA ANNOUNCED THAT ELECTION OBSERVERS MAY

SEEK COPIES OF THE CONSTITUTION AND THE ELECTORAL ACT

FROM THE OFFICE OF GOVERNMENT PUBLICATIONS. HE DID NOT

REFER TO THE MARCH 1 GAZETTING OF THE AMENDED ELECTORAL

ACT OF JANUARY 2002, WHICH WAS STRICKEN DOWN LAST WEEK

BY THE SUPREME COURT, ONLY TO BE REINTRODUCED AND

GAZETTED AS THE LAW IN FORCE BY PRESIDENTIAL DIRECTIVE.

Q AND A SESSION

---------------

5. (C) AFTER DEMONSTRATING A MOCK-UP POLLING STATION

AND HOW VOTING IS DONE IN ZIMBABWE, THE ESC FIELDED

QUESTIONS. WHEN ASKED WHEN THEY WOULD PUBLISH THE LIST

OF POLLING PLACES, THEY STATED THAT IT WOULD BE PRINTED

IN THE NEWSPAPERS, ALTHOUGH THEY COULD NOT SAY EXACTLY

WHEN. WHEN ASKED ABOUT WHEN LISTS OF DOMESTIC OBSERVERS

WOULD BE RELEASED, THEY ADMITTED THAT THEY DID NOT KNOW,

BUT WOULD CERTAINLY TAKE THIS UP WITH THE JUSTICE

MINISTER. WHEN ASKED ABOUT THE REDUCTION OF POLLING

STATIONS IN URBAN AREAS AND THE NUMBER OF VOTERS EACH

COULD PROCESS PER HOUR, THEY ADMITTED THAT THEY DID NOT

HAVE THESE STATISTICS IN HAND. HOWEVER, THE OBSERVERS

SHOULD REST ASSURED THAT THE GOZ HAS AMPLE EXPERIENCE

WITH SUCH MATTERS, AND CAN EXTEND VOTING HOURS, IF IT

COMES TO THAT.

COMMENT

-------

BEEN SUFFICIENTLY COWED TO PLAY OUT ITS DESIGNATED PART

IN THE FINAL SCENE OF THE ZANU-PF SCRIPT.
