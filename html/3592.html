1. (C) SUMMARY: A PROTESTANT PASTOR FROM DAK LAK

PROVINCE RECENTLY MET WITH CONGENOFFS TO DISCUSS THE

CURRENT SITUATION THERE, WHICH HE CHARACTERIZED AS THE

PROVINCIAL GOVERNMENT ATTEMPTING TO "ERADICATE"

PROTESTANTISM. HE SAID PROTESTANT FAMILIES WERE NOW

FORBIDDEN TO WORSHIP TOGETHER IN GROUPS AND HAD BEEN

TOLD - AT LEAST IN ONE VILLAGE -- THEY COULD NOT

TRAVEL OUTSIDE THE PROVINCE. HE BELIEVES IT IS

BECAUSE PROVINCIAL AUTHORITIES FEAR THESE VILLAGERS

WILL TRAVEL TO HO CHI MINH CITY TO TELL THE CONSULATE

GENERAL ABOUT THEIR SITUATION. AT THE SAME TIME, HE

ACKNOWLEDGED THAT THERE HAD BEEN NO PHYSICAL VIOLENCE

- NO BEATINGS, BROKEN FINGERS, ETC., AS WE HAVE HEARD

IN OTHER CENTRAL HIGHLANDS CASES. THE PASTOR WAS

AMBIVALENT ABOUT THE VALUE OF USG TRAVEL TO THE

HIGHLANDS, NOTING "IT INCREASES PROBLEMS FOR US." AT

THE CONCLUSION OF THE 90-MINUTE MEETING, THE PASTOR

ASKED CONGEN FOR "FUNDS" TO HELP DEFRAY THE COST OF

HIS TRIP TO HO CHI MINH CITY TO IMPART THIS

INFORMATION. END SUMMARY

2. (C) PASTOR Y SIOK NIE (ALSO CALLED BY HIS

CHRISTIAN NAME, "STEVENS") WAS INTRODUCED TO

CONGENOFFS BY A HO CHI MINH CITY CONTACT, DR. SIU YA

KOP (ALSO CALLED BY HIS CHRISTIAN NAME, "DR. JAKUB"),

WHO IS FAIRLY WELL KNOWN TO POST. JAKUB SAID THE

PASTOR WAS HIS BROTHER-IN-LAW.

CONFIDENTIAL

3. (C) ACCORDING TO PASTOR STEVENS, RECENTLY DAK

LAK PROVINCIAL AUTHORITIES AT ALL LEVELS (PROVINCIAL,

DISTRICT, COMMUNE, VILLAGE, HAMLET) HAVE CRACKED DOWN

ON PROTESTANTS WHO ARE PRACTICING OUTSIDE THE "LEGAL"

BOUNDARIES OF A RECOGNIZED RELIGION. CURRENTLY, ONLY

TWO (ONE ETHNIC MINORITY AND ONE VIETNAMESE KINH)

PROTESTANT CHURCHES OUT OF THE 400-PLUS CONGREGATIONS

WERE APPROVED. STEVENS IMPLIED, BUT DID NOT

SPECIFICALLY STATE, THAT THIS WAS BY CHOICE OF THE

CHURCH/CONGREGATION, NOT BECAUSE THE COMMITTEE FOR

RELIGIOUS AFFAIRS OR ANY GOVERNMENT ENTITY HAD

REJECTED AN APPLICATION FOR APPROVAL. PROTESTANT

FAMILIES HAVE BEEN WARNED BY AUTHORITIES NOT TO

WORSHIP TOGETHER AT HOUSE CHURCHES OR IN TRADITIONAL

"RONG" MEETING HOUSES. BUT IN FACT, STEVENS SAID THEY

CONTINUED TO DO SO WITHOUT INTERFERENCE.

4. (C) PASTOR STEVENS ALSO TURNED OVER VIETNAMESE-

LANGUAGE DOCUMENTS, WHICH WERE TESTIMONIES FROM OTHER

PROTESTANT BELIEVERS ABOUT THEIR TREATMENT BY THE

POLICE. WHILE ALL THE INDIVIDUALS ALLEGED HARASSMENT

- PHONE CALLS, VISITS TO THEIR HOMES OR WORKPLACES,

PRESSURE TO RENOUNCE THEIR FAITH OR ACKNOWLEDGE THAT

IT IS ILLOGICAL, CONFISCATION OF RELIGIOUS ITEMS -

THERE WERE NO COMPLAINTS OF PHYSICAL HARM. OTHER

DOCUMENTS WERE LETTERS OF PROTEST SENT TO VARIOUS

COMMUNIST PARTY AND GVN AGENCIES, NOTABLY THE CENTRAL

PROPAGANDA COMMITTEE, THE COMMITTEE ON RELIGIOUS

AFFAIRS, THE DAK LAK PROVINCIAL PEOPLE\'S COMMITTEE,

AND THE DAK LAK PROVINCIAL COMMITTEE ON RELIGIOUS

AFFAIRS. THE WRITERS HAD RECEIVED EITHER NO RESPONSE

OR A RESPONSE DIRECTING THEM TO MAKE A POLICE REPORT,

ACCORDING TO STEVENS.

5. (C) ONE UNSIGNED DOCUMENT, PERHAPS A PAGE TAKEN

FROM A SCHOOL NOTEBOOK, CONTAINED NOTES FROM A COURSE

CONDUCTED BY THE PROVINCIAL COMMUNIST PARTY\'S

PROPAGANDA COMMITTEE. THE WRITER SAYS THAT OPPONENTS

OF THE VIETNAMESE REGIME ARE "PUPPETS OF THE AMERICAN

EMPIRE." FURTHER, THE OPPONENTS\' GOAL IS TO DIVIDE

VIETNAM INTO FOUR PARTS - H\'MONG, DEGA, KHMER KROM,

AND CHAMPA. THE GVN IS "AWARE THAT THE DEGA

PROTESTANTS CHANGED THEIR NAME TO TAY NGUYEN

PROTESTANTS AFTER THE FEBRUARY 2001 DISTURBANCES."

THE GVN\'S OBJECTIVE IS TO ERADICATE PROTESTANTISM BY

2005, ACCORDING TO THESE NOTES.

6. (C) PASTOR STEVENS CLAIMED THAT EA KAR VILLAGERS

HAD BEEN FORBIDDEN TO TRAVEL OUTSIDE DAK LAK PROVINCE.

HE HIMSELF HAD OBTAINED PERMISSION TO TRAVEL TO HCMC

UNDER THE GUISE OF VISITING HIS SON HERE. HE SAID HE

SUSPECTED THE REAL REASON AUTHORITIES DID NOT WANT

VILLAGERS TO TRAVEL TO HCMC WAS BECAUSE THEY FEARED

THEY WOULD GO TO THE CONSULATE AND RELATE THEIR

SITUATION.

7. (C) PASTOR STEVENS ALSO ASKED CONGENOFFS WHY THEY

WERE NOT ABLE TO COME TO AN EASTER SUNRISE SERVICE AT

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HANOI 02090 02 OF 02 271017Z

HIS CHURCH JUST OUTSIDE BUON ME THUOT, THE DAK LAK

PROVINCIAL CAPITAL. HE SAID THAT CONGREGATION MEMBERS

HAD BEEN INFORMED OF THE VISIT AND HAD PREPARED A

WELCOME. ON EASTER MORNING, THEY WERE TOLD THAT

CONGENOFFS WERE "DELAYED", BUT THEY WAITED UNTIL

MIDNIGHT JUST IN CASE. [CONGEN NOTE: PER REFTEL,

AMBASSADOR BURGHARDT, CG, AND CONGENOFFS TRAVELED TO

THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS OVER THE EASTER WEEKEND OF MARCH

30-31. THE EXTERNAL RELATIONS OFFICE IN HCMC HAD

INFORMED CONGEN THAT KON TUM PROVINCE WOULD RECEIVE

THEM FOR AN EASTER CHURCH SERVICE, BUT TO SPLIT UP THE

GROUP BETWEEN KON TUM AND BUON ME THUOT (FIVE HOURS

APART BY CAR) WOULD BE A LOGISTICAL PROBLEM. WHETHER

THIS DECISION WAS EVER CONVEYED TO DAK LAK PROVINCE

AUTHORITIES, OR WHETHER THERE IS A MORE NEFARIOUS

REASON BEHIND THIS BREAKDOWN IN COMMUNICATION, WE DO

NOT KNOW. END NOTE.] CONGENOFFS APOLOGIZED FOR THE

MISUNDERSTANDING AND DISAPPOINTMENT, WHILE EXPRESSING

SURPRISE, SINCE WE HAD BEEN INFORMED OF THE KON TUM

DECISION TWO WEEKS BEFORE THE TRIP TOOK PLACE.

8. (C) COMMENT: CONGEN NOTES THAT THESE ABOVE

STORIES OF HARASSMENT ARE SIMILAR, THOUGH LESS

DRAMATIC, THAN PREVIOUS ONES THAT WE HAVE REPORTED ON.

THERE IS NO QUESTION THAT RELIGIOUS HARASSMENT

CONTINUES IN SOME PARTS OF THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS.

HOWEVER, THE PASTORS AND BELIEVERS THERE SEEM TO

CONSIDER ALL HARASSMENT - AS ABHORRENT AS IT MAY BE -

TO BE PERSECUTION. WHEN ASKED ABOUT THE VALUE OF USG

VISITS TO THE TROUBLED PROVINCES, PASTOR STEVENS WAS

FORTHRIGHT: "YOUR VISITS CREATE MORE PROBLEMS FOR US,

PAGE 03 HANOI 02090 02 OF 02 271017Z

BUT WE REALIZE IT IS PART OF YOUR DUTY TO GO

INVESTIGATE SITUATIONS." WHEN PRESSED AS TO WHETHER

HE MEANT WE SHOULD MAKE NO FURTHER VISITS, STEVENS

BECAME AMBIVALENT: "WELL, THEY WILL NEVER ALLOW YOU

TO SEE THE TRUTH, BUT WE UNDERSTAND THAT YOU HAVE TO

MAKE YOUR OWN DECISION ABOUT THE VALUE OF A VISIT."

9. (C) OF FURTHER CONCERN WAS THE REQUEST FOR MONEY.

THIS WAS THE FIRST TIME THAT ANY OF OUR FIRST OR

SECONDHAND SOURCES HAD REQUESTED FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE

FOR THEIR "TRAVEL COSTS." CONGEN UNDERSTANDS THAT LIFE

IS HARD FOR HOUSE CHURCH PASTORS, ESPECIALLY IF THEIR

CONGREGATION\'S NUMBERS ARE DIMINISHING DUE TO

HARASSMENT. HOWEVER, THE REQUEST FOR USD$33 FOR

TRAVEL EXPENSES WAS HIGH - FOR A PROVINCE WHERE THE

AVERAGE PER CAPITA INCOME IS UNDER USD$250.

CONGENOFFS EXPLAINED THEY COULD NOT PAY FOR TRAVEL

COSTS, AND IN ANY CASE WOULD NOT WANT ANY SUGGESTION

OF AN APPEARANCE OF THE USG PAYING FOR INFORMATION.

CONGENOFFS URGED PASTOR STEVENS TO FOLLOW HIS OWN

CONSCIENCE ON PROVIDING INFORMATION TO US. CONGENOFFS

ALSO SAID THEY COULD NOT SET A PRECEDENT BY DONATING

PERSONAL MONIES TO THE PASTOR. CONGEN CONTINUES TO

BELIEVE THAT RELIABLE SECONDHAND INFORMATION AND

DOCUMENTS CARRIED DOWN FROM THE CENTRAL HIGHLANDS ARE

VALUABLE REPORTING MATERIALS, AND WOULD BE DISMAYED IF

THESE SOURCES DRIED UP. DR. JAKUB EXPRESSED SHOCK AT

HIS BROTHER-IN-LAW\'S REQUEST, AND EMPHATICALLY TOLD

CONGENOFFS AFTER THE MEETING AND IN A FOLLOW UP PHONE

CALL THAT HE HAD HAD NO IDEA PASTOR STEVENS WOULD ASK

FOR MONEY.

10. (C) IN THE PAST FEW MONTHS, CONGENOFFS HAVE

RECEIVED A NUMBER OF E-MAIL AND FAX MESSAGES FROM

PROTESTANT PASTORS ABOUT EVERY POLICE DETENTION (SOME

LASTING AN HOUR OR LESS), "INVITATION TO APPEAR AT THE

POLICE STATION," HOUSE SEARCH (THE POLICE SOMETIMES

CARRY AWAY TELEVISION SETS, RADIOS, ETC.), OR PHONE

CALL WARNING PEOPLE "NOT TO FOLLOW DANGEROUS/ILLEGAL

RELIGIONS." MESSAGES SOMETIMES COME ANONYMOUSLY VIA

THE MESSAGING DEVICE ON CONGENOFFS MOBILE PHONES. CG

HCMC IS SYMPATHETIC TO THESE CONCERNS, BUT LACKS THE

RESOURCES TO FOLLOW UP ON EACH REPORT OR TO TRACK DOWN

THE ANONYMOUS ONES. THESE INCIDENTS ARE ALL RECORDED,

AND WILL BE REFLECTED IN OUR ANNUAL FREEDOM OF

RELIGION REPORT.
