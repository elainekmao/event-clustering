SUBJECT: PRIME MINISTER DELIVERS STRONG MESSAGE TO SYRIAN

PRESIDENT ASSAD

(U) CLASSIFIED BY DCM ROBERT S. DEUTSCH FOR REASONS 1.5(B) &

(D).

1. (C) MFA Director General for Middle East Tahsin Burcuoglu

briefed DCM July 11 on the July 10 telephone call between PM

Erdogan and Syrian President Assad. Burcuoglu said that

during the June visit of U/S Ziyal to Washington, Turkey had

been asked by the U.S to pass a strong message to the

Syrians. In Erdogan\'s call he told the Syrian President that

they needed to adjust their attitude on Iraq to be supportive

of the coalition; and on the Israeli/Palestinian issue to be

supportive of the roadmap and the peace initiative.

Furthermore he told Assad Syria needed to take steps against

terrorism to demonstrate to the U.S. that they were in the

camp of peace and stability.

2. (C) Assad\'s response was non-specific but positive,

Burcuoglu said. Furthermore Assad promised to send Erdogan a

more complete response probably in connection with the visit

July 28-29 to Ankara of Syrian Prime Minister Muhammad Miro.

Burcuoglu noted that the latter visit was intended to be more

economic than political but he promised to inform us as soon

the promised response was received.
