SUBJECT: TURKISH CABINENT APPROVES USE OF BASES AND PORTS

FOR OPERATIONS CONSISTENT WITH UNSCR 1483; EUCOM REQUEST TO

STATION TANKERS AT INCIRLIK ALSO APPROVED

(U) Classified by Deputy Chief of Mission Robert Deutsch.

Reasons: 1.5 (B and D).

1. (C) MFA DDG for the Americas Suna Ilicak told the DCM June

25 that the Turkish cabinet earlier this week had approved

the use of Turkish bases and ports for humanitarian and

reconstruction operations covered under UNSCR 1483. Ilicak

said the Cabinet\'s decision came in the form of a decree, is

now in effect, and does not require Parliamentary approval or

an MOU. The decree applied to all allies and friendly

nations. In terms of procedures, Ilicak said if the US

wanted to use a specific base or port, the Embassy should

follow "normal" procedures and submit a diplomatic note to

the MFA, who would then coordinate a response with the

Turkish General Staff (TGS). Pol-Mil officer sought

clarification, noting that the US previously had conveyed

requests for the use of Turkish bases/ports through military

channels (i.e. a letter from the Office of Defense

Cooperation to TGS), with the Embassy back-briefing the MFA.

He reminded Ilicak that EUCOM\'s recent request to station 10

tankers and one ISR aircraft at Incirlik to support OEF and

OIF missions was conveyed through military channels. Ilicak

said the MFA would confer with the TGS and get back to us on

whether the US should convey future requests via civilian

(i.e. the MFA) or military (i.e. TGS) channels.

2. (C) The DCM asked if the Cabinet\'s decision covered

EUCOM\'s request. Ilicak said that in her view the answer was

"yes." However, TGS would have to make the final decision.

Citing another hypothetical example, the DCM asked if the

Cabinet\'s decree would apply to a request for US aircraft

transporting goods and supplies to OCPA to stop at Incirlik

and then proceed to Iraq. Ilicak said she believed the

decree covered such an example.

3. (C) Later the same day, a TGS/J3 officer telephoned ODC to

inform that, consistent with the Cabinet\'s decree, TGS had

verbally approved EUCOM\'s request. The TGS officer said the

formal approval in writing would be forthcoming. ODC intends

to follow up with TGS in person June 26 to obtain more

details.
