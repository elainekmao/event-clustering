SUBJECT: MEDIA REPORT RACISM CONFERENCE; HARARE

1. THE INDEPENDENT DAILY "THE DAILY NEWS" DEDICATED ITS

AUGUST 23 EDITORIAL TO THE UNITED NATIONS WORLD CONFERENCE

ON RACISM TO BE HELD IN DURBAN, SOUTH AFRICA, ON AUGUST 31.

UNDER HEADLINE "POLITICAL POSTURING THREATENS RACISM

TALKS," THE PAPER COMMENTS:

2. "SO WHAT IF THE SUBJECT OF ZIONISM BEING EQUATED WITH

RACISM IS TABLED AT THE U. N. CONFERENCE ON RACISM IN

DURBAN ON 31 AUGUST? WOULD THAT BE EQUATED, BY THE

UNITED STATES GOVERNMENT, AT LEAST, WITH GRANTING ON A

SILVER PLATTER A PSYCHOLOGICAL VICTORY FOR YASSER

ARAFAT\'S FATAH MOVEMENT AND THE HIZBOLLAH GUERRILLA

GROUP OVER ARIEL SHARON AND SHIMON PERES? THERE WILL

CLEARLY BE LIVELY AND, ADMITTEDLY, EMOTIONAL DEBATE

OVER RACISM. . . WHAT IS VITAL IS THAT THE SUBJECT BE

VENTILATED CALMLY AND THOROUGHLY. . . THE U. S.

GOVERNMENT MUST, THEREFORE, CONCENTRATE ON THE BIG

PICTURE. RACISM IN THE U. S. ITSELF DID NOT DISAPPEAR

WITH THE END OF SLAVERY. FROM FREDERICK DOUGLASS TO

MARTIN LUTHER KING JNR., THE DESCENDANTS OF AFRICAN

SLAVES HAVE FOUGHT TO GAIN EQUAL STATUS WITH THEIR

FORMER MASTERS. THE LIKES OF THURGOOD MARSHALL PROVED

UNCLASSIFIED

THAT BEING THE DESCENDANT OF AFRICAN SLAVES WAS NO

BARRIER TO REACHING THE PINNACLE OF SUCCESS IN ANY

FIELD OF HUMAN ENDEAVOR. . . THE U. N. CONFERENCE ON

RACISM MUST SUCCEED AND THE THREAT OF A BOYCOTT BY THE

U. S. OVER ZIONISM BEING EQUATED WITH RACISM MUST BE

ELIMINATED BY OFFICIALS IN GEORGE W. BUSH\'S

ADMINISTRATION WHO KNOW THAT IF THE THREAT IS CARRIED

OUT, THEN THEIR COUNTRY COULD LOSE THE RESPECT OF MANY

FRIENDS."
