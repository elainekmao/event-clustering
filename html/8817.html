SUBJECT: NEPAL: GON RELEASES TIBETAN REFUGEE FROM

DETENTION

REF: (A) KATHMANDU 1074 (B) KATHAMNDU 1044

1. (SBU) ON JUNE 17, NEPAL\'S DEPARTMENT OF IMMIGRATION

RELEASED A MALE TIBETAN REFUGEE INTO UNHCR\'S CUSTODY

TWENTY-FOUR HOURS AFTER THE OFFICE OF TIBET PAID HIS FINE

OF 7,500 RUPEES (APPROXIMATELY 98 USD). THE DEPARTMENT OF

IMMIGRATION HAD DETAINED THE MAN IN LATE APRIL, SHORTLY

AFTER THE DETENTION OF THE 18 REFUGEES DEPORTED ON MAY 31

(REF A). THE OFFICE OF TIBET REPORTED THAT THE TIBETAN

MAN HAD SOMEHOW BEEN SEPARATED FROM THE LARGER GROUP IN

TRANSIT, AND SUBSEQUENTLY HAD NOT BEEN INCLUDED WITH THAT

GROUP DURING THE DEPORTATION. [NOTE. NINE TIBETAN REFUGEES

REMAIN IN DETENTION (DATING FROM JUNE 2000 TO MAY 2002)

WITH HEAVY FINES RANGING FROM USD 1,600 TO USD 10,000.

END NOTE.]

2. (SBU) UNHCR REPORTS THAT ON JUNE 19 IT WILL SUBMIT A

REQUEST TO THE DEPARTMENT OF IMMIGRATION FOR EXIT PERMITS

FOR A NEW GROUP OF 40 TIBETAN REFUGEES. (ONE GROUP OF 40

TIBETANS WAS GRANTED EXIT PERMITS JUNE 13 AND DEPARTED THE

SAME DAY ON A BUS TO NEW DELHI.) UNHCR GENEVA REPORTEDLY

IS DELIBERATING ON WHETHER TO INCLUDE THE NAMES OF THE

THREE CHILDREN WHO WERE MEMBERS OF THE ORIGINAL GROUP OF

21 REFUGEES (REF B).

3. (SBU) COMMENT: THE GON IS ANXIOUS TO REESTABLISH ITS

CREDENTIALS WITH THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY SINCE THE MAY

31 DEPORTATION OF 18 TIBETAN REFUGEES. FOLLOWING GON

PROMISES THAT ITS POLICY TOWARD TIBETAN REFUGEES HAD NOT

CHANGED (REFTELS), THE JUNE 17 RELEASE OF THE TIBETAN

REFUGEE IS A PROMISING FIRST STEP TOWARDS DEMONSTRATING

THIS COMMITMENT. POST WILL CONTINUE TO TRACK THE STATUS

OF THE THREE REFUGEE CHILDREN AS FURTHER PROOF THAT GON

POLICY DOES NOT SUPPORT FURTHER DEPORTATIONS. END

COMMENT.
