SUBJECT: MEDIA REACTION ON MIDDLE EAST

Summary

-- Lead stories in all papers today, August 5,

highlight yesterday\'s "wave" of Palestinian attacks

against Israelis, including a suicide bus bombing in

the north of Israel. Headlines mark the attacks as a

retaliation by the Palestinian resistance to Israel\'s

brutality against the Palestinian people. Stories

also carry President Bush\'s "condemnation" of the

attacks and the Palestinian and Israeli reactions.

Editorial commentaries hold Israel responsible for the

continuation of the violence

Editorial Commentary on Middle East Issues

-- "The occupation and the suffering of the

Palestinians"

Chief Editor Taher Udwan writes on the back page of

independent, mass-appeal Arabic daily Al-Arab Al-Yawm

(08/05): "Israel\'s crimes go almost unnoticed by the

international media and hardly receive any coverage if

compared with the coverage given to a suicide bombing

inside Israeli territories. This international

silence towards the Palestinians\' incredible suffering

under occupation is being used by Sharon and his

government to continue his cycle of killings and

destruction. Meanwhile, the main and only reason for

this tragedy continues to be overlooked, namely the

occupation. The return of the suicide bombings to

what they used to be puts Sharon and even Bush in

tight spot, simply because the Palestinian Authority

no longer exists on the ground, and the only real

presence of security in the West Bank is for the

Israeli army. This suggests their failure to bring

the Palestinian people down to their knees and to

strip them of their spirit and their resistance."

-- "Israeli terrorism springs violence in Palestine"

Daily columnist Rakan Majali writes on the back page

of center-left, influential Arabic Al-Dustour (08/05):

"Even Israeli newspapers are saying that Israeli

confrontations, escalation and the use of its military

machine will only lead to more suicide bombings.

Whatever Israel decides to call these bombings, the

outcome is the same, namely that the state of

frustration and desperation, that results from the

Hebrew State\'s exercise of humiliation and oppression

against Palestinian people and denial of their rights,

will only lead to more bloodshed.
