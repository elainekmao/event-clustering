-------

SUMMARY

-------

1. IN A LATE EVENING MEETING NOV 27, SENATOR MOYNIHAN

SHARED WITH PRESIDENT TUDJMAN HIS IMPRESSIONS AFTER

HAVING PERSONALLY VIEWED THE DEVASTATION IN SARAJEVO,

MOSTAR, AND THE DUBROVNIK AREA. THE SENATOR SAID HE

HAD BEEN TOLD BY UNHCR IN SARAJEVO THAT THE CITY IS

DOWN TO ONLY ONE DAY\'S FOOD SUPPLY.

2. TUDJMAN SAID SERBIAN EXTREMISTS HAVE CLEARLY

OPTED FOR WAR; SANCTIONS WOULD NOT STOP THEM. THE

OVER EX-YUGO AND ON RUSSIAN SUPPORT; SEVERAL HUNDRED

RUSSIAN "VOLUNTEERS" ARE NOW FIGHTING ON THE SERBIAN

SIDE. HE CALLED FOR SELECTIVE AIR STRIKES ON SERBIAN

MILITARY BASES AND FACTORIES AND NOTED SERB CLAIMS TO

TUDJMAN SAID THAT LIFTING THE ARMS EMBARGO WOULD ONLY

SUPERIORITY. ON CROATIA\'S WAR-RELATED ECONOMIC

DISPLACEMENT AND REFUGEE BURDEN, TUDJMAN COMMENTED,

"IT\'S A MIRACLE WE HAVE SURVIVED ALL OF THIS."

END SUMMARY.

"YOU ASKED FOR IT|"

-------------------

3. SENATOR MOYNIHAN TOLD THE PRESIDENT HOW DEEPLY

TOUCHED HE WAS BY THE AMOUNT OF DESTRUCTION HE HAD

WITNESSED IN CROATIA AND BOSNIA-HERCEGOVINA (B-H).

HE NOTED THAT DUBROVNIK APPEARED BATTERED, ALBEIT

REPAIRABLE. ON THE OTHER HAND, THE TOWN OF SLANO,

JUST NORTH OF DUBROVNIK, HAD BEEN UTTERLY DESTROYED.

THE ATTACKERS WHO HAD OCCUPIED AND WRECKED THE TOWN

LEFT GRAFFITI ON THE WALLS, IN ENGLISH AND SERBO-

CROATIAN, STATING "YOU ASKED FOR IT|" LIKEWISE,

MOSTAR WAS A DEVASTATED CITY. SERBIAN TACTICS WERE

EVIDENTLY TO SHELL CITIES FROM AFAR, KILL OR STARVE

THE POPULACE, AND ONLY THEN MOVE IN FOR THE CAPTURE.

SARAJEVO IMPRESSIONS

--------------------

6. SENATOR MOYNIHAN TOLD THE PRESIDENT THAT HE HAD

OVERNIGHTED IN SARAJEVO NOV 24, TOURED THE CITY, MET

WITH PRESIDENT IZETBEGOVIC, AND BEEN BRIEFED BY UNHCR

ON THE PRECARIOUS FOOD SUPPLY SITUATION. HE HAD BEEN

TOLD BY UNHCR THAT SARAJEVO WAS DOWN TO ONLY ONE

DAY\'S FOOD SUPPLY; INCOMING FOOD ON CONVOYS AND

RELIEF FLIGHTS IS DISTRIBUTED AND CONSUMED. TUDJMAN

SAID IZETBEGOVIC HAD PROBABLY INFORMED THE SENATOR

THAT SOME 128,000 BOSNIANS HAD BEEN KILLED AND 30,000

GIRLS AND WOMEN RAPED BY SERBIAN FORCES IN B-H. THESE

FIGURES MIGHT, OF COURSE, BE EXAGGERATED, BUT EVEN IF

THE REAL FIGURES WERE ONLY HALF, THE NUMBERS WERE

STILL OUTRAGEOUS.

----------------------------

SERBIAN AGGRESSION. SERBIAN POLITICS HAS BECOME

IRRATIONAL. SERBIAN EXTREMISTS FEEL THAT THEY HAVE A

BETTER CHANCE TO CREATE A "GREATER SERBIA" BY WAR

THAN BY OTHER MEANS. THEY CITE THE EXAMPLE OF HOW

TUDJMAN: LIFTING ARMS EMBARGO WILL PROLONG B-H WAR

--------------------------------------------- ------

9. GENERAL MORILLON, THE UNPROFOR COMMANDER IN

SARAJEVO, HAD TOLD SENATOR MOYNIHAN THAT HE WAS

STRONGLY OPPOSED TO LIFTING OF THE ARMS EMBARGO.

ASKED FOR HIS VIEW, TUDJMAN SAID THAT LIFTING THE

ARMS EMBARGO WOULD MERELY ENSURE THAT THE DEATH AND

DEVASTATION SPREAD AND CONTINUED FOR AT LEAST ANOTHER

YEAR OR TWO. THE SERBS ALREADY HAVE SUCH AN

ADVANTAGE IN COMBAT AIRCRAFT, TANKS, AND ARTILLERY

THAT THEIR MILITARY SUPERIORITY WOULD NOT BE

CHALLENGED ANYTIME SOON, EVEN IF ARMS STARTED TO FLOW

TO THEIR OPPONENTS IMMEDIATELY.

10. TUDJMAN SAID THE SOLUTION TO THE B-H CRISIS IS

TO STOP SERBIAN AGGRESSION AND AIR STRIKES OUGHT TO

BE USED TO THAT END. HE SPECULATED THAT B-H MIGHT

HAVE TO BECOME A UN "PROTECTORATE" TO BE SAVED. HE

SAID IT HAD BEEN A MISTAKE FOR THE EC COUNTRIES AND

ESPECIALLY THE UNITED STATES TO MAKE SO REPETITIVELY

CLEAR AT THE OUTSET OF THE CRISIS THAT THEY WOULD NOT

INTERVENE MILITARILY. THE SERBS HAD SEEN IN THIS

STANCE A VIRTUAL GREEN LIGHT FOR MILITARY ACTION

WITHOUT FEAR OF RETALIATION.

11. JNA/SERBIAN MILITARY AGGRESSION AGAINST CROATIA

LAST YEAR HAD AS ONE OF ITS GOALS THE RE-ESTABLISHMENT

OF THE FORMER COMMUNIST SYSTEM THROUGHOUT THE FORMER

YUGOSLAVIA. A SECONDARY GOAL WAS SERBIAN CONQUEST OF

PARTS OF CROATIAN TERRITORY. SERBIAN MILITARY

STRATEGISTS HAD COUNTED ON SUPPORT FROM SOVIET

MILITARY OFFICERS, INCLUDING MANY OF THOSE WHO

PLOTTED AGAINST GORBACHEV.

12. AT THE START OF THE YUGO-CRISIS, CROATIA\'S GOAL

WAS CONFEDERATION. TITO HIMSELF HAD FORESEEN THE

PROBLEMS TO WHICH THE COUNTRY MIGHT OTHERWISE FALL

CONFIDENTIAL

CONFIDENTIAL

PREY IN DESIGNING THE 1974 CONSTITUTION.

--------------------------------------------- ---

REFUGEES & ECONOMY ARE MAJOR BURDENS FOR CROATIA

--------------------------------------------- ---

13. TUDJMAN COMMENTED THAT SOME 700-800,000

DISPLACED PERSONS FROM CROATIA AND REFUGEES FROM B-H

ARE PRESENTLY IN CROATIA. THEIR CARE AND FEEDING IS

A MAJOR EXPENSE (NOTE: 20 PERCENT OF THE NATIONAL

BUDGET NOW GOES FOR DIRECT CARE AND FEEDING OF THE

REFUGEES AND DISPLACED.) THIS BURDEN COMPOUNDED THE

IMPORTANT LOSS OF THE FORMER YUGOSLAV MARKET AND THE

WIDESPREAD ECONOMIC DEVASTATION CROATIA HAD SUFFERED

IN THE WAR. "IT\'S A MIRACLE THAT WE HAVE SURVIVED

ALL OF THIS," TUDJMAN SAID. HE SAID THAT SUPPORT

FROM THE CROATIAN DIASPORA HAD BEEN CRITICAL IN THE

COUNTRY\'S SURVIVAL.

14. TUDJMAN SAID CROATIA IS COUNTING ON U.S. SUPPORT

FOR EARLY ADMISSION TO SUCH LFI\'S AS THE IMF AND

WORLD BANK. THE SENATOR ADDED THAT HE WOULD PUT IN A

GOOD WORD FOR THE COUNTRY WITH SENIOR IBRD LEADERSHIP

AND WISHED TUDJMAN AND CROATIA EVERY SUCCESS IN

OVERCOMING ITS ECONOMIC PROBLEMS.

15. THIS CABLE WAS PREPARED AFTER THE CODEL\'S

DEPARTURE FROM ZAGREB.
