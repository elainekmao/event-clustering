1. (U) SUMMARY: THE CATHOLIC CHURCH IN VIETNAM HAS SEEN A

NUMBER OF IMPROVEMENTS IN THE LAST TEN YEARS, BUT IS SEEKING

MORE, ACCORDING TO CARDINAL TUNG, ARCHBISHOP OF HANOI. THE

GVN HAS MADE IT EASIER FOR THE CHURCH TO RECRUIT AND TRAIN

NEW PRIESTS AND TRANSFER PRIESTS FROM PLACE TO PLACE, BUT

THE CATHOLIC CHURCH STILL NEEDS MANY MORE PRIESTS. SOME OF

THE CHURCH\'S CURRENT PROBLEMS ARE WITH LOCAL AUTHORITIES,

NOT NATIONAL. ACCORDING TO THE CARDINAL, THE GVN RECOGNIZED

THAT RELIGION WAS A BASIC NEED OF THE PEOPLE AND THAT THERE

WERE FEWER SOCIAL PROBLEMS IN AREAS WITH MANY RELIGIOUS

BELIEVERS. END SUMMARY.

2. (SBU) THE AMBASSADOR, TOGETHER WITH DCM AND EMBOFFS,

MADE HIS FIRST CALL ON PAUL JOSEPH CARDINAL PHAM DINH TUNG,

ALSO ARCHBISHOP OF HANOI, AT THE ARCHBISHOP\'S HOUSE IN HANOI

ON MAY 15. (NOTE: THIS IS THE FIRST TIME CARDINAL TUNG HAS

MET EMBOFFS SINCE OCTOBER 1, 2001 -- REF A -- APPARENTLY DUE

TO HEALTH REASONS. END NOTE) WHILE ACKNOWLEDGING PROGRESS

IN RECENT YEARS, THE CARDINAL SAID THAT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH

WANTS MANY MORE IMPROVEMENTS IN ITS SITUATION. WHILE THE

CHURCH DESIRES MORE PRIESTS, CARDINAL TUNG HIGHLIGHTED THAT

THERE ARE NOW ALMOST 3000 PRIESTS IN VIETNAM, WHICH HE

DESCRIBED AS "A SIGNIFICANT INCREASE." THE CARDINAL ALSO

STATED THAT IT IS NOW EASIER TO TRANSFER PRIESTS, EVEN

BETWEEN REGIONS, AND THAT IT HAS BECOME EASIER TO RECRUIT

SEMINARIANS. HOWEVER, THE CHURCH IS LOOKING FOR ADDITIONAL

IMPROVEMENTS ON ALL OF THESE ISSUES. MANY MORE PRIESTS ARE

NEEDED, THE CARDINAL EMPHASIZED, AND THE CATHOLIC CHURCH

WOULD LIKE TO BRING IN NEW SEMINARIANS EVERY YEAR, RATHER

THAN EVERY OTHER YEAR AS THE GVN CURRENTLY ALLOWS. (NOTE:

OVER THE PAST TEN YEARS THE PACE OF SEMINARY ADMISSIONS HAS

QUICKENED FROM ONCE IN FIVE YEARS, TO THREE YEARS, TO THE

CURRENT TWO YEARS. END NOTE.) MORE PRIESTS ARE NEEDED NOT

JUST TO SAY MASS, BUT ALSO TO CARRY OUT SOCIAL

RESPONSIBILITIES AND PUBLISH JOURNALS, AMONG OTHER DUTIES,

THE CARDINAL ADDED.

3. (SBU) THE CARDINAL STATED THAT THE NUMBER OF CATHOLIC

BELIEVERS HAS INCREASED SUBSTANTIALLY OVER THE LAST SEVERAL

YEARS, BUT THAT THE NUMBER WAS INCREASING MORE SLOWLY AMONG

THE YOUTH. (NOTE: THE NUMBER OF VIETNAMESE CATHOLICS IS

GENERALLY THOUGHT TO BE IN EXCESS OF SIX MILLION. END

NOTE.) HE ATTRIBUTED THE INCREASE TO: MARRIAGE INTO THE

CHURCH; INDIVIDUALS SEARCHING FOR "DIVINE TRUTH"; AND,

FRIENDSHIP. THE ATTITUDE OF "INTELLECTUALS" TOWARDS THE

CHURCH HAS ALSO IMPROVED, CARDINAL TUNG CLAIMED. THE CHURCH

HAS BEEN ABLE TO SUPPORT THE INCREASING NUMBER OF BELIEVERS

THROUGH CATECHISM, HE SAID, AND ALSO BECAUSE THE GVN HAS

ALLOWED THE CHURCH TO PRINT AND DISSEMINATE INFORMATION MORE

WIDELY.

4. (SBU) THE AMBASSADOR NOTED THAT THERE ARE CONSTRAINTS

ON RELIGION IN VIETNAM, BUT THAT INDIVIDUALS ARE GENERALLY

ABLE TO WORSHIP FREELY, WITH PAGODAS, CHURCHES, AND

TRADITIONAL TEMPLES FILLED WITH PEOPLE. THE CARDINAL

REMARKED THAT THE VIETNAMESE ARE A SPIRITUAL PEOPLE, A

CHARACTERISTIC THAT GIVES CATHOLICS CONFIDENCE TO SPREAD

THEIR BELIEF. HE STATED THAT THE TRADITION OF PAYING

RESPECT TO ONE\'S ANCESTORS WAS IMPORTANT IN CREATING FAITH-

BASED THINKING. IN RESPONSE TO THE AMBASSADOR\'S QUESTION,

THE CARDINAL ACKNOWLEDGED THAT THE CATHOLIC CHURCH ACCEPTED

TRADITIONAL VIETNAMESE FOLK PRACTICES SUCH AS PAYING RESPECT

TO ANCESTORS. THE CHURCH ALSO HAS WORKING LEVEL CONTACTS

WITH OTHER LEGALLY RECOGNIZED RELIGIONS, FOR INSTANCE

COOPERATING WITH PROTESTANTS TO PRINT BIBLES.

5. (SBU) ACCORDING TO THE CARDINAL, THE GVN, OVER THE LAST

TEN YEARS, HAS DEVELOPED A BETTER UNDERSTANDING OF THE

CHURCH\'S POSITIVE INFLUENCE ON SOCIAL ISSUES. HE CITED THE

CHURCH\'S ROLE IN POVERTY AND HUNGER REDUCTION AS WELL AS

FIGHTING SOCIAL VICES. HE SAID THAT GVN OFFICIALS HAD TOLD

HIM THAT THEY HAD NOTICED LESS CRIME IN AREAS WITH MANY

RELIGIOUS BELIEVERS.

6. (SBU) SOME OF THE REMAINING PROBLEMS THE CARDINAL

BROUGHT UP ARE WITH SOME LOCAL, NOT NATIONAL, AUTHORITIES.

IT IS EASIER FOR THE CHURCH TO CARRY OUT SOCIAL SERVICES IN

SOUTHERN VIETNAM, HE NOTED. WHILE MANY WOMEN HAD BECOME

NUNS IN RECENT YEARS, THEY FACED "PROBLEMS" IN OTHER PLACES,

HE ADMITTED. (NOTE: BY LAW, PROVINCIAL PEOPLE\'S COMMITTEE

DESCRIBES THE PROBLEMS OF WOULD-BE NUNS IN DANANG. END

7. (SBU) REGARDING VATICAN RELATIONS, THE CARDINAL (WHO

SPENT SEVERAL WEEKS IN ROME WITH SEVERAL OTHER VIETNAMESE

BISHOPS EARLIER THIS YEAR) SAID THAT THE POPE TOLD THEM THAT

MANY OF THE CATHOLIC CHURCH\'S ISSUES IN VIETNAM WOULD BE

SOLVED THROUGH "DIALOGUE." THE CARDINAL SAID THAT HE HAD

APPLIED TO THE GVN FOR A PAPAL VISIT THREE OR FOUR YEARS

AGO, BUT THE GVN HAD REPLIED THAT IT NEEDED MORE TIME "TO

PREPARE PROPERLY FOR THE VISIT OF SUCH AN IMPORTANT

INTERNATIONAL FIGURE." THERE ARE STILL NO FORMAL RELATIONS

BETWEEN THE VATICAN AND THE GVN. (HIS AIDES SUBSEQUENTLY

POINTED TO THE SITE OF THE ONE-TIME RESIDENCE OF THE PAPAL

NUNCIO, ADJACENT TO THE ARCHBISHOP\'S HOUSE; THE RESIDENCE

HAS BEEN TORN DOWN, THE SITE EXPROPRIATED, AND PLANS

DEVELOPED TO BUILD AN ENTERTAINMENT COMPLEX, THEY CLAIMED.)
