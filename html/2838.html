1. SUMMARY: A COALITION OF COUNTRIES (DEVELOPED AND

DEVELOPING)IS PROMOTING WORLD FOOD SUMMIT FIVE YEARS

LATER ENDORSEMENT OF A VOLUNTARY CODE OF CONDUCT ON THE

RIGHT TO FOOD AS A "DELIVERABLE" OF THE UPCOMING SUMMIT-

LEVEL MEETING. THE UNITED STATES OBJECTED TO A CODE OF

CONDUCT AT THE 1996 WORLD FOOD SUMMIT AND CONTINUES TO

OPPOSE ONE ON THE GROUNDS THAT A CODE WILL DO LITTLE TO

PROMOTE FREEDOM FROM HUNGER, AND IS LIKELY TO STIPULATE

POLITICAL AND ECONOMIC CONDUCT (NO SANCTIONS/EMBARGOES,

NO GMO\'S, ANTI-FREE TRADE) WHICH IS AGAINST U.S. PRIVATE

AND PUBLIC INTERESTS. HOWEVER, IF THE CODE\'S ENDORSEMENT

CANNOT BE PREVENTED, A LATIN AMERICAN PROPOSAL -- TO HAVE

AN INTERGOVERNMENTAL WORKING GROUP UNDER FAO AUSPICES

DRAFT THE CODE -- MAY BE PREFERABLE TO A NORWEGIAN

PROPOSAL TO HAVE THE HIGH COMMISSIONER FOR HUMAN RIGHTS

DRAFT THE CODE OF CONDUCT. WASHINGTON VIEWS REQUESTED.

END SUMMARY.

BACKGROUND

----------

2. A CODE OF CONDUCT ON THE RIGHT TO FOOD IS "UNFINISHED

BUSINESS" FROM THE 1996 WORLD FOOD SUMMIT. THE U.S.

SUCCEEDED IN KEEPING OUT A SPECIFIC REFERENCE TO A CODE

AT THE SUMMIT, BUT A LIVELY DEBATE ON THE CODE CONTINUED.

A CIVIL-SOCIETY DRAFTED CODE OF CONDUCT HAS BEEN

CIRCULATED, AND IS FREQUENTLY REFERRED TO IN RIGHT TO

FOOD LITERATURE (AND MIGHT BE DRAWN ON FOR ANY FUTURE

NEGOTIATED TEXT). COMMENT 12 ON RIGHT TO FOOD BY THE UN

COMMITTEE ON ECONOMIC, SOCIAL AND CULTURAL RIGHTS (2000)

STATED THAT "THE PRIVATE SECTOR - NATIONAL AND

TRANSNATIONAL - SHOULD PURSUE ITS ACTIVITIES WITHIN THE

FRAMEWORK OF A CODE OF CONDUCT TO RESPECT THE RIGHT TO

ADEQUATE FOOD, AGREED UPON JOINTLY WITH THE GOVERNMENT

AND CIVIL SOCIETY."

WHY IS THE U.S. AGAINST THE RIGHT TO FOOD AND A CODE OF

CONDUCT?

--------------------------------------------- ------

3. DRAFTING A CODE OF CONDUCT - IN ADDITION TO BEING A

VERY POOR USE OF FAO\'S SCARCE RESOURCES -- WILL NOT HELP

COUNTRIES IMPROVE FOOD SECURITY ON THE GROUND: IF THE

EXISTING CIVIL SOCIETY CODE IS ANY GUIDE, IT WILL BECOME

A POLITICAL DIATRIBE AGAINST TRADE LIBERALIZATION,

AGAINST MULTINATIONAL CORPORATIONS, AGAINST

SANCTIONS/EMBARGOES AND AGAINST GMO\'S. OF EVEN MORE

CONCERN, IT SEEKS TO SHIFT RESPONSIBILITY FOR HUNGER FROM

NATIONAL GOVERNMENTS, AND ONTO THE PRIVATE SECTOR AND

"THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY."

4. FURTHERMORE, IT IMPLIES ACCEPTANCE OF A LEGALLY

BINDING RIGHT TO FOOD WHICH IS INCONSISTENT WITH THE U.S.

POSITION AS STATED AT THE WORLD FOOD SUMMIT. THE U.S.

POSITION ON RIGHT TO FOOD IS THAT IT IS A GOAL OR

ASPIRATION TO BE REALIZED PROGRESSIVELY WHICH DOES NOT

GIVE RISE TO ANY INTERNATIONAL OBLIGATIONS TO REDRESS

VIOLATIONS OR COMPENSATE VICTIMS, NOR DOES IT DIMINISH

THE RESPONSIBILITIES OF NATIONAL GOVERNMENTS TOWARD THEIR

CITIZENS.

CURRENT STATE OF PLAY

----------------------

5. ALTHOUGH THE FAO HAS BEEN ABLE TO GENERATE LITTLE

ENTHUSIASM FOR ITS PROPOSALS TO SPEED UP WORK ON THE

STILL-UNFINISHED DECLARATION FOR THE JUNE 10-13 WORLD

FOOD SUMMIT FIVE YEARS LATER (WFS5YL), INTENSE ACTIVITY

IS TAKING PLACE IN AND OUTSIDE OF ITALY ON THE "RIGHT TO

FOOD" SEGMENT OF THE DRAFT DECLARATION. THE GOVERNMENTS

OF ITALY AND NORWAY HAVE SPONSORED (AND GERMANY WILL

SPONSOR MAY 22-23) SEMINARS ON THE RIGHT TO FOOD WITH A

VIEW TO GATHERING SUPPORT FOR A CODE OF CONDUCT ON THE

RIGHT TO FOOD AS A "DELIVERABLE" FOR THE UPCOMING WFS5YL.

(THE UNITED STATES WAS INVITED TO THE ITALIAN AND GERMAN

SEMINARS, BUT WAS NOT INVITED TO THE NORWEGIAN EVENT.)

6. POLITICAL "BUY-IN" HAS BEEN AT THE HIGHEST LEVEL IN

ITALY, WHERE THE ITALIAN MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE STATED

HIS SUPPORT FOR A CODE AT A SEMINAR ORGANIZED BY THE

ITALIAN MINISTRY OF AGRICULTURE AND THE JACQUES MARITAIN

INSTITUTE (REFTEL). (COMMENT: MISOFF LEARNED FROM THE

NORWEGIAN PROPOSAL

------------------

7. A NORWEGIAN NON-PAPER CIRCULATED AMONG EUROPEAN

COUNTRY MISSIONS IN ROME PROPOSES RE-OPENING THE ROME

DECLARATION AND PROGRAM OF ACTION TO INVITE THE HIGH

COMMISSIONER ON HUMAN RIGHTS TO DEVELOP A VOLUNTARY CODE

OF CONDUCT ON THE RIGHT TO ADEQUATE FOOD, "WITH A VIEW TO

POLICY AND PROGRAM DEVELOPMENT, MEANS AND METHODS OF

IMPLEMENTATION, NATIONAL LEGISLATION AND MONITORING, AS

WELL AS MAKING NATIONAL REPORTING BY STATES PARTIES TO

THE INTERNATIONAL TREATY BODY (THE COVENANT ON ECONOMIC,

SOCIAL AND CULTURAL RIGHTS) MAXIMALLY RELEVANT."

LATIN AMERICAN PROPOSAL

-----------------------

8. A LATIN AMERICAN GROUP (GRULAC) PROPOSAL TAKES

ANOTHER TACK (VENEZUELA IS THE LEADING COUNTRY AMONG THE

LATINS ON THIS ISSUE): FODAG RECEIVED THE FOLLOWING FROM

GRULAC 4/26/02:

BEING TEXT:

WE UNDERLINE OUR CONVICTION THAT A VOLUNTARY CODE OF

CONDUCT ON THE RIGHT TO FOOD WILL HELP STRENGTHEN THE

POLICIES DIRECTED AT ACHIEVING FOOD SECURITY FOR ALL, AND

PROPOSED ACTION

---------------

9. ASSUMING THERE IS OVERWHELMING PRESSURE FOR THE WFS5YL

TO APPROVE A CODE OF CONDUCT, FODAG SEES THE FOLLOWING

ADVANTAGES OF THE GRULAC PROPOSAL:

-- THE DRAFTING IS IN THE HANDS OF AN INTERGOVERNMENTAL

BODY(RATHER THAN THE HIGH COMMISSIONER FOR HUMAN RIGHTS)

WHERE THE U.S. CAN PARTICIPATE ACTIVELY;

-- FAO HAS PREVIOUS EXPERIENCE WITH CODES OF CONDUCT

(PESTICIDES, FISHERIES). NEGOTIATION OF THESE CODES OF

CONDUCT HAS INCLUDED PARTICIPATION BY INDUSTRY AND OTHER

STAKEHOLDERS.

10. THE U.S. WOULD NEED TO WORK FOR THE FOLLOWING:

-- CONSENSUS DECISION MAKING;

-- INDUSTRY AND OTHER STAKEHOLDER PARTICIPATION;

-- THE BODY STARTS FROM SCRATCH (NOT USING THE CIVIL

SOCIETY DRAFTED CODE AS A BASIS.) AS AN ALTERNATIVE, THE
