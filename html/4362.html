SUBJECT: ANOTHER MINISTERIAL-LEVEL VICTIM OF NAM CAM

REF: A. FBIS SEP 20021118000011 B. HANOI 1795

1. (U) OFFICIAL VIETNAMESE MEDIA (REF A) AND FOREIGN MEDIA

HAVE CARRIED REPORTS OF THE NOVEMBER 15 DISMISSAL OF VICE

MINISTER OF PUBLIC SECURITY MAJOR GENERAL HOANG NGOC NHAT

FOR "BREACHES OF THE LAW" LINKED TO THE NAM CAM "MAFIA"

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 HANOI 02777 181011Z

CASE. NHAT, WHO WAS ALSO DEMOTED TO COLONEL BUT ONLY

"DISCIPLINED" BY -- NOT FORMALLY SACKED FROM -- THE

MINISTRY, HAS BEEN DESCRIBED AS THE OFFICIAL WHO IN 1997

SIGNED TRUONG VAN CAM\'S INITIAL RELEASE FROM PRISON EIGHT

MONTHS AHEAD OF SCHEDULE. REF B REPORTED ON THE EARLIER

DISMISSALS AND EVENTUAL EXPULSION FROM THE CENTRAL COMMITTEE

OF TWO OTHER HIGH FLIERS -- VICE MINISTER OF PUBLIC SECURITY

LIEUTENANT GENERAL BUI QUOC HUY AND DIRECTOR GENERAL OF THE

VOICE OF VIETNAM TRAN MAI HANH -- FOLLOWING THE SIXTH PARTY

PLENUM JULY 4-15. ANOTHER, PHAM SI CHIEN, WAS FORMALLY

DISMISSED AS VICE GENERAL PROCURATOR OF THE SUPREME PEOPLE\'S

PROCURACY IN AUGUST.

2. (U) TRIALS IN THE NAM CAM CASE ARE EXPECTED TO BEGIN IN

HO CHI MINH CITY ON DECEMBER 10 AND CONTINUE UNTIL JANUARY

28. AT LEAST 156 PEOPLE WILL BE ON TRIAL, INCLUDING HUY,

HANH, AND CHIEN. IT APPEARS THAT NHAT WILL NOT BE

PROSECUTED. THERE IS CONSIDERABLE INTEREST AMONG FOREIGN

JOURNALISTS TO COVER THE TRIALS, BUT THE GVN HAS NOT YET

ANNOUNCED WHETHER THE COURTS WILL BE OPEN FOR THE MEDIA.

3. (U) IN HIS OPENING REMARKS AT THE SECOND SESSION OF THE

11TH NATIONAL ASSEMBLY ON NOVEMBER 12, PRIME MINISTER PHAN

VAN KHAI SPECIFICALLY CITED "NAM CAM" AS A NOTORIOUS EXAMPLE

OF CRIMINAL ACTIVITIES PENETRATING INTO THE WORKINGS OF THE

STATE APPARATUS, AND NOTED THE NEED TO HAVE "TRUSTWORTHY"

PEOPLE WORKING IN SECURITY-RELATED ORGANS. GENERAL

SECRETARY NONG DUC MANH ADMITTED TO THE CLOSING SESSION OF

SIPDIS

THE SEVENTH PLENUM OF THE 9TH PARTY CENTRAL COMMITTEE ON

NOVEMBER 9 THAT "ORGANIZED CRIMINALS HAVE MADE CONTACT WITH

CORRUPT AND DEGENERATE OFFICIALS AND PUBLIC EMPLOYEES,

INCLUDING A NUMBER OF HIGH-RANKING OFFICIALS," A SUBJECT OF

"GREAT CONCERN" TO THE PARTY AND STATE.

4. (U) COMMENT: THE CPV AND GVN WILL ATTEMPT TO USE THE

UPCOMING MASS TRIALS RELATED TO NAM CAM AS SHOWCASES OF

THEIR SERIOUSNESS IN CRACKING DOWN ON CORRUPTION AND IN

DEMONSTRATING THAT EVEN SENIOR OFFICIALS ARE NOT IMMUNE TO

PROSECUTION. HEAVY SENTENCES CAN BE EXPECTED, INCLUDING A

POSSIBLE DEATH PENALTY AT LEAST FOR NAM CAM HIMSELF.

FOREIGN JOURNALISTS ARE SKEPTICAL THAT THEY WILL BE ALLOWED

TO WITNESS THE TRIALS, GIVEN POSSIBLE GVN WORRIES ABOUT WHAT

NAM CAM MIGHT BE TEMPTED TO SAY IN HIS OWN DEFENSE (I.E.,

ABOUT HIS ALLEGED LINKS WITH FAMILY MEMBERS OF VERY HIGH

RANKING CPV AND GVN OFFICIALS). PUBLIC INTEREST IN THE

CASE DOES NOT APPEAR TO BE HIGH, HOWEVER (AT LEAST IN

HANOI), NOR IS IT LIKELY THAT EVEN SUCCESSFUL PROSECUTIONS

OF THESE CASES -- A FOREGONE CONCLUSION IN THIS JUDICIAL

ENVIRONMENT -- WILL WIN BACK MUCH PUBLIC CONFIDENCE IN THE

SANCTITY OF GVN AND CPV OFFICIALS AT ALL LEVELS.
