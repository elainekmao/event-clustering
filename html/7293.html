SUBJECT: TFIZ01: CHALDEAN PRELATE WANTS TO CELEBRATE PALM

SUNDAY MASS IN BASRA

REF: A. PHONE CONVERSATION: MANSOUR - NEA/NGA NATE

JENSEN APR 9

B. EMAIL: BASS - EUR/WE MARA TEKACH-BALL APR 9

1. (SBU) MSGR. PHILIP NAJIM, THE SENIOR REPRESENTATIVE OF THE

CHALDEAN CHURCH IN ITALY AND THE PROCURATOR OF THE CHALDEAN

PATRIARCHATE OF BABYLON TO THE HOLY SEE, ASKED TO MEET WITH

RELIGIOUS MINORITIES OFFICER AND NEA WATCHER APRIL 9. MSGR.

NAJIM THANKED THE USG FOR "OVERTHROWING SADDAM," AND

EXPRESSED GENERAL CONCERNS ABOUT THE STATE OF THE CHALDEAN

CHURCH IN IRAQ AND SPECIFIC CONCERNS ABOUT POTENTIAL

DISCRIMINATION AGAINST CHRISTIANS IN IRAQ ESPECIALLY FROM THE

SHIITES (UNDER-REPRESENTED IN A SADDAM GOVERNMENT THAT

PROTECTED THE CHRISTIANS IN IRAQ) IN A POST-SADDAM IRAQ.

2. (SBU) MSGR. NAJIM WAS PARTICULARLY GRATEFUL TO THE BRITISH

FORCES THAT WERE PROTECTING THE CHALDEAN CHURCH IN BASRA. HE

(A BRITISH CITIZEN TRAVELING ON A BRITISH PASSPORT) SAID HE

HOPED TO TRAVEL TO BASRA (THROUGH KUWAIT AND UMM KASR) APRIL

11 TO BRING ASSISTANCE AND MONEY TO THE BISHOP OF THE

CHALDEANS IN BASRA. HE WAS PLANNING ON CELEBRATING PALM

SUNDAY MASS APRIL 13 IN THE CHALDEAN CHURCH IN BASRA.

ITALIAN RAI TELEVISION WAS GOING TO FILM THE MASS SERVICE AND

AIR IT IN ITALY ON GOOD FRIDAY ALONG WITH THE HOLY FATHER\'S

CELEBRATION.

3. (SBU) MSGR. NAJIM SAID HE WAS HOPING TO GO TO BAGHDAD

APRIL 12 TO MEET THE TWO SENIOR CHALDEAN BISHOPS (CURRENTLY

LEADING THE CHALDEAN CHURCH IN IRAQ SINCE THE PATRIARCH\'S

HEALTH WAS FAILING) NOW STAYING IN THE NUNCIATURE IN BAGHDAD.

HE ASKED IF THE US MILITARY COULD PROVIDE A SAFE CONDUCT FOR

HIM FROM BASRA TO BAGHDAD AND BACK APRIL 12. EMBASSY

OFFICERS PROMISED TO CHECK WITH THE DEPARTMENT BUT ADVISED

MSGR. NAJIM THE US MILITARY WAS UNLIKELY TO BE ABLE TO

PROVIDE A SAFE CONDUCT FOR HIM GIVEN THE UNSTABLE SITUATION

IN THE REGION.

4. (U) THE UK EMBASSY IN ROME IS AWARE OF AND WORKING ON THIS

POTENTIAL VISIT TO BASRA.

5. (U) THIS CABLE WAS CLEARED BY EMBASSY VATICAN.

6. (U) MINIMIZE CONSIDERED.
