SUBJECT: IDENTIFYING FOOD NEEDS IN AFGHANISTAN: NOW

AND IN THE FUTURE

REF: ISLAMABAD 6723

1. SUMMARY. U.S. MISSION/ROME OFFICERS MET ON FRIDAY,

DECEMBER 28 WITH THE U.N. WORLD FOOD PROGRAM (WFP)

ASSISTANT EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR (AED) AND DIRECTOR OF

OPERATIONS, MOHAMMED ZEJJARI. AS WINTER FOOD NEEDS IN

AFGHANISTAN ARE BEING MET BY WFP\'S CURRENT OPERATION

(ALMOST 120,000 TONS DISPATCHED IN DECEMBER), ITS

FUTURE PROGRAMMING IS DISCUSSED. THE WFP AND THE U.N.

FOOD AND AGRICULTURAL ORGANIZATION (FAO) ARE WORKING

TOGETHER TO FORM A FOOD SECURITY ASSESSMENT UNIT FOR

AFGHANISTAN. U.S. MISSION REQUESTS WFP TO CONDUCT

SURVEYS IN A FEW DISTRICTS IN ORDER TO SPOT-CHECK THE

VAM FINDINGS. WFP\'S AED REGISTERED USG CONCERNS AND

WILL FOLLOW-UP WITH HIS STAFF AFTER THE HOLIDAYS. END

SUMMARY.

------------

BACKGROUND

------------

2. AS REPORTED IN REF TEL, WFP\'S VULNERABILITY AND

ANALYSIS MAPPING (VAM) UNIT FOR AFGHANISTAN IS IN THE

MIDST OF EXPANDING ITS OPERATION. WFP AND FAO HAVE

BEEN DISCUSSING WAYS IN WHICH TO CREATE A FOOD SECURITY

ASSESSMENT UNIT (FSAU) THAT WOULD COMBINE THE EXPERTISE

OF EACH AGENCY AND FOLD IN THE ELEMENTS OF THE VAM INTO

THE NEW UNIT. IN MID-NOVEMBER, FAO SENT TO ISLAMABAD

THE PERSON WHO HEADED THE FOOD SECURITY SURVEILLANCE

UNIT IN KOSOVO, FRANCESCO DEL RE. WFP/ISLAMABAD

PROVIDED HIM WITH A DESK IN ITS BUILDING, AND HE WORKED

VERY CLOSELY WITH THE HEAD OF THE VAM UNIT. AFTER TWO

WEEKS, HE COMPOSED A PROPOSAL THAT WAS SHARED WITH WFP

SOON AFTER HIS DEPARTURE. AS WAS DONE IN KOSOVO, THE

ACTIVITIES OF THE VAM AND NEW ACTIVITIES BROUGHT TO

BEAR BY FAO WOULD BE UNITED UNDER ONE ROOF, ONE

MANAGEMENT STRUCTURE, AND UNDER THE NAME OF THE FSAU.

3. IN THE MEANTIME, WFP HEADQUARTERS (HQ) WAS READYING

TO SEND ANOTHER PERSON TO HEAD THE VAM UNIT IN

ISLAMABAD AND HE ARRIVED AT POST ON DECEMBER 15. IN

ADDITION, A NEW WFP COUNTRY DIRECTOR FOR AFGHANISTAN

BEGAN MID-NOVEMBER. IN THE LAST FEW WEEKS, THE

FORMATION OF THE FSAU SEEMS TO HAVE SLOWED. AS OUR

GOVERNMENT IS INTERESTED IN LEARNING AND UNDERSTANDING

MANY FACETS OF AN AFGHAN\'S LIFE AS IT RELATES TO

HIS/HER ABILITY TO FEED HIS/HER FAMILY, THE IMPORTANCE

OF COMBINING THE STRENGTHS OF BOTH AGENCIES AND WORKING

TOGETHER TO GATHER AND ANALYZE THE INFORMATION REMAINS

EXTREMELY IMPORTANT AS WE MOVE FROM THE EMERGENCY PHASE

TO REHABILITATION AND RECONSTRUCTION.

4. U.S. MISSION/ROME OFFICERS LAVELLE AND DAVIS MET ON

FRIDAY, DECEMBER 28 WITH WFP\'S ASSISTANT EXECUTIVE

DIRECTOR AND DIRECTOR OF OPERATIONS, MOHAMMED ZEJJARI,

TO REVIEW CONCERNS AS WE MOVE FORWARD TO THE NEXT PHASE

OF WFP\'S OPERATION IN AFGHANISTAN.

------------------

COMBINED EFFORTS

------------------

5. MISSION OFFICER DAVIS OUTLINED THE PROGRESS MADE

BETWEEN WFP AND FAO IN ESTABLISHING THE FSAU AND

EXPLAINED THAT BOTH AGENCIES HAVE STRENGTHS TO BRING TO

SUCH A UNIT. SUCH STRENGTHS BECOME EVEN MORE ACUTE

WHEN THE FORCES ARE COMBINED. A PAUCITY OF DATA EXISTS

FOR AFGHANISTAN, AND WE NEED TO USE EVERY TOOL AT OUR

DISPOSAL IN GATHERING AND ANALYZING INFORMATION THAT

SERVES TO GUIDE THE USG AND OTHER DONORS, AS WELL AS

THE U.N., IN ITS PROGRAMMING.

6. MISSION OFFICERS CONGRATULATED WFP ON ITS

TREMENDOUS ACHIEVEMENT IN MOVING LARGE QUANTITIES OF

FOOD INTO AFGHANISTAN, BUT CAUTIONED THAT ONCE WE

EMERGE FROM MEETING THE WINTER NEEDS, USAID WILL BE

LOOKING CLOSELY AT WFP\'S DECISIONS ON HOW FUTURE FOOD

NEEDS ARE DETERMINED AND HOW FOOD IS PROGRAMMED. THE

OFFICERS POINTED OUT THAT IT WOULD BE IN WFP\'S

INTERESTS TO HAVE ANOTHER AGENCY INVOLVED IN THE

DETERMINATION OF NEEDS.

7. NOTE. U.S. MISSION OFFICER DAVIS IS ALSO IN

CONTINUED DIALOGUE WITH FAO\'S SPECIAL RELIEF OPERATIONS

SERVICE (TCOR) TO ENSURE THAT FAO/TCOR IS READY TO

PROVIDE APPROPRIATE STAFFING TO THE UNIT, AS SOON AS A

WORKING ARRANGEMENT IS ACCEPTED BY BOTH PARTIES. END

NOTE.

-------------------------------

SPOT-CHECKING THE VAM RESULTS

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ROME 00001 02 OF 02 020616Z

-------------------------------

8. WITH THIS IN MIND, THE MISSION OFFICERS STRONGLY

ENCOURAGED WFP TO CONDUCT "SPOT-CHECKS" IN A FEW

DISTRICTS OF AFGHANISTAN THAT ARE CURRENTLY ACCESSIBLE.

SUCH SPOT-CHECKS WOULD WORK TO CONFIRM THE VAM RESULTS

THAT ARE GUIDING WFP\'S DISTRIBUTIONS. DONORS WOULD

WELCOME SUCH CONFIRMATION, SINCE THERE HAS BEEN SOME

CONTROVERSY ABOUT THE RESULTS. SOME THINK THE NEEDS

ARE ACTUALLY HIGHER, AND OTHERS THINK THE NEEDS ARE

LOWER THAN WHAT VAM HAS DETERMINED.

9. ZEJJARI SEEMED TO UNDERSTAND THE IMPORTANCE OF

TAKING THIS ACTION AS SOON AS POSSIBLE. THE

USAID/DISASTER ASSISTANCE RESPONSE TEAM (DART) HAS ALSO

RECENTLY DISCUSSED THIS MATTER WITH VARIOUS WFP STAFF

IN ISLAMABAD.

------------------------

NUTRITIONAL ASSESSMENT

------------------------

10. AS STATED IN REFTEL, ONE OF THE FIRST PLANNED

TASKS OF THE VAM FOR THE NEW YEAR WAS TO CONDUCT A

NUTRITIONAL ASSESSMENT OF THE WEST (HARDEST-HIT BY

DROUGHT), FOLLOWED BY A NUTRITIONAL ASSESSMENT OF THE

ENTIRE COUNTRY. SUCH A STUDY COULD ALSO SERVE TO RE-

CONFIRM NEEDS OUTLINED BY THE VAM RESULTS. EVEN THOUGH

WFP HAD IDENTIFIED A NUTRITIONIST FOR THE WORK, THE

AGREEMENT FELL THROUGH, AND WFP IS NOW RECRUITING AGAIN

FOR THE POSITION. MISSION OFFICERS SUGGESTED ASKING

COMMENT

----------

11. ZEJJARI LISTENED CLOSELY TO U.S. MISSION\'S

CONCERNS, BUT MADE FEW COMMENTS OR COMMITMENTS. HE

DID, HOWEVER, CLEARLY REGISTER OUR COMMENTS ABOUT WFP\'S

NEED TO JUSTIFY ITS FUTURE PROGRAMMING. HIS SPECIAL

ASSISTANT TOOK DETAILED NOTES OF OUR CONVERSATION, AND

HE PROMISED TO FOLLOW-UP WITH THE APPROPRIATE STAFF

UPON THEIR RETURN FROM THE HOLIDAYS. U.S. MISSION WILL

FOLLOW THESE DEVELOPMENTS CLOSELY.

12. MINIMIZE CONSIDERED. HEILEMAN
