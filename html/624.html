SUBJECT: WAR CRIMES TRIBUNAL: CROATIAN RESPONSE

REF: STATE 385591

1. CONFIDENTIAL - ENTIRE TEXT.

-

2. AS REQUESTED IN REFTEL, AMBASSADOR GALBRAITH

CALLED ON FOREIGN MINISTER GRANIC JANUARY 13 TO

DISCUSS ASSISTANCE TO THE PROSECUTOR FOR THE WAR

CRIMES TRIBUNAL. HE LEFT A COPY OF THE TALKING

POINTS IN THE REFTEL.

3. THE AMBASSADOR STRESSED THE NEED FOR ASSISTANCE

IN THE AREAS OF IDENTIFICATION AND INTERROGATION OF

WITNESSES, AND APPREHENSION OF SUSPECTED PERPETRATORS,

NOTING THAT CROATIA ALMOST CERTAINLY HAS ONE OF THE

LARGEST CONCENTRATIONS OF POTENTIAL WITNESSES TO WAR

CRIMES COMMITTED IN THE FORMER YUGOSLAVIA.

4. GRANIC REPLIED THAT CROATIA WILL GIVE "VERY

STRONG SUPPORT" TO THE WORK OF THE INTERNATIONAL

CRIMES TRIBUNAL. HE STATED THAT, AT THE MOMENT,

INFORMATION ON WAR CRIMES IS SCATTERED AMONG VARIOUS

ORGANIZATIONS. GRANIC MENTIONED THE LEADING ROLE

PLAYED TO DATE BY DR. IVICA KOSTAVIC, DEPUTY PRIME

MINISTER AND CROATIAN AMBASSADOR FOR HUMAN RIGHTS.

HE ALSO NOTED THAT THE NATIONAL COMMISSION FOR WAR

CRIMES HEADED BY DR. (AND SUPREME COURT PRESIDENT)

VUKOVIC AS WELL AS SEVERAL PRIVATE GROUPS HAD BEEN

ESPECIALLY ACTIVE IN THIS AREA.

5. ASKED IF CROATIA HAD ANY PLANS TO CONSOLIDATE THE

INFORMATION ON HAND AND DESIGNATE A SINGLE INDIVIDUAL

AS COORDINATOR ON WAR CRIMES ISSUES, GRANIC REPLIED

THAT HE WOULD BE DISCUSSING THE MATTER WITH KOSTOVIC

"WHO IS THE ONE WHO KNOWS BEST." HE PROMISED TO

PROVIDE US WITH A LIST OF PEOPLE AND INSTITUTIONS BY

THE END OF THE WEEK.

6. WITH RESPECT TO IDENTIFYING AND DETAINING

SUSPECTED WAR CRIMINALS, INCLUDING POSSIBLY CROATS,

GRANIC REPLIED THAT CROATIA WAS IN PRONCIPLE PREPARED

TO COOPERATE BUT THAT HE WANTED TO EXAMINE OUR POINTS

IN MORE DETAIL AND WOULD WOULD GIVE AN OFFICIAL REPLY

IN WRITING BY THE END OF THE WEEK. WHEN IT WAS

POINTED OUT THAT AMBASSADOR ALBRIGHT WOULD LIKELY

RAISE THESE ISSUES DURING HER VISIT TO CROATIA,

GRANIC STATED THAT THE GOC WOULD BE READY TO REPLY TO

HER QUESTIONS ON THIS SUBJECT.
