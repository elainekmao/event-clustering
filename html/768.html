SUBJECT: SERB REFUGEES IN KNIN -- A STEP CLOSER TO

RELEASE

1. DECONTROL UPON RECEIPT -- SENSITIVE BUT UNCLASSIFIED -

- PROTECT ACCORDINGLY.

2. TALKS BETWEEN SRSG AKASHI AND THE PRESIDENT\'S CHIEF OF

STAFF HRVOJE SARINIC TO RELEASE THE 780 SERB REFUGEES

LOCATED ON THE UNCRO COMPOUND IN KNIN CONTINUED OVER THE

WEEKEND SEPTEMBER 9-10. (NOTE: OF THE 780 REFUGEES, 760

HAVE EXPRESSED A DESIRE TO GO TO SERBIA.)

3. ON SEPTEMBER 11 THE UN\'S HUMAN RIGHTS ACTION TEAM TOLD

US THAT, DESPITE CROATIAN PRESS REPORTS THAT AN AGREEMENT

HAD BEEN REACHED, THE DEAL HAD NOT YET BEEN FINALIZED.

DURING THE AKASHI-SARINIC TALKS, THE GOC AGREED TO NARROW

THE LIST OF PEOPLE IT WANTS HELD FOR INVESTIGATION OF WAR

CRIMES FROM 62 TO 35. THE MAIN STICKING POINT HAS BEEN,

AND REMAINS, FORMAL GOC ACCEPTANCE OF UN DEMANDS FOR LEGAL

DOCUMENTATION OF THE CHARGES PENDING AGAINST THE 35 SERBS

AND THE RIGHT TO REVIEW THESE DOCUMENTS TO DETERMINE THEIR

VALIDITY. IF THE UN CONCLUDES THE CHARGES ARE VALID THEN

WITH SAID IT IS EXPECTED THAT THE FORMULA FOR RESOLUTION

OF THE REFUGEE PROBLEM IN KNIN WILL BE APPLIED TO A
