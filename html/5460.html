1. HOLY SEE FOREIGN MINISTER ARCHBISHOP JEAN-LOUIS TAURAN

CALLED IN AMBASSADOR JANUARY 21 TO SHARE THE RESULTS OF THE

VATICAN NUNCIO IN ZAMBIA\'S ASSESSMENT OF THE FAMINE AND THE

DEBATE IN ZAMBIA OVER BIOTECH RELIEF FOOD. TAURAN HAD

PREVIOUSLY PROMISED THE AMBASSADOR "GOOD NEWS" ON THE

SUBJECT. TO THIS END, HE HIGHLIGHTED THE HOLY SEE\'S EARLY

CONTRIBUTIONS TO AMELIORATE THE FAMINE THROUGH THE ACTIVITIES

OF CARITAS AND LOCAL CHURCHES. TAURAN ACKNOWLEDGED THAT THE

ANTI-BIOTECH VIEWS OF THE JESUIT ACTIVIST FATHER HENRIOT HAD

BECOME WIDELY REGARDED AS THE VIEW OF THE CATHOLIC CHURCH IN

ZAMBIA, AND PROMISED THE AMBASSADOR THAT THE HOLY SEE WOULD

ENSURE THAT ITS GENERALLY PRO-BIOTECH VIEWS WERE PROVIDED TO

THE ZAMBIAN CONFERENCE OF BISHOPS. THE HOLY SEE\'S VIEW, AS

OUTLINED IN A LETTER TO SECRETARY POWELL LAST YEAR (REF C) IS

THAT BIOTECH FOODS SHOULD BE AVAILABLE TO POPULATIONS IN

DEVELOPING COUNTRIES, SUBJECT TO APPROPRIATE CAUTION

COMPARABLE TO THOSE FOR PHARMACEUTICAL GOODS. THE HOLY SEE

WILL ALSO ENCOURAGE THE BISHOPS AND, IF THEY ARE RELUCTANT,

THE NUNCIO, TO SPEAK OUT MORE DIRECTLY ON THE SUBJECT. A

RECENT EMBASSY-ORGANIZED CONFERENCE WITH A GROUP OF AFRICAN

FARMERS AND BIOTECH EXPERTS -- INCLUDING ZAMBIA -- (SEPTEL)

HELPED MAKE THE CASE TO KEY HOLY SEE OFFICES OF THE SAFETY

AND POTENTIAL BENEFITS OF BIOTECH FOODS FOR AFRICA. END

SUMMARY.

2. ARCHBISHOP TAURAN MET WITH AMBASSADOR AND DCM JANUARY 21

TO FOLLOW UP ON HIS PLEDGE TO LOOK INTO THE CATHOLIC CHURCH\'S

ROLE IN RESPONDING TO THE FAMINE IN ZAMBIA AND OTHER AFFECTED

AREAS IN SOUTHERN AFRICA. AT THE OUTSET, TAURAN SOUGHT TO

MAKE CLEAR THAT THE HOLY SEE AND ITS AFFILIATED AGENCIES HAD

BEEN ON THE FRONT LINES OF INTERNATIONAL EFFORTS TO RESPOND.

FROM FEBRUARY 2002, HE NOTED, THE POPE HAD DONATED $30,000

FROM HIS (MODEST) PAPAL FUND FOR GLOBAL RELIEF EFFORTS, AND,

MORE SIGNIFICANTLY, CARITAS HAD MOBILIZED ITS RESOURCES EARLY

AND HAD BEEN PLAYED AN IMPORTANT HUMANITARIAN ROLE THROUGHOUT

THE FAMINE PERIOD. HE ALSO NOTED THAT THE LOCAL CHURCHES HAD

BEEN ACTIVE ON THE GROUND IN PROVIDING ASSISTANCE.

3. ACCORDING TO THE NUNCIO, THE HOLY SEE WAS INFORMED THAT

THE ZAMBIAN MAIZE CROP SHORTFALL HAD "NOT YET HAD SERIOUS

EFFECTS, THANKS TO THE AVAILABILITY OF OTHER FOODS IN THE

COUNTRY." THE NUNCIO FURTHER MAINTAINED THAT GM FOODS WERE

NOT INDISPENSABLE TO MEET THE PROBLEM, AS THERE WERE ONLY

15,000 TONS OF GM MAIZE IN DEPOSIT AT THE CATHOLIC RELIEF

SERVICES HEADQUARTERS IN ZAMBIA, WHILE THE GOVERNMENT HAS

100,000 TONS OF NON-GM MAIZE IN RESERVE AND IS FINDING OTHER

SOURCES. THE NUNCIO OPINED THAT THE SITUATION "WILL PROBABLY

NOT GET WORSE IN THE NEXT FEW MONTHS."

4. TAURAN ACKNOWLEDGED THAT JESUIT ACTIVIST FATHER HENRIOT

HAD UNFORTUNATELY SHAPED THE PUBLIC PERCEPTION OF THE

CHURCH\'S VIEW IN ZAMBIA. THIS RESULTED, HE SAID, FROM A

COMBINATION OF HENRIOT\'S PERSONAL ACTIVISM AND THE SILENCE OF

THE ZAMBIAN CONFERENCE OF BISHOPS, WHO HAVE MADE NO OFFICIAL

STATEMENT OF THE ADVISABILITY OF ACCEPTING BIOTECH FOODS.

CONSEQUENTLY, THE NUNCIO REPORTED THAT "THE POSITION OF THE

JESUIT CENTER IS CONSIDERED TO BE THE POSITION OF THE

CHURCH." THE JESUIT\'S POSITION, WHICH HAS UNFORTUNATELY

HELPED FRAME THE DEBATE OVER BIOTECH FOODS IN ZAMBIA AND

WHICH MUST NOW BE OVERCOME WITH NEW INFORMATION, WAS

SUMMARIZED BY THE NUNCIO IN HIS REPORT AS FOLLOWS:

- THE SAFETY OF THESE FOODS IS STILL UNDER STUDY;

- THE CONSUMPTION OF GM FOODS IN THE U.S. IS INTEGRATED IN

THE CONSUMPTION OF ALL FOODS, WHEREAS IN ZAMBIA IT WOULD BE

THE ONLY TYPE OF FOOD CONSUMED, WHICH "WOULD WEIGH UPON THE

DETERMINATION OF ITS SAFETY);

- ZAMBIA IS RESOLVING THE PROBLEM BY IMPORTING

NON-GENETICALLY MODIFIED FOODS AND IS EDUCATING ITS PEOPLE TO

DIVERSIFY ITS EATING HABITS;

5. IN LIGHT OF THESE POSITIONS, TAURAN AGREED WITH THE

AMBASSADOR ON THE NEED TO ENSURE THAT THE HOLY SEE\'S MORE

BALANCED POSITION WAS WIDELY DISSEMINATED IN THE COUNTRY AND

REGIONS. HE PROMISED TO ENSURE THE RESULTS OF THE PONTIFICAL

ACADEMY OF SCIENCE\'S POSITION -- WHICH SEES NO REASON FOR

REJECTION OF BIOTECH FOODS AND THEY SHOULD BE PROVIDED TO

DEVELOPING COUNTRY POPULATIONS AS LONG AS THE PRECAUTIONARY

PRINCIPAL IS OBSERVED (AS IT IS WITH PHARMACEUTICALS) -- ARE

PROVIDED TO ALL THE BISHOPS. THE HOLY SEE WOULD THEN

ENCOURAGE THE BISHOPS TO ISSUE A STATEMENT AND DISSEMINATE

THE WORD IN THEIR CHURCHES. TAURAN ADDED THAT IF THE BISHOPS

FAILED TO DO SO -- WHICH IS POSSIBLE GIVEN THAT THE HOLY SEE

CANNOT ORDER BISHOPS TO TAKE SPECIFIC STANDS ON POLITICAL

ISSUES -- HE WOULD HAVE THE NUNCIO TAKE STEPS TO GET THE HOLY

SEE\'S POSITION OUT.

6. THE AMBASSADOR THANKED TAURAN FOR HIS COMMITMENT TO

ENSURE THE HOLY SEE\'S POSITION WAS DISSEMINATED MORE WIDELY

IN ZAMBIA AND THE REST OF SOUTHERN AFRICA, BUT STRESSED THE

IMPORTANCE OF MOVING QUICKLY GIVEN THE STILL PRESENT THREAT

OF FAMINE IN THE COUNTRY. WHILE THE AMBASSADOR RECOGNIZED

THAT THE WORLD FOOD PROGRAM HAD TAKEN STEPS TO WORK AROUND

THE ZAMBIAN GOVERNMENT\'S REJECTION OF U.S. AID, THE

AMBASSADOR STRESSED THE INCREASED COSTS AND TIME THIS HAD

IMPOSED ON THE RELIEF EFFORT. HE ALSO NOTED THAT OUR

INFORMATION WAS NOT AS SANGUINE AS THAT PRESENTED BY THE

NUNCIO, ALTHOUGH FOOD NOW APPEARED TO BE IN THE PIPELINE.

BEYOND THE IMMEDIATE SITUATION, HOWEVER, THE AMBASSADOR

POINTED OUT THAT THE ZAMBIAN DECISION REPRESENTED A NEGATIVE

PRECEDENT WITH POTENTIALLY SERIOUS IMPLICATIONS FOR BROADER

AFRICAN RELIEF IF MORE COUNTRIES BEGAN FOLLOWING ZAMBIA\'S

LEAD AND REJECTED U.S. FOOD. GIVEN THAT THE U.S. PROVIDES

OVER 60 PERCENT OF ALL RELIEF FOOD TO THE REGION, THE

AMBASSADOR NOTED, SUCH RESTRICTIONS WOULD MAKE IT IMPOSSIBLE

FOR HE WFP TO MEET ITS DEMANDS). TAURAN AGREED THAT SUCH A

PRECEDENT WOULD BE UNFORTUNATE, AND CONTRARY TO EFFORTS TO

FEED THOSE IN NEED.

-------

COMMENT

-------

7. THE HOLY SEE\'S RESPONSE ON THIS ISSUE, ABOUT WHICH THE

EMBASSY HAS BEEN PRESSING SINCE EARLY FALL, HAS BEEN SLOW IN

COMING. NEVERTHELESS, WE HAVE HEARD FROM SOURCES WITHIN THE

HOLY SEE THAT OUR REPEATED INTERVENTIONS -- RECENTLY

REINFORCED BY OUR CANADIAN AND ARGENTINEAN DIPLOMATIC

COLLEAGUES -- HAS GOTTEN THE HOLY SEE\'S ATTENTION AND LED TO

MOVEMENT ON THE ISSUE. SIGNIFICANTLY, THE POPE MADE TWO

REFERENCES TO FAMINE IN AFRICA IN HIS ANNUAL ADDRESS TO THE

DIPLOMATIC CORPS, REINFORCING THE FOCUS THE ISSUE IS NOW

RECEDING. THIS GROWING INTEREST WAS ALSO REFLECTED IN THE

BROAD VATICAN PARTICIPATION AT AN EMBASSY-SPONSORED

CONFERENCE JANUARY 27 ON BIOTECH IN AFRICA, WHICH WAS

ATTENDED BY REPRESENTATIVES OF THE SECRETARIAT OF STATE, THE

COUNCIL FOR JUSTICE AND PEACE, COR UNUM (RESPONSIBLE FOR

HUMANITARIAN WORK), THE JESUIT DIRECTORS FOR AFRICA AND THE

U.S., THE CARITAS CATHOLIC RELIEF AGENCY, THE SANT EGIDIO

COMMUNITY, AND MEMBERS OF THE HOLY SEE DIPLOMATIC CORPS.

EMBASSY LUSAKA\'S PRESSURE HAS ALSO BEEN HELPFUL IN RAISING

THE HOLY SEE\'S APPRECIATION OF THE ISSUE, AND WE WOULD URGE
