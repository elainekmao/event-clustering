SUBJECT: AGE PREVENTS POPE TRAVELING TO OCEANIA

1. POPE JOHN PAUL II\'S INCREASING INFIRMITY HAS PREVENTED

HIM TRAVELING TO OCEANIA TO PROMULGATE A NEW CHURCH DOCUMENT

FOR THE REGION. THE VATICAN CITED DISTANCE AND THE EFFECT OF

THE FLIGHT ON THE POPE\'S HEALTH AS THE REASONS FOR ISSUING THE

DOCUMENT IN ROME, NOVEMBER 22, VIA INTERNET (A FIRST FOR THE

VATICAN). IN THE RECENT PAST, THE POPE HAS CONDUCTED THE

PRESENTATION CEREMONIES IN THE REGIONS THAT WERE THE SUBJECT

OF PREVIOUS DOCUMENTS: THE AMERICAS, AFRICA, EUROPE AND ASIA.

2. POPE JOHN PAUL II IS 81. HE IS BECOMING FRAILER WITH EACH

PASSING YEAR. IT IS NO SECRET THAT THE POPE SUFFERS FROM A

NEUROLOGICAL DISEASE, PROBABLY PARKINSON\'S. THE VATICAN HAS

INSTITUTED VARIOUS PROCEDURES AND TOOLS TO MAKE LIFE EASIER

FOR THE PONTIFF - A SPECIAL LECTERN ON HIS CHAIR FOR THE PAGES

OF HIS SPEECHES BECAUSE TREMORS PRECLUDE HIM HOLDING THE TEXT;

A WHEELED PLATFORM TO MOVE HIM UP THE AISLE OF SAINT PETER\'S

BASILICA. (THE AISLE IS 211 METERS LONG). POPE JOHN PAUL NOW

WEARS A HEARING AID, ALTHOUGH HE DOES NOT REQUIRE SPECTACLES

TO READ, AND OFTEN USES A CANE WHEN WALKING.

3. COMMENT: THE VATICAN IS ADAPTING ITSELF TO THE NEEDS OF

THE AGING AND INCREASINGLY FRAIL POPE. THE PONTIFF INSISTS ON

PERFORMING AS MANY OF HIS DUTIES AS POSSIBLE. ALTHOUGH HIS

SCHEDULE HAS BEEN REDUCED TO THE ESSENTIAL, IT WOULD BE

DEMANDING FOR ANY OCTOGENARIAN. WHILE THE POPE\'S BODY MAY BE

SLOWING DOWN, ALL THOSE WHO MEET WITH THE POPE SAY HIS MIND

REMAINS ACTIVE AND ALERT. THE VATICAN WILL CONTINUE TO

ACCOMMODATE THE AGING PONTIFF SO THAT THE DIGNITY AND MORE

IMPORTANTLY, THE PUBLIC NATURE OF THE OFFICE IS MAINTAINED FOR

AS LONG AS POSSIBLE. END COMMENT. NICHOLSON
