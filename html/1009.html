SUBJECT: LTTE MASSACRES 54 CIVILIANS IN EASTERN PROVINCE

NEAR AMPARA

REF: (A) COLOMBO 2306, (B) COLOMBO 2269

1. (U) SUMMARY: THE LTTE ATTACKED SLEEPING VILLAGERS IN

GONAGALA DURING EARLY MORNING HOURS OF SEPTEMBER 18,

HACKING TO DEATH 54 CIVILIANS INCLUDING WOMEN AND

CHILDREN. LOCALS HAVE ASSAILED THE GOVERNMENT FOR

FAILING TO PROVIDE ADEQUATE SECURITY. ON THE OTHER

HAND, TAMILS HAVE CRITICIZED THE GOVERNMENT FOR ITS

ENERGETIC RESPONSE TO THE LTTE ATTACK EVEN AS IT HAS

REMAINED SILENT ABOUT THE AIR FORCE\'S ACCIDENTAL BOMBING

OF TAMIL CIVILIANS IN THE NORTH ON SEPTEMBER 15. END

SUMMARY.

2. (U) LTTE CADRES ENTERED THE SINHALESE VILLAGE OF

GONAGALA, APPROXIMATELY 13 KILOMETRES NORTH OF AMPARA

(NEAR THE DISTRICT BORDER WITH BATTICALOA) IN EASTERN

SRI LANKA, BETWEEN O13O AND O430 SATURDAY MORNING,

SEPTEMBER 18. REPORTEDLY DRAGGING SOME OF THEIR VICTIMS

SLEEPING FROM THEIR BEDS, THE LTTE KILLED 54 CIVILIANS

USING LONG KNIVES, SWORDS AND MACHETES. THE VICTIMS

INCLUDED 14 CHILDREN UNDER FIVE YEARS OF AGE, 17 WOMEN

AND 23 MEN. THIRTY-EIGHT OTHER PERSONS WERE ALSO

INJURED. DEPUTY DEFENSE MINISTER RATWATTE VISITED THE

VILLAGE SATURDAY AFTERNOON AND WAS ACCOSTED BY ANGRY

VILLAGERS ASKING, "IS THIS THE SECURITY YOU ARE

PROVIDING US?" HE PROMISED TO SEND MORE HOME GUARDS TO

THE AREA.

3. (C) SECURITY SOURCES TOLD EMBOFF THEY SUSPECTED THE

ATTACK WAS THE WORK OF ABOUT 6O LOCAL LTTE CADRES.

GIVEN THE LENGTH OF TIME OF THE ATTACK AND GONAGALA\'S

DISTANCE FROM KNOWN LTTE AREAS OF OPERATION, THEY THINK

IT UNLIKELY A STRIKE FORCE CAME DOWN FROM THE UNCLEARED

AREA ALONG THE COAST SOUTH OF BATTICALOA FOR THE ATTACK.

THE SECURITY FORCES ARE CONTINUING THEIR SEARCH

OPERATIONS.

4. (U) THE ATTACK WAS WIDELY CONDEMNED. PRESIDENT

KUMARATUNGA OFFERED HER CONDOLENCES ("I WAS DEEPLY

GRIEVED TO HEAR OF THE DEATHS"), SAYING "ALL CIVILIZED

SOCIETIES WOULD, NO DOUBT, DEPLORE CRIMES OF THIS TYPE

WITH UTTER CONTEMPT." THE TAMIL UNITED LIBERATION FRONT

(TULF) AND OTHER TAMIL COMMENTATORS, HOWEVER, CONTRASTED

THE PRESIDENT\'S STATEMENT AND OTHER EXPRESSIONS OF

GOVERNMENT CONCERN FOR THE VICTIMS (PROMISES OF

ASSISTANCE TO THEIR FAMILIES) WITH THE OFFICIAL SILENCE

AFTER THE SEPTEMBER 15 AIR FORCE BOMBING OF TAMIL

CIVILIANS IN THE NORTH WHERE 22 CIVILIANS WERE KILLED

(REF A).

4. (C) COMMENT: MOST OBSERVERS, INCLUDING GOVERNMENT AND

MILITARY SOURCES, VIEW THIS LATEST LTTE OUTRAGE AS

DIRECT RETALIATION FOR THE SEPTEMBER 15 BOMBING. WHILE

THAT MAY BE TRUE, THE LTTE ACTION DEMONSTRATES ONCE

AGAIN ITS ABILITY TO STRIKE THROUGHOUT THE COUNTRY. BY

REVISITING GONAGALA (A SMALL VILLAGE SURROUNDED BY

TAMIL ONES IN THE JUNGLE WHERE THE LTTE PASSED THROUGH A

YEAR AGO) FAR AWAY FROM THE FRONT LINES, THE LTTE ALSO

SPOTLIGHTED SHORTCOMINGS IN THE GOVERNMENT\'S SECURITY

BLANKET IN THE EAST. THIS WILL LIKELY INCREASE PRESSURE

ON THE GOVERNMENT TO MOVE MORE TROOPS FROM THE NORTH AND

PERHAPS FROM THE OFFENSIVE LINES TO INCREASE LOCAL

SECURITY IN THE EAST (REF B). THE WEEKEND PRESS CARRIED

MANY CRITICAL STORIES OF THE SHORT-LIVED (24 HOURS)

RECENT OFFENSIVE ("RANA GOSHA V") SUGGESTING IT HAD

FALLEN SHORT OF ITS OBJECTIVE AND TROOPS HAD RETREATED.

UNFORTUNATELY, THIS MASSACRE COULD WELL PROVE TO BE THE

LTTE\'S MOST EFFECTIVE WEAPON IN PREVENTING ADDITIONAL

SUCH OFFENSIVES FROM OCCURRING BEFORE THIS YEAR\'S

MONSOON. END COMMENT.
