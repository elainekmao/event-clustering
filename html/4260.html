SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) HOLY SEE DIRECTOR OF CARIBBEAN AFFAIRS PAINTED A

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 VATICA 05471 01 OF 02 080949Z

PICTURE OF A DEFENSIVE CUBAN CATHOLIC CHURCH IN A NOVEMBER 6

MEETING WITH POLOFFS TO DISCUSS CUBA AND VENEZUELA (VENEZUELA

REPORTED SEPTEL). MSGR. GIORGIO LINGUA SAID THE HOLY SEE AND

CUBAN CHURCH HAVE DECIDED NOT TO ENGAGE IN OVERTLY POLITICAL

SUPPORT OF DEMOCRATIC DISSIDENTS IN CUBA IN ORDER TO AVOID A

CRACKDOWN ON THE CHURCH BY THE GOC. HE NOTED THAT THE ONE

CHURCH-RELATED PUBLICATION THAT ENGAGES IN POLITICAL

COMMENTARY, "VITRAL," HAS CAUSED THE HOLY SEE SOME DIFFICULTY

WITH CUBAN AUTHORITIES, ADDING THAT THE HOLY SEE HAD

ENCOURAGED THE BISHOP RESPONSIBLE FOR IT TO RESPOND

POSITIVELY TO CUBAN AUTHORITIES\' DEMAND THAT IT BE REGISTERED

DESPITE THE BISHOP\'S FEARS OF CENSORSHIP. END SUMMARY.

------------------------

A CHURCH PLAYING DEFENSE

------------------------

2. (C) FOLLOWING UP ON RECENT USINT REPORTING ON CATHOLIC

CHURCH ACTIVITIES IN CUBA (REFTELS), POLOFFS CALLED ON

VATICAN COUNTRY DIRECTOR FOR THE CARIBBEAN STATES, MSGR.

GIORGIO LINGUA, NOVEMBER 6 TO DISCUSS THE HOLY SEE\'S VIEWS OF

THE STATE OF THE CHURCH AND DEMOCRATIC DISSIDENCE IN CUBA.

WE ASKED WHETHER THE PAPAL NUNCIO HAS DEVELOPED AND MAINTAINS

CONTACTS WITH THE DISSIDENT COMMUNITY AND WHETHER THE HOLY

SEE WOULD TAKE A POSITION ON A CUBAN HUMAN RIGHTS RESOLUTION.

LINGUA RESPONDED THAT HE WAS NOT AWARE OF ANY INTENTION OF

THE HOLY SEE TO ENGAGE ON THE HR RESOLUTION, EVEN BEHIND THE

SCENES. FURTHERMORE, HE SAID THE HOLY SEE SUPPORTS THE CUBAN

CHURCH\'S CAUTIOUS APPROACH TO POLITICAL ACTIVITIES, AND WAS

NOT AWARE OF ANY PLANNED OUTREACH TO CUBA\'S DISSIDENT

COMMUNITY BY THE NUNCIO. HE INDICATED THAT THE CHURCH (AND

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 VATICA 05471 01 OF 02 080949Z

THE HOLY SEE) PHILOSOPHICALLY SUPPORTED GREATER POLITICAL

FREEDOMS IN CUBA. HOWEVER, HE PAINTED A PICTURE OF A CHURCH

IN A FUNDAMENTALLY DEFENSIVE POSTURE. ITS OVERRIDING CONCERN

WAS TO MAINTAIN THE ABILITY OF THE FAITHFUL TO PRACTICE THEIR

FAITH AND TO AVOID A CRACKDOWN BY THE GOC ON THE CHURCH\'S OWN

LIMITED FREEDOM.

3. (C) AS AN EXAMPLE OF THIS CAUTION, LINGUA POINTED TO THE

FACT THAT ALMOST ALL CHURCH PUBLICATIONS IN CUBA AVOID

EXPLICITLY POLITICAL COMMENTARY. HE SAID THE ONLY EXCEPTION

IS THE PUBLICATION OF THE PINAR DEL RIO DIOCESE\'S NEWSLETTER

"VITRAL," WHOSE EDITOR, DAGOBERTO VALDEZ, WAS A RELATIVELY

OUTSPOKEN CRITIC OF THE GOC. LINGUA TOLD US THAT THE HOLY

SEE HAD RECEIVED NUMEROUS COMPLAINTS ABOUT VITRAL FROM THE

CUBAN EMBASSY TO THE HOLY SEE. IN RESPONSE, THE HOLY SEE HAS

ALWAYS UNDERSCORED THE PAPER\'S RIGHT TO FREEDOM OF

EXPRESSION. ON THE OTHER HAND, HE SAID THE HOLY SEE HAD

URGED THE BISHOP OF PINAR DEL RIO TO "REGISTER" THE PAPER

WITH CUBAN AUTHORITIES AS THE LATTER HAVE BEEN DEMANDING.

THE HOLY SEE THOUGHT IT NORMAL FOR REVENUE GENERATING

ACTIVITIES SUCH AS VITRAL TO HAVE TO REGISTER WITH CIVIL

AUTHORITIES. BUT, ACCORDING TO LINGUA, THE BISHOP FEARED

CENSORSHIP AND HAD SO FAR REFUSED TO REGISTER THE PAPER.

4. (C) QUERIED AS TO WHY THE CHURCH IN CUBA, AN

HISTORICALLY CATHOLIC COUNTRY, WASN\'T AS ACTIVE IN THE FIGHT

FOR FREEDOM AS HAD BEEN THE POLISH CHURCH, LINGUA NOTED THAT

THE CHURCH HAD ALWAYS CHARTED A CAUTIOUS COURSE IN CUBA, YET

IT WAS NEVER SUPPRESSED IN THE SAME MANNER AS HAD BEEN THE

CASE IN MANY COUNTRIES OF EASTERN EUROPE. CATHOLIC CLERGY,

EVEN BISHOPS, WERE ALL CUBANS, NOT FOREIGN, AND HAD RETAINED

THE RIGHT TO PRACTICE AND PROMOTE THEIR FAITH. THEY HAD NOT

BEEN THROWN INTO A GULAG, AS HAD BEEN THE CASE IN EASTERN

EUROPE, AND THUS DID NOT FEEL AS THREATENED BY "OLD FASHIONED

SOCIALISM" AS THEIR EASTERN EUROPEAN BRETHREN. NEVERTHELESS,

LINGUA RECOGNIZED THAT THE CHURCH WAS CONSTRAINED, AS IT

NEITHER HOSTED POLITICAL EVENTS NOR PROVIDED RESOURCES FOR

POLITICAL ACTIVITIES (SUCH AS PROVIDING MEETING SPACES) IN

ORDER TO AVOID PROVOKING THE GOC.

6. (C) POLOFFS PRESSED LINGUA WHETHER THE HOLY SEE HAD

GIVEN ANY THOUGHT TO PREPARING FOR AN EVENTUAL DEMOCRATIC

TRANSITION IN CUBA, AND HE OFFERED HIS VIEW THAT SUCH A

TRANSITION, WHILE INEVITABLE, COULD BE MANY YEARS AWAY, EVEN

YEARS AFTER FIDEL CASTRO LEAVES THE SCENE. HE NOTED THAT

MANY OF HIS CUBAN INTERLOCUTORS WERE REVOLUTIONARY "TRUE

BELIEVERS."

-------------------------------------

ENDING EMBARGO WOULD STIMULATE REFORM

-------------------------------------

7. (C) TURNING TO THE EMBARGO, LINGUA MAINTAINED THAT EVEN

THOUGH THE EMBARGO WAS NOT RESPONSIBLE FOR CUBA\'S DIRE

ECONOMIC STRAITS, WERE IT TO BE LIFTED THE GOC WOULD HAVE NO

MORE EXCUSES FOR ITS POOR PERFORMANCE; ECONOMIC AND POLITICAL

REFORMS WOULD NECESSARILY FOLLOW. REFLECTING VIEWS WE HAVE

HEARD AT CARITAS, LINGUA BELIEVED PHASING OUT THE EMBARGO

WOULD BE "JUST" AS WELL AS "SMART," AS A REFORM PROCESS WOULD

HELP AVOID A SUDDEN POLARIZATION OF CUBAN SOCIETY THAT COULD

LEAD TO VIOLENCE DURING A POST-CASTRO TRANSITION.

-------

COMMENT

-------

7. (C) THE HOLY SEE\'S CONTINUED CAUTION ON ITS APPROACH TO

CUBA AND ITS UNWILLINGNESS TO CONSIDER PLANNING FOR A

POST-REVOLUTIONARY CUBA ARE CHARACTERISTIC OF THE

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 VATICA 05471 02 OF 02 080950Z

TRADITIONALLY CAUTIOUS VATICAN DIPLOMATIC CORPS. IT ALSO

REFLECTS A GROWING RESIGNATION THAT THE POPE\'S HISTORIC 1998

HISTORIC VISIT TO CUBA HAS CHANGED LITTLE. IN THE PAST WE

HAVE HEARD DISAPPOINTMENT FROM THE VATICAN AT LACK OF

PROGRESS IN RELIGIOUS FREEDOM AFTER THE POPE\'S HISTORIC 1998

VISIT TO CUBA, WHEREAS NOW, THEY APPEAR WILLING TO TAKE

HALF-A-LOAF AND WAIT FOR POLITICAL CONDITIONS TO IMPROVE

BEFORE PRESSING HARD FOR GREATER FREEDOMS. AS A RESULT, WE

EXPECT THE HOLY SEE (IF NOT ALL OF ITS BISHOPS) WILL REMAIN

DISCRETE IN DEALINGS WITH CUBA\'S DISSIDENT COMMUNITY, WHILE

PATIENTLY SEEKING TO MAINTAIN AND EXPAND SPACE FOR RELIGIOUS

ACTIVITIES. WE WILL CONTINUE TO ENCOURAGE THE HOLY SEE TO

ENGAGE MORE DIRECTLY WITH DISSIDENTS AND RAISE ITS VOICE AS

THE POPE DID IN 1998 FOR BROADER HUMAN RIGHTS IN CUBA.
