SUBJECT: ITALIAN PARLIAMENT APPROVES 2002 BUDGET

REF: (A) ROME 6001; (B) MILAN 769; (C) MILAN 770;

(D) ROME 6613, (E) ROME 5358

1. SUMMARY: THE SENATE APPROVED ITALY\'S 2002 BUDGET ON

DECEMBER 22, WHICH HAD BEEN PREVIOUSLY PASSED BY THE

CHAMBER OF DEPUTIES. THE E17 BILLION PACKAGE, EQUAL TO

1.3 PERCENT OF GDP, PROPOSES TO REDUCE THE DEFICIT/GDP

RATIO TO 0.5 PERCENT, IN LINE WITH THE STABILITY PACT.

THE 2002 BUDGET GRANTS NEGOTIATING AUTHORITY FOR THE

EXECUTIVE BRANCH TO DRAFT TAX AND PENSION REFORM, AND

REFORM OF BANK FOUNDATIONS (REF D). IT ALSO RAISES

MONTHLY MINIMUM PENSIONS (FOR THOSE OVER 70) TO ONE

MILLION LIRE ($500); RAISES PERSONAL INCOME TAX

DEDUCTIONS FOR CHILDREN, AND FOR HOUSE REMODELING UP TO

END-2002; ABOLISHES SOME MINOR TAXES; PROVIDES FUNDS FOR

RENEWAL OF CERTAIN LABOR CONTRACTS, AND FOR EDUCATION;

AUTHORIZES PRIVATIZATION OF CERTAIN STATE AGENCIES AND

MUNICIPAL UTILITIES; AND PARDONS BUILDERS OF ILLEGAL

BUILDINGS ON STATE PROPERTY. END-SUMMARY

2002 BUDGET

-----------

2. BEATING THE DECEMBER 31 DEADLINE, THE SENATE APPROVED

ON DECEMBER 22 ITALY\'S 2002 BUDGET. THE 2002 BUDGET

TOTALS E17 BILLION (1.3 PERCENT OF GDP). THIS INCLUDES

AN E7.9 BILLION (0.6 PERCENT OF GDP) ECONOMIC STIMULUS

PACKAGE AND AN E9.1 BILLION REDUCTION OF THE DEFICIT/GDP

RATIO FROM 1.7 TO 0.5 PERCENT AT THE END OF 2002. THE

3. THE BUDGET INCLUDES E4.6 BILLION IN SPENDING CUTS,

SALE OF GOI REAL ESTATE (E7.7 BILLION) AND FROM LOTTERY

AND GAME PROCEEDS (E516.5 MILLION).

4. MAIN ELEMENTS OF THE 2002 BUDGET

------------------------------------

A. MINIMUM PENSIONS: MINIMUM MONTHLY PENSIONS WILL BE

RAISED TO E516 FOR THE 2.2 MILLION PENSIONERS WHO ARE

OLDER THAN 70 YEARS AND HAVE AN OVERALL INCOME OF NO

MORE THAN E6,700, EXCLUDING THEIR HOUSE.

B. PERSONAL INCOME TAX DEDUCTIONS: TAX DEDUCTIONS FOR

CHILDREN WILL INCREASE PROGRESSIVELY WITH THE INCOME.

C. HOME REMODELING: TAX DEDUCTIONS FOR HOUSE REMODELING

WILL BE EXTENDED TO DECEMBER 31, 2002.

D. ABOLITION OF SOME MINOR TAXES.

E. LABOR CONTRACTS, EDUCATION, AND KINDERGATENS: E1.3

BILLION IS INCLUDED FOR LABOR CONTRACT RENEWALS IN THE

COMMENT

-------

5. BERLUSCONI\'S ECONOMIC PROGRAM WAS ADVANCED BY THE

ENACTMENT OF THE 2002 BUDGET. DESPITE THE UNCERTAIN

ECONOMIC OUTLOOK, THE GOI WAS ABLE TO GAIN APPROVAL OF A

STIMULUS PACKAGE AND AUTHORIZATION TO DRAFT REFORMS OF

THE TAX AND PENSION SYSTEMS. ONLY THE REFORM OF THE

BANKING FOUNDATIONS (REF D) CREATED SOME CONTROVERSY,

TRIGGERING TOUGH OPPOSITION FROM THE FOUNDATIONS

THEMSELVES AND CERTAIN CENTER-LEFT POLITICIANS. THIS AND

THE PARDON FOR ILLEGAL BUILDERS ON STATE PROPERTY ARE

EXAMPLES OF HOW THE BUDGET LAW CAN BE USED TO GAIN QUICK

PASSAGE OF CONTROVERSIAL LAWS WITHOUT A SERIOUS DEBATE IN

PARLIAMENT. THE PARDON FOR ILLEGAL BUILDERS IS A

TRADITIONAL TYPE OF POLITICAL BENEFIT FOR VOTERS IN

SOUTHERN ITALY, WHERE SUCH ILLEGAL CONSTRUCTION IS

COMMON. BERLUSCONI\'S COALITION, AND THE CDU/CCD IN

PARTICULAR, IS VERY STRONG IN SOUTHERN ITALY. END COMMENT
