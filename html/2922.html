SUBJECT: ISRAELI EMBASSY READOUT OF MAY 10

PEREZ/TAURAN/SODANO MEETING

CLASSIFIED BY: AMBASSADOR JIM NICHOLSON FOR REASONS 1.5 (A) AND (D).

1. (S) ZWI TAL(PROTECT), DCM OF THE ISRAELI EMBASSY TO THE

HOLY SEE, GAVE DCM HIS EMBASSY\'S PERSPECTIVE OF THE MAY 10

MEETING AMONG ISRAELI FOREIGN MINISTER PERES, VATICAN FOREIGN

MINISTER-EQUIVALENT TAURAN, AND VATICAN PRIME

MINISTER-EQUIVALENT SODANO. TAL\'S VIEWS ARE BASED ON A

READ-OUT OF THE MEETING HE RECEIVED FROM HIS AMBASSADOR,

YOSEF LAMDAN, WHO ATTENDED THE MEETING.

2. (S) ACCORDING TO TAL, MEETING WAS MORE OF A BRIEFING ON

THE PART OF PERES, THAN AN ACTUAL EXCHANGE OF VIEWS. THE ONE

ISSUE OF ACTUAL DISCUSSION WAS THE ROLE OF ARAFAT AS A

PARTNER IN THE PEACE NEGOTIATIONS. PERES SAID THAT ARAFAT

STILL HAD TO PROVE HE IS A SERIOUS PARTNER AND COULD CONTROL

THE VIOLENCE; PERES SAID THAT THE GOI WAS WILLING TO GIVE HIM

A CHANCE. SODANO INSISTED, HOWEVER, THAT ARAFAT IS THE

LEGITIMATE REPRESENTATIVE OF THE PALESTINIAN PEOPLE, AND AS

SUCH MUST BE THE INDIVIDUAL WITH WHOM THE ISRAELIS ENGAGE.

3.(S) THE ISRAELI SIDE WAS MOST SURPRISED THAT THE VATICAN

RAISED THE SITUATION IN BETHLEHEM ONLY IN PASSING. TAL

REPORTED THAT SODANO "EXPRESSED SATISFACTION" THAT THE

SITUATION HAS BEEN RESOLVED, BUT "TRUE TO FORM" OFFERED NO

WORDS OF THANKS TO THE ISRAELIS. SODANO "REHASHED" THE

VATICAN POSITION ON "TWO PEOPLES/TWO STATES", AS WELL AS THE

NEED FOR DISCUSSIONS ON JERUSALEM. TAL SAID, "TO BE FAIR"

NEITHER SODANO NOR TAURAN EXPRESSED SERIOUS ANNOYANCE WITH

THE ISRAELI MILITARY ACTION, AND ALLOWED PERES TO EXPLAIN HOW

THE ACTION HAS SUCCEEDED IN DISMANTLING MUCH OF THE TERRORIST

INFRASTRUCTURE.

4.(S) TAL SAID THAT IN THE RIDE BACK FROM THE MEETING, PERES

TOLD LAMDAN THAT ISRAELI/VATICAN RELATIONS ARE "NOT AS BAD"

AS EVERYONE BELIEVES.

5.(S) POST WILL REPORT ON VATICAN\'S PERCEPTION OF MEETING

SEPTEL. NICHOLSON
