SUMMARY

-------

1. AT ITS THIRD REGULAR SESSION (OCTOBER 21-25), THE WORLD

FOOD PROGRAM EXECUTIVE BOARD APPROVED A PROTRACTED RELIEF

AND RECOVERY OPERATION (PRRO) FOR CENTRAL AMERICA - THREE

YEARS (MARCH 2003-FEBRUARY 2006), VALUED AT USD 56.62

MILLION, TO ASSIST 690,000 BENEFICIARIES/ANNUM. SEVERE CORN

CROP LOSSES IN THE FIRST PLANTING CYCLE OF THIS YEAR WILL

LIKELY FURTHER AGGRAVATE AN ALREADY DIFFICULT SITUATION IN A

CHRONICALLY FOOD INSECURE REGION. USMISSION PLANS TO WORK

WITH WFP HEADQUARTERS IN ITS EFFORTS TO SECURE GOVERNMENT OF

MEXICO FUNDING SUPPORT FOR THIS PRRO. END SUMMARY.

----------

BACKGROUND

----------

2. THE WORSENING ECONOMIC SITUATION, PRECIPITATED BY A

REPORTED 70 PERCENT DROP IN COFFEE PRICES AFFECTING MILLIONS

OF COFFEE GROWERS AND THEIR FAMILIES, COUPLED WITH

RECURRING NATURAL DISASTERS HAVE CONTINUED TO TRIGGER

EXTREME POVERTY AND FOOD INSECURITY AMONG RURAL AND URBAN

POPULATIONS IN THE FOUR COUNTRIES (GUATEMALA, HONDURAS, EL

SALVADOR AND NICARAGUA). IN 2000, DROUGHTS, EARTHQUAKES,

FLOODS AND TROPICAL STORMS NEGATIVELY IMPACTED FOOD

PRODUCTION AND ECONOMIC INFRASTRUCTURE, AFFECTING SOME

2.4 MILLION PEOPLE. PEOPLE ARE STILL FEELING THE EFFECTS OF

THESE SHOCKS IN 2002, WHILE SIMULTANEOUSLY CONFRONTING A NEW

ROUND OF DROUGHT AND A CONTINUING CRISIS IN THE COFFEE

SECTOR. IN ADDITION, PREDICTIONS ARE THAT EL NINO WILL AGAIN

CAUSE CLIMATIC DISTURBANCES IN 2003.

3. LOCALIZED NUTRITIONAL SURVEYS CONFIRM THAT 5 YEARS OF

DROUGHT AND ECONOMIC CRISIS IS CAUSING DETERIORATION IN

MALNUTRITION AMONG CHILDREN AGED UNDER FIVE IN CENTRAL

AMERICA. ACCORDING TO THE UN\'S FOOD AND AGRICULTURAL

ORGANIZATION (FAO), THERE ARE OVER 7.1 MILLION

UNDERNOURISHED CENTRAL AMERICANS (1998/2000) SHOWING AN

INCREASE OF 3 PERCENT SINCE 1990/1992. IN GUATEMALA, HUNGER

INCREASED FROM 14 PERCENT TO 25 PERCENT OF THE POPULATION,

WITH 69 PERCENT OF CHILDREN CHRONICALLY MALNOURISHED. IN

NICARAGUA, 33 PERCENT OF THE POPULATION IS AFFECTED BY FOOD

SHORTAGES AND MALNUTRITION. IN ONE DEPARTMENT ALONE,

MATAGALPA, EMERGENCY FOOD HAD TO BE PROVIDED TO 7,500

CHILDREN SUFFERING FROM EXTREME HUNGER.

--------------------------------------------- -------

WFP EXECUTIVE BOARD APPROVAL OF CENTRAL AMERICA PRRO

--------------------------------------------- -------

4. THE BOARD APPROVED THIS PRRO FOR THREE YEARS (MARCH 2003-

FEBRUARY 2006), VALUED AT USD 56.62 MILLION, TO ASSIST

690,000 BENEFICIARIES/ANNUM. THE BOARD AGREED THAT THIS

PROGRAMMING INSTRUMENT WILL PERMIT WFP TO RESPOND

EFFICIENTLY AND EFFECTIVELY TO SHOCKS, IN ORDER TO PREVENT

ACUTE MALNUTRITION AMONG FOOD-INSECURE CHILDREN AND FAMILIES

EXPOSED TO RECURRING CALAMITIES AND ASSIST IN REBUILDING AND

BUFFERING LIVELIHOODS ERODED BY EXPOSURE TO DISASTERS.

COMMENT

-------

6. USMISSION SEES A CONTINUING IMPORTANT ROLE FOR WFP AND

ITS UN SISTER AGENCIES IN RESPONDING TO THE FOOD CRISIS IN

CENTRAL AMERICA. SEVERE CORN CROP LOSSES IN THE FIRST

PLANTING CYCLE OF THIS YEAR WILL LIKELY FURTHER AGGRAVATE AN

ALREADY DIFFICULT SITUATION IN A CHRONICALLY FOOD INSECURE

REGION. FINALLY, USMISSION PLANS TO WORK WITH WFP
