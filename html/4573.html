1. ITALY UNDERSTANDS U.S. CONCERNS ABOUT CHR-59\'S

EFFECTIVENESS UNDER A LIBYAN CHAIR, BUT IS CONCERNED THAT

CONTESTING A REGIONAL GROUP\'S CONSENSUS CHOICE FOR THE CHAIR

WILL SET A BAD PRECEDENT FOR THE COMMISSION\'S FUTURE

OPERATION, MFA HUMAN RIGHTS OFFICE DIRECTOR CALVETTA TOLD US

DECEMBER 2. CALVETTA ARGUED THAT IT WAS "DANGEROUS" TO

REVISIT THE CHAIRMANSHIP PROCEDURES IN MIDSTREAM; THE

POTENTIAL BACKLASH AGAINST THE WESTERN GROUP\'S CANDIDATE NEXT

YEAR COULD UNDERMINE THE EFFECTIVENESS OF CHR-60 IN 2004. HE

WONDERED WHETHER THE U.S. AND OTHERS OPPOSED TO LIBYA\'S

CHAIRMANSHIP COULD PURSUE A "DECLARATION" AT THE JANUARY

COMMISSION MEETING EXPRESSING THEIR DISPLEASURE WITHOUT

FORCING A VOTE.

2. EU EXPERTS WOULD EXPLORE POSSIBLE ALTERNATIVES AT THEIR

DECEMBER 4 SESSION. CALVETTA REMINDED US OF ITALY\'S EFFORTS

ENCOURAGING LIBYA TO REJOIN THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY,

POINTING TO PM BERLUSCONI\'S "SUCCESSFUL" OCTOBER 28 VISIT TO

TRIPOLI AS ANOTHER USEFUL WAYPOINT. NONETHELESS, ITALY WOULD

NOT OPPOSE AN ALTERNATIVE UNDER WHICH THE AFRICAN GROUP WOULD

RETAIN THE CHAIRMANSHIP FOR CHR-59.

3. LOOKING AHEAD, CALVETTA SUGGESTED THAT PERHAPS THE BEST

LONG-TERM SOLUTION WOULD BE TO ESTABLISH MINIMUM CRITERIA FOR

CHR CHAIRMANSHIP. FOR EXAMPLE, ANY STATE FOR WHICH A UN

SPECIAL RAPPORTEUR HAD BEEN APPOINTED WOULD BE INELIGIBLE FOR

CONSIDERATION BY ITS REGIONAL GROUP.

4. MFA LIBYA DESK OFFICER SEPARATELY AGREED THAT ITALY WANTED

A WELL-RUN COMMISSION, BUT SUGGESTED THE CHAIRMANSHIP MIGHT

BE AN APPROPRIATE VEHICLE TO TEST LIBYA\'S COMMITMENT TO FULL

REINTEGRATION INTO THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY.

5. COMMENT: GIVEN ITS EMPHASIS ON ENGAGING LIBYA, ITS

LONG-STANDING COMMERCIAL TIES AND PM BERLUSCONI\'S RECENT

VISIT TO TRIPOLI, ITALY WILL NOT LEAD EU EFFORTS TO BLOCK

LIBYAN CHAIRMANSHIP -- BUT THEY WILL JOIN CONSENSUS. AFTER

STEPPING ASIDE TO CLEAR ROOM FOR U.S. MEMBERSHIP IN CHR-59,

THE MFA HAS SET ITS SIGHTS ON THE 2004 SESSION; IT CLEARLY IS

CONCERNED ABOUT INTRA-GROUP FIREWORKS THAT COULD SINGE

WESTERN GROUP CANDIDATES NEXT YEAR.
