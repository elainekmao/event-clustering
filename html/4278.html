2. THE 56TH SESSION OF THE UNIDROIT FINANCE COMMITTEE

MET ON OCTOBER 22, 2002. AS REPORTED IN REFTEL E, THE

55TH SESSION OF THE COMMITTEE HAD BEEN UNABLE TO APPROVE

A BUDGET DUE TO MEMBER CONCERNS (JAPAN, ITALY, FRANCE,

SWITZERLAND) ABOUT A LAST-MINUTE INCREASE IN THE BUDGET

PROPOSAL (TO ALLOW UNIDROIT TO CARRY OUT ITS FUNCTIONS AS

DEPOSITARY FOR THE RECENTLY CONCLUDED CAPETOWN CONVENTION

ON INTERNATIONAL INTERESTS IN MOBILE EQUIPMENT) AND THE

ANNOUNCEMENT OF THE GOVERNMENT OF ITALY THAT IT WAS

REDUCING ITS CONTRIBUTION.

3. AS A RESULT, THE SECRETARIAT WAS OBLIGED TO WITHDRAW

THE PROPOSAL AND REDRAFT THE BUDGET TO TAKE INTO ACCOUNT

THE LOWER ITALIAN CONTRIBUTION. AT EURO 1,802,550.00

(FOR ORDINARY EXPENDITURES) THE RE-DRAFTED BUDGET

PRESENTED TO THE 56TH SESSION CAME IN SLIGHTLY BELOW THE

ORIGINAL PROPOSED BUDGET LEVEL (REFTEL A) AND REPRESENTED

A NOMINAL INCREASE OF ONLY 19,673 EUROS. (NONETHELESS,

THE U.S. CONTRIBUTION REMAINS THE SAME (EURO 110,000) AS

IN THE ORIGINAL BUDGET LEVEL.) WHILE FUNDING FOR

SCHOLARSHIPS FOR DEVELOPING COUNTRY RESEARCHERS AND FOR

CONSULTANTS WAS REDUCED, THE RE-DRAFTED BUDGET DOES NOT

PROVIDE THE MEANS FOR UNIDROIT TO CARRY OUT THE FUNCTIONS

OF DEPOSITARY FOR THE CAPETOWN CONVENTION. IN PRESENTING

THE RE-DRAFTED BUDGET UNIDROIT SECRETARY GENERAL HERBERT

KRONKE CALLED THE PROPOSAL ONE "BORN OUT OF DESPAIR"

WHICH COULD BE TOLERATED FOR ONE YEAR, BUT WHICH, AFTER

THAT, WOULD BE "UNSUSTAINABLE."

4. THE FINANCE COMMITTEE APPROVED THE OVERALL BUDGET

LEVEL. USDEL STATED THAT, WHILE THE U.S. WOULD NOT BLOCK

CONSENSUS, THE U.S. POSITION WAS AND REMAINED ZERO

NOMINAL GROWTH.

5. IN DISCUSSION OF THE RE-DRAFTED BUDGET, MEXICO,

FRANCE, GERMANY, SPAIN, SWITZERLAND AND AUSTRIA EXPRESSED

DIFFICULTIES WITH THE SECRETARIAT\'S PROPOSAL TO REDUCE

"LEGAL ASSISTANCE TO DEVELOPING COUNTRIES" (PRIMARILY

SCHOLARSHIPS FOR RESEARCH). USDEL STATED THAT THIS WAS

AN APPROPRIATE LINE ITEM FROM WHICH TO SEEK SAVINGS, AND

ENCOURAGED COUNTRIES TO CONSIDER VOLUNTARY CONTRIBUTIONS

FOR SUCH SCHOLARSHIPS INSTEAD. AS A RESULT OF THESE

CONCERNS THE SECRETARIAT WILL JUGGLE SOME OF THE INTERNAL

LINE ITEMS WITH A VIEW TO PROVIDING MORE FUNDING FOR

SCHOLARSHIPS WHILE STAYING WITHIN THE APPROVED BUDGET

ENVELOPE. THE REVISED PROPOSAL WILL BE SUBMITTED TO THE

DECEMBER 6 GENERAL ASSEMBLY.

6. CONCERNING THE DEPOSITARY FUNCTIONS, JAPAN AND

AUSTRIA ALSO EXPRESSED SUPPORT FOR UNIDROIT CARRYING OUT

THESE FUNCTIONS, BUT STATED THAT THE PROPOSAL HAD COME IN

TOO LATE FOR CONSIDERATIONS IN CAPITALS. THESE COUNTRIES

SAID THAT THE NEED TO SUPPORT THE DEPOSITARY FUNCTION

WOULD BE GIVEN MORE SYMPATHETIC CONSIDERATION IN NEXT

YEAR\'S BUDGET PROPOSAL.
