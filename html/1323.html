SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE LIKELY TO VOTE IN FAVOR OF CUBA\'S UN

RESOLUTION AGAINST THE EMBARGO

REFTEL: SECSTATE 204623

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS: 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) CHARGE DELIVERED REFTEL DEMARCHE ON OCTOBER 24 TO

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 05969 241346Z

SENIOR MFA ADMINISTRATIVE OFFICER AND ACTING AMERICA\'S

DESK OFFICER ARTHUR CHINGONO IN THE ABSENCE OF MORE

SENIOR MINISTRY OFFICIALS. DRAWING ON REFTEL TALKING

POINTS, CHARGE URGED ZIMBABWE TO OPPOSE, OR AT A

MINIMUM, ABSTAIN ON CUBA\'S UNGA RESOLUTION CONDEMNING

THE U.S. BILATERAL EMBARGO ON CUBA. CHINGONO RESPONDED

THAT ZIMBABWE VOTED FOR THE SAME RESOLUTION LAST YEAR IN

HOPE OF MAINTAINING ITS GOOD RELATIONS WITH CUBA. EVEN

IF ZIMBABWE VOTES IN FAVOR AGAIN THIS YEAR, ZIMBABWE

HOPES THAT IT CAN USE ITS INFLUENCE TO CONVINCE CUBA TO

RESUME A DIALOGUE WITH THE UNITED STATES, HE STATED.

THE ZIMBABWEAN DIPLOMAT REPORTED THAT HIS GOVERNMENT HAD

ASKED CUBA FOR ITS ASSESSMENT OF ITS U.S. RELATIONSHIP,

AND CUBA RESPONDED THAT IT DETECTS NO MEANINGFUL CHANGE.

2. (C) CHARGE RESPONDED THAT IT IS VERY DIFFICULT TO

IMPROVE RELATIONS WHEN CASTRO DOES NOT RECIPROCATE U.S.

GESTURES, AND WORSE, INSULTS THE U.S. BY DERIDING ITS

HUMANITARIAN CONCESSIONS TO CUBA. CHARGE ASKED CHINGONO

HOW MUCH AUTHORITY ZIMBABWE\'S PERMREP IN NEW YORK HAD IN

VOTING ON THESE TYPES OF RESOLUTIONS. DEPENDING ON THE

ISSUE, HE HAS A FAIR AMOUNT OF DISCRETION, CHINGONO

REPLIED. ZIMBABWE WANTS TO AVOID MAKING THESE UNGA

RESOLUTIONS A RITUAL, AND INTENDS TO RE-EVALUATE THE

SITUATION IN CUBA EVERY YEAR, BUT OFTEN DOES NOT HAVE

THE PERTINENT INFORMATION TO MAKE AN INFORMED VOTE.

ZIMBABWE APPRECIATES ANY INFORMATION THE U.S. CAN

PROVIDE, CHINGONO CONCLUDED.

3. (C) COMMENT: CHINGONO PUT A GOOD, DIPLOMATIC FACE

ON WHAT IN ALL LIKELIHOOD WILL BE A ZIMBABWE VOTE IN

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 05969 241346Z

FAVOR OF CUBA\'S RESOLUTION. GIVEN PRESIDENT MUGABE\'S

DEMONSTRATED LACK OF CONCERN FOR HUMAN RIGHTS IN

GENERAL, HUMAN RIGHTS IN CUBA HAS NEVER BEEN A FEATURE

OF ZIMBABWEAN FOREIGN POLICY, AND CHINGONO EFFECTIVELY

CONFIRMED THIS. THERE ARE TOO MANY STRONG TIES, AND

PARALLELS, BETWEEN ZIMBABWE AND CUBA FOR ZIMBABWE TO

ABSTAIN OR VOTE AGAINST ITS OLDEST ALLY IN THE AMERICAS.

THERE ARE CURRENTLY ABOUT 100 LOW-COST DOCTORS IN

ZIMBABWE FROM CUBA, AND PRESIDENT MUGABE LIKELY VIEWS

CASTRO AS A FELLOW-IN-ARMS BESIEGED BY A HOSTILE WEST.

MUGABE ALSO LIKELY SEES THIS VOTE AS A COST-FREE CHANCE

TO POKE THE U.S. IN THE EYE OVER WHAT HE PERCEIVED WAS

AN ATTEMPT TO IMPOSE SANCTIONS ON ZIMBABWE WITH THE

ZIMBABWE DEMOCRACY ACT. END COMMENT.

IRVING
