SUMMARY

-------

1. THE VATICAN FOREIGN MINISTRY ISSUED A NOTE VERBALE

OCTOBER 26 TO ALL ACCREDITED MISSIONS OUTLINING THE HOLY

SEE\'S STANDARD POSITION AHEAD OF THE WTO DOHA MINISTERIAL

THAT DEVELOPED NATIONS SHOULD BE MORE OPEN TO IMPORTS FROM

THE DEVELOPING WORLD, WHILE WARNING DEVELOPING COUNTRIES

AGAINST FOLLOWING A "CRUDE PROTECTIONIST PATH." THE NOTE

ALSO INCLUDED A COPY OF ITS JULY 2001 TRIPS COUNCIL NON-

PAPER ENTITLED "INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY AND ACCESS TO BASIC

MEDICINES" WHICH PROPOSED GREATER ACCESS TO PHARMACEUTICALS

IN THE POOREST COUNTRIES. THIS RELEASE FOLLOWED AMBASSADOR\'S

MEETING (REFTEL) IN WHICH POST AND U.S. PHARMACEUTICAL

INDUSTRY REPS PRESSED VATICAN CONTACTS FOR GREATER

UNDERSTANDING OF THE NEED FOR IPR PROTECTION.

------------------

FAIR TRADE FOR ALL

------------------

2. THE VATICAN\'S PRE-DOHA DOCUMENT, ENTITLED "DEVELOPMENT

DIMENSIONS OF THE WORLD TRADE ORGANIZATION," CALLED FOR

"GESTURES OF FLEXIBILITY AND SOLIDARITY" AS A STEP TOWARDS A

MORE INCLUSIVE VISION OF WORLD TRADE. THE VATICAN SEES "THE

ENHANCED DEVELOPMENT OF POORER COUNTRIES" AS A PLUS FOR

GLOBAL ECONOMIC PROGRESS AND INTERNATIONAL PEACE AND

SECURITY. THE VATICAN BELIEVES THE LEAST DEVELOPED NATIONS

HAVE ONLY "A MARGINAL AND DIMINISHING SHARE" IN WORLD TRADE.

FAIR TRADE RELATIONS, ACCORDING TO THE VATICAN, ARE AN

ESSENTIAL COMPONENT OF ECONOMIC AND HUMAN DEVELOPMENT. THE

VATICAN WARNS THAT ECONOMICS AND TRADE MUST BE PURSUED

WITHIN THE BROADER CONTEXT OF HUMAN DEVELOPMENT, THE

PROMOTION OF HUMAN RIGHTS AND THE ELIMINATION OF POVERTY.

3. THE VATICAN CALLS FOR THE ELIMINATION OF "TRADE AND

PRODUCTION DISTORTING EXPORT SUBSIDIES." IT SEEKS MARKET

ACCESS FOR PRODUCTS IN WHICH THE POOREST COUNTRIES HAVE

COMPARABLE ADVANTAGE. HOWEVER, THE VATICAN WARNED DEVELOPING

COUNTRIES AGAINST FOLLOWING A "CRUDE PROTECTIONIST PATH,"

INSTEAD THESE COUNTRIES SHOULD FOLLOW A "SOLID AND VIABLE

PATH TO FREE TRADE THAT PERMITS THEM TO ENTER INTO AN

EQUITABLE DIALOGUE WITH INTERNATIONAL MARKETS." THE ECONOMIC

AND NEGOTIATING POWER DISADVANTAGES OF LESS INDUSTRIALIZED

ECONOMIES AND COMMODITY PRODUCER ECONOMIES CAN BE OVERCOME

BY FULLY IMPLEMENTING THE EXISTING WTO SPECIAL AND

DIFFERENTIAL TREATMENT PRINCIPLE. THE VATICAN URGES THAT

DEVELOPING NATIONS RECEIVE TECHNICAL AND FINANCIAL

ASSISTANCE TO MEET EXISTING WTO OBLIGATIONS. ASSISTANCE IS

ALSO ASKED TO BROADEN AND DIVERSITY THEIR PRODUCTION AND

EXPORT BASE AND TRADE PROMOTION EFFORTS.

4. THE VATICAN LAMENTS THE FACT THAT DEVELOPING COUNTRIES

ARE FORCED TO DIVERT VITAL RESOURCES TO IMPLEMENTING NEW

INTERNATIONAL DUTIES, ESPECIALLY INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

PROTECTION. THE VATICAN CALLS FOR A REVIEW OF TRIPS TO

BALANCE THE INTERESTS OF DEVELOPED AND DEVELOPING COUNTRIES,

ESPECIALLY WHEN "THE LIFE OF PEOPLE COULD BE A STAKE." THE

VATICAN ASKS THAT NO TRADE INTEREST HINDER THE MOST

ESSENTIAL ASPECTS OF THE RIGHT TO LIFE.

5. THE VATICAN URGES GREATER TRANSPARENCY FOR THE WTO IN

ALL ASPECTS OF DECISION MAKING. DEVELOPING NATIONS NEED TO

"OWN" THE DECISIONS THAT IMPACT DIRECTLY ON THEIR ECONOMIES

AND THEIR PARTICIPATION IN THE GLOBAL MARKET. THE VATICAN

ASKS THAT THE NEGOTIATING AND REPRESENTATIONAL DIFFICULTIES

OF THESE NATIONS BE CONTINUALLY ADDRESSED. IT ALSO WARNED

THAT THE WTO COULD NOT RESOLVE ALL THE DEVELOPMENT

CHALLENGES OF TODAY\'S WORLD AND SUGGESTED THAT ATTEMPTS TO

OVER-STRETCH ITS MANDATE SHOULD BE RESISTED.

-----------------------------------------

SOCIAL MORTGAGE ON PHARMACEUTICAL PROFITS

-----------------------------------------

6. THE TRIPS NON-PAPER SUGGESTS ACCESSIBLE PRICE IS A

DETERMINANT FACTOR IN ACCESS TO MEDICINES AND THAT

FUNDAMENTAL SOCIAL FACTORS MUST BE BALANCED AGAINST HIGH

PRICING TO ATTRACT INVESTORS AND MAINTAIN RESEARCH AND

DEVELOPMENT. THE VATICAN URGES AN "INNOVATIVE PRICING

SYSTEM" TO PRESERVE R AND D INCENTIVES, SUGGESTING LUXURY

AND NON-ESSENTIAL PHARMACEUTICAL PRODUCTS (COSMETICS) SHARE

A GREATER BURDEN FOR R AND D OF ESSENTIAL MEDICINES.

ACCORDING TO THE VATICAN, "A BROAD-BASED COMMITMENT OF

SOLIDARITY IS THE BEST WAY TO PREVENT POOR COUNTRIES FROM

FALLING INTO THE TEMPTATION OF WEAKENING THE INTELLECTUAL

PROPERTY RIGHTS FRAMEWORK."

7. THE VATICAN RECOGNIZES THAT THE COUNCIL FOR TRIPS

CANNOT RESOLVE THE ACCESSIBILITY TO BASIC MEDICINES PROBLEM.

IT URGES THE COUNCIL TO MAKE A FUNDAMENTAL CONTRIBUTION THE

IMPLEMENTATION OF TRIPS RULES THAT IS CONSISTENT WITH A

UNIFIED VISION OF LAW, BASED ON RESPECT FOR HUMAN RIGHTS,

AND APPLYING THOSE ARTICLES OF THE WTO TREATY THAT CALL FOR

A PRO-DEVELOPMENT INTERPRETATION OF THE WHOLE LEGAL BODY.

THE VATICAN SUGGESTS "THIS WOULD CONTRIBUTE TO A BROAD AND

NOT RESTRICTIVE INTERPRETATION OF ARTICLES 30 AND 31, WHICH

WOULD ALLOW THAT LICENSING FEES MAY BE FIXED IN ACCORDANCE

WITH THE REAL PURCHASING CAPACITY OF THE POOREST COUNTRY,

BALANCED WITH A SYSTEM THAT BLOCKS THE RE-EXPORT OF THE

LICENSED PRODUCTS TO THE ORIGINAL MARKETS."

8. COMMENT: IN NONE OF THIS DOES THE VATICAN APPEAR TO

REFLECT AN UNDERSTANDING OF THE PURELY COMMERCIAL INTEREST

OF THOSE "DEVELOPING" COUNTRIES WHICH HAVE A SUBSTANTIAL

PHARMACEUTICAL INDUSTRY (EG. INDIA, BRAZIL). WE WILL FOLLOW

UP ON THIS THEME WITH THE PONTIFICAL COUNCIL FOR HEALTH CARE

ISSUES. POST HAS FAXED COPIES OF THE VATICAN DOCUMENTS TO

EUR/WE. END COMMENT. NICHOLSON
