1. (C) SUMMARY: ON MARCH 14, ZIMBABWE FOREIGN MINISTER

STANISLAUS MUDENGE BRIEFED ZIMBABWE\'S DIPLOMATIC CORPS

AND THE PRESS ON THE FUTURE OF THE PROPOSED AFRICAN

UNION, CHANGES MADE TO SADC STRUCTURES IN WINDHOEK ON

MARCH 9, AND THE STATUS OF LAND REFORM IN ZIMBABWE.

MUDENGE ALSO TOOK THE OPPORTUNITY TO TAKE ANOTHER JAB AT

THE UK. THE FOREIGN MINISTER\'S STATEMENTS ON THE DRC

WERE REPORTED IN REF A. END SUMMARY.

AFRICAN UNION CLOSER TO FRUITION

--------------------------------

2. (C) MUDENGE BRIEFED HEADS OF MISSION ON THE EARLY

MARCH OAU EXTRAORDINARY SUMMIT HELD IN SIRTE, LIBYA.

MUCH OF THE SUMMIT WAS FOCUSED ON THE CREATION OF AN

AFRICAN UNION, WHICH WILL BE ALONG THE LINES OF THE

EUROPEAN UNION, THE MINISTER REPORTED. NEW STRUCTURES,

INCLUDING AN EXECUTIVE COUNCIL, A PAN-AFRICAN

PARLIAMENT, A HIGH COURT, AN AFRICAN CENTRAL BANK, AND A

VARIETY OF TECHNICAL COMMITTEES, WERE ALL AGREED UPON.

ALL 53 COUNTRIES ON THE AFRICAN CONTINENT HAVE SIGNED

THE ACT THAT CONSTITUTES THE UNION; 32 HAVE FORMALLY

RATIFIED THE ACT, AND ONLY FOUR MORE ARE NEEDED TO MAKE

THE UNION A REALITY, MUDENGE SAID. (NOTE: LIBYAN

AMBASSADOR MAHMOUD YOUSEF AZZABI LATER INTERJECTED TO

SAY THAT TWO MORE COUNTRIES HAD JUST RATIFIED THE ACT,

BRINGING THE TOTAL TO 34. END NOTE.)

3. (C) THE UNION IS EXPECTED TO BE OPERATIONAL BY NEXT

YEAR, MUDENGE CONTINUED. THE UNION\'S HEADQUARTERS WILL

BE IN ADDIS ABABA, ETHIOPIA, BUT ITS OTHER INSTITUTIONS,

SUCH AS THE PARLIAMENT AND CENTRAL BANK, WILL LIKELY BE

HEADQUARTERED IN YET-TO-BE-DETERMINED CITIES. THE OAU

STRUCTURES WILL EVENTUALLY BE FOLDED INTO THE AFRICAN

UNION. MUDENGE NOTED THAT ZIMBABWE AND SOUTH AFRICA

HAVE ALREADY RATIFIED THE AFRICAN UNION ACT, AND ASSURED

THE DIPLOMATS PRESENT THAT THERE IS A "VIGOROUS

COMMITMENT BY AFRICA TO GO FORWARD" WITH THE UNION.

RESULTS OF SADC SUMMIT DOWNPLAYED

---------------------------------

4. (C) THE FOREIGN MINISTER TRIED TO DOWNPLAY THE

RESULTS OF THE SADC SUMMIT HELD IN WINDHOEK ON MARCH 9.

HE STATED THAT THE SUMMIT WAS FOCUSED ON "MODERNIZING"

SADC\'S INSTITUTIONS, AS THE DECENTRALIZATION OF SOME

ORGANS HAD BECOME A PROBLEM. MUDENGE CLAIMED THE PRESS

WAS "CONFUSED" ABOUT THE DECISION MADE TO BRING THE

ORGAN ON POLITICS, DEFENSE AND SECURITY INTO THE FORMAL

SADC FRAMEWORK UNDER THE SADC CHAIRMAN. THE MINISTER

ASSERTED THAT THE DECISION TO ROTATE THE CHAIRMANSHIP OF

THE ORGAN WAS NOT NEW--THE CHAIRMANSHIP WAS SUPPOSED TO

BE ROTATED FROM THE ORGAN\'S INCEPTION IN 1996, BUT THE

MEMBER STATES HAD SIMPLY DECIDED TO RENEW PRESIDENT

MUGABE\'S MANDATE YEAR AFTER YEAR. ZIMBABWE "KNEW" THIS

COULD NOT GO ON INDEFINITELY. HOWEVER, MUDENGE

EMPHASIZED THAT MUGABE WAS NOT VOTED OUT OF THE

CHAIRMANSHIP AT THE SUMMIT AS SOME PRESS ARTICLES

ANOTHER JAB AT THE BRITISH

--------------------------

6. (C) THE FOREIGN MINISTER COULD NOT PASS UP AN

OPPORTUNITY TO TAKE ANOTHER JAB AT THE BRITISH, WHOSE

HIGH COMMISSIONER WAS IN ATTENDANCE AT THE BRIEFING. IN

RESPONSE TO A QUESTION FROM THE SPANISH AMBASSADOR,

MUDENGE ACCUSED THE UK OF TRYING TO SCUTTLE THE EU\'S

EFFORTS TO HELP ZIMBABWE OUT OF ITS TROUBLES. "HOW CAN

THEY CRITICIZE BELGIUM AND FRANCE FOR RECEIVING

PRESIDENT MUGABE?" HE ASKED. A PALL FELL OVER THE ROOM

AS MUDENGE CALLED THE BRITISH "RACISTS" IN FRONT OF THE

ENTIRE DIPLOMATIC CORPS AND SAID THEIR ATTITUDE WOULD

EXACERBATE RACIAL DIVISIONS IN ZIMBABWE. MUDENGE\'S RANT

WAS THE SUBJECT OF THE HEADLINE STORY IN THE MARCH 15

EDITION OF THE GOVERNMENT-CONTROLLED "THE HERALD."

COMMENT

-------

SPONSORED SUMMIT ON THE AFRICAN UNION SUGGESTS SOME KIND

OF QUID PRO QUO MAY BE AT WORK BETWEEN ZIMBABWE AND

LIBYA. ALTHOUGH MANY IN THE GOZ UNDOUBTEDLY AGREE THAT

THE IDEA OF AN AFRICAN UNION HAS SOME APPEALING
