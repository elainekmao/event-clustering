SUBJECT: UNDP RESIDENT REPRESENTATIVE ANGELO SHARES HIS

VIEWS ON THE POLITICAL AND ECONOMIC CRISIS IN ZIMBABWE

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR TOM MCDONALD. REASONS 1.5 (B)

AND (D).

1. (C) SUMMARY: AMBASSADOR MCDONALD, ACCOMPANIED BY

LABOFF, PAID A CALL NOVEMBER 6 ON RECENTLY ARRIVED UNDP

RESIDENT REPRESENTATIVE (RES REP) VICTOR ANGELO TO

DISCUSS THE CURRENT POLITICAL AND ECONOMIC CRISIS IN

ZIMBABWE. THE RES REP TOLD THE AMBASSADOR THAT WHILE

THERE ARE INDIVIDUALS IN THE DIPLOMATIC COMMUNITY IN

HARARE WHO WANT TO TAKE A WAIT-AND-SEE ATTITUDE

REGARDING THE SITUATION IN ZIMBABWE, HE THINKS THAT A

MORE PROACTIVE RESPONSE IS CALLED FOR TO PREVENT A MAJOR

CRISIS. THE AMBASSADOR WHOLEHEARTEDLY AGREED WITH THE

RES REP AND THEY DECIDED TO SET-UP A NUMBER OF MEETINGS

AIMED AT BRINGING TOGETHER THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE

(GOZ)/ZANU-PF, THE OPPOSITION POLITICAL PARTY MOVEMENT

FOR DEMOCRATIC CHANGE (MDC), AND OTHER STAKEHOLDERS TO

DISCUSS WORKING TOGETHER ON A WAY FORWARD. RES REP

ANGELO INFORMED THE AMBASSADOR THAT THE UN SECRETARY

GENERAL (SG) WAS PLANNING ON SENDING A REPRESENTATIVE TO

HARARE ON NOVEMBER 17 TO ALSO TRY AND BREAK THE ICE

HERE, BUT THIS TRIP HINGES ON PRESIDENT MUGABE AGREEING

TO SEE THE SG\'S REP. END SUMMARY

2. (C) AMBASSADOR MCDONALD PAID A VISIT TO THE UNITED

NATIONS DEVELOPMENT PROGRAM (UNDP) RESIDENT

REPRESENTATIVE (RES REP) IN HARARE, VICTOR ANGELO, ON

NOVEMBER 6 TO DISCUSS THE CURRENT POLITICAL AND ECONOMIC

CRISIS IN ZIMBABWE AND WHAT COULD BE DONE TO LOWER

TENSIONS. THE AMBASSADOR STATED THAT HE WAS VERY

CONCERNED THAT THE SITUATION HAD TAKEN A TURN FOR THE

WORSE IN THE LAST FEW WEEKS WITH NEGATIVE DEVELOPMENTS

INCLUDING FOOD RIOTS, THE MDC\'S FILING OF AN IMPEACHMENT

MOTION AGAINST MUGABE, AND AN UPSWING IN VIOLENCE ON

OCCUPIED COMMERCIAL FARMS. AMBASSADOR MCDONALD TOLD RES

REP ANGELO THAT HE WAS WORRIED THAT TENSIONS ARE

REACHING A BOILING POINT, AND THAT THE EMBASSY AND THE

UNDP NEEDED TO WORK TOGETHER IN GETTING THE GOZ AND

OPPOSITION TALKING.

3. (C) RES REP ANGELO TOLD THE AMBASSADOR THAT HE SHARED

HIS CONCERNS AND PERCEPTIONS. ANGELO STATED THAT WHILE

THERE ARE OTHER MEMBERS OF THE HARARE DIPLOMATIC

COMMUNITY WHO BELIEVE THAT A WAIT-AND-SEE ATTITUDE IS

APPROPRIATE, HE DOESN\'T BELIEVE THAT THIS IS PRUDENT.

HE ALSO AGREED THAT EVENTS COULD GET OUT OF HAND IN

ZIMBABWE BEFORE LONG. HE WAS ESPECIALLY WORRIED THAT

THE UNDP SECURITY OFFICER WHO IS THE LIAISON WITH THE

ZIMBABWE REPUBLIC POLICE (ZRP) HAD INFORMED HIM THAT ZRP

RIOT UNITS HAD BEGUN RANGE TRAINING WITH LIVE

AMMUNITION, A PRACTICE NOT SEEN BEFORE. THE CLEAR

IMPLICATION IS THAT THE ZRP WOULD USE LIVE AMMUNITION IN

ANY FURTHER CIVIL DISTURBANCES.

4. (C) AMBASSADOR MCDONALD AND RES REP ANGELO AGREED TO

ARRANGE THE FOLLOWING MEETINGS TO EXPLORE WAYS TO

INCREASE THE DIALOGUE BETWEEN THE GOZ, THE MDC, AND

OTHER CIVIL SOCIETY STAKEHOLDERS.

- A MEETING AMONG THE AMBASSADOR, THE RES REP, AND THE

PERMANENT SECRETARY OF THE MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS,

MR. WILLARD CHIWEWE. MR. CHIWEWE IS A CONDUIT TO

PRESIDENT MUGABE. THIS MEETING COULD BE USED TO

UNOFFICIALLY GAUGE IF A WILLINGNESS EXISTS ON THE PART

OF PRESIDENT MUGABE TO TRY AND DEFUSE TENSIONS AND BEGIN

DIALOGUE WITH THE OPPOSITION MDC, AS WELL AS TO RELAY

THE AMBASSADOR\'S AND RES REP\'S WILLINGNESS TO ASSIST IN

EFFORTS TO LOWER THE TEMPERATURE.

- A MEETING AMONG THE AMBASSADOR, RES REP, AND CHIEFS OF

MISSION OF THE OTHER MAJOR DONORS AND KEY PLAYERS IN

ZIMBABWE. AUSTRALIA, DENMARK, THE EUROPEAN UNION,

FRANCE, GERMANY, JAPAN, NIGERIA, NORWAY, SWEDEN,

TANZANIA, AND THE UNITED KINGDOM WILL BE INVITED. THE

PURPOSE WILL BE TO BRAINSTORM ON WAYS TO LOWER TENSIONS

AND GET DIALOGUE STARTED.

- A MEETING AMONG THE AMBASSADOR, THE RES REP, AND THE

COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION (CFU). THE PURPOSE WILL BE TO

ENCOURAGE THE CFU TO REACH OUT TO THE GOZ AND GET BOTH

SIDES TALKING AGAIN.
