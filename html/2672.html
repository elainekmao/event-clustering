SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) MFA\'S OFFICE DIRECTOR FOR SOUTHERN AFRICAN AFFAIRS

REPLIED TO OUR RECENT DEMARCHE AND LETTER ON ZIMBABWE. THE

GOI SHARES THE EU POINT OF VIEW THAT CONDEMNATIONS AND

SANCTIONS ARE NECESSARY. HOWEVER, MARINI BELIEVES THAT THEY

WILL PROBABLY PROVE INSUFFICIENT TO FORCE CHANGE. THE ROLE

OF REGIONAL ACTORS IS KEY, AND SOUTH AFRICA POSSESSES

ECONOMIC LEVERS THAT CAN INFLUENCE MUGABE. THE U.S. AND EU

NEED TO CONSULT CLOSELY AND CONVINCE REGIONAL STATES TO PLAY

A CONSTRUCTIVE ROLE. THE EU WILL MONITOR LEGAL AND POLITICAL

DEVELOPMENTS AND CONSIDER ADDITIONAL "SMART SANCTIONS." END

SUMMARY.

2. (U) ON MARCH 14 POLOFF DELIVERED REF B DEMARCHE ON

ZIMBABWE\'S ELECTION. POST DELIVERED REF C LETTER TO PM

BERLUSCONI FROM SECRETARY POWELL ON THE CRISIS IN ZIMBABWE

MARCH 6. IN A MARCH 21 MEETING, CONSIGLIERE GIORGIO MARINI,

MFA OFFICE DIRECTOR FOR SOUTHERN AFRICA, PROVIDED THE

FOLLOWING RESPONSE AND INSIGHTS:

ITALY SUPPORTS THE EU POSITION, BUT ...

---------------------------------------

3. (C) MARINI EXPLAINED THAT THE GOI FULLY SUPPORTED THE

EU POSITION ON ZIMBABWE. RECALLING THE FEB. 18 EU DECISION

TO IMPOSE SANCTIONS, MARINI SAID THE GOI VIEWED THESE

MEASURES AS A NECESSARY RESPONSE TO THE GOZ\'S REFUSAL TO GIVE

GROUND ON PRESS AND JUDICIAL FREEDOMS DURING A DIALOGUE WITH

THE EU BASED ON THE COTONOU AGREEMENT. THE EU APPROACH WAS

CALIBRATED TO AVOID WEAKENING THE IMPACT OF INTERNATIONAL

OPPOSITION TO MUGABE\'S ACTIONS BY LEAVING AN OPENING TO THOSE

WHO WOULD PAINT IT AS NEO-IMPERIALISM. HOWEVER, MARINI\'S

PERSONAL OPINION WAS THAT EU SANCTIONS WERE UNLIKELY TO BRING

ABOUT DEMOCRATIC CHANGE.

THE IMPORTANCE OF PEER PRESSURE

-------------------------------

4. (C) WHAT COULD BE MORE EFFECTIVE WOULD BE CONDEMNATION

OF MUGABE\'S ACTIONS BY REGIONAL POWERS IN ADDITION TO THE

WEST. MARINI SUGGESTED THAT ZIMBABWE\'S DEPENDENCE ON SOUTH

AFRICA FOR OIL AND GAS PROVIDED A KEY LEVER FOR PRESSURE.

SOUTH AFRICA\'S IMPORTANCE AS A MARKET FOR ZIMBABWE GAVE IT

FURTHER LEVERAGE.

5. (C) ASKED WHY HE THOUGHT MBEKI HAD EVENTUALLY SUPPORTED

THE COMMONWEALTH\'S EXPULSION OF ZIMBABWE, MARINI CITED UK AND

U.S. PRESSURE AND SOUTH AFRICA\'S KEY ROLE IN THE NEW ECONOMIC

PARTNERSHIP FOR AFRICAN DEVELOPMENT (NEPAD). ONLY WHEN HIS

"BACK WAS TO THE WALL" WAS MBEKI WILLING TO OVERCOME DOMESTIC

PRESSURES AND OPPOSE MUGABE. THE SITUATION WAS COMPLICATED

FOR THE GSA, BECAUSE, ON THE ONE HAND, MUGABE HAD STOOD WITH

THE ANC AGAINST APARTHEID IN SPITE OF HIS PERSONAL RIVALRY

WITH MANDELA FOR THE LEADERSHIP OF SOUTHERN AFRICAN

DEVELOPMENT COMMUNITY (SADC). ON THE OTHER HAND, SOUTH

AFRICA WANTED TO HEAD OFF A CIVIL WAR ON ITS BORDER CAUSED BY

POLITICAL OUTRAGE, IN ORDER TO AVOID AN INFLUX OF REFUGEES.

FURTHERMORE, MBEKI WANTED TO MAINTAIN AN UNTARNISHED

LEADERSHIP ROLE IN NEPAD, SINCE HE RECOGNIZED THAT THE KEY TO

AFRICA\'S FUTURE WAS WESTERN PARTNERSHIP AND INVESTMENT.

6. (C) PER REF A, MARINI CALLED FOR CLOSE U.S.-EU

COORDINATION THAT INCLUDED NIGERIA AND SOUTH AFRICA. SUPPORT

OF AFRICAN AND LATIN AMERICAN COUNTRIES IN THE UNHCR COULD

ALSO PROVE IMPORTANT.

7. (C) MARINI PROVIDED A DRAFT COPY OF A REPLY FROM

BERLUSCONI TO SECRETARY POWELL THAT HAD NOT YET BEEN CLEARED

BY THE PM\'S OFFICE. IT SAYS THAT BERLUSCONI SHARES U.S.

CONCERNS AND VIEWS ON THE LEGITIMACY OF THE ELECTION AND IS

PLEASED WITH THE RECENT COMMONWEALTH DECISION TO SUSPEND

ZIMBABWE. AN EU TROIKA WILL CONSULT WITH SADC COUNTRIES TO

IMPRESS UPON THEM THE NECESSITY TO STAND UP FOR HUMAN RIGHTS

EU\'S NEXT STEPS DEPEND ON MUGABE

--------------------------------

8. (C) ASKED WHAT THE EU PROPOSED NOW, MARINI POINTED TO

THE CONCLUSIONS OF THE BARCELONA EUROPEAN COUNCIL (MARCH

15-16), WHICH STATE THAT THE EU "RESOLVES TO MONITOR

DEVELOPMENTS CLOSELY ... AND WILL CONSIDER POSSIBLE

ADDITIONAL TARGETED MEASURES" AGAINST THE GOVERNMENT OF

ZIMBABWE. THE EU WILL MONITOR THE ACTIONS TAKEN AGAINST

ARRESTED OPPOSITION LEADER TSVANGARAI AS WELL AS WHETHER

MUGABE FORMS A UNITY GOVERNMENT. POTENTIAL FUTURE EU STEPS

INCLUDE PLACING SANCTIONS ON THE SPOUSES AND CHILDREN OF THE

HUMAN RIGHTS ABUSERS SANCTIONED IN THE FEB. 18 DECISION.
