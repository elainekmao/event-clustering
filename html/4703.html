1. (SBU) SUMMARY. GVN OFFICIALS CLAIMED THAT THE RECENTLY

CONCLUDED NATIONAL BUDDHIST CONFERENCE WAS ENTIRELY

INDEPENDENT OF GOVERNMENT CONTROL, BUT ADMITTED THAT THE GVN

MUST APPROVE ITS CONVOCATION AND HAD FINAL SAY ON ITS

ELECTED NEW LEADERS AND ORDINATIONS (OR PROMOTIONS) OF MONKS

AND NUNS. PLANS FOR ANOTHER CATHOLIC SEMINARY (NOW

DESCRIBED AS A "BRANCH" OF THE HO CHI MINH CITY SEMINARY)

SEEM TO BE ON HOLD AS CATHOLICS SEARCH FOR A NEW SITE. THE

GVN HAS ALREADY APPROVED A SEMINARY FOR THE RECENTLY LEGAL

SOUTHERN EVANGELICAL CHURCH OF VIETNAM (SECV) BUT THE SECV

HAS NOT TAKEN STEPS TO SET ONE UP YET. NEITHER THE SECV NOR

ITS NORTHERN COUNTERPART (ECVN) HAS ASKED THE CRA FOR

PERMISSION TO RECEIVE NEW MEMBERS FROM ETHNIC MINORITY

CHURCHES IN THE CENTRAL OR NORTHWEST HIGHLANDS, WHICH THEY

SHOULD IN PRINCIPLE BE ABLE TO DO AS LONG AS THEY ARE "LEGAL

CHURCHES," ACCORDING TO GVN OFFICIALS. THE ECVN IS WELCOME

TO REQUEST PERMISSION TO HOLD ITS LONG-AWAITED NATIONAL

CONFERENCE, BUT HAS NOT DONE SO, OFFICIALS CLAIMED. END

SUMMARY.

2. (SBU) IN A MEETING WITH POL/C ON DECEMBER 11, NGUYEN THE

DOANH, DIRECTOR OF EXTERNAL RELATIONS OF THE GOVERNMENT

COMMITTEE ON RELIGION AFFAIRS (CRA), AND TRAN KHANH DU, THE

CRA\'S DIRECTOR OF BUDDHIST AFFAIRS, PROVIDED AN UPDATE ON

RECENT DEVELOPMENTS RELATED TO BUDDHIST, CATHOLIC, AND

PROTESTANT GROUPS IN VIETNAM. POL/C NOTED THAT THE USG WAS

APPROACHING A DECISION ABOUT DESIGNATION OF "COUNTRIES OF

PARTICULAR CONCERN" UNDER THE INTERNATIONAL RELIGIOUS

FREEDOM ACT, AND HAD BEEN CONCERNED ABOUT RECENT NEGATIVE

BUDDHISTS

---------

3. (U) THE FIFTH NATIONAL BUDDHIST CONGRESS MET IN HANOI

DECEMBER 4-5, ELECTING ITS NEW LEADERSHIP AND DEVELOPING

GOALS FOR THE UPCOMING FIVE YEARS, AS WELL AS ORDAINING

AND/OR PROMOTING 967 MONKS AND NUNS. DU ADMITTED THAT THE

BUDDHIST SANGHA OF VIETNAM WAS REQUIRED TO "REQUEST

PERMISSION" FROM THE GVN -- BY A FORMAL LETTER TO PRIME

MINISTER PHAN VAN KHAI -- TO HOLD THESE CONGRESSES, BUT

CLAIMED THAT PERMISSION WAS AUTOMATICALLY GIVEN, WITH THE

CRA PROVIDING APPROPRIATE NOTIFICATION TO "LOCALITIES." THE

GVN DID NOT PROVIDE ANY FINANCIAL SUPPORT, DU NOTED, AND

EVEN CHARGED THE BUDDHISTS RENT FOR USE OF THE SOVIET-ERA

HALL OF CULTURE IN DOWNTOWN HANOI, ONE OF THE FEW VENUES BIG

ENOUGH FOR THE 1,000-PLUS ATTENDEES.

4. (U) DU STRESSED THAT, WHILE GVN OFFICIALS ATTENDED THE

OPENING CEREMONY, THEY DID NOT PARTICIPATE IN ENSUING

DISCUSSIONS NOR HAVE ANY RIGHT TO VOTE ON LEADERSHIP AND

OTHER ISSUES. HE SAID THAT THE LEADERSHIP ELECTIONS

FEATURED MORE CANDIDATES THAN SEATS, A DEMOCRATIC

DEVELOPMENT. IN RESPONSE TO PERSISTENT QUESTIONING, DU

ADMITTED FINALLY THAT THE BUDDHISTS WERE OBLIGED TO SEND THE

FINAL LISTS OF NEW LEADERS TO THE GVN FOR "APPROVAL," BUT

IN "ASKING THE OPINIONS OF LOCALITIES" ABOUT THE MERITS OF

BLESSING. HE SAID IT WAS "RARE" NOT TO APPROVE.

7. (U) DOANH REITERATED THE GVN\'S DETERMINATION TO

8. (U) WHEN ASKED ABOUT PROGRESS IN THE OPENING OF A

SEVENTH CATHOLIC SEMINARY (WHICH GVN OFFICIALS TOLD EMBOFFS

LAST YEAR HAD BEEN APPROVED AT THE CENTRAL LEVEL BUT FACED

SOME RESISTANCE BY PROVINCIAL AUTHORITIES), DOANH CLAIMED

THAT THE PLANS WERE NOT FOR A SEPARATE NEW SEMINARY PER SE,

BUT RATHER FOR A "BRANCH" OF THE EXISTING -- BUT OVERCROWDED

-- SEMINARY IN HO CHI MINH CITY. INITIALLY, THE CATHOLICS

HAD HOPED TO OPEN THE BRANCH IN DONG NAI PROVINCE (WHICH IS

WHERE MANY OF THE 1954-ERA CATHOLICS FROM THE NORTH ARE

CENTERED), BUT THESE PLANS HAVE NOW FALLEN THROUGH, DOANH

CLAIMED, WITHOUT OFFERING A CLEAR EXPLANATION OF WHY OTHER

THAN CITING "LOCAL DIFFICULTIES" OVER LAND. HE SAID THAT

THE CATHOLICS HAD NOT YET OFFERED ANY NEW PROPOSAL ON A

DIFFERENT SITE, BUT THE PROJECT IN PRINCIPLE CONTINUED TO

HAVE GVN APPROVAL.

PROTESTANTS

-----------

9. (U) DOANH ALSO CLAIMED THAT THE GVN HAD ALREADY

APPROVED IN PRINCIPLE A PROPOSAL BY THE SECV TO OPEN A

SEMINARY FOR PASTORS IN HO CHI MINH CITY, BUT THAT THE SECV

HAS NOT DONE SO YET. HE NOTED THAT THE SECV WAS STILL BUSY

SETTING UP ITS OFFICES (NOTE: AFTER WINNING GVN RECOGNITION

ONLY IN 2001 AND HOLDING ITS FIRST CONGRESS IN SUMMER 2002),

AND PREDICTED THAT THE ESTABLISHMENT OF A SEMINARY --

ESPECIALLY RECRUITMENT OF QUALIFIED FACULTY -- WOULD STILL

10. (U) DOANH CLAIMED THAT THE ECVN HAD NOT YET ASKED FOR

PERMISSION TO HOLD ITS NATIONAL CONGRESS (THE LAST WAS IN

1988), BUT ADDED THAT "THE GVN HAS NO REASON TO OPPOSE" IF

IT RECEIVES SUCH AN APPLICATION. HE CLAIMED NOT TO

UNDERSTAND THE PROBLEM BEHIND CONVENING THE ECVN\'S CONGRESS

AND ELECTING A NEW OFFICIAL ECVN LEADERSHIP.

11. (U) DOANH SAID THAT, AS FAR AS HE WAS AWARE, NEITHER

THE SECV NOR THE ECVN HAD RECEIVED APPLICATIONS FROM ETHNIC

MINORITY CHURCHES OR "ASSOCIATIONS" (CHI HOI) TO AFFILIATE

WITH EITHER ORGANIZATION, NOR HAD THE SECV OR ECVN NOTIFIED

THE CRA OF ANY SUCH DECISIONS. (NOTE: PER REFS E AND F,

BOTH THE SECV AND ECVN ARE PROCESSING AND/OR HAVE ALREADY

APPROVED SOME REQUESTS. END NOTE) HE SAID THAT "IN

PRINCIPLE" SUCH EXPANSIONS WOULD NOT POSE A PROBLEM, AS LONG

AS THE NEW MEMBERS WERE "LEGAL CHURCHES." HE STRESSED THAT

ETHNIC MINORITIES WERE "EQUAL CITIZENS" AND HAD THE SAME

RIGHTS AS OTHER VIETNAMESE, INCLUDING FREEDOM OF RELIGION.

HE WAS VAGUE ABOUT HOW HOUSE CHURCHES COULD BECOME LEGAL

CHURCHES ENTITLED TO MEMBERSHIP IN THE ECVN OR SEVN, OTHER

THAN NOTING A REQUIREMENT FOR A CERTAIN NUMBER OF MEMBERS.

COMMENT

-------

12. (SBU) WHILE TRUMPETING FREEDOM OF RELIGION, THE CRA

TAKES ITS OVERSIGHT ROLE SERIOUSLY, AND REMAINS EXPLICITLY

INVOLVED IN ALL MAJOR ORGANIZATIONAL ISSUES FOR THE MAIN

RELIGIOUS GROUPINGS. THIS LIKELY REFLECTS LESS A DEEP-

ROOTED DISTRUST OF RELIGION BY THE GVN OR COMMUNIST PARTY OF

VIETNAM (CPV) AS MUCH AS A FIRM LENINIST ORIENTATION ABOUT

THE NEED TO REGULATE AND SUPERVISE ALL SOCIAL ORGANIZATIONS

IN THE INTEREST OF "NATIONAL SOLIDARITY" AND THE NEED TO

MAINTAIN STRONG POLITICAL CONTROL.
