SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE REMARKS ON WCAR PREPCOM MEETING IN

DAKAR

REF: STATE 7580

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 00502 241447Z

REASONS: 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) ON JANUARY 23, POST DELIVERED REFTEL TALKING

POINTS TO ALFRED MUTIWAZUKA, THE PRINCIPAL

ADMINISTRATIVE OFFICER (PAO) IN THE INTERNATIONAL

ORGANIZATIONS OFFICE OF ZIMBABWE\'S MFA. IN A FOLLOW-UP

CONVERSATION WITH MUTIWAZUKA ON JANUARY 24, POLOFF

LEARNED THAT ZIMBABWE IS SENDING JUSTICE MINISTER

PATRICK CHINAMASA AND REPRESENTATIVES FROM ITS GENEVA

MISSION TO REPRESENT IT AT THE WORLD CONFERENCE AGAINST

RACISM PREPCOM MEETING IN DAKAR. MUTIWAZUKA SAID THAT

HE DID NOT YET HAVE ANY SUBSTANTIVE RESPONSE TO OUR

POINTS, AS ONE WAS STILL BEING DEVELOPED IN HIS OFFICE.

HOWEVER, A LETTER WITH OUR POINTS ATTACHED WOULD BE SENT

TO ZIMBABWE\'S DAKAR MISSION BEFORE THE PREPCOM MEETING

BEGINS JANUARY 27, THE PAO STATED.

2. (C) COMMENT: IT IS SIGNIFICANT TO NOTE THAT ZIMBABWE

IS SENDING ITS JUSTICE MINISTER, RATHER THAN A SENIOR

MFA OFFICIAL, TO THE SENEGAL MEETING. THIS SUGGESTS

THAT ZIMBABWE MAY BE SYMPATHETIC TO THE DAKAR DRAFT\'S

CALL FOR REPARATIONS FOR THE SLAVE TRADE. SUCH A POLICY

WOULD BE CONSISTENT WITH THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE\'S

RECENT PUBLIC RHETORIC AGAINST COLONIALISTS AND WHITES,

WHICH HAS ALSO BEEN SPOUTED BY MINISTER CHINAMASA. END

COMMENT.
