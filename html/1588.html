SUBJECT: TWO MINING OPERATIONS AND A FOOTWEAR MANUFACTURER

KEEP THEIR HEAD ABOVE WATER ONLY BY EXPORTING

REF: HARARE 711

1. TO ASSESS BUSINESS AND OPERATING CONDITIONS OF LARGE

EMPLOYERS LOCATED OUTSIDE THE CAPITAL CITY, ON MARCH 5-7

EMBASSY ECONOMIC AND COMMERCIAL OFFICERS VISITED THREE

BUSINESSES IN THE SOUTHERN MASVINGO PROVINCE OF ZIMBABWE.

THE COMPANY/OPERATIONS WERE:

= RAILWAY BLOCK CHROMIUM MINE, SHURUGWE, 750 EMPLOYEES

= BATA SHOE MANUFACTURING, GWERU, 3500 EMPLOYEES

= SHABANI ASBESTOS MINE, ZVISHAVANE, 3800 EMPLOYEES

ALL THE BUSINESSES ARE THE LARGEST EMPLOYERS IN THEIR AREA,

AND BOTH MINES HAVE BEEN OPERATING FROM THE TURN OF THE LAST

CENTURY. ALL REPRESENT VERY SUBSTANTIAL INVESTMENTS, AND

THEIR SHUTDOWN WOULD HAVE SEVERE NEGATIVE CONSEQUENCES WITH

WIDE-RANGING RIPPLE EFFECTS. ALL THREE OPERATIONS PROVIDE

HOUSING, UTILITIES, EDUCATION AND MEDICAL FACILITIES FOR

THEIR EMPLOYEES AND DEPENDENTS, AND HAVE SEEN THEIR OPERATING

COSTS RISE AND MARGINS SLIP IN RECENT TIMES DUE TO HIGH

DOMESTIC INFLATION. THE FUEL SHORTAGE HAS ALSO NEGATIVELY

IMPACTED THEIR OPERATIONS, ESPECIALLY THE MINES, WHICH HAVE

EXPORTING IS THE KEY TO SURVIVAL

--------------------------------

2. MANAGEMENT AT ALL THREE OPERATIONS TOLD US THAT THE KEY TO

THEIR VIABILITY IN TODAY\'S HARSH ECONOMIC ENVIRONMENT IS

THEIR EXPORT SALES, AND THE ABILITY TO CONTROL THE

DISPOSITION OF A MAJORITY OF THE HARD CURRENCY THEY EARN.

ALL THREE ARE PURELY PRIVATE-SECTOR OPERATIONS. BOTH OF THE

MINING COMPANIES ALLOCATE ABOUT 25 PERCENT OF THEIR TOTAL

FOREIGN EXCHANGE EARNINGS TO GOVERNMENT PARASTATALS,

PRIMARILY THE NATIONAL RAILROAD AND THE NATIONAL ELECTRICITY

AUTHORITY (BOTH MINES ARE LARGE POWER CONSUMERS AND THE

RAILROAD TRANSPORTS THE ORE AND THE COAL THEY NEED AS WELL AS

THE FINISHED PRODUCT). THEY DO SO ON A VOLUNTARY BASIS TO

AVOID A GOVERNMENT DIRECTIVE THAT MIGHT ALLOCATE MORE OF

THEIR EARNINGS, AND THEY WERE HORRIFIED BY THE SHORT-LIVED

RESERVE BANK DIRECTIVE THAT SEIZED 100 PERCENT OF THEIR

EXPORT EARNINGS FOR A FEW DAYS IN FEBRUARY (REFTEL). THE

EXPORT PERCENTAGES AND EARNINGS OF THE THREE ARE:

= FERRO-CHROME MINE, EXPORTS 100 PERCENT OF OUTPUT (ABOUT 15

TONS/ANNUM OF REFINED CHROME), VALUE: ABOUT U.S. $5 MILLION

AT CURRENT WORLD PRICES;

= BATA SHOES, EXPORTS AN AVERAGE 10 PERCENT OF BATA LABEL

PRODUCTION (ABOUT 13 MILLION PAIRS/ANNUM) AND 100 PERCENT OF

NIKE-LICENSED PRODUCTION (VOLUME NOT PROVIDED), VALUE

OF BATA EXPORTS: ABOUT U.S. 4 MILLION (OFFICIAL RATES);

= SHABANIE ASBESTOS MINE, EXPORTS 88 PERCENT OF OUTPUT

(72,000 TONS/ANNUM OF FIBER), VALUE: ABOUT U.S. $30 MILLION

3. THEREFORE, BASED ON THEIR ABILITY TO EXPORT AND COLLECT

HARD CURRENCY, THEY HAVE BEEN ABLE TO SURVIVE, ALBEIT

TENUOUSLY WITH HIGH DOMESTIC INFLATION AND LENDING RATES,

REDUCED SUPPLIER CREDIT, THE GROWING GULF BETWEEN THE

OFFICIAL AND UNOFFICIAL EXCHANGE RATE, AND CASH FLOW

DIFFICULTIES. IF THE GOVERNMENT REPEATS ITS GRAB OF HARD

CURRENCY FROM EXPORTERS, OR ELIMINATES FOREIGN CURRENCY

ACCOUNTS ALTOGETHER, THESE COMPANIES WILL CEASE OPERATIONS,

AS HAS BEEN THE CASE OVER THE LAST YEAR WITH MANY OF

ZIMBABWE\'S GOLD PRODUCERS. (COMMENT: GOLD PRODUCTION IN 2000

WAS DOWN OVER 20 PERCENT FROM THE YEAR BEFORE, FROM 28 TONS

TO ABOUT 22 TONS, IN LARGE PART DUE TO THE GOVERNMENT\'S

RELEASE OF ONLY 20 PERCENT OF THE HARD CURRENCY EARNED BY

GOLD SALES TO THE MINING COMPANIES. END COMMENT.) THE LOSS

OF OVER 8,500 JOBS WOULD BE TRAGIC FOR ZIMBABWE, AND FOR NOW

THE MANAGEMENT OF ALL THREE COME TO WORK EVERY MORNING WITH

ONLY ONE GOAL IN MIND -- SURVIVAL.
