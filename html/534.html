SUBJECT: CGYOOL-HUMANITARIAN ASSISTANCE UPDATE NO. 38

1. THIS REPORT IS INTENDED TO PROVIDE A BRIEF, DAILY

UPDATE ON THE HUMANITARIAN ASSISTANCE SITUATION IN

THE FORMER YUGOSLAVIA WITH REGARD TO CONVOYS,

COORDINATION, DISTRIBUTION OF U.S.-DONATED

ASSISTANCE, AND OTHER RELATED SUBJECTS.

AIRPORT UPDATE

--------------

2. THE SARAJEVO AIRLIFT IS ON AGAIN WITH 26 PLANES

SCHEDULED TO FLY TODAY. UNPROFOR REPORTS THAT BOTH

THEY AND UNHCR WERE READY TO RESUME FLYING YESTERDAY

BUT APPARENTLY ALL THE PILOTS HAD BEEN GIVEN LEAVE

AND THERE WAS NO ONE TO FLY THE PLANES.

3. REGARDING THE INCIDENT THAT HALTED THE FLIGHTS,

UNPROFOR REPORTS THAT GENERAL NAMBIAR FINDS THE

EVIDENCE IMPLICATING SERB FORCES INCONCLUSIVE. IN

FACT, UNPROFOR CLAIMS THAT THE BRITISH PLANE SEEMS TO

HAVE BEEN FLYING OVER CROAT/MUSLIM-CONTROLLED

TERRITORY WHEN LOCKED-ON BY UNIDENTIFIED

ANTI-AIRCRAFT RADAR.

JOINT COMMISSION UPDATE

-----------------------

4. THE "JOINT COMMISSION" SET UP TO OVERSEE AND

MONITOR THE PROCESS OF RESTORATION OF THE CRO?IAN

GOVERNMENT\'S AUTHORITY IN THE "PINK ZONES" WILL

ATTEMPT TO MEET TOMORROW (AUGUST 21). THE COMMISSION

FAILED TO HOLD ITS SECOND SESSION A WEEK AGO WHEN THE

CROATS REFUSED TO MEET CERTAIN PRE-CONDITIONS

DEMANDED BY THE SERBS. UNPROFOR IS HOPEFUL THAT THE

MEETING WILL TAKE PLACE THIS TIME.

5. THE CROATIAN GOVERNMENT HAS AGEED IN PRINCIPLE TO

RETURN THE EXHUMED CORPSES OF SERBS WHO DIED FIGHTING

IN THE AREA OF THE MILJEVICI PLATEAU. THEY HAVE ALSO

AGREED TO RELEASE A NUMBER OF PRISONERS HELD IN

OSIJEK AND CONVICTED OF WAR CRIMES. RUMOR HAS IT

THAT A PARDON MAY ALSO BE FORTHCOMING FROM PRESIDENT

TUDJMAN\'S OFFICE.

6. THE SERBS IN TURN HAVE AGREED IN PRINCIPLE TO LET

A GROUP OF INTERNATIONAL EXPERTS INSPECT THE PERUCA

DAM WITH A VIEW TO MAKING IT OPERATIONAL BEFORE THE

7. CONVOY (HCR-Z 4) TO BIHAC/TOPUSKO CONSISTING OF

TWO TRUCKS AND 48 TONS OF MEDICINES, POWDERED MILK

FOR CHILDREN AND BULK FOOD RETURNED SAFELY TO ZAGREB

TODAY (AUGUST 21). THE ONLY DIFFICULTY EXPERIENCED

ON THE TRIP WAS AT A SERB CHECKPOINT WHERE THE CONVOY

WAS FORCED TO GO BACK 30 KILOMETERS FOR A SLIP OF

PAPER BEFORE THEY COULD PASS THROUGH.

8. OFDA CONSULTANT TIM KNIGHT, WHO ACCOMPANIED THE

ABOVE CONVOY, REPORTS THAT THE HOSPITAL WHERE THE

SUPPLIES WERE UNLOADED TOOK THREE DIRECT HITS SHORTLY

AFTER THEY LEFT. HE WAS UNABLE TO STATE HOW MANY

PEOPLE MAY HAVE BEEN INJURED OR KILLED. THE BIHAC

HOSPITAL HOUSES 800 PATIENTS MOST OF WHOM, ACCORDING

TO KNIGHT, ARE OF CHILDREN WOUNDED IN THE SEIGE OF

THE CITY. MEDICAL SUPPLIES ARE SCARCE.

9. KNIGHT REPORTS A VERY TENSE AND DIFFICULT
