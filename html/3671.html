SUMMARY

CONFIDENTIAL

-------

1. (C) SUMMARY: BURUNDI PRESIDENT BUYOYA, ATTENDING A

SANT\'EGIDIO CONFERENCE IN PALERMO ON CONFLICT AND DIALOGUE,

REQUESTED A MEETING WITH AMBASSADOR NICHOLSON SEPTEMBER 1 TO

REINFORCE HIS INTEREST IN STEPPED UP U.S. AND TANZANIAN

PRESSURE ON THE CNDD FACTION OF THE FDD. MEETING AT THE

SUGGESTION OF THE SANT\'EGIDIO MEDIATOR INVOLVED IN BURUNDI,

DON MATTEO ZUPPI, BUYOYA SAID HE BELIEVED THE U.S. SHOULD

BOTH APPROACH THE FACTION DIRECTLY THROUGH ITS CONTACTS WITH

THEM IN TANZANIA, AND ALSO URGE THE TANZANIAN GOVERNMENT TO

DO MORE TO PRESSURE THE FACTION TO PARTICIPATE SERIOUSLY IN

NEGOTIATIONS. AMBASSADOR URGED BUYOYA TO APPROACH OUR

EMBASSY IN BUJUMBURA DIRECTLY, BUT PROMISED TO PASS ALONG THE

PRESIDENT\'S CONCERNS TO OUR EMBASSY AND WASHINGTON. END

SUMMARY.

-------------------------------------------

BUYOYA FRUSTRATED BY CNDD/FDD RECALCITRANCE

-------------------------------------------

2. (C) BURUNDI PRESIDENT BUYOYA, KEYNOTE SPEAKER AT THE

ANNUAL SANT\'EGIDIO INTER-FAITH CONFERENCE, "FAITHS AND

CULTURES WITHIN CONFLICT AND DIALOGUE," (SEPTEL) REQUESTED A

MEETING WITH AMBASSADOR NICHOLSON AT THE SUGGESTION OF FATHER

DON MATTEO ZUPPI, SANT\'EGIDIO\'S SPIRTUAL LEADER AND AFRICA

EXPERT, WHO HAS BEEN ACTIVE IN BURUNDI AND THE REGION FOR

MANY YEARS. BUYOYA OUTLINED FOR THE AMBASSADOR THE STATE OF

PLAY IN BURUNDI, STRESSING THAT ALTHOUGH MUCH PROGRESS HAS

BEEN MADE, THE GOVERNMENT\'S INABILITY TO BRING THE REMAINING

TWO REBEL FACTIONS INTO THE PEACE PROCESS, AND THEIR

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 VATICA 04376 01 OF 02 091559Z

UNWILLINGNESS TO ENTER INTO A CEASE-FIRE THREATENS THE

PROGRESS TO DATE. ACCORDING TO BUYOYA, THE CNDD/FDD

CONTINUES TO FIND "PRETEXTS" TO AVOID SUBSTANTIVE

NEGOTIATIONS, SUCH AS INSISTING THAT THE CURRENT GOVERNMENT

WAS NOT A COMBATANT, AND THEREFORE ONLY THE ARMY COULD BE

INVOLVED IN CEASE-FIRE NEGOTIATIONS. BUYOYA SAID HE HAD

REQUESTED INTERNATIONAL SUPPORT -- PARTICULARLY FROM TANZANIA

-- TO CONVINCE THE CNDD/FDD TO ENGAGE SERIOUSLY IN

NEGOTIATIONS, BUT THAT THE REBEL GROUP "CONTINUED TO PLAY A

DELAYING GAME."

--------------------

U.S. PRESSURE NEEDED

--------------------

3. (C) BUYOYA SAID HIS GOVERNMENT HAD CONCLUDED THAT ONLY

THE U.S., WORKING WITH TANZANIA, COULD OVERCOME THE CURRENT

STALEMATE. NOTING THAT U.S. ENGAGEMENT IN THE GREAT LAKES,

SUDAN, AND ANGOLA HAD LED TO SOLUTIONS, HE BELIEVED SIMILAR

ENGAGEMENT NOW IN BURUNDI WOULD BE PIVOTAL. DESPITE CURRENT

FRUSTRATIONS, HE BELIEVED THE "TIME WAS RIPE FOR A SOLUTION."

OBSERVING THAT HE UNDERSTOOD THAT THE CNDD/FDD REBEL LEADERS

WERE "WELL-KNOWN" TO OUR EMBASSY IN TANZANIA, HE URGED THE

U.S. TO PRESSURE THE LEADERS DIRECTLY TO ENTER NEGOTIATIONS.

IN ADDITION, HE URGED US TO APPROACH THE GOVERNMENT OF

TANZANIA TO STEP UP THEIR PRESSURE ON THE FDD/CNDD. HE

BELIEVED THAT TANZANIA HAD "ALL THE NECESSARY WEAPONS TO PUSH

THEM," BUT NEEDED MORE PRESSURE FROM THE U.S. TO BRING THEM

TO BEAR.

4. (C) THE AMBASSADOR THANKED BUYOYA FOR SHARING HIS

PERSPECTIVES AND ASKED IF HE HAD APPROACHED OUR EMBASSY IN

BUJUMBURA DIRECTLY WITH HIS REQUEST. HE SAID HE HAD NOT, BUT

THAT HE WOULD DO SO UPON HIS RETURN TO THE CAPITAL. THE

AMBASSADOR ENCOURAGED HIM TO DO SO, AND PROMISED TO CONVEY

HIS VIEWS TO OUR EMBASSIES IN BUJUMBURA AND DAR-ES-SALAAM AND

TO WASHINGTON. HE NOTED THAT THE U.S. SHARED THE PRESIDENT\'S

DESIRE TO BRING ALL THE REBEL GROUPS ACTIVELY INTO THE PEACE

PROCESS.

5. (C) ASKED ABOUT THE REBELS\' OBJECTIVES, BUYOYA SAID IT

WAS NOT CLEAR TO HIM AT THIS POINT WHAT MORE THEY NEEDED. HE

BELIEVED ALL THE CLAIMS THE REBELS HAD PUT FORWARD --

DEMOCRACY, INSTITUTIONALIZING SECURITY GUARANTEES, AND

SETTLEMENT OF PAST CONFLICTS -- WERE ANSWERED IN THE EXISTING

PEACE ACCORD. THE REAL ISSUE WAS HOW TO INTEGRATE THE REBELS

INTO THE NEW INSTITUTIONS, HE SAID. "WE ARE READY TO GIVE

THEM ROOM," HE ADDED, "BUT WE NEED THEM TO ENGAGE SERIOUSLY."
