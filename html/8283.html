CONSTITUTIONAL CONVENTION, MFA DIRECTOR FOR INSTITUTIONAL AND

TREATY SIGNING CEREMONY DURING THE ITALIAN EU PRESIDENCY WAS

HIGHLY UNLIKELY. BUCCINO STATED THAT IT WOULD BE IMPERATIVE

FOR THE IRISH TO CONCLUDE WORK ON THE IGC BY THE END OF THEIR

PRESIDENCY IN JUNE 2004 OR THE MOMENTUM COMING OUT OF THE

CONVENTION WILL BE LOST. ITALY SUPPORTS GISCARD\'S PROPOSAL

TO REPLACE THE SIX-MONTH ROTATING PRESIDENCY WITH A FULL-TIME

COUNCIL CHAIRMAN, ELECTED FOR A RENEWABLE 2 1/2-YEAR TERM.

ITALY ALSO ENVISAGES A MERGING OF THE CFSP HIGH

REPRESENTATIVE (SOLANA) AND COMMISSION EXTERNAL RELATIONS

COMMISSIONER (PATTEN) POSITIONS, INTO A SINGLE DOUBLE HATTED

EU FOREIGN MINISTER. ON THE MORE CONTROVERSIAL ISSUE OF

STRENGTHENED COOPERATION ON DEFENSE POLICY, ITALY INSISTS

THAT THE CRITERIA AND RULES FOR ENGAGEMENT MUST BE DECIDED BY

UNANIMITY (IN ORDER TO PREVENT ANOTHER ESDP MINI-DEFENSE

SUMMIT). ITALY WANTS TO ENSURE THAT THE CONVENTION\'S WORK

ON DEFENSE POLICY SUPPORTS BERLUSCONI\'S GOAL OF IMPROVING

TRANSATLANTIC RELATIONS. BUCCINO WELCOMED THE OPPORTUNITY TO

EXCHANGE VIEWS WITH EMBASSY POLOFF ON THE CONVENTION\'S

PROGRESS AND ANY POTENTIAL IMPLICATIONS FOR TRANSATLANTIC

RELATIONS. END SUMMARY.

2. (C) BUCCINO BEGAN THE MEETING BY STATING THAT THE

CONSTITUTIONAL CONVENTION IS A "STRANGE ANIMAL." ARTICLE 48

OF THE TREATY OF ROME REQUIRES ANY CHANGES TO THAT TREATY TO

BE DECIDED BY MEMBER STATES IN THE INTER-GOVERNMENTAL

CONFERENCE (IGC). BUCCINO ACKNOWLEDGED THAT DUE TO THE LACK

OF VOTING POWER AMONG CONVENTION REPRESENTATIVES, DISCUSSIONS

TEND TO BE RATHER GENERAL AND, THUS, THE PRESENTATION OF A

FINAL PRODUCT IN THESSALONIKI SHOULD NOT BE OVERSTATED.

ACCORDING TO BUCCINO, THE DRAFT CONSTITUTION WILL LIKELY

CONTAIN SEVERAL AMENDMENTS ON THE MAIN INSTITUTIONAL TIE-UPS,

E.G. ON A FULL TIME COUNCIL PRESIDENT AND ON THE SIZE OF THE

COMMISSION. BUCCINO ALSO ADMITTED THAT IT WOULD BE ALMOST

IMPOSSIBLE TO FINISH THE WORK OF THE IGC DURING ITALY\'S

PRESIDENCY, BUT STRESSED THE URGENCY IN COMPLETING THE

PROCESS BY THE END OF THE IRISH PRESIDENCY IN JUNE 2004.

OTHERWISE, THE EU WOULD BE FACING AN "INSTITUTIONAL MESS."

THERE ARE SEVERAL FACTORS IN 2004, SUCH AS EU PARLIAMENTARY

ELECTIONS AND REFORM OF THE COMMON AGRICULTURAL POLICY, WHICH

WOULD DISTRACT ATTENTION FROM THE WORK OF THE CONVENTION.

BUCCINO NOTED THE SYMBOLIC IMPORTANCE OF SIGNING THE NEW

TREATY IN ROME AND INDICATED THAT THE IRISH AND OTHER EU

MEMBER STATES WOULD AGREE TO THIS AS LONG AS ITALY WORKS

FASTIDIOUSLY IN HELPING THE IRISH TO COMPLETE THE WORK OF THE

IGC.

3. (C) ITALY SUPPORTS CONVENTION PRESIDENT GISCARD\'S

PROPOSAL TO REPLACE THE SIX-MONTH ROTATING PRESIDENCY OF THE

EUROPEAN COUNCIL WITH A FULL-TIME CHAIRMAN, ELECTED FOR A

RENEWABLE 2 1/2-YEAR TERM. BUCCINO DESCRIBED TWO MAIN ROLES

FOR THIS INDIVIDUAL: PREPARING, CHAIRING AND FOLLOWING UP THE

DECISIONS OF THE EUROPEAN COUNCIL, AND REPRESENTING THE EU ON

THE INTERNATIONAL SCENE (WITHOUT PREJUDICE TO THE

COMPETENCIES OF THE COMMISSION, OR THE DAILY CONDUCT OF

FOREIGN POLICY BY THE EU FOREIGN MINISTER). BUCCINO NOTED

THAT THIS PROPOSAL IS STRONGLY OPPOSED BY THE SMALLER MEMBER

STATES AND ACCESSION COUNTRIES, WHICH SEE IT AS A PLOT TO

CREATE A DIRECTOIRE OF THE BIG STATES. ITALY RECOGNIZES THAT

FAILURE TO REACH AGREEMENT ON THIS CONTROVERSIAL ISSUE COULD

ENDANGER THE CONVENTION\'S CHANCES OF SUBMITTING A FINAL TEXT

AT THESSALONIKI. ACCORDING TO BUCCINO, ITALY HOPES TO

FACILITATE A COMPROMISE ON THIS ISSUE BEFORE THESSALONIKI IN

ORDER TO EASE OF THE WORK OF THE IGC.

4. (C) ITALY ALSO ENVISAGES A MERGING OF THE CFSP HIGH

REPRESENTATIVE (SOLANA) AND COMMISSION EXTERNAL RELATIONS

COMMISSIONER (PATTEN) POSITIONS, INTO A SINGLE DOUBLE HATTED

EU FOREIGN MINISTER. ITALY\'S GOAL IS TO SIMPLIFY THE

INSTITUTIONAL STRUCTURE OF THE EU AND, THUS, BELIEVES A

FOREIGN MINISTER WOULD PROVIDE FOR MORE CONSISTENCY AND

EFFICIENCY IN EU EXTERNAL POLICY. A MAJORITY OF MEMBER

STATES SUPPORT THIS PROPOSAL AS LONG AS THERE IS A GENUINE

DOUBLE HATING WITH A LOCATION IN BOTH INSTITUTIONS. HOWEVER,

BUCCINO ACKNOWLEDGED THAT THERE ARE TECHNICAL DISAGREEMENTS

AMONG MEMBER STATES AS TO HOW THIS PERSON SHOULD BE NOMINATED.

5. (C) BUCCINO SAID THAT ITALY HAS OBJECTIONS TO THE

CONVENTION\'S WORK ON DEFENSE POLICY, SPECIFICALLY ARTICLE

30.6 OF THE PRESIDIUM\'S DRAFT TEXT. GISCARD\'S PROPOSAL CALLS

NO STRICT CRITERIA OR RULES FOR ENGAGEMENT. ITALY\'S

OBJECTION IS THAT ARTICLE 30.6 LEGITIMIZES A GROUP OF MEMBER
