SUBJECT: SADDAM\'S THREAT HELPS PULL KUWAITIS TOGETHER

REFS: (A) FBIS LD2007205690 (NOTAL),

- (B) 89 KUWAIT 6293 (NOTAL)

- (C) TUNIS 7242 (NOTAL)

1. (C) SUMMARY. IRAQI THREATS HAVE HAD THE EFFECT OF

UNIFYING SOME KUWAITI LEFTISTS AND POLITICAL OPPOSITION

SUPPORTERS BEHIND THE GOK. THE LEFTIST KUWAIT TRADE

UNION FEDERATION (KTUF), IN A JULY 20 STATEMENT,

DENOUNCED IRAQI ALLEGATIONS AS TANTAMOUNT TO A DIRECT

MILITARY ACTION AGAINST KUWAIT AND CALLED FOR DIALOGUE

BETWEEN IRAQ AND KUWAIT. DR. AHMED AL-RUBIE, A LEADING

"GROUP OF 32" POLITICAL OPPOSITION MEMBER KNOWN FOR HIS

MARXIST BELIEFS AND ANTI-GOVERNMENT RHETORIC,

EDITORIALIZED IN AL-WATAN JULY 21 THAT, WHEN IT COMES TO

THE ISSUE OF KUWAIT\'S NATIONAL SOVEREIGNTY, "PEOPLE ARE

NOT DIVIDED BETWEEN RULERS AND RULED, BUT WOULD RATHER

MANIFEST A UNIFIED NATIONAL KUWAITI UNANIMITY." END

SUMMARY.

2. (U) IN A JULY 20 STATEMENT CARRIED BY THE KUWAIT

STATE NEWS AGENCY (KUNA) AND REF A FBIS REPORT, THE

KUWAIT TRADE UNION FEDERATION (KTUF) DENOUNCED IRAQI

ALLEGATIONS AGAINST KUWAIT AS "TANTAMOUNT TO DIRECT

MILITARY ACTION." THE STATEMENT ASSERTED, "WE ARE NOT

HERE TO RECALL EITHER THE FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE EXTENDED

BY KUWAIT OR THE SACRIFICES INCURRED BY THE KUWAITI

PEOPLE BECAUSE WE BELIEVE THIS IS OUR NATIONAL

OBLIGATION AND WE REFRAIN FROM REMINDING BROTHERS OF

WHAT WE HAVE DONE FOR THEM." THE KTUF CALLED FOR

"SINCERE" DIALOGUE ON OIL ISSUES AND DEMARCATION AND

QUESTIONED WHY IRAQ HAD SETTLED BORDER ISSUES WITH

JORDAN AND SAUDI ARABIA DURING THE GULF WAR, BUT NOT

WITH KUWAIT. THE STATEMENT CONCLUDED WITH A CALL TO

"JOIN FORCES AGAINST OUR COMMON ENEMY. LET ALL GUNS AND

MISSILES BE AIMED AT THOSE WHO KILL PALESTINIAN

CHILDREN."

3. (U) KUWAIT UNIVERSITY PHILOSOPHY PROFESSOR DR.

AHMED AL-RUBIE, IN A JULY 21 AL-WATAN (ARABIC, LEFTIST)

EDITORIAL, WROTE: "IRAQ KNOWS THAT THE PROBLEM WHICH IT

RAISED HAS TO DO WITH THE NATIONAL SOVEREIGNTY OF

KUWAIT. IN SUCH ISSUES, PEOPLE ARE NOT DIVIDED BETWEEN

RULERS AND RULED, BUT WOULD RATHER MANIFEST A UNIFIED

NATIONAL KUWAITI UNANIMITY. IF WE BELIEVE IN UNITY AS

AN ARAB DESTINY, IT IS NECESSARY THAT WE BEHAVE

EQUITABLY, FOR THERE IS NO SMALL ARAB AND NO BIG ARAB OR

STRONG ARAB AND WEAK ARAB. ALSO, WE HAVE TO UNDERSTAND

THAT NOBODY CAN REALIZE HIS INTERESTS AT THE EXPENSE OF

OTHERS."

4. (C) COMMENT. BOTH THE KTUF AND AL-RUBIE, WHO IS

CLOSELY ASSOCIATED WITH THE KTUF, ARE KNOWN FOR THEIR

ANTI-GOVERNMENT, LEFTIST RHETORIC (REF B). THE

HARVARD-EDUCATED AL-RUBIE IS DESCRIBED AS A MARXIST AND

IS ONE OF THE MORE RADICAL MEMBERS OF THE "GROUP OF 32"

EX-PARLIAMENT DEPUTIES, WHO FORM THE CORE OF KUWAIT\'S

POLITICAL OPPOSITION TO THE GOVERNMENT AND THE

RECENTLY-CREATED NATIONAL COUNCIL. THE SUPPORT WHICH

BOTH THE KTUF AND AL-RUBIE HAVE EXPRESSED FOR THE

GOVERNMENT IS, THEREFORE, SIGNIFICANT. AS THE KUWAITI

AMBASSADOR TO TUNIS NOTED IN REF C, SADDAM IS MAKING A

MISTAKE IF HE THINKS HE CAN EXPLOIT POLITICAL

DIFFERENCES IN KUWAIT. IF ANYTHING, THE CRISIS WILL

PROBABLY HELP TO UNIFY KUWAIT\'S VARIOUS POLITICAL AND

SOCIAL GROUPINGS FULLY BEHIND THE AMIR. WE EXPECT THAT

IF AL-RUBIE HAS ALREADY CHIMED IN, IT WON\'T BE LONG

BEFORE OTHER OPPOSITION MEMBERS OF FAR LESS RADICAL

VIEWPOINTS, POSSIBLY INCLUDING CHAMBER OF COMMERCE

CHAIRMAN ABDULAZIZ AL-SAGER, WILL BE PRESSURED TO MAKE

SIMILAR STATEMENTS OF SUPPORT. END COMMENT.
