SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE ELECTION: VOTE COUNTING UNDERWAY ON MARCH 12

CLASSIFIED BY POLITICAL SECTION CHIEF MATT HARRINGTON. REASONS:

1.5 (B) AND (D).

SUMMARY

-------

1. (C) POLLING STATIONS IN HARARE AND CHITUNGWIZA CLOSED AT 7:00

P.M. ON MARCH 11, LEAVING LONG LINES OF VOTERS IN SOME AREAS UNABLE

TO CAST BALLOTS. THE VERIFICATION OF BALLOT BOXES BEGAN THE

MORNING OF MARCH 12 AND VOTE-COUNTING AT MOST BALLOT CENTERS DID

NOT BEGIN UNTIL EARLY AFTERNOON. GIVEN THE LATE START, WE DO NOT

EXPECT COMPLETE RESULTS TO BE ANNOUNCED UNTIL SOMETIME ON MARCH 13.

MEANWHILE, THE MDC SECRETARY-GENERAL WAS ARRESTED -- AND LATER RELEASED

AFTER

AN OVERNIGHT DETENTION -- AND FORMALLY

CHARGED WITH TREASON, WHILE AT LEAST FIVE MDC ELECTION AGENTS

ASSIGNED TO MONITOR THE COUNTING OF VOTES WERE ARRESTED. A SECOND

AMERICAN CITIZEN HAS BEEN ARRESTED, BUT WE HAVE BEEN UNSUCCESSFUL

IN GAINING ACCESS TO HIM TO LEARN ADDITIONAL DETAILS. END SUMMARY.

DISENFRANCHISEMENT OF HARARE AND CHITUNGWIZA VOTERS

--------------------------------------------- ------

2. (C) ALL POLLING STATIONS IN HARARE AND CHITUNGWIZA CLOSED ON

MARCH 11 AT 7:00 P.M. OR SHORTLY THEREAFTER (EVEN THOUGH MOST HAD

NOT EVEN OPENED UNTIL 12:00 P.M. IN DEFIANCE OF A HIGH COURT

ORDER). VOTERS STANDING IN LINE AT THAT POINT WERE NOT ALLOWED TO

CAST BALLOTS, DESPITE EARLIER ASSURANCES TO THE CONTRARY FROM

CONFIDENTIAL

SENIOR GOZ OFFICIALS, AND WERE FORCED TO DISPERSE BY HEAVILY-ARMED

POLICE. THE MDC INFORMED US THAT 1500 PEOPLE WERE STANDING IN LINE

AT BOTH ZUVARABUDA SCHOOL IN GLEN NORAH HIGH DENSITY SUBURB AND AT

DZIVARASEKWA COMMUNITY CENTER WHEN THE POLLING STATIONS CLOSED. A

NORWEGIAN DIPLOMAT TOLD US HE HAD PERSONALLY WITNESSED THE CLOSING

OF A STATION IN GLENVIEW TOWNSHIP WITH 700 WOULD-BE VOTERS STILL IN

LINE. THEY WERE ANGRY BUT LEFT PEACEFULLY AFTER BEING TOLD BY

POLICE TO DISPERSE. IT IS CLEAR THAT THERE WAS A SUBSTANTIAL

DISENFRANCHISEMENT OF VOTERS IN THESE TWO URBAN AREAS WHICH IS

EXTREMELY DIFFICULT TO QUANTIFY. OUR NORWEGIAN COLLEAGUE ESTIMATES

THAT A TOTAL OF AT LEAST 10-15,000 VOTERS WERE STANDING IN LINE IN

GREATER HARARE AND CHITUNGWIZA WHEN THE STATIONS CLOSED. THAT

FIGURE, OF COURSE, DOES NOT INCLUDE THE POTENTIALLY LARGE NUMBERS

OF PEOPLE WHO DID NOT VOTE BECAUSE THEY WERE SCARED AWAY BY THE

ABSURDLY LONG LINES. WE NOTE THAT IN HARARE AND ENVIRONS, THE

CONSTITUENCY LISTS AVERAGE 5,000 VOTERS PER POLLING PLACE, WITH

SOME AS HIGH AS 7,000. THE AVERAGE FOR BULAWAYO WAS 3,000.

ELSEWHERE IN THE COUNTRY, THE CONSTITUENCY AVERAGE WAS 1,000

REGISTERED VOTERS PER STATION.

COUNTING

--------

3. (U) THE VERIFICATION OF BALLOT BOXES AND THEIR CONTENTS

CONSUMED MUCH OF THE MORNING OF MARCH 12, ACCORDING TO THE STATE-

CONTROLLED ZIMBABWE BROADCASTING CORPORATION (ZBC). COUNTING AT

MOST CENTERS THEN BEGAN IN THE EARLY AFTERNOON. INFORMATION

MINISTER JONATHAN MOYO ANNOUNCED AT A PRESS CONFERENCE THAT RESULTS

WOULD LIKELY BE ANNOUNCED ON MARCH 13 AND HE EXPRESSED SATISFACTION

AT HOW THE ELECTION PROCESS HAD BEEN CONDUCTED.

4. (C) COMMENT: THERE IS ONE COUNTING CENTER FOR EACH OF

ZIMBABWE\'S 120 CONSTITUENCIES. WHEN THE COUNTING AT EACH CENTER IS

FINISHED, THE RESULTS WILL BE COMMUNICATED VIA PROVINCIAL COMMAND

CENTERS TO THE REGISTRAR-GENERAL\'S OFFICE IN HARARE, WHICH WILL

ANNOUNCE RESULTS. DURING THE JUNE 2000 PARLIAMENTARY ELECTIONS,

THE REGISTRAR-GENERAL ANNOUNCED RESULTS CONSTITUENCY BY

CONSTITUENCY ON THE GOZ-CONTROLLED TELEVISION AND RADIO. THIS

TIME, IF THE CONSTITUENCY RESULTS BEGIN TO LOOK BAD FOR THE RULING

PARTY, THE R.G. COULD WAIT TO RELEASE THE NUMBERS. AN EU CONTACT

WAS TOLD BY A GOZ OFFICIAL THAT THE RG WOULD WAIT TO RELEASE

FIGURES UNTIL HE HAS A "CRITICAL MASS" OF NUMBERS.

PLANNED CIVIL UNREST

--------------------

8. (C) MUDZINGWA TOLD US THAT DEMONSTRATIONS ORGANIZED BY THE MDC

AND ZIMBABWE CONGRESS OF TRADE UNIONS (ZCTU) COULD BEGIN AS EARLY

AS THE EVENING OF MARCH 12. ASKED WHY THE OPPOSITION PARTY WAS

CONTEMPLATING LAUNCHING SUCH ACTIVITIES BEFORE THE POLL RESULTS ARE

ANNOUNCED, MUDZINGWA SAID THE ANGER AT ZANU-PF IN URBAN AREAS WAS

AT ITS PEAK -- AFTER THE LONG QUEUES AND DISENFRANCHISEMENT OF SO

MANY VOTERS -- AND THE MDC WANTED TO BUILD ON THAT MOMENTUM. NOW

WAS THE TIME TO CAPITALIZE ON THIS SENTIMENT WITH ACTIONS INTENDED

TO PRESSURE ZANU-PF TO ACCEPT AN MDC VICTORY.

SECOND AMERICAN ARRESTED

------------------------

9. (C) A SECOND NON-OFFICIAL AMERICAN HAS BEEN ARRESTED -- THIS

TIME IN THE MASHONALAND CENTRAL TOWN OF MVURWI ON MARCH 11. WE

WERE INFORMED OF HIS ARREST BY A PRIVATE CITIZEN. SINCE THE

GOVERNMENT HAS REFUSED SO FAR TO PROVIDE ACCESS TO HIM, WE HAVE

BEEN UNABLE TO DETERMINE THE DETAILS OF HIS CASE, ALTHOUGH WE HAVE

LEARNED HE IS A FREE-LANCE JOURNALIST WHO REPORTEDLY ENTERED ON A

TOURIST VISA. WE ARE LODGING A STRONG PROTEST VIA DIPLOMATIC NOTE

WITH THE MFA CONCERNING THE GOZ\'S REFUSAL BOTH TO NOTIFY US OF THE

ARREST AND TO PROVIDE ACCESS. MEANWHILE, THE AMERICAN CITIZEN

ARRESTED IN MUTARE ON MARCH 8 REMAINS IN DETENTION ON CHARGES OF

"POSSESSING DANGEROUS WEAPONS." (COMMENT: HE REPORTEDLY HAD A

HUNTING KNIFE AND A CAN OF PEPPER SPRAY IN HIS POSSESSION, BUT WE

SUSPECT THAT THE POLICE WERE ANNOYED BY HIS EFFORTS TO HELP

EMBASSY OBSERVATION EFFORT

--------------------------

10. (U) TWENTY-FIVE EMBASSY OBSERVERS WERE DEPLOYED AROUND THE

COUNTRY ON THE TWO ELECTION DAYS PLUS THE EXTRA DAY OF VOTING IN

HARARE AND CHITUNGWIZA. DURING THAT THREE-DAY PERIOD, OUR TEAMS

COVERED 15,140 KILOMETERS AND VISITED 470 POLLING STATIONS

(APPROXIMATELY 10 PERCENT OF THE TOTAL NUMBER OF STATIONS). THE

EMBASSY ELECTION OBSERVATION EFFORT HAS NOW FULLY COME TO ITS END.
