1. (C) MFA POLITICAL DIRECTOR GIANCARLO ARAGONA IS SCHEDULED

TO MEET YOU 4/11, AFTER ARRIVING IN WASHINGTON 4/10.

ARAGONA\'S ROLE WAS INSTRUMENTAL, IF NOT DECISIVE, IN THE

GOI\'S DECISION TO POSTPONE ITS UNCHR CANDIDACY AND THEREBY

MAKE WAY FOR THE U.S. TO RETURN TO THE COMMISSION IN 2003.

ALTHOUGH NO "DEAL MAKING" WAS INVOLVED, IT\'S FAIR TO SAY THE

ITALIANS EXPECT SOMETHING MORE FROM THIS GESTURE THAN AN

ABSTRACT EXPRESSION OF USG APPRECIATION AND GRATITUDE.

2. (C) A THEME WE FREQUENTLY HEAR IN ROME IS THAT THE USG

DOES NOT TREAT ITALY ON A PAR WITH CERTAIN EU PARTNERS (E.G.,

U.K., GERMANY, FRANCE). THE ITALIANS WOULD LIKE TO BE (AND

TO BE SEEN TO BE) CONSULTED ON MAJOR ISSUES BEFOREHAND. SUCH

CONSULTATION WOULD NOT NECESSARILY CHANGE ITALIAN VIEWS. AS

IS THE CASE IN OTHER INTERNATIONAL FORA, ITALIAN VIEWS ON

MATTERS BEFORE THE COMMISSION TEND TO BE STRAITJACKETED BY

THE "EU CONSENSUS." HOWEVER, AS NOTED REF B, THE GOI UNDER

PRIME MINISTER SILVIO BERLUSCONI CONSIDERS THE ASSERTION OF

NATIONAL INTERESTS WITHIN EU FORUMS TO BE AS LEGITIMATE FOR

ITALY AS IT IS FOR COUNTRIES SUCH AS GERMANY AND FRANCE.

3. (C) UNDER THESE CIRCUMSTANCES, ADVANCE CONSULTATION

PRESENTS US CONSIDERABLE ADVANTAGES. WITH THE SHARP

EXCEPTION OF THE DEATH PENALTY, WHICH ARAGONA IS LIKELY TO

RAISE, GOI VIEWS ON MATTERS BEFORE THE UNCHR TEND TO BE

PARALLEL OR, AT LEAST, SYMPATHETIC TO OURS. WE STAND TO GAIN

FROM AN INVIGORATED GOI VOICE IN EU COUNCILS THAT HAS ALREADY

HEARD AND TAKEN INTO ACCOUNT USG VIEWS. AND THERE ALSO WILL

BE OCCASIONS WHEN GOI ADVICE CAN HELP SHAPE FOR THE BETTER

OUR APPROACH TO THE EU AND COMMISSION MEMBERS AT LARGE.
