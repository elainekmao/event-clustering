SUBJECT: ARMY CHIEF WARNS SYRIA ABOUT SUPPORT FOR PKK

REF: (A) ANKARA 8898, (B) ANKARA 8522

(U) CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR MARK R. PARRIS, EMBASSY

ANKARA. REASON: 1.5 (A), (B) AND (D).

1. (U) TURKISH LAND FORCES COMMAND (TLFC) COMMANDER

GENERAL ATILLA ATES WAS QUOTED IN THE LOCAL PRESS SEPTEMBER

16 AS SAYING THAT TURKEY WAS "RUNNING OUT OF PATIENCE" WITH

SYRIA\'S ALLEGED SUPPORT OF THE SEPARATIST/TERRORIST

KURDISTAN WORKERS PARTY (PKK). THE CONSERVATIVE "TURKIYE"

NEWSPAPER\'S IHA NEWS AGENCY REPORTED ATES AS ASSERTING THAT

BY "SUPPORTING THE `BANDIT\' APO (PKK LEADER ABDULLAH

OCALAN)," SYRIA HAD "CONTAMINATED TURKEY WITH THE CALAMITY

OF TERRORISM." HE WARNED THAT TURKEY "HAD NO MORE PATIENCE

LEFT."

2. (C) ATES\'S COMMENTS WERE ALL THE MORE POINTED BECAUSE

THEY WERE DELIVERED DURING A VISIT TO TROOPS IN HATAY

PROVINCE, SOVEREIGNTY OVER WHICH SYRIA CLAIMS AND WHICH IS

THEREFORE A MAJOR IRRITANT IN RELATIONS BETWEEN ANKARA AND

DAMASCUS. ACCORDING TO AN OFFICIAL OF THE TURKISH GENERAL

STAFF (TGS), WHO SPOKE WITH EMBASSY CONTACTS SEPTEMBER 18, TGS\'

SENIOR LEADERSHIP DECIDED THAT A PUBLIC WARNING TO SYRIA ABOUT

THE PKK WAS LONG OVERDUE. THE FACT THAT IT WAS DELIVERED BY ARMY

CHIEF ATES IN HATAY UNDERSCORED THE SERIOUSNESS OF THE MESSAGE

BEHIND IT.

3. (C) IN THREE SEPARATE CONTACTS SEPTEMBER 17, MFA U/S

HAKTANIR AND TWO OTHER SENIOR MFA OFFICIALS LIKEWISE

REITERATED THAT TURKEY IS "OUT OF PATIENCE" WITH SYRIA, AND

THAT THE DIPLOMATIC INITIATIVES OF EARLIER THIS YEAR HAD "COME

TO A STOP."

4. (S) COMMENT: IT IS CONCEIVABLE THAT THE TURKS ARE LAYING

OUT A GROUNDWORK OF DIPLOMATIC AND PUBLIC WARNINGS TO DAMASCUS TO

PROVIDE A RATIONALE FOR AN EVENTUAL DECISION ON A MILITARY

RESPONSE TO PKK ACTIVITY IN SYRIA. SOME OF THE LANGUAGE THEY

HAVE USED RECALLS OUR POST FACTO JUSTIFICATION OF OUR CRUISE

MISSILE ATTACKS ON OSAMA BIN LADEN FACILITIES LAST MONTH. WE

HAVE NO INDICATION A DECISION TO ACTUALLY CARRY OUT AN ATTACK HAS

BEEN REACHED. WE RECOMMEND U.S.INTELLIGENCE ATTENTION TO

INDICATIONS OF TURKISH MILITARY AND PKK ACTIVITY IN THE

SYRIAN-TURKISH BORDER REGION, AS WELL AS THE BEKAA.

PARRIS
