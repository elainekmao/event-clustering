SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE VIEWS ON DRC PEACE PROCESS

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 06032 251621Z

REFTEL: SECSTATE 200297

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS: 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) POLOFF SPOKE TO CONGO DESK OFFICER MAXWELL

CHIKOROWONDO IN ZIMBABWE\'S MFA ON OCTOBER 18 ABOUT

ZIMBABWE\'S VIEWS ON RECENT EVENTS REGARDING THE DRC. HE

STATED THAT THE OCTOBER 14-15 MAPUTO SUMMIT GAVE NEW

MOMENTUM TO THE LUSAKA PEACE PROCESS. REAFFIRMING THE

KAMPALA DISENGAGEMENT AGREEMENT REACHED IN APRIL, THE

STATE PLAYERS AT MAPUTO AGREED TO PULL BACK THEIR

RESPECTIVE FORCES 15 KM TO CREATE A 30 KM BUFFER ZONE

BETWEEN THEM; RWANDA UNDERSCORED ITS INTENTION TO PULL

BACK 200 KM. SOUTH AFRICAN PRESIDENT MBEKI APPARENTLY

SAID AT THE MEETING THAT THE DRC REBEL GROUPS WOULD BE

EXPECTED TO COMPLY WITH THE PULLBACK, TO WHICH THEY

ALREADY AGREED AT KAMPALA EARLIER THIS YEAR. THE STATE

DEFENSE CHIEFS ACCOMPANIED THEIR HEADS-OF-STATE TO

MAPUTO, AND AGREED TO MEET AGAIN SOON TO ENSURE THAT

THEIR RESPECTIVE MILITARIES IMPLEMENTED THE AGREEMENT ON

THE GROUND. WHEN ASKED ABOUT THE RECENT REPORTS OF NEW

CLASHES IN THE DRC, CHIKOROWONDO STRESSED THAT

ZIMBABWEAN FORCES WERE NOT/NOT INVOLVED AND THAT NO

CLASHES HAD OCCURRED IN KATANGA OR KASAI AS REPORTED IN

THE PRESS.

2. (C) ON OCTOBER 23, POLOFF LEARNED FROM CHIKOROWONDO

THAT THE STATE DEFENSE CHIEFS, AS AGREED IN MAPUTO, MET

OVER THE OCTOBER 21-22 WEEKEND TO HAMMER OUT AN

IMPLEMENTATION PLAN. THE DESK OFFICER DID NOT KNOW

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 03 HARARE 06032 251621Z

EXACTLY WHAT ZIMBABWE DEFENSE MINISTER MAHACHI HAD

WORKED OUT AT THE MEETING, BUT HE HAD SEEN NO SIGNS OF

NEW ZIMBABWEAN TROOP MOVEMENTS ON THE GROUND. HE DID

SAY, HOWEVER, THAT MAHACHI AND THE OTHER DEFENSE CHIEFS

ARE DUE TO REPORT THEIR ACTIONS OCTOBER 28-29 TO ANOTHER

HEADS-OF-STATE MEETING IN MAPUTO INVOLVING ZIMBABWE,

RWANDA, SOUTH AFRICA, AND THE DRC. CHIKOROWONDO

BELIEVED THAT THE MAPUTO MEETINGS WOULD GIVE NEW LIFE TO

THE PEACE PROCESS.

3. (C) COMMENT: ZIMBABWE\'S MFA REMAINS EVER-HOPEFUL

THAT A LASTING PEACE WILL SOON COME TO THE DRC. SEVERAL

REPORTS DO INDICATE ZIMBABWE DEFENSE FORCE UNITS WERE

INVOLVED IN THE RECENT FIGHTING IN LOWER EQUATEUR, AND

THAT CLASHES DID OCCUR IN KATANGA AND KASAI, ALBEIT NOT

INVOLVING ZIMBABWEAN FORCES. AS OF LATE, THE DRC HAS

BEEN ABSENT FROM THE HEADLINES IN ZIMBABWE, AND WE

SUSPECT THAT PRESIDENT MUGABE HAS NOT GIVEN IT HIS FULL

ATTENTION DUE TO THE DISTRACTION OF DOMESTIC POLITICS.

END COMMENT.
