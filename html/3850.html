SUBJECT: MEETING WITH AFM HUNG

1. (U) CIRF: IN A MEETING WITH AMBASSADOR ON OCTOBER 2

(SEPTEL WILL COVER DISCUSSION OF THE TRIAL OF NGUYEN THI

NGOC HANH), ASSISTANT FOREIGN MINISTER NGUYEN DUC HUNG

EXPRESSED THE GVN\'S "DISAPPOINTMENT" OVER THE RECENTLY

REPORTED RECOMMENDATION OF THE US COMMISSION FOR

INTERNATIONAL RELIGIOUS FREEDOM (CIRF) TO INCLUDE VIETNAM ON

COUNTRIES OF "PARTICULAR CONCERN" REGARDING ABUSES OF

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 HANOI 02369 01 OF 02 011045Z

RELIGIOUS FREEDOM. (UNOFFICIAL TRANSLATION OF DIPLOMATIC

NOTE IN PARA 4.) HE CLAIMED THAT THIS RECOMMENDATION DID

NOT "SHOW GOODWILL," NOR WAS IT "USEFUL" IN THE PROCESS OF

DIALOGUE ON RELIGIOUS FREEDOM AND HUMAN RIGHTS ISSUES. HE

EMPHASIZED THAT IT WAS "NOT GOOD" FOR THE PROMOTION OF

BILATERAL RELATIONS, AND THAT IT WOULD "NOT HELP TO

ACCELERATE" THE PROCESSES OF DIALOGUE. HE INSISTED THAT

INCLUDING VIETNAM ON THIS LIST WOULD BE "VERY BAD," ALTHOUGH

HE ADMITTED THAT DIALOGUE WAS BETTER THAN NO DIALOGUE. HE

URGED AMBASSADOR TO CONVEY THIS CONCERN TO THE USG, WHICH

AMBASSADOR AGREED TO DO, WHILE ALSO REITERATING THE VALUE OF

DIALOGUE INCLUDING WHEN THE TWO SIDES DO NOT AGREE.

2. (U) ACTOR: AMBASSADOR RAISED THE CASE OF NOTED

VIETNAMESE ACTOR DON DUONG, WHO IS REPORTED BY INTERNATIONAL

MEDIA (AS WELL AS IN NUMEROUS LETTERS FROM HOLLYWOOD

ENTERTAINMENT FIGURES) TO HAVE HAD HIS PASSPORT CONFISCATED

AND TO BE UNDER THE THREAT OF ADDITIONAL PUNISHMENTS BY

VIETNAMESE AUTHORITIES. HE NOTED THE HARMFUL AFFECT THAT

SUCH ACTIONS WOULD HAVE ON VIETNAM\'S INTERNATIONAL IMAGE AND

ASKED AFM HUNG\'S ASSISTANCE IN COORDINATING WITH THE

MINISTRY OF CULTURE TO DETERMINE THE TRUE FACTS OF THE CASE.

3. (U) APEC: AMBASSADOR EXPLAINED TO AFM HUNG

ARRANGEMENTS THAT WOULD BE IN PLACE AT THIS YEAR\'S APEC

LEADERS\' MEETING TO ALLOW FOR WHISPER INTERPRETERS AT THE

TWO SOCIAL EVENTS AND URGED THAT THE GVN PROVIDE ITS OWN

INTERPRETER FOR POSSIBLE DISCUSSIONS BETWEEN PRESIDENT BUSH

AND PRIME MINISTER KHAI AT THE DINNER AND LUNCH. AFM HUNG

NOTED THAT THE GVN HAD BEEN IN COORDINATION WITH THE

GOVERNMENT OF MEXICO ON THESE ARRANGEMENTS. AMBASSADOR ALSO

BRIEF HIM TO ENSURE A MORE PRODUCTIVE DISCUSSION. AFM HUNG

4. (UI) INFORMAL TRANSLATION OF DIPLOMATIC NOTE:

NO. 407/NG-CM

THE MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS OF THE SOCIALIST REPUBLIC OF

VIETNAM PRESENTS ITS COMPLIMENTS TO THE DEPARTMENT OF STATE

OF THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA AND WOULD LIKE TO INFORM IT

THAT:

ON 30 SEPTEMBER 2002, THE U.S. COMMISSION ON INTERNATIONAL

RELIGIOUS FREEDOM SUBMITTED A REPORT TO RECOMMEND THAT THE

SECRETARY OF STATE OF THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA CLASSIFY

SIPDIS

VIETNAM IN A LIST OF COUNTRIES OF PARTICULAR CONCERN IN THE

AREA OF RELIGIOUS FREEDOM. THIS IS AN ACT THAT VERY MUCH

LACKS GOODWILL AND IS FULL OF FIXED VIEWS OF THE U.S. CIRF

TOWARD VIETNAM.

IT NEEDS TO BE REAFFIRMED THAT IN VIETNAM THE RIGHT OF

FREEDOM OF BELIEF AND OF NON-BELIEF IS GUARANTEED BY THE

CONSTITUTION AND IS EXERCISED IN REAL LIFE IN THE SOCIALIST

REPUBLIC OF VIETNAM. THE REALITY OF THIS RIGHT OF FREEDOM

HAS BEEN WIDELY RECOGNIZED BY THE INTERNATIONAL PUBLIC

OPINION, INCLUDING THE OBJECTIVE PUBLIC OPINION OF US

CITIZENS.

IN THE LARGER INTERESTS OF VIETNAM-US RELATIONS, WHICH ARE

EXPANDING, AND WITH AN ATTITUDE OF GOODWILL, VIETNAM

RECEIVED REPRESENTATIVES OF THE USCIRF IN FEBRUARY 2002 SO

THAT THEY COULD WITNESS BIG CHANGES IN VIETNAM WITH THEIR

OWN EYES. THE COMMISSION ALSO RECEIVED ADDITIONAL

INFORMATION THROUGH MEETING WITH VIETNAM\'S DELEGATION OF

RELIGIOUS LEADERS IN WASHINGTON IN MAY 2002. HOWEVER,

UNFORTUNATELY THIS COMMISSION STILL DELIBERATELY EVALUATED

THE RELIGIOUS SITUATION IN VN INCORRECTLY AND SUBMITTED A

NON-CONSTRUCTIVE RECOMMENDATION. WE DEEM THE ABOVE

RECOMMENDATION, IF ACCEPTED AND APPROVED, WILL NOT HELP THE

PROCESS OF CONSTRUCTIVE AND CANDID DIALOGUE AIMED AT

STRENGTHENING MUTUAL UNDERSTANDING BETWEEN THE PEOPLES OF

THE TWO COUNTRIES, AND IT WILL AFFECT NEGATIVELY THE

BENEFICIAL AND DEVELOPING RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN VIETNAM AND

THE UNITED STATES.

THE MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS OF THE SOCIALIST REPUBLIC OF

VIETNAM STRONGLY REQUESTS THE DEPARTMENT OF STATE OF THE

UNITED STATES OF AMERICA NOT TO ACCEPT THE ABOVE- MENTIONED

RECOMMENDATION FULL OF FIXED IDEAS OF THE USCIRF.
