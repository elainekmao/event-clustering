SUBJECT: USDOL TEAM PRODUCES AMBITIOUS PLAN OF ACTION FOR WORK UNDER THE LABOR MOU

REF: A) 00 HANOI 2590; B) HANOI 145

1. SUMMARY: THE FEBRUARY 26-MARCH 1 TRIP TO HANOI BY A US

DEPARTMENT OF LABOR (USDOL) PROJECT DESIGN TEAM WAS A

SUCCESS. AFTER EXTENSIVE CONSULTATION WITH VIETNAMESE LABOR

OFFICIALS AND THE ILO AND OTHER TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE

PROVIDERS, THE TEAM PRODUCED A PACKAGE OF PROJECT DESIGNS

ADDRESSING A NUMBER OF PRIORITY LABOR ISSUES THAT WAS

ENTHUSIASTICALLY RECEIVED BY THE GVN. IF ALL GOES ACCORDING

TO PLAN, SELECTED EXECUTING AGENCIES, SUCH AS THE ILO , WILL

SUBMIT PROJECT PROPOSALS FOR GVN AND USDOL REVIEW BY THE END

OF APRIL AND PROJECT IMPLEMENTATION COULD BEGIN THIS SUMMER.

POST WISHES TO THANK THE PROJECT TEAM FOR ITS FINE WORK AND

IMPORTANT CONTRIBUTION TO USG EFFORTS ON THE ISSUE OF LABOR

RIGHTS. END SUMMARY.

2. A USDOL PROJECT DESIGN TEAM VISIT TO HANOI FEBRUARY 26-

MARCH 7 WAS A COMPLETE SUCCESS. THEY PRODUCED A PACKAGE OF

TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE IN ALL SIX AREAS OF COOPERATION

OUTLINED IN OUR BILATERAL MOU. THE TEAM, CONSISTING OF GARY

RUSSELL, DIRECTOR FOR MULTILATERAL PROGRAMS AND NICOLE

SAYRES, INTERNATIONAL PROJECT MANAGER, MET EXTENSIVELY WITH

REPRESENTATIVES OF THE MINISTRY OF LABOR, INVALIDS AND

SOCIAL AFFAIRS (MOLISA). THEY ALSO MET WITH THE OTHER

SOCIAL PARTNERS - THE VIETNAMESE GENERAL CONFEDERATION OF

LABOR (VGCL) AND THE VIETNAMESE CHAMBER OF COMMERCE AND

INDUSTRY (VCCI)- AS WELL AS THE ILO AND OTHER DONORS

PROVIDING TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE IN THE LABOR FIELD.

3. THE VISITORS WERE ENTHUSIASTICALLY RECEIVED BY VIETNAMESE

COUNTERPARTS WHO HAD PREPARED AND CLEARED AMONG ALL SOCIAL

PARTNERS A TIGHTLY FOCUSED, PRIORITIZED REQUEST FOR

TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE UNDER THE MOU. VICE MINISTER NGUYEN

LUONG TRAO FURTHER CLARIFIED GVN DESIRES AND PRIORITIES IN

THE OPENING MEETING. FOLLOW-UP MEETINGS WITH THE INDIVIDUAL

MOLISA DEPARTMENTS RESPONSIBLE FOR EACH FUNCTIONAL AREA AS

WELL AS THOSE WITH THE VGCL AND VCCI REINFORCED AND PROVIDED

FURTHER DETAIL TO THE ORIGINAL REQUEST. DONOR

REPRESENTATIVES DOCUMENTED VIETNAM\'S PROGRESS IN A NUMBER OF

GENERALLY SUCCESSFUL LABOR PROJECTS AND ASSESSED WHERE USDOL

ASSISTANCE MIGHT BEST BE ALLOCATED.

4. IMPRESSED BY THE WARMTH OF THEIR RECEPTION, THE LEVEL OF

PREPARATION OF THEIR INTERLOCUTORS AT MOLISA AND DONORS\'

STATEMENTS THAT THEIR ASSISTANCE IN THE LABOR AREAS WAS

BEING PRODUCTIVELY USED, THE USDOL TEAM FORMED THE OPINION

THAT VIETNAM WILL BE A GOOD PARTNER IN A PROGRAM OF

TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE AND PROPOSED AN AMBITIOUS PACKAGE OF

PROJECTS. THIS PACKAGE AIMS TO ADDRESS CORE LABOR STANDARDS

CONCERNING COLLECTIVE BARGAINING, ELIMINATION OF CHILD LABOR

AND EMPLOYMENT DISCRIMINATION. THE PACKAGE INCLUDES

PROJECTS TO IMPROVE EMPLOYMENT SERVICE CENTERS; TO INCREASE

GVN CAPACITY TO DESIGN UNEMPLOYMENT INSURANCE AND VOLUNTARY

SOCIAL INSURANCE SCHEMES; TO EXPAND EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITIES

FOR PEOPLE WITH DISABILITIES; TO PROMOTE INDUSTRIAL

RELATIONS AND APPLICATION OF CORE LABOR STANDARDS THROUGH

ASSISTANCE IN LABOR DISPUTE RESOLUTION AND TRIPARTISM; AND

TO ESTABLISH HIV/AIDS WORK-BASED EDUCATION AND PREVENTION

INITIATIVES. THE TOTAL BUDGET FOR THE PACKAGE, WHICH WILL

BE NO LESS THAN US $3 MILLION, WILL BE DETERMINED ONCE THE

FORMAL PROJECT DESIGNS HAVE BEEN COMPLETED AND APPROVED.

(THE PROJECT PACKAGE WAS FORWARDED TO EAP/BCLTV VIA E-MAIL.)

5. THE ILO WILL BE THE EXECUTING AGENCY FOR ALL BUT THE

PROJECTS INVOLVING SOCIAL INSURANCE AND PEOPLE WITH

DISABILITIES. THE SOCIAL AND UNEMPLOYMENT INSURANCE PROJECT

WILL BE EXECUTED BY THE NATIONAL ACADEMY OF SOCIAL

INSURANCE. AND THE VIETNAM ASSOCIATION OF HANDICAPPED, AN

ORGANIZATION WELL KNOWN TO BOTH VIETNAM AND WASHINGTON

AGENCIES, WILL EXECUTE THE PROJECT ON EMPLOYMENT FOR PEOPLE

WITH DISABILITIES.

6. THE DOL PROJECT DESIGN TEAM DID NOT DEAL DIRECTLY WITH

THE FINAL PROJECT SUBJECT UNDER THE LABOR MOU, ELIMINATION

OF ABUSIVE CHILD LABOR, A TOPIC WHICH IS THE RESPONSIBILITY

OF ANOTHER OFFICE AT USDOL. HOWEVER, THE VISITING TEAM DID

CONFIRM THAT THE USDOL WOULD SUPPORT ILO/IPEC EFFORTS IN

CONDUCTING A BASE LINE SURVEY AND DEVELOPING A NATIONAL

ACTION PLAN FOR THE ELIMINATION OF THE WORST FORMS OF CHILD

LABOR. ACCORDING TO MR. RUSSELL, SOMEONE FROM THE USDOL

OFFICE RESPONSIBLE FOR THESE PROGRAMS WILL EITHER TRAVEL

HERE SHORTLY AND/OR WORK THROUGH THE ILO REGIONAL OFFICE IN

BANGKOK TO MOVE FORWARD ON THIS PROJECT.

7. THE NEXT STEP IS FOR THE EXECUTING ENTITIES TO DEVELOP

FORMAL PROJECT PROPOSALS IN CONSULTATION WITH MOLISA AND

SUBMIT THEM TO USDOL. TEAM MEMBERS INDICATED THAT THEY

HOPED TO RECEIVE THESE BY LATE APRIL. ONCE USDOL HAS

APPROVED THEM, THEN THE PROJECTS WILL BE PRESENTED TO MOLISA

FOR FINAL APPROVAL. THIS STEP HOPEFULLY WILL BE A FORMALITY

SINCE THE MINISTRY WILL BE INVOLVED IN DRAFTING THE PROJECT

PROPOSALS. MOLISA ONCE AGAIN ASSURED THAT, BECAUSE THIS IS

TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE PROVIDED UNDER AN AGREEMENT WITH

MOLISA, THESE PROJECTS WILL NOT NEED TO GO THROUGH THE

MINISTRY OF PLANNING AND INVESTMENT\'S LONG, ARDUOUS APPROVAL

PROCESS APPLIED TO MUCH DEVELOPMENT ASSISTANCE. IF ALL GOES

WELL, PROJECT IMPLEMENTATION COULD BEGIN THIS SUMMER.

8. COMMENT: POST WOULD LIKE EXPRESS ITS SINCERE THANKS FOR

THE IMPRESSIVE JOB DONE BY MR. RUSSELL AND MS. SAYRES.

THEY IMPRESSED ALL WHO MET THEM WITH THEIR THOROUGH

PREPARATION AS WELL AS THEIR WILLINGNESS TO LISTEN AND

CONSULT. AS A RESULT THEY PREPARED A TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE

PROGRAM THAT MATCHES U.S. GOALS WITH VIETNAMESE OBJECTIVES

IN A WAY THAT COMPLIMENTS TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE BEING

PROVIDED BY OTHER DONORS. MR. RUSSELL AND MS. SAYRES HAVE

PLAYED A KEY ROLE IN SHAPING THE NASCENT WORKING PARTNERSHIP

BETWEEN THE U.S. AND VIETNAM ON LABOR ISSUES, AND THE

PROJECT PROPOSALS - ONCE IMPLEMENTATION BEGINS - WILL ADD TO

THE CONSIDERABLE DONOR ACTIVITY UNDERWAY IN THIS IMPORTANT

SECTOR OF VIETNAM\'S DEVELOPING ECONOMY. END COMMENT.
