1. (SBU) SUMMARY: THE UNDP RESREP IN HARARE INFORMED

HEADS OF MISSION AND AID DIRECTORS JANUARY 23 THAT THE

GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE (GOZ) HAS NOT YET RESPONDED TO

THE UNDP ADMINISTRATOR\'S LETTER TO PRESIDENT MUGABE ON

BREAKING THE LAND REFORM IMPASSE, BUT EXPECTS A RESPONSE

SOON. THE RESREP BELIEVES THAT GOZ TECHNOCRATS ARE

DEVISING A POSITIVE RESPONSE TO THE LETTER, AND THAT THE

GOZ LEADERSHIP IS CONSIDERING ALTERNATIVE LAND PROGRAMS.

THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION (CFU) IS PULLING TOGETHER A

NEW TEAM TO NEGOTIATE WITH THE GOZ ON WAYS TO HAND OVER

FIVE MILLION HECTARES OF LAND FOR RESETTLEMENT IN AN

ORDERLY WAY. THE RESREP\'S SUNNY APPRAISAL OF THE LAND

REFORM SITUATION DID NOT SIT WELL WITH MOST OF THE

DONORS, AND CALLS INTO QUESTION THE UNDP\'S UNDERSTANDING

OF THE LAND SITUATION HERE. END SUMMARY.

2. (SBU) THE UNITED NATIONS DEVELOPMENT PROGRAM (UNDP)

RESIDENT REPRESENTATIVE IN HARARE, VICTOR ANGELO,

CONVENED A MEETING OF HEADS OF MISSION AND AID DIRECTORS

JANUARY 23 TO INFORM THEM THAT THE ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT

HAD NOT YET RESPONDED TO THE LETTER UNDP ADMINISTRATOR

MARK MALLOCH-BROWN SENT TO PRESIDENT MUGABE ON DECEMBER

15 (REFTEL). ANGELO ADDED THAT HE EXPECTED AN ANSWER

PAGE 03 HARARE 00488 01 OF 02 241249Z

SOON, PERHAPS BY THE END OF THE WEEK OF JANUARY 29, AS

HE HAD TOLD SEVERAL SENIOR GOVERNMENT INTERLOCUTORS THAT

IT WAS URGENTLY NEEDED IN ORDER FOR DONORS TO UNDERSTAND

WHAT THE ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT WANTED NEXT AND WHAT KIND

OF ENGAGEMENT IT WOULD LIKE. BASED ON HIS SOUNDINGS OF

THE "TECHNICAL PEOPLE," THE UNDP RESREP EXPECTED A

"POSITIVE RESPONSE," AS THE TECHNOCRATS WISH THE DONORS

TO PERCEIVE THE ANSWER AS A SIGNAL THAT THE ZIMBABWEAN

GOVERNMENT WANTS TO RE-ENGAGE. HE CONTINUED THAT HE HAD

PROMPTED THESE INTERLOCUTORS TO INCLUDE IN THE RESPONSE

A COUPLE OF PARAGRAPHS DEALING WITH GENERAL ISSUES

(E.G., THE RULE OF LAW). HE TOLD THEM THAT THEY SHOULD

PICK A COUPLE OF PROPOSALS FROM THE MALLOCH-BROWN LETTER

THAT ARE EASILY IMPLEMENTED AS A START AND INCLUDE

LANGUAGE THAT PROMOTES INTERNAL DIALOGUE, A CREDIBLE

INFORMATION SYSTEM, A TECHNICAL SUPPORT UNIT WITHIN

UNDP, AND SIGNALS CLEAR SUPPORT FOR BOTH COMPLEMENTARY

APPROACHES AND THE PRIVATE SECTOR INITIATIVE.

3. (SBU) ANGELO CHARACTERIZED "COMPLEMENTARY

APPROACHES" (TO LAND RESETTLEMENT) AS "LOCAL-LEVEL

INITIATIVES" DEVELOPED AND IMPLEMENTED IN A HIGHLY

PARTICIPATORY MANNER BY CONCERNED DISTRICT/VILLAGE

STAKEHOLDERS. (NOTE: COMPLEMENTARY APPROACHES HAVE BEEN

PROPOSED AND SUPPORTED BY DONORS AND OTHER STAKEHOLDERS

SINCE THE 1998 DONORS\' LAND CONFERENCE AS ALTERNATIVE

RESETTLEMENT MODELS TO GOVERNMENT\'S MORE RIGID,

CENTRALLY-ADMINISTERED APPROACHES TO THIS ISSUE. THEY

HAVE MET WITH ONLY MIXED RESULTS TO DATE. THEY ARE SEEN

BY SOME KEY PLAYERS AS A POSSIBLE MECHANISM FOR BREAKING

THE CURRENT IMPASSE THROUGH A LIMITED RE-ENGAGEMENT BY

DONORS IN SPECIFIC, MUTUALLY-ACCEPTED SCHEMES. HOWEVER,

TO DATE, GOVERNMENT HAS YET TO APPROVE ANY SUCH

ALTERNATIVES TO ITS CURRENT "FAST-TRACK" PROGRAM. END

NOTE.)

4. (SBU) THE PRIVATE SECTOR INITIATIVE (PSI) IS ANOTHER

"ALTERNATIVE" PLAN DEVELOPED BY KEY LOCAL STAKEHOLDERS,

INCLUDING THE NON-GOVERNMENTAL LEADERSHIP OF THE LAND

TASK FORCE OF THE NATIONAL ECONOMIC CONSULTATIVE FORUM

(NECF). THE NECF IS AN IMPORTANT TRIPARTITE FORUM THAT

HAS BEEN LARGELY SIDELINED SINCE THE FARM INVASIONS AND

THE BEGINNING OF THE FAST-TRACK PROGRAM. IT FOCUSES ON

FINANCIAL SOLUTIONS UNDERTAKEN BY THE PRIVATE SECTOR FOR

THE ACQUISITION AND RESETTLEMENT OF AN INITIAL TRANCHE

OF 200 FARMS. ALTHOUGH SERIOUS CONCERNS REMAIN

REGARDING THE ALTERNATIVE PLAN, INCLUDING THE DEGREE OF

GOVERNMENT INVOLVEMENT AND CONTROL, AND ITS FAILURE TO

ADDRESS THE RULE OF LAW, THE PSI IS INCREASINGLY FAVORED

WITHIN GOZ CIRCLES, WITH A CABINET-LEVEL REVIEW

SCHEDULED FOR MID-FEBRUARY.

5. (SBU) THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION (CFU) AND THE

ZIMBABWEAN GOVERNMENT BELIEVE IT IS NECESSARY TO SIT

AROUND THE TABLE AND AGREE ON A WAY FORWARD, ANGELO TOLD

US. THE CFU HAS A FOUR-MEMBER TEAM LED BY DAVID IRVINE,

6. (C) FINALLY, THE UNDP RESREP ADVISED THAT HIS AGENCY

HAD BEEN WORKING ON A PREPAREDNESS PLAN, IN THE EVENT

ALL ITS EFFORTS COME TO NOUGHT AND A HUMANITARIAN CRISIS

EMERGES. THE PLAN WILL BE DISCUSSED WITH DONORS AT THE

TECHNICAL LEVEL ON FEBRUARY 7. WHEN ASKED ABOUT THE

UNDP RULE OF LAW MISSION THAT WAS TO HAVE FOLLOWED THE

OCTOBER TECHNICAL MISSION, A FLUSTERED ANGELO RESPONDED

THAT ONE WAS IN TOWN, KEEPING A VERY LOW PROFILE. HE

WOULD NOT ELABORATE ITS MANDATE EXCEPT TO SAY THAT HE

CHARACTERIZED IT AS "OPENING DIALOGUE" ON THIS THORNY

SUBJECT.

7. (C) COMMENT: ANGELO STRAINED HIS CREDIBILITY WITH

THIS MEETING. MOST HEADS OF MISSION EXPRESSED OPEN

SKEPTICISM ABOUT THE UTILITY OF HIS QUEST, WITH ONE

AMBASSADOR NOISILY WALKING OUT IN APPARENT DISGUST WITH

THE RESREP\'S SUNNY ACCOUNT OF GOVERNMENT INTENTIONS. AT
