SUMMARY

-------

1. AS UN/EC PEACE ENVOYS VANCE AND OWEN MET WITH

CROATIAN AUTHORITIES IN ZAGREB LAST FRIDAY, THE

COUNTRY\'S PARLIAMENT ERUPTED INTO DEBATE OVER THE

PASSAGE OF A LAW GRANTING AMNESTY TO SOME OF THOSE

WHO FOUGHT FOR SERBIA AGAINST CROATIA. A SECOND

SESSION OF CROATIA\'S NEW NATIONAL DEFENSE COUNCIL

APPROVED THE FORMATION OF A SPECIAL COMMITTEE TO

PROMOTE MILITARY COOPERATION WITH B-H. LOCAL

RETURN TO THEIR HOMES IN THE PINK ZONE AROUND

ATROCITIES IN B-H.

CROATIAN SABOR PASSES AMNESTY LAW

-------------------------------------

2. DURING THE SECOND AND FINAL DAY OF ITS LATEST

SESSION (ON SEPTEMBER 25), CROATIA\'S SABOR PASSED

THE AMNESTY LAW PROPOSED BY PRESIDENT TUDJMAN AFTER

SOME STORMY DEBATES. THE LEGISLATION EXCUSES ALL

THOSE WHO FOUGHT AGAINST CROATIA BUT DID NOT COMMIT

WAR CRIMES. THE LOCAL PRESS NOTED THAT THE ACTION

WAS

TIMED TO COINCIDE WITH THE VANCE-OWEN MEETINGS IN

ZAGREB TO PROVE THAT CROATIA IS DOING ITS PART TO

REESTABLISH PEACE IN CROATIA.

3. MOST OF THE OPPOSITION PARTIES WERE AGAINST THE

MEASURE. CROATIAN NATIONAL PARTY AND SOCIAL LIBERAL

PARTY LEADERS CALLED THE LAW ONE-SIDED, AND

SECOND SESSION OF CROATIAN DEFENSE COUNCIL

------------------------------------------

5. ON SEPTEMBER 26, THE CROATIAN DEFENSE COUNCIL

HELD ITS SECOND SESSION IN ZAGREB. THE AGENDA

INCLUDED THE TUDJMAN-IZETBEGOVIC ADDENDUM TO THE

CROATIA/B-H FRIENDSHIP AND COOPERATION AGREEMENT AND

ITS IMPLEMENTATION, THE ARMY\'S ROLE IN PEACETIME,

CROATIA\'S BORDER PROBLEMS WITH SLOVENIA, AND

INTERNAL SECURITY IN THE FACE OF TERRORIST AND

PARAMILITARY ACTIONS. THE COUNCIL ESTABLISHED A

COMMITTEE TO COORDINATE WITH B-H LEADERS IN ORDER TO

RESOLVE OUTSTANDING BILATERAL ISSUES, RESTRAIN

AGGRESSION AGAINST B-H, AND RESTORE PEACE.

RETURN OF REFUGEES TO THE PINK ZONE OF SECTOR SOUTH

--------------------------------------------- ------

6. FOLLOWING THE GOVERNMENT\'S RECENT DECISION TO

STRIP SOME INDIVIDUALS OF REFUGEE STATUS, SIBENIK

MUNICIPAL LEADERS ORDERED DISPLACED PERSONS (CROATS)

FROM THE SKRADIN REGION AND THE DRNIS DISTRICT TO

LEAVE HOTELS IN SIBENIK AND VODICE AND RETURN TO

THEIR HOMES. THIS IS THE FIRST GROUP SO AFFECTED.

7. MEANWHILE, UNPROFOR REPRESENTATIVES IN SECTOR

8. IN THE LAST 10 DAYS, 200 CROATS AND HUNGARIANS

HAVE BEEN EXPELLED FROM BARANJA IN SECTOR EAST--MOST

OF THEM FROM THE AREA BETWEEN BELI MANASTIR AND

HUNGARY. SERBIAN AUTHORITIES IN BARANJA HOPE THIS

WILL PREVENT THE ANNOUNCED RETURN OF CROATIAN

REFUGEES TO THIS TERRITORY. DURING THE LAST 15

DAYS, 11 PERSONS WERE ALSO KILLED IN BARANJA.

SABOR CALLS FOR MOVEMENT ON PRIVATIZATION

UNCLASSIFIED

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ZAGREB 01854 03 OF 04 281543Z

-----------------------------------------

9. DURING LAST WEEK\'S SABOR SESSION, THE GOVERNMENT

ANNOUNCED THAT IT IS PREPARING A LAW THAT WILL BREAK

APART THE STATE-OWNED ENERGY MONOPOLY "INA." THE

GOVERNMENT WILL KEEP MAJORITY CONTROL OVER THE

EXPLOITATION OF OIL AND NATURAL GAS ALTHOUGH OTHER

INVESTORS WILL STILL BE ABLE TO BUY SHARES.

-

10. MEANWHILE, THE AGENCY FOR RESTRUCTURING AND

DEVELOPMENT OF CROATIA HAS ISSUED 600 DECISIONS

ABOUT THE PRIVATIZATION OF OTHER REMAINING

GOVERNMENT-OWNED ENTERPRISES. THE ENTIRE WORTH OF

ALL THESE ENTERPRISES AMOUNTS TO 3.2 BILLION MARKS,

AND THEY EMPLOY 49,527 WORKERS. ABOUT HALF OF THE

SHARES WERE BOUGHT BY WORKERS IN SEVERAL IMPORTANT

FIRMS (THE CANDY ENTERPRISE "KRAS" AND THE SHIPYARD

"ULJANIK" IN PULA). PRIVATIZATION EFFORTS WILL

CONTINUE BECAUSE FEWER THAN ONE-FOURTH OF ELIGIBLE

FIRMS HAVE BEEN SOLD OFF THUS FAR. TO ACCELERATE

THE PROCESS, ENTERPRISES WILL ALSO BE OFFERED TO

FOREIGN PURCHASERS-
