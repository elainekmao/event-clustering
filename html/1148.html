SUBJECT: DEMARCHE ON NO ANCHORING PROPOSAL

REF: STATE 109225

1. IN RESPONSE TO REFTEL, WE CONTACTED MARTEN KOOPMANS,

SENIOR STAFFER IN THE DUTCH TRANSPORT MINISTRY\'S TRANSPORT

SAFETY DEPARTMENT, WHO WILL HEAD THE DUTCH DELEGATION TO THE

IMO SUBCOMMITTEE MEETING ON SAFETY OF NAVIGATION (NAV).

KOOPMANS TOLD US THAT THE U.S. PROPOSALS HAVE BEEN DRAWN UP

IN CLOSE COOPERATION WITH THE DUTCH. DUTCH TRANSPORT

MINISTRY ADVISOR LAMEIJER, WHO CHAIRS THE NAV SUBCOMMITTEE

WORKING-GROUP ON ROUTING, HAS BEEN IN DIRECT CONTACT WITH

NOAA POC LINDY JOHNSON.

2. KOOPMANS SAID THAT, DESPITE OVERALL DUTCH SUPPORT FOR

THE PROPOSALS, THERE IS ONE MINOR POINT FOR CONCERN. HE

SAID THERE IS A SMALL RISK THAT THE IMPLICIT EFFECT OF

ESTABLISHING "NO ANCHORING" AREAS MAY BE THAT IT WILL GIVE

SHIPPERS THE OPPORTUNITY TO USE OTHER DELICATE AREAS, THAT

HAVE NOT BEEN ASSIGNED AS SUCH.

3. THE DUTCH DELEGATION WILL CONSIST OF KOOPMANS, LAMEIJER,

AND NAV SUBCOMMITTEE CHAIRMAN KEES POLDERMAN.
