SUBJECT: ITALIANS SUPPORT ALBANIAN AMMO INCINERATOR PROJECT

BUT CAN\'T COMMIT FUNDING

REF: STATE 135759

1. THE GOI SUPPORTS THE AMMUNITION INCINERATOR FOR ALBANIA

BUT DOES NOT HAVE FUNDS AVAILABLE NOW TO CONTRIBUTE,

ACCORDING TO MAURIZIO MASSARI, HEAD OF THE MFA\'S BALKAN

AFFAIRS OFFICE. HE TOLD POL-MIL OFFICER IT WAS NOT A MATTER

OF WHETHER ITALY WOULD CONTRIBUTE, BUT WHEN. ITALY CONSIDERS

THE PROJECT POLITICALLY IMPORTANT, HE SAID. MASSARI NOTED

THAT IT WOULD NOT BE EASY TO CHANNEL FUNDS THROUGH THE EU

BECAUSE THE PROJECT INVOLVES AMMUNITION RATHER THAN ARMS,

WHICH WOULD REQUIRE REWRITING THE MANDATE OF ONE OF THE

RELEVANT EU COMMITTEES. MASSARI SAID HE WOULD WORK WITHIN

THE EU AND BILATERALLY TO URGE CONCRETE SUPPORT FOR THE

PROJECT.

2. POL-MIL OFFICER AND CANADIAN DCM NATHALIE DUBE INITIALLY

PRESENTED THE DEMARCHE TO ALESSANDRO CEVESE, DIRECTOR OF THE

MFA\'S ARMS CONTROL AND DISARMAMENT OFFICE. CEVESE CONFIRMED

THAT FUNDS ARE NOT AVAILABLE THROUGH HIS (FUNCTIONAL)

DIRECTORATE, AS HE TOLD THE CANADIAN DELEGATION THAT

DISCUSSED THE PROJECT WITH HIM IN JUNE. HE SAID HE WOULD

COORDINATE WITH MASSARI, IN THE RELEVANT GEOGRAPHIC

DIRECTORATE, SINCE ANY FUNDING ITALY CAN PROVIDE WILL LIKELY

COME FROM THAT SOURCE.
