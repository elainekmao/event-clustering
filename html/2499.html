SUBJECT: GOI ASSISTANCE TO PAKISTAN

REF: STATE 18844

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 02 ROME 00818 151738Z

1. THE MAJOR COMPONENT OF ITALY\'S BILATERAL ASSISTANCE EFFORT

IN PAKISTAN IS THE $80 MILLION IN DEBT RELIEF EXTENDED IN

DECEMBER, 2001. RAFFAELA DI MARO AND GIORGIO LECCESI, BOTH

WITH THE DEPARTMENT OF INTERNATIONAL AFFAIRS OF THE ITALIAN

MINISTRY OF ECONOMY AND FINANCE, TOLD ECOUN AND ECONOFF THAT

THE GOI SHARES US CONCERN ABOUT THE ECONOMIC STABILITY OF

PAKISTAN. DE MARO SAID PAKISTAN HAD PLEDGED TO USE THE MONEY

FREED BY THE GOI DEBT FORGIVENESS FOR HEALTH AND EDUCATION

PROGRAMS. LECCESI WAS INTRIGUED WITH THE DEPARTMENT OF

DEFENSE\'S PROPOSED REIMBURSEMENT TO PAKISTAN FOR ITS

LOGISTICAL SUPPORT. HE IS UNCERTAIN WHETHER THERE IS SUCH A

PROVISION UNDER ITALIAN LAW BUT INDICATED THAT HE WOULD RAISE

THIS ISSUE WITH OTHER MINISTERIAL COLLEAGUES. HE ADDED THAT

HE SEES A GROWING LEVEL OF DONOR FATIGUE IN THE INTERNATIONAL

COMMUNITY BORN OF CONTRIBUTIONS TO AFGHANISTAN BUT AFFECTING

ALSO PAKISTAN AS WELL AS CONCERN ABOUT PAKISTAN\'S POLITICAL

REGIME.

2. DONATO SCIOSCIOLI OF MFA\'S DIRECTORATE OF DEVELOPMENT AND

COOPERATION TOLD ECONOFF THAT THE GOI HAS ONE OTHER

INITIATIVE UNDERWAY IN PAKISTAN: THE GOI HAS OFFERED A $7

MILLION SOFT LOAN TO PAKISTAN TO BE USED IN THE PROMOTION OF

SMALL AND MEDIUM SIZE BUSINESS IN 2002. MFA IS AWAITING A

PAKISTANI RESPONSE.
