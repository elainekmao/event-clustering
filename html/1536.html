SUBJECT: GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE CONTINUES TO STALL ON

RESPONSE TO MALLOCH-BROWN LETTER

REF: HARARE 488 AND PREVIOUS

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS 1.5 B/D.

1. (C) UNITED NATIONS DEVELOPMENT PROGRAM (UNDP)

RESIDENT REPRESENTATIVE VICTOR ANGELO TOLD CHARGE ON

FEBRUARY 21 THAT HE STILL HAD NOT RECEIVED A FORMAL

RESPONSE TO THE LETTER UNDP ADMINISTRATOR MARK

MALLOCH-BROWN SENT TO PRESIDENT MUGABE ON DECEMBER 15,

2000 (REFTEL). HE HAD HEARD THAT A LETTER WAS READY

FOR SIGNATURE BY THE MINISTER OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS, DR.

I.S.G. MUDENGE, ON FEBRUARY 16. THE MINISTRY OF

FINANCE OBJECTED TO SOME OF THE LANGUAGE CONTAINED IN

THE LETTER, THUS STALLING ITS TRANSMISSION. MUDENGE

IS CURRENTLY IN NEW YORK AND WILL GIVE UN SECRETARY

GENERAL ANNAN A PREVIEW OF ITS PROBABLE CONTENTS AND

SEEK THE LATTER\'S REACTION. ANGELO REMARKED THAT THE

SITUATION AT PRESENT "DOESN\'T TAKE US VERY FAR," AND

THOUGHT THAT IT WAS "BECOMING LATE" TO WORK WITH THE

GOVERNMENT.

2. (C) COMMENT: IF THE RESPONSE TO MALLOCH-BROWN\'S

LETTER IN FACT DOES NOT GO "VERY FAR," THIS WOULD BE A

PREDICTABLE OUTCOME BASED ON PRESIDENT MUGABE AND HIS

LIEUTENANTS\' OFT-STATED PLEDGE THAT "THERE IS NO GOING

BACK ON OUR [FAST-TRACK] LAND REFORM PROGRAM." ALL

INDICATIONS TO DATE ARE OF AN INTRANSIGENT POSITION

DICTATED BY A HEAD OF STATE WHO REMAINS OUT OF STEP
