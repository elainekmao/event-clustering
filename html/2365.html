SUBJECT: AN INSIDER\'S VIEW OF RUGGIERO\'S SACKING

REF: 2001 ROME 06753

CLASSIFIED BY: A/POL MINISTER COUNSELOR GARY ROBBINS.

REASON: 1.6 X.6

-------------

GOOD RIDDANCE

-------------

1. (C) ON 7 JANUARY, THE HEAD OF THE MFA\'S BALKAN OFFICE,

MAURIZIO MASSARI (STRICTLY PROTECT) TOLD POLOFF THAT HE WAS

"NOT SORRY TO SEE FORMER FM RUGGIERO GO," AND THAT THIS VIEW

WAS SHARED BE MANY AT HIS LEVEL (ROUGHLY, THE EQUIVALENT OF

OFFICE DIRECTOR IN THE DOS). MASSARI SAID THAT THERE WAS

SOME "BITTERNESS" AMONG HIS COLLEAGUES OVER THE "LOST

OPPORTUNITY" CAUSED BY RUGGIERO\'S PUBLIC BICKERING WITH HIS

BOSS AND REFUSAL TO CONSULT WITH HIS FELLOW MINISTERS OVER

MATTERS OF GOVERNMENT POLICY. RUGGIERO COULD HAVE USED HIS

REPUTATION AMONG OTHER EURO-MINISTERS AND HIS SKILLS AS AN

ACCOMPLISHED DIPLOMAT TO ADVANCE ITALY\'S STANDING AS A WORLD

LEADER. THAT CHANCE WAS LOST WHEN RUGGIERO REFUSED TO PLAY

THE POLITICAL GAME. MASSARI ALSO CONFIRMED OUR VIEWS,

REPORTED SEPTEL, THAT RUGGIERO\'S DEPARTURE WAS A REFLECTION

OF INTER-GOVERNMENTAL AND PERSONALITY CLASHES, AND DID NOT

REFLECT ANY MOVEMENT TOWARDS A MORE ISOLATIONIST, LESS

PRO-EUROPEAN FOREIGN POLICY BY THE GOI.

------------

HE\'S NO DINI

------------

2. (C) MASSARI COMPARED RUGGIERO\'S PERFORMANCE WITH THAT OF

FORMER FM DINI, WHO WAS, IN MASSARI\'S VIEW, ALSO A SKILLED

INTERNATIONAL DIPLOMAT BUT, IN CONTRAST TO RUGGIERO, A TEAM

PLAYER. FROM A PERSONAL PERSPECTIVE, MASSARI HOPED THAT THE

NEXT FM WOULD PAY MORE ATTENTION TO ITALY\'S ROLE IN BALKAN

STABILIZATION, AN AREA ALMOST TOTALLY IGNORED BY RUGGIERO.

----------

WHO\'S NEXT

----------

3. (C) WHEN ASKED TO SPECULATE ON RUGGIERO\'S SUCCESSOR,

MASSARI SAID THAT IT WAS IMPOSSIBLE TO POINT TO ANY ONE

INDIVIDUAL. HOWEVER, HE HAD JUST RETURNED FROM A MEETING

CHAIRED BY THE DIRECTOR GENERAL FOR EUROPEAN AFFAIRS (A/S

EQUIVALENT) AND OFFICE DIRECTORS, WHERE CONSENSUS SPECULATION

WAS THAT WHATEVER THE CHOICE, IT WOULD BE MADE MORE ON THE

BASIS OF POLITICAL LOYALTY THAN FOREIGN POLICY ACUMEN.

MASSARI ALSO SAID HE WOULD "NOT BE SURPRISED" IF ITALY\'S

AMBASSADOR TO THE EU (AND FORMER MFA SECRETARY GENERAL)

UMBERTO VATTANI, WHO "ENJOYS PM BERLUSCONI\'S FULL CONFIDENCE"

WAS BROUGHT BACK FROM BRUSSELS TO RUN THE

TECHNICAL/SUBSTANTIVE OPERATIONS OF THE MINISTRY, POSSIBLY AS

AN UNDER SECRETARY OF STATE OR (AGAIN) AS SECRETARY GENERAL

OF THE MFA.

4. (C) COMMENT. WHILE MASSARI\'S VIEW MAY NOT BE SHARED

ACROSS THE ENTIRE MFA, PARTICULARLY AMONG THOSE BROUGHT IN BY

RUGGIERO, WE HEARD SIMILAR RUMBLINGS FROM HIGH-LEVEL CONTACTS

IN THE MINISTRY PRIOR TO RUGGIERO\'S RESIGNATION (REFTEL). WE

WILL CONTINUE TO PROBE OUR INTERLOCUTORS FOR THEIR THOUGHTS

ON RUGGIERO\'S DEPARTURE, AND MORE IMPORTANTLY, ANY CHANGES IN

THE DIRECTION OF ITALIAN FOREIGN POLICY HIS FIRING MAY

PORTEND.

5. (U) MINIMIZE CONSIDERED.

POPE
