SUBJECT: GERMAN GOVERNMENT (BMZ) PROPOSES AN EXTERNAL

INDEPENDENT EVALUATION OF WFP\'S DEVELOPMENT PORTFOLIO -

USMISSION REQUEST FOR PARTIAL USAID FUNDING

-------

SUMMARY

-------

1. THE GERMAN MINISTRY OF ECONOMIC COOPERATION AND

DEVELOPMENT (BMZ) HAS PROPOSED AN INDEPENDENT EVALUATION OF

WFP\'S DEVELOPMENT PORTFOLIO AND EXPRESSED THE WISH THAT THE

EVALUATION BE CONDUCTED WITH OTHER DONOR PARTNERS. DENMARK

AND THE NETHERLANDS ARE SUPPORTIVE. A TERMS OF REFERENCE

(TOR) PAPER WAS DISCUSSED INFORMALLY WITH DONORS DURING THE

RECENT THIRD REGULAR SESSION OF THE WFP EXECUTIVE BOARD AND

IS SUMMARIZED HEREWITH. USMISSION IS FULLY SUPPORTIVE OF A

U.S.G. PARTIAL FUNDING/"BUY-IN" TO THIS PROPOSED REVIEW OF

WFP\'S ENABLING DEVELOPMENT POLICY AND REQUESTS USAID/FFP

FINANCIAL SUPPORT. END SUMMARY.

----------

BACKGROUND

----------

2. THE GERMAN MINISTRY OF ECONOMIC COOPERATION AND

DEVELOPMENT (BMZ) HAS PROPOSED AN INDEPENDENT EVALUATION OF

WFP\'S DEVELOPMENT PORTFOLIO. DR. HORST BREIR (HEAD OF

EVALUATION, BMZ) ADDRESSED AN INFORMAL DONOR SESSION OF THE

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 03 ROME 05511 131052Z

THIRD REGULAR SESSION OF WFP\'S EXECUTIVE BOARD ON OCTOBER

23, AND PRESENTED A DRAFT TOR NON-PAPER. HE CONFIRMED THAT

GERMANY IS PREPARED TO ACTIVELY PARTICIPATE IN THIS EXTERNAL

INDEPENDENT EVALUATION AND EXPRESSED THE WISH THAT THE

EVALUATION BE CO-FUNDED AND GUIDED IN COLLABORATION WITH

OTHER DONOR PARTNERS. A QUALIFIED CONTRACTING FIRM WOULD BE

SELECTED THROUGH AN INTERNATIONAL TENDERING PROCESS. DENMARK

AND THE NETHERLANDS AFFIRMED THEIR SUPPORT FOR THIS

UNDERTAKING.

3. THE STUDY WOULD BE A FOLLOW ON TO A TRIPARTITE EVALUATION

OF WFP, CONDUCTED BY CANADA, THE NETHERLANDS AND NORWAY OVER

THE PERIOD 1991-94. IT WOULD SPECIFICALLY LOOK AT THE

PRESENT STATUS OF WFP\'S FOOD AID AND DEVELOPMENT POLICY, AS

ADOPTED BY THE EXECUTIVE BOARD IN MAY 1999; THE LEVEL OF

FUNDING, TRENDS AND SCOPE OF WFP\'S DEVELOPMENT PORTFOLIO;

AND THE ABILITY OF WFP TO MEET ACCEPTABLE STANDARDS WITH

REGARD TO TARGETING, PARTICIPATION, RESULTS-BASED MANAGEMENT

AND PROGRAM IMPACT. THE EVALUATION WILL PRINCIPALLY FOCUS ON

LESSONS LEARNED FROM THE IMPLEMENTATION OF WFP\'S 1999

POLICY, INCLUDING IDENTIFICATION OF DEVELOPMENT CONTEXTS IN

WHICH FOOD AID MAKES SENSE AND POSSIBLE NEED FOR GREATER WFP

FOCUS AND CONCENTRATION OF ITS DEVELOPMENT INTERVENTIONS.

4. BMZ PROPOSES A YEAR-AND-A-HALF TIME FRAME (JANUARY 2003-

JUNE 2004). A RESTRICTED STEERING COMMITTEE OF DONOR

EVALUATION EXPERTS (INCLUDING A WFP EVALUATION MEMBER) WOULD

BE ESTABLISHED (TOTAL 5-6 PERSONS) TO GUIDE THE PROCESS. WFP

WOULD BE ASKED TO PARTICIPATE IN ALL STAGES OF THE

EVALUATION AND ACT AS A FACILITATOR OF THE PROCESS, WHILE

THE DONORS ACTIVE INVOLVEMENT WILL ENSURE THE HIGH DEGREE OF

-------

COMMENT

-------

5. USMISSION SEES THIS BMZ PROPOSED INDEPENDENT EVALUATION

AND OFFER OF PARTIAL FUNDING AS BOTH TIMELY AND HIGHLY

DESIRABLE. THE REVIEW WILL ANALYZE BOTH THE STATUS OF WFP

PROGRESS RELATED TO ENGAGING HOST COUNTRY GOVERNMENTS AND

INSTITUTIONS IN THE PLANNING, IMPLEMENTATION AND MANAGEMENT

OF ITS DEVELOPMENT INTERVENTIONS AND THE DEGREE TO WHICH WFP

AND ITS UN SISTER AGENCIES HAVE BEEN ABLE TO EFFECTIVELY

LINK TOGETHER FOOD AID AND FOOD SECURITY STRATEGIES.

USMISSION RECOMMENDS THAT DCHA/FFP AND PPE GIVE DUE

CONSIDERATION TO PARTIAL FUNDING SUPPORT FOR THE PROPOSED

EVALUATION. HALL
