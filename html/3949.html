SUBJECT: BORDER SECURITY ACT - HOST GOVERNMENT VIEWS ON USG

FINGERPRINTING OF ITS CITIZENS

REF: STATE 187180

1. IN RESPONSE TO REFTEL, JORDANIAN MFA CONSULAR

REPRESENTATIVE INFORMED POST OCTOBER 8 THAT, IN PRINCIPLE,

THE GOJ HAS NO OBJECTION TO FINGERPRINTING APPLICANTS IN

CONNECTION WITH VISA ISSUANCE. HE EMPHASIZED THAT THE GOJ

IS SUPPORTIVE OF PROCEDURES DESIGNED TO PROVIDE GREATER

SECURITY TO LEGITIMATE TRAVELERS TO THE U.S. AND THERE ARE

NO LEGAL OR SOCIAL IMPEDIMENTS TO SUCH A PROCEDURE.

2. POST HAS MAINTAINED A CONTINUOUS DIALOGUE WITH THE GOJ

AT HIGH LEVELS TO EXPLAIN THE SIGNIFICANCE OF VISA REFORMS.
