SUMMARY AND COMMENT

-------------------

1. (C) A RECENT GOVERNMENT REPORT PROVIDED SOME LONG-

AWAITED STATISTICS ON DETAINEES, KIA, AND CIVILIANS

FROM THE MAY 1-2 SECTOR WEST BATTLES. IT APPEARS,

HOWEVER, STILL TO LEAVE OPEN QUESTIONS FOR THE

INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY AND FOR THE AREA\'S SERB

POPULATION. THE MAIN CASUALTY OF THE GOC\'S INADEQUATE

AND UNTIMELY EFFORTS TO PROVIDE NUMBERS ON CASUALTIES

AND REFUGEES IS THUS, IRONICALLY, ITS OWN CREDIBILITY.

END SUMMARYCOMMENT.

-

-------------------------------

GOC ISSUES A STATISTICAL REPORT

-------------------------------

2. (U) ON MAY 22, DEPUTY PRIME MINISTER KOSTOVIC

RELEASED A STATEMENT CONTAINING STATISTICAL INFORMATION

ON PEOPLE IN SECTOR WEST AFFECTED BY THE MAY 1-2 WAR

THERE. THE RELEASE OFFERS THE FOLLOWING DATA:

-- A "CENSUS" TAKEN APPARENTLY ON MAY 4 SHOWED 2922

CIVILIANS REMAINING AROUND PAKRAC AND ABOUT 500 TOTAL

IN OKUCANI, STARA GRADISKA, AND JASENOVAC.

-- BETWEEN MAY 8 AND 19, 1719 PEOPLE LEFT AND WENT TO

B-H. 6-7000 CIVILIANS WERE ESTIMATED TO HAVE FLED THE

AREA AHEAD OF THE ADVANCING TROOPS. OF THE REMAINING

SERB POPULATION, 675 HAVE BEEN GRANTED CROATIAN

CITIZENSHIP CERTIFICATES; 1070 HAVE APPLIED FOR SUCH

CERTIFICATES.

-- 188 BODIES WERE FOUND AFTER THE FIGHTING. 127 OF

THESE HAVE BEEN IDENTIFIED. 20 IDENTIFIED AS CIVILIANS

WERE KILLED "IN CROSSFIRE CAUSED BY PARAMILITARY

UNITS," AS WERE ANOTHER 34 WHO "ARE ASSUMED POSSIBLY

BODIES WERE BURIED "IN SEVERAL GRAVES" BY RELEVANT

AUTHORITIES; RELATIVES MAY MAKE INDIVIDUAL INQUIRIES TO

THE CROATIAN RED CROSS.

-- 1494 "MILITARILY CAPABLE" MEN WERE PROCESSED IN

"RECEPTION CENTERS." 917 OF THESE WERE RELEASED

WITHOUT INITIATION OF INVESTIGATORY PROCESS. OF THE

REMAINDER WHO WERE INVESTIGATED, 188 ARE STILL BEING

HELD IN "INVESTIGATION CENTERS."

DATA LEAVES MANY OPEN QUESTIONS

-------------------------------

3. (LOU) THE GOVERNMENT\'S "REPORT" AT LEAST DEFINES

THE PARAMETERS, BUT IT LEAVES OPEN AS MANY QUESTIONS AS

IT ANSWERS. WE NOTE THE FOLLOWING SHORTCOMINGS:

-- NO FIGURE IS PROVIDED FOR WOUNDED-IN-ACTION.

-- IT IS IMPOSSIBLE TO DETERMINE HOW MANY CIVILIANS

REMAIN IN THE AREA, AS THE TOTAL ATTRIBUTED TO A MAY 4

RED CROSS CENSUS (3422) CANNOT BE SQUARED WITH THE

4. (C) IN A CONVERSATION MAY 23 WITH ASSISTANT

MINISTER OF INTERIOR FOR POLICE JOSKO MORIC, POLOFFS

(WHO AT THE TIME HAD ONLY PRESS ACCOUNTS OF THE

"REPORT") ASKED MORIC ABOUT THE LAST DISCREPANCY

"BECAUSE OF INCOMPLETE MEDIA TRANSCRIPTION" (COMMENT:

A FULL RENDERING OF THE GOVERNMENT\'S PRESS RELEASE, AND

NO MORE COMPLETE REPORT HAS BEEN GIVEN TO THE UN.)

MORIC SAID THE NUMBER NOT ACCOUNTED FOR REPRESENTED

PERSONS NOT CHARGED WITH WAR CRIMES BUT OTHER "NORMAL"

CRIMES SUCH AS THEFT. (COMMENT: IF SO, THIS WOULD

VIOLATE THE SPIRIT AND LETTER OF THE GOC\'S AGREEMENT

WITH THE UN.)

CREDIBILITY IS A PROBLEM

------------------------

5. (C) IN ADDITION TO THE DISAPPOINTING INCOMPLETENESS

OF THE REPORT, ITS TONE AND OMISSIONS ALSO CALL INTO

QUESTION ITS CREDIBILITY. SP

ECIFIC POINTS:

-- MORIC ALSO CLAIMED FULL REPORTS, INCLUDING LISTS OF

THE NAMES OF DEAD, DETAINEES, AND OTHERS, HAD BEEN

PROVIDED TO THE ICRC AND UN. WE FOUND, HOWEVER, THAT

AS OF MIDDAY MAY 23, NEITHER ICRC NOR UN OFFICIALS IN

WESTERN SLAVONIA OR ZAGREB HAD ANYTHING BUT THE PRESS

RELEASE SUMMARIZED ABOVE. (ICRC PEOPLE MAKING THE

ROUNDS OF KEY SERBS IN PAKRAC AREA MAY 23 HAD ONLY THE

LIST THAT THE HEAD OF THE BJELOVAR DETENTION CENTER HAD

GIVEN U.S. POLOFFS MAY 18).

-- THE GOVERNMENT HAS MADE NO ATTEMPT TO RECONCILE THE

NUMBER OF KIA (188) IN THIS REPORT WITH THE MUCH LARGER

NUMBER (450) CITED A WEEK AGO BY DEFMIN SUSAK.

ALTHOUGH SUSAK\'S STAFF TOLD DATT HE PULLED THAT FIGURE

OUT OF THIN AIR, IT IS IN THE PUBLIC DOMAIN AND OUGHT

TO HAVE BEEN TAKEN INTO ACCOUNT. (WESTERN SLAVONIA

SERB LEADER VELJKO DZAKULA, TO WHOM WE SHOWED THE

NEWSPAPER ACCOUNT OF THE REPORT, PICKED UP IMMEDIATELY

ON THIS DISCREPANCY, OFFERING IT AS EVIDENCE THAT THE

GOC WAS HIDING SOMETHING.)

-- THE GOC ATTEMPTS TO BLAME ALL THE CIVILIAN DEATHS ON
