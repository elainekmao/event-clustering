SUBJECT: MUGABE DECLARED WINNER OF ZIMBABWE PRESIDENTIAL

ELECTION; MDC LEAVES ITS OPTIONS OPEN

REF: HARARE 644

CLASSIFIED BY POLITICAL OFFICER TODD FAULK FOR REASONS:

1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) SUMMARY: REGISTRAR-GENERAL TOBAIWA MUDEDE HAS

DECLARED PRESIDENT ROBERT MUGABE THE WINNER OF

ZIMBABWE\'S MARCH 9-11 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION. HE BEAT

HIS STRONGEST CHALLENGER IN 22 YEARS BY 427,000 VOTES,

OR 56 PERCENT TO 42 PERCENT. THE NATURE OF SOME OF THE

RESULTS, ESPECIALLY EXTRAORDINARILY HIGH FIGURES FOR

MUGABE IN MASHONALAND DISTRICTS, RAISE CONCERNS OF VOTE-

RIGGING AND FRAUD. INSUFFICIENT VOTER TURNOUT FOR AREAS

OF MDC STRENGTH WAS A KEY FACTOR. MDC PRESIDENT MORGAN

TSVANGIRAI REJECTED THE RESULTS AND CALLED THE ELECTION

SIPDIS

"ILLEGITIMATE." HARARE AND OTHER MAJOR URBAN AREAS

REMAIN CALM, BUT THE ARMY AND POLICE PRESENCE HAS

INCREASED. END SUMMARY.

2. (U) SHORTLY AFTER NOON LOCAL TIME ON MARCH 13,

REGISTRAR-GENERAL TOBAIWA MUDEDE DECLARED ON STATE

TELEVISION AND RADIO THAT PRESIDENT ROBERT MUGABE HAD

BEEN DULY ELECTED TO ANOTHER SIX-YEAR TERM. THE

DECLARATION FOLLOWED THE CONSTITUENCY-BY-CONSTITUENCY

ANNOUNCEMENT OF RESULTS THAT BEGAN IN THE EVENING OF

MARCH 12 AND CONTINUED THROUGH THE NIGHT AND MORNING OF

MARCH 13. THE NATIONWIDE FINAL RESULTS ARE BELOW

3. (C) MUGABE\'S EASY SURPASSING OF THE 50 PERCENT MARK

MEANS THERE WILL BE NO RUNOFF ELECTION. THE MARGIN OF

VICTORY OF NEARLY 427,000 VOTES WAS WIDER THAN EXPECTED.

THE THREE OTHER CANDIDATES WERE EVEN LESS OF A FACTOR

THAN ANTICIPATED, GARNERING LESS THAN 2 PERCENT OF THE

VOTE BETWEEN THEM. THE TOTAL NUMBER OF SPOILED BALLOTS,

132,000, OR 4.2 PERCENT OF TOTAL VOTES CAST WAS SOMEWHAT

HIGHER THAN IN THE 2000 PARLIAMENTARY ELECTION. THE

REGISTRAR-GENERAL DID NOT ANNOUNCE THE RESULTS OF THE

HARARE AND CHITUNGWIZA MAYORAL AND COUNCIL ELECTIONS,

NOR DID HE SAY WHEN HE WOULD. IT SHOULD BE NOTED THAT

NO INDEPENDENT CONFIRMATION OF THE RESULTS IS YET

AVAILABLE. RINDAI CHIPFUNDE OF THE ZIMBABWE ELECTION

SUPPORT NETWORK (ZESN), AN UMBRELLA ORGANIZATION

COMPRISING LOCAL NGOS, TOLD US THAT ZESN WAS STILL

COMPILING REPORTS FROM ITS OBSERVERS AT COUNTING

CENTERS. DAVID COLTART, AN MDC MP AND THE PARTY\'S

ELECTION AGENT FOR BULAWAYO SOUTH, HOWEVER, DID CONFIRM

THAT THE NUMBERS ANNOUNCED FOR THAT CONSTITUENCY ARE

CORRECT.

CONCERNS OF FRAUD

4. (C) A SCAN OF THE RESULTS BY CONSTITUENCY AND A

VARIETY OF REPORTS RAISE SERIOUS CONCERNS ABOUT THE

CONDUCT OF THE POLL AND THE LEVEL OF INTIMIDATION IN THE

PRE-ELECTION PERIOD. NEARLY ALL CONSTITUENCIES SHOW

INCREASES IN THE NUMBER OF VOTES FOR MUGABE OVER THE

2000 PARLIAMENTARY ELECTION FIGURES, EVEN IN URBAN

AREAS. IN ZANU-PF\'S MASHONALAND STRONGHOLD, THE NUMBER

OF VOTES FOR MUGABE INCREASED 20 TO 30 PERCENT OVER

2000, WHILE VOTES FOR TSVANGIRAI CHANGED ONLY

MARGINALLY. PART OF THE INCREASE FOR MUGABE CAN BE

ATTRIBUTED TO HIS NATIONWIDE NAME RECOGNITION VERSUS THE

LACK OF NAME RECOGNITION FOR SOME OF ZANU-PF\'S

PARLIAMENTARY CANDIDATES IN 2000. HOWEVER, THE HUGE

INCREASE IN MASHONALAND NUMBERS (THE MARGIN WAS 15 TO 1

IN SOME DISTRICTS) GIVE RISE TO SUSPICIONS OF VOTE

WERE CHASED AWAY, ABDUCTED OR ARRESTED, SEALS ON BALLOT

BOXES WERE BROKEN, OR THE MDC WAS NOT ALLOWED TO AFFIX

ITS SEALS TO THE BALLOT BOXES. FOUR OF ITS POLLING

THE MDC\'S REACTION

------------------

6. (C) AT A PACKED NOON PRESS CONFERENCE IN HARARE ON

MARCH 13, MDC PRESIDENT MORGAN TSVANGIRAI REJECTED THE

ELECTION RESULTS AND DESCRIBED THE POLLING AS "THE

BIGGEST FRAUD HE HAS EVER WITNESSED." HE STATED THAT

"THE RESULTS DO NOT REFLECT THE WILL OF THE PEOPLE AND

ARE ILLEGITIMATE." HE CONCLUDED BY SAYING THE MDC

REMAINS COMMITTED TO PEACEFUL CHANGE. IN RESPONSE TO

REPORTERS\' QUESTIONS, TSVANGIRAI SAID HE HAS NO

INTENTION OF LEAVING THE COUNTRY OR ABANDONING HIS

PEOPLE. HE DEMURRED ON WHAT THE MDC WOULD DO NO NEXT,

BUT INDICATED THAT COURT ACTION WAS ONE OF ITS OPTIONS.

HE REITERATED THAT HE BELIEVED THE ELECTION HAD BEEN

"MASSIVELY RIGGED." IN AN ASIDE WITH POLOFF, MDC MP

TENDAI BITI SAID THAT THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION WAS JUST

"ROUND 1" AND THE MDC WOULD BE THERE FOR "ROUND 6." HE

CONTINUED THAT HE HAD NO EXPECTATION OF SUCCESS WITH ANY

ALL IS QUIET ... SO FAR

-----------------------

7. (C) SO FAR, THE REACTION ON THE GROUND IN HARARE IS A

PEACEFUL ONE. POST HAD HEARD A REPORT OF A SCUFFLE IN

THE HIGH-DENSITY SUBURB OF MBARE, TO WHICH POLICE

RESPONDED. EMBASSY OFFICERS TOURING THE AREA AND OTHER

SUBURBS SAW NO SIGNS OF DISTURBANCES. POLICE ROADBLOCKS

HAVE BEEN ERECTED ALL OVER THE CITY, BUT FOR THE MOST

PART, THEY ARE ONLY SLOWING AND NOT STOPPING MOTORISTS.

WE HAVE RECEIVED OTHER REPORTS OF ARMY DEPLOYMENTS TO

BULAWAYO AND GWERU IN THE LAST 24 HOURS. ANOTHER REPORT

STATED THAT ARMY UNITS HAD SURROUNDED MDC HEADQUARTERS

IN BULAWAYO AND WERE DEPLOYED TO THE BULAWAYO AIRPORT,

POSSIBLY TO PREVENT MDC LEADERS FROM LEAVING THE

COUNTRY. WE WILL REPORT ON NEW DEVELOPMENTS AS THEY

OCCUR.
