SUBJECT: MUGABE PROMISES FREE AND FAIR ELECTION, NAMES OBSERVER

GROUPS TO BE INVITED

CLASSIFIED BY POLITICAL SECTION CHIEF MATT HARRINGTON. REASONS:

1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (U) APPEARING BEFORE JOURNALISTS ON JANUARY 28, PRESIDENT

MUGABE PROMISED THAT HIS GOVERNMENT WOULD DO ALL IT COULD TO ENSURE

A FREE AND FAIR ELECTION, AND ANNOUNCED THAT INVITATIONS WOULD BE

EXTENDED TO OBSERVER DELEGATIONS FROM THE FOLLOWING: THE OAU;

SADC; COMESA; ECOWAS; THE NON-ALIGNED MOVEMENT; THE COMMONWEALTH

(EXCLUDING THE UNITED KINGDOM); A JOINT AFRICAN, CARIBBEAN, PACIFIC

(ACP)-EU DELEGATION LED BY THE ACP (EXCLUDING THE UNITED KINGDOM);

THE NAACP; AND OTHER INDIVIDUALS AND COUNTRIES TO BE INVITED "IN

DUE COURSE," INCLUDING NIGERIA. HE SAID NIGERIA AND SADC COULD

SEND OBSERVERS "IMMEDIATELY" BUT DID NOT SPECIFY WHEN INVITATIONS

MIGHT BE FORTHCOMING FOR THE OTHER DESIGNATED GROUPS. MUGABE ALSO

APPEALED TO POLITICAL PARTIES, CHURCHES, WAR VETERANS, TRADE

UNIONS, AND OTHER KEY STAKEHOLDERS TO END INCIDENTS OF POLITICAL

VIOLENCE IN THE RUN-UP TO THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION SCHEDULED FOR

MARCH 9-10. HE ANNOUNCED THAT ZIMBABWEAN EMBASSIES ABROAD WOULD

ACCREDIT FOREIGN JOURNALISTS TO COVER THE ELECTION, BUT PROVIDED NO

DETAILS ON WHEN THAT PROCESS WOULD BEGIN OR HOW LONG IT WOULD TAKE.

2. (C) COMMENT: MOST OF THE GROUPS MUGABE NAMED HAVE LITTLE OR NO

CAPACITY FOR OR EXPERIENCE IN ELECTION OBSERVATION, A FACT OF WHICH

THE ZIMBABWEAN PRESIDENT IS LIKELY WELL AWARE. SEVERAL EU

DIPLOMATS TOLD US MUGABE PROBABLY EXPECTS THE EU TO REJECT THE

CONDITIONS PLACED ON ITS PARTICIPATION. ACCORDING TO ALEX KREMER,

A HARARE-BASED EU DIPLOMAT, THE ACP HAS A TINY SECRETARIAT IN

BRUSSELS AND NO EXPERIENCE IN OBSERVING ELECTIONS, AND VALUABLE

TIME WOULD NOW BE CONSUMED BY ACP-EU CONSULTATIONS ON HOW TO

PROCEED. MUGABE DID NOT SPECIFY WHICH SADC INSTITUTIONS WOULD BE

INVITED, BUT THE SADC PARLIAMENTARY FORUM\'S SECRETARY-GENERAL TOLD

US THAT BOTH THE FORUM AND THE COUNCIL OF FOREIGN MINISTERS WOULD

BE INCLUDED. INTERLOCUTORS FROM THE CANADIAN AND AUSTRALIAN HIGH

COMMISSIONS TOLD US THAT IT WOULD TAKE AT LEAST A WEEK FROM THE

DATE OF RECEIPT OF A GOZ INVITATION FOR THE COMMONWEALTH TO DEPLOY

AN OBSERVER TEAM, AND POINTED OUT THAT MUGABE HAD NOT BEEN EXPLICIT

ABOUT WHEN SUCH AN INVITATION MIGHT BE ISSUED. MUGABE DID NOT

MENTION IFES OR THE CARTER CENTER, AND WE ARE NOT AWARE THAT THE

NAACP HAS ANY CAPACITY FOR OR INTEREST IN OBSERVING THE ZIMBABWEAN

ELECTION. THE BOTTOM LINE IS THAT THE LIST ANNOUNCED BY MUGABE WAS

CONCOCTED IN ORDER TO BUY TIME AND TO ENSURE AS LITTLE SCRUTINY AS

POSSIBLE, WHILE TRYING TO MAKE THE GOZ APPEAR FORTHCOMING.

MUGABE\'S CALL FOR A REDUCTION IN POLITICAL VIOLENCE AND VOW TO DO

EVERYTHING POSSIBLE TO ENSURE A FREE AND FAIR ELECTION ARE

CERTAINLY POSITIVE MESSAGES, BUT ONLY DEVELOPMENTS ON THE GROUND

WILL SHOW WHETHER HE IS SINCERE. WE ARE NOT OPTIMISTIC.

3. (U) BEGIN TEXT OF PRESIDENT MUGABE\'S JANUARY 28 REMARKS:

SINCE MY ANNOUNCEMENT ON 9 JANUARY THAT THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION

WOULD BE HELD ON THE 9TH AND 10TH OF MARCH 2002, MY GOVERNMENT HAS

TAKEN CERTAIN STEPS, AND SET ON COURSE VARIOUS PROCESSES TO

FACILITATE THE HOLDING OF A SMOOTH POLITICAL PROCESS. I WISH TO

EXPRESS MY SATISFACTION WITH THE GOVERNMENT\'S PREPARATIONS SO FAR

FOR THE ELECTION.

THE FORTHCOMING ELECTION WILL, ONCE AGAIN, UNDERLINE ZIMBABWE\'S

ESTABLISHED DEMOCRATIC TRADITION SINCE ITS INDEPENDENCE IN 1980, OF

HOLDING REGULAR PARLIAMENTARY AND PRESIDENTIAL ELECTIONS AS

9. INDIVIDUALS AND COUNTRIES TO BE INVITED IN THEIR OWN CAPACITIES

STAKEHOLDERS IN THE COUNTRY, INCLUDING ALL THE POLITICAL PARTIES,

BUSINESS ASSOCIATIONS, MEDIA HOUSES, WOMEN AND YOUTH ORGANIZATIONS,

AS WELL AS OTHER CIVIC ORGANIZATIONS, TO COME TOGETHER TO EXCHANGE

VIEWS WITH THE OBJECTIVE OF ENDING INCIDENTS OF VIOLENCE OCCURRING

IN SOME AREAS SO AS TO CREATE AN ATMOSPHERE CONDUCIVE TO THE

HOLDING OF A FREE, FAIR AND PEACEFUL ELECTION. IN THIS REGARD I

SHALL BE INVITING THE ABOVE MENTIONED GROUPS TO CHART A PEACEFUL

WAY FORWARD.
