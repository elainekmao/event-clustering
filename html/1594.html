SUBJECT: NEW ELECTIONS FOR ZIMBABWE\'S UMBRELLA LABOR

ORGANIZATION NOT LIKELY.

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\' AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS 1.5 (B/D).

1. (SBU) THE MARCH 18 EDITION OF THE GOVERNMENT OF

ZIMBABWE (GOZ)-CONTROLLED "THE SUNDAY MAIL" RAN A FRONT-

PAGE STORY STATING THAT THE JUST-ELECTED LEADERSHIP OF

THE ZIMBABWE CONFEDERATION OF TRADE UNIONS (ZCTU),

ZIMBABWE\'S INDEPENDENT UMBRELLA LABOR ORGANIZATION, WAS

BEING PRESSURED TO HOLD NEW ELECTIONS BECAUSE OF

IRREGULARITIES AT ITS FEBRUARY 23-24 FIVE-YEAR CONGRESS.

NO OTHER NEWS ORGANIZATIONS HAVE REPORTED ON THIS

ALLEGED DEVELOPMENT. THE ALLEGED IRREGULARITIES ARE NOT

RELATED TO THE ELECTION OF THE NEW ZCTU NATIONAL

LEADERSHIP. RATHER, THE IRREGULARITIES ARE THAT THE

JUST-COMPLETED CONGRESS FAILED TO CONFIRM OR REJECT

AMENDMENTS AND CORRECTIONS MADE TO THE ZCTU CONSTITUTION

AT THE 1995 CONGRESS. ACCORDING TO THE ARTICLE, 15 OF

THE 32 UNIONS AFFILIATED TO THE ZCTU ARE NOW CLAIMING

THAT THE ELECTIONS OF THE NEW LEADERSHIP ARE NULLIFIED,

AND DEMANDING THAT NEW ELECTIONS BE HELD.

2. (C) LABOFF, THOUGH UNABLE TO REACH ANYONE AT THE

ZCTU, DID TALK WITH GEBRE GEBREMARIAM (PROTECT), THE

AMCIT REGIONAL REPRESENTATIVE OF THE AFL-CIO SOLIDARITY

CENTER IN HARARE, WHO MAINTAINS CLOSE TIES WITH THE ZCTU

LEADERSHIP. GEBREMARIAM STATED THAT THE ARTICLE WAS, TO

THE BEST OF HIS KNOWLEDGE, GOZ PROPAGANDA AIMED AT

DISCREDITING THE NEW ZCTU EXECUTIVE LEADERSHIP (WHOSE

MEMBERS ALL SUPPORT THE OPPOSITION MOVEMENT FOR

DEMOCRATIC CHANGE). HE OPINED THAT THE ALLEGED

IRREGULARITIES MENTIONED IN THE ARTICLE, EVEN IF TRUE,

WOULD NOT NECESSITATE THE HOLDING OF NEW ELECTIONS FOR

THE ZCTU LEADERSHIP. HE WAS ALSO VERY SKEPTICAL THAT 15

OF THE 32 AFFILIATED UNIONS DESIRED THAT NEW ELECTIONS

BE HELD.

3. (C) COMMENT: "THE SUNDAY MAIL" ARTICLE DEFINITELY

SMACKS OF A GOZ/ZANU-PF PROPAGANDA CAMPAIGN TO DISCREDIT

THE NEW ZCTU LEADERSHIP. THE ZCTU IS A MAJOR PLAYER IN

ZIMBABWE POLITICS, AND COULD HOLD A VERY INFLUENTIAL, IF

NOT DECISIVE, ROLE IN THE UPCOMING PRESIDENTIAL

ELECTIONS. THE GOZ/ZANU-PF ARE STILL FUMING THAT,

HAVING FUNDED THE PAYMENT OF BACK-DUES OF UNIONS TO THE

ZCTU AND, ALLEGEDLY, BRIBING INDIVIDUAL UNION DELEGATES

IN ORDER TO INFLUENCE THE ZCTU ELECTIONS, NOT ONE PRO-

ZANU-PF CANDIDATE WAS ELECTED TO THE ZCTU LEADERSHIP.

THEY WOULD DEARLY LOVE TO HAVE ANOTHER SHOT AT GAINING

CONTROL OF THE ZCTU, AND THE HOLDING OF NEW ELECTIONS

FOR THE ZCTU LEADERSHIP WOULD PRESENT THIS OPPORTUNITY.

WHILE "THE SUNDAY MAIL" ARTICLE IS THE FIRST ATTEMPT AT

FORCING NEW ELECTIONS, WE STRONGLY DOUBT IT WILL BE THE

LAST. END COMMENT.
