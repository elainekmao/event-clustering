PRETORIA FOR AG ATTACHE

E.O. 12958: DECL: 08/14/05

TAGS: EAGR, ECON, PGOV, ZI, Land Reform

SUBJECT: ZIMBABWE\'S COMMERCIAL FARMERS DECIDE TO GO

BACK TO THE COURTS TO PREVENT SEIZURE OF 3,000 FARMS

CLASSIFIED BY AMBASSADOR MCDONALD, REASON 1.5 (B), (D)

REFS: A) HARARE 4662, B) HARARE 4479, C) HARARE 4362

1. (U) AT THE COMMERCIAL FARMERS\' UNION (CFU) ANNUAL

CONGRESS THAT BEGAN SEPTEMBER 6, CFU PRESIDENT TIM

HENWOOD ANNOUNCED IN HIS OPENING SPEECH THAT THE CFU

WOULD REFILE A CLASS ACTION SUIT, WITHDRAWN A MONTH

AGO (REF B), TO CHALLENGE THE LAW GOVERNING THE

COMPULSORY ACQUISITION OF LAND FOR RESETTLEMENT. THE

CFU STATED THAT IT WAS TAKING THE ACTION AS A RESULT

OF THE BREAKDOWN IN NEGOTIATIONS WITH THE GOVERNMENT,

THE HALTING OF POLICE ACTION TO EVICT SQUATTERS, AND

BECAUSE ITS MEMBERSHIP UNANIMOUSLY AGREED TO

VIGOROUSLY PURSUE THEIR LEGAL REMEDIES. IN HIS SPEECH

HENWOOD STATED, "WE HAVE AGAIN SEEN MINISTERS\'

INSTRUCTIONS COUNTERMANDED AND POLICE ACTION

CURTAILED. MEANWHILE MASSIVE LISTS HAVE BEEN

PUBLISHED OF FARMS TO BE ACQUIRED, AND BANKS TELL US

THERE WILL BE NO FINANCE FOR AFFECTED FARMERS. ....IF

THE PLAN IS CARRIED OUT IN ITS CURRENT FORM, MOST

ZIMBABWEANS WOULD SEE THEIR LIVES SLUMP TO A DEPTH OF

POVERTY NEVER BEFORE SEEN IN THIS PART OF THE WORLD."

HENWOOD ALSO SAID THAT DUE TO THE HISTORY OF

INTIMIDATION, VIOLENCE AND DAMAGE TO PROPERTY ON THE

FARMS, THE UNION FEARED THAT IF INDIVIDUAL FARMERS

FILED SUITS, THAT THEY WOULD BE SINGLED OUT FOR

PUNSIHMENT AND RETRIBUTION.

2. (U) THE NEW CFU SUIT (AS IN THE EARLIER, WITHDRAWN

LEGAL ACTION, REF C), CITES PRESIDENT MUGABE, POLICE

COMMISSIONER CHIHURI AND WAR VETS LEADER CHENJARAI

"HITLER" HUNZVI AS RESPONDENTS. THE CLASS ACTION

CHALLENGES THE AUTHORITY OF THE GOVERNMENT TO TAKE

LAND FROM AN INDIVIDUAL WITHOUT COMPENSATION. IT

QUESTIONS THE VALIDITY OF THE PRESIDENTIAL POWERS ACT

THAT HAS BEEN USED TO AMEND THE LAND ACQUISITION ACT

OF 1992, CITING THE LACK OF APPROPRIATE PARLIAMENTARY

OVERSIGHT AND AUTHORITY. THE CFU HOPES TO FILE THE

SUIT BEFORE THE END OF THIS WEEK.

3. (C) COMMENT: WE WOULD WELCOME AN AGREEMENT BETWEEN

THE CFU AND THE GOZ. HOWEVER, THE CFU MEMBERSHIP IS

CLEARLY FRUSTRATED AND ANGERED AT WHAT IT SEES AS A

TOTAL LACK OF GOOD FAITH BY THE GOVERNMENT ESPECIALLY

IN LIGHT OF ITS WITHDRAWAL OF ITS LEGAL CHALLENGE AT

GOVERNMENT\'S BEHEST A MONTH AGO. IN SUM, NEGOTIATIONS

HAVE GONE NOWHERE, AND SPECIFICALLY, IN THAT TIME THE

GOZ HAS NOT ENGAGED IN ANY SERIOUS DIALOGUE OR

NEGOTIATION WITH THE UNION, AND HAS CONSISTENTLY

BROKEN ITS PROMISIES AND COMMITMENTS. IN MEETINGS THE

AMBASSADOR HAS HAD WITH A HOST OF MINISTERS, IT IS

CLEAR TO US THAT CONFUSION REIGNS THROUGHOUT

GOVERNMENT ON THE ISSUE, WITH ONLY THE PRESIDENT\'S

OFFICE PROVIDING SPORADIC, DICTATORIAL DIRECTION. THE

REST OF THE CABINET MOUTHS THE ZANU-PF PARTY LINE IN

DIRECT DEFIANCE OF REALITY. THE BOTTOM LINE IS THE

GOZ HAS NO LAND REFORM "PROGRAM." THE SEIZURE AND

RESETTLEMENT EFFORT, CARRIED OUT IN NO SMALL PART BY

VIOLENT AND ILLEGAL MEANS, IS A STAKE POISED OVER THE

ECONOMIC HEART OF ZIMBABWE. HOWEVER, NO ONE IN

CABINET IS WILLING TO TELL MUGABE THAT HIS POLITICAL

CALCULUS MAY WELL BE INCORRECT, AND THAT THE ONLY

SEEDS HE IS SOWING ARE SEEDS OF DISASTER. WITH VOICES

FROM WITHIN SILENT OR IGNORED, WE FEAR THAT VOICES

FROM WITHOUT WILL GET LESS RESPECT. MUGABE, WITH HIS

EYES ON THE 2002 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION, WILL GO IT

ALONE AND CONTINUE TO USE THE LAND PROBLEM AS A

BLATANT POLITICAL TOOL, EVEN AT THE COST OF A

FUNCTIONING ECONOMY AND THE COLLAPSE OF NATIONAL

INCOME. END COMMENT.
