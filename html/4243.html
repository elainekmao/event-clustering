1. SUMMARY: DURING THE UN FOOD AND AGRICULTURE

ORGANIZTION (FAO) COUNCIL\'S 123RD SESSION (OCTOBER 28-

OVEMBER 2), THE U.S. DELEGATION WAS SUCCESSFUL IN

ENSURING THAT THE TERMS OF REFERENCE OF AN INTERSSSIONAL

"FRIENDS OF THE CHAIR" GROUP ON DIRECTOR ENERAL TERM

LIMITS DID NOT PREJUDGE THE OUTCOME F THE GROUP\'S

DELIBERATIONS. USDEL HAD SOME SUCESS IN SHAPING THE

TERMS OF REFERENCE OF THE INTRGOVERNMENTAL WORKING GOUP

ON THE PROGRESSIVE RELIZATION OF THE RIGHT TO FOOD,

PARTICULARLY IN ENURING THAT THE FAO (RATHER THAN THE

OFFICE OF TH HIGH COMMISSIONER FOR HUMAN RIGHTS) HAS THE

LEA IN SERVICING THE GROUP. THE COUNCIL ADOPTED A

UNCLASSIFIED

PAGE 04 ROME 05446 01 OF 11 100135Z

EVISED CODE OF CONDUCT ON THE DISTRIBUTION AND USEOF

PESTICIDES; THE UNITED STATES DID NOT BLOCK COSENSUS,

BUT REGISTERED STRONG DISAPPOINTMENT THAT THE COUNCIL WAS

ADOPTING A CODE WHICH DID NOT COMMAND INDUSTRY SUPPORT.

ON PROGRAM AND BUDGET ISSUES, AUSTRALIA, SUPPORTED BY THE

U.S., TOOK THE LEAD IN ENSURING THAT THE COUNCIL

RECOGNIZED THE NEED TO ENSURE FUNDING FOR INTERNATIONAL

PLANT PROTECTION CONVENTION ACTIVITIES SO THAT FOUR

STANDARDS A YEAR COULD BE COMPLETED. COUNCIL MEMBERS

GREETED AMBASSADOR HALL\'S ANNOUNCEMENT OF U.S. SIGNING OF

THE TREATY ON INTERNATIONAL LANT GENETIC RESOURCES WITH

ENTHUSIASTIC APPLAUS. IMPORTANT WORK AHEAD INCLUDES

SUBMITTING U.S. VIEWS ON VOLUNTARY GUIDELINES ON THE

RIGHT TO FOOD FOR THE FIRST SESSION OF THE WORKING GROUP

AND WORKING WITH THE FRIENDS OF THE CHAIR TO FIND A

SATISFACTORY FORMULATION ON TERM LIMITS FOR SUBMISSION TO

THE NEXT COUNCIL. END SUMMARY.
