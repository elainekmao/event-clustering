2. (SBU) ILLUSTRATING BOTH ITS REACH AND DEPTH AS ITALY\'S

LARGEST TRADE UNION CONFEDERATION, CGIL (THE GENERAL

CONFEDERATION OF ITALIAN WORKERS) BROUGHT MUCH OF ITALY TO A

CRAWL, IF NOT A COMPLETE HALT, DURING ITS ONE-DAY STRIKE

OCTOBER 18. THE EIGHT-HOUR ACTION CREATED SUBSTANTIAL

DISRUPTION THE LENGTH OF THE PENINSULA, ESPECIALLY IN THE

TRANSPORT SECTOR. IT ALSO GENERATED FURTHER ACRIMONY

BETWEEN CGIL AND THE OTHER TWO MAJOR CONFEDERATIONS, AS THEY

SPARRED OVER THE STRIKE\'S IMPACT AND UTILITY, THAT WILL MAKE

THEIR RECONCILIATION MORE DIFFICULT. THE ONE-DAY STRIKE MAY

MARK THE END OF A PHASE IN WHICH THE THREE CONFEDERATIONS

HAVE SPENT MUCH OF THEIR ENERGY ARGUING AMONG THEMSELVES

OVER THEIR RELATIVE STRENGTH AND INFLUENCE RATHER THAN

PRESENTING A COMMON FRONT TO EMPLOYERS AND THE GOVERNMENT.

AT LEAST, THAT\'S WHAT POLITICAL LEADERS ACROSS THE CENTER-

LEFT HOPE. CIRCUMSTANCES SHOULD HELP THE CONFEDERATIONS

PATCH THINGS UP: THE BUDGET CRUNCH AND A CRISIS AT FIAT AUTO

HAVE PROVIDED ORGANIZED LABOR, IN ALL ITS CONFIGURATIONS,

WITH A TANGIBLE THREAT TO ITS MEMBERS THAT IS WORTH FIGHTING

OVER.

TIMING IS EVERYTHING

--------------------

3. (SBU) FOR CGIL, THE GAMBLE OF CALLING A STRIKE

INDEPENDENT OF THE OTHER CONFEDERATIONS (THE SECOND IN SIX

MONTHS AFTER ALMOST 40 YEARS OF JOINT STRIKES) APPEARS TO

HAVE PAID OFF. BUT IT\'S FAIR TO SAY THAT THE CONFEDERATION

WAS PERHAPS LUCKY RATHER THAN BRILLIANT. WHEN CGIL

ORIGINALLY CALLED THE STRIKE IN JUNE, ITS MOTIVE WAS LARGELY

TO OBJECT TO THE OTHER TWO (CISL AND UIL) CONFEDERATIONS\'

DECISION TO SIGN A NEW LABOR PACT WITH THE GOVERNMENT AND

EMPLOYERS THAT INCLUDED THE BERLUSCONI GOVERNMENT\'S PROPOSED

CHANGES TO ARTICLE 18 OF THE LABOR CODE (WHICH PROVIDE SMALL

EMPLOYERS MORE FLEXIBILITY TO FIRE WORKERS). ALTHOUGH

SYMBOLICALLY IMPORTANT, IT WAS HARDLY THE ISSUE THAT MANY

EXPECTED TO GALVANIZE PUBLIC OPINION OR PROMPT CHANGES IN

THE GOVERNMENT\'S APPROACH TO LABOR ISSUES (GIVEN THAT FEW

EXPECTED A SURGE IN SMALL FIRMS\' HIRING DUE TO THE ADDED

FLEXIBILITY).

4.(SBU) BUT A FUNNY THING HAPPENED ON THE WAY TO THE STRIKE:

TWO ISSUES WITH ENORMOUS PRACTICAL SIGNIFICANCE EMERGED.

FIRST, THE GOVERNMENT RELEASED ITS PROPOSED 2003 BUDGET (SEE

REFTELS FOR FULL ANALYSIS) TO WIDESPREAD OPPOSITION.

FINANCE MINISTER TREMONTI PROPOSED CUTS (DESIGNED TO KEEP

ITALY IN LINE WITH EU FISCAL CRITERIA) THAT INCLUDED

SUBSTANTIAL CONSTRAINTS ON PUBLIC FUNDS TO PROMOTE ECONOMIC

DEVELOPMENT AND INVESTMENT IN SOUTHERN ITALY (WHERE

UNEMPLOYMENT RUNS ABOVE 20%). AFTER INITIAL ANALYSES

REVEALED THE CUTS COULD JEOPARDIZE THE GOVERNMENT\'S ABILITY

TO MEET ITS UNEMPLOYMENT BENEFITS AND TAX REDUCTION

COMMITMENTS UNDER THE NATIONAL LABOR PACT NEGOTIATED IN

JULY, CISL AND UIL WARNED THEY WOULD HEAD FOR THE STREETS IF

THERE WERE ANY REDUCTIONS IN AGREED MEASURES OR OTHER

ALTERATIONS TO OTHER ELEMENTS OF THE PACT.

5. (SBU) THEN FIAT ANNOUNCED PLANS (SEE SEPTEL) TO STABILIZE

ITS AILING AUTO DIVISION WITH SHOCK THERAPY, PROPOSING PLANT

CLOSURES THAT WOULD IDLE OVER 8,000 WORKERS (INCLUDING

ALMOST 2,000 AT A PLANT IN WESTERN SICILY). IN A SOCIETY

WHERE LABOR MOBILITY IS THE EXCEPTION (AND SOUTHERN WORKERS

ESPECIALLY RESIST HEADING NORTH FOR WORK), THE PLANNED

CLOSURES FUELED A FIRESTORM OF PROTEST. WORKERS AT THE

SICILIAN PLANT BEGAN A SERIES OF WORK STOPPAGES AND SHUT

DOWN TRANSPORTATION ACROSS MUCH OF THE ISLAND, BLOCKING

FREEWAYS AND RAIL LINES. LOCAL OFFICIALS AND SICILIAN

PARLIAMENTARIANS JOINED UNION LEADERS IN DENOUNCING THE

PLANNED CUTS, CHARGING THE MOVE WOULD LEAVE DISMISSED

WORKERS AND THEIR NEIGHBORS LITTLE ECONOMIC ALTERNATIVE BUT

TO COLLABORATE WITH ORGANIZED CRIME. "DON\'T LEAVE US WITH

THE MAFIA AT THE DOOR," PLEADED THE MAYOR OF ONE TOWN. CGIL

ASTUTELY SHIFTED THE MOTIVES FOR ITS STRIKE, DECLARING THAT

THE ACTION WAS INTENDED TO DEMONSTRATE MORE BROADLY THE

CONFEDERATION\'S OPPOSITION TO THE BERLUSCONI GOVERNMENT\'S

ECONOMIC POLICY.

6. (U) THE STRIKE PROMPTED WIDESPREAD DISRUPTION ACROSS

ITALY, AS THE CONFEDERATION STAGGERED ITS ACTIONS ACROSS THE

VARIOUS TRANSPORT SECTORS TO CREATE MAXIMUM DISRUPTION.

ALITALIA WAS FORCED TO CANCEL 275 FLIGHTS (50% OF ITS DAILY

SCHEDULE) AND NATIONAL RAIL CARRIER TRENITALIA DROPPED 40%

OF ITS MEDIUM AND LONG-HAUL ROUTES FOR THE DAY. UNION

MEMBERS IN L\'ACQUILA (WHERE CLOSURE OF ANOTHER FIAT PLANT

DUELING STATISTICS

7. (U) UNION LEADERS FANNED OUT ACROSS ITALY TO RALLY THE

FAITHFUL AND SPEAK AT THE DEMONSTRATIONS. FORMER CGIL

LEADER SERGIO COFFERATI, OSTENSIBLY THE MOST POPULAR FIGURE

WITHIN ITALY\'S FRACTURED, CONTENTIOUS CENTER-LEFT

OPPOSITION, MARCHED WITH HIS PIRELLI COLLEAGUES (AND 250,000

OTHERS, ACCORDING TO CGIL) THROUGH MILAN JUST DAYS AFTER

REJOINING HIS EMPLOYER FOLLOWING A 20-PLUS YEAR UNION

CAREER. HIS SUCCESSOR, GUGLIELMO EPIFANI, LED 200,000 OF

THE FAITHFUL IN TURIN (HEADQUARTERS OF FIAT\'S FAR-FLUNG

HOLDINGS, INCLUDING FIAT AUTO). RALLIES IN ROME AND NAPLES

DREW UPWARDS OF 150,000 AND 130,000, RESPECTIVELY, INCLUDING

MANY STUDENTS. (NOTE: ATTENDANCE ESTIMATES REPORTED BY

CGIL; INDPENDENT ESTIMATES HAVE NOT YET BEEN REPORTED BUT

COULD BE LOWER). JOINING THE PROCESSIONS WERE MOST OF THE

OPPOSITION CENTER-LEFT PARTIES\' LEADERS; SOME WITH

CONVICTION (IN THE CASE OF COMMUNIST RENEWAL LEADER

BERTINOTTI), OTHERS WITH A DEGREE OF PUZZLEMENT (IN THE CASE

OF DEMOCRATIC LEFT LEADER PIERO FASSINO AND FORMER PM

MASSIMO D\'ALEMA). FASSINO URGED THE THREE CONFEDERATIONS TO

REDEVELOP A UNIFIED POSITION, SUGGESTING THE OPPOSITION

(ALREADY FRACTURED) COULD ILL-AFFORD DIVISIONS WITHIN ONE OF

ITS KEY CONSTITUENCIES.

8. (U) THE STRIKE WAS A ROUSING SUCCESS, EPIFANI DECLARED;

CGIL HAD "BROUGHT MILLIONS INTO THE STREETS" TO PROTEST THE

GOVERNMENT\'S ECONOMIC POLICIES. THE CONFEDERATION ALSO

CITED HIGH PARTICIPATION IN SEVERAL SECTORS, INCLUDING OVER

50% OF TEACHERS. PARTICIPATION WAS ESPECIALLY HIGH IN THE

HEAVILY-INDUSTRIALIZED (AND UNIONIZED) NORTHWEST, WITH 60-70

PERCENT REPORTED AT FIAT AUTO. IN CONTRAST, CISL AND UIL

DOWNPLAYED THE STRIKE\'S SIGNIFICANCE, CLAIMING NO MORE THAN

A THIRD OF THE WORK FORCE PARTICIPATED AND SUGGESTING THERE

HAD BEEN LITTLE CROSSOVER SUPPORT TO CGIL FROM THEIR

MEMBERS. LABOR MINISTER MARONI CHARACTERIZED THE STRIKE AS

"MODEST," AND EXPRESSED THE GOVERNMENT\'S HOPE THAT THERE

COULD NOW BE A BROADER DIALOGUE AMONG ALL THREE PARTNERS -

LABOR, EMPLOYERS AND GOVERNMENT.

NOW WHAT?

9. (SBU) MANY OBSERVERS BELIEVE THAT, HAVING MADE ITS POINT,

CGIL WILL ONCE AGAIN FIND COMMON CAUSE WITH THE OTHER TWO

CONFEDERATIONS. IN THE WEEKS PRECEDING THE STRIKE, EPIFANI

BEGAN HINTING AS MUCH, AVOIDING ANY SUGGESTION THE

CONFEDERATION WOULD SET CISL AND UIL\'S RENUNCIATION OF THE

SUMMER\'S LABOR PACT AS THE PRICE OF RENEWED COOPERATION.

SEVERAL OBSERVERS POINT TO EPIFANI\'S MORE MEASURED REMARKS -

IN CONTRAST TO THOSE OF HIS COLORFUL PREDECESSOR, COFFERATI,

UNDER WHOSE LEADERSHIP CGIL CALLED THE STRIKE - AS EVIDENCE

THAT HE IS PREPARED TO MOVE ON. BUT THE LEADERS OF THE

OTHER TWO CONFEDERATIONS BOTH SIGNALED THAT ANY

RECONCILIATION WOULD DEPEND ON CGIL\'S WILLINGNESS TO MOVE IN

THEIR DIRECTION. UIL LEADER ANGELETTI DECLARED THE STRIKE

"USELESS; IT DIDN\'T SOLVE ANYTHING," AND CISL LEADER LUIGI

PEZZOTTA SAID HE WAS "FED UP" WITH CGIL CRITICISM OF THE

NATIONAL LABOR PACT SIGNED IN JULY. ANY DISCUSSION OF A

FORMAL UNITED FRONT - SOMETHING CISL HAD BEEN ASKING

COFFERATI AND CGIL TO DISCUSS FOR 18 MONTHS - WOULD BEGIN ON

THE BASIS OF THE LABOR PACT, PEZZOTTA DECLARED.

10. (SBU) BEHIND THE RHETORIC, THOUGH, THERE ARE SIGNS THE

THREE CONFEDERATIONS ARE IDENTIFYING COMMON GROUND.

METALWORKERS FROM THE CONSTITUENT UNIONS OF THE THREE

CONFEDERATIONS ARE DISCUSSING A COMMON STRATEGY AND TACTICS

TO TACKLE FIAT AUTO\'S RESTRUCTURING PLANS AND REPORTEDLY ARE

CONTEMPLATING A SECTORWIDE STRIKE IN THE NEXT FEW WEEKS.

THIS TECHNICAL-LEVEL COLLABORATION MAY LEAD TO BROADER

DISCUSSIONS BY CONFEDERATION LEADERSHIP OF A SHARED

FRAMEWORK. FROM OUR PERSPECTIVE AT LEAST, THAT SHOULDN\'T

PROVE TOO HARD; BETWEEN THE PROPOSED BUDGET AND THE FIAT

AUTO CRISIS, THERE\'S PLENTY ON WHICH THE THREE

CONFEDERATIONS SHOULD BE ABLE TO AGREE. A UNITED FRONT

WOULD CREATE A SMOOTHER NEGOTIATING PROCESS (THOUGH PERHAPS

A MORE EXPENSIVE PRICE TAG) FOR THE BERLUSCONI GOVERNMENT AS
