SUBJECT: RWANDAN PRESIDENT\'S VISIT TO HARARE

REF: KIGALI 1856

CONFIDENTIAL

PAGE 02 HARARE 01572 101616Z

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\'AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS: 1.5 (B) AND (D).

1. (C) THE ZIMBABWE FOREIGN MINISTRY\'S DIRECTOR FOR

AFRICA, GODFREY MWEDZI, SPOKE TO POLOFF ON MAY 10 ABOUT

RWANDAN PRESIDENT KAGAME\'S ONE-DAY VISIT TO HARARE ON

MAY 7. MWEDZI STATED THAT KAGAME\'S LUNCH MEETING WITH

PRESIDENT MUGABE WAS "EXCLUSIVELY BETWEEN THEM" AND DID

NOT INCLUDE ANY MINISTERS OR OTHER GOZ OFFICIALS. ONLY

ON ONE OCCASION DID KAGAME BRIEFLY MEET WITH ZIMBABWEAN

MINISTERS. THE ZIMBABWEAN DIPLOMAT STATED THAT

DEBRIEFINGS ON WHAT WAS DISCUSSED HAD NOT YET TRICKLED

DOWN TO HIS LEVEL (WHICH IS JUST BELOW THAT OF PERMANENT

SECRETARY). MWEDZI STATED THAT THE PRESIDENTIAL MEETING

SIPDIS

WAS CORDIAL, AND THEY SURELY DISCUSSED THE DRC, BUT IT

WOULD BE "PURE SPECULATION" ON HIS PART TO OFFER ANY

DETAILS OF THE DISCUSSION. MWEDZI TRIED TO DOWNPLAY THE

SIGNIFICANCE OF KAGAME\'S VISIT, STATING THAT ZIMBABWE

AND RWANDA WERE NOT AT WAR, AND THEY CONTINUED TO

MAINTAIN NORMAL DIPLOMATIC RELATIONS; RWANDA DID SO

THROUGH ITS MISSION IN PRETORIA.

2. (C) COMMENT: DESPITE THE DIPLOMAT\'S DOWNPLAYING, IT

IS SIGNIFICANT THAT KAGAME WOULD COME TO HARARE, HIS

FIRST PUBLIC VISIT HERE SINCE THE WAR IN THE DRC BROKE

OUT IN 1998 (KAGAME MADE A SURREPTITIOUS VISIT BROKERED

BY SOUTH AFRICAN PRESIDENT MBEKI IN 1999). WE CAN

SURMISE THAT KAGAME SOUGHT TO BREAK SOME OF THE ICE THAT

HAS FORMED IN THE RWANDAN-ZIMBABWEAN RELATIONSHIP, AND

ALSO TO SHOW THAT HE CAN BE A REASONABLE STATESMAN WHO

IS NOT INTERNATIONALLY ISOLATED. IN ADDITION, KAGAME

UNDOUBTEDLY WANTED TO ENSURE THAT RWANDAN INTERESTS,

SUCH DISARMING THE INTERAHAMWE IN THE DRC, ARE TAKEN

INTO ACCOUNT BY ZIMBABWE AT THIS CRUCIAL JUNCTURE IN THE

DRC PEACE PROCESS. NOT TO BE OUTDONE, THE UGANDANS SENT

A SPECIAL ENVOY WITHIN 24 HOURS OF KAGAME\'S DEPARTURE.

END COMMENT.
