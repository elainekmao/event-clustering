CLARIFICATION OF USG POSITION ON "FAST-TRACK"

REFTEL: HARARE 815

CLASSIFIED BY CHARGE D\' AFFAIRES EARL M. IRVING FOR

REASONS 1.5 (B/D).

1. (C) ON MARCH 13, CHARGE EARL IRVING AND ECONOFF

(NOTETAKER) MET WITH COMMERCIAL FARMERS UNION (CFU)

PRESIDENT TIM HENWOOD AND DIRECTOR DAVID HASLUCK AT

THEIR REQUEST TO DISCUSS THE USG\'S POSITION ON LAND

REFORM IN ZIMBABWE. HENWOOD ASKED THE CHARGE ABOUT THE

MARCH 11 ARTICLE THAT APPEARED IN THE "THE SUNDAY TIMES"

OF LONDON (THE PAPER AND INTERNET VERSIONS) ENTITLED

"BRITAIN SCORNS \'SINISTER\' BID TO END LAND GRAB." THIS

ARTICLE STATED THAT THERE EXISTS A "\'SINISTER\' AMERICAN-

BACKED DEAL TO END THE CRISIS FACING WHITE FARMERS IN

ZIMBABWE" OVER LAND REFORM. CHARGE TOLD HENWOOD THAT

THERE IS NO VALIDITY WHATSOEVER TO THE ARTICLE. HE

CONTINUED THAT THE USG WAS NOT PURSUING SECRET TALKS

WITH THE GOVERNMENT OF ZIMBABWE (GOZ) ON LAND REFORM,

AND THE OFFICIAL USG POSITION IS THAT WHILE WE SUPPORT

THE NEED FOR LAND REFORM IN ZIMBABWE, IT SHOULD BE DONE

IN A LEGAL AND TRANSPARENT MANNER, BASED ON THE

PRINCIPLES AGREED TO AT THE 1998 DONORS\' CONFERENCE HELD

IN ZIMBABWE. HENWOOD WAS REASSURED BECAUSE HE STATED

THAT THE "CFU IS FEELING A BIT LONELY THESE DAYS." THE

CHARGE REASSURED THE CFU EXECUTIVES THAT THE USG

REMAINED DEEPLY CONCERNED ABOUT THE BREAKDOWN IN THE

RULE OF LAW ON THE COMMERCIAL FARMS AND THE VIOLENCE

DIRECTED BY "WAR VETS" AT COMMERCIAL FARMERS AND THEIR

WORKERS. HE ADDED THAT WE CONTINUE TO COMMUNICATE THIS

CONCERN TO THE GOZ, AND THAT ACTING PRINCIPAL DEPUTY

ASSISTANT SECRETARY BELLAMY HAD FORTHRIGHTLY

COMMUNICATED THIS CONCERN TO THE GOZ DURING HIS RECENT

VISIT TO ZIMBABWE.

2. (C) HENWOOD AND HASLUCK BOTH INQUIRED ABOUT THE

STATUS OF THE ZIMBABWE DEMOCRACY AND ECONOMIC RECOVERY

ACT (ZDERA), AND THE PROCESS BY WHICH THE ACT COULD

BECOME LAW. THE CHARGE PROVIDED THE CFU EXECUTIVES WITH

A COPY OF THE ZDERA INTRODUCED IN THE SENATE, AND

EXPLAINED THAT THE PASSAGE OF THE ZDERA WAS NOT ASSURED,

BUT THAT RECENT GOZ ACTIONS AGAINST THE INDEPENDENT

PRESS AND THE JUDICIARY HAD PUT THE SPOTLIGHT BACK ON

THE POLITICAL SITUATION IN ZIMBABWE. HASLUCK ALSO ASKED

IF A NEW REGIONAL ASSISTANT SECRETARY HAD BEEN SELECTED

AS WELL AS A NEW AMBASSADOR FOR ZIMBABWE. THE CHARGE

CONFIRMED THAT WALTER KANSTEINER HAD BEEN PROPOSED AS

THE NEW ASSISTANT SECRETARY FOR AFRICA, AND WOULD LIKELY

BE CONFIRMED. WHILE A NEW AMBASSADOR HAS NOT BEEN

CHOSEN, HE INFORMED THEM THAT THE PROPOSED NEW DCM WAS

ROBERT WHITEHEAD. THE CHARGE OPINED THAT BOTH MEN HAVE

SOLID EXPERIENCE WITH AFRICA AND ZIMBABWE, AND ARE

EXCEPTIONALLY GOOD CHOICES.

3. (C) CHARGE ASKED HENWOOD AND HASLUCK ABOUT THE

UPCOMING MARCH 21 CFU SPECIAL CONGRESS, AND WHETHER WE

SHOULD EXPECT ANY SURPRISES OR "FIREWORKS" TO COME OUT

OF IT. HENWOOD ANSWERED THAT HE BELIEVES THE CURRENT

CFU EXECUTIVE HAS THE SOLID BACKING OF A MAJORITY OF CFU

MEMBERS, AND THAT HE DID NOT EXPECT THERE WOULD BE A

NOR, TO THE BEST OF HIS KNOWLEDGE, HAD ANY OTHER MEMBERS

4. (C) COMMENT: BOTH HASLUCK AND HENWOOD WERE CLEARLY

CONCERNED THAT THE USG HAD SHIFTED ITS POSITION ON THE

GOZ\'S "FAST-TRACK" PROGRAM, AND WERE EQUALLY RELIEVED TO

HEAR DIRECTLY FROM THE CHARGE THAT THERE WAS NO

SUBSTANCE TO THE RUMORS OF "A SECRET DEAL INVOLVING THE

AMERICANS." WE EMPHASIZED TO THE CFU EXECUTIVES THAT

THE USG WILL SUPPORT LAND REFORM IN ZIMBABWE, BUT ONLY

WHEN IT IS DONE IN A LEGAL, TRANSPARENT AND SUSTAINABLE

MANNER AND WITH THE SUPPORT OF ALL STAKEHOLDERS. AS IN

OTHER RECENT MEETINGS WITH CFU OFFICIALS, HASLUCK AND

HENWOOD WERE NOT VERY CONVINCING WHEN STATING THAT THEIR

LEADERSHIP WOULD NOT BE CHALLENGED AT THE SPECIAL

CONGRESS. THE MARCH 14 EDITION OF THE INDEPENDENT "THE

DAILY NEWS" CARRIED AN ARTICLE INDICATING THAT SWANEPOEL

STILL INTENDS TO PROPOSE AT THE CONGRESS A PLAN TO REACH

A COMPROMISE AGREEMENT BETWEEN THE CFU AND THE GOZ ON

LAND REFORM. THIS PLAN, AS REPORTED REFTEL, INCLUDES

REPLACING THE CURRENT CFU EXECUTIVE LEADERSHIP. WE DO

NOT HAVE A GOOD SENSE OF SWANEPOEL\'S BACKING AMONG THE

GENERAL CFU MEMBERSHIP, AND THEREFORE, CANNOT PREDICT

HOW MUCH SUPPORT HIS PLAN MAY GARNER FROM FARMERS WEARY

OF STRUGGLING WITH THE FARM INVASIONS AND THE CLIMATE OF

INTIMIDATION AND VIOLENCE FROM THE "WAR VETS". ONE

THING WE CAN PREDICT IS THAT THERE WILL LIKELY BE

FIREWORKS AT THE CFU\'S SPECIAL CONGRESS. END COMMENT.
