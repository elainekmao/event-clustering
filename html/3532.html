1. SUMMARY - US MISSION REPRESENTATIVES MET WITH FAO

DG JACQUES DIOUF TO DISCUSS FAO\'S ROLE IN THE

HUMANITARIAN CRISIS IN SOUTHERN AFRICA. WE URGED

FAO TO TAKE A PUBLIC STANCE ON BIOTECH FOODS TO

CLARIFY MISUNDERSTANDINGS OF POLITICAL LEADERS

BASED, AT LEAST IN PART, ON MISINFORMATION

DISSEMINATED BY FAO REPRESENTATIVES IN THE SUB-

REGION. DIOUF, IN A JOINT LETTER FROM WHO DG

BRUNDTLAND TO WFP ED MORRIS, PROPOSES A UN POLICY

WHICH, IN OUR VIEW, RAISES MORE DOUBTS ABOUT THE

POTENTIAL RISK OF CONSUMING U.S. FOOD AID THAN IT

SETTLES. END SUMMARY

2. ACTING PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVE AND US MISSION

AGRICULTURAL COUNSELOR MET WITH FAO DIRECTOR

GENERAL JACQUES DIOUF ON AUGUST 16 TO DISCUSS

FAO\'S ROLE IN QUELLING THE GROWING

MISUNDERSTANDING IN SOUTHERN AFRICA OF BIOTECH

FOOD AID BEING OFFERED BY THE UNITED STATES

THROUGH THE WORLD FOOD PROGRAM. U.S. REPS

REITERATED OUR REQUEST MADE IN PREVIOUS DEMARCHES

TO FAO THAT THE FAO COMMUNICATE THAT, TO THE BEST

OF ITS KNOWLEDGE, THERE IS NO EVIDENCE OF ANY RISK

TO HUMAN HEALTH FROM BIOTECH FOODS, SPECIFICALLY

FROM FOOD AID SHIPMENTS IN RECENT YEARS BY THE

FAO. US REPS CITED MISINFORMATION STILL BEING

DISSEMINATED BY THE FAO (ZIMBABWE IS A CASE IN

POINT).

3. DIOUF\'S RESPONSE WAS BUREAUCRATIC AND DEFENSIVE.

FAO CANNOT GET OUT IN FRONT OF WFP WHICH HAS BEEN

ASSIGNED RESPONSIBILITY OF COORDINATING THE UN

RESPONSE TO THE EMERGENCY SITUATION IN SOUTHERN

AFRICA, HE SAID. FURTHERMORE, QUESTIONS OF HUMAN

HEALTH SHOULD BE DIRECTED TO WHO, NOT FAO. HE

SAID FAO HAD CORRECTED INFORMATION PROVIDED TO THE

FIELD, BUT WOULD NOT PROMISE TO REITERATE THAT

CORRECTION. AND THE FAO HAD NO KNOWLEDGE OF

BIOTECH FOOD BEING INCLUDED IN ANY SHIPMENTS OF

FOOD AID TO DATE. HE SAID THE UNITED STATES WAS

NAIVE TO THINK THAT MERE INFORMATION COULD QUELL

CONCERNS, THAT THERE WERE VARIOUS POLITICAL ACTORS

BEHIND THE CURRENT FUROR IN SOUTHERN AFRICAN

COUNTRIES.

4. THE WRITTEN REPLY FROM DIOUF AND WHO DG BRUNTLAND

PLACES RESPONSIBILITY FOR RISK ASSESSMENT ON

RECIPIENT COUNTRIES SINCE FAO HAS NOT UNDERTAKEN

SUCH AN ASSESSMENT. IT STATES THAT, "BASED ON

NATIONAL INFORMATION FROM A VARIETY OF SOURCES FAO

AND WHO SHARE THE VIEW THAT THE CONSUMPTION OF GM

COMMODITIES CURRENTLY ON THE MARKET IS NOT LIKELY

TO PRESENT AN APPRECIABLE HUMAN HEALTH RISK," BUT

GOES ON TO RECOMMEND CONSIDERATION OF PROCESSING

TECHNIQUES SUCH AS MILLING. IT CALLS ON

RECIPIENTS TO "WEIGH THE SEVERE AND IMMEDIATE

CONSEQUENCES OF INSUFFICIENT FOOD SUPPLIES AGAINST

THE POSSIBILITY OF INCREASED RISKS TO HUMAN HEALTH

FROM CONSUMING FOODS DERIVED FROM MODERN

BIOTECHNOLOGY AND THE ADDITIONAL RISK TO

BIOLOGICAL DIVERSITY AND SUSTAINABLE AGRICULTURE."

IT CITES THE ONGOING PROCESS IN CODEX ALIMENTARIUS

WHICH IS EXPECTED TO RESULT IN GUIDELINES ON FOOD

SAFETY IN ANOTHER YEAR OR SO.

5. THE FAO HAS BEEN UNHELPFUL IN SEVERAL ASPECTS. US

REPRESENTATIVES POINTED OUT THAT FAO HAD APPROVED

OF PREVIOUS SHIPMENTS OF FOOD AID WHICH, IN ALL

LIKELIHOOD, HAD CONTAINED BIOTECH FOOD SINCE IT

WAS INTRODUCED IN THE MID-1990\'S IN THE U.S.

DIOUF DENIED KNOWLEDGE OF SUCH CONTENT. DIOUF

ALSO PURPORTED TO BE UNAWARE OF THE TOWN MEETING

IN LUSAKA REPORTED REFTEL AND SEEMED NOT TO

UNDERSTAND THAT FAO\'S VOICE COULD HAVE PLAYED A

POSITIVE ROLE, AS DID WFP\'S. (U.S. MISSION

REPRESENTATIVES CONFIRMED WITH FAO OFFICES IN ROME

THAT NO RANKING REPRESENTATIVE OF FAO WAS PRESENT

AT THE LUSAKA MEETING, ONLY A NOTETAKER.) DIOUF

DID SAY THAT FAO HAD TAKEN PROMPT ACTION TO

CORRECT THE MISINFORMATION REPORTED BY USAID

6. COMMENT - US MISSION BELIEVES THIS ISSUE HAS

REACHED A CRUCIAL STATE. FAO HAS NOT BEEN HELPFUL

IN REACHING A POSITIVE RESOLUTION OF THE SOUTHERN

AFRICA CRISIS. THIS HAS BECOME EVEN MORE SERIOUS

IN LIGHT OF ZAMBIA\'S DECISION AUGUST 16 TO REJECT

BIOTECH FOOD AID, SPECIFICALLY U.S. CORN. WE

BELIEVE THAT FAO SHOULD HAVE COMMUNICATED THAT

SCIENTIFIC EVIDENCE DEMONSTRATES THERE IS NO RISK

TO HUMAN HEALTH FROM CONSUMING BIOTECH FOOD AND

THAT THOSE RECEIVING FOOD AID THROUGH WFP HAVE NOT

REPORTED ANY NEGATIVE HEALTH EFFECTS. INSTEAD,

FAO FIELD REPRESENTATIVES HAVE CONTINUED TO

TRANSMIT INACCURATE INFORMATION ON BIOTECH FOOD

AND TO MISINTERPRET THE CARTAGENA PROTOCOL RAISING

NEEDLESS DOUBTS AND FEAR. WE REGRET THAT FAO HAS

NOT PROVIDED MEMBER GOVERNMENTS THE BENEFIT OF ITS

SCIENTIFIC EXPERTISE IN THE CURRENT FOOD SECURITY

CRISIS. ALTHOUGH FAO CONTINUES TO CHARACTERIZE

THE SITUATION IN TERMS OF MALNUTRITION, WE BELIEVE

THAT LIVES WILL BE LOST AS A RESULT OF FAO\'S

FAILURE TO ACT IN TIME. END COMMENT.
