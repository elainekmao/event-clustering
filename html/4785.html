SUBJECT: FINI AND FRATTINI ON THE EU CONVENTION

1. SUMMARY: ITALIAN DEPUTY PM AND GOI REPRESENTATIVE TO

THE EU CONVENTION GIANFRANCO FINI AND FM FRANCO FRATTINI

TOLD PARLIAMENT THAT POSITIONS WITHIN THE EU CONVENTION ARE

STILL DISTANT AND THE CONVENTION WILL PROBABLY TRANSMIT

"DIVERGENT" PROPOSALS TO THE INTER-GOVERNMENTAL CONFERENCE

(IGC). THEY STRESSED ITALY\'S WISH TO SIGN A NEW EU TREATY

DURING ITS PRESIDENCY IN THE SECOND HALF OF 2003, AND

REMARKED THAT ITALY\'S APPROACH IN THE IGC WILL BE "OPEN AND

FLEXIBLE." FINI PROPOSED INSERTING A CLAUSE ON RELIGION IN

THE FUTURE EU TREATY. END SUMMARY.

2. DEPUTY PM FINI AND FM FRATTINI APPEARED BEFORE A JOINT

SESSION OF THE SENATE AND CHAMBER OF DEPUTIES FOREIGN

AFFAIRS COMMITTEES DECEMBER 17 TO TALK ABOUT PROGRESS ON

THE EU CONVENTION. FINI ARGUED AT LENGTH ON THE NEED FOR A

"CONSTITUTIONAL ARCHITECTURE" FOR EUROPE AND OF ITS

IMPORTANCE WHEN THE EU ENLARGES TO 25 COUNTRIES. HE SAID,

HOWEVER, THAT POSITIONS WITHIN THE CONVENTION ARE STILL

DISTANT AND THAT IT WAS UNLIKELY TO REACH AGREEMENT ON THE

CHAPTER ON INSTITUTIONAL REFORM IN THE CONVENTION\'S DRAFT

CONSTITUTIONAL TREATY.

3. THIS NOTWITHSTANDING, FINI SAID THE EU-15 AGREE THAT AN

ENLARGED EU CAN CONTINUE TO FUNCTION BY KEEPING ITS CURRENT

BALANCE OF POWERS, THAT IS, THE CURRENT "INSTITUTIONAL

TRIANGLE" BETWEEN THE EU COUNCIL, THE EU COMMISSION, AND

THE EU PARLIAMENT. HE ARGUED THAT REFORM MUST CONTINUE

WHILE PRESERVING THE CURRENT "BALANCE AMONG FUNDAMENTAL

POWERS." EUROPEAN PUBLIC OPINION, HE SAID, WANTS THE EU TO

BE "SIMPLE, TRANSPARENT, AND EFFECTIVE."

4. FINI DID NOT OFFER MUCH DETAIL ON THE STATE OF PROGRESS

IN THE CONVENTION. HE DID SAY THAT IN A 25-MEMBER EUROPE,

THE CURRENT SIX-MONTH ROTATIONAL PRESIDENCY WILL NOT WORK

AND THAT THE PREVAILING FEELING WITHIN THE CONVENTION IS TO

ADOPT A ROTATIONAL PRESIDENCY OF "AT LEAST" TWO YEARS.

THIS COULD BE COUNTER-BALANCED BY GIVING THE EUROPEAN

PARLIAMENT A STRONGER ROLE: THE EU COUNCIL COULD NOMINATE

THE EU ROTATIONAL PRESIDENT AND THE EUROPEAN PARLIAMENT

COULD "RATIFY AND SUPPORT" THAT DECISION BY MAJORITY VOTE.

5. FINI SAID THERE IS A "WIDE AND SATISFACTORY"

CONVERGENCE OF POSITIONS ON SEVERAL POINTS, MANY OF WHICH

HAVE THE GOI\'S SUPPORT. AGAIN, HE DID NOT OFFER MUCH

INFORMATION, LIMITING HIMSELF TO SAYING THESE POINTS

INCLUDE INVESTING THE EU WITH "FULL LEGAL RESPONSIBILITY"

AND ADOPTING THE PRINCIPLE OF SUBSIDIARITY IN DISTRIBUTING

POWERS AND AUTHORITY.

6. ON SUBSIDIARITY, FINI NOTED THAT THE EU WILL PROBABLY

WANT TO GIVE NATIONAL PARLIAMENTS A "STRONG ROLE OF

VERIFICATION," INCLUDING THE POWER TO APPEAL TO THE

EUROPEAN COURT OF JUSTICE IF THEY DETECT VIOLATIONS. HE

ADDED THAT THERE IS "UNANIMOUS CONVERGENCE" AT THE

CONVENTION ON NOT WEIGHING DOWN THE FUTURE EU CONSTITUTION

WITH A "RIGID LIST OF POWERS," BUT MAKING IT INSTEAD

"FLEXIBLE."

7. ON RELIGION, HE SAID A POSSIBILITY WOULD BE TO INCLUDE

IN ARTICLE 34 ON THE PRINCIPLES OF PARTICIPATORY DEMOCRACY

A SUB-ARTICLE REGARDING STRUCTURED DIALOGUE BETWEEN THE EU

AND CHURCHES (UNION OF CHURCHES), LEAVING EACH MEMBER STATE

FREE TO DEVELOP THE ISSUE AS IT DEEMS IT BEST FOR ITS OWN

COUNTRY. DEBATE ON THIS POINT WAS STRONGER OUTSIDE THE

CONVENTION THAN DURING ITS SESSIONS, HE COMMENTED.

8. FINI REMARKED THAT THE CONVENTION WILL INEVITABLY

TRANSMIT TO THE INTER-GOVERNMENTAL CONFERENCE (IGC) A

SERIES OF "DIVERGENT" OPTIONS. HE AND FRATTINI STRESSED

THAT ITALY WILL REMAIN "OPEN AND FLEXIBLE." THEY SAID IT

WILL MAKE EVERY EFFORT (IT IS ITS "RIGHT" AND ITS "DUTY,"

FINI SAID) TO HOLD -- AND CONCLUDE -- THE IGC DURING ITS EU

PRESIDENCY JULY-DECEMBER 2003, IN ORDER TO SIGN THE NEW EU

TREATY IN ROME. IT IS A QUESTION OF "PRESTIGE" FOR ITALY -

- THE TREATY CREATING THE EUROPEAN ECONOMIC COMMUNITY WAS

SIGNED IN ROME IN 1957; THE TREATY CREATING THE NEW EUROPE

OF 25 MUST BE SIGNED IN ROME IN 2003. THE NEW EUROPE WILL

THEN BE READY TO FUNCTION WITH ITS TEN NEW MEMBERS THAT

WILL JOIN IT IN MAY 2004 AND BEFORE THE EUROPEAN

PARLIAMENTARY ELECTIONS IN THE SUMMER OF 2004.

9. IN CONCLUSION, AFTER THANKING EU CONVENTION VICE

PRESIDENT GIULIANO AMATO FOR HIS WIDELY RECOGNIZED AND

"VERY VALUABLE" WORK, FINI AND FRATTINI SAID THEY WILL

RETURN TO PARLIAMENT OFTEN TO KEEP IT INFORMED OF THE

CONVENTION\'S PROGRESS AND TO SEEK ITS SUPPORT OF THE

ITALIAN GOVERNMENT\'S POSITIONS.
