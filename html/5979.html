SUBJECT: JORDAN REQUESTS SLIGHTLY LARGER QUANTITIES OF

SMALLPOX AND ANTHRAX VACCINES, AND ASKS FOR THE PRICE

REF: A. STATE 41006

B. AMMAN 1021

Classified By: PolCouns Doug Silliman for reasons 1.5 (B) and (D)

1. (U) This is an action request for the Department. See

paras 4-5 below.

--------------------------------------

GOJ WANTS VACCINES FOR CIVILIAN USE...

--------------------------------------

2. (S) Following up on ref b demarches to the MFA and

Jordan Armed Forces (JAF), Ambassador on February 23 called

on Health Minister Walid Maani and reiterated the U.S. offer

to provide -- on a reimbursable basis -- limited quantities

of smallpox and anthrax vaccines to Jordan. Prime Minister

Ali Abul Ragheb called the Ambassador February 23 and noted

that the GOJ would like to acquire 1,000 doses of smallpox

vaccine and 3,000 doses of anthrax vaccine to for use for

civilian health and emergency workers in the eastern regions

of the country (nearest to the Iraqi border). The PM said

the GOJ would try to find the funds to pay for these

purchases, and asked for the cost of the vaccines.

-------------------------------

...AND FOR THE MILITARY AS WELL

-------------------------------

3. (S) Concurrently, senior JAF officers indicated to the

DATT that the JAF would like to get from the U.S. 9,000 doses

of anthrax vaccine and 15,000 doses of smallpox vaccine. The

JAF asked for the cost of the vaccines before deciding if

they would use ACSA or FMF to pay for the vaccines.

--------------

ACTION REQUEST

--------------

4. (S) The GOJ has indicated to us that it would like

access to a total of 12,000 doses of anthrax vaccine and

16,000 doses of smallpox vaccine -- somewhat more than

offered in ref a. Embassy therefore requests that the

Department consider increasing the amounts of anthrax and

smallpox vaccines offered to Jordan to accommodate the

requests for both civilian an military use (i.e. 12,000 doses

of anthrax vaccine and 16,000 doses of smallpox vaccine).

5. (S) In addition, the Embassy also requests that

Washington provide separately the cost for the vaccines

requested for civilian use and those requested for military

use.
